Singlacia [2/1/2001-2:40:34 AM]  Comments[47]

"Who is Sgt. Pain orc?", a newbie asked as he was immediately slain by the orc elite that had suddenly showed up at the foot of Kelethin tonight. A group of GM's decided to show their might with a group of named lvl 40+ orcs on the Tholuxe Paells server.

Don't know what the command "/report" does? Absor explains it...

When you type /report it sends the lines of text that are currently visible in your chat window (to a GM for review). So if you want to report something, make certain that the text you want to send is in the chat window.

Monk Discussion Summary
Xanthus [1/30/2001-6:19:15 PM]  Comments[22]

Absor posted some of what he gathered from the Monk Discussion Thread:

NeoTwilight wrote:

>"Raise damage output by 10 to 15%. That would put monks nicely between Warriors and Rogues."<

The question here is, are monks supposed to do more damage than warriors and less than rogues. I don't know, really.

THE_FNORD said:

>"Give us triple attack"<

I'll assume that this might be one method of placing the monks damage output between warrior and rogues.

B_O_B said:

>"At 50 monks fists are 14/29 At 60 monks fists are 14/26 ( i think) Make it so fists become 17/26 so they can keep up with rogues."<

Ok. I'll add something like this to the list. Keep in mind, though, that I'm not a ratio person, and someone with more understanding of this will look at it.

THE_FNORD wrote:

>"Make one or all of our special attacks stun."<

I've now seen three requests from you on the first page. All one liners with no reasoning behind them. I'm going to have to leave such requests off the list. This isn't like the list of gifts young kids send to Santa every year. I need to know why these things should be done as a matter of balance.

DrewD wrote:

>"Too much armor and weapons for monks. Their "defining characteristic" was supposed to be unarmed combat, plus a refreshing indifference to loot since they had nothing to buy and couldn't carry it anyway. That all went out the window when items starting being used as kludges to increase the power of monks without really increasing the power of monks. Just go ahead and increase the power of monks directly. If you want it rare, implement some interesting monk quests where they can have their intrinsic AC or dam/delay raised through a quest rather than a item, possibly getting those quest items off the same mobs with the same frequencies as monk item drops now. They could be questhounds rather than gearhounds. But, alas, it's too late for that now, I guess."<

Well, it's never too late to ask. I like the idea of making monks more reliant on their hands than on weapons, on their skills than on armor. You're right, it might be too late to make such a dramatic change. But it warrants at least asking. There's no reason that a skill can't be just as hard to get as an item, just put the trainer at the end of a tough quest.

Pantheon wrote:

>"Throwing should get a chance of doing criticals"<

Interesting idea. This can also be used to increase monks damage a bit. That it, IF it needs to be increased. But if anyone is going to be the master of throwing, it may as well be monks.

Feilong Zai'Tian wrote:

>"Absor you can read many good suggestion here at…"<

Well, the whole idea of this thread is to get the ideas into one place where I can read them. I'm not going to run all over following links. If the ideas are good, sum them up and bring them in, I'll be glad to read them.

Terata wrote:

>"Beyond this my only real problem is the completely arbitrary way Feign Death starts working differently if the NPC is above level 32. Is there a purpose to this beyond making me stand up and hit Feign ten times just to be sure?"<

I can ask. I don't know if this was an 'arbitrary' decision or an informed one. But I can certainly ask.

brin_summer said:

>"- round kick(lv5), tiger claw(lv10), eagle strike(lv20), dragon punch(lv25) and flying kick(lv30). : currently, these serve nothing more than slight upgrade in dmg. but all of them are capped at 225...why? i have yet to see high level monk use anything other than flying kick because there is no reason to use lesser dmg-ing skills. : why not give each skill distinct trait? for example.. round kick would attain stunning ability(much like warrior's kick at 55+), tiger claw would attain debuff effect, dragon punch would do 2% of mob's health dmg, and flying kick would deal high dmg,etc.. i am sure you guys could come up with better traits."<

Certainly an interesting idea. It might be a nightmare to balance, though. And it might add too much to the power of the class. But it's a solid concept, so I'll add it.

Sskai wrote:

>"Something between the bo sticks/batons from Dalnir and the Wu's Quivering Staff/Wu's Trance Sticks from CoM would be nice. Decent 2hb for monks are rare anyway, and a few more weapons in the mid level ranges would encourage more people to train up their 2hb skill."<

Ok. Added to the list.

>"Please please make thunder kick less of an embarssment. Those 24 point kicks are just silly. Since monks get no special discipline at 55, how about adding one with a stun component?"<

That seems to be a good way to introduce a stun ability, if we decide to go that route.

Gestaltmind wrote:

>"Perhaps some old abilities might gain new twists as you got high level- like a criticial hit on a normal kick after you reach level 50."<

That thought occurred to me just as I was reading the first line of your post. I think you have chosen the right chat handle… I like this idea, it makes sense. But, once again, only if monks really need a combat boost.

Carissa wrote:

>"Lastly from me is something that I feel is above all these issues and that's look. I pulled up screenshots of myself from level 5. "<

Noted.

Devon11 wrote:

>"Monks should have greater damage evasion skills than they currently have post 50. By this I mean Dodge, Block, Riposte, and maybe another. They are martial arts experts after all."<

Well, this is something that I seem to have encountered with the shadow knight talk earlier. Some want offense, some want defense. Keep in mind that it is highly unlikely that getting both would be balanced. When compared to a warrior, a monk will either be more offensive and less defensive, or the other way around. Being better at both isn't balanced.

I'll have to find out what the, um…, Vision ™ is for this class, and then we'll look at both offense and defense.

Zadkiel said:

>"3a) Encourage usage and training of lesser monk special attacks.
Either allow us to use one type of strike and one type of kick at the same time by unhooking the special attack re-use timers post-50, or give the possibility of attack "strings". (This is the preferred solution, but would be harder to implement.) If a monk successfully uses Flying Kick on an enemy -- connects for damage -- then give a chance (based on Double Attack skill, preferably) for the monk to automatically use Dragon Punch/Tail Rake. If Dragon Punch/Tail Rake connects for damage, give a slightly lesser chance of doing an Eagle Strike. This is not only an interesting solution, it would make raising all of the monk special attack skills more important. This would be a post-50 enchancement, or when a monk reaches 200 skill in Flying Kick."<

Also interesting. Of course, this sounds an awful lot like triple attack… but done in a monkly fashion.

>"Allowing us to strike just as hard with the off-hand as the primary will solve the damage-dealing problem and bring us up to the correct damage level of being between warriors and rogues.

If this change is made, you will need to remove MNK as a class capable of using the moss-covered twig, a weapon changed to be off-hand only because of the large amount of damage it does when a damage bonus is applied."<

Maybe… it also sounds like it has a very high potential chance of bringing that damage output well above rogues… And having to go back and look over every monk-usable weapon list to make sure one of them isn't broken because of a change like this, well, that puts this idea very low on the list.

>"5) Fix the Celestial Fist effect.
Celestial Tranquility is a great effect on our epic, but it stacks with almost NOTHING. Most monks rarely solo post-50, and most common group buffs will keep the effect from working. (Call of Earth, enchanter haste spells, most bard songs, Speed of the Shissar, etc.) At least alter it so that even if the haste is not usable due to other, better buffs then the additional effects -- the extra +AC and +ATK -- will stack anyway, similar to the way the Robe of the Whistling Fists worked. It's pretty bad when a well-meaning ranger tosses me Call of Earth and costs me 54ATK and 12AC. The ranger buff especially does not make sense because Call of Earth is NOT a Haste Buff, and the Celestial Tranquility Effect will indeed stack with the upgrade, Strength of Earth."<

That seems to make sense to me.

>"7) At least consider spreading the weight limit increase out over the post-50 levels. Few monks will make 55. Even fewer will make 60. Therefore, weight increases at those two points are not terribly useful. A 2lb increase every other level (i.e., 52, 54, 56, etc) would result in a weight limit of 24 at 60. A 1lb increase every post-50 level would be another way to implement the changes, and would also result in a weight limit of 24 at level 60. If these are "too much" of a weight increase (the current max weight limit at 60 is 22lbs, from four-pound increases at 55 and 60) then skip levels 55 and 60, when we get our passive enhancements of +1 HP per tick while feigned."<

This also makes sense to me.

>"Skill Caps.
Kick caps at 250. So why does Flying kick, eagle strike, and dragon punch all cap at 230?"<

I don't know…

>"Also, it was recently announced that they upped the caps for the Hand to Hand skill to equal 1hb/2hb, but all that was raised was the 60 cap, the 50 cap is still only 225 compared with 1hb/2hb cap of 240, so the 50 cap needs to be increased to 240 as well."<

Noted.

>"In addition, since kunark came out, people have noticed that feign is seen through more and more when the monk is down below 2 bubbles of health. This is a situation where the monk has successfully met the skill check (no "fallen" message), no spell was cast on the monk since they fd'ed, but the mob keeps pounding away. In the monk community this is refered often to as the "frenzy code". When a mob decides that they are going to kill you, they will kill you regardless. I personally have had times where I fd 8 or 9 times successfully in a row, but still have the mob continue to beat on me. Feign at above 2 bubbles of health, and this HARDLY happens.
So, at higher levels fd is useless as an escape tactic."<

Thanks, that's a useful explanation of the situation. I'll use it.

>"a) revert the hole and sov so that mobs train back instead of walking back at the same time b) increase the fd skill significatly (post 50 I should not be getting the "fallen" message as much as I do.
c) post 50, spells that hit us after we feign, or at least aoe spells) should NOT break feign. Yes we can take the damage, dot, etc..but it should not break the feign.
d) give us a short reuse disicipline that will clear our aggro 100%. Seem overpowering? not at all, we can already do this, its called /q. remember your downtime thread? having a monk /q to clear aggro is just silly. with /q and the mages coh, there is no reason not to have this."<

I'm pretty sure that doing all of these things is just too much. But I can ask them to look at the situation.

>"Bug report: controls locked up upon standing from a fd.
Every once in a while when the monk stand from a feign death, they can only move forwards or backwards. not side to side. The only way to fix this is to fd again (waiting the 10 seconds). Bug report: Feign death does not work if the monk is in an area where they are forced to crouch. try feigning from the lava elememental just out side of his area in sola. the ceiling is low enough that the monk has to crouch, and fd will never, ever work there."<

Also noted.

Absor Gives Feedback on Donals
Xanthus [1/30/2001-6:07:14 PM]  Comments[15]

Absor replied to some of the concerns brought up by players on the boards today:

Marten wrote:

>"casting spite back make matters so much better."<

This is where I get confused. I have no desire to harm any person playing any class in the game. Nor does anyone here. This was not some secret plot to attack people that play clerics. It is simply a necessary decision. If I sounded spiteful somehow, that certainly was not my intent.

InnoruukSK wrote:

>"Heck,the blue names posting here have as much as said that this means they're retooling high level mobs in general. That's a fundamental change to high level play. Not just this one item changing,but quite possibly much more besides."<

No, we did not 'as much as' say anything. I was not alluding to anything, nor was I sugar coating something. I said that there are a few encounters that will be looked at once this change is made. I did not say, not did I even imply, that we would be retooling anything in general.

I don't want anyone drawing unwarranted conclusions from the responses like this that I have seen.

Healex wrote:

>"Putting a 7 and half min. recast "buff" does nothing more than to destroy this items original purpose, to cut down down time"<

Er… actually I would completely disagree with you. It will take you exactly 30 seconds per person to completely heal your party, or your raid group, or the whole zone if you are so inclined. I would consider that a dramatic reduction of downtime.

Indigarious wrote:

>"Since release, there have been so many issues that have been "tuned" or "fixed". For two years, I have seen many bugs/issues get placed on the backburner. Issues that we, as a player community have respectfully brought to your (Verant's) attention. But, whenever anything comes up that could offset the game balance to *any* degree, VI is quick to respond. This is just my perception and opinion."<

Well, I guess this is where this whole thing gets kind of strange for me. I'm reading here how a people are telling us that, since it has been so long since this item was introduced, we should just leave it. Yes, we move slowly sometimes, nobody is denying that. And yet, even with this 'nerf' having taken us six months to get to, it is still the bugs that people see as on the back burner for a long time…

*shrug*

>"I know there have been many favorable changes to the game, and as a paying customer, I appreciate them. However, I echo the voices of many when I ask you to please leave the game alone from an item-perspective."<

And I ask you to step back and really examine the effect that an item like this can have on the entire game. Look at it and try to understand how it would completely change the entire game, and not for the better.

I certainly understand your philosophy about only adding and never taking away. And it's the most pleasant route to take. But that route can only lead to disaster. If we never 'nerf' anything, then we have to spend every moment trying to push everything up to meet the standard of the best. If we make one tiny mistake and overpower one item, then we have to spend months (and I do mean months) boosting everything up to meet it. A few years from now we'd probably just have to start people a 50th level with planer gear so that they wouldn't be underpowered…

Sorry, as much as we would like to take the easy route, it's also the road to disaster.

Cattie_Luv said:

>"I just don't see the point in nerfing an item 0.05% of the server population will actually posess and make use of it."<

This is another sentiment that I don't understand. It seems, to me at least, self contradictory. You are saying that we shouldn't make this change because it only affects a very small number of players (which, by the way, I disagree with). But if it only affects 0.05% of the server population, than how can it be such a big deal that we change it?

Zariq wrote:

>" There is one (1) thing that stops it from being overpowered, rarity."<

And that may be true. But the rarity of any item decreases over time in EverQuest. As time passes, more of the item enters the game, but rarely do any leave. With an item like this, probably none ever leave. So the rarity decreases with every passing day. Soon, the overpowering effect of an item like this pervades the game, and ruins it.

Celdor wrote:

>"As a DM since the days when D&D was called Chainmail™, I can tell you that the worst thing a GM can do is feel offended that the players outsmarted you."<

That's absolutely true. And if you think that we are making this change out of some sort of anger over being outsmarted, you are mistaken. I've explained several times how unbalancing this item is. We're changing it because it is broken, and that is the only reason.

Jovius wrote:

>"Thank you for your well thought out presentation, although it was clearly lost on a lot of people . I think your doing a great job so far."<

Thanks. And, well, I was wrong to put that six month thing like I did. No matter the case, yes, we should have made a far more dramatic change earlier. We should have noticed the possible effect on the game a while ago. We didn't. I'm not, however, going to say that this should have been easy to see. Seeing that the item was broken is one thing (and we did, that's why the casting time was raised). But seeing the wide range potential problem with this thing is not as easy as your going along as it is when looking back at it.

Doktor_Nobbins wrote:

>"Honestly? I think we should have deleted the item the first time it became a problem." See? One or two jerks, out of something like 200,000 players, have gotten your goat. Now you're sounding hostile when there's no reason for it."<

Well, I wasn't intending to be hostile. It's just how I feel. I, personally, don't think the item should have been allowed to remain in the game. I didn't say that to get a rise out of anyone, but just to express how broken I think it is.

>"I think for the great majority of us, the nerf represents a reasonable attempt to work out a situation that clearly needed your attention. We (and I do play a high level cleric fwiw) appreciate Verant's efforts and can easily accept this nerf due to the well stated problem."<

Thanks.

Really, the last thing I want to say on this is to ask you to consider this: some people claim that they started their clerics only because they were looking forward to getting this one item. I am sorry if this change makes you unhappy about that decision. But the main thing to realize is that no one item should be the sole reason for the existence of any character.

Alan

Donal's Breastplate Clarifications
Xanthus [1/30/2001-10:24:09 AM]  Comments[25]

Some clarifications were made by Brad (Aradune) today on the boards regarding the recent Donal's Breastplate changes:

I just read through Absor's post and the replies to it.

I wanted to clarify some things.

First, it didn't take 6 months and 300,000 people to find the problem. The problem was only made apparent recently, with Velious and the removal of the HP cap. The breastplate didn't appear as a problem until encounters started lasting much longer than they had previously. We made one attempt to address the issue, found out it wasn't nearly enough, and now are faced with having to do this change.

Removal of the HP cap added more variety to the game -- it changed strategies and tactics. No longer were we forced to simply make mobs kill in a very short period of time. Overall, this is a very good thing for the game -- variety and encounters that require differing tactics is good.

That said, whenever we do change or add behavior or other significant aspects of the game, other unforeseen problems often surface. It's the nature of the genre -- these game's change and evolve, and every change, small or large, can have unexpected ripple effects.

A second clarification: less than 5 or so mobs in Velious were created assuming the breastplate would be used, and this was mostly likely in error (i.e. we shouldn't have made that assumption in those cases). We will be addressing those mobs and tuning them down accordingly.

In summary, I'm sorry we goofed up -- it does happen. It will happen again. It's the nature of the beast, but we will always do our best to minimize these occurrences. And we will also do our best to react as fairly as possible when we are forced to act, attempting to do the right thing for both the individual player as well as the game as a whole.

It is unfortunate that the item was created in the first place, given its potential for problems, and it's also unfortunate that we didn't realize how much more magnified that problem would become when the HP cap was lifted.

That said, again, we're not perfect, and we will continue to make mistakes as long as this game continues to grow and evolve. And, no matter how dedicated our testing staff is, or how carefully we watch for problems on test, we will miss things that 300,000+ players will find. I don't see this changing, and I certainly don't think this is something unique to EQ, but rather something inherent to the genre -- no other type of game changes as often and as significantly, nor involves such a huge community of players who both work with and compete with each other.

I know that what I've said probably won't stop the flaming, and I know this is disappointing to both players who have the item as well as those who were planning on obtaining it one day -- I don't expect to post and have happiness follow -- but I did want to at least try to explain the situation in more detail, and to clarify it. And I also think it's important for us to try to manage expectations as they relate to the future.

-Brad

Donal's Breastplate Changes Soon
Xanthus [1/29/2001-6:05:58 PM]  Comments[51]

Absor just announced on the boards today that the Donal's Breastplate will be changed in the near future:

We've changed this item before because we thought it was overpowered. Unfortunately, it is still overpowered. Let me explain why.

This item is a breastplate that is only usable by clerics. If you right click this item, it will cast a Complete Heal on your target with a casting time of 30 seconds. It uses no mana to produce this effect.

A group of two characters, one cleric wearing this breastplate and one warrior, can defeat any opponent, as long as that opponent can not kill the warrior in under 30 seconds.

Let me restate that.

One cleric and one warrior, using this one magic item, can defeat ANY opponent or any number of opponents that are not capable of killing the warrior in under 30 seconds.

I hope that the impact that this game dynamic has on the game is obvious to everyone. If we allow this item to remain in the game as it is, we will have to change the game so that monsters are capable of killing a warrior in 30 seconds. 15 seconds if the group consists of two clerics with this breastplate and one warrior, and it gets worse from there...

Of course, if we make the world so hard that this breastplate is not overwhelmingly powerful, we would have to, essentially, rebuild the game, rebalance the classes… No single item should be so powerful that the balance of the game revolves around it. Adjusting the game to fit this item is not a choice. We need to change the item to fit the game.

We have had a fair amount of internal debate over this item. It really comes down to two choices. Eliminate the item entirely, or change the way it works. We would rather change the way it works. After much debate and some conversations with players that have characters that use this item, we decided on the following change.

The item will cast a new spell (to be named later). The effect of this spell will be to completely heal the target. The spell will also leave a buff on the recipient that lasts somewhere between five and ten minutes (we are leaning toward a compromise of 7.5 minutes). This buff will have some small positive effect (perhaps a 1 point regenerative effect), but as long as this buff is on a character, he can not receive the effect of this spell again. This buff will not be removable until its duration runs out.

In practice, what this will mean is that the cleric with a Donal's Breastplate will be able to heal anyone he wants every 30 seconds. But he will not be able to heal the same person twice unless 7.5 minutes has passed between healings. The breastplate will still almost completely eliminate downtime due to hit point loss, and it will no longer destroy the game dynamic of EverQuest.

There is one concern that we have as to this implementation, and I'd like your feedback on it.

There are some cases where a character would have all of his buff slots full and be unable to gain a new buff. This new Donal's effect would be, essentially, a buff. We can either have it overwrite a current buff, or have the spell fail if the character has a full load of buffs on him. I can see arguments for either implementation, and I want to hear your thoughts on it.

Yes, we know this is a 'nerf'. And, frankly, we're willing to nerf this item to save the game as a whole. We are sorry about ever creating an item that is so easily unbalancing to the game, and we're sorry that we have to change it again. For now, I can't tell you when this change will take place. It won't be until after we've finished the work that has us on hold for updating the game. When I have a better timeframe, I'll certainly let you all know.

Alan
