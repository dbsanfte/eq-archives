The Arch Mage Answers section of the offical Everquest site has been updated yet again. Here is a sniplet:

Q: Why is there no Icon for the Bard Mana Song (Cassindra’s Chorus of Clarity)?

A: There are different types of Bard Songs. Most Bard Songs leave an icon on your character because the song has a duration - usually 5 to 15 seconds. Each time the Bard sings the song she renews the duration - this means that a Bard who continues to sing the song can potentially keep the song effect going indefinitely.

Network Status
[12/4/1999-1:57:20 PM]  Comments[0]

Network Status from the offical Everquest website:

*Nameless Server Update* Sat Dec 4 16:26:39 EST 1999

As many of you know, the Nameless server encountered a catastrophic hardware failure with the Raid 5 hard drive controller. The Raid 5 hardware has worked flawlessly in the past nine months in safeguarding against data loss in the event of a hard drive crash. However, last night, we had a severe crash on the Nameless server, which resulted in a complete loss of all current data on the server. Unfortunately, the most recent off-site character backups are from about 11am yesterday morning (December 3rd). This means that if you played a character on the Nameless server after 11am pacific yesterday, once the server comes back on-line, that character's stats, items, etc. will be reverted to what they were previous to that time. Due to these unfortunate circumstances, which were beyond our control, no reimbursements will be given whatsoever. Our technicians are now investigating a way to improve upon this near flawless form of data backup, to insure that an event such as this NEVER happens again. We apologize for the inconvenience this has caused.

- The EverQuest Team

EverQuest Server Maintenance Sat Dec 4 08:59:33 EST 1999

The Nameless server is still under going maintenance as of 6:00 AM PST. We do not have an ETA on when it will be back up yet. We apologize for any inconvenience.

- The EverQuest Team

Update: Brad on Alchemy
[12/5/1999-12:31:29 PM]  Comments[0]

Everquest Producer, Brad McQuaid replied to a post made on alt.games.everquest regarding the broken alchemy skill:

It's true -- we were testing it internally wrong, and it appeared to work. After investigating further and talking to many shamans in-game, it was clear it didn't, and we made a change to code today (which should be up on the test server soon). Once the fix is tested and goes live (perhaps 2 weeks or so), shaman should get the alchemy skill at level 25, be able to train in it, and be able to raise their skill by making potions. We are still not happy with the overall usefulness vs. cost of most potions that one can make, but at least it will work.

I apologize for this and for the fact that it took so long to be recognized.

---------------------------------------------
Brad McQuaid
Producer, EverQuest
www.everquest.com
Vice President, Verant Interactive Inc.
---------------------------------------------
