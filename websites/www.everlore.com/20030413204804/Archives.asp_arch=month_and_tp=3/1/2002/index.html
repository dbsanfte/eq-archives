High End Caster Balance Addressed by Verant
Georgia [3/1/2002-6:59:08 AM]  Comments[27]

From the Developer's Corner:
Caster Balance

It's been clear for awhile now that priests and casters haven't been as well balanced as we'd all like them to be. Balance is a delicate issue and any major changes to overall class power are always researched, debated, and taken slowly. There are some really big improvements in the works now for helping out with that balance, and we expect those to be on the Test server in the near future. These changes won't all come in one huge patch, but will show up a bit at a time until casting classes are right where they should be in relative power.

We are taking the needs and issues of casters seriously, and we think you'll be very happy with the results you see after we finish our caster balance effort. We hope you'll be patient just a bit longer, while we take the time to get everything implemented, balanced, and tested.

Ancient Spells

One specific area we are addressing for casting classes revolves around how much improvement they get from raiding and acquiring high-level items. When a melee class begins to acquire items from the hardest monsters, they experience a tremendous growth in power. A melee character sporting Vulak weapons and Temple of Veeshan loot is incredibly more powerful than player who only owns average quality items. Casting classes don't achieve nearly the same amount of increased power from raid items, and rapidly fall behind melees in relative power when they start raiding. It doesn't make sense for a wizard who has helped in slaying hundreds of dragons to be using the same spells as someone who has yet to take part in raid encounters.

To address this problem, we've introduced the new "Ancient" category of spells recently. Ancient spells are rare, high-quality spells that can only be obtained from the most powerful monsters. These spells are improved versions of spells already available to each casting class. They won't give casters any brand-new, or out-of-class abilities, but are intended to make the casters better at what they already do. Much like a warrior improves himself by getting a better sword from a dragon, a wizard can now acquire more efficient spells in the same way.

As you might expect, Ancient spells are going to be rare and take a certain amount of effort to obtain. We don't expect many casters to ever get all the Ancient spells for their class. This is a change from the traditional approach we've had to spells, which has allowed every caster to feel entitled to get every one of their spells. We'll continue to make core spells available to casting classes off of vendors, or as relatively frequent drops off of monsters, but we want to be clear that Ancient spells are rare and powerful items of treasure, and we intend them to be very hard to get.

Only the first set of Ancient spells have been added to the game. In the coming weeks, we'll continue to add more Ancient spells to the game and expand the selection for casters and priests. It takes some time to get the spells balanced, tested, and placed on monsters - so expect to see them coming in a few sets at a time.

One last thing to note about Ancient spells- the power of these spells will vary depending on what monster drops them. You can expect Vulak A'err to have more powerful Ancient spells than you might find on Trakanon, for instance.

Rich Waters
Lead Designer, Systems
Sony Online Entertainment

Source: http://boards.station.sony.com/ubb/everquest/Forum1/HTML/000509.html

A Note About News Submissions
Georgia [3/7/2002-5:49:57 AM]  Comments[11]

I just thought I would drop a quick note about news submissions and how you should determine if your news submission is actually news or not.

For starters, I'm receiving a lot of really silly news submissions here that are really meant for the message boards. If you want to ask questions regarding EverQuest, open a discussion on EverQuest, or ask system requirements/technical questions, please visit our forums for these sorts of posts. The reason I point this out is because about 70% of the news submissions are actually meant to go on the message board, and we cannot post these on the front page, as they are not news.

Another rule of thumb is to please try to be grammatically correct. In other words, don't submit your news unless you can write an understandable English sentence and actually capitalize. Just so you know what I'm putting up with in the mornings, here's a gem that I deleted today:

i playd everqeust a whle back..... do it still sux or is it bettar now?! i wuld really liek to know what u think of it. its been a yr since i playd. thx.
I mean, sure he can use punctuation, but it would be great if submissions could at least have a hint of education :P Also, notice that this post is best for the message board, and NOT for the front page.

Things that DO qualify as news are as follows:

  • EverQuest events (player AND GM events)
  • EverQuest patches
  • Verant Staff Quotes
  • Community announcements (IE: artwork, eq-related surveys, or petitions)

This has been a public service announcement by Georgia. Now we will return to your regularly scheduled news.

 

Patch Message
Georgia [3/7/2002-5:50:05 AM]  Comments[19]

Patch Information

  • Fixed a problem with Alternate Advancement ability Combat Fury that was causing rangers using archery and rogues using throwing to have a lesser chance to critical than normal. This did not affect warriors
  • We think we've fixed many of the issues that were causing 1018 errors. At the very worst players should be able to log back in faster now
  • Fixed a problem that was causing quests to give no Alternate Experience to a character when that character had all experience going into their Alternate Experience pool
  • Fixed a bug that was causing Claw of the Savage Spirit, Earthen Band and Breastplate of Day to show a negative statistic modifier
  • Fixed an issue with some ATI-based video cards that was causing the inventory screen to flash
  • All Levitation spells will now be stripped when entering a no-levitation zone. There was a bug that caused the game to only strip the first Levitation spell found
  • Earrings of Endless Speed are now no-drop
  • Fixed a bug that was displaying erroneous and duplicate faction messages
  • Also added some Tradeskill recipes for items dropping in Akheva and Maidens eye
  • Tradeskills (including Alchemy and poison) now take %skill modifying items and buffs into consideration
  • Scallywag's Arm Plates (Legends Only) are now wearable in the arms slot (was errantly flagged for the chest slot)
  • All soft wicker pieces are now wearable by Beastlords

Prime Heirophant Vek leaned over and looked into the jeweled case silently. The young mystic behind him nervously fingered the cudgel hanging from his belt. He knew what was coming. The hold of the faceless had firmly taken hold of his heart and was beginning to squeeze.

Vek turned to him quietly and simply said, "Find them." He then walked silently out of the room.

Hierophant Zand turned to the young Mystic, "Do as he has asked. Do not return until you have the skulls of the ancients in your possession." The young Mystic stumbled as he ran out of the room, visibly shaking.

Zand looked down into the empty skull case. Something glittered softly in the corner of the case. Zand reached in and picked up a small earring, emblazoned with the symbol of the Kunzar.

Zand walked quietly out of the room, and somewhere the soul of a dead emperor laughed with unholy malice.

Taruna Zonn`Esu writes:
As of late, a few odd things have been taking place in the realm of Morell-Thule:

Sunday, March 3

Halloween Replay:

Fighting with his scaly companion, my barbarian shaman reached the mighty season of fourteen in the Oasis of Marr. Shortly after this accomplishment, I made my way towards Halas (recieving a port to Surefall Glade from a kind druid). It was at this time that I began hearing, via guildchat, of Northern Ro being the haunting grounds of the Tische Virm cult. Needless to say, the priest was quickly dispatched and the event ruined for the vast majority.

As I made my way out of the Jaggedpine Forest and into the Qeynos Hills, I came into a haven of undead. My soul being bound in Freeport, I feared for my life too much to stop and see what all the fuss was about, and so I proceeded on to Everfrost. Just as I entered, I heard shouts of werewolves which were all, thankfully, now cleared from the region. After this, all was quiet... for a few short hours.

Kitties in Oasis:

Later on the same night, as I sat on the bloodied sands of North Ro, I began hearing a ruckus about many named cats in Oasis. Not one to miss such a sight, I dashed over the sands and got there as fast as I was able.

Running to the docks, I pieced together what was going on: There were four named kitties (Rico, Smokey, Kitty and Socks), and just before my arriving, one (Kitty) had been slain. Making my way to the docks, I ran into the adorably cute Smokey. Trusting my instincts, I at once set to the task of befriending the strange creature. Though she wasn't crazy about the Halfling Pickles, Smokey did seem to like my offering to play a game.

First came the idea of Tag, which we enjoyed greatly. When Smokey grew tired of this game, we played Hide and Seek (Smokey hid herself very well). After she was found, we organized a race from the docks to Southern Ro (Smokey was little more than an orange blur, leaving us in the dust). When finally we caught up to her, we decided on a dancing contest (though little dancing was done other than that of myself, as I tried teaching Smokey the art of the Happy Hampster Hop).

Again, we made our way to the docks, where Socks and Rico already awaited us. The few that tried attacking the poor things found quickly that with two shaman on heal and kitties that fight back, it was a relatively pointless thing to do. At this time, all cats hid themselves. Smokey was found first (and I refused to leave her side after she returned to the dock), followed by Socks (I believe) and finally Rico.

Alas, our fun was to end at the leaving of these beautiful creatures.. though the memories shall ever remain.



Thursday, March 7:

The Lost Brother:

With my magician I hunted in Highkeep. It was an ordinary night, until I saw a very unusual shout: that of one Tassan Tio seeking his lost brother, Hassan.

Bidding my group good luck, and wishing them well, I left in hopes that I might be of some service. Finding the distressed Feir Dal, Tassan, at the entrance to the Keep, I quickly grouped up with the assembled party and we made our way towards Kithicor (I arrived somewhat late, and therefore missed out on any story told by Tassan, though I've managed to fill in the blanks on my own, for the most part).

Upon arriving in Kithicor at 8am, Tassan was nowhere to be found. Luckily, we had a druid with us, who was keen to track the missing Hassan. Finding him, we coaxed him into following us back to the entrance of the damnable forest, only to find (you guessed it, folks) that Tassan had gotten lost when searching for his brother!

Re-evaluating our situation, we once again pulled together the courage to brave the forest. Leaving two guards for Hassan (to protect him and, more importantly, make sure he didn't run off), we set out in search of Tassan. It wasn't long before his location was discovered, and we returned him to his brother (and at 6pm exactly, no-less).

After a touching reunion (complete with hugging, cheering, etc.), we were thanked heartily. Tassan and Hassan passed out (quite freely) many gifts to those who helped (all of which were Blood Crusted, if I'm not mistaken).

I personally recieved a Blood Crusted Cloak (Ac 5, Str +2, Mana +8 - at least I think that's correct, though I haven't checked the stats again to verify). This night of fun came to a conclusion as Tassan and Hassan walked off, thinking up excuses to tell their parents who would surely be worried sick.

All-in-all, it was a very fun night, and would have been so even if no rewards were involved.

It was also the only item that a druid had any hope of getting from Vulak. Now priority will be given on this item to Clerics and Shamans because, as we all know, they are vital and important classes and druids are sideliners.

Others asked why there were not more class-specific loot drops in general.

Rich Waters had this answer to such questions:

Class specific items are good in cases where they are not going to be No Drop. When they can't be traded, it's often a very frustrating situation if you have no one present who can use the item in question. Having an item that your group earned and no one can use is very annoying, as you put in the appropriate effort and the reward rots on the corpse.

The Rowyl's Armguards are a good example. The item was changed because that's a case where 45 or more people participate in an event and on some servers the armguards were beginning to rot as the guild druids all acquired the item. Making the armguards useful to more classes lengthens the amount that can drop before a given group becomes saturated with them.

- Rich Waters

I suppose there will always be a fine line between the problem of classes wanting the same items and the frustration of having items drop that nobody in the group can use.

Lawrence Poe (not worthy of a full quote ) mentioned the revamp of Hate Plane and how the class-specific loot their wasn't so well received. Both make very good points. Obviously no decision on such things is going to be able to please everyone. But we'll continue to do our best to make the decisions that work out best for the game.

- Alan

I don't think I'm alone among the players in my belief that while manastones would not be functioning in any alternate planes or expansion zones, they would continue to work in zones in the "old world".

Was this simply an oversight, or is the reduction in the number of zones where this item can be used intentional?

[I have this from the development team:]

Since the time the manastone was introduced, the world of EQ has changed enormously. Players now have hit point totals that were impossible back in the day. Spells like Complete Heal, Torpor, and Nature's recovery make the mana/hit point heal ratio totally insane these days. Beyond that, the improved efficiency and availability of regeneration is also incredibly higher than it was pre-Kunark.

Manastones are crazy broken today. The speed, convenience, and efficiency they bring would unbalance today's high end player beyond repair. As an example, with the use of a manastone, a high end cleric can go from no mana to full mana in under a minute.

While we all understand that there was an implication that we'd let manastones continue to work in the old world, the new Temple of Cazic-Thule is basically a new world zone. It's a zone designed for the same high end people and content that we think manastones break. And, well, it seems to me to be a better idea to make a good zone than to worry about one "crazy broken" item.

- Alan
