Player Auction - Sunday, Sept. 9th (Nameless)
Baelinn [9/3/2001-4:45:57 PM]  Comments[4]

Event Type

Auction

Date/Time

Sunday, September 9th, 2001. It will begin at about 5:30PM PDT (GMT-7), and continue until all items are sold. 5:30PM PDT is the same as 6:30PM Mountain Time, 7:30PM Central, and 8:30PM Eastern.

Location

Druid Ring near the ruins of the Combine Empire in the Dreadlands. There are no wandering NPCs at the rings, and they can easily be teleported to.

Rules

--This will be a true auction. Items shall be called out by myself, along with the minimum bid, and minimum bid increments. Players shall then bid on the item until buyout is reached, or the players will pay no more for the item.

--Other players shall be able to put their items up for auction. Please give your items to me before the auction begins, and tell me the minimum bid and bid increments you want. I shall be in DL 2 hours or more before it begins.

--I shall take only 2 items for auction from each player to save space.

--Items shall be auctioned in the order they are recieved.

--At the end of each hour, there will be a 10 minute break period, after which the auctions will resume again. During this break period, if I have room, I will take more items for auction that a player might have. I will collect items in the order that tells are recieved.

--Players who have their items sold during the auction shall be given the money for their items when the auction has ended. I shall take only 5% of the earnings for myself.

--After Auctions have ended, players shall be paid in the order in which their items were sold.

--Items which are not sold will be returned to the player at no cost.

If you have any questions, feel free to send me a tell ingame. I look forward to seeing you there.

Account Security Policy Clarified
Xanthus [9/4/2001-3:11:40 PM]  Comments[16]

The account security policy page has been updated today on the official EverQuest site. Here is the updated policy:

As the population of Norrath increases, so too do issues regarding stolen or compromised accounts, and/or accounts that have been transferred from one player to another in violation of the User Agreement. These issues - and the time it takes to investigate and attempt to resolve them - hugely detract from the time that SOE's customer service staff have to devote to providing quality customer service to issues that impact upon the gameplay experience. Accordingly, SOE must remind all players that:

(1) Account security -- whether it be password protection, running virus checks, disabling file sharing, or any other element of making sure accounts are not stolen or compromised -- is the sole responsibility of the account owner.

(2)Any and all stolen and/or compromised accounts that are reported to SOE will be *suspended*, and no items will be reimbursed. Any player found to be involved in an account theft will have any and all of his/her accounts banned.

Prior to banning any accounts reported as compromised, we will conduct a thorough review of the accounts in question and determine the appropriate course of action on those accounts. Accounts will only be banned based on information gathered through our investigation and not merely as a result of external allegations.

- The EverQuest Customer Service Team

 

New EverQuest Servers in Europe - Press Release
Xanthus [9/4/2001-3:28:40 PM]  Comments[21]

Sony Online Entertainment to Introduce New EverQuest Servers in European Markets

- Company Leads Online Gaming Market Internationally with Localized Content and Expanded Payment Options -

SAN DIEGO, CA - September 4, 2001 - Continuing its international expansion in the pursuit of the first true global gaming community, Sony Online Entertainment (SOE) and Ubi Soft announced today that EverQuest®, the world's largest massively multiplayer online game, will be hosted on new servers in the European market. Beginning this winter, Europe's most popular online game will be even more accessible and international gamers will be able to enjoy a localized EverQuest experience.

"We are committed to bringing online gaming to everyone, everywhere," said Kelly Flock, President, Sony Online Entertainment. "The roll out of our first European servers mark the beginning of our global initiatives and we are committed to continuing these efforts to meet the demands of our growing global community."

EverQuest already boasts more than 400,000 members across the world and is poised to grow as servers are deployed into new nations. With over 6 million online gamers in Europe alone, localized servers will offer lower latency for a heightened interactive experience for this growing market. The first overseas hub will launch in the U.K. this winter and additional servers will rollout as each market ripens.

Congruent with its international plans, the company recently announced that its premier online gaming site, The Station (www.station.com), with more than ten million members worldwide, will roll out localized content and advanced translation software for premium games while offering new payment options to overseas customers. Through advanced translation software, players will enjoy in-game and web chat even if they do not share a common language. Phase one of the localization process includes human translating agents for French, German, Japanese and Korean beginning with the upcoming expansion pack EverQuest®: The Shadows of Luclin™. EverQuest® Game Cards™ are being made available around the world at retail giving the wider, cash holding audience access to The Station's premium content including EverQuest and the up coming PlanetSide™ and Star Wars® Galaxies™.

Sony Online Entertainment Inc. (SOE), the online gaming division of Sony Pictures Digital Entertainment, is a worldwide leader in massively multiplayer online gaming. Headquartered in San Diego, California, SOE develops, publishes and distributes entertainment for the PC and online markets. With more than 10 million registered users, SOE's award-winning website, The Station® (www.station.com) hosts a variety of entertaining games and player communities spanning all genres. In addition to blockbuster hits EverQuest® and Jeopardy! Online, SOE has an array of cutting-edge online games in development such as PlanetSide™, Star Wars® Galaxies™ and Sovereign™.

GM Event on Ayonea Ro
Bloodgiver [9/7/2001-9:36:08 AM]  Comments[9]

The sun was due to rise over the fine city of Qeynos when my enchanter friend and I heard the cries of Sir Vegalys Keldrane. He was asking for the assistance of the citizens in an investigation. After a small turn out of only 5 people, he told us what was in store for us. It seems that one farm in all of the Karanas was growing crops, while the rest seemed so barren. Sir Antonius Bayle himself ordered Sir Keldrane to investigate.

We all grouped, as to keep the younger ones alive. The party consisted of: Me, Bloodgiver - 21 Warror, Irwin - 20 Enchanter, Zaltak - Unknown Enchanter, Lonainy - Lower level Monk, and Elnoe - Lower level gnome. After hearing our mission, we charged into the hills, en route to the Northern Plains of the Karanas.

When we zoned into the Western Karanas, the green fog seemed thicker than ever, and the symptoms grew worse. At times the whole party felt blood in their lungs. Even a "burning, itching sensation" occured. Not only the members of the party felt these effects, shouts throughout the Karanas carried people asking what was happening, why was the plague so rough today?

After making our trek through the Western Plains, we stopped in North Karana for one final rest, not knowing what lay ahead. We ran onward, during this time accumulating one more party member, a paladin dwarf - level 35. Sir Keldrane led on to a barn, where a man named Farmer Jansen greeted us. He was wearing a leather hat that seemed very strange looking. Sir Keldrane questioned why hs crops were growing and the neighbors were not. Jansen only said it was luck, or skill. The next questions were all the same, all answered with answers that could have been true.

After Sir Keldrane was out of questions, he turned to us and asked us what to do. By this time 3 more had joined the party. I asked the man to remove his hat, and he resisted. Sir Keldrane ordered him to and he did, revealing a strange tatoo on his forehead, a Crimson Dagger. Noticing this as a mark of the Bloodsabers we all stepped closer, ready to attack. He immediatly started to cry and plead for his life. He claimed that the Bloodsabers approached him about his farm, and that he only allied with them to feed his family. He then went on about an idol they told him to bury in his field so that the plague would be resisted. After digging it up and giving it to Sir Keldrane, he cried more about sparing his life. Keldrane then turned to me, and the original party, asking us only what his sentence be. We decided on exile instead of death or jail. He thanked us and promised to confess his sins. He then walked off into the Karanas.

After that, we were all thanked for our help. Then, I was summoned to come before him. Being credited with the discovery on his forehead, I recieved a Coin of Honor to give Jaylia the Faithful. The coin was magic item, no rent, and only could be held by rogues and warriors. I bowed, and thanked him. I could not wait to see what I would get. Then he thanked the original party members, rewarding them with 100pp, each. While presenting it to the dwarven fellow, the dwarf would not click, trade. So the dwarf, foolishly attacked him. Keldrane could have easily slain him but instead ordered him to stop. I am not sure if money was re-given. Then he thanked the people who came during the investigation, and told them that nothing was to be given, because they did not heed the original call in Qeynos. They were enraged, and they demanded something.

Sir Keldrane gave the first man something, and he was insulted by it. I am not sure what it was. I then bid Sir Keldrane good-bye, and started my trek back to Qeynos. I was told that the swarm came soon after and blanketed the area, fully! The party remaining fought them off and rejoiced. Then they dispersed, Keldrane gating first. Only one person died during the swarm, and was resurrected by a potion Keldrane had.

I finally arrived in Qeynos and ran quickly to Jaylia the faithful. I handed her the coin and she said, "Hear ye, hear ye! Recognize now that you hold a hero of great worth and valor among you! Bloodgiver has been proven in the eyes of the gods! As within the mortal realm, this name shall be known within the eternal halls of the Realm of Heroes!". I gained a nice amount of experiance and a Earring. The earring was a Thexian Loop, a magic no drop item with an AC of 5 and the Effect:Haste.

The trip was a great adventure, and aside from people being dissatisfied with their pay,[even a gnome who received 100 pp at level 3 or so was disgusted!] the rest of the party was grateful. I appreciate the earring greatly and I am eager to learn of this Realm of Heroes that I am remembered in.

/ooc The GM was great, he was the funniest character I ever met. A true roleplayer who made frequent comments about the monks speech and terms. He greatly fulfilled his part and I thank him!

Bloodgiver 21 Warrior
Karana - Ayonea Ro.
