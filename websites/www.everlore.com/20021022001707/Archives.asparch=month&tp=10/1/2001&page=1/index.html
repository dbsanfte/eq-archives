Chickens Rampage Through the Dread Lands!
Malfactionus Pernicious [10/5/2001-12:48:27 PM]  Comments[5]

Last night while hunting in the Dread Lands with my fellow guild-mate and long time friend, Kalzran Upatree, it was business as usual. We were kiting Drolvargs up and down the southern wall of Karnor's Castle until we heard screams of terror. Out of the blue people were shouting, "Chickens!!! Run for your lives!!!" At first we giggled momentarily, then decided to take some action.

Kalzran tracked several of the yard birds and we proceeded up ahead. Now moving into the sands of the eastern wall, we spotted the first foul beast. His name was BokBok, and he was out for blood. We stood back in awe, as these were not your normal chickens. They stood 10ft. tall and as wide as a school bus. We took a breath and conned the roosters, indifferently regarding me red and blue to Kalzran. We then approached the horrid fiends and tried to communicate. Big mistake! Only knowing how to say "Bock Bock!" they reared their heads straight at us and charged!

No spells would stick and it seemed as only melees could strike them. We found ourselves deep down trouble creek short of a paddle. These were no ordinary birds indeed, harm touching the first things in sight while dotting with darkness and hitting for the hundreds repeatedly. We soon found ourselves retreating in fear with the many others who tried to bring home the dinner. Kalzran made it to the zone, but I decided to feign death once I had gathered some distance. I watched in terror as these homicidal batches of the Colonel's best literally tore everyone to pieces. I had the pleasure of seeing a fellow Sk fall before 4 of them. He like me tried to FD, but it was too late. They knew he was still alive and the pecked at his body tearing flesh from his bones (I snickered). I began to feel insecure, wondering if they knew I was still alive.

I decided to stick it out and remain calm along the floor. Yet another big mistake (sigh). They all gathered around me, scratching and pecking at the floor until (you guessed it) they found my flesh intact corpse. They all began to feast on me; luckily my Dark forge armor would yield them no meat (woohoo). As my hp's began to drop like the people who attacked the birds, I jumped to my feet and decided it was time to get the heck outta dodge. I met up with Kalzran inside KC where a train had just rolled over 6 people. He healed me on sight and we devised our final strategy. Once we were both fm and fhp we headed out for revenge!

As we walked outside my harm touch had just replenished, I grinned >:) We spotted Rawk (or something like that) he was a medium sized bird, but hit just as hard. We took to arms and began beating the batter out of him. Spells where sticking and dots were landing (praise Bertoxxolous) and my Harm touch hit home delivering the final blow. I immediately looted the corpse as the scavengers were about. Shocked beyond belief to what my eyes had seen. The evil bird dropped an 11k Hooded Black Cloak, a two handed sword, bone chips, 5 cat's eye agates and a phat 40 platinum pieces. I split the money between us and we set out for more white meat. However we soon to came to knowledge, that we had killed the last of the feathery fiends.

We high fived each other and sat down to relish in our hour of glory. That day shall be etched in our minds as The Day We Gobbled With Death!

Malfactionus Pernicious
42nd Shadowknight of Bertoxxolous

 

Alternative Advancement
Korenth [10/5/2001-1:27:57 PM]  Comments[18]

Here is a new release from Sony regarding Shadows of Luclin

DESIGN DOCUMENT SUPPLEMENT- ALTERNATIVE ADVANCEMENT SYSTEM FOR EVERQUST: THE SHADOWS OF LUCLIN

Date: October 4th, 2001

The alternative advancement system will allow anyone who has purchased and installed Shadows of Luclin to further customize their high level (51+) characters. Customized features may include things such as minor permanent statistic and resistance boosts, unique skills based on your class or archetype (fighter, magic user, priest), and general skills available to everyone. These skills may include anything from an enhanced metabolism, to improvements in skills already available to your class. You may also find some unique skills not yet available to anyone. Some enhancements may add a title to your character based on what choices you have made in enhancing your character; these titles will be visible to other players. 
In order to attain these skills, you will be able to toggle your experience into the alternative advancement pool. When you do this, your experience will be allocated to this pool in lieu of your “leveling” experience bar. Once a certain amount of experience has entered the alternative advancement pool, you will receive training points that may be allocated to acquiring your enhancements. When you acquire the proper amount of training points, you will have the option of choosing which enhancement you wish to gain, although some enhancements may have pre-requisites that will need to be obtained before you are able to acquire it.


Players will have the option of toggling between the normal experience pool to gain levels, or back to the alternative enhancement pool to gain enhancements whenever they wish (although this may require you to zone after you have toggled your switch). Switching between the two pools will not cause you to lose experience in the other. Experience loss due to death suffered by the character will be taken from the “Leveling” experience pool, it will not impact your alternative advancement pool. 
The alternative advancement system will allow for a variety of enhancements to choose from for each class. Those who chose to enhance their character with this system may find a higher level of individuality in their character.

GM Event on Druzzil Ro - Orc Attack in EC!
Alex H. [10/6/2001-11:27:41 AM]  Comments[2]

I was in EC looking to sell a few items when I heard cries from the Orc 1 camp that special Orcs had appeared. Apparently the orcs of the camp were tired of being attacked and had called in some extra muscle. I quickly ran over to investigate.

When I arrived a battle royal was in progress. Orcs named Orc Enticer, Orc Sage, Orc Conjurer and Orc Priest were taking on players of all levels. Seeing several players having a problem with the Orc Priest I cast a Drifting Death spell, then a Calefaction, only to discover they had no effect! Apparently higher level players could not affect these special orcs.

All was not lost, however, as the other players there who could attack the orcs rose to met the challenge. One in particular was an iksar monk named Jaeva was in there kicking, and dealt the death blow to at least two of special Orcs). There were many brave fighters in there, eventually wearing down the Orc invaders, who had some pretty potent spells at their disposal. Several players were feared or diseased and I was hit by a force spell which knocked me over a hill! I quickly ran back to the orc camp to witness the rest of the conflict.

The battle raged on for about 15 minutes, and one by one all the special Orcs fell, except for the Orc Sage who seemed to have the ability to Shadow Step, as he teleported short distances when threatened. But there was no escape for him, as the players chased him down wherever he appeared, and was finally killed by, you guessed it, Jaeva!

From what I heard the loot was some middle of the road jewelry and some dasmak armor. All in all it was a lot of fun for the lower end players without the higher end ones being able to swoop down and spoil the fun for them. My heartiest congratulations to everyone who fought so valiently to keep EC safe for commerce!

Journal Entry - Oct. 6th, 12:02am

I decided to set up my camp early this night. Perhaps I could wake early next morning, and get a fresh start on my journey south. I invited a couple members of my guild to join me this night (Aindraia and Ranidas), yet they haven't arrived yet. I only hope nothing has befallen... Oh wait, here they come now...

Journal Entry - Oct. 6th, 12:22am

My apologies to the scholars of Felwithe for ending my entry so abruptly earlier. My long-time friend Aindraia interupted my writings. She mentioned in excited tones that she's always wanted to hunt Hill Giants. Ranidas, in an effort to impress the lovely Ain, offered to lend a hand (such a misguided Paladin). And somehow, they both managed to talk me into coming as well. So, after a being ported to the Druid Rings by Ain, we settled to down to rest after fighting a Griffon. Perhaps soon, we'll find a Hill Giant, and I can finally get some rest.

Journal Entry - Oct. 6th, 1:04am

I sit now, just inside the border of South Karana. What brings me here is a mission that has been charged me by a Paladin named: "Vegalys Keldrane". It seems, that what started as an innocent trip to the guard tower, has escalated to our group delivering a message to Al'Kabor! Yes, scholars, I did say AL'KABOR! It seems that the Knight wished me to send a message to Al'Kabor, informing him that the Scholars of Qeynos may have information to aid Al'Kabor in his research of the Combine Spires, (or Quadraliths, as has been deemed by Al'Kabor). I am to deliver the message, and a note to him. I only wait for Ain and Rani to proceed. Vegalys mentioned he might be near the Combine Spires in this area, we know travel on to complete our mission...

Journal Entry - Oct. 6th, 1:30am

From Wizard, to errand boy. That has become my fate it seems. After speaking with Al'Kabor, he mentions that the note I delivered brought attention to a Gyspie named: "Tavrevla Nimellin". It seems she is a direct descendant of the Combine Empire, and wished to speak with Al'Kabor. Seeing how busy the Great Wizard was, my group and I offered further assistance in tracking down, and speaking with this Gypsie. He agreed. And after creating a rune to prove our validity to Tavrevla, he sent us, and a Dwarven warrior by the name of Bloodfurys, to North Karana. I sit now at the Gypsie camp, preparing for an inevitable battle. This Gypsie, has in her possesion a chest containing ancient manuscripts, and tomes detailing her heritage, and history of the Combine Empire. Despite the proof that Al'Kabor sent us, she insists that we prove our trustworthiness to her. She has charged us with finding an insane evil eye named "Lorrk" that was once locked away, but now has been set free. We are to bring back his "eyeball" as proof of our task. I ask a fellow wizard to join us in this task, I noticed him watching us for a bit, so I asked him to take a more active role. I only hope this wizard, Glix will be all the help that I need.

Journal Entry - Oct. 6th, 2:46am

It is done. We traveled to East Karana in search of this "Lorrk". There, a Hard-Fought, Hard-Won battle took place. This "evil eye" was most certainly insane. He kept mentioning fruit... and dwarves... (note to investigate this further). Like previously mentioned, the battle was won, but we lost two of our own. Aindraia, and Ranidas our now making their way back to us, and while the remaining of us rest, I now have time to make an entry. It seems this "eye" was more powerful than we believed. I had to recruit a Mage by the name of Sumonious in mid-battle. We now have the eyeball, and await for the return of our friends before pressing on.

Journal Entry - Oct. 6th, 3:12am

After returning the eyeball to Tavrevla, we recieved the locked box as promised. Our return to the South Karana spires was uneventful, and now we rest as our adventure draws to a close. After giving the box to Al'Kabor (and explaining why it took so long), we we're rewarded for all our efforts. It seems the information was useful enough to him, to warrant his approval. What started out as an early night's rest, has ended in an adventure that only fate (or luck) could throw together. I made new friends, experienced the world in a way that a scholar never thought possible. Perhaps now, I can get some rest...

I, Wizard

New Patch Message
Warrior Poet [10/8/2001-4:50:36 AM]  Comments[31]

October 8th, 3:00 am

------------------------------

*** Item Changes ***

- Increased the duration on Song of the Deep Seas (the proc on a bardic weapon) dramatically.

- Modified the effect of Feast of Blood (weapon proc) it is now a DoT lifetap

- Added a texture to Flayed Barbarian Skin Leggings.

- Added Illusion: Barbarian to the Flayed Barbarian Hide Mask.



*** Miscellaneous Changes ***

- The last language used by your character will be stored in your eqclient.ini file when you log out. This means that if the last character you were playing was speaking Elvish, then when you log back in with any character, that character will be speaking Elvish if he can, otherwise it will be set to a default language (most often Common).



*** Magic System Changes ***

There is a more detailed explanation of these changes posted on our Developer's Corner. Please visit there for more details (either at www.everquest.com or http://boards.station.sony.com/ubb/everquest/cgi-bin/Ultimate.cgi). Here is a summary of the changes:

- The level-based spell resistance bonus inherent in super-high level NPCs has been reduced significantly.

- Several NPCs have had their resistances reduced. Each of them was examined carefully, and resistances changed as seemed appropriate. For some of these NPCs other things were changed as well to compensate for their increased susceptibility to spells, such as armor improvements or perhaps greater hit points, if needed for that NPC.

- NPCs that were highly magic resistant in order to make them immune to certain spells can now be given specific immunity to those spells. This means that they can be made immune to critical spells, as intended, and still be generally less resistant to magic. Players will receive a message similar to the one that is already given for Mesmerization spells when they cast a spell on an NPC that it is specifically immune to.

IMPORTANT NOTE: It is very important to note that we have not made any NPCs immune to spells that they were not already immune to. Many NPCs that were previously immune to spells due to their high innate resistance to magic have had that resistance reduced and specific immunities added. So if you see an immunity message after attempting to cast a spell on an NPC that you hadn't seen such a message from before, understand that the NPC was already immune to that spell before due to high resistances, and the only difference is that it now has lower resistances in general and specific spell immunity (which is why you are seeing the message).



*** Melee Changes ***

After extensive research, testing, and evaluation we have completed the melee portion of our class balancing. To be clear, these changes apply just to the combat aspect of melee classes. Any issues with non-combat skills, spell effects, spell selection, or non-combat aspects of classes will be evaluated in the near future. With the completion of the melee combat balancing, we will be moving onto spells and spell casting classes.

Due to the extensive nature of these changes, we can't describe them in detail here. A detailed summary will be available on our Developer's Corner message board. Please visit www.everquest.com and our forums for more information.

* Summary of Melee Changes *

- Two-Handed Weapons have been given an increased damage bonus for characters over level 50. Also, certain post-epic quality two-handers have been improved.

- Warriors have had their taunt skill cap increased to 230 at 60th level. Also, the Blades of Strategy and Tactics can now be used in either hand.

- Rogues, as the primary user of piercing weapons, will benefit from an improvement in the quality of a few existing high-end daggers. At some future date new piercing weapons will be added to the game to fill gaps in equipment availability.

- Monks will now be on an improved combat table at levels 55 and again to a better table at 60. Monks now have the ability to Triple Attack at 60th level. The minimum damage done by Flying Kick will be greatly increased for monks starting at 51st level and scaling up to level 60. We also intend to add more monk weapons in the future to fill equipment gaps.

- Rangers have their Double Attack skill cap raised to 245. They have also gained an innate offensive bonus that starts at level 55 and increases until level 60. Ranger's Defense skill cap has been raised to 220, and their Riposte skill cap has been raised to 185.

- Paladins and Shadowknights, besides the benefits they gain from the two-handed weapons change mentioned above, have also been moved to the same combat tables as warriors. In the future we intend to examine the existing one-handed weapons useable by knights for possible upgrades. We are also increasing the Taunt skill cap for these two classes to 220.

- Bards have had their 1h blunt and 1h slash skill caps increased to 250. Their piercing skill cap has been increased to 240. Their Offense skill cap has been raised to 252. Bards are also being moved to the same combat table with all the other melee and hybrid classes (save monks who have separate tables after level 55). Bards Parry skill cap has been raised to 185 as well. Finally, the Singing Shortsword can now be equipped in the off hand and still give full song enhancing benefits.

Please read the detailed explanation of these changes on our Developer's Corner message boards at http://boards.station.sony.com/ubb/everquest/cgi-bin/Ultimate.cgi



*** Firiona Vie (Roleplaying Preferred) server goes Live ***

Our newest special rules server is now available. Firiona Vie is designated as roleplaying preferred. There are many special rules for this server, please read over them carefully before playing. We will be keeping an updated list of these rules on our web site at http://everquest.station.sony.com/guidebook/firionavie.jsp.



** Firiona Vie Server Feature List **

- This is a new server, not a split server. Nor will there be any transfers to this server. - Characters will be wiped before the server goes Live.

- This is a PvE (Player vs. Environment) server, NOT a PvP (Player vs. Player) server. In other words, it's a standard 'blue' server with the same rules and features regarding PvP as the vast majority of our servers. It is not one of the 'Zeks'.

- Auction restricted to city zones: Qeynos, Surefall Glade, Highkeep, Freeport, Rivervale, Erudin, Halas, Neriak, Grobb, Oggok, Greater Faydark, Ak'Anon, Kaladim, Felwithe, Paineel, Cabilis, Kael and Thurgadin.

- Beneficial buff spells will not work on characters 20 levels lower than the caster.

- Each character will be assigned an "alignment" upon creation. This alignment will be described in brief on the character creation screen (in the same location that their Deity is described).

- Each character will be able to change their alignment once any time after they reach 10th level if they choose. A character is not required to change alignments, but may do so any time after attaining 10th level. The player will be able to chose from a list of alignments based on their race, class and deity.

- A character's alignment will determine whether or not they can group with other characters.

- A character's alignment will determine an experience bonus or penalty when grouping with other characters.

- A character's alignment will determine if they can cast beneficial spells on other characters (including resurrections).

- Language skills will improve only through group chat in the same zone and from /say.

- Languages will only improve 1 point each hour and will be capped at level*5+5 skill points.

- Common will not exist. A Human language will be added and only made easily available to certain races.

- One character per account.

- The Trivial Loot Code will be in effect in all zones.

- Very few items will be No-Drop. Exceptions will include things such as newbie notes and epic weapons.

- Bind Affinity will be restricted for all characters to locations where melee characters can bind on other servers.

- /emote will be language specific

- All characters will be /roleplay.

- /alignment will display the alignment of your character and probably a more detailed description of that alignment.



** Trivial Loot Code **

Briefly, the Trivial Loot Code works like this: If a character kills an NPC and that NPC does not give them experience, then the Trivial Loot Code takes effect. If any member of a group does not get experience for a kill, the Trivial Loot Code will take effect. The Trivial Loot Code prevents magical, lore and no-drop items from appearing on the corpse. Normal items and coins will appear on the corpse in all cases as usual.

Melee Changes Clarifications
Korenth [10/10/2001-2:20:51 PM]  Comments[8]

Absor has updated the Developer's Corner Forum. In this latest edition he clarifies some of the changes to the Melee system in EQ:

Before level 51, paladins, shadowknights, and bards are all on the same damage table as warriors, and always have been. This patch, we changed it so that they remain on the same table all the way up to level 60. Just to make sure this is clear, here is a list of classes-

Pre-level 51 there are two damage tables, the "standard table" and the "high damage table"
1-50 Standard Table - Warrior, Rogue, Paladin, Shadowknight, Ranger, Bard
1-50 High Damage Table - Monk

Before this patch when characters hit level 51, some of them would move to a higher table. This is how things were prior to this latest melee patch.
51+ Standard Table - Paladin, Shadowknight, Bard
51+ High Damage Table - Monk, Warrior, Rogue, Ranger

With this patch, we added two new totally new damage tables, and moved classes around a bit. Here is how things are on live servers right now-
51+ Standard Table - No one.
51+ High Damage Table - Warrior, Rogue, Ranger, Paladin, Shadowknight, Bard
55+ Monk Table - Monk
60+ Monk Table - Monk

Also, please note that damage tables are not always a big damage increase. Moving from the standard table to the high damage table is a fairly large increase, but moving from the high damage table to the 55+ monk table is only a small increase.

Another important thing to know about damage tables is this - they only affect the amount of damage you do. Damage tables do not affect the chance to hit or miss a monster, your weapon skills, or anything except the amount of damage you do. Damage tables specifically affect your average damage per hit and how high your hardest hits can be.

If you want to read his entire update, head over the to developers corner over at the offical Everquest boards.

 

Brad Speaks!
Xott the Wizard and Trek [10/10/2001-4:02:26 PM]  Comments[45]

Hello again, I want to say thanks very much for all the feedback we've received since last night -- much appreciated, and very helpful.

On the whole, we've received both positive and negative responses. I'd like to comment briefly about the negativity.

First, there's certainly nothing wrong with sending it in -- we want players’ true feelings about the issue. And we are listening.

Second, though, we do understand that players have built up an emotional attachment to their existing characters and their character's appearances. And this is totally normal and expected, and ultimately it's why we're keeping an option in to display the older characters if desired. We certainly realize that some players will flat out prefer to stay the way they are – after all, this is a game that's been out a while, and changing the status quo always creates controversy. It is also worth noting that the responses to the Vah Shir and the horse (as well as the backgrounds) were almost universally positive, and by and large, we feel this is because there's no point of reference and no pre-existing emotional attachment to that content (they were, by the way, created by the same art team as the other models).

That said, we absolutely want as many people to be pleased with the new models as possible, and so we will continue to make tweaks and adjustments and to issue new screen shots reflecting those changes. The characters, while nearing completion, are still very much 'work in progress.' We also hope that as you see the new characters more often, you’ll get used the changes and start to appreciate even more the details and features this new technology allows us to explore. I know personally that I was a bit shocked and taken aback when I saw the first round of Luclin characters, but now that I’ve worked with the team day after day, watched the advances, and really become used to looking at them, I just can’t go back – the older characters just pale in comparison.

Let me address what we found to be the three main areas of concern based on the feedback:

1. The faces, especially on the females. Let me assure you that not only will they continue to be tweaked, but what you saw in these shots was the first draft of faces and all but the one shot of the barbarian male swinging his sword had NO facial animations applied to them. So, one of the reasons the faces look a little bland is because they are designed to have facial animations. Also, let me also assure everyone that we will make a very real attempt to creating new faces that match in style the older faces (in addition to, hopefully, having new ones).

2. The Ogre. Yes, he's definitely different stylistically, and this is on purpose. I can't go into too many details at this point, but part of a longer term story line we are developing (starting with Luclin but going far beyond) is that the Ogres begin reverting to their former intelligence level and sophistication. Remember, this is the race that invaded the planes and challenged the council of the Rathe, after which they were summarily cursed into the stone age. Well, the idea is that they will slowly be returning to their former glory, hence their differing appearance. And while I know that may not be appealing to some, I hope you do understand our desire to move the grand history of Norrath forward and that it might affect some of the major races.

3. The 'sexyness' of some of the new females... well, honestly, this one took me a bit by surprise. In actuality, we are purposely toning down the outfits of some of the base clothing sets of the females relative to the original character set (for example, no more thongs)... in fact, the majority of clothing you'll see is probably on par with what one might encounter at the gym or workout facility and certainly more covering than a typical beach scene in Southern California. In any case, this will undoubtedly continue to be a controversial issue, but all in all, EQ is a fantasy game with exaggerated, heroic player characters, both male and female, and it will stay that way.

In summary, we will be releasing more screen shots in the near future (another batch from yesterday's run just hit several of the major gaming sites, by the way). These newer shots will focus more on the characters themselves, their options as it relates to appearances, and less on backgrounds and action.

We continue to appreciate your feedback and will do our utmost to please as many customers as it is possible while making such a significant transition and change to a 2+ year old game.

We thank you for your understanding and support,

- Brad

Special thanks to Trek who submitted this as I was working on it!
