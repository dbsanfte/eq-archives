Feign Death Related Bug
[1/4/2000-4:12:38 PM]  Comments[1]

A recent thread was started by Gordon on the official Everquest Forums regarding a necromancer bug. He is asking for player opinion on the matter:

A bug has been brought to our attention. A necromancer can sic their pet (charmed or summoned) upon a critter, load on DOT spells, and then cast Feign Death. The pet remains, fighting the critter, and the critter ignores the necromancer despite all of the damage being done by the DOTs. What *should* happen is that the pet should behave as if the owner hid or became invisible. That is, the pet should suicide if summomed, or should uncharm if charmed.

Our inclination is that we should fix this bug, because the tactic is pretty lame and allows the necromancer to do things they shouldn't be able to do, such as soloing high level critters in closed quarters.

So, should we change this? Or leave it as is?

-Gordon

PvP Forums Annoucement
[1/5/2000-3:34:59 PM]  Comments[0]

Gordon officially announces the opening of the new PvP Forums at the Everquest website.

Welcome to the PvP Forum. We though we'd put this up to discuss some possible changes for PvP in the future. We'd really like to see more people taking part in PvP, and figure there needs to be some changes for that to happen.

Back when Tallon Zek first came up, over 5000 people jumped on the server at once to start playing. That lead to Vallon Zek coming up as well. Clearly, many people were interested in playing PvP, but left for one reason or another. Only recently have the numbers on the teams servers been increasing, and this may have been due to people just looking for a lower server population.

I've played PvP since the day the Baby Zek's opened, and really enjoy it.

Here's a few interesting ideas I've seen:

1. Experience loss on all deaths. Pro: Would give naked mage/monks something to lose. Would open some strategies that are currently illegal (e.g. Snare the kiting druid). Con: Raises some harassment issues with zone/bindpoint camping.

2. Binding in city-only for all classes. Pro: Should cut down on bind-rushing. Con: Might upset casters a bit.

3. "Sick and Tired". Make people come back from death with the "Ressurection effect". Roughly 50% hitpoints and zero mana. Pro: Should eliminate the remainder of bind-rushing. Con: Would be a pain for people who are fighting PvE at the time.

4. Changing loot to coin-only. Pro: Should make melee classes feel more at ease. Con: Could make it very difficult for people to obtain items from unfriendly areas.

5. Changeing loot to any item, in bag or otherwise. Though I still think the casters come out on top of this one, I believe there is a pretty large technology hurdle that has to be overcome for this to be possible.

6. /CON. Making so that anyone above the level limit cons RED, anyone below cons GREEN, and anyone in it cons WHITE/BLACK.

I'll be reading the posts on this board and will comment as I can. Right now we are just on a fact finding mission to determine where people want to go. After we've beaten everything to death for a while I'll make a hotlist and take it to the developers.

-Gordon

--Rain Issues--

Hasn't Verant/Brad/Gordon NOTICED the fog? shouldnt this be fixed? this is worse than human/eudite/barbarian night blindness

Yes, we've noticed it.

Prior to the introduction of the adjustable clipping plane, the fog was getting stuck as it was trying to come in when the rain started. The adjustable clipping plane freed it up, but then it came in too far. The next patch should "nerf the fog" by about 25% and we'll see how that goes. In the mean time, you can limit the distance that the fog comes in by adjusting your clipping plane slider to the left side of the bar.

I should note that this adjustment will only take effect if it's made before the rain starts.

-Gordon

--Game Speed Increase--

I propose a 10% to 15% global game speed increase. No, I don't mean everyone runs around fast or even attacks fast. I mean HP regen, Mana regen and spawn rates. By icreasing all of these it balances out.

We can't really make sweeping changes to the game at this stage. Despite the fact that all the people who leveled under the "old rules" would scream that they're accomplishment means nothing, the game is successful as it is. People are having fun and advancing with the global "speed" of the game as it is currently. Sure, we can make incremental changes to the game by adjusting loot, adding items, changing casting times and such, but to making sweeping changes to a successful game with a predictable rate of attrition, and risking upsetting a LARGE number of people, just wouldn't make any sense.

-Gordon

--RAID 5 Issues--

It seems to me that in this case, a higher level of protection is needed. For example, a RAID 50 set (mirrored RAID 5 sets) would provide the appropriate protection and data availability for EverQuest. With such a configuration, the array could completely lose 2 drives without affecting gameplay, and 3 drives without compromising data integrity.

Our network guys put together a plan and now just need to work out the implementation. Though dataloss is never a good thing, some time you have to sit down and say "We'll accept loss under X circumstance". Under the RAID 5 configuration, X="2 simultaneous drive failures". We're going to work on increasing the value of X :)

-Gordon

--Overcrowding in EQ--

I keep hearing various solutions proposed to the zone overcrowding issue, but I have yet to hear anyone propose a second guk or sol B. imho this would solve a ton of high-level overcrowding with minimal difficulty. Anyone have some thoughts on this to share with me?

One of the "solutions" that we joke about here is making the world a big circle where every zone is equidistant from a central binding location and where every zone is either a copy of a newbie zone, SolB, Lower Guk, Oasis, or South Karana. This is only in jest. We want every zone to have it's own unique feel. Certainly we could have added a ton more zones and dungeons if we would have resorted to having them randomly generated, but that's the easy way out.

You're heart is in the right place however. We will solve the zone crowding issue by adding new zones, and tuning existing underutilized zones to increase their draw for the players, and/or decrease the draw of the favorite camping spots.

-Gordon

Feign Death News
[1/6/2000-2:26:28 PM]  Comments[0]

I have recieved this from Gordon Wrinn last night before the test server went back up. The Test Server patch message now shows some updates as well as a part of this annoucement:

After a serious discussion regarding the Necromancer's Feign Death tactic, as well as reviewing public opinion on the matter, we are leaning heavily in the direction of making a change. As we would like everyone to understand why we feel this change is necessary, our Lead AI Programmer was kind enough to summarize our position:

Problem:
Feign death allows you to become an invalid target in all circumstances, providing offensive tactics that are abusive. For this ability to remain balanced, the pet should follow the same rules as invisibility. Offensive tactics used by other classes involve the creature getting a saving throw verses the effect. Since creatures do NOT get a direct save verses feign death (as you cast it on yourself), and since you can retry the combat once you have feigned death, there is very little risk to necromancer himself. As this tactic involves charming an NPC and turning it against another NPC, which could be part of the same spawn, it allows for Necromancers to solo MUCH bigger rooms than any other class could. This situation is well beyond what would preserve game balance for any other class (soloing the Bloodthirsty Ghoul room and Ghoul Lord room, for example).

Example:
While there are many examples of how the Necromancer Feign Death tactic is abusive, one of the most egregious occurs in the Ghoul Lord room of Lower Guk. The room consists of 2 Bok Ghoul Guards and the Ghoul Lord himself. Using the current system, a level 50 Necro can easily solo the room at virtually no risk to himself. The Necro can get into the room quite easily with Invis To Undead. To clear the room he uses Screaming Terror on one Bok and Charms the other. When the Ghoul Lord rushes him, he feigns. The Lord then turns on the charmed Bok and kills it, taking considerable damage during the fight. The Necromancer waits until the Ghoul Lord returns to its spawn point. When the necromancer gets back up, he is in no danger of being attacked due to the way Feign Death works now. He then charms the 2nd Bok and Feigns again. The fight goes on and the 2nd Bok is killed. When the Necromancer gets back up he has a VERY wounded Ghoul Lord AND nearly full mana. The Lord is now an easy kill, and the necro collects his special items.

Solution:
Ideally, Feign Death should work like Invisibility, where summoned pets suicide and the charmed ones break Charm. We do not want, however, to adversely affect lower level Necromancers to such a large degree. What we do recognize is the need to prevent higher level Necros from exploiting key spawn points. Hence, when you Feign Death, any charmed NPC will immediately break Charm. However, summoned NPCs (pets) will NOT be affected. While this still allows the use of the Feign Death tactic with your summoned pet, it does not imbalance the game and many dungeon spawns to the degree that charming an NPC does.

This change will be live on the Test Server when it comes back up. Baring any problems, and assuming that we have not missed anything, these changes should go live in the next patch. Please feel free to comment on our new solution further. We'll review your opinions again before making the final decision to implement it on the live servers.

-Gordon

Test Server Update
[1/6/2000-2:49:41 PM]  Comments[0]

This recently appeared on the Test Server Patch:

January 5th, 8:30pm
-------------------

Well, we have finally completed the hardware maintenance on the Test Server - which took quite a bit longer than we expected. Due to this hardware failure, some characters were restored from backup therefore a few hours of gameplay may be lost for them. We apologize for the inconvenience this may have caused.

The following changes have been introduced to the Test Server.

- Druid's Tracking skill base has been increased.

- The behavior of the Necromancer's Feign Death spell has been modified on the Test Server. Here's a description of the reasons behind this change:

Problem:
Feign death allows you to become an invalid target in all circumstances, providing offensive tactics that are abusive. For this ability to remain balanced, the pet should follow the same rules as invisibility. Offensive tactics used by other classes involve the creature getting a saving throw verses the effect. Since creatures do NOT get a direct save verses feign death (as you cast it on yourself), and since you can retry the combat once you have feigned death, there is very little risk to necromancer himself. As this tactic involves charming an NPC and turning it against another NPC, which could be part of the same spawn, it allows for Necromancers to solo MUCH bigger rooms than any other class could. This situation is well beyond what would preserve game balance for any other class (soloing the Bloodthirsty Ghoul room and Ghoul Lord room, for example).

Example:
While there are many examples of how the Necromancer Feign Death tactic is abusive, one of the most egregious occurs in the Ghoul Lord room of Lower Guk. The room consists of 2 Bok Ghoul Guards and the Ghoul Lord himself. Using the current system, a level 50 Necro can easily solo the room at virtually no risk to himself. The Necro can get into the room quite easily with Invis To Undead. To clear the room he uses Screaming Terror on one Bok and Charms the other. When the Ghoul Lord rushes him, he feigns. The Lord then turns on the charmed Bok and kills it, taking considerable damage during the fight. The Necromancer waits until the Ghoul Lord returns to its spawn point. When the necromancer gets back up, he is in no danger of being attacked due to the way Feign Death works now. He then charms the 2nd Bok and Feigns again. The fight goes on and the 2nd Bok is killed. When the Necromancer gets back up he has a VERY wounded Ghoul Lord AND nearly full mana. The Lord is now an easy kill, and the necro collects his special items.

Solution:
Ideally, Feign Death should work like Invisibility, where summoned pets suicide and the charmed ones break Charm. We do not want, however, to adversely affect lower level Necromancers to such a large degree. What we do recognize is the need to prevent higher level Necros from exploiting key spawn points. Hence, when you Feign Death, any charmed NPC will immediately break Charm. However, summoned NPCs (pets) will NOT be affected. While this still allows the use of the Feign Death tactic with your summoned pet, it does not imbalance the game and many dungeon spawns to the degree that charming an NPC does.

We would appreciate diligent testing of this change, to insure that no unforeseen problems have been caused by it.

-The EverQuest Team
