You can twist up to three songs at once. However it's not too effective with three until level 12. Start with two songs at level 6...healing and chant. Then when you get warsong use that and healing. Next when you get anthem use that warsong and healing...you lose it sometimes on a bash but three is better than two. At level 12 Use Bellow, Warsong, and anthem/healing.
For groups use healing, bellow, and anthem. Now get this...sometimes you have to decide on what ya wanna do. When fighting magic i may use two of the guardian songs. Trust me they really help. And to really confuse you as to what to use..lvl 18 you get a upgraded version the second song. It lowers AC of monsters..i am not sure how much and it does damage. At 20 you get a AC lowering and attack speed lowering also. When Combining these two i have noticed a difference in the creaturs. Be careful because they have a area effect. When I am Twisting, instead of timing my singing, I just wait until the text area notifies me that the song is in effect. Then I start into the next song and then the next ...

Seems to me that I have often been close to Twisting four songs. Instead of a complete overlapping of tunes, as happens with three songs, the first song usually ends just as the fourth song has started. Essentially, this is Twisting four songs ... with just one small dropped beat. However, a skillful Bard can work it so her audience -- who is not quite as knowledgeable -- will never even notice.

On a side note: I once banded with a party that included two other bards besides myself. You have never heard such sweet sounds nor seen such a spectacular light show as when 3 Spellsingers are Twisting 2 or 3 songs apiece. Magnificent!

Just my thoughts.
