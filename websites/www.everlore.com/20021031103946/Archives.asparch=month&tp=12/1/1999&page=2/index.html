Brad on RoK/EQ
[12/6/1999-3:34:45 PM]  Comments[0]

Everquest producer, Brad McQuaid replies to a post made on the official Everquest Forums:

Chipeast wrote:

It bothers me that Verant has spent so much time on this new release before they even bother to fix the current EQ. Currently there are very many bugs that exist, BIG ones, that Verant has been aware of and has not fixed. In fact they are so well known that I won't even post any. Just ask you local high level guild. Verant needs to listen to their people rather then try and drum up some extra revenue. Also, why is it that no one ever gets disconnected from Verants "competition" during game play. I'm sorry but if the internet is causing all of your connection problems and disconnects, why doesn't it affect other online games? I'm sure you know why.

1. We have fixed many bugs and continue to do so.

2. Fixing bugs and working on the Expansion are not mutually exclusive activities (they involve different people).

3. The majority of players want more EverQuest. They want more to do, more to see, more to obtain. That's why we're making the Expansion.

4. Some players, depending on their connection, ISP, and other factors related to the internet, will lose connection to Internet games period -- EQ is no exception.

-Brad

'Nerfs' vs. Enhancements -Brad's Post
[12/7/1999-2:32:09 PM]  Comments[0]

Everquest Producer, Brad McQuaid has recently posted a list of negative and postive changes that was made to Everquest from the start. This list can be found on the official Everquest Forums, or on alt.games.everquest newsgroups. Here is the entire post. Be aware that this is very LONG:

Several members of the EQ team, with some initial help from a player's email, put the following document together listing both enhancements and 'nerfs' we've made to the game since launch. And while we probably forgot some things, both negative and positive (please let us know and I'll update the document), I think it puts in perspective the ratio of positive vs. negative changes to the game we've made. I also feel it heartily
invalidates the assertion that we are out to make the game harder or to have players advance slower overall, or that 'all we do is nerf'.

The following is broken down by class, and then addresses those changes made that affect the whole game. Changes we feel are positive from just about any perspective are denoted with a '+', and those some players might consider a 'nerf', but that we felt were important for the long term health of the game, are denoted with a '-'.

(again, I'm sure we've forgotten some things, both '+' and '-'; please feel free to comment and add items).

WIZARDS:
+ Added additional means by which to obtain Ice Comet
+ Made Researching much easier
+ Added more high level spells
+ Added more Class Specific Quests
+ Added more Class Specific Items
+ Enhanced High Level (35+) Meditation
+ Enhanced AoE Spells to let Wizard effect more creatures
+ Added Plane Travel Spells
+ Enhanced Specific Wizard Stun Spells
+ Added AoE slow monster spell (c.f. Bonds of Force)

RANGERS:
+ Introduction of better average armor that is easier than most to get
+ Critical Bow hits
+ Many new quests requiring a tracker to find outdoor spawn
+ Made Ebony Blade group friendly
+ More variety of items obtained via the Forage skill
+ Increased defensive skill caps (parry, dodge)
+ Added innate resistance to fire and cold
+ Added more Class Specific Quests
+ Added more Class Specific Items
+ Added additional Ranger Spells
+ Changed Some Spell Casting Skills to allow easier Skilling
(c.f. Dance of the Fireflies)

DRUIDS:
- DOTs changed negatively, making Kiting less efficient
+ DoT changed positively to enhance group play
+ Many new quests requiring a tracker to find outdoor spawn
+ Increased druid's tracking ability
+ Added more Class Specific Quests
+ Added more Class Specific Items
+ Enhanced High Level (35+) Meditation
+ Enhanced Pet/Charmed Animal Pathing
+ Enhanced Some Druid Anti-Movement Spells (c.f. Ensnare,
Grasping Roots, etc.)
+ Lowered the Casting Level of Some Druid Teleport Spells

PALADINS:
+ Added Archery
+ Added innate resistance to disease
+ Added more Class Specific Quests
+ Added more Class Specific Items
+ Increased stats of Armor of Ro items

SHADOWKNIGHTS:
+ Spell changes (added lifetap)
+ Made Harm Touch less resistible
+ Added innate resistance to poison and disease
- DOTs changed negatively, making Kiting less efficient
+ DoT changed positively to enhance group play
+ Added more Class Specific Quests
+ Added more Class Specific Items
+/- Feign Death Changes Better for Low Level Character, Better
for High Level once learning curve has passed

+ Changes to Pet Appearances

New Producer Letter
[12/7/1999-2:40:19 PM]  Comments[0]

There was a new Producer Letter added to the official Everquest website. Internet Relations Manager, Gordon Wrinn writes:

12/6/99

Hello everyone!

My name is Gordon Wrinn, and I am the Internet Relations Manager at Verant Interactive, Inc. Some of you who participated in the EverQuest Beta, or followed EverQuest during the beta period may remember me as Guide Abashi.

I discovered EverQuest quite by accident in December of 1997 while perusing a message board for another game. The concept of playing a 3D role-playing game in a dynamic and persistent world immediately intrigued me. With the beta for the game still being some months away, I, like everyone else, was relegated to discussing ideas about the game on the public boards. Come June of 1998, I was quite excited when I received an invitation to the earliest phase of the EverQuest beta. In late 1998, I was invited to join the EverQuest Guide Program.

Due to some "real life" issues that took me out-of-town, I had to leave the Guide Program behind shortly prior to release. However, some five months later, I returned to EverQuest and my role in the Guide Program, and not to mention, my role as a player J I was quite pleased a month or so ago when I was offered my current position.

My job at Verant is to improve the quality and quantity of the information passing between Verant Interactive, Inc. and the player-base of EverQuest as a whole. Up until now, public posts made by Brad and other members of the EverQuest Team have been made during their ample (*grin*) free time. The Management Team here decided that their customers deserved more than this, and hence my position was created. No, I am not here to replace announcements made by Brad and other members of the EverQuest team, but to supplement and add to them. My job here is to know everything there is to know about EverQuest, represent the player-base in our meetings, and keep in touch with the public.

Ill be honest with you. Im not a "PR Guy" or "Spin Doctor". My professional background is in mission-critical subscriber systems management and development. EverQuest, being a subscriber-based service maintained in a mission-critical manner, is directly in my area of technical expertise. This experience, and my experience with EverQuest both as a player and a former Guide gives me the necessary skills to understand what EverQuest and Verant are all about, and keep a two-way dialogue going between the development team and the EverQuest player-base. Im not fond of beating around the bush about issues, and tend to speak my mind. Though I wont always be able to tell you what you would like to hear, I hope youll at least appreciate my candor.

In closing, Id like to say that I am looking forward to giving you, the customers, the attention and information that you deserve. Id also like to thank Brad for giving me the opportunity to hijack his letter, as it will hopefully cut down on the number of "Who is this guy???" posts on the public boards and newsgroups when I begin to make my appearances there in the near future J

Gordon Wrinn
Internet Relations Manager
Verant Interactive, Inc.
