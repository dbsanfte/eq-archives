1- Our internal network problems -- we think is fixed -- we replaced some hardware and reconfigure somethings and it seems OK from our end (meaning no internal packet loss) We will see how that goes as we get more people.
2- External UUnet problems -- UUnet is having their own problems with that same router that went down on tuesday. We are all over them to get this fixed. This is not likely explanation for PL.

Population Overgrowth
Trindalc [3/26/1999-2:37:18 PM]  Comments[0]

Aradune entered Fennin Ro last night and made the following statements in a series of broadcast messages:

"I know this is a popular server because many guilds and groups have chosen it. However, the other servers have far less players on them.

"I would suggest that players start characters new on other servers. No you cannot move existing ones. I really think that gameplay would be better on other servers. It's up to you though.

"When I say gameplay may be better on the other servers, I refer to the fact that the other servers are less crowded.

"People, I am not requiring that people move. You can go where you want, this is just a suggestion.

"We decided against moving characters from one server to another during beta. We believe it would adversely affect the economics of the servers so we're not going to allow it.

"So like I said, if you don't have too much time invested here, I'd move. Up to you.

"As for total number of servers, don't worry. The others aren't full yet and we can bring new servers online very fast. FYI the servers themselves can handle more than this no problem. It's a matter of crowding. I imagine it bothers some people more than others. So this has all just been a suggestion. I'm just trying to help :)"

 

A Few Good Ideas...
Trindalc [3/26/1999-2:41:32 PM]  Comments[0]

EverQuest's Producer, Brad McQuaid, took the time recently to address a fan's suggestions for improving the game on our General Board. Here's the post:

- The inspecting another player's inventory feature activating at inappropriate times (e.g. during battle) via mouse-over
Yes, we need to improve that.

- The boat to Feydwer taking 42 minutes real time to cycle through
We are thinking about adding more ships.

- The chat window color scheme making emotes, group messages, etc., difficult to differentiate and often impossible to read
In the future I'd like to make the color the various messages are displayed in user selectable.

- The super-powerful NPC's (e.g. Gynok Moltar at Befallen or Varsoon The Undying at BlackBurrow) preventing resting and slaughtering everyone needlessly
In general these are supposed to be there to keep people from sitting around for long lengths of times (camping). That said, they are almost always avoidable if you keep in third person view and look around. Also, before going into final we toned quite a few of these down.

- The characters which do not print, like "Hi :)" becoming "Hi )" or "ooc Go to http://www.eqvault.com" becoming "ooc Go to http www eqvault com"
This was done because the parser things : and / are commands.

- The command history editor (shift-up) not allowing scroll back past the leftmost part of the text editing line (e.g. preventing one from editing /tell's that have scrolled past the right side of the editing line)
Yes, this could be improved.

- The inability to purchase quantities of items with quantity sliders (e.g. buying 20 water flasks takes 20 clicks, instead of three with a quantity slider)
I very much agree with you here.

- The lack of a "take everything" button for looting corpses
We experimented with this, and it was very problematic. What do you do when you can't carry all of it? Leave it on the corpse? Drop it to the ground? I'm not saying the idea in general isn't good, though.

- The jump/stamina issue
This is in there by design -- hopping around Norrath to increase your speed was never our intent. Realistically, jumping does not increase your speed, but we left that part in for better gameplay.

- the Guide/GM's inability to actually say anything useful or to give any actual guidance (aside from resolving bugs, what can they do?)
Well, they aren't there to give you spoilers :)

- The need for a /reply command (to /tell the last person that msg'ed you, or the last person you /tell'ed)
Agreed.

- the fact that aside from other PC's, there is no way to cure extremely detrimental diseases such as rabies (when caught with rabies at 5am PST, currently the only time we can actually play, why not make a trip to town and visit the local NPC healer?)
Because this is a group oriented game, and we want you to find PC clerics when this happens -- heck, you'll probably make some friends in the process.

- The length of time it takes to actually play the game (licence agreement, splash screens, connect, server select, etc., should be just a click away)
It's a mouse click and 3 ESC key clicks away -- I'm getting pretty fast at this :)

- The enemy "teleport" bug where a wounded, fleeing foe moves instantly to a far corner of the zone away from the players, only to return a short time later with full health
Haven't seen this one, but I'll look into it.

-Brad
