In the Lava Storm Mountains, the sky turned red and snow fell from the sky. Shouts pleading for help could be hear on the wind. The goblins of the area were not prepared for such weather and were freezing in the climate.

The Champions of Marr had four members that happened to show up just as the first snowflakes fell. Spreading out across the area, the cleric, Keris Kincaade discovered the small goblin named Syrax. Upon asking what he could do, the goblin requested a torch. Not having one, Keris gave Syrax his Sarnak Tunic, banded pants, and spore covered cloak to help warm him. Afterwards, another goblin approached, Narsix. Keris offered him his bracers, though they weren't much. By this time, the druid Dakri had arrived and greeted her guildmate. After assessing the situation, she ported to the Commonlands to retrieve torches for them.

Once the other Champions of Marr, as well as other adventurers gathered, the goblins were given torches and clothing to help keep warm.

Once they had warmed up, the goblins and adventurers wondered what had caused the strange inclimate weather. The two goblins, saddened that their brother Marsix had succumbed to the snow, were still thankful of the adventurers' help. They were especially grateful to Keris for giving them, literally, the clothes off his back.

'Avatars Offline' Documentary
Athelyn [7/3/2002-12:40:47 AM]  Comments[0]

Daniel Liatowitsch writes:
Hello everyone!

We're thrilled to announce that 'Avatars Offline', the first full-length documentary on the MMORPG phenomenon, is now available for purchase on DVD exclusively at www.avatarsoffline.com.

The DVD version includes both the 75 minute documentary plus and an *additional* 75 minutes of extra footage not seen previously.

After two years of production, the film premiered at this year's Game Developers Conference in San Jose.

It chronicles the past, present and future of the MMORPG genre through interviews with avid fans, self-confessed addicts, developers, scholars and vocal critics. Not merely satisfied with the simplistic mainstream media approach to 'games', the film approaches MMORPGS as an evocative art form with both great promise and undeniable dangers.

Some of the film's topics and highlights include:

- A Tour of Britannia Manor and holding court with Richard 'Lord British' Garriott, creator of the Ultima Series (extended tour on DVD Extras!).
- David 'DelCabon' Noll, the first level 100 of Asheron's Call, on the car accident that left him disabled and the social experience of gaming.
- Inside EverQuest: Attending Fan Faires, quizzing the developers and understanding the long-lasting success.
- The First Couple of EverQuest: They met in Beta Phase I and are now happily married.
- Addiction Controversy: Can games be made to be addictive? What constitutes addiction?
- The Cross-Gender Phenomenon: Female Wood Elves with a twist!
- Dark Age of Camelot: Meet Mark Jacobs, Sanya 'Tweety' Thomas and Scott 'LumTheMad' Jennings.
- Lineage The Bloodpledge: 2 million subscribers and counting.
- Star Wars Galaxies: See the Star Wars universe come alive in amazing footage and go behind the scenes with Haden 'Shug_Nix' Blackman and Tom Sarris!

And much more!

We're very excited about finally being able to share the film with the public and look forward to the community's feedback!

Sincerely,

Daniel Liatowitsch
director, "Avatars Offline"
www.avatarsoffline.com

Bazaar on Test Server
Athelyn [7/4/2002-2:53:25 AM]  Comments[11]

From the Developer’s Corner, Absor writes:
For those of you with a little free time, you might want to log into Test and try out the Bazaar.

We’ve got the functionality in. Now we need your feedback and bug reports.

If you see something broken or if you have an idea for an improvement, you can send them to the Dev Corner (or use /bug for bugs).

Thanks for helping us out. We hope you like it.

Alan
Want to know what to expect? Well, here's today's Test Server patch message:
** LET THE BAZAAR TESTING BEGIN! **

VENDORS:
- First, create or purchase from Trader Satchels from an NPC merchant in the bazaar.
- Second, place tradable items in the bags.
- At this time you can type /trader and begin setting prices on the items you wish to sell.
- Once you have some prices set and you wish to open shop, you must go to a trading area. These are the rows of tents in the bazaar.
- Click Begin Trader. At this time people will be able to buy from you.

BUYERS:
- To search the bazaar for items for sale, type /bazaar.
- To purchase from a player merchant, right click on them as if you were buying from an NPC vendor.

NOTES:
- The bazaar only works in the new user interface! For instructions on how to switch over to the new UI, please see the notes at the bottom of this file.
- You can only be a trader in the trading stalls (rows of tents).
- The name over your head will change slightly when in trading mode.
- The prices that you set for items are stored in your EQ directory. The next time you attempt to sell the same item, the bazaar will assume that you want to use similar pricing until you say otherwise.
- When you close up shop, anyone viewing your current stock will automatically end trading with you.

** CHANGES **
Sorry about the random NPC bugs. It was all one bug.

- The EverQuest Development Team

More EQ2 Information
Skvorkian [7/9/2002-11:59:29 AM]  Comments[22]

Glix Bonecrusher sumits this:

Cut and paste directly from the article I wrote on our guild board:

Just read an article about EQ2 on PCGamer and it led to some of the storylines in EQ2. Here's just a few little tidbits to think about what's happening to Norrath:

*- We're back to the 3 original continents: Antonica, Odus and Faydwer

*- Freeport has finally been taken over by the dark side. Pallies build a new fortress in the wilderness and call it Marr.

*- The Qeynos rogues have been run out of town, but they have set up their own settlement nearby.

*- Iksar will still exist in game, but will not come from Kunark

*- No more Vah Shir (since there's no Luclin). Players will now be allowed to play the Kerran.

*- Zoning is no longer safe. Monsters WILL follow you across zones, but if they're outrun, WILL give up the chase.

*- No initial stat points and/or class selection as such. Much like Dungeon Siege, you will only select your appearance, race, and sex. At level 6, you will choose your first specialization: fighter, priest, mage, rogue, or tradesman. At level 15, you specialize that further, for example if you select fighter, you can choose warrior, crusader, or brawler. At level 30, you make your final selection.. a crusader, for instance, will choose the path of paladin, shadow knight, or ranger.

*- Certain items will render some skills unnecessary ie- By doing the compass quest, you will no longer need Sense Heading

*- Level cap will NOW be 100 (and possibly 200 with expansions) but leveling will be faster

*- Weapons and Items can and WILL now break. For example, if a level 50 item is given to a level 10 character, it's effectiveness will drop, and it's chance to break will significantly increase.

*- Corpse Retrieval is now history. You will have a corpse there for resurrection, but one option being looked at is that you will respawn in a 'graveyard' minus one or two items in your inventory. As well, another option being looked at is not taking an XP 'Hit' as such, but actually owing an XP debt. Part of your fighting XP will go towards paying off that 'debt'.

*- Banks are no longer networked. Movement of items across the world will now require a cart, horse or boat.

*- 'EQII will also introduce player-owned property. Buy a plot of land and build a one-room (upgradeable) building, then invite friends to locate nearby. After a set level, the game will introduce a guard tower complete with a patrolling guard, plus other civic buildings as the community grows.'

*- There will be facilities for weddings, and other get togethers. Let's take weddings for an example. You can pay a relatively small amount and have your wedding in a tiny chapel or building, but if you pay a lot, you can have a huge hall and guest appearances by Norrathian legends.

Cheers!

Glix Bonecrusher
Chief Assassin of The Panzer Brigade
New Elements of Power - Luclin
