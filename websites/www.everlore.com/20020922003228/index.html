Station.com Downtime Notice For This Weekend
Athelyn [9/20/2002-3:42:08 AM]   Comments[28]

From EQ Live regarding Station.com downtime:
This Saturday morning, September 21, Station.com will come down for maintenance and updates at 12AM PST. During this time our free games will not be accessible; however, Station Pass games (Tanarus, Infantry, Cosmic Rift) and EverQuest will still be playable through their dedicated launchers. We should be back online Sunday afternoon.
More on how this downtime could affect you:
In preparation, we will be backing up our member data late Thursday night. Any changes to your member information made on Friday (September 20), such as email address or password changes will not be saved. Please wait to make these changes until Sunday afternoon when we are back online.

 

A Message From EQ Customer Service
Athelyn [9/20/2002-3:42:03 AM]   Comments[9]

EQ Customer Service Manager, Alan Crosby, puts an end to rumors about customer service fees in a recent article posted in EQ Live's Developer's Corner:
Greetings all,

Rest assured that there is no plan to institute a "pay for Customer Service" model. We have a large staff of over 70 GMs and Supervisors for EverQuest alone and plan to continue with the same level of Customer Support, as part of the purchase price for all of our soon to be released titles.

Customer service will always be an integral part of SOE's products and while we may not respond to every issue as rapidly as we would like, we do try to resolve them all in time. If you find that your issue has not been responded to, please use the Contact a GM link on the EverQuest board. These emails are generally responded to within 48 hours thanks to the diligence of our Email GMs. In addition, while GMs are available in limited numbers 24 hours a day, you may have the best chance of contact during peak usage hours which are from 5PM to 8PM PST as this is when most GMs are available.

Thanks for your time and patronage.

Alan Crosby
Customer Service Manager
EverQuest Games

 

EverQuest Goes to China
Athelyn [9/20/2002-3:41:56 AM]   Comments[0]

From EQ Live:
Ubi Soft Entertainment, one of the world's largest video game publishers, and Sony Online Entertainment Inc. (SOE), a worldwide leader in Massively Multiplayer Online gaming, today announced an exclusive publishing agreement to release EverQuest in Mainland China. SOE will bring its already-acquired technological expertise to the partnership while Ubi Soft's online arm, ubi.com, will provide the necessary local infrastructures to host and operate the game.

Customize Your Stuff at RPG Wear!
Georgia [9/19/2002-1:57:14 PM]   Comments[0]

Design Your Own EverQuest Shirts

RPGwear.com (http://www.rpgwear.com) has just launched their newest innovation in online gaming apparel. Their new Design Your Own technology (DYO) allows gamers to custom design their own shirts. Giving you the ability to write text such as character names, class, race and guild name in a variety of fonts, font colors and sizes on the front of the shirt. You can chose from a quickly expanding library of art for the back of the shirt.

“Since the program opened two weeks ago we have seen people design some really great shirts” Cory Rafiner, President of RPG, Inc said “The program lets gamers add their favorite saying from in the game or any text they want. One of my favorites was “Stay back I know Harm Touch” on a Shadow Knights shirt. The program is a lot of fun.”

In addition RPGwear has announced a free program for Guilds that allows them to add their logo not only to DYO shirts but also to hats, mugs, steins, key chains and more for their guild members. “I have never been to one Fan Faire where some guild members didn’t ask if RPGwear could design items with their guild name on it” Cory said “Now we can design whatever they want”

RPGWear is also negotiating with the most popular Massive Multiplayer Online Role Playing Games (MMORPG) to make their logos and game related art available to their fans. Several new contracts are being negotiated with other types of games as well as MMORPGs set for release next year. RPGwear has also begun partnering with game information and support sites to provide merchandise caring their unique logos and tag lines as well.

Planes of Power Character Advancement Rumors Confirmed
Athelyn [9/16/2002-4:09:53 AM]   Comments[5]

Some rumors that were floating around last week about Planes of Power level advancement have now been confirmed in a recent article posted on EQ Live. Here's some of what the Planes of Power Development Team has to say:
The Planes of Power development team has been working hard to ensure development is on schedule and things are looking really fantastic. Initially, we wanted to avoid mentioning too much too soon; unfortunately the lack of details has lead, at least in part, to an increase in rumors as we get closer to the release of Planes of Power. We'd like to put some of those rumors to rest and give you a general outline of some of the things we have planned.

For Planes of Power there will be two forms of character advancement: level increase and Planar Advancement.
It's a pretty exciting update on an expansion only about a month away. For more details on this design update, click here!
