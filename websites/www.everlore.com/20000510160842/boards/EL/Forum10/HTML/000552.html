The two main stein's (Moggok and Crude) cant hurt anything, so if you have both equiped you will not be able to melee at all.

To get a crude Stein, i suggest going to the Oggok area, where they are common drop on the guards, so you just find someone who is taking them and ask.

Stein Of Moggok is a quest that can be easily completed by any DE enchanter over lvl 8, or a good enchanter over lvl 12. You need to get a Kiola nut from sister island in OOT (sold on the merchant), a Honey Jum from Rivervale (tiny little side quest... 3 honey combs from bixies and 30 gp to Kizzie(sp?) in Rivervale) a Koalandl fish from Qeynos (kill the fishy that swims around the pally/cleric guild, loot its corpse, mule reccomended) and an Eruds tonic in Erudin (Get an Innoruks Kiss of death from Hawks, give to Sinnikin Highbrow in library) and then you give all this to a bartender in East Freeport, Gregor is his name i think, he will give you a book to take to clurg in Oggok (faction must be at least indifferent) and Clurg will give you the stein,

Let me fill you in on the staff of writhing. It looks really cool, but for the most part is worthless to a low level character because it's effect Chaotic Feedback doesn't proc until level 27 or so. Essentially it would be the same as having a cracked staff. Also there is a stein in the game that can be used as a 1HB weapon, it is the fool's gold stein sold in Rivervale at the Fool's Gold Inn. It is lore also and does have another use in a quest that I have found. I hate spoilers, so I will give you a hint. If you have really good faction give it to a NPC associated with the stein quest. Really good faction is the key here. Other wise you get a thank you.

Also if you are going to be running around doing the stein quest look into doing mail quests for the Antonican Bards. You can cover the costs of donating for SOW and the Honey Jumm if you are lucky.

Shoboshi:

In most cases the hit for CHA isn't all that bad. Just put away the Staff of Writhing before you sell/buy. The problem lies in spells which require you controlling the minds of a person. And of course, plenty of enchanter spells lie in mucking around with minds. Although it is possible to de-equip the staff before you cast a mind spell, that can get old very fast. For any other caster but an enchanter, the CHA makes no difference. As an enchanter, however, using the staff is a rather hard decision. The best time for using the staff is at lower levels where you aren't as bothered about using your mind spells. However, the staff does not proc until 27, where mind spells are much more important.
