Jasmine,

As an software 'industry insider' maybe I can answer your question. I'm not 'defending Verant' this is just how things are and how I've seen them work for years.

What you're seeing is a truism of the software industry. It's called 'value added'. It is very very customary for 'next releases' to have significant new features added without a change in the price of the package. Usually, Sales&Marketting has already worked out 'what the traffic will bear' the first go around and increasing the price is just not an option-- EQ makes money at 40$ a pop. It may well make LESS money at 60$ a pop even WITH the expansion.

Moreover, usually, when a new release comes out, to get the new features you have to buy the WHOLE new release. Incremental expansions are a comparative rarity.

So, could pay 15 (10, 20 whatever) bucks and buy an expansion pack or you could pay 40bucks and buy the whole new release 'over again'.

Consider hardware. They keep adding features (speed, size, etc) but the price is relatively the same. Is it fair that a monster Pentium III costs the same as a 286 did 15yrs ago? Those poor 286 buyers really got ripped for not waiting eh?

As to your 'what have we been paying 10$ a month for'. You've been paying that for access to the servers. It's the difference between capital costs (the cd) and operating costs (being able to connect and play). The 10 bucks a month is paying for the current server farm PLUS those new servers we've been demanding and the lines to feed them and the people to babysit them and and and... The money you spend on service doesn't go in a big vault so they can give you capital items for free. It is the other half of the whole that makes EQ possible. Just like buying gas for your car doesn't make your next car cheaper. (a weak and easily overstressed analogy, but I hope you see the point).

Incidentally...

Three months ago I noted a universal DROP in the price of EQ (from 65$Cdn to 45$Cdn). This may well have been in anticipation of Kunark which will probably retail at around 65$Cdn again (for the FULL install). So I may just be getting Kunark for about the same price as a first time buyer. I can live with that!

Even if not, I really don't expect to get a whole new continent by virtue of being a 'loyal customer'. That's just unreasonable.
I'll get more and I'll pay for it. Fair exchange. If you don't think it's worth the money then don't buy it or go buy one of those other games you've been touting when THEY come out. But, you can lay off now. You've said your piece and we all heard you.

Regretably, I must agree with Jayle that you have become perceptively more hostile and negative towards Verant since you started your (very sensible and sadly ignored) spell cap petition.
