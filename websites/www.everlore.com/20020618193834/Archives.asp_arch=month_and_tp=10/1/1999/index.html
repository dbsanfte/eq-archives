PvP Fix Idea
Nate [10/4/1999-2:32:30 PM]  Comments[0]

New Article from The Knight Life - PvP fix idea

This week's article is written about the problems found on the PvP server.. Rallos Zek. The article deals with ideas on how to make life harder for people who PK. The Idea is to allow people to vote against someone if they do something wrong.. If your character receives allot of bad votes then you will certain penalties (found in the article). The best part about the article is you get to respond to it and tell us your ideas on PvP.

Here is a sample from the Article:

Most everyone will agree that their are problems with the way the PvP system works in EverQuest. I think I have come up with a solution that will, at the same time, allow characters to play versus each other in a more realistic fashion and let those who abuse the system get what they deserve.

In a nutshell; the solution is to have a character voting system to allow the large amount of "good" role-players to criminalize the few overly aggressive players.

A character could, at any time, vote for or against another character. This vote would be added to that of all the other characters that have voted concerning the same character. Each vote "for" a character would cancel out one vote "against" that character. If a character had a surplus of "against" votes, more and harsher penalties would be applied to that character (more details on these penalties later in this letter). Later on, a character could then change their vote to for, against, or neutral. Then a simple line could be added to the end of a "consider" message that shows each characters social tag. This would clearly identify which characters were liked or hated by the populous.

The rest of the Article can be found at www.corematrix.net/knightlife/article.htm

Thanks,
Nate Allen

Rathe Druid Coucil Prayer
[10/4/1999-3:04:23 PM]  Comments[0]

A prayer from the Rathe Druid Council.

I Brennos Oakkeepp, *raises head to the sky, stretches his arms up to the heavens* Leader of the Keepers of Oak and The Rathe Druid Council send out this prayer.

I call today on the Strength of Karanas and the compassion of Tunare. As Druids we are blessed with many powers and we thank thee for making us so capable as the keepers of the balance of nature. There are some issues I have been assigned with to pray to you for. It is believed my powers as a leader will gain greater favor and thus hopefully we shall see some improvements. I am but a humble woodsman, but others don't see me that way and so I pray to you know to please hear our requests and know that they may not be my requests, but the druid majority felt they were issues needing divine attention. As your devote follower and a capable, yet humble woodsman, I lend myself to help thee in anyway to resolve these problems.

We have several spells which are supposed to be improvements on others, yet they show know ability to be better in anyway. These would be the upgrades to snare, befriend animal and grasping root line of powers. Because of this imbalance we have spells many wise druids refuse to purchase and inscribe. It is felt that the gods are so busy with other issues, that these are being overlooked. It might be better to remove them from the world than to taunt us with the promise of improvement.

At this time I shall pray about our strength as a class. I know some have sinned greatly and taken the lives of the humanoid guards, thus making us all incur your wrath and lose our strength in our Damage over Time spells. However, as a keeper of balance and a spokesman for all responsible Druids, I must lodge a request to reinstate this power. Of the hundreds of Druids that attend the Druid Council, the majority still feel, even after emotions have calmed, that this power should not have been tampered with, and the punishment too severe across the whole class just to slow down those that choose the path of the Unkempt Druids (power gamers). We are certain that with all thy powers, there must be a better solution to slowing the progress of the few, without crippling the many.

And finally I would like to state that I see great wisdom in not giving us a pet spell as we are the keepers of balance and this would hurt that balance. However, we pray to you now to allow those animals that we befriend to journey on their way when our spell runs out, and not attack us. Mayhaps the animal would make the decision to leave or attack based on how much of it's health it had left. If a Druid had healed and buffed it, or nay used it as a battle partner at all, it would go on it's way, not realizing anything had been done, however, if it took damage and had not been healed, then it's anger and pain would be turned on the Druid.

Although, I have explained to my brethren that part of our job is to help curb the overpopulation of predator animals and we shouldn't feel badly about culling the herds a bit, it still leaves us empty to kill one we have befriended. I know of only a few druids who use our befriend line of spells, and even then as a novelty, not a legitimate battle tactic. It has been suggested that mayhaps taming animals be a trainable skill for Druids as opposed to a spell. This also might be a solution and a way to be sure our "pets" are not too powerful as we would only be able to tame certain leveled creatures.

The Druid Council tries esperately to form some sense of community to Norrath. The economy is in desperate need of attention, which although would be unpopular, would be fixed by making items repairable, and breakable. Suddenly people would have a need for each other and items that had been camped and handed out across Norrath, and thus lost their worth, such as the Runed Totem Staff, would again be worth something, as would the skills of tailors and blacksmiths.

There are, of course other issues which we shall leave for now, but our main one is the feeling that we are not the favored children of Tunare or Karanas, but instead are the siblings of misbehaving children, being punished along with them. I am sure the wisdom of Tunare sees through this, as does the strength of Karanas and know that the majority of Druids are responsible and love and care for our home of Norrath.

As your humble servants we pray to you now, to hear our words and know our love and respect and please guide us to find our path, balanced once more, by your hand, and your skills, we shall bring Norrath to a pinnacle of splendor in the universe.

*lowers his head, drops his arms, and kneels with his hands on the moist earth of Surefall Glade*

I, you humble servant Brennos, beseech thee to answer our prayers and help us.

By the strength of Heaven,
Light of Sun,
Radiance of Moon,
Splendor of Fire,
Speed of Lightning,
Swiftness of Wind,
Depth of Sea,
Stability of Earth,
and
Firmness of Stone...
Amen

Brennos Oakkeep
Elder Druid

Appearance of Karana
Janathusius X'Frar [10/5/1999-2:17:32 PM]  Comments[0]

GM Event or was it?

While walking along the Karanas in hopes of getting to Qeynos before the sun set, a rain storm began. This storm seemed a little more wet than the other storms I had seen.

Then, all of a sudden, the great god Karana himself shouts "Believe in the power of storms, my children, and be saved." Apparently, the god Karana had appeared somewhere in the Western-most parts of Karana. After much searching, I finally discovered a withered old man standing in the middle of the road.

Me, being an evil character, knew I couldn't possibly try to touch Karana, but he apposed everything my god, Innoruk, taught. I summoned my guild, and within ten minutes, my guild of all evil characters showed up. They quickly found me, and the hunt began.

Karana was surprisingly fast for an old man, but after much searching, we found him headed to another part of the Karanas. We all screamed praise to our evil deities and charged for Karana.

Karana had resisted all of my spells and had parried, dodged, and ripoted every attack thrown at him. He then told us that our evil ways will not be rewarded where we are going. Then as quickly as he appeared, he vanished again.

We continued the night knowing that we had enough courage to face the god Karana himself. We celebrated our victory by wiping out the smelly, disgusting Barbarian fishing village and by killing the treants of the land.

Quest for King Kazon
RuneSerpent [10/6/1999-3:01:48 PM]  Comments[0]

This is in reference to the server wide GM quest held on Saturday 2nd of October.

As Aanak has already done an eloquent job of describing the events starting with Fenric and Bric I shall be the messenger of what started the whole quest leading up to that point in Butcher Block.

I was blessed by the 6 hammers to have the opportunity to exact justice against those in the darkness of deceit.

The day was much like any other. The air in the cauldron was humid yet fortunately cooler than normal as my northern blood cares not for heat.

Walking along the edge of the water past the 1st party camp on my way to bring judgement to those restless souls in Unrest, I was surprised to come across a dwarf by the name of Duskan Ironhand. Now coming across a dwarf in the Cauldron is not surprising in the least but this one in particular was laying on the rock bleeding profusely barely able to utter the plea that brought me to him.

In between deep heavy breaths he asked for help. As my duty and my blessing I healed him as best I could in my 18th winter. Duskan could now stand, albeit hunched over, as he tried to convey the urgency of his need. He had a message, something to tell, King Kazon in Kaladim. I offered my services to escort him to Kaladim urgently. I called upon the spirits of wolves past to possess our bodies so that we might make the journey in time.

Stopping several times along the way for Duskan to rest I was pleasantly surprised to see he was already getting much stronger by the time we reached Kaladim.

Upon entering we went to the underground castle immediately and the King himself was upon the stairs awaiting our arrival!

We bowed in his presence and exchanged similar pleasantries. Duskan then took the King aside to explain the issues. At this point I was still much confused but knew that there was a great trouble in the land.

Duskan thanked me again and went into the castle. King Kazon commended me and offered me an unrequested reward. His kindness and magnificence was most appreciated. He then queried if I would be available for another quest. One where I would fight along side Fenric and Bric, two dwarven warriors of great power. I could barely utter words of agreement as I was still in stupor at the honor and faith the King was entrusting me with.

He advised to gather as many noble and valiant people as we could along our journey. It is from here we went out unto Butcher Block on a quest cloaked in urgency and secrecy. And it is here that Aanak's story doth pick up.

I crafted many drawings after this event and will perhaps post them soon. If you have any space for such a gallery I would be most appreciated.

RuneSerpent
Barbarian Shaman of the 19th winter.
