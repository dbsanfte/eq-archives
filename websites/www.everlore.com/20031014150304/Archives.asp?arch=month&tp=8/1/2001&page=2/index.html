Misty Thicket Raid - Sunday, 12 Aug, 22:00 UTC - Morell Thule
Iillil Brainpiercer [8/10/2001-11:12:37 AM]  Comments[13]

The Situation:
Tired of being chased by annoying little haflings because they don't like the colour of your skin or the city in which you were born?

Want to get them back for killing you just because you were hunting bixies for honeycombs and bixie parts?

Annoyed by their picking pockets and denying you your rightful prize when you kill something?

Tired of dog-shaped halflings killing your guards, invading your space, hassling your allies and charging a fortune to teleport you around?

The time has come to wreak vengeance of the annoying cretins of Misty Thicket. That's right, an army is being raised to give the halflings what-for. No longer is it necessary for us to put up with their haughty ways.

They smell funny, they eat children, they comb their feet, and they kill wayward travellers who aren't like them! Time for them to die I say!

The Mission:
To gather an army to raid Misty Thicket. Our goal is to kill every halfling that we can, starting with the Hamburglar/Zorro guards, and ending with the merchants and anyone else. If we feel up to it, maybe even invade Rivervale

Odds are there will be no loot of note, and limited XP. This undertaking is to happen for the sheer joy of killing runts and leaving halfling corpses. Halflings kill our guards in Neriak, Oggok, and Grobb. Let's give some payback.

The Execution:
I would like to get enough people to organize into a small number of level related groups so that if there is XP, some people will get some. Since there will be deaths, any costs associated with that should be born by the assembled army, although if we have a few clerics that will be less of an issue.

Inhabiting Misty Thicket is Deputy Budo. He is a mean mofo and has repulsed a group of 6 lvl 25-45 people in the past. He is a prime target. He is very magic resistant. There is also a female halfling of high level to deal with.

The army will gather, organize and buff itself in Misty Thicket at the entrance to Runnyeye. From there it will venture forth, leaving a wake of dead halflings, to the gates and city entrance to Rivervale.

We start the assault at the misty thicket gate. Kill the Deputies there. This will almost certainly draw out Budo, and everyone must put the smack on him. Once he's down we can proceed to the other deputies and NPCs

Service and Support:
Hopefully we'll have wizard transport and cleric rezzes. In any case, misty thicket can be gotten to from Runnyeye via the Gorge of King Xorbb and East Karana. You can also go THROUGH rivervale from Kithicor to get to Misty.

You probably won't get xp or loot. This is a shortcoming of this mission, but it is believed that the joy of smoking halflings overcomes this limitation.

Command and Signals:
The assembled army will communicate on the /shout channel so everyone there will know what we are up to. Although I (Iillil Brainpiercer) am organizing this, I welcome the assistance of an experienced general in putting this together.

Groups will have a leader, and that leader should be listened to in order that individual safety is maintained at a high level.

Once the NPCs of Misty are put down like the dogs they are, we may advance into Rivervale if there is interest.

As far as I know, nobody has ever really toasted rivervale or Misty on any server.

Also, it is necessary for anyone who participates to realise that doing this will ruin your rivervale faction. One of the nice things about that is that unless you're a halfling, it's not a big deal.

August 8th, around 9pm pst, I had entered the East Commons looking for spice to continue working up my brewing skill when I saw come up in the dialogue box, 'Little Feather shouts, Food...feed me!"' Curious I started looking around the zone looking for Little Feather along with a few others. A couple of people shouted that they had seen a baby griffin bearing his name by the tunnel, but he had disappeared after some of the people began taunting it in the shout channels. With the sudden stop of anything from Little Feather and no one seeing anything from him, I left back to Freeport to continue brewing.

After a few minutes one of my friends in the zone, an ogre warrior by the name of Shmash, told me that Little Feather made a reappearance in the zone. I finished what I was doing and prepared to return when he told me that people had started attacking him and he died. Only a minute later he told me that Little Feather was back on his feet.

By the time I re-entered the zone a good sized crowd could be found near the tunnel. They surrounded Little Feather and was giving food to the named griffin. Little Feather would only take bread from the fifty or so people who gathered around him. Looking around as carefully as I could I spotted a human named Veolae Avainoir who seemed to have command of Little Feather. Little Feather was Veolae's pet and she apparently had lost him in the zone. She could command him to do tricks - play dead, fetch her something shiny, sit - and Little Feather would happily comply. As people fed Little Feather he kept getting larger and larger until he could eat no more. Then with one more command of getting something shiny Little Feather and Veolae left.

This event was a difficult one to particiapate in for a few reason. The sheer amount of people made it very hard to find Veolae if she moved. Most of the people ignored Veolae completely and tried talking to Little Feather or attack Little Feather who seemed impevious to any attempts at his life. Other people were telling Little Feather to find them something shiny. When Veolae and Little Feather left they did leave something on the ground that many people crowded around immediately trying to pick it up. The barbarian shaman Owwiefixer dealt Glooba the final blow. One last orc swiftly followed his leader to the grave. Cheers and shouts echoed through the canyons of Everfrost.
