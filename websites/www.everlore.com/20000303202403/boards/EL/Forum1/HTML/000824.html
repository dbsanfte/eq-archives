I have found the secret to EQ is in interaction. Sometimes I go out solo (easier as a Ranger) to maximize my looting potential but I always have more fun going out in a small 3 or 4 person group.

There are about 10 characters out there that I group with whenever I go out and the conversations we have between the fights are very enjoyable. That is the essence of roleplaying - the interaction between the characters, what quests people are working on, how bad it was they just got slaughtered, that sort of thing.

I would suggest you find a few other characters you enjoyed hunting with, add them to your friends list, and stick with them. Your enjoyment level should go up.

I have played most games out there, having come from the games industry. This is the most enjoyable game I have ever played up to this point.

As stated in previous posts, there are a lot of ways to gain experience besides killing all the time. Also, if that's all you did in the game you haven't even touched on the possibilities. There are many skills you can work on like baking, tailoring, blacksmithing, etc. These are enjoyable. Especially baking because you can travel the globe putting together recipes. Quests are good for a change in pace as well---and you get experience and money for doing them. Interaction with people greatly improves the fun of the game. I have so many friends that I look forward to getting on and visiting with. Also, if you are a caster you can start your own business (like I did), or a baker for instance can sell his goods to other players. Some servers are better than others too. I am on Brell Serilis, which is a great server for character interaction and roleplaying. Of course, if all you want is a hack and slash game you'll be disappointed. You don't have to fight the same kinds of creatures, there is a whole world to explore with countless creatures to fight.
