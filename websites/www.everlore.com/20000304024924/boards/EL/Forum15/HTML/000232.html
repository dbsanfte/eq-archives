Here's my reasoning regarding Hymns/Heal:

With a heal, you have to wait until enough damage has been received to justify using the mana on a heal. This means, the heals come later, thus mana used comes later. The maan is expended more toward the end of the fight, meaning your clerics are not at full mana at the end of the fight. Depending on how much they've used, your party may be sitting around on their duffs waiting for healer mana to be able to pull. My melee contribution to the speed of taking down a mob does not make up for the time spent post-fight on healer meditation, in my observations. (Note: This assumes three melee'ers are in the group, other than myself. If there are only 2, it generally works out better to play the third melee role.)

With Hymns, you are doing the healing during the damage receipt. There is no need to wait until the tank is down to 40% health to heal -- in fact, with 3 solid tanks sharing the damage, it's unlikely they'll get below 70%. When the fight is over, health is in good shape, cleric has good mana, and you're free to pull.

Same reasoning applies to DoT/DD, I assume. DoT'ers can DoT, then sit and med, and be at full mana when the fight is over. DD'ers can't nuke at the beginning, or risk pulling the mob onto themselves. DD'ers can finish the mob faster, but then have to med post-fight. Each way probably takes about the same amount of time, but the DoT'er has less chance of pulling the mob onto himself, from what I've seen.

Heya, Tgcid,

Don't worry, we're definitely not advocating that clerics are not needed. The thread would probably be more appropriately titled something such as "How we can ease the burden of the cleric."

With my favorite group mentioned above (3 tanks, enc, bard, healer [cleric when available]), there is a big difference in downtime when I use the lute during the fight and when I don't. Mana song can not be effectively twisted (requires 2 out of 3 song twist slots to twist it), whereas Hymns can be. Thus, while us playing pure mana song may be more beneficial than us playing pure Hymns, we wouldn't be able to do the other things we do during the fight and still be able to achieve that. Hymns, however, only requires 1 of 3 song slot twists for full effectiveness, thus allowing two other combat or crowd control related tasks.

Again, do not think the bards are advocating that we don't need clerics. If there isn't a healer in the group, I get very nervous doing the things I normally do. Bards love clerics. As Moshkiae pointed out, it isn't an issue of who can heal best (I'd say Clerics, hands down), but how we best can complement each other. With one twist, we can very easily lighten the load on the cleric, and even allow him/her to use some of the spells they don't get to often use (ie, a nuke or two -- everyone needs to have a little fun).
