New Edition of Ask Brad -- 8/14/01
Xanthus [8/14/2001-6:13:39 PM]  Comments[5]

The latest edition of Ask Brad was posted today on the Developer's Corner. Here is a little sniplet:

Almost everyone in the game knows that Qeynos is Sonyeq backwards. My question is first, whose idea was this, and second, are there any other names like this that have eluded us thus far? I have always personally thought that "feerott" (to reef backwards) expressed your desire to retire to some sunny tropical reef following the phenomenal success of EQ. ;-)

Wow, if I recall correctly, I think it was mostly Steve Clover’s idea. He and I named the continents and the starting cities pretty early on, and then Bill Trost and Kevin Burns came on board and began to fill in the rest of the detail. It was also, by the way, Steve Clover who originally named the game ‘EverQuest’. And I think Qeynos was originally called Keynos, but Bill later convinced us to change the K into a Q – I guess to be less cryptic.

As for other ‘Easter eggs’, as they are often called, I recently ran across the following site: http://amtgard.pinkpig.com/everquest/easterquest.htm. It claims to have uncovered all sorts of hidden meanings and word arrangements in the game… that said, I certainly can’t vouch as to whether any of it’s really true or merely coincidence :)

 

Residents of Misty Thicket help Wild Life
Illona [8/15/2001-11:44:33 AM]  Comments[4]

DRINAL -

Today while visiting some of the quaint Halfling shops in Misty Thicket, I heard a cry for help. Bungee Jummer, a distressed Gnome, had lost his pet rat, Fang. Fang was a mechanical Rat and while Bungee usually let him run free, this time Fang had gotten lost!

The search parties were sent out, and we searched high and low throughout the dark woods surrounding us - until we came across a shocking sight - there was poor Fang, in pieces, the steam leaking out of his mechanical body.

Poor Bunjee was obviously upset, but there was still hope yet! Bunjee told us that if we could find him some Rat Ears, Whiskers and Fur along with a Bandage, he might be able to repair his metal friend. We quickly rose to the challenge, and within minutes we had procured the necessary items, I mself providing the Ears and Whiskers (ick!).

We stood around in nervous anticipation as Bunjee went about the work of repairing his friend, and with the final twist of a bolt and the casting of an invigorating spell, Fang once again was on his four feet! A great cheer went up, and Bunjee, obviousy overjoyed to have his friend back, rewarded us all handsomely with a bag of platinum!

All in all it was a fun little event, and I believe everyone had a lot of fun during this Impromptu scavanger hunt! Oh, and if you happen to see a large mechanical rat being chased at high speeds by a little gnome in black, please say hi to them from me!

Illona

Giant fight in West Commonlands (Drinal)
Xamtrov Mutai [8/15/2001-11:45:56 AM]  Comments[1]

Last night about 5:30 pm, an epic bout between two hill giants was about to begin. As I arrived, Haz Frigidbreath and Daldrum Oakbreak were exchanging insults, awaiting the go ahead from The Referee. Once the bell rang, the two giants exchanged furious blows, each hitting the other for massive blows, but niether one's health seemed to fall.

After about ten minutes of nonstop fighting, both giants came to the realization that they couldn't really hurt each other...and that they were hungry! And what better to satisfy a giant's hunger than...the halfling Referee!

As soon as both giants attacked the referee, every player assembled to watch the battle descended upon the giants in an attempt to save the referee. Even this lowly 28th season monk dived in and tried to assist the tiny halfling. I was hit once for about 1/4 of my total health, but mend fixed that.

Eventually both giants fell with surprisingly few deaths among the spectators, but then the referee said "Haha! Fooled you!" and turned into a hill giant herself! A battle of epic proportions ensued. The Referee would get down to half her (his?) health then run off and heal. But s/he eventually fell to the combined might of nearly 70 combatants.

This monk breathed a huge sigh of relief, but was a bit confused by the final engagement. Was the referee good or bad? Was s/he attacking the observers or merely defending herself from an unprovoked attack? I must ponder on this more.

Here is the latest patch message for today:

August 15th, 3:00 am
------------------------------

*** Guild Membership Requirements ***

We wanted to remind everyone that the minimum size for a guild is ten members, each on separate accounts. At the beginning of each month we delete any guild that does not meet the minimum membership requirement. Therefore it is very important that you maintain a guild membership of at least ten members.

*** Message about our Guide Program ***

Hail citizens of Norrath!

Hear my words, for I bring news of great importance.

Today I present to you an adventure, perhaps the greatest the Lands of Norrath have ever offered. It is a quest of great proportions, and a journey fraught with danger. Fear not though my friends, for the spiritual rewards of this journey far outweigh any hardship involved.

As of now, we are once again gathering an adventuring party to protect and serve the lands of Norrath. Scribes are lining up even now so that they may take your application to become a Guide of Norrath.

We are seeking those strong in character, bright in wit, soft with compassion, and filled with joy.

If you've ever felt good helping a fellow adventurer retrieve his corpse, or with helping someone when they felt all was lost, then this is truly the adventure for you. Or perhaps you feel your sense of humor could bring smiles to the faces of Norrathian citizens. Maybe your renowned acting skills might be of help. If you are kind, compassionate, and helpful, then look no further for the rewarding journey you've sought.

If you are accepted, I will not mislead you, the journey will be difficult at times. You can be assured that you will be tried... and hopefully you will be found true. In the end though, the citizens of Norrath will benefit from your thoughtful participation, and the lands of Norrath will continue to become a better place.

If you feel this is something you must be a part of, then I invite you to point your browser to: http://guideapp.everquest.com

We ask so little of your time, but the benefits you can bring to Norrath are grand.

A few things to remember:
1) You must have an active EverQuest account in good standing.
2) You must be 18 years of age, or older.
3) Your account cannot be shared with any other individual.
4) You will need good communication skills.
5) Positive attitudes lead to positive results.
6) Ability to participate as a part of team is a must.

Come one, come all... help us to make the lands of Norrath better than they already are... come be a part of the EverQuest Guide Program!

- The EverQuest Guide Program

August 15th, 3:00 am
------------------------------

** Spell Changes **

- Celestial Tranquility and Call of Earth will now stack
- Some stacking issues with Torpor have been corrected.
- Celestial Cleansing will fizzle less now
- Cannibalize should fizzle slightly less often now

** Zone Changes **

- Updated some pathing in Runnyeye, Paineel, Siren's Grotto and Kedge.
- The Drowned Citizens are back, for sure this time.
- Fixed a bug that was not allowing some NPCs in Kael to see invisible creatures as they should.
- Restored "charmability" of most NPCs (merchants will remain uncharmable) in Kael due to fix of combat AI bug listed bellow under ** Miscellaneous Changes **.

** Item Changes **

- The Giant Tree Flayer is now Large instead of Tiny.
- Staff of Temperate Flux has been changed to main hand only.
- Ring of Frost Spiders can now be worn.
- Robe of the Seacaller should now be equipable.

** Trade Skill Changes **

- Imbued Field Plate Boots should be working properly now.

** Sullon Zek Changes **

- Changed the way resists are calculated when a Player Character casts a spell on another Player Character on Sullon Zek. Resist modifiers based on level will now be more smoothly distributed and will allow lower level characters a slightly better chance to land spells on higher level characters. At the same time giving lower level characters a slightly better chance to resist spells cast on them by higher level characters.
- Characters below 6th level cannot be pick pocketed in any zone.
- Guards have a better chance of aggro'ing a rogue that fails at picking pockets near guards.

- Fixed a recently introduced combat AI bug that caused NPCs to focus unduly on attacking pets rather than PCs

Norrath Economic Survey 2001
Edward Castronova [8/15/2001-6:03:18 PM]  Comments[28]

Hi,

I'm a professor at Cal State Fullerton (USA) doing a research paper on the virtual economy of Norrath. Most economists don't know that Norrath exists, let alone that hundreds of thousands of people are active in its markets. So I've started writing a report about it for them.

I can get a great deal of info on such things as prices just from going around Norrath myself. But I need help from other people to find out a few things, such as how a typical person spends time on a character, and what kinds of accomplishments they build up – tradeskills, levels, plat, and so on.

I've designed a brief survey of economic activity, modeled on standard RL government surveys, and I would like to invite Everlore's readers to take it. If you're reading this and you have a minute, could you take the survey?

The survey takes about 5 minutes and has 16 questions. It can be found at this address.

There is also a FAQ if you have other questions.

The results will be used to write a paper for an audience of academic economists. You will not be asked to give an email address, so there will be no follow-up.

I'm more than happy to answer questions and take suggestions :), please email me at ecastronova@fullerton.edu

Eventually the report will be posted at my website at http://business.fullerton.edu/ecastronova

Thanks for your time.

Edward Castronova PhD
Associate Professor of Economics
Cal State Fullerton (USA)
