Well the easiest piece of banded is the gorget (neck piece) which becomes trivial at 102 and the hardest is the cloak (50% sucess rate at skill of 135) or the mantle (30-50% chance of sucession, this one seems to give the most problems)
Begining smith (1-27) - Sharpen weapons and create metal bits, save metal bits for later levels.
Apprentice smith (28-49) - create scalers until level 40 and then start on skewers. These each take the appropriate mold (skewer or scaler mold), one metal bit and one flask of water.
Journeyman smith (50-100) Now you can finally make banded armor! congrats, start wth banded gorgets, move on to bracers at lvl 60, boots at 75, mask and belt at 85, helm at 95, shoulders at 100.
Master smith - 100+++ skill will give you this rank . Make cloaks at 120, tunics at 115, and legs at 110.

Don't make banded armor before you are ready! this could cause serious money down the drain.

Okay, here is the low down in smithing from my perspective. Firstly, I agree with everyone who has warned against making banded too early. I didnt even attempt banded armour until skill 122, and this has proved to be the cheapest and best way to become a good Master Smith:

Scalars to skill 41
Lanterns to skill 68
Skewers to skill 115
Pots to skill 122

I then went on to banded with very high success rates in almost all pieces, making it profitable to sell at 1pp per AC, and still easily and quickly make up my initial investment.

The hardest items are leggings, mantles and gauntlets all of which became trivial at 168, with cloaks the last items to become trivial at 175 where I am now stuck until I can find something else to make.

I have taken some abuse on my server recently for selling at 1pp per AC - other smiths accuse me of selling too low and harming the market. I am open to their opinions and respect them immensely as fellow smiths, but I do not agree. At 1pp per AC I make over a 100% profit on my outlay (and have been since I started making banded at 122). I made up my initial outlay to get to 122 within 2 days, so everything since then has been pure profit. I am not twinked, yet am one of the best equipped level 22 players on my server, all thanks to smithing, and I therefore do not feel it necessary to charge any higher prices.

Please feel free to comment on my thoughts as I enjoy hearing the opinions of my fellow smiths.
