I JUST HAD TO REPLY TO THIS POST!

It seems that most every druid character is determined to gain plus Wisdom items over any other stat consideration. At 30th level I am more like a ranger with a druid's spells than I am your basic druid. My original character was a dwarf warrior and when I decided I wanted to try something else I went looking for just the right class for my playing style. What I ended up choosing was a Halfling Druid. Their melee stats are the best of any druid race, they can teleport like wizards, have better armor choices than a pure caster, heal spells like cleric, so seemed the best choice for me.

Non-tweaked,(my warrior was only 15th level) at 30th level my stats are : STR123(self buffed), STA 107, AGI 124, DEX 96, WIS 126, INT 73, CHA 60(or 80 with 2 items changed out). while my primary stat(WIS) is lower than most for my level, I still feel confidant that I will easily have it to 170 by 50th level. Enough for me, since most of my melee stats should be right around 150. Add a shaman buff or two then I am hovering around the 200 maximums for the first 5 stats. If you want to kite your entire career then definitly put everything you can into wisdom but DO NOT GET INTO MELEE. As stated earlier in this post you will go down quickly. With my stats, I presently go into melee with no problems. The nice thing is as your dex goes up you can fight and cast at same time since dex affects your ability to cast even after being hit in melee. I have noticed a big difference in the affect of stats as they rise to about 105 or better. AGI for instance, when it hit 105 I saw definite decrease in the number of times I was getting hit in melee. Now I get hit on the average of about 25 to 30 % of the attacks made. As higher level mobs hit for more damage this is a real advantage. (Also real nice if you are running for the zone since you are getting hit less and running faster). With my strength at 123 I have a X2.5 damage multiplyer for my weapon. I use a 2 handed blunt weapon(Staff of the Observers) It's stated damage is 12 but I hit for up to 34 now. Most of the time it does about 20 to 24 points though. Add a damage shield and coat spell that does 14 points of dage to any mob that hits ya, thats alot of melee damage. Plus druid spells; Firefist(+14 ATTK), Strength of Earth(+4 ATTK), & Wolf form(+40 ATTK) all stack and give you almost a +60 to your attack rating so increases your chance to hit. Plus since you are NOT a warrior use the ol' rogue trick and get behind the mob your group is fighting for even better plusses to hit. Time is short for me right now but lots of good info in this thread. My goal is to work up to 50th level and be a balanced druid able to help in melee, burn fairly well(I didn't even touch on specialization), and get the group the heck out of a bad situation by porting if neccessary. Just my 2 coppers worth.---Sully
