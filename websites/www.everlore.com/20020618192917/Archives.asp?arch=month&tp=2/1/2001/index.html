Orc payback on Tholuxe Paells
Singlacia [2/1/2001-2:40:34 AM]  Comments[67]

"Who is Sgt. Pain orc?", a newbie asked as he was immediately slain by the orc elite that had suddenly showed up at the foot of Kelethin tonight. A group of GM's decided to show their might with a group of named lvl 40+ orcs on the Tholuxe Paells server.

A good time was had by all as Sgt. Pain, Sgt. Slaughter, Captain Kirk, Colonel Klinger, and Captain Picard tore their way through the forest surrounding Kelethin and Crushbone. The green terror made their way from the newbie lift, to the POD lift, orc lift, orc hill, and finally to the entrance at Crushbone where they dug in and took many lives before finally being overpowered by many higher levels who had come to the zone intent on killing a GM.

When all was said and done, Greater Faydark was abuzz with excitement. Screenshots were taken and should be popping up on here very soon, I however was not among the photographers as I, a level 17 warrior, was killed with 2 hits for 300hp each!! To those warriors who took this mob, as I was not fortunate enough to meet because I was gathering things from my corpse at the time, I salute you, and kelethin salutes you for so valiantly defending our home in the trees.

Player Event Report on Vallon Zek
Korinin Moredhel [2/2/2001-2:22:14 AM]  Comments[12]

Late night thursday, a player event was held for the noobies on Vallon Zek in Nek Forest. A green draped noble wizard named Vadeivian appeared on the newbie log to proclaim that his daughter had been murdered in the middle of the night by a froglok enchanter. The froglok escaped the nobles courtyard by changing into the form of a fire beetle. The Lord's Dragoons tracked the fire beetle to the newbie log. From that point the newbies ran around killing all of the fire beetles to find the one that would produce froglok blood.

After some time passed Vadeivian's Head Dragoon, Varlus arrived to show one brave soul where the fire beetle was. Unfortuatley the dragoon was slain by the fire beetle and it was up to the young squire, Sinji to battle the evil Froglok Enchanter all by himself.

He got the froglok blood and returned it to Vadeivian and recieved a Blackened Sapphire, the daughter's prize possesion.

After that, the Lord's Necromancer, Korinin resurrected Varlus and the dragoon rewarded the young one with a Whistling Dagger.

We got bored and decided to hold this event for the players to give a change of pace from all of the PvP. It was a good stress reliever and everyone involved had a good time. We are hoping to bring more and more events to Vallon as the next few weeks progress. So keep your eyes open for the froglok enchanter. You know, I have heard tales of a Halfling in Nek that has stolen something from an Ogre squire....look for that one very soon :)

Event News Roundup
Xomiach [2/4/2001-7:46:26 PM]  Comments[8]

The crowds eagerly awaiting entry to the Fan Faire were remarkably energetic and seemed unfazed by the partying from the night before.

The previous day was slotted for registration between the hours of 1 and 5pm with a reception in the evening. What actually happened was that the people did not disperse after registration and instead stayed to chat and socialize. The net effect is that the reception started in the early afternoon until it peaked at the time of the official reception.

There were a couple of unconfirmed reports of a fight that occurred in the hotel bar that ended with one participant being arrested and the other participant vacating the scene before police showed. Given the vast number of people who showed (approximated 1200 people) what’s surprising is that there was just this one incident. It actually speaks well of the basic nature of the extremely diverse crowd of people who gathered there this weekend. The folks who showed that day spanned all ages, races and genders not to mention location of residence.

Saturday started with Cindy the mistress of ceremonies introducing all the folks from Everquest who made the trip from San Diego from Baltimore. It’s my understanding that a number of them actually paid their own way to attend. You can debate what this signifies but it’s obvious that to many of these folks their work is not just a job.

The first activity of the day was the traditional Real Life Quest. Attendees were organized into groups and worked together to solve the quest by finding a number of quest items. RL NPCs were on hand to interact with questers. People were VERY eager to win the awards and they showed it by moving like their lives depended on it. The competitive spirit was definitely alive for this event.

A big hit at the faire was an online quest with fluffy the bear who was languishing under the effects of rabies and needed help from the residents of misty thicket. The quest was run on two servers first Torvonnilous followed by Rathe. The crowd greatly enjoyed the quest and wasn’t shy about shouting suggestions as to Fluffy’s behaviour. If the reaction of the crowd is any indicator we can expect to see more online quests run at future events.

Throughout the event and in particular at a designated times people were encouraged to chat and hobnob with the EQ staff that came.

The dinner was scheduled to start sometime between 7-7:30 so the hall was emptied for preparations. Xanthus and myself were slotted to be on a plane by 8pm so we said our goodbyes and made our getaway. Its always a pleasure to get home but no place looks good at 3 in the morning.

The WAEQ community did not make an appearance Saturday or as far as I can tell at any time that weekend.

All in all its clear that this event was yet another success in a long string of EQ events. Each event attracts an ever-increasing crowd. The first event had less than 100 people. Each successive event increased the number of people attending by 15-30%. Given the size of the last event and the fact that it sold out weeks before it occurred it seems likely that the population for the next event will probably be dictated by the size of the venue. I’ve heard that the next event is slotted to be run either in LA or San Diego. This would be the first time since the very first event. You can bet that given how close this event will be to Verant headquarters all your Verant favorites will be in attendence.

Here is a bit of Test Server news from last Friday:

February 2nd, 7:00pm
-------------------------

1) Superior Healing moved to level 51 for shamans and druids. casting difficulty reduced
2) New level 55 Druid and shaman spell, Chloroblast, similar to remedy for clerics.
3) New Druid spell at level 60, upgrade to superior healing, Natures Touch.

4) Paladins:
a) Add Endure Magic spell at level 30
b) Add Resist Magic at 55
c) Move Guard spell from 49 to 39
d) Move Armor of Faith from 53 to 49
e) New level 53 paladin spell, Divine Glory, clone of divine strength but 100 hp instead of 200

g) Defense skill cap made exactly same as warriors

5) Shadowknights:
a) Remove artificially inflated mana cost for pre level 50 lifetap spells
b) Move Spirit Tap to level 55
c) Move Drain Spirit to level 57
d) Add Drain Soul at level 60
e) Grim Aura added at level 22
f) Move Shieldskin to level 34
g) Mini Intensify Death pet buff added at 29, 10% (min) haste 5 str 5 ac
h) Add Diamondskin spell at 59
i) Defense skill cap made exactly same as warriors

6) Rangers:
a) Add Endure Cold at 22
b) Add Skin Like Nature at 59
c) Add FireFist at 22
d) Move Bramblecoat from 49 to 39
e) Add Spikecoat at 49
f) Add resist cold at 55
g) Add Panic Animal at 22
i) Offense and weapons skill made identical to warriors

7) Bards:
a) Cassindra`s Chant of Clarity for Bards at level 20
b) Bards defensive skill made exactly the same as a warriors

8) Magicians:
a) New level 59 spell, Valiant Companion, makes pet immune to fear (own pet only)
b) New level 39 spell, Summon Companion, if pet not noticed by any hostile npcs, pet returns to you instantly.
c) Rain spells no longer count pets against total number of entities they can damage (all rain spells)

9) Warriors:
