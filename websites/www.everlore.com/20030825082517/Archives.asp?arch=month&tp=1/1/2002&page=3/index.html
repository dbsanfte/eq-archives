More on Ragefire Changes
Georgia [1/24/2002-5:39:25 AM]  Comments[4]

Absor spoke up on clerics' concerns regarding the changes being made to the epic quest:

First let me just say THANK YOU on behalf of the cleric community. I already have my epic, but I'm very happy that future clerics do not have to endure the painful and dreaded Ragefire camp.

I only see one problem with the change, and that is on most servers Kunark dragons are usually killed very quickly after spawning. If Ragefire indeed spawns and remains with his previous loot table, he will be "farmed" for Cloak of Flames and other great loot, making the quest even less attainable for clerics not in first tier guilds.

The way around this, I believe:

a) Keep Ragefire's dragon form as is (like Nagafen) and not Talendor (I hope I have the right dragon)

b) The loot is removed completely from him except the container

c) The container is NO DROP/NO RENT until combined with the pearl, when it becomes NO DROP only

This will clear up most issues with the changes and would certainly accomplish what you are trying to achieve!

a) I don't know really how the appearance of the NPC is a factor. And, well, I don't actually know what he'll look like.

b) We'll keep our eyes on that. Right now the loot hasn't changed, but this is something we'll keep our eyes on. Remember, it works both ways. If there is no good loot for your friends to gain for helping you with your epic, then they might be less willing to help.

c) The container is indeed no-drop. I don't know about the no-rent thing. That seems like a bad idea to me. How unpleasant would it be to have your cleric go link-dead during the battle?

In any case, we'll see how this goes.

Thanks for the efforts! Can you consider making it necessary to give the swirling pearl to the merchant in Skyfire for him to spawn the killable version and then the pearl returned (much like the Faydedar trigger is now)? This would ensure a cleric would have to be ready for this step to get the heart and would avoid the dragon being farmed for loot and twink clerics who may one day do the quest. It would also avoid grief players killing the trigger and gating or killing the dragon even out of spite.

This is one of the things we were trying to prevent. There is a real problem with the whole triggered Ragefire thing. It becomes a problem when character A hands in the trigger item and group B kills the NPC. Not only is character A out of luck for getting his item, he's also lost the item he needed to trigger the NPC in the first place.

For most epic quests this hasn't really been a problem. But for this one it has. The tremendous demand for this one item has caused people to do things like jumping other's triggered spawns and all sorts of other nasty things. Short of removing the item from the game, there really isn't any way to prevent that behavior short of paying someone full-time to watch people at this one encounter.

There probably isn't a perfect solution here. Let's see how this one works out.

- Alan

Now, imagine yourself as a cleric in guild A, turning in your pearl, and way across the world the killable version decides to spawn and guild B kills it, and you're out of luck. Hrmph. Doesn't look like this change actually solved anything... just makes clerics have to camp two times instead of one. We'll have to see. You can discuss it on our Cleric Forums!

News article on the Everquest economy
Xott the Evoker [1/25/2002-8:21:22 PM]  Comments[6]

By David Becker
Staff Writer, CNET News.com
January 25, 2002, 12:00 PM PT

The business model for online gaming is still evolving, but at least one popular online game has already created a powerful free-market economy, according to a study. Edward Castronova, associate professor of economics at California State University at Fullerton, recently completed a study of the economic activity surrounding "EverQuest," a popular online role-playing game. In "EverQuest," players control characters that acquire skills and possessions that can be bartered within the game or sold for real money on online auction sites such as eBay.

Based on a review of thousands of completed auctions for "EverQuest" items and in-game currency, Castronova concluded that players earn an average wage of 3.42PP for every hour they play the game and collectively produce annual gross "exports" of more than 5 million PP.

And if the "EverQuest" universe of Norrath were a country, its per-capita gross national product would be $2,266--comparable to the 77th richest country on Earth and ranking it between Russia and Bulgaria. Platinum pieces, the in-game currency known as pp, end up with an exchange rate of about a penny per pp, making "EverQuest" currency more valuable than the Japanese yen and the Spanish peseta.

"It's a robust, free-market economy filled with wealthy, hardworking people," Castronova said. "What you see with 'EverQuest' is that economies happen by themselves. If you get a bunch of people together and they have things they can produce and opportunities to exchange them, you've got the makings of an economic system."

Castronova also sees "EverQuest" as an example of what can happen when economic regulation goes awry. Sony officially forbids players from exchanging "EverQuest" items for real money, claiming all game items are its intellectual property. Yet the official bartering system within "EverQuest" is so cumbersome and expensive that players feel compelled to make outside transactions on auction sites, Castronova said, resulting in a Cuban-style system where U.S. dollars trump the official economy.

"Outside the game, it's really easy to exchange goods," Castronova said. "In the game, it's more like a medieval bazaar...It's like if somebody in Afghanistan had some really valuable thing they wanted to sell to someone also in Afghanistan. They'd want to find a safe place outside the country to conduct the transaction in U.S. dollars."

AA Titles - Absor
Georgia [1/29/2002-5:33:09 AM]  Comments[12]

Absor writes on the Developer Forums:
Hail, and well met. I have a concern, regarding the titles recieved with the new AAxp. Namely: I really don't want one. I am pretty sure I am far from alone in this After going 60 levels as a character with a single name, its become somewhat of a trademark. I would like to keep my single name. Call it a roleplaying thing if you will.

While your chosen titles may sound good with some names, sadly, others they make into a laughingstock (please see a recent thread on the safehouse regarding "Master" Bahter):P I'm sure you can understand that a name in EQ is a very personal thing: almost the only thing that makes a character truly unique. To have this changed arbitrarily is very frustrating.

Would it be possible to have a command implemented wherein one could turn off AAxp titles? Is there possibly one coming down the pipe? A very large number of your players would like to know I'm sure.

While the titles are a neat idea, and work well for some, for others of us, they are an unwelcome change to our characters. A /titleoff command would be a wonderful thing.

Evenshadow, Tunare server

Our Technical Director, Scott Hartsman, posted about this on a public board. It's been picked up by a few fan sites already, but I figured I'd post it here for you all to read and comment on. It is taken out of context, so it might read a little funny:

quote:

Before this one gets too far out of hand, I wanted to mention something:

After seeing this thread lastnight, I talked to the GM in question as well as one of the Lead GMs.

We all agree that this isn't Sir Bahter's fault, and that the "more correct" solution is to add a switch that allows GMs to turn titles on and off. Any name changes due to this, until that point, are only temporary.

(This is a feature that we would really like to add in for players as well, but the two gentlemen who'd be doing that are both handling engine/zoning/memory optimizations and drops to desktop at this point, which have to take priority. Unfortunately, it's a more involved change than one might think.)

Please don't be too hard on the GMs - They're doing their jobs, and quite well at that. If anything, it's a failing on our side that the need for a switch like this wasn't forseen when we started adding things in front of people's names.

I'm definitely not comfortable with any change we make that forces a need for a person to lose, even temporarily, an identity he's had for quite some time. That's just not fair. We'll find a way get the switch added in the next couple weeks.

Best,

- Scott Hartsman
Technical Director, EverQuest

I talked to Scott a bit about this over email in an attempt to anticipate your questions.

Scott is talking about a switch that the GMs can use to correct issues like the one that Bahter has. He's not talking about a switch that would allow players to turn off their titles. Those are two different issues. In this case we need the GM switch to correct this naming issue (though I suspect it only affects a single or a very small number of players), so that such players are not inconvenienced by the introduction of these titles. Allowing players to turn off their titles doesn't solve the problem, it actually would make it worse. This would allow a player (and I'm certainly not saying that Bahter would do this) to toggle on and off the combination, which allows that combination to exist against the naming policy. We need to be assured that the name can't exist.

That means that even if we had both a player and a GM toggle for titles, the GM toggle would overwrite the player one. A GM could toggle off Bahter's title (Master) and the player would not be able to turn it back on.

So, with all of the other stuff we're working on right now, the GM one would have to come first.

I hope that made sense.

Alan

Kenwood wrote:

did I miss something? maybe I was dreaming but I could have sworn I saw in a patch message or something that there is a command now to allow us to set what parts of the character names show. On a global basis of course, not just for an individual character, but I don't see that the poster was concerned about other people's titles showing.

Yes and no.

There are some switches now available for those that want to customize how other people's names appear.

The problem for Bahter is that his title needs to not display to anyone, regardless of their setting. It can't be dependent on how others set their names, but on how his name looks to everyone. So it has to be set for his character, regardless who sees him.

Kenwood also wrote:

This was a command mentioned in one of the patch messages:

1 = show only first names of players

Wouldn't this be exactly what the original post was requesting? It doesn't allow a player to toggle his own title on/off but it does give him to ability to turn off titles globally

This works on what you see, not what you show to others. A subtle but important difference.

Alan
