EQ Log Parser v1.00 Released
[9/1/1999-11:22:30 AM]  Comments[0]

Hello folks,

EQ Log Parser have reached version 1.00!

  • Added 25+ more statistical analysis.
    1. Slash hit, miss, total damage, ratio.
    2. Blunt hit, miss, total damage, ratio.
    3. Pierce hit, miss, total damage, ratio.
    4. Punch hit, miss, total damage, ratio.
    5. Kick hit, miss, total damage, ratio.
    6. Bash hit, miss, total damage, ratio.
    7. Max. single melee hit
    8. Max. single non-melee (spell) hit
    9. Spell fizzle, interrupt, resist
    10. Successful cast ratio, spell cast/resist ratio, interrupt/regain concentration ratio
    11. Player list.
    12. Equipment check.
    13. Name of mobs that killed you.
    14. Zones visited
  • Combat analysis divided into melee and non-melee mode.
  • Added Summary to grids and summary tab.
  • Added Stop analysis button
  • Can move column now (when analysis is completed).
  • Re-organized the analysis info tabs layout.
  • EQP now parse over 80% of all userful information in eqlog.txt.
The official page for EQ Log Parser is :

http://kqp.mpog.com/EQParse - Howard Roy

EQMessenger Beta 001
[9/3/1999-2:47:41 PM]  Comments[0]

Joe Reifel has emailed us this article about his first Beta of EQMessenger.

My biggest complaint with Everquest is the lack of ability to leave your online friends messages when you are offline. It gets frustrating waiting for a friend to come on and play, only to find out that he won't be on because of an emergency. ICQ is a solution, as well as Email, but that requires knowledge of numbers or addresses, and it also reduces anonymity that we all crave so much in online games.

To solve this problem, I have created the Everquest Messenger, and am ready for its initial beta. It is a messenger similar to ICQ, but without all the features. You just enter in your username (or names for multiple accounts), your email address (in case you forget your password) and you're done. No advertisements, no begging to purchase the products, no configuration. It serves one purpose - to tell your friends that you will or will not be online. That's probably the only purpose it ever will serve too (it keeps the database small and private).

If this appeals to you like it appealed to me, you can download it at the following address:

http://members.home.net/jreifel/EQMessenger.html

This is the first beta, but the program is simple and I don't expect a lot of bugs. You can email me with feature requests or bug reports, that's fine. This program will always be free, and I will probably publish the source once I have verified that it works correctly. Sorry the download is almost a meg in size, Install Shield puts on a bunch of junk when making the build. The executable itself is only 84K. *sigh*

When enough people have downloaded it for test, I will take it off my website. The build expires at the end of September, I'll have a full version by then.

Thanks for your time!
-Yappie, 24th Level Warrior on
Bertoxxulous-
