Madranger,

I understand your dilemma. At around 20th level, my friends were outpacing me pretty well, and I got pretty discouraged. For me, I really like playing a Ranger. Versatile, well rounded, and fun. Just my choice.

I decided to try some other classes, and played some up to level 10. I played a Monk up to 8th lvl and it was fun, but little variety in combat, and of course no magic. Later in life, Monks come into their own with FD, Instill Doubt, cool special attacks, and Disarm. There is a lot pf posts that indicate Monks are poor to average solo players after lvl 30. In a group though...watch out!

I've tried 2 warriors, a Troll and a Halfling. Nice toys, no magic. Ran both up to 10th. The do have a 'cool' factor in playing/roleplaying, but I didn't see they would be viable to solo with in later levels, above say 21 or 22. The halfing warrior is the fastest levelling class in the game, with 10% class bonus and a 5% racial bonus.

An Erudite Wizard; this one made me smile. With a base INT of 142, his skills go up almost everytime you do something. Very cool. The problem for me is that I disliked NOT being able to 'physically' defend myself (warrior mentality); although Wizzies can be soloed well if you know what you're doing, it takes work and caution. Not my bag of tea. I did keep this guy around and he'll be my trade skill guru and transport man.

So, long story short, I ended up back with my Ranger. You're right about the XP hit, it's annoying. It is, however, the price of being a casting tank. Paladins and SK's pay the same price. Troll SK's have it worst of all, with a racial hit and a class hit. Still, I see 50th lvl Troll SK's now and again so it can be done (ask that bad ass Mekor guy!).

I encourage you to stick with your Ranger. I would suggest finding the ideal hunting grounds for your level. At 28, you might consider Mistmoore; very, very good XP and even the yard trash drops plat. Cazic Thule is another. Ask around, dig through other posts on the boards. Play your Ranger to his/her strengths. Two Rangers grouped can be devastating if played well.

If you need inspiration, do a /who all ranger or /who all 50 ranger and see how many there are. For me, seeing a 50th lvl Ranger decked out in Thorny Vine and toting a Briarzephyr is motivation big time!

Best of luck to you.
