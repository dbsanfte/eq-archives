News and Daily Vision Posting Information
Strahm Grymblade [3/8/2001-1:48:25 PM]  Comments[1]

We will be adding this information below to the news FAQ page that will be available shortly. Below is a list of criteria that should be expected when posting your events. Remember, you can either submit your news through the site or through an email. The links are listed on the main page.

We accept news and information on the following topics:

General News - GM events, player events, contests, and general issues that are related directly to EverQuest and or a particular server.

Articles - If you have a guide on how to play a particular class, race, or trade skill, feel free to post it.

Annoucements - This includes people submitting anouncements from their own website.

We do not accept the following topics:

Huge Pictures - Anything over 100kb.

Word Documents, or Similar Attachments - We prefer plain text, without formatting. Our staff is quite capable of reading even Trollish tongue.

Improvements, Petitions, or Ideas - They can be sent to eqmail@verant.com or posted on the official gameplay boards.

Rants - Horrible things that happen to you in game, there is nothing we can do for you.

Auctioning Player Characters/Accounts - Or any website that endorses such activity.

When posting events, make sure to address all applicable information (sever, guild, time, date, where, etc.). In addition, we encourage well thought out and informative posts. If there are any links you would like to add to your post and have HTML knowlage, feel free to code it in. If not, just add a comment at the bottom of the post with the applicable URL's and our staff will attach it as needed.

Not all posts will make it though the screening process and the origional content may be subjected to editing for grammatical and structural errors.

Daily Vision:

We have been getting emails on how to submit screenshots for the Daily Vision. The link listed as "screenshots" under the Game Info section on the main page will direct you to our bin. Not only can you view the screenshots others have sent us, but one of those sent in will be selected as our Daily Vision. The Daily Vision is chosen by content and originality.

If you have any questions or concerns, please feel free to contact Xanthus or myself.

 

Player Letter to Verant
Xanthus [3/8/2001-10:51:51 AM]  Comments[12]

A long letter was written on the forums today that discusses various gameplay and customer service issues. Here is a sniplet from Absor's Post:

>"Many people who play your game are, quite frankly, addicted. I do not disparage them but this subset of your customer-base is largely unaffected by poor customer service. They will argue and complain, but do little else. However, when a suitable alternative to your product is available, they will likely shift their addiction in that direction. What about Ashron's call? Quite frankly, that game, from what I hear, doesn't hold a candle to what EQ was (pre expansions) It just wasn't good enough to peel away your customer base."<

Fair enough. I'll accept that there are customers (and not just customers of Verant, but just customers in general) that will be satisfied with very little customer service. I will also concede that there are a group of players that have already reached the end of their time with our game and will move on when a suitable alternative comes along. But I would argue that their reactions have little to do with customer service, and everything to do with the kind of customers they are (neither good nor bad, just their nature). Some people will move on to the next best thing regardless of what we do, and others have played themselves out on EverQuest. We do our best to retain these people, obviously.

>"I think the major issues with the game came when you expanded the characters beyond lvl 50 in an enviornment that was never designed to support it. You have major balance issues between melee and casters because of the wealth of melee items and the relative scarcity of both spells and caster items."<

I disagree. I think that the environment could support extended levels. Though I readily admit that the implementation of those extended levels wasn't perfect. That does not reflect the ability of the game to support such expansion. It only exposes the fact that we need to work more on making it work. We've already made several strides to reach that goal, and I think we'll have it all fixed up soon enough.

[...Open Letter to Gordon, Brad, and Alan.]

Breastplate Change Next Patch
Xanthus [3/8/2001-10:36:36 AM]  Comments[0]

Absor comments on when the the cleric BP change will be on the official boards. We have included Absor's post from the original thread regarding the Cleric BP Change:

This is regarding the clerical Plane of Growth breastplates, the ones with the ability to cast Celestial Elixer (the Chestplate of Stability, Akkirus' Chestplate of the Risen, the Breastplate of Forbidden Rites and the Templar's Chestplate). For many of the same reasons that we made recent changes to the Donal's Breastplate, we are going to make some changes to these items with the next patch (which will be next week).

Existing instances of these Plane of Growth breastplates will have the casting time for Celestial Elixer increased to one minute.

No new instances of the Plane of Growth breastplates will occur (they will stop dropping).

In places where the Plane of Growth breastplates were being created (off of NPC corpses or as the result of quests) new breastplates will be created.

Basically, the existing breastplates are having their casting times increased and they won't be dropping anymore, new breastplates will take their place.

I'm sorry that these items are being changed so long after their introduction. But this is something that we feel needs to be done for the integrity of gameplay in EverQuest.

Alan

>"Alan, can you give us a timeframe for the "fix" please?"<

We want to have this new effect in for the next patch. Right now, I don't know the effect or when the next patch will be. I'll let you know when I do.

Alan

The Future of Questing?
Georgia [3/6/2001-4:09:48 AM]  Comments[122]

Absor comments on questing in EverQuest in this thread.

I wanted to talk to all of you about the way turning in quest items works. We are thinking about making a change here, and I'd like to talk about it.

Let me set up a fictional example. Let's say that there is a quest NPC that gives out the final item for the quest in exchange for three other items. Currently, if you give the NPC two of the correct items and a third item that you think is the correct item but isn't, the NPC will take all of the items and give you nothing. This seems like a rude thing for the NPC to do.

Also, if someone has come along and given the NPC one of the three items before you arrive, and you give him the three correct items all at once he'll give you the quest result, but the person that gave the one item previously will get nothing (the NPC is, basically, letting you complete the transaction that he started).

Both of these situations cause someone to lose out. That's not something that we like.

We've been thinking about making a change to the way this whole turning in quest items thing works. The most reasonable and eloquent solution to this problem would be to simply have the NPC give the items back if they are not enough to complete the quest. This would mean that if you give him one item, he would give it back to you and tell you that he needs more than just that item to give you what you want. If you give him two correct items and an incorrect item, he would give the quest items back to you and tell you that you made a mistake (he might or might not give you back the mistaken item, depending on the technical ability to get him to do that).

This would fix a bunch of problems with questing. But, as you all have probably guessed, it would eliminate the ability to 'multi-quest'. We know that the whole concept of multi-questing is a touchy one.

So I have a question for you. Would eliminating the problems with losing quest items to NPCs be worth the loss of the ability to multi-quest?

Alan
