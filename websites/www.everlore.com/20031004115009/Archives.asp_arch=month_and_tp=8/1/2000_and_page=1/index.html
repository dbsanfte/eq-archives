Abashi - Scammer warning!

I woke up this morning and logged onto EQ. I was in ecom and did my usual auction that I have been for the last 2 weeks: "rokyls channeling crystal and plat tiara for sale/trade. Looking for Bonecaster's Robe."

I see, after about 2 minutes, an auction for a bonecasters robe for sale. I ask Trallin what he would like for it. He replies " a gnoll hide tome". Now, admittedly, I didn't know exactly what the tome was worth. I knew it was pretty decent tho. So I just figured he wanted one badly or didn't need money. After about another 3 mins, I see someone auction a gnoll hide tome for sale. I ask Kristee what she wants for the tome. She replies "well, it's worth about 14k, so something around there" I offer up my crystal, thinking I'm getting a bonecasters any minute now, I didn't really care. I ask for all 3 parties to be present for the trade and they both agree. I see Kristee immediately. She starts to hurry the trade from the get go. "hurry up, my parents are yelling at me! I have to go!" I then ask Trallin "are you coming?". He replies "yes, finishing up a trade, brt".

Not 10 seconds after I complete the trade with Kristee, Trallin is nowhere to be found. He logged. 1 minute after that, Kristee logged. Another person, Suffocate, came up to me just after my trade and asked if I had traded with her. He immediately told me I was probably scammed because they had tried the same thing with him, but he didn't fall for it.

Though I understand the original posters concerns, I'm not really sure how we could get involved in something like that. Our policy here is that a good trade is a trade where an item changes hands in exchange for an agreed price. If the item offered is the one you buy, then the trade is good, regardless what representations may have been made about the item.

This is a case where it appears that caveat emptor (let the buyer beware) applies. If a trade is too good to be true, it probably is. If we were to get involved with this, it would be tantemount to implementing GM controlled price-fixing on these items.

-Gordon

Why does there have to be downsides on the best Armor?!

Why does there have to be downsides on the best Armor?!

Basically, in order to make "best" an opinion. If there were no downsides, everyone would make the same choices regarding armor, and each new level of armor would make the previous one obsolete. Take the new Kunark armor as an example. Most of it doesn't have any stats, but it's high in AC and has a spell attached. This may not be the "best" armor to wear, espicially if you want stats. Yet if all you want is AC, then it may be the best.

The concept of "best" is subjective in this case, allowing each player to make decisions that best suits their particular style and specific encounter.

-Gordon

Gordon updates the official board policies
Catalyst [8/4/2000-10:54:43 AM]  Comments[6]

A couple more board policies

Hello all,

We're quite pleased with the way the boards are working out so far, and hope that they will continue in this fashion. That said, there are a couple other issues that we need to address in order to facilitate the board's continued usefulness:

Nerf Requests: We're going to begin closing requests to nerf others classes. It becomes a fairly common occurance when it's apparent that we aren't going to upgrade another class. Nerf requests are very inflamatory and are a breeding ground for all-out flame wars. As we don't want to have to liberally ban people if it can be handled via other means, closing the threads is probably the best action we could take.

Dead issues: We'll also begin closing threads, explicitly or implicitly addressed to us, that we consider dead issues, issues that we've addressed already and consider "locked down". You all can feel free to continue discussing the issue amongst yourselves, but if it's a "Verant please read!" thread, and we consider the issue closed, we'll close the thread as well. We're going to do this in hopes of making room for new threads on other subjects that just can't seem to draw much interest on their first go-around. Rather than have a dozen threads pop up asking us to continue explaining the issue, this might be the best course of action.

In addition, I'm going to create a "dead issue" section of the FAQ for these things as they continue to come up. We realize that not everyone can read everything on the boards every day, so we should have the data available someplace.

-Gordon

Concerning the "dead issue" stuff, please take note that it only speaks to threads addressed to us. Like I said in the original post, feel free to discuss it amongst yourselves.

What I'm trying to avoid is this:

1. Post shows up asking for an issue to be addressed. We address it.
2. More posts show up asking for it to be addressed further, we clarify.
3. Yet more posts show up demanding it be addressed. We run out of ways to address the issue and sooner or later stop responding.
4. More posts show up asking why we're ignoring the issue. We post that it's already been addressed several times.
5. More posts show up saying that it wasn't addressed well-enough to begin with, yet we've said all that we can say on the issue.

Where does it stop? A solution is needed, and this one seems better than having a bunch of "why are you ignoring..." posts on the front page.

-Gordon
