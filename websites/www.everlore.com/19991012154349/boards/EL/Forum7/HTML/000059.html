Well lets look at it at a variety of ways.

Mages:
Pets: Pets are a very good thing. You can solo things with them, and you wont need to med as much if you didnt have them. They take a little exp, but this is irrelevant because you don't have to wait to gain your mana back. The sucky thing is that pets suck in dungeons and are not as effective as you get into the higher levels. (note the "not as", i'm not saying they are worthless, anyone that has sacrificed a pet to a sand giant knows they are VERY useful.)

Spells: The mage spell selection is good IMHO at all stages of advancement. However, people can say that wizards spells do more damage, I say so what. You can do the mana/damage conversions if you want to see the best ratios, and mages and wizards equal out. (somewhat)
All in all pets make up for any spell damage loss.

Wizards:
Spells: Since this is all they have the better damn well have the most damaging spells. I would seriously look at the commitment it takes to level a wizard. The are very slow to advance because you are constantly medding. Grouping is essensial with a wizard, not so with a mage. But, once you get higher in rank then, boy oh boy, you can just about solo anything. (ala Root n Shoot) This is why you always see a group of high level wizzes camping all the hotspots. Curse you spellflingers!
And don't forget about teleport too. Go first class anywhere!

All in all it just depends if you want to group or not. Wizzies at high levels are a staple of any group, mages not so much. Mages can solo better than wizards at almost any stage of the game (in the right areas!)

So do you want it easy at the beginning and hard later (mage) or do you want it hard now and easy later (wizard)?

Me, i pick mage but thats me. =D

This is a post from someguy in "wizards suck" thread.


A rather longer post than intended -- and one that I have not proof read...

The most popular comparison seems to be Wizard to Mage, and, I would agree, that these two are the most
compatible for the types of comparisons that are being made.

I have played both classes past mid-level (Considering 25 to be mid-level). It seems that damage is what
everyone wishes to compare. At every? level a wizard does SLIGHTLY more damage per cast, with SLIGHTLY less
mana, for a SLIGHTLY faster casting / recycle time. The wizard also gets a much wider variety of types of
damage, making the wizard more versitile, and forcing the mage to choose mobs much more selectivly. All of the
wizards damage spells are Evocation (not so with the mage -- some are summoning), this makes specialization
more valuable for the wizard. Wizards get gate spells -- giving them a Much wider selection of zones and mobs.
And while the mage spends 30 to40 minutes running across zones, the wizard is able to spend that time finding a
group/getting exp/or just realizing that there is not good opportunity in this zone and gating someplace else --
oops, thats another 30 minutes for the mage.

The wizard gets the root line of spells -- and yes, the get resisted about 60% of the time, and a DD can knock a
mob free early. But they are much better used to keep a mob out of combat, stop an early runner, allow a
wounded player to retreat giving the healer a little extra time to get that heal off.

The wizard gets stun spells. resisted more than i would like, but very valuable if used properly -- I'm not going
into this.

The wizard gets the Skin series of spells.

The mage gets summon spells. These are not especially valuable, except for summon bandages, and the summon
(20) food and water. But really, ive found even as a mage I would just buy a backpack full of food, because
summoning it was rather a bother and is by no means unique to the mage.

The mage gets a pet, and, quite a nice pet if used intelligently. This is most usefull when fighting single mobs, or
even using the pet to buy you enough time to run/gate. On a solo-note, I am able to solo whites and
occationally yellows with a Strong pet in my high 20s, by carefully keeping a mob balanced between attacking
myself and the pet. This would result in myself ending the fight with about a bubble, the pet at 10%, and having
expended all of my mana. You can usually tell if the fight is going to go badly, and gate out early, so it is fairly
safe, if done properly -- but certainly not a good/fast way to gain any experience. And the pet can be valuable
to certain group composition and/or fighting situations.

But, how often do you see pets in a dungeon? Almost never. At least not in Guk or Solusek, or Perma. Certain
places in CT and Unrest, but not all.

Without the pet, the mage is a Slightly underpowered wizard, that can't stun/root/evac when needed.

I've found both classes a LOT of fun to play. I would try to solo Neither for exp, at least not past low level. Both
can be very valuable to a group. MANY situations make the mages pet a great reason to choose the mage over
the wizard. Especially in a smaller group fighting in the open. The wizard, though, is by far the more versitile, and
more usefull in a full and/or well balanced group. And certainly more useful in most dungeons. But this always
depends on playing the proper role for the group that you are in.

To end what has become longer than intended. A situation for comparison can easily be chosen, such that the
mages pet can be fully utilized, and such that the mages DD is just as effective on the chosen mob as the
wizard's DD at the chosen level, and such that ... etc etc. and in these certain instances (which are many) the
mage will out perform the wizard. But dont be fooled by the perfect situation in an argument like this, it is
dangerous. Ask a high level mage how many times he was in a situation where he was unable to use a pet, and
was relegated to being an underpowered wizard. And how often he wanted to go to perma, when all he had was
a cold-based spell, or solusek, armed with a fire-based spell. Or when he finally got a new DD and it relied on
summoning and he has speciallized in evocation.

Just try to look at All that both classes are when making these types of comparisons, don't focus on one set of
numbers or just what each class can solo, or just how they each perform in PK situations. -- again, though, i am
sorry about the long length, and rambling nature of the post.
