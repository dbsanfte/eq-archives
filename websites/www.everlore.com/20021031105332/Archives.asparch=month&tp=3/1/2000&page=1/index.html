Catalyst [3/2/2000-11:53:32 AM]  Comments[1]

Gordon has revisted the EverQuest message board at Everquest.com. I've posted two of his comments about the Ring of the Dead, and Shadow Knights lacking good 1HS weapons. As always Gordon's remarks are in Bold.

Gordon can you give us a "PR" reason why Ring of the Dead was taken out of the game? I don't think this is a "PR" reason... but...

It was removed because it was out-of-character for a melee class to have the item. It was decided a long time ago that only the pure casters would have the ability to bind anywhere.

"We dont tell the users if a items drops any more or if we have decreased the amount it drops" I asked why they didi this with the manastone and Rubisite Armor.

-Gordon

SKs still have no good 1H slash non-plane weapons. The best thing we can use now is the EBW, but that is now camped by Lev49-50 Rangers looking for CoF. Also, the only 1HS weapons we can use, were tuned for Duel Wield and thus are weak for a class that can't DW.

We currently have several quests in the works to match non-plane SK weaponry up with the Paladin stuff, to a certain extent. Two of those quests were supposed to go in the week before last but weren't quite ready for primetime. Just be aware that we do consider this an issue and are making forward progress with it.

-Gordon

Wrinn on overcrowding, and time issues
Catalyst [3/2/2000-12:07:21 PM]  Comments[2]

The two real issues for players with limited time (I'd call it casual gaming but apparently we can't decide on what that means) have are very simple.

1. Getting items. We do not want items to become more common. We don't want to diminish the sense of satisfaction one has upon earning an item. We do not feel that we should get items as readily as people who spend more time online...that would be silly. We do want to be able to make progress towards getting them. Camping is the only approach that works in getting high level items (which is sad and not roleplaying at all...but obviously that's a separate thread). As a player with limited time I have about a two hour window to get online. Therefore camping isn't an option because 1)most good camping areas are taken round the clock. and 2)If the spot isn't taken, the camp times are so long that there is a slim to none chance the mob will spawn during this time. Add in the time to get a group who is willing to camp there, as well as the coordination time and it's virtually impossible. I would love to be able to sink 30 hours into getting a level equivilant item for my class, but the game does not allow this to happen in intervals. It's either a ten hour camp...or nothing. You've even implemented changes to quests such as Jboots making things No Rent, which accomplishes little except making the item impossible for a person with limited time to aquire. There isn't an easy solution to this problem, but it's a problem that Verant should acknowledge and work toward addressing. But please READ what I'm saying, not easier, just POSSIBLE. Allow us to earn bits and pieces while we are on line.

2) Gain XP at higher levels. Hopefully the expansion will go a long way at curing this, because the problem is really overcrowding. Simply put again I have a two hour window. I log on at Mistmoore, try for 30 minutes to get a group and fail. As a cleric there is nothing I can do on my own to gain xp so I'm faced with a choice. Sit on my butt and hope a group opens up, or cross the continent to Guk or an equivelant dungeon and try to find a group. This is not an "enjoyable" use of my two hours, so usually I simply log off, and sadly its gotten to the point where I don't really log back on anymore. I don't want to argue the whole soloing issue again, although I firmly believe that every class should be able to gain some XP while waiting to group. However, logging on for an hour without accomplishing anything simply due to overcrowding and an inability to solo is a problem. Again, you need to address the problem, but LISTEN...we don't want an easier game with, we want it to be easier to accomplish something in a limited time frame. Reread that, there is a distinction. It can still be challenging while at least semi profitable in a limited time frame.

To be honest I cancelled my account about 2 weeks ago and I have no idea why I'm still posting to this board. The only thing I can figure is that I honestly loved this game for the 8 or 9 months that I've played. These issues are important, real issues. Please don't trivialize them or strawman the argument. Most of us "casual gamers" or people with limited time don't want an "easier" game, we want a game where we can accomplish the same thing everyone else can, even if it takes us a lot longer.

You've made some very valid points, not the least of which is your statement about overcrowding being the root to most of the "casual gamer" complaints. I think that before we can make a lot of progress with a bunch of "what if" arguments, we really need to get a handle on the crowding issue and get the expansion out.

The expansion will add tons of new areas which were created by people who play the game actively and understand what the players expect as well as what they want. These zones are in many ways much better than the zones of EQ Proper, and can hold a larger number of people in each zone without them stepping on each others toes. In addition to all of this, many of the new areas in the expansion are geared towards the higher level people who've hit that ceiling and run out of things to do from their perspective. Imagine how nice the world will be when 90% of the people above 45 run off to the new land to do something else besides Lower Guk and Solb :)

-Gordon

Are there any future plans to split any other servers? I play on Tarrew Mar, and can atest that there is an overcrowding issue. I was just wondering if the Fennin Ro split was a one time thing only?

It's too soon to comment at this time on the possibility of splitting other servers. However I will say that assuming everything goes well with the Fennin split that it would make sense for us to continue to use the infrastructure we built to accomplish it in the future.

-Gordon

Overcrowding & overcamping: we need a solution NOW

It took a year for the servers to get this crowded, it certainly can't be solved in a day. There are only two viable solutions, more zones and more servers. We are working on both.

First, there need to be more high level items and mobs

Making these items less rare is not a solution. It only encourages camping and farming. Finally, all those people who weren't willing to camp it when it took 8 hours will jump in because it only takes two. If *anything*, they need to be made so rare that it's just not worth camping it. For instance, what if only 1 ykesha would drop per week. How many people who "camp" it then. It wouldn't be worth it to camp it, and instead the ykesha would go to the person who "happened" across it while "doing" the dungeon.

-Gordon

First, let me say that I appreciate the fact that you are posting such a well-thought-out and level-headed constructive plan.

1) Put a cap on Fennin, no new accounts.

overcrowding doesn't just hurt one class or one ability, it hurts everyone's playability.
