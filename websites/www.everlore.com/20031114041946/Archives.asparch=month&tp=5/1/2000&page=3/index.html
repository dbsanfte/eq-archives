I finally got my CD key yesterday so I upgraded and killed something to get level 51. Then I decided to go off to Firiona Vie to buy my new spells.

My first thought: the leaves on the trees are red! Thats a novel experience for an EQ player.

I did actually manage to climb so high on the mountains (my Z coordinate of loc was well over 2000) that the graphics started screwing up. The mountains in the distance would actually wobble uncontrollably. Not wanting to take a hideous amount of falling damage I levitated to the ground.

After some wandering around I found the cave that leads through the mountains. Transparent ice. Neato. I was moving around invis so I discovered that mountain giants (and yetis and some other critters) can all see invis. Bugger. All green to me though.

One thing that struck me was how populated the zone is. You could barely turn around without running into a giant, yeti or drachnid.

Anyway after walking across the zone along the wall (I should've gone through the middle and had a look at Karnor; I heard it was quite impressive from the outside) I finally reached the zone to Firiona Vie. The dreadlands was a fairly long zone (can anybody say West Karana?).

More drachnids.

Once I got out of the tunnels I actually thought "wow" when looking at the landscape. The "fogginess" of the forest was really well done. The trees looked good. The road was an actual road! Not a texture on the ground polygons thats merely a path but an actual depression-in-the-ground road. Neato.

I found the coast (cliffs--again: neato) and came to the outpost. The outpost I didn't find all that impressive. I guess it was a city-in-construction though. Like many things I'd seen thus far everything had the feeling of "big" to it. Even the dock was huge. You could land a 747 on it.

Anyway, time to wander around and explore some more so I headed north for the Swamp of No Hope. There were some funky looking critters here like the man eating plants. They look like they come out of "Day of the Triffids" (show on the BBC from the 70s or early 80s based on the book).

My thoughts:

1. The zones themselves (cosmetically) look brilliant (imho). I really hope that new zones (like the Hole and the Warrens) are done to the same standard.

2. It doesn't look like Kunark is meant to intermix quite as easily with the other 3 continents as much as those 3 intermix currently. If you're from Antonica, Odus or Faydwyer then you're probably not going to hunt in Kunark until the 30s as there is only one place you can really bind (for melee types).

Likewise the iksar have huge problems getting off Kunark and doing things unless they have invis/bind.

Then again theres nothing you really have to leave Kunark for. The zones (I played around with an iksar shaman for a bit) seem very well designed. Lots of room in newbie zones and an extremely nice progression through the low to mid levels. Thumbs up to Verant for that.

Still it would be nicer (imho) if low to mid levels had more incentive and capability to travel between all 4 continents and see the world.

3. If you're in the 30s and up (right until 60 probably) there is absolutely no shortage of things to kill. Some people said that hunting in the dreadlands was hard (as an example) because the mobs could get as thick as flies. Personally I think it'd be quite possible. There are a lot of monsters but its a big zone. And they're not packed in as tightly as they are in Paw for example.

Having said that I don't know if things like drachnids, MGs, etc are good exp (compared to what else you could be killing). Time will tell. Its certainly had an impact. One evening (US time) I went to both Lower Guk and SolB and there were between 20 and 30 people in each zone. Of course some of this would be the novelty factor at play but I see some of my 50+ friends living in Karnor, Chardok, Trakanon, etc.

I don't know what this means to the planes though. I'm really not sure if people will bother for a long time (if at all).

4. The restriction on using Kunark evac spells from within Kunark dungeons is kinda lame though. Why bother? If I want to get to the Skyfire Mountains and I going to walk or simply teleport to the Dreadlands, zone into Karnor and then cast the appropriate Winds spell? I'll give you a hint: the non-walking option.

Thanks very much for the comments -- much appreciated.

-Brad

Sunday posts from Brad McQuaid
Catalyst [5/7/2000-3:44:56 PM]  Comments[3]

Can you give us an update on the clipping plane bug please?

We're still trying to isolate the problem.

We've not been able to reproduce it in-house.

We are trying to see if it's related to certain video cards, but have yet to see a pattern.

Here's how the clipping plane works in EQ:

Each zone has information about it in a 'Zone Header' -- this includes the type of zone, the name of the zone, the chance of rain/snow, etc. It also includes a min and max view distance (clip distance).

Your clipping plane slider bar works on a percentage basis. So, let's say you have it set in the middle, approximately at 50%. One zone might have a min of 500 and a max of 1000 feet viewable distance. In that zone, you'd see 750 feet. Another zone, however, might have a min of 500 and a max of 1500, in which case you'd be seeing 1000 feet unless you adjusted the slider.

There are two factors related to how we set the maximum distance: 1. ambiance and 2. how the zone was created. Some zones, for example fog enshrouded forests, have a max clip purposely set lower for effect. Most zones, however, have the max very close to the drawing distance set by the zone creator when he exported the zone. In other words, if we set it any higher you'd see visual anomolies or simply not see the world at all.

In any case, you should see some change when you adjust your slider. The more variance between the min and max, the more variance you'll see.

We will continue to try to isolate why there are some reports that the final 25% to 50% of moving the slider does nothing.

-Brad

New players arrive, Old game Vision remains, Same complaints continue and grow. Why?

"And 70% retention proves them at least 70% right."

Where are you getting this figure from?

He got his info from Abashi, Khadgarr, and he is correct.

More precisely we're at 73% retention. Of all the people who've subscribed to EQ only 27% of them have cancelled their accounts. This is over a period of 13 months.

Despite the fact that this is unprecedented and extremely high, we continue to improve the game and listen to our customers. While we cannot, of course, make everyone happy all of the time, we continue to do our best and the sales and subscriptions continue to let us know we made a game that a lot of people seriously enjoy.

What can customers look forward to? More. More of what makes EQ great, and more adjustments and refinements to make it even greater. I think the advancments made in Kunark are ample proof of our dedication and commitment to EQ as it evolves.

-Brad

On Old Sebilis...Why are the Froglocks still the same??

I mean, come on...The Froglocks in Old Sebilis (the major mob in the dungeon), have the same exact model and skin they've had since we were fighting their level 1 counterparts. Why didn't you at least change the skin for them to make them a little bit different than the Frogs on Antonica? This is one of the major dungeons in Kunark, as it is part of the required quest to get into Veeshan's Peak, not to mention it's a great dungeon for 51-60. Would it be possible to make new skins for these Frogloks specifically for this dungeon? Would be greatly appreciated.

2 resons, really.

1. We wanted to nice mix of old and new models in the Expansion -- blending familiarity and newness has always been key to EQ (including the setting, the creatures, etc.)

2. There's always finite time in creating a product. Would I like a new froglok model like there's a new goblin model? Sure! Heck, I'd like there to be tons more models... but one has to draw the line somewhere in terms of how much content you can get into a game in so much time.

Overall, I'm very proud of how many distinct models are in EQ, both in the original release and in the Expansion -- I don't think any other 3D game even comes close to the variety we have. The EQ character model team has a LOT to be proud of.

-Brad

I dont think Abashi is making the rules...he just takes the heat for them

I dont think Abashi is the bad guy here people, hes not the one making your class "less fun" to play. You are and Verant is. Not Abashi personally. Hes just a guy who reads the posts, talks to Verant, and writes what they tell him. So why dont you all lay off Abashi and go after the men who REALLY make the laws in EQ....Brad and John.

You are correct in a sense. Abashi primarily posts answers to questions, policies, etc.

He absolutely has influence in decisions, but no less and no more than most people.

Game decisions related to EQ are made by commitee. We have tuning meetings where game designers, programmers, and other interested parties participate in debating current issues, coming up with new ideas, and deciding in general where things should go.

Smed rarely if ever gets involved in in-game issues.

For a long time I moderated and led the tuning meetings, but that is done primarily by the Producer of the EQ Live team now.

In any case, we are a team, and we collectively make decisions and preserve and protect the Vision(TM). Which is why, IMHO, EQ is such a great game: the teams behind the game care deeply about the game and have a lot of passion for it. This is the game we wanted to make, it's the game we play, and it's the game we will continue to build and enhance, all the while making sure it remains true to what EQ was (and is) all about.

But, then, if you really want to blame someone in particular, you might as well blame me :)

And so be nice to Abashi -- thanks.

-Brad

Anything to say about the Kunark Stratagy guide Verant?

Sure.

Overall I'm pretty pleased with it.

It does certainly have some inaccuracies as well as some wordings I don't care for.

Considering its length, the scope of the game, the nature of an MMORPG, and the fact that Verant didn't do it themselves (which is the norm, btw -- developers rarely do strategy guides), I think it's a pretty good one.

It also contains few spoilers, especially at the mid and high range of the game, which I'm very happy about. Most guides are simply cheat books, but this one actually has strategy in it, as well as some good tips.

Now, I'm sure this thread will be followed by all the criticisms, lists of inaccuracies, etc. Fine, feel free. I've read them already. So have the guide's authors, and they've responded as well. Perhaps they could do a second printing with the errors corrected? I don't know -- not my call.

-Brad

how about a way to link groups for exp

I think verant needs to add a way to link groups, so that they split the exp of any mob killed. This would benefit everyone and not unbalance the game one bit.

They have created a group-oriented game, and created some situations where very large and well-coordinated groups (apparently) are the keys to success, yet have given no easy ways to facilitate communication OR share the experience.

There seem to be a couple of issues here in the thread, the first being linking groups for shared experience, and the second being linking groups for enhanced communication. In both of these cases, we've weighed the various pros and cons of each situation and decided against them.

Linking groups for shared experience isn't feasible from a technical standpoint. The code that determines how experience is split up, and the group tracking mechanisms in game are part of the central core of EverQuest, something that we would rather leave intact and instead focus our time on adding new content to the game.

Shared communication has been ruled out both for that reason (though communication would probably be easier), and to increase the challenge of encounters that require multiple groups. One thing that is difficult about these encounters is that communication is difficult, where it should be. You have 30 people in a room all going after the same critter, and it takes real skill to keep everything sorted out, even with everything filtered out via the chat filters.

-Gordon
