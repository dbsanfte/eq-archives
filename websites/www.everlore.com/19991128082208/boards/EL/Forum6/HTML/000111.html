This was originally posted in the Wiz forum, but since it applies to all casters, I thought I'd post it in the appropriate forums.

This is a proposed fix to the lev cap. It's actually very close to what runs in most MUDs after far as damage
computations go for spells.

Base -- base damage range for all spells. Although most spells in EQ have a fixed damage point to start at,
providing a range is a simple change in the routine for the spell definition.

Assume a lev 29 wiz DD has a range of 230-250 damage.

Damage = Base - (((Creature level - Player's level) * .04) * Base)

This equation effectively raises the absolute lev cap on spells to 25 levs higher than the caster, and provides
significantly reduced returns for great level differences.

Example:
A 30th level wiz casts on a 40th lev creature. Base is randomly determined to be 235 for this cast.

Damage = 235 - (((40 - 30) * .04) * 235) = 141 points on a mob 10 levs higher.

The same wiz casts on a lev 20 creature.

Damage = 235 - (((20 - 30) * .04) * 235) =
329 damage on a mob 10 levels lower.

This would free up a caster to use a proportionately less powerful spell to deal with weaker annoyances when they
happen along. If you are medding, and a mean ole lev 8 critter jumps you, shock of lightning won't do the piddly 85
damage but instead would deal 160 damage. This would represent the casters relative mastery of powerful magics
over a significantly weaker and mentally defenseless opponent.

So what do you all think? For weapon that proc spells, the effective level of the proc would be the level at which
the character would be able to evoke that ability. So a stiletto of the bloodclaws proc would be a lev 15 or so
Bloodclaw spell. This means that the bloodclaw effect can do damage to creatures up to 39th level, but at that
point would be so negligable, that it really wouldn't matter anyway.

Belalthor, new wiz on the Nameless.
