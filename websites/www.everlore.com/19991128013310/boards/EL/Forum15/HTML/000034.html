AGI and CHA are important. STR is also very important. Bards have the ability to wear plate armor which is very heavy. In full bronze my bard is at 100 WT with only a backpack some food and water and a few plat. Also +STR items seem to be far more expensive then +AGI and +CHA items. So adding some STR now may save you some plat in the future. CHA is used for your songs I believe so it is also quite important but Bards start with a fairly high base CHA so not too much should be needed there. Also AGI helps you dodge those blows so try to up that a little too. There's my thoughts hope it helped.

Hail Young Bard-
AGI is most important, put at least 15 points on it. The other 10 can go on STR/?. AGI items tend to be a bit spendy in the game whereas STR, DEX, STA are quite a bit cheaper and more accessible. As for CHA, don't even bother with it since all it will do for you is slightly reduce the vendor's prices. CHA would have been good if it had some affect on either our songs or instruments, but it clearly doesn't. I made the mistake of putting all the extra points on STR when I started so now I'm having to pay out the big bucks for AGI items. You want your base AGI to be at least 110 or your going to get hit a lot. Getting hit a lot = death. Rangers have/get a real cool spell called 'Feet like Cat' that adds a bunch of AGI points and I always do better and live longer when this is cast on me. In closing, here's my $.02 on this- +15 AGI, +10 STR. One more tip, Save up you money early and buy a 2H Dwarven axe ASAP (around 40pp). It is one of only two 2 handed slashing weapons that a bard can use, the other is a double-bladed bone axe which is found in South Karana and is extremely rare. If you are smart, you can have the axe by the time you are 'very good' at 1H slashing (around lvl 8 or so). Good luck to you-
