Greedy troll slain by Guardians-Shanks intervine
Skvorkian [6/3/2002-11:04:56 AM]   Comments[0]

Rallalron Deadsbane writes:

I was in Unrest, camping the knight and running down to the basement while i was waiting for him to respawn, and word came to the guild from Dexon, one of our druids, that there was a troll in the Karanas guarding the bridge. At first i figured it was one of those uber-troll PKers with the fungus and the cloak of flames, all ubered out to kill masses of players without receiving a scratch themselves, but Dexon said it was asking for, "5 yellow shineys" and it would let you pass to east karana across the wooden bridge. He warned the troll that Guardians would not tolerate such harassment of the common people, and ported away to pick me up. After a small skirmish with a disgruntled naked dwarf at the BB docks, we made it to North Karana. Upon my arrival to the wooden bridge, i met up with Fiztann, an Elvin wizard in our guild, and saw the troll receiving payment from a young Vah-Shir beastmaster, who seemed to be trying to annoy the troll to death with senseless questions... i quickly told the youngster to get out of the way before he got hurt, and he replied, "give him hell." with three Guardians staring the troll down, he simply told us "joo pay meh or joo git off meh bridge!" with Dexon and Fiztann taunting him with insults, and me in his face waving my Soulfire around and threatening him, i was sure he would leave. Alas, he remained, stubborn as an ass, demanding payment to cross his bridge. I asked him to state his Deity, and he would simply growl at me in response. I suppose he knew if he had given me a truthful response, I would have attacked him immediately for worhipping foul Innoruuk. I had lost my patience, i began to count backward from 10, and he sat there dumbly. when i reached 0, we attacked. The battle went of forever, the damn thing couldn't do enough damage to kill me, but even with Fiztann blasting him away, and Dexon providing DOTs and misc. Hindering spells, the bastage wouldn't go down. His mana would not run out, and he would heal 1/4 of his HP every time he cast heal on himself. halfway through the battle I had run out of mana to stun him during his heals. It seemed the battle would go on forever, till two old comrades of mine, Jaeg and Chahk, ran across the bridge. Being Shanks, (for those of you care bears out there, the Shanks are a guild of over zealous, twinked, bad-arse barbarians who kill everyone who isn't a human, barbarian, erudite, or vah shir) they saw Fiztann, and jumped my poor friend. After they had slain Fiztann, they proceeded to attack the troll me and Dexon were beating on. Being as powerful as they were, and the troll at half damage, he quickly fell. Chahk quickly grabbed the loot, and said the troll dropped nothing of great value. I later looted, and found black iron legs and a two handed hammer, though I assume he carried a lot of money, seeing as how he charged everyone who wanted to cross the bridge. Once Chahk had inspected the loot, he attacked Dexon, who was not immediately attacked because he was in wolf form, and not an immediately obvious target. After saying goodbye to my friends, who slew my other friends.... heh heh... I sat down near the bridge to prepare camp, when a GM, a barbarian woman in brilliant purple platemail with a sword surging with electricity appeared and hailed me. she told me to wait, then she left for a moment. when she returned she had 5 magical ales which i had never before seen, {they were no rent, so I had to drinkem all =( } and 5 gnome sandwiches for me. Dexon soon returned, as well as Fiztann. they were awarded with magic beer and gnome sandwiches as well. I had to leave, but I did have time to chat and chow with the GM and my comrades for a little bit. I hope to keep in the eye of the GMs, and rise above my peers to display the power and glory of the Knights of Truth, as well as all followers of Marr. one more note... Shanks aren't such bad guys, as long as you aren't short, a darkie, or have pointy ears)

one of the biggest EQ scams ever.
Skvorkian [5/31/2002-10:54:01 AM]   Comments[29]

Be careful what you do, Kris Olsen writes:

In one of the biggest EQ scams of all time involving roughly 20million platinum and about 30 pairs of boots of the flowing slime, 5 people ended up perma-banned 6 or 7 were banned for a week, and roughly 90 peoples' characters were reset. the scam had 2 parts.

First, a glitch in a PC allowed a player, who's computer was so slow that the world server disconnected him thinking he was link dead.  When he zoned, the problem was his avatar remained, so he could give away all of his items, then zone and have them all back. needless to say this created vast amounts of wealth in a short period of time.

Second was a bug in Cazic-Thule; a safe spot in the zone where players were invulnerable to attacks but could melee mobs who dropped boots of the flowing slime which could be sold for roughly 130kpp on Veeshan.  Everything was fine and dandy until me, and 3 of my friends were running through east commons were summoned to the Gyenos Catacombs to the "interrogation room" where Saphios, the lead GM on Veeshan and the over all head GM on VI greeted us not so warmly. After an hour or 2 of the GMs yelling at us about how many times we we broke the EULA, and how many people were going to be reset, and how big of a scam it was I was banned for a week, and many others suffered more sever fates.

Localized Servers and ubi.com to provide
an unparalleled level of access and service

Los Angeles - 24 May 2002  Ubi Soft® Entertainment, one of the worlds largest video game publishers, announced today that it has signed a major new European publishing and support agreement with Sony Online Entertainment Inc., to launch localized versions of EverQuest®  the worlds most successful massively multiplayer online game.

From fall 2002 onwards, European fans will discover brand new European-based servers, with fully localized game versions in French, German and English. The European EverQuest experience will be enhanced, with European-based customer services, both in-game and offline, provided by local teams at European times in English, German and French. The agreement will result in improved connectivity to brand new EverQuest worlds and easier subscription with local payment methods offered. While Sony Online Entertainment continues to operate the infrastructure, ubi.com, the online arm of Ubi Soft, will provide local Customer Service to European players. Ubi Soft and Sony Online Entertainment will be sharing the online revenues that are generated.

In the growing sector of massively multi-player online gaming, the success and popularity of EverQuest is unmatched. This partnership signifies a key milestone in Ubi Softs online strategy. It also signifies a further strengthening of the excellent relationship between Ubi Soft and Sony Online. said Gilles Langourieux, Managing Director of ubi.com.

Commenting on todays agreement was John Smedley, senior vice president and Chief Operating Officer of Sony Online Entertainment: These are exciting times for everyone at Sony Online Entertainment. EverQuest continues to go from strength to strength. Through this new agreement with Ubi Soft we aim at doubling the number of European subscribers to EverQuest, and we have absolutely no doubts that the future of EverQuest in Europe is in very safe hands.
