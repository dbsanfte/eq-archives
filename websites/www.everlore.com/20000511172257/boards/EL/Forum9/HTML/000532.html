Hi, Delph

Maybe I can help you out. I play a human SK (only level 12, so my advice will be limited) and I definitely have some advice for fellow humans out there.

1) First off, everyone is going to want to kill you. You're pretty safe in Qeynos and North Qeynos, with a few exceptions. One, is Gash Flockwalker. He will make your life rough for the first several levels if you run into him. My suggestion is don't ever go into the Lion's Mane Inn, and at night he takes a little walk from the Inn to the docks (this is in North Q, btw) and stands there for awhile. Being the fisherman that I was, it was hard not to run into him at times. It was very rewarding when I reached level 11 or so and was finally able to kill him.

Also, if you kill Lashun Novashine too often (which, you want to roleplay a good SK really SHOULD wipe the floor with him when you are of proper level) he will KOS you. (He's in S Qeynos) which isn't too big of a deal, but his buddy who's name I can't remember just now, will wipe the floor with you. Be careful at the gates, too, because the guards will join in.

2) Your guild is a ***** to find. It's below the Grounds of Fate. It's down there with the Necro guild and if you have trouble getting there ask another SK (they'll be rare) or a Necro to help you out. I was told "follow the path of bones" and that should help you out. Head below the arena and follow the bones into the lions cages. You'll again follow the bones into one of the cages and a secret "door" exists on the very right (again, follow the bones). You'll need that little candle you'll get in the beginning to see becuase it's VERY dark in there. Upgrade to an LS asap. It took me an hour of begging to find this place when I first started.

3) I'm really not a quest person, so I can't really help you there. The one quest I did do was to obtain prayer beads from Brothers Estle and Chintle in WK. They're fairly low level, but you certainly couldn't take them right away. You might know this already, but if you don't, do NOT kill Brother Trintle. He's a mole and you will be killed when/if you try to turn in his prayer beads. You get this quest from the same guy you give your letter in the beginning to. As for other quests, you might want to look at spoiler sites. Oh, there is also the quest for returning the cleric's Order of Thunder to Kane Bayle, whom you will alsp speak to in order to obtain that quest. He explains what you need to do and can occasionally be found down in the guild. This is the only time he will acknowledge you. If you speak to him up top he will ignore you.

4) When you go through Q Hills, watch it. Pretty much every NPC here is going to want to ace you. Don't go near Surefall and watch yourself on the way to Karana and Blackburrow. About 6 NPS'c roaming around here just waiting to dust you.

5) Since you've played a rogue you're probably used to a challenge. But even with a human, you are going to have a rough time with an SK. You are going to have so many frustrations and irritations and surprises it will be amazing. If you can stick with it, and like a good challenge, it will be worth it. At level 12 I already feel a sense of accomplishment because there are so few human SK's out there and I've been KOS so many times I can't even count anymore. My last piece of advice is just to be patient and prepare yourself for a challenge. It's going to be rough.

Best of luck and welcome aboard!

Sadd
SK of 12 Seasons
Bertoxxulous Server

Well, I have recently played up to 14th lvl as a human shadow knight on luclin, so I will tell you what I can. First, I should tell you that I started in Freeport, which may have produced different results, I notice the guys on previous posts saying that Qeynos is a better place for us. The human SK was my second character. My first was a DE Necromancer, and for some reason I had a very tough time playing him, dying frequently and not really getting anywhere. One reason for this was that I had connection problems at some times, and a pet-dependent class is in trouble when going LD frequently. The SK was good in that he was more of a survivor, and could handle a reversal in fortune without dying immediately. I also thought that humans got an xp bonus, like so many people, and I continued to believe this for quite some time. I did lvl much faster than with my DE, but part of that may have been increased skill with the game, as well as figuring out better times to play. I really liked my character. I was considered pretty original, though the numbers of human SKs were on the rise at that point, and I started seeing a lot more after hitting 10th or so. I got into the roleplaying aspect, and had fun being the dark, mysterious warrior at times. I had one group confused as to what I was for a while, since I had /roleplay on, and was not doing anything in their presence to indicate i had other abilities. One of them was a member of the good races, and I didn't want to reveal myself. The other was a dark elf. The Teir'dal eventually figured it out after spotting me using siphon strength and disease cloud on a creature i was fighting while the others were temporarily elsewhere, or so i thought. There are definitely fun things about roleplaying this type of character. They're definitely renegades, liked by few, and not for those who follow the crowd. However, I chose an even harder road than I had at first suspected. I don't think the game designers thought that anyone would want to be a human SK, so we don't seem to have much in-game support. For newbie quests, I did the necromancer quest once or twice, didn't do much for me. Most other npcs don't like you enough to tell you about quests, and your guildmaster won't give you one. I found that I could also do monk sash quests for the Ashen Order, found that out when I was just bored and fooling around, that's kind of funny, but not much use. Money was getting to be a real problem, others of my lvl were running around in metal armor, and wielding magic blades, while I still wore leather, and carried a minotaur axe, and a 2-handed sword for those times when I felt the need. I also noticed that the ogre and troll SKs I adventured with seemed more powerful than I, as well as better equipped, for the same lvl. I used more spells, but we are not supposed to rely on magic, being 75% warrior. I admit it, I was jealous. The higher lvl I got, the more the differences were pronounced, and the worse time I had soloing creatures that others of my lvl could take easily. Humans don't have an xp penalty, true, but that means we're underpowered for our lvl, and we have to fight things lower lvl than us, and get less xp. I know, some of you have done really well despite this, and something that is challenging is not necessarily bad. However, I have a long history of choosing the hard road, without even meaning to, even from my days of pencil and paper rpgs, and it is getting rather frustrating. I wouldn't mind being the tough one for a change, I must admit. And the troll and ogre boys seem to be having the time of their lives roleplaying their characters. I still like my character, but at the same time I think I got jacked in too many ways for me to continue with it. Also, I really want to play on a newer server, luclin is starting to get infested by high lvl campers and so forth, like every other place, and it's hard to play alongside them with a relatively young character. Either you can suck up, and get all kinds of neat things you shouldn't have yet, or you can try to work around them, and be the poor man on the block. But I digress. Like I said, there is not as much in-game support implemented for us, so be prepared to do without many of the quests that others take for granted, or items that you can't, as a human, use. The grobb cleaver thing always ticked me off, sounded like a really cool weapon, but every type of SK OTHER than a human can use it, even the smaller dark elves. It would make more sense if it had just been restricted to large races. That's just one example though. Another would be the armor issue, it's weight seems to be intended more for the strong races, the smaller SKs complain about carrying it, from what I hear. They will probably come up with more things for this type of character in the future, but as it stands now, it can be really frustrating. I imagine I will now receive many flames, and be called a wimp, and so forth, for giving up on that, but it simply does not suit me to continue with it. I have the ability to do so, it's just that I think I'd have more fun trying something else. You may have more fun as a human, though, yourself, it's a matter of personal preference, not just effectiveness. I am trying to figure out what I'd have the most fun playing. The two factors tend to form a compromise of sorts, since being effective is more fun than being disadvantaged compared to others, I can't deny that. I guess a more superficial gripe I suppose I have is that they make humans look kind of stupid. I think human SKs should look cooler than they do, rather than looking like the tin man from the wizard of Oz when they get some metal armor,lol. Not that I had that problem, sigh. Do what you like, though. The choice is yours. There are many options, and many great ideas for characters out there, which is something I like about this game. The variety is great, with all the different races and classes out there, each with different ways of dealing with things. Good luck, whatever you choose.
(and sorry about the length and rambling nature of this post, it's late and I'm thinking too much,lol.)
