Today's Patch Message
Xanthus [7/10/2001-10:41:36 AM]  Comments[25]

July 10th, 3:00 am
------------------------------

** Bug Fixes **

- The "NPC unerringly sticks on pet" AI bug implemented in the last patch has been fixed. This resolves a bug that occurred a short while ago that would cause pets to be the focus for most attacks by NPCs.

- Fixed the animation problem where an animation played on the upper body would get stuck. This could be seen by starting to cast a spell and then walking forward while it was playing the casting animation.

- Fixed a bug that wouldn't allow a player to target an invisible PC or NPC with the F7 or F8 keys even if they had See Invisible up.

- Stacking issues with 'change form' type spells have been corrected. Lich and Clarity will no longer stack, nor will Spirit of Wolf and Wolf Form.

- The bug with screenshots that some people experienced when using 32-bit color mode should be fixed.

** Item and Vendor Adjustments **

- Changed the forged Antonian Javelins to be stackable.

- Gnome Skin Sleeves are now droppable and rentable.

- Spiderling and Spider Silks are now stackable

- Spider Legs are now stackable.

- Tarnished weapons worth slightly more to the vendors than the rusty ones.

- Reduced the weight of the monk only Black Pantherskin armor.

- All Sheets of metal and Folded Sheets of metal are now stackable.

- Rangers will now be able to wear Girdle of Reflection

- There is now a vendor in Felwithe that sells the Enchant Ores series of enchanter spells.

** Zone and Quest Changes **

- The Crazed Ghouls should be less of a problem in Unrest.

- The Monk red headband quest should now be available again.

- We've added a few more Yew Leaf spawns.

- Reevaluated the values of the various fish fillets. All fish vendors should return to normal, but they won't be paying as well for certain fishy items. This resolves a problem that was created with the increased value of player made food items all over Norrath. Some of food items were made too valuable, and merchants were complaining to their union leaders...

- The EverQuest Team

The Battle of Blackburrow: Sullon Zek
Mihwa [7/12/2001-11:52:51 AM]  Comments[22]

The Battle of Blackburrow
(in character)

As I entered Blackburrow after dropping off some recent booty, something didn't seem quite right. There were more corpses lying about than from the usual trains of gnolls spilling down to the entrance.

I shrugged it off and headed back to my favorite hunting spot. I had been successful prying several teeth from the mouths of the dead reptiles and was looking forward to a night of fine profit.

As I headed towards the door leading to my prey, a strange feeling overcame me ... as if I were shrugging off an attempt to be rooted in place. The next eerie feeling came in the form of a strange barking sound in my head.

Villains! The so-called 'good' of Everfrost was invading! The poor hapless bodies of several young adventurers were laying strewn about the cavern as I raced deeper inside. "Don't panic!", I said to myself. I needed to find a safe corner. I leaped off a ledge and ducked around a corner, panting frantically. I reached inside my pouch and covered myself with some magic dust and faded into my surroundings.

I had to scout out the enemy, and what an enemy! Dozens and dozens of those goodie goodies were chasing down my brothers and sisters, hacking mercilessly at their vulnerable bodies, shocking them with lightning and singing them with fire.

I cried inwardly and strengthened my resolve. I reached back into my pouch and spread some dust around the floor ... praying to the Gods of Nature to take me back to Qeynos. A few moments later I arrived in Qeynos to chaos! Everyone was shouting that the goods had taken BlackBurrow!

With a mighty call to arms, many of us rushed back to confront the hoard, but we had sadly underestimated their strength! As we tried to re-enter Blackburrow, we were stopped by a mighty wall of warriors and casters. We tried again and again to no avail. They were just to strong!

But behold! A mighty shout came out that another group of brothers and sisters were on the way! With a cry of defiance, we rushed back thru to face the invaders! Several of us died, many of us were hurt badly, but we were determined to weaken the foes until our reinforcements arrived. Suddenly, through the tunnel they came! And what an army we had. The enemy line started wavering as a mighty battle ensued! And soon they started to fall, and then they started to run.

Showing similar compassion as the enemy, we chased them down and hacked them to bits! We held them magically in place while our warrior brethren bloodied their swords. We drove them all the way back through their tunnel to Everfrost!

But ho! We weren't finished. The blood of our brothers and sisters cried for more! We formed into groups and blast our way into their homeland. As we entered the frigid air of Everfrost, we were met by a reinforced hoard of good! But our hearts cried out for our fallen, and we plunged into their ranks!

Many of them were already weak from our previous battle, and many were poorly equipped, and we tore through them with a vengeance! We decimated their forces and chased them deep into the snowy plains of Everfrost.

As they came back to retrieve their corpses, we mercilessly slaughtered them. Finally, when our thirst for blood had been satiated, we allowed them to grab their belongings and run back home to lick their wounds. We left them with a final warning never to enter Blackburrow again, or face the same fate!

Now, all is quiet back on my ledge, and the snake fang business is back on track.

Mihwa
13th level druid
Reaper for life, Reaper of Death!

EverQuest: Elseworlds is back!
Ryoma Cinderbane [7/12/2001-12:04:48 PM]  Comments[8]

Hail all.

Those of you who follow the comic strip "EverCrest" by Tim Fries may remember the "Elseworlds" series that appeared alongside EverCrest for a short time.

I was the artist of Elseworlds. I apologize to all those who sent me encouraging letters during my short time with EverCrest...some things in life came up that forced me to stop drawing the series, and indeed stop playing EverQuest, for over a year.

Well....now I'm back in EQ, and Elseworlds is back also! I decided to put Elseworlds on its own site this time, so that I may have more freedom with my style. For those of you who remember the old Elseworlds, some of the characters you know still remain, and I have added several new ones to replace extinct characters.

Also, I can no longer call it "EverCrest: Elseworlds", obviously, because I am no longer affiliated with EverCrest....so in a burst of creativity I renamed it "EverQuest: Elseworlds".

The old Elseworlds series can be found at the following address(click on the yellowish dates):

http://www.evercrest.com/archives/1999

And the new Elseworlds site address is as follows:

http://www.the-dudes.net/Elseworlds/Elseworlds.html

If you get a chance, go check it out.

-Ryoma
