Vox Slain by the Karana Council of Guilds
Shoji Tsunami [2/5/2001-10:20:32 AM]  Comments[16]

Last night, February 2nd, the combined forces of the Karana Council of Guilds under the leadership of Haemish MacClellan and the Sacred Band Alliance, invaded Permafrost Keep to slay the fair Lady Vox. There were a number of interesting things that took place that made this raid so unique.

- The last server split took most if not all of our talented raid leaders and strategists, so Haemish, a Barbarian Warrior, whom at the tender age of 39 Seasons, took up the mantle of leadership by organizing and leading this raid.

- The final raiding parties consisted of 8 groups ranging in level from 52 at the high end all the way to our lone bard at 37(who was critical in keeping the forward group alive long enough for the nukers to kill Vox). All in all, the average level was about 46.

- The servers went down halfway through the raid – but we regrouped and cleared the castle again for a second push and then launched our attack.

- We did this raid with only 2, yep count em, 2 !!!! clerics (a 51 Vicar and a 40 Cleric) and 1 Bard.

- The raiding parties consisted of players from a collection of over 15+ small to medium sized guilds that all belong to the Karana Council of Guilds.

Lady Vox was very generous on this raid and dropped the following (sadly no FA books for the Paladins):

(2) McVaxius’ Horns of War – One defaulted to Merithment 37 of Illuminatus, and the other was won by Edina Elvandar of Haven of Light

Crystalline Spear – Tanniss Riverwinds - 51 Champion of Forever Knights

Staff of Forbidden Rites – Drusila (DRU) of Kindred of the Moon

Warhammer of the Divine Grace – Azuriem Angelgrace – 40 Cleric and Leader of guild Illuminatus

Tobrin’s Mystic Eyepatch – Morgail D’unistar - 51 Wizard of Fell Omen

King room loot – Vox Bracers to Salani (47 Warrior) and Crystalline Sword to Cretar (51 Ranger). With all the talk of Magic and Cold resists – you can imagine how hot and heavy the rolling was for the Vox Bracer .

We also managed to retrieve 2 Prickly Pears for the Shadowknight Lifeleech spell quest won by Silver Daggers and Fell Omen to be passed down to their respective ranking Shadowknights AND, 2 Blocks of Permafrost – marking the beginning of the epic quests for 2 more of Karana’s Warriors.

The KCoG would like to publicly thank Jzonn and Halagar on Ayonae Ro, Steelheart of Avenging Crusaders, Solonel, and Jannelle Alcedonia of Ashborne, and Pippi Langstrump of Silver Daggers all of whom provided the new management team with assistance in planning, strategy, and execution of the raid. Your advice and guidance in advance of this raid was the lynchpin of our success.

The Karana Council of Guilds is a loose community of dozens of small to medium size guilds. The KCoG sponsors a variety of player driven events ranging from player run “Battle-Chess” type chess tournaments in Butcher Block, to low level Tournaments all the way on up to Planes and Dragon raids. For guilds that are not planes or dragon capable, the KCoG provides a fantastic opportunity for players of sufficient levels in those guilds to experience these large target encounters. For more information on the KCoG please visit us on the web at http://go.to/karanacouncil.

New Class Talk Discussions
Xanthus [2/6/2001-9:52:40 AM]  Comments[15]

Rogue Talk Dicussion

Hello again.

This will be the thread for discussion about the rogue class. The idea is to look at the things that need to be changed to make the class 'balanced'. With that as the primary goal, I am still interested in hearing about bugs and other such things. But, primarily, I want to talk about balance.

My take on the rogue is pretty similar to that of the warrior. They are pretty well balanced. Obviously with EverQuest as a primarily player vs. environment game, some of the things that thieves traditionally do are not really part of the package. That's why, in EverQuest, they are titled rogues.

Again, I have had little time (actually, no time at all) to do research on rogue concerns before starting this thread. So please be as detailed as you need to be to get your point across. But, at the same time, please remember that this thread will get long. So being concise and readable is important too.

I do want to report one thing that we have already discovered internally about rogues that is broken and will be fixed when we make these changes. Yes, it is a nerf, but not in the true sense of the word.

In the old days, NPCs had a limited size to their hate-list (basically the way that they track who they are angry at). In those days, evade would just drop you to the bottom of that list. Recently we increased the size of that list dramatically. This allows us to do a lot more with NPCs. But evade was never changed to reflect this, so it still drops you to the bottom of the hate-list. Essentially, this allows a rogue to do significant damage to an opponent, and then with the use of evade, become less 'hated' than someone that has done no damage to the NPC at all, but just has a somewhat negative faction with that NPC and is standing someplace on the other side of the zone. The rogue can then beat the NPC up with impunity. Basically, evade is far too useful for avoiding aggro from an NPC, and we're going to change it to be more in line with its original function.

I wanted to let you know that at the outset. Please don't make it the focus of this thread. It’s certainly something we should talk about. But there are certainly other things that are worth discussion as well.

So let's hear what you think.

Alan

So let's hear what you have to say.

Alan

Clerics Rest Assured
Xanthus [2/6/2001-2:54:22 PM]  Comments[9]

Another thread about clerics getting the shaft started on the the boards:

I'm really going to be busy today, so I'm sorry for only skimming this thread before responding.

Clerics will always, let me repeat, always be far superior healers than any other class. Keep a few things in mind here. Clerics get any shared healing spell many levels earlier than any other class. Clerics have a larger variety of healing spells to choose from, making them much more flexible in their healing powers. Clerics are also innately better healers than any other class. They will heal more hit points with the same spell than any other class (they have a built in bonus on all their healing).

I had the pleasure of talking to several clerics at the Faire. After talking with them in depth about this change, each of them was willing to admit that it seemed like a fair enough thing to do. Admittedly, they had their reservations, but they accepted the changes as probably a good thing for the game.

I wish some of you that disagree would have been able to come to the Faire and we could have talked about it in person.

But I am convinced that this is the right thing to do. And, from my skimming this post, I really see no arguments that validly suggest otherwise.

Alan

EverQuest Creates A Trail Of Cyberwindows
Giraffa [2/6/2001-10:30:46 PM]  Comments[51]

This article has been reposted from the Chicago Tribune:

By Joe Salkowski
Tribune Media Services
February 5, 2001

Cheated on? You're in good company. Left in the lurch for someone with more money? Hey, it happens.

Sure, it hurts being lied to, locked out or just flat dumped. But you can take comfort in knowing you're one of many who have been though the same thing.

But what if you lose your lover to a video game? That's what happened to Manda Erickson, a 24-year-old mother from Chippewa Falls, Wis. She says her fiance, the father of her 7-month-old daughter, has abandoned her for a Net-connected game called EverQuest. Is she exaggerating? You decide.

She says her fiance stays logged into the fantasy role-playing game for as many as 20 hours a day, leaving her and her daughter alone at the dinner table and everywhere else. He was in the delivery room for the girl's birth. But he brought along a laptop computer so he could help a less-experienced player kill something called "Dorn."

"My fiance and I have no relationship," Erickson says. "I speak, he grunts. I ask him to do something, I do it myself. I want to go back to work, but I do not trust him alone with our daughter, simply because when I am here she will be crying and he will not do anything about it."

She may feel alone, but only in real life. Online, sad to say, she has got plenty of company. There's Mel, a 31-year-old Seattle resident who's convinced her live-in boyfriend cares more about EverQuest than he does about her. And Dee, 20, who cries herself to sleep while her boyfriend spends his nights jacked into the game. And Gidel, who moved from Europe to the United States to spend time with someone she met playing the game herself.

She returned home after he refused to move their relationship offline. "Four days ago I asked him to choose between me and EQ," she wrote. "He has been on EQ ever since." They call themselves EverQuest widows. When they can find a few free minutes at the computer, they get together in online discussion groups to swap stories.

"My fiance has never had any other addictions," Erickson wrote. "Before EQ, he was hardly on the computer at all." The story is the same for others among the 250,000 people who pay $9.95 a month to play EverQuest, an online fantasy.

Players direct elves, gnomes and other characters through fantasy lives in a massive online world they share with tens of thousands of people at a time. Like most addictions, you can't win at EverQuest--you just buy in deeper and deeper.

There's no ultimate battle in which you kill something, trumpets play and you get on with your life. Rather, the goal is simply to build a good "life," gaining experience, buying equipment and keeping a step ahead of everyone else.

That takes time--and lots of it. Fans of such games praise their social aspect, and with good reason. They create a unique environment where people can meet like-minded souls and share experiences. But for someone who has friends and family, the fantasy world of EverQuest--"EverCrack," as some widows call it--can cause serious problems.

E-mail tribtech@tribune.com

Necromancer Talk
Georgia [2/7/2001-10:55:05 AM]  Comments[58]

Absor began his class balance discussion of the Necromancer class in this thread:

So let's talk about necromancers.

As with all the other threads, I want to talk about class balance here, not about bugs or epic weapons. You may certainly present bugs and concerns about the epic weapons, but they will not be the main concern of this thread.

Unlike the other threads, I have a fairly good list of ideas already garnered from several other posts to start us out. It is not complete, I'm sure. But it is certainly a good place to start our discussion.

Also, we are not looking to make necromancers any more effective at soloing than they already are. And, well, the necromancer is a pretty powerful class as they are now. The primary issues that I see revolve around the battle with the boss monsters. But I certainly want to hear from necromancers with concerns about all levels of the game. And, once again, I have to warn you that since we will be looking closely at this class we may very well feel that something needs to have its power reduced to achieve balance.

Anyhow, here's what I have already.

Ignite Bones: Despite the coolness factor, this spell can't really be used on the big raids because it suddenly pulls the enemy out of range, and by the time you relocate your range, one warrior could conceivably have done more damage than the spell is going to do. The best solution is to make the skeleton match the monster, if we can find some way to do that. Fixing this one spell might solve a lot of concerns. If we can’t size the skeleton, then we probably should remove it and find some other cool way to make sure that the spell 'sticks' to the target to prevent chain casting and to make it more than just another DD. Note: Any change to this spell should also be made to Chill Bones (55).

Lifetaps: The number one request is to allow Lifetaps to land on the big monsters. If we are telling necromancers that they have a valid place at the final encounter because they can give mana to other casters, then they will need a way to regain mana. Lich and Lifetap are the way they do that now. I am not convinced that this is something that we should change. But this is the single most asked for thing by the necromancer community. Perhaps, if made in conjunction with the fixing the bug in Lich, then perhaps this could be balanced.

DoTs and corpse poofing: This affects everyone. Hopefully this is now much improved, but I’m not taking it off the list until I’m convinced it has been fixed.

Mana transfer: Again, if their ‘place’ against the boss mob is to transfer mana, then perhaps we should allow them to do it at a better rate. A 2:1 transfer rate might be enough. I'm not at all certain that this should be done in conjunction with any change to Lifetaps in those encounters, though, as a change in one makes the other far more effective and might just be too much of a change. But this could certainly be considered if we fix the bug with the Lich spells.

Infusion: Make infusion targetable. The necro might still see it as a 'worthless spell', but if another caster wants to sacrifice their experience for 1000 mana, they might actually cast this spell. Otherwise, those Essence Emerald’s will only be used for raising the dead.

Defoliation: There seem to be plenty of plants that this just doesn't work on. I fully understand how we need to limit the use of any spell that is overly efficient against any one monster type. But it might be worthwhile to evaluate the things that are not considered plants that maybe should be. I've asked the players for a list that they'd like reviewed, but I've received nothing yet.

Track Corpse: Broken spell. Is it going to stay that way?

Epic Weapon: The look and effect are not well loved in the community.

Lich Series: This series of spells is broken. It is more powerful than it should be. It takes the necromancer's hit points and turns them into mana. It was calculated at a certain ratio to be 'in balance'. Unfortunately, these spells were adversely affected by the reduction of damage to spells used by players on players. This change also reduced the hit points taken from the necromancer by Lich spells, so that the ratio of mana gained for hit points lost is nearly 1 for 1 (instead of about 3 for 2). This is a bug that we have chosen not to change in the past because it would be a 'nerf'. I think in conjunction with a serious look at the entire class and the balance of all the classes, we should also look at this series and decide if we should put it right. If it remains as it is, the necromancer is going to be limited in the improvements it can gain, as the class is already using a powerful and broken spell series. Note: Series includes: Arch Lich (60), Demi Lich (60), Lich (49), Call of Bones (34), Allure of Death (20).

Alan
