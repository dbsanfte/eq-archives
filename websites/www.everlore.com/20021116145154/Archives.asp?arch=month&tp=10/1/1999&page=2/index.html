Take note of this GM run quest that occured a few nights ago on the Fennin Ro Server.

On october 11, a GM quest was run on Fenin Ro. The Daughter of Hate, Lanys appeared to retrieve a part of her armour given to her by her father Innorukk. This piece was stolen by an undead ??crimson'dal?? named tovin who lurked in Najena. Lanys' bodyguard chose 4 dark elves, Costak, Serrin, Obliterator, and Dharmon to accompany her as a group, although many more joined as the group moved towards Najena.

Halfway there, the Grudge Bearers, a dwarven guild, decided to attack the Daughter of hate and her bodyguard, and nearly 20 of their finest warriors were slain by the 2 tier'dal! After that disturbance, we proceeded to najena's catacombs, where Tovin attacked while protected by 2 level 40 demons, Strife and Agony. Tovin was invincible until his guards were destroyed, and many died while attacking him, including Serrin and I.

Finally, the demons and Tovin were destroyed, the armour was retrieved, and what was left of the group returned to neriak. Serrin recieved the bodyguard's Blackened Iron Bastard Sword as a reward, and we went down to the blind fish to get drunk and celebrate! Dark elves, be on the lookout for Lanys' return, there are other pieces of her armour to be found, and who better than you to help her.

[10/13/1999-3:35:45 PM]  Comments[0]

Results from the Freeport Assualt that took place last Monday.

Monday October 11, Frostito gathered a band of humans and dark races together to strike at the Freeport guards. Said Frostito, 'Bunch of humies was attackin our guards, we figures time for revenge!'

The assault began at South Gate, more or less on time, with at least one dark guild, the ShadowStriders, on the way. The assaulting forces were forced to zone frequently, but continued to return and as more guilds arrived to view or participate the numbers of guards in the area also increased. A GM also made an appearance to assist the attackers. By the end of the evening (2 days EQ time) several guard's lives were taken, as well as several attackers. The Wind Riders Guild confronted the ShadowStriders, and peace was brought about between the two Guilds.

Sisterhood Guild

The Tunare division of the Sisterhood met in Kithicor for induction of new Ladies and awards for generosity, plus a new lantern award for guiding newbies. After posing for group pictures, the Ladies split up to visit Lake Rathe and Everfrost, where over the weekend almost 10 new Sisters from Karanas to Freeport to Hallas were inducted.

The Sisterhood Guild on Tunare server meets each Saturday.

EQ Mentor Program
Crysta [10/15/1999-1:08:15 PM]  Comments[0]

Crysta sent us the following:

Hello,

I am officially announcing a new project, called the EQ Mentor Program. I have copied and pasted the information below from the website. The website is located at http://www.arina.net/eqmentors. People should email crysta@arina.net if they are interested.

Thanks!

The EQ Mentor Program is here to provide answers and help.

The Mentor Program will consist of experienced players who are willing to volunteer their time to help the inexperienced community. Holding "answer sessions" in newbie areas, participating as a Mentor for an assigned newbie, providing your services free, are some of the duties of EQ Mentors.

Don't guides hold answer circles? Guides in the game can hold answer circles, but some guides lack the play knowledge or just can't answer questions like "What's the best shield for a paladin at level 12?". When was the last time you actually stumbled upon a guide holding an answer circle? They are busy with petitions dealing with everything from corpse recoveries to "Do I lose exp in a duel?".

If you are an experienced player on the Povar server and wish to be an EQ Mentor, please contact me at crysta@arina.net.

The Duties of a Mentor:

  • Must dedicate at least 4 hours of playing time per week to the Program (this can be done by spending time with your assigned newbie, or by holding answer/helping sessions).
  • Has to hold at least 1 answer/helping session in a newbie area per week.
  • Mentors are assigned newbies of the same class (or class you are experienced in) so you can provide helpful advice on everything from equipment to skill building. NOTE: This does NOT mean you are twinking this newbie or have to give him/her any items or money. It is your choice to spend time with him/her in person, provide advice in tells, etc. However you want to help him/her is fine.
  • You must log the time you spend mentoring. The more time you mentor, the more praise you get ;)
  • An optional thing is to submit a summary of what you did at the end of each week. Include suggestions, things people asked/requested, etc. Reports can be submitted to crysta@arina.net and will be posted on the Reports page once that goes up.

A Few Goals:

What I am hoping for if the Mentor Program turns out how I intend, we will be working closely with the Guide Program; taking the questions players ask in petitions that any experienced player could answer and answering them.

Instead of a guide telling a player "I'm sorry, I can't help you." because a player died in an area where he cannot retrieve his corpse himself, the guide could respond with "Because you have not died to a bug or technical error, I cannot help you. However, if you contact someone from the Mentor Program, they may be able to assist you."

I also wish for the Mentor Program to expand past Povar and branch onto the other servers. If you are not on Povar and wish to manage the Mentor Program branch on your server, please contact me.

Other Things:

As the program expands, I will be adding much more onto this website. I hope to get a form up (possibly today) that will allow you to sign up for the program -to become a mentor- through this website. I will put up an FAQ about the program because I know there are a lot of questions people will ask (like "The newbies that you assign to people, where are you getting them from?" etc.) and they are questions that get repeated constantly.

I feel this program is going to give the bored higher-level people something to do. I know as a level 46 cleric that I'm in no rush to 50 because I see how bored a lot of people are. I, personally, find it fun to sit in greater faydark for a few hours and buff newbies, heal, bind -- most of all ANSWERING the 100 tells I get asking very simple questions.
