Clarification on Name Change List
Athelyn [7/16/2002-12:23:42 AM]  Comments[2]

Here's the latest from the Developer’s Corner:
I thought I'd just add my voice to the others expressing their concern about the recent revealation that the character name changes will not be available to the rest of us. I worry that some of the nasty little people that I've met online will be using this feature to their advantage, sometimes to the extreme disadvantage of the rest of us.

Sure, I'm positive that some name changes will be for the better, some people just have bad names that they are better off without, or perhaps their characters have outgrown the frivolous names they put in when they were first starting the game.

It's always seemed that your policy was players had to live with the reputations they made in game. Doesn't this seem to create an easy way out of them?

Tsumite
Monk of the Void

There was a bit of miscommunication recently about this. We do still plan to put up the name change list in the near future. It’s certainly taking longer to implement than we’d hoped, and for that I apologize.

The integrity of the game is always one of our premiere concerns, and the list of names changed by this service is part of that.

Again, I’m sorry if we caused a bit of a stir. And thanks for keeping us on our toes.

Alan

Update on Caster Balance
Billy Bacus [7/16/2002-11:40:41 AM]  Comments[2]

This was found over at The Druids Grove,
Update on Balance

I wanted to make a brief comment about caster balance. Unfortunately for those that are anxious to see the changes completed, we’re going to insist that this be a slow process. Introducing a lot of balance changes at one time isn’t a good idea. We want to keep the number of changes at one time small so that we can judge their effect without too much overlap.

So while everyone, including us, wants to see the “end” of this caster balancing thing, we don’t want to rush to get there without careful evaluation of each change. Please be patient with the process.

We certainly haven’t forgotten to look at the priest classes. With the next patch to Test we will be removing the 10% penalty to healing from non-clerics. Clerics will still get the 5% bonus at all levels, and will still get their heal spells many levels earlier that the other priests.

We want to see how this plays out on Test, obviously, before we consider moving it Live, and before we consider other changes to the priest classes. This is the Test server, so you should neither expect the change to go Live, nor assume that it won’t. Right now it’s there to be tested, and whether it does or does not go Live will be dependent on what we discover on Test.

We are also looking at some changes to help to make clerics be a little more well-rounded, along with addressing some concerns put forth by druids and shamans. Those ideas are still in the development stage, and we’ll talk more about them when we get done looking at the healing changes.

Thanks for your patience with us on this.

Alan

Everquest Beyond the PC and Beyond the MMORPG?
Billy Bacus [7/18/2002-11:48:05 AM]  Comments[5]

More exciting news from EQ Live:
EQ Announces Development Deals for New Platforms

On July 18th, Sony Online announced plans to develop EverQuest-based products for the PC and console platforms, and as a role-playing game for the Playstation 2 computer entertainment system.

Snowblind Studios and Rapid Eye Entertainment are the two developers involved with these projects, which will be marketed under the SOE label.

Rapid Eye Entertainment, founded by Mark Caldwell in 2001, is best known for their highly successful Might and Magic series of role-playing games. Snowblind Studios is best known for the development of the hit PS2 role-playing game, Baldur's Gate: Dark Alliance.

"We've made it a point to work closely with leading developers to create the best possible gaming experience," said Russell Shanks, Senior Vice President and Chief Technology Officer, Sony Online Entertainment.
This is news indeed! Rapid Eye Studios may be visited here and Snowblind Studios here.
