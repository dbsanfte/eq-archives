Necros aren't very valuable in groups unless you're in undead infested dungeons like befallen, unrest, or lower guk. In these areas, necros are just as valuable as clerics, if not more so since they can charm undead (which clerics can't).

Most of the time a necro will spend soloing or partnering. The reason groups don't want them (except at 24 - 28 when they get shock of poison ) is that their damage is dealt with DoT's, which just take too long to kill the mob.

Since a necro solos often, this creates a problem in plane runs and dragon runs. If your necro is your primary/only character, you'll have to learn how to group as a caster before you'll be accepted as one (ie no chain nuking, knowing when to cast what, etc.) However, I've brought a wizard and warrior both past 20, so I knew how to group with a pure caster.

If you want to be a very group oriented class that is also very fun, be an enchanter. They suck at lower levels, and you'll have trouble getting in a group until level 12 to 16. However, past level 20, any group worth its while will have an enchanter. They deal relatively little damage, but they can easily turn a bad pull into a cake walk (ie "oops I pulled all 5 specs" enchanter says "np I'll charm one and mez the rest"). Just one side note: a level 50 enchanter is the only class, besides possibly a necro, that can solo spectre island in oasis.

Hi there fellow Children of Evil

I have been a necro for over a year now - and level 50 for way too long (not saying to show off - but to show I have played through "nerfs")- and in that time I can tell you that Necros are amazing in groups.

A solo necro is fine. You can read while you solo - get exp etc... but a solo necro is VERY different to a group necro. The spells used, when to cast, and how to interact with others in a dynamic situation are very different. Yes, you can solo to 50 - but do you really want to?

Group necros that are GOOD group necros were once very rare - but becoming more common as more and more of the solo'ers hit 50 - go to the planes, dragons etc - and find that they have to relearn their class to some extent. I pretty much grouped my way to 50 - I played for the social aspect rather than just exp camping - and I did have to adjust my play style almost constantly depending on level, the group content, and the baddie... but I do think that such experience made me a better group player for that tough times that you can meet in the planes.

Luaxana has a REALLY good point! Say, I am in the following group killing non-dead critters - Warrior, Chanter, Ranger, Cleric, Bard and Necro (me). My pet double hits for 55's (so on average around 80+ dmg a tic), in comes Asy (500dmg and debuff - making it easier to be hit by melees and pet), I cast BoD (800dmg - hp to me), I pact Warrior and Ranger (saves cleric mana), and I cast IBlood (1.2k dmg). This ALL adds up. A necro in a group - at any level - cuts down the time of a fight dramitically. At higher levels they can play cleric (I have kept 4 tanks alive with pacting - oh wait that was pre-nerf I think I know I can do three now on sea fury clops)

What I am trying to say is that, in a group, a necro is even more powerful than alone. (Two necros have saved a group of 16 people from an 8+ plane train death - by pets, BOD, Lich and Covetous Mana.)

Sure - there have been some changes to the necro classes - some great - some not so great - but all of them make the class MUCH more interesting to play. I for one, do not want to play a dull super hero .
