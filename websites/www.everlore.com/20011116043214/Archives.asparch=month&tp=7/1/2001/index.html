Kithicor Revisited- Grand Battle on Sullon Zek
Scavenger382 [7/2/2001-12:09:12 AM]  Comments[14]

For those of us that never saw Bloody Kithicor, last night came very close. All was normal in Misty Thicket under the moons of Sullon Zek. The halflings and other neutral persuasions were gaining seasons with ease. Word filtered in that the Evils had seized Kithicor Forest. They were camping each end of the woods, ambushing and slaughtering any that ventured further. It was soon pointed out that while the Neutral factions owned much of Antonica, their power rested on controlling Highpass Hold. But nothing could be done.

Maddo, a young and ambitious halfling, threw up the shout that perhaps action should be taken. Charien, a Dark Elf wizard of Solusek Ro barely into her sixth season, took up the cry as well. A few trickled forward for action. Scouts indicated that an ambush lay at each zone of about five Evils, consisting entirely of other Dark Elves, Trolls, Ogres, and even feared Iksar. Charien took over the cries for aid, and slowely an army massed.

Halflings about 7 in number jumped from Rivervale under the lead of Maddo. The darkies were surprised, but not for long. The battle began to swing badly for the neutrals when a second band of Neutrals stormed from the town. The Evils were butchered and sent back to the Western Commonlands.

Charien sent criers into the town and thicket for more able bodies, and proceeded towards Western Commonlands. She traveled in the dead of night when Kithicor was at its worst, and arrived at the Commons in time to confirm what other scouts Maddo had sent were claiming: a large force of Evils were massing. Charien called for the forces to retreat into Rivervale and group.

The army had grown. Halflings the most numerous, with Cherian being one of three dark elves, and a handful of humans and elves. The Criers heralded a coming conflict of immense proportions.

Charien appeared to be trying to coordinate a flanking maneuver, but time had run out. Daylight fell on Kithicor Forest, and with it came the Darks.

Scouts shouted of the attack, and Charien ordered the attack to commence. Group leaders took up the cry, and the force exploded from Rivervale. The Darks placed a valiant struggle, but it was their blood that spilled on the steps of Rivervale. Shouts in halfling and darkspeach permeated the air, along with the dying screams of both neutral and evil. Finally, the Darks were driven once again back to the Western Commonlands border.

"Forward! We press on now!" Cried Charien after the "Newts" had regrouped. Every soldier sprinted for the Commons, dropping several Evils along the way. They reached the line to find it lightly guarded, and drove the Darks completely out of Kithicor. The first objective had been achieved. Dark Elves finally realized Charien was in the midst of the attack, and called for her blood. "Forward! We cannot stay in the womb of Misty Thicket forever!" Thus came the third charge. It would be the farthest the army went. Stiff opposition lay on the other end of the zone. The entire workings of the Commons had been uprooted and thrown into the fray. Area of Affect spells slammed into the charging forces. Many neutrals lost there lives, but many Evils did as well.

The Neutrals were forced back into Kith. Their zeal remained, but their strength waned. Mounting casualties took their toll, and the Dark's superior numbers finally prevailed. The Neutrals were driven back to the Rivervale line, with Charien finally calling for a retreat.

The battle for Kithicor had ended. At best a stalemate, at worst Status Quo Ante Bellum, more than land had been won. The Neutrals had proven that they were not mobs to be camped. Macen, the head of the Evil resistance, negotiated casualty management with Charien, and the Evils admitted to being swamped in the beginning. If not a victory for Charien, if not a loss for the Evils, the Battle for Kithicor promised one thing: the war for dominance was not yet over.

OOC: Wow this was fun. I didn't know something like this was possible short of a GM event, but this was purely made by players. A GM even came in and watched! He applauded the Newts for a great battle just before the Darkies attacked again. A great many things are happening on Sullon Zek, and I hope this lets people realize what a little imagination and demagoguery can do.

Across the ages, the elven race has endured innumerable setbacks from all fronts.

* The first elven king and queen used by Innoruuk as the templates to build the Teir'dal.
* The assault by the giant races of Rallos Zek when the majority of Tunaria fell under their shadow.
* The ruination of the Elddar Forest by the forces of Solusek Ro.
* The flight to Faydwer.

Today, on Vallon Zek, even the elven cities of Felwithe and Kelethin are overrun by non elven races and the fabric of elven culture appears stretched to the limit.

Yet our elder race has survived and managed to prosper often enough. Today, we are positioned to strike back at the minions of darkness in their most powerful realms, the outer planes.

All elves regardless of your guilds and crossteaming past are welcome to unite for this demonstration of our strength. Living legends and names you have only heard whispered in folklore from noteworthy guilds are even now, making their way to attend.

Tunare has called us, her most prized creations, to unite and send a message to her immortal rivals. If you hear the call of your people and are of elven blood, then you are welcome to join this endeavor.

May Tunare's enemies feel the steel, song and magic of her elves on this day.

Fennin Ro Wizard Rune Faire -TODAY @ 3PM PST
Xanthus [7/2/2001-11:59:56 AM]  Comments[1]

Received from Dynamite Shortfuze

Hail my fellow wizards of Fennin Ro!!

As you know, in past months, I have hosted a rune trade faire for wizards, that we may help each other further our research and development of spells. Last month I was not able to host the faire on account of my writing my final exams, but this month the faire is on!

As in past months, the idea is simple: wizards of all levels meet together and bring their runes. If you need a rune, ask for it, and if anyone has it, they will give it to you. Conversely, if you have a rune someone else is in need of, that you yourself do not need, then it is expected that you shall give it to them freely. This has been a great success in past months, and I'm looking forward to this month being even better!

I have had requests to postpone the faire past this weekend as I am told a number of guilds are having events that would coincide with the faire were it held this weekend (I understand one guild is even having an all-weekend scavenger hunt!), and even though that would mean putting the faire past the end of the month, I see no reason why not to postpone it. As such, the faire will be held at the following place, date, and time:

Where: Toxxullia Wizard Spires
When: Monday July 2nd
Starting Time: 3PM PST (6PM EST)
Ending Time: When all is said and done, and we have finished having fun!

As always, I ask that if you ply a trade in which others may partake of your wares and enjoy them, please feel free to bring some of your fare with you! I myself brew, and always ensure I bring enough of my wares to keep everyone happy. If you would care to bring some of your own stock, or if you bake and would like to share your wares, then by all means, bring them with you! These are always welcome!

While this is a faire geared primarily at wizards, I invite everyone to attend. It's good fun and you can get to know your community better. Since I started hosting this faire, I've gotten to know my fellow wizards much better, and am constantly conversing with them... I like to think that this has helped me gain many friends, and will help to gain many more. For this reason alone, even if you aren't a wizard, or if you're a wizard that cannot yet research, or has no more use of research, you should attend anyway! Especially if you are beyond the point that research can advance you, as you can help your fellow wizards who are not quite as advanced as yourself research their spells. Higher level wizards have always been at hand in the past to help younger wizards research their spells, and there is no reason for this to change now.

If you have any questions, suggestions, or comments, by all means, either send me a tell in-game, or contact me at fenninrunes@hotmail.com, and I will get back to you as soon as possible.

I am using the faire this month as a guage of sorts. I intend to use its success in order to determine how popular the faire really is, and whether it should continue. So the more people that attend, the more likely it is that you'll see another faire! I hope one day to limit the faire not only to wizards, but to all casters to come together and share their research, help each other, and get to know each other in the community.

I look forward to seeing you there this Monday, my friends, and I hope this will be the greatest faire yet!!

Dynamite Shortfuze
Fennin Ro

The Adventures of Stacks....Gnome Mage
Stacks [7/3/2001-10:35:36 AM]  Comments[5]

Stacks log Sunday July 1st:

I had finally wondered away from my home in Aka anon, barely making it past a war between the nuetrals and goodies in Butcherblock. I ran as fast as I could to the docks and seeing that the entrace was blocked by two extremely intimidating dwarves, I ran to the water and swam around. Quietly waitin at the docks I met 2 refugees from the war. We three agreed that fighting would be no solution seein as we were ll bound in areas we didnt wish to go back to. As the boat arrived I thought to myself about my decision to worship the Plaguebringer himself. Was it a wise choice? I wasnt sure at the time. Finally After a few days or riding the boat to the human filled town of freeport, I made my way to the mage guild and with the help of a human enchantress, purchased some supplies. From there I walked to a nearby area to sit and look at my maps to see where I could test my new spells at. At that time a human shadowknight appeared. He introduced himself as Anharat, Knight of Innoruk. We sat and talked about a war being fought in kithicor and how the dark armies needed more to join to fight a plague of halflings that were trying to infest their way into an area known as the West Commons. After a few hours of chat, (and Alcohol,) we decided to travel to WC together.

After a day of travel we finally came to the West Common area. Smoke filled the air as fires blazed the land from the magic used to scorch its target. Seeing a dark elf in need of assistance, Anharat and I ran to his aid chasing off his enemy. After a few minutes we learned the Dark Elf was a wizard named Velael. He was recruiting for the war and was accosted by the halfling on his journey. I volunteered my services but Anharat was wounded badly. After finding an inn nearby and leaving Anharat behind, Velael and I moved towards Kithicor. As we approached the undead forest, an army of dark elves and ogres could be seen camped by its entrance. Looking around I had realized I was the only gnome in this army. Conjuring a pet, I smiled knowing it would be my only true companion. I looked up at Velael and we moved to findd one of the leaders of the army. Joining up with them we began to cover ground and move into Kithicor to search for the miniture demons of nuetrality.

As the daylight hit the trees of Kithicor, I felt the strange presence of one nearby, Velael somehow was out or earshot and I felt a slight chill. Making a silent prayer to my lord, I suddenly felt a pain as a dagger pierced my side. I screamed in agony as my pet gave chase to the attacker. Hearing my pet casting and the attacker's voice cursing himself for being caught by the pet of a mage I stood, my hand covering the bloodstain on my shirt. I moved to my pet's side, anger filled my eyes as I muttered the words to my damage spell. As I finished the words my pet threw one last punch and I almost mischieviuosly grinned as my spell's victim laid motionless on the ground. Soon asfter I was attacked by 3 more of the halfling's..these three were druids, thus I had realized from the spells they cast upon me. My pet finally was able to move me back however to a safe haven to where I laid unconscious for hours. Upon awakening, Velael was standing over me chuckling at me for my own misfortune. "You werent made to take such punishment my little short ally" he jokingly remarked. I smiled and stood, slowly realizing that the pain was not yet gone. after I was healed by the dark army cleric we went back to the kith forest. The dark armies were preparing to have one last charge. The halflings had held the forest for some time and we needed to get in. Finally dozens of dark elves, iksars, and ogres ran into Kith. I followed behind and as I entered begant to fire every damage spell I could, hitting halflings all around. If felt like days as the fighting ensued, bodies of thy allies and enemies filled the forest as finally all became silent. Tired from fighting, and casting I looked up at a smiling Velael. We had won the forest back. The halflings retreated and we began to cheer. I smiled as I realized Bertoxx had been with me. For he gave me the power to eliminate his enemies and I gratefully took it. Although, I thought to myself we had won the day, we had been given a fight like no other. I was glad to stand there with my evil brethren.

Smiling to myself, I waved goodbye to the army of evil, and conjured a new pet. I chuckled as it said, " Following Master". "Let us be off," I said. And off we went, to find a new adventure.

Vazaelle - Iksar Madman in Lake of Ill Omen
Ranajul Mindthief [7/3/2001-9:28:42 PM]  Comments[0]

Today in the Lake of Ill Omen an two Iksar monks, who had apperently gone mad, began attacking and killing various people. The first "Iksar Madman" was seen on the shore of the lake, in front of the Sarnak fort. He was drooling over those who came near him, attacking some, but not pursuing far. He would feign death often, and sometimes would not even rise when attacked. A small group of Iksar began questioning him to see if had anything to say, but the mad Iksar merely wandered and drooled.

A group consisting of the Iksar and several other interested adventurers gathered to see the disturbance, and he began wandering towards the fort, attacking a small group of people right outside. He seemed deadly to the people watching, who were mostly in their twenty to thirtieth season. After killing one, the onlookers decided to make an attempt at putting the Iksar out of it's misery, considering the only words he said were "Jibba Jabba" and shouting, "My father was an Aviak!!" Although they reduced his health slightly, the Iksar did seem to be too interested in retaliation. Instead, he continued to run in and around the fort, eventually stoppingat the shore. One powerful Iksar necromancer named Likwit, who was beyond his 50th season, reported that his spells did no damage to the Iksar. Soon others also discovered that they could not attack him, however then the shout was heard that a second insane Iksar was sighted at the mill.

After running to the mill, I found the odd monk yelling for help. I and several others after asked him how we could help. "Let's play a game," he said, "It's really easy."

"What game?" I asked.

"ME VS ALL of you," he said, addressing the 20-30 people that had gathered around,"Winner gets pie." The Iksar proceeded to attack me with all his might. After I awoke and recovered what I had lost, I found the others still attacking the Iksar. I followed them, and they took a wrong turn around the mill, leaving me and a charmed Sabertooth Tigress to taake the Iksar on. He quickly killed the lioness, and once again attacked me until I lost conciousness. As I awoke for the second time, I found the Iksar and his adversaries still locked in battle. Soon it was over, and the Iksar succumbed to the crowd.

Although many mourned the death of their confused brother, I and many others knew that the Iksar was a danger that had to be stopped. After a somewhat confused period (ooc see screenshot to know what I'm talking about) it was shouted that the Iksar had a jagged band, a silver ruby veil, and a wedding ring (exact type unclear). These items were distributed to several of those present by Israi, who appeared to have looted him. After all the items were handed out, those who had helped kill the Iksar dispersed, some regretting the sad deed that they had done, some joyous at the end of another threat in the Lake of Ill Omen.

OOC - This was my first GM event, which I assume it was. I suppose the Iksar might not have been a GM but his actions (shouting odd things, not responding to aggro as most mobs do) make it quite obvious. I had fun although I could do little to help win the Iksar's game, and I believe that many others that saw the event would agree.
