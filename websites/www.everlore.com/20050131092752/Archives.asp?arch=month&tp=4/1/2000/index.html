Brad McQuaid on the new Guidebook
Catalyst [4/1/2000-11:17:51 AM]  Comments[3]

Abashi, are you involved with this "official strategy guide" for Kunark?

Obviously buying the original guide was a mistake, just wondering if buying this one will be as well. Any information you have regarding this guide would be greatly appreciated...

New Guidebook for Kunark Link

We are involved with the strategy guide for Kunark, yes. Definitely moreso than with the original guide.

That said, we don't have total control. We are the developer, not the publisher (Sony), nor the company in charge of writing/creating the guide.

There is also another problem with strategy guides (and even manuals) when it comes to MMORPGs: these games change. The manual/strategy guide can't.

My view of strategy guides for games like these would be that they really be STRATEGY guides, that they are full of valuable information that is more timeless. For example, grouping tactics and such.

But let me tell you, the strategy guide makers want to put in list after list of spells, items, skills, and other stats. Which is all find and dandy, I guess, for single player games.

But it makes for problems in games that change and evolve. I'm also against 'spoilers' in general, but that's another topic.

Another thread mentioned that the original guide said Humans have an experience/leveling bonus or some such. The reality is that they are one of the easier races to level in that some other races have experience penalties.

But they didn't word it that way, for whatever reason (miscommunication? who knows).

Anyway, to make a long story short, we'll do our best to work with the outside companies that write manuals and strategy guides, and we seem to be having more influence in the Kunark guide than in the original. That said, things are going to change, miscommuncation will probably occur again (let's just hope to a lesser degree), and things will be wrong.

*shrug*

-Brad

Verant's fear of exploitation is ruining the realism, and role playing...

When will they draw the line? I will cover one topic that effects me, but feel free to add your own experiances, for im sure there are many similar to this.

I am a ranger. Master of the Bow. Sounds enticing right? From the earliest of its exsistance, the bow was a great weapon. why? well because you could kill (or do damage) from a distance.

Sometimes we have to put some limitations on realism for the sake of gameplay. This takes many forms, some of which are considered both good and bad. As an example, it's "unrealistic" for someone to heal from near-dead to full simply by sitting on your can for 20 minutes. Even in game-time, that only accounts for about 8 hours. Embracing realism in this case would lead to tedium in the game.

The same philosophy is true for things such as archery. Though realistically, a person with a really good bow could kill something in but a few shots, from a gameplay standpoint that would be too powerful. Though tedium isn't an issue with this, the sanctity and meaning of gameplay (e.g. challenge) is at stake.

-Gordon

EQ is awesome, but something better will come along, if only because of inevitable technical advances. Where will Verant be then?

We'll be releasing a new game :) We are fully committed to remaining on top of the newest technology available.

Too many companies have this "We're successful right now, so we don't have to improve" attitude. But while you are top dog now, your apathy is going to hurt you in the future.

We're not among those companies that believe that. But we do need to recognize that there is a limit to which current products can be improved without encountering major problems. When that limit is reached, it only makes sense to start a brand new project that takes advantage of all of the technology that will be available at its release.

-Gordon
