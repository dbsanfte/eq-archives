Povar Newbie Givaway
Garst [8/1/1999-4:04:48 PM]  Comments[0]

Thousands of plats in items and cash were given away, along with huge amounts of lag. At one specific point in time, there were about 150 people in South Qeynos, and lines extended almost into the next zone as it wavered around infront of South Qeynos gate. Games, races, and riddles were held. People of levels 1 - 39, all races and classes were there, but the event was targeted towards levels 4-12. Garst, Druid of Povar

 

Editorial: Did Verant play the TZ Fiasco
Nastrado [8/1/1999-5:33:13 PM]  Comments[0]

First Let me start off by saying I love everquest it is one of the best games I have ever played. There were many talented people who where brought together to create this game and it is appriciated. Since Tallon Zek was first announced there has almost been a mass cult following surrounding it. There are more Web Sites and Guilds for it than any other server brought up to date. I find it very hard to believe that Verant had no clue that the server was going to be overloaded. They even took a poll about it. The message from Mr. Smedley was that the poll was only taken to see if people would play. For him to say that this poll was not to see how many people would play but, to see if people would play at all and that they did not count how many players said yes, they would be playing is a insult on my intelligence and everyone else who was there. There is no way you can tell me that the Tallon Zek poll did not give them a idea of how many people were going to play. So once the server was shut down and Mr. Smedley came on to proclaim that "they had no idea it was going to be this popular" some very good solutions were brought up such as a player cap, no cap and let it run and what i believe to be the best solution at the time covert the test server over to a real server and make it the new Race War Server. Mr. Smedley said no to the first two but paused for the test server idea. then he came back and said it was to valuable to them to convert it. Even though they planned on getting a new server within a week they could not live without the test server that seems strange since only 150 people play on the test server and over 5000+ were angry at Tallon Zek being shut down. While there were people cussing and swearing at Mr. Smedley in the main general chat room and spamming like i have never seen before and Mr. Smedley ignored them and contuinued on. Then i made a stament that went something like this. ME: Verant how stupid do you think we are there is no way that you could not know the server was going to be this busy once you have proven that the customer does not come first only that money is king around here I got a immediate private message from Mr. Smedly i dont know it word for word but this is VERY close to what was said to mw SMED: Listen dude i dont want to hear any more crap like that from you. Out of all the people there using every vulgar word in the book at him he decided to choose me to vent on. He immediatley shut me up so i could not talk again. after he left i told everyone and no one else claimed to have recieved a message from him which i found to be very strange because it seemed to me that he was making a threat to ban me for my statement. Now think of this. Verant has been hyping Tallon Zek for weeks. Ask your self this how many of you were ready to not re-subscribe to everquest and go to one of the many new online RPG's that are in beta then Tallon Zek was announced. How many of you have re-subscribed in the last few weeks waiting for it. Its the end of the month now and many players free month of EQ has expired. What better way to keep people paying who otherwise would not than by luring them in with a server that you know will have well over 5,000 people who want to play. Then give them a teaser one day knowing full well the server could not handle it but knowing that even more people will get involved with it. Then drag them on for another week or so and yes you may get a few people who quit but you are going to get even more people who dont even play EQ but hear about that the server that is so popular they could not keep it up. In closing i want to say that yes i heard the were some rude gm's in the chat rooms but over all most of the Gm's handled themselves in a very respectful manner i was not glad to see them take such a bashing as they did but i have to also say that alot of people needed to vent after that fiasco. Players need to remember that the normal GM's have nothing to do with what Verant does, heck they dont even get paid! I just find it very very suspicious that every thing went they way it did friday the Verant staff are not stupid they had to see the writting on the wall 90% of the people who planned on playing knew that one server would not be enough why didn't vernat. Well who know what will happen from here I am sure that once my little rant get posted somewhere and a certain someone hears about it that i may not be welcome on the EQ servers anymore but, I had to speak my mind and there are others that i have disscused this with and they agree with me. Let me also say that i have no true facts to back this up that it could just be a big coincendence but i really dont believe that. Nastrado a.k.a David Sovesky mozart@flinthills.com

Step into the shadows...Tzek shortie guild
Fizzleheart [8/2/1999-10:55:37 AM]  Comments[0]

"Come, step into the shadows where at last you will be safe." You enter a set of tunnels that after walking only the shortest of distances, you realize may be more complex than you will ever be able to fully grasp. You are led quite some distance, past rooms that all appear to have some importance, though what many of them are used for you cannot tell. At last you are led into a cozy chamber, furnished with the finest makings of the dwarves and gnomes. There is a table in this room, set upon it fresh fuit, breads, and cheese, the likes of which can only be of halfling gourmet. A gnome, his age somewhat a mystery as his eyes still retain a great youth that his grey hair and wrinkled face betray, motions for you to sit. "Please, eat if you are hungry, you are welcome to what the Fellowship of the Mouse has to offer." The gnome sits across from you. "My name is Fizzle, though I am sometimes known as Fizzleheart....the um, tale behind that is a long one, I do not have time for it now I must apologize. You are most fortunate that we were present in the forest, the dark one was almost upon you," Fizzle smiles. "Welcome to the Fellowship of the Mouse, I trust you will be staying with us for some time now, it is dangerous and they have likely not given up search. You are, of course, safe under our protection." Fizzle regards you for a moment, and see's that you are confused, maybe even uncomfortable. Pulling an old pipe from his robes, and quite literaly from his robes as there was no pocket from where he produced it, he begins to puff upon it. It is, amazingly, already lit. "Once, long ago, the Band of the Mouse was concieved long ago of rogues, bandits, and social misfits who could not agree with the rules of society. The history of this order is indeed, complex. I have studied long and hard, but most of the records are lost within a library deep beneath Freeport, beyond our reach. Therefore I will forgo the history lesson until I have learned more of it myself. "When I joined the Fellowship it was for my own safety. There are those who have sought my knowledge and, indeed, my head! I was barely lucky enough to evade thier grasp this last attempt, had not the Fellowship been all seeing and saved me from my death. Back then, they were a band of, shall I say, shady individuals. They thrived off the misfortune of others, making a living off of smuggling, extorsion, paid hits, and out right thievery. "But they have with them now a leader who goes by the name of Rizzen. He has seen the potential of the folk who have banded togethor under the name of Mouse. And under his leadership, the numbers have grown. He has taken the practices of these people and the practices that once provided a meager existance, and made something much greater. Indeed, almost like a messiah, he appeared in time for the coming war, and under his leadership, the Fellowship of the Mouse has turned from thier holes and tunnels and focused upon something much greater...." Fizzle appears frustrated at this point, and surprisingly turns red in the face. Coughing horribly he exhales from his pipe, and barely manages a gasp as suddenly the object bursts into flame. "Egads!" Fizzle leaps to his feet and begins to stomp furiously on the chunk of wood, succesfully grinding it to bits all about the stone floor. Breathing heavily he gathers himself up and continues, as though nothing was amiss, "Well....where was I? Oh yes...Something greater. As you know all of Norath has been plunged into a horrible war. Elves piercing humans, ogres bashing gnomes, dark elves being sinister as always, yaddy yaddy yah! Well, Rizzen, in all his insight and wisdom saw this coming, he rallied all the members of the mouse and put them instantly to work securing resources and contacts. And he prepared them to use the shadowy arts at which they all excelled. But now, instead of scavanging for scraps, the Fellowship is geared toward war. "Rizzen is no fool, he realizes that we as short folk are outnumbered. And to fight upon the field toe to toe with the other forces a fools endeavor. We would lose too many with accomplishing too little. And so, we fight from the shadows, waging a war of strategy, striking where it hurts the most. Our battle relies on gathering knowledge and smuggling supplies. Oh, most certainly we have bled the battlefield, but the death of one leader is greater than the lives of a hundred of his men. The ability to see an attack coming, an asset never to be ignored. Rizzen has led this fight, using the shadowy arts to move where we are not seen, to take what we need, and hamper our foes as best we might." Fizzle suddenly looks somber, "Alas I fear our numbers are not great enough to win this war, only to fight it. We recruit new members daily, each seeking something different, but aiding our cause non-the-less. We have assassins, bounty-hunters, spies, rogues, lore seekers, rugged warriors, and even a small preisthood who are devoted to our war. We are an underground resistance, simply put, self reliant and with vast ability." There is a knock at the door, It appears from the message from without is that Fizzle is needed urgently. Something to do with a fire in his laboratory. Fizzle's face explodes with shock and he hustles out the door. he pauses for only a moment to say this, "Enjoy your stay good friend, and I trust that you will look more deeply into our order and what we do here. We will offer you are services, and in return, we would appreciate yours. Good day to you sir." And suddenly he is gone. (ooc: We are a roleplaying guild, if this interests you, please, check us out, we are at war with the largest dark elven factions and are in need of good allies and members. We are not looking for power, however, and only desire mature players! We would die a million times over before we begin to accept people just for numbers and power. We are also seeking agents from other races....we have some, but they are alone and in a dangerous role, if this appeals to you and you can handle it....we would be most happy to have you) Fizzleheart Supreme Battlemage of the Fellowship of the Mouse
