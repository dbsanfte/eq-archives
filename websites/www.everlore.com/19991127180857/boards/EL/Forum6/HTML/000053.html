As a mid level druid I have found out several things for certain.
A: Druid are made to solo primarily, and always play a secondary role in groups. This is obvious to those who see that only a small portion of the buff spells accessable to druids are group compatable. Besides, roleplaying a druid the way I do requires me to be of in the Hinterlands insuring the easiest access possible for others.
B: Druids are part warrior, part healer, and part animal tamer. If you spend all of your points on Wisdom you have limited yourself to the role of spellcaster. Granted your Mana will be formidable, as will be your consistancy. But with the right weapons, (i.e. Screaming Mace, Staff of the Observers, Bloodforge Hammer, etc.) and the right buffs,(i.e SoE,SoT,TC,FF,Sow,SLR, etc.) you can become an effective fighter. At least to those conning white, with the occasional yellow. Now add in Summon Swarm, or Creeping Crud. Its all in the application I think. Dont forget your charmed friend either!
C: Level advancement is as hard as you make it. Scouting out areas in LF and BB enabled me to ascend through the ranks in terms of hours instead of days. The liberal use of Harmony made places like overfilled Orc camps and haunted Shrines the best place to solo. I would still lend my hand to groups, but it is never the source of experience soloing is. I join groups because even a confident druid sometimes needs companions around the campsite.

At first, I really was getting bored with my Druid. You try to solo to gain some levels, but the reward is low and risk high. Plus, you have to med so long at these low levels to make sure you have enough mana for those nasties that just will not seem to drop. Guess what, we do not get Meditate till later. What a bummer. This made my life tough at low level.

Until you get to level 9, life is tough. When you get to 9, you can solo more effectively and live. Levels start to fly by and the rewards are high. You can not be a very effective fighter as well as caster.

I myself gave my character a little Strength and Agility and put the rest in Wisdom. Lets face it, you have to melee at low levels to survive. You have to occasionally at high levels as well. Sometime you get jumped when you just have the mana to get away. The Agility will help you get missed more and the Strength will allow you to max your damage.

Lastly, the life creed of a druid is to be in harmony with nature. This does not mean that you walk around not hugging trees and all things natural. It means that you respect nature. You protect the weak as well as the strong because all things are necessary to be in balance. Druids kill animals when necessary for survival and to keep nature in balance. Too much of anything is bad. We are more comfortable in the woods roaming around than in cities. We prefer to be in the company of our brother and sister druids as well as rangers. Since we are lovers of nature it only follows that Wood Elves are the best class to take if you choose, if not in reality then at least in spirit. We prefer to work alone than with groups. That is why we are the optimal solo class.

With all this said, this does not mean that you have to be everything as said above to be a successful druid in game or in spirit. It just means that there is no perfect path. Do what makes you feel comfortable and live with the consequences and challenges that you set forth. Not adding to your Wisdom and adding to your Strength and Agility only hurts you in the middle levels. That is what magic items are good for. Open your pocketbook and buy some Wisdom enhancing items to make up for that deficiency. Soon you will be as effective as any other Druid who maxed their Wisdom with your spells and possibly a good attacker as well.

Good luck.
