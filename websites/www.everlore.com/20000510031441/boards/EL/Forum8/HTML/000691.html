Dueling and one-on-one PvP is all about tactics and initiative. When dueling, you can prep, but in PvP, you don't have that luxury.

So,
Try to be prepared and don't leave yourself vulnerable.
Know EVERY class/race strength and weakness.
Develop tactics that work for common scenarios.

How does a necro beat a L50 cleric?
It depends. If the necro gets the first shot, attack from distance, send pet, send 3 pumice shots, darkness, dot, etc. Try to stay out of sight and hope the pet interrupts. Might work...

How does a cleric beat a L50 necro?
Again it depends. Buff up and med. Root or fear pet. Hope the necro doesn't pumice pet. Blast stun blast stun blast. Might work...

Some classes are much better at PvP than others because they can react to different scenarios better. IMHO, necros are very strong with versitile attacks and defense as well as a pet. Clerics and palys are very tough to kill. Enchanters are very dangerous and the mes/stun lines can be devistating. Bards are perhaps the most flexible and therefore the most dangerous - killing a high level bard solo is damned near impossible.

But, not every fight is one-on-one. A warrior makes a great PvP partner for an enchanter, cleric, shammie, druid, ... You name it. They suck solo but with some buffs can be very nasty.

People come on these boards and boast about how their class is unbeatable or how they can whoop up on some other class. I say it all depends on the player who puts themself in the situation where their strengths are maximized and their opponents weaknesses exploited.

I play many classes including necro, mage, bard, druid, wizard, warrior, monk.

Any spell that is a shock spell, like cleric dd spells or some magician spells, are magic based. Only your magic resistance will help against this. Most other spells you can tell what they are based one (ie ice comet is cold based, lava bolt is heat based).

Necros, shamans, and shadowknights also get an array of poison and disease based spells. On the necro side, poison bolt, shock of poison, and venom of the snake are poison based, while disease cloud, infectious cloud, and scourge are disease based. One note: the heat blood line is heat based (obviously), but the heart flutter line is disease based (it stacks fine with disease cloud line, though).

Dependning on the class you're dueling (a thought out duel, not spur of the moment), you'll want to go for certain things.

Wizards: Cold and magic resist.
Magicians: Heat and magic resist.
Enchanters: Magic resist (need a TON because of Tashania).
Necros: Heat, disease, poison, and magic resist.
Druids: Heat, cold, and magic resist.
Shamans: Disease, poison, and cold resist.
Clerics: Magic resist.

* The hybrids have the same resist needs as the father classes.

As a necro, I have some all around good resist stuff, but I normall don't wear it unless dueling or taking on a certain caster-type mob.

1) Crown of the froglock kings (20sv cold/fire)
2) Cloak of the Icebear (20sv cold)
3) Mammoth hide leggigns (5sv cold)
4) Stein of Moggok (25sv disease)
5) Hollowed Bone Bracers (7sv magic)
6) Dragoon Dirk (3sv magic)

I also get a +60sv cold/disease buff and a +70sv poison buff. So, if I really want to, I can get (with buffs):

130sv cold

95sv poison
55sv magic
