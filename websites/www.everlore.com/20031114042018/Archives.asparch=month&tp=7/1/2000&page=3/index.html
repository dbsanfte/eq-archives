Ask Hook Updated
Catalyst [7/12/2000-11:43:31 AM]  Comments[0]

The Ask Hook column on VoodoooExtreme was recently updated today. Brian Hook talks about assembling a good team for an online RPG, general advice about making games, and also offers up his opinions on salary's in the game industry, interesting stuff, here's a snip.

I'm not a coder, a programmer, a graphic designer, or a businessman. I'm a player and I've been playing face-to-face RPGs since the dawn of time. I've tried CRPGs and online MMRPGs and MUDs and MUSHes. This has left me frustrated. One conclusion I've come to over my long experience with role-playing games is that a quality of the game is dictated not by the game designer, the dungeon master, the administrators or the wizards but by the players themselves. The engine and the setting can only contribute to attracting high quality players - players who given the tools are quite likely to make things very interesting without any help from the studio audience. Realistic settings, modeling social and cultural dynamics, economic and political factors, dynamic missions based on all the above and toss in some accelerated (condensed) timeframes and real mortality. That's a recipe for role-playing gumbo.

 

Abashi with a letter from Ester the Tester
Catalyst [7/12/2000-11:44:12 AM]  Comments[8]

A Message from Ester the Tester (from Quality Assurance)

Hello all,

There's been a bit of misconceptions lately about the function of our Quality Assurance department. One of our QA Reps, affectionatly known as Ester the Tester, asked me to post this for her.

-Gordon

--------------------------------

Hello!

Let me introduce myself. I am Ester the Tester (yes it rhymes) and I have asked Abashi to post a letter from the testing department to all of our players. I attended the EQGathering this weekend and spent quite a bit of time listening to players concerns. I felt that having Abashi put up a post detailing what the testing department is and is not may help the communication process between us and you the players.

What we do:
1. We read each and every email sent to eqtesting@verant.com
2. We read the bug reports as often as we can
3. We attempt to the best of our abilities to reproduce the bugs the players send us
4. We assign bugs to the Development Team.

What we do not do:
1. We do not have creative control over anything in game
2. We do not continue reading emails if they are abusive or flames
3. We do not do reimbursals
4. We do not respond to any emails, if we did this nothing else would get done

I would like to list a few things that if included in the emails and bug reports that would make it much easier for us to reproduce the situations.
1. Zone name
2. Location -- /loc
3. Full names of NPCs
4. Full names of items not abbreviations, I dont know all of them!!
5. Location
6. Any mitigating circumstances even if they seem trivial (youd be surprised)
7. Location
8. Server even on bug reports, some times they get shuffled
9. Location
10. Did I mention Location???
11. Server name
12. Location

If you are reporting a broken quest PLEASE include the starting NPCs full name, the zone it is in, the NPCs loc, and the phrase used to trigger the quest. This will save us SO much time when reproducing these bugs.

Something else that is useful is the threads that talk about broken quests, or bugs in the game. It is a little difficult to sort out the wish lists, flames, complaints, but we have pulled bugs out of those threads and some of them are already fixed.

I am a firm believer in communication. I as also a firm believer in EverQuest, and the team of testers I work with. There are some things we just dont catch. We havent played every class race combination to 60, so we desperately need your input. Please remember that the testing department can not change anything fundamental in the game, so the rants and flames are a waste of your and my time. To be honest, when I get an email that is offensive I stop reading right there. It is the same with posts. I do lurk on these boards, and I communicate frequently with Abashi. Sometimes he will ask for more information and clarification. This is usually me asking him to find me some more information so we can reproduce whatever bug you are talking about.

Thank you for playing EverQuest and caring so much about this game. I do promise to lurk and read. Ill never post, but if Abashi says Ester the Tester said well that was me.

Ester the Tester
Quality Assurance
Verant Interactive, Inc.

Aradune on EQ Lightsources
Catalyst [7/13/2000-11:47:47 AM]  Comments[6]

light sources don't always give off light

Not sure if this is a design "feature" or not, but I believe it to be a bug: light sources don't always give off light. If i have an armor item equipped that gives off light (I believe the Blackened Iron Crown does this) or, say, a Greater Lightstone in my offhand or a top-level inventory slot, it gives off light... occasionally. It seems to illuminate the ground/walls about once every 5-10 steps.

Indoors, if I unequip the item/GLS and reequip it, I get light again for a second. Outdoors, this doesn't seem to work, but it might just be my perception. When the light source is in "off" mode, I see about as well as I would with no light source at all.

This isn't technically a bug, but rather a deficiency in the engine. EQ uses vertex based lighting and if you're walking on large polygons (ones that are nearly as wide or wider than the range of your light source) then it won't light the polygon.

We do have some code in internal testing that dynamically increases the polygon count in those situations (so you'd see the light), but I'm not sure when/if we'll be able to fully implement it.

-Brad
