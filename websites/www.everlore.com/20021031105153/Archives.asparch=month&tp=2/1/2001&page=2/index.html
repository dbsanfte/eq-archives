Absor adds to Rogue Talk
Xanthus [2/8/2001-9:43:51 AM]  Comments[19]

Some interesting information posted by Absor on the Rogue Talk Discussions:

Hi folks.

I'm just popping in to relate to you the latest message from the dev team. This is from one of our designers:

---

We've ran through the Evade mechanics I described yesterday, and made a few refinements.

If Evading while at the top of the hate list, you will still be moved between the current #2 and #3 person on the hate list. In addition, if the amount of hate reduction calculated is less than 500 actual points, it becomes 500.

This insures that Evade will always be at least as effective as Jolt, and it will mitigate the Multiple Rogue Syndrome many of you fear. Example:

Rogue1 has 10000 hate
Warrior2 has 9800 hate
Ranger3 has 9795 hate.

Using the formula (#2hate-#3hate)/2(+#3hate)=Rogue Evade hate, the Rogue1 would be set to #2 with 9798 hate. 2 points lower than the new #1 player, and 202 points lower than his initial hate. Sucks, doesn't it?

Since 202 is less than 500, the actual hate would be adjusted 500 points lower, setting Rogue1 to 9500 hate, which could actually move him lower than #3. Doesn't suck.

Now if the three players at the top of the list were all rogues, the rotation of the three would not be as likely, since each one of them drops by a minimum of 500 points, IF there are other non-rogue damage dealers around. The non-rogues are much more likely to claim top spot rather than the trio of rogues. Lets throw some more math out here for you:

Above example, Rogue1 just evaded, setting his hate to 9500.

Warrior2 is actually Rogue2, with 9800 points of hate, now the top spot. He evades, placing him between 9795 and 9500, which is 9648. 9800-9648 is only 152, so he is bumped down 500 to 9300.

Ranger3 is actually Rogue3. He is now in the top spot with 9795. He evades, placed between 9500 and 9300, to 9400. Again, less than 500, so he is moved to 9295.

Now, you say that Rogue1 at 9500 should be back on top of the hate list, right?

This could be the case, but you have to realize that while these three Evades were going on, there may well have been other damage dealers in the encounter who have exceeded 9500 hate.

Each time a string of rogues evades, it becomes more and more efficient for the evader, pushing them down to the point where they are eclipsed by the other damage dealers.

We're testing this out now, and so far it looks pretty good.

In addition, if Evading while not #1 (pre-Evade), your total hate is reduced by 10% [i]OR[/i] 500, whichever is greater, not to go below 128.

We've also looked a few more rogue issues, and expect to have them adjusted in the following weeks. None of these are promised, keep this in mind, but we are looking into them.

Reduction of Min Range for throwing weapons. Not a removal, but a reduction, to around 3-5 feet. Should make them easier to use.

Instill Doubt still on the same timer as it is currently, but allowing that first backstab or special attack to land after ID goes off. This will happen. Still rewards those of you with good timing.

Apply Poison putting the Rogue in kneel mode similar to bind wounds, and releasing him when complete. Great player idea, this will probably happen.

 

Baltimore Fan Faire Press Release
Xanthus [2/8/2001-2:27:05 PM]  Comments[6]

Just received this press release in the mail. Here is a bit of the announcement:

Sony Online Entertainment's Baltimore Fan Faire Attracts Over One Thousand "EverQuest" Enthusiasts

Sold Out Baltimore Event Enthralls East Coast Game Fans

San Diego, CA - February 8, 2001 - Sony Online Entertainment is pleased to announce that the sold-out EverQuest(r) Fan Faire Event in Baltimore, Maryland was a resounding success this past weekend. Attending in record numbers, EverQuest players met their fellow Norrathians and shared stories with the creators of the most popular online role playing game on the Internet.

Over 1,200 fans attended the EverQuest Fan Faire, hosted by Sony Online Entertainment at the BWI Marriott in Baltimore; some Europeans had even crossed the Atlantic just to attend the event. EverQuest, a massive multiplayer online role-playing game that currently boasts over 330,000 active subscribers, brings tens of thousands of people together for online quests each day.

"We couldn't be happier with how things went here this weekend," said Cindy Archuleta, Community Relations Event Manager for Sony Online Entertainment. "This gives our development team and opportunity to keep in touch with the people who are responsible for EQ's success and it gives players the opportunity to meet face to face with some of their online friends for the first time."

At the event, fans tested their knowledge of EverQuest facts in the Trivia Quest, interacted with the creators of EverQuest in discussion panels featuring members of the development team, and submitted original art inspired by the game in the Art Show. Other events included a Real Life Quest, in which players had a chance to role-play and solve puzzles for special prizes. Another very popular attraction was EverQuest Jeopardy! which was set up for the event through www.station.com

Special in-game rewards were given to the winners - the Staff of Gathering and the Sword of Gathering. Specially created for the event, these items can be used by the winners' characters in EverQuest online.

In addition, Sony Online Entertainment announced the next EverQuest Fan Faire will be held in San Diego, California this April.

Test of Tactics II -Finalized Rules
Xanthus [2/8/2001-6:00:03 PM]  Comments[8]

Posted a few days ago on the Harpy's Head Tavern by Lady Daegarmo:

Finalized Rules

Test of Tactics II
Single Elimination 6 vs. 6 competition for all rounds.

Dates: February 7th though the 22nd on Live Servers, times will be posted at least 48 hours in advance.

Final is on Test Server, February 24th and 25th, at Noon, all capable servers will be represented.

Spectator and General Rulings

Guides and GM’s are considered judges and therefore restricted from being attacked. Anyone found attacking a GM or Guide may be banished from the Arena or death touched without warning.

The Arena is off limits to unauthorized PvP 15 minutes before and 15 minutes after the event. This is to ensure that the contestants and spectators are safe.

Spectators shall remain seated and not casting during the match. Any spell casting shall be considered aimed at a participant, and either harmful or helpful to someone in the match. Anyone who casts any spells (including Bard songs) during a match can be death touched and removed from the Arena.

Spectators shall not use /shout for any reason, but use /ooc instead. This is so that those involved in the match can use /shout, and the /ooc channel can be turned off to reduce distraction to the contestants involved. Use of /shout at anytime can result in a silence command being issued, rendering the player unable to use any form of communication, or banishment from the Arena.

Spectators shall not interfere with this event. Severe penalties are used only when directly attacking a player involved in the match, attacking a judge during the match, or healing a player involved in a match.

VI may limit the amount of people in the zone to control conditions of lag or frame rate for the participants. The event shall start open seating, but may close off at any time. The Event Staff may use any sort of decision on who stays and who leaves to ensure the event is a success. This should not be implemented until a minimum of 150 players in the zone has been reached.

AoE Spells ARE allowed in game. This has the potential of killing off some of the younger spectators, if you are killed by one of these spells, please pull your corpse out of the PvP area and await for a GM or Guide to give you a resurrection.

Combatant Rules

An entry fee of 5,000 platinum pieces will be required per seven-man teams. Money is to be given to an Event Coordinator and is due before the first match. Teams may waive the fees with the understanding of waiving the prizes.

First Place on the Server will be awarded the title of "The Tactician" to the captain of the team; in addition, all team members will be awarded the Golden Championship Ring. First place in Finals competition, all team member’s rings will be replaced by the Platinum Championship Ring.

Seven-Man teams can be made up of any race/class combination of levels 52 or greater. Only six may compete per match and teams may switch out any team member for the seventh player between the matches. Once the buffing period starts, switching may not take place until after the match.

No more then two of any one class on a single team will be allowed.

The seventh man out is free to coach his teammates from the side lines or top of throne and will be allowed to use any in game channels to do so.

All participants will be debuffed before the match.

A buffing period will be set for the first 30 seconds of a match followed immediately by combat. Mana will not be replenished after the 30-second period. The seventh man out may not help buff his teammates. Any created pets must stay in bounds just the same as any member of the team. If they cross the line into the other team’s area, the judges shall immediately kill the pet. Pet control will be key and pets should be made as far back from the other team as possible.

Limited Activatable items will be authorized, these items include ONLY the following.

Journeyman's Boots
Crystallized Pumice (Egg-shaped Pumice is not allowed)
ANY Epic Quest item is authorized to be held and right clicked.

With exception to the aforementioned items with activatable properties, all items, weapons, and armor will be allowed in this event. Activatable items of any kind may only be used for stat bonuses and processes. The right clickable effects are prohibited and will result in disqualification if used.

Unauthorized Spell List

Any form of Fear, unless directed at a pet
Any form of Charm, unless directed at a pet
Gravity Flux or Invert Gravity
Group based wolf form of any kind
Skeletal Illusions, not including Lich or Demi Lich
Bard Travel Songs

In the event that the combat exceeds 30 minutes of real time, then the Event Staff will cast an AoE Snare spell in the direct center of the platform every 2 minutes until combat is over.

The top of the throne is off limits to the combatants during the matches. The only people that may use the top of the throne are the seventh person of the teams and any press that are allowed to witness the match. The four firepots are also off limits to the combatants.

Starting positions are allowed anywhere that is legal (meaning not on the throne or in firepots) that is on your half of the tiles.

Judges reserve the right to disallow fleeing to take advantage of regeneration by giving warnings for delay of match. A third warning will result in disqualification of the player. This is to prevent 60+ minute matches that were seen in Test of Tactics I.

Any use of exploits or bugs will result in immediate disqualification from the event, and is a break in the rules of conduct as set forth on www.everquest.com.

Rallos Zek Specific Rule: There shall be no looting of any item at an event, this includes spectators. Anyone found player killing for profit at an event shall have action taken against their account.

Tallon Zek and Vallon Zek Rule: You may request to have your faction restored to the condition it was in at the last available save. This is to ensure that no unnecessary faction hits are taken at an event.

Test Server Finals

Those champions who go to Test are bound by additional rules: The character used for the event cannot adventure on Test for any reason. Handing off even the smallest items to a player on Test is considered duplication, resulting in a warning against the account, and loss of that item on your home server. Those found extremely abusing this rule may have further action taken against the account at the discretion of the EQ development team.

The transfer of characters to Test is midnight (give or take an hour) PST the night before the main event. Lake Rathetear will be shut down several hours before the main event. The only characters allowed in the Arena on Test for the main event will be the champions, the judges of the event and the event staff, other members of Verant Interactive, and members of the press who signed up with Lady Daegarmo at least three days in advance via email.

All other characters on Test found in the Arena during the main event will be summoned out of the zone and bound so they cannot return. If a second character is found to enter the Arena for any reason, it could be considered disruption and subject to action taken against the account.

Test of Tactics II Schedule is available at www.everquest.com events link and on the Harpy's Head Tavern

Be advised that we will be holding a competition on Test Server to determine the Test Server Champions and "Home Team"

More information about the Test Server rules will be released shortly

Lady Daegarmo
