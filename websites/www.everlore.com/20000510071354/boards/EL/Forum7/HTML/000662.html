I also did level 45 (the hardest non expansion hell level in the game) in one day. At level 7 when your pet is the weakest, stop trying to solo mobs that con yellow and even. Fight the blue mobs that you were killing at level 5 and 6, and though the exp will be slower than killing the higher level mobs you will die a lot less, making the exp much better in the end. After playing a magician to 48 and a druid to 31 this is what ive learned: Exp that gains at a slow steady rate with minimal risk of death always beats the hell out of exp that gains very fast with a higher risk of death.

If i've learned anything from my Magi experiences is that it truly makes a difference "where" you are fighting that determines how quickly you level.

If I knew what I do now, I would have taken my Erudite Mage to Qeynos as soon as I got my guild tunic instead of wondering around Toxx Forest for what seemed like forever.

This point was validated when I first entered my teens. I was simply wasting time in Blackburrow and the Qey Aquaducts when on a whim, I jumped on the boat and headed to the Feydower. I discovered Crushbone!! I easily went from 13-17 inside two weeks. Why? Tons of Spawns and Tons of people to group with. Dispute what my fellow Magi's say. Never underestimate the power of grouping. You quickly learn just how valuable you are in a group setting and how versitile as well.

After leaving Crushbone at 17. I went to Oasis and I hit 20 within a month or so.
After Oasis. Made a Beeline for South Karana. Been there ever since.

I am of the 28th circle now. I think as soon as I reach 29 I will make my way to Cazic Thule.

Long winded post I know.
Point is. If you are not leveling as quickly as your peers. It's probably "where" you are. Not "what" you are doing : )
