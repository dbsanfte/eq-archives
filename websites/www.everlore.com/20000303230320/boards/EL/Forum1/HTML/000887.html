Making good money in EQ is not easy, even at lvl 23(my monk level). I once killed a Gnoll Soothsayer in HH and gained a big 2cp from it and a Gnoll gave me a big 2gp...and both are tough dudes for below 20 characters. They had no armor pieces

But if you are after money, try to locate creatures that give the best loot that you can kill(if it gives xp, it's even better).

Tips
----
If you see a Decayed Skel with a weapon, jump on it, they sell from 5sp to 2gp.

If you see black/brown bears, jump on them, HQ bear pelts sell to players for 10pp this days IF you are lucky enough to get one from them...You can also practice your tailoring with the ruined pelt and double you investment in patterns with PW armors

Later, when you become able to kill Giant snakes and spiders, kill them all on sight, they often drop poison bags(about 4.5 gp), Giant snake fangs(4.5gp) and Spider Silk(1gp at shop, 5gp to players). With Spider Silk, you can make swatchs which stacked and sell for 1pp to players.

In the 20s, there are plenty zones to make good money. High Hold is a popular one but hunt there in group.

Um, no on the HQ pelt.

On Brell, the asking price has jumped to 10PP. I started playing a bit over two months ago and the price was 5PP on our server at that time.

But I will happily pay you 1PP for any you find...

Also, a 4th level melee type can handle Black Bears alone. They aren't that tough. I did it with a Paladin in some cloth armor and a guild tunic with a rusty longsword and no shield. You will be sitting afterwards though... sitting for a while...

And don't forget spider silk (not spiderling silk). Large Spiders (not giant spiders) are in the level range for a 4th level anything. They hit you with a very mild poison (which just stops you from healing, not DoT like Giant Spiders). Large Spiders carry up to two spider silks. These things will sell from a low of 2GP each to PC tailors to the ridiculous high of 1PP each if supplies are in high demand.

I know the tailor supply game, I am a tailor by Trade and there are times that I just back out of the auction market as raw material prices skyrocket. They do come down... ebb and flow, very fun to watch!
