Clarity Items -Gordon Responds
[1/2/2000-4:24:55 PM]  Comments[0]

A question was brought up on the EQ Forums about adding items that cast Clarity in Everquest. Gordon Replies:

>>>Any possibility of a clarity item in game at least?

In all probability, no. This is one of the defining spells of the enchanter class, and makes them very useful to the group. I would suggest that if you find clarity that beneficial, you might want to group with an enchanter regularly.

We don't want to put items in game that eliminate the need for class interdependance. Class interdependance is one of the core concepts of EverQuest and one of the things that makes it so successful.

-Gordon

Festival on Bristlebane
Tendaen [1/3/2000-2:27:54 PM]  Comments[0]

WHAT:Festival
WHERE: Qeynos hills on Bristlebane
WHO: Leaders of Justice
WHEN: 3:00 pm pacific time.

The Leaders of Justice are having there first festival this staurday.

Here are the events we plan to have:

FIND THE TREE-DRUIDS:

In this event we will have every one who wishes to join pay 1pp for the entrnace fee.

Then you will start a half elf druid with LOJ at the begaining of your other char's name. (THIS IS SO WE DON'T HAVE TRACKERS)

The first person to find a a druid gets a prize like banded of some sort of weapon.

ONLY THE FIRST PERSON WILL GET A PRIZE FROM THE DRUID HE FINES. Who ever finds all the druids will then get 50% of the platium entrance fee.

LOTTERY:

The Lottery will simply use the /random command. There will be a ticketmaster. The ticketmaster will stand at the gate.

There will be three stages to the lottery. The first will happen first, second will happen second and so on.

  • FIRST STAGE - 2pp per ticket
  • SECOND STAGE - 5pp per ticket
  • THIRD STAGE - 10pp per ticket

All lottery contestants must give the Platinum to the ticketmaster. The ticketmaster then writes down their number. Fore example, if Sarina was the 6th person to buy a ticket, she would be listed as No. 6. Then, the ticketmaster will use the /rand command for how ever many people there are entered. Whoever wins gets 50% of the lottery earnings.

DUELS/ DRUNKEN BRAWLS

Duels will be held a ways away from the Qeynos Gates (To avoid the guards).

Here are the rules to the Duels (Not the Drunken Brawls)

- A challenger must issue a challenge against someone else. The person challenged must be at least 2 levels below or above him.
- The challenged must accept. Once accepted, they are ushered to the "ring" (Area away from guards).
- Both fighters pay 2pp to the person in charge.
- The duel commences. There is a victor.
- The victor receives their 2pp back as well as the other person's 2pp.

Now, Drunken Brawls will be different.

- A challenger must issue a challenge against someone else. The person challenged must be at least 2 levels below or above the challenger.
- The challenged must accept. One accepted, they are ushered to the "ring".
- Both fighters receive 3 ales and consume them. They must then shed all their armor and weapons. (No Monks)
- Both fighters pay 2pp to the person in charge.
- The duel commences. There is a victor.
- The victor receives 2pp back as well as the opponent's 2pp

We can have as many duels as we want, and overall have a fun time with these. Perhaps we can work in a betting system (Getting a bit too farfetched here.) Now on to the last and final event.

ARENA PILE-UP/ARENA FREE FOR ALL

By now, we should have everyone's interest if the event hasn't flopped, so we should tell everyone who wants to participate in the last and final big event should come to the Qeynos Arena. We should send shouts throughout all adjacent zones.

Once everyone is in the Arena, we tell them to take a seat somewhere and listen to the rules of the events. Here are the rules. The first event we will hold is the Arena Pile Up:

Arena Pile Up:

We have one person from the guild quickly discard his/her stuff in the bank, then come back. The contest is to see who can strike the killing blow on the person running around.

There will be a person running around, and the objective is to strike the killing blow. The person will be buffed out of oblivion and given all sorts of great buffs and SoW etc. Whoever strikes the killing blow (Will be determined by the person killed) wins a prize.

And finally the last event

Arena Free For All:

EVERYONE IN THE ARENA. Basically, we have everyone just jump into the arena and start fighting. We must have at least a decent crowd. Then we just fight and fight and fight. In the end the last person standing wins a prize.

After this, the festival will be officially over. We'll say thank you to all who participated, and have a good feeling knowing we actually had some honest to goodness fun! But thats not all, folks.

THE WEDDING OF MARON AND REKINOE:

Those two lovebirds are getting married, and I am marrying them. After the last event in the festival is over, at Surefall Glade, all who wish are invited to the wedding at the Druid Guild Hall to witness the wedding of Maron and Rekinoe. There they will be wedded and then we will all rejoice.

Character Transfer Petition
[1/3/2000-3:48:33 PM]  Comments[2]

Received from Apocalypseious. You can also chat this in Forums: Character Transfer Petition

Hello all, my name is Apocalypseious and am a member of good standing in Regulators of Norrath on the Eci Server. I come to you today with hopes that what I have to say in this petition will pass in an optional patch format.

Have you ever thought of creating a new account and transferring a character of yours from one account to the other? Well I know I have, for many reasons which I will get into later, and I know my friends have to, but the sad thing is- we cannot. We as Everquest Hard-core Players paying $10 a month in fees do not have the ability to take a character and transfer it on to another account, not even on the same server. Now most of you are thinking, "What is the purpose of transferring characters into different accounts Apoc?"

Well I am glad you asked. Have you ever wanted to play with your brother, sister or best friend and let them use one of your characters while you are playing on one on another computer? Well you can't.

You cannot have more than one person logged in an account. So you think,

"No problem, I can just create another account and transfer the character to it, on the same server of course, and I can play with them on another account." Well you can't do that either. Here is another example that I bet most of us have gone through. A family member created a character, on your account of course, and like all of us, got addicted. This family member is playing their character for the duration of time our local 7-11 is open. This would, of course, result to less, if any, time spent on your character resulting to HAVOC! If we were allowed to create another account and be able to transfer that family members character into it, it will bring relief to all those hard-core EQ players who were going through withdrawal due to the lack of play time.

If you want to give your best friend or co-worker a character you started for them by putting it into another account for you or both of you to play whenever you wish, you should be able to. Now it maybe hard or difficult to transfer a character from one server to another, but creating two accounts and having them both on a server for your friends and family to play while you are in your own account, should not be overlooked.

I do believe it is the gamers choice however to have this option or not, so I am not looking for a mandatory patch, just an optional patch that we rarely use anyways.

I have brought up this issue to you today because I have a great deal of respect for the makers of EQ and the game itself, it has changed the say we look at Online RPG, I just hope that now that I have brought this issue/topic up to all of you, that maybe you fell the same way too and want to do something about it. Take care and if you see me on Eci, and can spell my name, tell me how you feel about this issue.

Sincerely,

Grodon on Experience Gain
[1/4/2000-3:45:27 PM]  Comments[0]

Gordon explains some things about the experience gain in Everquest. Found on the EQ Forums:

>>>What if say you had a group of a level 40, 38, 38, 35, 33, and a 32? Does the exp given come from the level 40 (meaning the exp changes dependings on their level) or is it based on a level 36 person (Average level of group) or is the amount of exp given the same no matter what level the party is but that higher levels need more exp and thus their bar raises more slowly than the level 32? I mean how exactly does this work?

We choose not to release any formulae in regard to how experience is divided up. That is probably why you haven't seen explained "exactly". What I will say is that it is divided up in such a way that a group of friends who always play together will level at a similar rate.

>>>My druid could solo up to level 50. I could kill say 6 mobs in an hour, or as much as 30-50 in an hour in a group but my druid consistently got more exp in a shorter amount of time then in a group.

This can really only be done by kiting. The changes to kiting a few months ago should have cut this down a bit. Additionally I wouldn't be surprised if you see some additional changes in the future to put a check on druid powerleveling. They weren't meant to be a solo class.

>>>A group of 4 levels much slower than a group of 2. Is this not supposed to be so or could there be a glitch? If not then how could this be based on your example you gave?

A group will only level faster if the group takes things harder or faster than they could with one less member. If you aren't doing that, then you'll level slower, but you'll still have less downtime.

>>>Why exactly were hell levels placed in the game? When I first hit the hell level (I was second on my server to do so) I thought it a bug and reported it. GM Solist said he couldnt understand the hell level and said he'd look into it as well as several senior guides but after a while it was considered a game feature. Why though?

It's the way the experience curves work. Unfortunatly, going in and fixing it at this point isn't an option. Anyone who had gone through a "hell level" would instantly get another level or two. Additionally, people who had already fought there way through several of them would be cheated out of the work they did.

There is one upside to them. It helps groups stay together.

-Gordon

Gordon replies to a post on the official Everquest Forums about a recent bug involving pets and grouping:

I haven't really noticed that pets hurt group exp, in fact I like a good pet user in a group. But something really wierd happened this morning that leaves a big question in my mind about pets in groups.

I was in SolB looking for a group in the pool room. There was 13 people there including me, two full groups. I was able to get to the pool room party and was waiting to be able to join since one person was leaving in less than a hour. The party consisted of a mage with an air elemental, two rangers, a druid, a paladin, and a warrior. I asked if it was ok for me to work on my piercing with the group. I do this a lot and most don't mind at all. I had a FS dagger in my primary hand and a dragoon dirk in the off hand. I wanted to max my piercing so I could use the unicorn shield effectively. (Any weapon with an AC component seems like a good idea to me) My piercing was around 110 and I needed to max it out to 195.

Then the wierdness started. I KSed two kobolds somehow! I would /assist their puller and wait until they got in a couple of rounds before attacking. Somehow the pet was enabling me to put in more damage and get the kill for 1/2 bubble total of exp!

Don't get me wrong, I can tell very little difference in the exp pool when in a group with pets. Almost none at all actually. But there seems to be an anomoly that affects group damage with a pet in the group. The best I can tell is that the following was true in this case:

Group damage = PC's damage - pet damage

In cases where the pet was hitting a lot and I was also, my single damage would outwiegh the group damage.

The group kind of laughed about it and didn't really care that this happened twice in a hour. Me assisting them just gave them less downtime overall and since the only other party was in the king room there was plenty of kobolds to pull.

Has anyone else seen this happen before?

I've actually heard a couple reports of something alone these lines, about melee types KSing from others. I'll forward it to the test team and try to find out what the deal is. I don't think it's related to the pet, but we'll see.

-Gordon
