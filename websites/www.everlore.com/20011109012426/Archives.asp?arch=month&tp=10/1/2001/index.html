What inspires your character?
Korenth [10/2/2001-10:00:30 AM]  Comments[10]

Yesterday, Absor updated his ongoing thread "What inspires your character your character?"

know we've said this before, but it bears saying again. The setting you get in games like EverQuest are just that, a setting. What folks do in that setting is going to be up to them. This 'efficiency' thing isn't a function nor requirement for playing EverQuest any more than it is for any other game, including pen and paper roleplaying games. However, one can only hope that with a rule set that inherently makes the game itself a little more realistic (and at the same time less efficient) we can urge people to worry more about being in character than about being efficient.

/em shrugs

You mention something that I've found very helpful when making characters. Having someone to play off of can make your character seem more involved. If you've got someone there with you a lot of the time that knows your character, you can get into a sort of rhythm and it often makes playing the character easier and more meaningful. Sometimes you can meet those people in game - I had a blast with an ogre I met in Blackburrow with one of my crazy gnomes. After many "Eeek!"s and "Yikes!" we managed to find each other amusing, though he had to promise not to eat me. A little goofy, but the interplay between the two was fun for everyone.

I've had similar experiences with far more serious relationships between characters that were built to be related (half brothers, mine a half elf and my friend playing a half dark elf – a very interesting couple that get along but definitely have differing views on life). It's also a good mentoring tool for experienced roleplayers that want to help someone else get into it.

To catch the rest head over to the developer's corner over at the offical everquest site.

GM Event on Prexus
PathwalkerII The Forsaken [10/5/2001-8:22:13 AM]  Comments[6]

Last night, news passed throughout Greater Faydark that the Keepers of Ivy cried for revenge from the smaller wood, and that something was afoot in Lesser Faydark. 

Quickly grabbing my gear (which I'd stripped to be more comfortable in the tree-top city of Kelethin) I ran as fast as I could to Lesser Faydark, calling out that I was there to help in any way I could. Maybe not in direct response to me, Bristleblue shouted "Off with you now! Find Mr. Green Greaves!" or some such (upon returning to Kelethin, my guild-mates and I had a bit too much to drink, and I fear the beer and mead has clouded my memory). I was still lost, not knowing where Bristleblue was or where this Mr. Green Greaves he sought might be. A kind wood elf called out to me that a small group was convening near the Brownie Village, and so it was there that I turned my boots. 

Bristleblue kept asking whether the group had found the target they sought, and at first the group replied that they had not. Then, in a moment of triumph, the shout was heard that Mr. Green Greaves had not only been found, but slain! 

By the time I arrived at the Brownie Village, the group had converged around Bristleblue, a gaunt little brownie who made reference to Mr. Green Greaves' boots having stomped on his kith and kin. I was horrified by this, as I have nothing against the brownies and they nothing against me... to the best of my knowledge. Bristleblue thanked the group for their assistance, saying that his kind may not think highly of others, but he certainly now did. 

By sitting around and hoping to hear the group share the tale of their adventure, a dwarf named Dalmask asked me if I knew anyone that could wear plate armor. I replied in the affirmative; my cousin Rohnyn, a warrior of Kelethin, loved plate armor more than I. Personally, it's restrictive to my ranger abilities. He handed me a pair of charred boots, the same boots that Bristleblue had used his magic to burn off the blood of his kin from the bronze plate. I accepted the gracious gift with humility, and waited a short bit in the hopes that the group would share with me their tale, but alas as soon as Dlamask had handed the boots to me, he and a rogue (Seeda?) ran off on their own way. A gnome named Womack had been present, but said little, and he too was now gone. Only myself and the wood elf who had guided me to the brownie village by voice remained, and he apparently was trapped in reverie of the evening, for he did not even reply to my thanks for his help. 

I ran off to Kelethin again, where I joined my guild mates in celebration of the event, and then sought out my cousin to grant him a gift of the boots. 

Probably a great event, albeit short (in the time it took me to run from the GFay zone to the brownie village, the group had been given their objective, found their prey, and dispatched it, AND had time to get back to the event coordinator), and I wished I'd acted sooner in getting myself there.
