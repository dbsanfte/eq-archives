I'd be lying if I said I died everytime I set foot in Kithicor, but here's a story for you...

My 18th Level warrior plunged into Kith from the Highpass side. (This was before I knew that on Halloween the zone was transformed into No Man's Land). I reached the part of the path that breaks off to Rivervale.

Suddenly, I was struck by the force of some incredible spell. It took away half of my 600 hit points in one fell swoop. Before I could turn to run or to see what had hit me, the second blast killed me.

I still don't know what killed me. An "undead knight" or something like that. Needless to say, I was bound in Qeynos! After getting bound in Highpass, it still took a 31 level druid to fetch my corpse for me. (I died 3x trying to drag it back to HPH!)

Since then I have stuck to the wall along the north side and have always had SoW. I have travelled during the day, too. I have not died using this method, but still marvel at all the corpses in the zone, even along the north wall.

At any given time the number of corpses in the zone outnumbers the players 5 to 1!!!

I'm sure someday soon I'll appreciate this zone, but not yet.

I remember the first time I was ever in Kithicor. As a level 6 Halfling rogue I had gotten bored with the Misty Thicket and decided to explore. Not knowing any better I went to Kithicor Woods. Luckily it was day when I entered and started down the path. I was not prepared for what I saw: nothing. No players, no NPCs, no animals, NOTHING. Just the fogged up trees and the wind whistling. And corpses. It seemed like ever 5 seconds I passed a corpse. The place finally got to me; there I am, safe at home, cozy in front of the glow of my monitor, and am scaried silly wondering what kind of instant death is lurking around.

I haven't been back since.
