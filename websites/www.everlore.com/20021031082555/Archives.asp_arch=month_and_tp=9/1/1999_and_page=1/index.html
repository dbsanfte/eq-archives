The Latest on Dot/Kiting
[9/7/1999-11:15:37 AM]  Comments[0]

Geoffrey Zatkin, Verant Game Designer discusses the latest on Damage Over Time spells and Kiting.

If you are getting this mail, it is because you e-mailed me concerning the recent modifications to DoT spells in reference to Kiting on the test server. I am sorry that you are getting a group reply, but there were too many e-mails to respond to each of you individually. I did want, however, for you all to at least get a response.

DoT spells will most likely be changed with the next patch. I can not go in to detail about what they changes are yet - we are still testing and do not want to prematurely announce what we will do.

Be assured that the changes will not eliminate Kiting, nor will they make DoT spells useless. For many people, the changes that we make will increase the power of your DoT spells. For those of you that kite, you will lose a bit of efficiency kiting.

Kiting in its current state is bad for the game because it allows certain classes to take out creatures of levels that other classes have absolutely no chance of taking out. This kind of imbalance is not something that we want in EQ.

The reason that we are putting these changes in now is that we finally heard a solution that we found acceptable AND that had minimal impact on the rest of the game. Most other suggestions involved getting rid of or limiting SoW (which would effect everybody) or getting rid of all movement spells together. Kiting in its most common form involves SoW and a DoT. Kiting can also be done with Snare and DD or a Bow, the Necromancer Darkness series, Journeyman boots and DD, Root Bombing, etc. Any time that you can move faster then your opponent, there is trouble (and you are doing some form of kiting). If we were going to completely fix kiting, we would have to take EVERY instance that let you move faster then a monster and fix it. That would have a huge impact on EQ - we do not want to do it.

We have a change that has a much softer impact going in - look for our announcement about it soon. I think you will be pleasantly surprised about what we are doing - at least compared to what you probably think we are due to rampant rumors.

As a side note, we played with different powers of DoT's and Kiting on the test server. People saw this and started posting. Now, just as a reminder, test is for testing. Thank you all for your prompt e-mails noticing that were we testing DoT spells. Please realize in the future that just because we are testing something on test does not mean that it will go live.

As a side note, we think that we got most of the DoT bug fixed. This means that you will be able to use your DoT's in a party situation, making your class much more powerful (the Necro's should be very happy with this one). I have heard reports that there still may be a case where there is a bug - but it happens MUCH less frequently. If you experience this bug, please e-mail use a report of what spells you were using, what you were fighting, the classes in your party and the size of your party. Any info (the more specific the better) you can give us on this bug (if it exists) would be greatly appreciated. Please e-mail this to ggrobson@verant.com. This address is ONLY for bugs related to DoT spells.

If you still have issues with this, please wait a week or two before e-mailing me. I have a backlog of e-mail that I want to get answered - e-mailing me later will ensure that I have more time to read what you send me.

Have a good one, and feel free to repost this.

Geoffrey C. Zatkin
Arch-Mage of Norrath

 

GameSpot UK on the EQ Expansion
[9/7/1999-11:15:46 AM]  Comments[0]

GameSpot UK has published a preview of the upcoming EverQuest Expansion. Here is the full article.

Verant Interactive is planning to expand EverQuest's world of Norrath to epic proportions in its upcoming add-on for the popular online roleplaying game.

Through patches, the company has already added numerous monster types, provided hundreds of tweaks to game balance, and even added an entirely new zone - The Plane of Fear. So it's no surprise that the planned add-on is not just a handful of monsters and a few new areas.

Verant vice president Brad McQuaid wouldn't go on record about how many zones would be included with the new continent of Kunark, but he did say there would "probably" be 15 to 20 new zones, depending on how many the designers could finish before the expansion ships early next year.

McQuaid said the plan was for the new continent to be a bit bigger than Faydwer, but not as large as the main continent of Antonica. As for new monster types, it is assumed there will be a proportionately large number. In the two new zones we were shown, new monsters included a giant mosquito and an enormous inch worm as well as variations on old favourites.

The most impressive aspect of the expansion is simply how much better the game looks. Verant president John Smedley said the team is taking time with the expansion to add visual features that didn't make it into the original. A number of these features are immediately apparent, including transparent water, more detailed textures, and moving parts on buildings.

All of these new features were on display in the zone we were shown, The Swamp of No Hope. Populated by Frogloks (which have a large village replete with large, rotating towers in the new swamp) and Iksars (the new playable race of lizardmen), the swamp was as good a place as any to show off the game's new visual features. The Iksar look quite impressive, with highly detailed scales and reptilian eyes. A neutral evil race, the Iksar once had a kingdom on Kunark, and the ruins of their once-mighty people - and large, dilapidated statues of lizard warriors filled the swamps.

McQuaid said Verant has still not finalised the price of the expansion, but he did say that the entire game would be repackaged for sale at retail to include the new continent. He also said the price for the expansion would be "reasonable".

GZ's Message about Pets
[9/10/1999-2:16:03 PM]  Comments[2]

Geoffrey Zatkin recently wrote this letter about the recent changes on the Test Server and upcoming updates.

The changes that were made on the test server involved the highest levels of pets - pets from level 44 and 49th spells. Now, I realize that many of you did not actually have access to these spells, but wrote based on what your guildmates experiences were, or from what you have seen posted on message boards. Because of this, I am going to go in to detail on this a bit more then the rest of you may need. Stick with me. We recently ran some tests and came up with results that startled us. Upon checking the source, we found that extremely high level pets were using incorrect data - they had their damage statistic set higher then it should have been. This was brought to our attention because certain pets were defeating melee types of equal level in duels. This is obviously unbalancing - one ability (spell) of a class should not be more powerful by itself then an entire other class. Now, there are lots of arguments as to how this is not so - players think and react where pets do not, player can use tactics, have better gear, etc. These are all true - but the very highest levels of pets were still too powerful.

On the test server, we played with different powers of pets. People saw this and started posting. Now, just as a reminder, test is for testing. Thank you all for your prompt e-mails noticing that were we testing different power of pets. Please realize in the future that just because we are testing something on test does not mean that it will go live.

The current pets on test (again, on "test") are more powerful now then the initial reports that came from test a few weeks ago. As mentioned, we have played with their stats and ended up raising them a considerable bit from what we initially lowered them to. The 44th and 49th level pets on test are currently not as powerful as they were, but they still pack a very respectable punch. We have done a lot of recent testing, and are pretty comfortable with the amount of damage the pets are doing.

On the plus side, one of the things going in this patch is better pet pathing (pets navigate outdoors and indoors better). We also have quests in for Necromancer pets (check Neriak). Magician pets' spells will do more damage - it will no longer be disadvantageous to have your pet cast a spell during a combat. High level Enchanter Animations will now look a bit different - as will high level Necromancer skeletons. Magician pets will get tweaked a bit on looks soon.

So, for those of you not effected, sorry for the long e-mail. For those of you effected, we are sorry for the little bit of damage that you lose. We hope that the ability for your pet to travel with you in more circumstances will help. None of you are "useless" classes - I personally tested level 44+ casters of each pet class, and they all did quite well.

Have a good one, and feel free to repost this.

Geoffrey C. Zatkin
Arch-Mage of Norrath

Patch Update
[9/13/1999-2:13:35 PM]  Comments[0]

The latest from the Patch server!

September 13th, 9:00am
----------------------
Bug Fixes:
- Players levitating should be easier for other PCs to see.
- When charmed/feared and killed players should not be returned to the character selection screen.
- Lore items in containers that are dropped should not be destroyed.
- Charged items, such as the Staff of the Observers, should correctly report the number of charges remaining after zoning.
- Corpses should remain lootable even if a player goes linkdead while looting
- A player under the effect of charm or fear can no longer open their spellbook or sit in order to escape the effects of these spells.
- A player using online help should no longer be locked into the help screen if another party initiates a trade.
- Non-melee items equipped in weapon slots should no longer mutate if the pc is charmed.
- Extremely large NPCs, ie Vox, should not suicide when stuck.

New emotes:
- Here is a list of new emotes that have been added to the game: agree, amaze, apologize, applaud, plead, bite, bleed, blink, blush, boggle, bonk, bored, brb, burp, bye, cackle, calm, clap, comfort, congratulate, cough, cringe, curious, dance, drool , duck, eye, gasp, giggle, glare, grin , groan, grovel, happy, hungry, introduce, jk (just kidding) , kneel, lost, massage, moan, mourn , peer, point, ponder, puzzle, raise, ready, roar, salute, shiver, shrug, sigh, smirk, snarl, snicker, stare, tap, tease, thank, thirsty, veto, welcome, whine, whistle, yawn

PVP Changes:
- In order to make PVP combat between spellcasters and melee types more viable some changes to PVP spell effects have been made. All damage spells cast in PVP combat will do less damage to the PC than the same spell would do to an NPC. When a PC is under the effect of a root-type spell there is a 20% chance that they will break free when a direct damage spell is cast upon them.

Spell Changes:
- Levitate: All players may now cancel this effect.
- Paralyzing Earth now Wizard and Necromancer useable.
- The Mana cost on some Magician damage shields has been slightly reduced
- Alter Plane: Hate and Alter Plane: Sky have been changed into higher level spells.
- Succor: Ro (Druid) is now a level 39 spell (previously a typo as a level 49 spell)
- Curse of the Simple Mind (Enchanter) now lowers both Int AND Wis.
- Selo`s Consonant Chain (Bard) should now work better.
- Shadow Vortex (Necro) is more effective.
- Screaming Terror is now an all or nothing save - this should stop the 'train' problems that it was causing.
- Gravity Flux will now research correctly for Wizards.
- Lich and Bond of Death are now researchable for Necromancers - check your tomes.
- The different types of Words of Collection and Words of Acquisition are now more easily identifiable.
- Dead Man Walking (Necromancer) has been renamed Dead Man Floating
- Ignite Bones (Necromancer) has had the movement component removed. This means that it will no longer break root and snare spells, and that it will now stack with the darkness line.
- Syvelian`s Anti-Magic Aria (Bard) has been fixed.
- A bug in some of the AoE code has been fixed - AoE spells will now effect more monsters.
- Some Magician Summoned items are now lore items. Items that have turned lore have had other small benefits added to them.
- Dance of the Fireflies (Druid) has been changed from Divination to Conjuration.
- Rangers have gained the spell Dance of the Fireflies (level 15)
- High Level Enchanter Illusion spells have had the remainder of their effects implemented.
- Magi research components have been added to more monsters throughout the world.
- There is a new pet command: /pet taunt and /pet notaunt. Pets default to /pet taunt
- The Rods for the Ice Comet quests will now pop much more often.
- Envenomed Breath (Shaman) has had its damage raised slightly.
- Scourge (Necromancer and Shaman) has had its damage raised slightly.
- Enchantment of Light and Enchantment of Brilliance (Enchanter) have been replaced by new enchantment spells. These spells have been added to a hidden vendor somewhere in the game.
- New quests for Necromancers to research their pets have been added

Pets:
- High level pets (44th and 49th lvl spells) do less damage
- Pets will now navigate some outdoor and dungeon areas better

DoT Changes:
- If the monster is in melee with you, there is no change to how a DoT spell works.
- If the monster is running away from you (fear, wounded, etc.), there is no change to how a DoT spell works.
- If the monster is moving when the damage from the DoT is applied (happens every few seconds), it will take 66% of the damage that it would have taken.
- DoT spells have all had their duration slightly increased. If the monster moves for 18 seconds during a fight, it will take as much damage from the DoT as it would before the patch. If the monster moves for less then 18 seconds during a fight, your DoT will do more damage then it would have done before the patch. If the monster moves more then 18 seconds during the fight, it will take less damage then it would have before the patch.
