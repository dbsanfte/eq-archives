I am not a Cleric, Im a druid but I think to some extent the same question applies...

And I wanted to say "Hi" to Fargo who I met in Freeport (if I remember rightly) but haven't seen since.

My opinion is this:

You don't have to roleplay a kindly wise cleric to roleplay a cleric, that's oversimplistic and I 'd argue against the statement that it is "better".

But even if you are kindly and wise, you don't have an obligation to buff any sword-brained idiot who demands it, any more than they have an obligation to rescue you if you get in melee and shout for help.
Its down to individuals either

a) out of character being helpful, returning a favour, or responding to bribery or simple politeness

or b) in character, behaving as they feel _their own personal character_ would, not how they feel a stereotype of a priest would.

If you play your character consistently people will come to know your name and react accordingly; if you just give out a standard buff/heal/response to every hail, you might as well be an NPC.

Just my 2pence worth.

If you respond to every hail and request for heals and buffs and give them for free, some people may start to seek you out for these free spells more often. I'm sure it can get quite overwhelming to try to keep up the requests sometimes. If you're healing and buffing for free, then you're paying to play a game so that other people can advance their characters while you sit around and meditate. If this is what you enjoy, then by all means, do so! I'm sure that lots of people will thank you for it, myself included. That's the point of view from a power leveling, non-role playing perspective. If you want to make money and have time to advance your character, then charge a flat fee or ask for donations.

I am not, however, saying that I advocate this. It's your character, you paid for it...play it how you choose. Do whatever is the most fun for you. Role playing can be an absolute blast. I would have LOVED to have been there to see that dwarf taunt the rude little elf. I'm sure you can have quite a lot of fun playing the wandering healer. I really like that idea of preaching to those wanting healing before you will cast on them. It probably would work as a rather good deterent for too many people wanting spells cast on them. Instead of saying the standard 'donations accepted' you could try to come up with some other way of making it known that you would accept cash in return for your time and effort. Emote that you rattle the donation box or something.

Personally, I always at least offer to pay when I have people cast spells on me and I have cash on hand. More than half the time my offers are refused and I get the spells for free. I also cast what buffs I can on the person who cast theirs on me.

OOC

I typically will heal freely when I am able to. If I am in a group I heal others before tending to my wounds. However, as many have mentioned, it is completely up to you. From an RP point-why should I charge something that my god gives to me freely to help others?

One thing I have seen is that most folks will donate for buffs (ie sow etc) but very few offer any payment for healing (I have been offered payment, but typically just ask for them to help another in a time of need). Is this being taken for granted? I am not sure, maybe folks view them as distinct and separate.

I know many who buff/heal for no charge, especially so for young characters (yay for those kind folk!). The only time I have flatly refused to heal someone is when they wanted me to help them powerlevel by healing them as they fought creatures that out classed them.

One policy that seems to work:
If you have the mana and feel charitable heal others without them asking.

If someone asks for a heal, well then its up to you isn't it =)

Coming from 16 years of Catholic Education, I can tell you that there is a lot of variation in the character and personalities of priests: gruff to tender, taciturn to loquacious, pious to rakish, generous to stingy, etc. And these are all of the same denomination of the same religion.

My opinion is you should roleplay your character's take on how to serve his/her diety. Maybe you freely help co-religionists, but anyone else has to pay. Maybe you won;t help those of other races. Maybe you make only co-religinists pay. you get to decide. Then you can discuss this with other clerics of your own deity and try to get some consensus -- even start an Order!

My shaman character freely heals other barbs, especially the young warriors and is always good for a SOW for a higher level barb going after a corpse. However, he only helps Outlanders he feels to be particularly deserving. And when he gets scolded for not helping strangers, he can get pretty shirty and haughty in return.
