New Graphics DLL Patch Today - - June 6, 2001
Ksian [6/6/2001-5:08:22 PM]  Comments[11]

Servers were patched today in an effort to help performance problems while in the rain or snow.

We have just put up a new patch. It's a small patch and it's of the type that will not require that we take servers down. So you'll get the update the next time you log in.

The patch is a fix for the problem with older ATI cards and cards using Savage chipsets that caused them to crash during rain and snow and while using F10 to change views.

The patch may improve performance in general and might solve some other crashing problems.

Account Security Addressed by Absor
Lybernia Belisama [6/7/2001-12:22:21 PM]  Comments[32]

The EverQuest team posted a reminder to the boards today regarding account security:

As the population of Norrath increases, so too do issues regarding stolen or compromised accounts, and/or accounts that have been transferred from one player to another in violation of the User Agreement. These issues - and the time it takes to investigate and attempt to resolve them - hugely detract from the time that SOE’s customer service staff have to devote to providing quality customer service to issues that impact upon the gameplay experience. Accordingly, SOE must remind all players that:

(1) Account security -- whether it be password protection, running virus checks, disabling file sharing, or any other element of making sure accounts are not stolen or compromised -- is the sole responsibility of the account owner.

(2)Any and all stolen and/or compromised accounts that are reported to SOE will be banned, and no items will be reimbursed. Any player found to be involved in an account theft will have any and all of his/her accounts banned.

- The EverQuest Customer Service Team

Poe Responds to Spell Questions
Feltink Ithink [6/9/2001-8:07:54 PM]  Comments[0]

Taken from the Everquest Official Forums:

xole wrote:

Here is a possible way to implement critical hits on evocation spells:

base number = X*(Evoc skill/Spell Level) + (research skill/10) + (specialization Evocation/10).

Where spell level is the level of the spell being cast. X is a modifier based on class. 2 for wizards, 1 for other classes (if crit hits are not wizard only).

Thanks for the well written suggestion. Critical hits on spells is something we have discussed in the past. I personally think it's an excellent idea, and that it could be balanced to account for the different power scales over our continents. That being said, we don't have the time to allocate adding this type of functionality right now. Critical spell hits would be a big change requiring a lot of play testing. currently there are no plans to add it, but I won't rule it out as a feature that we may decide to add at some point down the road.

Thanks for the feedback, and glad you enjoy the game so much

- Lawrence Poe

View the rest of the thread Here.

Ezrick Blackpriest wrote:

I've brought this up as a bug with Esther twice with no response. I'll assume its not and then simply ask you: why?

When the wizard spell Evacuate is cast every mana user in the party is charged mana for the spell. As a 57th cleric with a 200 wisdom this spell costs me a full bubble of mana when cast by a wizard. As far as I know this is the only spell in the game that subtracts mana from every caster in the group when cast.

This is not the largest issue in the game certainly, but its, well, annoying. Because this spell is normally cast in an emergency when things are going badly the additional mana drain just adds to whatever frustration you already had over things going badly.

So is this a bug, or a 'feature'? If it is intended, why?

This *is* intentional. Evac is a group spell. One of its components is a mana drain. That component, just like the Evac, will hit every member of the group. This was added to stop people from Evacing to a relative safe area then have the MOBs come after them where they are near the zone line and can easily escape should things go bad.

Recently we have changed the interzone teleports to clear agro, so that feature of the spell is a little less important. We have talked about removing the mana drain but have yet to make a decision. Hopefully I can give you an answer sometime soon.

Hope that clears things up for ya.

- Lawrence Poe

Jaeden_Avengarious wrote:

For the Deity imbue spells, the level for them set is for 29. The only different one is Imbue Ivory, which is level 34.

Is there a special reason for this?

Yep, It should be set to the correct level of 29 in the next patch, thanks for the feedback.

- Lawrence Poe

I realise this may have been intentional as part of the patch but I just wanted to express my concern as part of the SK community. Shadow Vortex and Shroud of Pain are minor AC drains which are very commonly used on raids and in normal grouping situations to manage aggro. The problem is how Speed of the Shissar is built in our spell database. This is the same problem that Call of Earth has. I'd like to be able to tell you "I'll move some stuff around and throw it up on test to see if it works" unfortunately I can't fix it right now. I've looked over the problems with speed of the Shissar a few dozen times. All I can really tell you is that a number of stacking complications we currently have should be rectified in the coming Months.

SneakyAmp - Winamp Plugin for EQ
Kithanos Uth'Majere - Torvonnilous server [6/10/2001-7:43:35 PM]  Comments[9]

Sneaky Amp is a funny little Winamp plugin that allows you to control Winamp's various functions without direct acces to Winamp.

That sounds awfully complicated, but it's not. All Sneaky Amp does is watch the keyboard, and when you type certain commands it manipulates Winamp for you. Quite useful for using Winamp within alt-tab unfriendly games (such as Everquest) or just for playing .mp3's without having to dig through tons of mp3 folders.

Go here to download this nifty little plugin and listen to your mp3's headache free.
