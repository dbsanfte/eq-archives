ABSOR or ABASHI-59 Necro Pet Not Dual weilding/Double Attacking, PLEASE HELP

Our level 59 necro pet only attacks 1-2 attacks per round. It is not know for sure whether or not he isnt dual weilding or isnt double attacking, but he will not accept more weapons as the other pets will.

The weapon integrated into the model for that character and their subsequent attacks were accounted for when the pet was designed. Pets, as they exist, are as strong as they can be given the overall balance of the world. While I agree that it would make sense for the 59th pet to do better than the 49th pet (which I believe it does under most circumstances, even if it is close), this is a case where the 49th is stronger than it should be as opposed to the 59th being weaker.

*FIX* Feign Death

If you feign while a mob is casting a spell on you you feign death has a ZERO percent chance of working on that mob.. but still can fool any others around you till they RE-AGRO YOU becuase the caster didnt forget you.. NPCs need to STOP casting when a pc FEIGNS.. Its a BUG that they dont and completely makes the skill useless in any bad situation with casters.

While I know some will disagree, this is how it is intended to work. Once an NPC begins casting a spell, only a stun will stop them. Even if you die mid-cast, the spell will still go off on a corpse.

It's one of the vulnarabilities of Feign.

-Gordon

Drinal: Event report by The Simcorps
Randy Mayhew [11/5/2000-10:45:54 AM]  Comments[1]

The event took place today and it started at 1:00 pm PST. It was sponsored by a web site called The simcorps. http://www.simcorp.com

Our mission was to locate a messenger by the name of Lowthan. He was a courier that had never made his last delivery. We were given information of a friend of his who might know of the route he took.

Finding this person was not that difficult, untill we tried to talk to him. The difficulty was in the fact that our group of five lvl 3 and 4 humans and half elfs did not have the necesarry skills to communicate with the informant, a very unlearned dwarf who spoke only in Dwarvish. We enlisted the aid of some nearby dwarves and soon had our information.

We then headed to the West Commons to hunt for our friend Lowthan. After searching the Druid ring we checked the local bandit camps and found him alive but not responsive. We soon discovered that we needed to feed him edible goo to revive him. The bandits gave us no problem so we left one of our troup to watch over our fallen charge and proceeded to acquire the skills and ingredients to make this goo.

Upon returning to the bandit camp, much to our dismay, two of our group were attacked and killed. Very quickly in fact. It seems the thugs that arrived were less happy about our presence than the cutthroats. Once again we enlisted the aid of a higher lvl druid, one Soaffa, and after his short work of the thugs we were able to revive or friend. Our mission was a success and we were rewarded with 2 plat each for our efforts and we were able to loot the thug corpses and we all got a little something from that as well.

OOC Bear in mind that this was a low lvl event,(limited to lvls 1 - 4), sponsored by the Simcorps.
I urge you to visit this website not only for EQ events but they have missions for around 15 popular games including several Janes titles, Age of Empires II, Unreal Tournement, just to name a few.
