The answer is all of them are fun to play. I have played Necro and Enchanter and will play either one depending on my mood. If I want to group I play the Enchantress. If I want to go solo I play the Necro. The advantages to these two classes is the great variety of spells. Both classes are versatile and fun to play.

Enchanters require a lot more concentration than the other two as you are constantly timing your Mez's so you can refresh them. I personally get a lot of satisfaction from the drive-by buffs I cast on players as I run through a zone. Most players are ecstatic to find that someone cast Clarity on them while they were in combat. The downside with Enchanters, in my experience, has been that the lower levels crawl by. With the upcoming addition of the Breeze spell (a 16th level Clarity spell) it will be easier to find groups so this may not be an issue shortly.

Necro's are great too. Their mystique is a lot cooler than the Enchanter (IMO) and their pets just rock. It is satisfying soloing a creature that would completely decimate my Enchatress. However you will find it hard to group as a Necro, at least that has been my experience. DOT's are not very group friendly If you have a regular group you play with this may not be a concern. The only other thing I did not like as a Necro was that all my buffs are self only.

Now I have not played a mage yet but am actually starting one in the next few days so I can join a RL friend new to EQ. The main problem of the Mage (this gained from Mage friends, I do not claim to be an expert) is that you can master their strategies by level 8. They do what they do very well but are not very versatile. However it is my opiniion that mages make better groupers than Necro's and make better solo'ers than Enchanters. If you want to be able to reliably do both than a matge is a good choice.

I hope this is helpful. Goodluck in your choices!

Welp, first off, it sounds like you may think Enchanters are a pet class. They quite simply aren't, so you'd probably be better of not thinkin that from the start. Their abilities simply don't compare to those of the Mage or Necro. You will be able to use your pet effectively possibly up through level 12, but afterwards charm is quite simply the better alternative. So if you're looking to be able to summon a monster of a pet, read no further and forget about Enchanters.

I enjoy being an Enchanter because they are capable of things that no other class can manage, or even dream of. In fact, I run into many players every time I play, including other Enchanters, that I find myself having to explain the affects of various spells to (even mesmerize!). I can only imagine that people rely heavily on outdated or misinforming webpages to learn about their spells. I also get the impression that most people play this class so that eventually they can cast clarity and make jewelry. Those people are probably why people don't like grouping with Enchanters.

Anyway, we are severely dependant on other players, whether in groups or not. That is possibly the biggest downfall of the class. I've had trouble like I've had in no other class with grouping. I can only imagine that's because people generally don't understand us or have grouped with Enchanters that don't understand themselves (or are simply bad at doing what we do). Of course, when you hit level 29 you'd be lucky to go for more than 5 minutes without receiving a request to join a group.

Any Enchanter can go to and buy from and do quests in any city by level 20, and Dark Elf Enchanters can do the same by level 4. I think that's one of the coolest things about being an Enchanter.

My only regret, again, is that so few people truly understand the role of the Enchanter in a group until much later levels. If you ever find a good tank that knows better than to start whomping on a mesmerized mob or actually listens when you say to attack the scythe instead of the borer, handcuff yourself to him.

Above all, if you pick this class, study all of your spells' effects on your own, not on web pages, and you should most certainly take anything you hear on Everlore with a grain of salt. If you find that one spell doesn't work all that great at a particular level on a particular mob, dig it up again later and see if anything changed. I've had a couple shocking surprises myself.

Enjoy =)
