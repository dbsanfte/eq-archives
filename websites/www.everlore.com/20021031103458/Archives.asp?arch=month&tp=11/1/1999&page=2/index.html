Intrepid explorers have returned from the edge of the EverQuest world bearing tidings that will forever alter the history of Norrath. A new continent, Kunark, has been found south of Faydwer beyond the Timorous Deep. It is rumored that ships will soon set sail from Antonica and Faydwer bound for this new world.

You will be able to explore this new land in March of 2000, when the The Ruins of Kunark Expansion Pack becomes available. The Ruins of Kunark Expansion Pack will add startling new features to EverQuest, including more than 20 new zones - lakes, jungles, crypts, dungeons, ancient towers and underground castles.

Brad's Message on RoK Site
[11/12/1999-2:20:48 PM]  Comments[0]

Producer Brad McQuaid has recently posted a message in the forums on the Ruins of Kunark website. In this message he mainly talks about what will not be implemented in the expansion pack.

While it is generally a good idea to focus on what WILL be in the Expansion, I do want to make sure there are no false expectations and that I'm clear as to what the focus of the Expansion is. The primary focus is to add a new continent, a new playable race, and to update the graphics.

The new continent should help quite a bit with some of the crowding in the game (I know, some people think the game is overcrowded and some don't). In any case, the world will get a LOT larger. We're talking 20+ zones, and the outdoor zones are HUGE.

The new race will allow people who've played through a lot of race/class combos or who want to try something totally new in a new land to create a new Iksar character.

And the new graphics allow us to keep up with ever advancing technology, so EQ continues to look good and becomes even more immersive.

Ok, now let's talk about what's NOT planned for the Expansion.

First, changes and enhancements that affect the entire game will be made via patches over time, before and after we release the Expansion. A lot of the great ideas I see posted here are more applicable to EQ as a whole, so I just wanted to say that we're listening, but many such ideas, if implemented, would just appear as regular patches.

Second, I'm going to post a short list of enhancements that we have NO short term plans to implement, and that won't be part of the Expansion. Like I said earlier, I want to make sure people don't have false expectations and are then disappointed. It's really easy to dream up all sorts of cool things that may or may not become part of EQ one day, and that's fine -- we do that all the time too, trust me But it's also good to temper that with realism and time constraints.

The following are NOT on our short term list, nor will they be part of The Ruins of Kunark. They may or may not be part of our long term plans (long term plans meaning perhaps additional expansions, sequels, or other EQ related projects).

More Classes
Housing
Mounts (horses, etc)
Flying
Additional New Races (other than the Iksar)
New Skills not related to the Iksar race

Anyway, I hope that helps. The Ruins of Kunark will be AWESOME and I'm very excited about it, but I did want you all to know what was and wasn't planned.

thanks for listening,

-Brad McQuaid
Producer, EQ: The Ruins of Kunark
