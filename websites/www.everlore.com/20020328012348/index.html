Wednesday, March 27 2002

Holland-Based Server Coming Soon!
Georgia [3/27/2002-5:26:56 AM]  Comments[3]

The EverQuest Live Homepage had a news update this morning regarding a new server coming to you soon for our Holland residents ;)

We're opening a new server, the second EverQuest server to be located outside of the United States. The new server is called Kane Bayle and it will be located in Amsterdam. We will be opening the server as a test server for a while to help test network and connection issues, so we need to have a lot of people testing it out. You will be able to access the new server by running TestEverQuest.exe. Once you do, Kane Bayle will be one of the servers on the Test Servers selection screen. Remember, though, that this is a Test server and all of the Test server rules apply.

Important note: Because this is a Test server, we will be wiping characters when we take the server Live.

Keeping that in mind, we want to encourage you to log into the server and play for a while. We really want to see how it holds up under a full server load, and for that we need players logged in and playing. Now is also a good time to try it out and see if Kane Bayle will provide you EverQuest with a better Internet connection. So please give it a try.

Thanks for playing EverQuest, and have fun on Kane Bayle!

- The EverQuest Team

Game Card Updates
Georgia [3/26/2002-11:47:07 AM]  Comments[14]

A lot of people were asking questions regarding the Game Cards, so I emailed Absor to see if he knew anything regarding the status of Game Cards being effected by the new subscription rates. He replied shortly thereafter, pointing me to a thread on the official site's Developer's Forums which answers your questions.
Game Cards:
a. Have they been adjusted to meet the new pricing?

Retailers have set, and will continue to set, their own prices for game cards.

b. What if I already have game cards with the old value?

Game Cards are good for 3 months paid play -- regardless of the price any particular player paid for his/her game card(s).

Thanks for clearing that up Absor ;)

Monday, March 25 2002

Subscription Rates to Increase
Georgia [3/25/2002-12:57:16 PM]  Comments[90]

Unfortunately, I'm about to bring you bad tidings :( The subscription rates for EverQuest are going to increase, starting on April 25th, 2002. The rates aren't huge... it's nothing really to get worked up about, especially if you enjoy playing the game. The following is the announcement straight from the EverQuest Live page:

Due to the increased costs of running the EverQuest game service, we will be increasing our subscription rates in April. Effective April 25, the new monthly subscription rate will be $12.95/month. We will continue to offer discounts from the new rate plan on multi-month subscriptions. You don’t have to do a thing; you will automatically be migrated to the new billing structure under your current subscription plan when your current subscription plan expires.

New EverQuest rates as of April 25, 2002:

  • $12.95 per Month
  • $35.85 for 3 Months
  • $65.70 for 6 Months

HOWEVER, for a limited time only (from April 10 through April 24, 2002), you may extend your current subscription for an additional 12 or 24 months by signing up for the new EQ 12 or 24 month plan – and reap great savings off of the new rates:

Extend for 12 months for $109 US – and get a 30% discount off of the new monthly rates!

Extend for 24 months for $190 US – and get a 39% discount off of the new monthly rates!

Now is the time to lock in these rates, so don’t miss out.

All pricing plans (except for the 12 and 24 month extension plans) are recurring, meaning you will continue to be billed at the appropriate interval, until you cancel your subscription. Note that purchasers of the full game will receive their free month of game play after supplying valid billing information. If you cancel your subscription during the free month, no charges will be made to your credit card.

New York State and Texas residents will be charged sales tax.

All subscription fees will appear on your credit card statement under the heading "SOE*EverQuest".

Friday, March 22 2002

Planes of Power Live!
Georgia [3/22/2002-12:54:59 PM]  Comments[43]

The Official Planes of Power website went live just moments ago. And yes, I was refreshing all day ;)

For those that were saying it wasn't an expansion, you may please place your 20 bucks in the gnomish backpack right over there *points to her right* ;)

Here are some of the features listed by Verant for their latest expansion announcement:

  • Up to 20 new planar zones that push your teamwork abilities to the limit
  • Hundreds of new items and monsters
  • Exciting new quests and challenges await even the most experienced players
  • Two planar cities that provide hubs for travel and player interaction
  • A central storyline links the content from every zone into one compelling quest.
  • Further character progression making players even more powerful

From looking at the FAQ, you will find out that there will be one central zone similar to that of the Nexus, a zone that was released with the Luclin expansion pack, which allows players of all levels to enter and travel easily throughout Norrath as well as provide tons of quests for everyone. The planar zones, however, will require players to be at least level 46 to enter.

Details are slim at this time, but hopefully they will release more on what will be available to the younger leveled players.

An interview was also posted over at Gamespy regarding this new expansion.

Sounds sweet to me :) Go check it all out!!! Oh, and don't forget to drop the 20 bucks off at the door before you leave :P

 

Thursday, March 21 2002

Planes of Power, Round 2... FIGHT!
Georgia [3/21/2002-10:37:55 AM]  Comments[73]

Now that we've given you a couple of days to think about the puzzle that is spreading rapidly across all major EverQuest sites, how about some more pieces to add to the confusing web quest that we've all been placed in? :)

Back on /GU Comics the thread that started all this mania has had a recent addition! It seems as if another clue has been dropped for the EverQuest players to pick up and discuss. What is this clue you ask? Well, it's another mysterious picture of a Verant employee's office, with a larger printed version of Woody's latest comic, and an updated shot of the monitors' screens. This image contains not only Woody's comic, but also a shot of what looks to be the website design for Planesofpower.com. In the tradition of the other EverQuest expansion websites, this screen looks hauntingly familiar. Not only do their designs look familiar, but their URLs do as well. As posters over at The Safehouse pointed out, the URL for Planesofpower.com also has a sister URL: http://everquest.station.sony.com/power/index.jsp. It was also pointed out that the Screenshots and Features directories actually exist, for when you try to visit http://everquest.station.sony.com/power/screenshots.jsp it actually says "Directory Listing Denied" as in "Hey, guess what... this directory exists, but I'm not letting you see it yet *smirk*!"

What an evil thing to make me... err us wait for so long ;) I have to sit here for another 24 hours, twiddle my thumbs, and refresh the screen to make sure I'm not dreaming this all up :P

So far it looks to be an expansion, though no official word has been uttered from Verant's lips regarding this mystery. Others strongly believe it is the Pen & Paper version of EverQuest being announced, but why would Verant host it's own website on The Station for something being published and operated by another company? Curiouser and curiouser... :)

I'd like to thank Letifer's informative post on The Safehouse. Without his insight, I wouldn't have found these clues ;) Good digging around for clues Letifer! :)

For some reason, however, I feel like I'm in a Rat Race :P Hopefully Verant will offer some insight into all of these rumours tomorrow so we can stop busting arteries over it!
