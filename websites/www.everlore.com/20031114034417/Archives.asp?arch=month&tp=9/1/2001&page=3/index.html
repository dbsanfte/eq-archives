Yet another GM event on Tallon Zek
Kenadi [9/20/2001-10:00:15 AM]  Comments[5]

My Druid Kenadi and my significant other's Warrior Cappicard decided to do some hunting in East Karanas late last night. As we entered the zone, Cappicard hailed The Knights Eternal and was promptly summoned to the Druid rings by Aenedori who said there was a GM event going on. We quickly rushed over and found ourselves looking upon the smallest griffawn we've ever seen. His name was Little Feather. Veolae Avainor was his keeper. I couldn't help but comment out loud how cute he was and even gave him a hug.

Veolae said he was dropping "eggs" and that he was hungry, but she was running out of his favorite food (crackers) and didn't know if she could afford to keep feeding him. None of us knew where to find crackers, so we attempted to feed him whatever we had on us. At first he did not seem interested and would ignore our handouts. Veolae said he had never eaten anything but crackers before and didn't think he would try anything else. But, he was getting very hungry so we decided to try again. This time he was accepting food from us and we noticed a significant change almost instantly--he was getting larger! I kept feeding him my rations, but when I saw I was getting low, I tried foraging (my skill isn't that high yet) and summoning instead. I gave him some summoned bread as well, which he seemed to enjoy. He grew to be a full-size griffawn, and of course we all thought he was more deserved of the name Big Feather now rather than Little Feather (but it stayed Little Feather). It seemed as if he was still wanting more food, but he suddenly let out a big belch and returned to his previously small size again.

Three of us (that I know of) were awarded prizes. Little Feather gave me an opal, Aenedori received a sapphire, and Bvargo received the big prize (which he told me dropped from Little Feather when it went from small to big), a diamond (lucky gnome you :). All in all, it was a lot of fun. :)

Kenadi

No Trivial Loot Code For Luclin
Xanthus [9/20/2001-8:23:19 PM]  Comments[61]

Brad posted the following announcement regarding the trivial loot code for luclin:

Hello all,

I wanted to update you all on our current thoughts regarding the Trivial Loot Code as it relates to Shadows of Luclin zones.

As per my last couple of 'Ask Brads', we've made both an effort to explain what the Trivial Loot Code does and why we were considering it for all Luclin zones. I'd also mentioned, however, that if most players appeared to be against its inclusion we'd not use it.

After talking with players, reading emails and feedback, and then very recently running a poll, it is indeed apparent that the majority of our playerbase does not feel that the Loot Code's advantages outweigh its restrictions.

In fact, after polling over 200,000 players, most want the looting system left alone, almost as many want us to explore a different solution for bottom feeding and rampant farming, and a minority were in favor of the Trivial Loot Code as it exists today in the Warrens and Stonebrunt Mountains (the remainder either didn't have an opinion or were unfamiliar with TLC).

Well, so be it

We will head back to our drawing boards and keep brainstorming on this issue, but at this current time we do NOT intend on implementing TLC for the zones in our upcoming expansion, the Shadows of Luclin.

I do want to thank everyone for all the feedback and discussion regarding this issue -- there was a lot of debate surrounding this issue, and we've done our best to pay attention.

-Brad McQuaid
Chief Creative Officer & Vice President
Sony Online Entertainment/Verant Interactive

New Patch Message
Korenth [9/25/2001-9:54:02 AM]  Comments[7]

September 25th, 3:00 am

------------------------------

** Spell Changes **

- We have removed the changes to bard song stacking that were made with the recent emergency patch. We will be reviewing those changes for possible update in a future patch.

** Item Changes **

- The Shaman Epic effect has been returned to its previous effect (and it should conflict with fewer Damage over Time spells, as was the intention for the change made last patch).

** Quest Changes **

- Ivory Sky Diamond is now dropping as it should.

- Fixed an error in a Stonebrunt quest where an NPC was handing out the wrong reward.

** Miscellaneous **

- Added some code that might fix a bug with female hair and Erudite hoods that caused them to fail to appear sometimes when a helmet was removed. This would cause some characters to look as though the back of their head was missing at times.

- Mouselook will now work a little differently when in the first two third-person camera modes (overhead and chase). They will work similarly to first-person mouselook (i.e. your character's heading will change when you move the mouse left or right, the left and right movement keys will cause your player to strafe, and the camera angle will pitch up or down when you move the mouse forward or back). These changes should make those first two third-person camera modes more useful.

- The EverQuest Team

Firona Vie Server Explained
Xott the Wizard [9/26/2001-6:48:20 AM]  Comments[60]

The new "RP" server is explained on the Developer's Corner

Firiona Vie Server Feature List:

· This is a new server, not a split server. Nor will there be any transfers to this server.

· This is a PvE (Player vs. Environment) server, NOT a PvP (Player vs. Player) server. In other words, it’s a standard ‘blue’ server with the same rules and features regarding PvP as the vast majority of our servers. It is not one of the ‘Zeks’.

· Auction restricted to city zones: Qeynos, Surefall Glade, Highkeep, Freeport, Rivervale, Erudin, Halas, Neriak, Grobb, Oggok, Greater Faydark, Ak'Anon, Kaladim, Felwithe, Paineel, Cabilis, Kael and Thurgadin.

· Beneficial buff spells will not work on characters 20 levels lower than the caster.

· Very few items will be No-Drop. Exceptions will include things such as newbie notes and epic weapons.

· Each character will be assigned an "alignment" upon creation. This alignment will be described in brief on the character creation screen (in the same location the their Deity is described).

· Each character will be able to change their alignment once any time after they reach 10th level. The player will be able to chose from a list of alignments based on their race, class and deity.

· A character's alignment will determine whether or not they can group or guild with other characters.

· A character's alignment will determine an experience bonus or penalty when grouping with other characters.

· A character's alignment will determine if they can cast beneficial spells on other characters (including resurrections).

· Language skills will improve only through group chat in the same zone and from /say.

· Languages will only improve 1 point each hour and will be capped at level*5+5 skill points.

· Common will not exist. A Human language will be added and only made easily available to certain races.

· One character per account per server.

· The Trivial Loot Code will be in effect in all zones.

· Bind Affinity will be restricted for all characters to locations where melee characters can bind on other servers.

· /emote will be language specific

· All characters will be /roleplay.

· /alignment will display the alignment of your character and probably a more detailed description of that alignment
