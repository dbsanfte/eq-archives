I am a cleric, so I may be way off here, but my rogue friends seem to have a lot of trouble finding groups. I have to tell you. I admire you rogues. You obviosly didn't pick your class because it has many sought after abilities. Some people choose their class based on how powerful it is or how easy it would be to play it *cough* druids *cough*.
A rogues life is hard, but you would have to be a freakin IDIOT to say they were not useful. With that said, I would like to mention an idea that I have heard to make rogues more viable and wanted in groups.
It is an ability called "lure" that enables a tricky theif to bring a mob towards it (pull) WITHOUT aggroing it or surrounding creatures. This ability is not too powerful, but it would make some ppl say, "Well we will need a rogue to camp that". There would have to be of course the drawback: failure = aggro, and aggro all monsters in range.
Well, I am sure that no one at Verant cares about this idea, and rightly so, because they hear this all day: "My class doesn't have this" and "Necros are better because they can do this". Really, I get tired of people complaining about their class. If you think druids are more powerful than other classes (and being powerful is all you care about) then pick a druid... simple as that. But, as I said before, I tend to have a little respect for you rogues so I don't mind breaking my own rules to dream a little dream about how your lives could be a bit easier. But then again, maybe I might lose a tad (just a tad) of respect for you, knowing that you may have chosen to be a rogue for some powerful skill.
I hope you haven't heard this idea before.
And I hope I know what the HELL I'm talking about.
