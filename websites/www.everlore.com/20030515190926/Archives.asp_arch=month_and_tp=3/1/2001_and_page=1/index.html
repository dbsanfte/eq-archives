Two new episodes this week as the chapters "The Blood of Friends" and "The Lair Beneath" continue, so be you a newcomer to our tale or an avid follower please drop by a take a read . .

* The Blood of Friends Part 4 *

A great-carved platform stood out from the centre of the platform. Judging by the foliage arranged atop it, Safka could only imagine that it was the resting-place of the dragon herself. A series of ornate steps lead up to smaller man sized platform some twelve paces or so above the main structure.

“That must be some form of audience area,” Safka guessed, “so that you can speak to the dragon at it’s eye level.”

“Heh,” laughed Iste, “aye, I guess straining her neck to lean down to our level might get a bit painful.”

The cleric smiled and nodded, “Although I don’t think she has had too many visitors of late. Rumour of a place like this would spread like wildfire amongst the adventurers of Norrath had any found it.”

A pair of the smaller dragons swooped down in a swift arc and landed before them, immediately propping up on their hind legs and spreading their wings to their full extension, barring their way any further down the path.

By now Iste was not surprised to find that they talked in common speak. Half an hour before he might not have believed anyone who had told him of talking dragons.

“Sssssstate your business.” Rasped the first of the dragons, pointing at Safka with a curled claw.

“Your lady Gorenaire has invited us down to her home.” She explained bowing serenely, and tugging Iste’s arm to make him bow as well.

The two dragons looked at each other briefly before the one that had addressed them stepped forward.

“Invited you sssay?” it stated, looking them up and down, “ssstrange stature of guests our lady seeks out today, bessst get to the audience podium then you had. If you are lying ssshe will soon make dinner of you.”

The other dragon flapped its leather like wings excitedly. “Dinnerssss, yes we have not eaten today, Elf tastes good from whats I remember.”

Safka gulped and looked at Iste

“Buts nothing to worry you if you are indeeds invited.” The first dragon confirmed smirking, before turning to its brethren, “barbarian is too tough though, not as sweet as the elf.”

“Well at least I would give them indigestion,” Iste leant over and whispered to Safka.

*** Patch Day ***
Georgia [3/6/2001-4:06:28 AM]  Comments[80]

*** Patch Day ***

*General Changes*

-- Doubled the chance gain skill points in skills that are above 200.
-- Fixed a bug that disallowed casting of the Donal's Breatplate on others once casted upon yourself.
-- The spell scrolls for Grim Aura should now all reflect usability by SKs
-- Fixed a bug that would allow melee players to open a spellbook via /book or alt+b.
-- Merchants will now recognize Iksar as a valid race.
-- New bind locations have been added to three zones in the world - One on Antonica and two others on Kunark. NPCs at these locations can fill you in on the details. Good hunting.
-- The casting time for four cleric brestplates sporting the "Celestial Healing" effect has been increased to one minute. We understand that this severely limits the use of that effect and we are currently discussing alternate effects to place on those items in the next patch. We did, however, need to address the 300pt per tick regen that could be placed and kept on the group tank. When we've decided on the new affect, it will be applied to those items retroactively.
-- Numeric skill values will be shown on character skill display for skill values over 100. Values under 100 still show the qualitative identifiers.
-- Players have expressed concern about accidentally inspecting other players. You can now turn on or off your ability to inspect other players by typing /inspect.
-- Those of you with z-axis mouse wheels on your mice may use them over the chat window.
-- Fixed "Breath of the Sea". It was mistakenly set to be unresistable.
-- Same-zone evac-type spells will now cause you to reload the current zone. This is a necessary step to ensure that you are removed from all hatelists and clean up your pet.
-- "Voice Graft" will no longer show the spawn number (Jobaber00) of your pet.
-- You will now receive a message when you are being bandaged.
-- The tracking skill cap for Druids has been raised to 125. It was the intent for druids to be better at tracking than they were, however before the scroll bar, smaller skill numbers were often considered better. With the tracking improvements from the last patch, this increase was appropriate.
-- Increased the number of hitpoints healed by Celestial Cleansing.

*NPC AI Adjustments*

-- Level 60 players with the taunt skill will now have a chance to taunt creatures that are level 60 to 65. Previously, players could only taunt creatures below their level.
-- NPCs that are mesmerized or stunned will now only add a very small amount of hate when they see you cast beneficial spells on their enemies. Prior to this, they would add full hate when they saw you do this. This is why NPCs would always jump the cleric after breaking Mez.
-- Fixed a bug that would cause NPCs to add too much "temporary hate" when a player would sit down in front of them.
-- The maximum amount of "hate" that an NPC can add when witnessing a heal to targets above level 50 has been decreased substantially.
-- Hate from heals is also now calculated at a lesser number than the number of hitpoints healed. Previously, healing one hitpoint would cause one point of hate. Now, healing 3 hitpoints will cause 2 points of hate.

*Velious Music*

Velious subscribers should run the Velious Patch to pick up new mood music for some Velious Zones. Let us know what you think of the new compositions.

*EQGems*

A new "mini-game" has been added to the EverQuest client. EQGems is our first effort aimed towards allowing people to engage in different activities while playing EverQuest. Based upon the success of EQGems, we may explore opportunities for other mini-games in the future, even some that are multiplayer in and of themselves. Type /gems to play.

- The EverQuest Team

 

The Future of Questing?
Georgia [3/6/2001-4:09:48 AM]  Comments[123]

Absor comments on questing in EverQuest in this thread.

I wanted to talk to all of you about the way turning in quest items works. We are thinking about making a change here, and I'd like to talk about it.

Let me set up a fictional example. Let's say that there is a quest NPC that gives out the final item for the quest in exchange for three other items. Currently, if you give the NPC two of the correct items and a third item that you think is the correct item but isn't, the NPC will take all of the items and give you nothing. This seems like a rude thing for the NPC to do.

Also, if someone has come along and given the NPC one of the three items before you arrive, and you give him the three correct items all at once he'll give you the quest result, but the person that gave the one item previously will get nothing (the NPC is, basically, letting you complete the transaction that he started).

Both of these situations cause someone to lose out. That's not something that we like.

We've been thinking about making a change to the way this whole turning in quest items thing works. The most reasonable and eloquent solution to this problem would be to simply have the NPC give the items back if they are not enough to complete the quest. This would mean that if you give him one item, he would give it back to you and tell you that he needs more than just that item to give you what you want. If you give him two correct items and an incorrect item, he would give the quest items back to you and tell you that you made a mistake (he might or might not give you back the mistaken item, depending on the technical ability to get him to do that).

This would fix a bunch of problems with questing. But, as you all have probably guessed, it would eliminate the ability to 'multi-quest'. We know that the whole concept of multi-questing is a touchy one.

So I have a question for you. Would eliminating the problems with losing quest items to NPCs be worth the loss of the ability to multi-quest?

Alan

This is regarding the clerical Plane of Growth breastplates, the ones with the ability to cast Celestial Elixer (the Chestplate of Stability, Akkirus' Chestplate of the Risen, the Breastplate of Forbidden Rites and the Templar's Chestplate). For many of the same reasons that we made recent changes to the Donal's Breastplate, we are going to make some changes to these items with the next patch (which will be next week).

I'm sorry that these items are being changed so long after their introduction. But this is something that we feel needs to be done for the integrity of gameplay in EverQuest.

Alan

Player Letter to Verant
Xanthus [3/8/2001-10:51:51 AM]  Comments[19]

A long letter was written on the forums today that discusses various gameplay and customer service issues. Here is a sniplet from Absor's Post:

>"Many people who play your game are, quite frankly, addicted. I do not disparage them but this subset of your customer-base is largely unaffected by poor customer service. They will argue and complain, but do little else. However, when a suitable alternative to your product is available, they will likely shift their addiction in that direction. What about Ashron's call? Quite frankly, that game, from what I hear, doesn't hold a candle to what EQ was (pre expansions) It just wasn't good enough to peel away your customer base."<

Fair enough. I'll accept that there are customers (and not just customers of Verant, but just customers in general) that will be satisfied with very little customer service. I will also concede that there are a group of players that have already reached the end of their time with our game and will move on when a suitable alternative comes along. But I would argue that their reactions have little to do with customer service, and everything to do with the kind of customers they are (neither good nor bad, just their nature). Some people will move on to the next best thing regardless of what we do, and others have played themselves out on EverQuest. We do our best to retain these people, obviously.

>"I think the major issues with the game came when you expanded the characters beyond lvl 50 in an enviornment that was never designed to support it. You have major balance issues between melee and casters because of the wealth of melee items and the relative scarcity of both spells and caster items."<

I disagree. I think that the environment could support extended levels. Though I readily admit that the implementation of those extended levels wasn't perfect. That does not reflect the ability of the game to support such expansion. It only exposes the fact that we need to work more on making it work. We've already made several strides to reach that goal, and I think we'll have it all fixed up soon enough.

[...Open Letter to Gordon, Brad, and Alan.]

 

News and Daily Vision Posting Information
Strahm Grymblade [3/8/2001-1:48:25 PM]  Comments[3]

We will be adding this information below to the news FAQ page that will be available shortly. Below is a list of criteria that should be expected when posting your events. Remember, you can either submit your news through the site or through an email. The links are listed on the main page.

We accept news and information on the following topics:

General News - GM events, player events, contests, and general issues that are related directly to EverQuest and or a particular server.

Articles - If you have a guide on how to play a particular class, race, or trade skill, feel free to post it.

Annoucements - This includes people submitting anouncements from their own website.

We do not accept the following topics:

Huge Pictures - Anything over 100kb.

Word Documents, or Similar Attachments - We prefer plain text, without formatting. Our staff is quite capable of reading even Trollish tongue.
