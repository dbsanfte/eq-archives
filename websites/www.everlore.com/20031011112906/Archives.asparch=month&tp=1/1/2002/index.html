More Absor Info On Textures
Georgia [1/3/2002-11:22:04 AM]  Comments[2]

Absor further answers player questions regarding textures in EverQuest in the Sony Station Thread. Here is his response:
I'm not sure if this has been asked before, but why were/are the textures loaded over the network in the first place? It seems like a waste of bandwidth to have relatively static data sent over the network every time a client connects, as compared to storing it on client machines and updating it with the patcher. My guess is that it was done that way to keep disk space requirements down, since we didn't all have hard drives in excess of 20GB back when Everquest was first released. Am I right?

Whatever the answer is, I appreciate the addition of the texture cache. It's also amazingly cool that I have a way to pose this question to an authoritative source. Other commercial software shops could learn a few things from you guys. Maybe you should write a book. :-)


No textures are loaded over the Internet. It's all on your hard drive. They are, however, loaded into RAM, and that's what takes time. The process is something like this (and I might be wrong on some of the fine points...)

The data for the textures is stuffed onto your hard drive when you install the game, but they are installed in a compressed format (it's more complicated than just compression, but that's the basic idea).
When you load the game, these files are decompressed (again, not the right term, but close enough) and put into RAM.
It takes time to decompress these files. It also takes time to copy them into RAM.
The texture cache just decompresses these files and stores them in a format that can be copied right into RAM. But putting all that data into RAM still takes time.

So you're right, we are trying to use as little hard drive space as we can during installation. But all the textures on your hard drive, and they are indeed updated with the patcher and stored on your hard drive.

Heh, I might be authoritative, but I don't know much. Just enough to ask the right questions of the right people.

And I don't much like reading technical manuals, I sure wouldn't want to write one. :)

Alan

 

Randomizing NPC appearances
Korenth [1/3/2002-11:22:40 AM]  Comments[3]

I noticed an interesting post on the developer forums today where a user made a relevant request to the dev team:

mordea2 wrote:

Any chance for you guys to randomize the hair type/hair color/eye color for both:
a) NPCs
b) people without Luclin or people who haven't yet chosen their styles?

I've read how you have matching faces depending on what face you chose pre-new models which was a good idea, now applying it to the hair would be a Good Thing. :-)

It looks a bit silly to have the entire city of Felwithe full of identical red-headed ponytailed high elves. I guess it isn't really noticeable for other races because a more "normal" color was chosen (usually browns) but still.. not very realistic.

Anyway, it would be awesome if you randomized for NPCs and people w/o Luclin, for diversity and realisticness all that keen stuff. :-) or at the very least pick a neutral color.

Ok, I'll admit it, I'm partly biased because I am red-headed high elf by choice (I'm a firey wizard with an attitude, you see, so it fit me well), but it isn't so cool when everyone without Luclin (or more generically everyone who hasn't chosen a new model face, not owning Luclin or not) and ALL high elf NPCs show up exactly the same!

I don't really know about randomizing the look of folks that don't have Shadows of Luclin. I don't know how that is being done. I'll have to look into it.

Unfortunately, right now all the old world NPCs do indeed have default face and hair (if they have a new model – some are special models that don't have new model equivalents and you still see the old model – you can read about that here).

We do plan to go back and assign new faces and hair to those NPCs. But it's going to take some time, there are a lot of them to fiddle with. Eventually we'll make it so that you are once again a rather unique elf.

Sorry for all of you who expect to gain something more, but what were the Gladiators of Rome awarded with when they won thier fights? Nothing except the privilage of fighting again. Same rules apply here. The following are the rules for this tournament:

1. Any and all levels are accepted.
2. In order to compete, simply show up (in other words, no pre-registration) about half an hour to an hour prior to the event.
3. There will be seperate brackets for each level range. The level ranges go by fives (1-5, 6-10, 11-15, etc.). All brackets are single elimination.
4. No contestant can be buffed prior to his match, there will be 30 seconds before battle for this to take place.
5. If any contestant flees the PvP area during their battle, their DQed.
6. As with any event in the arena, behind the throne and the corner firepots are off limits.
7. After all tournaments have taken place, a large battle royal will take place between all willing competitors (thus, it is optional). Last one standing wins. Spectators who did not fight in the tournaments may compete in the battle royal as well.
8. This is the one you'll love...there are no restrictions on items or spells which can be used! (Therefore, you can use Fear spells or Fungis or whatever else there is)
9. Although you can't win prizes for winning, I MAY (key word is MAY) be able to arrange for a GM to broadcast the winner of the tournaments and the battle royal.
10. If you do not have fun during your battle, you will be DQed (j/k)

The tournament will be held at the Arena (off Lake Rathe) at approximatley 6:00PM EST on Friday, the 11th. If you want to compete, but you are having trouble commuting, we will arrange an escort for ya :). This tournament is sorta experimental. If it turns out to be a good event, I plan to host a tournament every Friday! So, if you want to have some PvP fun, come down to the Arena on Friday. If you have any further questions, send a tell to Sehhoie (my current main) on the Druzzil Ro server! Don't send a tell to Allanonne, though, he...err...doesn't like to be bothered :) And if I left any rule out, or you just want to make a suggestion, feel free to add comments.
