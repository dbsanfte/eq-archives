A Compelling Reason

This is NOT a class envy post. I just want to thank you, Gordon, for supplying us with the answer to the basic question of "what makes a class ballanced?" I, for one, think that you and Verant are using entirely the wrong yardstick to measure ballance, but at least I understand where you are coming from when you say class x, y, or z is in fact ballanced in spite of my opinions and observations otherwise.

I respect your opinion, and thank you for voicing it, however it is very important that everyone is aware that balance does not equal equality in all things. The pinnacle of a good class-based system is interdependence, and homogenization of the classes doesn't promote that.

1. Is there any available information as to how many characters of each class exist in Norrath?

2. Is there comparitive information as to the number of class-hours each class has since the game began (I know, this is a bit much, but I want to know if the 8.6% of the existing characters that are of class X are actually played or just hold twink items, etc.)

We have access to the information, but it's not something that we'd generally release in a tabulated form. One thing I should mention is that we had no expectation that the number of characters in game would be spread across the classes evenly. This can easily be illustrated on the group level. For instance, there is no point of diminishing returns when adding warriors to a group. In other words, the benefit to the group that one warrior adds can always be doubled by adding another. Some other classes can't do that. For instance, adding a second bard to your group will not do as much as adding the first one did.

-Gordon

Idea for class communications

Would it be possible, or difficult, to implement a way for members of a class to talk to each other over a channel (like guildtalk). I think this could be useful for players communicating with others in their class regarding quests, strategy, exchange of research components, etc. e.g. /class Anyone have any information on the spell call of bones?

Blanket communication channels tend to reduce the sense of community in game, rather than enhance it. Instead of dealing with a specific person, and forming a relationship with them, it ends up pretty cold. This is somewhat apparent with zone-wide communications such as /shout and /ooc, however it's not as bad as a server-wide mechanism would be. In addition to this, there are some technical issues surrounding this idea (e.g. the number of additional packets to broadcast) which make the idea unattractive.

-Gordon

For all those who had complaints about Kunark drops

With good random drops, casual players could get nice items. No more camping a cyclops for 3 days, or having to pray that no one in one of the 3 uberguilds involved on a dragon hunt needs one of the items. No more 10 hour Fear and Hate breaks, which made it impossible for casual gamers to advance.

The updates that we made to item drop percentages and locations were not directly based upon complaints from people about loot being too comon, but rather economic reports that we run to determine the health of the economy and the rates at which items enter the world.

Basically, once we got 100K people over in Kunark, we'd have found the problems regardless, so it isn't the "fault" of the players who brought the issue to public light.

-Gordon

Well we don't have any way that we could easily implement skill-increase potential based on what is in your inventory on a per-skill basis. I suggest you train the skill up a bit. The way skill increases work is that they require a successful skill check before they'll roll for a skill increase.

PLEASE show COIN AMOUNT looted!

Grouping gives no xp bonus? Please answer Gordon/Brad...

Is there a bonus in xp when grouped?

There is both an XP bonus assigned per kill based upon the number of people in your party, and it is also expected that you can take more critters in the same amount of time in excess of what you could do solo.

For instance, if a solo person can kill 1 blue mob every 2 minutes, then a group of two might be able to do 3 in that same amount of time, yielding 50% (plus the bonus) more experience per person per time unit.

-Gordon

Gordon, Proposed PvP Policy

If you are defeated in PvP in a zone, you are expected to retrive you corpse prompty, and leave the zone immediately upon looting your body. The loser shall not return to this zone for a period of at least 2 Real time hours. Conversely, the victors of the PvP combat shall allow the defeated enemy to "loot and leave" freely without being attacked. This policy protects the defeated character while at the same time allowing Victors some measure of zone control, and reward when they defeat an enemy. This is in response to the current phenemena of Naked (invulnerable to risk) Zone Camping. Whereas, a character binds himself in an enemy zone and while being defeated numerous times, can not be purged from a zone. On Valon Zek, this results in what is referred to as "Naked Item Ganking". Taking items from others, while presenting no risk to yourself. On Talon Zek, this results in the total lack of zone control, and is unacceptable on a Race Wars server. Failure to obey the "Loot and Leave' policy will result in disciplinary action as does any other infraction of the rules.

New policies have to have some way of being enforced, and I don't see how that would be possible with an artificial 2-hour rule.

-Gordon

The gameplay has remained almost exactly the same as in the original game. There are still static, non-random spawns, and special items are still dropped by specific monsters. If you never liked EverQuest, there is nothing here that will really change your mind.

The only big change comes to those who exceed level 50. Melee classes get Disciplines, which are specific abilities that are normally used during combat to increase the characters effectiveness in battle. Casters get new spells, usually a couple per level after 50. Also, classes after 50 get new titles. For example, a Cleric may become known as a Vicar or an Enchanter as an Illusionist.

Shots with projectile weapons are allowed or disallowed based upon the delta-Z (Difference in the value of the Z-coordinant) of your feet versus the creatures feet.
