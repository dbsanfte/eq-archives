Well this is kind of a tricky one to awnser. It depends on what kind of mob you are hunting and if you are going solo or in a group. You are probably going to get ten thousand diffrent posts and ten thousand diffrent awnsers, but this is the one that has always worked for me. This may be kind of long so bear with me.
Now with any stratagy listed below, if at all possible, find a "safer camping spot and tell you pet to guard there "I.E. /pet guard here".
SOLO:

Living:

1: Cast darkness
2: Run like hell back to your pet and send him into battle. (dont panic if your darkness spell got resisted.)
3: Cast heat blood, this is VERY important since you have had that spell longer, and it will not be resisted as much as Heart Flutter.
4: Cast fear on the mob.
5: Recast Darkness, it usualy will hold after you pet dose some damage to the mob.
6: Run after mob stacking Heat Blood and Leach on it.
7: This part is optional, you can A)Melee the mob, B)life suck it to death or C) Refear it and med.

Dead:

1: Pull with Darkness
2: Send in pet
3: Cast Spook the Undead, This part is important, do not and I repeat DO NOT use Fear, fear is highly resisted by undead mobs.
4: Stack the rest of your dots on the mob.
5: As with above, melee, life suck, or refear and med.

GROUP:

This one is tricky too so bear with me. Assuming you are with only one other person, (class unimportant) you do as above, find a "safer" place and have pet guard there.

Living:

1: Weather you or your partner pulls, cast darkness.
2: Send in pet
3: Cast Heat Blood.
4: Wait for you partner to do some damage or the mob will go hella agro on you and wont stop for NOTHING.
5: Cast your other dots and melee, life suck or sit and med.
6: DO NOT under ANY circumstances cast fear, unless your group is in danger, or unless you tell/ask your partner if he wants you to fear in non critical cirumstances.


Dead:

1: Cast Darkness
2: Send in pet
3: Ward undead the mob to death, the reason I prefer this approach is because ward undead dose 41 dmg to the mobs and uses less mana that any of your other spells.
4: Do not use spook the undead in this situation eighter as with above.

One a side note, if you partner is a druid/shaman, this is VERY fun. You get free SoW's and you run waaaaaay far from you camp, cast darkness, run, cast dot, run, cast dot, run untill it is dead/low on health and you bring it in for the final kill. Then you sit and med while the druid/shaman kites in a simular way. Necromancer kiting ROCKS!!!!!!!

Ok onto a full group, I am skipping the lesser numbered groups because you get the idea by now. With a full group make sure you have a healer type and at least 2 tanks. You and the healer sit and med behind the tanks.

Living and Dead: (yep they are both the same here)

1: Tank pulls, you ALWAYS send in pet, no matter what.
2: You cast 1 or 2 dots depending on the mob they pull. I.E. green agro mobs and blue mobs, dont bother, yellow 1 dot, red 2 dots. And not matter what they pull, always send in your pet. The rest of the time, you and the cleric are sitting and medding while drinking tea and eating english muffins and the tanks batter the poor mob to death.

If your lucky you will get an intellegent group togeather which will have no down time and you and the cleric/other healer NEVER get into the battle directly. I did this with a cleric, me, a rogue, a pally and a worrior. The cleric and I were lucky to have 40% mana when the mob got to us, but it was VERY fun. We never had to rest for down time unless the cleric was OOM and the tanks were hurting. I got soooooo much exp from that group it is not even funny.

Anyway, this is how i do it. Now just to let you know, no way is "safe". You will always run the risk of dieing. So eighter be close to a guard that will help, close to the zone, or have feign death memorized. Like I said WAY up there somewhere, this is only one way to do it, but it works for me and was taught to me by a lvl 50 necro on my server. It was nice to put it into practice and log on as a lvl 17, play for 4-5 hrs and camp as a lvl 18

Things change dramatically from 24 - 33, becuase of shock of poison (EGADS, a direct damage spell that does in the 150dmg range). Level 20 is a big disappointment (no new damage spells except a mana-innefficient drain spell and an undead dd spell), but 24 changes all of that.

When I wasn't in a druid/treant group, I soloed the birds in the aviak village in sk (except the avocets - couldn't do them until 28). The beginning is the same:

1) Darkness
2) Send pet in
3) Fear
4) SOP
5) SOP
6) Go back to 3 and repeat until mob is dead

Shock of Poison does a lot of damage at once, so as soon as fear breaks you know the mob is coming right for you. If I'm taking on low-blue rooks, 1 fear was enough for the entire battle. If I had a high blue harrier, I usually feared two times. If it was an avocet, I feared inbetween every sop.
