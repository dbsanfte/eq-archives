Isnt everquest supposed to be fun?

How many ppl remember everquest is actually a game?

Of course that's the goal, but as "fun" is entirely subjective, with each person having their own idea of what "fun" is, we try to provide as many different definitions of "fun" as possible in game.

There are certain ideas of "fun" that are incompatible with other ideas. For instance, some might think that it would be fun if they were provided with items, levels, skills, and abilities with little or no effort. While we think that some might think this is fun in the short term, we firmly believe that those things will be more fun, and fun for a longer period of time, if they are earned rather than given.

Much in the same fashion that sunny days aren't appreciated without the cloudy ones, the amount of fun in the game is related to other factors that some might consider aren't fun. For another real world analogy, I spent the last 8 years in Arizona. There, with over 300 days of sunshine per year, we learn to appreciate the cloudy ones.

EQ is very much like this. Without the rarety of items, the amount of fun that people have by acquiring items is reduced. Without some downtime, those non-stop periods of edge-of-the-chair excitement that everyone has experienced would not be as special. Without the difficulty of advancement, advancement would not be as special.

Fun remains subjective, and will be subjective for every game, especially in MMORPGs where the goal of the developer is to attract long-term subscribers. People will quit these games and go on to the next one when the fun that they get from playing it is no longer worth the things that are necessary for the fun to exist in the first place. Our goal of course is to make sure that it takes a long time for the average person to traverse that course.

-Gordon

Something I don't understand.

Why do you penalize the players so heavily time wise for dying. I know its your vision but why? Do you think it draws people back. It doesn't. What draws me back is the people and interaction why not enhance that -vs- penalize us for the occasional or not so occasional death.

This issue has come up in several threads over the past couple of days, many that I've posted in. I'd like to see them consolidate to make having a real discussion a bit easier :)

Anyway, your question above was, "Why do you penalize the players so heavily time wise for dying". Setting aside for the moment that I don't think that calling the penalty a penalty of time is appropriate since there is no "end" to the journey in the game, "heavily" is a subjective term. What one person thinks is a heavy penalty might be no-sweat for another. Since people have differing opinions on what these subjective terms mean, we set a value, and provide methods that allow the mitigation of the time involved.

The concept of risk and reward is very much part of the core of EQ. In general, one taking greater risks should have the ability to reap more rewards. Players determine what risk is acceptable to them when they play the game. The player who chooses to play without clerics or paladins around to do an XP rez face greater experience losses on death, but reap the reward of having less restrictions on the way that they play. Conversely, the person who chooses the reward of power-soloing to into the mid-50s will face the risk of greater experience losses on death since they don't have friends to rez them.

There have been a lot of posts here recently from people who have asked us to lessen the risks involved in the game without reducing the rewards. All the while, they have been in control of the amount of risk that they subject their characters to. ABASHI --- What is Verant's Official Stance on EQ Addiction, Excessive Playtime, etc?

Does Verant employ any professional consultants from any area like Psychology, Sociology, Psychiarty, etc to offer opinions on the matter?

Not specifically for that purpose. One of our game designers does have a degree in psychology, and most of our people have degrees, of which psychology and sociology classes are often included in the curriculum, but we don't purposely design for addiction, we design for excitement and fun, which are characteristics that some find addicting.

Does Verant plan to limit play time in any way, shape or form?

No. We provide a persistent world where one can feel free to come and go as they please. It is in the end the responsibility of each player, whether an adult themselves, or whether the account was purchased by a responsible adult, to regulate their own playtime to what works for them.

Does Verant reccommend a certain play time limit for its players? Does Verant believe that there is such a thing as excessive play time?

There is certainly such a thing as "excessive play time", but it's not a rigid value that we can set, but is more subjective in terms of each individual player. Certainly playtime becomes excessive when gameplay begins to interfere with responsibilities, every day life, and commitments to a job or family.

Has Verant received any complaints from parents of children that play EverQuest, or from players themselves regarding playtime?

Not to my knowledge, of course it wouldn't make much sense for parents of minor players to hold us responsible for a service that they pay for with their credit card.

Just like television, sports, and computer usage in general, parents are ultimately responsible for the content to which they allow their children access. Please play responsibly.
