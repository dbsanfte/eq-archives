Hail! I am not going to flame you, I figure that you have not played a druid, and to you it probably does look like we have everything.
Let's clear up some misconceptions:
1. Druids cannot tank. We can take a few more hits than pure casters, but not many. In order of tanking strength: Warrior, Monk, Pally/SK/Ranger/Rogue, Shaman/Cleric/Bard, Druid, Casters. Based on the fact that of all the classes every one of them can tank better than us, except for the casters, I wouldn't exactly say that we can tank.

2. We have a lot of spells, but none of them are great. Of all our spells, everyone one of them has a superior version of it that we cannot use. There is only one skill that we excel in, and that is travel. Even then, wizards can teleport and Shaman's can SoW. Therefore, it is not a skill left solely to us. Our root spells are horrible (look at the many other posts about this), Bards have a faster speed song, Necros get better Damage-Over-Time spells, Wizards get better direct damage spells, Clerics get better heals....see where I am going with this?

3. Druids have location limits. We are the only class that has our skills limited by where we are. We cannot SoW inside, we can only charm animals, etc. Put us in a dungeon and we suck.

I am not really sure why you chose to post this here, but hoepfully this will clue you in a little to the shortcomings of playing a druid.
