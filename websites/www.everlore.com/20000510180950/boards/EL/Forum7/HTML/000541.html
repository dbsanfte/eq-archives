My main character is a level 20 bard and is very challenging to play in the fact that there are a lot of differnt ways to play. All songs and instruments vs. weapons and melee, solo vs group, outside vs. dungeon. Everything takes a while to figure out the best strategy. I have been able to solo up to 20, though I prefer groups and try to group mostly. I hear it becomes more important to group later, but not necessary if you are willing to spend the time (read: die a lot) to figure out how to solo effectivly. You can do pretty much any quest with your high charisma and faction song. I've started many characters and keep coming back to my bard. Fighting can be slow, all of our songs take a while to work, but I have almost no downtime between fights unless I'm in a group and the casters need to med.

I also have a 12th level magician and he is also a lot of fun. I tried an enchanter as a pure caster, but he was harder than the bard to solo and it was hard to find groups at low levels. The magician is nice to play when I wnat to nuke things and have fights end quickly. Also I enjoy being a pure caster when the mood hits me.

So I suggest starting a magician and a bard and play them up for a while. Both are very different and you might find you prefer one or the other depending on your mood.

Aye, tis a hard choice....Marziale is a lvl 20 Bardiebabe and Vonda is of the 11th lvl mage.

I LOVE Marz, but mostly because I have friends to group her with. Many of the bard songs tend to agro creatures because you are casting on the whole group, so a good group is essential especially if you opt to put down your weapon and play an instrument during battle!!!

The bard gets a song every lvl, which seems to keep me going and gets me excited about lvling. You'll never run slow and you'll never lose your corpse. Though I know some who have gotten to higher lvls and said they feel like nothing more than a caster's battery with the mana song.

The Mage is great for soloing and I don't mind grouping her either, because I have the protection of the pet and if I am in a not so hot group the pet going down is my indication to RUN!

I adore my pets, they make so much possible and being able to heal them is a definate plus!!!

I guess it depends on what you are looking for...I play them both and play them in various moods though. If you want another character that can do a lil of everything go for the bard....if you want to spend time in your spell book and don't want to really tank anything yourself go for the mage.

Either way best of luck and I enjoy both classes.
