Just so you know Race + Faction + Charisma all come into play.

Charisma is a little odd, too.

As a Dark Elf Enchanter, My best prices are from Dark Elves who regard me Warmly, when I have my full Charisma Gear on (+ 24) and get Cast RV cast on me.

You still cannot rely on this to make a profit.

Fletching can be profitable, but first you need to gauge and test the market.

Talk to ANY ranger above 20th (/who ranger 20 50) and find out if he makes his own arrows. Chances are he will - but if he's a power gamer - he's not interested. Check his location and ask him if he's interested in your player made arrows.

Warriors/Paladins (Usually level 10+) Usually just use their bows for pulling.
Range + Damage is important for them. (But they probably won't be interested in Heavy Damage/long range/ damn expensive arrows)

Rangers, who hardly go anywhere without SoW - will kite with arrows - they usually want the best and will pay the price. Unfortunately, the majority of them are Fletchers too. (Even so - treat em fair and you'll get a regular customer) Especially if they will pay you extra to get them additional items and bring them to their loc.

Hope this helps a bit..

My friend Rhex, you know nothing of probability. I have read your other posts from this date, and I appreciate your curiosity and zeal. However, the advice you gave above is just plain wrong.

I am fairly new to this game (my ranger is 17th level and he was my first character), but I am not new to the laws of probability. I do not claim to know exactly what mixture of skill, wisdom, and intelligence add up to a successfull attempt. I DO know, however, that once you calculate that probability, it does not change until your stats change. If you have a 60% chance of producing something, and you fail 40 times in a row, you have a 60% chance of succeeding on your 41st try, provided your stats have not changed.
There's no such thing as being "due." This applies to all mathmatical probabilities. Don't get caught up in that "gamblers" mentality, as it will cost you in real life and in EQ.

You will realize this when you start trying to fletch the Trueshot, and I hope you listen to me so you don't have to learn the hard way. (Get a lot of supplies.)
