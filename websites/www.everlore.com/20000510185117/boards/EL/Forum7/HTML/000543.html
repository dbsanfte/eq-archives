I think what you are experiencing is due to two main design flaws in the game.

The first flaw is obvious: ovecrowding. More people than monsters, too little space, and not enough to fight. It does not appear the game was ever designed to support the number of level 30+ people out there.

Secondly, since the foes become tougher and tougher the higher level you get, it forces groups to be more and more picky about what classes they put in their parties. At low levels, it doesn't matter nearly as much what combination of classes you have. You just get a few people together, use the strengths of each class and presto...lots of fun and quick killing. Many levels later, group composition plays huge factor in success and failure.

Its enough to make you decide to go back and just twink a new character.

So, I don't believe problems with grouping are a result of any given class being group-useless, rather, its because the game has some inherent design flaws. I have no problem with Everquest encouraging grouping, but in the case of high levels it goes so way overboard that it hurts all but the most group-friendly classes.

Mages are in the middle range of desirable classes. You have a few classes that are unbalanced on the high side: enchanters, clerics, shamans maybe, warriors, maybe bards. Then you have most classes that are desirable in groups but nobody is going to shout for them specifically: Mage, necro, druid, SK, paladin, monk (might make the first group in certain higher-level raids). Then you have the undesirable classes: Wizards (although you have to have them for plane raids), rogue, ranger.

But I have never had any particular difficulty getting into groups, compared to most anyone. Sometimes nothing will do but a healer or a tank or an enchanter and so they get picked before the mage, but other than that, a well-played mage is fairly well respected, especially at higher levels, where the pets are simply awesome.

As far as life in general, mage is lucky in overcrowding situations. You indeed often have to wait for many hours to get a group in SolB or LowerGuk, especially in the higher-end camps. However, the inventive mage, unlike the warrior or cleric, can usually have a pretty good time soloing.

And actually, soloing in dungeons is a lot more FUN that being in a boring camp, because it is a lot more challenge. You have to be skillful or you will die too often to make soloing a positive experience, while in the frenzied room of LGuk or the king room of SolB, all you do is sit there and hammer away at an endless stream of mobs.

The most fun is getting named loot solo. If you are clever in SolB, you can on rare occasion kill a wounded named mob that has been trained to the zone, and has thus become ffa. (NOBODY is going to call you a killstealer if they have trained the zone -- and if they do, give them a royal butt-chewing. Everyone will be on your side.) I love that 100% chance in the random!

And in fact, I will say categorically, that a mage Level 42-46 can get xp faster in SolB solo (or with an appropriate partner like shaman or bard) than in a group. Similarly for a mage 38-42 in LowerGuk. And soloing Gornit in OOT is the best xp/loot in the game for a mage 44-45, as long as you have gotten a higher HP pet than fire.

I never die solo, btw. Well, er, almost never. (Gornit killed me once while I was learning how to fight him. But I got over 3 bubbs of level-45 xp killing him, and over 2000pp in the process. I also got nailed once in SolB when I got stunned by a sonic bat and then found the entrance to the zone blocked.)

One hint -- the biggest fear soloing in SolB or Guk is that the zone entrance will be blocked by some ogre and screw up your fine timing. So if there is a high-level train of LDC's/bats/beetles, you have to rely upon gate and get out while your pet is still alive.
