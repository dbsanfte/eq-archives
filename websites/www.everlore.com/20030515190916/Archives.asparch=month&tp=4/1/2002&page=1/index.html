Online EverQuest Documentary Reviewed by Film Site
Korenth [4/25/2002-9:22:13 AM]  Comments[5]

FilmThreat.com has released a review of a popular Everquest documentary. Here's an excerpt:
EverQuest Players LFG" is the latest work by Nathan Bramble, the talented young Pennsylvania-based filmmaker who has created an intriguing series of short documentaries celebrating seemingly normal people who harbor slightly off-beat obsessions. This time around, Bramble aimed his camera at the excessive devotees of EverQuest, an online multiplayer game which takes the role-playing faux-medieval fantasies of the old Dungeons & Dragons board game into the 3-D digital realm of cyberspace.

To be frank, I've never heard of EverQuest prior to viewing this film and I can't say that I am sufficiently intrigued by what I've seen here to seek out the game. As presented in this film, EverQuest looks like a digital arcade game on steroids: plenty of bulked up heroes running about dark passages in dangerous castles, chopping and socking anyone or anything that gets in their way. Players have the ability to give personalities to the various on-screen characters, but strangely they all seem like variations of a single theme: either the hero is a big galoot with brown hair parted down the center, or he is a big galoot with blonde hair tousled like a surfer.

You can find the full story here!

 

EverQuest Raid Management Tool 1.0 - Release Candidate 1 - Build 36
Korenth [4/25/2002-12:05:02 PM]  Comments[6]

We traditionally do not promote 3rd party software on Everlore but I think an exception can be mad for this program. Any large size guild will want to get their hands on this:

Download from: http://www.sincioco.com/eq/download.htm

EverQuest Raid Management Tool 1.0 (RMT) was designed specifically for raid leaders who need a tool to do the following:
Assist in raid sign-ups and group creation.
Manage loot drops during and after raids.
Export groups and loot drops as text files for posting to EQVault.

Historically track player's raid attendance.
Historically track player's loot awards.
Historically track all players who got a particular loot.
Please email comments, feedback or bugs to clerule@hotmail.com. If you are submitting a bug report, please include a step-by-step instruction on how to reproduce the bug.

Thank you for testing. Have a good day,

--Clerule

NOTE: Win9x users, you need to download the latest MDAC version from Microsoft at http://www.microsoft.com/data/download_270RTM.htm or you will receive database errors. Thank you Clawfury for reporting this problem.
As with any 3rd party software, there are risks involved with installation.  Make sure you virus scan any software you download and always keep updated virus definitions.
