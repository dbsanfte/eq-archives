Alan On Luclin Patching Status
Georgia [12/12/2001-6:37:44 AM]  Comments[8]

Alan updates the players over at the Developer's Corner regarding the status of all of these Luclin patches and updates in a recent post:
This is just an update to move several things from the "not working" to the "Fixed" category and add a few to the not working category. Also added a tech tip at the end of the post.

We're still working on some issues and we've fixed others. Keep in mind that we have a bunch of people working on things, so most of these items are on the top of someone's list.

While some of these issues only affect a tiny portion of our customers, we want to give a full and complete rundown of issues we are aware of. We will be keeping you updated as things change. Also, check the patch messages for more information.

Fixed since release (in no particular order, by the way):

- Bard songs and group buffs (these were related problems)
- Familiars no longer get added to hate lists and don't cause you to crash if you die
- Wolf form factions with the new models are now in line with intent
- Several face/hair issues, such as not being allowed to choose bald and some facial hair.
- /follow bug that caused folks to crash
- Transparency issues with i810 display adapters
- Crash bug related to root and gate
- Various item and content fixes
- Crash problems in certain zones
- K6 Processors: We're working to fix, or provide a work-around for problems AMD K6 users are experiencing when entering some zones. Shadowhaven is one of those zones.
- A bug that was giving classes that had the old experience penalties more alternate advancement experience than they should have been getting.
- Some people are seeing Vah Shir as male humans
- Working on getting facial hair tinting for the half-elves, dark elves, high elves, and dwarf females working
- Slow framerate on some fast computers
- Some players are crashing in Hollowshade Moor
- A few people that don’t have Shadows of Luclin installed are seeing "Entering Luclin" when logging in and then crashing
- A bug exists that causes random crashes when exiting game to server select, and then going back into game.


Stuff in the works, some of these will be fixed with the next patch (again, in no particular order, most are priority one for the person working on them):

- Reducing loading and zoning times
- Crashes occurring for some people when logging in
- Some characters being 'stuck' if game crashed while playing that character
- Large fonts and text that extends outside their boxes is affecting a few users
- Choppy, buggy, faint or flat sounds for some sound configurations.
- Some systems have mouse pointers which are being lost or duplicated
- Characters changing to wolf form look tiny
- Some spells (mostly 'bolt' spells) not displaying effects
- Faces that sometimes don't remain as selected
- New Bard Area of Effect songs are acting strangely

Note: Those without Velious should be able to access the Alternate Advancement interface by hitting the 'v' key.

Also note: The inability to adjust gamma setting with some cards is an issue with cards that just don't support that ability. It's not really something we can fix in code. We are investigating to see if there are any possible workarounds out there for these older cards that are no longer supported by the manufacturer.


Tech Tip

- Pauses with Win 98/ME ONLY: A possible solution for those with a fair amount of RAM (technically, anyone with 128 megs or more…) who are seeing pauses while the hard drive runs a bit, might benefit from this simple change (it's also easy to undo). It might also improve load and zone times.

Add the following line to the [386Enh] section of the SYSTEM.INI file: ConservativeSwapfileUsage=1

For best results, change only this setting (don't change a bunch of other stuff at the same time) and see how it performs. And if it isn't better or seems worse you can just erase that line from your System.ini file. I should tell you that this change might make EverQuest better and it might also cost you some performance with your system overall, though this does not seem to be the case for most people. Keep that in mind if you decide to use this tip.


Thank you all for your patience. We are working hard to get these issues straightened out as soon as possible.

Alan
Thanks Alan!

 

Wednesday's Patch Message
Georgia [12/12/2001-6:38:02 AM]  Comments[35]

Today's patch message (patch was at 12AM PST - 4AM PST):
  • Several changes to hair functionality: Expanded beard color choices for Erudite males, pushed back the camera a bit for human males when making hair selections (so they can see themselves) and fixed it so that the camera will no longer rotate around Erudites while in hair selection mode of character creation. Also fixed a bug with the left selection arrow sometimes not changing hair color.
  • Fixed the "Entering Luclin" message bug. This was only an errant text message, it was not causing any crashes or keeping anyone out of the game.
  • Fixed a bug where Vah Shir with an intelligence over a certain amount could not be created.
  • Fixed a bug with faction-affecting items: They were giving a bonus to all factions instead of just the intended faction
  • Fixed a problem with arrows and quivers that would result in the person being logged out.
  • Adjusted the cost of Combine Platemail to be inline with its stats and other armor available within game.
  • Made several gameplay changes to The Grey. A single group having the ability to simultaneously engage and prevail against 35+ experience-giving creatures is not in line with the desire for risk vs. reward and the promotion of balanced groups.
  • Removed some bad NPCs in Chardok.
  • Tuned encounters in The Fungus Grove, Maiden's Eye, and Dawnshroud Peaks.
  • Clarified some quest dialog in Sanctus Seru.
  • Tuned The Deep, also improved the variety of drops.
  • Improved pathing in The Temple of Ssraeshza
  • Improved pathing in Sanctus Seru
  • Improved pathing in Echo Caverns
  • Added some newbie beastlord quests in the old world.
  • Removed the "Lore" tag from the "Darkened Knight's Bracer"

- The EverQuest Team

Looks like The Grey was tuned heavily, so tread carefully when attempting to form your AE groups tonight ;)

    Useless Tech Trivia info
    Zackron [12/14/2001-12:47:28 PM]  Comments[54]

    Since it's Friday, I figured I would share with everyone, a little useless tech information for EverQuest.

    How many computers does it take to run EverQUest?

    That is an interesting question I got in the mail not that long ago. Unforatunatly the Video Card / Win95 stuff got in the way of me being able to post about it right away...

    If you ask any seasoned EverQuest player, they sould be able to tell you that there are currently 41 Servers that make up everquest that one can select to play on (or you can count them from the official everquest site..). However, I single computer can not run an entire server on it's own... It takes many "HELPER" or "ZONE" servers to assist in running the game.

    As of the Luclin release, all game servers now have a total of 33 of these zone servers. That means that each Zone server holds from 3 to 6 physical game zones (depending on how big the zone is and how much traffic is in those zones).

    When you do some quick math... 41 game servers... 33 helper zone servers per game world server... , and you end up with 1,394 server computers!!! That's alot of computers just to run a single game, and that's not counting the chat or login or patch or other background computers that are needed to make your game play possible...

    So, next time you are asking yourself why the server patch time is so long, remember that Verant does not just have to update 41 game servers, but they have to update 1,394+ server computers across multiple internet backbones! That's an accomplishment in itself!
