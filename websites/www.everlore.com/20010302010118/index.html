Official EverQuest Board Rules
Xanthus [2/27/2001-11:54:40 AM]  Comments[6]

Auric Flamewalker made a pretty lengthy post on the boards yesterday to clarify the official board rules:

Hail and well met all.

I have seen recently many questions regarding why a post was closed or deleted. The main reason we close or delete a thread is when it is an attack on one person or a group of people. Below are the guidelines for posting on this forum. Please read through this carefully if you post frequently on this board. Any posts that do not meet the criteria below will be deleted or closed. Any infraction of these rules can result in warnings issued to your Everquest accout, suspension or banning of your Everquest account, depending on severity of the offense.

1.0 Official EverQuest Forums
This section of the FAQ is primarily concerned with the Official EverQuest forums, and the conduct that is considered appropriate and inappropriate when posting to those forums.

Q: What is the purpose of the Official EverQuest forums?

The Official EverQuest Forums exist as a place for EverQuest players to gather and discuss issues related to EverQuest. Some forums (e.g. Customer Service, General Discussion, Quality Assurance) are visited frequently by official representatives regularly, and serve as a place for players to communicate with some of the various EverQuest teams (e.g. Development, QA, and Customer Service). These forums allow us, as developers, the opportunity to discuss issues that the players feel are important.

Q: Who may post on the Official EverQuest Forums?

In the initial implementation, "Customer Service / Technical Help" will be open to the public. While anyone may read all of the forums, the remaining forums will only allow posts from active EverQuest account holders.

Q: Why is posting to the Official EverQuest Forums restricted?

While we would like to keep posting open to everyone regardless of their account status, in the past, the official EverQuest forums have had some problems maintaining a suitable posting environment due to the relative anonymity of the posters. With little to no tangible reason to keep posting behavior in check, some posters took advantage of the anonymity provided by the forums to post often vulgar and otherwise inappropriate material on the boards.

As EverQuest is a family game, with people of all ages participating in the gaming community, we as developers have a responsibility to maintain a suitable environment to the best of our ability. The only way to accomplish this goal in regards to the Official Forums is to reduce the relative anonymity of the forums, thus allowing us to eject posters who post material that is inappropriate for public consumption.

Q: I'm concerned about security of my account. If I must log in with a valid Station Name to post, doesn't that give someone half of the key needed to hack my account?

No. Though you will be logging in (through a secure connection) with your valid station name and password, the station name will not be displayed. Instead, entirely within our local network, the Station login server will connect to the registered account database and obtain the "Chat Handle". This is a unique identifier that you chose when creating your EverQuest account.

All posts made to the board in restricted forums will display your "Chat Handle" rather than your "Station Name". Should you wish to change your Chat Handle, you can do so within the EverQuest chat server. Type #help within the chat server for a list of commands.

Q: What are the rules for posting in these forums?

As the Official EverQuest Forums are part of The Station, posting to these forums is bound by The Station's Terms of Service that can be found in their entirety at http://www.station.sony.com/services/tos.jhtml

In addition to these terms of service, we have a few supplemental rules that apply to the EverQuest Forums.

In general, you may post any material, written in a courteous and mature manner, providing that it is on-topic for the forum to which you are posting. This includes material that disagrees with the way that we, the developers, operate the game. We will not interfere with the communication of thoughts and ideas as long as the presentation is appropriate for all those capable of reading the forum.

You may not, however, post any material that:

  • Attacks or insults others on the board. Feel free to debate the idea, but do not turn your disagreement into an attack upon the poster.
  • Engages in name-calling, harassment, or threats. See above.
  • Contains obscenity, vulgarity, or profanity. Though we do have a filter in place to catch the unintentional obscenity from time to time, this is not a license to abuse it.
  • Disparages any religion, race, nation, gender, or sexual orientation.
  • Is considered inappropriate for children 13 or over as governed by general standards of decency in the United States.
  • Infringes on anyone's privacy rights.

Furthermore, you may not:

  • Make multiple posts on the same subject for the purpose of getting attention paid to your issue.
  • Post off-topic comments in threads for the purpose of drawing attention to another thread on a different subject.
  • Monopolize the front page of any forum by "bumping" threads of the same or similar content to the front. We may intervene by closing threads any time that this occurs.
  • Use the official forums as a staging-ground for creating general unrest within the game (e.g. Sit-ins).
  • Use the official forums as a place to advertise the sale of EverQuest characters or items in exchange for real-world money.
  • Advertise web-sites with content that violates these rules, the EverQuest EULA, or the Station Terms of Service.
  • Advertise products or websites that are not related to EverQuest or other Verant/Sony Online Entertainment products.

Q: What happens if these rules are broken?

Action that we might take in regards to a post in violation of these rules on the Official EverQuest Forums will vary depending upon the transgression. It could include any number of the following:

  • Editing of the post.
  • Deletion of the post/thread.
  • Closing of the thread.
  • Suspension (results in user being unable to play EverQuest for the duration of the suspension).
  • Banishment (results in the user's station account being canceled. User will no longer be able to access EverQuest, or any Official EverQuest Forum).
  • Legal Action (I.e. Contacting law enforcement personnel in regard to a threat.)

People who violate the rules of the boards may or may not receive any warning before any action is taken. In the event that a warning is given, the warning will be recorded upon the game account and may result in further action should the player show a pattern of engaging in prohibited conduct. Philosophically and administratively, there is no difference between conduct in game, and conduct in the Official EverQuest Forums.

Q: Ok, we know what we shouldn't do, what should we do?

Give your posts some thought, and try to be courteous and respectful. Disagreeing with someone does not mean that you have to be anything less than courteous in your dealings. Read over what you plan on posting before hitting the "Submit" button, and imagine how you would feel if the post was aimed at you. Would you be personally offended? If so, you should consider rephrasing the post or canceling it altogether.

Thank you for your time and thoroughness.

Auric.

Druzzil Ro Alliance Announcement
Xanthus [2/26/2001-6:21:43 PM]  Comments[6]

Received this from Banegrivm Shadowblight:

Greetings,

I wanted to take this time out to make everyone aware of the Druzzil Ro Alliance. What is the Druzzil Ro Alliance? The Druzzil Ro Alliance is a collection of Guilds on the Druzzil Ro Server that have formed together for the purpose of doing high level dungeons, planes, and dragons, without having the necessity of being in a high level guild. With such an alliance all the participating guilds remain in their present guild and are never forced to lose their guild tag. This way they are not forced into giving up their home or their friendships with those they have grown to know and love, and still get to play the game...on their own terms.

Their is now an option for those that want to be involved in the high level end of the game. No longer will people be forced to be in a guild with people they do not care to associate with on a daily basis. Now people will have the option of remaining within the confines of their guild and doing said raids and staying in their home without ever losing their guild tag. When raids are conducted no guild will ever demand any guild member from the alliance ever to disband from their guild for the purpose of the raid. All those in the raid remain tagged into their guild for the conductivity of the raid. No one ever loses their guild tag under any conditions where it corresponds to the Druzzil Ro Alliance and its operations with regards to raiding.

The Druzzil Ro Alliance has also come up with some base policies such as looting and simply working together as a team. The later will force guild leaders to be guild leaders and help to keep relations friendly between guilds. Not everyone will see eye to eye one hundred percent of the time, but that does not mean we can not be civil and respectable and try our best to bring about an honest and honorable resolve to whatever the situation may be.

In short the Druzzil Ro Alliance is in it for fun. We pay monthly fees a month to have fun, so fun we shall have, and what better way to do it then with our friends.

If you are a respective guild leader and interested in joining the Druzzil Ro Alliance please visit the forums at www.druzzilroalliance.cjb.net and read the looting rules under the General Discussion forums. If you agree with these rules then please leave a post in the General Discussion forums requesting to join and you shall be prompty added to the Druzzil Ro Alliance.

Please make sure you leave your website URL if applicable in the post so we may add it as a link. This way anyone interested in being in a guild apart of the alliance will have an easier time in chosing a guild in which they can call their home.

If you have a greivance with an alliance member and wish to enter your guild into the alliance please leave said greivance at the door upon entering. No one will hold anything upon any other alliance members. The past is simply the past and this is a new beginning for us all. As well, no one will be refused admittance into the alliance as well...so long as they agree to the loot rules. We shall endeavor in all things to enjoy our company, and our fellowship, and the game we have come to know as EverQuest. Here it is an even playing field, and for us involved it shall be our fun and our enjoyment of the game. To those of you who may be joining you are welcomed with open arms. May the gods and godesses bless you in the days ahead, and may the hunting begin.

Sincerely Yours,
Banegrivm Shadowblight
Sovereign Lord of the 1st Fist of Light
Member of the Druzzil Ro Alliance - www.druzzilroalliance.cjb.net
