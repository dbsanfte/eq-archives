Magical Kicks and Punches
Xanthus [4/6/2001-7:48:06 AM]  Comments[20]

Absor explained why some classes cannot use magical attacks via kicking or punching:

I was fighting willowisps a couple months ago to get some extra cash and another light stone. I had noticed that throughout my fighting career that the boots I wore determined if I could hit ghouls, wisps, etc with kicks. Regular bronze boots yielded the INVULNERABLE message, while my crafted boots, because they were magic, yielding connecting hits that caused damage. On the flip side later I wanted to work with my hand to hand skill since I am supposed to be a fighting machine as a warrior. I had on crafted gloves that were classified as magic, yet everything such as ghouls and wisps that I tried to fight gave me the INVULNERABLE message when I tried to punch. If kick is a secondary skill but allows us to cause dmg to mobs if magic boots are worn, why is a primary fighting skill like hand to hand not able to share the same effect? I haven't tried this in a couple of months because I haven't seen anything in the patch message to tell me that this issue has been addressed!

You're correct. Initially, fists were never hurting creatures that required magic weapons to damage. Special coding was added to allow Monks to cause damage to such creatures with magical gloves. It was not given to other classes because it is a special code for Monks.

Obviously the same thing could have been done with kicking as well, only allowing Monks to kick for damage with magic boots against resistant creatures. But Warriors use kick quite a bit, and so it was decided to let them kick for damage under those circumstances. Or it could have gone the other way. It could have been, as you suggested, that everyone with magic gloves could punch resistant creatures.

Either of those choices would have been more consistent, I suppose. But it's just a perk for Monks, really. It can easily be explained in game by the fact that Monks are skilled in the use of all their limbs as weapons and that's how they can use magical armor as a weapon. The Warrior only knows how to do so through practice with kicking and that he hasn't the skill with his fists to do so (and you'll find that it's probably true that few Warriors have much skill with Hand to Hand)

Alan

GM Event on Innoruuk Server
M.G. [4/7/2001-6:29:12 PM]  Comments[2]

A battered and worn group of adventurers arrived at the entrance of Dalnir in a flash, startling a nest of rats nearby. Some were seen congratulating each other for a job well done, while others were assisting the wounded and cleaning their weapons. A tall Erudite in a blue robe issued terse instructions to one of those present, and then disappeared in a flash of fire. The various party members paused in their tasks to wish him well in his travels and then turned back to their work when he disappeared. Off to the side, a gnome could be seen writing carefully in a small book. Every now and then she would pause in reflection, and then resume writing as soon as she gathered her thoughts....

"It could have been any other day near the evil outpost in Kunark. Auctioneers throughout the area were hawking their wares, poor souls who had been stranded in this strange land were calling out for assistance, and the Dragoons were happily wreaking much havoc among the adventurers aligned with the one known as Firiona. However this day was marked to be different, for as many people were settling into their routines, one man was beginning a quest for knowledge and power. A wizard in shining robes wielding a staff of unknown origins stepped forth near the Warsilik's Woods treeline and issued a call for assistance in his quest, and thus begins our adventure."

"The one known simply as Al'Kabor announced that he was striving to compose a new spell, however it was in a language he was unable to decipher. He believed that he would find something to aid in his research in Dalnir, but his assistant Sork Talvazon was late as usual. He pulled together what he called a 'motley group' of battle hardened adventurers who were willing to aid him in his quest. He was dubious at first, for many of us who wandered up seemed soft and untrained. However, not willing to shun any help offered, he was heard muttering, 'Let us go before I change my mind,' as he led the charge into Warsilik's Woods.

"Many of us shuddered when we discovered our final destination was the bowels of Dalnir, however we stood firm to the challenge and followed bravely. A few skirmishes ensued as we made our way to the crypt, but any resistance we encountered was put down quickly and efficiently. The most difficult obstacle to reaching the crypt proved to be the thick undergrowth and bushy trees. Several became lost and we feared for their safety, however it wasn't too long before we were all gathered together again next to the river. With high spirits, we marched to the forbidding entrance of the crypt.....

"Once inside, we worked our way through room after room in attempt to reach the entrance to the second level of the dungeon. We easily cleared the route, and prepared to venture deeper into the earth. One after another, our foes fell before us as we searched through the second level for the elusive information Al'Kabor so desperately needed. After several arduous battles, Al'Kabor announced that what he sought did not reside in that area. Many of us had never ventured this far, so we knew not what to expect, however the grim countenances of those who had been this far before did little to encourage us. We gathered our wits (and a high level enchantress) and prepared to follow Al'Kabor even further.

"Once we reached the final level of the crypt, the many corpses that lay strewn throughout the rooms were a grim testament to the ferocity of the creatures that were waiting for us. We had many foes to defeat, however we were strong in the face of all opposition and not one party member fell before the hordes of klys that came forth to do battle. We even earned the grudging respect of Al'Kabor himself after one particularly grisly encounter, for the only corpses present were those of our enemies. In the final room there were a pair of cabinets containing piles of ancient scrolls in many different languages. We gathered close while he rummaged through the worn documents, finally pulling out an extremely old and tattered piece of parchment, held closed by a dusty velvet ribbon. He pored over the ancient writings for a few moments, and then smiled and placed the scroll in his pouch. He had found what he had been searching for....." - Z~

The party members began to slowly disperse. Many new acquaintances and friendships had been formed, and all walked away a little bit wiser and their pockets a bit heavier as well. None knew the importance or even the contents of the document that Al'Kabor had been after, however they all knew that their journey had been a glorious success, and that hopefully they would be called forth again to wage battle against the creatures within Dalnir.

GM Event on Rallos Zek
Ramzzaa Omegadive [4/10/2001-9:43:52 AM]  Comments[7]

Me and some friends were hunting near the front door of the sarnak fortress in the Lake of Ill Omen when suddenly a named sarnak began to shout to the whole zone. It didn't catch his words but someone in my guild did and starting shouting GM EVENT.

A level 49 gnome cleric had been waiting next to us(healing us every now and then)and said he was after the sarnak that shouted. After a while I saw a large gang of Sarnaks heading our way with an equal amount of wolf pets following. At first glance I figured it was someone's sarnak train. I conned one, and it was deep red and had a name like Gorpuz Di'Zok. It tried to cast root on me but by the hand of Cazic Thule I resisted it and wow did I run. He only got one nuke off on me and it took out just about two bubbles of life. I got away but almost my whole group died. After I got away I began to shout for reinforcements.

As I surveyed the battle I noticed the gnome cleric and a high elf mage standing up to them. A higher level monk showed up as the zone started to fill. I switched to my wizard and began to teleport across the globe shouting for reinforcements. When I came back the number of players in the zone had more than doubled. By then most of the sarnaks had been killed and many players were sitting outside or inside the fortress looking for any survivors. After they were sure all had been dispatched everyone began to disperse. I stayed behind and spoke with some of the people still there. A shaman and bard told me they must have been around level 35.

No one released information on what kind of loot had been obtained. It was very fun since Rallos Zek doesn't have events too often.
