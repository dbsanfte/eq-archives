Who would win in a duel between a magician and wizard depends greatly on several things. The level of the duelers, the location of the duel, whether or not the mage gets to summon his pet before hand, and who has SoW or jboots.

At lower levels, the magician would wipe the floor with the wizard. Near-identical dd spells, but the mage has a decent tank to attack the wizard. At higher levels, when wizards get better dd spells (less mana and faster casting time than similiar-damage mage spells) the wizard has a much better chance at winning.

Location is probably the most important factor. In a small area like a city (or the Freeport arena, for example) the mage will win every time because the wizard cannot get away to nuke. This brings up SoW and jboots. If the wizard has either of these, he can easily outrun the pet in an outdoor area (like the karanas) but not in a closed in area. At level 50, for example, the wizard only has to run away from the mage for a while until he can get a spell off on the mage before the pet will reach him. Even if the mage has jboots or SoW, he cannot nuke nearly as well (if the pet gets left way behind the wizard will win every time). Lavabolt or other bolt spells are useless, as the wizard only has to run sideways to avoid the bolt.

Lastly, if the mage does not get to summon his pet before the battle, the wizard will win every time.

To summarize: In close places (or a firefight where they just stand there and heave spells at each other) the mage and his pet will always win. However, in large areas the wizard (who needs to have SoW or jboots) can kite extremely well and will almost always win.

My 2cp.
