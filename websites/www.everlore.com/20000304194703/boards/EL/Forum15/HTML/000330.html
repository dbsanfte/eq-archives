If you're coming from the Feydark area, I have some suggestions. I leveled from 11-15 off the Drake-Mino area in Steamfront, and the Orc camps in LFey. Tough to find blues in Feydark past about 14 though, so I suggest the Derv camps in Ro. At 18th, I still got exp off some of them. When you can find a good group, Oasis will really rock. You'll know very quickly if it's a good group by whether or not you're picking up your corpse. Watch out for the occasional Spectre train and wandering Sand Giant. Another great area is East Karana. If you bind in Rivervale or HighPass, you can hunt Carrion Spiders, Reavers, etc in EK. You can also go to Beholder's Maze and group hunt Minotaurs there atabout 15th. I used the zone level chart on Allakhazam's to pick out places to hunt. It's a great resource, and having travelled and tried many places; I can usually find good hunting no matter how crowded the server is.

I was reluctant (still am) to leave the continent of Faydwer for experirence. You probably would get better experience going overseas to Oasis, but if you want to stay "close to home", bound in a zone that is close to everywhere you want to fight (short corpse runs!), you might try staying on Faydwer.

I started in Unrest at 14th level, and you could probably do well in there if you have a magic weapon and well-balanced group. Death Beetles, Greater Skeletons, Tormented Dead, and Jack O'Lanterns are great so long as you stay against the wall and away from the house. Bards are particularly appreciated in Unrest for their Restoration song, because that lets a Cleric meditate after battles to do damage against nasty undead things, rather than blowing their mana to top everyone's health off. And Selo's Accelerando provides a quick escape for your party to the zone if a train is pulled over you.

Regarding fighting a green bandit and losing, don't worry yourself too much about it. All magic users in the game are seriously under-conned since they have limitless mana and cast almost constantly with little chance of being interrupted compared to players, and tend to be able to deal out a great deal more damage over time than a melee type mob 5 levels below them.

It didn't beat you because you're a Bard. Trust me. Even Warriors get slaughtered by casters constantly. The only thing that sets melee classes apart vs. green casters is bash/slam. At level 20 (I'm a Bard) I still dread those packs of bandit casters. You'd have to be one tough cookie to take on 4 of em, with half of em invoking lightning and the other half casting damage shields, ignite, and whatever wind spell they get at level 9. Just thank God they don't cast DoTs.
