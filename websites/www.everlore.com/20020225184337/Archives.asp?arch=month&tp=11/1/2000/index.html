Absor & Abashi post on Bard Song Changes
Catalyst [11/1/2000-11:35:14 AM]  Comments[1]

Alan and Gordon teamed up for a few posts on a Bard Song Changes thread. Below Alan's remarks are in Italic's and Gordon's comments are in Bold.

Bard song changes

I have some information about changes we will be making to a few bard songs with the next patch.

Songs with a damage shield component will have that component removed.
Pixie Strike will have its secondary resist reduced.
Song of Twilight will be singable at any time of the day. Its secondary resist will be removed completely. It will have a longer duration. And it will require an instrument.
The single target haste spells will be self-only.

We will also be revising the bardic epic weapon.

This should all go live with the next patch, which for now we are tentatively scheduling for late next week.

Alan

Absor, any comment on whether or not there has been a timer-esque quality added on Test that makes continually sung songs stop ("Your song has ended abruptly")?

If anyone can include a clear and concise description of the problem, we can look further.

-Gordon

Ooops, my bad.

It will be the absorbtion component that we are removing, NOT the damage shield component. Sorry, my mistake.

Alan

Yes, self-only means that there will be no targeting, it only works only on the bard.

Alan

Just a follow-up on this "song ends abruptly" thing that was reported from Test. We spent a good deal of time today looking into it, and were unable to reproduce any spontaneous ending of the song.

The only time we were able to get a song to "end abruptly" was when the bard was stunned. The message wasn't displayed to the bard, but to people around the bard.

I know that Absor was going to look at it tonight from home on a 28.8K modem to see if it's latency-related, but baring that we'll probably have to call it until someone else can offer evidence on a way to reproduce it.

Here's what we know, no intentional change has been made by the programming staff in regards to this. The programmers are also very confident that no other changes made would create this problem. As of now, I've only seen one person with first-hand reports.

We'll of course be open to hearing from anyone else with insight on this issue.

A few posts from Absor and Abashi
Catalyst [11/1/2000-7:17:20 PM]  Comments[25]

Last night's events...

I've noticed that there are a bunch of threads on the events last night. Some people enjoyed themselves, others didn't. I think that this is pretty much going to be standard for virtually everything dynamic event that happens in EverQuest.

We cannot have a situation where we provide ample warning for everything that is supposed to be a surprise in game. That surprise is a big portion of what makes these events exciting. If we were to start giving explicit warnings before things happen, the target zones would immediatly be innundated with people ready to take part when an event is launched, and the objective of the event would be overcome in very short order.

Yes, characters die in these types of events. They'll even lose experience if they are killed by an NPC. It's part of the game, and part of what makes EverQuest exciting.

As mentioned by Bill Coyle, one of our game designers, the characters who die in some of these events are the victims who need to be saved. Whether you are a victim, or one heros who overcome the challenge, you are still part of the story.

-Gordon

Alan

It does. The example in your post is not a large enough model to see the difference. You could certainly proc 5 times in a row even with minimum dex, but the chance is far less.

Remember that previous successes or failures have nothing to do with the chance of future successes or failures. The fact that you just proc'd does not make the chance of your next proc any closer or further away, probabiltywise.

Regarding the random number generator, people think that because streaks happen that there's some problems. Random does not mean equal and regular distribution over the course of the sample. In any 10 flips of a coin you still have a .1% chance of never having it come up "heads". The fact that you may sit down and flip a coin 10 times and not get "heads" doesn't mean that your coin flip isn't random.
