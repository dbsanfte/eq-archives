If you want a cute little guy, be a gnome.
If you want an evil guy, be a DE.
If you want a really cool looking guy, be an Erudite.

The truth is that once you get past level 20 it really stops mattering so much what race you are. Currently as a non-twinked level 15 necro, I have a 175 int -- as much as anyone at my level would have or more. By this point, your race matters very little. My high agility doesn't affect me much since I'm not ever meleeing... just as an Erudite's slightly higher intelligence doesn't matter much since an Erudite at my level probably doesn't have much more intelligence than I do.

Part of the reason I have so many good int raising items is that Ak'anon is without a doubt the best starting point. I played in the Steamfont mountains from 1 - 12 and made over 400pp looting guards and helping out higher level players who kill the guards. I also found my guild that way. Ak'anon is just a fun place, it's as simple as that. So if you like the idea of being a 1.5 foot tall runt who can kick anyone's butt, be a gnome.
I liked the idea and wanted to try a smaller character because I think it's fun being the little cute guy.

If on the other hand you want a badass looking necro, be an erudite. I think they look awesome .

Necormancer race doesn't matter a whole lot (as long as you can get your int up to 200). I recommend either dark elf or gnome. Both have natural night vision (infra for gnomes, ultra [the best] for dark elves). However, gnomes are a little more accepted (not in their own town, but everywhere else) than dark elves. Stats a pretty identical (one more int for a dark elf at max, gnomes have better agi/dex...doesn't matter).

However, there is one real advantage to being a gnome - actually two. One - the newbie zone is the best in the game in my opinion. The steamfont mountains have plenty of stuff to kill, two kobold camps which are good up to 8, a skeleton graveyard nearby, and the higher newbie mobs are seperated nicely from the lower newbie mobs.

The second reason is the guard campers. At first this may seem bad (no help with that skeleton on you), but it can actually help your financial standing. In addition to a fs weapon and ringmail armor, the guards drop a regular sword and shield that go for a little over 1pp each. Most guard campers leave them on the guards, so you can snatch them up (or ask) and sell them for 1pp each to the druid near the newbie kobold camp.

My level 4 gnome wizard (who I gave up to start my current necro) on lanys tyvnal made almost 100pp by level 4 just picking up shields and swords off the ground and off the corpses. I even went in a got the campers food/water, and usually got a piece of ringmail or fs weapon in return for a stack of each. Be nice to them and you'll be rewarded (unless they're like me and leave only the rusty stuff on guards).
