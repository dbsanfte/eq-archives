More on Ragefire Changes
Georgia [1/24/2002-5:39:25 AM]  Comments[0]

Absor spoke up on clerics' concerns regarding the changes being made to the epic quest:

First let me just say THANK YOU on behalf of the cleric community. I already have my epic, but I'm very happy that future clerics do not have to endure the painful and dreaded Ragefire camp.

I only see one problem with the change, and that is on most servers Kunark dragons are usually killed very quickly after spawning. If Ragefire indeed spawns and remains with his previous loot table, he will be "farmed" for Cloak of Flames and other great loot, making the quest even less attainable for clerics not in first tier guilds.

The way around this, I believe:

a) Keep Ragefire's dragon form as is (like Nagafen) and not Talendor (I hope I have the right dragon)

b) The loot is removed completely from him except the container

c) The container is NO DROP/NO RENT until combined with the pearl, when it becomes NO DROP only

This will clear up most issues with the changes and would certainly accomplish what you are trying to achieve!

a) I don't know really how the appearance of the NPC is a factor. And, well, I don't actually know what he'll look like.

b) We'll keep our eyes on that. Right now the loot hasn't changed, but this is something we'll keep our eyes on. Remember, it works both ways. If there is no good loot for your friends to gain for helping you with your epic, then they might be less willing to help.

c) The container is indeed no-drop. I don't know about the no-rent thing. That seems like a bad idea to me. How unpleasant would it be to have your cleric go link-dead during the battle?

In any case, we'll see how this goes.

Thanks for the efforts! Can you consider making it necessary to give the swirling pearl to the merchant in Skyfire for him to spawn the killable version and then the pearl returned (much like the Faydedar trigger is now)? This would ensure a cleric would have to be ready for this step to get the heart and would avoid the dragon being farmed for loot and twink clerics who may one day do the quest. It would also avoid grief players killing the trigger and gating or killing the dragon even out of spite.

This is one of the things we were trying to prevent. There is a real problem with the whole triggered Ragefire thing. It becomes a problem when character A hands in the trigger item and group B kills the NPC. Not only is character A out of luck for getting his item, he's also lost the item he needed to trigger the NPC in the first place.

For most epic quests this hasn't really been a problem. But for this one it has. The tremendous demand for this one item has caused people to do things like jumping other's triggered spawns and all sorts of other nasty things. Short of removing the item from the game, there really isn't any way to prevent that behavior short of paying someone full-time to watch people at this one encounter.

There probably isn't a perfect solution here. Let's see how this one works out.

- Alan

Now, imagine yourself as a cleric in guild A, turning in your pearl, and way across the world the killable version decides to spawn and guild B kills it, and you're out of luck. Hrmph. Doesn't look like this change actually solved anything... just makes clerics have to camp two times instead of one. We'll have to see. You can discuss it on our Cleric Forums!

Brad McQuaid and Jeff Butler have settled on a name for their new company. Here's their latest press release.

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

"EVERQUEST" VETERANS BRAD MCQUAID AND JEFF BUTLER FORM SIGIL GAMES ONLINE, INC.

SAN DIEGO, CA -- January 22, 2002 -- Brad McQuaid and Jeff Butler, formerly of Sony Online Entertainment and key figures in the development of the popular "EverQuest" series, today announced the formation of Sigil Games Online, Inc. The new company is dedicated to the production of massively multiplayer online role-playing games.

Sigil's official website is set to launch this spring, and the company will begin adding designers, artists, and programmers to its staff later this year.

McQuaid and Butler are both creators of the massively multiplayer runaway hit "EverQuest," developed by Verant Interactive. The game shattered all multiplayer game records after its release in 1999, exceeding all industry expectations and inspiring three top-selling expansion packs.

McQuaid is President and CEO of Sigil, and Butler has assumed the role of Vice President.

Ragefire to Undergo Changes!
Georgia [1/23/2002-5:49:38 AM]  Comments[10]

Remember way way back when I wrote an editorial about a Ragefire incident? Well it's about darned time that cleric's concerns regarding their epics were heard. Here's what Absor had to say about the upcoming changes:

Normally this isn't something I'd do. But, well, I'm going to outline the changes we're making to this quest in rather close detail. But first, let me try to explain the concern that players had with this quest.

With arguably the most popular Epic Quest item, the Cleric trying to gain his Epic item usually finds plenty of people to help in the process. Clerics are commonly the first in line with guilds to help with their epic quests. So Clerics tend to get further in their epic quest faster than many others. This has lead to a very nasty 'bottleneck' for Clerics trying to complete the quest. The Ragefire portion of the quest has been creating a lot of static among players.

So we have decided to make a change to the end portion of this quest. To reduce confusion and to help people understand why we're making this change, I'm going to give out a few specific details. These changes will go Live with the patch tonight.

For those that are not interested in quest details, consider the rest of this post SPOILER INFORMATION:

First, the 'merchant' version of Zordak will now appear at a different location in SoldungB. He will appear once each day (except after a server comes down, then he will spawn in one to three days). The appearance of this NPC will no longer require the absence of Lord Nagafen.

The cleric still turns in his Shimmering Pearl to Zordak. But now the merchant Zordak will return a Swirling Pearl to the Cleric and disappear.

At that point the Cleric will need to head out and look for him. A killable version of Zordak will appear independent of the pearl turn-in every few days (except after a server goes down, then he will appear a little faster). Killing Zordak will transform him into dragon form, and the Cleric (with help…) must kill him immediately.

The new dragon Zordak will drop a heart that is a container that requires the Swirling Pearl to be combined in it to create the Heart that is turned in to Omat.

The rest of the quest remains the same.

The goal of these changes is to reduce the problems with the bottleneck that existed before. They will not remove that bottleneck, but hopefully will remove the nasty competition for a single spawn with so many people willing to be… rude to each other.

- Alan

Necromancers Questions Answered
Georgia [1/23/2002-5:39:16 AM]  Comments[3]

Insidious Retrogression - our 49th level DD/Snare/Debuff appears to be allowing mobs to attempt a resist each tic.

That's how snare components work. Nothing new about the way this spell works. Multi-effect spells do tend to break earlier.

Chill/Ignite Bones - the illusion effect of these two spells are blocking the Dragon/Giant Bane line of the Wizard class.

That makes sense, considering that they change bodies when you use those spells. Giant and Dragon bane spells won't work on creatures that are not Giants or Dragons at the time. Not much to do about that except get the other spells, which you mention below.

Incinerate Bones - is our 44th level non illusion version of Ignite Bones. This spell drops off a named mob deep in Chardok, a 50th+ level zone. Is it me or does a 44th level spell dropping in a 50th+ level zone seem wrong?

Conglaciation of Bone - is our 55th level non illusion version of Chill Bones. This spell drops off the Royals and some Dragons in NToV, all of which are 60th+ level. Why is a 55th level spell dropping off mobs 5+ levels above the spell?

These are reward spells, variants of old spells created for those that want the variety. Making them harder to get is part of the plan. And soon enough these spells will saturate the higher levels and become available for purchase by those unable or unwilling to gain them personally.

Both of those spells where meant to deal with the raiding problems of Ignite/Chill Bones but they where implemented in a terrible way to keep them out of our hands.

Actually, they were meant to be special variant spells that a character could earn. The fact that they are considered 'better' spells should also explain why they are harder to earn.

Quivering Veil of Xarn - please remove the illusion effect of this spell, then it would stack with our lich line and not conflict with illusion effects we might have up. It is not much of an emergency spell if I have to check to see if anything is going to block it. Until the illusion is removed it will never be as useful as our 20th level Harm Shield.

Illusion spells cannot be overwritten, which is why Veil of Xarn will not overwrite Lich form spells. While I understand the desire to make it simple to use this spell, in the long run it's those that can use their spells the best that make the best casters.

Defoliation - Why is it that the Shroom mobs in the lower levels of Old Sebilis and The Deep are considered Plants but the ones in Fungus Grove and pretty much every other zone are not? Can we please finally have this spell work upon plant mobs.

I'll mention this to the team.

Horses - it appears that their are several stacking issues with horses. We can no use any of our Lich line spells that have an illusion effect nor can we cast Ignite/Chill Bones while upon a horse.

You can only change into one of the given PC forms on a horse. You can't turn into a spectre or any other undead on a horse. This is due to the way horses work. You'll just have to get off the horse to cast those spells.

Shissar Deathspeaker Staff - This staff come from Veeshan's Peak and has the right click effect of Soul Well. Soul Well is an HP drain over time but unlike our other HP drain over time spells it does not stack with any Lich line spell nor Envenomed Bolt.

That is probably as intended, but I'll mention it to the team.

Pet Life Tap procs - have been broken for months now and really needs to be fixed. When you consider how few HPs our expansion pets have these are often needed to keep our pets alive during fights.

Looking into this one again right now.

- Alan

Edward Castronova sends this note:
Hello,

Some months ago a large number of Everlore readers were kind enough to participate in a survey about the economy of Everquest. Many of you asked to see the report when it is ready. You can now get a free download through the Social Science Research Network at

http://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=294828

(Scroll down for the "Click Here to Download Document" button.)

I would like to thank the entire EQ community for supporting the study. Let me stress again that all of your responses have been kept confidential and that the data have not been used for any purpose, commercial or otherwise, other than as background information for this report. Also, please be aware that the intended audience is a group of academics who have never played EQ, so some things may seem trivial to you. You may find the report somewhat outdated in certain respects as well, since it is based on information as of Summer 2001. Let’s just say that the world of EQ moves a lot faster than the world of academic publishing.

Nonetheless, I was able to construct a series of macroeconomic indicators for Norrath, which show, among other things, that the GDP per capita is higher than that of China and India; the PP-to-dollar exchange rate is higher than the yen and the lira; a typical person can earn about $3.42 an hour by hunting in Norrath and selling the loot; and the deflation rate is almost 30 percent annually. People in the EQ community already know some of this, of course, but the report confirms these facts with hard evidence. It also offers some microeconomic analysis to explain these trends, as well as an overview of the MMORPG market as a whole. Colleagues who have seen the report have basically been astonished at the vitality and richness of Norrath’s society, especially when so many “real” societies seem to be locked in a malaise.

I would once again like to thank the EQ community for supporting the study. If you ever spoke kindly to Jakwes, Wilfrid, Khaaro, Maaya, Perceval, Naana, Uwila, Kipure, Qigl, or Comolan, you helped me write this report – thanks.

Sincerely,
Edward Castronova
Associate Professor of Economics
Cal State Fullerton

Thanks!

The GUILDWARS of the Gods
Banegrivm [1/21/2002-5:49:44 AM]  Comments[9]

The GUILDWARS of the Gods, is based loosely off of the recent writings of "The Codex of War" which contains various precepts of Norrathian History, located at www.1stfistoflight.com off of the Forums link, under "The War of the Gods".

The GUILDWARS of the Gods will be taking place only on the Firiona Vie Role Play Server. It is a running storyline that started between two guilds and their allies; the 1st Fist of Light and the Fist of Hate. They are nemesis guilds created for the intent of fighting one another in an evolving storyline through the use of creative guildwars that will have predefined rules and set conditions that will apply to them.

Examples of such rules will be:

Death rules, when your dead your dead and you do not re enter a fight. To help add to a sense of realism. This also includes no attacking dead people who are dragging corpses to a designated Graveyard (See Below).

Rules of area engagement, I.E. fights will be set per area. If you flee the area you are out of the battle and cannot re enter the combat area until its resolved.

Graveyards, where the dead can be drug to and looted so that they may then flee the combat area. When you loot and are dead in a Graveyard you must then flee the zone.

These are just a few examples of some of the rules that may be implied during the course of an engagement. Of course you also may not know when there will be an engagement. After all, that trip to go save someone might end up with Guildwar being thrown in secret beforehand and you just never know what exactly is going to be happening.

The entire scope of this evolving multi-guild server wide guildwar shall be engineered in a fashion similiar to that of a Dungeons and Dragons type game. The storyline will unfold based around character backgrounds and histories as they become relevant and exposed through the course of gameplay and interactions upon "The GUILDWARS of the Gods" forums located at www.1stfistoflight.com

This will be a war that spans across the Firiona Vie server, with multiple battles, that all take place, nestled within the shadows of the Prophecy of Trakanon and the end times of Norrath. All those interested whom are interested and currently not in a guild or involved in an allied guild should "sign up" for "enlistment" at the forums under "Recruiters Hall" and specify which side you are interested in fighting for. Those guilds that wich to particpate and not allied yet should also post within the "Recruiters Hall" as well.

The first battle which shall to tie into the storyline shall be "The Battle for Takish-Hiz". A small sample of what is to come, and yes folks it is a guildwar fought INSIDE a dungeon. All events involved with the war as they transpire shall be maintained upon "The Sages Scrolls", found upon the war forums. May the heavens shake with thunder, and ground tremble, for the fate of the world lies to the dogs of war.

Banegrivm Shadowblight Sovereign Lord of the 1st Fist of Light Firiona Vie
