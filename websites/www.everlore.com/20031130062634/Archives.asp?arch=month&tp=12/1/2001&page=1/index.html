    Win95 and GFX Card Update
    Zackron [12/10/2001-6:57:55 AM]  Comments[134]

    I wanted to take a moment to update everyone that is no longer able to play EverQuest due to the fact that they are not able to upgrade to DirectX 8.1 as a result of still using Windows95 OS, or using a video card that will not post video drivers that are DirectX 8.1 compatable.

    Over the last 5 days, I recieved a total if 602 e-mail messages from concerned EverLore members in regards to Win95 issue, or Video Card issue. Out of those, 13 were people were complaining that we should not worry about Win95 / older video card users, and that they should get a life. The rest were very valid concerns and voices of disapointment on Verant's poor handling of this situation. Even now, they continue to falsely advertise their software as being Win95 compatable, when it is not. (see SOE Online Store).

    However, many of the e-mails also indicated that they were in the process of upgrading to another OS. Between those users, the users that have been in game and have agreed to the license agreement, or those users who have newer versions of the retail box which say in very fine print (I had to use a doulbe magnifying glass to see it on mine...) that system requirments may change without notice... The avalibility of legal actions in this matter is very slim. One good thing Verant has working in their favor is a very good legal team, who know the art of CYA very well.

    At best, through the mis-guided signals, and Verant's unwillingness to propery inform the EQ player community ahead of time of these changes (Verant did know of this many weeks in advance, since the boxes and CD's had to be printed a long time before they went to stores), the handling of this issue falls in line with many past blunders on Sony Online Entertainment and Verant's part, this added to a history of consistant and repetitive displays of a total disregard to the customer in many regards.

    With that being said, we have come up with one option that players who have Win95 or a no longer supported video card, and who will not be able to upgrade, may be able to take, and that is to contact EQ Customer service and see if they would be willing to give you a refund for the remander of your subscription time, especially those who have multi-month subscriptions.

    If any of you do take this course of action, in contacting EQ customer service, and they refuse to assist you, please send me an e-mail copy of the letters that you have sent to you and they have sent back rejecting your request. I'm sure that some of them may make great front page news for the Lore site, demonstrating SOE/Verant's continued lack of concern for their customers.

    Those that have contacted me via e-mail in regards to this, I will continue to keep informed of any additional options that may become avalible. Until then, I will go ahead and do what Verant and SOE are unwilling to publically do, and appologize to all the loyal EverQuest fans who have been with us through the years, and whome will will now be loosing. Good journies to you all!

    - Zackron
    - X-EQ Guide
    - RPGLore Admin

We are pleased to see verant take these steps to ensure future customers are properly informed of their product's requirements, and hope to see Verant take a more proactive approach to these issues in the future

    Patch Message
    Georgia [12/10/2001-1:30:47 PM]  Comments[15]

    Ok, chop my head off and serve me to some Iksars why don't you :P I'm a tad late putting this up... sorry:
    Patch Changes

    Fixed a problem that resulted in excessive swapping for people with particles turned off.
    Fear spells should again be working correctly.
    AoE fear spells will no longer work versus players on PvP Servers.
    Fixed a crash caused by a familiar dying.
    Fixed a bug with the "Alternate Advancement" system. An artifact from the removal of the class-based experience penalty several months ago resulted in hybrids gaining AAXP much faster than they should, and warriors and rogues gaining it slower. AA-point cost was designed to be the same regardless of class.
    Increased the experience cost per AA-point overall after analysis of point accruals accross the game.
    Corrected several zone-crashes. Also implemented a workaround to prevent the Hollowshade crash while we investigate the problem further.
    Fixed several miscellaneous bugs that were causing crashes or had the potential to cause crashes on varied systems.
    Fixed a bug that caused some people to crash when reentering the game after exiting.
    Addressed a good number of content-related issues (quest problems, bad items, etc).
    Luclin Install

    We found that the default install directory for Luclin may be wrong for some people, specifically those who have copied their EverQuest files into a directory different from their initial installation.

    Also, we've received a number of calls from Luclin subscribers who've tried logging in to their character on a friends computer that does not have an install of Luclin on it. This will not work as the files necessary for you to view Luclin content are not present unless Luclin has been installed.

    We've added code to point out both of these cases in the future.

    Continued Plans

    As we wrap up the technology-related issues over the course of this week, making sure that everyone who wants to play EverQuest can play EverQuest, the programming team will shift their focus to aesthetic issues such as animation speed/timing, armor tinting, face-picker operation, etc. We do indeed plan on getting to all of these issues in the very short term.

    The content-team, however, is hard at work on content-related issues right now, and stands ready to address any broken quests, items, encounter-imbalances, etc., that you may run across in your adventures. Please feel free to visit our forums off of www.everquestlive.com and submit a comment through the Developer's Corner.

    Thank you for your patronage,

    - The EverQuest Team
