Like with all the tradeskills, I think they're far more fun than they are profitable. posted 01-11-2000 09:58 AM           
Fishing rules, and you non-roleplaying types can go back to Quake-ing or whatever. When I was younger, I fished for free eats and re-salable junk. Then I fished to pass the time waiting for painfully slow boats. Now I'm fishing to make fish wine, which sure does sound disgusting, but raises my brewing skill very cheaply so I can get better at making the more expensive Heady Kiola, which makes Cured Silk Armor. And ask some monks and mages if they think CSA is useless; I think they'll say no.

And like someone else said, sometimes after I've just gotten wiped out or if I'm tired, fishing can be relaxing and chill me out. When I'm trying to think about what to do with my character next, its easy to just sit there and fish at the same time (and practice skills, as others say - personally I practice Hiding). I also get into good conversations with people while I'm standing there, sometimes, and end up giving junk away to people who need it.

Trade skills can be rather unprofitable, in terms of making money, getting better things to kill with, and whatnot. However, I've made tons of acquaintances and some friends in the process, and I seem to receive many more fun and interesting gifts from these people, and when they see me in trouble they come running. Within EQ, I have a life outside hack n slash, and it pays off, as a better gaming experience, as better role-playing, as more fun, and even materially. Fishing in particular is an easy and fun way to have a non-combat skill that has some fun rewards. It's also entertaining to see what junk you might come up with (although I think they should expand the variety of junk items you can find - why not?).
