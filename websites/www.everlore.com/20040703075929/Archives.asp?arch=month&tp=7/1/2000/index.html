Saturday morning news posts from Abashi
Catalyst [7/1/2000-11:19:14 AM]  Comments[10]

A couple of exciting new changes that we are working on.

Hello all,

I thought I'd drop a line and let everyone know about some exciting changes that we are working on:

First, the next EQ client will, unless a problem is discovered in testing, include a slash-command that will allow players to turn off display of corpses. This will be a client-side option that will just repress the rendering of the corpses themselves. This will allow corpse-related actions (like sense-corpse) to be taken even while corpse display is repressed.

The command (/hidecorpses) will have three possible parameters:

None - Shows all corpses
All - Hides all corpses but that belonging to the player.
All but group - Hides all corpses but those belonging to the player and group members.

As asking people "Has anyone seen my corpse?" is an issue that we feel builds the feeling of community and encourages people to help each other out, the command will reset to showing all corpses upon zoning. Also, as a note, in its current implementation, any corpses created after the command is run will be shown.

This command should help people who experience framerate problems after zoning in to areas known to cause a large number of corpses.

The second change has to do with quested keys for entrance into the keyed dungeons on Kunark: Sebilis, Charasis, and Veeshan's Peak. It's apparent that the risk of dying in these zones is too great to risk going with only one key. Furthermore, it's become standard practice for people to leave a corpse at the entrance of these dungeons with the spare key.

As we feel that the idea of dueling and getting killed just to leave a corpse for a bank isn't in the best intrest of roleplaying or our vision for the game (e.g. It's cheesy), we've decided to nerf this practice :) Our plan is to have keys for these three dungeons stay with the owner when they return to their bind point. This new ability that we are working on also gives us the opportunity to explore "soulbound" items in the future, but no promises just yet on that.

-Gordon

Brad/Gordon -- Straight Answers Required, Account Hangs in Balance

Brad/Gordon -- Straight Answers Required, Account Hangs in Balance

As much as I'd like to be able to give you firm answers to your questions, either pro or con, it's not realistic for me to place hard limits on what we may or may not be able to accomplish. I'll answer your questions to the best of my ability however.

1> In light of this statement by Abashi/Gordon, are enchanters ever going to have the ability YOU PROMISED ENCHANTERS ON YOUR OWN WEB PAGES [before editting *most* of these statements off after I brought them to your attention] to enchant weapons to cast spells?

Giving enchanters the ability to directly enchant weapons at this point is highly unlikely, as the core gaming engine does not support it. More likely might be enchantments that the enchanter could cast on people in the group that would make them proc. However, though the latter is more likely, enchanters are balanced and anything that we do will have to take this into account. We're not actively looking for a way to make enchanters more viable. They are already considered essential by most groups and well-played, make awesome soloers. Furthermore, they've got the market cornered on jewelry as it is, giving them the ability to permanently enchant items would give them yet another market to corner. Neither is necessary at this juncture for the enchanter to be worthwhile, and expanding upon the abilities might reduce others dependency on other classes.

2> If you are not going to fulfill your promise to enchanters and they are not going to be able to enchant weapons to cast spells, will enchanters be compensated for this by allowing them to assist in the production of magical armor & weapons that don't cast spells? For example, by giving the trade skills a massive overhaul and making mana vials part of a large number of magical armor & weapon items. If so, what is the timetable?

This is far more possible than the first question, but again not guaranteed. The mana vials were created for this purpose, so all that really needs to happen is for the weapons to be created. Should we decide to implement it further, items made will not be better than items found through adventuring. They will probably be powerful enough to help equip characters who've lost their corpses, as well as those who don't rampantly camp for items and choose to have fun instead. Since this issue is not on our current list of projects, but rather on our wishlist, I can't give you a timetable for implementation.

3> In light of this statement, am I incorrect in believing that Tinkering will never be made viable or otherwise improved?

This is the same thing as #2. We'd like to improve all of the trade skills at some point, but it's hard to say when it will make it on to our current project list. However, I can say that this issue has been spoken about recently here in the office and is probably closer to being made a current project than many things.

Video Card Information Guide
Catalyst [7/2/2000-3:53:39 PM]  Comments[8]

Here's is a small guide I've put together to help people find their proper drivers and additional information about their video cards. As EQ and it's expansion the Ruins of Kunark requires a pretty hefty machine to run properly, I am sticking to 3dfx and nVidia, as these are the most popular and best performing cards out there currently.

  • 3dfx: If you're looking for information on 3dfx and it's current line of products, their homepage is a great resource for information. http://www.3dfx.com. 3dfx cards are also the "recommended" card to play EQ with as they can run in Glide mode. In Glide mode the game looks a little crisper and the game engine runs a might bit faster than in Direct 3D.

    If you're looking for drivers for your 3dfx based card, whether it be the very ancient Voodoo 1 or the brand new Voodooo 5 or anything in between 3dfxGamers has the drivers for it. They even have newer Windows 2000 based drivers for those early upgraders, and of course Windows 9x drivers as well.

  • nVidia: nVidia makes the TNT, TNT2, GeForce and GeForce 2 GTS line of video cards. Among the most popular cards out there on the market currently, nVidia makes a great chip; a highly overall recommended card. Full information about the cards can be found on nVidia.com and the official drivers can be found via this link here.

    If you're looking for those nifty leaked drivers that are floating around on the internet, Reactor Critical and 3D Chipset are a great place to find them. Although I recommend being a bit of a tech head before using leaked drivers and such.

    A great GeForce FAQ was put together by the guys at Tweak3d.net to help people solve those problems that come up with their card. www.tweak3d.net/faq/. Many solutions to help anyone having problems with their GeForce. The troubleshooting area's of the FAQ are excellent, and the best that I've found on the internet. Again I recommend you have a decent working knowledge of PC's to do some of the steps listed in this guide, and as always use at your own risk.

    Lastly if you're looking to upgrade but can't find good prices, you can try and buy online at PriceWatch.com. They cover just about everything you can buy for your computers, so if you're considering an upgrade of any kind, they're a good place to check prices at. Enjoy!

    Catalyst [7/3/2000-11:23:57 AM]  Comments[3]

    karana/bertoxx split: are we guaranteed to move this time?

    In the past, server splits have always had a limit on them.
