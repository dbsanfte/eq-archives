If it took really long to get to 24th with your wizard, it'll take even longer to get to 24th with a bard. For instance, I have well over a month of playing time now, and I only hit 25th last night.

First, you will have to find a group that is ALWAYS on at the same time you are, and that group will have to be bard-friendly and know how to utilize a bard to his or her greatest potential. You will have to LIVE with that group for the rest of your bardic career, because that is the only way you will be able to fight -- in groups -- and strangers don't like grouping with bards.

You will probably have to get used to losing levels.

Bards cannot solo easily and they will not level quickly (at least until they get their charm song, maybe... I'll check back in a couple of levels). Unless they're powerleveled, and if you've got someone to powerlevel you, then you can just ignore all of this.

I'm not saying that wizards solo and level easily -- wait a second, yes I am. A friend of mine who plays a wizard fought solo in Mistmoore from levels 24 through 30, (managed it in about a week) whereas if I solo in Mistmoore, I die in my first fight.

I think you will get frustrated with a bard's weaknesses quickly. We're not really a fighting class. . . We don't do a lot of physical damage, nor can we take a lot of physical damage. Meleeing means we get stunned. When we're stunned, we can't sing. When we can't sing, we can't regenerate our group members or sing Selo's to get the hell out of a fight. And if we can't do that, we're useless.

I think you should play a ranger. Or a warrior. They're easier, and easier to level, at least up to 20th. Or play a Shaman -- they get Spirit of Wolf at 9th level, if speed is your thing, and Shamen come from pretty sturdy races if you want to melee.

But if you want to play a class that lets you roleplay a lot, and if you want to risk life and limb in situations where your peers won't have a hair on their head harmed, and if you're patient and willing to play a class that is really hard at times ... play a bard.

A lot of truth in this thread, but I can see a difference between my experiences at 31st and the sub-20th Season bards who have posted here.

Yup, before 20th, a Bard is super-songweaving second class tank that can run fast. However, at 20th Level, the damage cap comes off, and warriors, rangers and shadowknights gradually start pummelling for 100 a shot, while rogues are backstabbing for the same amount. Meanwhile you're plugging away with your combine or your PGT or whatever for possibly up to 14, 15 pts per hit. More to the point, the mobs you're fighting are hitting for more and more, to keep up with the tanks' damage and the casters' spells. That's why you'll be switching to instruments more often than not.

After about 25th, when you're grouping with up to 35th level people comfortably (and getting exp too), you'll be asked by every group you join to 'sing the mana song' - something you only get at 32nd (and thank goodness by then they've realised that Jig O'Vigor doesn't affect mana at all)!

Soloing has been covered already: it's easier to solo two yellows than one white once you get charm, but pick your opponents carefully. If they stun you a lot, you're toast, since charm only lasts a short time and you need to keep renewing it. Personally, I don't attempt it these days, I can't heal up enough between charms.

Bards are good fun to play, and they truly are the life and soul of any party. However, they are very difficult to play, and you need to constantly change tactics from group to group and as the group members change in order to remain effective. Their range of abilities is very broad, and therefore you really neeed to think about what songs to have learnt, and what to have in your bank of six combat songs. Any bard that has the same songs learnt 24-7 (after a certain level, of course!) is not doing his group the most good.

As for best race, I like my wood elf bard a lot. He starts with forage and hide, and can see in the dark too. He can wear small armour which weighs and costs less, which offsets the 5pts less strength than half-elves. Half elves have infravision too, and have a slightly higher dexterity than wood elves, which is a bonus for avoiding missing notes (fizzles)! Definitely a choice between those two, I would guess.

Hope that helps!
