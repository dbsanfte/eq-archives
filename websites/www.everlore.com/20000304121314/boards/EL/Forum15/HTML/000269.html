All things considered, I really like my Half-Elf Bard. I enjoy playing it, and after having started one other character, I immediately noticed one thing. I miss the speed and the ability to regenerate HP so nicely and so fast. I really do.

There is one serious downside to this. It is hard to group with a Bard, at level 14, and the people that are grouping now can only think of Tanker of Thrower (caster) and not realize how we can augment both of those. Unffortunately these are the same people that we will be grouping with 10, 20 and 30 levels from now, so it appears that we may have to wait until Level 25 or so, before these players realize how helpfull we can be in any of the three forms that we come.

At the higher levels, almost everyone I have talked to has nice things to say about Bards, which specifies they would love to have them in a group in a minute.

Thus, if you can stand having lapses in the teens and in the early twenties, where you may have to solo and force-learn kiting so you can solo, until some other players realize the potential and the help that we can in making them level ... but I have to admit that I am having a hard time learning it, since Bellow only doing 5 or 6 damage points is not going to help much even after 15 minutes of the critter chasing you at level 12, or so.

Still, I love it a lot. Just wish others would understand the hard work I have dedicated into it, ie: all my skills are top'd off (75=14x5+5) and I do not miss too many songs starting, and only two people have died in all the groups I have been a part of ... one a reckless group in Crushbone (we all died because members would not stick together), and Enchanter yesterday in N. Karana (group of 3 only -- not advisable) when they pulled a momma grif by accident. Other than that I have been able to hold up every single group I have been a part of, by paying special attention to the HP bars, and making decisions as to which songs to twist depending on the critters. Have been very successful in Oasis protecting group from Rabies and Poisons, for example, but unffortunately it has bypassed a few people's notice ...

Still, after starting a warrior already in Level 5, I do MISS awfully, the speed and the strings.

There are only three Bards, I think. The two elves seem to be about even. I don't remember why I selected the Half Elf rather than the other two other than night vision. I have a hard time with dark monitors already, and this was going to make it easier, or something like that. Still have to run my monitor on a lighter mode than normal, as the gamma correction distorts the colors way too much.

I really like my Bard. The only thing that is bad is that it is difficult to group and I am now starting to become a "group master" in that I find solos and group them to me and next we know we have a bona fide group. If you shout and advertise, no answers get to be a bummer after a while.

I log in, look at the player list and do tells until I find a group and then join, or go after one myself.

There are inherant difficulties in this character. The songs are not helpful, until Accelerando and Restoration. The Chant is supposed to be a help, but it matters not much in that early level. If becomes more efficient when you group and come to level 6, 7 and such, when you notice that others are getting a sizeable boost.

In the early levels you gotta tank (what else is new), unless you can get smart and find two other newbies and group with them. You'll just fly to 5 in no time. After Accelerando I recommend doing mail runs for fun (Buthcerblock/Kaladim/Felwithe to Kelethin) since you can do a whole level without handling a sword here. At 14, I still do mail runs, since any boost, however small, is a gain. Basically I figure that it is going to cover my death at least once in each level, which is expensive to your total experience in gaining a level.

The only other suggestion is to make sure ALL your skills are top'd off, including swimming and fishing. Swimming will be needed later and you got to be in the 100's with it, and fishing, because it's free money and food when the skill is top'd off. The general advice is that you save your Practice points since later on you get Skills that you MUST have proficiency in them "now" and you can not afford the misses.

I play a secondary chatracter, and let me tell you , I really miss the speed and the restoration ... that's how addictive the Bard is.
