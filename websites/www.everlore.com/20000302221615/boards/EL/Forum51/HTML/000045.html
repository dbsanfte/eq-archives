Just thought i'd let ya know...

Though the bows with silk and linen strings have better delay and are harder to make, if you want to sell the bow (as most of us do), people want bows with better damage. This is, of course, a generalization, but one that i have found to be repeatedly true. I had a friend who made a carved elm recurve with silk string. This combination forms the fastest bow in the game. However, the bow only had 11 damage, and it took him considerably longer to sell his bow then it took me to sell my carved elm which had 13 dmg, but more delay. In summary MAKE BOWS WITH MORE DAMAGE IF YOU WANT TO MAKE MONEY.

A note on speed vs damage...saw a post ehre on Elm Silk being the fastest in the game and how everyone wants damage more than speed. This isn't exactly true, newer players who don't know any better may want more dmg, but the older ones prefer speed and range more than they do damage.

As for using the book..it does help keep your failures down and increases your chance of success...having it opened will increase your odds for success...sort of like taking an open book test in school .

Speed vs damage depend of the usage. Warriors, Shadow Knights, rogues and Paladins if they don't have root will certainly want the highest damage bow possible using the highest possible damage arrows since they can only get one shot at a creature BUT, they might want to keep a fast bow with nice damage and a whole bunch of cheap arrows with them also in case they can team up with someone that can root and snare. That's what I'd do if I was to play a warrior.

Rangers will usualy try to get the Trueshot bow anyway One of the best(not the best) speed vs damage bow(and far less expensive than the best bow) for them and it can get enchanted later(I'm not sure I like the magical version that much though...I'm not sure that the 100 more range is that much usefull but it has stats modifiers at least).
Anyway, a Ranger should always go for the best speed vs damage bow for the root/snare and shoot combo

I have one question though: If I hit a willowisp with the RainCaller but using normal non magial arrows, will it be immune?
If not, the Raincaller would be only good for stats modifier...I would really prefered a speed modifier than a range modifier from Trueshot to Raincaller.

Also, it is always a good idea to keep the fastest possible bow at hand as a practice weapon to max your skill faster(the fastest is 39 delay and made by bowers)
