Hail one and all, the Norrath Geography section of Everlore is almost completed. We wish to thank those who have contributed greatly to the progress of our maps section. Within this section of Everlore, one may explore Norrath by simply pointing and clicking on the zone links on each map that is shown. You will then jump to the desired map. Desdinova [6/2/1999-4:22:34 PM]  Comments[0]

INVASION OF KERRA ISLE ---------------------- WHAT: A mass gathering on Kerra Isle, to give those Kerrans the thwacking they so richly deserve. Kerra Isle hasn't been explored en masse like Blackburrow/Crushbone/Paw because nobody goes to Odus and because Erudites with any sense head to Antonica at around 4th-6th level. While people like to gripe about having 40+ people in Blackburrow, the fact is that all those people keep the spawn down to a manageable level so that you can walk down to the main cavern without getting greased. This does NOT happen on Kerra Isle for the reasons cited above, and so it's a particularly deadly place for low level characters. I'm hoping to change that by getting it packed with folks, just like Blackburrow. If enough people show up, the low-level players can hang out near the bridge, the more advanced players can swarm the dock area, and the 12th+ level characters can explore the depths of the zone. This will keep the spawn down and the trains to a minimum. N.B.: Kerra Isle is NOT the small island in the Erud's Crossing zone. That island is referred to either as Wisp Island (because it has lots of wisps) or as the Isle of Erud's Crossing (because when you enter the zone, it says "You have entered Erud's Crossing"). Kerra Isle is a zone that is reached by swimming a short distance out from the Western coast of the Toxxulia Forest. It is called Kerra Isle because when you enter the zone, it says "You have entered Kerra Isle." And because Brad McQuaid says so. :) SERVER: Innoruuk WHEN: Saturday night, June 5th, 7:00pm EDT (4:00pm PDT) SO WHAT'S KERRA ISLE?: Kerra Isle is the "dungeon" zone for the continent of Odus. I NOTICE YOU HAVE "DUNGEON" IN QUOTES. WHAT'S UP WITH THAT?: Well, Kerra Isle is not a dungeon in the traditional sense of the word. It doesn't have lots of twisty passageways and it's not underground. SO WHAT MAKES IT A "DUNGEON?": Because it shares the characteristics common to the other dungeons on Norrath: Swarms of low-level MOBs, interspersed with higher level MOBs. WHAT'S THE LEVEL RANGE?: Same as Blackburrow or Paw, i.e. Levels 5-15 will get the most enjoyment out of it. For purposes of comparision, consult the following chart: Kerran Catfisher = Gnoll Scout Kerran Scout = Scrawny Gnoll Kerran Male = Gnoll Kerran Female = Gnoll with 5th level shaman spells Kerran Pridesman = Gnoll Guardsman Kerran Huntress = Gnoll Guardsman with 9th level shaman spells Kerran Clawknight = Elite Gnoll Guard Kerran High Guard = Gnoll Commander ARE THOSE THE ONLY KERRANS?: Dunno. Never explored that far into the zone. There is, however, a level 20 (or so) Necromancer on an island in the zone. WHAT'S IN IT FOR ME?: Exploration. Possible riches. Possible death. A change from the Blackburrow/Crushbone routine. Since this zone isn't massively camped like Blackburrow or Crushbone, it's anybody's guess what neat things one can find on the rotting corpses of higher-level Kerrans. Last time I was there, I greased a Pridesman and found a Carnelite (a jewel of some kind) that netted me a smooth 7 gold and change at the merchants. ANY OTHER INFO?: Yep. If you're a druid or a ranger, this is the "dungeon" for you because it's almost completely open air. So all your "outdoor only" spells and skills will work over most of the zone. There will be at least one spellslinger (me) on hand to bind folks to Erudin so they don't have to make the long trek back from some other continent to retrieve their corpses. Volunteers to help out in this regard will be appreciated. OKAY, I'M ALL HIP. WHAT DO I NEED TO DO TO JOIN THIS SHINDIG?: I will be at the Qeynos docks around 6:30pm EDT, and will take the next available boat to Erudin. I will then wait on the Erudin docks until around 7:15, doing bindings. Then I'll head to Kerra Isle. If you have any questions, or if you wish to volunteer as a guide or a binder, feel free to email me at aburner@rcn.com Desdinova Humble Erudite Conjurer of the 4th Circle Innoruuk

KERRA ISLE INVASION UPDATE -------------------------- I've gotten Lots(tm) of email from folks about the proposed Kerra Isle Invasion that was announced here on 6/2/99. For those who missed it and don't wanna scroll down... WHAT: A mass invasion of the Kerra Isle zone, which is off the western coast of the Toxxulia Forest. WHEN: Saturday, June 5th, at 7:00pm EDT (4:00pm PDT) WHICH SERVER: Innoruuk Anyhow the mail I've recieved has brought some information to light that I felt worth sharing with the rest of the class. First, there was apparently a similar invasion on the Solusek Ro server a month ago. The participants put up a website detailing the action. The website is at http://www.prodes.org/kerra/. Note that I take no credit for this site and am not affiliated with it in any way. Someone else besides me did all the hard work to put that site up. But if you're interested in the sort of fun that awaits us on our invasion on the Innoruuk server, this site is recommended. Thanks to Fanir and Kalston for bringing this URL to my attention. Secondly, my estimate of the level range of the zone was a bit on the low side. While 5th level characters should be able to find challenging sport here, there are some high level Kerrans I didn't know anything about -- Kerran High Priestesses, and apparently a named NPC, High Priestess Mitty. The description I've had of these critters is that they're around 17th-19th level shamen, and Mitty is around 22nd-25th level. As if that weren't enough (and you'd certainly think it would be), there are at least FOUR (count 'em) 20th level (or so) Necromancers in the zone. I'd previously reported that there was only one. (And apparently they all hang together, so you high-level types will have to take on all four at once...) Thanks to Trump and Jaax for this info. Thirdly, I have an unconfirmed rumor that a bazaar *may* be set up after the invasion, thus making this something of a mega-event. But this is still just rumor, so don't *expect* it to happen (unless we tell you here first). Finally, the schedule has been adjusted somewhat based on the reports from the Solusek Ro invasion. I've never done one of these things, so I didn't have any idea of what time frame would be reasonable. Based on the website listed above, I will now be at the Erudin docks for bindings at 6:00pm EDT, an hour before the invasion commences. And so as not to exclude our vile, evil Troll, Dark Elf, and Ogre brothers and sisters, I will also be casting Invisibility as necessary. (NOTE: There are two NPCs that sit on the docks, and that you cannot avoid if you come into Erudin. I have no idea how they'll react to an evil race. Assuming they don't freak, I can cast Invis on you at the docks and lead you past the guards into the Toxx.) There will also be other spellslingers available to do bindings and (perhaps) invisos as needed. I will hang out on the docks until 7:00pm, then head to Kerra Isle. For those of you who arrive late (or early), the http://www.prodes.org/kerra/ website has directions on how to get to Kerra Isle from just about anywhere on Norrath. Thanks for all the feedback, and I hope to see you there! Desdinova Humble Erudite Conjurer of the 4th Circle Innoruuk

Enlightenment Gathering on the Bristlebane
Gwynn [6/8/1999-1:16:36 AM]  Comments[0]

There is to be an Enlightenment Gathering on the Bristlebane server on Sunday, June 13th in the ever-popular Tunnel to North Ro Desert in the East Commonlands. The events will commence 3:00 PM Pacific (5:00 PM Central). All races and classes are welcomed to come and share in this festival of knowledge. (So far these are the events planned to take place. More may be added later. The information for this gathering will be updated daily at http://www.stonepool.com/hands. This event is sponsored by a newly forming guild, The Hands of Quellious.) Philosophy Discussion All are invited to debate in a somewhat ordered discussion of the gods, war, ignorance, and other important issues. (Of course this will be done in character and will be a great opportunity for role-playing. We wish to also argue some of the player written Norrathian history contained on the web page above.) Trade Discussion We will devote a bit of time talking about each trade, including research. Hopefully we will answer any questions and peak the interest of those who haven’t discovered the wonders of the different trades. (If you wish to lead the discussion of a particular trade, especially if you are experienced, please contact me, I will post a running list on the web page in a few days.) Language Lessons This will mainly be devoted to sharing the normal race languages so that we may all understand each other better. Any citizens wishing to share their knowledge of the elder languages is certainly welcomed hold free classes, but we ask that there be no selling of languages. Participants my tip their teachers but (If you wish to share your language please contact me, I will post a running list on the web page in a few days.) Scavenger Hunt This will be for some of our younger attendees (anyone 7th level or lower). Participants may form groups if desired but must share their prize. All the items will be found close to the tunnel (only in the zones adjacent to the east commons). There will be prizes for all that partake in this quest. (There will be a story to go with the hunt.) Open Market And of course as with every event in Norrath, there will be the selling of goods. We would like mainly self-made goods to be sold here. Though the selling of acquired items is not prohibited, we encourage tradesmen to make a bigger showing. (The method of sales will be announced later on the web page.) Any questions or comments can be sent to weeze@id-base.com or message Gwynn the Enchatress on Bristlebane.
