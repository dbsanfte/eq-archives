Everyone knows there is a huge difference between character development in AC and UO. In UO (someone has already pointed this out at least once on this board), if you wanted to add a new skill, all you did was start working at it! Some other skill you were not using would go down, but it allowed for huge diversity in character development over _time_.

I am certain that the Turbine people took this into consideration when they were designing the game. From the way they have put together the system of character creation and devleopment, it seems quite apparent to me that their initial philosophy about character development is fundamentally different than UO.

Everyone on AC is trying to develop SOLO characters... that's why everyone is stressing about what skills to start with, which to specialize and train, what to add with skill credits... But the way i see it, turbine set up this "ceiling" in character devlopment for a dual purpose -- 1) to "discourage" development of characters that are simply one-man armies; and 2) to _encourage_ development of characters that will take up a specific role and job in a GROUP of adventurers. Why did they go through all the trouble of setting up the (in my opinion) absolutely great allegiance and fellowship systems, if not to encourage people to band together and cooperate?

I think this fundamental idea in their design is just being overlooked by the majority of AC players. Some recent patches have added the AWESOME meeting halls -- more proof that Turbine is trying to encourage development of a social, cooperative environment. I was so psyched about the potential of these meeting halls when they first came out, but have not ONE SINGLE TIME seen them being used in the manner that I had hoped. Sure, people go in and hang out... but organized, purposeful meetings are few and far between. Even if someone did go to the trouble of scheduling a meeting, and trying to get people to come, I would think that the majority of people would skip the meeting in favor of going to their favorite hunting spot to try to get that 95k experience they need to get to level 27...

I can't say I am a "role-player," or an advocate of role-playing. But the more I think about it, I guess that is what I am turning out to be here... Does anyone else remember the old cartoon Dungeons & Dragons? Every one of those kids had specific skills, a specific duty or task... but put them all together, and the group as a whole had way more potential than any of them alone.

I've seen people arguing about which allegiance member should be delegated to spend skill credits on lock-picking skill... this is not the kind of job specialization I am talking about. I don't feel the need to be everything all at once with my character. I am good at what I do, and that satisfies me. I may not be at the foreground, swinging a sword and taking the hits from a group of lugies, but I love getting the "thx!" from the fighters whose weapons and armor I've buffed up, and who I healed the whole time they were fighting.

I'm not saying the way anyone's style of play is bad. I am saying that people are getting caught up into thinking that they have to produce a godly character that can do EVERYTHING. Just ask yourself though, is that really what you want? Can you see yourself taking the role of healer, or thief, or defender in a close-knit group of adventurers? If you can, then explore some of the possibilities that exist in AC for that kind of playing. I am willing to wager that there are enough of us out there who would like to do this, to put together an awesome group -- a group that has potential beyond the sum of its idividual members.

Well, once again, that's my story. Don't make me repeat myself.

I'll give a good example of a problem with fellowship. I finally convinced my wife to give AC a try (lucky me), so my usual hunting partner and I create new characters specifically to hunt with her when she's online.

He created an unarmed fighter, I a mage, and my wife choose an archer. Well of course to start out with we let him tank so that she could get a feel for the game. Thru this he was able gain exp a little faster then the both of us.

No problem's yet, then he lvl's and the xp distribution goes to 50% him, and 25% for the both of us. WTF 1 lvl difference and he's gaining xps at twice the rate we are.

So he drops out of the fellowship to let us catch him and my wife says "hey why did he leave?" I explain it to her and she is like hey that IS stupid. I mean really, what is a lvl 2 gonna do, powerlvl us or something? This also happened and lvls 3 and 4 too.

IMO there should only be a 1-5% difference in xps divided between lvls and this should decrease as the lvls get higher. Also, all xps gained from a kill should be divided amongst the group, not just the person who got the kill.

Just my little rant about fellowship at lower lvls.

P.S. My wife did enjoy the game if your interested (lucky me again) or at least she now understands how 3 hours can go by so fast.
