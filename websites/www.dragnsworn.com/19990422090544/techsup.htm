Is my account gone?

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 One thing which I have discovered is that when you think your account is gone, IT REALLY IS NOT.

If you cannot log in, don't immediatly panic. DO NOT CREATE A NEW ACCOUNT. Your stats are savable!

I saved RedHen (my other char) from such a fate. All you need to do is exit StarCraft, log off your ISP, and run "REGEDIT.EXE".

Expand HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE, expand SOFTWARE, expand Battle.Net,
then click on Configuration. In the other panel, find the Server List. Double-click on the words "Server List" to edit it. Now copy the contents of the whole thing to the clipboard, and paste it into some text file or something, in case something bad
happens. Now replace all the highlited text with "exodus.battle.net" press OK and quit RegEdit.

Now log into your ISP, start up Starcraft, and log in as normal.

Your account should be fine. If it isn't fine, then you can panic... the New Account button is only inches away...

If for some reason you can't log into starcraft after the edit, get out that temporary file, and restore the original Battle.Net server list.
Is your CD Stuck?

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 OK. Unplug your computer, to be safe. On the front of your computer, on the CD-ROM drive panel, there is a small screw or a small pinhole.

If there is a screw, remove the screw with a small screwdriver.  Now there's a pinhole there, too.

Take a paperclip and straighten it into one long segment.  Push this paperclip into the pinhole. You will quickly feel resistance, and if you push a bit, it will trigger the tray
release. Now pull the tray out, and remove your CD. Push the tray back in, and replace the screw (if there was one).

If there isn't a pinhole or screw, look to see if you can just pull out the tray by hand. Don't pull to hard, if it doesn't come out then I'm sorry, but it must really be stuck.

Do you ever get errors about UDP packets?

 

 

 

 

 Try rebooting your computer... There's a bug in the Windows internet code that causes this sometimes, and restarting Windows clears it up. If that doesn't solve it, you might be behind a firewall. Ask your ISP or network administrator to "open up port 6112 for incoming and outgoing TCP and UDP traffic". If they don't know what you're talking about, or can't do it, get a new dial-up ISP.  NOTE: If you are trying to connect through a proxy server, forget it.  Connecting through WinGate, WinProxy, etc. will probably never work right.
How do I reduce my lag? Close the MS Office Toolbar if you have it. Disable any virus scanners when you play StarCraft (don't worry, its safe). Don't run ICQ in the background... close it. I don't mean Do Not Disturb mode, I mean CLOSE it. It hogs the internet connection. Also, don't run any web browsers or internet applications in the background. If you have anything slower than a Pentium 150mhz, then turn off music with CTL-M. Of course, try re-connecting to your ISP a few times. Finally, occasionally rebooting can help clear up lag.
