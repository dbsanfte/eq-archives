Fellowship Associate 		posted 09-14-1999 17:44            
The new forage thing is interesting. I think the shadowmans are even more interesting. I killed them over and over in wcommons near kithicor. So far, I have seen 3 types of shadowman. Warriors, Clerics, and Necromancers. The warriors are fairly easy, mostly greens to some occasional blue cons to me, a lv 35. The warrior types all drop shadow weapons. I have 11 different types of weapons so far. I heard they will disappear upon log out, so I assume after I get back into the game they will be gone.
The clerics are about the same lv as warriors. They can cast greater healing, so they are lv 24+. They are not that hard to take out, but real time consuming as they will heal themselves over and over until they are oom. Usually I have to deal with 4 to 5 greater heals when taking 1 out.
The necro ones are a bit tough. They got a pet, and sometimes more than one pet. Almost all the necro ones conned blue to me at 35. Their pets are weak, but their spells are a pain. Usually they will cast shock of poison (160 dmg) or fear me. And their Banshee Aura spell (8 dmg shield) is a pain. So before u take these out I suggest casting cancel magic on them multiple times then enter melee as quickly as possible because they will instantly rebuff themselves. I have seen as many as 5 of them spawning in the same spot. I used harmony to pull them out one by one.
They run very fast. Actually, I wouldn't consider it running, it was more like warping. Even with SoW, they seemed to never miss a melee round when they were chasing me.
They also drop a mask that cast 1 charge of gather shadows, and an eye that casts deadeye, and a fist that casts grim aura.
Their factions are also interesting. My faction with faction 416 got worse (whatever that means) and with 415 got better (again, whatever)
Also another note. You can't predict whether they will run or not when they are near death. Some did run, some just fought until I killed them. Some greens fought to the death, and some blues flee. Weird, that's all I can say.
