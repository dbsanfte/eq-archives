Ranks and Responsibilities

The following is a constructed list of ranks and their responsibilities for each rank and office. I understand that these responsibilities may seem tedious. That is why all offices (tier officers only) are allowed to enlist the aid of another member, who is not of officer rank, to aid in the performing of his duties. First Tier officers are allowed to enlist from officer ranks as an aid in performing tasks befitting the responsibilities of held office.

The collective officers of First, Second, and Third Tier are known as the Citadel of Darkness. Within the guild they are more commonly known as the Conclave of Overlords. All officers without office or title are to be Overlords in training. All enlisted personnel shall start at the rank of Iblith. You must earn your rank as enlisted. This is to promote competition within the guild.

I want only the strongest, most cunning, and intelligent individuals holding leadership within my army. With that said, lets’ begin with the “Citadel of Darkness”.

Citadel of Darkness

Guild leader:
His Divine Darkness and master- Overlord Thelaskir

First Tier Officers
The Voice of Thelaskir- Lady Chamberlain Skathe, High Priestess to the Order of the White Hand.
The responsibility of this station is to sit as overseer of the Order in the advent of the Overlord's prolonged absence. Secondary duties shall include, but are not limited to, the administration of the Church of The Order of the White Hand. Directly responsible to the Overlord.

The Sword of Thelaskir- Lord General Baalzebub, High Executioner of the Order of the White Hand.
The responsibility of this station is to collect readiness and strength reports from the Second Tier Officers on a monthly basis to make assessments on the overall suitability of the Order as an army. Secondary duties include, but are not limited to, as the Lord General of the Order of the White Hand; the Lord General shall assess reports given to him and oversee the deployment of forces during guild events and raids. Directly responsible to the Overlord.

The Eyes of Thelaskir- Dark Shadowmaster Knarl, High Inquisitor of the Order of the White Hand.
The responsibility of this station is to implement and monitor a spy network. Shall review reports monthly, or on a case by case basis, the reports made from scouting expeditions made for guild raids or events an findings made by the spy network, Lidless eyes.

The Law of Thelaskir- Dark Soulmaster Rhuin, High Justicar of the Order of the White Hand.
The responsibility of this station is to aid the Overlord in reviewing guild charter, laws, and policies, making changes and connections when needed under the direct authority of the Overlord.
Secondary duties include, but are not limited to, enforcing discipline within the Order and to act as prosecutor during the "Trials of Shame".

Third Tier Officers
Third Tier Officers are responsible for handling the training, recruitment, promotion, and punishment of their respective castes in details as follows:
Training: Each Dk. Lt. shall ensure that the members of their caste are receiving adequate training in their trade related skills and combat strategies. Combat strategies shall be compiled and placed within the appropriate caste forum. In addition all quests, which are pertinent and mandatory for that caste, will be available for members if they so desire. In addition each dark lt. shall compile a list of researchable spells (for sorcery and enchanters) and lists for item creation.
Recruitment: Each Dk. Lt. Shall set aside a day out of the week to actively recruit members for their caste. Each member of the caste will move to relevant newbie zones to recruit. This day can be rotated. (See Recruiting)
Promotion: See promotion and advancement.
Punishment: the Jabbuk of that caste shall handle the punishment for those within the caste. If the Jabbuk is unable to solve the problem at his level the individual's crimes shall be forwarded to the Dark LT. For disciplinary review. If at this level the Dark Lt. Is not sufficiently able to correct the problem, the member shall be sent to the Shadowcommander of that caste and god help them. If the Shadowcommander finds that the individual warrants a "Trial of Shame" or subsequent removal, the file shall be forwarded to Dark Soulmaster Rhuin to administer judicial punishment within the system.
Dark Lt's are encouraged to come up with a title for their castes. See SK's, Crimson Tier. A short history and background behind the name would be most appreciated.

Section 3 Rank Advancement. Enlisted & Officer

Requirements for enlisted rank advancement are as follows:

Trials of Position
Trials of Position shall be held on the first and second Saturday of every other month. Location of trial of position shall be within the Arena. Members shall duel 1 on 1 in rounds of elimination. These are single elimination. Bottom line is, you lose, and you're out of the contest. A list of applicants shall be taken at the first of the previous month. Scheduling battles shall be done by Second tier officers. Guild attendance at said trials is strongly encouraged.

Prerequisites for participation: Applicant must apply for sponsorship from member holding rank of officer. Member must then have earned selection points equal to rank desired. (For selection point system, see figure below) Member must have held previous rank for two months.
Selection point system:
Selection, or rather, Reputation points, is cumulative.
Jabbuk- 6400pts
Sargtlin- 3200pts
Oloth- 800pts.
Kyorlin- 400pts.
Kyorl- 200pts.
Malla- 100pts.

Gaining Reputation points:
2pts. Per level of individual successfully recruited into the Order.
100pts. Per duel fought and won against person of equal level.
10 pts. Per duel fought and won between Order members during role-play.
Points will be awarded during guild quests and events.
Points will be awarded for extremely good role-playing.
Points awarded for significant items donated to guild through role-play or battles fought. Not by buying or twinking.
…and more ways to acquire points at a later date.

Losing Reputation points:
Demerits. Demerits shall be given in failure of assigned duties (discussed under leadership) given for non role-play. There are other ways…. Generally if you fail. Just think of it that way. You fail… you lose.

All Tier officers are able to bestow reputation points. This job is usually left to the Dark. Lt's and their aides however. Acts mandated as being worthy of reputation, not witnessed by members of the Order, must have a log or screen shot to verify performance with.
