Guild Charter

Over the course of time, the populace or the world polarizes itself, begetting war, strife and chaos throughout the lands of Norrath. A small band of intrepid adventurers has recognized the certain demise of the spirit of the populace brought on by this strife and chaos. They have banded together and begun travelling the lands in an attempt to restore the lands and people of Norrath to their former glory and rid the world of the forces that would cause this strife and chaos.

Note: Leadership is by selection only. This is not a democracy.

 

Guild Officers

Quality, rather than quantity, of leadership will be emphasized. The number of officers in the guild shall be limited to six. Guild Officers shall serve as the primary leaders for the Guild Ranks, with the support of the Guild Leader.

Guild Officers shall be appointed and maintained at the discretion of the Guild Leader

 

Guild Membership

Unlimited, other than that an effort will be made to maintain a numerical balance amongst the classes.

 

Membership Rules

Joining

  • Prospective Guild members should be at least level 25. The minium level for secondary characters of primary Guild Members will be 15.

  • If the prospective member has requested membership into the Guild, a Guild Officer should contact the prospective member as soon as possible. The officer should inform the prospective member of the process to become a member (i.e., point them to these rules).

  • If a current Guild Member notices a person who would be an asset to the Guild, that member should talk with said person to determine their interest. If interested, the prospective Guild member should be directed to an officer or to the Guild website for more information and the membership process.

  • At least 2 Guild members who have grouped/worked with the prospective member and 2 Guild officers need to sponsor the prospective member.

  • Once the above are done, a prospective member will contact the Guild Leader, informing him of his sponsors. The Guild Leader will review the prospective member's application with those sponsors and other select officers and members, which shall vary at any given time.

  • Upon the Guild Leader's approval, the applicant will become a "member initiate" in the Guild on a 30 day "trial" basis. The officer who
    invited the member will then provide the new member the Members Only message board password and any other relevent information. This trial period is for the new member and the rest of the Guild to get to know each other further (group, raid, etc.) and establish their roots in the Guild. At the end of this 30 day period, the new member's membership will be reviewed by the Guild Leader and officers - and barring any issues, will become a full member of the Guild.

  • Once a new member is added for their "trial" period, the officer that invited the member must contact the Admissions Officer informing him/her of the addition; including the initiates full Name (Character, not RL), Class, Race, and Level, so that they may be properly added to the Guild Roster.

  • There will be no requirement to perform quests or any other initiation rights.

  • Each member will be allowed a profile page. The character’s full name, class, race, deity, and level should be emailed to the Guild Leader (jaandir@prexus.com) so that a profile page can be established. The member will be responsible for updating equipment information and providing a screen shot for that profile page.

Officers

  • A prospective Guild Officer guild must have attained level 50, unless waived by the Guild Leader for the better good of the guild.

  • The Guild Leader will have the sole power to appoint and dismiss officers.

  • Anyone may apply for officer status in the guild. Reasons and qualifications must be presented to the guild leader. Sufficient reason must be provided detailing why an additional officer is needed. Secondary characters of guild officers will also be appointed as officers of the guild.

Removal

  • Removal from the guild, other than by the Guild Leader, must be made by agreement of three officers.

  • In all cases of dismissal from the guild, the Guild Leader shall be notified by e-mail or a post on “The High Council” forum, on the Aequus Message Boards, of the causes, reasons and approval of the dismissal by each of the three or more officers approving the removal.

  • The Guild Leader has the sole power to override any guild removal.

Conduct

  • In spreading the good name and reputation of the guild, all members shall not use an anonymous tag. Those members wishing to avoid in game "/tell hell" and requests from other players should use the roleplay tag. Your guild name should remain clearly visible above your avatar at all times.

  • All guild members shall act honorably and responsibly in the game and treat other players respectfully, as they would wish to be treated.

  • Members, especially officers, should be active within the game and the guild. Activity within the guild shows your loyalty and value to the membership of the guild.

  • In so much as it is possible; guild members are expected to be self-reliant. Though other guild members may be around and have the capability of providing you the assistance you need, don’t expect them to abandon a group, a quest or a journey solely to render you assistance.

  • Guild members are expected to provide any assistance possible to other guild members as well as the general gaming populace. The assistance, however, shouldn’t require you to create a hardship to your current gaming environment at the time.

  • Guild Members should not assume that the guild is their general store. The guild doesn’t exist to equip you. Although the guild may assist you in obtaining items you may want or need, you are solely responsible for acquiring them. The main focus in the creation of this guild is camaraderie between its members.

  • No guild member shall take any action that could feasibly result in sullying the reputation of the guild. Any such action will result in immediate dismissal from the guild by the guild leader.

  • Any conflicts within the guild should be brought to the attention of your guild officer only after failure between the respective parties to resolve those conflicts. Your guild officer will relay pertinent information to the Guild Leader, whose resolution to said conflict will be final.

Loot Distribution, Grouping & Guild Raids

  • Class balance should be maintained as much possible in groups established by and/or for guild members.

  • Guild members should try and group with their guild mates as much as possible. This will benefit everyone, allowing us to better function as a whole when needed.

  • The Guild will setup raids for it's members. It's not the sole responsibility of the Guild leader or officers to do this all of the time. ANY member can setup a raid, adventure, etc. The Guild will schedule monthly raids to the planes (mostly Fear and Hate), as well as maintain the Guild on the Dragon rotation. Only the Guild Leader and Guild Events officer will be able to schedule these raids, as they will be the only members with the necessary access to the Prexus Raid Calendar. All other events should be scheduled on the Guild Calendar. It is preferred that members setup a message on one of the Raids message boards for members/non-members to sign up. If an event is for the guild only, please state such in your sign-up message, and post it in the appropriate forum. If it is an open event, please be considerate of those outside of the guild who are willing to schedule time to assist us.

  • The Guild will have regular raids to assist those from the lower level ranks. These raids will NOT be to "powerlevel" lower level members of the guild, but rather to help them gain the skills in order to raid successfully. Higher level members will be present at these raids most times in order to help with corpse recovery, trains, etc, that may result from these raids. The goal here is for all of our members to have fun and learn how to play the game better.

  • Quest Assistance - quests along the lines of the Epic Quests are usually able to be scheduled ahead of time. However, there are times where some mobs have a random timer, or rareness about them that doesn't allow us to do so. In these cases, the person needing to do the quest (or portion thereof) needs to notify their intent to the guild as soon as possible. All guildmates online at the time that are able to lend assistance should do so upon the questing Guildmate informing them of the mob being up and requesting assistance, etc.

  • Some raids will require that an outlay be made by it's members (usually in the form of reagents). It will be the raid leader's responsibility to obtain any reagents needed to gain access to where their raid will be taking place (i.e. Hate and Air stones). It will also be each individual's responsibility to provide sufficient reagents for any spells they will use on said raids (Enchanters/Clerics - peridots, etc). All members who have reagent requirements such as the above shall be reimbursed from the "money loot" on said raid and the remainder shall be deposited to the guild bank. This will not count towards any other loot distribution. At times, the raid leader may wish to provide all of the reagents needed for everyone, other times, the guild will pay to get these reagents.

  • Please put any events you wish to schedule on the guild's calendar of events - being sure to include a link back to the message you posted containing the event information. PLEASE be considerate of other's events already scheduled on the calender so we can try and have everyone involved in as much of the guild activity as we can.

  • Determining who gets to go on a guild raid - below are the rules for determining on who should get to go on a guild raid (i.e. pecking order). However, it is 100% up to the raid leader to determine what they will need in order to obtain their objective and to have fun:

    a. Guild Members
    b. Allied Guild Members
    c. Friends of Guild Members
    d. Friends of Allied Guild Members
    e. All others

    Again, the order above is to be used with all else being equal (i.e. if you need another druid to do something, you would take a Guildmate before a friend). However, again, it is up to the raid leader to know what they will need to accomplish their task. Thus, if they need someone of a higher level (due to needing particular skill, spell, ability, etc) or skill (i.e. a monk who HAS pulled the plane of Hate over a monk who has NOT pulled the plane of Hate), etc, they may shuffle the order of a-e above in order to accomplish their task. HOWEVER, if they do decide they need someone by not following the above order, they should explain to the others of that class (or whatnot) why that particular person (or persons) were selected over them. All effort must be used to utilize guild members first and foremost, and if unable to, try and find a reasonable alternative (i.e. in the monk example above, if you have room for the pulling monk, and the guildmate monk, take both, and have the guildmate learn from the pulling monk HOW to pull, so in the future, they will be able to do so).

  • Loot Rules:
    These guidelines are to be used only in large-scale raid situations (Plane of Hate, Fear, Dragons, Venril Sathir, etc). Any other time, it will be the group/raid leader's sole discretion how to distribute loot.

    Raid Loot Rules - the following rules should be used as standard guidelines for all raid level events (i.e. planes, chardok, dragons, etc):
    a. If you are eligible to random on an item (i.e. CAN & WILL USE), you are welcome to submit your name for the random on the item.
    b. We ask that if you feel you wouldn't utilize the item, not to submit your name for the random.
    c. If no one who is eligible to random wants in on the item, the item will be deposited into the guild bank for disposal by the guild.
    d. Once you have won an item, you are not eligible to be in for another random until either
        1) everyone who can use a particular item has won an item, OR
        2) everyone else who can use a particular item has said item, thus you get it by default (in this case, this will count as your second,
            third, etc, loot, which will remove you from the second, third, etc, rounds of randoming).
    e.God, Boss Mob, Breastplate, Robes and Leggings and major quest items shall be merited by the raid leader and Guild Leader, at their
       discretion, following consultation with other guild officers and allies officers. Their say is final.
    f. There are certain "crap" drops in some places (i.e. the wands in Hate, etc) that do not qualify as item drops or money drops. The raid
       leader may award/random these items as he sees fit, and they will NOT be counted as an item loot.
    g. If there are any NO DROP items dropped, and no one who is eligible to use them wants in on the random, the item shall ROT. We will
       not have people, just because they do not like the stats of an item, etc, not wanting in on the random, only to hope they are just given up
       freely to someone and not have them count as loot.
