Being a Mage: The First 20 Levels from Tenser

 

   First let me say that I am not a professional writer. I decided to jot down a few tips about being a mage in hopes that it will help those people out there who are playing one, or would like to play one, but would like more information about what to do once the basics are discovered. This guide assumes you have figured out how to cast spells, engage in combat, and research new spells. I will not be giving away spell formulae in this guide, so if spoilers are what you are looking for then move along.

   The first thing that all mages need to know is where to focus on as far as skill training goes, and what
skills to try and make sure they have. There are several ways a mage can be built, from a pure war mage, to a healing/fighting life mage, to a mage that dabbles in all of the magical arts. You need to have a good idea of what kind of mage you want to be before you choose your starting stats and skills, as you will be stuck with them forever. My knowledge is that of the multi-mage, meaning a mage who uses multiple types of magic, so that is where I will focus. I can tell you that if you have desires about becoming a war mage that can swing it out in combat, forget it. Asheron's Call combat is such that in order to be considered even decent at melee combat you need a certain set of skills. Those skills; melee defence, a weapon, and a way to heal yourself, are very costly to even train, let alone specialise in. War magic comes in at 14 points to train, leaving you only 36 points in which to finish your character. Melee defence will chew up another 12 of that, putting you at 24, a weapon will cost you another 8 or so, dropping you down to 16, and you haven't even begun to think about such skills as Arcane Lore, Mana Conversion, Healing, or Missile Defence. Needless to say, a war mage with a weapon can be done, but you will never be nearly as powerful as a specialised character of the same level. I have always encouraged role-playing, and I applaud character creation that uses it, but just remember that without careful thought towards your character in the beginning, you will most likely end up rerolling as all your friends blaze by you in levels and move on to better places.

For the Multi-Mage I suggest thinking about the following points when choosing stats:

  • Strength: You need some, not tons. 25-30 points is enough for the beginning.
  • Endurance: I'd get at least 60, build it up soon. This will give you important stamina to be used in the 'Stamina to Mana' spell, a staple with Life Mages.
  • Coordination: 10, no more. Build it up to 20-30 once you get 20th or so, but it's not used in any of your main skills.
  • Quickness: 50 or so. Not too low, but don't blow points on it. Only thing it will really help with is running away, which is a good thing.
  • Focus: Your main stat, shoot for at least 80, not higher than 90. This will power 90% of your skills, and you will be raising it a lot.
  • Self: Also important, try for at least 75. It will be built up as you go along.

       So now you have trained your stats. Time to pick skills. This is where everyone will have their own opinion about what is best to go with. Myself, I picked to specialise in Mana Conversion, and train war and life magic, arcane lore, and lockpicking. If I had it to do over I would probably drop lockpicking and move arcane lore and run to specialised. Run becomes very important down the road, it needs
    to be high.
       What skills are important for a mage, and why? The following is a short list of important skills for a mage, and why a mage needs them:
  • Arcane Lore: It is presumed this helps control spell component burn, which makes the higher level spells get expensive and a pain to keep stocked. It also determines what magic items you can use. A very important skill as you will want to use special wands and jewellery that will greatly enhance your skills as you get higher level. Try getting this skill to 60 or 70 by 10th level, higher if possible.
  • Mana Conversion: Probably the single most important skill for any mage. This helps by reducing the amount of mana it takes to cast your spells. This in turn lets you stay in fights longer, blow up more creatures, buff yourself when needed, and otherwise be more useful to your group. A skill of 100 is recommended by 15th level.
  • Life Magic: The bread and butter of the schools of magic. With it you will heal yourself and your friends, regain mana by converting unused stamina, make creatures more vulnerable to your spells, and otherwise do neat stuff. An example of how useful this school is: a Force Bolt II will do on average 21 points to a Lugian, but when this Lugian has been hit with Piercing Vulnerability II (a life magic spell) he will take in upwards of 34 points of damage. Thus by using one spell you make all your other attacks against a foe much more powerful.
  • Creature Magic: The "Buffs". With this magic you can raise any stat or skill in the game, or reduce it. It helps in many ways. Sprint is a useful spell, as are any of the magic skill raising spells. Your War Magic skill might be 120, impressive, but with War Magic Mastery III it becomes 140, very much more impressive, and as simple as one spell. In general, a mage with Creature Enchantment is roughly as powerful as another mage 2-5 levels higher than him.
  • War Magic: The meat of magic. You blow things up with this, enough said.
  • Item Magic: This school is a neat thing for warriors who want to beef their weapons. For a mage it serves one purpose, Portal Spells. Try to get this by 24th level, you will know why once you reach that experience level.
  • Identification Skills: You will be a natural at these, even if you never train them. As your focus goes up so will these skills, on a 1 for 1 basis. This will help you determine what items to sell, and which ones you should hold on to for a vassal or yourself.
  • Run: You will do this a lot. Get this to 130 by the time you hit 20th. Earlier if possible. The Direlands are a profitable place for the mage who can flee in time.
  • Magic Defence: Pump points into this from time to time. As you stand back and dish out death to your enemies they will do so to you as you level. This skill can make the difference between a well timed resist for your or a quick trip back to your lifestone.

       What to spend your gained exps on? This is all personal preference, although I will give you some advice and goals to shoot for. By 15th you should have over 100 stamina and 60 health, more if possible. 130 mana is enough, but more helps. Get your War, Life, and Run skills to 130 by 15th if you can, no later than 20th. Try to pick up Item Enchantment by 24th. Creature Enchantment by 12th. Mana Conversion should be 100 by 15th, or 150 by 20th. Those 3rd and 4th level spells will take a beating on your mana if this is not trained well enough. Arcane Lore should be 100 no later than 20th level. Most people suggest 135 by 20th level if possible. Strength and Quickness should be 70 by 20th level. This isn't a must, but some points here and there will help. Raise endurance early, it will give you the hit point and stamina boost you need to survive as a young mage. I built this up to 75 before I spent points on anything else. Self and focus should be 150 by 20th level, or 125 by 15th or so. Remember to raise these first, before raising your actual skills, as they will contribute points to skills. Every 4 points in focus gives you: 1 point in all spell schools, 1.33 points in arcane lore, .85 points in mana conversion, 4 points in identification skills. Your Focus should always be 5-10 points higher than your Self.

       I hope this small guide has been of some use. With it perhaps you can see where your goals should be set for your first 20 years. While you may seem weak when you are young, know that when you hit 20th, using this guide, you will be fighting things that most 40th level warriors would only dream of seeing. You will also have items and weapons that people will trip over themselves to acquire from
    you.

    Good hunting to you!
