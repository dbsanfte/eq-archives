Ranger's Twilight Account 12:45 PM
The following is a player's account of participating in the quest kick-off for the Ranger's Twilight saga finale, a player-run quest on the Bertoxxulous server:

Huddled in his cloak, damp, Byrch awaited the others. Invisible against the wall of the canyon, he inspected his weapons once more. The axe. The tomahawk. The spear. The dagger. And his newly crafted bow, smooth ashwood and taut silk.

A dark elf, his blue skin concealed beneath his heavy armor and enveloping clothes, crept by, looking about cautiously. A cleric, one of Terrak's tools. Not part of Tunare's intent, for her fire was not yet present.

Aranic arrived first, followed by Kazied, in guise as one of the Teir'Dal, Xiao, and Aenen. Grim faces all around. Byrch stepped from his concealment, his hands clenching with anticipation. The Mother of All watched close, her presence about the small band, overwhelming.

"I will go ahead, to see that the way is clear of the Dragoons of Neriak."

He vanished once more, the Mother's power covering him, and slipped into the Nektulos Forest.

One of Neriak's guardians had, in fact, made his way to the borders of his land, but finding nothing but the cold wind rustling fallen leaves, he left once more. Nervous, though; something heavy hung in the air this night.

Terrak's nose, preternaturally sensitive, caught scent of elves in his private domain, for he shouted his insane threats, challenges, and hatred. The others had followed into the forest, and Byrch moved back, waiting for Tunare's hand to give him leave to strike.

The Huntsman, stepping out of shadows and darkness. Striking. Aranic fell, too quickly, his blows useless against Terrak's evil. Kazied fell, and Byrch began to simmer... (Mother, give me leave to act, and end this thing!) Aenen was treacherously ambushed by one of Terrak's Teir'Dal henchmen, the sneering countenance of the Dark Elf showing what he, and his race, thought of this Feir'Dal and his pretensions of lordship.

And then the blue fire. And then the Hand was upon him.

Darkness.
Something cold.
A hand.

"Byrch? He lives, though I know not how."

Aranic.

Byrch opened his eyes. The Commonlands. "The others?"

"Kazied escaped somehow, through some magic. My wounds were not fatal, and we monks harness our body's healing abilities. Aenen is here, but dying slowly of some magical disease. But -- how is it with you?"

Byrch raised himself on one hand, shook his head. "There was the Fire, and She was with me... I know no more. Does... does the huntsman live?"

Aranic began to speak, when from the north, a cry of pain -- and death. Xiao.

Cold fury swept the ranger, and all his limbs and body shook. (MOTHER!)

"No. Not this. This cannot be." Staggering unsteadily to his feet, Byrch was as a man struck by lightning. Aranic called after him, but Byrch was insensible.

"No! Mother! You promised victory!" He was shouting now, screaming from the hillside, his axe and spear fallen at his feet.

A young monk raced by, terror in his eyes, for he knew what had passed within the forest, and knew his love's fate.

Byrch stood, blankly, a cipher, numb to the rain, to the world, as Terrak leaped once more from the darkness and felled Chungtao. A dark, horned shape loomed over the Huntsman.

Eyes blurred with hot tears, Byrch shrieked to the heavens. "MOTHER! You BETRAYED me! You LET THEM DIE!"

Casting off his armor now, throwing aside his bow, not hearng the string snap.

"You denied me your power when I needed it! You ABANDONED us to the lord of HATE!"

Clutching his head now.

"Mother! I RENOUNCE YOU!"

A stab of pain, terrible, through his temple, and Byrch felt the power leave him. All of the power. Her gifts were gone. He was a Ranger Knight no more.

"To HELL with Tunare! To HELL with Innoruuk, and all the Gods! Take your schemes and plots and DAMNED divine will, and LEAVE US BE!"

Byrch collapsed, sobbing.

Drakain's Tale II 9:15 AM
This is part 2 of the Rathe server tale recently sent in by a creative adventurer:

Drakain's Adventure

Chapter 2

    The dream came only once, but I will never forget it. One doesn’t forget a deity-influenced dream. Many days later, I was sitting outside an Inn in the Common lands when I noticed the hilt of my father’s sword was somewhat coming unscrewed. I took it off and found a rolled up letter. I read it and was shocked by the contents…

To my dearest son, Drakain,
   If you are reading this then we are probably gone. We knew a time would come when the evil one would find us, we only hope that you and your sister will be all right.
   Your father and I have decided that you should know the truth about who your parents really were.
   I am Empress Alanisa Rebecca El’Aaron. I was once the Empress of the Island Elven Nation of Val’Halak. I was married to the Emperor Kailen when I was 16 and I loved him very much. One year after we were married, Kailen was murdered in the night by a person that left no trace of their passing. I was alone for a while but then one day I met a human smith named Glen, your father. We soon fell in love... true love. I knew that having a human as a mate was looked down upon so we left. Not long after out departure, we got word that Val’Halak was destroyed by an evil army under the control of The Prince of Hate, Innoruuk. We knew that it was our fault but there was nothing we could do, so we kept running. We soon settled in a small village outside of Freeport. I knew I was pregnant with you the whole time we were in flight so the home was very welcome.
   We know as we write this letter, the evil one is looking for us, and it won’t be long until he finds us. We are no longer afraid nor should you be. Please Drakain, take of Celes and keep her from the hand of Innoruuk. Remember that we love you and we always will… make us proud Drakain.
   There is one more thing. You have another sister Drakain, a half sister. She is an elf woman named Alanisa, after me. I gave her to my trusted aids after Kailen was killed to protect her. I do not know her location but you must find her. The evil is after her too. The evil is after all of you. He is scared of you Drakain. That is why he wants to kill you now. You will become very powerful one day. Fight till the end Drakain…

         We love you,
         Mother and Father

   I cried then. I told no one of the letter either. I had to know the truth, so I set off for the lost Isle of Val’Halak.

Chapter 3

   When I got there, there was nothing left but the foundation a great castle. I had the proof I needed. I am the son of an Empress, a prince in a sense. But I do not think of myself as one. I am still a holy warrior of Marr and a member of the Holy Order. I then told Lord Finncanon everything. And when I returned to Freeport, I found that the guards knew me by my face and I was to be killed on site. Another one of Innoruuk’s evil deeds. I knew I was no longer safe in Antonica, so I left for Faydwer to find Alanisa.
   Upon reaching Faydwer, I was overwhelmed by the forests that covered the land. As soon as I stepped in to Lesser Faydark, I became disorientated. The sheer size of the Butcherblok Mountains awed me. It was a relief to find that the guards here didn’t know me and I could walk in the towns unmolested. Finding Kelethin wasn’t a problem; it was finding places in Kelethin. After asking around for a few days, I got word that Alanisa was a member of a secret Ranger guild named Nalk’Un Roath, witch I later found out means Noble Rangers in old Val’Halak tongue. It wasn’t long before I was granted entry to the Guild’s Headquarters. There I met Lady Alanisa El’Aaron, guild master of Nalk’Un Roath. We looked into each other’s eyes and we both saw our mother. She embraced me and cried.
   “I was so worried about you.” She whispered.
   I couldn’t say a word. I just stood there. It had been so long since I felt the love of a family member. Sure there is love in the Holy Order, even a family love in the guild, but nothing that compares to the love of your true family.
   “Come, there is much to talk about.”
   We spent the rest of the day and into the night talking about our lives and our family. I told her of my father Glen, and she told me of her foster parents.
   “I envy you Drakain,” she said, “Your father was a good man.” “I never knew my father.”
   “Alanisa…?”
   “No, enough of this talk… we have much to prepare for. Innoruuk knows where we are and he will be here soon. Don’t be afraid. This will protect you.”
   She gave me a golden chain with a crested shield on it.
   “This is our Family Crest. I was given this from my foster mother, now I give it to you.”
   “I can’t take this.” I said
   “Please Drak, take it. I will do me no good. My time in this world is almost up…” she trailed off.
   “Don’t say that!”
   “Come, we must rest. You will need all your strength tomorrow.”
   That drove the fear into me even deeper. I had another Dream that night.

   This time Innoruuk was there from the beginning. He said nothing but just looked at me and grinned. I couldn’t move for some reason. Then Alanisa was there and He took her by the neck and I heard a terrible scream of true pain and terror. That is when I awoke…

Chapter 4
   The next day was a day I will never forget. I ate with Alanisa in silence and then we left for Greater Faydark. In the middle of the forest was when I got the chill that I had dreaded since the first dream. Innoruuk was there. Waiting for us, toying with us. Alanisa then began to chat and I was surrounded by a blue aura. She then seemed like she was talking to someone, telepathically. That’s when the dark one came. He stood there in his hunched form watching her. But the weird thing was he couldn’t see me. I watched in terror as he reached out and grabbed her by the neck. Even though I couldn’t hear anything, I knew what he was doing. He was talking through Alanisa to whomever it was she was talking to. Then it was all over. Innoruuk was gone and Alanisa lay there on the leaves, still as death. As soon as her spell wore off, I ran to her and held her. I do not know how long I was there. I was lost again, nothing, no one. That is when I somehow got in contact with Lord Finncanon. He told me to be strong and not to greave. I will always have a home with the Holy Order. That is when I Finally cried. Belmass Festival Recap - Prexus
Sent by: Sovereign Mikall Ravenmoon

The dust has settled and the discarded ale jugs cleared away, and as I sit here in Freeport gathering my thoughts regarding the Belmass festival that my family, the Moon Clans, hosted on Sunday the 19th, I can only think good thoughts.

The day started smoothly enough, with the Belmass Overseer Arbor Arjentia running around like a desert madman taking care of last minute details. People started gathering early in the tunnel, and the shouts of those wondering where the festival was to be held and what time it began echoed off the cool walls of the cavern. The nameless swashbuckler in the tunnel was still very nervous about the whole affair, but he fell silent at the prospect of making a lot of coin with the numerous citizens that were sure to attend. If only we knew what number that would grow to beforehand *sly smile*.

Once the 6th hour rang, the festival began. I gave a short speech to the assembled (at this point closing in on the 150 mark), and we started off Belmass by a concert by the VERY talented Bards Clef and his partner Sayeni. They had a HUGE audience sitting before them in the caverns, and I'm frankly surprised that they could stay focused on their beautiful music with all the shouting, dancing, and revelry going on around them. The Prexus citizens were hungry for a celebration...that much was certain.

The races and duels started soon after the performance began, with the duels being held in the Freeport arena and the races formed at the tunnel entrance outside. We also had wandering members giving out refreshments, a linguist giving free language training, and a fortune teller providing mystical services to the curious, as well as the Moon Clans Master Blacksmith Gadian at the forge in Freeport hard at work making items for those who were interested.

I began to sense trouble when the numbers in the East Commonlands grew to over 220, and my anxiety was proven true when suddenly the entire world went dark. It was as if a deep sleep was forced over all those in attendance....likely the work of the prankster Bristlebane. (ooc: Certain zones, including EC, crashed...probably due to the numbers involved...). Only a short time passed...no more than 20 minutes or so, before the veil of sleep was lifted and we all began to rise to our feet. We wanted to get things rolling as quickly as possible after the delay, so we hurriedly reformed the races and duels and made plans for the Belmass Beauty Pageant to begin in the cavern.

Soon after a majority of the events had ended and most of the action had moved to the tunnel entrance, we were blessed by the arrival of at least four members of the Order of Prexus (ooc: GM's). They stayed for some time...conversing with the citizens and watching the festivities around them. I'd like to personally thank all of them for stopping by and adding to our event.

(ooc: We next began our guild-sponsored quest) Belmass was not free from danger, nor were the leaders of the Moon Clans as we soon found out. The entire Lord Assembly was taken hostage, almost simultaneously, and groups were formed from those present at the festival to help find and free the captives before it was too late. I personally was taken deep within the dungeon of Befallen, while my other leaders were held in areas around Freeport including the West Commonlands and the Northern Ro desert. The rescue teams searched far and wide for clues to the kidnapping, some traveling as far as Rivervale and the Nektulos Forest for information. Luckily, the teams persevered, and all members of the Lord Assembly were rescued intact. The Moon Clans are investigating the reasons behind the attack and will hopefully discover those who gave the orders soon.

I was taken away by the goddess Erollisi to the OtherWorld shortly after my rescue (ooc: ISP problems), and so could not be present for the remainder of Belmass. I would like to send my heartfelt thanks and gratitude for all those who took part in the quest...you showed great patience and understanding and you all should be commended.

Although we had our rough spots, as any event of this scope is bound to have, I feel that everyone that took part came through with flying colors and it was a great success. This was the first time that my guild had attempted to run anything like this, and it was certainly a learning experience. Trust that we will take the mistakes of this festival and learn from them, which will only serve to improve future events that we plan to sponsor. Personal thanks go to those in the Moon Clans who helped with the design and running of the event: Arbor, Took, Tahoe, Torunt, Dorthur, Malicio, Smomb, Tendaila, Gadian, Moonriser, Nnore, Tharki, Sythanas, Ozure, Holden, and Cambron. Also, to those allies that helped us: Adunaphel, Elderan, Clef, Sayeni, and Oculus. I know there are plenty of names that I am forgetting (including our linguist, our fortune teller, and others), and I do apologize for that. When you have close to 250 adventurers in one place, it's hard to keep tabs on everyone *smile*. But your help *was* noticed and very much appreciated. We couldn't have done it without all of your support. Thank you all for helping make Belmass come to life!

Regards;
Sovereign Mikall Ravenmoon, guildleader of the Moon Clans

Straylight Saga Update 12:15 PM
Here is the latest about Rallos Zek's player run roleplaying epic:

Roleplay on Rallos Zek: The Straylight Saga

Tired of Naked Foot Races and Bazaars being your only source of roleplay on the Blue Servers? Tired of your assaults upon cities revolving around spamming outside the city walls and attacking the guards, while the real enemy stands around and watches under their blue name tags, untouchable?

If you are, then perhaps the Rallos Zek server is for you. Here is a synopsis of the Straylight's three major plotlines (and one minor plot with some growing interest):

The Straylight Gem

The only source of information on the Straylight Gem being the so-called Straylight Tome. An ancient book of Feir'Dal origin. Stolen by the Teir'Dal over a two hundred years ago and recently stolen back by the Erudites. Trying to ferry this tome to Felwithe, the Erudites were laid upon by a number of Teir'Dal at the Freeport Docks and slain ... but the captor of this tome was one of the Spurned and had no love for the Clerics of Innoruuk. Instead he gave the Book to a mysterious Tier'Dal child by the name of Sazami, a devout follower of Erollisi Marr.

The original thieves of Book, the Teir'Dal clergy, were wary that it might one day change hands again and they cast upon the Book all manner of enchantments. The Book could not be gated or teleported to prevent easy theft, just one such enchantment. And as Sazami plumbed the depths of the work, she slowly and unknowlingly was growing mad with hate and jealousy and rage. Fearing that even the Priests of Marr were turning against her, she stole the Book and fled into the sewers of Freeport.

Many weeks passed and both sides of the conflict searched for her ... eventually learning of her whereabouts in the sewers. Forces closed in on both sides. The protectors of Sazami were fortunate to find her first, but her madness was great and she fought them, thinking them enemies here to steal her treasure. Their attempts to subdue her was time spent in favour of the enemy forces and soon battle was engaged. For two hours the forces fought, but in the end a sister of the Blood Coven, Tenacious Val'Sharess, was able to kill Sazami and in the continuing melee, able to make off with the treasured Tome.

At this time, the Straylight Tome lies within the Spires of Neriak. Recent research has uncovered the true purpose of the Straylight Gem ... to rid all of Norrath, once and for all, of Cazic-Thule, Innoruuk, and Rallos Zek's creations. The Blood Coven wishes to destroy the Book, to keep it from falling into their would-be destructor's hands. The Clergy wishes to study the Book, for another great Gem exists, unenchanted, one that could be used to construct an opposite, a Gem to rid Norrath of all the races of Light. More information has come to the foreground, that papers on the original Gem's construction were stolen along with the Book from the Feir'Dal ... but that the Gnoll conspirators never handed them over. Neriak is eyeing Infected Paw with great interest now. And what does the Undead Knight, Tiram Saeger, in the Estate of Unrest have to do with all this? What information may he have possessed while he was a living breathing Paladin of Marr?

Mahlyce's Child

Mahlyce had an immaculate conception outside Qeynos. The Clergy of Neriak believe it to be the daughter of Innoruuk himself. The forces of Light believe the child to be the link to everlasting peace, the bond that will bring all the races together in unity.

Ordieth Lightblade leads the forces of Good. He is the strongest believer in what Kallystra, the child, represents. He seeks her, to turn her from the path the Teir'Dal wish to place her upon. He wishes to raise Kallystra as his own daughter, free from the supposed lies of the Teir'Dal clergy.

And what of Petunia, Ogre friend of Mahlyce, her closest companion. He has recently escorted the young Kallystra from Neriak to Oggok. He wishes to be her father, to teach her in the ways of Rallos Zek. He to believes Innoruuk is bad for the child, but only because of the pain and suffering he sees the God of Hate inflict daily upon his friend, Mahlyce.

And where does the Nameless come into all this?

Isili Thepa (The Minions of Thepa)

In preparation for the EverQuest Expansion (introducing a race of Lizards), Amano Jyaku has created a wonderful plotline revolving around a Lizard Demon, Isili Thepa, that, much like a plague, is taking over the minds of anyone it comes in contact with, Amano being the first of the Taken. As yet, the demon's plans are unfolding and much is unclear. The only clarirty being the amount of pain and suffering the demon wishes to inflict upon the world. Here is a short snippet by one of the most recent Taken, Zifeer:

Bica Thepa! Isili sel’a tosa zekka!

After some initial confusion, I find myself fitting in to Faydark life well. These ‘Elves’ are a curious breed, how they bicker so. They seem full of unsatisfied needs and wants. I have learned a new skill, Master. This puppet used to do a great deal of something it called ‘helping’, and I am trying this out.

“Have you got any spare food?” they bleat. Ah, I think, ‘zekka!’ At first I believed they meant food in the sense you do, Master, and simply went about my merry business providing them with a private little world of pain and misery. (The ears of these ‘Elves’ are particularly sensitive, yet stretch for inches before ripping.) Then I came to realise they meant nourishment of a different kind. My baking skills have improved, and I get a strange fuzzy feeling inside as the young wood elf wanders away munching greedily on the fine repast that I have prepared for him, using the meat of his kin. Indeed, I fear I may be starting a cultural phenomenon here, many come back for more. I keep my recipe secret.

(Did you know that they come in three flavours? Wood Elves are distinctly spicier in taste and are good smoked, whereas High Elves are blander, but work well with white wine. The Half Elf variety is more filling, but somewhat stodgy.)

Of course, the meat must be drained first, and I am never one to let nature’s goodness go to waste. I find I can now turn one bottle of white wine into two bottles of red, and again, my new friends are grateful.

The Church of the Nameless

This is a minor plot that is gathering some interest. Created by Valarea, it revolves around the Father of all the Gods, the Nameless. Already, other false prophets, such as Leexa, are popping up, spouting their own brand of Nameless doctrine. Or do we have that backwards? Who knows for sure who or what the Nameless truly represents.

Petunia is most recently a convert and wishes that Mahlyce daughter, Kallystra, become a great leader in this new burgeoning religion.
