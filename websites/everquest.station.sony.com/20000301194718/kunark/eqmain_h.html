February 25, 2000 - Friday
 
 

RoK Preview 9:40 AM
The gaming news site, CoolInfo.com, has posted a preview of the EverQuest: Ruins of Kunark expansion. Here is a bit of what they had to say:

"The graphics in Kunark, compared with the rest of EQ, have undergone a serious overhaul. Sporting over three-times the polygon count of the other continents, Verant is aiming to not only please with enthralling gameplay, but with plenty of eye-candy, as well. For the less fortunate -- those who don't have the hardware to enjoy the pretty new face-lift -- there will be a plethora of 3D settings available, allowing you to tweak the performance level to your liking."
  

February 22, 2000 - Tuesday
 
  IGNPC Kunark Interview 2:50 PM
Tal Blevins, of IGNPC, recently wrangled an interview from Brad McQuaid. Much of what they discussed covered The Ruins of Kunark. Here is a sample:
IGNPC: One of the biggest additions for EverQuest players in the expansion pack will undoubtedly be the addition of the Lizardman race the Iksar. Tell us a bit about them and their homeland of Kunark.

Brad McQuaid: The Iksar are a neutral-evil race of Lizardmen who once ruled Kunark with an iron fist. Their empire has since declined, leaving ruins of their once great cities strewn across the continent, inhabited by exotic races and monsters.

 

February 11, 2000 - Friday
 
  Kunark News  1:50 PM
The FAQ at the Ruins of Kunark website has been updated, including this information:
1.5 What opportunity will existing EverQuest customers have to upgrade to the Expansion?

All existing EverQuest customers will be given the opportunity to upgrade to the Expansion directly (via the Login server) and at a discounted upgrade-only rate. This opportunity will most likely happen during the month of April, prior to launch. The upgrade package will include an upgrade CD-ROM and a new world map.

IMPORTANT: The retail EverQuest box will be replaced with a new box that will include both EverQuest and the Expansion. You may have already begun to see advertisements for this box at a regular retail price. If you are already an existing EverQuest customer you do NOT need to purchase the box at retail -- you will be given an opportunity to buy an upgrade only package directly and at a discounted price.

Pricing for the upgrade will be announced shortly.

 		 

January 11, 2000 - Tuesday
 
 

Gamespot Honors EQ 2:50 PM
It's not Kunark news, but we are still proud to cover it. EverQuest has been selected as Gamespot's Game of The Year for 1999. Here are some quotes from the article:

"GameSpot broke with tradition this year by awarding its highest honor exclusively to EverQuest"

And...

"Following EverQuest's release in March, the whole gaming industry effectively ground to a halt. At least one prominent game developer blamed EverQuest for product delays, and for several weeks GameSpot's editors were spending more time exploring Norrath than they were doing their jobs. That's because exploring the world Verant created is an incredible experience, especially when you can do so with friends. And knowing that Norrath will continue to grow, both through Verant's continual addition of new zones and the forthcoming expansion, only adds to the impact of the game."

November 22, 1999 - Monday
 
 

Tanarus Free Weekend 2:30 PM
Here is the Press Release regarding our Free Play Weekend on Tanarus:

VERANT INTERACTIVE ANNOUNCES FREE TANARUS™ THANKSGIVING WEEKEND

SAN DIEGO, Calif., November 22, 1999 -
Verant Interactive Inc.™, a premiere online gaming developer, announced that, in honor of Thanksgiving, Tanarus will be opened to non-subscribers at no charge. The free weekend will start Wednesday (11/24) at noon Pacific Time, and end the morning of Monday (11/29). Verant's Tanarus is a massively multi-player online game featuring futuristic tank combat.

Tanarus has been heralded by reviewers as, "highly addictive" and "an example of what Internet gaming should be about". Featuring multiple cities, a variety of completely customizable lethal tanks and unmatched team play, Tanarus pits players spanning the globe against each other in a real time battle to control cities through teamwork and heavy firepower. Fighting in ever-changing, dynamic battlefields that range from post-apocalyptic cities to industrial wastelands, players have more than 30 different weapons and modules at their disposal. Emphasizing strategy and skill, Tanarus remains one of the premier action titles on the Internet.

For more information and for the free Tanarus download visit http://www.verant.com/tanarus_promo.html

Verant Interactive is an independent development studio that develops cutting-edge online games. Based in San Diego, California, Verant Interactive has a staff of over 70 employees who consist of the development teams responsible for the online games EverQuest®, Tanarus™, and Sovereign™.
