The EverQuest Webmaster

We've been getting a lot of mail lately at the eqwebmaster@verant.com address that we really should not be getting. This address can be used for many things, but there are quite a few things it should NOT be used for. We thought the article Steve Fuller wrote on email addresses would have helped to alleviate this problem. In case you missed it, here's a recap.

If you're having an in-game issue with your account, please contact eqmail@verant.com
If you're having a technical problem with the game, such as disconnections or video problems, please contact eqtech@verant.com
If you have feedback or a suggestion about the game, in whatever form, send it to eqfeedback@verant.com
If you wish to inquire about a suspended or banned account, send email to eqaccountstatus@verant.com

We can not and will not answer inquiries about patch messages, server downtime, connectivity or anything else not having to do with the web sites.

The Webmaster address goes to the web content people, and we have NO information on ANY of the above issues. They are completely separate departments.

Things you can and should email the webmaster about:

Web page errors, whether they be informational or code issues
Game related events, whether they be in-game, or out of game
Game related news bits, such as reports of in game events
Feedback about the webpages, and suggestions as to what you might like to see on them

Thanks for your understanding!

1/23/01

   
A Reminder

EQ Customer Service has reported that the Technical Support Representatives are being inundated with calls for in-game assistance. Technical Support can not help with any in-game related issue. Technical Support are not GM and can only offer technical support for technical issues. Any game related issue that is not a technical problem must be petitioned in game for a Game Master to handle. If a customer calls technical support for an in-game related issue such as lost items, experience, or any such issue that can be handled in game by a GM, all the technical support rep can do is advise the caller to petition in game. Please only call technical support for issues of a technical nature. They can not help with any in-game issues.

EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark  
Prepare to enter an enormous virtual environment; an entire world with its own diverse species, economic systems, alliances, and politics. Choose from a variety of races and classes, and begin your quest in any number of cities or villages throughout several continents. From there, equip yourself for adventure, seek allies and knowledge, and head out into a rich world of dungeons, towers, crypts, evil abbeys -- anything conceivable -- even planes and realities beyond your imagination. Learn skills, earn experience, acquire treasure and equipment, meet friends and encounter enemies. A multitude of quests and adventures await, but you choose your role, you define your destiny.

  EverQuest: The Scars of Velious
 
Veeshan the great Crystalline Dragon marked Norrath by striking the continent of Velious with her foreclaw, and deposited her brood there to dwell. The legends of that time also tell that Veeshan blasted Velious with her breath, encasing it in eternal ice and creating a barrier of deadly ice floes and stormy weather. This marked the beginning of the Age of Scale.
