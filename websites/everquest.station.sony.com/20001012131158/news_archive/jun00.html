Storms billowed beneath a malevolent sky. Mordeki's steed could barely be seen within the fold of night, black upon black. His long ebon cloak shielded his obsidian armor, and a cowl tapered his sinisterly chiseled features. He spurred his steed forward as he descended the valley in the Rathe mountains to where he saw the encampment's glowing embers that burned into the midnight sky.

Sometime later the proud shadow knight of the House of Dra'Vedd stood before the old crone, Madame Eva. "tell me what you see..." he inquired as he sat across the table from the gypsy. Odd trinkets hung throughout the wagon. Skulls, swirling beakers that overflowed with mists, charms, sigils, and other strange things that laid trapped in the confines of jars and other containers.

Upon a velvet cushion the crytal ball filled with a smokey hue. "I see the son of hate will rise up. He is strong and his hands run red with blood. His one is touched with a particular venom", she spoke with her eyes focused still upon the smokey sphere. She stammered a bit, "Vengefull spirits from the beyond..."

The cowl concealed the features of the Teir Dal whose hate began to boil, "Bah I do not have time for spirits...tell m of the book...I must have it...". The dark elves lips turned upwards in a snarl beneath the hood of his cloak as his hatred mounted. It was then a cold wind blew through the wagon. An inky cloud of blackness filled the wagon and their was silence.

Mordeki sat atop a hill overlooking the encampment, a black bound tome folded under one arm covered with the face of what seemed a groaning spirit carved out of its "hide" cover. The flash of crimson orbs and haunting laughter filled the air as the child of Dar'Vedd crossed spurred his steed into the night and vanished into its embrace.

In the wagon the sage gypsy muttered her last breath of life. "I curse you son of Dra'Vedd...may the eye of Mal'Arigar swollow you whole with the unborn son of the futures wrath...", she gurgled past dying lips...and faded into the void of death.

Off on the horizon were two brothers, both the heads of armies. One who embraced Hate and the other Valor. Now would be the dark lord to come, and his army would bring damnation in its wake. The cursed son of a misbegotten house...and now the servant of armageddon itself. This was the future...and written upon it were not just the dogs of war, but the demons who preyed upon them.
