Deities

According to an early chat with the EQ design team on EFnet, the choice of worship will influence gameplay.  In practice, this is reflected by the inherent Factions of the game, the intricate web of NPC's who judge a character initially by their race and profession, and continuously judge a character by their ongoing actions.  Tread carefully.

The history of Norrath tells of the great dragons, great godlike beings who were among the first races. Consider this quote from the history, which describes the rulership of the Plane of Sky by Veeshan:

It is said that thousands of years ago the world known as Norrath was "noticed" by Veeshan, the great Crystalline Dragon who rules the Plane of Sky. Pleased with the conditions on Norrath , Veeshan deposited her brood onto the frozen continent of Velious. Then, with one swipe of her mighty claws, Veeshan opened several great wounds on the surface of Norrath, thus staking her claim to this promising new world.

There is also the Plane of Underfoot, which is ruled by Brell Serilis, Duke of Underfoot, who used a magical portal to bridge the mystical gap between Norrath's underground expanses and his home plane. Through this magical gate, he begat the underdwelling races and creatures that call the darkness their homes. It is a commonly told tavern tale that the magical gate might still exist, protected by a labyrinth or Living Stone...

Other Planes of Power & Influence exist, and thus far we have been enlightened as to the existence of these beings:

  • Innoruuk, Prince of Hate, who rules a dark, cruel, dangerous kingdom of the Plane of Hate. He is accredited with (or blamed for) the creation of the Dark Elves.
  • Fennin Ro, Tyrant of Fire, who rules the plane of Fire, which is the blackened, burning home of the fire elementals and other fiery denizens.
  • Solusek Ro, the son of Fennin Ro, who rules the Plane of Flames. It is not known whether this being is merely a "demigod" or how much influence over the domain of heat can be called his own.
  • Brell Serilis, Duke of Underfoot, who is credited with creating all underground dwelling races, such as the Dwarves and Gnomes.
  • Rallos Zek, the Warlord, whose Plane of War is in constant flux, reflecting the eternal battles being waged. His attributes include jet black skin, "lava-like" bulging veins, and the iron fisted rulership of a tyrant of the battlefield.
  • Bristlebane, King of Thieves, whose free-spirited followers strive for the witty mischief that their deity embodies.   Bards, jesters, individualistic gypsies, and many other festive folk emulate Bristlebane's pranks and antics.
  • Cazic-Thule, the Faceless, who exists only for to inflict horror, misery, fear, and torturous violence, destroying hope.
  • Karana, the Rainkeeper, whose followers respect the might of the storm, from both the nourishing rain to the destructive sandstorm.  Many are nomadic, following the winds, besides the settled ranchers and farmers.
  • Erollisi Marr, the Queen of Love, whose worshippers are devoted to the passion of love, but who are more than willing to die to defend those places, people, and ideals which they so dearly love if the need arises.
  • Rodcet Nife, the Prime Healer, instills humility and kindness in his followers, who seek to combat disease and death at all costs.  The symptom is not merely to be treated; they seek to eliminate the cause of an outbreak.
  • Prexus, the Oceanlord, whose followers feel an affinity for being near large bodies of water, such as many sailors and fisherman  They believe that the powerful ocean once spawned all life and that one day it will re-take its creations. 
  • Quellious, the Tranquil, whose enlightened followers seek internal peace, often living nomadically to better learn of the world and thus themselves.  Many a monk walks the path of Quellious.
  • Tarew Marr, one of the three Lords of the Plane of Water, of which little is known.  He apparently is connected to Erollisi Marr and to Mithaniel Marr in some debated manner.
  • Mithaniel Marr, the Lightbringer, brother to Erollisi Marr. Many Paladins follow Mithaniel, who personifies the ascension of "Good" and the absolute destruction of "Evil."
  • Bertoxxulous, the Plagebringer, whose followers often include Necromancers, and who embodies the spread of death, decay, and disease to the lands of Norrath.
  • Tunare, the Mother of All, who is believed to have been created by the living entity of Norrath by her followers, and who in turn created all of them.  Many Elves, Druids, and Rangers follow this deity.
  • The Tribunal, the Six Hammers, a beloved deity of the hardy Barbarians, and the embodiment of Justice.  The followers of the Tribunal are sometimes seen as mercenaries, because they follow no court's decisions, but the words of the Tribunal in the dispensing of Justice.
