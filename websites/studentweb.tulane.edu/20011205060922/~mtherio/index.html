Project Synopsis:

            The data parsing and synchronization device to be developed is an integral component of the Three-dimensional Acoustic Tracking System being developed jointly with Rachel Stone and Chris Hardee. The purpose of the system is to identify a sound source somewhere in a pre-defined three-dimensional space, and track it. This is to be accomplished by use of an array of acoustic sensors (either all hardware or hardware-based simulation) being built by Ms. Stone. The sensors with receive sampling synchronization signals from the data parsing and synchronization device, which will in turn read in positional vector information from the sensors. Data will then be organized and sent on to the computer-interface device being built by Mr. Hardee. The data will be processed by the computer, and the calculated positional information will be displayed.
