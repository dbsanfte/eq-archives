AOL Connection Issues

A few points on AOL:

  • The AOL software controls the modem, protocols and flow control.
  • AOL bypasses any Windows settings concerning the modem including initialization strings.
  • AOL servers have a limited number of first-come-first-served 56k modems.
  • Any late log-ins get 33.6k and 28.8k connections.
  • AOL customers can be "bumped" from their server... first in means first out.
  • AOL connections are timed.  Every 45 minutes online the customer will get a pop-up that they must click on in less than 15 minutes or be disconnected, even while downloading.
  • AOL mailboxes are small and tightly controlled. An AOL customer cannot receive an email larger than 1.5MB, and may not keep more than 10MB in their box. If larger mails come in they may never get to the customer or may not be intact if they do make it through. Also, AOL uses dumbed-out anti-spam software that will block many incoming greeting cards, etc.
  • Some AOL technicians will insist that your modem has an incorrect UART (Universal Asynchronous Receiver/Transmitter) setting.  Call your modem's manufacturer to determine if you have a hardware or software modem (Winmodem). In order to have a UART you must have a hardware modem.  Hence any Winmodem CANNOT have an incorrect UART setting because there is no UART.

Here is what you can do as an AOL customer:

1. Contact your local phone company's repair center and request a "Point to Point Line Test" (PTPLT) between the phone number the modem uses and the phone number of the internet connection.  The phone company will pulse the lines to find flaws and perform necessary repairs at their own expense.  The average dial-up will go through up to 30 connections before reaching the server.  All it takes is noise or corruption on one of those 30 lines to hobble the connection.  Most phone companies guarantee a minimum connect speed and quality.

2. Contact local electronics shops to purchase a "Noise Reducing Phone Line" (NRPL).  This looks much like a standard phone line (usually 6 feet in length) that has a rubber "U" on the line near one end.  This is a resistor about the size of a thumbnail.  Use of this device usually produces an extra 2000 - 6000bps and may be placed in-line with longer phone lines.  Average cost is $1.00.

3. Contact AOL technical support at 1-800-827-6364 for assistance with uninstalling and reinstalling the AOL software. Be sure to pressure them into a FULL uninstallation, including the AOL Dial-Up Adapter and the file folders.

If none of the above accomplish anything it is recommended that you change your ISP.  Not all ISP's are created equally, nor are their equipment and interest in customer satisfaction.  Some of the higher quality mainstream ISP's are Mindspring, Flashnet, Earthlink, WT-Net, CyberRamp and CoolLink.
