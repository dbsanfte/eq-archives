Slow Connection Frequent Disconnection Tool

If you are using AOL as your ISP click here.

1. Goto Start | Settings | Control Panel | Modems | Properties | Connection | Advanced. 

Software Modems: Put a dot in "Software (Xon/Xoff), add "AT&F1" to Extra Settings, put a check in "Append to log".  Click "Ok", "Ok" again then "Close". 

Hardware Modems: Add "AT&F0" (zero) to Extra Settings, put a check in "Append to log".  Click "Ok", "Ok" again then "Close".

Click here to see more available "AT" initialization commands.

2. Contact your local phone company's repair center and request a "Point to Point Line Test" (PTPLT) between the phone number the modem uses and the phone number of the internet connection.  The phone company will pulse the lines to find flaws and perform necessary repairs at their own expense.  The average dial-up will go through up to 30 connections before reaching the server.  All it takes is noise or corruption on one of those 30 lines to hobble the connection.  Most phone companies guarantee a minimum connect speed and quality.

3. Contact local electronics shops to purchase a "Noise Reducing Phone Line" (NRPL).  This looks much like a standard phone line (usually 6 feet in length) that has a rubber "U" on the line near one end.  This is a resistor about the size of a thumbnail.  Use of this device usually produces an extra 2000 - 6000bps and may be placed in-line with longer phone lines.  Average cost is $1.00.

4. Once the first 3 steps are completed call your ISP and ask them to verify your protocols.  They will walk you through this.  Sometimes having just one incorrect protocol setting can cut connection quality in half.

     The goal is to accomplish a 40,666bps or better connection speed with fewer than once per hour disconnects.  If none of the above accomplish that it is recommended that you change your ISP.  Not all ISP's are created equally, nor are their equipment and interest in customer satisfaction.  Some of the higher quality mainstream ISP's are Mindspring, Flashnet, Earthlink, WT-Net, CyberRamp  and CoolLink
