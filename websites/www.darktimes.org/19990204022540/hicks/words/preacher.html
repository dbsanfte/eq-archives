A LITTLE DARK POET...
GARTH ENNIS
From Issue #31. Reprinted without permission.

My admiration and respect for Bill Hicks and his work are second to none, and when the opportunity to have him make an appearance in PREACHER arose, I grabbed it with both hands. This guy was - is - one of my very few true heroes, and I'd like to take a little time out here to explain why.

I've never heard anyone or anything quite like him, before or since. On his recordings, in his live shows (one of which it was my privilege to see) Bill Hicks told truths and asked questions and gave answers in a way that left me both grateful and stunned. Grateful, because - as Jesse says in this issue - I was glad someone was there to raise and comment on these issues; stunned, because he managed to do so while damn near making me laugh my guts out.





Page 10 - Page 11 - Page 12

I can't possibly do the man justice in a few paragraphs - in fact, I couldn't do Bill and his career justice if I wrote from now 'til doomsday. Instead, let me recommend you check out his work on tape or CD. Currently available from Rykodisc are Dangerous, Relentless, Arizona Bay and my personal favourite, Rant in E-Minor. Try 'em, folks. I very much doubt you'll regret it.

I cannot recommend Bill Hicks's work highly enough, people. At his heart, I think he came from an earlier time: in a world of empty sound bites and thirty-second heroes, this is a comedian worth listening to, and a man worth remembering.
