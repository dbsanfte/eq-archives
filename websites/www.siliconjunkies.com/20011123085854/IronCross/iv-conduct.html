IV. CONDUCT

 

A. EVERQUEST RULES OF CONDUCT: 

Guild members may not participate in any activities that would violate the rules of conduct established by Verant Interactive. These activities are identified as those that could result in suspension or banning of any account, and include, but are not limited to:

  1. Kill stealing
  2. Ninja-Looting
  3. Accessing any account without permission
  4. Direct in-game or out-of-game threats
  5. Disruption of any zone and/or the activities of other players by means of intentional misconduct
  6. Soliciting, encouraging, and/or participating in any criminal activity

B. GUILD RULES: 

All guild members are expected to abide by and uphold the ideals and rules as set forth in this charter.

C. CHAIN OF COMMAND:

  1. Requests, complaints and other relevant matters must go through the chain of command. Guild members direct matters to their Class Leaders. Class Leaders direct matters to the High Council. Additionally, orders, decisions, and directives from superiors must be followed and supported by subordinates whether they agree with them at the time or not .
  2. The purpose of the chain of command is not intended to declare some people as being better than others. Rather, is merely a means to establish organization and consistency with guild functions.
  3. The purpose of the chain of command is not to establish special privileges or loot rights over other guild members. To attempt to claim any under color of authority is a violation of this rule, as well as the fundamental trust of every member within guild.
  4. The guild's leadership structure has three tiers:
    1. The High Council
    2. Class Leaders
    3. Remaining Guild Members

D. MIGHT DOES NOT EQUAL RIGHT: 

When interacting and grouping with non-guild members, the presence of Iron Cross en masse does not warrant treating others unfairly. The rights and views of others must always be respected. Ensuring fairness and equality for all participants is the responsibility of every guild member. The manner in which we treat others directly reflects on our integrity and respect within the community of the server. Fairness for all must always be maintained, even if it may not be convenient for us at the time.

E. ARGUMENTS: 

Instigating and/or fueling arguments, both public and private, will not be tolerated. The stance should always to be diffuse a situation and maintain a demeanor of respect and open mindedness, even to those who do not deserve it. The responsibility for doing so should deferred to the Class Leader or High Council member on scene. Compromising and diffusing situations should not be viewed as a sign of weakness, rather, the first important step to returning to enjoying the game.

F. OPEN FLAMING AND VERBAL ABUSE: 

Openly flaming and verbally abusing people in any manner will not be tolerated. It is perfectly fine to agree to disagree and debate any matter, however, it must be done so in a manner of respect and free of any name calling and derogatory remarks.

G. PROVIDING AID: 

Never refuse the request of assistance from those in distress or having difficulties, especially if those people are fellow guild members. Rendering aid to those in need is always top priority and takes precedent over all other things when it involves Iron Cross members. Requests for assistance is handled within reason when it involves those from outside the guild, however, guild status alone should not be a basis of refusing aid. If in doubt, consult High Council members online at the time, and do unto others as you would have them do for you.

H. PERSONAL PRIVACY: 

The privacy of others should always be protected. Account and other personal information should not be openly broadcasted in any manner, even if the subject of that information is the one doing the broadcasting. Additionally, details regarding diverse lifestyles and interests should be kept to oneself, especially if such things would be deemed by some as socially unacceptable and/or offensive.

I. RESOLVING MATTERS:

When differences arise, all persons involved should be afforded the opportunity and time to debate the issues and come to some reasonable compromise in resolving matters. Class Leaders and/or High Council members should act as mediators when necessary and all discussions should be handled on a courteous and professional level. Matters of guild import should be addressed to the High Council so that they may convene and vote on appropriate course of action to take. Bear in mind that no issues can be resolved if concerns aren't voiced, and resolution of matters may take time before tangible progress is evident.

  1. Acts done in protest that affect guild standing of any member or guild resources held in trust are not an acceptable way of handling matters. Such acts include, but are not limited to:
    1. Guild removal
    2. Theft of guild property
  2. In the case of guild removal, such instances are only acceptable course of action when all sides have been afforded enough time and opportunity to resolve any outstanding matter, and the outcome, or lack there of, is found unacceptable or intolerable for one side or the other.
  3. Acts done in protest may result in re-evaluation of guild standing and the imposition of a probationary period prior to reinvite, as determined by the High Council and Class Leader involved.
