V. LOOT

 

A. DEFINITION: 

The term "loot" as referred to within this charter, will encompass all things within game that will add to any character's in-game wealth and/or effectiveness, no matter it's level of significance. This includes both dropped and questable items.

B. DEFAULT LOOT RULES: 

The default loot rules that shall be used in all Iron Cross events are as follows:

  1. Plat items are to be awarded to clerics and other classes casting spells requiring the expenditure of components at a ratio of no less than three(3) to one(1) with those classes not using components. This means, for example, that for every three plat items a cleric would receive, everyone else within group will receive one.
  2. High end gems, specifically diamonds, blue diamonds, jacinth, and black sapphires, are to be randomed openly with all participants and as a separate category from everything else.
  3. All quest items are randomed openly with all participants that need them. This will be as a separate category from everything else, unless the quest item itself falls within any other category, as listed within this charter. For example, winning a black sapphires (needed for a step in the Veeshan's Peak Key quest) would be considered an ordinary high end gem won and not a quest item.
  4. Spells are to randomed among all participants that do not have and as a separate category. If no one present needs, then they are randomed among all participants while still maintaining a separate category.
  5. Single-class and "No Drop" items are to be randomed among all classes that need and as a separate category, as the items themselves are meaningless to any except those that can use. Droppable items that no one needs become standard loot as described in V.B.6. below.
  6. Multi-Class items and all other droppable items are to be randomed among all participants and as a separate category.
  7. The term "default" in the context of these loot rules refers to standard practice in the interest of fairness during normal game play.
  8. Any deviation to the above standard loot rules must be be reasonably justified and announced ahead of time to all participants. This includes meriting items for whatever reason.
  9. It is common practice to honor last piece rule for suits of armor and quest items, and are randomed only among those that are on the same last piece.
    1. This is a merit issue which always needs to be announced ahead of time to non-IC members who join our events.
    2. Last piece rule is always honored, even if the person won an item already during the same event.

C. DISTRIBUTION OF GUILD ACQUIRED LOOT: 

The term "guild acquired" refers to anything acquired or accomplished during an Iron Cross event, as defined in VI. GUILD EVENTS below.

  1. The practice of open random for loot items is merely an exercise of fairness for those not within our guild, as we cannot expect others to always concur with our philosophies regarding loot.
  2. Iron Cross guild members who win an item wins it for the guild in general, and that item is subject to distribution to guild members as the Class Leader(s) and/or High Council sees fit.
  3. First priority to receive any item acquired goes to those people who will use it and are actually present at the time of the drop. If no mutual consensus can establish an order for looting, then it defaults to an open random to those that will actually use the item.
    1. The term "use" in this context applies to the actual wearing or wielding of an item, whether under regular or situational use conditions, for someone's primary character only.
  4. If no one present has need of the item, then it is surrendered to the appropriate Class Leader or High Council member, who will then determine who within guild will make use of the item.
  5. In the case of droppable items that would be awarded to new members currently under a probationary period, those items are to be given to a High Council member to safeguard until such time that the probationary period has ended, or until the awardee has been judged to have earned the item.
  6. If it is determined that no member within guild will have use of any particular item, then the item is returned to the person who originally won it to do what ever they want with it.
  7. Drop-able items that are replaced by a newly won item are surrendered to the appropriate Class Leader or High Council member to distribute to other guild members as needed, provided the item replaced would no longer be used by the person receiving the new item.

D. ALTERNATE CHARACTERS: 

The following rules apply to all alternate characters regarding loot issues, whether guilded with Iron Cross or not, and whether the only alternate present or within a entire group or more of Iron Cross alternate characters.

  1. No Iron Cross alternate character shall ever be allowed to compete with Iron Cross primary characters in any manner, wether in regards to loot or implementation of guild resources, except in matters that would directly affect and improve that alternate's primary character. For example, a caster who plays their tank alt, would not be allowed to compete for tank items, but would be allowed to compete for caster items if that person's primary character would actually use the item. Terms under V.C.6. and V.C.7. still apply.
  2. In the case of no-drop items, the alternate must defer any items won through random to Iron Cross primary characters present first, before eligibility to loot is permitted for that alternate.
  3. Exceptions can be made in instances where a particular class is absolutely needed in order to accomplish any given guild event. In such cases, High Council approval is required and the alternate may either chose to compete for items that apply to that person's alternate character OR primary character. Not both. Items acquired for the class not chosen will be subject to guild distribution rules and the person in question will not be eligible to receive those items at all.
  4. The rules prohibiting alternates from competing against primaries do not apply in zones that are predominantly "green" to 55th level groups, or portions thereof. The rule always applies to any item encompassed by Eminent Domain. There are too many zones to list here, however, some examples where the rule does apply that alts may not compete against primaries are as follows:
    1. Howling Stones
    2. Sebilis
    3. Chardok
    4. Western Wastelands
    5. Any Alternate Plane (Hate, Fear, Growth, Mischief)
    6. Fire Giant and bug area of Sol-B

E. VIOLATION OF THESE RULES: 

The fundamental idea behind our guild charter is to place enjoyment of the game over all other things, especially the arbitrary value placed on in-game items. Enjoyment of the game revolves around improvement of one's primary character and success in things we all work together on. The sense of accomplishment and trust we instill within each other with the things we do and the way we interact will last much longer than the Everquest game ever will.

The most noteable actions that would damage the enjoyment of the game and fundamental trust between people has to do with the misappropriation of loot and the hard feelings that are associated with those occurrences. The establishment of these rules is intended to prevent mishandling of loot and clearly identify everyone's eligibility to receive the same. It is a standing order that all members within Iron Cross agree to abide by. It is a responsibility we will all share in ensuring they are enforced because we know it is in our best interest of fairness to do so.

With this is mind, intentional violations of these loot rules will be considered a violation of trust and will not be tolerated or allowed to go unanswered. The High Council will work with anyone in resolving injustices or correcting mistakes, however, in the event that a speedy resolution can not be accomplished, we can and will /guildremove and/or /petition for ninja-looting as the circumstances warrant. While this may seem harsh to some, understand that it is a message that those who participate in such activities are not welcomed within our ranks, and is intended to protect and advocate the rights of those who fall victim to them.

F. EMINENT DOMAIN: 

The High Council will, until further notice, apply eminent domain over the following items. This means that no matter how they are acquired, they must be surrendered to High Council to be used for the declared purpose.

  1. DIAMONDS and BLUE DIAMONDS: All of these acquired shall be turned over to High Council or Kalaan so that they may be made in to the appropriate resist gear items that Iron Cross members require to be effective for various raids. This decree shall be lifted when every member within guild is adequately equipped with these pieces of resist items.
  2. EPIC QUEST ITEMS: Any epic quest item that is drop-able must be surrendered to the appropriate Class Leader, if single classed, or High Council member, if used for multiple class quests, for safeguarding. Distribution of these items shall be to those who require it as their last piece to complete any epic quest or step within. Implementation of this is not required if sufficient quantities are obtained to equip every member within the classes in question.
  3. ITEMS OF GREAT SIGNIFICANCE: Items such as Cloak of Flames, Manarobes, and other highly coveted, extremely rare, and drop-able items, that are acquired through guild events, shall never lose their status as guild property. Use of such items is based upon online presence and good standing within guild membership and is subject to distribution as determined by the High Council.
    1. Permanent ownership of these items may be awarded to any guild member through unanimous High Council vote.
    2. Permanent ownership of these items may be awarded to High Council members once unanimous High Council vote and majority Class Leader vote is obtained, with no "No" votes present.
    3. Items will be declared by High Council as an item of significance at the time of the drop, or at such time as High Council is informed that the item has been acquired.
    4. Votes requesting for permanent ownership may not be held more than once per 30 day period.

G. CLASSIFICATION OF ITEMS:

  1. HASTE: Items with haste are considered a tank class only item, and non-hybrid casters are not eligible to compete for or receive these items.
    1. Significant haste bonuses, those greater than 20%, take precedent over all other stat considerations. For example, if a pair of boots gave 10 intelligence and 40% haste, it would be considered a tank-only item, because of the haste, and not a tank + caster item.
    2. This precedent does not apply if all eligible tanks possess an item with equivalent or better haste bonus.
  2. INTELLIGENCE and WISDOM: Items with significant bonuses in Intelligence and/or Wisdom are considered caster items for the appropriate classes, and tanks may not compete for or receive these items.
  3. CHARISMA: Bard, Magician and Shaman Class Leaders may review these items on a case by case basis to determine suitability for their class to use, as many of their spell effects are affected by this statistic. Suitability for use will determine eligibility to compete for these items.
  4. STRENGTH and ARMOR CLASS: Items with significant bonuses to Strength and/or AC are considered a tank item, and non-hybrid casters may not compete for or receive these items.
  5. One or more of the above conditions (V.G.1.-4.) may apply to any item at the same time. All classes that apply to the appropriate conditions are eligible to receive. For example, the "Belt of Dwarf Slaying" is an item all classes are eligible to roll for.
  6. Classification of any particular item is no longer applied if all eligible members have a comparable item with equivalent or greater stat bonuses.

H. GUILD PROPERTY:

  1. Items acquired during guild events or added to guild treasury are deemed guild property and subject to distribution to guild members as Class Leaders and/or High Council sees fit.
  2. Tentative ownership of items are transferred to the guild member at the time it is distributed to them for a period of 30 days.
    1. V.C.3.a. is the basis of awarding any item and only guild primaries are considered.
    2. If V.C.3.a. does not apply to any guild primary, then the item is no longer considered guild property.
    3. If an item is awarded and V.C.3.a. no longer applies to the awardee for that particular item, then it must be returned to guild treasury for distribution to another member.
    4. Ownership of item becomes permanent after the 30 day time period ends so long as V.C.3.a. applies.
  3. Guild property may not be traded or sold, except through express permission from a higher tier within guild structure.
    1. Guild members must acquire approval from their Class Leader only. Class Leaders of other classes may not authorize trading of guild property for members not within their class.
    2. Class Leaders must acquire approval from the High Council member online.
    3. Items of great significance (e.g., Cloak of Flames, Manarobe, etc) require High Council vote before any such item is traded away.
    4. In general, guild property may only be traded if it would be considered trading up, or if a greater benefit can be obtained from the item being traded for.
  4. Items being replaced by a new item, where V.C.3.a. no longer applies, becomes guild property and is subject to guild distribution.
  5. Retaining items by members under terms as stated in V.C.3.a. is expected, and required, at all times for that member's primary character. Basically, if one says they will use it under situational or regular use conditions, then they are expected to do so for their primary, and at no times should it apply to one's alternate character.
    1. Items not used for their declared use must be returned to guild treasury for distribution to another guild member. For example, if one accepts a crown on the basis of using it for fire resistance, then they are expected to do so when increased fire resistance is called for.
  6. In the event that any member leaves guild, all items identified by these rules as guild property must be returned at the time of guild removal. These include the following types of items:
    1. Items possessed under tentative ownership (possessed for less than 30 days) as stated in V.H.2.a-d.
    2. Items subject to Eminent Domain:
      • Diamonds and Blue Diamonds
      • Drop-able Epic Quest Items
      • Items of Significance
    3. Items held in trust where guild distribution as not been determine.
    4. All items and moneys belonging to Guild Treasury that was held in trust.
