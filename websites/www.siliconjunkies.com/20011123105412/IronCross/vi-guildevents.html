VI. GUILD EVENTS

 

A. DEFINITION: 

A "guild event" is defined as any activity where two or more guild members participate in. This definition applies whether Iron Cross is hosting the event or not and regardless of whether characters played by members are alternates or primaries. This encompasses all in-game activities, from basic experience camps, to epic encounters such as gods and dragons, and everything in between.

B. OBLIGATIONS TO ATTEND:

  1. Events scheduled and approved by High Council will generally indicate the attendance requirement for success in accomplishing the event's purpose. In general, such scheduled events are mandatory to attend and is on the basis of ensuring success more than anything else.
  2. No member will be required to attend an event where substantial loss in faction will be incurred, provided no convenient means to repair such faction loss exists. This applies only to factions of significance, usually in regards to one's home town and areas they frequent. For example, losing faction with bixies is not a reasonable basis to not attend, where as, losing faction with home town of Grobb would be.
  3. Scheduled events and spawns of opportunity take precedent over errands, experience and item camps. For example, being settled in an exp camp in Sebilis or training up smithing skill is not an acceptable basis for not attending.
  4. Time constraints is not an acceptable basis for not attending scheduled events or spawns of opportunity. For example, only having two hours of play time left is not an acceptable basis for not attending. Those two hours can be devoted to assisting everyone else getting off to a good start.
    1. For spawns of opportunity, if travel time would exceed one's remaining play time then attendance is not required. This, however, does not apply to scheduled events as it was previously announced and traveling to the location in question should have occurred before the start time.
  5. Members are required to attend with their primary character, unless an alternate is specifically requested by the raid leader.

C. SCHEDULING AND REQUESTING GUILD EVENTS:

  1. During each 30 day period, High Council will declare an area as the guild's base of operations. All members should be bound at a location such that obtaining easy access to the area can be achieved.
  2. Requests for the scheduling of guild events begin with each individual guild member, who determine their priorities and needs and submits that list to the appropriate Class Leader. Such requests should, but are not required to be, based on things that can be acquired and accomplished within the vicinity of our base of operations.
    1. Class Leaders can establish a limit on the number of requested things to acquire or achieve, but should be understood by all that not all requests may be able to be fulfilled during any given time period.
  3. Class Leaders will then consolidate this list and submit it to the High Council for consideration.
  4. High Council will then coordinate and schedule events so as to best fulfill as many requests as possible, and in a manner that will benefit the most players.
  5. No event may be scheduled on behalf of anyone's alternate character, except through expressed approval by the High Council.
  6. Guild members are free to do their own events, provided it does not conflict with events scheduled by the High Council. Also, they are with the understanding that attendance is voluntary and not mandatory, and that all guild rules concerning loot, alternates and conduct will be upheld.
  7. Members from outside the guild are always welcomed to join us, provided it is approved by the raid leaders and would not be disruptive to other participants.
  8. Requests to schedule an event to assist non-IC members must be approved by the High Council if mandatory attendance is required.
    1. The High Council is free to impose any reasonable terms to providing such assistance on a case by case basis, and agreement to such will generally be required before assistance is rendered.
    2. If an event is not scheduled by the High Council, then attendance of such is voluntary and may not conflict with events that are scheduled by the High Council.
