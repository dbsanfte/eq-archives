July 27th, 2006

Two Fights in Three Nights!

Yeah yeah, update faster you say... I do have a job ya know? :P

We've had somewhat of a slow start to our month, finishing up our latest round of recruitment, backflagging, farming... All that good stuff. While fun, it's left us craving for more.

We went into Demi on Monday night really hoping to get our first Hatchet kill under our belts. We were set, we were focused, nothing was going to stop us. Well, maybe a teeny tiny little incident with a nearly harmless little red skeleton might stop us...

After uhm, a very long time rezzing, dying, logging on alts, leveling said alts, moving skeletons, and other dead things we were finally recovered. We figured the focus was gone, that too many people had gone to sleep or that too many people had snuck out for the night. We almost called it a night. The officers were burnt and frustrated and just wanted to go to bed pretending the night hadn't happened. But we decided that since we had spent so much time getting to this point, we had to at least throw ourselves at Hatchet for a quick TL to bind.

Well, once again, things didn't go as planned. After about 5 minutes we realized that the focus was still there, that we really had a chance at winning still. A short time later (ok, a long time later) we came up with this!

And of course he coughed us up some loot!


Congrats Valerys!


Congrats Florance!


Congrats Kanuuan!

And then Tuesday night came, and well... We won't talk about Tuesday night other than that we had an interesting discussion about what kind of torture devices would work best on the game developers.

And then Wednesday night came. Once again we were psyched, we knew we were close to another fresh kill. We took a while to get ready, making sure we didn't have any flaws in group setup, or anything else we could later kick ourselves over. And then finally we engaged...

Then within 30 seconds we had a death...
And then a short time later we had another...
Uh oh, this might not go so well...
But then we pulled it together. For the next 45 minutes or so, we ripped the event to shreds. Everybody was doing exactly what they needed to be doing, hardly anybody died, and we came up with another corpse that wasn't one of ours!

And of course we got some more nice loot...


Congrats Dumathin!


Congrats Soulkeepper!


Congrats Welaker!

It was pretty amazing really. An event we have tried so few times to go down the way it did was a sight to be seen. Everybody did an amazing job, from the clickers, to the healers, to the people standing around with their thumbs in disgusting places wondering why an event was designed in such a way.

Way to step it up Pak'cafan with two fresh kills in three days!

- Kaktharka, Paladin of Brell
