Today I went out and bought Unreal Tournament. I have always avoided first person shooters in the past because I have all the reflexes of a three legged mentally handicapped mule on jumbo ether, but ... well, someone told me there was a nuke in it. There is something about a nuke that seems to undermine the need for dexterity.

Speaking of nukes, how many of you out there are worried about Y2K? I am, but not for the reasons you expect. I am scared by Y2K because I have absolutely no plans and am terrified that for the rest of my life when people ask me where I was on December 31, I will have to tell them that I was at home scratching myself, drinking Cream Soda and watching the 24 hour MASH marathon on channel 19. Y2K also frightens me because of all the idiots out there.

I am also pleased to announce that Gamespot gave the idiot driven Ultima Ascension a bad review. I applaud Gamespot for having the guts to deflate the overhyped balloon that is the Ultima series. I now wait for the whoring PC Gamer to call it Game of the Year. For those of you who do not know, Ultima Ascension really would have been a great game, but unfortunately, hardware and software technology refused to sit still for 5 years so that the genius programmers at Origin could program a game that would work.

Speaking of pushing the envelope, I was interested to read that the developers of Shadowbane (Wolfpack Studios) are creating an online RPG that will return to MUDDING's roots by not relying so much on fight, sit, fight, sit. Hmmmm ... where have we heard that before? Interestingly, when asked how players will gain experience and advance, the Shadowbane guru said characters will have to ... fight. Thank God a developer finally has the balls to sit on its ass and let a genre stagnate.

Finally, who out there is ready for the game The Sims to come out? I definitely am. I am eager to replicate my life on the computer and see where it is headed. If I play a game and learn that people won't come to my house because a pizza has been left on the floor for 3 years, I will consider it $49.95 well spent.
