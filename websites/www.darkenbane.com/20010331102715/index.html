    Scams and Fraud on the rise after Verant shut down Ebay Auctions13-03-2001
    Submitted by Grayrage on 13-03-2001 15:09

    Verant Interactive has surely destroyed legitimate player auctions on the popular auction site, EBAY. They did not support these auctions in past which had inadvertantly led to many scams and fraud. EBAY did lend credibility to some of these auctions which prevented ALOT of fraud through their user grading system.

    Now, however, scams and fraud are rampant as auctions are removed and buyers who are eager to acquire items and accounts send money without even the protection offered by EBAY to unsavory types who happily steal their money and disapeer.

    EMails have been sent to Verant regarding the obvious increase of scams and fraud as a direct result of their actions taken against ebay with no response having been received by this writer to date.

    As Verant continues it's crusade against out of game player trading, some auction sites still exist and try to add some safety and legitimacy to out of game transactions. Start ups such as PlayerAuctions.com advise that they will resist Verant's attempts to stop them from allowing legitamate player trading with every possible effort in order to provide players a safe place to trade items and accounts without fear of being victimized by thieves.
