    PvP Rules Evolution06-06-2001
    Submitted by Grayrage on 06-06-2001 16:40

    As our PvP rules evolve and all of us pray daily for some sort of book or guide, we learn more stuff everyday. Previously I was under the impression that we only had to let someone who was AT their corpse loot then scoot. It turns out that this is wrong. Here is some new info gained from Ozuri during a multitude of conversations after killing some sheep.

    1. We MUST NOT interfere in someone who is trying to retrieve their corpse. Regardless of WHERE they are. We are not even allowed to intercept them from reaching their corpse. Bascially, delibertely preventing someone from doing a CR is considered player harrassment. The exceptions are as follows:

    a) The person MUST go directly to corpse, loot then leave.

    b) The person cannot med, linger, attack or otherwise do ANYTHING but get his shit and go.

    2. Zone "camping" is still a very VAGUE and scary PvP rule. It's scary cause SO many fights happen near zones, but it seems to be a pretty arbitrary place or activity for Guide/GM warnings which could lead to bannings. Ozuri indicates that the ZONE is NOT a viable ESCAPE route, but then says that you cannot attack someone until they have control over their Avatar. Who determines control? How much time do you have to give? Is there REALLy such a thing as not being in control of your Avatar before being attacked at a zone? I personally find this difficult to deal with as people will zone back and forth repeatedly to avoid PvP death and the only way to MAYBE get a shot in on them is to nuke RIGHT away and even then they often zone back before a nuke can go off.

    IMHO, Zone camping SHOULD NOT EXIST as a PvP rule as it is UNPOLICEABLE and allows for ENORMOUS exploits by fleeing victims.

    Please feel free to put your opinion regarding the issue HERE.
