Editorial: Playing a Hothead in Norrath

The barbarian ducked under the doorway to go into the tavern that felt just a little too cramped. He grumbled to himself about having Talos, his High Elf friend, choose Kaladim as their meeting place. Oh well, better then getting lost in Faydark I guess, he thought to himself. He took a seat at an empty table and ordered ale to help ease the wait.

A large dwarf, well just about as large as they come, after all, it is a dwarf, walked up to him, "Hey there, me large fellow, you be in my seat. Time fer you to be moving."

"I don't think so little fella, besides how do you plan on moving me?"

"Listen ya skirt wearing little pimple, my newborn nephew could kick your puny little arse back to that wretched wasteland you call a home!"

Ok, at this point you have to ask yourself what did you just see. Well, it looks to me like a pretty heated interchange between two hotheaded warrior types. Could it come to blows? Probably not, but that depends on how good of role-players these two are. What you don't realize is that the little fella is about 10 levels higher then the barbarian (I know, I didn't say that up in the text, but trust me ok!).

With the PvP switch being in place like it is, which I might add I like more and more as I play, there obviously isn't going to be an instant brawl unless both of these people have completed that particular quest. For the time being, let's assume that they haven't, since that is the way that the majority of the people play in Norrath. It has been my experience that to some degree it has limited role-playing in one way, that being the attacks of rage or anger. With the switch in place, it makes it hard to play a hothead for some people, myself included until recently.

Any good role-play knows that at about this time there is a good brawl coming, and someone is going to get a pounding. But I have noticed in EverQuest two things that have made this a little less likely to happen, and let me show them to you in the way the barbarian who is thinking of starting the fight will more then likely do them.

  1. He is going to target our friend the dwarf, and type /consider. "What would you like your tombstone to say?"
  2. Maybe he isn't that much higher then me… /who (name). Wow, 25th level Paladin. Pretty big jump for my 15th level warrior.
So in the game, the barbarian just starts a word fight, or maybe just starts to /ignore our friend the dwarf. Is this really role-playing? After all, this barbarian serves the god of war! He knows that he is one tough stud!

Now I realize that we have all spent a lot of time and energy in getting our characters up to the level that they are, and we don't really want to have our barbarian to have to travel all the way back from Halas, after all he does have an appointment to keep! What I am suggesting is a good use of the /duel command that Verant Interactive has been so kind to put into the game. I think that this could be a great use in role-playing if we allow it to be! Wouldn't it be fitting for the barbarian to punch, not draw out his mighty two handed hammer and start to bash the dwarf? After only a few minutes, it would become painfully obvious to the barbarian that he is out classed here.

At this point, it would be a good idea for the barbarian to back down. It would also, in my opinion, and based on the type of Paladin that the dwarf is playing, be fitting for the dwarf to heal any wounds that his fists caused on the barbarian at this time. More then likely, the two will continue to talk for a little afterwards, and who knows, they may even meet up again a few weeks later, with the barbarian being closer to his level, they might even go as far as actually go out adventuring together and becoming friends (Remember in grade school, that guy that you fought at lunch, how next year the two of you were great friends playing football together).

This little example brings out a few good points if you ask me.

  1. It is not necessary for deadly violence to be the answer to a dispute. For that matter, would a barbarian and dwarf really attack each other with weapons inside a town that frowns very heavily on that sort of thing?
  2. Go ahead and accept that /duel before you find out how tough they are. Assuming that the situation is being role-played and it would fit with what you think your character would do.
  3. Finally, don't be so concerned if you see the statement of someone running away from a duel. Maybe it was really completed and they let the other person live. They just zoned first. If you do this, then it is a start that everyone else will as well.
Obviously, this solution will not work all of the time. There are times when you are running along and someone, lets say they are playing a bandit, sticks you up and role-plays out a heist on your butt. I say that if it is a REALLY good job at role-playing, go ahead and accept it. On the other hand, there are times where this just isn't appropriate. Here is an example of something that happened to me last night.

I was playing Juntao, my monk on the test server, and I had to get over to see my friend and guild leader, Windrunner. I'm 5th level, he is 13th and a wizard. So he was playing around in Befallen, a nice little dungeon that is just a little too hard for a 5th level monk, crazy or not, to go around in it. Once I zoned into Befallen, I decided to have a little fun and shout something across the zone to the effect of, "Windrunner get your sorry ass up here so that I can kick it all the way back to Qeynos!" Those were not the exact words, but a fun little jest to a friend in that same context. A little banter went back and forth, and I found myself faced with someone running from a greater skeleton towards the zone. Decided it was a good time to zone as well, and followed him through.

So now that I am back in West Commons, the guy tries to duel me! Now I don't even know him, and I asked Windrunner, he didn't know him either. He was obviously of a much higher level then me, and while I do not think that is a good reason to decline a duel, the fact was that he didn't even say hi to me! He just challenged me to a duel. Also, I was in the middle of trying to give something to my friend, and I, as a 5th level character just ran from Qeynos with little to no help (Thanks Hika & Sobbo). It wouldn't make since for me to do it at a time like this, let alone with someone who obviously wasn't role-playing out a situation (really, I probably shouldn't say obviously, maybe he was mad at me being arrogant to someone that is much higher in levels then myself, and didn't feel the need to tell me why he was attack?).

I guess the bottom line that I am getting at is this, lets all start to use that /duel option the way it was suppose to be used, as a tool to role-play with. A way around the PvP switch that helps keeps the random acts of c0ol d0oDs from hurting the role-playing experience for the rest of us. As always, remember this, and I will whisper it incase it offends anyone.

"It's just a game, have fun."
