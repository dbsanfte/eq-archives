Thanks for your comments on Charisma. But I choose to disagree with your conclusions. Having a 100 Charisma makes a big difference. I've played a human cleric, with a low Charisma, and I reached my Rank 3 spell level and could not afford 1 spell while wearing partial leather armor.

Now my mage buy and sells at a profit both ways compared to others. I was able to purchase Wolf pelts in the commons for 1 silver each and buy patterns for about 5 silver each. I would load 4 backpacks with the pelts. My high intelligence made my tailoring skill rise very quickly. I sold each item for 3gp. Quite a profit. But you may say anyone could do that. Well that got me through the early levels. I was able to purchase all my 8th lv spells by the begining of my 7th lv. Killing orcs provided lots of rusty weapons and clothing. These would average close to 2gp each with the vendors. I didn't have to waste precious time trying to auction stuff to the tightwads out there. I'm just made 11 and have already purchased my 12th lv spells. My next plan is to purchase Combine weapons (54pp for me, 62pp for a woodelf) and make a tiny profit re-selling those. This allows me to hunt un profitable creatures without bumping elbows with the othe poor character types.

PS: I make my own food and water and only wear clothing.
