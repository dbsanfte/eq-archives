Fiction: The Trials and Tribulations of a Ranger - by Agathorn

 Part I   Part II   Part III 

Part I
After a long journey from my homeland and the city of Felwithe, I finally find myself standing in the strange and uncomfortable town of Qeynos. Now, in order for you to really understand my discomfort, you must know that my homeland is one of a rural setting. I grew up living hand in hand with nature. Here in the city of Qeynos, I don't even see anything natural, besides the ocassional plant placed decorativley in the window. What trees I do see are simply dead husks--mere shadows of the majestic fauna from my homeland. So, one might ask, why did I come here? Well, my reasoning is simple, although the explanation is not. You see, I am what some rudely call a halfbreed. My mother was a human and my father an elf. An elf of some noble stature actually. While humans and elves get along just fine, I found that growing up as a half-elf was difficult at best. I was constantly teased and taunted. In addition, while I loved my homeland and the deep forests within, my father strongly dissaproved of my interest in the ways of the druids, and wanted me to become a cleric like himself and my mother. Now please understand that I have nothing against clerics. I simply decided that I prefered to live my life in harmony with nature. I wanted to be a druid.

Unfortunately for me it seems that my human half, though I love my mother dearly, has cursed me in a way. I am simply unable to gather and control the vast amounts of magical energy required to be a true druid. Refusing to be swayed however, I vowed to become a ranger. Rangers are respected by many a druid and non druid alike, and while they are not as mystically powerful as the druids, they do wield strong spells of thier own. I decided that, considering my choice of career, and my father's equal dislike for that choice, it would be best for me to leave home. I had heard of a great training camp for rangers somewhere near the city of Qeynos, and so I set out. Which brings us to the present: Qeynos.

This large city may very well be one of the shining symbols of peace and prosperity, but I find it to be loud, crowded, and a virtual maze of streets. After nearly an hour of running this way and that, I finally find a helpful woman who points me in the direction of a settlement camp called Surefall Glade. She indicates I can find the training I seek there. I thank the woman and set off on my way through the lands outside of Qeynos. On a side note, it seems to me that this city has a serious rodent control problem. Everywhere I look outside the city gates I see countless rats and snakes. Now while I may love nature in all of its forms, I have trouble with the thought of living amongst vermin such as this. Hopefully I can make my home in this Surefall Glade area.

Suprisingly, I find that the journey to my new home is rather short, but the change in scenery couldn't have been more profound. As much as I love my homeland, I must say that Surefall Glade is the most beutiful place I have ever seen. After wandering around a bit, I find two guards standing outside a tunnel cut into the side of the mountain. Upon inquiring I find that what I seek is through there. One of the guards, whose name I believe was Effin, offered to let me use a torch, but I assured him that I would not need it. My half-elven abilities allow me to see just fine in the dark. I'm quite relieved to find that humans in this area have nothing against people of my kind. I'm starting to feel better about this trip. I thank the guard and press on into the tunnel. It only takes me a few minutes, and I come out onto the other side of the mountain into the settlement I have been looking for. It is however, extremely late and I feel that I had best wait 'til morning to start my training. I stop by the local inn to find a place for the night. Unfortunately it seems I neglected to bring any money along with me. I explain my situation to the inkeep and she agrees to let me stay the night. It seems that the people around here are very kind.

When I awake I find a small parchment has been left for me. Upon reading it I find that it is a letter left by one of the Druids in the area. It seems he knew I would be coming and has arranged for my indoctrination and training into the local Ranger's Guild. I gather my things and thank the innkeep once again for the room. I quickly locate the guildmaster and begin my training. The next week was a long and hard one, but by the time I finished I was well on my way to life as a ranger. My guildmaster has taught me important skills such as tracking and sense direction and I am off to make my mark in the lands of Norrath!



Part II
"I'll give you 1 gold for the lot of 'em."

"1 gold? My friend, you insult me. These aren't imitation kerra eyes like you have around here. These are genuine Eastern fire beetle eyes. You would have to travel all the way to Freeport to get these, and even then you would pay at least a platinum for the lot here. I'm offering them to you for only 8 gold peices."

"8 gold is outrageous. I'll give you 3."

"My friend, once you buy these, you will be the only one in this whole area with a supply. You could sell them for whatever you wish. Surely 8 gold pieces is a pitance compared to what you could get for them here."

"I'll give you 5 gold for the lot of ten. No more."

"5 gold plus you through in a week's worth of food and water."

"Hmm. You drive a hard bargain Muerte, but its a deal."

I hand over the bag of ten fire beetle eyes to the shopkeep and take my gold and supplies in return. I thank the man and head out into the area of Erud that one might loosely call the town square. It is here that I am supposed to be meeting my old friend, Agathorn. Those of you who have done some traveling may know exactly where I am, but for the sake of the geographically stunted crowd I will try to explain where I am and how I happen to find myself here.

It all started one night back in my home of Surefall Glade. I was out on the archery range practicing when I man stumbled into town. Maybe stumbled is a bad choice of words, as the man more properly barely walked into town and fell unconscious near the archery range. I dropped my longbow and rushed to the man's side. Carefully picking him up I brought him into the inn and called for my instructor. When my instructor arrived he tended to the man's wounds, and believe me whan I say there were many, while I arranged for a room for the stranger.

The next morning we payed the man a visit and it seemed he would be alright. Physically at least. Shala, the innkeep, told us that emotionally the man was a wreck. It seemed he was the sole survivor of a Gnoll raid on his village. We talked with the man to get more details. As it turns out his village was raided by the Gnoll clan of Darkpaw with the aid of a Human necromancer. Everyone in his village was either killed or taken as slaves. He alone escaped because the raiding party took him for dead. This news was rather alarming in the fact that while Clan Darkpaw regularly wandered the area blaming Humans for thier problems and ocossionally attacking lone travelers, they had never banded together and made such a bold strike before. The addition of a necromancer, and a Human one at that, made things even more alarming.

And so began my quest. I vowed to the man that I would do all I could to free those of his village that were still alive. With a necromancer thrown into the equation, things became decidely more difficult and I knew I would require assistance. While my skills had progressed rapidly in the last year of training, I was still unable to wield the power of the arcane arts. I therefore sent word to an old friend, which is what brought me to the city of Erud; home of the High Men, the Erudites. The city of Erud lay across the sea from Qeynos, which is a major port city not far from my home in Surefall Glade.

Enough of the geography lesson though. It appears as though my friend Agathorn is approaching. I stand up from my seat and smother my boyhood friend in a great embrace.

"Agathorn my dear friend, it is good to see you again."

"And it is good to see you again as well, Muerte. I must say you have changed greatly from the boy I knew back in Felwithe. A change for the better no doubt."

"Yes, getting away from there was the best decision I've ever made. Growing up in my father's shadow would have done me no good. I see you too have left Felwithe to return to your homelands here in Erud."

"I'm afraid the High Elves have been getting a bit too superior in attitude for thier own good. It has gotten to the point that other races do not even travel to the fine city anymore. I was forced to leave by the council if you can believe it. Enough about me however, lets talk about your little adventure."

"Well you obviously got my message, so you know as much as I do at the moment. It will take some tracking to find the villagers, but I thought we might start in the caves near Qeynos. Those caves seem to be the home of Clan Darkpaw. I doubt we'll find what we seek there, but it should point us in the right direction."

"Sounds like as good a plan as any. Lead on my friend."

And lead on I did. We spent the journey back to Qeynos talking about how best to carry out our goal. I was astonished to find how far Agathorn had advanced in his chosen profession as an enchanter, and found that his skills would be extremely useful to us. By the time we reached Qeynos we had a solid plan of action in place.

Next Week the adventure continue as Muerte and Agathorn find the missing villagers, and uncover secrets that could spell certain doom for all of Norrath...



Part III
What remains of the story has been pieced together by various individual accounts, and as always, people are prone to exaggeration from time to time. It is unfortunate that we could not get Muerte himself to finish the story for us, but the reason behind this will become painfully clear once you have read what follows.

We do know for certain that Muerte and Agathorn made an excursion into the caves near Qeynos that Clan Darkpaw claims as their own. Not much is known about what went on inside, but it is rumored that the party, despite Muertes amazing skill, found themselves lost within the mazes and were soon captured by the clan of gnolls. Through talks with the city guards, who keep regular record of who travels in and out of the city, it was discovered that Muerte and Agathorn were gone for nearly two weeks before they were seen again. When they finally reappeared they looked, as was so eloquently put by the guard on duty, "Like death warmed over."

The two were brought to Falor, Muertes Ranger instructor, in Surefall Glade. There they received the care they needed, and Muerte told his instructor what they had seen.

"It was awful, Falor. Absolutely awful. Ive seen death before but this was a hundred times worse. It was more than just bodies laying all over, although there were plenty of those. But rather just the overall sense of death that atmosphere. You could just feel it. It was like Death himself was standing right behind you waiting for you to fall into his domain."

"It was more than that, Lord Falor." Agathorn explained, "I did not tell Muerte at the time, but even the Gnolls that wandered around were dead. Thanks to my training in the arcane, I could see the magical aura given off by their bodies. They were dead, and re-animated by a very powerful Necromancer. I would suspect that this individual is the same Necromancer that was seen during the village raid."

"Are you sure about this, Lord Agathorn?" Asked Falor.

"I am afraid so. Whomever it is we are dealing with, he is very powerful. In addition, it would appear that he has a much bigger plan in play."

"But I dont understand why a Necromancer would want to make everyone think that Clan Darkpaw was still alive and well." Muerte exclaimed. "Why go on with this deception? If he is raising an army of undead, which is obviously what he has done, then why not simply treat them as such?"

"Animosity." Replied Falor.

"What?" Asked Muerte.

"Animosity. Bad feelings, hatred. Now that I think about it, Clan Darkpaw only started harassing and attacking humans about a year ago. Up until that point, they lived in harmony with us. It seems logical that this Necromancer took control of them back then and has been using them to fuel hatred in the area. I would say it is designed as some sort of front for whatever he has planned."

"Yes, " Agathorn agreed, "that would make sense. The big question is, however, what exactly does he have planned?"

"Very good question." Replied Falor. "This large scale raid on the village of Aranok is the first one he has made. Its also the first time he has shown himself. Whatever he is planning, I would say it has only begun."

"Wait a moment." Said Agathorn. "Did you say the name of that village was Aranok?"

"Yes. Why?"

"Last I had heard, Aranok was where the powerful Druid TalLor was living."

"Yes, " replied Muerte, "Master Lor was living there. I had visited him a few times during the Solstices."

"Then that explains it." replied Agathorn, snapping his fingers. "This Necromancer is out to harness the power of the Five."

Seeing the confused look on the faces of his friends, Agathorn explained.

"Legend writes that one day, thousands of years ago, a powerful Necromancer succeeded in capturing five powerful spellcasters. One Druid, one Wizard, one Cleric, one Enchanter, and one Magician. Then, using the powers of the Dark under his control, he was able control and harness the power of these five casters. The Necromancer became incredibly powerful and managed to transport himself to the plain of the Gods, where he proceeded to do battle with them. The Gods, completely surprised by the attack, quickly fell to the evil Necromancer. Once the Gods fell, all of Norrath was blanketed in a perpetual darkness that none could lift. It was known as the Time of the Red Moon. During this period evil ran rampant and all of the lands were coated in the blood of innocents. It seemed as though all was lost."

"Hundreds of years passed in this way, and then the Necromancer made a mistake. Throughout these hundreds of years, the Gods had been cooperative. So much so that the Necromancer finally gotten careless, allowing one of the Gods, Rodcet Nife, to slip away from their plain and enter Norrath. Once on Norrath he mated with a Human female, and then returned to the plain of the Gods."

"Shortly after, this Human gave birth to a son. The child, named TrandDoshan, was immediately taken by the monks of a local monastery and raised to respect the virtues of good. Blessed by his fathers power, the child grew quicker than any human and soon became a formidable warrior. He then set out to learn the ways of the Clerics."

"In only a few years Doshan mastered the clerical ways. Once his training was complete, he was told of his destiny and Rodcet Nife opened a portal between Norrath and the plain of the Gods. It was there that the Necromancer and the mighty Cleric did battle. Legends reports that the battle went on for centuries, but in the end the Necromancer fell and order was restored."

"It is also written that TrandDoshan became the founder of a new Clerical order and it is because of him that Clerics are now such formidable warriors. Before his coming Clerics were recorded as simple spellcasters with no use for such armor and weapons as they now use."

The two men were fascinated with the story, as well as frightened, for they realized what it meant.

"So it seems as if this Human Necromancer we have been hearing about is trying to again harness these powers. We know that he has a Druidic master, but we have no way of knowing what others he has gained control of, and we still dont even know where to find him."

"Im afraid that things are even worse than we fear." Said Falor, putting his hand on Muertes shoulder.

"What do you mean, Master?" Asked Muerte, a sudden feeling of dread washing over him.

"While you were gone I received a letter from the High Lords of Felwithe. A band of Orcs allied with Clan Crushbone raided Felwithe and your father was taken prisoner. Im so sorry, and as unfeeling as it sounds, I must assume that the Necromancer now has control over your father, himself a mighty Cleric."

So goes the story of Meurte, the wandering Ranger, as recorded by the great scribe Agathorn (no relation to the enchanter).
