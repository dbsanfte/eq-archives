Playing a Magician

 Basics   Reagents   Grouping   Advanced Tactics   Pet Control 

Basics
Lets begin with how the low level fighting should go. First off, don't believe what others tell you about how your pet should always tank while you burn it to death, not only does this waste mana, but you end up losing your pet because while your burning you can't see what kind of shape the pet is in. The key to the first 10 levels or so is that first, soloing will be your best bet. First, your going get a ton of exp on your own, and second, you get to keep every bit of loot you get. Once you've found a good place to solo, find the nearest safe area (guards or zone) in case you get in trouble, because one of the best things a magician has is the ability to run like no other. What I mean by that is your pet is a perfect fleeing support mob while your butt is high tailing it to the nearest safe area or zone point. Your pet is back keeping the enemy busy by taunting every 2-3 rounds and generally knocking him upside down, which really angers the bad guys. When your running, the monster will most likely turn to the pet since it's still fighting it. Only once or twice has the creature been so mad that it just came right after me and ignored the pet. Now with that in mind, you may ask how are you supposed to get away when the pet is dead? Simple, your pet won't be the one tanking, very rarely is a pet a good tank for a magician, that is. You will be the tank. You have higher AC at low levels, you can keep the enemy targeted and you know exactly what your HP is. It's a lot easier to step back, and let the pet take over for a bit while you deal some burn spells then the reverse. Sometimes you just can't make the enemy attack you and leave the pet alone, and thus it dies. The advantage to this is you can flee when you need to, so feel free to take on new enemies that might be yellow and black/white.

Reagents
Now for those of you not familiar with the reagent system, let me break it down for you. There are certain spells in the game that require an added item to cast, in which it destroys the item and the spell is cast. These are called reagents, and magician summoning is one of those that requires one. This discourages quite a few level 4 magicians when they run to the guild and spend their gold on the spell and try to cast their first pet only to find they are missing an item. I was one of these actually. I began to question if this class was for me, seeing as how every time I wanted to cast the main spell in my arsenal I needed an item. Well it's not as bad as it seems. I never got into higher level spells, but for the first 20 levels all your going need to make elementals is malachite. The general price is 6 silver, give or take a few copper. This may seem like a lot to a level 4 and it generally is, but let me tell you, just buy the malachite in quantities of 4-5. By the time your out of it, you will have made close to ½ a platinum easy. Once you hit level 8, you'll start making runs that end up giving you an average of 2 platinum a piece for a single elemental (for me anyway). At that rate, you can't help but treat malachite like a piece of trash, because it comes down to being petty change. By the time I was level 10 I never left the town without 15 or so malachites in my backpack.

Grouping
Believe it or not, magicians actually do have some rather impressive damage Spells. Not nearly the strength of a wizard, but Id like to see a wizard solo. Now then, when your grouping, there are a few things you'll want to do. First off, always tell your pet to "follow me" as soon as you create it. What this means is he will not attack anything that didn't attack you first or that you directly told him to attack. In my early days as a magician, I used guard me quite a bit and more then once the little beggar got me killed doing that. It's the worst feeling in the world to start seeing "taunting attacker master" flash on your screen a few times only to look around and notice your pet is no where in sight. In a situation such as that, which I'm sure will happen many times to you as it did to me, you can only try to find the pet and step in to save his butt before whatever he's fighting kills him, or if your lucky, he'll win the fight and return to your side. Should you not be able to find him when he meets his maker (you?!?), you'll soon feel the wrath of whatever killed him on your back. Now, keep in mind that the invisibility spell, while being ideal for a wizard to sit down and meditate, is a curse to a magician. While you want to invisibility yourself, you know that if you do, your pet goes bye-bye. (dealing 32000 points of damage to himself in the process, why can't he do that on command?) Now, for those of you who have annoying group mates, you might get them saying "no pets they take experience." In which you simply remind them that you're no idiot and will promptly call the pet off long before the final hit is dealt. Secondly, that the pet is more for my protection then to help the group. If your group is more then effective on its own at killing whatever you've chosen to fight, simply tell the pet to follow you and don't have him engage. If worse comes to worse, he's still an excellent "sacrificing lamb" to save everyone if the situation turns unfavorable.

Advanaced Tactics
After level 10 or so you'll notice that you are no longer the ideal tank while your pet kills the creature. This tactic is still useful when messing with blue things, but if you want to keep going on that speedy leveling, your going to have to get a cleric at your side for such a tactic. I had a good friend who played a dwarf cleric that I traveled with quite often, and we spent a good deal of our time killing undead things that were red to us. Considering you have a damage dealer, you, a tank, the pet, and a cleric your 2 person group is unstoppable. While magicians "can" solo, its best to group regardless of what you think after level 10.

Gold will start to be easier to come by when you can find things like bandit females in Lesser Faydark to kill. These little girls pack a bronze weapon quite often (one half the time usually), and they can be sold for at least 1-2 platinum. The trick to taking out a lonely enemy that you know has a good item on it but is a little over your head is simple, have the pet attack while you stand a good distance back (need full mana for this) then unload the best damage spell on it till your out of mana or its dead, then walk up and between you and the pet. Finish it off hand to hand, if the pet gets near death get in the monsters face and tell the pet to "back off" for a few seconds and then have him re-enter the fight while you tank for a bit. This is a little risky because you have no mana should you get in trouble, but more often then not, it was successful which then produced a rather nice item of greater value then any warrior of the same level would be able to get in his wildest dreams.

Pet Control
Let me take a moment before I end this to talk about controlling your pet. I'll start with the commands:
/Pet guard me --- the pet goes where you go and attacks anything that can be considered dangerous within the general area.

/Pet guard here --- the same as guard me only the pet sits in one spot and kills anything dangerous that walks by. A key tactic to changing your faction is using this. I wanted to become more liked by high elves again after killing some pixies so I took my pet to the orc village, found a nice spot and had it guard here. I then walked away and did some other stuff while he was busy racking in the faction points for me.

/pet follow me --- fairly self explanatory. The pet will follow you around and only attack what attacks you or what you tell it to attack.

/pet kill --- also known as /pet attack. Target something and type that (number 6 on my hot-key list) and the pet runs up to the target and begins smashing for all he's worth.

/pet back off --- (number 5 on my hot-key list) forces the pet to stop whatever he's fighting and return to your side. If he is in 1on1 combat with the enemy, you can hit this many times to make him drag the enemy back to you, but he will still stop every time he is hit and resume fighting. This makes pets a nice fishing line if you will if you want to save mana.

/pet as you were --- this makes the pet forget any commands you may have given him, be it guard or kill or anything else.

Now, let me explain how dangerous pets can be if you don't control them correctly. I have been killed at least 3 times in phase 4 because I grouped with another magician who couldn't control his pets. They ran off and attacked things and came back almost dead with a train or dead leaving 3-4 yellow/red monsters hunting us. If you don't issue the right commands at the right times, you'll quickly end up on the "do not invite" list of most parties. Also I recommend you hotkey everyone of the pet commands. I'm a rather fast typist, so I leave the commands like guard and follow to my typing, and keep the /kill and /backoff commands hotkeyed. Lastly, I'll talk about range of commands. I'm not sure if this was a bug or if this was by design, but unless the pet is within "say" distance of you, he will not respond to any commands you give him. More to the point, you can't control a pet if you can't see the pet.

This is not the final word on magicians, and keep in mind I only had a few weeks to play one during the beta. I'm sure I'll learn a lot more when the full game comes out. My first character I intend to play is more then likely going to be a necromancer, because I just love having pets. They make the game so much easier and interesting. Enjoy your magician and always keep a tight leash on your pets.
