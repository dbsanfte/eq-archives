EverQuest Goes To China
GeneralNews - 12:07 PM PDT
In a press release from EverQuest Live Site, SOE announced they are going to China with EverQuest:

Wednesday September 18 2002
Customer Service Status
GeneralNews - 5:25 PM PDT
This was posted in the EQ Developers Corner:
Greetings all,

Rest assured that there is no plan to institute a "pay for Customer Service" model. We have a large staff of over 70 GMs and Supervisors for EverQuest alone and plan to continue with the same level of Customer Support, as part of the purchase price for all of our soon to be released titles.

Customer service will always be an integral part of SOE's products and while we may not respond to every issue as rapidly as we would like, we do try to resolve them all in time. If you find that your issue has not been responded to, please use the Contact a GM link on the EverQuest board. These emails are generally responded to within 48 hours thanks to the diligence of our Email GMs. In addition, while GMs are available in limited numbers 24 hours a day, you may have the best chance of contact during peak usage hours which are from 5PM to 8PM PST as this is when most GMs are available.

Thanks for your time and patronage.

Alan Crosby
Customer Service Manager
EverQuest Games
Hope that gives answers to some of you who need Customer Service.
 - Cosmoo

Tuesday September 17 2002
SidlWidl - UI Utility
EQ Skins - 5:57 PM PDT
Several people have emailed me asking for a guide to skin making, or utilities to help with creating their ultimate skin.

I'm no XML expert, but there is a very nice utility called SildWidl. It is still in Alpha test, but is very usable, and has a lot of features built into it. Instead of having to manually edit the xml files, figure out coordinates, etc... you can click, drag, and drop your way to a customized interface. To use the utility, you will have to have the Microsoft .NET framework installed.

For those of you interested, it can be downloaded from the SildWidl website, or from www.eq-toolbox.com, which also has several other XML utilities as well.


 - Lueamdar

Ragefire In The News!
GeneralNews - 2:55 PM PDT
BBC News has posted another article about the Ragefire spawn issue. Here's an excerpt:
The practice of barging in and taking someone else's chance to deal a monster a mortal blow is called "kill-stealing" and is banned under EverQuest's terms and conditions.

Now Verant has changed the method of triggering the dragon form of Ragefire which means it appears much more often. The result has been lots of clerics completing their epic quest much faster than they otherwise have done.

The change has been widely praised on many EverQuest discussion groups because it removes the bottleneck but remains as challenging because good teamwork is needed to complete the new part of the quest.

Others fear that the proliferation of powerful magical items the changes will cause could make some parts of the game too easy.
Read the full article.

Syndicate 2002 Everquest Conference
CommunityNews - 2:08 PM PDT
This was sent to us via email:
Sept 13/14/15 marked The Syndicate's 2002 World Conference in Washington DC. As one might expect from a guild of 550+ members, who's average age is 28+ years, turnout was huge with close to 100 people attending. On Saturday we held a large guild meeting. Another 50+ members participated in the meeting via a conference call.

Verant was invited to attend to talk about things like Planes of Power expansion and things like raising the level cap in PoP and new AA skills. They declined to attend. That was fine, since Ubisoft clearly showed they value their largest guilds and sent a rep to extoll the virtues of Shadowbane which was very well received.

Everquest was a major topic of the conference though as Syndicate members planned raids and events to be held on our server and expanding relationships with other guilds.

Planes Of Power was also a hot topic. Speculation on what the new zones would entail and how the new levels would affect the older expansions and lands were commonly discussed.

The Syndicate has been around far longer than EQ has been around. We plan to be here until EQ ends. EQ was a breakthrough in MMORPG gaming and still pulls in more a huge number of subscribers. EQ2 has even more promise and we are proud to be part of that community.
 - Cosmoo

Download Astonia III today for FREE!
Stratics Network Announcement - 10:43 AM PDT

Astonia
III is a massively multiplayer online role playing game (in short, MMORPG). Each player assumes the role of a battle-hardened warrior or a powerful mage. He or she enters the world of Astonia and begins to train for battle. The experience gained as characters solve quests, discover new places, and kill evil monsters, can be applied to raise any of a large number of attributes, skills and spells. Characters gain rank and power, qualifying them for better and more powerful equipment for the new and greater challenges that lie ahead...

As a true online game, is available in a free download. Once you have downloaded the game's Client, you can test the game for a period of 28 days. If you would like to test the game, go to Create Account and follow the instructions. Once you have created a character, you will be able to download the client (~32 MB). You will find these instructions in greater detail in the game Manual.

If you wish to continue playing after your free test period is over, you will have to subscribe to the game. The subscription fee is US$ 9.95 per month per account (one account can have several characters, but only one character can be played at a time). To find out more about the subscription fee, you may find more information here.
