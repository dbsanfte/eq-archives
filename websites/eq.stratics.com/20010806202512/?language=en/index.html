Ask Brad (8/2/01)
GeneralNews [ 4:06 PM EDT ] - Witt
The latest issue of "Ask Brad" has been posted over on the the Developers Corner forum again. Highlights include :
"Currently, yes, we do plan on turning the Trivial Loot Code on for all Luclin zones on all servers. Keep in mind that Luclin will have zones for levels 1-60, and we hope the Trivial Code will allow us to put some pretty enticing and powerful items in the lower and mid level zones without fear that some higher level players will bottom feed there."

Content with trivial combat code might as well not exist for us, because we have no reason to go there.

That is the point, yes :)

While you and I'm sure some others might not want to make another character, we do know a few things:

1. There are still a LOT of players who are in the low to mid levels with their first characters.

2. There are a LOT of players who have multiple characters they play concurrently, often at all different levels, from low to mid to high.

3. There are a LOT of players who want to start a new Vah Shir Beastlord (or any other class, for that matter) and enjoy the content designed for them without fear of high-level players interfering with that enjoyment.

I'm sorry if this is frustrating or unappealing to you, but in the end, we think implementing the Trivial Loot Code in Luclin will make EverQuest a better game overall.
I have to agree with Brad on this point. While there are a lot of high level players in the game, there are also many who have never leveled up into the 50's and I would be surprised if level 50 players didnt have other characters that they could play these zones with and probably have more fun while doing it. As for items for faction, quests and similar Brad had this to say :
The Trivial Loot Code isn't complete and we're continuing to improve it. By the time SoL launches we should be able to mark or flag items such that we can override the Loot Code if we desire. In other words, given that the new looting rules are known by the game designers from the beginning, they are populating the new zones with that code and functionality in mind. And they will have the flexibility to set up quest components and/or other items to use all, part, or none of the new Loot Code.
Next up, Epic quest rewards and the new character transfer service.
Hey Brad, just a quick question that has me a little bit concerned with the Character Transfer services.

I realize that spells are being transferred, but not items (when changing servers). My question involves epics. I would assume that Warriors, Clerics, Wizards, etc. do not get to keep their epics. What about magicians? Their epic is a summoned item, and therefore a spell that would normally be transferred. This hardly seems fair.

If I had been the only person on my server with the Mage epic, then another mage transferred over with his, I would be, to say the least, upset.

That's about it, glad you are doing this for all of us :)

Daabu Zugzugg
55 Troll Myrmidon
Luclin

This is a very good point, and honestly, it's not entirely fair. Because the Mage Epic Item is tied to a spell, they'll still have access to it after a character transfer. We debated this for a bit and decided that, overall, it would be best not to go into characters' spellbooks and change the data there. We're sorry it's not totally fair but the alternative could have been very messy and we decided in the best interests of everyone to keep the transfers simple: spells transfer, but items don't.
On multi-monster encounters :
Brad,

I found it interesting that you originally envisioned multiple-mob fights as opposed to single-pulling. Is this what area effect spells were meant for as well? It seems that these spells are only used for quadkiting (and I'd be amazed if anyone ever uses the Al'Kabor spells at all).

I agree that multi-mob fights would be a lot more fun and I hope that you find a good way to implement them.

BJ from Torvonnilous

Thanks for the feedback on multi-mob fights, and you're right, that was the original intent behind the AoE spells. They've not been as viable as we've wanted them for a variety of reasons (mob pulling, framerate, and other balance issues), but we did originally want them to be an integral part of high level combat.
Offline EverQuest??? :
I've been playing EQ for a long time now, and I think its an amazing game. I've been extremely impressed with the high amount of detail and animation in the game, the size and number of animated items in a zone - and yet my 56.6 modem doesn't cause me any lag issues. This game is a smoother ride than any online game I've played!

So my question (yep there was one there, not just to compliment ye) is this: Have you ever given a thought to making a non-online EQ game? Something that uses the game engine and world, but is a play at home game? It's something that I would love to see - while I do get online sometimes to play I like to just play a one player game. I would love to buy a single player EQ game, and wander the realms. Just pump in some more NPCs, add in allies to join you in your quest (like in other computer RPGs), and you'd be good to go.

What do you think?

- Nexhus

We've definitely given it some thought, and we like the idea. It would also be a great way to explore and establish a lot of the backstory and history of Norrath. Beyond agreeing that's it's a great idea, however, there's nothing I can really announce.

Fennin Ro - Wizard Rune Trade Faire
CommunityNews [ 2:34 AM EDT ] - Witt
From Dynamite Shortfuze :
Hail, Wizards of Fennin Ro!

I come before you once again, as I have in the past, with news of this month's Wizard Faire! I have been collecting runes earnestly in anticipation of this month's faire, and I hope all of you have been doing the same!

Firstly, I would like to apologise to one and all for my lack of presence last month. I was there for the first half hour or so, but then my ISP went down for the rest of that night and I was unable to attend properly. I received several tells and emails, however, thanking me for
organising the event, and with a couple requests pertaining to the time. These requests shall be honored, but first I would like to share with you some exciting news.

Taedirk Fyreslinger, fellow wizard of Fennin Ro, has been wanting to organise a language faire, and we have decided that we could hold our faires together for all wizards to benefit. To this end, we would like to organise the language training area near a zone line. This must be easily accessible by most, and must be in a neutral area where none shall worry of being KOS. As the rune trade is normally held at the Toxxulia wizard spires, Taedirk suggested that we do this at the Kerra Ridge zoneline, which I feel is a good idea. If anybody has any objections, or feels that there is a better place to do this, then we will welcome all suggestions.

At the present time, the details of the faire are as follows:
Where: Toxxulia Wizard spires / Kerra Ridge
Starts: 4 PM PST ( 7 PM EST ) Saturday 4th August
Finishes: When we're done! =)

For newcomers to the faire, the concept is simple: bring all your runes and research materials to the faire. If you are in need of a rune, then ask for it, and if anyone has one they are not in need of, then you will get it. Consequentially, if you have a rune that you have no need of, and someone else asks for it, then it is expected that you will give that rune to your fellow wizard. The language trading part, I will leave to Taedirk to decide the rules at the faire, but I imagine people will be split into groups of five students and one teacher and then go about learning.

This faire is held for the community, by the community, and we are all here to help each other. Even if you haven't any research to do, I strongly encourage you to come out and meet all your fellow wizards.We can only grow stronger as a community, and we can only grow better as we gain friends.

Even though this faire is primarily geared towards wizards, I invite everyone to attend. We have had mages, enchanters, even warriors and monks visit us in the past, and we continue to welcome any who wish to join us. The community is not restricted to any one class, and friends do not come in any specific race or class.

As I have in the past, I will put forth an effort to brew sufficient refreshments to appease the masses, but I am only one person. If you practice a trade skill, then I encourage you holeheartedly to bring with you the product of your trade. Taedirk, I know, makes some of the
finest lizard on a stick I've had yet, and I hope he will bring some of his own work to share with us, as I hope that you will bring some of your own.

If you have any questions, comments, concerns, or ideas pertaining to the faire, please contact me at fenninrunes@hotmail.com. I will get back to you as soon as I possibly can, and I hope to see you, one and all, at the faire.

Dynamite Shortfuze
Fennin Ro

EQ RolePlay Server Update
GeneralNews [ 1:21 PM EDT ] - Witt
The discussion of the proposed roleplay only EverQuest server has been continuing over at the the Developers Corner forum. Many topics have been covered regarding the possible rules and restrictions, the most recent of which is the possibility of removing class and race restrictions on the server :
Majelix wrote:
I realize you are busy with SoL, and the "RP server" hasn't gotten much more than beyond the discussion table at Verant, but I'd like to ask, will the race/class restrictions be removed for the rp server?

IE, it would be fun to play a halfling paladin or a drunken ogre bard. Of course, this presents the problem of the odd combinations becoming common, which is exactly why you disallow them on normal servers.

Rather than race/class restrictions, perhaps you could have diety/class restrictions? IE, since iksars -have- worship CT, they can not be paladins.

It seems unlikely that we'll change any of the class/race combinations for this server. There is no roleplaying need to do so, and there are no balance or gameplay issues that would be created by this server ruleset (unlike Sullon Zek) that would require such changes.

No doubt someone could come up with a reason for a halfling to be a paladin, or an ogre to be a bard. But the race/class combinations are set up as they are to represent the way things are in Norrath.

And if there is some concern that there would be a disproportionately large number of halfling paladins in Norrath on a regular server (though I've never heard anyone voice a concern over it…), then the same would apply on a roleplaying server.

Alan
