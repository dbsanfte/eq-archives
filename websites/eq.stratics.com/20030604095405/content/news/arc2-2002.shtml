Norrath Lore
GeneralNews - 4:23 AM PST
One concerned player pointed out the small inconsistencies between the official sites amd true 'Norrath Lore'. Here is what Absor responded with:
A player wrote:
I was browsing through the PoP site and while I can say I was impressed with some of what I saw, I was disappointed in a couple of inconsistencies with the lore of Norrath, as we've known it. Most are minor, but one was the fact that in PoP, Solusek Ro rules the Plane of Flame, not the Plane of Sun as has been stated before and is noted on the website. Another small thing was the screenshot of Tarew Marr on Gamespy.com looks cool, but absolutely nothing like the art on the deities page on Everquest.com. Are we going to be able to use what we've learned of the lore of Norrath to guess what's coming up in this expansion or are we going to see other little discrepancies such as these?

It's important to understand that lore is written by folks with a viewpoint. The Dark Elves don't view history the same way that the High Elves do. Until recently very few Norrathians believed that wild tale that Ogres accepted as truth, that one about them once being like gods in the far distant past. In fact, I can think of a few interesting tid-bits of information that Norrathians take for granted as true that are not as true as they think

But I can't talk about that.

The EverQuest world is very large, well defined, and has a ton of lore. But when history is written by individuals with agendas there can be a conflict in that lore. Such things are not wrong, or even bad, for a game world. Quite the opposite is true, especially in a shared game space like EverQuest. The idea that Dark Elves and High Elves can argue all day about the truth of the origins of life, or that Gnolls believe themselves to be children of Brell - something that a Dwarf would disagree with using the blade of an axe, all add to the depth of the world. The truth in such matters is dependent on the character speaking.

However, as we move forward creating actual gameplay and physical game space for all that lore, sometimes we have to make those myths into reality.

In the case of Tarew Marr, for example, we let our excellent artists do what they do. If you read the description on the web site, it's pretty clear that Norrathians don't have a clue what Tarew Marr looks like. It's also clear from seeing the model that he doesn't look much at all like the sketch on the web site. In this particular case it was just best to let our crazy-good art folks do something creative. The result is a fantastic looking Tarew Marr. Personally, I love the idea that this god doesn't look as much like a humanoid as others. I also think it makes perfect sense that Norrathians who have never seen this god would think of him in terms of their own appearances.

Yes, there are times when the 'reality' of a completed game concept such as a zone or an NPC might conflict with known lore. Some times that is because the people of the world just have the lore wrong. The Gnolls and the Dwarves can't both be right. And some times we change the lore to make a better story or to fit the real needs of game construction. If lore describes, say, a location in such a way that would be unworkable in game, we're going to have to change the lore. That can't be helped. But we strive to make it all make sense. I can't tell you how often we talk about how to mesh lore and gameplay with game mechanics and good old-fashioned story telling.

So in answer to your question, there will be rare times when we'll change things in small portions as needed for the over-all story and gameplay. But the lore of Norrath has been the foundation for everything we've built, and we always take efforts to make sure that stories are told from the point of view of those living them.

Alan

Focus Items In The Spotlight
GeneralNews - 2:22 AM PST
Well, with the community in an uproar over the suspected 'Nerf of Focus Items, the EverQuest Team thought it best to explain them. Here is what they say:
With all the excitement and concern that focus items have brought up lately, we wanted to take a minute and talk about them. For those of you unfamiliar with them - focus items are armor, items, or weapons that enhance an aspect of spell casting for the character that wears them. Focus items can have a wide variety of effects, including things like increased damage or healing, lowered cast times, decreased reagent use, increased spell range, and decreased resist rates.
Focus items are a new and exciting concept for EverQuest, and one that we expect to change the game for spell casting classes. These items will allow casters to see the same level of improvement from earning items that melee characters do from acquiring better weapons and armor. For too long it's been the case that an item for melee classes is a much bigger upgrade than an equally hard to acquire item for casters. As more focus items make it into the game in the near future, casters of all levels should be able to obtain a number of useful focus items to improve their character.

We recently introduced focus items as Magician spells - this was done for a couple of reasons. First, it allowed us to get the items into circulation fairly rapidly and into the hands of players. This let casters get a look at the new focus abilities and give feedback on them. Second, because it was such an important feature, putting the first batch in as No Rent summoned items made it more painless to players if we needed to make adjustments to the specific details of each item. Because the items were easy to acquire and didn't require anyone to lay out a great deal of time or effort to try them out, it was the best way we could introduce them at this time, and we think it worked out fairly well.

We certainly considered leaving the focus items on the Test Server for a couple of weeks to try to work out all the little details of item balance and presentation before rolling them out to live servers. But we decided to go ahead and include them in the recent patch so we could get a broad range of feedback, show casters that we recognize some of the issues bothering them in game, and because we were pretty excited about focus items and expected players would be too.

We understand that adjusting things after they have come into the game causes people some concern. As a rule, we try to not make changes to existing items when possible and to recognize the impact this may have on players when we do make changes. We will always try to notify our customers of changes of this type when we can, unless we want to keep something a mystery. Discovery is an important part of the game. This change, however, wasn't something we were trying to keep hidden, and it wasn't something we could have kept hidden had we tried to. We apologize for failing to mention these changes in the patch message.

We understand that every player isn't going to agree with every change to the game or decision we make regarding game balance. However, the dev team makes each decision with the best interests of the game in mind. No decisions are ever made to antagonize players or characters of a certain class, or any players at all for that matter. When we make a change to the game or add a new feature, we do it with the intent of making the game better and keeping EverQuest running as an interesting and challenging game for years to come. As EverQuest continues to evolve and develop, more things will change. We'll continue to add new content and things to do. Things will continue to change for the better, but in a game of this size and complexity we need to have the option to change things that are too easy, too powerful, or too great a reward for the effort. At the same time we need to continue to fix and upgrade any part of the game that is too hard, unenjoyable, or inadequately rewarding. We're working on that each day, and we appreciate your patience and understanding. We want EverQuest to be a game you enjoy playing, and keeping the game balanced is crucial to this goal.


On to the case in point - focus items. After we introduced focus items in the patch two days ago, we began to get a lot of very valuable feedback from players about the items. Some people loved them, some people hated them, and many people had suggestions for specific ways to change the items. We read over the suggestions, paid attention to the points they made and then decided to make some changes to the Magician summoned items we'd introduced. There were good points about the items made by people on both sides of the issue, and we feel we were very objective about the changes we made.

Here are the changes that we introduced in last night's patch:

- Jedah's Choker items - This item gives a chance of not using a reagent when you cast a spell that normally would use a reagent. The items were modified so they no longer effect Enchant Metal spells, or Imbue Gem spells. The goal of the item was to save reagents used for summoning pets, casting buffs, or using Translocate fragments, not to give people a chance at a free platinum bar every time they enchanted one.

- Naki's Ring items - This item decreases spell cast time when worn. Initially, it worked on any spell. As of last night's patch, it works on spells between 4 and 9.5 seconds casting time. We are currently working on a feature to allow us to exclude certain spells from working with a focus item, and when we get that in we will alter this item to not have a max casting time limit for spells it works with.

This item was altered because the decrease in casting time for Complete Heal proved to be too powerful, and was potentially unbalancing to the game. Complete heal is already an incredibly powerful tool for the Cleric, and was not in need of improvement. The 4 second minimum time was added to prevent some very fast casting spells from becoming even faster. We'll continue to evaluate fast casting spells and may improve this focus item in the future if it's needed.

- Gallenite's Bracelet items - This item increases the amount of damage done by direct damage spells. This item was changed to not give additional damage on Wizard's Bane spells. Bane spells are already very efficient and are not in need of further improvement.


Overall, the focus items in game now are well balanced and just needed a couple of minor tweaks so far to bring them in line. Once you see how the summoned focus items fit in with the ones that will be gotten in other ways, we're sure you will find they fit in nicely.

---

We feel that these changes were fair and that it is reasonable to adjust new items that are added to the game in this way. We appreciate your patience and all of the very insightful feedback that players sent in.

In the coming weeks, we'll be adding more focus items to the game as quest rewards, trade skill items, and as loot off of monsters. There is a high chance that we'll be upgrading many existing caster items to include focus effects, to keep casters from needing to start from scratch with their gear selections. When the process of working focus items into the game is done, we feel that casters will be quite happy with the results.

- The EverQuest Team

- Metabolism should be working properly now
- Scribble Notes should reduce memorization times to an even greater extent than it did before

- Fixed a song bug that was causing songs to be stuck on, triggered by contact proc buffs procs (such as Boon of the Garou)

Alan:

I appreciated the comments on the manastone. Having played with parties that had one I realized how difficult it would be for the designers to accomodate both parties that had a manastone and parties that did not.

This led me to comment on a number of very positive changes that represent change in position for Verant. Each of these changes in position have their own story, but taken together they show a company committed to providing the best possible product for its customers:

1. Manastones--Clerics without manastones sometimes had trouble getting into raid groups. The ability of a cleric to manastone down and then do a complete heal was clearly and visibly unbalancing. It also diminished the class-defining Cannibalize and Dark Empathy lines. Tough call to restrict usage to the Old World...but right call.

2. Donal's Breastplate of Mourning--Mana free complete heals used strategically mean that the players always win all single fights with a main tank plus complete heal cycle...and the clerics are at full mana at the end. Again notice that clerics without the breastplate were often left out of raid groups. Tough call to remove the complete heal...right for the game.

3. Soulbinders--This game is designed for interclass dependence. You assemble the parts to make a stronger whole than you can make with just parts. But late at night and particularly for overseas players, bind affinity wasn't always easy to come by. Compromise? Soulbinders are not always in the most convenient locations, but they give you a bind when you need one. Excellent choice.

4. Rangers and defense--Rangers by design are offensive melees with spells. They are based off of a chain-armor class 'template' that they share with Rogues and Shaman. The chain disadvantage first becomes apparent around 39th level when the Ranger's Defense skill starts falling behind Warrior's. Rangers were finding themselves unable to be 'light tanks' and actually take damage the way they were used to. First Verant implemented Jolt to mimic Rogue evade and permit the Ranger with personal aggro control.

Then after letting that change sink in they came back and added defense to the Rangers. Result is a class that is VERY powerful in melee but can still take an appropriate licking (and continue ticking). Kudoes for the principle to implement the vision of the design and for tweaking it where appropriate.

5. Removing teleport restrictions for Emerald Jungle, Skyfire, Great Divide, Wakening Lands, and Cobalt Scar--When the Kunark and Velious expansions came out, the designers decided there would be three ways to get to Kunark (Firiona Vie, The Overthere, and the Combines/Dreadlands portals). You had to port from inside a Kunark dungeon to use Emerald Jungle or Skyshrine evacs. Similarly when Velious came out you could either take the boat to Iceclad or get a port to the Iceclad Portal--you had to get a tooth to go to the other portals. These restrictions were sensible storytelling limitations that controlled how players progressively saw Kunark and then Velious.

When Shadows of Luclin came out, Verant properly felt that the world was 'big enough' and removed the Kunark Dungeon and Velious 'Tooth' limitations. Tough choice, good for the game...allows the teleporting classes to get near fellow guild members and friends so they can be moved to a spire so they CAN spire up to Luclin.

Each of these decisions took time to make. Some of them were accompanied by cries of "Nerf". Others were generally well accepted by the playing community. But each of them demonstrates the character of the company to do the right thing for the game and for the players. Congratulations on making tough choices and sticking to your guns.

Please continue to give us the big picture perspective as you make these kind of tough decisions. It helps us players see that what seems right or fun for an individual player may be very harmful when the whole game is considered.

Tuesday March 5 2002
Magicians Return
GeneralNews - 2:17 AM PST
Recently, Absor posted his Q & A about Magicians. He answered a lot of questions, and added a lot of responses. Here's his post:
I recently responded to an email list of questions from the folks at the Mage Compendium. I wanted to copy it here. Some of my comments may seem out of context because it's basically the last response in an email conversation.
---

First, let me mention that I was looking at an old list of questions before. Ronaldor pointed me at the correct list. Thanks Ron.

Unfortunately, this list is a heck of a lot longer. It also contains a lot of questions that are opinion based rather than fact based. Right now our feeling is that casters need some work, and that includes mages. But I don't have any hard numbers, solid figures or anything like that to present about, for example, how warriors and magicians compare for damage output. Heck, I'm not even real sure if such a comparison is a fair one, but I'll leave that to the systems expert. Answers to questions like that will have to wait until the balancing stuff is done.

So what you're getting here is opinion mixed with fact. I know that might be confusing, so I'll try to be as clear about what is my opinion and what I know as fact. I apologize for not being able to answer all of the questions. With such a long list I decided I'd rather answer most of the questions now and get back to you on the rest.


Questions For The Magician Class

General

1) All classes in EverQuest have a role that grows with them as they progress upwards in levels. A different role for each different class, some healers, some melee damage dealers, some magic damage dealers. What is the intended role for the Magician Class to fill, in relation to other classes like Warriors, Wizards, or Monks? Tuni Gearspinner

A) Magicians are about pets and blasting things, along with a few debuffs and damage shields. Of course they also summon a bunch of stuff. But this question seems to be about combat potential. Pets allow a mage to deal melee damage and tie up an enemy while regaining mana, a sort of living (kinda) melee DoT. So I suppose I see their 'role' when it comes to combat as both melee and magic damage dealers with some utility. I get the feeling that I'm missing the point of this question, since that answer seems pretty obvious.

2) Do you feel Magicians are fulfilling this role well in comparison to other classes filling their roles at present? Tuni Gearspinner

A) Obviously we feel that there are some adjustments to be made. I understand the problems post 50, for a lot of casters. And I understand how twinking items affects, or rather doesn't affect, casters. The team understands it too. You'll just have to give us some time to work on it.

3) Would you say our role defined by Question 1 has changed for Levels 50 60? -Ronaldor Vladimir

A) In practice, yes it has. I'm not adverse to roles changing over the growth of a class. Shamans, as a simple example, start out beating up on things with clubs and sticks and pointy things at low levels, but lose that as they grow. At low levels a druid is nearly as effective at healing as a cleric, but that too changes as they advance. I'm not so much worried about a change in roles. I am, however, worried that mages suffer from a lack of a role at levels post 50. That's one of the things that will be looked at as caster balancing moves forward.

4) Where exactly do we stand compared to Warriors, in a typical high-end dungeon encounter in terms of damage output? -Euriped Moped

A) We'll find that out as the balance process continues. Right now, I don't know. But I would warn you about such a comparison. It's certainly of some value, but conclusions shouldn't be drawn without considering all the factors. Also, we're talking about different kinds of damage. Against different NPCs and/or with different equipment any such comparison will be different.

5) Does Verant truly believe we are still overpowered after three expansions, especially from level 50 upwards? -Biggerin Allahya

A) Not really (that's my opinion, but I'm pretty sure most folks on the team would agree). Again, we're going to have to determine things like that in a more quantitative way than just "believe". Personally, I think magicians are pretty much in line up to about 50. After that, if a few issues could be worked out, I think they'd be right back in line. But don't discount the value of being able to solo pretty easily in the early levels. Such ability doesn't define a class, nor does it mean that a class should be underpowered later. But it has value as well.

6) Does anyone on the EverQuest live team play a Magician as his or her main character? -Kalryn Mavric

A) Yes.

7) Was chain casting pets intended to be a primary ability of the magician, specifically 50+? Zebanar

A) Intended? Probably not during the design of the class, though three or four years later that's hard to tell. Is it a strategy that we are aware of now? Sure. Primary ability? In so much as pets are a large part of what Mages do, yes. THE primary ability? Probably not.

8) Given the increased competition in the MMORPG market, what specific measures, if any, does Verant plan to implement that will improve the quality of their software development process? -Razamatazz Pizzazz

A) We have made many internal changes to our development process over the past few months based on our Luclin experience. We will let our customers judge as to their effectiveness by the quality of adventures in Norrath over the years to come.

9) What is Verants view of Mages? Are we perceived as unrepentant whiners or are we hard-core players that have valid concerns about our class, or something in between? Faustian

A) Seriously? There are two answers to this question. The simple company platitude, assuring you that we value the opinion of all our customers and that none of them are considered whiners in any way. The other answer is to just point out how silly it is for you to ask a question like this when you know there is only one answer that I'm going to give you.

People are people. I don't look at all the players of one character class as anything at all. I look at individuals and deal with them that way.

10) Do you feel magicians are balanced against other player classes in a player versus player environment? Aedwen

A) We readily admit that EverQuest is not geared to be a PvP game. When we talk about 'class balance' we are going to be primarily talking about PvE EverQuest. That being said, we know there are some bugs that cause problems in PvP for Mages, such as the whole issue with charming pets. Barring those bugs, and assuming that we do a good job balancing the casters, then the answer would be yes. Yes, they are as balanced as any other class in PvP, with advantages against some folks and weaknesses against others. But I'm certainly not an expert on PvP.

11) Will any of those monsters, which I could quite easily suggest as being summoned, be changed to summoned monsters as there are not actually that many summoned monsters in the game? E.g. Golems, Goo, Rockfiend, just to name but a few. Runemaker

A) Golems are created, not summoned. Goo who the heck knows where Goo comes from. Rockfiends are natural creatures (meaning that they don't come from somewhere else). We may make some adjustments to certain NPCs at times to make them 'summoned' or not, and since Norrath is a fantasy world it would be just as easy to say that Goo is indeed summoned from someplace. It's also just as possible to say that some elementals are summoned and some are natural. In the end it really has to be decided by the designer of a given zone whether or not certain NPCs will be affected by summoned-specific spells or items. We'd certainly look over any suggestions for changes, as long as those suggestions take into account the overall balance of the game and zone.

12) Since melees have enjoyed several upgrades, and everyone has been able to gain 10 more levels since the 66% damage spell cap was introduced pre Kunark, are there any plans to remove the spell/pet damage cap in the player versus player environment? Mavermage

A) Not that I know of. And we're not really going to be able to look at how casters match up in PvP until after we've balanced them for PvE. But we've certainly talked a little bit about the 66% thing. We're not inclined to remove it, it has value. But we might alter it in some way to make it more functional. I don't have any more details since we've just been tossing the idea around a bit.

13) Because Magicians lack defensive spells e.g. root, snare, or blind and the ability to escape or avoid combat when we dont have a pet present, should we not be more adept offensively especially from level 50 upwards?

A) Magicians lack defensive skills as part of their class design. "More adept offensively" compared to Wizards? Compared to Enchanters? Compared to Magicians as they are now? We'll look all that over when we review caster balance. But just because a class doesn't have root doesn't, on it's own, mean that the class should have some sort of extraordinary damage dealing ability.

Pets

1) Is there any chance that in the future the experience penalty for a pet doing over 50% of the damage while soloing to any monster will be removed? -Ghalon Soulburn

A) There's always a chance. Again, we'll look at that as well when we review casters. However, remember that whatever 'soloing ability' Mages have now (whether considered great or average) would be dramatically increased if pets suddenly stopped earning experience for all of their hard work and sacrifice.

2) How much experience does a pet take from a group is it still 1% on average (Various player tests have indicted that a pet takes the same amount of experience as a player)? Ascii

A) A pet will take a fair share of the experience (as if it was an additional party member) IF the pet does more damage than all other group members (individually). If any party member outdamages the pet, the pet only takes 1% of the experience.

3) Will the new pet animations for the new pet graphics be fixed/added in the near future? Pitounet

A) The new pet animations will be added. Right now, though, we don't have a timeframe this.

4) When will /target once again target the pet and not the pet's master? Toem

A) We got this one already. - Pets can now be targeted by typing their full name after the /target command (or at least enough of it to distinguish it from the owner)

5) Will a pet health bar ever be added either to the group display or the individual character display? Ronaldor Vladimir

A) It might. It's not something we're averse to doing at all. But you certainly won't see it before you see the new interface. There's no point in adding it to the old one.

6) Will chat filters ever be added for pets so that we may turn off melee battle spam, but sill be able to see our pets melee damage and spell effects? Kalamos

A) More filters is something we want to do for several cases where they are needed. Again, this will probably have to wait until after the new interface is done.

7) As a magician, my pet is my lifeblood. It is what sets me apart from being a poor mans Wizard. Does Verant believe our pets (Particularly 50+, and our Epic pets) are overpowered, and if so in what situations (i.e. experience grouping, raiding or soloing)? -Silvasa T`Karr

A) We're looking at that right now. Reviewing pets is part of the process. I could give my opinion, but it might vary from the opinion of the guy sitting next to me, whose opinion might be different than the next guy. Instead, I'll wait and see what kind of results come out of the review.

8) Will a pet equip armour, jewellery and weapons that are not NODROP and receive the statistical benefits they offer? Siadhail

A) A good rule is this: If it will equip a weapon it will probably equip most items, if that item is better than one it may already have. Better in the pet's mind might not match what the owner thinks is better, and the value of some items to pets is questionable (giving your pet + Int items won't make the pet choose better targets). Also keep in mind that the value that your pet gains from the use of a piece of armor probably won't be the same value that a PC would gain from it. Skill levels have a lot to do with how a character absorbs or avoids damage.

9) Why can pets no longer taunt a monster off of any player character that has aggravated a monster (No matter how slightly) that is within the monsters melee range? -Nandail Mandragoran

A) I don't have an answer for this question at this time.

10) Currently there is a player versus player bug that allows Bards and Enchanters to destroy player pets at will through the use of Charm will this be addressed? Grumples

A) Yes, it will. I don't know when.

11) Will the Magician epic quest ever have Quillmane changed to a triggered spawn, or have a similar monster added to Southern Karana that can be triggered? Ronaldor Vladimir

A) We don't have any plans to change that part of the quest. My suggestion, at least for younger magicians, would be to get the cloak when you're of a level to gain experience in the zone. Play there and have fun, snag Quillmane while you're there.

12) What is the difference between "/pet follow" and "/pet guard me, they appear to work the same? The Dweller In The Darkness

A) No difference at all, really. There may have been a time when "/pet guard me" would cause the pet to attack anything that came near the pet owner, but now both commands do the same thing.

13) Do you have any intention on improving current pet control mechanisms in the near future, and/or adding new controls and /pet commands to pet control, if so what do you have in mind? Noonie Vilotskies

A) Not really. The current control commands fall within the range of effective but not too effective. Remember, part of the value of a live player over a pet is that the player is smarter (though maybe not as easily controlled).

14) Is there a code imposed limit on the number of pets that can be actively engaging a single monster, and if so what is that limit? Istaron

A) Yes. No more than four pets can engage a single target. This is basically to prevent use of only pets to defeat opponents. We think it's important to have some player characters involved in the combat at times.

15) Why has pet offensive and defensive abilities not been scaled up with the expansions to meet the needs of the new encounters, especially from level 51 upwards? Eandori

A) See question #7 about pets. This is a repeat (with a little more spin on it).

16) Is there any plan to try and improve pet pathing in the future, if so what is it? Kysis Ka Tra

A) "Pet pathing" isn't something that we can just flip a switch and make better. It's a complicated system. We make improvements where we can.

17) Has the epic pet been altered in any way since the Magician Epic quest went live, and if so in what way? Ronaldor Vladimir

A) Not that I know of. I suppose it would help if you told me what you think had been changed.

18) Why are pets taking so long to buff themselves? Halves

A) There are a LOT of pets in EverQuest. Pets casting buffs use up bandwidth and CPU cycles. Zones with a lot of pets could be adversely affected by all the pets casting stuff. We increased the time that all pets will take between buffs to reduce resources used.

19) Now that pets are capable of zoning with their masters why dont they? - Fadzen Silverthorn

A) Some do, some don't. The familiar is, essentially, a buff with a visual representation. The warder zones, but it loses its buffs when it does so. In reality all pets need to be 'recast' when the owner zones. The warder gets all tiny when he zones and the buffs needed to bring him back to speed are very much in line with a Mage recasting his pet. Basically, we decided not to dramatically change that part of gameplay.

20) Are there any plans for removing random generated pet names, and instead displaying them similar to how Beastlord pets are Character's pet/elemental? Noonie Vilotskies

A) We've encountered nothing but trouble once we started naming familiars and warders after their owners. It sorta messes with things that refer to entities by name. Right now I don't know of any plans to change the way other pets are named.

21) The Monster Summoning line of spells is in effect no different to our normal pets except in the fact they look different, will they ever be changed to behave more like the monsters they actually are? -Zebanar

A) We don't have any plans to change Monster Summoning. And if by "behave more like the monsters they actually are" you mean "do a ton more damage and have a ton more hit points", then the answer is no. Changes to these pets might happen during the balancing process, but any such changes would probably be small when compared to "the monsters they actually are".

A) Well, that depends on the utility spell in question. Spirit of Wolf? Probably not. Ultravision? Sure. True North? Absolutely! We'll certainly be looking at this as well during the balancing pass.

2) Are there any plans to change Call of The Hero so that it will function in all zones without trivialising the game encounters, if so what are your current ideas to achieve this? Qalenie

5) Will we see the Mage class receive more elemental DD spells like ice, lightning? We should have control of elemental spells as magicians are Elementalists. Ielen

A) Unlikely. It's a good idea to avoid using labels like Elementalist when talking about class balance. And besides, Elementalist could mean just about anything. I see it as "controller of elemental beings." Wide spread damage types is pretty much the purview of the wizard. But I'm not going to rule it out completely. Who knows what solutions will come from the balancing.

6) Why can a level 49 Mage cast for 810 damage (72% as good as Wizards damage at level 49) but a level 60 mage can only cast for 1005 damage (48% as good as Wizards damage at level 60)? Eandori

A) Another thing to be reviewed when balancing casters. But it's best to avoid comparisons like this. You're not including all of the factors that make up these spells like casting time, recast time, type of damage, mana cost, range the whole thing. Just tossing out damage numbers isn't enough. We'll take a more in-depth look at this sort of thing and make the adjustments we think are needed.

7) Why is there a 6 level limit for what a caster can land spells on, but no apparent cap exists for melee? Eandori

A) There isn't really a six level limit. Here's how it works: There is a level limit for spell effects that has a minimum of six levels. A spell will not affect an NPC that is more than 1.3 times your level, or 6 levels higher, which ever is greater. So at 15th level it's 6 levels. At 40th it's 12 levels. Now, how often is a 40th level warrior going to actually hit while attacking a 53rd level NPC?

8) What does the level 56 spell Exile Summoned offer over the level 52 spell Char (It is a better spell in nearly every application, even mana cost)? Eandori

A) One is magic damage, one is fire damage. Otherwise, they do indeed seem very similar.

9) Is the drop rate and difficulty of obtaining Muzzle of Mardu and Velocity correct? Ronaldor Vladimir

A) It is now.

10) Why does Dyzils Deafening Decoy have lower melee and level then the Vocerates (Level 51 54 pets), yet it costs almost as much mana as the Greater Vocerates (Level 57 60 pets)? Eandori

A) /em shrugs. Another thing to look at. I don't know what other features that pet might offer.

11) Is there a possibility that Mages would ever receive any type of Voice Graft spell? Ronaldor Vladimir

A) Personally, I wouldn't give it to mages. Of course now their pets look more human and might be reasonably argued to be capable of speech, something that wouldn't have made sense before. It's just one of those things that another class gets to differentiate it from other classes. I would just leave it with the necros. However, that's my opinion. I can't tell you that it will never be given to magicians. But I also can't tell you that it will.

12) Will Magicians ever be able to cast Rage of Zomm while having a pet up, if not why not? Ronaldor Vladimir

A) Probably not. More than one pet = badness. If the reason you ask this question is because you think that the spell needs improvement, then perhaps there are other ways to make it more useful.

13) Does the Haste effect of the Burnout series of spells stack with other Haste spells cast on our pets? Wilme

A) I got this answer straight from Lawrence Poe: "The Haste components of the spells will stack, but whichever haste component is highest is the one that is applied. Swift like the Wind and Burnout will stack, the pet will get all the effects from Burnout, but the haste will come from Swift like the Wind."

14) Any plans for the Magicians to receive some kind of "Lure" spells, some monsters (e.g. Venril Sathir) are immune to all Magician spells, but not to wizard Lures? Reinolds

A) I don't know, but I don't think so. There are very few creatures that are immune to all Mage spells. In fact, a quick look at Venril suggests that he is no more immune to "all" magician spells than he is to any other caster (Lures being an exception).

15) Does charisma have any effect on the resistance check for casting Malosini? Wilme

A) I don't think so. If it does, it's minor.

16) Have you ever thought of a % increase to our spell damage when we dont have our pets summoned? -Nahualli Uoxtitla

A) I haven't thought of it. The team has these questions, though. It's up to them to decide what changes to make. Personally, I'm not so sure it's a good idea to make it so that magicians don't want to use their pets. I'd rather see it them find inspiration to use their pets more.


Items

1) What happened to caster focus items supposed to be introduced with Luclin? -Raiste Parragon

A) They didn't get in. Time constraints took their toll.

2) Why does our Velious robe have a level 49 damage shield while the Druid breast plate has a 58 group regeneration, and the Necromancer robe has a level 57 corpse summon? -Xerath Dar'Khan

A) It's not entirely fair to just point at spell levels and use that as a basis for the 'balance' of such items. I'll leave it to the balancing folks to make any determination about this sort of change.

3) Why do our quest given focus items not work on pets higher then level 49? Eandori

A) My thought is that when those pets were created, they were created to be powerful enough without focus items. Again, we'll be looking at pets.

4) Since we sacrifice the Staff of Mastery: Earth and Water in order to obtain our epic, can the Orb of Mastery act as a pet focus item if we decide to summon an earth pet instead of an epic pet? Anjohl

A) /em shrug. I suppose it could. Should it? I don't know.

5) Will Mage low level Staff summons ever be changed to 1HB? Ronaldor Vladimir

A) Again, I don't know. But the team has this list of questions.

6) Why does the Staff of Elemental Mastery: Air quest and the Epic Quest share a common item? -Bouya

A) I don't know.

7) Why are the Staves of Elemental Mastery 2Hb and have no additional statistics on them? -Bouya

A) Again, I have no idea. For a lot of these questions you're asking why something was done. Unless I can get in the heads of the folks that designed these things, there's no way for me to know. If what you really want to know is "Why don't we change these things to something we think would be more useful?", then my answer is, we just might. Let's see what comes of the caster balancing.

8) Why do Magicians have so many items with Effect: Reclaim Energy? Bouya

A) See the answer to #7. But I went and checked. There are 15. One of those is the spell scroll. So that's 14. 4 of those are the interchangeable basic focus items (torch, broom, etc.). 4 more of them are the Staves of Elemental Mastery. So, really, those are all the same item (or 2 items). That makes 6 or 7 items that have Reclaim on them. Many of those items are better than others. So you can upgrade items as you go up levels and still have a right click Reclaim. There are other spell effects that are just as frequent on items get over it already.

Skills

1) Is Frenzied Burnout working as intended, and if not what do you see as being wrong with it (I am not just referring to the area effect explosion when the pet dies)? Ronaldor Vladimir

A) As far as I know it's working as intended. If you have some specific concerns about the ability you'll have to let me know.

2) Will research ever be extended to include some of the more common Kunark and Velious spells? Ronaldor Vladimir

A) It was the spell casting community that asked us to stop using research to give them spells. Personally, I'd just as soon not bring it back. And I don't know of any plans to do so. But there are some interesting ideas about how to use it floating around out there.

3) Is the Begging skill implemented and working as intended? Caeaedyen (There were rumours that begging certain non player characters provided hidden quests.)

A) Mages do a lot of Begging? It's not an exciting skill. It's pretty much working as intended, it's not meant to be a skill to write home about. And we're not saying anything about any hidden quest, then they wouldn't be hidden anymore, now would they?

4) Can we ever expect to receive dire charm/control the elements type skill for summoned monsters? Ielen

A) Probably not. We talked about it, but there are two problems. There aren't really enough summoned NPCs to make this seem like a worthy skill, and you can't use it with a pet. We figured it wouldn't be all that attractive to Mages.

5) According to the text in the online manual we are limited to one Elemental Form is this correct and if so why? Omel The Magician will be required to attune himself to a single element, and will not be able to attune himself to the others once he has done so. The Online Manual

A) The initial idea was to differentiate high level Mages. The concept of specialization is one way to allow characters to have different personalities. However, we decided to allow the magician to choose all four types if he wants. They are all on the same timer, though. The manual needs to be updated.

6) Why does our new quick summon skill not work with all conjuration based spells, or at least all our item/pet summons? Dreadnir

A) I've asked for more details. I'll send out an answer when I have more information.

7) Will our new skill Group Buff ever be replaced as Magicians only have one group buff? Finraziel

A) We knew it would be of limited appeal to most Mages in the short term. But who knows what spells we might choose to add in the future (and, no, this is not a promise that we'll be adding such spells). Not all Alternate Advancement abilities are going to be extremely appealing.

8) Why do we have to get 27 levels of Alternate Advancement for /pet hold when that should be given to us for free by the 50th level at least? Lord Gojirax

A) Because we don't feel that it "should be given" to you any earlier. It's represents a complete mastery over your pet, something only achieved after much work. Earning your way to the level needed to get that ability is part of the effort of becoming a master.

9) Will Elemental Pact be changed to remove the need for Malachite altogether? -Jochan Galadar

A) That would be nice. We're looking into it.

Wow! That's a lot of Q & A! So what do you think of it? Post below!

GeneralNews - 6:17 AM PST
Absor once again stopped by the EQ Developers Corner to answer some more questions about Caster Changes that are being worked on. Heres what he said this time:
suleain wrote:
Hello and thank you for letting the casters know you are looking into things.
I do have a favor to ask however. On various sites, there has been a lot of question and I will admit flaming that these fixes seem to be aimed that the 55-60 crowd. I would just like to have an answer saying that yes we are looking at ALL levels of play and balance issues.

We're looking at the whole thing, just as we did with melee.
The conclusion might be that only changes are needed post 50. It might not. But we're not neglecting any stage of the life of the caster.

vallon_sk wrote:
Clerics, shaman and druids are Pure Casters??
I've been under the impression since I started in '99, that wiz, mage, ench, and necro's are the only Pure Casters-as they have no other abilities but their magic (with out going into huge discussion that necro can 'tank/melee" with their pet-to a degree). Let's nail down some terminology, for everyone's sake. I'll edit Rich to make sure he follows the same terminology.
Pure Caster means any non-hybrid, non-melee (Necromancer, Magician, Enchanter, Wizard, Cleric, Druid and Shaman).
Int Caster are Necromancers, Magicians, Enchanters and Wizards.
Wis Casters (or Priests) are Clerics, Druids and Shamans.
Hybrids are Bards (though they will probably have to be considered separately for most things), Rangers, Paladins, Shadowknights and Beastlords.
Melee are Monks, Rogues and Warriors.
