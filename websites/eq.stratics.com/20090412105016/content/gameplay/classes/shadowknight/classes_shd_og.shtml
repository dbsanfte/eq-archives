The Ogre Shadowknight


So yu want tu be uh ogre SK eh? Ha! HAHAHAHAAHAHA! Guud luk! But if you really want to you can read this guide. This is, in my opinion, the best way to get past those early formative levels of being an ogre SK. First off chose a name that is not intelligent sounding. No Edwards or Malcomes please gods. Second put all your bonus points into int because it’s something that you need really bad that you don’t have a lot of. Okay you start out in Oggok, city of the ogres. Go find Soonog your guild leader and give him your note. Then go out into the feerott. Go to the left wall as you come out and follow it for a little ways. When you reach some enemies you must kill, kill, and kill some more. Save your harm touch for in case you get into trouble, a practice that often saves my life. Pick up the cloth and meat that the lizards drop and slap it on. Save the meats till you have 4, then go back to Soonog. Give him the meat and he will give you a rusty two-hander. This is the weapon your ganna wanna use for now. You will also want to train a point or two into two-handed slashing, one into taunt, one into sense heading, and one into hand to hand. These, along with slam, are the skills you are going to be using constantly for the next 8 levels. Fight in that area east of Oggok untill you reach level three. Then go south to the river and follow it southeast until you get to the zone wall. Don’t cross the river ever at this point because it’s a jungle over there. Follow the wall north till you get to innothule swamp. This is your home now because the feerott sucks. Go south along the zone wall here until you get to Grobb. Get bound here. Don’t go to the shadowknights guild here, ever. They hate you and you are kos to them. Go out into innothule. Follow the zone wall to the right, remembering where the bashers are, as they will save your life if you get into trouble. Fight lesser kobolds and frogloks until level 5. The frogloks drop netted armor, which is good, because it’s weightless. Never spend money on armor you will get it off of the enemies you kill mostly. Then go farther along the right wall till you get to the front of guk entrance. You can fight froglok guards and fishermen here, as well as the fungus man trackers. Don’t go into guk, its way too dangerous for two spawns that are harder than they should be. Only zone into guk if you get into trouble with a train or multiple mobs. Don’t go any deeper than that entrance hallway and always watch out for trains in guk, as they are much more deadly than trains in innothule. Fight the frog fishers and guards and fungus mans until about level 7-8 or you can get a group of 2 or three lvl 6 or so. Then go further along the right zone wall until you see an Island with a stone tower on it off to your left in the swamp. Go to the base of the tower but don’t go upstairs because a tuff mummy is up there some times. A glitch some times lets him hit you when your downstairs even some times so don’t linger around in here for too long. Kill the skele’s and save the bone chips. Also kill the froglok foragers (which drop mesh armor, better than netted.) and kobold hunters around here. Watch out for multiple enemy’s because that’s usually lethal unless you have a harm touch left. Fight here until level 9 when you’re going to want to go back to Oggok to get your spells and magic skills. First spell to buy is your pet because that’s the most help right now. Bet your glad you saved your bone chips right? WHAT? You sold em? Haha! Get some more. Slowly buy all of your spells, you need every one, sort of. You can now go many more places and get exp, as innothule is not your best option any more. My suggestion is the derv and orc camps of south ro. Not too far from Grobb and profitable too in the case of the dervs (derv rings.). Might want to buy some patchwork if you can from your fellow adventurers. Almost as good as leather and costs and weighs less. Upgrading your weapon whenever you can as it effects your killing quite a lot. Int jewelry is good because int is good. At this point where you go is up to you mostly but watch out for the forces of good, especially the guards, cuz there tuff. The world is your oyster, pick as many pearls out as you want. Undead dungeons are good because your invisibility vs. undead actually helps here and it coincides with your need of bone chips. You get dodge at level 10 which helps some with fighting. Meditate at level 12 really helps out so get it right away. New spells at level 15 which are much better than the last round. Double attack is one of your best skills and your get a lot better when you get that. New spells again at level 22, and again at 30. Watch out for reds though you’re not a titan yet. Be safe and kill all that you can!
