Bard AA Clarification
The Bard - 6:29 PM PDT
Absor posted the following in the EQ Developers Corner:
Ok, heres what happened with Instrument Mastery.
Though this seems obvious to me, I want to make it clear how this was supposed to work.

Singing Mastery is designed to improve the bards ability to sing songs.

Instrument Mastery is designed to improve the bards ability to play instruments.

Now there is a bit of possible confusion when we start talking about songs that are instrument based when the bard is not using an instrument. At that point the bard is singing and instrument song, and it really becomes a matter of taste as to whether Singing Mastery or Instrument Mastery should apply. Weve decided to base it on the skill used, not whether an instrument is in hand. So Singing Mastery applies to all Singing skill based songs and Instrument Mastery applies to all instrument based songs, whether or not the bard is using an instrument at the time.

No, these two abilities were never meant to overlap. It was a bug when Instrument Mastery gave a bonus to songs that use the Singing skill.

That bug was fixed this patch. Instrument Mastery no longer affects songs that use the Singing skill.

What weve decided to do is increase the amount that Singing Mastery affects Singing skill songs by 50%. That should go in the next time we patch.

Im sorry for not catching this in the patch message. Basically because this was the way it was always intended to be, we were not aware that wed fixed a bug.

Alan

Gem Colors Explained
GeneralNews - 6:25 PM PDT
With the new UI, the color of the spell gems have been changed:
With all of the new spell gems and with the change to the interface, it seems inevitable that wed get a few spell icons or a few gem colors crossed.
For example, I hear that Nektulos Portal has the Gate spell icon. We might want to change that to match the other group teleport spells.

And Pillar of Frost seems to have an orange spell gem and it should probably be green.

Heres a list of the gem colors and what they should mean, for reference:

Yellow: Personal - spells that you can only cast on yourself.
Red: Single Target - spells that affect (and require) a single target.
Blue: Point Blank Area of Effect - spell affects an area centered on the caster, but the caster is unaffected.
Green: Area of Effect - spell affects targets in the area centered on the target.
Orange: Area of Effect Rain - spell affects the area around the target, often in a continuing fashion.

The distinction between Green and Orange is pretty narrow.

So if you see something like this let me know (just reply to this thread). Ill gather a list.

Thanks.

Alan
Hopefully, that will remove some of the confusion.

*** New Interface Available ***

The new EverQuest user interface is now available for use on Live servers. Many of you will find that it runs faster than the old interface. It is extremely customizable, and offers all sorts of features including resizable windows, fading windows, a pet control window, and flexibility through the XML files. We decided to offer it for optional use as a sort of Beta II for folks on the Live servers that haven't had the chance to try it out on the Test server.

To activate the new interface:

* Log into the game.
* Type /newui into the chat bar and hit enter.
* Log out and exit to your desktop and then log back in.

or

* Before you log in:
* Open the EQclient.ini file in your EverQuest folder with a text editor (notepad works fine)
* Find the [Defaults] section of the file
* Add a line that reads: NewUI=TRUE
* Log in

We want to thank all the folks that have helped us test the interface and have offered a wide variety of useful ideas.

Enjoy the interface!


*** Bazaar Now Available ***

Those of you that own Shadows of Luclin now have access to the /bazaar and /trader functions. These functions only work in the Bazaar. You can find the Bazaar on Luclin, adjacent to the Nexus and Shadowhaven.

/bazaar opens up the Bazaar Search Window, which allows you to easily locate specific items for sale by Traders in the Bazaar. /trader opens up the Bazaar Vendor window, which allows you to make items available for sale to others in the Bazaar.

You can only use the Bazaar if you have the new interface turned on. See the message above for details.

Again we'd like to thank the folks that helped us test this feature. And we'd like to apologize for the delay in delivery of the Bazaar.

We think that you'll find the finished product to be a good one.


** ViewPort **

One of the new commands available only with the new interface is /viewport. You can use this command to change the size of the gameplay area.

/viewport [distance from left, distance from top, width, height]

All of the distances and numbers for the viewport command are in the same measure as your screen resolution numbers.

For Example: Let's say you are running in 800 x 600 video mode and you want to create view the game only in the top half of your screen (not that you would). You would type:

/viewport 0 0 800 300

The top left of your screen is 0 and 0. 800 is the width of your screen, and the 300 is one half of the height of the screen.

/viewport reset - will reset the viewport to the default setting.

We have developed new models for the Vah Shir for use with the old world. You may now choose to turn off the Luclin version of the Vah Shir models and use these new models if you wish. These new Vah Shir models require less memory and you may want to use them in place of the Luclin models to improve system performance.

- Bind Affinity can no longer be cast on any ungrouped player in arenas.
- The caster of Voice Graft will once again see what is being said.
- Spells that transfer hit points over time from the caster to a target will no longer take effect on the target (the hit point gainer) if the caster (the hit point giver) isn't also affected by the spell.
- Added a spell effect message to Magician epic.
- Beastlords now get Endure Disease at level 24 and Resist Disease at 51

- Pet Resistance Changes: Pets will now use their master's level and resistances when saving against spells cast by NPCs (against PCs pets will use their own resistances and level as they always have) - unless the pet has special resistances, in which case it will use that resistance.
- Altered pet summoning spells so that they always summon the most powerful pet possible, instead of pets within a 5 level range.
- /pet report health now uses the same user defined color as spell text.

Please be aware that the new Planar Rifts are No-Drop, and should only be converted in a forge by the intended owner. Pet Resist Changes
GeneralNews - 5:31 PM PDT
Rich Waters posted on a few forums about some changes being made to Pet Resists:
Hi,

We've had a change up on Test for about a week, and I wanted to share it with you guys and see what you think. Here's the basis of the change -

When a pet is hit with a spell, it uses the resistances and level of it's owner to see if it resists the spell.

This means that if you're a level 60 magician for instance and you've got on various buffs and gear that raise your resistances, your pet will save vs. spells as if it was also level 60 with your exact resistance scores.

Using the owner's level is going to make a huge difference in how well the pet resists harmful spells, and should make a noticeable difference in how well the pets can survive AEs for instance. Allowing the pet to also benefit from your items and buffs means that it will be living a lot longer when fighting casters.

There is a drawback to this, although I think the good outweighs the bad. If we go with this new scheme, it will mean that the pet always uses the owner's saves. Some types of pets currently are highly resistant to poison or disease for instance. Going with the new scheme would mean they will potentially not resist those types of spells as well, depending on the owner's resistances.

Overall, this change would mean that pets are much more resistant the vast majority of the time. I think this would be a very positive change for pet classes, but I'd like to see what people think before we go ahead with it.

Thanks for your time,

- Rich

Thursday July 4 2002
New Interface/Bazaar Review
GeneralNews - 11:18 AM PDT
Today I had so free time before work so I’d figure I should log onto Test Server and check out the new Interface and Bazaar.
After about five minutes of patching I reached the New and Improved Character Selection Screen. One word, “Wow!”. You choose your characters in a small 3D castle where you can walk around and choose who you want to play. Or you can just scroll through them. This is a huge improvement over the original Character Selection Screen.
Character Creation screen is still the same. No fuss there though. I decided to make a Gnome as my little guinea pig. I made my way through Steamfont and crept through Lesser Faydark. I arrived at the Spires in Greater Faydark and found I wasn’t the only one going to test out the Bazaar. While waiting for the ride up I went through most of the windows of the new UI.

-The windows are much more cleaner and user-friendly. The new friends list and pet attack windows are spiffy too. And even if you don’t like them it is all skinnable. Here is a screenshot.
-Along with the rest of the windows the Alternate Advancment Window got a face-lift.
-The new and improved spell book now contains 8 spell slots per page. There is also a shortcut to the spellbook under your spell lineup. So no need for another hotkey.
-Viewing your skills also has become easier. Instead of only knowing your amount of points in a certain skill when you reach 100 in it, the new skill window shows it no matter what your skill level.
-The only downside to the new interface I think is the inventory arrangement. It is just to unorganized and will take some getting used to. But it is all customizable. An upside to that is you can now enter quantity and coin amounts in numerical form instead of the crummy scroll bar.
-Lastly, the options menu got a well-needed upgrade. All the categories are on tabs and very organized.

The Nexus finally activates and I am beamed to the Moon. After a brief lag spike from all the bodies running around I head to the Bazaar.

-Now when you zone into the Bazaar you are greeted with the standard welcome message plus it also displays how many traders are in the zone and also how many items total. It also gives you the two basic commands to start trading.

-First I give the /bazaar command a try. I am welcomed with a nice window that lets me sort all the items in the Bazaar to what I am looking for. Search options are Race, stats, class, Item Name, and Player Name. When you do a query it shows the Item Name, Price, and the Seller. It doesn’t display the stats though. To purchase an item just find the merchant and right-click on them. That will bring up the merchant window that is the same as the NPC Merchant windows. Simple as that. No more than 3 people can look at a Trader’s inventory at a time. So if you happen to get the “I am busy now” message just hang tight and keep trying.

-Selling your items is a bit tricky but easy once you know what your doing. First thing you have to do is buy a Trading Satchel from the Merchant (can’t remember his name now but when you zone in from Nexus run past the bankers to the next building and he is the one behind the desk.) It costs around 1pp. Thanks to Dankuo of the Tundra I had enough coin to buy me one. I also bought a rusty dagger, wasp wing, and split paw tunic to use as my uber loot. Next thing you do is just place the items you are selling into the Satchel. After you have done that type /trader. This will bring up the Vendor Window. On the right it will show you what items you have in the satchel. Keep in mind that No Drop items won’t show up. This is where I got stuck. I clicked on my Rusty Dagger and highlighted it, but then all the other buttons grayed out. Rottenrumps on “Gnomish Corner” gave me a hand. You then have to set the price with the coin buttons then you will be able to use the "Set Price" button. After that it’s easy. You must be in one of the tent stalls to begin selling your wares. Then just set the prices of all your items and then hit “Begin Trader”.

I didn’t experience any bugs but it was a tad laggy in the Bazaar. The new interface and Bazaar look great to me. I can’t wait till they go Live. Go give it a try yourself!

Cosmoo
