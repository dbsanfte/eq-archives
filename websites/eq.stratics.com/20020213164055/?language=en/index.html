What Is Benefical?
Game_Patch [ 3:20 PM PST ] - Abombpk
It seems a poster was confused on exactly what 'beneficial' meant in the EverQuest Realm. Here's Absor's Answer:
Hello,
I have a question regarding the caster archtype alternate advancement ability spell casting deftness. It states that this only works with beneficial spells. In most cases it is clear cut what is a beneficial spell, and what is not, however, in the case of the necromancer, there is some ambiguity I think. So my question is, are the various tap spells that benefit the caster but harm the target considered beneficial or not? Also, are the new group taps considered beneficial? Are some considered beneficial and some not? If so, which ones? I think this question is quite pertinent for any necromancer to have answered, since the value of this ability greatly depends on what spells are considered beneficial.
Best,
Lambik

p.s. Are necro pet's lifetaps still being looked at?

A 'beneficial' spell is one that helps the caster. This will exclude spells that harm others. Tap spells are not 'beneficial' spells. Beneficial is a category that we use to define spells that are essentially not damage-dealing spells.

Yes, we're looking at the lifetap thing. I don't know when we'll get it fixed, though.

Alan

Thanks for that explanation, Abosr!

Ok, well not really. It seems there has been talk of some problems with Beastlords. Here's what Absor said about it:
The team has been thinking about these issues as well. I have a few answers for you. I'll put them in with your questions.
Just a couple of Beastlord Issues that I would like to bring up.

- Beastlords do not get the Hybrid ability to not have to wait for weapon delay to refresh before attacking after casting a spell. This ability was given to Rangers/Paladins/ShadowKnights a while ago. Were we overlooked? Or is this intentional?

That got overlooked. We should have Beastlords delayed at the same rate as other hybrids when we can. How soon I don't know.

- No level 50+ Disciplines. All Hybrids have 2 class specific Disciplines, except for beastlords. Are these in the works or will we never get any Class specific Disciplines?

They are designed and in the hands of the programmers. With any luck (not that we have a lot of that) they'll be in with the next patch. No, I'm not going to give any hints about what they are.

BlackSnow Interactive Sues Mythic
CommunityNews [ 12:28 AM PST ] - Lord_Of_Dreams
This is from Camelot Exchange
BLACKSNOW INTERACTIVE SUES MYTHIC (DAOC) IN FEDERAL COURT FOR MMORPG PLAYERS RIGHTS

Mythic Entertainment is named as the defendant in this case filed on Febuary 5th 11:50AM involving various anti-trust, copyright, and anti-competitive issues. BlackSnow Interactive (BSI) is a group of individuals that play, buy, and sell in various Massively Multiplayer Online Role Playing Games (MMORPG). Growing from only one person nearly two years ago, to seven full-time people, this group has successfully developed a market in which they are able to supply players with gaming currency, items, or characters at competitive prices. Mythic wants BSI to cease all sales immediately.

After entering the DAoC market, shortly after its release, BSI created a website and began listing their sales on various auction sites, such as Ebay. BSIs Director of Sales, Lee Caldwell, was quoted as saying, What it comes down to is, does a MMORPG player have rights to his time, or does Mythic own that players time? It is unfair of Mythic to stop those who wish to sell their items, currency or even their own accounts, which were created with their own time. Mythic, in my opinion, and hopefully the courts, does not have the copyright ownership to regulate what a player does with his or her own time or to determine how much that time is worth on the free market.

Caldwell goes on to say, Mythics attempt to stifle competition in their own game makes it possible for only full-time gamers to succeed in the game and most MMORPG players cant compete on that level. The person that plays just a few hours a week, cant put in the time required to build their character or collect the items needed to join others in the online battles. No one has stood up to any of these software giants, until now.

If you would like to take a look at the actual court document, please visit Court Documents.
I look forward to seeing how this lawsuit will progress.
