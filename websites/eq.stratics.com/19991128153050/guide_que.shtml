Guide to Quests

 Introduction   General Rules   NPCs   Key Words   Conclusion 

Introduction
For the purposes of this article, there are two kinds of quests: NPC quests and PC quests. PC quests are those that are put on by a player run organization of some sort (guilds, role play groups, etc.). This guide is meant to give you the knowledge and skills that you need as a player to find and enjoy the many NPC quests that have been created by Verant for the world of Norrath. The creators of EverQuest have created a network of quests that are specific to race, class and character level. They make use of special NPCs, secret passages, hidden areas in zones, special tools that are supplied for that quest, and special quest items that are only available as a quest completion reward.
 top


General Quest Rules
Quests can be extremely interesting and add a deeper element to the game. However, they are only fun if the player is discovering the quest and its eventual conclusion without someone telling them how to do everything. Verant has made several public statements condemning those who would spoil quests for others by publicizing the quest solution, prize, methods to succeed, NPCs to talk to, etc. My hope is that Verant will not have to assert their authority because players will respect others’ wishes to solve the quest themselves. So, as a rule, do not discuss quests with others who have not completed the quest in a public area (/shout, /ooc, /say in a crowded area, the chat server, etc).
 top


Non-Player Characters (NPCs)
As you advance through the world of Norrath you will meet new NPCs. Most NPCs are placed where they are for a purpose. For quest purposes there are only three types of NPCs; Vendors, Common NPCs and Quest NPCs. Vendors generally do not get involved in quests. An easy way to check if an NPC is a vendor or a quest NPC is to get close to them and right-click or "use" them. If a vendor menu comes up they will probably not be of assistance in a quest. Common NPCs are the ones without a special name. For example, "a gnoll" is a common NPC and Frumpy Mudpaw is name like a special NPC gnoll that may yield a quest item. If you target an NPC and say "Hail [NPC Name]" (type /h) the NPC will respond with an answer that is tailored to your class, race, and level. On rare occasions the NPC will refuse to talk to you if your character is out of the scope of the quest that NPC is part of.
 top


Key Words
When an NPC judges your character suitable for a quest it will respond with an introduction to the quest. Your job is to squeeze as much information out of that NPC as you can so that you will know what to do or who to talk to next. The way to do this is to ask questions that include the exact key words that the NPC includes in his or her response to your hail. The NPC key words will be bracketed. Below is an example of me talking to a made up NPC named Lumux Questor.
Tormod: Hail Lumux Questor

Lumux Questor: Good day! You look like a young member of the [Wolves of the North]. I have heard that the snow storms of Everfrost are something to behold. I have a problem that requires the attention of one who can brave the [cold wastes of the North], but it requires a truly brave warrior. It is truly perilous…in fact forget I mentioned it.

Tormod: I am a member of the Wolves of the North .

Notice that I responded with the EXACT key words that the NPC mentioned in a complete sentence. Also the punctuation at the end of the sentence was separated by a space from the last key word so that it would not interfere. Also notice that there were two sets of key words that the NPC mentioned. After mining the "Wolves of the North" discussion I would backtrack to the "cold wastes of the North" discussion to get as much information out of that line of questioning that I could. Also, an NPC will sometimes expect your response to be in the form of a statement or a question. This requires that you start you sentence with an interogative such as "where, what, when, who, etc." In my experience, end punctuation has no effect on the NPC response.

Beta Test Note and Coclusion
Because Verant is still developing the quests that currently exist and creating more for final release, there are still some quests that need some work. I have found a few that do not supply the correct tools to begin although the NPC message would you lead you to believe that you should receive something. For example, the NPC says something like the following, "Take this chest and fill it with gnoll paws and bring it back to me when it is full." but you do not receive a chest. If this occurs, make sure and type /bug and fill out a description of the problem and make sure you note if it is a non crash bug and whether you can duplicate the bug. There are also issues with collecting more than one item of a kind and having the item dissapear because of code that limits your inventory to only one quest item. When the quest requires you to collect more than one of that item and return them to a quest NPC, this is a broken quest and needs to be reported.

Although I have found several broken quests, I have also found quests in which Verant obviousy spent a large amount of creative energy and were loads of fun. I hope this article allows you to find quests and enjoy the game more. Hopefully, it will also help you help Verant perfect the quest system so that final release fulfills the potential we all see in this game.
