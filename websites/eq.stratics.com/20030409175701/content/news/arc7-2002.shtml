    Absor Speaks Out About Priest Changes
    GeneralNews - 5:12 PM PDT
    Absor commented about Priest Class changes at the The Ranger's Glade Forums:

    You shouldn't really blame VI for this. It's the screaming and ranting of the cleric community. There is so much jealousy and (power) greed among EQ players that VI has to try and play a zero-sum game: adding power to one class requires a reciprocal addition to other classes.

    Actually, you can and should "blame" us for anything that goes into the game. While we certainly listen to player input, in the end the decision about what goes in and what doesn't is ours. We get credit and blame for the whole thing.

    And I disagree about the whole 'zero sum' idea. In fact, I think that such thoughts are a huge hinderance. If we add something to one class we immediately get called upon to 'even things up' by giving something to a bunch of other classes. The truth is, if we're adding an ability to one class it's usually because it needed a boost incomparison to other classes. 'Throwing a bone' to the other classes after such a change defeats the purpose of the change.

    When it comes to this particular change, I think Rich explained it pretty well. The changes to Druid and Shaman healing are needed to remove a problem in the end game where some things just can't be done if there aren't enough clerics. It's a problem for gameplay when something like that exists.

    But the fix itself creates a problem for Clerics, not in the raid situation - where the fix should have the desired effect of actually reducing the absolute need for clerics - but in other portions of the game. We certainly are not planning to make it so that clerics are unwanted in exp groups and similar situations. So we're adding a fix for that issue.

    So, in this one case it turned out that giving something to Druids and Shamans also meant giving something to Clerics. But there is no zero sum.

    Alan

    Test Server Patch Clarification
    GeneralNews - 12:39 PM PDT
    The Test Server patch message was updated this weekend:

    ** Experience Clarification.

    Yesterday's patch message regarding experience gained from harder NPCs wasn't worded right. It gave the impression that only white, yellow, and red /con NPCs would yield a change in the experience given.

    Instead, let's try it this way:


    - Experience: Post-50 characters will now gain more experience from hunting NPCs closer to their own level than ever before. (Meaning: Higher level blues, whites, yellows, and reds will pay off considerably better than they have in the past.)

    This is a test of a potential new system. Briefly, the idea isn't really that much of a stretch: The larger the risk you take, the larger the reward should be.

    The additional time that it takes to gain experience from high blues, for example, should now be rewarded more appropriately. Groups who test their limits by fighting NPCs that are hard to them should reap the greatest reward.
    Hopefully that should clear up some confusion.

    State of the Druid
    The Druid - 9:09 PM PDT
    Gwynhyfar Foxfyre, over at The Druid's Grove, posted an editorial about "The State of the Druid" after recent balancing:
    The petition drew so much public awareness that some of VI couldn't not know about its existence. In an indirect response, Absor went public to say that petitions were never really the right method of approach. In other words, just get to the point and leave the prose at home we're busy! The community succeeded in its goal, but the challenge remained because there was so much to be said about and done to caster balancing. The EQ Live team wasn't exactly responsive in the immediate term, but once progress was made with focus items, pet changes, and intelligence casters, they were able to be more communicative.     The mana cap has been a big topic lately, so I'd like to take a minute to address it. Mana caps have been in EverQuest since the game was released. They haven't been changed, made more restrictive, or added at a later date in order to keep casters down. The mana cap as it is now has been there since you created your character, and only recently become a topic of concern for players.

    Most things in EverQuest have a point of diminishing returns - a point where adding more of a resource yields little or no improvement. This is most obvious in your basic stats, where you can easily see that after your strength score hits 255 it does you no good to put on more strength enhancing gear. With all the attention focused on the cap in recent days, the dev team looked it over and we agree - there's no reason to place an artificial cap on mana enhancing items.

    This change just affects + Mana items. We didn't change the way a players mana is calculated from intelligence or wisdom, and we're happy with how that part of the system works.
