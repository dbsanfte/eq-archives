Test Server Patch Clarification
GeneralNews - 12:39 PM PDT
The Test Server patch message was updated this weekend:

** Experience Clarification.

Yesterday's patch message regarding experience gained from harder NPCs wasn't worded right. It gave the impression that only white, yellow, and red /con NPCs would yield a change in the experience given.

Instead, let's try it this way:


- Experience: Post-50 characters will now gain more experience from hunting NPCs closer to their own level than ever before. (Meaning: Higher level blues, whites, yellows, and reds will pay off considerably better than they have in the past.)

This is a test of a potential new system. Briefly, the idea isn't really that much of a stretch: The larger the risk you take, the larger the reward should be.

The additional time that it takes to gain experience from high blues, for example, should now be rewarded more appropriately. Groups who test their limits by fighting NPCs that are hard to them should reap the greatest reward.
Hopefully that should clear up some confusion.
 - Cosmoo

Test Server Patch Message
GeneralNews - 9:12 AM PDT
There was a new patch message put up on Test Server yestarday. The biggest change comes in the form of:
- Experience: Post-50 "hell" effect levels are gone. There are no longer any levels in EverQuest that are anywhere near twice as hard as their previous levels. Please note: This does not change the amount of experience required to go from "No experience in level 1" to "Full-bar in level 60." It's just about smoothing out the trip.
Other change are a way to delete and change petitions, more exp Post 50 for killing mobs higher than you, work on the resist system, and a new sound system.

Free Guild Portal Service
CommunityNews - 10:16 AM PDT

Hello!

State of the Cleric
GeneralNews - 8:44 AM PDT
Rich Waters, EQ Lead Designer, posted in the EQ Cleric Forums about their goals for the Cleric Class:
Lately, we've been working up some changes to help balance out the cleric class. This is important to get done before Planes of Power, as it's the foundation for the growing role of priest classes in the future of EverQuest. The goals behind these changes are to -

- Provide clerics with useful group and raid roles as other priests gain raid quality healing
- Decrease the cleric's mandatory dependancy on grouping for any reasonable gain
- Increase the level of variety and interest involved in playing a cleric

Rich Waters on Test Server
GeneralNews - 9:50 PM PDT
Rich Waters spoke about using the Test Server in a post made in the EQ Developers Corner. It's been all about the Test Server this week:
Putting new ideas on the Test server is really an interesting thing to do sometimes. On the one hand, players are constantly requesting upgrades, changes, new features... yet when we need to test some of these new ideas out to see how they work in a real situation, lots of people jump up and down in a rage. This is aggravated by the fact that often people have incomplete information, so the new change may not even be what they think it is. New features, changes, and ideas on Test server are there to be tested. They may go live after some testing, they may get significantly changed, or they may end up not working out and being removed from test.

If you see changes on Test that you like or dislike, by all means send in some feedback and let us know what you think - we're always happy to get more input from players. It also works to just post your opinions and reasons on one of the many class message boards in the EQ community, everyone on the dev team reads many of those boards each day and keeps up on the hot topics concerning each class.
For the rest of the post, click here.

State of the Druid
The Druid - 9:09 PM PDT
Gwynhyfar Foxfyre, over at The Druid's Grove, posted an editorial about "The State of the Druid" after recent balancing:
The petition drew so much public awareness that some of VI couldn't not know about its existence. In an indirect response, Absor went public to say that petitions were never really the right method of approach. In other words, just get to the point and leave the prose at home we're busy! This public statement confirmed that the petition was known at least, and it even led to two Woody Hearn /gu comics in which one satirized the response with a bit of office fun. And let's not forget that looming ever so silently in the background was the enormous caster balancing project that the EQ Live team announced on February 27, 2002. If any time was appropriate for laying the cards on the table, this was it our petition wasn't drafted out of the blue. In a period of increasing communication between players and the EQ Live team, the petition raised issues in an effort to get others to start thinking about ways to improve druids, which are essentially dualistic by design and hard to balance. The community succeeded in its goal, but the challenge remained because there was so much to be said about and done to caster balancing. The EQ Live team wasn't exactly responsive in the immediate term, but once progress was made with focus items, pet changes, and intelligence casters, they were able to be more communicative. And it's not over yet although direct word has been brief and to the point.
It is an interesting read. Click here for the full article.
 - Cosmoo

Dear Mennix Returns
CommunityNews - 7:17 PM PDT
A new "Dear Mennix" is out:
Hail Mennix, do not laugh, for I fear you will.

Now I know us Elementals don't speak too often, but we can at least write letters. I wish to ask your advice on a problem us Elementals have - very often - which ruins our day to day life. That problem... is mages.

Why do they have to conjure us up day to day to do their bidding? And its not like we have a choice. It hurts too! I mean fair enough we don't die, but that doesn't mean throwing us at rampaging hands and all sorts doesn't exactly keep us healthy! All my friends, Jober, Gonaner, Jabtik, everybody asked me to voice this issue with you; we are all fed up being summoned from our dinner, party, the tavern, meetings, the lot, to go and hit some giant so that the mages greed can make them some money!

Is there an anti-summon potion we could drink? Is there some way of leaving the never ending torment and actually just dying? Nope.

So Mennix, what this boils down to is, is there any way we can summon the mages to us? I have seen an Ancient Cyclops do it once to one of my masters - I have never laughed so hard in my decades of planar existence! At least before, things followed me back to my masters but not any more. What a shame. I enjoy the look on their faces when I find some new friends.

Please get back to me as soon as possible and let me know how to do some 'conjuration' myself! Mwuahahahahahaha!

Yours faithfully,

Goner

Dear Goner,

Although I understand the plight all pets face, I am sorry to inform you that there is no known way for you and your much abused people to summon your masters to you.

I can see the retribution aspect such a skill would provide you, and I'm all for it if it can be done.

As a warrior, I am not much into all that magic stuff. A sharp sword, quick feet, and the ability to bleed is all that is really needed when confronting any beastie.

I have heard there are some instances that a big bad beastie will summon an attacker during the right circumstances. You could try to find such a beast, give it a tickle, and hope it summons your master instead of you. It may, or may not work. I don't really know since magics and me don't really see eye to eye.

Of course, you can always keep leading your new "friends" back to your master. I always get a laugh out of that when I see it happen.
