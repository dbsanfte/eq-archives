EverQuest Crack Program a Hoax
Just this weekend, EverQuest players got word that a reported "game-enhancement program" called EQExtreme had surfaced on the Internet, which a web site claimed would enable players to change the behavior of the game and their characters. Analysis of EQExtreme and Other Fraud Programs
Community News [ 04:26 PM PT ] - Den
Andy gives us a warning and analysis of fraudulent programs such as EQExtreme, which can be used as a Trojan to house harmful processes:
Upon analysis, the EQE program turned out to be a simple VB6 program with no references to vital DLLs that a true data stream modifier would need - but it could be a password stealer Trojan. Dr. Twister also thinks its a PW stealer. So be careful with this one - it could easily be used to take your password and send it to parts unknown. And most clueless wanna bees who use cheat programs would likely be none the wiser until they find their account jacked, their characters naked and +PvP and their reputation trashed (or worse banned for language, etc).
I pointed out that the EverQuest client does not save your account password to local hard disk, at which point he clarified the warning:
It doesn't, but anything that launches the program has the potential to "hook" the DX keyboard access and read the key presses - and use that to capture the password (with some Win32 calls from VB, although that is the awkward way - real programmers use C++ or C). I've had some time to look at this further, and its likely not even a Trojan - just a complete hoax.

But a warning is in order to folks who use such things, because they are likely to (irony) be the less security and technically minded people.

FYI This was a trick used by some UO hackers to rip off accounts. Since I'm involved in security work from time to time (I was one of the systems people who put in data stream security for DirecTV) I've found a lot of this stuff informative: reading the UO newsgroup over the past years has been very enlightening as to how attackers go about compromising a client and data stream - and now the assault on EQ will be started.

The main point: Be careful with what you download and install on your computer. Thanks Andy!

Microsoft Proxy

(Follow-up to "DirectX 6.1, Sound and Proxies" post, see below.) The MSP client doesn't really "modify" the existing winsock.dll and associated files. If the MSP client determines that the requested site/information is on the local network, it simply passes the calls off to the original winsock files. If it determines that the information being requested is on a different network (ie, the internet), it redirects the request(s) to the proxy server.

Community News [ 04:44 PM ET ] - Den
Here's an in-depth overview of the EverQuest Guide Program, as provided by Elder Guide Ozymandius:
The EverQuest Guide Program
For those of you that do not know, the EverQuest Guide Program is a volunteer organization that is working in conjunction with Verant's Customer Service group to support the players of Norrath. The role of the Guides is "To support, educate, and direct the customers of EverQuest to create an enjoyable and immersive Role Playing experience." The Guides use their powers to resolve in-game issues arising from bugs and reduce the damage done by such bugs, preventing small anomalies from turning into large scale disasters. This is done with as much role playing and as little focus on the "mechanics" of the situation to reduce the impact to the "immersion" factor of EQ.

The Guides also provide information, but in such a way to encourage the customer to find out on their own, providing direction and education, not simply handing out facts. If the information is available in the manual or through simple exploration, the Guides will encourage such and not reveal the information directly.

The Guides are not a shortcut, nor are they present to bypass any portion of the EverQuest gaming experience. If something can be found by walking around and looking for it, a Guide is not going to simply tell the player where it is. They do not spoil quests, nor do they fetch corpses unless these corpses were created as the DIRECT result of a bug. Having to go back into danger to retrieve your corpse is a fundamental part of the game, and one of the "penalties" for dying. The Guide Program is not here to circumvent that.

To be a Guide requires a high level of commitment, creativity, maturity, and patience. Players rarely call us when they are happy, and they are frequently unhappy with the answers we provide, but that is not our job. We are not here to make the customers happy. We are here to make sure EverQuest runs smoothely, even the parts that the customers are not particularly fond of. The job entails a lot of abuse but is also a lot of fun.

Guides will be required to sign a contract with Verant, making each Guide accountable for their own actions, so all Guides must be 18 years of age or older. This is a non-transferable contract, so Guardian's signatures will not be accepted. This is a requirement of Verant legal and not something that Verant or the Guide Program have a great deal of leeway on.

Each Guide is required to commit no less than 10 hours a week to Guiding, and this Guiding is done on a server they do not have and never will have player characters on. Any time committed beyond the minimum 10 is appreciated but not required.

If you are interested in becoming a Guide, please send an email to eqseniors@989studios.com expressing that interest and we'll get a questionnaire out to you. The Questionnaire is a long list of questions and scenarios that gives the Guide Program some indication of how each candidate thinks.

If you do not have a plethora of game knowledge, that is quite all right as the game knowledge section is not the most important part. We are looking for candidates that can express themselves clearly and intelligently using the written word, as that is the medium we use to communicate with the customer. Imagination and good judgement are also in high demand.

Customers that run around asking for information from other players end up with their applications set aside and not reviewed. They either know the info or do not know it. Customers that constantly badger for replies on their application or re-submit their questionnaire over and over have their questionnaires put in the same place.

There will be no reply to any questionnaire that is not accepted, and the length of time that it takes to process a questionnaire, especially with the new loads we've received, ranges from 2 weeks to 2 months. Please be patient, as patience is one of the most fundamental requirements of any Guide.

Not everyone gets to be a Guide, and far more applications are turned down than accepted, but those that make it in truly enjoy the experience.

Yours,

Ozymandius the Traveller, Elder Guide
