Wilderness Zone Guide - Greater Faydark
Introduction

The Greater Faydark is one of the darkest zones in Norrath. Therefore, it is easy for even the more experienced players to get lost. What follows is a description of where each path leads, how to get to the different zones which are connected to the Greater Faydark, and what to kill while you're there.

Following the Paths

Starting from Felwithe, there is path which goes forward, then forks to the right and left.

The left path curves to the right, goes around a small hill and continues in the opposite direction. The first thing you will encounter along this path is the Lesser Faydark zone on your right. Continuing along, you will come to four spires, which are used for wizard teleportation spells. The path continues up and around slopes, then comes to another fork in the road. The left fork leads to Butcherblock (see guide here), and the right path leads to a dead end.

The right path travels along a wooded slope, then curves to the left, where there is a fork. The right fork leads to dead end, shortly after which is the Bandit Camp. The straight fork goes to Kelethin. When the path finally leads to Kelethin, it will come up to a lift. To the right of the lift there is a hill with a path on it, this path eventually leads to the Main lift, which is often referred to as a meeting place. You will know this lift by the large torch to the right of it, and the Priest of Discord standing near it.

And that is all the major paths which go through the Greater Faydark. There are other minor ones beyond Kelethin, but they mostly just go through the Orc Camp and connect to each other.

Other Important Locations

From the main lift, if you traverse the path which extends from it, you will be able to see another lift. If you hang a left, through the large patch of open land, you will come upon what looks like a smaller version of Stonehenge. Now, these ruins themselves have no real significance, but around them, in that strip of land, there is a large spawn of newbie-like creatures. Giant wasps, bats, widow hatchlings, black wolves, and the occasional Orc Pawn can all be found here.

If you go past the ruins, down the small valley and up the other side, you'll come to another lift. Extending from this lift is a path, that path leads to the Orc Camp. Here you can find a plethora of Orcs, ranging from Pawns to Centurions. The Orc fort continues through the general surroundings until it comes upon Crushbone.

The gate to Crushbone is a large hole in the server boundary with torches and banners on either side. It is usually guarded by two higher level Orc Centurions. The description above gives a general idea on how to get there. The other way to get there is, when you get out of Felwithe, make a sharp right, and follow the server boundary until you eventually get there. Keep in mind it is a ways from Felwithe, so don't be discouraged if you don't see it immediately. For more information on Crushbone, see the Crushbone guide.

Hunting Strategies

At levels 1-2, the best bet is to stick close to your starting city, and fight the wasps and bats that hang around there. The best areas to do this are directly straight from Felwithe and near the Ruins located near Kelethin. Always remember, if something comes along that you cant handle, run for the guards. They are located on every lift in Kelethin and at the gates of Felwithe. At level 2, start killing decaying skeletons and widow hatchlings which spawn in the same general areas. Also, there is an abundance of Black Wolves near the Ruins. (when people aren't rushing to kill them all) These give good experience at level 2 and they often have ruined wolf pelts, which can be sold to other players for 2-4 silver, or made into armor with the Tailoring skill.

Once you get to levels 2-4 you should try to find and kill as many Orc Pawns as you can. They often spawn around the Ruins described earlier. Also, if you see them, kill pixie tricksters. They will give good experience at levels 2 and 3, and also good loot. At Level 4, you can usually find Orc Centurions in the Orc Camp to kill, but I recommend going in a group, as there are also higher level Orc Centurions and Oracles there.

When you're at levels 5-8/9 kill Orc Centurions and Orc Oracles in the Camp. This is the best thing you can do. There are Centurions there which range from level 4 to level 9. You can also try Fairy Maidens, Fairy Nobles, and Fairy Guards, although I'm not entirely sure what levels these are good for, as I haven't had a terrible amount of experience with them.

Beyond that, it's time to move on to another zone. Butcherblock is good for levels higher than 9, as is Crushbone - which is nearby.

Conclusion

Greater Faydark isn't one of the greatest zones for steady leveling, as it doesn't have many level specific creatures. You'll often find yourself killing things which are a level or two below you, but if you know what you're looking for, then you can do alright, especially at later levels. If you cant seem to get the experience you're looking for, then Butcherblock is a great place to go. It will give you the variety you are looking for.
