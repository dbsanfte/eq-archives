The Dwarven Warrior (Levels 1-16)

Character Creation
1 - 4
4 - 10
10 - 16

So you have decided to join the ranks of the Stormguard and crack some skulls, eh? Well sit back and I'll pass some advice along that will help you in your quest for fortune and glory!

Why a Dwarf? Certainly there are other races (Barbarian, Troll, etc.) that seem a more obvious choice, and I have played them all at one time or another. But of all of these races, I have had the most fun in the role of a warrior playing as a Dwarf. There are a variety of advantages to playing as a Dwarf; good starting stats, some unique weapons, and very nice role-playing opportunities just to name a few.

So without further delay, lets get into the nitty-gritty of the Dwarf Warrior.

Character Creation
When allocating those first 25pts to stats I recommend the following:

20pts - Strength
5pts - Agility

Strength is a very important stat for a warrior for two main reasons; the amount of damage you deal and the ability to wear very heavy armor. As a warrior one of your primary jobs is to absorb punishment. To that end, you will need to always have the best armor to keep that AC as high as possible. As most metal armor gets rather heavy until you can find magical armor (which is often weighs half as much) it is important to have a high strength so you can bear more weight. One of the earliest armor types you will acquire will be bronze. This armor is heavier than banded, but offers great protection until you can upgrade to various magic armor, such as the Dwarven Ringmail Tunic. Strength also affects, later in a warrior's life, your maximum damage per blow cap.

Agility greatly affects the amount of damage you take when hit and also seems to be directly tied to dodge, which prevents you from being hit altogether. A Dwarf starts relatively low in this stat, so get it up ASAP.

Now it's time to pick your deity. This is surprisingly important to the Dwarf warrior. I highly recommend choosing Brell Serilis as your god. The reason may not seem readily apparent at the low levels, but become monumental later on. This is because the EQ Team has recently added a few Dwarf only items that are dependent on Brell as your deity: the Stormguard Battleaxe/Hammer of Brell and Bloodforge Armor. The armor can be received if not a Brell worshipper, but your faction will need working on to get to that reward if you are agnostic. Also, several Dwarf quests, and several Gnome quests, are not open to you if you are agnostic. Even though these items may not be on your wish list, it appears Verant has taken steps to make more items deity exclusive so later on, it may become VERY important.

Levels 1 to 4
Now it's time to enter Norrath. The first thing you will want to do is find your guild hall. The guild is located in South Kaladim and can be found by looking for a marble white building that architecturally resembles a church. There is a small pond behind this building, and behind that is the Stormguard Hall. Enter the building, and go left up the stairs. Here you will find Furtog Ogrebane, the warrior guild master, on a balcony. Turn in your note for some exp, faction and an AC 4 tunic.

The next thing you will want to do is set up your hot boxes. This is an important feature to consider for a warrior and you will want your first series of hotboxes to resemble something like this:

Inventory

I recommend these keys (placed in any a pattern you are comfortable with) for the following reason; a warrior should be skilled in at least 3 (arguably 4) weapons. Those weapons are 1H Slash, 2H Slash, Archery (and 1H Blunt if you so desire). With this set up, you can place a weapon of each category in the slots for on the fly changing during a fight, and a backpack in inventory slot 2 for quick changing of shields and other weapons. Just drop the next weapon in the melee slot and you won't miss a beat. Ranged Attack is used for pulling creatures, and Sit is something warriors do as often as mages meditate.

You can play with the setup, but in order to master all weapon skills, you need to have the appropriate item easily accessible. Also, it is very important to train ALL of the weapon skills at an early level and this setup allows you to interchange a weapon in the middle of a fight. Raising a skill from basically zero when you are in your teens because you neglected it in the early levels is painfully slow.

You will notice that Run is not in that setup. This is because almost everyone always runs, so you will have that clicked from the moment you logged on anyway, and won't touch it afterwards.

Now it's time to enter the Butcherblock Mountains. Ask for directions out of the zone, or look for the cave exit that is the largest opening in the zone. This takes you out of Kaladim. When you enter the open air, you will see a dreadful sight; the land is overrun with Bats, Snakes, Skunks, Rock Spiderlings, Decaying Skeletons and Goblin Whelps. Dwarfs have a saying, "Never leave an Elf to do a Dwarfs job!" Stay between the Kaladim entrance and the Guard Tower to the left of home and start clearing this area of beasts. Here you will easily get from level 1 to 4 in no time at all, but be VERY poor for all of your effort. Butcherblock is a great training ground, but a horrible place to make money at low levels. As soon as you are 4th level, I recommend going to Greater Faydark.

Levels 4 to 10
Getting from Butherblock to Greater Faydark is a relatively simple process, especially if a kind soul casts a Spirit of Wolf on you. Don't worry about dieing on the way since by now, your skill at dieing will most likely be maxed! When you get to Greater Faydark, have someone bind you at the Orc Lift below the Wood Elves' tree city of Kelethin. If you are not sure where that is, ask around and a kindly Elf will surely guide you. If you are new to Norrath and don't know how to get to Kelethin, just take the road going right out of Butcherblock and follow it the city or better yet, visit one of the many EQ map sites and print one out.

From the Orc Lift, you should head to Orc Hill. Orc Hill is located on the road leading from the Orc Lift, toward Crushbone (the Orc stronghold). It is to the right halfway between the two. It has a relatively close proximity to the lift (for emergency run to the guards), a very high spawn rate, and a good number of people your level to give you a hand when things get nasty. The best part about Orc Hill will be the sudden influx of good loot that you missed while in Butcherblock. Here you will easily fill out a complete set of cloth armor and gather enough coin to make your first major purchase; the Dwarven Ringmail Tunic.

The AC protection (AC 12) and the boost to magic resistance (+8) make this armor excellent for the low level Dwarf. Considering you already have a higher than average magic resistance, this addition makes it easier to stand up to the spells that Orc Shamans, Oracles and such are always trying to cast on the tank of the group. The tunic is also very reasonably priced between 12 and 15 plat (with slight variations depending generally on the age of the server you are on). Some unscrupulous sellers will try to convince you that they are worth 20 plat or more. Don't be fooled into spending that kind of money; be patient and get one cheaper. They are rather common items and I have seen them auctioned EVERYTIME I am in Faydark for no more than 15 plat.

Levels 10 - 16
In short time, you will hit level 10 and be ready to enter into Crushbone itself. These will be very fun times for you as a Dwarf; cracking Orc skulls and spreading revenge in the name of Kaladim throughout the zone. The coin will come in loads and you will collect many Orc belts and Legionnaire shoulder pads. Hold onto these, and take them back to Conloe in Kaladim to show how you have brought honor to the Stormguard name. You can easily stay in Crushbone until 16th level, fighting the Orc Trainer and getting the Shiny Brass Shield, or crushing the Warlord for those valuable Bracers of Battle. This is also the place where you can complete the Screaming Mace quest and use it either to build your 1H Blunt skill or sell for between 75-100 plat (again depending on your server). If there comes a time when you need a new taste of adventure, head on over to the Steamfont Mountains, and fight Minotaurs and Lesser Ebon Drakes. The loot is a little slower, but the adventure is great. There are also some quests in Ak'Anon for you to have fun with while there.

By this time you will be a pro and ready to head off and bring great honor to your clan. I would recommend filling out your armor with either Bloodforge or banded before you leave. Don't forget to put at least 1 point of training into each new skill acquired at the appropriate level to get it started. Many Dwarves also enjoy smithing and/or brewing as a trade skill. Be forewarned, smithing is a money pit until you become skilled and you will dump 100 - 200pp before you start making a decent profit.

A Note on Role-playing a Dwarf
The many role-playing opportunities the Dwarf provides is one of the reasons I enjoy the race. I have included some classic fantasy themes that may help you develop the personality of your warrior. I have received many laughs and praise for the various "Dwarf quirks" emotes I keep hot keyed. Here are some ideas:

  • The angry Dwarf.
    Dwarves are renown for their short tempers and generally sour dispositions. I like to emote things like: Barthalomew looks extremely angry for no apparent reason.
    Always be griping about how such and such really makes you angry and how if YOU had a choice, you'd have done such and such differently. One of my favorites (that always gets a smile) is: Barthalomew mumbles a thank you and looks rather embarrassed and Barthalomew wonders which is worse; a goblin marrying his sister, or YOU!

  • The sleepy geezer.
    Some Dwarves are very old and wrinkled. This character is always nodding off and will always enjoy a good hearty meal. It is fun to emote things like: Barthalomew digs into his pack pulls out a rather large leg-of-lamb and starts eating, and Barthalomew dozes off again with rather large snores, or Barthalomew seems half awake and rather ancient.

  • The brewer.
    Dwarves and ale are synonymous. Pick up the brewing skill and brag about your family brew. Build up a high alcohol tolerance and keep a hearty supply of various brews on hand at all times. You can also take this to the extreme and emote some drunken Dwarf actions like: Barthalomew has had FAR too much to drink and gives a sexy wink to the Wood Elf.

  • The racist Dwarf.
    This is the most difficult to pull off due to the inherent nature of EQ that requires grouping. Dwarves are very suspicious of other races and even more so with Elves. Their history is dotted with many wars and currently involves a tenuous truce. A hardcore role-player would NEVER group with a Dark Elf and would only join a group with Elves under dire need. Of course this is unrealistic in EQ considering the game structure and a lot of people won't understand or appreciate your role-playing. So a way around this is to create clever, but not too insulting, emotes: Barthalomew eyes the Elf with suspicion as he deliberately sharpens the blade of his axe or Barthalomew questions the wisdom of allowing one of the pointy ear people into the party. A clever Elf will pick up on these small pokes and will play off of it for some fun. Be sure to add some mild compliments especially after a hard fought battle such as Barthalomew thinks this Elf ain't half bad for an Elf at least

The point is, the game is much more enjoyable if you do more than fight, sit, fight, sit gaining levels mindlessly. I have found that a few well placed emotes will make people remember you for many months to come. I have had people I vaguely remember run up to me, say "Hi!" and eagerly want me to join their party just because of some silly thing I did in Dwarf character months before while in their group.

Good luck! May your beard grow long, and your family even longer.
