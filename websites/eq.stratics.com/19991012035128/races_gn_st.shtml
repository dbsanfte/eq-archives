Starting as a Gnome

Gnomes
I have to say it, of all the creatures I've played, I prefer playing a gnome. They aren't the strongest or cutest race, and they aren't the smartest, but they are the shortest! The first thing you'll find when starting out as a gnome is that Ak'anon, the gnome city, is absolutely stunning. The Ink Sea is inside the city. It's a huge lake with many buildings in it, and a massive waterfall near the city's guarded entrance.

Ak'anon is worth exploring, and I mean that. Explore everywhere, there is a lot to Ak'anon. There's the zoo, the bank, the mines, the mechnamagica guild halls, the abbey of deep musing, the forge of defense, and Gemchopper Hall. I must warn you however, to watch out for the ladders. They can be tricky at first, and falling from a ladder as a level 1 or 2 can mean instant death. So be careful! There is both a rogue's guild and necromancer's guild inside Ak'anon. They are both hidden, so you'll have to search for them a bit.

The rest of the guild halls are fairly easy to find, but if you can't find something, feel free to ask the many guide clockwork gnomes that are strewn about the city. Ak'anon is also a great place to fish, and the first order of business may be to obtain enough gold to purchase a fishing rod, as fishing is a great source of income until about level 5 or 6. You may also catch rusty weapons and rotting footwear if you're lucky! The best kept secret about Ak'anon is that you can level up to level 3 without ever leaving the city.

I won't completely spoil the surprise, but let's just say there are places inside Ak'anon where you can fight and get lost, both pretty easily. Having said that, my best recommendation is that you shouldn't leave Ak'anon until you are level 3 or 4. There are plenty of very nasty creatures in the Steamfont mountains, but here is a description of what you should be fighting. Level 1, again, rock snakes are your staple. You can try the occasional decaying gnome, but not if the gnome has a weapon. Avoid the rogue clockwork spiders, rats, and gnomes at this point. They are reserved for level 3 and 4.

Level 2 lets you take on several snakes at once, as well as decaying gnomes with weapons. At Level 3 you can try the occasional rogue clockwork, but also rock spiderlings. Level 4 lets you pretty much kill with impudence inside the city, but watch out because those rogue clockworks will gang up on you pretty quickly, and they are very fast. There is and will be a bit of competition to begin with inside the Ak'anon fighting area, but respect your fellow gnomes and everyone will have enough beasties to go around.

Let's get back to outside Ak'anon, in the Steamfont Mountains. This is what you want to avoid unless you wish to perish when you first walk outside. Anything that is not a regular brown rat. Infected rats, large plague rats, rogue clockworks, spiderlings, skeletons, minotaurs, drakes, pumas, kobolds [scouts and regular] will all kill you pretty much instantly, so keep clear of them. You can fight decaying gnomes and kobold runts, and some of these give fair loot to keep you with food and water. There are several watchmen that guard the paths, and it's an extremely good idea to know exactly where they are in case you are a victim of someone else’s problem.

Here again I refer to people biting off more than they can chew, and running down the path to the guards with 3 pumas, a skeleton, and 3 large plague rats in tow. The odds are that if you try and help them before level 12, you will only get two gnomes killed, so watch your step. There are windmills not far from the city, and these are an ideal base point from which to fight from level 4 to 8. Once again, respect your fellow gnomes, and there is always plenty to go around. There are also 2 kobold camps (one harder than the other) and a minotaur cave to explore in Steamfont, as well as ruins and madmen! It's a very dangerous place, and corpses are commonplace, so you need to keep your gnomic wits about you when adventuring.

You can buy and sell items at the windmills or near the city gates, but the bank is inside Ak'anon, so you'll be making several trips back there as you advance your diminutive character. Ak'anon has seen a very high influx of dwarves and elves as of late, largely due to the overpopulation of the elven zones, and the overhunting in the dwarven zone. Don't let those longlegs and furryfaces push you around! Steamfont is a great place to live, but visiting can be dangerous.
