Playing a Magician

 Basics   Reagents   Grouping   Advanced Tactics   Pet Control 

Basics
Lets begin with how the low level fighting should go. Firstoff, don't believe what others tell you about how your pet should alwaystank while you burn it to death, not only does this waste mana, but you endup losing your pet because while your burning you can't see what kind ofshape the pet is in. The key to the first 10 levels or so is that first,soloing will be your best bet. First, your going get a ton of exp on yourown, and second, you get to keep every bit of loot you get. Once you'vefound a good place to solo, find the nearest safe area (guards or zone) incase you get in trouble, because one of the best things a magician has isthe ability to run like no other. What I mean by that is your pet is aperfect fleeing support mob while your butt is high tailing it to thenearest safe area or zone point. Your pet is back keeping the enemy busy bytaunting every 2-3 rounds and generally knocking him upside down, whichreally angers the bad guys. When your running, the monster will mostlikely turn to the pet since it's still fighting it. Only once or twice hasthe creature been so mad that it just came right after me and ignoredthe pet. Now with that in mind, you may ask how are you supposed to getaway when the pet is dead? Simple, your pet won't be the one tanking, veryrarely is a pet a good tank for a magician, that is. You will be the tank. You have higher AC at low levels, you can keep the enemytargeted and you know exactly what your HP is. It's a lot easier to stepback, and let the pet take over for a bit while you deal some burn spellsthen the reverse. Sometimes you just can't make the enemy attack you andleave the pet alone, and thus it dies. The advantage to this is you canflee when you need to, so feel free to take on new enemies that might beyellow and black/white.


Reagents
Now for those of you not familiar with the reagent system, let me break itdown for you. There are certain spells in the game that require an addeditem to cast, in which it destroys the item and the spell is cast. Theseare called reagents, and magician summoning is one of those that requiresone. This discourages quite a few level 4 magicians when they run to theguild and spend their gold on the spell and try to cast their first petonly to find they are missing an item. I was one of these actually. I beganto question if this class was for me, seeing as how every time I wanted tocast the main spell in my arsenal I needed an item. Well it's not as bad asit seems. I never got into higher level spells, but for the first 20 levelsall your going need to make elementals is malachite. The generalprice is 6 silver, give or take a few copper. This may seem like a lot to alevel 4 and it generally is, but let me tell you, just buy the malachitein quantities of 4-5. By the time your out of it, you will have madeclose to a platinum easy. Once you hit level 8, you'll start making runsthat end up giving you an average of 2 platinum a piece for a singleelemental (for me anyway). At that rate, you can't help but treat malachitelike a piece of trash, because it comes down to being petty change. By thetime I was level 10 I never left the town without 15 or so malachites in mybackpack.


Grouping
Believe it or not, magicians actually do have some rather impressive damageSpells. Not nearly the strength of a wizard, but Id like to see a wizardsolo. Now then, when your grouping, there are a few things you'll want todo. First off, always tell your pet to "follow me" as soonas you create it. What this means is he will not attack anything thatdidn't attack you first or that you directly told him to attack. In myearly days as a magician, I used guard me quite a bit and more then once thelittle beggar got me killed doing that. It's the worst feeling in the worldto start seeing "taunting attacker master" flash on your screen a few timesonly to look around and notice your pet is no where in sight. In asituation such as that, which I'm sure will happen many times to you as itdid to me, you can only try to find the pet and step in to save his buttbefore whatever he's fighting kills him, or if your lucky, he'll win thefight and return to your side. Should you not be able to find him when hemeets his maker (you?!?), you'll soon feel the wrath of whatever killed himon your back. Now, keep in mind that the invisibility spell, while being ideal fora wizard to sit down and meditate, is a curse to a magician. While you wantto invisibility yourself, you know that if you do, your pet goes bye-bye. (dealing32000 points of damage to himself in the process, why can't he do that oncommand?) Now, for those of you who have annoying group mates, you might getthem saying "no pets they take experience." In which you simply remind them thatyou're no idiot and will promptly call the pet off long before the finalhit is dealt. Secondly, that the pet is more for my protection then tohelp the group. If your group is more then effective on its own at killingwhatever you've chosen to fight, simply tell the pet to follow you anddon't have him engage. If worse comes to worse, he's still an excellent"sacrificing lamb" to save everyone if the situation turns unfavorable.


Advanaced Tactics
After level 10 or so you'll notice that you are no longer the ideal tankwhile your pet kills the creature. This tactic is still useful when messingwith blue things, but if you want to keep going on that speedy leveling,your going to have to get a cleric at your side for such a tactic. I had a good friend who played a dwarf cleric that I traveled with quiteoften, and we spent a good deal of our time killing undead thingsthat were red to us. Considering you have a damage dealer, you, a tank, thepet, and a cleric your 2 person group is unstoppable. While magicians"can" solo, its best to group regardless of what you think after level 10.

Gold will start to be easier to come by when you can find things likebandit females in Lesser Faydark to kill. These little girls pack a bronzeweapon quite often (one half the time usually), and they can be sold for atleast 1-2 platinum. The trick to taking out a lonely enemy that you knowhas a good item on it but is a little over your head is simple, have thepet attack while you stand a good distance back (need full mana for this)then unload the best damage spell on it till your out of mana or its dead,then walk up and between you and the pet. Finish it off hand to hand, if thepet gets near death get in the monsters face and tell the pet to "back off"for a few seconds and then have him re-enter the fight while you tank for abit. This is a little risky because you have no mana should you get introuble, but more often then not, it was successful which then produced arather nice item of greater value then any warrior of the same level wouldbe able to get in his wildest dreams.



Pet Control
Let me take a moment before I end this to talk about controlling your pet.I'll start with the commands:
/Pet guard me --- the pet goes where you go and attacks anythingthat can be considered dangerous within the general area.

/Pet guard here --- the same as guard me only the pet sits in one spot andkills anything dangerous that walks by. A key tactic to changing yourfaction is using this. I wanted to become more liked by high elves againafter killing some pixies so I took my pet to the orc village, found a nicespot and had it guard here. I then walked away and did some other stuffwhile he was busy racking in the faction points for me.

/pet follow me --- fairly self explanatory. The pet will follow you aroundand only attack what attacks you or what you tell it to attack.

/pet kill --- also known as /pet attack. Target something and type that(number 6 on my hot-key list) and the pet runs up to the target and beginssmashing for all he's worth.

/pet back off --- (number 5 on my hot-key list) forces the pet to stopwhatever he's fighting and return to your side. If he is in 1on1 combatwith the enemy, you can hit this many times to make him drag the enemy backto you, but he will still stop every time he is hit and resume fighting.This makes pets a nice fishing line if you will if you want to save mana.

/pet as you were --- this makes the pet forget any commands you may havegiven him, be it guard or kill or anything else.

Now, let me explain how dangerous pets can be if you don't control themcorrectly. I have been killed at least 3 times in phase 4 because I groupedwith another magician who couldn't control his pets. They ran off andattacked things and came back almost dead with a train or dead leaving 3-4yellow/red monsters hunting us. If you don't issue the right commands atthe right times, you'll quickly end up on the "do not invite" list of mostparties. Also I recommend you hotkey everyone of the pet commands. I'm a rather fast typist, so I leave the commands likeguard and follow to my typing, and keep the /kill and /backoff commandshotkeyed. Lastly, I'll talk about range of commands. I'm not sure if thiswas a bug or if this was by design, but unless the pet is within "say"distance of you, he will not respond to any commands you give him. More tothe point, you can't control a pet if you can't see the pet.

This is not the final word on magicians, and keep in mind I only had a fewweeks to play one during the beta. I'm sure I'll learn a lot more when thefull game comes out. My first character I intend to play is more thenlikely going to be a necromancer, because I just love having pets. They makethe game so much easier and interesting. Enjoy yourmagician and always keep a tight leash on your pets.
