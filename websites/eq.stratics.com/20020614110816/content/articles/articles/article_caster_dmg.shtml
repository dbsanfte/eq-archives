Indepth Spell Research
After all the debating and complaining about spells, damage, and mana efficiency, I decided to take the time to look into it more in depth. I put together several scenarios on figuring out what spells a caster would use in order to maximize their efficiency. All scenarios are in terms of the caster Vs. a single target; so area effect spells are not used, but I have included those in a different ranking. Resist factors were not taken into account, for there are ways to always lower a MOBs resist rates. If you are having resist problems, have your casters debuf the MOBs. I didn’t have time to include Shamans in the evaluation, but I can make a reasonable guess as to where they reside in the rankings.

In order of Direct Damage:
Class: Damage Rating:
Wizard 1120
Magician 810
Druid 612
Enchanter 528
Cleric 519
Necromancer 500
Shaman 430

In order of Direct Damage Efficiency
Class: Efficiency:
Wizard 2.8
Magician 2.7
Druid 2.45
Necromancer 2.38
Enchanter 2.2
Cleric 2.16
Shaman 2.15

In order of AE Damage (4 mobs used for normal AE, 5 for point blank):
Class: Damage Rating:
Wizard 4272
Druid 1908
Cleric 1320
Cleric 1272
Magician 1100
Necromancer 620
Shaman no high level AE DDs

In order of AE Damage Efficiency (4 mobs used for normal AE, 5 for point blank):
Class: Efficiency:
Druid 8.15
Wizard 4.88
Cleric 4.34
Enchanter 3.77
Necromancer 3.63
Magician 2.93
Shaman no high level AE DDs

In order of max damage able to be done with 2000 mana, and a time frame of 2 minutes:
Class: Damage Rating:
Necromancer 5731
Wizard 5600
Druid 5248
Shaman 5199
Magician 5196
Enchanter 4656
Cleric 4231

Max damage for each class was calculated the following way:
Class: Spells Cast:
Necromancer Necro leads with Ignite Blood, Cascading Darkness, Scourge, and Venom of the Snake, followed by 3 ignite bones, repeat Cascading Darkness and Venom of the Snake, and finally Lifespike.
Wizard Wizard casts ice comet 5 times
Druid Druid casts Drifting Death, then Starfire 6 times, another drifting death, then careless lightning and ignite
Shaman Shaman Leads with Plague, followed by Envenomed Bolt. Then they cast 4 blizzard blasts, followed by another Envenomed Bolt (shaman still has 60 mana after this so they could actually do another 100 dmg)
Magician Magician casts 6 lava bolts followed by 1 cinder bolt
Enchanter Enchanter leads with Grasping Embrace, followed by Discordant Mind 6 times, another Embrace, then Anarchy
Cleric Cleric casts Retribution 8 times, then Furor 1 time

Now I didn’t have all the information for casting times, or even recast times, but one thing is certain, Wizards by far have the shortest casting times and recast times of every class. This allows them to pump out spells non-stop. Regardless of this, the damage done over a period of time for a single battle would stay the same. However, wizards would actually do their damage in far less time than any other class. From what I have seen and heard from other casters, here is the approximate order of casting time lengths, from shortest to highest:
Wizard
Druid
Shaman
Cleric
Magician
Necromancer
Enchanter

Now from what Verant has stated, "Wizards are the masters of dealing damage"; this is true if you look at a damage per second view. Wizards grossly outdistance any other class when using their area effect spells as well. According to Verant’s philosophies, the actual statistics back their claims and goals up. If there is a problem that you are having, either you are not playing your class the most effective way. Also some people might say that they cant just pump out all their mana like that or they would die. The fact is that any caster, regardless of class would die if they did so. You need to learn when to cast, and more importantly, what you can cast in a certain situation. Again, if your spells are being resisted often, Shamans, Magicians, and Enchanters are great at lowering resist rates. The only resist rate that cannot be lowered (From what I have seen) is the Disease Resistance. Since only Necro’s and Shamans use this type of spell, its not really a factor for the majority of the casters. 

Addendum

I wrote this article and based it on a spell to spell comparison, not a complete class ability listing. Many have asked " What about pets or damage shields?".

Now a level 49 pet does 56 dmg max per hit, 4 hits per round. Plus a bash of 26 dmg every third round. Now if you figure this into the equation, the average damage a 49 pet does is about 35 pts dmg per hit, and they normally get just under 3 attacks per round (accounting dual wield and double attack chances). Bash normally hits around the 18 mark on average, but hits only half the time. I am not going to include burnout III in this either. As for the fire shield, a level 49 fire shield does 25 dmg per time the pet is hit, and the pet is only hit maybe half the time as well. So taking this all into account, in a 2 minute round because of the pet, a mage would out damage every class but the necro. And the reason they wouldnt outdo a necro is simply because of the serious dots a necro could lay down as well. Their pets do 2 less damage per hit, but seem to take much less hits. Also, a pet isnt a sure fire way to kill something, because the minute you start nuking your pet wont being able to taunt it off of you, thus the damage shield would only actually work for the first round.

Since most fights when nuking for full mana (not accounting for interupts) do not last longer than 3 "Casting rounds" (meaning the time it takes to get a spell off), we can assume the following:

A wizard does Ice Comet 3 times for 3360 damage. Now wizards have other useful spells they can cast, such as stuns, roots, rune shields. A wizard also could get almost 4 Ice Comet spells off in the time it takes the Magician to get off 3 Lava Bolt Spells, so using that time frame their damage is upped to 4480 dmg.

The magician pet: first round, pet does 35*3 dmg +20 dmg, +25 dmg from FS. Second and third round, the pet isnt getting hit but it still does 35*3 dmg. Fourth round pet does 35*3 +20, followed by the fifth at only 35*3 dmg. At the fifth round the fight is over, the mage got their 3rd bolt spell in. Total damage from this is-- 3020 damage. **Note this is well under the amount that the wizard could do**.

I have been flamed for posting paper statistics instead of posting actual game statistics. No matter what the situation is, if you pit a level 50 wizard against a level 50 magician, and sick them both on a high level mob, the wizard would always get the credit for the kill. This is a true fact: "Wizards do more damage per second than any other caster". Abashi, even after he was asked to do more research, comes to the exact same conclusions to which he has been stating since the beginning.

In an "actual" game setting, you have to take in resist factors, interupt factors, whether you are in a group or not, line of sight factors, how many MOBs are hitting you at once, etc... In a group, which is where the most complaints come from, you simply do not have to stand back and nuke non-stop. There is a role for each class, and each role is clearly defined. This is what makes EQ such a great game, the fact that each class is so different from the other. Now I do not mean to insult people by stating 'if you cant do what you are meant to do then you cant play your class right'. What I meant by this is that there are some wizards who chain cast and then yell at the group members why they let the MOB kill them. This is a poor tactic. What about that fleeing mob that might get to his friends for help. A wizard excels at stopping these, with either a root, stun, or outright nuke. When the MOB is at half health, the wizard excels at killing them quickly so the party can turn to the other MOBs fighting them. Its a good tactic, and one that which wizards do better than any other class. Now if anyone wants to dispute these claims, do so, put the numbers up, or even meet me in game where we can equally test my claims. The article was meant to help show some understanding, not put down any classes, so please take it for what its worth, just some spell data knowledge.
