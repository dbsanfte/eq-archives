Scribing Spells


If you play a druid, you are constantly asked to sow people. If you are an enchanter, legions of players ask to be breezed/claritied. If you play a wizard, armies flock to you for taxi services and binding.

We don't view these things as bad. Quite to the contrary, players relying on other players is a big part of the game by design.


This causes a lot of wasted time for those that can provide these types of services. For example, if a wizard is asked to teleport, the wizard has to meet the person somewhere, he has to memorize the spell, he has to cast it, he then needs to get back to where he wants to be and finally, at some point, he has to meditate to regain the mana lost by teleporting someone

Very true, but there must be some cost to the dependence otherwise it is trivial. For instance, if there was an item in game that created unlimited money, the value of money would drop significantly as these items showed up in game.

When it comes to SOW, breeze/clarity, teleport, and binding, I think other solutions should be available.

Some other solutions for some of these are available in the form of Alchemy. Not for everything of course as we wouldn't want to destroy class interdependance, or turn one class (shamans) into the class that replaces all others.

How about giving casters a new skill called ‘spell scroll’ or something to that affect. The ‘spell scroll’ skill could then be learned like any other skill. Using the ‘spell scroll’ skill, casters could create single-use spell scrolls

Any further expansions in the area of providing a craftable item that invokes a spell will be made via alchemy. Though I appreciate your opinion, we don't want to see classes excluded from a group on the basis of the people in the group being able to cast a bunch of those spells.

Risk in PvP
Official_EverQuest_Forums [ 11:30 PM PDT ] - Alethal
I often get email from a few folks who make some very good arguments concerning risk in PvP. Basically, the standpoint is that if the environment does not contain risk, there is no sense in engaging in it. "Item loot" itself does not handle this risk, as players of casting classes are not affected to the same extreme as melee characters.

One argument is that we should implement experience-loss in PvP. I like this idea, unfortunately some core issues with the way life in EQ works makes implementing this unreasonable. For anything that couldn't be handled in code, an additional load would be placed on the CS staff. Believe it or not, even though people playing on PvP servers should be more tolerant of interaction with other players, 90% of the petitions there are for some kind of perceived harassment.

The core EQ system, meaning, the things that define EQ as EQ, do not allow some of these issues to be resolved without drastically changing the PvE portion of the game which we want to remain intact.

That said, I still firmly believe that some form of risk associated with PvP needs to be in game. Since that risk cannot be experience-based, and item-based risk doesn't hurt people who can bag their items or fight without them, I'm considering the following option: Making it so that everyone, regardless of class, can only bind in cities on the PvP Servers only.

City-only binding (again, on PvP servers only) would allow people to, for instance, eject an opposing force from a zone, and provide the additional risk of making all of them run back to recover their corpse and engage again.

What are your thoughts on this? Do you feel that PvP needs more real and equal risk associated with it regardless of class?

- Gordon

Today's Patch Message
Patch_Server_Announcement [ 12:10 PM PDT ] - Ogamok

June 22nd, 9:00am
-----------------

***Today's Updates***

*The Hole*

We are pleased to announce the opening of The Hole, a high-level dungeon on the continent of Odus. The Hole is a complementary addition to EverQuest, available to all subscribers regardless of their Kunark status. The files necessary to enter The Hole are available via the "Optional Patch" button at the bottom of the patch program interface. The files are quite large (about 9MB total), so if the download is going slowly, you may want to postpone downloading the files until later.

As mentioned above, The Hole is a high-level dungeon, designed to support characters starting at about level 40. It should be regarded as extremely dangerous. As a note, jumping in The Hole from Paineel is NO LONGER A SHORTCUT TO TOXXULIA FOREST. The GMs will not recover corpses for people who fall, or jump, or attempt to levitate into The Hole.


*Vallon Zek is now Coin-Only*

As we announced earlier this week, due to the number of people specifying interest in "coin-only" loot on Vallon Zek, versus those wishing to retain item- loot, we have implemented "coin-only" looting rules for PvP combat on Vallon Zek.


*Resurrection*

Previously, players had to be resurrected within two hours after death in order to receive any experience back as part of the resurrection. Due to the increased size and difficulty associated with Kunark zones, we've raised this limit from two hours to three. This should assist players and make it easier for them to obtain an experience-returning resurrection within the time limit.


*Warping*

We put in some code that should help decrease the "ghosting" (NPCs appearing to be some place they aren't) being reported in some of the larger Kunark zones. Please let us know how this goes.


*Locked Doors*

We noticed few weeks ago that it is common practice to use NPCs to open locked doors rather than by using the services of someone that is able to pick locks. In order to protect and increase the value of the abilities of the lock-picking classes, NPCs have gained the ability to walk through locked doors without opening them.


*Vox and Nagafen*

Over the past couple of weeks, we've received more and more reports of these dragons being bested by smaller and smaller groups of higher-level (high 50s) adventurers. As we've always felt that Dragons should be special encounters that require a large number of people, we were left with some choices.

One choice available to us was to increase the power of the dragons to a level where the level 60 folks would find them challenging. This would have the disadvantage of forever placing them out-of-reach for those who did not buy Kunark, and place them further out-of-reach for those who did, but are just now approaching the levels where they could go on Dragon raids. Another possible solution was to make the dragons flee (depop) when engaged by a more powerful group of people. This would have the undesired effect of allowing some people to deny others the experience of fighting a dragon.

Instead of raising the bar as mentioned above, we've instead decided that dragons, or at least these two dragons, should have the magical ability to pick their own fights. Players of level 53 or above will now encounter some difficulty when attempting to assist in combat with these dragons. This should help 40s and low 50-level characters with their chances to encounter a dragon without worry of getting forced out by much higher level players. In addition, it also has the added effect of creating a natural progression from the younger dragons of Antonica to the elder and more formidable dragons of Kunark.


-The EverQuest Team

Test Server Wipe Update
Official_EverQuest_Forums [ 9:28 PM PDT ] - Alethal


Hello all,

After a good deal of discussion on the Test server status, we've decided that having the right or the ability to do something doesn't necessarily mean that we should. Our thoughts were that everyone was aware of the risks of playing on the Test Server, and always did so with the understanding that something such as a character-wipe was always a possibility. It's apparent at this point, given feedback from players on Test, that many didn't understand the risk.

That said, we do need to restore the integrity of the testing environment in order to insure that we are able to catch and address issues prior to them going to the live servers. Test has served well in this capacity since release, however item loot issues and other factors have reduced Test's ability to do this. Given these issues, we still feel that a complete character-wipe represents the best possible solution for continued functionality of the Test server. However, this is a case where, in the interest of keeping our current testers and players satisfied, that we need to compromise on the solution.

After our discussions today, we've decided that an item-wipe, without deleting characters, will accomplish the majority of our goal to restore the Test server as a functional testing platform. Given that, the EQLive and Operations teams are currently at work restoring all characters that were wiped yesterday, with the exception of characters known to be artificially buffed. This process should be completed later today.

Furthermore, in order to allow those that did not understand the purpose or risks associated with the Test server a chance to avoid future problems of this nature, we will be allowing built-up (not buffed) characters a one-time chance to move when the next split-server comes up, sometime in the near future. As Brad mentioned in his message earlier, we do recommend that those who are on Test more for playing, and less for Testing, seriously consider moving when we open registration. The possibility of a future wipe, whether it is a player or item wipe, is not necessarily unlikely in the future.

We hope that the players on Test will find this compromise acceptable and continue to participate in the testing process. Furthermore, we apologize for the insensitivity of our previous decision to completely wipe the server.

On behalf of the EverQuest team,

Gordon Wrinn
Internet Relations Manager
Verant Interactive, Inc.

    Level Limits
    [ 8:14 AM PDT ] - Dhark
    A post about people going to PoF/PoH/PoA on the Official EverQuest Forums :


    We really don't intend to close off areas of the game based on people being too high in level. There are a number of reasons for this, not the least of which is that some high-level areas require passage through lower level areas.
    Lower Guk, for example, requires passage through upper Guk. If you are in Qeynos Hills, EverFrost and Permafrost require passage through Blackburrow. There's also the fact that we'd have a few problems coming up with an RP reason to restrict higher level people from entrance into lower level areas.

    We only put the level restriction on the Planes because we wanted the items there to be a badge of honor for the person wearing them. When the plane of fear was first opened, people were taking level 1 characters up there to loot things that people in the group could not otherwise use.

    This limit also let us bend some of the previous game mechanics that were required in order to insure that twinking does not have a huge impact on the game.

    -Gordon

    The process that attaches the "Resurrection Effects" is on the back end of the resurrection code.     For instance, you attack NPC A, who yells for help to NPC B, who yells for help to NPC C. NPCs A and B are close enough to you to chase after you, however NPC C is not. NPC C wanders around the zone and yells for help to NPCs D, E, F, and G, who are all too far away to come after you. However, since they all have a copy of the hatelist, whenever they are killed you will take a faction hit.

    Abashi Says No EXP Loss For PVP Deaths Ever
    [ 8:42 PM PDT ] - Triomferus
    Abashi replied to a post in this thread made on the Official EverQuest Forums suggesting a method of EXP loss for PVP. Here are a few quotes from the slightly lengthy suggestion:


    This is designed with RZ in mind, but could work on the other PvP servers as well. It would require both additional coding and an additional two pieces of data per character.
    Each character would require a new counter, TimesLooted that would begin at zero. (I can think of some characters on RZ that should start at 1000 or higher). Each time that the character loots another, the looting character's TimesLooted counter is increased by one. Each time a character suffers a PvP death, is PKed, they lose exp equal to 1% of the amount needed to completely fill out the exp bar at their level, TIMES the current value of the times looted. So, somone who has never looted another character w/o consent, would never lose exp to PvP death, while someone with 100 loots to their 'credit' would lose an entire level each time he was PKed. This would permit angry victims of frequent PK item-looters to delevel the PKer. Meanwhile, you may PK to your heart's content with no risk of exp loss, as long as you NEVER loot. And, if you succumb to temptation once and loot that item you have been dreaming of, it would hurt as you lost exp every time you were PKed, but it might still be worth it...

    Ideally, one should allow for the possibility of a character 'reforming'. To implement this, we would need a second counter, LastLootedTime, that would be assigned the character's playedtime value at the point of looting w/o consent. Every time another loot happens, LastLootedTime is advanced to that playedtime. When the playedtime exceeds the LastLootedTime by 10 days (10 days of on-line time w/o looting) the TimesLooted counter, if not zero, is decreased by one.

    This is hardly the perfect solution. [It would also require a change in the harrassment policies].

    To read the whole thing, check the link above. The post is lengthy and details a fairly well-thought method. However, EQ was not designed form the ground-up as a PVP game, which would make this hard even if EXP loss were easy to implement/were implemented.

    Being very honest with you, any type of experience loss for PvP deaths is not at all likely to be put in EQ. The GM/Guide staff is already inundated with requests for customer service based upon harassment even though there is substantially little risk. The core engine of EQ cannot support the changes that would be necessary in order to implement experience-loss on PvP in an equitable manner.
    That said, I do recognize the need for risk in PvP. One idea that I'm partial to is making it so that binding is *only* allowed in cities, regardless of class. That would encourage people to win battles, punish them if they lose, and be fair for both melee and casters from a PvP perspective.

    Pick Pocket Explained -- (Fairly Long)
    [ 7:40 PM PDT ] - Towers
    The Skill of Pick Pocket, and it's pros and cons.

    I play a Rogue, which happens to be the only class that gets the skill "Pick Pocket." This means that if the player base were equally divided, at most less then 8% of the player base (over level 7) would have ever even used this skill, let alone understands it. Rogues are one of the least played classes, which makes this percentage even smaller. It is my goal to share with you the workings of Pick Pocket.

    Pick Pocket has been an issue nearly since release, and is often the deciding factor on a Rogue getting a group. Despite good, fast damage, unrivaled scouting and excellent corpse recovery, the Rogue class is still looked down upon simply because of this skill. Let me explain a few things about it.

    Rogues acquire "Pick Pocket" at Level 7. It has a 10 second recycle time. First and foremost, nothing can be Pick Pocketed that is marked MAGIC or any items that are equipped; only humanoid creatures can be Pick Pocketed (i.e. no undead or animals). On top of that, no MOBs level 45+ can be Pick Pocketed whatsoever. Also, the Rogue has no control over what is taken items or coins. Even though items can be pick pocketed, it is a rare thing in the life of the Rogue. The majority of the time, coins will be stolen. Even then, it's not guaranteed that very much will be taken. At level 15, it is very rare for me to Pick Pocket Gold, Silver is almost common, but the majority of what I steal is Copper. I have never stolen Platinum. The deciding factor on how much is stolen (and how much can be stolen) is ultimately based on the MOB's loot pool. Although there was previously a bug that allowed a Rogue to endlessly steal small amounts of Copper, this has been corrected and everything stolen is now taken from the loot pool. So if the loot is considerably lower then normal, there has probably been a lot of Pick Pocketing going on (or an avid fan of the skill "Beg" hehe); you can also tell when a Rogue steal because many (mind you, not all) MOB's will yell, "Stop Thief! "

    As unfriendly as this skill sounds it can be useful to others besides the Rogue. Although Rogues are a pure melee class, we don't get the skill "Taunt." Many of us would like it, to have even better personal crowd control (in addition to Evade). As Backstab has a taunt factor built into it, so does Pick Pocket. It is possible to taunt a MOB off someone with Pick Pocket.

    One of the main reason we like to Pick Pocket is simply to keep this skill maxed out. Around level 20+, Pick Pocketing becomes more of a hindrance then a help to a Rogue. The constant loot of small change quickly adds to his weight, thus making the coins virtually useless.

    I, and the majority of the Rogues I know, ask before Pick Pocketing in a group. If you are a Rogue reading this, or an aspiring one, I encourage you to do the same. I hope that we can make this skill more understood and less rejected.

    The Safehouse (*THE* Rogue message forum) has many excellent FAQ's and guides about the Rogue class. One of them happens to be about Pick Pocket. If you are interested to know more, I'd advise you to go to this link to learn more: Tome of Mastery: Picking Pockets.

    Kambic the Rogue
    Rallos Zek
    Of the Safehouse

    June 6th, 8:00am
    -----------------

    *Patch Day*

    Greetings! The live servers have been updated to reflect the newest data on the Test Server:

    *General*

    - Some new spells are available for the hybrid classes. Check your Kunark spell vendors.
    - Found and corrected a problem causing people to fall to their deaths upon zoning. The majority of these issues should no longer occur.
    - Corrected outstanding issues with several quests, including the Brain Bite, Shackle of Rock, and the Barnal Flamehands quest.
    - Increased treasure in many Kunark zones including The Emerald Jungle, Sebilis, and the Frontier Mountains.
    - Upon review we've increased the drop-rate of level 51+ spells in Kunark. They should be more plentiful now.
    - Updated pathing information in many zones, making it easier for pets and other NPCs to get around the zones.

    *Items*

    - A problem with the spell effect for the Luminary Two-Handed Sword. Current owners of this weapon will need to contact a GM to trade their sword out for a working one.
    - The Gunthak Harpoon is now rentable. It will remain in your possession when you leave the game.
    - A couple pieces of Blood Ember armor have been reduced in weight.
    - Reduced the weight on several Monk weapons found in Kunark.

    *PvP Teams Servers (VZ/TZ) and Illusions*

    Due to an outstanding bug that allowed a player to permanently mesmerize or stun opposing team members when coupled with an illusion, illusions will now automatically cancel when the person wearing the illusion casts an offensive spell upon any player-character. Players should keep this in mind before attacking with spells in an area where their faction standing is less than desirable.

    Illusions are defined as any spell that makes a character look different than they actually are. This includes all racial illusions, as well as other illusions such as Treeform, Wolfform, and the Call of Bones series.

    *Risk and Reward*

    The concept of risk and reward is very important to the game of EverQuest. Ideally, situations that provide a greater reward should also provide substantial risk. Over the past several weeks, we've been identifying areas in game where risk and reward are at a disparity, and making necessary adjustments.

    In most cases, those adjustments are in favor of increasing the reward for a specific action. For example, we recently increased the experience awarded in many Kunark zones to offset the sometimes-considerable risk associated with adventuring there. We also do this quite frequently by increasing the reward associated with a quest by enhancing the item, as we did last week with the Burning Rapier quest.

    However, in some cases there are instances where reward is far in excess of the risk involved, and we will be correcting these issues like those above as we come across them. For example, there are many cases where a solitary and stationary outdoor creature has a "fast spawn", yielding the reward of fast experience-gain without the commensurate risk.

    Though it is not our policy to announce changes to individual NPCs, we did want to let you know that you will see risk/reward disparities corrected over the next several weeks.

    *PvP Teams (VZ/TZ) Character Transfers*

    As many people playing upon the PvP-Teams servers are aware, Vallon Zek and Tallon Zek have different rules regarding looting rights from PvP combat. Vallon Zek allows the victor to loot all coin plus one item from the fallen, whereas Tallon Zek allows only coin-loot with no right to loot items.

    Due to the fact that the looting rules were changed on Tallon Zek post-production, we've decided to allow characters on both servers a one-time character-move. Registration for the move will open at 6:00pm PDT on Friday, June 9th, 2000, on both servers, and will be open through 12:00pm PDT on Monday, June 12th, 2000. You can register for the move by executing the "/movelog yes" command during the registration window. There is no limit to the number of people that we will allow to move between Tallon and Vallon Zek, so there is no reason to rush to enter the command.

    Please remember that once you register for the move, your decision can not be rescinded. The actual character-move will take place sometime next week, at a time to be announced later. Please keep in mind that corpses are not moved in a character-move, so we'd recommend against leaving unclaimed corpses around after Monday of next week if you've asked to be moved.

    Questions about the move process will be addressed in the Gameplay Message Forum available via www.everquest.com.


    - The EverQuest Team

    Chain Casting
    [ 5:30 PM PDT ] - Kamero
    A message found here on the Offical Everquest Forums:

    Since the last patch, I've noticed that if I cast Tashania as soon as it becomes available, either due to casting another spell and waiting for the spell icons to reset or casting tashania and waiting for its icon to reset, it will go through a full casting time then display a message that states something along the lines of 'The recast delay of this spell was not met'.
    If I wait a second after the spell icon fades back in, this isn't a problem. But, being the kind of person who makes the most of the time they have, I invariably am casting the spell the instant it is available and have been seeing the error message every time I do that.

    I've noticed for a while that a lot of spellcasting is controlled client-side. It sounds to me like someone came up with a hack/exploit to ignore re-cast time on spells, so Verant made a fix to check recast times on the server side as well.

    It's actually not "controlled" by the client, but rather controlled by *both* client and server in a manner that allows it to prevent hacking, while negating the need for communication between the client and the server.

    For instance, if it was purely the client, the server would just cast any spell that it was instructed to regardless of any other rules, thus it would be hackable. If it was controlled solely by the server, the server would have to send a message to the client telling it when it was OK for it to cast another spell. By having it done on both sides, the server only has to send a message to the client in the event that it was told to do something illegal.

    Doing it in this method also reduces the effect of internet latency. Given pure server side control, any purposeful delays would be exacerbated by internet latency. For instance, someone running 1000 ping would have to wait an additional second before they could cast the next spell.

    Anyway, we're checking on this issue, and should have a fix up the next time we patch the client, probably early next week. In the meantime, I'd suggest that people wait an extra half a second before casting the next spell in a chain-casting situation.

    - Gordon
