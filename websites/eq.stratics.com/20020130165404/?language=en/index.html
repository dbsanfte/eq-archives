Magician Questions - Answered!
GeneralNews [ 7:54 PM PST ] - Abombpk
It seems more and more questions about Magicians kept popping up. Absor kindly answers them, saying this:


I think the mages in this game have waited long enough. And now the quotes from the Fan Faire are even more disturbing.
Explain your passion for hating mages?

I can't explain it because we don't hate mages. I can't even imagine how you could think such things. There is no reason nor point to any one here 'hating' any class. We don't.

First off - why are so many zones non-Call of the Hero compatible?

Because the use of Call of the Hero in those zones allows characters to skip content that we do not wish to be skipped.

Grodin is quoted as saying he wished they never made the spell. Well that makes us mages hopeful for anything doesn't it? You made it. Now use it.

Well, based on the fact that Magicians don't like it when we disable the use of the spell in a few zones, and the fact that allowing the spell to work in those zones invalidates the whole risk/reward concept in the zone, I can certainly understand how the existence of this spell could be seen as troublesome.

However, we are not prepared to allow this one spell to prevent us from implementing interesting content. As I've said before, we'd rather disable one spell in a few zones than never explore content that that spell would invalidate.

It's not like we do much else. We have basically 2 nukes - fire/magic (some places those are useless), pet pathing is no good almost everywhere (no one wants you to use them thanks to that.)

Indeed, some NPCs are practically immune to either fire or magic. Very rarely, however, are both of those magic types highly resisted. And pet pathing isn't perfect, but it certainly isn't as bad as you make it sound.

This was the desired use of this spell, to let members of your raid join late. Sadly, that isn't the only use for the spell.

Havneq - 57 mage Tribunal

Mages are not a pointless class at all.

Frenzied Burnout is bugged. The explosion isn't working properly. We're trying to fix that.

If you look at the life of the class, this places them pretty much third or fourth in over all solo ability, by my estimation. I know that it's tempting to only look at the end game, especially when you're playing it. But the life of the character is more than that.

And if I seem like I'm rattling your cages a bit, maybe I am.

I really need you folks to be more precise and less emotional when sending in comments. It makes it much easier for me to present your ideas to the team.

Quick Earth & Beyond Primer!
Stratics_Network_Announcement [ 4:25 PM PST ] - Sparrowhawk

Earth and Beyond--the upcoming Massively Multiplayer Online Game (MMOG) under development at Westwood Studios, the same kind folk who brought you the Command and Conquer series--is now undergoing Beta testing!

Let's browse through a summary of the game's storyline, which should help provide a further understanding of Earth and Beyond's interstellar backdrop of war, trade and exploration:

The age of the Universe has been placed at more than fifteen billion years. When the first Star Gate was discovered on the fringes of Sol we had no idea that it was one of the countless Gates that linked the oldest and most distant of the galaxy's planets. The scientist-monks of the Jenquai had tried to keep their discovery of the Star Gate a secret, fearing the other two factions incapable of grasping the significance of their discovery. But the Terrans, watching from the boardrooms of their star-spanning corporations, had spies everywhere. Seeking to prevent a Jenquai monopoly of this discovery that allows near instantaneous travel across vast distances of space, the Terrans forged an alliance with the aggressive Progen, an ordered and warlike clone culture.

So it came to pass - the Gate War. Humankind fell upon each other in an epic war, fought for the same reasons wars were fought since the beginning of civilization - territory. With the weapons of mass destruction that technology has wrought, we almost destroyed ourselves completely this time.

That was well over a century ago. Now, with the 23rd century nearing its end, a tenuous state of Cold War exists between the Three Factions of humankind. Each of the factions has claimed a portion of the Human Sphere as its own, linked now by the same Star Gates that broke them apart.

The Peace is maintained - only just - by fragile treaties and the creation of neutral peacekeepers, a byproduct of the War that nearly plunged the human race into extinction. But the Universe is large. Danger stalks the darkness between the stars. There exists countless worlds, countless discoveries, all drifting through the cold vacuum that spreads outward from Earth and Beyond.
This new sci-fi MMOG is nearing release and now Westwood needs your help in refining the game through a limited opportunity to play test the Earth and Beyond Beta. Be sure you do not miss the chance to become a tester; sign up for that beta NOW!

AA Titles : Questions Answered
GeneralNews [ 7:34 PM PST ] - Abombpk
It seems more and more questions are arising about AA Titles. Absor took it upon himself to answer some of these questions. Here's what he covered:

Evenshadow wrote:
I have a concern, regarding the titles recieved with the new AAxp. Namely: I really don't want one. I am pretty sure I am far from alone in this After going 60 levels as a character with a single name, its become somewhat of a trademark. I would like to keep my single name. Call it a roleplaying thing if you will.
While your chosen titles may sound good with some names, sadly, others they make into a laughingstock (please see a recent thread on the safehouse regarding "Master" Bahter):P I'm sure you can understand that a name in EQ is a very personal thing: almost the only thing that makes a character truly unique. To have this changed arbitrarily is very frustrating.

Would it be possible to have a command implemented wherein one could turn off AAxp titles? Is there possibly one coming down the pipe? A very large number of your players would like to know I'm sure.

While the titles are a neat idea, and work well for some, for others of us, they are an unwelcome change to our characters. A /titleoff command would be a wonderful thing.

Our Technical Director, Scott Hartsman, posted about this on a public board. It's been picked up by a few fan sites already, but I figured I'd post it here for you all to read and comment on. It is taken out of context, so it might read a little funny:

quote:

Before this one gets too far out of hand, I wanted to mention something:

After seeing this thread lastnight, I talked to the GM in question as well as one of the Lead GMs.

We all agree that this isn't Sir Bahter's fault, and that the "more correct" solution is to add a switch that allows GMs to turn titles on and off. Any name changes due to this, until that point, are only temporary.

(This is a feature that we would really like to add in for players as well, but the two gentlemen who'd be doing that are both handling engine/zoning/memory optimizations and drops to desktop at this point, which have to take priority. Unfortunately, it's a more involved change than one might think.)

Please don't be too hard on the GMs - They're doing their jobs, and quite well at that. If anything, it's a failing on our side that the need for a switch like this wasn't forseen when we started adding things in front of people's names.

I'm definitely not comfortable with any change we make that forces a need for a person to lose, even temporarily, an identity he's had for quite some time. That's just not fair. We'll find a way get the switch added in the next couple weeks.

Best,

- Scott Hartsman
Technical Director, EverQuest


I talked to Scott a bit about this over email in an attempt to anticipate your questions.

Scott is talking about a switch that the GMs can use to correct issues like the one that Bahter has. He's not talking about a switch that would allow players to turn off their titles. Those are two different issues. In this case we need the GM switch to correct this naming issue (though I suspect it only affects a single or a very small number of players), so that such players are not inconvenienced by the introduction of these titles. Allowing players to turn off their titles doesn't solve the problem, it actually would make it worse. This would allow a player (and I'm certainly not saying that Bahter would do this) to toggle on and off the combination, which allows that combination to exist against the naming policy. We need to be assured that the name can't exist.

That means that even if we had both a player and a GM toggle for titles, the GM toggle would overwrite the player one. A GM could toggle off Bahter's title (Master) and the player would not be able to turn it back on.

So, with all of the other stuff we're working on right now, the GM one would have to come first.

I hope that made sense.

Kenwood wrote:
did I miss something? maybe I was dreaming but I could have sworn I saw in a patch message or something that there is a command now to allow us to set what parts of the character names show. On a global basis of course, not just for an individual character, but I don't see that the poster was concerned about other people's titles showing.

Yes and no.

There are some switches now available for those that want to customize how other people's names appear.

The problem for Bahter is that his title needs to not display to anyone, regardless of their setting. It can't be dependent on how others set their names, but on how his name looks to everyone. So it has to be set for his character, regardless who sees him.

Kenwood also wrote:
This was a command mentioned in one of the patch messages:

- /shownames now works with the following parameters:
no parameter = display the current setting.
off = don't show player names over their heads
1 = show only first names of players
2 = show First and Last Name
3 = show First, Last, and Guild (if any)
4 = show Title (if any), First, Last, and Guild (if any)

Wouldn't this be exactly what the original post was requesting? It doesn't allow a player to toggle his own title on/off but it does give him to ability to turn off titles globally

This works on what you see, not what you show to others. A subtle but important difference.

DaxD wrote:
So does this mean we can petition in game for the GM to turn our titles off?

Absolutely not. When this feature is added for the GMs, it will only be used for the rare instance of the character with a name that violates the naming policy when combined with a title. It's not a very efficient use of our GMs time to run around turning off titles.

Alan

Thanks Alan! What do you think about this topic? Comment Below!

What’s in a Name..? Aradune Lets Us Know.
Site_News [ 10:32 PM PST ] - Lord_Of_Dreams
There was a small bit of a debate on the forums about company names and where some of them had come from. There were also a few questions about the inner workings of these companies along with some speculation. Brad McQuaid (Aradune) thankfully stopped by and was able to clear up the questions and illusions that had been presented for us.

Hi all,

Here's the scoop on all the names/companies/etc:

Steve Clover and I were hired by a company called Sony Interactive Studios in March, 1996 by John Smedley (who was then a Director of Devlopment for that company). And Milo (a good friend of ours, who helped us on a previous project years before) was indeed already employed there and working on sports titles. Steve and I quickly started work on the game and wrote the original design document.

SISA was focussed mostly on Playstation games, but Smedley wanted to do online PC games, so we ended up forming a department within SISA devoted to online games. In fact, he'd already started an online game before EQ called Tanarus (the EQ engine and client-server code was originally based on Tanarus').

I became Producer on EQ soon after joining and the team began to grow as we needed more resources. The hiring process began in earnest, and we were lucky enough to be able to hire people like Bill Trost and Kevin Burns who had also worked with us on previous projects. And eventually Milo was able to join the EQ team as well.

Sony Interactive Studios then later changed their name to 989 Studios (which was indeed based on their Foster City address). And then in mid-late 1998, with the Playstation 2 on the horizon, decided to completely focus on console games and so gave Smedley the opportunity to spin the online PC department off into its own company. So Smedley approached me, invited me to be his partner, and Verant was formed (although we did briefly call it RedEye, but there was a name conflict).

989 agreed to keep funding EQ, but we needed a partner for the other games we'd begun (Planetside, Sovereign, and the game that turned eventually into SW:G). So we found a completely seperate Sony entity called Sony Online Entertainment who was focusing mostly on ad-revenue based games like Wheel of Fortune, Jeoprady, etc. They were interested in exploring subscription revenue based games like EQ, and so agreed to be Verant's publisher and funder.

When EQ launched, it turned out to be a much bigger success than even the most optimistic of us ever imagined. So not long afterwards, Sony Online and its parent companies began to show interest in acquiring Verant, and that is eventually what happened. So, at this time, Verant and SOE are really the same company, for all intents and purposes.

989 is now pretty much devoted to sports games, with their other titles being part of their parent company, SCEA (Sony Computer Entertainment, America). Yeah, Sony has as LOT of divisions.

It is also interesting to note that Kelly Flock was CEO of 989 and then also eventually became CEO of SOE, and was very instrumental in the success of EQ & Verant (as, of course, was John Smedley, without whom there would be no EQ).

-Brad

Thanks for stopping by and clearing that all up! Knowing the truth is always better than guessing at it.
