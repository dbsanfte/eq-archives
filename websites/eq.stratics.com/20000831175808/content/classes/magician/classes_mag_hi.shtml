Playing a High Elf Magician

 Introduction   Magicians vs. Wizards   High Elf vs. Other Races   Char Creation   In Combat   Levels 1-7 

Introduction to the High Elf Magician
So you’re thinking about becoming a Magician, High Elf style. Well simply put, this is an excellent choice. Elves are naturally very Intelligent, and are extremely Wise (read, extremely high Intelligence and Wisdom attributes). In addition, they are extremely beautiful--they start out with 80 Charisma and as a result see better prices when both purchasing and selling items. They are also highly Agile, which means they don’t get hit a lot when in combat. One more advantage is that High Elves can wear both small and medium sized cloths, which in turn means that you can wear practically all clothing within the game when playing one.

So, practically speaking their primary stats are wonderful, but this is a balanced game, which of course means there is a drawback--namely Strength. Their Strength is pitiful (they start at 55) and their Dexterity is set at 70, which are both below average (average being 75). As a result, don't expect to be a heavy hitter when engaged in melee combat.

Magicians vs. Wizards
Indeed, Wizards have insanely powerful offensive spells and they can cast them from very far distances from the enemy. But that's essentially all that they can do, since they're restricted to attack and transport spells. As a Magician at high levels you might be disappointed that, unlike your Wizard cousin, you can't use that crazy area effect spell that kills everything around you. However, Magicians still have some very powerful combat-related spells, but you'll have to play according to what they're good at: Summoning anything from weaker Elementalkins to powerful Great summonings.

Lastly, forget those trade skills! Well most of them anyhow. Magicians can create food, armor, magical weapons and pets from thin air. They are completely self-reliant. More on what makes them so great will be explained in detail later in this article.

High Elf vs. Other Magician Races
A High Elf may not be for you if you wish to be a tank Magician due to the High Elf's low Strength (which is set at 55, ouch!). High Elf Magicians do have have an extremely high base Charisma--the highest base in the game for a Magician--and it definitely helps. Charisma will get you that cool spell that no one else can get at a reasonable price and will also will make other miscellaneous items a great deal cheaper. In order to look beyond their weaknesses you need to understand how you must play a Magician. Magicians use pets, so their pets take care of the melee combat for you, thus greatly decreasing the use of Stamina, Agility and Strength. Scroll down further to see in-depth combat issues involving Magicians in combat and you'll see why I favor High Elves.
  • The Human Magician has very low Intelligence (85) for a magic user, and has nothing that is too high. No weaknesses and no strengths--to me that's a weakness within itself.
  • Gnomes are my second favorite Magician race, after High Elves. Gnomes have high Intelligence and high Stamina which are the main characteristics of a Magician, but they do have below average everything else and extremely low Charisma. Remember that Charisma is very important for a magic-using character--especially at higher levels when spell costs are colored platinum.
  • Now for the Erudite. Erudites are decent due to the fact that they posses the highest Intelligence in the game. They have the same low points as the High Elves, but not as many high points. Later I'll explain why Intelligence isn't as important to a Magician as you may think.
  • Dark Elves. For one thing, they're evil, which is a death knell for most beginning players. As far as attributes are concerned, their stats include good Intelligence and Agility, low Charisma and only average other stats. They're a decent race, but to decide between a Dark Elf or another race would be deciding between good and evil.
Here is a comparative list of all the stats for all of the Magician races:

Human   75  85   75   75   75   85    75
Erudite   60  80   70   70   83   117   70
Gnome   60  80   85   85   67   108   60
Dark Elf  60  75   90   75   83   109   60
High Elf  55  75   85   70   95   102   80

Creating your Character
Magicians are spellcasters, so let them cast. Intelligence, or rather mana, is very important to spellcasters, but comparitively speaking not as much so to Magicians. Why is Intelligence not as important? Well, simply put, as a Magician you will cast most of your spells before combat begins. Magicians do have spells they can use during battle, but they don't rely on them as much as, say, Wizards do when in the thick of battle. You cast all of your buffs, summon some food, daggers, maybe a bag and then your pet, let your mana regenerate and then go hunting. In addition, the High Elf Magician's base Intelligence of 102 is already outstanding.

Remember however that Intelligence not only determines mana--which is any spellcaster's lifeblood--but also aids in skill advancement, which is of course important to any class. To say that Intelligence is unimportant is a gross mistatement, but when playing a High Elf Magician it is not all-encompasing. I recommend that you spend 10 to 15 of your initial bonus points towards increasing Intelligence and most of your remaining points towards the Magician's other primary attribute: Your rather low Stamina. If you're satisfied with both of your primary attributes' levels and have additional points left over, dedicate them towards Agility, which determines how hard a time an enemy have when attempting to hit you in combat.

Magicians in Combat
You are extremely pet-oriented and need to stay away from direct melee combat, particularly at lower intermediate levels. Of course, until you get Summon spells at level 4 you will have to fight melee with your staff or dagger. Since pets do the fighting for you, allocating those extra beginning attribute points in the towards offensive attributes such as Strength and Dexterity is a waste. Stamina and Agility will save you if your pet fails and you get yourself into some trouble, although instances like that should merely teach you not to make mistakes.

Remember to equip your pets with all the buffs and weapons you can. Your pets are what you rely on, so help them as much as possible. As far as High Elf Magicians go, they comparatively stink at solo combat. You'll be a nervous wreck if solo. Example scenario: You are in a dungeon with your pet after fighting a really hard enemy or a lot of lesser enemies. You are low on mana and your pet begins to attack some gnarly minotaur or something. Your pet falls and that enemy comes chasing after you. Predictable result: You die faster than you can Gate. You are weak and pathetic without your toys and you need backup in order to survive. Those magic weapons, buffs, pets and other items you summon can be extremely handy in any group, so keep this in mind when considering group vs. solo adventuring.

Levels 1-7
From levels 1 to 3 you will be playing in a similar fashion to that of a Wizard. You will engage in solo melee combat almost exclusively and be using those two spells that you began your life with. Don't bother purchasing any other spells until you reach level 4. If you're thinking of getting that Summon Food or Drink spell, forget it--you'll die enough to replenish your supply. Remember that during this period of your character's development dying will provide little to no consequences, so don't be afraid to perish. You'll most likely be a ho, skip and a jump away from your home point and will not suffer any experience penalties.

Once you hit level 4 purchase the spell called Elementalkin, which will provide you with your first pet. In addition, if you have the cash, buy the Gate spell. When you're in combat and think you're about to die, don't take the risk and use up all of your mana. Just Gate home and bask in your newfound safety. Merely being a Magician puts you in the rare position of not having to burn most of your mana on combat-related spells--again, you'll be able to summon everything you need beforehand and have most of your mana throughout a battle. This makes Gate extremely convenient for you, since you'll actually have the mana to use it in the heat of combat.

For levels 4 to 7 you'll definitely want to consider grouping, even though you're still relatively weak. Warriors might not want you around but other magic-users should. As for higher levels, 8 and beyond should be easy enough for you, since you'll most likely have gotten the hang of playing your particular class. For higher level and complex tactics, look to the EQSS Advanced Magician Strategy Guide.
