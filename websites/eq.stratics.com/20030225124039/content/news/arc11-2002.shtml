Tuesday December 24 2002
Tale of an In-Game Event
CommunityNews - 9:42 PM PST
We've received word of many GM/Guide run events taking place during the holiday season, and we'd like to share the description of one with you.

I was hunting in Oasis today on Rodcet Nife around 4pm EST when someone in the zone asked in OOC who "Misses T Baken" was. I didn't pay much attention but then I saw a female troll standing on the beach between the docks and the N Ro zone with people around her, so I ran up and saw that her name was "Misses T Baken". I could tell almost immediately by the way she turned to follow people as they went by that this was a player, not an NPC. But thinking about it for a second I realized that no player can have a middle initial, and so I knew that it was a GM doing some kind of event. Below is the conversation I had with her after I walked up.



Misses T Baken says, Hello Sycrus, my name is Misses Baken. I love to bake! What do you like to do?
You say, figure things out
Misses T Baken giggles at Sycrus.
Misses T Baken says, I love this time of year! so many good things to eat!
You ponder Misses T Baken. What is going on with her?
Misses T Baken takes a deep breath in, smelling all the food aromas.
Misses T Baken beams a smile at Sycrus.
Misses T Baken says, I love to bake! Cakes are my favorite things! But I am out of ingredients!
Misses T Baken lets out a deep sigh and her lower lip begins to quiver.
You say, so you need ingredients for a cake?
Misses T Baken says, YES!
Misses T Baken bounces around Sycrus.
Misses T Baken says, Do you have any?
You say, any particular kind of cake?
Misses T Baken says, anything you think would be yummy!
Misses T Baken says, I am a fabulous cook! I can bake with almost anything!
You say, anything?
Misses T Baken nods at Sycrus.
Misses T Baken says, What is your favorite kind of cake?
You say, the only thing I have that is food is sandwiches, is that useable?
You say, wedding cake :)
Misses T Baken says, hmmm that would be interesting I could try :)
You say, worth a shot then
Misses T Baken says, ohhh wedding cake is very yummy!
(at this point I try to give her a 20 stack of Patty Melt (sandwich icon, made by a friend))
Misses T Baken has cancelled the trade.
You have cancelled the trade.
Misses T Baken says, I only need one to try!
You say, ok
Misses T Baken says, I do not need THAT many!
Misses T Baken giggles at Sycrus.
(now I give her just 1 Patty Melt)
Misses T Baken beams a smile at Sycrus.
Misses T Baken cheers at Sycrus.
Misses T Baken says, Thank you for helping me!
(now she gives me some food items, 1 Bixie Crunchies, 1 Chocolate Cookie, 1 Bixie Berry Buns, 1 Chocolate Marr Cherries)
Misses T Baken bows before Sycrus.
You say, wow you really can bake!
(anyone who can turn a patty melt sandwich into four types of desserts is a baking genius)
Misses T Baken giggles at Sycrus.
You bow before Misses T Baken.
Misses T Baken says, Be well!
You say, you too


At this point she moved away and when I came back a few minutes later I couldn't find her anywhere along the beach so I guess it was a short event. There were other people who participated before me. The guy before me seemed to not understand what was going on so I edited out the part of his conversation that I caught the end of. A monk (barely visible in screenshot) was chomping on some misc desserts so I think he got it. This was a fun thing to experience and it really made my day.

Friday December 20 2002
More Interface News
GeneralNews - 6:19 PM PST
From the EQ Developers Corner:
After much faithful service, it's time for us to bid a fond farewell to EverQuest's Classic and Velious user interfaces. In yesterday's Test Server patch, the old interfaces were disabled.

While adding many features to the new interface over the past six months, including the built-in MP3 player, item links, compass, and enhanced /bug reporting tool, we've done the best that we could to keep all three interfaces, at the very least, usable.

However, some of the features that we've been quietly working on over the past weeks have unfortunately forced our hand.

We don't want to spoil all of the upcoming changes at once, however, in yesterday's Test Server update, we released the first run of the new EverQuest windowed mode that's been in the works for quite some time now. (In a game that was originally written for a much earlier version of DirectX, it's a lot more work than many would think.) Soon, it will behave in a manner similar to other applications that support both full-screen and windowed mode and can be toggled back and forth. As a part of this change, the account bans related to the use of third-party EverQuest windowing applications have already been reversed.

In addition, the long-rumored "Bard Box" has also been revealed on the Test Server. For those who are not aware, we've been wanting to add a second Effects window that would hold selected short-duration beneficial effects, most notably many bard songs. Initially, for tuning, the new effects area will allow six additional songs to be placed upon your character.

These updates will remain on the Test Server only until after the new year, as we expect that much additional testing will be required.

We hope you'll be as pleased with these latest changes as we are, and we look forward to bringing you many new features in the coming months.

- The EverQuest Development Team

Patch message
Game Patch - 5:23 AM PST
Todays Patch message is out.



** Zones **

- Spectral Parchments, Ethereal Parchments, and Glyphed Rune word drops have been increased significantly in the third tier Planes of Power zones.
- The trials within the Halls of Honor have been changed slightly. Please proceed with caution.
- Several Planes of Power NPCs are no longer angered quite as badly when they notice a healing spell being cast on a player they are angry with.
- Made improvements to the Planes of Power quest armors.
- Assorted quest, item, and dialogue fixes.


** Planes of Power Zone Progression **

As most know, progressing through Planes of Power zones requires that certain tasks be performed. In many cases, one of the tasks required involves being on a raid that defeats a particularly powerful foe.

One side effect of this progress is that if the tasks were not accomplished in a particular order, a person on a raid may not gain the flag for completing the event, as they did not complete the prerequisite tasks.

The majority of "zone boss" style encounters in the first three tiers of Planes of Power zones have been changed to allieviate this problem of completing tasks out of order.

As of today, if you defeat one of these foes and do not have all of the prerequisites completed to receive the flag at the end of the event, you will be able to go back and complete the tasks that you have not yet performed.

Once you've done that, you may speak with the Seer in the Plane of Knowledge who will make sure that you are then able to progress as if you had performed the tasks in their proper order.


- The EverQuest Team
