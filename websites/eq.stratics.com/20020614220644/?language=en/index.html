Enchanter Guide
CommunityNews [ 11:58 AM PDT ] - Lord_Of_Dreams
EQspoilers has an enchanter guide to share, and we figured we'd let you know.

Here's the announcement:

EQ Spoilers( www.eqspoilers.com ) has placed an Enchanter Class Guide ( http://eqspoilers.com/class/enchanter.shtml ) online. Heres a clip from the PVP strategy portion of the guide:
 At level 51 with my enchanter I was able to kill 4 people simultaneously. It is very difficult but that is the sort of PvP task a skilled enchanter is capable of. My basic strategy is as follows: Have several mesmerize spells ready for use including the AoE one. Start by casting an AoE mez on them if they are near each other. This will freeze them for a bit. During this time you should cast tashan on each of them. Now, start giving them mesmerize to keep them down. One after another mez them and then cast your highest level tashan spell. You should now have them all mezzed and debuffed. Take the weakest link (normally a caster) and nuke them. Follow it immediately by another mez on them and then sit down to gain mana back. Re-mez or dazzle all of them to keep everyone frozen. Repeat this, always gaining your mana back. When one falls you go to the next and then the next. It is extremely difficult and I sugest that you start doing this by facing one opponent and then two and then three. When I faced 4 it was not by choice and I was extremely lucky. Dont waste time looting them unless there is someone that is going to ninja or they are bound close. If you dont know, mez everyone, loot and then gain some mana back while everyone is mezzed, for even 2-3 mezzings of everyone. This will allow you to gain enough mana to take down the second person quicker.
Thanks for the heads up!

Today's Patch Message
Game_Patch [ 3:34 AM PDT ] - Fsck
June 6th, 2002. 3:00am
------------------------------

** Gameplay **

- In hopes of providing more things for more people to do, certain raid level encounters in the world now return to existence a small amount faster than they used to. This was actually included in lastnight's patch but was not mentioned.

- To address the competition between epic-seeking bards and other classes, the infamous White Dragon Scale is no longer used in the quest given by Karam Dragonforge in Rathe Mountains. The quest now requires a White Dragon Hide. We did not reduce the rate at which White Dragon Scales enter the world, but rather added new potential for the new White Dragon Hide to be found.


** Focus Items **

- Spell casting time foci should report their new casting time properly.

- Spell duration foci designed to affect DoTs (the Affliction line) should now work reliably.

- Spell duration foci affecting beneficial spells are no longer random. They now always extend the affected buffs for their maximum. (We never wanted to make it harder to time your buffs.)

- The Affliction focus line should now only be affecting spells that do damage.

- Bards should no longer get focus item messages that weren't actually affecting them.

- We've re-assigned quite a few of the focus items to have their originally intended effect.

All of the same items have focus effects. However, as many people noticed, quite a few effects were mis-assigned due to a tools error. (Common, mid level items were frequently being assigned high level effects, and vice versa.)

This situation has been fixed, and the items now have the effects that we originally planned for them.


** Firiona Vie **

- Your server should once again have Trivial Loot Code enabled. Sorry for changing the rules on you.


** Chat Channels **

- Should once again work reliably for people who are using NAT or IP Masquerading via certain networking devices.


- The EverQuest Team

Sunday, May 26
The Future of EverQuest
GeneralNews [ 6:03 PM PDT ] - Lord_Of_Dreams
Those who attended the E3 conference and expo this year were treated with the vision of what the world of Norrath is to turn into. We were shown the first glimpses of EverQuest 2 in action, one of the first in-depth looks at EverQuest: Adventures, and then plans for the future of the classic EverQuest 1. All I can say about all three is that they are amazing in their own right. EverQuest is being taken to new heights and both EverQuest 2 and EverQuest: Adventures are making ground breaking entrances into the gaming world of MMORPGs.

Ill concentrate with the two games that Stratics covers in this article however, and treat you to what was learned about the games and my personal impressions of them. (EverQuest and EverQuest 2)

While I dont have much experience in the plane raid aspect of EverQuest, I do know that the Planes of Power expansion is doing some really awesome things to that aspect of EverQuest. The new planes look spectacular, and the epic battles involved with it look like they are going to be tremendous. There were also some very interesting facts we learned about how the expansion is going to be set up. Unlike many of the previous expansions, there is going to be a good deal of content added for existing lower level characters so that they may fully enjoy the experiences of the expansion. There are also plans to ship this new expansion with copies of Everquest Classic so that new players joining the game will be able to delve right into the new content without purchasing a separate box for it. I think this is a great idea, it allows people to hop right into the expansion action as soon as they receive the game. Im personally looking forward very much to being at the level where I can go and test out some of these new mega-mobs.

One extra tidbit that we learned about the latest EverQuest expansion is that high level advancement is going to be beefed up big time. We asked if they would be raising the level limit, and while they wouldnt give us a yes or no it sounded like that is the most obvious choice to me. But, that is only me guessing and nothing is definite yet at all from SOE regarding what they will actually put into the game regarding new high level advancement opportunities.

After the Planes of Power expansion demo we made our way over to the EverQuest 2 station all I can say is WOW. EQ2 took the cake in the graphics department for any MMOG at the show in my opinion. The level of detail that is etched on armor and walls while also being spread throughout entire towns is truly amazing. We saw a few monsters in action whose movements and graphics were incredible. There was also this one frog that they were showing off to viewers, who in all honestly looked more real than a real frog! Well, maybe it looked a lot like one of those tropical frogs but it was still beyond belief in what they are managing to pull off with this game.

Unfortunately they were not able to release much game info at this time, but from what they were able to show us the game is going to be great. (If they are able to keep along with the same level of work that weve seen so far from them)

The one thing that we were able to solidly gather from the demonstration was that EQ2 was going to be a whole new game from the original EverQuest. Features like decay of items, no XP loss upon death, and a whole new story line set it apart from its predecessor.

The story takes place during a new era of Norrathian history a good number of years after the original EQ. We were told that there will be enough time between the two to dramatically change the landscape of the world of Norrath, but still a short enough period of time so that we will see some familiar NPCs scattered throughout the world.

All in all I was very impressed by both of the games, and think they will add whole new dimensions to the world of EverQuest; both as we know it today and in the future. I cant wait to see what comes next in this line of award winning games from SOE.

Physical Enhancements
GeneralNews [ 3:51 PM PDT ] - Abombpk
One poster asked why certain races got Physical Enhancements, and others did not. Absor answers with:
""This ability adds an additional 2% to the Natural Durability, Combat Agility and Combat Stability archetypes.
There is no pre-requisite that these archetypes be trained in, however the greatest effectiveness is achieved from doing so. It is believed that you need at least 1 point in an archetype to get the 2% increase in it (i.e. you would get no benefit in Natural Durability if you only had points in Combat Agility and Stability).""

Most every melee type has this skill under Archtype, Paladin, Ranger, Shadowknight, even Beastlord. But seems Warrior, Rogue and Monk are missing it, or were left out specifically. What was the reasoning behind this?

Renaan Hellfire
Tarew Marr

This ability came out of beta testing for Luclin.

Originally there were just the basic archetype abilities.

But there was some discussion among the testers about the usefulness of those abilities for hybrids. The logic went something like this.

Lets assume that a theoretical hybrid was 75% as effective as a warrior in each of those melee aspects (the warrior being the base at 100%).

If we boost the warriors ability by 5% (of the 100) he gains 5%.
If we boost the hypothetical hybrid by 5% (of the 75) he gains (gets out a calculator) 3.75%.

So the warrior with one level of the skill is 105% and the hybrid is 78.75%.

So rather than the hybrid being 25% less than the warrior, hes 26.25% less

As you add the levels of these bonuses up, you end up increasing the gap between the hypothetical hybrids melee skills and those of the warrior.

Now there was indeed much discussion about this, because if you look at it the other way, the hybrid is still 75% of the warrior (105 x .75 = 78.75). But in the end we decided to allow the hybrids to purchase a little more effectiveness.

Thats why its only available to the hybrids.

And, no, I dont really want to debate the numbers. My head already hurts.

Alan
Thanks, Alan!
