Wilderness Zone Guide - Cazic Thule
Introduction

Cazic Thule is a dungeon suited for levels 25-45. It is located in the Feerott, the ogre newbie zone. Cazic Thule is the home of the lizardmen, and they make of the great majority of the dungeon population. One reason for the dungeon's immense popularity is that the much sought after armor rubicite is located here, and it is dropped as loot by a few rare spawns. However, there are other excellent pieces of loot to be found here as well, such as the lizardscale cloak, lizardscale mantle, bladed thulian claws, and the lizardscale tribal mask.

Hunting Strategies

There are a number of areas to hunt in Cazic Thule, but where exactly you wish to hunt depends on whether you are seeking rare loot or good experience. Of course, you can get experience while camping a rare loot at the same time, but some areas are suited to it than others. While on the subject of rares, in some cases there is not only the loot order within the group, but a loot order among the various groups as well. This is an indication of how much people want rubicite =). So if you really want to get some, be prepared to wait your turn for a long while.

Now for specific strategies. Starting in the level 25-30 range, you'll most likely want to group up and pull from the maze, a convoluted area that is found directly to your right after emerging from the entrance hallway (also, the area right outside the entrance hallway is referred to as the courtyard, or just CY). Inside the maze you can find warders, defenders, and sentinels, all of which give excellent experience (along with as much as 3 plat and the occasional fine steel weapon! =)). There is a rare spawn in the maze itself too, the clay golem. He either has a potion or the lizardscale tribal mask, a good piece of equipment for monks and druids alike. If you are seeking rubicite at this level, you probably should head to the throne room and try to group up there for the rares. Be aware, however, that this is a hotbed for killstealers. In the throne room, one of two rare lizards can spawn on the throne; the Tae Ew Templar or the Tae Ew Diviner. In addition, a NPC called the stone golem will spawn rarely on the upper floors of the throne room. All three of these do potentially have rubicite.

Starting at around levels 28-35, you might want to consider heading to the temple or steel golem pit as alternatives to the maze and throne room. The temple can be be found by traversing the maze, while the steel golem pit is found by making a quick u-turn after coming out of the entrance hallway, climbing up the ladder there, going through the door, going down the hallway to the pool, then swmming down the pool and back up to the surface to eventually emerge on a ledge. You then follow the hallway from the ledge to another green pool, and it is within the far end of this pool that the steel golem rarely spawns. While waiting for the steel golem to spawn (btw, most of the time a npc called the lizard champion spawns in its place), you can hunt the alligators which spawn in abundance around here, including in both pools. The temple is one of the best sources for experience in the zone. Around the outside of the temple in the four corners one can find lizard protectors, heralds, sentinels, and pages - all of which are excellent kills for lower level groups. In the temple itself (which is accessed by a stairway in the back), one can find fanatics, zealots, justicars, and judicators - which are excellent kills for higher level groups =P. And, at the very very top of the temple, another rare spawn can be found; the Tae Ew Archon. This lizard also has rubicite, so high level groups may wish to simply camp him while pulling lizards from the temple in the meantime.

The very highest level groups (meaning 33-45), may wish to camp the Avatar of Fear (or Cazic Cenobyte, which is found next to the Avatar of Fear's green bubble), which isn't even a lizard, but a very mean, nasty critter. He, however, has the much coveted rubicite breastplate, as well as bladed thulian claws. I do not know exactly how to get there, as I have never camped him myself =P, but I hope to find out soon.

Past level 45 or so it's time to move on to places such as Permafrost, Mistmoore, Lower Guk, or Solusek A or B (all of which I plan to do zone guids of, eventually =P). I personally have found Cazic Thule to be an *excellent* source of great experience and loot in my time there, with only the occasional bad apple stealing kills and such. I highly recommend it to anyone in the level ranges mentioned above. And, as always, my zone monster chart can be found below
