Races and Classes - Who's Playing What?

What's the most popular character race? Most popular class? Least popular? The answers will probably surprise you. They sure surprised me.

As I was trying to decide what type of character I wanted to play in the final, I became curious as to what others were playing. The programmers of EQ have given us an ingenius command called /who. Most people probably use this to see the others that are in their current zone. However, this command can also have parameters attached that let you see, for example, how many halflings or how many paladins are present, and not just in your current zone, but in the whole world of Norrath! Using this command, I did a series of surveys to see how many of each race and each class were online. By comparing these numbers to the total number of players online at the time, I was able to calculate what percentage of players were playing any given race or class. These results are averages of surveys taken on the Fennin Ro server March 8th -10th, with surveys done at several different times of day to hopefully get a more accurate result.

When looking at the numbers, keep one thing in mind. At any given time, approximately 12-15% of EQ characters are anonymous. Since anonymous characters don't show up on /who (parameter) searches, the numbers below reflect only those characters that aren't anonymous, and are slightly lower than the actual percentages.

Races:

Human: 16.74 %
Half Elf: 11.58 %
High Elf: 9.53 %
Dwarf: 8.81 %
Wood Elf: 8.70 %
Dark Elf: 6.98 %
Barbarian: 6.31 %
Erudite: 4.43 %
Gnome: 4.32 %
Halfling: 3.43 %
Troll: 2.93 %
Ogre: 1.99 %
Classes:
Warrior: 11.64 %
Cleric: 10.20 %
Ranger: 8.36 %
Paladin: 8.14 %
Wizard: 6.83 %
Monk: 5.90 %
Magician: 5.79 %
Necromancer: 5.41 %
Bard: 5.30 %
Druid: 4.92 %
Rogue: 4.04 %
Shaman: 3.50 %
Enchanter: 3.33 %
Shadow Knight: 2.46 %
So, there you have it. As the numbers indicate, most players' favorite flavor is vanilla. Warriors (no special abilities) are the most played class, and humans (no special abilities) are by far the most played race. Players have obviously decided that the severe NPC reaction penalties are too big a drawback for the "evil" races (particularly Ogres and Trolls), despite their formidable physical abilities.

One other thing I discovered. The vast majority of players, when deciding to be a (pick a class), will choose the race that they think makes the "best" (pick a class). Alternatively, they might have their heart set on playing a (pick a race), so they choose a class that their (pick a race) is really good at. While there is nothing wrong with this, it does tend to make certain race/class combinations *extremely* rare. If you want to play a truly unique character, one that you will definately NOT see many of running around, consider playing something like an Erudite paladin or a Barbarian rogue.
