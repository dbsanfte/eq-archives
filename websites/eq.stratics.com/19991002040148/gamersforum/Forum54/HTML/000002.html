I would suggest waiting till you start to haul in at least 1pp per hunting trip. By then, depending where you hunt, you should be around lvl 6-8. Keep putting points in jewelry craft until your trainer says you need to get advanced skills in the field. This will help eliminate a lot of failures in the beginning which is a time when money doesn't come easy. This is what I have done with mine and the basic jewelry tends to be successful most of the time. Hate to think how much I would have lost with a skill of 10. Plus at this level you are around the corner from enchanting which will help recoup some of the money you spend when you sell to players.

Two other considerations for the low level Enchanter.

First - Enchanting metals isn't available until 16th level, so I suspect you won't want to focus on Jewelry until then.

Second - You said you wish to be a "Jack of All Trades". Oddly enough, I did the same thing this weekend. Same race too. Great minds and all. Anyway - take this point well: Brewing. That is your best friend at early levels for funding your trade skills. Here's a recent history for your consideration.

I created as a test a newbie Enchanter (human) in Freeport on the Quellious server (no twinking). Rather than invest in spells, I spent every penny I could get on brewing. I picked Freeport for precicely this reason, but it turns out Qeynos is a fine choice as well and just a hop across the pond for an Erudite. Anyway - I would go out and attack rats and bats, harvest the wings, wiskers and other items. I usually left the teeth and such and only collected the items that I could get about a silver or more for. Once my mana and or hps were low, I would go sell. As soon as I had enough money for at least 6 tries, I'd go buy the materials for the brew I was working on at the time (based on skill) and go to work. I put *no* training points in brewing. As soon as I finished brewing, I went right back outside.

In the course of under three hours, I had capped brewing at 135 making fiery brews, and had just reached level four. Making Fiery Brews was yielding about 4pp profit on a batch of 20 attempts (80-90% success). After three batches at mastery, I sauntered over to the Enchanter guild and bought - at one time - all my first AND second rank spells. I then quaffed 20 Fiery brews to celebrate, got rip-roaring drunk, and scribed my spells. By the time I finished scribing all 18, I was back down to a slow wobble, and had several pp to spare.

Enchanters - Jewelers Everywhere - if you're low level and need funds for other skills - Brewing is your friend! And when you master it - its fun to throw a party at the local Newbie Zone!
