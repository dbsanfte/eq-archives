Shh! Listen! Do you hear that?

That is silence. It is what I was enjoying before you arrived with your questions. But you are young, and so I will forgive you. You wish to understand the life of a monk, do you not? Very well, then. Come with me.

There, in the practice yard. Watch Brother Tenshin as I speak. Perhaps his movement through the seventy-three phases of Tiger Hunting the Moons will suggest an outlet for that silence-disturbing energy of yours.

That is what you will do with much of your time, should you pursue the life of a monk. We are much involved with the focusing and redistribution of energy, especially that within ourselves. You will learn to tap into the energy that resides within you, and to use it to lend strength to your limbs, and power to your actions. Your hands and feet will become weapons; your skin will become armor; your blood will become a healing fire. But it is your mind that is your true power, never forget that. Without a disciplined mind, you will never realize your full potential or master Crane Stands in the Lotus, like Sister Essh'iial, over there.

Don't look so surprised, there are many among the scaled people who follow our path. They are different, yes. Harder, darker, more dangerous; but they are monks nonetheless. We are the two halves of the Flowing Circle, the light and the dark. If either grows too weak or too strong, the circle collapses on itself. Here, I will draw a Flowing Circle in the sand for you. Meditate upon the dual nature of our order and ourselves as you practice Kitten Swims Upstream. Perhaps, if you learn something from your meditations, Brother Tenshin will teach the kitten to become a tiger.

Sunday August 4 2002
The Monk F.A.Q.
The Monk - 5:05 PM PDT
Greetings, friends.

For some time now I've been working of a slew of Monk guides, the biggest being the Monk FAQ. The first draft is nearing completion, but there is still much work to be done. What I find it lacks most are the ideas and opinions of Monks other than myself. Another matter is that while I've played the Monk class for a couple of years now, the high end game has still eluded this casual player.

So, I ask you, the Monk player base, to please drop in and take a look. Fire away anything that comes to mind. Even if it's to say, "Gee, Khamon.. Does your wife remember what you look like?" Any insight on the Monk class beyond level 41 would be great!

How is the layout? What sections have I missed? Have I made any errors in my completed sections? Should anything be added, or even removed?

Well, I'm eager to read your ideas. :) If you have writen any Monkly guides that you want to share, even if you already see a version of the same one in the Guides section, please let me know.

See you in the Forum!

Thursday July 25 2002
Introducing the EQS Monk Portal!
The Monk - 2:12 AM PDT
A cool breeze weaves its way through the high mountainous peaks, refreshing the body as well as the mind. It was a long climb, but you've finally reached the summit, and gaze upon the Temple grounds and surrounding gardens for the first time.

All is quiet here, save the sound of wind chimes hanging in an opened window. The strong smell of blooming flowers chase away the woodland scents, now far below you. In the distance you see a man fast approaching, drapped in long brown robes.

Greetings, stranger.. I bid you welcome here. I'll be your guide and am known as Brother Khamon, of the Ashen Order. Aye, we are a long ways from Freeport, Grasshopper. Shall we begin?

Hey, there! Welcome to the EQS Monk Class Portal! Here you'll find updated Monk News, Guides, and More! I'll be adding more guide content soon and perhaps some of you will write a few of your own. The Staff is always looking to make improvments on the site, so if you have any suggestions I would love to hear them! I can be reached here, or settle yourself into our very own Monk Forum! I can also be found on the Rodcet Nife server as the Monk, Khamon Dhar'ken.
