Basic Tactics
There are two basic, successful tactics that I have seen successfully used in combat. Each is meant to be used in specific circumstances. All of these methods’ success are based on the ability of the party to outnumber its prey and to pick the time and place of the battle.

The first is what I think of as old-fashioned ambush. A hunter is sent out to find the prey (usually a tank, rangers using their track skill are in high demand). Once they find the prey and attract its attention, they lead it back to the predetermined ambush site and the battle commences. This method is best used with a well-balanced party with at least one tank (who is usually the hunter as well). In particularly tough situations, the tank may choose to just run by the group with the prey in hot pursuit to let the group lob spells and/or missile weapons at the prey to soften it up for a toe-to-toe battle.

The second is particularly good for use in a party that is heavily magic oriented. This method requires some sort of missile capability, which means throwing items, shooting arrows, or casting spells at the maximum range from the monsters den as possible. When this is done effectively, the monster that has been injured will be enraged and encouraged to charge the group. This allows you to choose the ground at which the battle will take place and to have your group set up in proper battle formation (magic users in back, healers in the middle, and tanks on the front lines).

Different areas of the world use varying tactics to outmaneuver their prey. If you want more information on hunting tactics and group strategy I recommend visiting the message boards at the various EQ focused web sites and requesting advice. From message board posts that I have read, Tassader seems to be a very knowledgeable source. You can catch him at the EQSS QuestHall and at EQBoard.

Improved Keyboard Configuration
Note: This is written for right-handed users, but certainly usable by person of the left persuasion.

Keys (left hand): W – move forward; A – move left; D – move right; S – move backward; Q – primary attack; X – secondary attack; Ctrl – strafe; Alt – auto run; Space Bar – crouch, jump

Mouse Functions (right hand): Left-Click (on target) – open door, targeting, execute commands in numbered slots while maneuvering; Right-Click (on target)– use item, /consider, mouse look; Double Right-Click – used in fast looting of prey

Most frequently used abilities, socials, and spells that are not in the keyboard setup menu can be assigned to a number (1-6). After the six numbered slots have been filled you can use shift-2, for example, and the second set of six slots will be ready for use. There are six sets of six slots for your use, giving you a total of 36 slots for frequently used commands.

These improved key locations will allow you to keep your right hand on the mouse and left hand on the keyboard for almost 100% of the time you are on EQ. They also reduce the frequency with which you need to look away from the screen to find a key. We will always be experimenting with this setup to find a more comfortable and more convenient interface with the world of Norrath. However, I think the basic setup of keeping all of the important keys within easy reach of your left hand and leaving the right hand on the mouse will remain throughout the life of the game.

"What do you have in your 36 slots, Tormod?" You ask. I have certain Socials that allow me to /say or /emote several things to roleplay an action. For example, I play a barbarian so a I have series of grunts, roars, and battle oriented emotes that I often use in battle to add to my groups enjoyment of the roleplaying aspect of the game (more on how to create a Social later). Here is a list of the things that I have in my first six slots (the most frequently used):

  • Kick
  • Taunt
  • Sense Heading
  • Persona
  • Invite/Follow toggle
  • Sit/Stand toggle
The first two (taunt and kick) are skills used by tank types that added to combat effectiveness. The slots are also the logical places for combat related spells as the 1 and 2 keys are easily reachable with keyboard configuration already mentioned. The second two (sense heading and persona) are often used in interactions with the environment and with other persons. Persona shows your stats, skills, and inventory. Sense heading is a crucial skill if you don’t like getting lost. The third set of two (invite and sit) are toggles that change appearance depending on your current state. Invite turns to follow once you have been invited into a group and can be used to invite others into a group that you have formed. Sitting increases your regeneration of hit points and mana as well as increases your chances of being hit by a foe. I find that these commands are the most frequently used and deserve the first six slots. Of course, everyone is unique and has unique tastes.

How to Create a Social
In the windowed view, the default interface in Norrath, click Socials (right hand side of the screen). Right-click one of the default social buttons (Wave is one of them) and you will see the name of the button along with an area to insert commands below the name. This is where you will enter the commands to be executed. For example, I have a Social called "Clean" in my second row of six slots. I use it after a particularly difficult battle to /emote the following message, "Tormod cleans the blood and gore from his weapon." In the Social set up screen (after right-clicking one of the defaults on the lower right hand side of the windowed interface) you would see the name "Clean" at the top and under it you would see this line "/emote cleans the blood and gore from his weapon." It is possible to add several commands under one Social. This comes in quite handy and amuses every group that I have used it on. To be entertained, entertain.

The Kanly Factor
Sportsmanship counts in Norrath. For instance, there are some characters that zone into an area and are immediately invited into groups because their fellow players know that they are trustworthy and loyal companions. However, there are others that are not only not invited into groups by those who have heard of them, but are also shunned by those that happen to be in the same area as them. Let’s use the example name of Kanly ThroatSlitter. Let’s say that every time Kanly entered a room he complained about how the groups in the room were attacking their prey and would be heard continually complaining about various topics using the /shout and /ooc commands (these are two communication methods that are heard throughout the zone). Kanly would be quickly put on the /ignore list of many of the people in his zone and would not have the advantage of requesting help from those people in times of peril. Hypothetically, he might even get written up in an article about sportsmanship in Everquest. So, my friends, it pays to be fair to those around you. Whether you play an evil character or a knight in shining armor, those around you will judge you by your actions and their judgements can mean the difference between death at the hands of a gnoll train or instant and happy help from your comrades-in-arms. To coin a phase, don’t be a Kanly.
