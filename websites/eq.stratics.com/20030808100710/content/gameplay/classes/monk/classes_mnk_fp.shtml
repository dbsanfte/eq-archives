A Human Monk In Freeport

 Levels 1 to 4   Levels 5 and 6   Levels 7 and 8 

Levels One Through Four
Over the past few days, I have been experimenting with the human monk. This report will detail information concerning the first four levels of my investigation into this character class. As many of you know, the monk can only be human, and as a result, the starting locations are limited to Freeport and Qeynos. I decided to start out in the city of Freeport. To give you a brief introduction to the monk, the monk is a class like no other. The monk is dedicated to the belief that the use of weapons and armor is a sign of weakness, and the monk tries to perfect his body into a lethal killing machine. As a result of this belief, the monk does not use most weapons and armor. The starting equipment for a monk is very modest. You get five loaves of bread, five flasks of water, the player killer switch scroll, and a guild scroll.

Once beginning in the beautiful and immense city of Freeport, I began to investigate the city itself. Freeport is divided into three major areas: North, West and East. The West area is mainly shops, the North is the same, and the East has shops and also docks for boat travel. I started in West Freeport and eventually found my way to the monk guild. This simple task should be your first. Read the directions carefully on your scroll which you receive upon creation. It will give the location of the guild house. It is hard to find, but search West Freeport, and you will prove victorious on your first quest. Once you find this house, remember where it is in the city, because you will return here every time you level up.

After I had completed this task, I decided to check out some combat action from the gates of West Freeport. Outside the gates, you will find anything from snakes to skeletons. First level monks are able to fight large rats, bats, and snakes. The key to any first level character is be ready to run. Always keep in mind where the guards are, and if things get bad, run as fast as you can to them.

At level one, the monk has three basic modes of combat: punch, kick, and throwing. These maneuvers do not inflict a lot of damage, but you get many attacks. NOTE: Stick to punch and kick for the time being until you have enough extra money to invest in throwing knives, shurikens, or throwing axes. The monk also has two basic modes of non-combat: mend and bind wound. Mend allows you to attempt to heal your own wounds. Mend costs nothing, but it takes awhile before you can use it again. Bind wounds is a skill which allows you to heal another player. You will need to buy bandages in order for this skill to work. Once you have done some fighting, you will soon find yourself at level two. Once you have reached level two, go back to your guild house and get some training. Consider improving your combat skills as best as possible.

At second level, you will find large rats, bats, and snakes easier to defeat. You may also begin to attack decaying skeletons, but watch out for deathfist pawns and deathfist centurions. They can take the form of humans or orcs and can be very nasty! Once you have fought enough to reach third level, go back and train some more.

At third level, you should be able to take on decaying skeletons, bats, giant rats, snakes, fire beetles, and deathfist pawns. The key to third level is know your own limitations. If you start to get pounded on, just run. There will always be more to fight. At this point in my travels, I began to realize that the south gates in East Freeport was even a better place to do combat. At the south gates, you will find a higher concentration of more difficult monsters. Watch out though! Deathfist pawns and deathfist centurions spawn in groups sometimes, and you can find yourself out numbered quickly. Also keep in mind that the fire beetle is an opportunist. This monster typically will leave a prepared warrior alone; however, should you be engaged in combat, the fire beetle loves to take advantage of the moment. Also, do not be surprised that if you attack one fire beetle, others may attack you as well. Once you have done enough combat to move you up to level three, head back to train some more.

At forth level, you should be able to defeat decaying skeletons, bats, giant rats, fire beetles, deathfist pawns, armadillos, spiderlings, black wolves, some deathfist centurions, and snakes. Be cautious as you travel further out, you may encounter black bears or giant spiders. These creatures are not to be trifled with as of yet. As you fight more, and reach level five, it is time to retrain again. At this point I will leave off, but not without tantalizing you first. At level five you get the right to earn a new skill, Round Kick! Expect more soon about Round Kick and new monk information.

Levels Five and Six
Once you have reached level five, a whole new world opens up. This article will address levels five and six, as well as, mention quests, armor and weapons, and new skills. The first thing you will notice upon reaching level five is the ability to train in the skill of Round Kick. This skill is similar to the regular kick, but can do more amounts of damage. I highly recommend that you spend one of your five practice training classes on this new skill and the other four towards Mend Wound. As a level five monk, you will also begin to realize that the monsters which you can kill by yourself no longer give the experience they used to at lower levels.

You may want to begin experimenting with joining and forming groups. Grouping is a valuable and exciting part of EverQuest. You get to meet people, fight with them, and die with them. A good, well-rounded party is always the best. I recommend based on my experience: one fighter, one wizard, one bard or cleric, and one other spell caster. While a total of six members can be in a group, the more efficient you are, the more experience you get.

Once you are a member of a group, it is time to size up your opponents. The rattlesnake, orc raider, puma, and skeleton are the least difficult for moderate leveled groups. You may also run into giant scarabs or giant spiders. These are more difficult and can quickly put you in pain. Make sure you have sufficient muscle before taking on these monsters. The madman and deverish thug are probably beyond your group's ability. These humanoid creatures pack a mighty punch and have the potential to eliminate your entire party.

Also at level five, you may want to invest some of that hard earned gold into items you can use. The armor and weapons a monk can use is very limited. The armor a monk can wear is restricted to cloth, leather and some magical. The weapons a monk can use are limited to throwing knives, shirkens, throwing axes, clubs, staffs, maces, and warhammers. I recommend that you stick to your hands and feet for the time being, so that you can improve your skills.

Further, I found that fighting monsters all the time was getting a tad boring, so I decided to investigate the Quest part of EverQuest. The easiest way to see if an NPC has a quest for you or not is to hail them. First, select the target NPC, and then click the hail button under your Socials menu. If there is a quest needing to be accomplished, the NPC will instruct you on how to complete the quest. You have to search pretty hard for quests right now, but you might find something of interest in your guild house (grin). Once you have fought your way up to level six, it is time to get some more training. As of level six, here are the skills you can invest in:

One-Handed Blunt

Two-Handed Blunt
Hand to Hand
Kick
Round Kick
Mend
Bind Wound
Swimming
Sense Direction
Fishing
Tailoring
Fletching
Safe Fall
And a few others I cannot remember off hand.

You will still probably want to stick to your basic fighting skills, but you may also look at investing in some other useful talents.

At level six, I stuck with my groups and continued to fight against giant spiders, giant scarabs, pumas, skeletons, and orc raiders. I also began to fight against madmen. Madmen are quick and deadly. Make sure you have a well-rounded or powerful group before taking on this humanoid. Deverish thugs are still rather dangerous. I would advice, RUN!

Level six is a very long level indeed. As you get more daring, you will find you die more often. As a result, you loose experience and valuable time. Keep doing what you are doing, make friends, and have fun. Once you have done enough combat to reach level seven, you will be where I am who knows what could be next?

Levels Seven and Eight
Wow, I cannot believe I finally made it to level 9. It seemed to take forever. Between the transition from phase 3.5 to phase 4, time began to slip away. I apologize for the wait on this article, and hope you enjoy this final installment of "The Monk's Canon." Not only we will discuss the tactics of the Monk at these levels, but I have also managed to gather up the specifics on each skill the Monk can train.

First off, at level 7, you will find yourself coming into your Monk abilities. As before, you will still want to continue pressing on through group combat, but you may want to try some solo combat in relatively safe areas. For the most part, I found myself combating in the Deserts of Northern Ro. This area is an excellent place for a moderately skilled warrior to do combat, either grouped or solo. When grouped, you may want to try and combat giant tarantulas (formerly giant spiders), sand scarabs (formerly scarabs), orc raiders, rattlesnakes, pumas, jackals, and the occasional madman.

One difference at level 7 is to try and be as efficient as possible with your group. Do not take extra members into the group unless you find yourself defeated often. As for soloing, always use the consider command (/con) before entering combat. Typically, creatures that you consider and give the message "risky, but you might win" are safe opponents to solo, but be cautious. If you decide to solo, always keep yourself near the guards or another zone. If the creature begins whoop you, run to a different zone or the guards. Also, if you are soloing in Northern Ro, the guards will not attack a puma or a madman, so need to run to the guards if one of those is on you.

After 7 levels of solid fighting in limited areas, I began to get curious about the world outside of the areas I had been fighting. You might just find a boat ride to the Dwarven city of Kaladim fun and relaxing. Combat can be very repetitive at times, and taking a little journey to another place can be a good relief. NOTE: If you decide to stay in that area and fight a lot, make sure you get a wizard to "Bind" you to the city. This is very important because if you were to be killed without being bound, you would come back to life back in Freeport. Better to come back to life at the closest possible location to your corpse. After you have done some adventuring and done much combat to make it to level 8, time to go back and do some training. I suggest you begin some heavy investing in your Mend skill.

At level 8, soloing creatures should become easier. Do not forget to consider everything you fight just in case. At this level, you should be able to solo pumas, jackals, orc raiders, some tarantulas, and some rattlesnakes. With a well-rounded group, you should be able to take everything in Northern Ro but deverishs and Dorn (an NPC human). Also at level 8, you will be able to train in a new skill, Sneak. The sneak skill allows you to quietly bypass NPC's without being detected. The sneak skill is something I would not necessarily spend your training points on (except the first point to learn it), rather I would just practice it whenever you get the chance.

I also began to develop a philosophy about my character. I had never really thought about what my character should believe in, if anything. I began to, I assume, role-play. At level 8, I decided that my monk would combat any orc creature, regardless of the odds. This added a sense of fun to it. I said to myself, "Any time I see an orc, I will attack it regardless of my physical condition." While most of the time it was not dangerous, I did find myself dying to a couple orcs right after I had completed combat with a tarantulas. This element of role-playing can make the game more interesting for you and your party. After you have fought and fought some more, it will be time to get some training, and begin to explore Norrath without knowing what to expect. See the EQSS Class Reference section for a full list of the skills that you can attain, and the level (if applicable) needed to train in them. Farewell to you all, and thank you to all my fellow group members for the fun and experience!
