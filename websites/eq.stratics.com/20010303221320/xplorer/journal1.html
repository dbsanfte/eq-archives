First it took about 2 hours to get through the patching and the billing thingies. I think the login server disconnected thrice before the billing went through ok. Then my friend created his character, Thalandin, a wood elven ranger.

Aaahhhh, a tear went down my cheek as he entered the game for the very first time. I'd been in the same seat 2 months ago, and I could understand his confusement :). I couldn't help him at first, as I was also a newbie when it came to the ranger's departement. A person named Captmorgan appeared though, and taught him the basics of being a ranger. Thalandin also received a rusty battle axe, a round shield and a cloth cap from this friendly person.

Then it was time for the first combat. Thalandin picked a decaying skeleton, and after some 3-4dmg blows and a lucky 12dmg, it went down. That's right, 12dmg! That's what my Atherion scores at most, being level 11, oh well :). It was pretty frustrating in the Faydark though (as always) and we both wanted Thalandin to go to Rivervale as soon as possible. So we changed seats and I pointed Thalandin to what I thought was Kaladim. Turned out I was wrong, and that my memory from the trip I made failed me, and we soon saw ourselves lost in the big ol' woods. Thalandin shouted across the zone if there was somone there, willing to fetch him. Awaiting the reply of any friendly ranger I foolishly walked Thalandin right into an Orc Centurion. It turned out we'd somehow managed to end up near the crushbone camps!

Crack! Ick, those centurions really hit a lvl 1 character pretty hard :). I panickly jumped away and to my amazement, we found ourselves at the newbie ruins. The blood rushed through our brains as we were trying to help poor Thalandin from certain death. And then, when we were almost at the top of the hill, almost overlooking the lifts... Thalandin was knocked unconcious, and later the orc finished him off.

LOADING, PLEASE WAIT...

Ah, back in newbie clothes. Oh wait a minute, we started in that :). We went back to the corpse, took the shield and axe and thought of making another try, this time letting a guide do the hard work, to find the way to Butcherblock :). A ranger, named Jralm, approached and told Thalandin that he would take him all the way to the docks.

This time we did better, and soon we found ourselves at the passage that led to the Butcherblock mountains. Btw, Thalandin is using a 56k modem, and zoning goes so much faster at his place. I don't know if it's because of his 56k modem or 128 RAM, or both.

After we'd run through the butcherblocks we finally came to the docks. We'd seen on shout messages that the boat had just gone by, so we were forced to sit down and wait. At this time Thalandin's connection was doing loops of packet loss and soon we heard the infamous "t-click"! Turned out it had been my father calling, I guess time goes by when you're having fun :)

We resumed our journey the next evening and when I came to my friend's house he'd already taken the boat. At his last zoning to Freeport his connection had gone down and once he came back the boat was LEAVING Freeport. Our brave Thalandin had no choice but to throw himself overboard and swim to shore. He succeeded and when I sat down next to him he was looking for a capable binder. We found one (forgot his name... grrr!) and we proceeded to West Freeport. We checked Thalandin's equipment, and strangely, his tome of discord was there. So we had to go to North Freeport to deposit it in the bank (no, this doesn't mean he'll be a PK, it's just better to have it in the bank than not at all, EQ is always changing). It was tedious to zone, and for safety reasons we got bound in West Freeport too, just near the western gate (forgot the binders name again... grrr!).

Ok, so there it was... the plains outside Freeport. Last time I walked here I was trying to hang on to Soma's pace as he guided me to Rivervale. With Thalandin just being level 2 I quickly ran him to the borderzone, that would lead us to the East Commonlands.

You have entered the East Commonlands.

I quickly moved Thalandin to safety, up on the wall side. Once again I saw the stuff that Soma had shown me; The passage to Ro, the ghoul camp, and then later in the West Commonlands, the entrance to Beholder (at least I think it was that order). The East Commonlands wasn't as bad as I had thought. This time I took time on me, checking the surroundings. In TEC all I could see were giant spiders and with Thalandin's tracking (which is an awesome thing) we saw asps and other snakes.

In the West Commonlands came our horror - the hillies (Hill Giants). Even though it looked like they were just 30 feet away Thalandin made it safetly to the most southwestern part of TEW. Now it became a lot harder. We had to run down in the zone to spot the passage, leading to the Kithicor Forest. Just one problem though, there was a puma standing right down there looking very hungry (and through a shout we were told that they were very very aggressive against a level 1 character :).

We cramed (typo?) the western wall as much as we could, avoiding the Puma. To our luck we saw a high level paladin resting up on the wall (Amorphius something). He escorted Thalandin to the Kithicor Forest and told us that he'd get us safetly to Rivervale. I remembered from our previous treck that Soma had done a wrong turn and we had been lost in here before. And ironicly, Amorphious took a wrong turn himself :). I didn't know what he was thinking, but I told him that we should have gone right at the Freeport sign. We got split up somehow and I proceeded alone to the cavern which led to Rivervale.

Wow, the Vale Patrol, what a sight!

LOADING, PLEASE WAIT...

Finally, our level 1 friend Thalandin had made it to the vale. After a total playing time of 3 hours the polka music kicked in, and what a lovely tune it is. We found a binder pretty fast and proceeded directly to the Thicket.

I know it said LOW to MODERATE difficulty playing a Ranger, but I never thought it would show so much as it did when Thalandin started to do battle. It was somewhat of a struggle during the first level, but once he had doubled his amount of hps and got his offence/defense/slashing up a couple of points he could wack fire beetles and spiderlings with no problem at all. Even the mangy rat, which had scared me to the bone were no match for our Thalandin.

At one point where Thalandin had gone back to Rivervale to get a container a friendly player named Teni (Half elf druid) had given him two backpacks, and almost a full suit of leather. That's right, leather! And at that time he was level 2, or was he still level 1 at that time?

Anyway, all the suddenly Teni SoWs over to Freeport to get Thalandin some more stuff. I could just sit there, staring as my friend were getting all the goodies from this friendly player :).

So, bedecked in leather and storing slots like no other newbie, Thalandin made it back to the Thicket. He had absolutely no problem at all now, killing fire beetles. They came grouped once though, but Thalandin was able to knock one of them down. Then when he started to pound on the other fire beetle, yet another one joined the battle. All the suddenly Thalandin's health bar went down to two bubbles and he had to run back to the guards. What was this strange beast of supreme power we wondered?

It can't be true, the Queen!!?? Ugh... Here I'd been with my Atherion the previous weekend trying to find the rumoured Queen, and now it was here, pounding on Thalandin? Oh well, at least I know it exists for sure now, still I feel miserable :).

The time had gone by again, and when I left my friend's house Thalandin was almost halfway through level 3. I guess we'll both go queen hunting later today. He'll get to kick the fire beetles, and I'll work on my conjuration skill, whirling winding to finish them off (and yes, skills DO increase even though you fight stuff which wont give you experience, just at a slower rate I guess).

After experiencing the trip from Kelethin and Rivervale again I feel an even more urge to go exploring. I just hope I'll get out of this powergaming philosophy I'm having at the moment :).
