More 'Fun' Spells wanted
[ 2:24 PM PDT ] - Kang
This Post was found on the Offical EverQuest Forums:
>>>I have been thinking lately that there should be more "fun" spells in the game.. Spells that dont' neccisarily (misspelled) have a purpose in the game other than to play around with.
I do like the idea of fun spells, and would like to have more of them around. We have several in now, including a line of magician summons that are there for fun. Of course, one of the problems with fun spells is that people have an expectation that all spells should be "useful", with "useful" being defined as something that helps advancement in one form or the other.

It would be interesting to hear how many would like to see more fun spells of this nature in the game.

General Issues: Answered by Abashi
[ 2:19 PM PDT ] - Kang
This Post was found on the Offical EverQuest Forums:
>>>GM's can do an Exp res when there is a game play problem right? Well then why do you have exp loss (you still don't get all your exp, but most), you still get res effects, and you lose all your mana and most of your HP. If a GM has determined that you deserve a GM res, why not have it a full, no strings attached res?
The process that attaches the "Resurrection Effects" is on the back end of the resurrection code. At the time that the effects are applied, all it knows is that you have been resurrected, not by what spell, which is why it's attached to all resurrections. I agree that this is a bit of a pain, and I'll see if we might have another way to determine what kind of Rez has been cast.

>>>2.) What is with all the random faction hits in the Lake of Ill Omen?
They aren't precisely random. They occur when you are on the hatelist of a creature killed by another group. Basically, it's part of the Hatelist propagation issue described here a few days ago.

For instance, you attack NPC A, who yells for help to NPC B, who yells for help to NPC C. NPCs A and B are close enough to you to chase after you, however NPC C is not. NPC C wanders around the zone and yells for help to NPCs D, E, F, and G, who are all too far away to come after you. However, since they all have a copy of the hatelist, whenever they are killed you will take a faction hit.

-Gordon

PvP Issues
[ 2:16 PM PDT ] - Kang
This Post was found on the Offical EverQuest Forums:
>>>I'm sorry....by taking away a benefit to non "gankers" who can bind isn't going to be a fair solution. Part of what makes having a cleric/necro who can bind in a area close to where you are doing a dungeon raid to get a rez is to lower down time. If on PvP, you in sense want to increase down time because a few "exploiters" want to ruin the game, then you are causing more problems than you are fixing.
The challenge has always been to add PvP as a viable option without adversely affecting or trivializing the PvE (Player versus Environment) aspects of the game. That said, PvP needs to have risk associated with it. Since item loot creates a caster/melee risk disparity, and since coin-loot is only a negligible risk, and since experience loss isn't going to happen, restricting binding might might be the best way to go.

-Gordon
