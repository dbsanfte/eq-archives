Thursday, October 22nd

EverQuest Game Engine

   On EQ Vault's Developer's Board, Brad McQuaid talks about the EverQuest engine and its ancestors.

   the actual engine is based on the return to krondor engine, not the tanarus engine. I'm pretty sure they're using an offshoot of tanarus's network and communications code though. - Kur

   The EverQuest engine is not based on the RtK engine, but rather they have common ancestors. The 'original engine' diverged almost 3 years ago -- our version turned into the Tanarus engine after some modifications, and then that engine turned into the EverQuest engine after HEAVY modifications. And I'm sure the RtK guys have made major mods to their engine as well. I'd have to say there's probably very little linking the two engins at this point other than history. - Brad

Saturday, October 17th

3D Issues

   On the Avault's EverQuest newsgroup, Brad McQuaid explains the reasons for polygonal graphics in EverQuest.

   Isn't it possible to make the graphics in EQ more like Final Fantasy 7? Use the CGI, or whatever it's called, to make the background, floors, and anything that doesn't move; and use polygons for moving objects? As long as they don't make box arm poppyes for characters, they could make graphics that are better then they are now. - Smoke

   That would be pre-rendering the background scene, as opposed to rendering it real time. You would no longer have a true 3D game at that point, with freedom to walk where you choose and move the camera around you. Nor could the world be nearly as large. - Brad
