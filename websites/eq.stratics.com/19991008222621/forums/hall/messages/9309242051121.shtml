Hello fellow players!
*********************

Most of you will not have heard of our project "EmuQ", yet we already
got the second e-mail from Verant's president John Smedley, in which we
were requested to remove our homepage and stop all activities related
to the project within 24 hours. Why? Well, "EmuQ" is a server emulator
for Everquest and it is illegal, according to Verant ...

When we started the project, we did not have any fixed intentions,
actually we never thought we could succeed. But the first few problems
were solved faster than expected, and our curiosity drove us further.
Soon we could log in the game and run around in the door- and player-
less Felwithe created by our own server-emulator. The first
multiplayer-session, the static Npcs and the Items followed.

At first we did not realize that EmuQ was against the "User Agreement".
Once detected, we mailed Verant, in order to hear the exact reasons.
The responses we received were always short threats, but never reasons
explaining why emulators are forbidden. Can it be that there are no
profound reasons?

Our project does not harm EQ in any way. EmuQ is no rival for EQ. They
have quite different goals: While EQ offers an enormous and complex
on-line world, EmuQ wants to make it possible to create your own world,
with your own quests and play them with a small number of your friends.
EmuQ will never be a replacement. Every member of the EmuQ-team
regularly plays on Verant's servers and will not stop, should EmuQ
ever be playable.

Some will say that an emulator abandons many secrets, e.g. unexplored
zones, rare monsters and items. Yes, you can go looking at all zones,
admire all items and monsters. But the zones will not be populated the
same way, the items have different names and characteristics. The
excitement, curiosity and gameplay remains the same as without EmuQ!
By the way, each Everquest site has more game-relevant spoilers than
EmuQ.

We do not steal customers from Verant. We do not make money with EmuQ.
No resources of Verant are used. We need neither assistance nor support
from Verant.

In order to clearly state our attitude, we wrote this EmuQ-codex:

EmuQ-Codex:
***********
- You have a valid EQ-account.
- You will not use any components or information of EmuQ to
interact/modify/cheat/exploit/harm the real EQ servers.
- You will help Verant by submitting bugs, exploits and suggestions.
- You will not spoil any information to players not expecting it and
thus destroy their gameplay.

This statement goes to all larger EQ Sites. We do not require
acknowledgment of Verant or their support, what we want is that Verant
tolerates EmuQ. Verant must permit that all of you can use your client
to connect to an EmuQ-server. As of now, you will become criminal doing
so. The EmuQ-team will meet rules and restrictions of Verant. We know
that we can find a solution acceptable for both sides, as soon as Verant
get rid of their stubborn, negative and "no-discussion" attitude against
EmuQ.

We ask all of you supporting this idea ( or rejecting ), to discuss this
on one of the many message-boards, in the newsgroups, on your own site,
in-game or wherever. We need your support to show Verant that their
customers stand behind EmuQ and see something positive in it.

Supports us!
************
