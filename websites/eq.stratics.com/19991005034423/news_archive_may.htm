New Dragon Information

   Brad McQuaid has posted new information in regards to Dragons, on the Developer's Board on EQVault.

   It is our view that dragons are very exotic and rare creatures. Because of this, we are not going to list different types, nor their different attacks, alignments, colors, etc. Coming face to face wt. a dragon and doing combat with it (regardless of whether you live or die) will be something you can be really proud of. Many people who play EQ will never see a dragon -- they'll only hear tales and such. In fact, from a purely gamers standpoint, I wouldn't have wanted to even show screen shots with the lava dragon. It's even my hope that those who do encounter dragons in EQ will keep the details to themselves... in a MUD I used to play quite often the ultimate foe was Tiamat, and I remember asking people about the dragon... where it was, how did it fight, what treasure it guarded, and I received very few, and then always vague, answers to my questions. And then, well over a year later, when I finally was able to face the creature, the awe and honor was that much greater.

Beta Application Adjustments

   The official EverQuest homepage has posted information about updating your existing Beta applications:

   We've been receiving a steady stream of emails when it comes to changes in people's beta applications. If you need to adjust your information, please fill out the form again. First phase beta testers will maintain their spot regardless of any change to their application. However, those beyond the first phase will find their application adjusted each time they re-submit an application. In other words, don't re-submit your application unless you have to. Please do NOT send any adjustments to the webmaster, or anyone else; all adjustments must be made in the form of a new application.

   Those who wish to adjust their Beta application can do so here.

Saturday, May 9th

EverQuest Racial Interactions

   A recent post on the EQVault Developers Message Base by Bill Trost (Lead game designer for the EverQuest team) reveals a few of the "Racial Interactions" we can expect from a few of the "Evil" races. Here are quotes from that post:

The evil Teir'Dal (Dark Elves) tolerate Trolls and Ogres but view them as simpletons who can be used to enforce Dark Elf agendas.

Trolls hate everyone, including other Trolls. They are basically vicious anarchists.

Ogres, while just as evil (to non-Ogre eyes), are less likely to prey on thier own and have a more tribal like culture with a defined social structure.

Trolls and Ogres are marginally more intelligent than they are typically portrayed.

Friday, May 1st

Monthly EverQuest Chat

   The monthly chat with Aradune (Brad McQuaid) went off without any problems. (other than a little nuking at the end) Here are a few of the items brought up during the chat:

No restrictions forming parties in regards to Race and Class
A +PvP finding a way to harm a -PvP could result in banning
Bards will have short songs related to their singing and instrument skills
Higher-level monsters are more likely to have exotic items
Some cities will be "sensitive" to alignments
Necromancer "Create Dead" spells will require a target corpse
Experience is currently awarded with the final blow in a battle
Some classes will have combat related skills available while fighting
A "Common" language will be available, as well as race-specific languages
Guild affiliation information will be stored along with your character information
"Push-Past" will be implimented for characters which may block doorways
The scale for Armor, Attributes and other stats are different from other systems
Charcters will be able to become anonymous to others, except for their name
Clerics will be able to wear armor and wield maces
Monsters may display cowardice, depending on their intelligence and the situation
Weapons will not show damage and will not wear out
PvP deaths may be announced
Necromancers have a Lycanthropy spell
Obtaining some items may require quests to have them created
Different spells have different casting times, which can be affected by certain skills
There will be a profanity filter and most likely a switch for blood/gore
Teleport spells may be limited where you can travel to, or how often they can be cast
Guards will attempt to slay criminals, not take them to jail or capture them
Spell scrolls can be used to enscribe spells into your spell book, then they are gone
