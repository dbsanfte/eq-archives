Details on PvP Server

Here's Guide Abashi on some of the rules that will govern the upcoming PvP-only server:

Please don't call it a "chaos" server, as chaos implies no rules, no penalties.

A PvP server will simply mean that all people will be PvP+. This means the loot limits (one item), the level limits (+/- 4 levels), and benefits and penalties will still be applied. If you kill out-of-character it will indeed have serious reprocussions, though you will certainly have the *ability* to do so.

Also, like with PvP on any other server, there will be no experience or skill gain from PvPing.

-Abashi, Senior Guide

Brad on RP Server

There was some dissent in response to Brad McQuaid's comments regarding a Roleplaying-only server (see below). Here's Brad's response, from the EQ Vault's message board:

EverQuest is indeed an RPG, and in the context of your post, ALL the servers are RP servers.

EverQuest as it is now encourages role playing in many ways. A few examples would be the races and classes, the ability and advantages to grouping, the PvP switch, and the fact that dynamic quests and other special events will RP oriented.

Many people are concerned that their attempts to RP will be interfered with by those who choose not to RP. So they want an 'RP' server. And while I understand and even share their desires, there's really no way to enforce such an environment.

For example, we have neither the resources nor the desire to listen in on everyone's conversation and make sure they're speaking about in-game topics only. So we've provided an OOC channel (which you can turn off) and we encourage people to use it, and not the /say or /shout or /auction for out-of-character communcations.

I don't think anyone wants a 'Big Brother' totalitarian environment in which 'our' vision of RP is enforced upon the populace. (It's very likely you or someone else will strongly disagree with our strict vision).

-Brad

In-Game Guild Commands

Here's a list of current guild-related commands in EQ, from the patch server:

Guild Commands:

/guildinvite - Invite a person to join the guild, if this person is to be an officer, include the argument (as in oscar) for the rank. Usable by Guild Leader and Officers, but only the Guild Leader can make officers. You would also use this function to promote a member to an officer.

/guildremove - Remove a member from the guild. Only the Guild Leader may remove Officers.
/guilddelete - Disband your entire guild. Only the Guild Leader may use this command.
/guildstatus - Will return information on whether the named person is a member, an Officer, or the Guild Leader.
/guildleader - Transfer guild leadership to.
/guildsay - Sends a message to all guild members.
/guildwar - Initiates a guild war against the guild of Guild Leader which appears in the field. sets the duration in real days of the guild war. This defaults to one real day if left blank. The opposing Guild Leader must send the same command with the of the other Guild Leader to fully initiate the conflict.
/guildpeace - Ends a guild war before the duration has expired. is the name of the opposing Guild Leader. Both sides must send this command to end the war prematurely.

Heartstone Project Roleplaying Group

The Heartstone Project sent us this announcement:

In a few weeks, tens of thousands of adventurers will be entering the world of Norrath. A month following that, hundreds of adventurers will also join the fray. They will be all be in Everquest, but how many of them will truly be questing?

Quests, one of the most enjoyable elements of the roleplaying experience. Unfortunately, in most online game, Everquest included, players are not truly on an "everquest." Instead, quests are more like extra events tossed in. Most are automated with a few being run by GMs or players. Few of these last more than a few hours before they are over. Then players return to the daily routine of "leveling up" their characters. Does this sound like an "everquest" to you?

Introducing the Heartstone Project, an epic roleplaying experience. Here's a summary of the project.

In game:

The Heartstone Project will be gathering a sizable group of roleplayers on each game server. These groups will work together to provide a positive roleplaying experience through frequent quests. Each group will be sub-divided into different factions who will be working for different in character goals. Freelance players who are outside these factions will also be accepted. All these factions will be tied together with a common roleplaying plot that centers around the idea of Heartstone, a medium for providing an epic wide-scale quest system.

Heartstone is difficult to describe (for obvious reasons). Some factions believe it is a mythical, magical artifact with immense power. Other factions believe that it is the "heart" of Norrath, a physical representation of the lifeforce of the world. Others believe that it is a vile infection that threatens the safety of the world. And others have no idea that it exists. Some seek to control it. Some seek to protect it. Some seek to destroy it. Some have no idea what's going on.

Members of the Heartstone Project will be working with this central idea, adding their own ideas or putting a different spin on it. The idea was made to be as broad scope as possible, allowing many different roleplaying variants while still providing a central theme that connects a wide variety of players together.

Quests will be provided by the players, factions, and questmasters. In theory, there will always be multiple quests going on since interaction between the factions are considered quest themselves.

Web site:

The web site, currently found at http://www.ji.nu/eq/ will be a central meeting place for all those involved in the Heartstone Project. It will also house the many stories, journals, song, artwork, etc. created by members. It will also provide an area for roleplayers, even those not involved with the project, to meet, discuss, and interact. Message boards, member pages, faction pages, stories, journals, chat rooms, searchable databases, artwork, fantasy fiction; you name it, we'll have it.

Now that you have a fairly good understanding of the project, you can decide for yourself if you want to help. Staff positions are now being accepted. Even if you do not decide to join the staff, all everquest players will be given the opportunity to be members of the Heartstone Project. The application form for staff signups can be found at http://www.ji.nu/eq/staff.html and the membership application can be found at http://www.ji.nu/eq/membership.ht ml.

Thanks,
Heartstone

Beta Signup Security Issues

We know that a lot of you are concerned with the beta sign-up page and whether or not your credit card number will be securely transmitted. We've contacted Sony and the rep that wrote back insists that their application site has a secure connection (Secure Sockets Layer--or SSL--version 3.0). Sony claims to be using RSA/RC4 encryption in order to keep your credit card account safe.

On the other hand, neither Netscape nor Internet Explorer reports the sign-up site as being secure, which is leading to many a tester's unease. The form itself is located on a secure web server (HTTPS), but it redirects you to a server that is reported by the browser as being unsecure. I have yet to see both NS and IE report a secure site as being unsecure. Apparently this is just one of those situations where you'll have to decide whether or not to take someone's--in this case Sony's--word for it.

Patch Info on Guilds

The EverQuest patch server was updated last night with the following information relating to forming in-game guilds:

GUILD FORMATION: In order to form a guild the following process must be followed:
  1. The leader of the guild must email Eqguilds@sonyinteractive.com. The header of the email must contain the NAME of the guild. In this email the leader will include a short Charter that includes the guild's role in Norrath. In addition, the leader needs to list at least 9 other characters that have agreed to form this guild.
  2. The leader and 9 other players need to email Eqguilds@sonyinteractive.com with the NAME of the guild as the header. The body of this email must include the name of the character that will be joining the guild and the ACCOUNT on which that character exists.
  3. Once the 10 prospective members (including the leader) have completed the above steps, the charter and name of the guild will be reviewed by 989. If both are approved, the leader of the guild will be notified of approval via email and told to approach a GM for formation of the guild. If 989 has any reservations about either the name or the charter, the guild leader will receive an email listing these concerns and be offered an opportunity to modify the charter or name as required.
This will most likely be a beta-only method for guild registration. Thanks to Jenkins for the news!
