Playing an Ogre Shaman

 Introduction   The Creation   Ranks   Conclusion 

Introduction
An Ogre Shaman is, hands down, one of the best classes available. You will have the greatest strength and stamina of any race, and you will be able to cast extremely powerful spells including heals. Although any experience in EQ is enhanced by grouping, these features will allow you to survive much longer solo then any of the weaker races. As you progress in levels you will become sought after by groups for your 'Spirit of Wolf' spell (SOW - allows individuals to "pull" or flee much faster), for your heals, and for your considerable fighting/tanking ability. Granted, you will never be able to tank as well as a warrior, or heal as well as a cleric, but your mixture of powerful strengths makes an Ogre Shaman a best friend to many.

Another wonderful aspect about being an Ogre is that most Ogres are good role-players. I believe that this is because it's rather fun to talk like an Ogre. "Me bash gud." "You puny." "Hmmm, lizzie is gud to eat." Etc. In addition, there are not many Ogres presently in existence, which makes Ogre territory less occupied and easier to find kills and good loot. It's also enjoyable to be the most massive PC in existence. You will inspire awe in others at your sheer size (not to mention the role-playing aspects of running away from a Gnome, like an elephant would run from a mouse).

Disadvantages of being an Ogre include being hated by most races and being huge. Travel to other areas is much more dangerous because you are KOS (Killed on Sight) by most races except the Dark Elves and Trolls. Having such a large body can also be a disadvantage in that in certain dungeons (especially Upper Guk) with narrow hallways, it can be hard to maneuver or get through if anyone is in your way. However, both of these drawbacks are tolerable and there are ways to work around them. For example, you could kill what you need to, to improve faction if you really want to be in a particular region. Also, you can stay in dungeons that tend to be more open (Crushbone and Unrest have been good).



The Creation
One of the first questions is where to put your 30 additional attribute points. I have often heard a rumor that attribute points are less valuable when over 100 - I have not found this to be the case. I tested out a variety of classes and races looking at HP and Stamina as a benchmark, and have found that class has more effect on eventual HP than does Stamina.

Stamina / HP Comparison
Class / Race Stamina Level 1 HP
Enchanter / Dark Elf 65 19
Enchanter / Dark Elf 90 20
Warrior / Ogre 132 36
Warrior / Ogre 150 38
Shaman / Ogre 127 26
Shaman / Ogre 150 27

As you can see, the additional HP from adding 25 to stamina is the same for a Dark Elf Enchanter, as it is for an Ogre Shaman (aka whether you're above 100 or below it, the additional HP/lvl is not huge). The additional HP will eventually make a difference, which can be seen by multiplying the level 1 HP by 10 or 20. The Ogre Shaman does not compete with an Ogre Warrior for sheer tank ability, but will definitely hold up under fire better than any other full casting magic user (of course, keep in mind that a Shaman can bind him/herself wherever they desire, where a Warrior can only be bound in certain spots by someone else). Ogres start out with very good Strength and Stamina, and most other things are average, except Charisma, which is very poor. The decision of where to place your additional attributes will probably not have a large effect until you reach higher levels, so it is important to decide what kind of character you will want. If you think you would be content to stand back and cast spells - put a majority into Wisdom. If you are more interested in getting dirty and occasionally tanking - put a little more emphasis on Stamina. Strength is another good area to place some additional points - as this directly correlates to how much you can carry with you (something that does become very important when you're in enemy territory). I would not consider putting any points towards Charisma because you will never be getting great prices from NPCs as an Ogre, and secondly, because most of your selling will be done with other PCs who couldn't care less about your Charisma.

Weapon choice is somewhat clearer. You can use 1H blunt and a shield, or 2H blunt. Most shaman prefer 2H blunt because it does considerably more damage, and the loss of a shield does not make much of a difference in how often you are hit (a good offense is ). Use your 1H blunt club that you get at the start, until you can get your hands on a decent 2H blunt, then switch. Obtain a magic 2H blunt (aka a Glowing Wooden Crook, etc) if you can, because you will then be able to hit any magic undead you run into (shouldn't come to these monsters until your teen levels).



Ranks
The Formative Years (Rank I - Levels 1-4)
During these tender years of Rank I, I would suggest not wandering too far from Oggok. As you exit Oggok, if you follow the path and turn left at the 2 forks, you will come to an innkeeper. Adventuring behind her is a very good place to start. First rule of thumb - ALWAYS /con what you are about to attack. Green, blue and white (if you're feeling brave) are your best bets, where yellows and reds will likely end up in a corpse recovery trip. Second rule of thumb - Always keep a reference point so that you know where you are. Pick unusual landmarks and focus on recognizing them, so that when you do die (and you will die) you know where your corpse is. Third rule of thumb - Get accustomed to picking off wandering critters with spells - using Burst of Flame to bring them to you instead of chasing them down is a valuable, timesaving skill, plus it helps keep your bearings. Grouping is usually not recommended at Rank 1-4, but can always be fun for periods.

The Troublesome 2's (Rank II - Levels 5-8)

I say that these years are troublesome because by these levels you have gained substantial power, but still have to show restraint as to where you wander. After level 4, when you die you will lose experience, so restraint can be very helpful in the long run. From levels 5-8 I would suggest hitting the lesser lizzie camps - there are three of them which you will find if you wander behind the inn. One of the nicest things about these levels is that grouping becomes more helpful. When you are hitting the lesser lizzie camps and suddenly a couple Lizardman Mystics respawn and blind you; it will be helpful to have a friend close by. During this period you should also start working on getting better armor and weapons - admittedly a life long struggle.

Introduction of Spirit of Wolf (Rank III - Levels 9-13)

Level 9 brings one of the greatest boons to Shamen - Spirit of Wolf (SOW). You will become a very valuable member of any team, simply for this reason, not to mention the rest of your skills. This spell is incalculably helpful in that it allows you to run much faster than normally. It allows you to travel faster though difficult zones, and it allows you to flee from a fight that has turned out to be much harder that you initially though. At level 9, armed with SOW, the brave can consider wandering over to the stronger lizzie camps with friends or the smaller temples outside Cazic Thule. It is here that you can receive incredible payoffs from the lizards, but the region is typically busy for this reason. You should attempt to replace whatever armor you have with the stronger banded armor - which can be purchased from other PCs.

The World is your Oyster (Rank IV - Levels 14-18)

By level 14 you should now have a good idea of what to expect from the temples, and are starting to find it easy, being less wary of lizzie trains. The greatest blessing of level 14 is Bind Affinity. Bind allows you to set your gate (or death) point. One word on obtaining this spell - it presently is not available in either Oggok or Grobb, so you have to get it in Neriak, or pay someone to buy it for you. One of the major advantages of being able to cast the spell (as opposed to having someone else cast it on you) is that you can bind yourself anywhere. Common sense become very handy in deciding where you should bind yourself needless to say staying close to zones and choosing safe locations is very wise. With this ability, exploring the world becomes much safer. You can travel anywhere and bind yourself near the zone, knowing that if you die you will not have to walk back all the way from Feerrott. Because of your newly found freedom, you can wander the lands, amazing people who have never seen an Ogre before. Take caution however, because most lands will be very dangerous to you, not because of the monsters that may lay ahead, but because of the foul guards that may inhabit the region. Any regions that have a city in or adjacent to them (besides Feerrott and Innothule Swamp) will have wandering guards of very high levels (higher 30's) who will likely kill you on sight (KOS).

A little dying trick - If you die and your corpse happens to be in a relatively dangerous area, there is an easy way to get it out of harms way. 1) Make a social button (by R clicking it) into a corpse retrieval button - /Corpse. 2) Pay someone for an invis (Enchanters have the strongest invis) while standing as near as SAFELY possible to where you died. 3) Quickly run close to your corpse, and start hitting the Corpse button as you walk away from the dangerous region. Voila - your corpse is now somewhere where you can loot it with peace of mind! Now I would say that this is something you should only do if it is very necessary. For example, if you corpse happened to be right in front of the Felwithe guards.

A New Role (Rank V - Level 19-23)

At Rank V, you finally get a strong healing spell and you will find that your role in groups tends to shift to a healer more often. It is nice to have the option of standing back and healing Warriors or other Tanks, or choosing to get into the fray. At level 20, you can have a last name and will have more than enough experience as to what you want to do, so I will no longer presume to advise you.
