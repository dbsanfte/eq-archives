Playing a Gnomish Necromancer

 What More Do You Want?   Why a Gnome?   Where Do I Begin?   Off to the Fighting Grounds   Those Later Levels 

What More Do You Want?
The Gnomish Necromancer is the best combo in all Norrath. You start out as a pathetic humble little Gnome, then become a thirst crazy megalomaniac, and hungry for other people's essence. The wonderful thing about a necromancer is that you love to "feast" off your victim which no other class can do, but as a little gnome, it makes it all the rage! When you're a level fifty necromancer, and some huge as Ogre comes by and say, "Ug muv little ting!", and you'll say, "MAKE ME you dumb monkey!" And you feast on his superior strength and make his muscles just flab! Then enchant yourself and your skeleton pet and feast. You might be wondering why I am saying "feast" a lot. One simple answer, it's what you do, nothing else. You "feast" on there Hit Points, you "feast" on their strength, stamina, agility, intelligence, and every other stat there is, while your pet cooks up a storm with his lanky self.

Why a Gnome?
That's an easy question, they fit the necromancer character so well, standing at 3 foot nothing. These small, big-nosed charismatic megalomaniacs, thirst for knowledge. I mean their city is made of metal and is all mechanical. They are born with a skill known as tinkering which allows them to make weapons, objects, and armor that no other race can obtain. Such objects include, spy glasses (allowed to increase vision). Also imagine being a small half-pint necromancer in a huge world of your own to feast.
 top


Where Do I Begin?
Yet another easy answer, you start in the town Ak'anon. This illustrious town of machines is full of wonders including the world zoo and the Mines of Ak'anon. To find your guild hall, this took me at least 1 hour, and I am not going to tell you, sorry, think of it as a quest. I'll give you a hint, it's near the other magic users, Enchanters, Magicians and so forth. Once done with that, memorize and learn your two spells: Lifetap and Minor Shielding. These two will be your bread and butter for a while. Once you reach enough money, buy another spell, I suggest Syphon Strength, coveting bones, or whatever you want!
 top


Off to the Fighting Grounds
Now your off to fight, BUT WAIT, not outside the gates, but within the town. If you would like to know the location of this secret fighting area for levels 1-5 highlight below and it will show you the location and secret tips to help you advance pretty fast (I did):

The secret location to the newbie area is the mines below the cleric guild, right around there. There are Rock snakes, Runaway Clockwork, Runaway Bandits, and spiderlings. At the beginning, fight rock snakes only. Then around level 3, kill runaway spiderlings, but watch out, they gang up on you. Once you reach level 4, attack everything in sight!

If you don't go to the secret grounds, which I suggest you do, you can go out to the SteamFront Mountains. I suggest fighting snakes and rats at first. I suggest you watch out for spiderlings. They can gang up and kill a reasonable level 4 necromancer. Its happened to me several times, the runaway clockworks obtain help from the spiderlings too, so be careful. After level 4 or so, group up and go to the Kobold camp to the right of the main entrance of the SteamFront Mountains. There you will face evil Kobolds and their brethren Shamans. The Shamans are deadly to a group of 3 level 4 any class. Once your done with the whole deal with the kobolds, hopefully you would have reached level 6 or 7. If not, keep fighting there.

 top


Those Later Levels
The later levels are a pain. Skeletons here, Pumas there! Watch out for everything in those far out lands in the SteamFront Mountains. From high leveled skeletons to large infected rats, you better watch out for them. If your attacked by a puma, and your dying, don't run away, just give up.

Try to reach the Windmills far out in SteamFront Mountains. Once there, group up with about 3 people and kill everything in sight. As a necromancer, it's your job to feast, feast, feast on the target. Also it's your responsibility to use and sacrifice your pet. I know, I know, you don't wanna loose the pet, but magicians pay 5 silver for their pet reagent, but you find yours on every skeleton (practically), so be prepared to sacrifice it many times. If you decide to equip a weapon on the skeletons, be careful and plan to loose that weapon. It will disappear if your pet dies, you die, you zone with your pet, and you log out.
