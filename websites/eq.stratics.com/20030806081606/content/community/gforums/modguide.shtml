EverQuest Stratics GUILD Forums

Forums and Categories

The EverQuest Stratics Guild forums are divided in many sub-groups. Each Guild that sets up forums will be given a Category of forums, usually containing 3 of them: a public forum, a private forum, and a "secret" board.

All forums can be accessed from this page: http://boards.stratics.com/php-bin/guilds/eq/ubbthreads.php?Cat=

To obtain your own Guild forums, fill out this form: http://eq.stratics.com/content/community/gforums/guild_form.shtml

To add a new forum to your pre-existing category (currently limited to 3 boards per guild/category), send an eMail to eqguilds@stratics.com

Users and Groups

The EverQuest Guild Forums database is fully separated from the main Stratics Boards database. This means you will have to register a new username before starting to use the Guild boards. Users are then assigned to one or more group(s) depending on their guild affiliations. Unregistered users have read-only access to all "public" Guild forums. All registered Users belong to the main group (namely: User), which only allows them to read and post on each public Guild Forum.

Each specific Guild is also awarded two "special" usergroups to allow entrance to the Guild's two private forums. Administrators grant access to these groups upon request from the Guild Leader/Forum Contact/Moderator. A single user can belong to multiple groups, depending on his affiliation to different PAs.

To request the addition of a user to a specific group, the Guild Leader/Creator of the forum/Moderator should fill the following form: http://eq.stratics.com/content/community/paforums/pa_userform.shtml

Moderators

Moderators are assigned to one or more specific forum(s) inside a Category (= Guild) by Stratics Administrators as requested by the Guild Leader. Admins grant local moderating privileges to these posters, who are expected not to abuse them. Their task will be to ensure a proper posting atmosphere for each and every poster to enjoy their stay on the Guild boards.

Moderators belong to a specific usergroup (Moderators) and are granted a specific title. They are usually granted access to the private forums of the Category they moderate. To request the addition of a moderator to one or more forums, the Guildmaster/creator of the forums should submit the requested information here: http://eq.stratics.com/content/community/paforums/pa_modform.shtml

Rules of Conduct and FAQ

The eq Stratics forums are subject to Rules of Conduct (http://boards.stratics.com/rules/eq_roc.php). The Guild forums are also subject to them, though a little lighter: you can decide to allow attacks between/against your members, but some important rules will be enforced by admins on the public Guild forums (no porn, no exploits, no illegal material, etc.). Guild Forum Moderators also agree to act as representatives of both their Guild and EverQuest Stratics, and to lead by example. Important breaking of the RoC by a Moderator may result in the termination of the Guild's forums and the banning of the person from the Guild Forums.

Help on how to use the forums is available on the boards FAQ (http://boards.stratics.com/php-bin/eq/faq_english.php?Cat=). You will probably get many questions on how to use the forums and this page answers most of them. However, if a question isn't answered there, you can try eMailing eqguilds@stratics.com or visiting the main Stratics Help forum (http://boards.stratics.com). Locking a Local FAQ at the top of your board may be a good idea (ie : What your Guild is about, How people should behave on the forum, Whatever you feel is useful).

Moderating : An Introduction

The following guide will be the base outline for all of the available commands and features found in the UBBThreads forums. The guide will be broken down into sections. The first section of this document covers a number of issues including lessons learned, a brief Q&A and also tips on how to best manage forums and their respective users. The second section covers command options available to moderators.

Throughout this document the terms boards and forums are used interchangeably. They both have the exact same meaning as far as I am concerned. The UBBThreads are broken down into categories and then boards or forums found within those categories. It is important to understand that distinction.

Goals for Stratics Message Forums

The main purpose of having the Stratics Message Forums in the first place is to help promote interaction between players in the Online Gaming Community. Each forum provides a place where players can come together and get to know one another outside of the game. It provides a means for players with a wide variety of personalities and backgrounds to come together and interact in a friendly, non-argumentative and mature manner.

The primary goal is to provide a centralized location for players to come together and get to know one another. By encouraging users to offer their opinions and inviting insightful dialog, everyone benefits. There is a fine line between a mature debate and flaming. One of our goals will always be to encourage discussion while at the same time, throwing water on any flames we might come across.

Forum Moderators

Moderators have the ability to edit/remove messages from their particular forum(s). They can also move and lock threads within the forums that they moderate. They cannot change permissions for particular users, however. Only administrators can change user permissions. Technically the moderator is not required to do anything. They are there to oversee a particular forum in order to make sure of the following things:

  1. Make sure that everything is functioning normally (e.g. the forums work)
  2. Make sure that visitors are able to read/post messages
  3. Encourage dialog between visitors (encourage people to post)
  4. Keep an eye out for obscene, lewd or otherwise abusive material (e.g. someone links to porn images, uses racially hateful remarks, etc., etc.)
  5. Help the users with the forum by answering any questions they have regarding the fundamental operation and/or use of the forum.
  6. Try to also be informative regarding the subject matter of the forum in which you moderate (ie : your PA).

That is essentially it! The forum moderators act in a "support" role. Their realm of responsibility revolves around their own forum(s).

Tips for those new to Moderating Message Forums

Many of the visitors we now have came from other web sites. Others found us through their friends, by word of mouth. No matter how they find us, we have been able to keep most of them there. How do we do it? By using a basic philosophy for our forums...

The first thing anyone who posts on message boards should learn is patience. A lot of irritable people roam the web. Here are some tips that have really worked well for me in the past. If you follow them you may or may not get the same result, but it certainly can't hurt to try.

  1. Never threaten to ban anyone in public! This can create a riot of sorts within the forums. So please be very careful about ever using the word ban. In fact, moderators should keep all disciplinary issues to private messages between themselves and the parties involved. Publicly discussing issues will only serve to upset the parties involved and the other users as well as appear unprofessional.
  2. Try not to take sides. This is a tough one, since you will be moderating your own Guild's forums. There's usually a way to express your opinions freely without necessarily "attacking" the other side. Just disagree, then a mature debate may ensure. Should they start being really rude towards your Guild or one of its members, making personal attacks, ask them to leave the forum without attacking them.
  3. Understand that everyone is different and some people interpret text differently that others, especially those whose first language is anything other than English. It is absolutely amazing how often you type something with a particular meaning, yet others can read something entirely different in the same text.
  4. Use smiley faces a LOT. :) It helps smooth over misunderstandings VERY quickly.
  5. Never take anything personally. This is very important. The first thing you'll notice when a flaming war begins is that two or more users have taken a discussion too seriously and start focusing on the other user rather than the issue at hand.
  6. Allow people to debate, but squash flame wars ASAP. Tell people that they are free to debate as long as they do not personally attack another individual, group or company in public. We encourage mature and intellectual debates. Personal or abusive remarks against others is simply not tolerated on public forums and we are not afraid to crop them right out of the forums - but at the same time, allow for people the freedom to express their opinions as long as they are civilized. It tickles the mind and really draws in those who are willing to actually read and post messages without resorting to personal attacks (flames). You may meet great new friends and prospective members through well-managed arguments.
  7. Never hesitate to drop an offensive post/user. Yet at the same time tolerate stuff on the edge. I've found that if you allow for some borderline issues, then you'll be better respected by the masses because you are showing you do not delete just anything - only truly offensive and/or abusive material. Also, never delete a post just because it may offend YOU. If it does not break the rules for the forum or the General RoC, it should not be deleted.
  8. If you ever get really pissed off at a user and want to respond that way, type out your response and really lay it into them, then delete it before saving it. NEVER submit posts written in anger because I know for a fact that more often than not, you'll return later and wish you hadn't posted that message while being angry. When you allow for a topic to get to you personally, you tend to lash out. This shows very poor judgment and it reflects negatively against the guild as a whole, not just against that individual. If a Moderator breaks our own rules and becomes abusive, we will remove them immediately. It simply cannot be allowed. The same goes for administrators: lead by example.
  9. NEVER abuse your authority. Never, ever let your position go to your head. I know that we all say, what? Id never let that happen. Just because you're the moderator of a forum you do not have the right to verbally or otherwise belittle anyone. There's nothing worse than someone who gloats about their power or authority over others. I have seen it happen to some people who take on moderator roles. They were subsequently removed. So just keep in mind that the moderators are just like everyone else and they have to answer to the same rules as the visitors do. They are simply there to take responsibility for a guild forum, not act like a king or queen over their subjects.
  10. Understand that some posts are pure sarcasm and meant to be funny, not a personal attack. Some of the best humor can be found in some sarcastic posts.
  11. Give us some feedback. We're always willing to listen to what the users have to say and to respond to them. There is nothing better than a group who can offer you solid yet constructive criticism.

There is a very fine line we all must walk. On one hand we do not want to be too restrictive in what we allow (on the contrary we should allow anything as long as it is not vulgar or abusive). On the other hand, we must show a willingness to be very firm when needed and not fear reprisals from anyone. We can lock out IP address, entire domains if need be and also email addresses. Stratics also will follow-up directly with any ISP as long as they have a policy against abusive customers.

Dealing with Personal Attacks within the forums

The following article was written by Greebo for EQ Stratics, and it is so good we actually use it for all forums. It is imperative that you avoid allowing your public forums to turn into nothing but flame wars and pathetic whine fests. The article below demonstrates perfectly the to handle users who are becoming abusive to other users within the forums.

As the population of the forums increases, the number of personal conflicts is on the rise. To deal with the increasing count of personal attacks, Stratics has implemented a strict warning policy.

  1. When a user demonstrates that they are in the habit of making personal attacks in their posts, you should issue a Warning to that user that Personal Attacks are not allowed on your forum, and that they should refrain from such attacks in the future. If the attack is particularly offensive, it should be removed - otherwise the user can be encouraged to edit it. The offensive message, and your Warning, should be sent to admins - eqguilds@stratics.com .
  2. If the user then posts a second attack created after the first warning is given, the user should be warned again, reminding him again that his posting rights may be removed for disruptive behavior.
  3. If the user makes a third personal attack after the second warning, then you should inform the user that you are sending the admins a request for suspension, and send us a copy with URL(s) for the attacks and your notice. Upon receipt of this information, we will evaluate the attacks and the warnings, and if warranted, will suspend the access of the user to these forums temporarily, and send an email to their registered address explaining such.
  4. If the User returns and resumes making personal attacks, or if the User bypasses the Suspension by creating a 2nd account under a different address, the evidence of this (4th Attack or post by the Bypass account) should be sent to us again. The User will be permanently banned at this point under both Email addresses and IPs of these points.
  5. It remains the judgment of the Moderator to determine whether a Personal Remark qualifies as a Personal Attack and to send it to us. However, we will personally investigate complaints about any user/thread which are sent to us.

Stratics Message Forums Q&A

There will probably be times when you will be forced to act in response to a vulgar post or something along those lines. Here are some examples that should help give you an idea of what to do in various situations.

Someone makes lewd and explicit sexual remarks in a NEW post.

If this is a brand new post, then you might wish to delete the message. It pays to go in and obtain the following information before doing so. Get their username and the date and time of the post. This information will help us if we find a need to ban them in the future.

Someone posts pornographic images using the [IMG][/IMG] tags, and/or places links that point to pornographic web sites or otherwise inappropriate or illegal material.

This requires that the images reside on a server somewhere on the internet. The same applies in this case as our previous example. First obtain all of the relevant information (username, date and time and also, the URL for the link of the image so we have someone to go after) and then delete the post altogether. Treat links the same way you do pornographic images.

Forward all of the information youve gathered to eqguilds@stratics.com with an explicit header.

A user starts to really flame another user and gets really nasty, but at the same time is not using any profanity.

The message is just dripping with sarcasm and the message comes across as if the person is really upset. In this case, you may wish to intervene with a calming message for both sides. Basically remind them that we're all here for debate, but to please tone it down. If they persist, you may warn them, moderate the thread and/or call an admin for help. You do not wish to delete or lock every heated debate either. The last thing we need is to get a reputation for locking/deleting every controversial post.

A user posts links in the form of ADVERTISING.

If someone posts a message for advertising purposes (i.e. to sell a product, his characters, eBay ads, etc.) then you should delete them. This is a hard fast rule - No advertising in the forums. This is disclosed in the usage agreement when people create their accounts so they have no excuses. Please Note: When I refer to the term advertising I mean advertisements for web sites, bid sites and so on. We strongly encourage players to actually try and sell their goods on the boards when they're talking about such sales being handled in the game. An example would be a guy on a particular server wishing to sell a weapon. He can try hawking his weapon in the forums, that is fine. But if he has an eBay ad he is using to sell a character, that is not an acceptable form of advertising.

Additionally, should you have trouble with a poster spamming your board with any sort of advertisement (even regular ads) after being asked to stop, please let an admin know of the situation.

A user asks you for help.

Depending on the question(s) asked, I leave this one totally up to you. If they are concerned with the features of the forums and you're not sure of an answer, direct them to the Q&A/Help forum available at http://boards.stratics.com . Remind them to check the Boards FAQ first (http://boards.stratics.com/php-bin/eq/faq_english.php?Cat=).

The forum is not working right, there is something really strange happening. Users cannot post, the forums seem broken.

Send an email to eqguilds@stratics.com. We will fix the problem as fast as possible. Please note, that as with every site on the internet, you may experience brief slow-down periods with user load.

A user writes to you and says they have forgotten their password.

Whenever someone complains that they have forgotten their password. Be sure to direct them to the login page of the forums. Here they can enter their username and then click on the "I forgot my password" button. This will automatically generate a new temporary password for them and mail it to the email address used for that account. If they have changed email addresses and have not kept their account current. They will need to contact eqguilds@stratics.com for assistance.

Commands & Options available to Moderators

The following commands are available to moderators. These same commands are also available to administrators as well.
View Board Statistics

A Moderator can view stats on total number of Users, moderators and admins. Also get average number of posts per day for each board.

To View Statistics:

  1. Click on the Admin link in the top menu.
  2. Next click on the Show Statistics link. This will show the stats for all of the boards/forums.
Ban and Unban Users

A moderator has the ability to ban and unban users by Username or by Hostname. However, you may not, except in extreme circumstances take action yourself against or in favor of a User. If you have a problem user, you must report all details, including links to posts to us. We will investigate and resolve the issue based on the Stratics Rules of Conduct. IN AN EMERGENCY YOU MAY TAKE ACTION, BUT ANY BANNING MUST BE DOCUMENTED BY EMAILING WITH DETAILS ABOUT WHY A USER IS BEING BANNED, INCLUDING REFERENCES TO THREADS WHERE THE USER IS ABUSING THE FORUMS AND COPIES OF ANY WARNING MESSAGES SENT. It is far far easier for you to report a trouble user to us and have us look into it than to have us post-investigating because you banned someone without talking to us about it. If we find you have abused your privileges as a moderator, you risk being banned yourself and losing your Guild's forums. Abusing the ban system of these forums will result in your entire Guild forum being permanently removed from these forums.

To Ban a User:

  1. Click on the Admin link in the top menu.
  2. Click on the Ban a User/Host link. Enter the appropriate information for that user by including the method of the banning, the username and then be sure to include the reason you are banning that person. NEVER ban an IP address. ONLY an Administrator is authorized to do this type of banning.

Further Explanation: Enter the Username you want to ban. If you ban a Username then that user will not be able to post any messages or send any private messages, except by creating another username. Note that if you banned by an IP you may be banning a dynamic address that might affect more than one user.

To Unban a User:

  1. Click on the Admin link in the top menu.
  2. Click on the Unban User / IP link. Next, highlight the appropriate username and then click on the Unban button.
Close & Reopen Threads

A moderator may close or reopen threads from any board they moderate. Closed threads will not allow new posts except by a moderator or administrator. Posters can still delete or edit their own posts in a closed thread.

To Close a Thread:

  1. Click on the Admin link in the top menu.
  2. Click on the Close threads link.
  3. Select the board/forum that has the thread you wish to close. Then, click on the submit button.
  4. You will see a list of all of the threads in the board/forum you selected. On the left hand side of each forum is a check box. If you wish to close multiple threads you may click on as many as you need. Once you have selected the appropriate board(s) then click on the submit button located at the bottom of the page.
  5. You may also close a thread from the Edit Post link on the post.

Generally speaking, the only time when it is good to close a thread is if it becomes too long. Sometimes it helps to close a thread after a discussion gets out of hand. It is important to use good judgment when closing threads. Some people can take offense to having their threads closed. Make certain you have a good reason for doing so.

To Open a Thread:

  1. Click on the Admin link in the top menu.
  2. Click on the Open threads link.
  3. Select the board/forum that has the thread you wish to open. Then, click on the submit button.
  4. You will see a list of all of the threads in the board/forum you selected. On the left hand side of each forum is a check box. If you wish to close multiple threads you may click on as many as you need. Once you have selected the appropriate board(s) then click on the submit button located at the bottom of the page.
  5. You may also open a thread from the Edit Post link on the post.
Mark Messages as Keepers

A moderator can mark threads as kept or unkept. Kept threads will not be deleted by the expire feature. Unkept (e.g. normal messages) files will expire after a specified number of days if this option is chosen by an administrator. By default none of the messages will expire. However, depending on the amount of activity within any given forum, a message lifetime may be created and this command will then come into play. Please be certain to mark messages you wish to keep to ensure that when Stratics sets expiration dates you do not lose important threads/messages.

To Keep a Thread:

  1. Click on the Admin link in the top menu.
  2. Click on the Keep threads link.
  3. Choose the board/forum that contains the thread you wish to modify. Then, click on the submit button.
  4. A list of all of the threads for that forum will be generated. Place a checkmark by any threads you wish to keep. Next, hit the submit button at the bottom of the page.
  5. You may also keep a thread from the Edit Post link on the post.

To Unkeep a Thread:

  1. Click on the Admin link in the top menu.
  2. Click on the Unkeep threads link.
  3. Choose the board/forum that contains the thread you wish to modify. Then, click on the submit button.
  4. A list of all of the threads for that forum will be generated. Place a checkmark by any threads you wish to unkeep. Next, hit the submit button at the bottom of the page.
  5. You may also unkeep a thread from the Edit Post link on the post.
Move a Thread/Move this post & any replies

A moderator can move a thread or post and its replies between forums he moderates (ie : moving a thread from a public guild forum to a more private one). To do this:

  1. Click on the Edit Post link inside the thread you wish to move.
  2. Click on the Move this Thread or Move this post & any replies link.
  3. Choose which forum you would like to move this thread to. Then, click on the submit button.
  4. Choosing the forum the post already resides in will force it to become a new thread of its own in that forum.
Expire Threads

A moderator can select which threads to delete by marking them as expired. Each night a script executes which will search for expired threads. All threads marked as expired will be removed/purged from the database. This CRON job runs every evening.

To Expire a Thread:

  1. Click on the Admin link in the top menu.
  2. Click on the Expire threads link.
  3. Choose the board/forum that contains the thread you wish to modify. Then, click on the submit button.
  4. Place a checkmark by any threads you wish to expire and then click on the submit button at the bottom of the page.

Please be certain about which threads you wish to delete. Once they have expired and have been deleted, there is no way to get them back. So be sure what it is you are doing when using this command If you are ever in doubt, please contact an administrator first.

Make a post sticky/Return to normal date

A moderator can make a post "sticky." This means the post will stay stuck to the top of your forum. This is great tool for rules or announcements you may have for your forum. You can also return a sticky post to normal date, allowing the post to return back to its place amongst the other threads on the forum. Keep in mind that many users choose to view the forums in the threaded view. This means that threads that are stickied with many replies will cause the user to scroll down quite a bit or even seach for other new posts on a second page. It is thus recommended that when you choose to sticky a post that you also close it to keep others from making relpies to it. To do this:

  1. Click on the Edit Post link inside the thread you wish to move.
  2. Click on the Make post sticky or Return to normal date link.
  3. Click on the Continue button to finish.
Create a Poll

Moderators are the only ones allowed to create polls on the forums. This will be particularly useful in your private forums to take votes as each registered user is only allowed to vote once. Putting a pull in your post is simple, but remember: Posts with polls in them cannot be edited but they may be deleted. To create a poll use this format:

[pollstart]
[polltitle=Name of your poll]
[polloption=First Choice]
[polloption=Second Choice]
[polloption=As many choices as you would like]
[pollstop]

Administrator Help

Administrators may help you by using the following options, which are only available to them:

  • Assign/Remove Moderators
  • Create Usergroups and Assign Users to Groups
  • Setting Read and Write Privileges
  • Create/edit Boards (Forums)
  • Change the order of the boards
  • Edit or Delete User Profiles

They will also be able to help you in most matters related to your Guild's forums. To contact them, send an email to eqguilds@stratics.com or contact us through the boards' Private Messaging system.
