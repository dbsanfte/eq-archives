Playing a Rogue

 Introduction   The Novice Footpad   Getting Started   Skills   Conclusion 

Introduction
In the darkness of mid-night, a tiny shadow skirts the flickering torchlight on the edge of a large goblin encampment. Without the slighest noise, a form approaches the vigilant green-skinned sentry. Then a glimmer of metal flashes rapidly and is gone as the goblin guard slumps to the ground, killed from behind by skilled hands. Now seen more clearly in the light, a gnome motions to the dark of the trees for his adventuring companions to continue on into the slumbering goblin war camp.

Hello I am Gnimsh the Goblin Sticker. It's my job to keep those nasty greenies on their toes and away from our lands. No no, I don't go in for all that kicking and bashing… I prefer to be a bit more sneaky. Please allow me to illuminate the situation for you.

Rogues are often frowned upon, misunderstood, and outcast from society, that sure is shame because many times they are really wonderful to have around. The rogue in EverQuest provides a tremendous amount of opportunities for the player both in terms of hack 'n slash adventuring and role-playing.

The Novice Footpad
Life starting out is a bit rough. We haven't got the hitpoints of the tougher melee types, we start with the dagger that the casters do, and yet we are spellless. Let's go over some basics that will help ease this rough start.

What race you choose will really have no bearing on your "power" in the game. The rogues races are all very balanced (i.e. the halflings incredible agility and dexterity vs. the barbarians hitpoints and slam vs. the gnome's intelligence). So make sure you choose your race based on how you want to portray your character. If you're the kind that really is only after power-gaming, then my suggestion is the dark-elf or gnome. The reasons will be obvious later on

Ok, after race would be your stats, since the effects of increasing a stat beyond 100 provides diminishing returns on your investment, it would be well worth it to balance whatever your race is lower in terms of strength, stamina, agility, and dexerity. If your race has a really low intillegence (like halflings), 5 or 10 points won't hurt you there either. Now punch in a name, and be born into the wide dangerous world with your tiny, tiny dagger.

Getting Started
Find your guild! This can be hard at times, but other players or city maps online will help you out with that when in a jam. Once found, turn in your note and wear that tunic. It doesn't do much, and at least for now, you lose it the first time you die. Spend those first five points in piercing. Walk out to the local newbie area, and start stabbing snakes and large rats that con easy or even (it is hard to find easies at level 1)

Piercing is incredibly important and the only time you shouldn't be using it is when you are already maxed out for your level (cap is: level * 5 + 5 ). As soon as possible, trade in that dagger for a rapier or a spear, it is a major difference. Equipment wise, try to barted or buy from players to get a few pieces of patchwork leather, having a full set (note: patchwork does not cover every body part only 7 or 8 slots) starts to be rather important after 4th level. If you end up with some extra money considering /auction in a few popular zones to see if a giant snake fang is available. They are the best rogue weapon for a very long time. I have seen them sold for anywhere from 7 gold to 5 platinum.

Skills
This is where rogues are superb as we have so many to choose from. As you read some of the options, you will notice that advancing will really be a game of saving practices up for the skill you really want. You will have to spend one practice to start off all skills you get after level 1. These are in only marginal level order (and you don't get a new skill every level), but you can check what levels you get them at as soon as you get in game.

Sneak

You start with this one and can raise it pretty fast with use.

Pick Pockets

Use this on monsters right before hitting your attack key.

Dodge

Spend 1 or spend 5 depending on your intelligence. The more you are attacked, the more it goes up.

Sense Traps

Hard to raise if you want it. You will probably have to put 10 or so points in it.

Hide

Just 1 point, then practice a lot.

Apply Poison

Not yet implemented, buts sounds great.

Parry

Just one and fighting will suffice.

Backstab

Save up 15 before you get to level 10 to put straight into this skill- this is where the game really changes for a rogue.

Safe Fall

Never fear heights again. Raise by slowly increasing height you jump from. When you get good, you can jump off a cliff with a train on your tail, then finish them since they will be damaged from the fall.

Double Attack and Dual Wield

High teens. Try and have 15 saved up for each.

Riposte

Combat will do the trick.

Remove Traps

Unless you want them to blow up in your face, train in it a lot.

Make Poison

Not yet implemented.

I think there are more, but hey I'm not that high of level yet.

Conclusion
When playing EverQuest, I kept making classes over and over till I hit the rogue, then I fell in love. Hopefully some of you will too. To conclude with I will give some role-playing tips/possibilities:

  • you're the outcast half-elf no one ever likes and turned to robbery to put food in your mouth
  • as a dark-elf you just love seeing the agony on your victim's faces as your poison courses through them
  • you're a halfling whose just got into this adventuring bit too deep
  • if you're are the shady type, go ahead and emote some scowls at the paladin in your group, and tell the cleric what he can do with "his" god
  • emote some "glances around" and "looks paranoid" when doing something sneaky
  • pretend you're up to something even when you're not
  • con someone into joining you, because you just know where the secret treasure is

Ok folks, go out there and have fun, and stick some goblins for me!
