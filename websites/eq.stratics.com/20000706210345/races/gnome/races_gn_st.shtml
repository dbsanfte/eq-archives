Starting as a Gnome

Gnomes
I have to say it, of all the creatures I've played, I prefer playing agnome. They aren't the strongest or cutest race, and they aren't thesmartest, but they are the shortest! The first thing you'll find whenstarting out as a gnome is that Ak'anon, the gnome city, is absolutelystunning. The Ink Sea is inside the city. It's a huge lake with manybuildings in it, and a massive waterfall near the city's guarded entrance.

Ak'anon is worth exploring, and I mean that. Explore everywhere, there isa lot to Ak'anon. There's the zoo, the bank, the mines, the mechnamagicaguild halls, the abbey of deep musing, the forge of defense, andGemchopper Hall. I must warn you however, to watch out for the ladders.They can be tricky at first, and falling from a ladder as a level 1 or 2can mean instant death. So be careful! There is both a rogue's guild andnecromancer's guild inside Ak'anon. They are both hidden, so you'll have tosearch for them a bit.

The rest of the guild halls are fairly easy tofind, but if you can't find something, feel free to ask the many guideclockwork gnomes that are strewn about the city. Ak'anon is also a greatplace to fish, and the first order of business may be to obtain enough goldto purchase a fishing rod, as fishing is a great source of income untilabout level 5 or 6. You may also catch rusty weapons and rotting footwearif you're lucky! The best kept secret about Ak'anon is that you can levelup to level 3 without ever leaving the city.

I won't completely spoil thesurprise, but let's just say there are places inside Ak'anon where you canfight and get lost, both pretty easily. Having said that, my bestrecommendation is that you shouldn't leave Ak'anon until you are level 3 or4. There are plenty of very nasty creatures in the Steamfont mountains,but here is a description of what you should be fighting. Level 1, again,rock snakes are your staple. You can try the occasional decaying gnome,but not if the gnome has a weapon. Avoid the rogue clockwork spiders,rats, and gnomes at this point. They are reserved for level 3 and 4.

Level 2 lets you take on several snakes at once, as well as decaying gnomeswith weapons. At Level 3 you can try the occasional rogue clockwork, butalso rock spiderlings. Level 4 lets you pretty much kill with impudenceinside the city, but watch out because those rogue clockworks will gang upon you pretty quickly, and they are very fast. There is and will be a bitof competition to begin with inside the Ak'anon fighting area, but respectyour fellow gnomes and everyone will have enough beasties to go around.

Let's get back to outside Ak'anon, in the Steamfont Mountains. This iswhat you want to avoid unless you wish to perish when you first walkoutside. Anything that is not a regular brown rat. Infected rats, largeplague rats, rogue clockworks, spiderlings, skeletons, minotaurs, drakes,pumas, kobolds [scouts and regular] will all kill you pretty muchinstantly, so keep clear of them. You can fight decaying gnomes andkobold runts, and some of these give fair loot to keep you with food andwater. There are several watchmen that guard the paths, and it's an extremelygood idea to know exactly where they are in case you are a victim ofsomeone else’s problem.

Here again I refer to people biting off more thanthey can chew, and running down the path to the guards with 3 pumas, askeleton, and 3 large plague rats in tow. The odds are that if you try andhelp them before level 12, you will only get two gnomes killed, so watchyour step. There are windmills not far from the city, and these are anideal base point from which to fight from level 4 to 8. Once again,respect your fellow gnomes, and there is always plenty to go around. Thereare also 2 kobold camps (one harder than the other) and a minotaur cave toexplore in Steamfont, as well as ruins and madmen! It's a very dangerousplace, and corpses are commonplace, so you need to keep your gnomic witsabout you when adventuring.

You can buy and sell items at the windmills ornear the city gates, but the bank is inside Ak'anon, so you'll be makingseveral trips back there as you advance your diminutive character. Ak'anonhas seen a very high influx of dwarves and elves as of late, largely due tothe overpopulation of the elven zones, and the overhunting in the dwarvenzone. Don't let those longlegs and furryfaces push you around! Steamfontis a great place to live, but visiting can be dangerous.
