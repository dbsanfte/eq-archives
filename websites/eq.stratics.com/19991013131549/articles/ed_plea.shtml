A Plea to Brad McQuaid

Multiple Characters Per Server
A virtual world with one character per server will force us to be accountable for our actions and simultainiously foster the player interaction which is in the end the reason to play an online RPG. It will also help make the game a challange, the lack of which, in the end, has a great deal to do with how fast players become bored with a particular game and move on. A virtual and hence ever changing game need not suffer the same fate. It is with that in mind that I am writing to you.

Let me start buy saying that I do play UO but am tired of it and hope EQ can learn from one of its greatest mistakes. Namely allowing players multiple personalities on the same server, or in the same "world. Roleplaying, to my mind, is destroyed in large part through the use multiple charcaters. It allows and even encourages PowerGaming, destroys the need for relience on other characters and creates a world filled with mistrust. Playing different roles with different charcaters is important, but it should be done on different worlds.

Now to make my case.

First off I think it safe to assume that most people will seek to get any advantage they can. This is only natural and occurs in the real world as a matter of course. The same holds true for a virtual world only there is even less of a detterent to self control as the down side to ones actions are nil. If we look at UO as an example, we can see how intercharacter relations end up. In player killing of course, granted EQ has a switch, but by letting us have say 5 characters per shard, the need for smiths, carpenters, alchemists etc becomes none. With multiple slots one can never really trust anyone and so the logical step is to create your own "in House" charcaters or dummie charcaters.

I the player have now greatly reduced my options and have no need to make the perilous journey to whatever place to find the great smith or alchemist. It is exactly these journeys and the need which created them which expands the depth of a virtual RPG and keeps a game company from constantly trying to keep players amused. A game where I max out my power and simply run in circles looking for the next orc is is dull and wont lead to account retention.

Granted even with 1 character to represent us trust would be risky, but that is the nature of trust. If betrayed, then I at least know who not to trust in the future, and that person will have to deal with a downside to thier actions. But, if we have 5 slots, I have no idea who just scammed me, or killed me, I may an instant later be next to the same person under a different name. All this I belive, pushes a virtual world away from roleplaying, which is in no small part dependent on the interactions of and between different people.

As to PowerGaming, with 5 slots what is the most lucrative course of action? Again assume that players will take this course to a large degree. The most lucrative course of actions in UO is to play both sides of the field, good and evil. I can make money and gain items through Pking, and still have my dummie charcater run to town, hold the bank account and buy my castle. All this with no risk or downside. But if I had just one charcater, I as the Evil Lord would have to, as is realistic, use the services of middlemen or fronts. They might betray me, or charge a fee but this would in itself be and entire RPG sideline plot.

If I want to be the self sufficient ranger type, and rarely enter town then some of my skills had better be geared toward self sufficiency. I will not be able to spend all my skill points on swordsmanship and what have you. At some point I too will need the services of others. It is this need to interact that adds greatly to the depth of a virtual or paper RPG. Without any need for interaction, as opposed to desire, virtual games like UO degenerate into a version of Daggerfall played in a chat room with 10000 other people and some lag.

A virtual world with one chracter per server will force us to be accountable for our actions and will rid the programmers from attempting to create bizarre code sequences to control human behavior.

As a former stockbroker I realise that you must also make decisions in an economic light and many players may want to have 8 charcaters per world. But I caution you that many of OSI's problems with Uo stem not so much from the demons in the code but from an attempt to give everyone what they want. I recall an old childrens tale about trying to please everyone and wind up pleasing no one. Not all players will like all decisions but you are selling a game whose income stream will largely come from the addictive nature of the game.

I sincerely hope EQ will not allow multiple charcaters per world and I look forward to playing your game.
