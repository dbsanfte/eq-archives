WELCOME to Jennafae's Everquest Screenshot Archives. The purpose of this project is to provide a resource of interesting and exceptional background and scenic screenshots for the vast community of Everquest fan artists. Please note that the purpose of this collection is NOT to represent every single zone in Everquest. This gallery was inspired by my peers and friends at Norrath's Studio. I am forever in debt to their undying support, creativity, inspiration, constructive criticism, and most of all their friendship on my journey as a screenshot artist. In gratitude, I dedicate this Gallery to the amazing talent that lives in the N.S. "Studio Regulars."

Click on thumbnails to view larger images, rightclick to save images for use in your fan-art. Please do not direct link images from this site.

Rules for submitting images to the Archives:
If you would like to contribute to the project, I am seeking screenshots of exceptional subject matter and quality. Please email your unedited screenshots to JennaEQaddict@aol.com. All screenshots not meeting the following criteria will be disregarded. These perameters have been set in place to save time and work on my part. For the most part, I think all of the regulars on Norrath's Studio know how many hours I have put into this project and understand the need for these guidelines. For newcomers, I hope these rules will not deter you from contributing to the project.

  1. All images should be no larger than 1024 x 768. If you run EQ at a higher resolution, please resize them. Please do not alter the images in any other way.
  2. Convert images to JPEG and rename them appropriately by zone or something I can recognize. Please save all files in the highest quality possible.
  3. Create a copy of the image and resize to a 200 x 150 thumbnail. Height is more important than width, always make them 150 pixels in height where slight size discrepencies occur. Save this file with the exact same file name, only add a 't' to the end of it. For example, image pok357.jpg should have a coordinating thumbnail called pok357t.jpg    (please, no more shots of PoK, thanks)
  4. Please use WinZip or WinRar to zip the files before attaching them to your email. If you have an excess number of shots, zip 10 files at a time, then zip all of your zips together  (if that makes any sense).
  5. Please write "Screenshot Archives" in the subject heading of your email and include your NAME as you would like it to APPEAR on the list of contributors. I continually have to reply and ask this question again, and will not do so in the future.
  6. Please maintain my standards of exceptional and high quality screenshots..... i.e. no more shots of PoK, please. I will use and discard screenshots at my own discretion.
  7. Although it is not requested or required, donations are greatly appreciated. You will find a "Paypal Donate" button below. Many thanks in advance for your support and generosity.
