Welcome!

My name is Erin, I am the face behind InkyMouse.com.

My ventures in digital art began when I created a "screenshot" portrait of my Everquest character, Jennafae. I then began creating artwork for friends and guild members, and finally started accepting commissions for custom portraits. Although I don't play Everquest as much as I used to, I still use my experience as a screenshot artist as a spring board in all my current artistic endeavors.

I discovered the EQ fanart community of Norrath's Studios more than two years ago, where I am now an Administrator. N.S. has helped my interests and talents in digital art, graphical design, and web design to grow by leaps and bounds. I self-taught myself the HTML web design language and built 5 of my own websites, as well as contributing graphics to several others. I have also self-taught myself the basics of graphic design. I generally use Adobe Photoshop 7.0, along with my Wacom Intuoso2 graphical tablet.

I created "Inky Mouse" to be the new face of my art as a whole, which is branching out in new directions every day. With the closing of my former website "Portraits by Jennafae" and the opening of "Inky Mouse" comes the birth of a journey that will hopefully lead me towards design school sometime in the future. As soon as I work up the guts and the cash, I plan to take classes in web design and computer graphic design.

Things I have done that have nothing to do with art ...
I went to college for less than one semester as an English major, then dropped out to start school at a techincal college. I graduated from American Trans Air Aviation Training Academy two years later with an Associates degree in Aviation Maintenance Technology. I then said to hell with aviation and got married instead! I've had various retail jobs since then, and currently I work for Barnes & Noble where I'm moderately happy.

Many thanks to Xorrin Aarikis for continuing to host my corner of the web on the best server in EverQuest, Vazaelle.net. If you would like to continue reading below, I have started keeping a virtual scrapbook of things in art and life that influence my work.
