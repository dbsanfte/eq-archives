DKP Rules & Regulations


Earning DKP
DKP, or Dragon Kill Points, are earned by attending raids and helping to kill various mobs. Each Mob we kill has an assigned point value. If you are there when we kill the mob you will earn that amount of points. If it is the first time the guild kills that mob, you will earn double points. There are also a few instances where you may earn points even if the raid fails. Not everything we kill will have a point value.

Example: We head into Fear and kill Cazic Thule. He has a point value of 10. Each member there who helps kill him will receive 10 points. If it was the first time the guild killed him you will receive 20 points, double his worth.

Earning IDKP
IDKP, or Incentive DKP, is earned by such things as arriving at the called raid site within a set amount of time, helping other members with quests, scouting for mobs, and so on. IDKP will be a separate total from DKP and is used in tie-breakers. IDKP is temporary and resets to Zero on a monthly basis.

Example: A raid on ToV is called. If you get to ToV within 15 minutes of when it is called you receive an IDKP point.

Using DKP & IDKP
When we are on a raid and an item drops, That item will be assigned a point value. People will then be able to send a tell to say that they want it. Whoever has the most DKP of the people who sent a tell will be awarded the item and have the value of the item subtracted from their DKP total. If two or more people have the same amount of DKP, then their IDKP total will be used as a tie breaker. If both have the same IDKP total then they will do a random roll of numbers set by an officer, highest rolled number wins.

Example: We kill Vindi and he drops his BP. It is assigned a value of 5 points. Warrior1 has 15 DKP and warrior2 has 10DKP. Warrior1 Wins the BP.

Example: Vindi BP drops. Warrior1 and Warrior2 both have 15 DKP, but Warrior1 has 7 IDKP and Warrior2 has 10 IDKP. Since Warrior2 has more IDKP, he wins the BP.

Other DKP Rules and Answers to Possible Questions:

All current members will start at zero DKP. New members will start at zero DKP.

Recruits do not earn DKP until they become a full member. They may however be allowed to loot a rotting item if an officer permits it.

Honorary members, such as non-guild members who raid with us alot, can earn DKP.

You can go into negative DKP. Meaning if you have 10DKP and the item is worth 15, you can use your DKP and go to –5. You must then wait until you have positive DKP to try to win more items.

2-3 boxing: If you 2 or 3 box, all of your chars will receive equal DKP if they are considered to be valuable to, or needed, to make a raid successful and they meet current recruitment standards. Such as Truking/Leanora or Terrorus/Figgy/Anna. If you bring a lower level alt to a raid it will not receive DKP. IF you want your alt to get loot you must spend you own DKP and can only do so if no other members need the item.

If you loot an item, it will cost you its assigned DKP value. There are NO exceptions to this rule. It doesn’t matter if it’s going to rot or if no one else wants it. IF you don’t want to spend the DKP, then let it rot.

There will be NO transferring points between your characters, or to other guild members.

Some items will still be merit, such as ST keys.

Item values will be assigned as they drop. The same item may not always be worth the same amount of points each time, but we will try to be consistent.

Loot can be returned to the guild for DKP. The amount of DKP will be less then what you paid for it. This is to encourage you to give it back so it can be handed down and further enhance the guild as a whole.

There is no max DKP total you can have, and it will not be reset unless you leave the guild or are away for an extended period of time.

These are the rules. There will be NO exceptions. Period.
Direct all questions to an officer.
