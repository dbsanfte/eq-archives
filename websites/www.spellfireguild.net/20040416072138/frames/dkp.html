DKP RULES & REGULATIONS

 

Earning DKP

DKP, or Dragon Kill Points, are earned by attending raids and helping to kill various mobs.  Each Mob we kill has an assigned point value.  If you are there when we kill the mob you will earn that amount of points.  If it is the first time the guild kills that mob, you will earn double points.  There are also a few instances where you may earn points even if the raid fails.  Not everything we kill will have a point value.

(Example: We head into Fear and kill Cazic Thule.  He has a point value of 10.  Each member there who helps kill him will receive 10 points.  If it was the first time the guild killed him you will receive 20 points.)

 

Using DKP

When we are on a raid and an item drops, that item will be assigned a point value.  People will then be able to send a tell to say that they want it.  The person with the highest DKP total will be awarded the item. The DKP value of the item will then be subtracted from their DKP total.  If two or more people have the same amount of DKP, then the officers present will merit the item.

When making loot decisions, the officers will use the totals as they appear in the DKP tool at the time of the raid. Until the raid is entered into the tool, you do not have the DKP from that raid. Every effort will be made to enter raids into the DKP tool on a nightly basis. The only time the totals in the tool will not be used is when a player has looted something earlier in the day. The amount of DKP spent will be taken into consideration when making loot decisions later in the same day.

(Example: We kill Vindi and he drops his BP.  It is assigned a value of 5 points.  Warrior1 has 15 DKP and warrior2 has 10DKP.  Warrior1 Wins the BP.)

(Example: Vindi BP drops.  Warrior1 and Warrior2 both have 15 DKP, but Warrior1 can use as a bigger upgrade and/or is more commonly on during raid start times than Warrior2.  Warrior1 wins the BP.)

 

Losing DKP

Characters that have not attended a raid in 45 days are automatically marked "inactive" in the DKP tool. You may not spend DKP while your character is inactive unless yours is the only tell on the item. When a character has been inactive for 30 days, 10% of their DKP total is deducted. Each 30 day period that passes will result in an additional 10% reduction of their DKP total. This cycle will continue until the character becomes active or their DKP total reaches 0. To return to "active" status, the character must attend 10 DKP events within a 45 day period.

If you remove yourself from the guild for any reason, the following DKP penalty will be applied:
At the officers discretion, an immediate penalty may be applied to your DKP total.
After 3 days, 25% of your DKP total will be removed.
After 10 days, 50% of your DKP total will be removed.
After 20 days, your DKP total will be set to 0 and your status will be changed to "recruit".

DKP can and will be docked for skipping raids. If you cannot attend a raid, it is YOUR responsibility to inform an officer. If you are online during a raid and do not attend, you will be docked if you have not cleared it with an officer. The amount of DKP docked for skipping a raid will be 3x the normal amount of DKP for that mob. DKP will also be used for other forms of punishment when necessary to restore order.



Other DKP Rules and Answers to Possible Questions:

Recruits will not gain DKP until they are tagged.

New members will start at zero DKP.

Honorary members, such as non-guild members who raid with us alot, can earn DKP.

You can go into negative DKP.  Meaning if you have 10DKP and the item is worth 15, you can use your DKP and go to –5.  You must then wait until you have positive DKP to try to win more items.

2-3 boxing: If you 2 or 3 box, all of your chars will receive equal DKP if they are considered to be valuable to, or needed, to make a raid successful and they meet current recruitment standards.  If you bring a lower level alt to a raid it will not receive DKP.  Alternate characters may be considered 2nd main characters, but will only receive DKP if the character is played well.

If you loot an item, it will cost you its assigned DKP value.  There are NO exceptions to this rule.  It doesn’t matter if it’s going to rot or if no one else wants it.  If you don’t want to spend the DKP, then let it rot.

There will be NO transferring points between your characters, or to other guild members.  (Exception: If you retire a character, you may transfer the points to another NEW main if it's replacing the old one and has not earned DKP previously, but cannot go back and forth between the two.)

Some items will still be merit, such as ST keys, spells/runes, etc. Also, items that the guild leadership feels should be merited to the MA chain such as Bloodfrenzy and Blade of Carnage will be merited to the appropriate individual by the officers. BoC and certain other droppable items remain the property of the guild, and must be returned to the guild bank when the holder decides to move on for whatever reason. These items are identified as such at the time of drop, and include but are not limited to the Blade of Carnage and Helm of Rallos Zek.

Item values will be assigned as they drop.  The same item may not always be worth the same amount of points each time, but we will try to be consistent.  If an item changes in value, we will base that upon what items of similar or better stats we are currently capable of obtaining and the value of that item to the member's advancement.

Droppable loot that was obtained from the guild can be returned to the guild for DKP.  The amount of DKP refunded will be the current DKP price of the item at the time it is returned.  This is to encourage you to give it back so it can be handed down and further enhance the guild as a whole.

These are the rules. There will be NO exceptions. Period.
Direct all questions to an officer.
