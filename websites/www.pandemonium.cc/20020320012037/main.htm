Ach! Das Nerfensticht!
 
It's that time of month again! The bloating. The bitching. The headaches. The copious blood loss. That's right, it's "balancing" season at Verant HQ, as nerf bombs are being dropped all over Norrathghanistan. Apparently The Vision, written in the later part of the last decade by a secret organization of traveling circus monks, high-ranking carpet salesmen, former 1960s superhero Birdman, and this guy, did not foresee a time in EverQuest where high end PvE was actually more fun than receiving a Tabasco enema. Now Verant has gone apeshit, busting out the nerf batons to beat and kick our ethnic minority of a game as it lays crippled and bleeding on the side of the L.A freeway. Except for one VERY SPECIAL server that I'll mention later. For now, let's get started on just what's happened since our last update.
First on the nerf list was the Queen Bitch herself. Doubled in size and packing the latest in nerfonomic technology, the new Tunare was about as hard as beating Christopher Reeves in a game of soccer, only much less fun and rewarding.
Yes, it's a poor picture, but on the other hand, stfu!
She dropped on the same day she was nerfed, so much for that "revamp." Loot was decent, with daggers for Tzuten and Razzorin, an earring for Briseis, and a branch for Flertz.
Now, as we all have heard on practically every EQ forum and site in existence, North ToV, the loot-spewing gold mine of EverQuest, was ninja nerfed a few patches back at the same time as Eye of Zomm pulling was gimped. Verant snuck in in the middle of the night and without warning, dragon HP were buffed, pathing and aggro was changed, and one Mr. Vulak'Aerr was replaced by buggy mobs, which when first noticed, made for some very fun mindgames in guildchat.The Temple of Veeshan 2.0 was then set and ready to go, now possessing the same fun factor as waking up one morning to find a box full of clowns on your doorstep. Only the clowns have all died during shipping because someone forgot to poke in some air holes, and so now you're stuck with nothing but a smelly box and potential clown zombies.
Anyways we ganked our way through Ntov once more, and lo and behold, who do you think awaited us at the end of our raid?!
Not that guy! But what the hell did you expect, a Vyemm killshot? Another picture of the twin dragons laying dead next to each other? Whereas once Vulak was the reward for clearing an assload of dragons, our only reward this time was a boring ass buggy Ring of Fire where one screwup means you get to try again in a week. Needless to say, that's one zone that's falling down a few places on the to-do list, and I'd certainly like to donkey punch the grandma of whatever Verant copyboy put this idea in place.
The nerf parade continued as we ganked THO 2.0 a couple more times and also went to Akheva and Ssra temple to pay these guys a visit:
I won't even mention the IV loot. The Arch Lich dropped yet another godamn paladin suicide weapon for Sanaleb. I guess Verant decides the 20,000 fucking pal/sk only weapons from Ntov/Tunare/Rhags wasn't enough. He also dropped a shield for Arimedes. We also ganked the Exiled and Glyphed, but they're just a bunch of fucking snakes too, so I won't bother with another boring screenshot. Exiled ended up dropping Elphorn's Cleaver for uh...Elphor, and a Jade Skull Ring for Henrik. As for the Glyphed, I refuse to ever type the words "SK/PAL weapon" on an update again.
Now with all that covered, allow me to go on a small rant. Verant has taken the time to nerf Tunare, Ntov, the Eye of Zomm, AA points, the THO, hell, even the Fungal Grove shrooms were nerfed for the second time around and given lure DDs that also happen to bug your P.O.V. Why Verant does this is as clear as night and day. They're trying to extend the life of this game by slowing down the progression of players. But that's not the real issue. Even though VI has completely lost sight during this nerf rampage of the fact that this is a game and games are intended to be fun and not chores, that's not what pisses me off. It's this.

January 14, 2002 - Krowe
SimMolestor has arrived!
 
It's been little over a month since Verant has graced us with yet another expansion, and Christmas/Hanukah/Kwanzaa break through a wrench into things, so what better time to update? It wasn't until last week that I could actually buy Luclin for myself, but we'll take a look anyway, cuz it sure beats Tormax kill #541! This latest release strays off the path of the previous two expansions and takes place on a whole freakin' moon instead of a long lost continent. Now you're probably thinking, "The moon? That's crazy, there must be some rollercoaster edge-of-your-seat kind of thriller plot that led up to these lunar shenanigans!" Well calm down there, little Ebert, because sure enough, Verant has devised what has to be the most fascinating space storyline since Arthur C. Clarke's 2001. Don't take my word for it, see for yourself in the SoL manual the story of why through a long series of earth-shaking events, a powerful Combine empire reeling from an assassination was sent to, of all places, the moon!
 
Lcea stood up and raised her hand to the moon. "That is where we will rebuild."
 
RIVETING! As soon as I read the part about the Combine leader being poisoned, I knew I was in store for a trip to the MOON!
Of course, with every expansion the graphics have been upped, and in this case it's no different. Despite having a relatively new computer with 1Ghz and 128mb of RAM, more than enough to play Velious and Kunark smoothly, I was greeted with a pop-up warning me about my system specs upon trying to log in:
As disheartening as this was, and after a few quick calls to Grandma, I decided to turn on a few models anyway and they ran fine, although those models just happened to be "gnome male" and "gnome female" and we have an excess of neither in the guild. While the gnomish females followed suit with the majority of new models we've seen, in that they're horribly disfigured and sometimes cause those who see them to shed tears of blood, the new gnome males struck me as something different. Specifically, they struck me as EXTREME PERVERTS. Gone are the little gnomes of yesterday, with their mischievous little expressions and tiny little frames. Now we're left with 56 year old balding Danny DeVito clones that have somehow left the middle-school playgrounds they were prowling to take up careers in dragonslaying.
Hey little girl, you wanna try some CANDY?!?
Another thing that caught my attention was what Verant developers consider the female body. It doesn't really say alot about how often they meet girls when this latest virtual model is some sort of bizarre bodybuilder/pornostar hybrid that doesn't resemble any woman ever born on this earth, outside some Japanese hentai cartoon. See for yourself the massive torsos on these strange woman-man combinations.
I like to call them "wo-mans."
That aside, the guild managed to step out of Sebilis and put off working on Improved Natural Metabolism points for a little while to drop the Thought Horror Overfiend. I wasn't there, but uh, grats!
 
The THO ended up dropping Horns of Chaos for Flertz and a bunch of wacky new spells for everyone else. I have no idea what the hell Horns of Chaos are, or what they do, and no EQ site I've searched has any info on them, but once again, congratulations anyway!
 
Later one day, a startling revelation was made in guildchat. We didn't have any OUTRIDER'S TAMING WHIPS. Panic soon arose in the guild, and chaos broke out, resulting in several political coups that saw numerous Columbian druglords and at one point level 5 gnome enchanter Professorpickle take leadership of the guild. It would have been the end of us had we not decided right there and then to give Yelinak a long overdue visit. While we're a pretty heavy raiding guild, we've never really raided Skyshrine for any other reason than getting a Cobalt Scar key. Just look at the zone. The home of the dragons and 80% of the population is mudmen and Jell-o cubes. Let's not forget gargoyles which were probably put in because they kinda look lizards, if lizards mated with winged pigs. Either way, the zone was steamrolled over, and to make a long story short, Ol' Jellydick took a dirtnap. Don't ask me why they call him Jellydick, I honestly have no idea.
Thanks to Verant's tendency to have more patches than a railroad hobo's wardrobe, we actually managed to gank Yelinak before the server crashed, then gank him again when it came back up. 3 Yelinaks in seven days resulted in 3 Taming Whips, and peace was restored, thanks to this powerful whip worthy of the king of dragons. Grats to Kuin on one of these beauties, the other two were destroyed in a tearful parting ceremony. Once again, the Outrider's Taming Whip, ladies and gentlemen. A true work of art. Yelinak also dropped some nice pairs of GoDS for Xantille, Rummol, and Mythd, a Dragon's Tear Earring for Soleenia and Touch, a Mask of the Dragonslayer for Quiknuss, a Dragon's Eye Sage Ring for Nighmarez and Allizan, a Dragon's Blood Earring for Shelrabbi, a Celestial Essence Robe for Nighmarez, and Sleeper's Keys for Glodson, Toth, and Grousha. Grats all, and may many more whips be in our future!
 
And while I've been on vacation until last week, the guild also managed to make Ntov a ghost town and keep it that way. More than once Vulak stood alone ready to be pummeled when an emergency patch was announced. Nonetheless the guild managed to drop him four more times, resulting in Camii's Bracers for Cydd, Talsar, and Sanaleb, Sirran's Boots for Tzeenth, Tolan's Longswords for Clairdelune and Keamina, Sal'Varae's Robe for Mythd, Crystal Rings for Chemosh and False, and Feeliux Cords for Smashyou, Croazil, and Xantille.
 
And finally, congratulations to Razzorin on completing his 10th ring war quest and getting a long overdue Dain ring. Grats Razz!
I've said it before and I've said it again, the 10th ring war is the most fun you'll have in a PvE event. The whole zone goes into a frenzy wondering what happened to all the kodiaks and giants, and the newbies come out to beg for items they believe to be worth billions and billions of plat. We were even graced by the presence of Darkoan, who once gained our Prison Bitch of the Week award for ninja looting a Ragefire heart. All those golem wands on our tanks and trying to train Narandi away was for naught, what a shame ;( Maybe he'll learn to train Narandi in the right direction next time. Special thanks to Xantille, who valiantly fought in the war..sorta.
