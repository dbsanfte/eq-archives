DB wrote:

>"Drums... I really dont thing drums look like a stick and im 100% sure you dont strum them... unless ive been wrong all these years :-)"<

I was aware of it. But since I'm pretty much starting fresh here, give me things that you know I know, just so I don't forget to put them on the list.

NOR_Bard posted, but someone else wrote:

>"In general, melee damage triples from 50 to 60 and spell damage increases 50%. Bard "spell" damage is really a form of melee damage, since the damage is slow yet takes no mana. Yet for some reason it follows a lesser curve than regular spell damage. I have no idea why."<

Well, because you are comparing 1 song to all of the melee damage done by a rogue (why not pick a warrior, or a caster?). Now, add the other things that the bard does into the equation, and let's talk further. Look at damage by twisting two songs plus the damage done by increasing the attack speed of that same rogue. Or look at the increased damage of that rogue when bard hasted, and the increased damage output of the wizard under the mana song AND whatever third song you want to sing…

I'll agree that perhaps we should look at the damage dealing ability of the bard. But I'm not convinced that it's too low by this argument, as it fails to include all the other things that a bard does. It's kind of like saying that an enchanter doesn't ramp up his damage as fast as a rogue after 50th level. Bards are not just about damage dealing.

>"Most bard songs don't get upgrades. They're designed to get better with level. If you take our Chant series as an example (a targeted DoT), from level 40 to 50, the song increases from 39 damage for 3 ticks to 48 damage for 3 ticks (+23%). From levels 50 to 60 it'll go from 48x3 to 57x3 (+19%). You designed the level 50+ spells to represent a larger increase in power than just 10 levels. "<

Far better argument. Self contained and useful. Thanks.

>"- Tweak the formulae used for song effects in the 50-60 range, to accurately reflect the large increase in damage/effectiveness other classes get in the 50-60 range."<

This is the best balance method, I think. It keeps the balance within the class itself. Then we can make items that tweak that balance slightly later. The idea that a class would need to have an item just to be balanced doesn't sound right to me.

>"The level 35 song (Denon's Dissension) is AE pulse based and drains 24 mana/pulse (i.e. you lose mana every time I sing the song). If I chain-sing this song (restart it every 3 seconds), I need 4x longer to drain as much mana as a like-level enchanter. It works out to about the same amount of time as the enchanter if you factor in med time, but you have to understand that an enchanter can haste and clarity his party before the fight, and mez extra mobs while he's draining mana. If I chain-sing this song, I can do *nothing* else except melee to contribute to the fight. Also, from level 37 to 57 this song only increases 9% in the amount of mana drained. The enchanter mana drain goes from 229 to 352 in the same level range (+54%). The way this song scales with level needs to be improved *badly*."<

Well, 'badly' might be an overstatement. But I can see your argument.

>"The level 57 song (Cassindra's Insipid Ditty) is single target and drains a laughable 1.5 mana/sec at level 57. The drain rate is so low that most bards mistakenly thought the song didn't drain mana at all. It is 21x slower than enchanter mana drain. I would need 4 minutes to accomplish what the enchanter can do every 11 seconds. If you factor in meditate time, in the 4 minutes takes me to drain 350 mana with this song, an enchanter can drain 2100 mana, sit down, and med back all the mana he used. If we haven't killed the mob by then. Consider removing or lowering the resist check as well so we can use it on level 63 mobs. Ditty has a wis/int debuff component so it will not stack with itself, and only a single bard at a raid can be using it on a target."<

Seems like it need looking into. But keep in mind that the debuff component that you casually mentioned is probably considered a useful thing, and has to be taken under consideration when we look at this song.

>"Solution: Remove the "soft cap" if it exists. Improve the player resist rate against these high-end targets with maximum resist, so that there's a significant benefit to getting up near 255. Double-check to make sure there's no roll-over bug when resists exceed 255 (or whatever the max is in your code)."<

I know nothing! I'll look into this.

>"Comparing Rondo (57) to our level 54 song is a joke. If you're singing Rondo, a mob hitting your party on average 1.5 times every 3 sec will take 4 dam/sec from the damage shield. For the damage shield to offset the 23% loss in haste, your party must be doing less than 12 dam/sec melee damage for Rondo to be better than Celerity2…

…

Please either fix hastes/slows so an instrument increases them, or change these songs to have double their current haste/slow (optionally making them instrument-only)."<

This whole part of your discussion got completely confusing. You are trying to compare the haste component on a haste only song with a song that does several things (not just haste and damage shield). My first thought would be that of course the haste will be less when you get a ton of other bonuses with the song.

Frankly, I am very dubious of any comparison of things that are rather closer to apples than oranges. But I'll add the request to the list, and we'll hash it out internally a bit.

And, once again, comparing song with the spells for classes is a tough thing to do, and may not always be a fair comparison.

By the way, none of your links are complete…

>"- The BP is a self-only 100 point heal with a 12 second cast time. In 12 seconds, a level 50 bard using a Plane of Hate lute can heal 80 hp for each party member. I don't even know what the point is making the BP's heal self-only.

- The vambraces have an effect of Invigorate - a targeted stamina replenishment. Our level 3 song replenishes stamina for the entire party. Our level 34 song does it while healing and giving mana."<

Ok, I can see it. The logic might have been that this is a 'free spell slot' for bards, but since you will have that song memorized to help your party, the clickable effects are not really useful.

>"- The boots summon 1 water at a time with a 5 second cast time. PLEASE change this to summon 10 or 20 at a time so it's at least useful. As it is, I just buy my water in bulk instead of taking 2 minutes to summon a stack of water that'll all disappear every time I go linkdead.
- The greaves summon 1 food at a time. See boots above."<

Welcome to the world of magicians…

Here I find it very easy to argue that not all items are supposed to be super useful. These two can save you a trip to town and allow you to put it off until a more convenient time. It's certainly not the most awesome effect in game, but that's not really a good argument.

>"For the other classes that get it, Kunark armor effects give them something they didn't have before."<

Here is where you get yourself in trouble with me. I do not like the idea that classes now have the ability to do things they were not intended to do by the use of items. I am strongly against such items, and their existence is certainly not going to convince me to ask that more such things go into the game.

>" I suspect the same thing is happening with the Velious bard armor effects. Currently the Melodic BP right-clicks Song of Travel. Song of Travel has 12 second duration and an invis component so it drops every time you right-click to re-activate it. Since you have to re-activate it every 12 seconds, it's dropping every 12 seconds. It's pretty silly to put this on an item as an effect (actually, it's pretty silly to make right-click effects out of any song that bards usually leave playing, since you have to refresh it every 12 seconds)."<

Now that's an idea (even if you don't actually state it). Make the benefit of these items that the duration is a lot longer, so that these songs don't need to be resung once started. I don't know how that would work balance-wise, or if it's possible in the game. But I'm much more in favor of that than giving bards the powers of other classes through items.

>"This nerf affects us even when we aren't fearing, and you didn't compensate us for it in any way."<

To be fair, you should know that this is a red flag for me. Compensation for a needed change (even if it was a nerf) is never something I will support. Show me how this change unbalanced the class or something, but don't tell me that you deserve more power because something was taken away… if it was made weaker, it was because it needed to be weaker. There is no compensation.

>"Chain, Largo's Absonant Binding (51), and Assonant Strane (L54 AE movement slowdown) are all random in duration. They could last the full 12 seconds, they could wear off immediately. No other movent slowdown spells in the game have a random duration, even though the spell versions last 10-50 times longer than Chain. It can't be because Chain has a slow component attached because no other slow spells in the game have a random duration either. I hear all the movement slowdowns except the level 1 snare used to be random duration, and were gradually changed to have a fixed guaranteed duration - except the bard ones."<

A logical enough argument. And you weakened it with this last line:

>"In light of the above one-sided nerf, perhaps you could give these songs a guaranteed 12 second duration?"<

But it does not invalidate your point.

>"There's also an infrequent bug that affects all the movement debuff spells. If you try to chain/snare/darkness a mob that's already begun fleeing, sometimes the spell/song will land but will have no effect (if this happens, repeated attempts to chain/snare/darkness it will have no effect). Due to the random duration of Chain, bards are the ones who get bit by this one the most."<

Never heard of it. I'll list it and see if I can get someone to look into it.

"Generally what I found is that power for all classes rises proportional to level squared, except for bards. For bards power rises proportional to level."

And he may have been right. But also, keep in mind that most bard songs remain useful for the life of the bard. This is completely unique to the class, and requires a completely different scheme of increasing power.

>"At high levels, that margin simply doesn't exist. The 10% haste on the level 60 song doesn't come anywhere close to giving us the 45% haste advantage that's needed to keep us in line with the other melee classes."<

One of us is making a logical error here... Bardic hastes are not, primarily, designed to make the bard into damage dealer comparable to a warrior. That's not the intent. The intent is that the bards can improve the melee ability of his entire group by that amount. Keep in mind, bards were designed from the ground up to be a party based class.

>"You need to make sure the people designing this stuff understand that bards are a plate class."<

Just because a bard CAN wear plate, that does not mean that all of the armor pieces designed for bards MUST have plate armor class. That's a false assumption. I agree that the designers need to keep the idea in mind, but don't go overboard.

>"OTOH, if you just don't want bards picking any high level locks, leave the cap as is, but give us the skill at about level 10. That way it's at least usable by low level bards in Befallen and Najena. I'm not sure why you'd want to do this though, seeing as rogues already have several ability monopolies (backstab, pick pocket, invis+invis to undead, poisons)."<

*sigh*

Well, of course rogues have a skill monopoly on basckstab, pick pockets… this is another one of my pet peeves. I LIKE the idea that classes remain unique. Telling me that bards somehow deserve a skill just because rogues have it is silly.

I'll add the pick lock cap to my list…

>"Song of Shields (49) has no message. It went from bad (spamming us with a buff message for each group member), to worse (no message at all). Please put in a single line message (flagged bard song so others can filter it) just like all our other songs."<

Seems like this needs fixing.

>"The "song ends" "song starts" "song ends" bug is still around. I understand you're aware of it and trying to track it down. I can verify that it's a server-side problem, not a client-side one. If it happens to a damage shield song, I do not get the benefit of the damage shield.
I also suspect the bug can also happen to mobs, who then unwittingly benefit. Several bards (including me) have noticed that sometimes we'll successfully re-charm a mob, get the "You don't have a pet to command" message, and 12 seconds later get the "Your charm has worn off" message. It sure sounds like the same bug only happening with a debuff on the mob."<

I can't believe this is still around. I'll add it.

>"Dirge (level 43 song). Mana cost is 800. Bards don't benefit from any form of mana regen, so 800 mana takes 40 minutes to regenerate sitting, 80 minutes to regenerate standing. The song does about 311-340 points AE damage to a maximum of 4 targets. Could you please reappraise the mana cost on this song? We can't meditate so if you factor in sitting mana regen time, this song does best-case (4 targets) 0.6 dam/sec."<

But you also don't suffer any ill effects for being low on mana, with the exception of the use of this song. So I don't see this as a big problem. All it does is determine how many times you can use the Dirge in any given time period. Unlike other casters, it does nothing to prevent you from using all of your other songs.

>"Aria of Eagles (31, levitate) and Song of Travel (51) don't give any message when they're about to expire. You removed the message in response to complaints about the spam (we were constantly getting the message even with the song playing). The correct fix is to increase the duration of these songs by one tick so the message isn't generated for at least one tick, by which time the song's next pulse will refresh it. You had this correct fix in place with Aria. Then you removed the message entirely from both songs when you "fixed" Song of Travel."<

Makes sense.

*Patch Day*
The primary focus of today's patch is to implement changes noted in the new Producer's Letter which can be found at
http://everquest.station.sony.com/news/letter.shtml

It is HIGHLY RECOMMENDED that you read the Producer's Letter before playing EverQuest. It contains some unprecedented and exciting enhancements to gameplay.

In addition to these changes, the following changes have been implemented: 
*Bug Fixes*
- Fixed a bug that caused "root" and "snare" type spells to function incorrectly when stacked. They should now stack properly.
- Fixed a bug that negated the "instrument modifier" for bards using Selo's, causing them to run slower than they otherwise  would.
- Fixed a bug that allowed two or more spells that grant levitation to stack. Levitation spells should not stack.
- The "Track Corpse" spell now works.
- Tracked down and eliminated several bugs that were causing a very small percentage of people to crash when zoning or upon entering the game.
- Fixed a problem with some Ulthork armor that caused it to disappear on death if the wearer was of extremely low level.
- Tserrina's Whip has been fixed. If you received one prior to today's patch, you will need to trade out your whip with a GM in order to get one that works correctly.
- Last week's patch attempted to fix Celestial Elixir, Celestial Healing, and Torpor, so that they would stack another similar spell, unintentionally causing it to stop stacking with standard regeneration spells. This has been fixed. The downside to this fix is that these spells will now overwrite the "Call of Bones" series. As the three former spells are cast to save a character's life, this solution appears to be the most beneficial for all of those involved.
- The stronger /lootnodrop options will no longer prompt you for confirmation when looting your own corpse. It is highly suggested that everyone reset their option to the strongest security available: /lootnodrop ALWAYS (always ask for confirmation).
- Added some code to address an issue reported by a few players, that being that an NPC named "Ragefire" would die and not leave a corpse. Though we were unable to reproduce this problem in testing, the code we added should function as a catch-all for this problem. Please be sure to let us know how this goes.

*Changes/Enhancements*
- While not mentioned in the Producer's Letter, the Zone Experience Modifier has also been increased in Guk (guktop), by roughly 12%.
- "Lay on Hands" has been enhanced again to heal more hitpoints.
- Overlooked in the last patch message, new spells were made available to Rangers and Paladins in the upper levels.
- "Voice of the Berserker", a beneficial spell, no longer drains mana over the duration of the spell.
- The maximum duration of "Strength of Nature" has been increased.
- A new spell for magicians is now available in game.
- The "Shield of the Eighth" spell will now last a number of minutes equal to your level.
- You can now use the arrow and enter keys to select characters in the character select screen. In addition, the last character you chose will be automatically hilighted when entering that screen. This value is stored in the EQCLIENT.INI file as "LastCharSel=0-7"
- The ranger's "Trueshot" discipline has had its damage increased after analyzing data from its fix last week. Prior to the last patch, all bows, while under the discipline, were hitting as if they were 45dmg bows regardless of the delay. Last week's patch fixed it so that damage was based on the damage of the bow. This weeks patch increases damage bonuses in relation to delay (longer delays yield better damage bonuses).
- You can now reverse the left and right channels on your speakers using the /reversesound command. This should be very useful for those who have had to place their speakers backwards due to cable lengths.
- Made some improvements to Paw, Mistmoore, and The Hole, to address some concerns brought forth by the players. Both zones had some problems that would cause them to be exceedingly difficult in some cases.

- The EverQuest Team
