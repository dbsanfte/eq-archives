Fallen Lights

Fireflies are dancing to their death, tumble to their graves.

Winged girls are singing like bug-eyed, dark little things.

A stomping hit the ground, tens of thousands of leaves quaked nearby. Shuddering, cold, blue-eyed she danced more, weaving about the flame like some nerotic fool. Lost in dreams. Sleep comes quickly. Sometimes never at all.

Men chased her. Round and round she goes.

Bells rang in the city, falling, distant cries for help.

The king is dead.

Dead is the king.

Oh woe is you and me.

Fallen lights burn low on the grass, suffocating in the wet.

Bells ring long in the city, distant from the field. Trees shook, a girl danced, chased by men in her dreams.

Long and wide they sang.

The king is dead.

Light that keeps the shadow at bay fell hard that night, fallen into the grass to drown.

The crystal shattered, the queen grasped hard. The witches coveted and the man proclaimed he king of all.

The goddess cried, the races snapped. War betwix the three.

The witches watched, the singer stopped and all begain to fear.

She lifted her head, hidden for near a dozen years. Body changed to fit the race, her eyes of gold shined strong.

The goddess wept, she drew a blade.

The bells rang hard, "he died" they told.

She cried.
