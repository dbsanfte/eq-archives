quiet whispers

Waiting. Hands placed quietly folded under a tired chin, bleary, greenish tinted eyes watching the flickering light that makes up a picture on the tv.

Hiding. Sneaking quietly up the stairs to the door that hardly ever stays open, listening as a mother talks on the phone to a great aunt. Searching and searching to keep her from leaving this forsaken island of overpriced objects of desire, overtaxed people with atitudes harsh enough to make anyone cry in fear. Waiting to hear if her cousin can help, hoping that she cannot. She doesn't want to be here any more, and no one will let her go without a fight. It always has to be a fight.

The room is her sanctuary. There is a computer there with games on it. Books piled on shelves and on the floor where they had fallen down to, never picked up til they are read.

Less then a week left til her first surgery ever in her twenty-one years on this planet. Never been knocked out before, trying to find courage to not be afraid, that the pain won't start til after the sleep wears off.

All she can do now is wait.
