Scalpel was not in a good mood. He was glad to get out of the tiny tent, but the pungent smell of incense still clung to his clothes and had made his eyes tear. He was not overly fond of the gypsies, but still, they often had useful, if not cryptic information to be had...for a price.

He strode out into the sun with a frown firmly affixed to his thin face, but was stopped sunddenly by a small tug on his hand. The frown soften, as he looked down to the face of his son; The reason for his visit to the gypsy. Max was a beautiful child with a tossle of straberry-blond locks, but was somewhat pale of complexion. "Thin." Thought Scalpel. "But then again, he comes by it honestly." Scalpel then glanced to his son's eyes. Ah, those eyes; soft blue-green orbs, alwyas darting and always taking in. Scalpel just wished Max would tell him what it was that he saw and what it was he was looking at.

For 9 years his son had been thus: Growing slowly, seldomly speaking, and when he did speak, it was usually only one of a handful of words.

"Why did you bring him to me? He's obviously retarded. Better fit for a work camp than wasting time on him." The leech in the city had told Scalpel. That DID bring a smile to Scalpels' face - He wondered if the Lugweilers' Dismal Itch spell had finally worn off the quack by now. How he hated doctors!

He had consulted Gort and Farreach too. They had been more hopeful but still baffled. "Hey der." Gort said. "Dis child ain't no dummy, I can tell you dat. But why he don't speak?...I can't say."

The gypsy had seen the same thing. "No, he's not retarded - More like asleep. Deep waters flow strong beneath the clam surface. Perhaps he just needs the right pebble tossed in to break the surface." The gypsy went to a shelf and picked up a trinket. "Here," She said. " I see you gonna take the boy fishing after this. For only 5 more platinum I throw in this charm, helps you catch the really big one's, eh?" Scalpel still wondered why he had bought the foolish charm - A small green dragon with opal eyes. "Oh well," He thought. "Maybe Alrad (his wife) would like it."

So they walked, he and his son, enjoying the bright day and the sounds of spring all around them. "I'll stop by Mcdfuffys'," He thought. "Maybe he'll go with us."

"Want to see Mcduffy, Max?"

His son looked up at him and fairly beamed. Max liked the old halfling. He was patient with him, and didn't try to get him to talk. Subsequently, he usually said more to Mcduffy than he did most people.

They soon arrived at Mcduffys' but found the halfling not at home. "Gone to get pipeweed and porkrinds," said the note on the door. Scalpel laughed. The old halfling was, of course, now so fabulously wealthy he could have had servants do his shopping for him, but he knew that what he really enjoyed doing was going into town to dicker with the merchants.

Scalpel got out the small dragon charm and thought to leave it behind, as a joke, for Mcduffy. As he got ready to place the dragon by his doorstep, something odd happend. The dragon turned its head! Suddenly the small figurine felt warm and heavy. Scalpel gasped as waves of emerald light came corruscating out of the charm, enveloping he and his son. His vision blurred as they were drawn into a shimmering green vortex and taken to a differnt plane.

When he gathered his senses, Scalpel took in the new surroundings. Grassland, as far as they eye could see. He looked to his son, who was used to seeing his father use magic, and found the child looking back, calm and secure. "This is not my doing son, but hang on - we'll get out of here soon."

He had just let go of his son's hand to get his book out of his backpack, when he felt a sharp tug on his robes. "It's here." Said his son's small voice.

Scalpel looked to his son, and then around. He din't see anything. "What son? What's h-.....?"

"Oooof!" The air exploded out of Scalpel's lungs as he was hit hard in the back. At the same time he heard a loud "Whoosh" above his head, and he was knocked to the ground.

"What the...?" Scalpel felt something warm and wet running down his back, soaking into his robe. He looked at his son, whose face was filled with terror."Daddy!" He screamed.

"Whoosh!" Scalpel was rocked back on his heels as he was struck in the forehead. This time though, he caught a glimpse of the foe, and it nearly buckled over - It was a dragon; Huge, fast and terribly strong. He knew then that it was just toying with him. It could have killed them both in its first attack.

Scalpel threw himself into his spell, using all his training to keep concentrating despite the pain of his wounds. "Liberatas est agronamus magnus me aida!" He shouted.

The earth at his feet began to bulge, and a rent formed. Liquid rock began to bubble out of the crack and form into a huge muscled shape. Scalpel had little hope that this elemental could defeat the dragon, but he had other ideas.

"Yessss Massster" It slowly said.

"Protect the boy" Scalpel ordered, and he turned to meet his foe.

"Whoosh!" Scalpel was again knocked back as a talon drove into his chest and broke a rib. He looked down to see bright red foam coming out of his chest. He tried to talk, but found that little air would come to his lips. He fell to his knees. He knew then and there that he was done for.

Huge wings beat the earth, as the dragon settled in. Scalpel turned and tried to rise to his feet to meet the monster, but found that his legs would not obey. "Run son." was all that he could manage to say. He heard and felt the ponderous steps of the beheamoth as they shook the earth coming toward him. He was on the very verge of blackness.

Scalpel reached to his neck and pulled out the small crystal rod, and with his last ounce of strength, he broke it into. " My freinds, come quickly...help save...my child."

He looked to his son, but saw determination, not fear in his face. What was he saying? Something he had heard Mcduffy say in some spell at one time he thought. Scalpel then felt the talon drive into his back and begin lifting him into the air. The pain was incredible!

All at once, he stopped rising and slid off the talon. He looked up to see the dragon, frozen, inches from his face. With that, darkness overtook him and he knew no more.
