Balecross's Story
Sitting here, in this strange land, Balecross studied the waters of a great lake on the shores of
which he stood. As he watched the ebb and flow of the waters he remembered the days of his
youth, so long ago. Balecross had traveled long and far in his journeys. Once a simple Erudite
student of the arts, he was anxious and impatient for adventure, questing and glory, much to the
chagrin of his teachers.

As a young student, Balecross often longed for far off places and the sight of distant lands. Erudin
had been his home, and to his young mind his prison. Ships would doc at the Erudin port, and
from their passengers would come tales of Humans and Barbarians. Frightening adventures
would be spoken of at the local pubs, stories lush with strange creatures and stranger lands,
stories of powerful magic and skilled warriors.

As time passed, Balecross learned the deep arts of magic, focusing his studies in the teachings
of the Gate Callers. As but a first year student, he helped to fight back the onslaught of barbarous
Kybolds - a primitive and vicious race of creatures that often plagued the dark recesses of the
Toxxulia forests of his homeland. He gained a great deal of experience during this time of strife,
as well as the pain of lose as he watched comrades ripped apart in the jaws of this cruel enemy.

Balecross soon attained his fifth level and stood amongst the ranks of junior mages. A well
respected student at the academy, his masters worried about his impulsive tendencies and rush
to leave his home land. To cool his adventurous spirit, his master Terran Halfmoon, challenged
Balecross with a quest. Terran said to Balecross, do you know of a beast across the great bridge
in the Toxxulia forest, a beast that calls for the death of all Erudite. Balecross knew of this
creature, he had always thought it a myth, a story to keep children in their beds at night. He said, I
have heard of the story, it is but a trite myth a bed time story for children. No said Terran, it is real.
It is a Troll, a cleric to the evil god Cazic-Thule, Lord of Fear. Find it, and dispatch if from our
lands.

Balecross, sensing the risk and challenge of the quest, set off with high hopes of glory. He
traveled through the Toxxulia forest and to the bridge, looking for the evil troll. Upon the span he
waited, not truly sure that this troll was real, let alone would come to the bridge. Before long a deep
voice shouted out "Beware all Erudites, for I will slay you all!" At last, thought Balecross, not much
longer will this beast threaten our people.

Confidently, Balecross stroll across the bridge, towards the sound of the voice. Just as he crossed
the bridge, he saw a figure through the mist. It was heading towards him, slowing as if it had
nothing to fear. As the figure became visible, Balecross felt the hairs on his neck raise, and a
flicker of fear shoot through his body. This was a great beast, it must have been eight feet tall, and
huge. It was green with glowing eyes, almost purple in color. An incredibly ugly beast, Balecross
noticed its foul stench in the air. What have I done, thought Balecross. Can such a creature exist?
Garnering his courage, Balecross spoke in the strongest voice he could muster "Stop! foul beast.
Dare you threaten the lands of the Erudite!"

The beast paused for a moment, and then a crackling sound came from it. Thinking back on it,
Balecross now knew it was laughter. "Weakling Erudite, is this the best your masters can send
against me." Balecross new that he would need all his power to slay this beast, and began to
recant a spell to call forth the magic of an Elemental to assist him. Before the words could form,
the world went dark. A screaming fear enveloped Balecross, a fear the likes of which he had never
known. Through the haze of pain and fear, Balecross heard the Troll utter strange words, Magic
words. How could such a beast know the ways of Magic, Balecross thought. Just then, a spike of
pain shot through his body and Balecross recognized his danger. This was no simple beast this
troll, this was a creature of evil magic.

Then, Balecross felt the fear lift, and the pain recede. He looked up in wonder, why am I not dead
he thought to himself. Looking across the bridge, Balecross saw his friend and rival Asheron, a
student of the Wizard arts, casting a spell of ice onto the beast. It reared up and screamed as the
ice shredded its green skin. More the pleasure the beast said, in a voice as cruel and cold as the
ice which cut into it. It began to recant magic words, praying to its evil god for power, and Asheron
was hit by a blast of divine power. The blast knocked him from his feet and across the bridge,
Balecross could see the pain streak across his friends face.

Pulling his wits together, he quickly finished his incantation and called forth an Elementalkin from
the Earth. The great magic creature grumbled to life, an incarnation of the Earth and Stone from
which it was created. Balecross sent the beast to attack the Troll. The Troll concentrating on
calling forth a blast to finish off Asheron, did not see the Elementalkin coming toward it. Just as
the troll started speaking the words to call forth his magic, the Elementalkin hit him with a crushing
blow to the head. The Troll was dazed, and lost its concentration. It stood up to attack its new
enemy, rage and pain screaming from his wild eyes. Shaking off the pain, Asheron called out for
the Magic, and the Troll once again reeled from a storm of cutting ice. Balecross, with his last bit
of magic strength, called forth fire, which engulf the troll, searing its skin and weakening it further.

At last the troll knew fear, and attempted to flee. A last volley of ice stuck the fleeing beast from
Asheron, just when the Elementalkin called forth the Earth magic to shutter the earth under the
tolls feet, knocking him to his knees. Now almost completely exhausted, Balecross managed to
call forth one last burst of flame, just enough to sear the last life from the lungs of the troll.

Balecross, exhausted and hurt crawled over to Asheron, who was also wounded and leaned on his
friends shoulder. Ah my friend, forgive me all the many barbs and taunts, you have just saved my
life. Asheron said, it is the least I can do, such a beast cannot be allowed to haunt our forests.
Walking over to the beast, Balecross, removed its foul head with a dagger he had and put it in a
sack. Together, Balecross and Asheron began to walk slowing towards home.

After returning, he went to see his Master Terran. Master said Balecross, I have failed. How can
that be said Terran, I see you have a bag - does it not contain the head of the foul troll. Balecross
said, yes master, but it was little more then luck that I survived. I would have died, but not for the
aid of a friend. An apprentice Wizard by the name of Asheron, risked his own life to save me. In
my pride and overconfidence, I thought to dispatch this create alone, and would have died for it.
Terran looked at Balecross with pride, you have learned a great lesson my apprentice, he said.
You have learned the value of companionship. In this world, you will need your friends and
companions to survive as you brave new lands and face new adventures. Remember this lesson
well, it will serve you in the future.

As Balecross sat on the shores of the Lake of Ill Omen, he remembered well this first lesson. A
mage of the 30th level, now powerful in his art, he thought of that first quest and the mistake he
had made. I will need companions now he thought, for this is a strange land, and the dangers that
lay ahead will be far greater then those he had faced in the past. With that he stood up and
strolled over to join his group of adventures, ready to explore, learn and gain rich reward.

-Balecross

What is EverQuest?
An RPG designed from the ground up to be a massively on-line game. The world of EverQuest was created to
support over 1,000 players simultaneously per server. On average, you will find as many as 2000 players on any
given server during peek hours. EverQuest is truly a community game, these are real people you are battling with,
or at times against - not just technology and software.

Choose from 13 races and 14 classes, including dwarves, ogres, trolls, rangers, wizards, rogues and the new
iksar. EverQuest has a broad range of character classes and races to choose from. You can play an evil troll,
stomping your enemies into submission, or a wise Erudite mage always seeking knowledge and magical power.
Perhaps the call of a graceful High elf suites your personality, or the mischievous halfling thief. The possibilities are
as wide as your imagination will allow.

Over 40 unique skills and innate abilities. From bread making to weapon making, each class and race has its own
skills and abilities. Learn to bind wounds to keep you alive in battle, or forage food from the landscape around
you.

Thousands of items to earn and collect, both magical and mundane, including artifacts of great power. The hunt for
power is always on in the EverQuest world. That special armor that increases your strength, or magical staff which
increases a mages power. Thousands of items, great and small can be found and put to use in the world of
EverQuest. These are bought and sold, given the EverQuest world and economy of its own.

Four enormous continents to explore, as well as other dimensions and planes. EverQuest is also ever growing and
changing. As an online world, the developers are constantly changes the game, adding items and spells, creating
new locations and events. Each time you log in, EverQuest offers you new things to explorer and experiences to
discover.

Adventuring alone is supported, and forming parties to complete quests encouraged. Each class and race has
strengths and weaknesses, and the stronger groups will be those who balance their parties and work as a team.
This is a community game, groups that learn to use and leverage the strengths of others will do well in this world.
Those wishing to go it alone, can do so - but will have a much harder time of it then their companions.

Hundreds of spells to learn, with five separate magic-using classes. Also, weapons can be enchanted to cast spells
themselves. A spell caster in the world of EverQuest is an adventure unto itself. Hundreds of spells, to be found,
bought or researched - spells of both minor and incredible power. A caster in the world of EverQuest can bring
great power to bear in support of a group of fighters.

Player Killing (PKilling) is controlled such that those who wish to engage in it may do so, however those who do
not are completely immune. Tired of the open warfare of other games. EverQuest lets you choose to fight, or not
to fight other players.

True 3D environment, with multiple camera views. Switch from exploring in first person to any number of top
down views to facilitate tactical combat. Truly an amazing visual world. Creatures and characters come to life,
making EverQuest incredibly immersive. Once you start, it is hard to stop.Beautiful SVGA (640x480 to
1024x768) 16 bit color scenery, with adventure zones including indoor dungeons, arctic tundra, vast deserts, and
even completely underwater environments. Truly polygonal characters, both players and NPC's - no sprites here.
Optimized for the latest graphic accelerator technology with Direct3D support.
