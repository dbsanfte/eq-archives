The Bard's stock and trade is his ability to
play songs. His songs are actually a spell of
sorts, and their magic effects last as long as
the Bard continues to sing and play his
instrument. They have some passing
knowledge of the ways of war, and can defend
themselves as needed. However, their focus
is always on their skill and quality of their art.
A Bard's song can effect all members of a
party, making a Bard a very popular addition
to many groups.
Important Attributes
Dexterity for quick movement, Wisdom for the
magic of your song and Charisma for influence.
Specific Skills
Instruments: Percussion (lvl 5), Stringed (lvl 8),
Wind (lvl 14), Bras (lvl 11)
Skills: Dodge (lvl 10), Forage (lvl 12), Sneak
(lvl 17), Safe Fall (lvl 24), Hide (lvl 25), Disarm
Traps (lvl 30), Track (lvl 35), Pick Locks (lvl 40),
Track (lvl 35)
Character Guides
Bard Character Guides
Clerics are holy men and women, charged by
faith and devotion to enter battle and spread
the word of their god. A Cleric feels that the
path to righteousness comes in showing
others that way through actions and deeds. A
Cleric is a much requested member of any
party. Their ability to heal makes then
powerful allies, and their offensive spells can
add a great deal of punch to a fight.
Important Attributes
Wisdom for Mana and Spells, Strength for battle
and face to face combat and Stamina to stand
toe to toe with the enemy in the heat of battle.
Specific Skills
Magic related: Abjure, Alter, Channel (lvl 4),
Conjure, Divination, Evoke, Meditate (lvl 8),
Specialization (lvl 30)
Skills: Dodge (lvl 15)

Cleric Character Guides
Druids are protectors of life and herbalists of
no small regard. A Druid is completely at
home in the outdoors and is a friends to all
animals. Because of this, no animal will
attack him unless first attacked by the druid.
They are spell casters whose focus is all
things natural, allowing them to call on these
natural forces for aid. The share many of the
woodland skills of their brothers the Rangers.
Druids excel at traveling, they are the best
class for those that want to explore wide and
far, and do so quickly.
Important Attributes
Stamina for building hit points and Wisdom for
learning the casting spells.
Specific Skills
Magic related: Abjure, Alter, Channel (lvl 4),
Conjure, Divination, Evoke, Meditate (lvl 8),
Specialization (lvl 30)
Skills: Forage (5), Dodge (lvl 15), Specialize
(30)

Druid Character Guides
Enchanters are a subtle class. They master
the art of illusion and action through objects
and people. Their spells are crafted to
enchant people, places and things. This art
allows them the charm beings and make
them fight for their new master, or apply
magic to a sword in order to enhance its
capabilities. An Enchanter is not a fighter,
and does not do well battling alone. He is
however, a powerful addition to a group -
supplementing their ability and casting a
cloak of confusion and chaos on their
enemies
Important Attributes
Intelligence for learning new spells and
developing the mana to cast them, Charisma
for influencing those that the spells are cast on.
Specific Skills
Magic related: Abjure, Alter, Channel (lvl 4),
Conjure, Divination, Evoke, Meditate (lvl 4),
Research (16), Specialize (20)
Skills: Dodge (lvl 22)

Magicians are the summoners of the arcane
arts, also none as the gate callers. The have
the power to call forth everything from a loaf of
bread to a powerful elemental to fight for
them. Although the Mage does not have the
offensive capabilities of a Wizard, they can
still pack a significant punch.

The Mage is one of the best classes for single
combat. A Mage and his pet can take on
multiple creatures of equal level and can often
take down beasts that will quickly kill other
classes of them same level.

Magician Character Guides
Monks are dedicated to the pursuit of shaping
their bodies into a pure weapon and because
of this, they shun almost all weapons and
most forms of armor. The are a very religiose
class, and the gods are known to bless them
by magically enhancing their attacks.

Monks seek self discovery through trials by
combat and vows of faith, developing unrivaled
skills in unarmed combat. Monks are a
difficult character to play - with no armor, they
take a great deal of punishment and with no
weapons they cannot hit from a distance.
However, they are quick and efficient tanks
and do not require the significant investment
in spells a caster requires or the investment of
arms and armor the fighter class require.
Important Attributes
Strength and Stamina for prowess in battle,
Dexterity and Agility for fleetness of foot.
Specific Skills
Mend, Dual Wield, Dodge, Safe Fall (lvl 3),
Round Kick (lvl 5), Sneak (lvl 8), Tiger Claw
(lvl 10), Block (lvl 12), Double Attack (lvl 15),
Feign Death (lvl 17), Eagle Strike (lvl 20),
Dragon Punch (lvl 25), Disarm (lvl 27), Flying
Kick (lvl 30), Riposte (lvl 35)

Necromancer Character Guides
Paladins are the very picture of a holy knight,
fighting for the cause of good in all aspects of
his life. They share some of the power of the
Cleric and a good deal of the fighting
capability of the Warrior and they have a few
unique powers all of their own.

Paladins are a shining example of virtue,
bravery and honor, melding the dedication of
faith with the power of raw strength. A Paladin
can be a powerful alley to any adventuring
group. They can stand toe to toe with the
Warriors of the group, while healing those that
are injured and strengthening those that have
been weakened in battle.
Important Attributes
Strength and Stamina for battle, Wisdom and
Charisma for the workings of holy might.
Specific Skills

Conjure (lvl 9), Divination (lvl 9), Evoke (lvl 9),
Meditate (lvl 12)

(lvl 17), Double Attack (lvl 20), Riposte (lvl 30),
Disarm (lvl 40), Lay on Hands (used once a

Paladin Character Guides
The Ranger is a meld between a Warrior and
a Druid, sharing some of the skills of both.
They are at home in the outdoors and fierce
protectors of nature in all its forms. Rangers
are on with the wilderness, making them
strong allies for those interested in
adventuring in wild reaches or Norrath.
Rangers skill with the Bow and Arrow and
unmatched by any other class in Norrath.
After having spent so many years in the
forests and jungles, Rangers are able to pick
up the smallest sign of a creature's passage
and track them regardless of terrain.
Important Attributes
Strength, Stamina and Agility have all been
honed by years of living in the wild, while a
Ranger's Wisdom has been sharpened by the
necessity of survival.
Specific Skills
Magic related: Abjure (lvl 9), Alter (lvl 9),
Conjure (lvl 9), Divination (lvl 9), Evoke (lvl 9),
Meditate (lvl 12)
Skills: Tracking, Taunt, Forage (lvl 3), Dodge
(lvl 8), Sneak (lvl 10), Bind Wound (lvl 15), Dual
Wield (lvl 15), Parry (lvl 18), Double Attack
(lvl 20), Hide (lvl 25)

Character Guides
Ranger Character Guides
A Rogue is a secretive, mysterious class,
they tend to book looked at by others as
nothing more then thieves and cut throats.
Some say they are assassins, killers that
sneak through the night and attack from
behind. The truth is that a Rogue's character's
lies somewhere in the middle, they are
capable of both - but dedicated to neither.

They are quite skilled with many weapons,
but cannot stand toe to toe with a Warrior of
equal level. The Rogue tends to prefer to kill
from behind, with a quick and sudden blow of
great power - a skill at which they have
become extremely adept.

The Rogue does not like to stand out in the
open, in fact they would prefer not to be
discovered at all. They are skilled in the art of
locks and the art of thievery. The Rogue is a
difficult class to play, they are a mix of
attributes, making them capable of good and
evil and playable as either or both.
Important Attributes
Agility and Dexterity are the two most
important attributes for a Rogue to concentrate
on.
Specific Skills
Sneak, Pick Pockets (lvl 3), Sense Traps (lvl 5),
Dodge (lvl 5), Pick Locks (lvl 6), Hide (lvl 7),
Apply Poison (lvl 10), Backstab (lvl 10), Safe
Fall (lvl 12), Parry (lvl 13), Double Attack (lvl
16), Dual Wield (lvl 17), Make Poison
(lvl 20), Disarm Traps (lvl 20), Instill Doubt
(lvl 22), Disarm (lvl 27), Reposte (lvl 30).

The Shadow Knight is the Evil twin of the
Paladin class. Shadow Knights derive all of
their powers from the evil gods whom they
serve. They are a meld of Warrior and
Necromancer, and share some of the abilities
of each. In their ways, Shadow Knights are
more dangerous then any other class as they
view themselves at war with all things good
and pure. Shadow Knights are Chaos in
physical form, bringing with them nothing but
sorrow and pain.
Important Attributes
Strength and Stamina are important for Battle,
while Intelligence is used for the Necromancer
arts.

Shaman Character Guides
Warriors are the masters of armed combat,
they are creatures of battle - living for war and
the honor and glory it brings. The are at home
on the battlefield, and are trained to take
punishment as much as dole it out.

Wherever there is conflict, groups battling for
supremacy - risking life and limb you will find
Warriors. A simple class, trained from the first
days of their lives to the day they fall in
combat to make war on their enemies, yearn
for the rush of fear and excitement that comes
from armed aggression, and morn the loss of
those that have fallen before them.
Important Attributes
A Warrior needs to have great Strength,
Stamina and Agility in order to survive his
chosen path.
Specific Skills
Kick, Taunt, Bind Wound (lvl 5), Dodge (lvl 6),
Bash (lvl 9), Parry (lvl 12), Dual Wield (lvl 15),
Double Attack (lvl 15), Riposte (lvl 25), Disarm
(lvl 35)

Wizards are the masters of magic and
magical power. They are pure magic, they live
and die by the power of their spells. They are
master seekers of knowledge and their talents
and spells are focused on this all
encompassing goal. They have powerful
spells of incredible power, allowing them to
see great distances, teleport across the land
in an instant, and call forth furies of fire and
ice to crush their enemies.

A Wizard is the blaster of the magic class;
able to dish out incredible power, they are not
as capable in the area of defense. In a group,
they have no equal in pure damage potential -
but keep your fighters close by in case the
enemy manages to survive long enough to
reach you.
Important Attributes
Intelligence for learning new spells and
developing the mana to cast them, Stamina for
the physical energy needed to summon forth
the magic.
Specific Skills

Conjure, Divination, Evoke, Meditate (lvl 4),
Research (16), Specialize (20)
