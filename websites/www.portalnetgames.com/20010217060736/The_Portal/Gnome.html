Gnomes are another of Norrath's underground residents. They spend most of
their time in the deep caverns and caves of their homeland. A Gnome
resembles a Dwarf to some extent - being short and stocky. However, a
Gnome has ruddy brown skin and is wiry and gnarled. The male Gnome's hair
is white and often sparse.

Gnomes are the technicians of Norrath. Where Dwarves spend much of their
time mining ores, Gnomes focus on refining ores into metals and then creating
machines out of it. The Gnomish city of Ak'Anon is a truly amazing place -
everywhere are machines and magic - blended together to make servants,
workers and guardians.

Given their propensity to stay underground, the gnomish people have
developed a keen sense of sight, allowing to see clearly even in the darkest
places. A unique skill to the Gnome is the ability to tinker - allowing to put
together object to make machines (compasses and the lot).

The Gnome does not have the brute strength of a Dwarf, preferring thought over
muscle. They are a fast and agile race from years of scampering through the
endless tracks of the underground home. Their inclination towards the
mechanical world and their fundamental understanding of mystical arts have
made them an intelligent race and quite capable magic users.

Gnomes are a much more light hearted race then their Dwarven cousins, and
tend to take the more physical pursuits less seriously then other races. Given
their innate intelligent, Gnomes make excellent mages and clerics as well as
other "pure" caster classes, such as a Wizard or Enchanter. Given their high
Agility and Dexterity, a Gnome can also be a very good Rogue. Some
Gnomes have taken on the challenge of the Warrior, being small and quick,
many have been successful in this field of study. Sadly, even the light hearted
Gnome cannot escape the temptation of Evil, and some of their number have
fallen victim to the seductive arts of the Necromancer.

Gnomes begin adventuring from their home city of Ak'Anon, located in the
southern reaches of the continent of Faydwer - in an area known as the
Steamfont mountains.
