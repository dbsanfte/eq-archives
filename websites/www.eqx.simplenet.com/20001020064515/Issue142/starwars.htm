ISSUE # 142

 September 23rd - September 29th, 2000


Star Wars MMORPG, Who’s ready?

Recently I had an event happen in my life. Perhaps not so recently as it was actually shortly following the Vegas EQ trip in July. My hubby’s family had a crisis and I have had to be in another city except for three or four days out of a month. This sojourn has provided me with a step back from EverQuest, and one I didn’t particularly want. When the crisis came I was becoming even more heavily involved in the community than ever before and enjoying the heck out of the interaction it provided with all of you. In fact one of my last logged times on Oxesiess can easily be ranked as one of the highlights of playing her (the “fans” were just too dang cute and great role-players that I met at the bank {waves}).

Since I was without a computer I found a tremendous amount of reading time and little else. Modem withdrawal is a terrible thing for a person to go through. Missing that ‘bshshshshchchchchhc’ is an awful feeling. Computers, games, and even the hated writing process are all creative outlets for me and loved hobbies. Not to mention a dang cheap way to communicate with my friends long distance. Finally I decided to track down a public computer I could use. I hit libraries and while I never got to hear the modem sing, I was at least able to surf a bit. Since these computers have some restrictions on what you can do with them, I found myself being in the “look but don’t touch world”. That was a pretty big pouty moment for me as I was dying to leave posts all over the two message boards I track regarding EQ. However, sucking it in I decided to put this “look but no touchy” time to good use. So I went surfing on something that’s up coming and perhaps for a lot of us, a world that’s like no other. Star Wars.

The information available out there regarding this universe is astounding. I had no idea the fans had been so busy, nor so intense. Sites from completely breaking down the statistics on ships, to physic realities behind the myth (making an actual living breathing light saber), and fan fiction. The Star Wars community has taken everything from the weight of a blaster right down to how many energy charges it would take to fire one and given it life. There’s boards dedicated to literature and the very author’s writing for that universe talking to the readers. It was almost like being in never-never land.

I am going to date myself here by stating I was a Star Wars generation baby. When the original film came out I was four years old. Born near LA, my father took me to one of the theaters that ran the film the first two weeks it was released (before all the other theaters were scrambling for it.) We stood in line for about three hours to get tickets and this young girl suddenly had her scope of imagination widen farther than anything else I have experienced in comparison. It was the scene where Luke sat there looking at the twin suns. Perhaps such a view seems so simple to include in a sci-fi movie, however I have always felt it was something so simple and yet dramatic that the whole idea of a double sunset would escape most of us.

Perhaps for some people they feel the characters made the show, or the special effects. For me the whole beauty of Star Wars has truly been the scope of such of universe. I am not one of those who fell in love with Mark Hamill or Harrison Ford and wanted to meet either one so I could say I met “Luke” or “Han. It was the background depth of such a different universe and culture that enticed me.

I fully realize as an adult that if you boil Star Wars down into its essence, the whole story is a space western. But what is it about the world that made an adult woman clutch her husbands arm in a movie theater and lean over to whisper “Love, I never thought I’d see those yellow words on a star field and the trumpets play like that again.” What was it that made all the adults in the theater around me start screaming and cheering when Vader picked up the Emperor? They would have risen to their feet but it was already standing room only.

Star Wars has captured our hearts and minds because of the challenge to our imagination. I am not saying George Lucas reinvented something better than the wheel. Far from it, he took the wheel and gave us such a vastly improved version we became totally captivated by it. And soon this captivating wheel will be open for us to live in together on a mass scale. I do not know about the rest of you but for me personally, that is a scary thought. The fact is that I have played other LucasArt games, just never a role-playing one with all of you on his ground.

It’s scary in the sense that I have seen incredible worlds made and what people do to them. Yes, I speak of EverQuest and other games. EverQuest in its own right is brilliant. Take Halas and the first time you saw snow, the first time you saw the snow change color from the sun going up or down... beautiful. Take Kelethin, take many of the visual aspects of the game and we, I think, can all say “Wondrous”. Now take us out of the game and look what we have done to it. Yes, we. Not anyone else but us in the long run that’s reduced EverQuest to a mere world of numbers and our own definition of “best”. What will we do to the ultimate universe, from a Star Wars baby’s point of view?

Will we reduce the universe we can live in into what is the current topic of the week on unfair? Will we start yelling out in OOC “Is Vader Camped?!” Will we start asking other’s how to get Fett’s super-duper-meany-of-all-time wrist poison dart? Perhaps make spreadsheets on the most efficient bounty hunter for collecting money at 1.5% per hour? What about the consuming debates of perhaps corrupt Moffs because they repoped an area due to a bug? Are we really all ready to see the possibility of those twin suns reduced to the level that might follow on such a mass scope?

Sure we can say “But the game was made this way so I have to {insert reason here}”. Forgive me if I don’t fall in line with the reasoning because of this “have to”. I might feel that is to the most advantage, but that doesn’t mean I have to anything. To be frank, I am almost terrified to play in this new universe, this universe that expanded my scope so much with the simple touches and with its daring. To watch the circumference of a different set of rules merely for overnight “Hahah I am bigger than you {insert derogatory remark that makes someone feel better for putting down someone they don’t know}” thrill or “It’s the way I like to play.” In such a universe as Star Wars the thought makes me shudder.

Star Wars seemed the last untouchable world. True, it’s up to the makers to try to help make the game truly be an RPG for the players. They, however, can only do so much. They can make it a hard game and hurt, which I hope they have the courage to do. They can make us dream of owning a specific blaster, and make it a mark of true skill to have such. They can try to make us want to wet our puter chairs as we are being hunted for our actions, which I hope they do. I recently had a dream about what this new Star Wars able-to-play-in-world would be like. Allow me to indulge myself and your patience a moment relating a dream I had while thinking about all this up coming stuff and being so exhausted after two days sleeping barely a few hours in a chair.

I dreamt about being a bounty hunter “warrior” type of character. I was skilled in any blaster you could name. My character could do really friggen neato rollies on the ground while firing. In short not only could she kick butt, she had lots of friggen style while doing it. Why bother to blast if you can’t look good doing it? That was her philosophy on life. My partner was an old Internet friend of mine in the forums and EQ named TwoHead. He was a bounty hunter as well, but a slicer mainly. He was a character that is talented with hacking computers. We went after a bounty in cloud city. I think my writing skills lack the talent to portray the scope of the “job” we pulled but let me try

He and I were dashing through piles of junk metal, crossing catwalks blasting away while the sun set behind us and the clouds turned pink. Security systems were sounding off while we jumped out of turbo lifts and spotted because our ID’s had been tagged. Driods battling us at every direction and lots of neato rolling shoot moves were being pulled while he sliced into a panel door behind our position. This whole adventure developed all because we dared to pull a job off on target that was popular and living on a floating trap of a city. It was a race and a fight every step of the way to see if we could steal a ship to get out of there and actually collect the money. The running commentary must have had me laughing while I was sleeping with TwoHead and his “Damn it WOMAN! Do you have to do your rolly move while I am WORKING?!” He had almost been hit by gun fire, barely missed since they were aiming for me in front of him. And my reply of, “Dead is dead man! You want me to die without looking my BEST?!”

They, the creators, can make it many things. However, the question is, despite how hard or how soft they make this new world, what are the rest of us going to do to it? And are we ready for what happens when we ourselves make the uncommon, common.

    Article by Onnie
