Q:  I have a question about resistances. When a MOB casts a fire or ice based spell on you, is your heat or cold resistance checked, is your magic resistance checked, or is it a combination of the heat or cold and magic?

Reras E'ci Server

A:  From what my Pillars of Wisdom, Crav, Tagad and Faerin, tell me, the resistances all follow the rule of like vs. like. Thus, if a mob casts fire or ice, your heat or cold resistance will be checked, and if the mob throws a magic spell against you, your magic resistance will be checked. Of course, some mobs will have both types of spells available to them but your resistance will be checked only against the appropriate one.

Q:  Not a question, but I noticed you said that skills can only be improved on blue or higher monsters... this isn't strictly true. Combat skills (1 hand slash, pierce, etc) can be improved on high greens (con something like: 'looks to be reasonably safe.. but can't be certain'). These same greens will give experience, so its my impression that if a monster gives you experience, it can improve your skills. Also, non-combat skills such as pick pocket can be improved on _any_ monster. This means that as a level 15 rogue you can quite happily try to pick pocket a kobold runt.. and it will go up. Spell casting is the same. It seems to improve on successful castings, rather than what you cast it on. Casting skills will improve on green monsters, yourself, or even other players, even if you don't have PvP set. Also a handy tip, if you want to get that beg skill up, try quickly flicking off autoattack, begging off the monster you're fighting, then flicking autoattack back on again.. you can also beg off pets, or even the boats!

Q:  In regards to the question about last names in issue 107, here is an answer. Last name standards vary from server to server. Some servers don't allow multiple capitals, some servers don't allow apostrophes and some don't allow multiple players to have the same last name as another player without the first player's name permission. Now these are actually guidelines. The naming system supports all of these. The /surname command that players can use to assign their names has the same filter that the name filter uses when you create your character. If another player already has that surname or first name, then you will have to petition to have your surname done by a GM or Senior Guide. If you want a surname with 2 capitals or an apostrophe, my surname showed as "SteelPalm" then you'll have to petition for it. My advice is if you get an apprentice guide or even full guide that won't give you a last name with either 2 capitals or an apostrophe, then tell them you want the call escalated to a Senior Guide or GM that would have to assign the name anyway as this is not a normal guide power. Again, the standards aren't consistent across servers and even on servers that have standards many guides do not know them fully. Duplicate last names is a big one. Quellious allows multiple players with the same last name without permission of the original owner but many guides don't remember the standards or are confused with other servers standard's that they may have heard....as for the length...not sure...that's never been really covered.

Thentar SteelPalm
