Bards in Everquest

Bard

The Bard's stock-in-trade is his ability to play songs. His songs are actually spells of a sort, and their magical affects last as long as the Bard continues to sing and play the correct instrument. They have some passing knowledge of the Warrior ways (mainly for self-defense), but their main focus is always their art, and so they will never be as skillful as Warriors. 		If you read the previous two articles (part 1 and part 2) on bards, you will have seen that bards are, essentially, the bearers, transmitters, creators, and repositories of their respective cultures. Whether the dignified and imposing bards of the Celts, or the romantic and lyrical troubadours of medieval France, the bard character is one whose knowledge, training, and talent allow him/her to reflect the culture back to it's members. Words and song are the bard's tools to accomplish this, being the best way to transmit information in the "fantasy/medieval" environment. In Everquest, bards use their songs as power to affect events around them, as medieval bards did, but the effects are spells rather than the power of language and culture. Whereas the Celtic bard had the weight of his entire culture behind his words, giving him power over the members of his culture that believed in the power of a bard, EverQuest will use song-spells to obtain the same result. It's highly unlikely that a warrior player who meets another bard player will pay as much attention to their words as would a Celtic warrior. Reputation or prestige of a single individual could do that, but not just some guy who is playing a bard.
