Letters to the Editor

I suppose this a "letter to the editor" regarding your last editorial (in issue 22).

As a DM I too have found this a difficult thing to judge. But I dont think people are necessarily a bad judge of it. I try to modify the rules and give experience where experience is due. Sticking to the classic, "You kill monsters and thats how you gain experience" is ok for some but when you are serious about roleplaying it just doesn't work. 		What a good DM must do is be in touch with player's characters and their goals. You have to be able to spot behavior that is out of line. Usually for such transgressions I try to mention something to the player. "Now didn't you say your character was deathly afraid of water? He seems pretty anxious to hop the boat to treasure island...." and try to remind them of things they may have forgotten. Of course the player is the final judge of how the character is played. It must bring about in game consequences though if their roleplaying goes very far afield from their concept. The character must be forced to adjust to circumstances. (i.e. "Ok, so your out on the high seas and the boat springs a leak. I'm assuming everyone can swim; well of course except the guy who was only until recently deathly afraid of water...")

Letter from Russell Linton.
