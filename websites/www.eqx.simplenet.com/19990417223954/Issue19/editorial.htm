ISSUE # 19Apr. 25th - May 1st

EDITORIAL

Welcome to Issue 19!

Any of you Doctor Who fans? Sure, some of you must be. I am. I always got up early on Saturday morning to watch the good Doctor. I’ve probably seen all of them that have ever been shown in the U.S.

So what the heck does that have to do with EverQuest. I’m not sure. But I’ll think of something.

So now we wait for EverQuest. We read the newsgroups, check the web sites, read our e-mail hoping for that fateful call. Hmmm… Anticipation. That’s certainly one thing that EQ and Dr. Who have in common for me. But that’s not enough for me to fill a whole editorial…

So when we get the game, what will we do? Roleplay I hope. That’s what I want to discuss this week. Roleplaying. I’ve had a lot of reason to be thinking about it lately. The newsgroup is full (too full) of discussions about what roleplaying is. I’ve been involved in a roleplaying project, and that’s got me thinking about it. I’m heading out to see some old friends this weekend for a "convention" of sorts, and that’s caused me to think of roleplaying. In fact, it’s these friends of mine that I want to talk about. I hope they won’t mind.

I’ve been going to game conventions for many years. Many of those years ago my group and I bellied up to a new phenomena on the convention scene. Someone had built an enormous castle. It was to scale for miniatures. And leading to this castle was very long bridge. The castle could be taken apart by layers. It was one of the most amazing things that I had ever seen, or have ever seen since. For me then it was as exciting as EQ is for me now. It promised a great new gaming experience.

The game that they ran using this castle was called the Monster Mash. The rules were simple. Make a character using the AD&D rules by spending a million experience points. With these characters, the players were to assault the castle. After many hours of scribbling and scratching, while playing in the boring tournament games, I had a character. I can say that I did a pretty good job. I only ever played that one character. And he had some nasty tricks.

So we sat down and played. We romped all over the poor DMs and their "Defenders". Defenders were players from previous games that were so good that they were chosen to defend the castle. Well, this particular year my group decided to have some fun. We built a tower in the middle of the bridge. We laughed our arses off watching people try to blast us out or go around. Even the defenders tried to get rid of us, to no avail. We continued to laugh. Then one group walked up and asked, politely, if they could pass. We said, "Certainly," and opened the gates. We teamed up with them next year and were the first group to cause the castles top to be removed. We made them take off 4 levels of the tower before they killed us.

We had one heck of a time playing the Monster Mash. But we were NOT roleplaying.

There was no character interaction. Each round, which would take about an hour to actually run, was a simple matter of strategy. We never talked about our characters wants. Heck, our characters had no background at all. We were playing a D&D version of chess. And we had a blast.

But that was what we signed on for. If we had joined that game to roleplay, we would have left after the first round or two.

Now, about these friends. We figured that they were so much fun to hang with at the Mash that we would game with them at the convention. And we did. But you know what, several of them had no idea what roleplaying was. Not a bit. I was stunned. Most of them are brilliant roleplayers, but to a few of them games were a way to get experience points and treasure for the next game. And each of their characters could kill any, heck, all of mine. So I could never get away with protesting when the thief would steal from a poor merchant, the one we were hired to help. And never would they actually do anything without payment. Of course they were always playing immoral thieves… similar to the Pkiller excuse of supposedly playing a fantasy version of Jack the Ripper.

I am happy to way that most of these folks are great roleplayers. And I have high standards. And some of the not so great ones are actually learning, I think.

I guess my point is that there is a place for Quake and a place for roleplaying. EverQuest is a place for roleplaying. As for Dr. Who… Well, I guess I just wanted you all to know that I liked Dr. Who. I mean, I could use it as an example of what makes the essence of a great game, plot, challenge for the heros, and conflict. But you already know that, right?

   Absor
