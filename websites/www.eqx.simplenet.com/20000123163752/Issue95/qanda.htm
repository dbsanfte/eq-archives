Q:  Okay, please be patient with me as I am a new player. I am wanting to use hot keys more and more so I can have a full view of the action. My question is this - Is there a hot key to target your nearest enemy? Thanks for your help and may the burning flame of timpanogos be with you!
~Tylvech
A:  Thanks for writing in and I have a feeling there are others reading Q &A who might have the same question. You may already know there is a key -- F8 -- which is the default key set up to target your nearest enemy. The computer version of the Everquest Manual, found in your Everquest Folder, also shows that you can target the nearest PC with F7 (with the other function keys being similarly assigned). These keys can be reassigned under your Options/Keyboard box in the game to fit your personal preferences. For instance, I have moved the enemy target designation to my "T" key as I got used to it in beta and have a natural keyboard which makes reaching up for F8 a little slower than just hitting "T". However, if there is a way to put another keyboard key into your hotkey bank, I don't know of it. Hope this helps and write again to tell me what in the world the burning flame of timpanogos is.

Q:  My friend and I are curious about how the friendly corpse looting of no drop no trade rare items are handled in EQ. Suppose I died and have a friend that I would trust enough to loot my corpse if I was a spectacular distance away. Could he pick up my no drop no trade items.. after granting him consent to loot my corpse? Is it possible for him to give them back to me if its no drop on him now.. and if he can.. would that be considered a new way of bartering no drop items? Thank you!
Sil
A:  No-drop items in Norrath are indeed no-drop, no-trade. This means that if you do die and /consent a friend to loot your corpse, the friend will not be able to give you back any no-drop items he may have looted from your corpse. (A word of note here too: If you have a lore item in your corpse and your friend loots it but is already in possession of the same lore item, one of the two will disappear). For the most part, it is not advisable to have a friend loot a no-drop item. However, you can use /consent and then your friend can use the /corpse button to move your corpse to a more accessible place for you to loot when you do return to the scene of your death. This can be very helpful if you are far away and your group must leave before you can retrieve your corpse from a dangerous area.

Q:  I am trying to find out exactly which prime reqs have a direct effect on your ability to hit in melee. I always used to think that it was dexterity that influenced your chance to hit along with your skill level of course. But now after playing several different melee types of characters I can see no discernable difference in relation to dexterity and the ability to hit (other than missile combat). Any information would be appreciated. I want to create a tank that actually hits its target most of the time. Right now I'm sick of not being able to hit a white or blue mob more than one in four times at any level. So to sum it up, I don't care about how hard I hit (within reason), I don't care about how many hitpoints I have as a tank, I actually want to HIT the target MOST of the time.
Regards, Krysan
A:  It took promises of exotic ales but I managed to catch up with some level 40-somethings long enough to ask about what it takes to hit well in melee. The consensus was that dexterity is actually very important to accurate hitting. It is good to put skill points there and to acquire +dexterity equipment. However, your weapon skill level is crucial too. You simply have to raise it! A melee player who chooses to have a friend help by twinking levels, misses out on necessary skill experience. Do make sure you fight and swing a lot! I'd like to add a note here too. When I finally reached the level for dual wield, I took the recommendation of a friend and used the fastest weapons around for my level so that I would get as many chances to hit as possible. The damage wasn't great and I wouldn't want to do this in a fight where I thought I might lose because of it, but my dual weapons skills shot up remarkably. So I think along with dexterity and lots of battles, I would recommend doing a fair number of your fights using a speedy weapon to raise your skill level. Finally, out of curiosity, I asked the players which melee class they would play as a pure tank who would hit often and accurately --- they all chose a warrior.

Q:  First off I just want to say thank you for an excellent publication. I look forward to reading it start to finish every week. Ok, enough butt kissing *yuck*. I was hoping you could answer what is probably a silly question, but one that none-the-less, I don't know. A:  Oh dear, the getting-on-the-boat part is a ticklish question as the dockmaster would definitely be angry if people found a way to avoid using the docks and his source of income from "head taxes" on boats dried up. So let's pretend I'm not really answering the question but merely imagining a "what if". That way I can look him in the eye and smile with a little wink, knowing he couldn't possibly think I was giving away port secrets. The "what if" that I can picture might involve you swimming alongside the boat, using a little pageup perhaps if necessary, and if, and only if you were very lucky, you might find yourself inside the boat. Some have said that this might feel like going through a wall, but who is to say that a high wave couldn't come along and boost you up over the railings, spilling you downstairs so that, at last, you find yourself a passenger on the boat. Note, however, that in the oceans you cannot zone by swimming. You must be on the boat. The boats seem to need to return to drydock now and then leaving travelers high and dry on the beach. If this happens, a friendly petition asking that the boats be reset is a good idea. If for some reason there is no one available to reset the boat, I have often found guides to be ready to help by using their powers to shuttle players across the sea.

I left East Freeport fresh and eager for sea breezes on my journey to find the gnome city of Ak'Anon and all those cute little gnomie heads to pat. No sooner had I zoned into the Ocean of Tears when I discovered I had left my rosewater and glycerine lotion behind in the inn. Hoping against hope the maid would have left it, I paid a gater for a quick trip back to Freeport. Yes, it was safe and soon, at sea once again, I stood by the railing of my boat enchanted by the sound of the water and wind until siren music wafted over the waves and it seemed like a speedy exit was necessary. Back to the mainland again. The third sea voyage was a delight, and soon I found myself in Butcherblock positively captivated by a darkhaired dwarf who swooned at the idea of me sprouting a curly beard to meet his dwarven ideal. Finally, when he realized that no hair was forthcoming on my chin, he escorted me to the entrance to Greater Faydark. Now, travelling through that land is an experience to raise the hairs on anyone's skin. If you read from Nep's Journal of Journeying, you'd soon find the words, "When lost, sit down, cry and scream for help!" Luckily, it worked and soon I was traversing the forest and then wandering through Lesser Faydark. However, there is no decent restaurant or deli in Lesser Faydark, so I had to go back to Kelethin (where I also had to re-use the aforementioned chapter from my journal) to fill my pack. Finally, I made it through Lesser Faydark again and into Steamfont where I looked and looked and looked for a place to soak. Harummph, has anyone found a useable hotspring there! A charming baldheaded gnome wizard with a strange gleam in his eye offered to escort me through Steamfont to his beloved city and being the unusually naïve bard that I am, I accepted. Well it turned out well enough actually although I will leave you to discover the pleasures of Ak'Anon with your own gnome guide. Sooooo, if I counted right, my "direct" route took me through 12 zones, I think. Of course, you may not travel in Nep style, so you might want to re-count as if you were doing the trip yourself!
