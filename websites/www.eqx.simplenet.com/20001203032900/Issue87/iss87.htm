Rogue: A Tale of Treachery

Part Fourteen

Katha had connections that Kinory could only dream of. In an hour, she had arranged horses, supplies, and everything else they needed for a long journey. Katha introduced Kinory to the extensive system of underground tunnels that honeycombed the land beneath Freeport. They were hollow, echoing, and damp, and Katha had to navigate around key points held by the Necromancer guild, but before long, they came out into the night air again. They met the horse dealer beside the city walls, and then followed the hard packed road into the dark.

    Tale by Aklar


Etiquette and Me

When playing EverQuest we are often confronted with situations that require some understanding of the etiquette of the game, and of the Internet in general. What I often take as a given, like inkies will hassle my Half-Elf bard at any opportunity and that training giants on people is wrong, some players don't understand at all. Some of the basic, often unspoken, rules of etiquette of EverQuest might be too unspoken for some players.

    Article by Absor


Survival of the Fittest

I sat in the trees twenty feet in the air. Below me lay a scene of hatred and carnage. Dark Elf guards lay slain by the hands of the lesser races. They enter my home and kill my kind, then expect me not to hate them? Pathetic beings. Don't get me wrong, not all the members of the so-called lesser races are bad people. Some of them pay rather well. And who could argue that there exists anything more beautiful then a wood elf? It's the arrogant self-proclaimed do-gooders that enter my land and kill my people. It is those people who will die for their crimes.

    Tale by Kardde
