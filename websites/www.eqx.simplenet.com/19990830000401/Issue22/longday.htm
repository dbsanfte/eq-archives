ISSUE # 22May 16th - May 22nd

A Long Day

The bright glow of the Xystran moons filled the square as Lilan slowly walked home, smiling to herself with pride. It had been a difficult task for her parents to convince the village elders to teach her the secrets of her people, the arcane mysteries which had saved her kind so long ago. She couldn't believe her happiness, though; just two years ago she had wished for death at the thought of being enrolled in such a boring, forgotten field of study. Now she smiled at the thought of doing well. Unbelievable, she thought. She was so lost in thought she barely noticed the shrill wind echoing in her ears. Her footing followed the familiar path, even though she was barely conscious of the stones beneath her feet. She daydreamed, smiling to herself occasionally, ignorant of the outside world. She was so engrossed in her thoughts that she didn't realize how sleepy and tired she was getting... Her steps slowed and became awkward, her head became heavy as her breathing became deeper. She was having trouble keeping her eyes open until the first pains of nausea swept over her. Her eyes shot open in icy panic. She could barely move, let alone struggle, as she was lifted off the ground. It felt like some powerful hand gripped her, squeezing her neck, choking her, as she coughed and wheezed for breath. The pain moved down her back, along her spine, as she darted her eyes back and forth to clear her clouded head. She saw whirls of color racing past her downwards, blots of light and dark plummeting below. Tears streamed from her eyes to be swept away in the torrent of wind as she hurtled to ... where? What is this? Why is it doing this to me?

Tale by Amoroth.
