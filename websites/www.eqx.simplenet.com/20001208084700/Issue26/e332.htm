EverQuest vs Asheron's Call

Asheron's Call has a simpler interface for skills (a text listing with numeric values of your skills) and inventory (all done on the right margin, atop a humanoid representation of yourself). This is a lot easier to use. AC also lets you use the right mouse button to move around, as well as the arrow keys. While I found it cumbersome to control, I'm sure others out there will find it easier to maneuver in this way.

Advantage: Asheron's Call 		Combat

EverQuest has an attack mode toggle button that gives the server control over the basic combat events. Each round, you swing. This is what all of us with a MUD background are used to. This also has the advantage of being lag proof. You aren't required to click your way to victory, which means if you lose a precious few seconds of data, you won't find yourself a bloody corpse. EQ also lets you use your combat skills as additional attacks, which gives those with a clicking fixation something to do during battles. 		Asheron's Call requires manual aiming and manual clicking to attack. You hold down the mouse button and let the power bar reach the max. Meanwhile, you move your cursor over your target, and it has arrows telling you if you are too low, too high, or whatever. When you release the button, you attack. I think this system will have to be scrapped when they realize a little lag will destroy the whole thing. If it is lenient and lets the client report whether it was a hit or miss, they will find Diablo-like cheaters taking over.

Advantage: EverQuest
