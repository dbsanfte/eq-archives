Interaction between PVP and Non-PVP

Take for instance, you are a Non-PVP and a PVP approaches and begins role-playing the bandit role. Speaking a bit of the perils of the wilderness, and how such a heavy purse will surely just weigh you down. I mean, what if you feel in water! You would surely drown. Here is where we must as the Non-PVP, not react in a negative way and just run away, or go, "Shut up PK.." Take full advantage of the situation, role-play it. If they do well, reward the person with gold. A small portion of gold is very much a well met trade for a good role-playing situation. Reward them for trying. This is where the PVP's come into play. The PVP's must learn to role-play also. Now that there is a switch, you aren't going to find that you have to chase down people throughout hundreds of miles of land, to get their plate armor. If they are PVP, they are going to know the drill. If they are not, they will have it set to Non-PVP, and you can't hurt them anyway. So, with this, take the moment to challenge your opponent. Introduce yourself in some long winded history of your character. Then, slice his head off if need be. Everquest is going to either be made a great game, or a terrible one, by us as players. The gaming system aside, we are ultimately the ones responsible for the upkeep and enjoyment of the game, as we are the community of players that live in the fantasy world of Norrath. Take the time to interact with your fellow players. Be it a confrontation, or a friendly introduction, spend it doing what makes a Role-Playing game worthwhile, Role-Play.
