As a RPGer of 10 years (sr yr of HS & beyond), who in fact met her 2nd husband via a fellow gamer, I don't mind being considered "odd". My husband has been playing D&D since the first printing - he's 32 now. And most of our group has been playing since their mid-teens as well, and they are in their mid-to-late 20's now. Nearly all of my friends were either directly from RPG's or indirectly via the internet/BBs (and played RPGs). For an average investment of $21 a month, I'll gladly take the hours of entertainment provided by RPGs vs a 2-hr movie that costs a min. of $14 per show for the 2 of us - with little to no ppl interaction. And for all those nay-sayers of us gamers, if you check with most gamers, they are avid readers, have decent-to-excellent vocabularies (from the reading) and tend to be a bit more open-minded about difference peoples & cultures - though this last varies greatly. And my current group has been together in one incarnation or another for over 5 years. So for those that think we should grow up - I say give me $50 a month or butt out :-)

Janna of Mithaniel Marr
