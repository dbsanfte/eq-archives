Q:  As a human necromancer who worships the Plaguebringer, I persist in my attempt to convince my acquaintances that rot, disease, and death can be friend as well as foe. My attempt has so far been unsuccessful. Here is my dilemma: My friends would like me to join them hunting Orcs in Crushbone. While I have never backed off from bringing disease and death to the Deathfist Orcs near Freeport, I have so far avoided doing the same to the Crushbone Orcs. Killing Crushbone Orcs (and their friend Ambassador D'Vinn) will bring me disfavor with the Dark Elf Warriors in Neriak. I worry about doing so, should I in future need to travel to Neriak on professional business. I have not yet had such need, and, as a result, I don't know how I am currently received there. Are my fears warranted?

Jekyll

A:  Your friendly instincts toward the Crushbone Orcs and their distinguished, if nasty, visitor, Ambassador D'Vinn, are quite correct and your fears about misinterpretation of your smiting behavior are warranted. Although it might be tempting to go visit Crushbone and smite a few smelly orcs, it would indeed hurt your efforts to be received in Neriak someday. However, a word of caution -- it is possible that unless you share a religious viewpoint with the guards and inhabitants of Neriak, that they will never learn to appreciate your charm and good looks, no matter your skill in necro-ology. Therefore, when you do choose to approach that sinister city, you might want to do so very, very carefully.

Q:  Hey, I just finished reading Issue 110, and loved it. I was wondering what tips or strategies you could give me, I'm a level 3 druid and looking for something interesting to do. Thanks and keep up the awesome work

Therien

A:  Hmmm well if I could sum up my suggestions in a little ditty, you'd find four words repeated there: questing, smiting, exploring, and friends. Even at level three, you can find intriguing quests from your guild master and from inhabitants of your home town. Smiting can, of course, be a bit repetitious, but if you combine it with exploring the realms, there are true adventures to be had. The real key to finding interesting things to do, however, is find friends to do them with and making the most of your character's personality and wit while grouped. Don't hesitate to smile and say "Hiya" to those you meet; ask questions of high-levels, exchange death stories with those you encounter close to your own level. At level 3, remember, you lose no experience when you die. Thus, if you are really looking for excitement, put your items in the bank for safekeeping and streak across the country experiencing the wonders of Norrath and running as fast as you can from its terrors. There is probably no faster way to burn out on Everquest and to miss all it has to offer, than to stick in one corner day after day beating away on some boring old critters with no spark of personality. Make sure you take time for fun and conversations that nurture your spirit of adventure. Have a great time!


Q:  Here's a question that's been bugging me for awhile and I was hoping you could shed a little light (pun intended) on the subject. I was wondering if the time in EQ is divisible by "real world time" so I can always be able to log on when it's morning in Norrath as opposed to 7 or 8 p.m.? For example, when it's 11p.m. my real time, will it always be a certain time in Norrath? If so, I'd like to chart the time so I always could start during an EQ morning. Thanks and love your work.

Sandywen

A:  It seems to me you'd have a really hard time charting the hours of Norrath but I did go and check with that stalwart Pillar of Wisdom, Tagad, who pointed out that Norrath has its own counter that is based upon when the servers came up. He did say that his calculations were that one day in Norrath passed every 72 minutes of our real life clock, i.e., an hour in the realms would equal about 3 minutes of rl times. When you log on, you really do have no idea of the server uptime, however, so there is really no way to tell whether it will be morning or night. Theoretically if you knew exactly what time the server reset and knew exactly what time it was in Norrath when the game came up, you might be able to chart the equivalent times of day. However, given that the servers do reset, I think it'd be better to spend the time getting some great light sources, learning where the night meanies are in Norrath, and just logging in anytime you have a few free minutes.


Q:  Ullo, I would like to know what buff stand for, I know what kind of spell, but the word "buff"? Is it for Buffer? You create a buffer of protection? Anyhow, thank ya old silly Elf!

Vajzar

A:  The EQ term "buff" is primarily from the American slang "buff" which means to be very strong -- you know the kind of people going around with rippling muscles displayed to great advantage, the ones everyone points to and says, "Wow look at those buff guys"! Thus, if you are using the term to "buff" someone, it primarily applies to spells and equipment that will make the person stronger. In EQ, the term is often applied to protections and enhancements other than strength too. Some people use the term "prots' from the word protection for spells that raise resistance to magic and add ac to the player. Another old mud term, rarely used, is "vits" which came from raising the vitality of the player by adding hitpoints and stamina. It seems to me that your explanation of "buff" as referring to a buffer of protection is also accurate if you emphasize the strengthening aspects of that buffer.

Q:  In regards to: " My understanding from them is that if a level 26 or higher bard is in a wide open area with not too many mobs and cares to gamble that this is not an exploit, he or she can use the chaining song to bind a mob in place and then use the screech song to fear it, making it run away. While it is running, the bard can chase it, whacking away with whatever weapons are at hand, and then repeating the cycle again until the monster is dead. … Since it does involve a tactic where you are basically rendering the monster unable to hit back at you, there is a question in my mind as to whether your local friendly guide or GM might have an issue with this too." This is no more an exploit than a good two thirds of the spells usable my enchanters, or any other snare/fear tactic. The reason it is not an exploit? These songs are resistible. If they were not resistible, then it would indeed be an exploit, because the monster would have no defense against this tactic. As it is, the monster's defense is his resistances.

Bryarr SkelbaneCleric of UnderfootBertoxxulous

A:  Thanks to you both for writing and I see your points. After discussing this with a Pillar of Wisdom, I would like to point out that even though in instances like those you have both described where the songs are not exploiting a situation, these same songs have been used for major exploits in high level zones. It's important for all of us to be able to distinguish between proper bardly behavior and that which might bring down the wrath of the GM's and guides. So be careful out there!
