Q:  Baaarrrrrkkkkk!!grrrr... Why do all those humans keep attacking me, when I run down the road? grrrrrrryap yap, baaaarrrrkkkk

Fippy

A:  My dear blue gnoll, they aren't really attacking you any more than you are attacking them. It is simply a confusion of languages I think. They hear "Baaarrrrkkkkkd!grrr" and then translate that to mean you're saying "I want blood" when what you are actually saying is, "Would you like some Fippy Snax?" You see the problem? Now, the solution. First, may I suggest you start with a "yap" and dispense with the comments about what you are going to do to those you meet on the road. This is another case of cultural displacement I'm afraid. So while, of course, the gnolls expect to be greeted in such a manner, others without exposure to gnoll conventions will simply misunderstand and hold out their sword instead of an empty hand to be filled with gnoll sweets. Next, after a friendly yap and perhaps a light bark, slow down just a little so the would-be attackers can see that you are merely on your way to town for supplies and are gladly sharing your trademark candies as a friendly gesture. I can't assure you that all will understand your change of behavior so I suggest you take along a friendly bard to interpret and to reassure those who think you actually would use those big canines to bite. As for the humans, well, they haven't written to me yet, but if they do, I'll ask them to remember that even blue gnolls need a friendly smile and a wave of greeting along the road.


Q:  Hail! I am new to the land of Norrath and have enjoyed it very much! In fact I highly recommend it to those who have no real life. I'm kidding. . Okay. I am very interested in role playing with my 6th level Pally vs power gaming. I have tried to learn more about what is expected of a Paladin. I am learning this slowly. Having said this, I fear I may have already killed things that I should not have (e.g., bears, wolves, pumas). These are not evil creatures, as opposed to skeletons, orc's etc. How do you raise level's without killing? Or do I need to slay these beasts in order to level up? Also, I wanted to journey to other lands to explore what Norrath has to offer but I did not know if this would find me looking for a corpse that has 'poofed'. Is it safe for me to travel? How does a Paladin not jeopardize what she stands for? Leveling up is great but I also want to practice the many skills available. Sincerely,

Jorelei

A:  Ah I have a soft spot in me heart for Paladins. Just think of the evildoers wending their chaotic way along the roads, making travel miserable for all those who cross their paths. And then along comes a Paladin, shining in goodness, ready to make the world a better and truer place. I don't know what your guild leaders allow you to kill but of course, you will agree that, if by killing that which would harm others, you actually save lives and the happiness of families across the land, then killing is part of your duty. There is no way to level in Norrath without killing, but you can be as persnickety as you wish as far as where you choose to go. Perhaps if you truly wish to help the world you will help clear the dungeons of their foul inhabitants. Now that you are 6th level, you can travel the world without fearing the consequences of death as much. My suggestion if you choose to go exploring is to leave your best equipment in the bank, find some travelling companions to speed your journey and share the dangers, and turn your face to seeking new vistas. If you die, as long as you are level 6 or above, you have 24 hours of in-EQ time to find your corpse (this does include chat time by the way), or up to 7 days of real world time. Travel can be dangerous but you never know when you might meet those in distress who will benefit from your skills and attention. Between journeys, in those quiet times, you will, of course, want to add to your non-fighting skills. Choose wisely and seek the advice of older paladins for those trade skills that best fit your life. Good journeys!

Q:  Ok, I have been looking over a bunch of different Q and A's you guys have, here and there, and I haven't seen ANY questions pertaining to rogues. Are there just no rogues out there? Anyways, what kind of creatures can see through Hide/Sneak, and how does evade work with it? One other thing, got any tips for a lvl 5 Dark Elf Rogue pertaining to exp or armor?

Jerid, Rogue on The Rathe

A:  Oh my, you know, rogues are certainly difficult to find for question-answering, what with their abilities to just fade out of view if they don't find the matter interesting. Luckily, a couple of rogues did take pity on me -- how could they resist helping a young rogue? -- and told me that you should remember that Hide/Sneak is a skill based on level and use. You should practice them as much as you can to raise your skill quickly. At lower levels, random mobs will see through hide and sneak. However as you level up, you'll find that many npcs that can see through invisibility spells, can't penetrate the shadows of your hide skills. So it's definitely worth it to max your skills. As for evade, the advice I was given to pass on is this: stop attack; hit sneak; hope it works; if it does you are now at the bottom of the mob's hate list. Evade is a combat skill so you'll need to treat it accordingly. When in doubt, you might want to check with high level rogues you run into for information and advice.


Q:  Hail, and well met. I am an orphaned barbarian taken in by the druids of SureFall Glade. During my stay in the glade I met a halfling named Cormach Anklebiter. He told me wonderful stories of his town, RiverVale. I was entranced by his voice and longed to see this place. After a perilous journey, ,for at the time I was only in my first season, I made it to the quiet and charming village. I was disturbed to discover, however, that they didn't trust me. I have always protected the animals and forest woodland creatures. I understand the halflings have a right to be distrustful with the human encroachment of their land. So my question is this: How do I gain their favor and trust? It has already been 4 seasons now, and they are still apprehensive of me, except my druid brethren. I have done my best to protect them, and honor their customs. I humbly await for your response.

Sincerely,

Archeon of the Hidden Path (5th)

A:  It sounds like you were a lucky child from the chill then to run into Cormach. I can see how you wish his countrymen were as welcoming and as kindly toward you. Of course, halflings are partial to their elvenses so even though they might be wary at first, you can join them at their repasts and if they hear of your exploits ridding the world of those they detest and fear, soon they'll be buying you a pint or two to show their regard. You are aware, I'm sure, of the goblins of Runnyeye, those nasty predators of hairy toes and round cheeks. I think you need to get your strength and skills up so you can begin to assist in the ongoing effort to keep Rivervale and the misty woods free of such greenskinned nasties. Then my guess is you'll find that as you offer this protection to them over a period of time, they will soon come to trust you, even if you tower above them and scare them just a bit. I believe you might also want to come to the aid of a hurt halfling in Misty who seems to need a perpetual supply of bandages. However, you choose to help the halflings, I think you'll find that it won't be too long until you feel more welcome in Rivervale.
