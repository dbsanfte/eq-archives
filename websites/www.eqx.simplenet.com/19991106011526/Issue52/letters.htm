Letters to the Editors

I just read the recent EQX article, "A Woman's Point of View", by Saskia, and I have a few comments to make on it. Firstly, I heartily agree with most of her analysis about RPG games from a feminine viewpoint. I also play for the interaction and teamwork of the game. (Even though I *do* enjoy at times, the action oriented games like Quake - there's a certain time of every month when almost any woman just loves to be able to blow things up!)

BUT, I certainly don't take any offense at a well-turned come-on line by friendly male players. I know they're not in any way serious, and they know that I know. So it's all in fun. I struck up many friendships in UO this way before the PK'ing got too much for me and I tossed my CD. Sure, the stupid, un-thought-out ones are a pain, but it's easy enough not to answer those. Guys are not stupid. They get the hint if you don't reply. But I've had some really sweet lines too. After all, how serious can you take any comment about how beautiful you are, when all they are looking at on the screen is nothing more than an avatar - a fictional representation that (hopefully) you can tailor to your imagination? After all is said and done, a pick up line is really a compliment to your ability to create a character!

My advice to any male readers out there is: No one woman can claim to speak for ALL women. I like what I like. Someone else may not. So, if you see something you like, compliment that person!!! Who knows...maybe it'll be me! I won't mind. :-)
