Q:  Hello all I have a cleric question. Does the Divine Aura which gives brief invulnerability to self work? I scribed it into my spell book, then sat to memorize it and was ready to use it but the icon never appeared and was grey when it looks like when the icon is ready to appear. It said "you haven't recovered yet!" P.S. This is the first time reading your letter. I'm very impressed at the layout. It's simple and easy to read. Also love the Q&A. Keep up the great work.
Narsiss
A:  Yes, the Divine Aura spell does work. However, there are two things you need to know about spells like this one. The first one is that the Divine Aura spell can only be used about every 15 minutes, so your button will be grey for the interval. When the icon comes up again, you can re-cast the spell. The second thing you need to know is that when you mem the spell after scribing it, the same time limit kicks in. Thus, when you mem the spell, the button will stay grey for 15 minutes before the icon will appear. If by the time you read this, you still have not gotten the Divine Aura icon so that you can use it, you should petition and ask a guide to go through the steps with you and see if something weird has happened. Thanks for the compliments!

Q:  Let's say you have a base int of 110 and you use magic aids such as rings to up your int to 140. Then you level. If you take off the int increasing items in favor of others, will you lose the mana benefits gained by leveling at a higher int?
Aaraorn Ebonheart
A:  I thought I was going to have to give you an "I Stumped Nep" award on this one and then got so many differing answers when I asked other players, that I figured I'd never be able to respond. Enter Zeddic, the wizard, now duly inducted into my Pillars of Wisdom, with the following explanation: Your base int is like a glass of water and the contents of the glass are your Max mana. If you tried to put more water in the glass when it was already full, it would overflow and would not be able to hold anything more than you have in it. However, if you make a bigger glass by getting more int from wearing items, there's lots more room so your Max mana can be larger. Now, you take off your int items and guess what? You are back to a smaller glass again and it can't hold all the mana the larger one could. What is interesting here is that if you put your int items back on again to get the larger glass, it won't be full of mana immediately but will take time to fill up again. Thus, if I understand it right, no matter what level you are, you will still need the extra int in order to get Max mana. I hope I got it right and by the way, this explanation on int is only for the mage/casters as cleric/casters use wisdom for their mana.

Q:  Oh, wonderful know-it-all... Just a simple question... how do you hotkey a message? For example, /loc or an auction message?
Tyressia
A:  Grin, if I knew it all I wouldn't need the dozens of people I run to every week for help, particularly the Pillars of Wisdom and a very knowledgeable gnome. (It's just plain hard to imagine a gnome as a pillar. Hmm, gonna have to have a new category for him!) However, the answer to your question is actually fairly simple. In windowed mode, you could click on your socials key and it will bring up the preprogrammed social keys. Right click on a social key you don't mind re-programming. You will then get a box where you can change the social designation and the message. Thus, if you want a /loc, you could type Loc on the title line, and the below type /loc. When you have finished with the social, click done and you will see the new designation on your socials key. From that point, you click on the social key, holding it down until a little floating box forms and then you simply slide the box over to an empty hotkey slot and click it in. With an auction message it would be essentially the same except that in the box to change the social you would start with /auction and then type your message. It looks like there is lots of room there for a long message but it really doesn't work that way so prepare to be brief. If you have a long message, you may have to create more than one social button and more than one hot key to get the entire message out. I sure wish they would change that as the public only gets to hear my short poems and I never get to read the long ones out loud in a smooth uninterrupted manner.
