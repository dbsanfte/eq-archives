A Rambling about Roleplaying in Everquest

How many Ultima Onliners reading this have ever seen someone in UO say "cool dudez" or "hey man, wuz up?" or, more commonly, something totally incoherent like "yah, wel if you're moms' lazey were then I think your a farry"? Probably about 95% of all Britannians have heard something along those lines. Unless you play with a very select minority of players, you play UO with the rest of the populace, who neither "speak" in the context of the role-playing environment they're in, nor form understandable, gramatically correct sentences. Some people reading this might say to themselves, "That's nitpicking. You shouldn't criticize people's grammar or spelling. Yours isn't perfect, is it? Besides, no one cares, and no one's perfect, and it's just a game, and etc etc." Whoever says that is right, at least in part. My grammar has never been perfect, I've been known to say and type some unintelligible things, and I agree, no one is perfect. However, grammar and how you "talk" can have a very large impact on how enjoyable you make an online game for everyone else in it. To make this article have something do to do with EverQuest, I'll use it as an example in what I say next. The idea behind a roleplaying game is to act out someone else's life, right? If you play an RPG, you probably play it and enjoy it because you can remove yourself from the real world and enter a much more interesting and fantastic one, right? Let's just assume both of those statements are true. If that's the case, then you also probably envision the romantic world you want to enter as something much more perfect than the real world.
