Rogue: A Tale of Treachery

Part Twelve

Gwyn was at the bottom of the stone steps that led to the upper palace when his sister caught up to him, seizing his arm. He spared her a furious glance and wrenched out of her grasp.

"He's getting away!" he shouted at her.

"No, we are," answered Kinory, and dragged him back the way they'd come. He dug in his heels.

    Tale by Aklar


Darkest Betrayal

I have never truly understood the human race. I myself am the product of the union between two half-elves. What amazes me so is how humans can vary between the two extremes of pure goodness and blackest evil so much and so often. While for the other races, there is a general attitude that can usually be assumed true. With humans, that is totally impossible. You can never be sure which way a person leans unless you really know him or her. In a recent experience of mine, I encountered two persons who each embodied totally opposite ends of the spectrum.

    Tale by Baratol Alaryn


Lazy Days and Crazy Nights

Part I

The sun hurts my eyes as I sit at the hard oaken table. The torn curtains do little to block out the sun, and the alcohol I had consumed the night before seemed to want to escape my body through my head. The life of a treasure hunter is never done. Many people deserve to have their property borrowed, and those whom are particularly daft, taken. I look around the small inn, my bowl of bread and last nights stew steaming. The barmaid strolls through the area, filling up mead and water when necessary. Only two travelers other than myself sit on the benches this morning, more evidence of the lawlessness of the land. The roads have become even more dangerous then ever. Not like I care, as long as the economy is good, I'm happy. The more money being carried around means more money for me to pilfer, which is of course the job of a treasure hunter.

    Tale by Kardde
