Client Editor Utility:

EQ_Ini.zip  ~ version 2 ~ (1465k zipped)
Here is the full setup for the eqclient.ini if you want to customize your colors easily.

For the updated Client Editor Utility (version 2.2), instructions from Verant on how to customize your colors, an RGB Color chart for commonly used colors, and some sample custom color schemes that you can download and customize to your liking, please visit EQ Stratics. They have a very good page with all the info you need to make your colors work for you :p
 

 EQDiary (1988Kb zipped)
Here is a great parser for your eqlog file. It parses out all information relating to your battle stats, casting stats, loot, melee, deaths, fizzles, foraging successes, etc. You can either look at individual play sessions or at your characters totals for skill levels. It also has a list of all spells for all casting classes with mana cost, time delay, where to purchase, etc. 

NOTE: due to them adding server numbers and character names to the eqlog file name, you will have to rename that file (one at a time of course if you play more then one char) to eqlog only if you want EQ Diary to be able to parse out what you did that play session.

EQParser (719Kb zipped)
This is similar to EQDiary, but gets more in depth. It will list everything for you like /tells, /says, GM petitions, players (/who), zones visited, battle stats, casting stats, list out each mob you attacked or that attacked you and what damage it did to you and you did to it :) Also does graphs of some of these. I use it to double check email addresses that were sent to me via /tell and levels of members from /who in case I was in battle and missed it.

Written by:
Howard Roy
http://kqp.mpog.com/EQParse
 

 Necro.xls (34Kb)
Here is a Excel file that helps Necros keep track of all their words and other components needed for research spells. All you do is mark how many you have of each word and when you have all the necessary words for a spell, it will mark Yes in the box next to the spell. Pretty cool huh??
