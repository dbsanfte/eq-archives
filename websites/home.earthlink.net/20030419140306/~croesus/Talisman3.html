Day Three: Moonday

 

          As he hid in the shadows of an evening moon he fumed to himself, remembering the mornings events. That stupid slut had beaten him at his own game. He had tried everything he knew of that could be done to hurt her without scarring her and still she had kept her screams and whimpers to herself. 

 

          It had denied him the satisfaction he had desired and that was bad enough but then as she left his room this morning, bruised, tired, wincing in pain as she walked, she had looked back at him.  In that look were so many things.  There was the disgust that she felt for him, the loathing that such a creature had put himself on and in her, and the certain knowledge that she had deprived him of something he had hoped for.

 

          However what had really made him shriek in his head like a gibbering baboon was the look of both triumph and pity she had sent. Triumph because she would never have to deal with him again and had beaten him at his own game. The look of pity was even more infuriating though, for he had nothing to be pitied for. He knew he was a strong, smart thief and she was nothing, a body to be used and tossed aside so why should her look matter? Still that look had burned through him like a fire from God and he had nearly wept with the fury of restraining himself.  She knew she would never again have to see him and was out of his power and so, smirking at him as she whispered

the traditional "Come again" through a bruised throat, she turned her back on him like he was nothing and walked out of the room.

 

          As he had left the House, he had seen her talking to the other whores and making gestures and they had snickered and laughed.  Even the Venus guild guards had a smile on their faces when they looked at him and there were too many for him to think about fighting, even if fair fights and not knives in the back had been his style. He knew he would never be able to show himself at this house again and if he did the sluts would not serve him. Perhaps even now they were telling the women and boys of other houses about him? He d left the House of the Pink Rose in a state of extreme agitation,

as he thought up a thousand deaths for the slut with

the Jade green eyes.

 

...............

 

          One of Xhorghat's favors bought him the rights to see the body fished out of the river on the wrong side of town. He had been a City Guard for awhile but didn't like working the wall at night under the endless sky. He still had friends in the guard though and they had shown him the body when his questions and description had led him to it. 

 

          The body, bloated and rigid had been washed ashore on the property of one of the Cities most respected Citizens and his daughter had received quite a fright. Consequently the body and its murderer were now the result of an official, if loosely pursued investigation.  Loosely pursued because

the body was obviously someone from trashtown and thus of no consequence and if one trasher wanted to kill another why not let them?

         

          Xhorghat on the other hand wanted the investigation to be conducted thoroughly and tried to push it that way but favors could only do so much.  So the best he got was a complete inspection of the body and a sketch of the mans face by an artist sometimes employed by the Guard for that purpose. He also retrieved a small pinky ring from the corpse, nothing spectacular but valuable enough to show that either the killer wasn't a thief or that he had no time after the killing or that such a ring wasn't worth his time.  He knew the killer was a thief so he assumed the second or third or possibly both assumptions were true. This indicated to him the man had been killed in daylight

since death was always harder to conceal in daylight, and even in trashtown publicly disposing of a body would arouse too many questions, so it had to have been quick.

 

The place where he had met the 'Merchant' was close to both the edge of trashtown and the river, so perhaps he would circulate the drawing there, although he would have to spend silver like he drank beer if he was to get any answers out of the residents of trashtown. Well, better a poor freeman then a well off slave in a Salt mine where his silver lions would do him no good.

 

...............

 

          Gordo watched as the man, sweating hard and with shivers running through his big frame placed his hand on the bloody table next to the other severed finger and picked up the knife in a quavering hand. For a second the man thought about throwing the knife at Gordo but he knew

he would never get the chance and would be dead before he tried. Gordo knew this thought went through him, as it went through all people who had to demonstrate their remorse for failing Gordo a first time, but he also knew that the man wouldn't try. Perhaps someday he would misjudge and die, but not today.  The mans partner sat gritting his teeth in pain at a table farther back, clenching a bloody rag to his now four fingered hand.

 

          The man, Dork was his name, placed the knife over his pinky, and held it there.  With a savage grunt he pushed down, and a scraping noise as of bone and gristle separating filled the room which had gotten very quiet.  It always amazed Gordo that such a small sound as a finger being removed could make such a loud sound. The man stood for a second, swaying in shock then was lead away to the far table with his partner. 

 

          "Well", Gordo said "does anyone have any news on this matter?" None spoke and the silence was deafening. 

 

"He may not be the brightest of my sons, but I will not tolerate him just disappearing from under the noses of my watchers. That would invite reprisals from other Clans and be a show of weakness. I want to know where Jug is and I want to know by MidDay night or I will be

most displeased." Many in the room seemed to tense, for when Gordo Yrdiani, head of the Nightblade Clan became displeased people died in many ways, all of them painful, gruesome and long lasting.

 

...............

 

          She never came home that night, which is all that saved her life. Even Twist had limits to his patience and as MidDay dawn approached he left his hiding place near the stairs to her small room and headed for his own room, vowing to himself that she would not live the week out.
