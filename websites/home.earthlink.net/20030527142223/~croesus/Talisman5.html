Day Five:  CloudDay

                          

          Xhorghat was no longer in physical pain, the Mage had taken care of that. He had also given Xhorghatinstructions.  "You will walk around trashtown asking questions and looking at people until your feet bleed. Then you continue doing it some more. We must find that Heart! This cities survival depends on it and neither your nor my carcass is worth that. I will be searching as well in my own ways and should you need me simply break this small stick in half and I will be alerted and come.I suggest you begin your search at the last place you can remember since that was where others knew

of Jug, and Jug and his partner must have been together somewhere."

 

          So now he found himself sitting at a small table in the lounge of the House of the Pink Rose, tired and hungry but no longer in pain. This was the fifth House he had tried since the House of Silk where his last memory ended two nights before. He had observed each house for a while, shown the drawing, seen nothing and moved on. Now he was hungry and rather then march back to a decent part of town was willing to pay the overly high prices of the brothels kitchen.

 

          A small short, blonde in a very loose weave cotton long shirt came over to ask him what he wanted and he told her. A she was about to leave he put the drawing on the table and asked her if she had seen this man before.

 

          A door closed behind her eyes as she shut off inside to try and hide what she knew but it was too late and Xhorghat decided to put pressure on her. 

 

"Look," he said " I can have you arrested and questioned by professionals, and  that might not be very pleasant, so why don't you just tell me what you know about Jug eh?"

 

          "If you know who he is then why ask me about him?" the woman responded.

 

          "He's dead and I am looking for who killed him, so there is no reason for you not to talk to me unless of course you had something to do with sticking that knife in his back? Hmmmm?

 

          "He's dead?!? You know he is the son of Gordo don't you? I don't want to get mixed up in this!"

 

          "Yes, I do know, and it's to late you are already mixed up in it. Gordo himself is one person who wants to find the person he was with when he was last seen. Maybe I should tell Gordo you know and let him question you? I hear he's very good at it?" he smiled lazily as if at the thought of time off from an odious chore. "Then you would be his problem, not mine."

 

          "I don't know much, just that he came in here regularly, got drunk, broke things, always paid in gold, and was lousy in bed. He was in here a lot and last SkyDay he found a drinking buddy, someone who was buying for him instead of leaching off of his fathers wealth like most of his 'friends' did. Jade could tell you more though, she was with his friend last Sunday all night."

 

          "Where is this Jade? Is she here?" asked Xhorghat.

 

          "No she was abused by him pretty badly and hasn't been in here since then. She has been staying with Amber for a few nights since then, while she recovered. “I can show you where Amber lives but please don't hurt Jade she didn't have anything to do with this death."

 

          "Let's go. Now!"

 

...............

 

          Jade was on her way home from Amber's not wanting to overstay her welcome, but since it was such a nice day she headed first towards the markets on the nicer northern side of town. She had come to the realization that the only way for her to survive as anything other then a whore was to head west, away from Talisman and the lands where she had serviced hundreds of men. Perhaps in the Far West she could start over, although what kind of work she could find she did not know.  A few hours haggling in the markets would ease her spirits and lift her mind from her problems.

Strange that she always wanted to shop when she needed relaxation. She enjoyed the haggling and feverish pace of the markets.

 

...............

 

          Dwango sat in the corner feeling severely outclassed.  Sitting comfortably before Gordo at his desk sat a man all in shinny armor with a sword at his side. That a mage should be thus armored, sent a feeling of wrongness down Dwango's back and he suppressed a shiver. The man's name was Sinistar and he was a headhunter among other things.

 

          "You are certain, you can accomplish my orders?" asked Gordo.

 

          "Of course," said Sinistar, "I am more then a match for this Silverlock, you speak of. Not only are my spells of the 127th circle, but I am equipped for any contingency. My armor will protect me and if I grow bored I can always use my sword to finish him off."       

 

          Now Gordo tensed visibly. "127th circle?  What nonsense is this?  Silverlock is a master mage and yet his spells are only of the 18th. Do you expect me to believe that level 127th spells exist? And if your armor is so great why is he not wearing it, why would your sword be of use against a mage?I have hired you from reputation alone but there are many inconsistencies here, and I do not like inconsistencies."

 

          "Normally, there are only 20 circles of spells but with the use of certain magics, one can do almost anything. This armor and sword are the best possible, the Godly Plate of the Whale, and the Kings Sword of Haste, but neither is usable to him lest he want to rely on these same magics and his 'Brotherhood' prohibits its use. Theydo not have the stones to exploit this power of the Null

magics but I do, and will use it to aid you.For a fee of course."

 

          As Dwango listened to the exchange he grew more and more anxious.

 

"What 'fee' will you demand mage?" asked Gordo.

 

          "Nothing really, just the Heart"

 

          "WHAT!!! Why would I give you such an artifact,  when I can retrieve it myself using my Nightblades?  I'll grant you this, you have stones asking such aprice"

 

          "Hahaha", chuckled the mage in his shiny armor, his ungodly sword clinking against his armor with each laugh. "The stone is of no use to you for it requires a mage to use it and it's use will alert all

other mages within a hundred leagues. I however could, with the right preparation make use of it. Though it take a week or a month, eventually I would pervert it's power enough that I could use it to enact the final spell of Null Magic; limitless Mana! No more relying on a finite supply of mana potions, I would be unstoppable then. 

 

At this Dwango burst from his chair, "This is madness! The magics he speaks of warp the very fabric of the laws of our universe. They are a danger to us all and could destroy us and the world we live in! This 'limitless Mana', where do you think it will come from but the very fabric of our world? It is madness to use this man or his Null magics! The Hearts are the very essence of Order, and warping one of them to use in the creation of Null magic is like mixing spark and lamp oil! I beg you Gordo, do not use this... mage? Let myself and a few others kill Silverlock for you, without the use of this Null magic or the trinkets it allows?"

 

          In a move so fast as to almost leave an afterimage Sinistar's sword moved quickly, striking the unready foe, once twice and then a third time to behead the helpless Dwango.

 

Gordo sat, watching the body spurt its lifeblood onto the dirt as the head of Dwango rolled a foot or two away, said "That was hardly fair, even without your advantages he was unprepared to fight.  Hmmmm I like your style, attacking an opponent while he is unready is one way to guarantee a victory. One way I myself have used many times." A slick smile crossed Gordo's

greasy face as he said "I want you to bring me Silverlock's  ear, as for the stone perhaps you may keep it but I will need payment for it's loss and it is worth far more then the bounty I have placed on Silverlock's head.What will you offer me in return?"

 

          "Ahhhh, I see you admiring my armor and sword.",  Sinistar mused, "Perhaps  in exchange for the Heart you would allow me to provide you with one hundred such swords and armor for your private Elite Guards?  And see this ring and amulet?  They are made from the finest Obsidian

and blessed with the powers of the Zodiac, they will strengthen the wearer in every way and help protect against the ravages of the Elements. One hundred of each of this ring and amuletwill I also provide, and a special gift for you and you alone. Behold the fabulous Demonspike Coat Ring!"

 

          "Demonspike Coat RING? How can a Coat be a Ring? And Demonspike doesn't sound like an item which will do my soul any good?" muttered Gordo.

 

          "Well it's a long story... suffice it to say this ring used to be a Coat of armor but through the use of Null Magic its true Form was altered. As for your soul, well we both lost ours long ago so lets not pretend otherwise."

 

          "Very well then." agreed Gordo, "But I want Silverlock's Ear, for no one comes into my den and mocks me there... no one! I will expect the Arms and Jewelrybefore you deliver his Ear, do not fail me Sinistar."

 

          "Of course not," said Sinistar, "but I will need to borrow a few of your men in case I have need of some manual labor or of their particular talents."

 

          Gordo agreed, and the meeting concluded with Sinistar showing Gordo his armor which he said allowed almost all attacks on him to miss. Gordo was more and more sure he had made the right choice, for the hazards of using the Heart would be all Sinistar's and the benefits of the

Armor all his. He said so to Sinistar.

          "No problem..." grinned Sinistar, smiling broadly through crooked and stained teeth, "I love collecting Ears." 

 

...............

 

          Xhorghat jerked himself awake, scolding himself for almost sleeping. After visiting the home of Amber he had been taken to Jades place and had waited under a broken staircase across the street in the heat of a long afternoon. Being a man of action it was hard for him to stay awake although he was certain this was the first time he had actually fallen asleep and it had only been for a few minutes at most. As he continued to wait he nervously fingered the calling stick. Slowly, the warm drowsy heat

and the buzz of the flies lulled him to sleep again on the quiet afternoon, hidden in the shade of the stairs.

 

...............

 

          Twist woke up from a dream of the night before. Secluded inside Jades humble two room home he waited for her to return. He wanted even more to cause her pain because of what had happened to him last night.

 

He had attended three separate Houses but in none of them would any of the whores consent to go upstairs with him, either quoting outrageous prices or simply informing him that they were waiting for a regular, as if a whore had ever turned down a quick lay before?

 

          In his blooming rage and fury, a desperate hunger to hurt had occurred and he had punched a whore. Immediately the Venus Guild Guards had appeared and escorted him none too gently to the door. He had contented himself with the rape of a young acolyte on the grounds of a local convent.  Her body, had already been found no doubt but there was no way to connect a rape in a walled convent with him on the other side of town. He had taken out enough anger on her to appease himself, at least for then.

          Later he had found more anger though and after again coming to the whores house he had decided that the most comfortable place to wait would be inside. So there he sat waiting for her,

drowsing and dreaming of the pain he would cause her.

 

...............

 

          The mortal scream of terror and pain woke Xhorghat, and before he was even three steps from his hiding place he was cursing himself for a fool and a sloth. He had fallen asleep again, and missed her homecoming, for the scream, sharp and quickly cut off but full of agony, had come from Jades house. As he ran toward the stairs he snapped the summoning stick and a small spark burned his palm for an instant. He didn't notice, as running full tilt towards the stairs he pulled his aged but still serviceable BattleAxe from his back holster and wrapped the thongs around his wrist. He was ready.

 

...............

 

          DAMN and DOUBLE DAMN!!! Twist shrieked in his head.  He had lost control again.  He had spent so long planning exquisite tortures for her and yet as soon as he had seen those eyes again he had been overcome with a beastial rage.One second and a savage down slash later and she

Was dead as the knife descended thru its arc and cut her scream off as it slashed her larynx to shreds.

Her blood fountained out in a torrent as she shuddered and collapsed, gutted by a gash which nearly severed her head and continued down to desolate her right breast.

The scream. He hadn't meant for her to scream!! Damn he must leave now and quickly, before others came.Although this was trashtown still it was close to the edge and the city guards might come and investigate.

With a quick smile at his handiwork he ran for the door to make his escape.

 

...............

 

          "I should have taken my chance and left when I had it," she thought weakly as her life blood drained away. "So many things I haven't seen or done. I've never loved or traveled, never been free of the slums of Talisman. A deep despair filled her as she felt her heart flutter and then with a last final thud, stop, and she wished with all her remaining strength that somehow she might get a second chance, even if just for vengeance upon the worm who had done this to her.Her last sight

was a face she knew and hated looking back at her and smiling then running for the door. Then her vision faded and she experienced no more.

 

...............

 

          He reached the foot of the stairs in the same instant as a man came out of the door above and a sharp crack came thru him as the face above snapped into focus in a picture in his head of the crowd around him when he had been confronted by the 'Merchant'. He had no need of the spell placed on him though to tell him this was the man he sought for spell or not he would have known it in his soul on seeing this man.An instinctive and brutal hate filled him and he hefted the Axe and charged up the stairs.

          A shocked face showed the man recognized him as well and instead of trying to fight his way past the charging axeman he vaulted the stairs rail and leaped to the ground. He landed badly and a sharp pain told of a sprained ankle.  Still standing, with most of his weight on one leg he spun and in a smooth throw, practiced from years of throwing at peoples backs let one of his throwing knives fly at the former guard and now pursuer who had turned half way up the stairs and was heading

down in pursuit.

 

          Unfortunately for Twist the turned back of a trashtown bum and the front of a former City guard and acknowledged Axemaster are two entirely different things. Xhorghat easily deflected the knife with a slight movement of his BattleAxe which spoke volumes about years of practice

for just that move.  He continued forward, murder in his eyes against a quarry who could not now outrun him he saw.

The scream from above, so brutal and cut short had driven any thought of his mission from him, and it is well that at that moment there was a flash of light and Silverlock appeared between the two enemies, for otherwise Twist would have lost the top half of his head and the secret

of the Hearts location would have been forever lost.

 

          That flash, which snapped Xhorghat out of his killing rage, also gave Twist a new target as he released his second blade at the person now standing within ten feet of him and in between himself and his foe. The blade flew through the air at the mage, who was facing the other direction, and Xhorghat shouted out a warning, far, far to late. There was a sudden glow as the blade encountered a

veil of shimmering blue mere inches from the mages skin. With a sharp "Clang!" of metal on metal, and a grunt from the mage as he absorbed the inertia and stepped forward to regain his balance, the blade bounced off a temporarily visible shield which rippled like the waters of a deep blue pond, then stilled and faded from view once more.

 

          "Uh oh" thought Twist,as the mage turned, glaring at him and with a flick of his hand and a sound like a ringing chime sent Twist flying thru the air to land against the wall and stumble on his now broken ankle. Pain roared in his head and blocked him from concentrating on the words as he saw the mage and his pursuer talking animatedly now. The Guard kept gesturing towards the stairs and then at him and Twist knew his intentions were far from friendly but the mage kept shaking  his head and instead turned towards Twist and with another magical gesture there was a blinding flash of light and he was elsewhere.
