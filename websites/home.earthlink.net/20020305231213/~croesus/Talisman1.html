Beyond Talisman: A World of Diablo story.

Day One: Starday.

        As the old man gasped at the fumbling sideswipe and mumbled apology, Twist casually, carefully and with the skill of one long practiced in such arts slit the leather cord holding the money purse on the old mans belt. No one noticed, not the man or his equally shabbily dressed companion.

        Although they both appeared to be peasants and the old mans eyes searched for a clear path through the crowd as was normal, his companions eyes were never still, jumping from face to face, staring for a second then moving on. Hard eyes. The eyes of a professional guard.

        Still Twist was good, far better then your average thief and as smart as he was quick, so the guard didn't have a chance to question him as the Accident occurred. Twists partner bumped the guard and said, just a little too loudly, "Watch where you're going Peasant!". The crowd around the trio made a sound like a soft sigh as it prepared to watch something
exciting happen.  The guard, tensed and his hand moved toward his jerkin but then stilled as he realized his predicament. He and the courier were supposed to be incognito, dressed as peasants, and peasants never talked back to people wearing the kind of clothing his assailant was wearing. The clothes of an upper middle class merchant and the arrogance of one too, expecting obedience and respect for his stature and relative wealth from the poor who lived in this part of  Talisman, whether he had earned it or not. The guards orders were to deliver the courier unharmed and unnoticed to his destination and a confrontation with an obviously higher social class citizen would not do that.

        The guard bowed his head and said "Please forgive me, Sir! I meant no disrespect."  The merchant looked for a second like he would continue but then turned and walked away and the crowd again began to move in its myriad directions and errands.  By then of course it  was far to late, for Twist had already hidden the pouch in his own cotton jerkin and, using his small size to its best advantage disappeared into the bobbing heads of the milling throng.

        Finding a convenient alley Twist examined the purse. Yes! Inside the unusual looking bluish leather pouch, were a key, 20 gold Crowns, 6 Silver Lions, a few dozens coppers, and a coppery looking oval stone with a series of small bumps and holes on it. A convenient sewage pit took care of the small stone and key. The money was more then enough to see him through at least a few weeks and a little left over for celebration, if he had luck at the gambling table.

        Now to get back home, the part of town that made this poor but honest area of Talisman look like a wealthy suburb and settle up with his partner.  His part of town was called Trashtown not for the filth in the streets, although there was much of that, but for the filth that lived there, all manner of whores, thieves, murderers and worse.

        Sometimes having valuables sent via courier who looked poor, as opposed to a heavily armed escort was a safe bet to avoid theft but not always. Not ever when Twist was involved. In addition to the sixth sense he seemed to have about trouble and how to avoid it or transfer its wrath to others, he was a very bright person and liked to investigate his crimes beforehand.
He had long known of the various courier services in Talisman and had decided that since such unarmed and "hidden" couriers had to be carrying something worthwhile and with only one guard he had decided to become much better acquainted with the couriers. He had spent six long months finding out the homes and memorizing the faces and styles of each.  Now he had a fairly
easy way to get goods, with no competition since no one appeared to have thought of it before, and low risk. The perfect job, he thought as he smiled to himself, practically the only time he did smile.

        He met his newly acquired partner at the specified place and time to give him the second half of his payment and to get his "Merchant Clothes" back (they had cost a small fortune after all). After receiving the leather nap sack with the clothes he paid his new found and easily forgotten partner in the usual way. After he disposed of his ex-partners body in the customary fashion, ie dumping him in the river, he contemplated the stupidity of people.  It always amazed him that God had created so many trusting fools, or that otherwise clever men would turn their backs on him. None ever got the chance to stop being a silent
partner of Twist for he was not a man to take chances or tempt the fates. In a few weeks time when the gold was spent and the need again arose he would scout the bars and whore houses for a suitable new partner.  Then like he had with the recently dead Jug (an appropriate name since he had drank like one of those mythical Oiliphants) he would manage an introduction in a bar, buy the man drinks all night, tell imaginary stories and false secrets like men in their drink sometimes do and slowly but surely gain the confidence of his newly needed partner. Until then he was on his own again.

    As was usual, he felt the customary satisfaction at the killing and felt the usual stirrings of lust in his groin as he always did after violence and death, so he headed towards Harlots Row. Yes it had been a good day....
