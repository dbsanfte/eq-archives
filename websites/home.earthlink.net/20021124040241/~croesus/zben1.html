Zben's Diary

Day 1: Adventures in Ak'Anon

I thanked my teacher and left the Library MechnaMagika for the last time as an apprentice. For two years
I had studied with and done endless chores for Mistress Ulmcaller.  Now my free apprenticeship was over and my whole life ahead of me.  Having little money, no family (who wanted to talk to me anyway) and no idea what to do with my life I decided to sit and think. Being naturally inclined to climb (a trait unusual in Gnomish kind) and always looking for higher ground I eventually found myself atop the smokestack for the great Forge of Defiance.
Here I am atop the smokestack, those are tents with merchants in them down below. The Forge is where the Gemchoppers, the master Warriors of my people get all their weapons from, it is said to contain Enchanted Anvils.

Not afraid of heights, I decide to get real close to the edge, and stare off at the ground far away. Unfortunately, the smoke from the Forge interrupts my thoughts and I decide to head to the surface, I shall need income and there is copper to be made controlling the area's pest problems.
 

As I pass by the Ink Sea  I stop and stare out across it at knowing that soon I will be leaving my homeland.
I want to fix the image of the great Homeland of the Mighty Gnomish people in my mind, so that I might always remember home throughout my long journeys. So here is the Great Ink Sea with the Kings Castle in the Center.

Finally I reach the surface world and after adjusting to the light (How you surface dwellers stand the brightness all day I will never know) I cast my very first spell. Tada!

I spend the day practicing and exploring the entryway and shops of Ak'Anon, it is surely the finest city in the whole world.

Once I see a Gnome running for his life chased by a whole group of smelly Kobalds. He reaches the Guards and collapses and the Guards spring into action swiftly dispatching the Kobalds. I have no spells to aid the fallen brother and he wears the robes of a Magician so I stand to the side and watch. Eventually he rises and turns back toward the valley of the Kobalds. I realize that I too must learn to hunt, must if I am to afford new spells and books and to learn the art of Enchantment. Yet the beasts of the Valley seem to strong for me? How will I succeed?  Still, it is late and the answer to that question can wait, a test for tomorrow.

Not having any copper for a hostel,  I climb the hills over the entrance and bed down for the night near a steampipe knowing it will keep me warm. Tomorrow will be an interesting and possibly unpleasant day.

Until next time... Watch out for those smelly Trolls and Ogres (i.e. all of them) and be well....
