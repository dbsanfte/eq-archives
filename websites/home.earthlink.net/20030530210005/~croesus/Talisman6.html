Day Six:  SkyDay

        It had been a glorious day in Talisman, spring was finally on its way.  The late afternoon sun streamed in the window and its warmth was welcome, as she lay there, half awake, with a glorious feeling of drowsiness flooding her body. "Wake up!", said a strong and gruff voice beside her, and she did.  As her beautiful jade eyes opened and she saw the rough hewn, gruffly handsome soldier sitting by her bed it all came back to her: the terror of seeing the sadist in her room; the feeling of pain as the knife had sliced clean through her throat; the salty taste of blood filling her throat and cutting of her scream, and finally the deep remorse and regret that was her last feeling as her life had drained away and she had looked back on her life and realized she had not really lived but only existed.

        For a second she was to startled to speak, but then she ventured the obvious.."I was dead! How did I get here? Who healed me?"

        The soldier glanced at her as he continued the work of polishing and sharpening the massive and much worn BattleAxe he carried, gently as if it were his child or lover. "The Mage resurrected you. I knew not that mage's had that power, to restore life to a corpse, for you are right that is what you were. He labored for several hours last night incanting spells and consuming and using many potions and I thought him crazy or a liar when he claimed he could revive you until I saw your wounds knit up of themselves and eventually you began to breathe again.  The mage, Silverlock is his name, looked haggard and worn from the ordeal and left you in my charge while he went to regain his strength. As for where we are, God only knows for we were all brought here in a flash of light, in the manner these mages always seem to use to travel."

        "Why did he heal me though? I do not know him? I know no mages, at all!"

        "Nor he you," spoke the warrior," but when I was ready to behead the Thief for his crime and what he had done to you, he looked at your corpse and a funny look came over his face. He said, and don't ask me why or how he knew this, but he said 'Her time is not yet, she has many journeys to make yet'. Then he did his magic and your corpse was gone and here we are.  Now what are you giving me that stare for?"

        "If that sadist is still alive then take me to him. I have a score to settle and much pain to repay him! Twist you called him, well twist I shall as I remove his stones and shove them down his throat for dinner." A fire burned behind her Jade green eyes and Xhorghat for the first time, noticed her not as a whore but as a person, and one who might have the steel necessary to carry out her threat, if not the means.

        "It's not to be, Lady," he grumbled and felt instantly self conscious in the use of the title for although he now had little doubt she had the soul of a Lady,  how could he call one who had opened herself for so many and for coins a Lady?  Yet Girl, and Whore both felt wrong as well and condescending. "He has stolen something and the mage needs it back, hell from what
I understand the whole city needs it back. Once he has finished with the swine he has promised him to Gordo,  for Twist has killed Gordo's son, Jug."

        At that a radiant smile swept her face and her eyes danced as she laughed, a deep rich sound like the purr of a housecat, and one which shook her body in most tantalizing ways. "Ah that is even better! Gordo's son eh? Twist will be flayed alive inch by inch and that will be the least of his troubles. He deserves it to for what he has done. " After a second the mirth left her eyes and she continued, "but I am not a Lady and don't deserve the title.  Are you a warrior?"

        "Yes. I was a City guard until I retired and then I was a Guard for couriers until Twist theft of the item removed me from that position. Now I don't know what I will do. Why do you ask?"

        "I want you to teach me all there is about fighting, I want never to be in the position where I cannot defend myself again. I have little money but for payment will give you myself one night a week to do with as you wish. Is that a fair bargain?"

        A deep blush spread across Xhorghat's face as she looked at him with a steady eye, not at all ashamed of  what she offered or the bargain she was willing to make.

        "Uhhhm,... Ahhghh that is I appreciate the offer, and the payment is more then generous but I have little to teach you. I would not make a good teacher since I am an Axeman  and you would not have the upper body strength to use the axe effectively in combat.  There are other masters in other weapons, perhaps you could seek out a Swordsman or a Ranger?"

        She looked at him steadily, and as she saw the blush cross his face, she wondered for no man ever blushed when a harlot offered to do what was perfectly normal for harlots to do.  This man considered her a lady, if not in title and history then in bearing at least.  She softened her gaze and asked "Is there nothing you can teach me? I will be leaving town shortly and have no time to find someone who will consent to teach a mere woman to fight?"

        Xhorghat, master Axeman and seasoned veteran of the Fifth war of the MudWater States, vanished for a second and in his place a voice he knew to be his, yet one that sounded far more like a teens own blurted out "You are leaving? So am I! Perhaps we can travel together?"

        Instantly, an icy cold hand clenched in his gut as he recovered himself and thought "Dear god I am acting like a lovesick puppy? What am I doing?  She will never want to travel with someone who is so eager to do so." Yet even as he spoke he heard her answer, "Yes. I wouldn't mind traveling with you. When shall we leave? Perhaps you can teach me what little I can learn of Axes on the way?"

        Somewhere inside him Xhorghat wondered if perhaps she might not make a good Axemaster, she certainly had the spirit for it.  Somehow the thought of leaving Talisman, the town he had spent his whole life in, which before had seemed a tragic fate now seemed more like the beginning of an adventure. "Uhm, well we can leave as soon as the mage has recovered the He... the stolen object, not before"

        "Ok" she chirped happily, with a grin on her face he found himself mirroring unconsciously, as she turned to stare out the window at the sun slowly riding it's arc towards sunset.
 
...............

        The room was grim, as Silverlock made his way in and around the table to his waiting chair, all the while sensing their eyes on him. He was bone tired. Days of expending magical energy keeping track of Xhorghat at a distance, while searching the ether for any glimmers the stone might have made followed by the resurrection last night and the thought of the coming hours of torture necessary to recover the information all weighed heavily on him. He felt he could sleep for a week but knew he had not the time.   The most recent gnawing and queasiness in the pit of his stomach though was the real cause for this meeting as he knew that all of his fellow Keepers felt the same. Null magic was in use somewhere in Talisman! The eddies of rotting order, the tatters of physical laws stretched then broken was a malaise they all felt, in the deepest marrow in their bones.  A sense of wrongness that the user of the Nullmagics would long since have grown accustomed to and failed to notice, but which swept through the soul of ever member of the Brotherhood like an icy wind.

        "We have troubles," announced Mandrake, the Red Mage, and Keeper of the First Heart of Talisman, "You all feel it! Null magic is in the air, and we without all the Hearts together to defeat it."

        As he spoke they all looked at Silverlock, as if  blaming him for the theft. It wasn't so of course, the ritual cleansing and blessing of the Hearts was done each year and had been done in the same way and manner for generations without a problem. The Hearts needed the ritual blessing and cleansing to function yet even a Keeper could be overcome by a group of mages as he followed it first to the spring, then to the Abbey, then back to the Temple. Without the many protective and augmentative spells laid on the Temple over the last hundred generations each Keeper was again just a mage. Powerful mages surely, but not powerful enough to stop a group of other mages from attacking and possibly wresting the Heart away.  Since Keepers were tied to their Hearts, and leaving the other Six less guarded to watch over the one being purified would have been unthinkably dangerous the first founders of the ritual had devised a method based on trickery and deceit. Nobody save a select few knew that at seven separate weeks of the year at least one of the Hearts was gone and being purified.  This reliance on deception had worked for generations.  The Keepers were forbidden to follow the Hearts as the left the temple since the very act of doing so would alert any other watching mages that the Heart was no longer within the much spelled and protected temple where they
usually rested.

Still as the youngest of the seven and the Keeper for the currently missing Heart, Silverlock knew that it was only human nature to blame him in some way for its absence so he did not respond with anger towards the looks merely considered the problem and confronted the others with his own solution. "There are few NullMages in this part of the world, and the only thing which would draw one to Talisman  is the thought of possessing one of the Hearts. I think we must assume he knows it is no longer at the temple and has come to seek it."  he said.

        "Yes," responded the others and a debate ensued as to what course of action must be taken. Silverlock listened and thought but finally he could keep silent no longer. "If this mage is here to get the Heart then we must defend it and stop him from doing so!"

        "But if he is using the Null magics what chance do we have against him." said Iceman, the Blue Mage and Keeper of the Fourth Heart.    "With his Null magic, which we dare not use,  none of us can singly stand against him, yet one must. We all cannot, for to do so would be against our oaths and a sure signal to every other evil mage out there that the other Six Hearts were unprotected in the Temple, or with us out of its protective shield."

        "If he gains the Seventh Heart he can escape and over time subvert it to his evil Nullmagic and then we would be totally unable to stop him when he comes, as he surely will, for the remaining Six Hearts! If you on the other hand gain the Seventh back," and here he looked at Silverlock, for it had been decided in this speech as surely as if a vote had been taken that he and he alone would be the one attempting to recover the Heart with no other Keepers to aid him, "it will profit you not at all since it's magic power is in linking with the other Hearts, and singly it could not aid you in any way without weeks of preparation! How then do we win, when all avenues are closed to us? How!?"

        "I might triumph, if you will lend me a few adepts and some Guardsmen, a little preperation and concealment, and let me follow my plan?", said Silverlock, with a haggard smile on his face. "It will be very risky, but I have a feeling it may just work."

        "What plan is that?", asked Mendelinkin, the Green Mage, Keeper of the Third Heart.

        So he told them. And as their voices raised in argument and consternation, he knew the night was barely starting.  Somehow, he must convince them to go against all they held as gospel for without doing so he knew that he, they and eventually even the fine city of Talisman, sitting like a target on the only bridge across the MudWater river for fifty leagues in either direction, would lose, though it would certainly take some longer to die than others.

...............
 
        The debate and argument lasted for many hours but in the end, what choice did they have? Now Silverlock,  stood before the table to which was strapped Twist.  Twist was afraid, but he still bluffed, threatening violence and death, like a cornered rat.


        "I need to ask you some questions and get some answers. It will be better if you talk to me now and will save me the trouble of torturing you, which I do not want to do, as it will stain my soul. I do need to know where the Stone is though"
 
        "What stone you, purple idiot! I don't know what you're talking about so why don't you..." at which point he began to describe a series of actions both immoral and physically painful, if at all possible without being very limber.

        "Fine, you do not know what you stole." interrupted Silver, "Still I need to know what you did with the Stone you found in the Couriers pouch? Small, oval, copper colored, with seven small bumps on its surface."

        "What's so important about a chunk of Copper?", asked Twist, so Silverlock told him.

        "Now do you understand why I must get that stone back?", asked Silverlock.

        In answer a big blob of spit spattered his purple robe, and with a cackle Twist said, "You're as stupid as that whore was if you think I'm gonna help you recover something like that. Once I get out of here I'll help myself find it and maybe I'll sell it back to you, if you let me go right now. Maybe instead I'll sell it to someone who can put a major hurt on you instead, eh?"

        With a sigh like a old man, and a muttered "I’m araid not", Silverlock began.  Fire and healing, ask a question; fire and healing, ask a question. Soon he had his answers but not soon enough to save what remained of Twists sanity,  if sanity he ever had. When he had his questions answered Silverlock healed his subject and attempted to spell him to sleep and forgetfulness but the blank look on his face, said he had failed.  That look and the echoes of Twists screams, haunted Silverlock's dreams that night and on waking he knew that no matter what else, once he recovered the Heart he could no longer continue as a Keeper since Keepers had to be pure of body and soul and his soul was now stained with the slime of his previous nights torture of Twist. He faced the dawn of the Sun on the last day of the week, appropriately named LastDay and hoped for the best in the coming struggle.
