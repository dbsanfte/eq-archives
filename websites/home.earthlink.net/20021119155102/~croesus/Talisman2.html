Day Two: Sunday

As he studied the dregs of his beer, Xhorghat Axemaster pondered his future.  After the fiasco yesterday he was no longer a Guard and in fact had only a week to find and recover Talisman’s Heart before he would be locked away for a very long time doing hard labor.  He glanced in the beaten copper mirror and saw his watchdogs, three hardened men assigned, none too discretely, to make sure he didn’t leave town until the Heart was recovered or he had been sent to the salt mines.

As he thought over the previous days journey he saw only one point where the pouch could have been stolen, and that was when the “merchant” had challenged him.  Xhorghat had a memory for faces and had that one imprinted on his brain right now. If he could find the merchant he could find the partner, the one who had actually done the slitN’slip. Then he and his BattleAxe would be only too happy to make this person familiar with the penalties of crime. It would be hard to steal with no hands would it not? For extra measure he was planning on removing the thieves stones as well, since he had cost Xhorghat the best job he had ever had, and even if he recovered the Heart he would never work as a courier guard again. Currently though it was more a matter of life and death then of losing a job.

Well, time to start calling in favors hard earned over years of work... all of them. He needed information and he needed it yesterday. Find the ‘merchant’, make him talk then find his partner and he would find the Heart.  Find the Heart and he would at the least keep his life in his own hands rather then in the hands of a Salt mine taskmaster.

She was sleeping peacefully, and so he casually reached out and squeezed her breast hard. She awoke with a strained face but little more then a whimper. She had been silent all morning, except when asked a question, since taking the Screamers place and now even though in pain she kept quiet. Her eyes were a peaceful shade of green, like Jade but shiny with tears of pain.  Twist liked that. This one knew the rules, no talk unless asked a question and also knew that it could as easily be her out there dying in the street.  Feeling aroused again at her pain Twist thought that perhaps he
would make a game of it. Yes. She was trying so hard to be quiet so he would make her cry out. He wouldn’t actually maim her, he couldn’t afford that, but pain... well that was another matter.

        Fate is fickle, and the MudWater river which usually flows strong and steady through Talisman, instead of sending poor Jug’s body on a trip from which it would never return, deposited him on the shore of a private residence on the other, richer side of the river.  There his bloated corpse was found by a small girl, daughter of the house.
