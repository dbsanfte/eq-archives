By decree of the members present at the formation of the guild known as House Kel'Dar we acknowledge our government herewith and declare ourselves unified with all the powers and judgment of formal assembly. Recognition of name shall be observed herewith as House Kel'Dar.



We of House Kel'Dar bind ourselves together in friendship and camaraderie for the purpose of adventure and fun. We stake our claim to the principles of honor and valor. We abide by the law set forth in the Writ of House Kel'Dar and honor our leaders with our duty. We shun spoilers of fun. We do not propose war with any other. We will defend our members and the honor of House Kel'Dar at all costs. We will uphold the virtues of the Guild's way in all things.



The members of House Kel'Dar shall bear the reigns of government through democratic process by representation through participation. Each member will possess a unique title that represents his/her role in the guild.



The Charge of House Kel'Dar describes and ordains the individual duties of each rank and office within the Guild of House Kel'Dar.



The Leader is the highest-ranking position within the Guild. His/Her duties are to lead House Kel'Dar with honor and valor, to oversee the actions of all members, to uphold the Writ of The House Kel'Dar, to regulate the guild's relations with foreign guilds, and to settle disputes within the ranks. The Leader may implement any actions he/she deems worthy. The Leader may perform promotion and induction ceremonies. Upon the advice of the Justice of The House Kel'Dar, The Leader may implement his/her recommendation for action against a member. The Leader has the right to veto any action of the other members.



The High Lord is second in command, and his word is the same as the Leader’s when she is not present. His leadership must be observed in all situations, and he stands by the Leader in the guild’s decisions.

It is the Officers duty to provide advice to the Leader upon his/her request. This may include reporting on guild status, reporting on foreign guild activity, reporting on guild morale and welfare, reporting on guild activities and events, and any and all other needs as deemed necessary by the Leader. The Officers may perform ceremonies to invite new members if the Leader and Second in Command are unavailable.



The Members must conduct themselves with honor at all times. They must carry the name of House Kel'dar with pride and power, spread the values of the guild and always serve his/her brothers and sisters in loyalty and friendship.



All members are encouraged to recruit. Members must inform the interested party of our theme and what we represent. If a potential candidate has a continued interest in membership they should be directed to the House Kel'Dar web page, review our charter and fill out the application. Two (2) members of House Kel'Dar must support all applications with letters of recommendation, one of them being either the Leader or the Second in Command. Applicants who are chosen to become members, will serve House Kel'Dar on a probationary period to be determined by the Leader on a case by case basis but no less than 1 week. During this probationary period, the new member will be afforded every right and responsibility as that of any member of House Kel'Dar. New members will possess no title untill the Leader and Officers deem it time to make the initiate a full fledged member. An initiate can be removed from the guild during his/her probationary period for any reason and without advanced notice. Any items given to House Kel'Dar or its members will not be returned as they are considered guild property.



Marriages will be observed and respected by all of House Kel'Dar in and outside of our ranks. Marriages of a character between more than two persons will not be recognized. Marriages between a member and a non member and between different races are allowed. Marriage does not require a formal ceremony. If persons wish to make known their marriage in order to have it acknowledged and recognized by Hpuse Kel'Dar, said persons should inform the Leader of their status. Divorce will be recognized by all members. It is the responsibility of the divorcees to inform the Leader of their status if they wish to make known their separation.



The Leader is requested to submit his resignation to the remaining members of the House either in person or via email. All members are requested to submit their resignations to the Leader.



All members of House Kel'Dar shall:
Conduct themselves with honor at all times. One who walks with honor is one whose words, deeds and actions bring respect and the recognition of respect shown to others.
Encourage the feelings of friendship and family to all members of House Kel'Dar.
Be courteous to all persons of the land, even those whose opinions differ from your own.
Help all those in need and offer whatever assistance you can. Special attention should be paid to "newbies". The most valuable help to a newbie is information. Be patient while showing them our world.
Uphold the virtues of House Kel'Dar and represent the guild in a positive way. Each and every member is a representative of House Kel'Dar and the actions of one reflect upon the guild as a whole.
Promotions are based on services to the family, building character and time in guild.
We offer equipment and reagents to guildmembers before we sell them.
We never charge our members for anything, including SOW or other services, even with second chars. If the item sold is valuable, such as armor or weapons, we charge the minimum possible price.
Members of the House Kel'Dar protect each other and die to save a brother's / sister's life.
Our ooc communication is e-mail based. By becoming a member, one will agree to engage in the mailing list as a constant sender/receiver of the messages concerning the guild.



Suggestions are encouraged and desired from all members regardless of rank.
