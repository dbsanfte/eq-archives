HISTORY

All available recordings can be obtained, e-mail us

1978
Andy, Karl and Steve join school band with Bob, Meaders and Cappy called The Defects.
Play 1 gig covering Devo, Joy Division, The Police and Hawkwind songs

1979
Andy and Karl form a second school band called ...and Scratch the Festering Scab.
Play 1 gig at school.

1981
Andy and Karl form Two's Company.
The format comprised of hauling scrap metal onto stage, de-tuning and feeding back the guitar, bass and microphone.
Andy would run round screaming and shouting, dodging bin lids and plastic pipes that were being hit and thrown by Karl.
This would often be preceded by Andy opening the set with a badly played Elvis number.
Sets would typically last about 10 - 15 minutes.
Play many gigs in the North of England, Darlington, Leeds, Sheffield and London.
Thrown off stage in Redcar for being too extreme.
Had all gear siezed by The Leadmill, Sheffield after damaging house PA system.
Played 2 nights at the City Varieties, leeds as part of the Fringe festival

Recorded several songs using multiple tape decks, open reel tape recorders and huge tape loops.
Dogs and C-saw recorded at Lion Studios, Leeds

1988
Bernie forms The Jellymen:
Original members

The jellymen were formed in 1988 by the above to play original non pop agressive indy style music [ for themselves]. If people liked us this was a bonus. The songs were written mainly with bernie & cloughy and arranged by All.

March 2001
Karl meets Jellyman in a pub.
Andy and Ant come to first session and Crumbling Spine is formed.
Format comprises Karl on bass, Jellyman on guitar, Ant on tape loops, Andy on mic.
Nothing is rehearsed because there is nothing to rehearse.
Each session will produce whatever the chemistry and feel dictates, loosely based around background noises and tape loops.

Gigs played by Crumbling Spine
May 2001 - debut gig at the Adelphi Leeds supporting Fred Frith as part of the Termite Club.
July 2001 - Otley Labour Club with Vibracathedral Orchestra and Ronald Arthur Dewhirst supporting.
6th September 2001 - The Packhorse, Leeds with Vibracathedral Orchestra and Ronald Arthur Dewhirst supporting.
