Initial Impressions
The Moongates team arrived at the booth of 989 Studios on May 28th 11:00am to meet with Shon Damron. Nick Pleis met us there as well. I personally went in there with very low expectations for EverQuest. I didn't think too highly of the screenshots I had seen before hand nor was I happy with the non-existent story or the lack thereof. I was there mainly for 1 big reason, hoping for an Ultima Online replacement since I was totally disappointed with what happened with Ultima Online. It says a lot about a game, when the pre-alpha is more fun than the released version. With a mentality like that, I stepped into the 989 Studios room and began hoping for the best. All I can say is that I came out a very happy man.

Shon Damron started showing us a little bit of the game engine by moving around in the town of Qeynos. A little bit later, Brad came over and started giving us a more in-depth look at the game. From the few sentences I exchanged with Brad, my conclusion was that this is a man who really cares about the game he is trying to make. This is the kind of mentality that I wished more games developers have. The thing that struck me the most was when I commented on how similar many of the commands in EverQuest was to MUDs, Brad replied, "Why try to change something that works well and fix something that's not broken." I began to understand more of where the design of EverQuest is headed.

Most of you are probably wondering what I thought of the graphics engine of EverQuest. Five words, "More impressive than I expected". It's one thing to look at screenshots, but seeing the engine in motion really shows how good the engine is.

I did not see one thing I didn't like about EverQuest (barring the unimplemented stuff and the bugs). This is scary, something's not right. I haven't encountered a game in many many years that didn't have something I didn't like. Of course I didn't get to see everything that EverQuest was suppose to be but my impression of EverQuest as of now is flawless.
