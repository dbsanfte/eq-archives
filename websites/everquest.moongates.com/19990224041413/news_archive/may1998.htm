Sorry for the lack of updates here for the past week. I have been extremely busy scouring the E3 floor for juicy bits of info on many many new games that will be coming out. I have gotten mails and irc threats from people asking me to get the E3 interview with Brad McQuaid online. Relax guys, the interview will be posted in due time.

Why the interview isn't posted yet are due to several reasons. I'm extremely exhausted, I had about 12 hours of sleep in total for the entire time I was at E3, there were a lot of "off the record" stuff that was discussed during the interview and I will have to be editing them out and sending the final draft of the interview transcript to Brad for his approval before posting the transcript.

The transcript will be split into 3 parts, each part of the transcript will be residing on 3 different sites, namely EQ Vault, GameWise and of course right here at Moongates Press. There will also be an in-depth review of the stuff that Enj (GameWise) and I saw at E3. There will also be an article on EQX discussing the interview with Brad and some comments about what is happening with the development of EQ from all the participating sites in that article.

The Moongates Press exclusive screenshots will also arrive soon after the EQ Public Relations guy, Shon Damron (He's the MAN!), returns from Hawaii. We will also have some scans of the photos we took during the interview with Brad post up that will include some very interesting scenes that the EQ team members are very excited about. These photos will again be posted on the 3 sites I mentioned earlier. So rest assured that YOU will be getting real juicy bits of info by the end of the week.

On a final note, EQ is going to rock hardcore, I sh*t you not, I haven't seen 1 thing I didn't like about the game after playing it at least an hour. The #everquest folks who showed up as well had almost the same thing to say too, EQ was so much better than what we had expected.  SeVeN

May 3 1998
2:25am EST
I read a letter tonight that hit a tender spot and I'd like to share some thoughts on it. I know that a lot of people who are interested in EQ are UO refuges. Being either frustrated with the bugs or lag in UO, or with the almost non-existent Role Playing element. Sith Dragon wrote this letter I'm referring to which is mainly a farewell letter to the people who frequent his Ultima 9 site informing them as to the reason why he is shutting down the site. Here is an excerpt from his letter :

"I've seen it go from simplistic, nearly non-existent gameplay to being enthralling epics that kept me glued to my computer for far longer than was healthy. I've watched as Ultima went from being a disk in a ziplock bag to being one of the cornerstones of the most-developed genre in computer gaming and, unfortunately, I watched as it died."

Ultima is one of the most beloved CRPG series and is my personal favorite RPG series for the PC platform. Sith Dragon's letter really said it all for me, about what I feel is happening to the Ultima series and more or less to many new games coming out nowadays, where economics takes precedence over quality of the game.

You might wonder why I'm putting up such a long blurb about Ultima on an EverQuest related website. Well, I've been following the development of Ultima Online since the pre-alpha test. I have seen the members of the UO team go from friendly with everyone to outright refusing to admit anything is wrong with the game. They also begin to give those websites who would only say good things about UO more benefits than the sites that would be more unbiased in their appraisal of the game.

Honestly, I'm getting a sense of deja vu here with EQ. The EQ team is very much like the UO team at this point in their development period. Due to my disappointment with UO, I'm dreading that the day might come, if EQ doesn't live up to it's promises, that the EQ team would end up like the UO team. Hopefully this will not come to pass and that EQ would be the game that UO was suppose to be.  Let economics not dictate what EQ should be but what you as a lover of the RPG genre want it to be. I would like to wish the EQ team Godspeed and Good Luck on their endeavour to bring the game out to us.

You can also read Sith Dragon's letter in it's entirety at Sith Dragon's Ultima : Ascension site.SeVeN
