Q&A With Brad McQuaid - editorial by Kildaren
Page 3/3
Question: I'm asking this again in case you lost it with all the disconnects... Will there be strength limits on weapons and armor? to balance out the game. Having powerful mages with very powerful armor is not good, and will your hands have to be empty to cast spells?

Answer: There will be limits to who can wield and use different times, and this is indeed to balance the game. To cast spells you do not have to remove the weapons in your hands. *done*

Comment:  It only makes sense in a class-based system. In my opinion, this was one of Ultima Online’s greatest failings: there are certainly "best weapons" and "best armor" that 75% or more of the players wield. Of course, this stems partially from the fact that in UO any character can learn any skill, with no restrictions. It will be nice to see weapons equippable only by specific classes, or armor restrictions on spellcasters. It should also make balancing out the play classes easier.

--------

Question: When people talk in EverQuest will the text go above there heads or on a chat screen? I think it would be good if there was a chat screen but have it so that you only hear the closest 12 or so people on the screen. I think that would be better than it appearing over there heads as they walk around.

Answer: There is a chat screen under the 3d view -- messages do not appear above player's heads. One of the reasons for this (but there are many) is that you can receive 'tells' from people anywhere in the world, not just people in your view.

Comment:  I prefer seeing text messages appear above the players’ heads. It gives a sense of realism, and it means that it’s much tougher to talk in front of other players without them overhearing, so to speak. Additionally, I find it easier just to look at the main play screen, rather than occasionally glancing down to see if somebody is talking to me. (I hated this system on Meridian 59, for example.) Maybe EverQuest will surprise me, however.

--------

Question: Will the game have weather effects? if so, will the terrain and its surrounding change with the weather?

Answer: There will be weather effects, including rain and snow, and they will change the physics model of the area you are in. An example: surfaces will become slippery when it snows.

Comment:  Weather effects will be nice, visually. I wonder if they’ll affect characters? Will players in plate armor suffer from heatstroke quicker than those in robes? Personally, I hope that this level of realism isn’t introduced. It only serves as an annoyance, since it requires players to change clothing and thus battle strategies when traveling. There is a limit to how much realism we need in a game.

--------

Question: Can you tell us anything about the availability of catapults and siege engines? I seem to recall a screenshot something that looked suspiciously like on in the background.

Answer: Can't comment as to siege engines, other than to say, yes that's what you saw in that screenshot.

Comment:  If siege engines are actually player manipulable, it could add a whole new dimension to combat. Imagine breaking up a mob of orcs with your trusty ballista!

--------

Question: Are boats hollow? Will things be able to be stowed away below deck, and will you be able to go down into the boat?

Answer: The larger ships are indeed explorable and have rooms and such.

Comment:  I must admit that this sounds great to me. I’ll really enjoy being able to board a ship and roam through it, fighting my way into the treasure hold. It doesn’t really add much else, but the fun factor could potentially be boosted by this enhancement.
