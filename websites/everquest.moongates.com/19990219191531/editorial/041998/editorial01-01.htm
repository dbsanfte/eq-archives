Q&A With Brad McQuaid - editorial by Kildaren
Page 1/3
A couple of weeks back, EverQuest designer Brad McQuaid was kind enough to participate in an IRC session and answer some fans’ questions about the upcoming game. We here at wewp.com thought that the gaming community at large might have an interest in seeing exactly what was said. Some of the questions and his corresponding answers are reproduced below.  Questions are in bold; answers are in normal text. My personal commentary follows in italics.

Question: Are the classes of magic in EverQuest individual to each class that uses them? For example, can any class learn from 2 different classes of magic?

Answer: As to how long they'll be -- well, until we've tested what we've wanted to for that phase. There is really no way to predict this stuff accurately ahead of time.

Most of the spells are unique to that class, however there is some overlap. For instance, both a cleric and a shaman can cast healing spells. The exact nature of these spells and their names might be different though.

Comment: Realms of spellcasting is a great idea. Having every player able to cast every spell quickly cheapens the mystery and excitement of magic. I’d also like to see monsters that can cast spells unavailable to players. Maybe we’ll even get to see magic that’s only accessible after an arduous journey to some hidden temple or library.

--------

Question: Does the new modeling system allow for guild skins or are you going to wait till post ship to implement that (if ever)?

Answer: The new modeling system does indeed allow for guild skins and it is our intent to support those at ship, not after. How many guilds we can support though is still undetermined.

Comment:  "Guild skins" sounds like an interesting idea, if a bit Quake-ish. At a minimum, it’ll let warring factions easily pick each other out in a large battle.

 --------

Question: Could you tell us more about how experience will be used to buy levels, stats, or skills?

Answer: Experience isn't used to buy skills or stats per se. Your skills go up as you use them, but they can only go up so much per level, and then also at certain levels you are allowed to learn new skills.

Comment:  This seems to me like a limited implementation of the Ultima Online system. Personally, I’d rather see skills rise by a set percentage once a level is gained, or alternately allowing a character a set number of skill points to assign as he wants each level.

 --------

Question: Will Monsters gain experience? Will they be tougher in their native habitats?

Answer: Monsters don't gain experience, no, but they will fight differently depending on the terrain and time of day.

Comment:  If the designers can pull it off, this will speak volumes for the enjoyment of EverQuest. Smart monsters whose tactics vary according to the surroundings would be great opponents. It may even cut down on player killing: many of the pkillers I know hunt other gamers because it’s more of a challenge. The smarter the monster AI, the less reason there is to hunt players.

--------

Question: Do you intend to allow for volunteer GM's who can lead their friends on quests, or will it be your hired ones only?

Answer: At this point the plan is to allow some members of the public to be GMs, but I doubt at the same level of access and abilities as those who are Sony Employees.

Comment:  Sony should reconsider this. UO implemented a very similar system with Counselors and Seers; all it did was breed resentment and lead to widespread abuses.

-------- 

Question: Will quests be reserved for larger guilds, or will we see NPC dictated adventure for those who opt for a party-based system?

Answer: There will be plenty of quests for all sorts of people, whether individual, or in parties, or members of large guilds. As for GM run quests, we don't have infinite time and resources, so these will be limited, but not necessarily limited only to guilds.

Comment:  Questing is the meat of a CRPG: the more, the better. A common complaint in other online games is that there’s a lack of focus; nobody feels like there’s anything to do except kill monsters, gain gold, and increase stats. Adding even generic quests can alleviate much of this. Custom quests are even better, of course, since they can change and react to player actions.

-------- 

Question: Will there be player owned vendors in the game, and will they have a weight and item limit?

Answer: We think a significant portion of the economy will be people who buy, sell, and trade in towns and elsewhere. We don't feel we need to make them merchants 'officially' for them do be able to do this, nor do they require any additional abilities. Basically, anyone can be a merchant or a vendor.

Comment:  UO had the same system, until Origin realized that player owned vendors were much desired. Perhaps we’ll see the same evolution as a result of the EverQuest beta test?

-------- 

Question: How severe will the NDA be? For example, can we maintain journals of our beta adventures if we do not make specific technical references or include pictures?

Answer: the NDA for the first few phases will have to be very strict, which means no information regarding the game can be published.

Comment:  This is unfortunate: it may mean that everquest.moongates.com and other gaming sites can’t provide the coverage the gaming public desires.

-------- 

Question: Are you guys gonna try to make sure that every class is equal and has the same advantages? In a lot of games magery has been the most powerful skill and over shadows others.

Answer: game balance is very important to us, including class balancing. We want to make sure all of the classes have compelling reasons behind them to play them, and want to make sure they are all useful in the game.

Comment:  Hopefully this will be the prime focus of the beta test. Code stability, minimizing lag, and other technical issues are all important, but the main thing that Sony needs to remember is that this is a game. "Game" implies "fun." Other online games have suffered from having one or two essential skills, without which the game is minimally enjoyable. Similarly, other online games have more useless skills than chickens have feathers.

--------

Question: How is the gameplay going to be through a 28.8 connection? I have played all the online RPGs and the lag seems to be a big problem. Do you think EQ is going to be better in terms of "lag" than, say, Ultima Online currently is?

Answer: I don't think most of the lag people are experiencing on these games is because they are using a 28.8. It's usually either server based lagged or their ISPs, or somewhere in the internet between them and the servers. We are designed the game such that we aren't sending out more data than a 28.8 can handle.

We are doing several things to reduce lag, including using very mature network code (descended from our Tanarus game), as well as keeping the number of people down per server, to reduce server lag.

Comment:  All I can say is this: I hope it’s true. I’d be happy to see a game where the maximum number of players in a world is limited in order to keep playability high. The problem here is that this essentially sets a limit on profitability. Can Sony actually be persuaded to take a potential profit hit in exchange for satisfied customers? It’s a rare thing in today’s software world.
