Q&A With Brad McQuaid - editorial by Kildaren
Page 2/3
Question: Beta Testing: when, where, how... what can you tell us?

Answer: The applications will be up within the next couple of weeks, but as to when the actual tests will start, all I can say is when we are ready. We're trying not to rush things so we can do it right, however we are working long and hard to get the game to a point where it's testable by people outside Sony.

Comment:  The application is currently up, as most people reading this know. One of the closed site apps mentioned that people chosen would be contacted in early April.

--------

Question: will hybrid races be able to cast their respective spells with weapons armed?

Answer: You don't have to remove your weapons to cast a spell, no. *done*

Comment:  Good! This removes what I felt was one of the greatest irritations in Ultima Online. Allowing spell use while holding weapons especially makes sense with the Paladin and Shadow Knight classes. For that matter, why should a mage have to put away his staff when casting a spell?

-------- 

Question: A lot of debate has been brought up about how hard it will be do raise skills etc., how will skills be raised? Will it be on a level/distribute points scale or more along the lines of practice a lot while you level?

Answer: Your skills go up as you use them, but they can only go up so much per level, and certain skills won't be available to the player until he reaches certain levels.

--------

Question: Can you comment on the availability of boats and houses? Will they be in the individual ownership or guild only price range?

Answer: I don't want to say 'guild only', but they will be very expensive and you won't see many who have them -- boats and houses are exotic items in EverQuest, something you'll have to work hard and long for and can be very proud of owning.

Comment:  Hopefully this will prevent the world from getting too crowded with player housing. Houses and boats should be special items, not something you can buy after a single day’s work.

--------

Question: Will combat be conducted in a 3d shooter style manner or in a UO style where you simply double click to attack and let the AI take over?

Answer: Closer to the UO style, because this is not a 'twitch' game, but not identical to UO either.

Comment:  No comment, except to say that I’m glad we won’t be playing a "twitch" game. Lag makes that style of game too unplayable by some people.

--------

Question: Will there be small scattered islands surrounding the larger continents, or just 1 or 2 islands?

Answer: I suggest you explore the world and find out -- I don't want to take away the surprise and fun of exploration.

Comment:  I’m hoping that this comment means that the world of EverQuest will be huge, taking at least several play weeks to thoroughly discover. The bigger, the better.

--------

Question: Will the programmers actually play the game or will they just take input from their "playing" designers?

Answer: When I assembled the team I made sure many of the artists, programmers, and world builders were into this genre. The entire team's input counts and they've all contributed to this game quite a bit. Accordingly I wouldn't be a bit surprised to see most all of them play the game quite often. This is a game we've all wanted to make and PLAY for many years.

Comment:  This is important. I’d also suggest that the designers play under actual playtesting conditions: namely, using a system with minimum requirements, connected to the game through a modem dialing into a local ISP. If this is done, then the designers will have a much better idea of what the average player faces, and can possibly empathize with lag or networking complaints. It’s also important that the designers create a character from scratch and run through all the gauntlets a regular player faces. Otherwise it’s too common for employees to think that "code is stable" or that "there’s really no problem with spawning/skill gain/stat gain/gripe of the day," because they haven’t really tried to examine those issues from a player’s point of view.

--------

Question: How do you intend to build an online cohesive society in EverQuest? What elements do you think will add to this environment?

Answer: I think the focus we have on this not just being a player vs. player game but rather also a very cooperative game will help build a sense of community. Allowing people to group, to talk to other party members, and to talk to people regardless of where they are will help this. And the ability to split experience amongst a group will add to this as well. Also, keeping the server population down will enable people to recognize names more easily.

Comment:  Experience splitting is a great idea. The Ultima Online pre-alpha had this feature, and it was one of the things I was very sorry to see disappear. It’s a good way to promote player interaction on a level other than as mutual cannon fodder.   It also allows experienced players to bring friends that may have just joined the game "up to speed," so to speak. Allowing chat between groups/guilds regardless of world location may be a bit out of character, but it’s really just an acknowledgement of reality. I have yet to meet a regular adventuring group in UO that doesn’t use ICQ or IRC to stay in contact. These ideas suggest that EverQuest is pointed in the right direction: namely, ensuring that players enjoy the game environment.

--------

Question: Will there be unique items, such that only one or a few people will have them? And also, are magic items in general in abundance or scarce?

Answer: There will be unique items called artifacts and I don't expect too many people to be awarded them, but please feel free to prove me wrong :). As for magical items, yes, many. A significant part of EQ is the amassing of magical items with all sorts of nifty abilities.

Comment:  This is something I’ve wanted to see for a long time. Some MUDs have implemented this, and it’s always fulfilling when a character completes a quest and obtains a legendary item. The flip side is that it will be really important to have these beta tested thoroughly: one "superweapon" can easily imbalance the entire game. It’s incumbent on the beta testers to watch out for this problem and honestly report it if it occurs, even if that particular beta tester has the item.

--------

Question: How much will the God you choose to worship affect your character? Is spirituality just a role-playing guideline, like alignment, or does it have in-game effects (say, different blessings from different gods)?

Answer: It definitely affects your player in the game.

Comment:  This is just great. Paladins, clerics, and monks should certainly be different from each other based on their deity of choice. It would even make sense that other classes would derive some small benefits or restrictions from their choice of god. Perhaps a mage’s fire-based spells are more effective if he worships the God of Fire, but he takes a hit when casting ice or water spells, for example. This single statement presents a way for EverQuest to stand out if the designers pursue it.
