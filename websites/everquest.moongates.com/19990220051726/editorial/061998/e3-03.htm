E3 Preview/Report - by SeVeN
Page 3/12
Interview
Brad: When?

Us: At release.

Brad: I’m not saying that we’ll get the game totally bug free at release, so we will be able to update the game. In addition to that, let’s say we add a new area to explore, you could grab that. But during closed beta expect a hell of a lot of patches.

Us: Will the patches be real time as you’re playing the game?

Brad: It will check your version when you log in -- if we’re doing a major change it will force you to log out.

Us: Can you tell us a little bit about the storyline?

Brad: Well… We’re kind of purposely being vague about that. That’s where the quests come in. EverQuest, hence the name, is going to really be heavy on quests. We can’t promise a quest for everybody all the time but there will be a lot. What we want to do is reveal the history of the world and storyline through quests. So that’s why you’re not going to see a lot of the history of the world published. We want people to play the game – they might find a clue here or a mystery there – or maybe you’re looking for some staff of the ancients – whatever – that is pretty much how we want to reveal it. We’re not going to spill the beans too much on that. We think it would take a lot of fun out of the game if we did.

Us: Do you think you have a plan on how you want the plot to develop? Or will it basically be players who kind of push the storyline forward?

Brad: Both. What we’ve done is create a very detailed history encompassing several thousand years, which we have been working on for a couple of years. Bill Trost has tons of stuff written and others on the team have contributed -- we have a lot of really big gamers [on the team]. So a lot of that is written, and you’ll find quests and documents, etc. You might start out with a scroll telling you a little bit about your character – (points at his character on the screen). I’m a ranger so my scroll tells me a little bit about my guildmaster. You’ll find these scrolls and books and different things in the world and they will reveal different parts of the story. Then once we start the game it’s you guys and it’s your exploits. We’ll have boards in the game where you can post your roleplaying stories -- I’m sure it’ll be all over the web where you can tell of your adventures -- "We went up against the Wyvern and died", etc. That’s the fun of it. We don’t want to take that away. We want to give you guys the foundation to roleplay with.

Us: I’m looking at something on the screen now where in your quest you can earn the title of "Protector." Are you doing something along the lines of Might and Magic 6 where you have titles within your class?

Brad: Yeah we can grant titles, we can basically do that. There’ll be different descriptions for different levels. (Brad begins to show us around the Ranger guild. It’s a beautiful house floating on water)

Brad: There’s just so much to show guys.
