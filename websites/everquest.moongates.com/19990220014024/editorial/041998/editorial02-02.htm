So what's the most important aspect of an online game? - editorial by Abzzstain
Page 2/2
So what can we expect from Everquest? Well, so far I think the screenshots look very promising. The chat is where it belongs, in a separate area from the actual game view. Comments from the Sony staff show that they understand the importance of chat and online relationships, and all they mean for the community. As great as the game looks in all the other areas, the chat system and support for the community are what make me so anxious to play Everquest.

Because after all, someday I'll have seen it all and done everything there is to do. But I expect that I'll have many old friends, and many more new ones, that I'll want to see every day. The world gets old after a while, but friendships never do. It just depends on whether the gamemakers want to go for the quick bucks and wow us for a few short months, or want to keep us coming back from now on.

Give me lots of bells, whistles, and other cool THINGS to see and do, and I'll keep coming back ... for a while.

But give me someONE to come back to, and I'm here for good.

It all comes down to communication ... Origin obviously didn't care that its customers had things to say. Here's to hoping that Sony won't turn a deaf ear.
