E3 Preview/Report - by SeVeN
Page 2/12
Interview
Us: How about if you show us a bit of the game first?

Brad: Here’s character creation. You pick your race, and here you can customize from a lot of different faces (seemingly endless face variations fill the screen. As Brad switches races the facial types change also). As you can see based on what race you select, it determines what class you can be. Like I’ve said on the net many times, the reason we haven’t released what classes you can be depending on the race you select is because it may very well change during beta. So what you see here may change. Our intent is that humans are the most flexible [race] because they’re also the most mundane in a sense, so since they’re disadvantaged in other areas, we made them the most broad in terms of what classes they can be (whereas an Ogre is much more limited to warrior type classes, but again all this might change during the beta). Since Ogres and Trolls have such an innate advantage in terms of size and strength (for example, Trolls can regenerate), they are much more limited in terms of classes. (Brad now creates a human ranger, which he describes as "more my style")

Brad: You have different ability points to choose from… we kind of went away from rolling your character since you’ll just get somebody who will roll it all day long anyway. So you have so many ability points to distribute, and that is based on which class and race you selected. If I switch to necromancer, you can see he gets more and I can use these to adjust my initial stats. I’ll give myself a little more strength here, etc. Then you go over to the deity selection screen, which is again based on your race and class. What choosing a deity does is determine how the NPCs in the world will react to you. Once you’re in the game what you do is up to you, but this gives you a little personality from the get go. Then you bring up the city screen which basically because I am a human I can choose to start in Freeport or Qeynos. This kind of gives you a perspective of where you’re starting out in the world. About a quarter of the world is up and running on our servers. When we start closed beta about a quarter of the world will be there. You’ll log on one day [during the beta] and say "Oh a new city is up!" and we’ll continue to expand and expand. So I’ll start out in Qeynos here. So I just created a character, we’re currently at 8 characters per server. Let me select my character and we’ll go into the world…

Us: How much of the world will actually be on the client side?

Brad: Basically like any other game, your graphics and world is on the client side, but your critical data like characters are on the server side. Static information that isn’t going to change is on the client side. And that typically is most of your graphics -- you don’t want to be downloading those.

Us: This brings up the idea of patching. Will you be doing a lot of client side patching or more server side patching?
