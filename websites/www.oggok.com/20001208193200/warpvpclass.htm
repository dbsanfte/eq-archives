"Warrior PvP" nice play on words eh? Its a well known fact that warriors (and all Melee classes) are the most difficult classes to play for PvP encounters. This guide will hopefully shed some light on the best way to play a Warrior, and to stay alive, and hopefully be successful in player vs. player combat. This guide is also geared for the level 20+ players. If I do not mention a class here then it is one I have never had experience fighting against.
WARRIOR PvP GUIDE

PVP vs. Melee Classes:
One of the most important rules you will learn is to NEVER stop moving. This is extremely important, why? Because your opponent won't stop moving. Your target will do their best to use your size against you (especially for Trolls and Ogres). They will do everything they can to get behind you, beside you, or as close to you or just out of your weapon range, so that you can't hit them. There are many good methods to combating your opponent. A good method is to act like your running away then suddenly pull backwards until your target is in front of you, then move in for a shot to there backside. In my PvP experiences the most successful warriors are those that master controlling their character with their Keyboard/Mouse. One of the most potent styles of moving through a fight is the circling melee. It is performed by hold down your right mouse key and using your direction keys on your keyboard to circle around your target leaving them in one spot for a few seconds enough time to land a good blow. It also gives you advantages of keeping your target in view most of the time. Many players when faced with these tactics will stop and spin in place to try to keep you in view. This is often their deadly mistake. They become open to your attack and their attack becomes useless.

Rangers:
Rangers are a tough lot to kill, but fortunately for us there damage spells aren't very powerful, so they can't simply sit back and nuke like a caster can. This means that they will have to do some type of melee with you to do damage, and that is a bad position for them. Their first attack will be to snare you. If you are snared, wait for them to come to you and time your attack by keeping your weapon attack turned off, then quickly turn it on as they get close. This causes your weapon to go off just as they come in range. After a few shots for 40 to 50 points and possibly a double attack on them for close to 80+ they will back off and will usually run. This is where a ranged weapons (bow & arrows) come in handy. This is a skill that is Way under utilized by the warrior class. Many use bows to pull monsters and such but don't take the advantage that it gives seriously. To a warrior with full armor and 600+HP a bow is little threat to our lives on their own. But to a ranger that has half as many HP and not near as good AC this can be a weapon to be dealt with when in a warrior's hands.

Monks:
Monks pose a real problem especially at higher levels. They are extremely fast and do a lot of damage in a short period of time. There are 2 schools of though on fighting monks.

1. Because they are so fast, a warrior would be best to use two 1H Slash weapons that has as slow of a delay as possible (Dragoon Dirks or FS Spears). This will give you the best chance of hitting a monk when they come in for the attack. Warriors are very dependant on HP and AC at this point. If you can nickel and dime them for long enough they will run out of HP before you do.

2. Because they are so fast, you won't be able to hit them very often regardless of your weapon. Since your damage shots will be at a minimum against this class it is best to do as much damage as possible with a powerful 2H Slash weapon. When they charge for the attack they're probably going to hit for around 30+ damage in that combat round (base level 20) but you hitting them once for 50 (with something like a BIBS) is really going to upset the balance in your favor.

Shadow Knights:
This class can be a tough class to fight. At level 20+ they can do massive amounts of damage in a short period of time. They can use dot spells, and can deliver a Harm Touch (Level x 10). There best DoT spell is "Engulfing Darkness" which is a very long lasting snare type spell. This is a fight that you want to treat similar to fighting a pure caster. You need to get on them and attack relentlessly, to try to disrupt their spells. When they do get a spell off this is where you want to have as much Magic Resistance as you can get. With enough Magic Resistance you can resist these DoT spells, and you can even resist their HT. If possible you want to force them into an enclosed space and force them into pure melee combat (this works very well in the Qeynos sewer system) save bash/slam for when they start to cast. If you can force them into a pure melee fight you will win hands down. No other class can out damage a warrior in pure melee. If the fight isn't going their way, they are going to cast "shadow step" so be ready to bash/slam if you see it coming. If they do get the ST off immediately look around you especially in wide open areas with no cover. A bow can be very handy to keep them from casting again, but be prepared for them to get away.

Paladins:
Not nearly as tough as a SK but darned annoying, especially if they use lay on hands. Paladins have no major offensive spells and can be dealt with mostly as a monk except with better defensive qualities and less offensive qualities. Its very important to make sure they cant cast heal on themselves or you will be in a world of hurt later, but even with a few heals if they are around the same level you should still be able to win. Now, dealing with lay hands is a different matter. If they cast Lay Hands and you still have around 2 1/2 bars of life left, keep fighting for it takes them a while to switch to themselves cast the spell and then start attacking again, that is usually enough time for 2 combat rounds, (now imagine hitting for max damage and having double attacking going off twice, 200 damage with something like a BIBS, and around level 20 they will only have about 650+ HP, that's a major blow to them.) Another thing is that more than once has a Paladin in his haste cast Lay Hands on me by accident. :)

Rogues:
If your fighting a good rogue there much more difficult than you think, if there skill is level 30+ at level 20 they can backstab for 70+ damage, not including there other weapons, duel wield and double attack make them little machine guns. Of course, what you want to do is keep your back to the wall at all time making sure they can't get a backstab in, the problem with this is more than once has a rogue snuck behind my backside while it was next to a wall and preformed a back stab. Fortunately for us, that's all they really have going for them, they have the same amount of HPs as a Shaman and if you can land a few good blows they will almost always run. Many will use the Hide and Move tactic so that they can sneak behind you and give you a good shot. Their problem is that they need to stop moving to hide. If you stay on them you can usually land a shot and disrupt their hide attempt. If they DO hide then keep trying to shoot your bow because you will still have them targeted and you don't have to see them to hit them it, and that will bring them out of hiding and give you time to use your melee weapon. Don't worry to much about poisons, you need to be a master potter to make them and they are quite expensive so most rogues don't even bother. But if the do poison you be careful.

Warrior:
Stay on the move! They are your equals so you will have to use strategy. Move around constantly and every once in a while go in for the attack and pull out, being a warrior yourself you should know what to expect and how to counter it. If you have more hit points than them and better equipment you can always just pull them into a pure melee. Generally when similar characters meet the person with the best control over their characters movement will win.

Wizard:
These are the classes that devastate us, of course no one is invincible and we can always gain the upper hand if we know how. There are a few important things to remember about fighting any caster. GET ON THEM! If you can engage and hit they will have to spend time retreating. Casters have the lowest HP and AC of any one you will face in the game. A few good shots mixed in with a bash/slam here and there can disrupt their spells and keep them off balance. Magic resistance is of great importance here as well. Get as many magic resistance items as you can. It can be the difference between life and death. If a caster gets resisted once or twice they are in serous trouble and generally won't have enough mana left to kill you by nuking you. Another important factor against casters: always use ranged weapons. Some bows have a range that is further than the casters range, so you can nickel and dime them from a safe distance if they Shadow Step. Don't get your hopes up though. Once they Shadow Step you can bet they are running for the closest zone or safe spot.

Enchanters:
I HATE these guys. Enthrall is what is going to bring you down. GET ON THEM! As SOON as enthrall drops try to interrupt there casting so they can't cast again and if they pull off another mez or enthrall, your in bad shape. Another problem is the Rune series of spells, you wont be able to hit them but they still can cast. When fighting a enchanter, its best to get on them and do major damage to them or to get to a safe spot.

Necromancer:
They have similar spells to SK's but with more powerful dots and pets. Unfortunately due to dark pack and allure of death you can't count on them running out of mana. The best you can do is GET ON THEM and try to disrupt them as much as possible. Get them into an enclosed space if possible. Also count on having their pet on you but I suggest ignoring it. If you can kill the Necro the pet dies as well. The main problem your going to run into is their life drain. They will life tap you and dot you then kite you. If you feel yourself falling into this situation, my only suggestion is to get out of it. Run to a zone or safe spot. Most of the time the best you can hope to get against a Necro is a stale mate. Magic Resistance can help you accomplish this.

Mage:
With the mage you will face some of the same problems as with the Necro but your chances of victory are somewhat higher if you are prepared. As you probably know mages use the earth pet and they use root as their attack. There is one sure way to nullify their pet though. MAGIC RESISTANCE! MAGIC RESISTANCE! MAGIC RESISTANCE! Their pet becomes virtually useless once you can resist their root spell. Once this happens you can ignore the pet and go after the mage. When you do happen to get rooted, switch to a bow and arrow. Small amounts of damage still effect the casters in a larger way than they do us. Then the mages are just going to sit back and cast on you. Always try to keep them at bay to make sure they can't cast, and like most other casters, just try to use ranged weapons to keep them from pulling off spells. You are probably going to have to ignore the pet for it has too many HPs and casts root too often it wont be effective to devote your time to it. When the earth elemental's Root spell starts to fail chase down the mage and keep him running until he starts to try to cast a DD spell, then go in and use bash/slam and a weapon attack.

Druid:
Probably one of the toughest class to face in PvP. Basically your only hope is to lure them into a space where you can keep track of them and where they don't have many places to run, unfortunately that's not going to happen to often. They are, basically no matter WHAT going to kite you. Snare, SS, DD, that's what is going to take you down. I suggest investing in a few Pumice Stones to take those spells off yourself. This is also another case where MAGIC RESISTANCE is important. If you can resist one or two spells, and pumice off one or two spells the druid isn't going to have enough mana to kill you. Also another trick is to run up and pumice them when they look like that stupid mutt dog. Once they're back in their true form they will almost always run for the hills because their class is only one or two spells deep, and that mutt spell is the main one. Nullifying it, usually nullifies them. If you have no MR, and no Pumice Stone, then my suggestion is to run.

Cleric:
Clerics are kind of a strange class. They can do melee damage (not enough for us to be extremely worried about) and magic damage (again not enough to kill us)but their biggest attribute is HEAL. If you have good magic resistance and can hit them for a few good damaging blows be prepared for a blind spell or them to try to heal themselves. Keep a bash/slam saved for them once you see them start to cast. If you can stay on them long enough they are yours.
