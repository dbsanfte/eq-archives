TALE of OGRE HISTORY

"Gather now young ones" Lorgrim shouted out "It is time for another story." It seemed as if the last word of his sentence had caused a stampede. At least two dozen children appeared out of tunnels cut into the wall of the chamber or from behind the large stack of practice weaponry and armor that hung from racks long the wall. The guild had grown and so had the need to have someone watch the members children as they hunted and so the guild officers agreed to take turns watching over and teaching the young the arts, physical and magical. Some of the officers balked at this though, it was like a jail sentence and they would rather be stuck baby-sitting orc pawns, but Lorgrim knew that they felt the same as he did when they saw the future of the guild, proud. It was times like this Lorgrim wondered why he had never tried to have another child since Lori, she was so talented, imagine the first Ogress necromancer since the great war of the gods. A wave of sadness passed over Lorgrim, how much he missed his daughter.

"Uncle Lorgrim, we are all here now" a small voice chimed waking him from his memories. He recognized the voice even before he saw young Selrina's face or the tattoo of his guildmate Ashensoul's house upon her arm. Selrina had shown amazing leadership qualities for such a young age and all the officers watched and groomed her, to help her take her position as one of the guilds leaders one day.

Glancing around Lorgrim did a quick headcount to verify that all the children where present. He only counted twenty four, there was one missing, then lorgrim noticed a small set of flailing legs and heard the muffled cry of an elf child.

"Zurgre, how many times must I tell you watch where you are sitting?! You have sat down on Tramble again!" Lorgrim worked hard not to laugh, it was Zudz young boy and even for an ogre he tended to be a bit thick but like his father the boy was all heart.

"Huh, oh, ya" as Zurgre stood, Tramble, a tiny dark elf boy scrambled out from under the ogre child and sat catching his wind with a terrified look on his face.

"I think I saw Innoruk" Tramble reported, then his face contorted into one of those faces only children can do "He was laughing at me." This sent an instant ripple of laughter through the group of children, even Lorgrim smiled. Tramble, Just like his father Thambala was always the joker, though in that family it was a good way of avoiding jail when they where caught with their hand in an others purse.

"OK enough now children its time for your lesson". Twenty five faces stopped and looked up at him in sudden disappointment.

"But Uncle Lorgrim you said it was time for another story, not a lesson", Azeranth announced very even and diplomatic voiced. Agerath had taught his son well, the boy was growing to be a very level headed troll and was a natural at all the trade skills already making earrings for the girls in the class as well as mock armor for the boys. He was a very popular young troll.

"Your right Azeranth I did say story," Lorgrim smiled at the boy "but many things in this world will teach you lessons and I hope this story will be one of those things." This seemed to please the children after all a story is a story even if a lesson is part of it.

"As you all know this age has been dubbed the Age of Strife, but today I am going to tell you a story that will span from the Golden Age till the Age of Darkness". Lorgrim settled into the chair cut out of the stone face of the wall. "This story begins within the Golden age when Ogres ruled over the lands, wars were brief and cities stayed untouched by the machines of war, as was laid out by the law. The Great Tier'dal, or dark elf as they are known now, built towers for the sole purpose of studying and storing knowledge both arcane and academic. This knowledge was shared as the Tier'dal held open classes and debates for all to attend, these libraries grew so large that a single being could not read all that was stored within even one of them".

"But then times changed, the humans came brought by their gods to this plane. These despicable beings waged war among themselves constantly, and this violent mentality began to surface even in the great ogre legions. The humans also stole knowledge from the Tier'dal. Spells and information that would be free to them in time was stolen not learned. With the stolen spells not properly monitored the humans opened portals to other planes and freed the creatures there to attack our world. General discord spread over the land as brother fought brother and monsters roamed the lands destroying all they could see".

"The Ogre Emperor, Rogan Soulcrusher, called for his trusted allies in the Tier'dal and Troll nations to rally their troops and help cleanse the lands of these monsters, and so began the Age of Cleansing. For many years the combined troops fought back the tide of creatures till only a trickle came through the portals. The magic users of the allied nations began the long process of sealing the portals, and were forced into seclusion to finish this massive spell. For all the years that the allied nations fought back, the monster hoards, the humans, and their new allies, the forsaken Elves, Dwarfs, and Gnomes, grew in strength. For every fallen Ogre, Troll or Tier'dal another warrior grew to power in the human's alliance.

Then the day fell upon the allied nations, the Human alliance attacked the allied nation while their mages were away sealing the portals and the allied troops were low in numbers from the long battle with the monsters. This began the Age of War, when it looked as if the great alliance would fall to the Humans and their allies, but it was not to be. The Ogre Emperor had a hidden weapon. Even as the Ogre stronghold itself was being sieged, the Emperor used a magic stone to call forth the hidden mages from their seclusion. With the arrival of the great mages, the humans were caught by surprise between the remainder of the allied troops and the most powerful mages of Norrath. The attacking armies were decimated in a single day, but only at a great cost. Many of the mages fell, and most of the remaining troops where lost. With the loss of so many mages the allies had no way of closing the portals and were forced to monitor them and attack the forces that would trickle though. The Ogre Emperor enraged at the damage done to the lands by the Human alliance, ordered the rebuilding of his armies at all cost, and with a great decree announced war upon the human nations. The ferocity of the allied forces was a sight many would wish they had never seen. The allied forces destroyed village after village, razed city after city till only a few gathering places were safe for the humans. Once again victory was taken from the allied forces as the pathetic humans cried out to their gods. Their gods answered with a wraith that smashed the strongest allied armies in moments. As the human gods forced the allied nations to flee, many of the human nations stole what remained behind in the allied cities. The human alliance gained so much knowledge so fast that their greed kept them from joining the attack their gods had begun. Instead they sat back laughing, and growing in knowledge. The lack of help that the human gods received may have been what saved the allied nations as the Tier'dal retreated to the place that the mages had created to work their portal closing magic, and sealed the great underground vault to rebuild with most of their knowledge intact. The Ogre and Troll nations did not have such a place and ran for the depths of the swamps, the ogres found a chasm that was easily defensible, and began the long process of rebuilding, and the trolls with their healing powers ran deep into the swamps to hide from the human gods".

"And so began the Age of Discord, with no enemies to fight the humans and their allies quickly fell upon each other separating the lands into distinct realms. With the stolen knowledge of the Tier'dal, a group of humans set sail for the island Odus and began to study the Tier'dal knowledge. Others turned the great citadels of the trolls into ruins out of hatred and still other great structures where taken by the now unchecked tides of creatures that came to Norrath".

"It has been many ages since those times, but today the allied nations have begun to rise again and seek war with their enemies. This is what I wished you to hear, it was in one such raid that a young Ogre saw his mother and father slain by human knights in gleaming armor. Do any of you know who that Ogre was?"

Many of the children were unready for a question still daydreaming, placing themselves next to the Emperor as he charged down a human village, or by one of the great mages as he cast spells that would shake the world.

One hand raised into the air, "Yes Edrial, you may answer" Lorgrim sighed, he should have know that Echloe would have told her daughter the story already.

Edrial stood, a small framed Tier'dal, she had all the pride and beauty of her mother "It was Regent Dorje uncle, and he hid, knowing that the knights would slay him to."

"That is right Edrial, and after many years of holding that hatred inside, the Regent joined the Ogre Shadowknights and trained to be one of their elite. The Regent hoped that the dark knights would help him find and slay those who killed his parents. He was saddened to find that most of them were only interested in warring with the Ogres militia, in a long past attempt to regain their name". Lorgrim sighed, he remembered the fights with the Craknecks and how useless it was to attack each other.

"Soon the Regent found others in the ogre city that felt as he did, that the real enemy was the forces of good that ravaged the land in the names of their gods. With these few friends he set out and attack a few minor villages but found that he could not even put a dent in the number of humans, not even in those that were sparsely populated the Rath Mountains."

"The Regent was caught by the desire to gather many under a single banner and punish those of the good races in the name of The Ogre Emperor and so he gathered Ogres and Trolls under his banner and his guild grew. Soon The Regent was told of the great allies that the Tier'dal were, and so he set forth to find the lost city of the Tier'dal." Lorgrim smiled at the children each glued to his every word "After much searching he found the city and made many allies there, with so much force The Regent decided it was time to show his might and lead an attack upon all the villages of the human's in Rath Mountains. The battle raged for only two days and left most all of the villages in ashes, but one thing stopped their blood lust charge." Lorgrim paused to take a drink knowing the children would squirm anxious to hear what happened next.

"Uhmm... lets see where was I?" in unison the children cried out "the villages, the battles." Lorgrim smiled "Ahhh yes."

"The Regent was leading a charge on a small farm stead and the defenders were a small pathetic band of farmers who fell quickly to his troops, but Dorje noticed a small boy hiding under the stairs leading to the front door of the house." The children looked shocked and horrified at the mention of a child in the battle, they all been taught the guild tenants - Never harm the innocent they are not our enemies.

"This is when the regent realized it was not these farmers he must punish but those who harm others in the name of the spiteful gods of good, and so the guild changed. Since then we have adopted a more honorable stance in this matter, and seek to defend those who are innocent, against those who are blinded by their own goodness." The children sat caught in thought, it was a lot for their little brains to soak up.

Lorgrim turned sharply to see the person who walked into his class, "Alight children, enough story it is time for you to find your parents and remind them to eat" it was Darruk one of the guilds great Necromancers and a fellow leader.

The children jumped up, many hugging Lorgrim as they passed, this was the feeling he missed most with his Lori being gone, he thought to himself. Lorgrim stood slowly giving his old bones time to adjust to his weight.

"How long have you been listening Friend?" Lorgrim said in a calm pleasant voice to Darruk.

"Long enough friend, you know I enjoy how you tell the story. It often helps my spirit lift on those days that I spend more time meditating than fighting." Darruk smiled at Lorgrim, and Lorgrim remembered why loosing his family did not hurt so much. He still had so many other family members in this band of nomads.
