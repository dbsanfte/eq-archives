The Ogre Warrior is a well rounded character. They tend to be dumber than normal Ogres, but what they lack in brains, the usually make up for in brute force. The warrior is perhaps the most straight forward of the three Ogre characters. They possess a few unique skills and cannot cast any forms of magic. Warriors can use most weapons but must wear either magic or large armor and sheilds. Ogre warriors tend to have more health than other races because of their high stamina. With the ability to counter strike and a boosted resistancy to magic, the Ogre Warrrior is not some one you will want to cross.

Getting Started
Level
Advice
1
 The best bet for new warriors at level one are Lizardman Broodlings, Snakes, Froglock Tads, and Decaying Skells. Do not cross any bridges you come across and be mindful of the lizardman for they will destroy a newbie in under 6 seconds. Lizardman broodlings sometimes have a peice of larg cloth armor. Decaying skeletons often carry weapons or bone fragments which can be sold for money. This is the best place to begin equiping yourself. The hardest thing new Ogres have to do is learn the Feerrott and how to get around. Its very dark and can be very dangerous. Stick to the paths and always practice sense heading. Do not stray far from the paths until you have at least 10 points in SH or you may become lost and die. Recommended Starting Skills: 2pts for Sense Heading, 2pts for Taunt, 1pt in weapon of choice. Weapon skills are not that hard to advance or learn on your own so the last point could be spent on a skill of your choice. What ever you feel rounds your character out.
2
 Getting your first level is usually rather easy. By this time you should be getting a better idea of the paths and the layout of Feerrott swamp around the entrance to Oggok. Try and remember where vendor locations are outside the city so you don't have to zone when you want to sell items or purchase supplies. Bup is a good ogre to remember as he sells milk, the cheapest drink that can be bought from a vendor. At level two continue fighting the same things you did at one, perhaps adding some bats. Make sure you con everything before engaging it.
3-4
 At level three you can begin attacking jungle spiderlings and lesser scarabs. Be mindful spiders, spiderlings, and scarabs will aid each other in battle, so if you fight one you may end up fighting five. Choose your battles carefully, always con the insects as they range in level from 2 to 4. Avoid Jungle Spiders. If you die after level three you will not start out with food and drink. You will need to begin purchasing these items at this time. At level four you can test your might against Jungle Spiders.
5-6
 At level 5 save your practice points for the next level as you get two skills at six. This skill will allow you to apply bandages to yourself or other PCs if you have some in your inventory. This skill allows faster healing upto half of the recipients total HPs. At level five you should be strong enough to fight lizardman scouts and mystic if they are alone. The best place to gain experience is to group with like level characters and challenge the lizardman camps. If a mystic casts Blind on you, just keep swinging. Your character will not stop attacking as long as your opponent is within range. At level six you can try soloing a camp, but more than likely you are just going to die. You can also cross the large bridge across the river. Avoid the lizardman camps, giantbats, and zombies. A level six character should be able to fite the occasional random skeleton or minor scarab. If you do choose to cross the bridge, con everything and be ready to run. Stick to the paths on the otherside of the bridge until you know the territory. Also, avoid the NPC lizardman and the Wraith. The lizardman NPC is rumored to be level 14 and he has a pet. The Wraith, or specter, hits for 70-90pts damage. Zombies will also kill a level six character. Its best if you do not cross the bridge at this time. As soon as you get to level 6, put five of your points into Dodge and the five from the previous level into bash. You must have a shield to use Bash, but you can still practice the skill using slam.
7-8
 A level seven warrior that keeps his head on straight in a battle should be able to solo a lizardman camp on the easy side of the river. Teaming up with like level characters will allow you to fight the skeleton camps on the hard side of the bridge. Avoid zombies and the lizardman camps here. On the easy side of the Feerrott you will also find a small stone tower that has been pushed over to form a bridge. Do not cross this tower at this time. Try locating all the exits to Innothule Swamp and RatheMountains. Both can be found by sticking to the paths. Innothule is on the easy side, while Rathemountains is on the hard side. If you die at level seven, or thereafter, you will begin to notice a visible drop in your experience.
9-11
 At level 9 find a group of PCs that are your level and go across the fallen stone bridge. You will find a large temple, the entrance to Cazik Thule, and five smaller temples. Lizardman warriors and mystics spawn on top of these towers which means you can usually fite them one at a time, as long as no one has started a train. Do not attempt to fight these lizarman one on one until level 11. There can be much reward for these lizarman can give out up to a few gold and Rawhide Armor. By level 10 you should have bought at least two peices of leather armor, if not more. By level 11 you should have leather on all parts of your body. Another excellent spot is Southern Ro. A groupd of level 9 players usually will do quite well agains the orcs there.
12-13
 Two good places to get experience at these levels are Southern Ro and RatheMountains. In Southern Ro fight dervishes, being with a group will help your survival chances. Avoid Burning Skeletons and ghouls as ghouls can only be hurt by magic and magic weapons. Burning undead are usually level 14-15 material. In RatheMountains basilisk, obsidian drakes and bears are a good choice. Two level 12 characters can take most Kodiak bears which are level 13-14. They offer good experience, and bear pelts for tailors who wish to sew large armors. Grizzlies are good to kill too, not especially for the experience, but for the hides. A grizzly hide can bring 3g each easily. With the new patch, make sure you learn Dual Wield at level 13. This skill in one of the few equalizers in the game that make the warrior character formidable in battle.
14+
 Norrath is a wonderful place to explore. From this level on you are on your own. No one wants to be hand lead threw the entire game. Go, find your own techniques. Two words of advice: One, grouping is very, very beneficial. You can take on stronger things and you don't have to wait to heal so long inbetween healing and fighting. Two, Remember how to get back to where you died. Enjoy!
