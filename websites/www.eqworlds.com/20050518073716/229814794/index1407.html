Picture a world where the french fries you eat are registered as a pesticide. Where corn plants kill monarch butterflies. Where soy plants thrive on doses of herbicides that would kill any normal plant. Where multinational corporations own the life forms that farmers grow and legally control the farmers' actions. That world exists. The above events are happening now, and they are happening to us all. Genetically engineered foodsâ€”plants whose genetic structures are altered by scientists in ways that could never occur in natureâ€”are already present in most of the products you buy in supermarkets, unlabeled, unwanted and largely untested. This is the first book to take a comprehensive look at the many ramifications of this dangerous science. The authors explain what genetic engineering is and how it works, then explore the health risks involved with eating newly created lifeforms.
