It is not unreasonable to expect rangers to have self-only ports. Rangers give up ALLOT for their spells, in terms of armor selection, and lower capped defense skills, and yet these very same spells that ehy as a class sacrifice for are avaialble to classes that give up nothing, such as warriors. For example, a ranger can have Sow, and maybe it's worth it to you to give up tankability and armor class for sow, but a warrior can get jboots and keep his armor, hit points, etc. Rangers heal, and so do warriors, rogues, and other melees with their velious chestpiece. Other rangers standbys, such as snare, also have been given out to other tanks, in the form of procing swords, whips, etc. I don't think it is unbalanced request for rangers to have self-only ports. Not ny more unbalanced than it is to give ranger/druid spells to classes that didn't have to sacrifice for them. Infact, in support of this request, the ranger Kunark chest piece from Trakanon has a self-only port to Lesser Faydark, that the range can use anytime. So the request is both within the concpet of Rangers as Verant sees it, and not unbalancing enough that they would never give to rangers, because in a sense, they already have.

Will such a thing really be overpowering for the ranger class? No. Nor will warriors, rogues, monks, shadow knights, or paladins flock to the ranger class if they had them ..becuase rangers STILL can't tank at all in the high end game. And, every class can already use the nexsus to ports already. Rangers "should have" had self-only ports to begin with. It would console them when class x calls them of a gimp of a tank, which is is true --and I should know because I have a 52 level ranger, and one that is well equipped at that (gimp tank or no, the class is still "fun"). Ports would add tot eh character of the ranger, but would be useful only to him. Will Verant give rangers ports? I sincerely doubt it. They see no reason to upset the class balance this late in the game. Rangers pretty much have all they are ever going to get. Too bad, though.
