Welcome to madness of EQ.
If you are making money the old fashioned way - killing stuff, looting, and selling - then yes, money is in scant supply. It is part of the dynamic. Now you can feel proud about being able to afford your first equipment improvements. The cash flow is part of the game balance.

Don't forget your Sash quests.

A Monk faces several challenges as a character class. There is lots o' information online though.

http://www.everlore.com/races_classes/classes.asp?CID=11&mode=details&type=races

as an example.

Mostly the game is about people. You will find it very hard to go it alone for long. Start early grouping with people and you find the game more fun. Soon you will probably find yourself grouping with the same people and adding them to your friends list. Maybe getting into a guild.

On guilds, you might get offers early on to join a guild. It is up to you if you want to join. I suggest waiting till you have played with or talked to others in that guild to get an idea of what they are like. Sure, you can quit later - but don't get a reputation as a guild hopper. Guilds have much to offer. Some ask for a lot in return. some are just a bunch of friends having fun and helping each other. Find one that fits yoru play style.

As long as you are polite and friendly, most people will answer questions and lend a hand on quest info or other things.

Good luck.
