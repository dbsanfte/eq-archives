I know this is a bit long, but I promise, you are looking for a new video card or you currently own ANY VisionTek product I’d suggest you read this for your own protection...

I’ve had a VisionTek Geforce4 Ti 4600 card for about 13m now and for some strange reason the fan died on it and overheated - burning out the VPU this weekend… I figure no big deal, it came with a lifetime warranty – and at near $500 when I bought it I’m glad… But guess what – apparently VisionTek went bankrupt and some company bought the VisionTek name and the Xtasy product line so I get this:

[Form letter quote]
We are sorry but the warranty on the card is no longer valid. The company that provided the warranty for your card went bankrupt. Our company Hartford purchased the rights to the name Visiontek and Xtasy. We did not purchased their debt. As a service to previous customers, we can offer the upgrade option which will give you an opportunity to upgrade your card to today's higher end cards. This is the only options for the Nvidia-based cards.
[end]

Yet 4 of their companies press releases from Dec 2002 (when they bought the assets) to the latest in March 2003 state the exact opposite – that they WILL honor all customer’s warranties regardless of the transition – nice little marketing lie there, huh?

So instead they offer me this joke of an offer: $125 in “upgrade credit” towards the purchase of 1 of 6 cards (it should be noted that only two of the highest end cards will allow me to use the full $125 credit – the other 4 cards are on a scaling “max credit” system of $80-30 that basically wastes away the remainder of my credit – capitalistic greed at it’s finest).

My suggestions to anyone:

First off if you own a VisionTek card – watch it like a hawk – my fan didn’t die – it only slowed to a point where it couldn’t keep the card cool anymore – so keep the fan as clean as possible or maybe even replace the fan now before there is a problem (the VisionTek forums are full of people having Geforce4 fan problems) – because when you’re card dies you are screwed.

Second, if you are looking to buy a new video card keep VisionTek OFF your list – any company that states one thing in public but has a corporate policy that goes completely against it should be avoided at all costs – IMHO there are plenty of other card makers that will be happy to have you as a customer instead of finding the fastest way to screw the very people who when happy will continue purchasing cards from you on a regular basis (how many of us gamers keep a card more than 2 years, even if there is nothing wrong with it )

Now it should be noted, I’m not mad that the original company went bankrupt or the new company that bought it – before today I didn’t even have any knowledge that VisionTek was a new company. If the new company had made it a public policy to NOT offer warranty support for pre-purchase customers I could at least understand (though not agree) with it – but to state in public press releases REPEATEDLY that you will honor warranties, yet not when some actually needs it is beyond dirty, it’s the worst form of consumer deception – and it just goes to underscore why consumers don’t trust corporate entireties anymore. Frankly I think it’s wrong for this new company to be operating under the VisionTek and Xtasy brands – it deceives customers into believing they are dealing with the same honorable company that they had originally purchased the product from.

I urge anyone who has a problem with this to email VisionTek and/or its new parent company Hartford your frustrations and hopefully if enough people (both current VisionTek card owners and future potential customers) let them know that we don’t like business practices like this they may reverse the current position and actually honor their warranties like the press releases state.
