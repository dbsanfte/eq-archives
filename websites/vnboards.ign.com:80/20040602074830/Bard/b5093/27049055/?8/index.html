Be aware that if you plan on using fear/snare, stamina is a bit of a consideration. I use fear/snare a lot, and usually on con blue mobs by the time I've killed it (sometimes earlier) my stamina bar is completely drained (using dual wield, that is). Now, you can twist in our level 3 stamina song but wouldn't you rather twist in a DoT or something?
Also, just be aware that in your low 20's you will probably get tired of your bard and want to move on to another class. Don't worry about it. I stopped using my bard at levels 12 and 25 (started up a monk and a druid, respectively). Now my bard is 35 (monk is 17, druid is 28). You will start to have a LOT more fun with bards once you get fear at level 27, and then charm and soon after the mana song that makes you oh-so needed in groups.
So, don't be discouraged. I've known other bards who took a break in their low 20's, but you will come back eventually and things will only get better from there.
One last note: post low-20's, you can solo or group effectively. Makes choosing your bard based on how long you want to play a much easier decision

STR - Increase your chances for max melee DMG (up to but your dmg cap i.e. lvl 20, 30, 40 etc.)
STA - HP and how long you can stand and melee and swim also.
AGI - Incease defensive skills (i.e Defence, Parry, Dodge) and also how likely the MOB will miss you.
DEX - Increases offensive skills (i.i Offence, 1hs, 1hb, etc.) and how often your weapon procs.
INT - Bards are INT based, this effects your mana pool for the 2 songs that i know of that use mana, and also your trade skills.
WIS - Who cares
CHA - Effects nothing more than prices with merchants, helps with trades also.

Now with that covered, i can tell you being 50+, that STA is by far the hardest stat to come by when it comes to gear. I would pump at least 10 points into STA. Maybe 5 into STR, 5 into AGI and only 5 into CHA.

When i made my bard i put 10 into CHA and wish i had only done 5, so that's my 2cp.

As you level up, you **typically** get better gear...I followed EverQuest Casters Realm creation guide for bards. I play a Half-Elf, so followed their advice on what points to put into each stat. I think that Dex is by far the most important class...for bards it determines your missed note rate. If you have low dex, you will "fizzle" more in my experience. Charisma is nice, but you can always ask a close chanter to cast a cha buff on you, if you ask nice I'm sure they won't bother with a donation.

Agility determines ac, and as a bard you will most likely be getting hit. Although it isn't a main concern of mine, I know bards that have less than 900ac at lvl 62, while I'm sitting at about 1100 at lvl 56.

Stamina determines your max hp. This is a nice stat, but since bards don't typically take up the role of a MA, it's not a primary attribute in my book.

If I were you I would just follow the creation guide at http://eq.crgaming.com/creationguides/bard.asp
