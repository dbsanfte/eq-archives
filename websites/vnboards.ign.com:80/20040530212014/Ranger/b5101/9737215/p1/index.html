By "to-hit" i assume you mean your chance to hit the mob.

It is based mainly on your atk value. That value can be affected by your weapon skill, your str, and spells.

From different log parsers, i don't think that str (while it does affect atk) affects your chance to hit.

It is a MYTH that other stats like agi/dex increase your chance to hit. The most important factor is your skill with the weapon you are using. Other factors include: your lvl vs. mobs lvl, mob AC and defensive skills.

The damage you do to the mob is also affected by several things.

First, below lvl 10(i think) you can't hit for more than 20. And below lvl 20, you can't hit for more than 30(i think again).

Before lvl 20, max weapon dmg is 2 X (weapons listed Damage) +1

Example, a 6dam weapon will have a max hit of 13.

After lvl 20, your damage max will increase with your strength and lvl.

My 48 sk can hit for over 130 with a 30dmg weapon and 225 str.

Melee classes (not sure about casters/priests) get a dmg Bonus as they increase in levels. That is, they have a certain amount of dmg that is tacked on to EVERY hit.

So if i would normally hit for 40, i would hit for 50. A good way to see if you have a dmg bonus is to watch if you ever hit for 1. If you ever hit for 1, you have no bonus, because even a bonus of 1 would yield a hit of 2....

Toddtw, let me try to explain WHY str does not affect your chance to hit.

When EQ was in beta, there were 2 separate values listed for ATK rating, not the single one you see on your char now.

One rating was a pure atk rating, the other was a max dmg type rating. This was too confusing for the avg player, so verant combined them in to a single ATK rating that we see now.

The only thing that affects your chance to hit a mob is any ATK buffs you have, and your weapon/offensive skills. STR only affects the max ammount of dmg you will do with a particular melee weapon.
