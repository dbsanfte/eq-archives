Geez... this topic won't die.

Here is the 'real deal' as you call it:

Iksar monks have a naturally higher ac. Iksar monks get enhanced vision. Iksar monks have certain stats that are higher (they also have stats that are lower, such as strength).

To offset these things, Iksar monks have a slight xp penalty and will have SLIGHTLY less of a natural haste at higher levels. That means that human monks, using FISTS, will be able to do more damage, but iksar monks will have a higher AC and thus avoid more damage. It will all balance out... humans more of the damage dealers, iksar more of the damage takers (while using FISTS, mind you).

If you throw some weapons into the mix, it might change slightly, but all in all, I think the two are made to be balanced. Personally, I'm tired of hearing the debate. Choose the race that you think will be the most fun and go with it.

The only things we know for sure are the obvious racial advantages of being an iksar: night-vision, regen, innate swimming ability, and naturally higher AC (did i forget a couple?), and that there is an experience penalty for the iksar monk compared to the human monk. As for stats, the Iksar has 5 extra stats points overall than humans, with emphasis on dex and agi. The iksar cannot get his/her base str as high as a human however. Humans can bump starting str to 100, not sure for iksar - but not as high. This will be a factor when determining damage output at higher levels.
As far as damage dealing, the only thing we have to go on is a statement from one of the guys from Verant (Gordon maybe) in a very ambiguous statement stating that humans will be able to deal more damage than a monk, and that their fist delay will cap at a rate that is slower than humans. So, what this does *not* tell us is what the actual difference in delay is between human/iksar at lvl 50 (human fists = 14/30 at lvl 50). This suggests that the human offensive advantage could be offset by using 1hb/2hb that are superior to our fists at a given level. However, it has also been suggested that there may be a difference in the *offense* skill cap between human and iksar which would indeed guarantee that the human would be a better damage dealer (assuming all other skills/stats are equal).
So, my main point is that we do not know the full extent of the differences in offensive power between human/iksar monks at this point, but we can assume that everything else being equal (similarly armored/equipped monks) that humans will in general outdamage iksar, partly because there may. But the iksar have the higher natural AC to offset this.
If I were to start a new monk now, it would probably be an iksar - mostly because of the fact that it's a new race and I haven't played it before, and I would like to experience Kunark from a newbie point of view also.
Just play the one that you think will be the most fun. We have seen nothing to suggest that human monks will become obsolete at high levels due to iksar being far superior.
I however do not feel that the iksar exp penalty is unfair - the natural AC boost that the iksar get at lower levels is very beneficial and will allow them to solo mobs 1-2 lvls higher than the human monk. On top of this, add the regen ability of the iksar and the down-time is also less. So I feel the exp penalty is both warranted and fair to both races.
