Lvl 53 and can still solo evens and yellows (assuming they hardly resist my songs) There are 2 goods, effetive ways to solo as a bard in higher lvls.

A) Selo's Kiting has been made ten times easier with Luclin. Before when Selo's Accelerando (lvl 5) was the only run song, it was Run, DD, DD Run, DoT, DoT, etc. (DD and DoT being interchangeable) With the addition of Selo's Accelerating Chorus (lvl 49) now, it lasts 2.5 minutes when only sung once. What does this mean for the soloing bard? Run, DoT, DoT, DoT, DoT, DoT, DoT, DoT, DoT, DoT, DoT, Run, etc... Mobs fall in half the time now.
What i do when i am Selo's Kitting is get Selo's going.. Then twist Fufil's Curtailing Chant Target (lvl 30) - Tuyen's Chant of Flame Target (lvl 38) - and Tuyen’s Chant of Frost Target (lvl 46). These 3 songs all stack and add up to about 300 hp of damage over 9 or 10 seconds (that's about 1000hp in a little over 30 seconds.. not bad for kiting). Just keep twisting them and make sure to keep Selo's up. The good thing is that all 4 of these songs are Drum based and you will get Max damg. and keep up your max speed.

B) Charming. Charming is exactly that. Charm MOB A sick him on MOB B. One dies, you finish off the other, get EXP for both. The best thing about Bardic Charming, and yet the worst thing as well, is the duration. I believe after the last modification made to it, it only lasts 12 seconds. Which means you have to be on your toes because at any second you will have 2 pissed off mobs after you, that is the bad part. The good part about the duration comes into play when considering that you get no EXP if the pet, your "charmed" mob gets killed first while he is your pet, so if you notice half way through the battle your pet has less HP and is loosing, you only have to wait 12 seconds to change pets.

Deveraux pretty much said it all, but, as a bard who has more or less soloed exclusively from level 1 to my current level of 46, I have a thing or two to add. You will be able to keep with your current soloing tactics until the mid 20s or so, however it will get more and more inefficient as you go along. Thankfully, as the aformentioned poster said, more options open to you around those levels. At level 27 you get the first charm song, and once you become comfortable with its use you will be hands down the most efficient soloer in the game. You will be able to take on any number of mobs, getting full exp for each in a relatively short period of time, with no downtime (unless you accidentally get hit). What other class can do that?

This tactic can take you well into your 40s, perhaps even to 50 if you're patient, although the level 27 charm spell will only work on mobs that are level 37 or lower. There is an upgrade to it, though unlike its predecessor, it uses mana (not a whole lot, something to the effect of 20 per use).

That will more or less take you up to where I am, when you can start chant kiting, which Deveraux mentioned. As far as I can see, it is an equally efficient method of soloing. Formidable blue mobs take about 2 or 3 minutes to take down barring a large amount of resists. This amounts to a yellow bubble of experience every 45 minutes or so, which isn't bad by any class' standards.

So in short, we solo just fine Sorry if I rambled on a bit, I tend to do that Enjoy your bard! Oh, and great guides on each of these types of soloing can be found at http://www.eqdiva.com

Re hunting tips, get PoP if you haven't already, take a portal from the Plane of Knowledge to Cabilis - which actually takes you to Field of Bone, and run across there to Kurn's Tower.
