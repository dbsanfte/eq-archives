Anon209.210.1.x
 Date Posted: 5/22/00 5:26am Subject: RE: why?????
Lizard monks are by no means "harder" to play. They start with infravision, regen, and a inate AC bonus. So if anything they are much easier to play. Now if you are talking about faction or amount of experience needed to level then yes lizard monks take longer to level.

Now on to your question about stats. Dex and Agi are rather worthless stats. Agi does not effect how often you get hit. This has plainly been stated by Verant and players doing extensive testing. It does add 1 AC point per 5 Agi. So a lizard man receives a additional 3 AC over a human. IMO not worth losing 5 STR (Most humans put all the points into STR for a total of 100). Agi also helps you learn defensive skills faster.

Dex effects the number of times a weapon might "proc". Now this too is not to important as testing has shown that you need large amounts of Dex to effect the amount of "procing" to any noticable effect (Upwards of 30 to 50 to show noticable improvement). Dex does help you learn some offensive skills (Duel wield, Double Attack, etc) faster.

So neither monk is "better". It is more of a personally choice. I for one think the lizard monks are ugly and look dumb while fighting, so I picked a human. To me being a monk is about style (on top of other things) and IMO that is the area lizards are sorely lacking in. Anyway Verant has set it up to where Lizards at 60 are more defensively oriented with inate AC and higher Agi and Humans follow the path of offense.

Anon192.146.101.x
 Date Posted: 5/24/00 7:39am Subject: RE: why?????
I also am a high level monk (level 46 over 10 months of play) and have done extensive experimenting with STR, AGI, DEX, and AC. First off, I'll just say that AC is the way to go. After that, strength is king. Contrary to popular myth, Agility doesn't really have much of an effect at all, and certainly not just 20 points of it. If you add 50-60 points of agility, you may see a bit of a difference, but not much.

I took my agility from 90 to 170 (approx) while experimenting, and saw little to no change in how many times that I was hit or how many times I hit the mobs.

I took my strength from 95 to 147 while experimenting, and I hit more often, I hit harder, the fight was over much faster, and thus I took less damage.

Dexterity of course is completely worthless.

All of these experiments that I have done for myself have been done even better by others (on a shaman board... when I find the URL I'll post it) several months ago, and their tests are very complete and accurate. Their tests revealed the same things that I am telling you now.

Whether or not you choose to believe it is up to you.

Why iksars will not ever be as good at being monks is simply as a balance mechanism between the two races that can be monks.

Iksars get quite a bit for this trade off, including night vision, and regen, better agi, and better dex. Any single one, especially regen is imho worth quite a bit more than what they give up. To put it simply, losing 4-6 dmg at higher lvls, and taking slightly longer to lvl(long time away for most iksar monks) is a VERY small price when you consider that most high lvl monks hardly ever melee outside of groups with at least 1 tank to take and give serious punishment.

IMHO, Verant killed the human monk as a newbie when they gave lizzie-boys the ability to BE monks. That is not to say that I will never play a human monk. I do, but as a matter of personal pride and stubborn perseverence.

Yes, I am proud of the fact that if I die in Guk, I have to go kill 20 fire beatles to get a gd eye before I can even zone through the tunnel to Nro from EC. And yes, I am proud that my fragile HUMAN body is a more powerful weapon than an lizard boy could EVER be.

Iksar monks are clearly superior, considering the weapons that now drop in Kunark gives them the dmg that humans can do, and their innate regen alone, but you will NEVER catch me playing one. Why? I feel, as a lot of others do, that Iksar STOLE our class, and our sacred traditions, and I kill them every chance I get.

It's simply a matter of personal preference. A dynamic of roleplay if you will. But imho, if you play an iksar on my server, I will never call you brother.
