For me, Ogre is the only way. If you care about ever walkin into "good" cities, then go ahead and be a Barbarian. But as for me, tht did not matter. I looked at base stats when I started my shaman. Equip and spells can make up for ANY stat that you personally think is important. Even getting to 200wis( More WIS, the main claim to being a barbarian shaman..feh).

BUT, what are you doing with all that mana? What is the role of a shaman? Sure, if you are going to go and outright nuke a NPC, then sure, more mana is great. But is that what a shaman excels at? Not in the least.

If you want to solo, fine. Root/DOT them to death. We have two lines of DOT's that work GREAT together. But in this process, does MAX mana really play a role. Not really. You root, maybe Malise, and then stack your DOTs. Then you sit, med, regen, and canibalize. That is all that happens. You can say that having more mana is good. But, you are sitting there regening and using canibalize. Not really using MAX mana at all. Me personally. I run around with 165wis(went ac happy), I am an ogre, and when I do the solo game, I don't run out of mana. So is having 3 more in my base mana is going to make a difference? Or even if i were a troll, would it really matter? Not in the least.

Then we come to grouping. The thing that makes shaman shine.

We buff like no other. All the important stats that all the tanks need, we have. We make them hit harder and take less dmg. But when is this done? Not in combat, but when you are preparing for a fight. And it will take approx the same time to buff and regen your mana that you spent.
We become a secondary healer. For this job, mana is important. Having more mana can sometimes be that fine line between life and death. But as a secondary healer, you also have the ability to isolate mobs so your team can focus on a mob and destroy it. Being able to pull a mob off from your team a little bit, and root him, can save more mana then if you spent it just healing the person getting raped by multiple mobs. Now in an ideal team, you will have an enchanter for crowd control, or even a bard. So with that said, is having 3 more base wis to start going to make a difference as a secondary healerin an ideal team? not really. In a not so ideal team? Even there, not really.

We also debuff. We have, hands down, the best speed debuff in the game. You cast that at a mob and watch as the fighters get 2,3,4 rounds of combat for everyone single attack the mob gets. As your level increases, so will that spell, and it's ability to make mobs your teams play toys. We also have our stat/ac debuffing spells. Though others get better versions of these, they are still very helpful.

We, as shaman, have so many spells to make the teams we are in better. We can swing the production of xp into high gear and we can enhance almost any situation.

So.... You chose whatever race you want. Everyone of them has it's pos/neg side. Choose what best fits you and what you want in the end. For me, going into "good" cities and having 3 more wis was not enough to ignore the abilities of an Ogre. To each his own. Or her own.
