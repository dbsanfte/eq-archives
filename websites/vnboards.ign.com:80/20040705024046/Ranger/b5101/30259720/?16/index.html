Rangers can tank just fine in normal xp grind groups at higher levels. Besides AE groups (which give the best xp/hour) my fave xp grind group is three people: enc, ranger and druid. With those three, we can pretty much clear VL of spiders, or handle most places in Seb with no problems, etc. In fact, subtract the druid and we have still completely dominated the guardian/necrosis camp in Seb, just enc and ranger. And don't REALLY need the druid in Seb either if the enc is good, but the druid speeds things up with heals and damage shields. (This is 60 ranger, 60 enc, and any druid 50+.)

My ranger is 53 now and I routinely am the MT in many situations. My AC is well over 1000, as is my ATK, and with a few cleric buffs I am hitting around 3500 HP. That's plenty adequate to be the MT in many xp grind situations. No, of course I won't be the MT for Vindi and such, but the time I spend getting normal xp vastly outweighs the time I spend fighting Vindi anyway. And I'd rather be the MT than spend all my mana Jolting constantly because the warriors can't taunt off me.

So far as raids are concerned, rangers have some very nice buffs at the high end for increasing ATK. While not VITAL, the ATK buffs certainly are useful enough that any raid would want at least one or two high level rangers, if not more. Especially desireable are high level rangers with lots of the archery AA skills, because they can dispense huge gobs of damage. Some guilds form ranger groups for raids just for this purpose: to use Trueshot + AA to blow away mobs with massive archery damage.

So, rangers have plenty of use in normal groups (tanking, utilities, crowd control, melee damage, peeling), and plenty of use in raids as well (Trueshot, ATK buffs, melee damage). And in a pinch they can solo too (pincushion tanking, bow kiting, fear animals).

I have to agree- Manaburn obviously isn't very experienced in this area, and frankly I hope for his sake that he isn't very experienced with the game in general because if he is...well, his comments don't say very good things about him. Luckily, you can always separate people like this out from the rest of the crowd, who generally tend to be fairly intelligent and open minded people that realize the largest difference between characters are the people who play them, not the classes they're playing. You can tell where Manaburn is coming just by looking at his language- he says things like he hates "to break it to you" but that Rangers are "useless" after a certain point, which of course is a very silly thing to say in the first place because if it were true then there wouldn't be ANY Rangers who had gotten past whatever that certain point was, much less all the way to 60.

I have a few very experienced friends playing Everquest, and from the people that really know what they're talking about the concensus seems to be that Rangers are a class which is at this point more geared towards solo'ing than towards groupwork, but that they do have a very important role in a group. A friend I used to have who primarily played a wizard actually took a Ranger in a group before any other class because of the Ranger's ability to gain control of nearly any mob. Rangers also make fine pullers- not as great, of course, as someone who can FD, but a good puller nevertheless outside of certain zones.

They aren't useful as healers obviously- but this is actually an important pro Ranger point to make. The fact that Rangers have healing spells so much lower than those of other healing classes shows that Verant specifically intended for a Ranger to use his healing spells to reduce HIS OWN downtime, not to heal other players in the midst of battle. And Verant has certainly been successful in carrying out their intentions with the Ranger class- a class that can go it on their own if they know their multitude of skills. Verant say right upfront that Rangers are capable of many skills, masters of none, and indeed this is the defining quality of a Ranger- a much much broader range of expertise than any other class. This makes it so that when using one skill a Ranger SHOULD be dominated by a creature who uses that same skill entirely. A ranger attempting to immediately melee a combat based enemy should get thwomped- a ranger attempting to kill a magic based opponent using spells should be equally thrashed. But both types of opponents can be bested by a Ranger who knows what he's doing.

Hence the difference between an intelligent and UN-intelligent Ranger. An intelligent Ranger knows that in order to thrive he must use all of his skills at the appropriate time- using kiting strategies at the right times, using his spells at the right times, and entering melee combat at the right time.

By the way, I have played a Ranger and while I have not gotten up to high levels I have been a member of an almost Ranger exclusive guild, and many of the 50ish level Rangers had never hunted animals. Many Rangers hunt animals because they can fear kite, obviously the safest way to go- but some Rangers, myself included, roleplay the part to the degree that we would not even consider attacking an animal- just so you know, it is another piece of legend that Rangers are unable to hunt past a certain level because animals are no longer available...it just means that a Ranger cannot dominate his prey quite so entirely, and most Rangers are used to using a fear kite strategy and so become lost when they suddenly need to use their other skills.
