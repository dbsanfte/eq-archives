Oh yes I had forgotten, halfling warriors get the best experience modifier. However, monks cannot solo for any length of time. I watched a friend play his monk from level 1 to 50 and sometimes played the monk myself... they just don't do enough damage or have enough hit points to successfully solo after the low 20's. Mend is nice, but even when you reach 100 in that skill (when it always works) you'll find that it is impossible to solo many blue and green monsters (to say nothing of PC pets). In my opinion, due to the way different classes are 'balanced' in EQ, if you want to solo all the way without spending 10 hours a day online, every day, for a year or more, the only viable classes are (in order of increasing difficulty) necro, mage, enchanter, shaman, druid. Bards are probably somewhere between the casters and tanks, and can definitely solo in the hands of a skilled player on a fast connection (necessary for stacking 3 songs at all times). Rogues and warriors can solo but it takes a lot more play time. As best I can tell, wizards, clerics and monks are not effective solo classes. I never played the hybrid classes much so I can't vouch for them and as far as I am concerned, shadow knights suck, period.

Anon64.194.131.x
 Date Posted: 3/2/01 6:37am Subject: RE: rogues soloing
I soloed my warrior thru all of 30th hell in 7 hours and 22 minutes. The key was having enough AC and a good enough weapon to be able to kill a blue with less than half my health, and buttloads of bandages. I never got a buff or heal from anyone that entire time. I didn't have any regen items. I did have good AC and a good weapon, that's it. I'm 50th now, so not many blues I can solo left at all, much less on half my health, and my HP are so high that even if I could, the sheer bandaging time wouldn't make it worthwhile, but it's not hard to do into the 30s at least.

My rogue is 53rd. I did some soloing with him, up until around 40th, off and on, but after 25th or so I tried to stay grouped as much as feasible. Not always an easy task.

My cleric is 30th, and he still solos quite well. I don't really see any end to that unless I run out of undead mobs that are blue. Actually, at 30th, I can still solo red undead mobs with no difficulty.

My enchanter is 57. I solod with him off and on up until the early 50s. I could probably solo with him now at 57, but it really isn't very practical, so I don't. It's more efficient to find a group and since I rarely never have problems in that area as an enchanter, there's no reason to solo.

My necro is 54. He still, as always, solos just fine, never any problem there. He's what I play when I absolutely can't find a group with any of my other characters. Since that rarely happens, he's been 54 for quite a while now.

My druid is 39. He solos fine, and I think always will. I have friends who were able to solo with their druids up to and into 60th. It's boring as all hell, but it's doable, and it's very doable at 39. But druids start sucking as they increase in levels, so he's been pretty much relegated to the character I use for buying and selling and moving stuff around, and that's it.

My ranger is 24. He still solos just fine. I can usually kill two yellows or three whites on down before I have to take a break (which is better than I could do with my druid or necro, since rangers are only partially dependent on mana, whereas druids are pretty much totally dependent on mana). I'm sure that as he increases in level that this will change for the worse and ultimately he will be unable to solo much at all.

ALL classes can solo, for a while. I suppose it's even possible that every class could solo to 60th. But for some classes, most classes even, it isn't efficient in terms of time to do it. Casters can generally do it the longest, and the best.

But...soloing isn't really what it's all about, is it?
