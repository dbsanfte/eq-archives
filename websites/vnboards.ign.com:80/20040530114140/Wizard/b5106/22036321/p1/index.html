I love being a wizard. I think we've got such a bad reputation because of the way we're designed.

In a group, if you're doing your job each mob drops significatnly faster than it otherwise would. This is good when camping a spawn, the group will be amazed at how easy it is.

However when you're in a constant pull group this speed causes a problem. Because each mob drops faster, the tanks are less injured and the healers have more mana, meaning they pull sooner. As quicker pulls mean less time for mana to come back the wizard's effectiveness is limited. In effect we have to choose between doing a little damage (compared to the tanks) to each mob or else to really burn a fight and then sit through a fight or two. In either case it doesn't look like we contributed much, the fact that the group did 4 pulls in the time it would've normally done 3 is rarely noticed or commented on.

In a way what I'm saying is through our strength we make ourselves look weaker, inefficeint and like we're not contributing as much.

Couple that oddity with the fact that at low levels (where people form their impressions of the classes), our mana limitations make us incapable of competing with twink melees. I mean really, I've seen equal level melee twinks clear entire dungeons, where I had to root, nuke and med through my battles one by one.

These factors together make us look weaker than we really are. It never really occurs to the party that ultimately a wizard can single handidly reverse a "bad pull" into a great fight, that a wizard can terminate a mob that would otherwise have it's way with the party or that if they looked at a log of the experience they got (rather than the damage each character did) they would see that they got more experience with the wizard in their group than they did without.

Wizzies get two clash spells, I would cast either one of those. After that, if it isn't dead. Let the tanks handle it, it should be almost dead at that point.

There is your asked for 2 cp. Stay safe, and nuke on!!!

>>I will stand in one place and attempt to Enstill the mob<<

Try using root bud Casts faster so it's less interupted. Enstill, yes, is an upgrade, but it's slower casting. When all your worried about is getting the mob back on the tank, use root. Also, remind your tanks about "proximity aggro." The closer to the mob you are, the more angry the mob is at you.

>>I won't cast a spell until the mob we are killing is at 75% or less health.<<

At 25, that is ok, but as you get higher, that won't be an option. Unless you use a lower level nuke. As we go up in level, our nukes become big aggro creaters. Wait till 49 and Ice Comet. I had some problems finding the right time to cast it. Doing over a 1000 damage a strike tends to really piss off a mob. I could cast IC when the mob is at 50% health and have it come charging right for me.

You have to remember the phases of combat to be a successful wizard. I did a big write up on this a while back, but here is a basic recap:

There are 3 phases of combat:
1. Attack
2. Beserk
3. Flee
Attack phase occurs at the beginning and continues till the mob is at about (changes for different mobs) 50% health. During the attack phase, I usually stick to stuns. They don't create a ton of aggro (remember proximity aggro above? works here too), and it saves the tank from taking damage. Once the mob hits about 50%, it goes into the Beserk phase. This is where the mobs damage output goes way up. As a wizard, our job is to use a well placed nuke to take the mob from the Attack phase to the Flee phase completely skipping Beserk. This not only saves the cleric mana, but also makes the tanks much more pleasant and the pulls keep comming. Also, I would not recommend nuking or casting any sort of damage spell during the Flee stage. It's really pointless as no one is taking damage at that point. Better off medding

Jyoti4 is exactly correct about the stun aggro effect. But what some wizards to not realize is that you can in a lot of cases keep the mob stunned for the entire fight if you use your stuns effectively. In order to do this you will need one important spell, "Brain Bite" or Concussion spell! This is a quested spell that is a must for any wizard. This spell when casted puts you back down to the bottom of the hate list, which in the cases of stuns is a very significant thing. So if you really want to be loved in your group, learn to put up 3 or 4 of your stun spells and figure out how long each one lasts. Then cast each one back to back, just before the next one runs out. Just remember to cast the longest one first and work your way down. Inbetween the 2nd or 3rd stun, cast the Concussion spell, and you are ready to go for another series. If you find you have done a lot of damage and the Concussion spell is not sticking. Simply root the mob until you do get a Concussion spell to stick.

That also comes in very handy when you have nuked a bunch of the HP from the mob and would like to sit down and med. Normally when you do this the mob instantly attacks. All you need to do is cast this spell and you are now able to sit down and med without instant aggro.

That is my rant about the Concussion spell. Now go and get it!!!
