Naydra is right we are not wizards although we do have some good nukes. We are not a buff class, the only buff we get is a good damage shield (mine currently does 45 damage every time it procs). What we are is a pet class and summoner!!

Learn to manage your mana and your pet. If your pet is getting killed by a mob and you are too low on mana to summon a new pet, walk up to the mob and let it hit you. With the phantom armor and shield line of spells you can take a bit of damage while your pet finishes off the mob. This will also get your defencive skill up. Admitedly in the end game getting hit can be fatal (remembers a spider that hit for 600 damage).

Keep burnout and the damage shield up at all times on your pet, the run line of spells is handy on pets as it enables them to keep up with fleeing mobs. If you are soloing keep the damage shield up on your self as well. Pets are cheap a res isn't, so don't worry about killing your pet as you are summoning a new one. At low levels this trick works very well with earth pets. Don't waste all your mana healing your pet. if your pet has uber buffs (it prolly won't need to be healed) then heal but otherwise it saves mana to summon a new pet.

In groups keep the main tank damage shielded and turn your own pet taunt off. Still keep your pet buffed up with burnout and the damage shield tho. Pets are great for crowd control, if you get an add to the group send the pet on it. If a chanter mezzes the mob 99% of the time the pet cannot break the mez and will break off. ( the only time the pet will break mez is if it is casting a dd spell before the mez lands) Make sure the group is aware that the pet will back off once the mob is mezzed (some people seem to think that pets still break mez).

Our second role is to summon stuff (focus items and resist gear) we summon some nice resist gear and the focus items have ac, health and mana stats so these are always welcome.

My magi is my second created character and has been around since November 1999. I am not a powergamer and play a fairly casual game, so end up soloing quite a bit. Getting harder past level 60 to find and solo blue mobs but it is possible.

Hi Finn,

I'm not sure what level your mage is, so I really can't address the specific problems you see happening with the class.
I've found that with PoP, I'm usually sent a number of tells asking for me to group. The mage class has become far more than a passing fancy or the "filler" member for a group.
You mention that the mage pet is only marginally better than the necro, I beg to differ. A summoned necro pet at lvl 60 is toast compared to my own lvl 49 pet summon spell (epic). I know, fear is a concern however, nothing a little casting of valiant companion can't cure.
If you get a chance, check out the new spells available for mages. Right now, I opt not to wear one ring, one bracer and one earing and summon those instead. I definately do not see a downside to this simply because with all the other gear I have, my intel hits 284. With the present intel cap, I'm still at 275 without KEI or any form of mind buff. Again, this is just my own case, but it may show what can be achieved.
When it comes to summoned items, I love the stuff we can give to our pets now, the Summoned Belt of Magi Kot is amazing!
Pet only belt.
STR + 10, dex 10, sta 10, agi 10, HP 250.

The weapons we can summon for our pets are also extrememly awesome, even to the point where my melee guildmates and friends whine when they realize these are only for pets. Sure, it would be nice to be able to hand out stuff like this, but that would put mages back into the catagory of "vending machine". People would beg us for the items, but not for us to join in. With these items being available only to our pets, having the mage and it's pet in a group is far more beneficial.

Again, not knowing your level and your abilities, all I can do is show you how things change for a mage in the higher end game. If it is worth the time and effort to get there, go for it. If not, then no biggy.

My only advice to anyone is to play the character you really and truly enjoy. When you totally love the character you play, the downsides may make you grit your teeth at times, but you will always find an upside and a means to adapt.

Good luck
