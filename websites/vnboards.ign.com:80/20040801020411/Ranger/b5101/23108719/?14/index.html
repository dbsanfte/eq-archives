They don't.

The best bows are good at low levels when mobs have relatively low hit points. You can kill them before they get within minimum range. After that they're pretty much worthless for killing anything that will give you experience in a reasonable amount of time.

The do become somewhat useful for Rangers again at 55th with the addition of the Trueshot discipline. Altho 2 minutes of useful bow damage against a handful of targets with a 72 minute reuse time isn't exactly overpowering.

You don't need good arrows. At 53 I can solo anything that is snareable and doesn't summon me, using summoned arrows, provided I have the space to bow kite. At the very VERY high levsls (e.g. endless quiver), sure, get some high damage arrows, because you'll never run out of them. Otherwise, summoned arrows from Tolan's Bracer fed thru a Fleeting Quiver are just fine. If you're smart, you'll be a Tunare-worshiping ranger and use a Blessed Feydark Thunderbolt. There are better bows, but not that are tradeable.

In an xp group, without using any disciplines, and with no AA skills, I can get a normal bow hit with a summoned arrow for around 120 damage. With a crit, up into the 20os. At 51 I actually KS'd a 51 monk using nothing but my bow. It was an accident, I was just trying to help him (while practicing archery), and I didn't expect to see the xp message, but guess I got in a few too many crits.
