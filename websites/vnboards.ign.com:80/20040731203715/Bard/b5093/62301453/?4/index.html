Just wanted to chime in on your carpal tunnel syndrome part

I'm not sure what method you are using now, but this is what I use:

(not my design, I got this off a guide on eqdiva.com which is a great site)

I use several hotkey banks, for different situations. Most of the time I will be using a hotkey bank that looks like this:

1) Song 1
2) Song 2
3) Song 3
4) Song 4
5) Song 5
6) Song 6
7) Song 7
8) Song 8
9) Assist
10) %t incoming message

Now, for the song keys, I use a real short and simple macro like this (example is for song 1)

/stopsong
/cast 1


And put that in hotkey slot 1. Basically, it allows you to switch to any song with a single keypress. Lets say you are twisting songs 1,2, and 3 for example.

You press 1. This ends your current song, and starts the casting countdown on song 1. When that song activates, press 2. This will end song 1, and instantly begin the casting countdown on song 2.

Repeat as needed.

By eliminating the need to press a button once to stop your current song, then again to begin a new one, you are cutting out half of the keystrokes, and eliminating a lot of delay.

Also, I suggest all Bards check out Keiz UI. Keiz is a moderator at eqdiva.com, and his UI is aimed at bards, but I liked it enough to use it on all my characters.

The biggest plus of this UI is the inventory/hotkey window. You will have to see the screenshots to really understand, but basically it adds your inventory spots to the hotkey window. This allows you to swap weapons and instruments without either a) having your inv open, or b) using a hotkey for an inventory space.

Also, it allows you to access your bags without opening your inv screen, which is a nice things for all classes.

You can find it at http://www.eqinterface.com/list.php?skinnerid=5344

Version 8 was just added to address a few issues in last weeks patch, as well as some other nice changes like making the target bar show the con color of your target.
