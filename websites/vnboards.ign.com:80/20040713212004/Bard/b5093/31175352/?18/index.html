First of all, Bard songs stack with shaman/chanter buffs. And if you've only played your bard to 50 then trust me, you have no idea what the "upper-end" game is like. Yes getting a bard to high levels is harder than some classes but they are essential for most raids. Using ae manasong along with KEI allows ALL the casters on a raid to be regenerating mana as fast as possible. In zones like ToV (temple of veeshan) without a bards ae resist song the white and red dragons ae's are a lot harder to resist, which, when they cast nearly a dozen 300-350dmg ae's a fight, makes a huge difference. Also, once a bard hits 55 they are invaluable for one song along, Occlusion of Sound. This resist debuffer is pretty much required for any upper level boss fight (rares in tov, ct, vindi, etc..) because it is unresistable and stacks with the 60 shaman malo spell. Without the bards help the boss mob by his resists renders most if not all the aggressive casters helpless. And I agree with you, a bard can't compete with an enchanter in the field of crowd control, they aren't supposed to. I can keep 3-5 mobs busy at a time in Sebilis with song of twighlight and charm (that is on a good day) but a chanter can easily keep 8 or more, because for an enchanter that is SPECIFICALLY what they are designed for. And while I'm going on about bards u don't even have to group for experience, using snare/fear song u can get solo exp very very easily, and in higher areas you can use charm to cut down the mobs hp quickly. You act like bards are worthless but in actuality they are required for raids, their buffs stack with everyone elses to make them group friendly, and frankly, bards along with I believe its shamans and some other class are the only ones I have ever heard about that can effectively solo Ice burrowers and lesser dragons in Western Wastes. So perhaps before you bash the class u should understand more fully what a bards job is, and I don't mean at level 50.
