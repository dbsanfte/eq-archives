The Rogue
The Rogue class in Everquest is probably one of the tougher melee classes to play.  A rogue has many skills that he/she can use over time that make this life a lot easier but there are also a couple hardships along the line.

The Rogue's biggest skill is the ability to Backstab.  I've seen people and screen shots of people Backstabbing for over 400 points.  Backstab recycles every 8 seconds.  Its like having a unlimited nuke in your hand.  Doing almost 4 times the damage of a normal blow, a successful Backstab can give you the upper hand quickly in a battle.  It can also save lives in a group if a MOB is aggro’d on a group member and one more hit would kill your companion.    It can also taunt a Mob off someone.  (They usually will agro you a lot but there are some tricks to that also) Backstab is the single most important skill a rogue will have and it will be your call sign.

Another skill the Rogue receives is the ability to Hide and Sneak.  These skills do exactly what there called but the best thing about it is you'll never need to have anyone cast invisibility.  A successful Hide then followed by a Sneak will make you invisible to all.  This is particularly useful when you need to recover your corpse out from under a MOB.

Dual Wield and Double Attack are also handy little melee skills you'll receive along with Disarm and Riposte.  In addition you will have Dodge and Parry for defense also.  Instill Doubt is another skill that basically will cast fear on your opponent if successful.

A Rogue Only trade is the ability to create poison.  You get the ability to apply it at level 18 but at 20 you can make your own.  It is very hard and costly but it is worth the time in a long run.  Poison will give you an added attack if it is successful on your first attack.  I wont bore you with the trade right now but if you need to know more about this trade, check Everlore, Casters Realm, or other fan sites.

Often overlooked, we have the ability to throw things and score Critical Hits.  When you can afford it or have a magician handy, ask him to make you stuff to throw.  Its also a good skill to pull with.

Looks like a good class doesn't it, well, a rogue has its drawbacks also.

A rogue isn't a tank. Repeat, you are not a tank.  It is really hard to solo as a rogue because of your low HP’s.  Stamina boosts are important but a rogue wasn't designed to take a beating.  We can deal the greatest amount of damage in a span of 8 seconds (4 attacks + Riposte + Backstab) but we cant take a lot of punishment.

Backstabbing can only be done with piercing weapons.  The problem with that is that piercing weapons in the beginning aren't very powerful.  As you level you'll gradually get into a good one but they are hard to come by and very expensive to buy.  Its a good idea to practice 1HS along with Piercing so that when you achieve the ability to Dual Wield you can plant a stronger weapon in your off hand.

We do not have any Magic abilities.

Not well liked in some places.  Faction isn't the best in some Good towns.

Rogues between levels 1 - 9 are basically a really weak warrior.  At level 10 we get the ability to Backstab which will make anybody fear you but gaining those first 9 levels is tough.  Group as much as you can in the beginning because again, soloing isn't easy.

Play a rogue as a warrior and you'll die a lot.  Play the rouge as a pansy Wizard and you'll hate it.  A rogue isn't for everyone.  We are very group dependent but it is within that group that we truly shine.  The abilities we have are unique only to us and melee vs. melee in a dual, I would bet on the rogue.  Rogue vs. Caster…haven’t tried it but something tells me it would be interesting. (Watch out for Root Though)

(As written by Blackpoint Sorrowblade, Rogue of The Shadows of the Ebon Moon)

Attribute Tips
Strength:
Besides how much you can carry, it affects how hard you hit and more importantly backstab.
Agility:
The higher this is, the less likely you'll be hit, of course this is very handy.
Dexterity:
The higher this stat is the more likely you'll hit your target
Stamina:
How many HP’s you have.  There are items out there that can boost this stat.  Should be above 75.
Wisdom:
Besides the advancement of certain skills, rogues don’t use this attribute very much.
Intelligence:
Same as above
Charisma:
Will affect how much vendors will charge or buy things back from you.
