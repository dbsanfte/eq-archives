The Enchanter
Enchanters, the traffic guards of a group, and one of the most sought after members after level 29. Critter control is one of your most vital functions in the group, along with spells that will weaken your enemies and make for much easier kills with less damage taken to the group as a whole. While Enchanters can solo using some careful tactics, they are not as effective at it as Necromancers or Mages and grouping is where you will find your true usefulness. Enchanters are also a great class to roleplay if you desire to do so. Now on to the fun of playing an Enchanter.

Starting out an Enchanter can melee about as well as any other class, and your first few levels should be easy enough to gain you a few gold to buy your first spells with and buy a few pieces of cloth armor. The first few spells you will need as an Enchanter are Minor Shielding, Minor Illusion, Shallow Breath, and Pendril’s Animation. A few words on them, Minor Shielding is a small Hitpoint and Armor Class buff helpful for meleeing. Minor Illusion will make you look like the closest thing to you…useful because enemies will not aggro on you so you can rest in peace, and a lot of fun to cast with others around. Drop a piece of copper on the ground and use the spell to look like a piece of copper…watch as others try to pick you up. ~smiles~  Shallow breath will attack your target for damage and weaken them to help you kill them quickly. Your first animation will summon a “pet” for you. I use the term pet loosely because you cannot control their actions, they will simply guard you and attack whatever attacks you. The additional damage is very useful and the pet can take a few hits for you when you are low on life. One note on your animation line of spells is that they require a tiny dagger to cast. These can be bought at most Enchanter or Casters guilds in your hometown.

The true joy of being an Enchanter becomes evident with your second and third tier of spells available at levels 4 & 8. Additional animation spells at these levels and a few new types to play with. At level 4 you gain Haze and Gate, two spells that will help keep you alive. Gate being the most important as when you cast it, you return to your spawn point or the last place you were bound at. Very useful for when your pet is dying and you have no hope of winning the fight, cast Gate and you are transported to safety. You also get Mesmerize at level 4; mob control comes into play now with you able to keep the enemies from attacking you from multiple angles. One of the nicest spells at level 4 however is fear, with this spell you cast on an enemy and watch as it goes fleeing from you. If you have been hit, your pet will chase it down attacking as it runs. At level 8, Root, Alliance, and Enchant Silver are your new friends in addition to the new de-buffing spells and DoT’s (Damage over Time). With Enchant Silver you can begin learning the basics of jewel crafting, a lucrative trade that Enchanters own the market on. Root will allow you to stop an enemy in their tracks so you can back away and cast on them, nice if your pet dies and you need time to either gate or summon a new pet. Alliance gives you better faction with anyone you cast it on, bringing down prices at merchants and allowing you to complete virtually any quest.

By level 16 when you gain Breeze you will hardly be able to keep the tells from coming asking you to join a group, this is also a big level in your spells with an additional Enchant line of spells and an Area Effect Mesmerize, and a memory blurring spell that will cause your enemy to forget he was attacking you. By now your pets are becoming weaker, and will do much less tanking for you. With some careful planning you can still use them to solo with but it will be much more dangerous and you will need to keep Root and Gate memorized at all times when attempting to solo.

Enchanters are one of the most spell intensive characters to play, and you will have a number of spells to buy at each Tier or every 4 levels. This can be fairly costly, so save your coppers and do what you can to keep them current. Checking out websites such as Everlore, Allakhazam, and Castersrealm can keep you informed as to which spells are most important. Remember that even low level spells can be good for building your level in that skill as every time you use it you gain the chance of increasing your skill level. Most of all have fun; you have very few Hitpoints and cannot take a beating from even green mobs at higher levels so be ready to use your spells to the fullest. Enchanters can be frustrating when you forget your primary purpose, and a true joy to any group when you know your position and how to maximize your potential.
(As written by Krystallia Fierceblade)

When creating an Enchanter you have five races to choose from, each with their own advantages and disadvantages. With all of them you have 30 additional points
to throw in to their base stats. Intelligence (INT) and Charisma (CHA) are the two most important stats, with INT affecting your mana level, and CHA affecting how
often your Charm line of spells stick. CHA will also lower the prices you pay merchants, important in jewel crafting.

Humans: The most averaged set of stats, starting with the highest Strength and Stamina and the second highest Charisma. They do however have the lowest
Intelligence meaning the least amount of mana to call on. Quite a severe disadvantage when playing a caster, as STR and STA will only help for a few low levels.
High Elves: Second lowest INT, though it is still much higher then a human and not far behind the other classes, they have the highest CHA stats. They have a natural
advantage of infravision to see in the dark. They also have the lowest STR of all the races.

Dark Elves: Second highest INT rating, but low starting CHA…they have a natural Ultravision that grants perfect night vision. This makes them KoS in every good
aligned town, but with Illusions and Invisible you should be able to overcome this eventually. Dark Elves are also a great race to roleplay with if you desire to do so.
Gnomes: With only one less INT point then a Dark Elf, they have the same problem with low CHA. Gnomes are a bit easier to get around with then a Dark Elf to
begin with as they are accepted by most Races as a neutral. This can be a big advantage later in life.

Erudites: The highest INT of all the races and good CHA make them very desirable Enchanters. Low agility can be a problem at first, but is little problem later as you will find your INT and CHA is far more important. There are few disadvantages to Erudite Enchanters, with easy access to Qeynos and the hunting grounds there.
I personally prefer High Elves and Erudites for their stat advantages, and Dark Elves for their challenge and roleplaying options.

Whichever race you choose, taking an Agnostic stand is a necessity as casting Illusions will make you appear different most NPC’s will see past it to your religious alignment making you KoS even though you look the part.
