Blackpoint Sorrowblade
 
Sometimes my dad would tell me to, "Stop and smell the roses or everything will pass you by and no one will ever know of you in this crazy world we call home."  I was very young when he told me that but it has stuck to me my whole life.  My parents had both died by the time i decided to one day leave Qeynos and venture forth into the world to find my treasure.  My father was always obsessed with finding a rare weapon that could make him the most feared rogue that ever lived.  I had what was left of the notes and set a foot into the new land.  My younger sister, Whitepoint, argued with me to come back for I had nothing to prove.  I guess thats the difference between a rogue and healer though, I try to change the world one whole at a time while she tried to change it one person at a time.  We said our farewells and I left.
    I ended up with a tall warrior for a lot of my combat training.  I could never swing a sword effectively but i was highly skilled with the smaller blades.  He instructed me early and never lost patience.  Selovare will always be a close friend to me.  We practiced on many of the BlackBurrow Gnolls that run amok in the hills but soon I had surpassed my teacher.  I had graduated to my 10th season and soon was finding my fathers heritage beginning to flourish inside me.  I began to sneak around a lot more.  Stealing from those that were unaware.  I can say I was very sneaky, but foolish.  I was caught by a small dwarf once and he rightfully sent me out of the zone...it was with that incident I moved away from Qeynos and my sister.  I sent her a letter to tell her I would not be able to return to Qeynos anytime soon but I shall sometime be back.  I said my goodbyes in that letter but never really knew what would become of my sister.
    I traveled far..through freeport, and also across the ocean to Kaladim and then into Greater Faydark.  I knew my half-elven linage would grant me some sort of acceptance.  I enlisted to fight Crushbone orcs to help the local guild out and it was there I found my traveling companion Cendarien.  He was spunky, arrogant, and kept calling me his "pretty slave" because i would obey his command when he sent me to attack.  It was odd but somehow we managed to form some sort of alliance and soon became friends, maybe even more.  My patience factor was tried early on but with enough "glaring" I can now say I fully understand him.  I just wish he would stop whipping me :)
    We've traveled the landscape together..his sharp tongue getting us into trouble and my sharp blade getting us out.  His arcane ability has served its purpose more than once but all in all, I would throw my body in front of loose arrow for him.  Unroguish as that may sound, he was my best friend and only friend until now.
    A group knows as the Shadows of the Ebon Moon granted Cen and I passage into their fold.  Without ever being in a true family enviroment, I can say the tough rogue exterior I display at times has melted away.  I'm very passive and not as easily startled.  I would name names but I wouldn't want to foget anyone but the guild has been especially helpful and are always there to talk to.  But then again, a rouge is a rogue and I will never forget my father or his teachings.  The Sorrowblade name must live on.
    It wasn't until a recent trip into Felwithe that I found my sister at the local tavern handing out heals and bandaids to all that had found the wrong end of an orcs sword.  United again, we spent the day talking about the days past, my life as a rogue, her life as a cleric, gods, orcs, and Kunark were all mentioned.  Everything was fine until a civilian came into the bar and told us that there were many elves wounded and any help would be needed.  It was then we saw our differences, she was to heal wounds while I created them...but somehow, it works.  Hopefully, we will once again sit down, Whitepoint, myself, and maybe Cen if he's lucky, and talk about the past without any interruptions.
