The Ranger
The Ranger is considered by many to be one of the most under powered classes out there and with some of the limitation they have, one can understand why. Unlike the other hybrids Rangers do not get the benefit of a "Once-a-Day" skill, they can not wear plate and their defense is the lowest of all the hybrids. On the other hand, Rangers are the only hybrid class that can duel wield and our spell list is better then any other hybrid class out there as well as the Rangers ability to hit critical with a bow. The travel ability of a Ranger is also unsurpassed by all melee classes (except maybe the bard), we have the ability to cast Spirit of the Wolf (increased movement spell) and camouflage, not to mention that we can pass most animals without the worry of being attacked.

    A player should keep all this in mind when deciding to play a Ranger, because the Ranger can be a very difficult class to play but can also be a very rewarding one when all is said and done. As with any class, a player new to Ever Quest should read up on all the classes before choosing one. While some our great at low levels other truly shine when they reach mid twenties. With a little practice a Ranger can be one of the best pullers in the game by using the talents that are often taken for granted, Tracking, Harmony, Snare and their bow and arrows. A  Ranger can pull exactly what he wants and bring it back to his party. Being part Druid also allows you the ability to cast heal, but your healing powers are extremely limited and should only be used in case of an emergency or during down times to save your cleric/shaman/druid some mana. Remember despite the fact that you have some extremely useful spells you  are a fighter through and through and your place is up close and personal with whatever mob your party is fighting. A  Ranger is geared for melee combat, the damage out put of the ranger is equal only by that of the warrior and monk, and your ability to taunt is surpassed by NONE, keep in mind though that you don't have the hit points to tank and your taunt should be used only to pull a mob off your casters and healers.

As a Hybrid most of your stats are important in one way or another, you should prioritize how ever you feel comfortable. One  way would be to build your strength (for damage with your critical hits and weight allowance), dexterity (your ability to land your attacks and to hit critical with a bow), agility (to avoid being hit), wisdom (for mana), and stamina (hit points). However you decide to use your bonus points remember, there are a ton of  stat adjusting items in the world of norrath that can be obtained to boost a low stat.

A Word from Salian...
Well I hope this has helped some of you out there and that the other sections of the newbie help will also help you, I know most of the information here is known by most players already  but I didn't want a total spoiler site. Well anyway this part of the Ranger page is going to be my personal views and of what I feel would be a Rangers  views of the other classes. Well here goes...
 
Bard - Annoying musicians who sing when they should fight and flee when the battle finally starts to get good. Worse then Rogues, a Rogue will steal money and goods and at least you know to distrust a Rogue but the Bards have spent years manipulating others convincing them they are friends just to learn their skills. Be weary around this bunch and guard your family secrets close...
 
 
 

Cleric - Misguided healers of whatever god it is they serve. Very useful in a fight  but other then that what can you say about a group that follows the path of  Tunare & Innorruk except misguided...
 
 
 

Druid - This family makes great allies in any situation, their spells are extremely useful to us and they share pretty much the same goal and beliefs that we do. Make  friends with as many of them as you can as in the later years you will need them as much as they will need us.
 
 
 

Enchanter - A group of finger wigglers that specialize in mind control, make sure their spells are upon the enemy and not yourself. Not much to say about them other then toss em a few shinny gems and watch them drool.
 
 
 

Magician - Another finger wiggler, only this group is a little mysterious but helpful. I have yet to figure out how they do it but they always seem to have what you need when you need it. Another thing to note is that they can summon some pretty nasty elemental pets to help them so if you have to cross one, make a joke about spending to much time with their pets and then disappear...
 
 
 

Monk - I have not had the pleasure or desire to meet one of these yet, but from what I hear they are almost as  bad as the Paladin's. Devoted to mastery of body and mind blah blah blah, if you kill one of em make sure their dead they have a tendency to appear dead when their not. 




Necromancer - The opposite of the clerics, these priests of the dead are an odd group. One of which I have had very little interaction with and intend to keep it that way if at all possible. They carry a reputation for being nasty soul stealers, however the one I fought with didn't seem to be very interested in my soul.



Paladin - They almost have the making of a great class of warrior but their misguided beliefs in a holy crusade make them one of the worst groups out there. Obsessed with cleansing the world of evil, but their quest for peace makes them a greater evil.




Ranger - Perfection...




Rogue - Half thief, half assassin all untrust worthy. While they are notable fighters and half-way  decent scouts their habit of theft tends to make you wonder  how much money that bandit you just killed really had on it. Use them if  you can but never let one get behind you.




Shadow Knight - I know only one of these strange class of person. A knight of death, a very noble warrior who does not try to cover his thirst for blood up in some kind of quest for greater glory or religious crusade. If only they weren't so bent on using the world as one big battle field they might make good allies, but until we convince them to join us in protecting the Mother's creation then watch your back around them and never let one touch you.




Shaman - They have made great allies to us in the past and some still remain with  us to this day, however with the discovery of Kunark and the Iksar Shaman's  there has been some tension in their ranks and have gone off back to their own lands to assist their pilgrims in the exploration of Isle of Cabalis.




Warrior - Really can't say much about these people, they are as varied as the sands on the beach. Every race and religion, good and evil has their warriors and each is set in their own ways. Take em or leave em...




Wizard - Finger wigglers with very nasty spells. They come from all walks of life and all  have one desire, the mastery of their craft. Very dangerous until you cut out their  tongue and give it back to him. Of course even then watch yourself around them, their spells can kill a man rather quickly if you allow them to cast em.
