The Druid
So, let me guess you are here reading this page…does that mean you are wishing to be a druid?  The question is do you have what it takes to become a druid?  I mean what it really takes?  In my honest opinion druids are the best class in Norrath. They are able to cast magic and melee, but after reaching their 20th season, they really shouldn’t have to melee again.  Druids are awesome soloists; we are the best kiters, using our Snare, Dot’s and Root system. We also work well in a group because even though we may not specialize in any one particular area, we can get good in them all, so we are prepared when an emergency may arise. We are welcomed for our group heals, buffs and pack abilities.  Druids are the only priest class that can wield a sword in battle, albeit a scimitar. We are healers of ourselves and of others. Our command over nature through spells is unprecedented by any other class, we are the only class that when we are high enough we can make the rain cease. Druids are able to cast wolf form on their party members at higher levels, and protect them with thorns to pierce their enemies at the touch. Protectors of Nature as we are often called, Druids have the ability to move pass most wild life without being harmed…why only most?  It seems the felines have problems with druids…the question why is still a mystery to me to this day.

Even though Druids seem to be the perfect class, and have a very good offense, we do pay the price.  We are limited by our choice of armor… mostly leather or cloth.  We may not wear the heavier armor that we may feel to need, such as chain and plates.  There may be hope on the horizon though as new armor appears, the Wicker, a type called Tizmak, and gnome skin.  There are other types out there we just have to search for them, and hopefully more to come with the new addition Luclin.

When you begin to make a Druid, they tell you to focus on Wisdom.  Wisdom is very important to a Druid as it will judge the amount of mana you have at your disposal and the rate at which you will gather experience as you hunt and do quests.  I personally place a few bonus points into strength and stamina, for as a soloist in the beginning I was my tank.  So this helped me to be able to hold my own.  Though keep in mind there are many stat-increasing items to be found in game, and it seems strength items are very common.


Also, when you choose a race, I personally prefer the wood-elf.  Why you ask?  We have infravision (the ability to see in the dark), we start out with the ability to forage (50 to begin) even though we get the ability later on, we have the ability to hide which is good when you are meditating alone, we have great wisdom, and agility, decent dexterity. Further more we can wear either small or medium armor, which is a big bonus.  Another thing about the Wood Elf is that their home city is Kelethin, the only city where you do not have to zone to bank!


When playing your druid she/he can be the loving caregiver of the group, or may be the fierce storm before the calm.  Druids are many things, healers, protectors, teachers…but never passive.  Druids are Wanderers, protector of the wilds… so they often make many friends along their journeys.

Remember be generous with your gifts, for it doesn’t take much to do so.  When you are buffing casters remember to include their pets, especially enchanters… for unfortunately for these “head candy dealers” they have to be the meat shield till their pets realize the master is being attacked
 
When buffing Mages and Necromancers make sure you buff those pets to the best of your ability once again sowing the caster in case the pet fails and a quick getaway is needed.

As for the Paladin, Ranger and Warrior make sure you buff them with everything including a damage shield for the main tanks… just remember them as pets and you will be fine. ~Smiles.

Monks and Shadowknights should also be buffed with everything except damage shields, for this will really mess up their feign deaths…of course unless you wish to play a practical joke on them ~smiles Have you ever seen a Shadowknight drop in unrest as a train of 20 decide to leave him alone, and you cast Bramble coat on him?  Nasty ~grins.
If the Shadowknight casts a pet don’t forget to buff it to as above.

Clerics will always trade a better heal than you have for sow. Defensive buffs and regen as well.  They will be appreciative for it seems as if everything in its brother jumps them, especially if it’s dealing with undead.

Wizards are in dire need of defense, regen and sow because of the vast damage their spells do as direct damage, they in themselves are a challenge to keep the creatures taunted off of during a battle.  So many times you and the Cleric are the Wizards lifeline.

It is imperative if you play a Druid that you know your spells and when to use them.  If you have to go out in a field somewhere and practice kiting even if you are using a creature as a guinea pig…this way you can kill two birds with one stone.  Until you get the art of kiting down you will not enjoy the full potential of being a druid in battle.  The sweet taste of success as you drop that Cyclops 4 seasons above you that conned red.

Druids may not be for everyone, or the faint of heart. They are a very versatile and enjoyable class to play.  However, you need to realize, that if you make a druid be prepared that as you move up in seasons that everyone in the brother will want your time for the porting abilities you can do, the gifts you can bestow as in share wolf form and pack abilities.  All in all not too much of a sacrifice for having the pleasure of saying,
“I’m a Druid.”

Aianya,
Wyld Druid,
Daughter of Tunare
Child of Shadows
