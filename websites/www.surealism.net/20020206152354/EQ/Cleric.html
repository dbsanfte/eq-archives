The Cleric
I have heard the opinion that all Clerics are misguided.  At first glance this might appear to be true.  For how else could one explain choosing a class who’s melee abilities are mediocre to poor on the best of days? How else can one explain choosing a class who’s nuking abilities pale in comparison to that of the Wizard?  Then to add insult to injury Paladins, Rangers, Druids and Shamans are out there healing.  What is the point?  For sure the Cleric’s life is not an easy one for it would seem that we have taken a vow of poverty and when others are boasting of their ability to do damage we will be silent, with our head buried in a spell book.   Some may say the answer is that we are misguided, but before you are quick to judge I would claim that Greatness awaits the Cleric. It’s just that Greatness decides to wait for us further on down the road.

The life of a Cleric is one of service. 

A Cleric has the ability to help others without taking away the thrill of the kill. The Cleric has three lines of buff spells that raise HP and AC that are unsurpassed and stackable.  With the combination of these three buffs a cleric can buff a player to extremely high statistics that are unattainable with the buffs of any other class.  A Cleric can answer the desperate call for a cure to rabies, disease or poison. A Cleric can provide food or water to the undernourished and dehydrated far from civilization. When it comes to attacking the undead, the Cleric has no equal.  The Cleric has a higher AC than any other caster. Most importantly, the healing abilities are the best of any healing class.  We get the healing spells sooner, and we are the only ones to get Complete Healing.  We also get the Revive to Resurrect line of spells 20 levels sooner than the Paladin (the only other class to receive these spells). Need more?   Decide to be a Cleric and you will seldom have trouble finding a group that wants you amongst them.

That said I agree with Eloair from his article on The Art of Healing.

“Don’t get to focused in on all the numbers and what not. How you use your skills, and the style you chose to do so in will dictate your success and happiness with this class more than anything. But I would say that if you are not willing to stand your ground, giving up your last mana in a heal so that a party member might escape knowing it is your doom, don’t play the class.”

A Cleric is meant to travel in groups


Can a cleric solo?  Sure for levels 1 – 4.  This is when the cleric is about the same as everyone else. You kill bats, wasps, try to stay alive and make a few copper.  Once you get to level 5 your soloing days are over. Your direct damage spells are not as strong as any other caster. And although it may seem at first that you hit well, as your levels increase you will get weaker and weaker compared to anyone else. Remember you are a healer not a fighter.

One notable exception, if soloing you must, chose to pick on the undead.  The cleric gets a line of undead direct damage spells that will knock your socks off. Add to that Invisibility vs. Undead, Sense the Dead, and a line of Undead only Fear spells and you are uniquely equipped to take on these terrors of Norrath. Go to Befallen or Unrest and have a blast. Better yet form a group of Clerics and Paladins and really Rock and Roll.

A Life of Poverty/The Need for Armour


Start saving your copper from day one. You will need it for purchasing your spells and they get more expensive with each passing level. You will also need to obtain armour.  Get the best you can afford. This is one of the benefits of being a cleric.  We have the highest armour class of any of the casters and can wear almost any type. The down side is that you will need it.  Once you start casting heal and greater heal you will discover that the baddies will agro on you.  In fact there is no better taunt than a Greater Heal.

 You will also want money for buying items that increase the various statistics.  This is how you can compensate for any weaknesses in your race’s base level skill allocation. For example the dwarf may want to work on his agility.  If you are an elf you need to increase strength and stamina.  And of course, no matter what race you chose, I suggest that you concentrate on Wisdom.  In fact, wisdom is so important to the cleric that when the choice is to be made between more mana or more wisdom, chose wisdom. Wisdom affects how much mana you get at each level, the higher your level, the better the payback on this investment


1H Blunt vs. 2H Blunt


A very limited choice, you can fight with clubs, maces, hammers and staffs etc.  I recommend using 1H Blunt.  At level 9 you get Hammer of Wrath, the first in a series of spells that gives you the ability to summon a magical 1H Blunt weapon.  Not a bad weapon either.  Also, using one hand Blunt leaves the other hand free to carry a shield or wisdom-enhancing item.

Channeling


Channeling first becomes available at level 4. This may seem useless at lower levels.  Keep working on this skill, as it gives you the ability to get a spell off even when under attack.  Believe me when I say, you will be attacked.  When your Channeling skill gets high enough, instead of “Your spell has been interrupted” you will start to see the message,  “You regain your concentration and continue casting”.

Meditate
Until level 9 dieing and re-memming your spells is a major bummer. Once you get meditate that little red line moves at a pretty fast clip. Meditating will become a way of life for the cleric.  Parties where there is a bard or an enchanter will be cause for celebration. It is important to conserve mana. It is key to the battlefield strategy.   The puller will often wait on the cleric to restore mana before pulling a mob to the group.  A cleric who melees or uses up mana for nukes and then makes the party wait while he meditates will soon be a lonely cleric. Let the Wizard’s nuke, leave the tanking to those who do it well.  Sit down and meditate during the battle so that the group can have more time fighting and less time sitting around.  That said, you will sometimes find yourself in groups where the party is handling the battles quite easily.  If you are at full mana, by all means join in on the melee and raise that 1H blunt. Or cast a nuke or two and work on your Evocation skill.  My point is, make sure you are ready when your group needs you.

Proving that there is an exception to every rule, another strategy especially in an add situation is that a good offense makes for a good defense. Sometimes it is wise to assist the primary tank with a few nukes to kill off one beastie quickly and then med as the tank takes care of the second.

Once you get some experience, you will develop your style and find the right balance of medding, buffing, nuking that works best for you.

Dodge


Don’t forget to put a point in dodge at level 15.  This is the only additional defensive skill we get.  There is no hope at ever being excellent here. This skill maxes at Very Good for the Cleric.  But we need every bit of help we can to stand tough against the mob when they agro.


Finding Greatness
 ( I give credit to Eolair’s  The Art of Healing)

Welcome to level 39 …. At level 29 you gained Revive and the ability to raise the dead without reclaiming of experience. And while that one may save some folks some time an effort (particularly if they are bound far from where they died), Resuscitate will curl their toes. Also at this point you are now armed with Superior healing and Complete healing as well as buffs that will raise AC ratings by 40 points and hit points by 400 – 500 points. You are now the glue that holds parties together. Your DD spells are doing respectable damage, your stuns and roots are sparing people a few choice hits and kicks, and you can stand up and take a beating for a few moments when you make an attacking creature mad with your masterfully timed Complete healing that just healed a warrior two thousand hit points (Are there any others out there casting spells that can do two thousand hit points of anything?).

But use the wisdom (and I don’t mean your wisdom stat) you have developed over many levels of how to mix up offense and defense to maximize your mana and effectiveness. Know when to stand and fight and when to sit and med. Know when a quick offense is a good defense and when your party is just going to need all the healing it can get. Your strengths are unmatched at higher levels so use them wisely.  You will be cheered and cherished by many.

So bask in your glory; you earned it by toughing out level after level while others claimed the title of hero.  And remember, your casting Resuscitate costs you 500 mana, but, to the person you just rezzed, you just gave someone the only thing in all of Norrath that money can’t buy and everyone wants…. Experience points. 



(As written by Kirbee Healmore, Cleric of the Shadows of the Ebon Moon)

Strength not only helps in battle, but more importantly to the Cleric it affects how much you can carry.  Know this, you will need good armour.  You must be strong enough to wear it.
