                                   Salian - Q: There is a lot of controversy about weather or not spoiler sights such as Allakhazam ruin the game for
                                                     the players and take away from the excitement of discovering new quests and such. How do you feel
                                                     about this?

                           Allakhazam - A: Well obviously I am prejudiced in this matter, but I think spoiler sites and other fan sites add to the
                                                     game.  First, and most important, our sites build up an even closer sense of community.  Even when
                                                     not playing the game, you can still go to one of the various message forums and continue to communicate
                                                     with other Everquest players.  You can read about what others have accomplished and share tips and
                                                     stories with each other.  It's a community game, and the sites that have grown up around it reflect that.


                                                     Second, this game already takes enormous amounts of time to play correctly.  People have jobs, family
                                                     and friends beyond Everquest.  When it already takes from 12 hours to several days to camp most of the
                                                     major items and when even the more minor items can take many hours to get, you don't want to spend
                                                     attitional time just trying to figure out where to go.  The quest and item spoilers help direct your time and
                                                     make it more productive, and thus more fun.  Since having fun is the purpose behind any game, this is a
                                                     good thing.


                                   Salian - Q: What made you start Allakhazam.com?


                           Allakhazam - A: I was in beta and fell in love with the game.  I had convinced several friends to go out and buy the game
                                                    (their wives still won't forgive me for that), so I decided to write up a guide on how to play it.  I also
                                                    wanted to learn how to develop a website, just because it seemed like useful knowledge.  I wrote up a
                                                    fairly elaborate guide and posted it up on a free web service and asked for feedback on various
                                                    message forums. People liked it and suggested additions, and I just started adding things to it and it
                                                    kind of snowballed.  I never imagined it would get as big as it has.


                                  Salian - Q: Do you consider Alakhazam .com a job or is it something you do for the sheer enjoyment of doing it?


                         Allakhazam - A: It started out as a hobby and continued as a hobby for quite a while.  As it got bigger, I reached a point
                                                  where I was spending a good portion of my free time on it.  I had also teamed up with Illia, who was
                                                  adding some amazing database elements to it.  I knew that if I wanted to continue to build it up and
                                                  make it better, and if I wanted to cover other games as well, I would have to do it full time.
                                                  The allure of doing something for a living that I really enjoy and of being my own boss was pretty hard
                                                  to deny.  Therefore, I decided to make it a business.  So to answer your question, it is a job that I do
                                                  out of the sheer enjoyment of doing it.


                                Salian - Q: What is the least thing you like about Allakhazam.com?


                        Allakhazam - A: Now that the internet advertising market has crashed, I have to spend far too much time trying to come up
                                                 with funding to keep the site afloat and bring in enough money to pay my bills as well.  Far too many of
                                                 the companies that deal in internet advertising are amoral and unscrupulous.  I practiced law for 13
                                                 years, so I know how to deal with those types, but I don't have to like it.  I've also had to become a
                                                salesman, which is not something I ever wanted to do. I also have to go on the site and basically beg for
                                                contributions from the site visitors, which neither I nor the visitors really enjoy.  Still, it is all part
                                                of running a business, and I enjoy the business enough to continue to do what it needs to make it thrive.


                               Salian - Q: Do you ever regret starting Alakhazam.com?


                       Allakhazam - A: Not at all.  It's been not only a load of fun, but an incredible learning experience for me.


                                Salian - Q: Do you consider other spoiler sights such as, everlore and castersrealm competition?


                       Allakhazam - A: I guess they are competition in the sense that they are the standards I have to live up to.  I've always
                                                 been driven to be the best at everything I do and have never put anything less than my full effort into
                                                 anything I have tried to accomplish.  So in that respect, I tend to measure my progress against that of
                                                 the other sites.  But I don't consider them competition in such a way that I feel their success
                                                 will somehow affect mine.  We all do things in a somewhat different way, which I think enhances the
                                                 community, and I have always tried to encourage people to visit all of the sites out there, not just mine.
                                                 I've met the guys from both Caster's Realm and Everlore, and corresponded with the folks at other
                                                 sites such as EQ'Lizer and EQ Stratics, and I like them and only wish them the best.
