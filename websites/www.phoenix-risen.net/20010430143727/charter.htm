We are the children of the Phoenix; we arise from the ashes of those who have fallen before us, reborn and stronger than ever.  We draw strength in adversity; we stand true and unafraid in the face of that which challenges us.  We soar to the heavens on our collective strength; we remain individuals yet we function as one.  We embrace diversity for harmony does not come from a song with but a single note.  We know that respect is earned, not demanded; our actions define who we are.  We are guided by the principles of love, honor, loyalty, and family.  To live long, love deeply, and laugh much…these are the rules that we live by.

 

CODE OF HONOR

  • Phoenix Risen is about friendship, fellowship, and honor.  Every guild member will treat one another with kindness and respect.  PR members are to discuss grievances privately with one another first, then seek the guidance of guild officers if the issue cannot be resolved.  Guild members should NEVER air their grievances with one another publicly or over the guild channel; doing so will be dealt with swiftly by Phoenix Risen’s Executive Counsel.

  • Guild members will help one another at every opportunity possible, within reasonable limits.  Should a member put out a call for assistance, guild members are encouraged to respond if at all possible.  Reasonable exceptions to this rule are members who are more than 3 zones away with no teleport abilities, members engaged in a raid, members engaged in a long encampment, members in transit (i.e. – taking a boat, etc).  There are other exceptions to this standard that may arise but are not listed here; if you have any doubts, please ask a guild officer. 

  • Each member of Phoenix Risen will conduct him or herself in a manner befitting the principles on which we stand.  Killstealing, ninja looting, intentional training, and fraud are absolutely forbidden; we are a guild of honor.  If a problem arises, members should first try to work out differences with their fellow players.  Should no resolution come about, contact both a GM and either the Guild 1st Officer or the Guild Leader to report the situation.  Members should not respond in kind to any of these acts, for doing so means sinking to the offender’s level of immaturity. 

  • All members should be aware of Phoenix Risen’s alliance partners.  Members should greet players from alliance guilds as they would a member of PR – with the open hand of brotherhood.  Our alliance mates look to us as friends in both times of plenty and times of need; all Phoenix members should respond in kind.  Be gracious with your time, a SoW, a teleport…you may never know when you might have to call upon an alliance mate for the same. 

  • Phoenix Risen maintains a Treasury wherein the guild keeps items that members may find useful and a store of funds to be used to assist our Enchanters with their jewelry-making efforts, for festivals and events, and to be loaned out for purchases by members.  Members are encouraged but NOT required to donate unused equipment, spells, and money to help this effort.  The Treasury rules are: 

  1. One guild officer will be assigned to maintain the guild treasury, report on its current balance, to accept donations, and to dole out funds and/or equipment as requested.  This officer will create a “mule” character designated solely for this purpose.

  2. Any member in good standing may borrow equipment and/or funds from the Treasury, subject to these rules:

    • The equipment borrowed must be returned when the member can no longer make use of it so that other members will have access to it.

    • Any funds borrowed must be returned within one Everquest calendar year.

  3. Enchanters may request funds to further their jewelry-making skills provided that they offer the result of any successful jewelry-making attempts to guild members first at below-market prices or free of charge.

  • Phoenix Risen does not have any restrictions on the size of its membership, however, members are asked to remember that our numbers are not increasing for the sake of having a large membership roster.  PR has a specific code of conduct and certain expectations that all members are expected to follow; we want only new members who will build upon this foundation.  If you have a candidate for entry into the guild, members are asked to put the candidate in contact with our Recruitment Counselor.

  • Phoenix Risen has intentionally left its rules and disciplinary structure loosely defined; members are expected to conduct themselves in a manner that is honorable without strict rules and regulations being dictated by guild leaders.  However, there may come a time when words or events require action, including removal from PR’s ranks.  While it is hoped that no member ever suffers this ultimate sanction, members can expect the following, should this occur:

    1. If a member is considered for removal, the final decision shall lie with and only with the Guild Leader.  Members are welcome to voice their concerns to the Guild Leader and the Guild Executive Counsel, however, the final decision is his and his alone.  The only exception to this rule is requests for reinstatement to Phoenix Risen.

    2. The member will be stripped of all rights and responsibilities granted to him or her by Phoenix Risen, including entrance to PR events and raids.  The member will no longer have access to Phoenix Risen resources including the guild treasury and items camps.  It is up to each Phoenix Risen member to decide whether to continue providing SoW's, teleports, or resurrections to the removed member.

    3. Any punishment, including removal from Phoenix Risen will be presented and discussed with the member in question.  No member will be sanctioned or removed without first being given the opportunity to discuss the issue at hand.

    4. If a member is removed, he or she may request to have the removal re-evaluated after one RL month has elapsed.  The Guild Leader, the 1st Officer, and a member of the Executive Counsel designated by the Guild Leader will then review the removal, the circumstances leading to it, and the removed member's reasons for wanting to be reinstated.  If the panel agrees to lift the removal, the member will be reinstated as a probationary member of Phoenix Risen for a term of no less than one RL month.  After one month, the member may be returned to full membership or may be removed permanently.  While on probation, a member may not access the Guild Treasury and his or her actions may be closely monitored.

    5. A member may not return to Phoenix Risen after being removed a second time.
