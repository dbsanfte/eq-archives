"I need a response from everyone to this."
Posted by Dalimaar on 30-Sep-01, 09:59 AM (EST)
I have given a lot of thought to this, and I don't ask this question without a lot of reservations for a lot of reasons.

I have opened a dialog with the Ambassador of Soldiers of Fortune. The subject of this conversation is the merger of PoI into SoF. PoI would be disbanded and its membership taken into SoF.

Before you go postal on me, let me give you my thoughts on this subject.

Over the last few months I have become less and less interested in playing. After a lot on consideration I came to realize the reason for this was that all I ever do these days is level one character or the other. I got to thinking about the old days when the guild was at its height and realized that the reason I liked to play the game so much was not the game, but the people I came to know. Deadly, Dazzle, Moulolen, Knomlic, Warlar, Diin, Sadarie, the list goes on and on. Most of these people were the founding members of the guild. We used to group together and raise all kinds of hell in Rathe Mountains or some damn dungeon that Deadly would drag us into.

As with all things, times changed. People left the game, the face of the guild changed. New members came in but slowly over time, the guild grew smaller and smaller. Now, it is not uncommon to spend hours online and never see another guild member. I think the most I've seen on all summer at one time was maybe 4 or 5.

One member made the comment the other night that all they seemed to do anymore was level. I've heard many times "Wow, someone else is on. I've been on all day by myself".

To draw this to a close, I want to get back to having a guild that always has someone online no matter what time of day you might be on. SoF is a very large guild. I did a /who all Soldiers tonight and the list was too long to list them all. We have had associations with them in the past and I've found most of their membership to be good people.

SoF is not the only guild that we can consider for a merger either. Some of our recent members that have left have gone to Wolves of War. I'm sure each of you knows of a guild that you would not mind being a member of. This is open for discussion.

I need each guild account holder to respond to this message with your thoughts on where we want to take the guild in the future.

Dalimaar Dark(heavy)Heart

1. "RE: I need a response from everyone to this."
Posted by Eilsel Loveangel on 01-Oct-01, 02:25 AM (EST)
The special spark to me that made POI so special was that it was close and tight. Everyone knew everyone else. It seemed like anything any one did, every one did -- and did it together. POI travelled together and did things together, in game or in life it seems, as POI even had RL socials together.

SOF is a large guild. I'm not sure it if will be the answer. Large organizations have always seemed to me to be places to get lost in. It'd be to me just the same kind of death for the guild, whether merged or just disbanded, as the special spark would not be able to really exist in the same way.

As for levels, the lack of leveling in the old core members is one of the best things about POI. I remember many other raids I've been in with other organizations where people were arbitrarily rejected just because of level. I do believe that everyone can be useful. Yes it can be sometimes dangerous, but that doesn't make a raid impossible, it just makes it more demanding on the execution. It is this facet that brings one of my fondest memories of POI to mind, how when SOV first opened and POI raided the RyGorrs, the guild made sure everyone had a role and a part to play if they wanted in. Some people were way low, but that didn't mean that they could not still twitch, or buff, or heal, or anything that still contributed to the overall effort. In the end it was a smashing success. People walked away with loot, and experience -- BOTH the experience in the game and more importantly in just enjoying a game together and working together. Level differences never really seemed a big thing to POI in those days. It appeared many other raids were held in this manner, such as in Dalnir when ROK first openned, or in the Wastelands of SOV. I remember often how many members of POI would sit around for days not leveling to help the others members around them.

I believe all of this just arises from playing together. Guild rules, and laws, and recruiting, and officerships -- those are all artificial constructs at worst and administrative frameworks at best. All that POI really needs is people who enjoy going around and doing things. If a guild rises again from this, then there really is a guild. If it doesn't, all the rules and stuctures and membership lists will never really ammount to much other than a guild in name only.

This leads to the real point of the message -- the answer to the question of where we want to take the guild. My vote is not to change things. If it dies, let it die. It had a good life and gave us some of the most fun we've had. I'd rather not lose that memory by having it just be assimilated by a larger group. But what I'd prefer is just to have all the established members just try to play more with others. I know that potential is there. I know Buffy has been trying to round up folks and play together. I see many of our new ones looking for assistance and for groups with other members. I think Buffina's approach is the right one and is where the guild once had its strength and what will build a guild again.
