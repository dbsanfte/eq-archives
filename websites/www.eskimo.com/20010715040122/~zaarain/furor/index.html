Monday, June 11, 2001 Millay
Only 4 of us were able to meet on Sunday night (Gruff, Cole, Faulk, and Millay) so we hesitated to go to the Tower of Frozen Shadows as we'd planned. Instead, we headed off to Rathe Mtns to help Faulk with an item for his epic weapon quest, which was quickly accomplished.

That done, we decided to try for Millay's first item in her quest, which involved swimming to an underwater cave in Lake Rathe and killing Lord Bergurgle. According to all we'd heard, it should have been easy enough for a group of  4 40's to take him out. Alas, he was guarded by deepwater goblins, some of which could cast heal. We'd use up all our resources on just 2 of them and not have enough to go after the Lord, and by the time we were ready, they'd respawned. Kedge Keep was good practice for this place! We decided we'd come back another day when we had more resources and headed off to Eastern Wastes to slaughter snow griffins.

There were no snow griffins to be found, though, so we settled for merely getting experience at the Rygorr fort. After an hour, Kera arrived so we felt brave enough to try the Tower. We had nothing to fear: we went to the 2nd floor, cleared it in short order (half the spawn is green to us now), got the Angry Librarian to spawn and drop the Student's Homework and a key to the 3rd floor (giving the group its second key). Fearlessly (ok, not fearlessly, we were nervous), we made our way to the 3rd floor. Much to our suprise, even though all the mobs here conned blue to us, it was easier work than the 2nd floor had been when it was all blue. We found the Enraged Shadow Beast three times, for 3 keys to the 4th floor and a lovely war/sk/nec no-drop scythe that turned out to be useable only by evil races. Gruffydd, Faulkner, and Coleridge all leveled so it turned out to be a very productive night, in spite of being short-handed.

Tuesday we return to the Tower to take on the more challenging fourth floor.

OK, Lesson number one from Wednesday night:

Kael can suck.  Big time.

About an hour or more after starting our evening we had 5 of 6 across, and we decided to venture into WL and check it out.  We cautiously moved North along the zone wall looking for our goal for the evening:  Tigeraptors and Tigersaurous.  There is a static spawn of 3 or 4 a ways in from the zone, so we set up camp and pulled those and wanderers in the forest.  The Tigeraptors are pertty easy, and the Tigersaurous' are quite a bit more difficult, though they were no match for our party.  We probably killed 15-20 and ended up with several hides and Tigeraptor meats.  Unfortunately, we were unsuccessful in getting the guts part of the Ice forged shackles quest, so we will need to revisit another day.  I attempted to pull one Hologresh (an elemental) but was somewhat relieved to outrun him back to camp as he unloaded several DD spells onto me.  We'll need MR items when we hunt them to finish the 2nd half of the shackles quest...

It was a long and disappointing night, but I think we learned quite a bit about WL.  I think this is a place we'll be visiting quite a bit in the mid-40's, but until we have getting through Kael perfected, it just isn't a whole lot of fun.
