OLD -----Inside info on the Vendor Delete

Well the vendor delete bug worked well. I can give you a little inside info after testing so often. Vendors seated in thrones or stools are exempt from this bug. This is your only defense. First cast the Energy Field on yourself so the field is in a North/South position. If there is a row of vendors in a straight line you can cover all of them with the spell if you are correctly positioned. Then simply drop 10gp or more (6gp will not work) on the ground under the vendor then pick it up. The vendor will be booted out of the house and since any vendor can not be placed away from a house it is wiped. I was wiping multiple vendors at one time very easily. The dev team immediately fixed this by disabling the EF spell on Pacific. I am not sure about the other shards yet as I have not had time to log on and test.

Deleting Items in a Large Forge w/out wait

First place a communication crystal on the counter ( I did this on the middle part ) Then you place a small wood table then put a torso on top of that then remove the table so the torso falls on top of the communication crystal then place the table on top of the torso (the table on placed on top of the torso when you made the torso drop first) then put what you want to delete on top of the table that is on top of the torso then remove the table all the stop that was on top of the table will drop and disappear forever.

Cheese.. Its back

I have known this bug has been back. I've choose to ignore it but this e-mail was just too damn good to ignore. Anywhom don't bother asking for credit for this bug because its probably the most well known bug we know of today! But alas it is good for making lots of money as a newbie. -- SickBoy

One word: Cheese.

That's right. Those good ol' cheese wheels at yer local Tavern. All you need to do is find an NPC that will buy cheese wheels. They are at Inns and Taverns. Then do the following:

  1. Buy all the cheese wheels they have in stock.
  2. Sell back five cheese wheels.
  3. Buy the cheese wheels back again. Here's where it gets tricky. When you go to buy back the cheese wheels, only one shows up on their inventory. Buy that one, then spew out your "Vendor Buy" macro again. There will be another cheese wheel in their inventory. Repeate till they have no more cheese wheels for sale.
  4. Sell back five cheese wheels again...
  5. Repeat till you are overloaded with gold, or the NPC stops wanting cheese.

Here's how it works: When you originally buy the cheese wheels, they are priced at 25 gold a piece. After buying the vendor's whole stock, you can sell back the cheese wheels at 25 gold a piece. Here's where the trick comes in to play... All cheese you sell... you can buy them back at 3 to 5 gold a piece. Once you cycle the cheese to start, you then make approximately 100 gold each time you complete a buy/sell cycle. If done fast enough (and with a short character name), you can make about 200 to 250 gold a minute. That's about 15K an hour... just as good as tailoring... but no skill needed. Add in some skill practice (anatomy/eval int/hiding/what-not) and you've got a nice way to make money and gain skills.

If you're going to use the cheese, do it fast... as I'm hoping it will be fixed soon.
