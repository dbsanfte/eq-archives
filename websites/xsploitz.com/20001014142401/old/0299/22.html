Tip -

Ok guys listen up, thievery is really really easy to build, you just have to know how to do it.  I built up a thief to 97. 3 skill in two nights.  First and foremost you need a partner, someone with patience, because you have to be manually doing this.  It takes maybe 6 - 8 hrs in total.  Thievery is all weight based.  What I did with my partner, is take a bag or a sack or whatever, and put an amount of weight in it, start off at like 2 stones for new thieves, and when you get so you don't raise from stealing that, raise the weight up a notch.

Here is the full explanation.

Make up several bags, as much weight as your skill will provide, also make a couple bags, less by one stone, and more by one stone.  Or you can also use items that weigh as much as a made up bag would.  Example is the bow, instead of making an xxx amount of bag, just use the single item that weights just as much.  Then just start stealing the bags/items from each other.  That's all there is to it really.  When you or your partner stop gaining skill off of one weight class, up the ante, and add another stone.  The maximum you can steal in a bag is nine stones, it slows down a lot when you reach 90, but it will continue to rise as long as you continue to steal the maximum weight.  For me, at around 95 it was horrible slow, like thirty steals for .1 skill.

I've heard that you can use UOP or UOE for something like last targeting, so see if you can set it up so you can keep stealing the last target off your partner, but I have not done this, so I don't know if it will work, also, RANDOM STEALING SUCKS!!!!, it does not raise skill very fast and it will raise it only to a point, it cuts out at around 40 I think, it's really useless. You MUST steal the item directly, meaning snoop the guy and click what you want to steal.

Note: BEING FULL IS A GOOD IDEA, I was full when I made my thief, and it seemed to really speed things along, also I trained my thief in my friends house, so we where not disturbed.  The night before, just make a fisherman, or find some food, plenty of it, and snack away while your build up your char.

And as for snooping, you all know its real easy, just macro the damn thing, do it with your partner for a night, (a night is all you need, since there is no waiting in-between snoops), and your come out at like 95 - 100, easy.  Or an alternative method is hire an NPC, give him enough gold for a couple days, and take him to some remote location, trap him in with boxes, he will eventually move them, but it gives you some extra time, if your cash runs out, and snoop the NPC in a macro, it works really well.  Same thing, do that for a night, and you are finished with snooping.

After that, you also have max DEX.  Then build up some STR, gain skill in the weapon of your choice, and you now have a tank thief.  Or if you like stat playing, (which I can't stand), make him into a mage/thief.

Start your thief with some fencing skill, DON'T EVER DOUBT THE POWER OF THAT PRACTICE SPEAR, OR KNIFE, I personally have killed, TONS, of "I hate thieves", warriors, and mages, when I was wearing no armor, and at around 70 skill in fencing, They would be in bone, and I could take them, it was almost unbelievable, but it kicked ass, bad thing is, it eventually wears out, and when I tried to get it repaired, it was not the same, but it lasts about a month, and you never lose it when you die.  One thing I've notices about UO is it all comes down to skill in a weapon, armor does not seem to effect that much with swordplay.  Sometimes you got lucky, and you could take a plated warrior.  And this is still at 70 skill.

One thing I hated where archers, if you got lucky, you could steal there arrows/bolts, and then they're useless, an easy target, but they move around so damn much, and of course damn OSI made it so you could not steal when in combat, it gets pretty hard to steal their ammo, so first thing you do, is see who the archer is, and take his stuff.

A good thing to use is UOE, it has the "bags and boxes wont automatically close", function, which works really well, run past the guy, while using last skill, and you can open his bag, look at what he has, from really far away, decide what you want, and then run up and take it, no body expects this, hey you where just running by weren't you?.

One thing I noticed is, people can be pretty stupid, they stand right beside you, when they are trying to track you down.  (you are hidden in this situation)  Leaving you free to snoop and steal at will.  Minoc mines, is always a promising place, and eventually, you get the one idiot, that will attack you, in full plate, loaded up, and will be just a little to close for the guards to come, and WAM, he dies, and its free loot for you.  Anger is a wild weapon.

Lastly I recommend building up hiding, it has saved me many times, and its a good tool all around, a thief without hiding is a dead thief.  And in the future, OSI plans on making it so that you can take a couple of steps when you are hidden, and you don't unhide!!, think of the possibilities of this one for thieves.

P.S, snooping will not reveal you when hidden, stealing will, just a small note for those new thieves out there.

Sincerly Deadly Arts, Proud Thief on Somona
