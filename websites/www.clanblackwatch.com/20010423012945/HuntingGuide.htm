Hunting & Adventuring Guide

Here are some suggestions on where to hunt and some strategies to use.  I am planning to provide a table to summarize the information, but I have not had time yet.  The picture is of sunrise in the Frontier Mountains, included because it suggests the adventure that awaits on a brand new day, in a brand new land...

Before I forget, here is my disclaimer: EverQuest, much like real life, can be enjoyed in many different ways.  I do not "play it safe", nor have I any desire to do so.  Some of my strategies and suggestions significant risk, but with the greater risk comes greater reward.  Also, I think it is more fun :)

Starting Out (1-4)

I don't want to say too much here, as there are many "starting out" guides already available. I will try to point out a few things that I have found useful, and maybe provide a few suggestions different from the usual.

You will enter the world with very little. Regardless of where you start, there are a couple of things that you need to do (after memorizing your initial spells and finding your guild, of course). Get your Burst of Flame and Flamelick spells as soon as you can. Flamelick requires a fire beetle eye, so if there are no fire beetles in your zone, see if anyone has an extra one. Snare will become a very useful spell, but don't worry about it right now. After a level or maybe two, it can be helpful to leave your starting zone, but don't go too far.  Many starting or near-starting zones will provide you with some minimal armor (courtesy of orcs, goblins, gnolls, etc).  Not only will you increase your faction, but even this cloth armor is much better than none.

If you are a halfling, have no worries. You start in a great place. Misty Thicket is not only well guarded, but provides excellent hunting up to at least level 9 or 10. Stay right where you are, and enjoy your nice home. Just don't go past the wall until you are around level 5. 

If you are in Faydark, I recommend leaving for the Butcherblock Mountains as soon as you can (level 2). Many people wait a bit, but there are some reasons not to do this. First, I have found the Greater Faydark is one of the only places where people will frequently steal your level one monsters. I find this annoying. Butcherblock provides a wondrous and unpopular creature for you to hunt -- the large skunk. Now you will not smell very pretty after fighting these creatures, but neither will you be level 2 for very long. Also, they occasionally drop scent glands, which are worth a few gold.  As you progress, you can hunt large spiders, large snakes, dwarf skeletons, goblins (5-10), giant bats. Just stay away from those basilisks. They will not hurt you unless you mess with them, in which case they will kill you.

  • Don't forget to train channeling at Level 4

Next Steps (5-8)

Now you get some new spells, but probably you don't want to wander too far just yet. It's still a long run back with no SoW if you die (which seemed to happen to me frequently). Keep your burst of fire and your new burst of flame both memmed. You can be interrupted very easily, so sometimes you will need the faster casting of the level 1 spell. You get a root spell, but it will usually be resisted, so use snare instead. Keep using your flame lick. The AC debuff helps your fairly weak combat skills a bit.  Try your wind spell when you have extra mana.  It will do more damage than anything you have, but it is expensive and will be resisted a lot.  You can gate home, but you can't cast it fast enough for use as an escape yet. Don't forget the wondrous harmony.

  • If you don't have the ability, you can learn to forage at level 5.
  • You get meditate at level 8.  This makes your life much better.  Don't forget to go train it.

Thorns & Being a Tree (9-13)

At level 9, some wondrous things happen to you.  First, you get a damage shield.  This easily underestimated spell is a HUGE increase in your offensive power.  Now at first it is a bit expensive and won't last long, but do not be without it when soloing.  If grouped, do not let your main tank be without it.  You also get a new DD and a new heal, both huge improvements on your old ones.  Treeform is nice if you have the money. Water breathing is useful later, but don't worry about it right now.  These spells get expensive, so you may have to pick and choose.  Unless you are human, don't worry about the light spell.

Solo Hunting Suggestions: Butcherblock (higher goblins, drakes, basilisks (!)), Crushbone entrance area, EC (spiders, scarabs, lions, orcs), WC (orcs, spiders, some undead), Befallen, Misty (Giant Wasp, Scarab, Goblins), Runnyeye (stay near entrance), NRo, Oasis, West Karana, Blackburrow (ick)

Group Hunting Suggestions:Crushbone, WC Dervs, N Ro Dervs, some Oasis Orcs (watch out for annoying "helpful" people who tell you to go back to NRo)

Can I Get a SoW Please? (14-18)

Well, now you can finally SoW.  Equally important, you can bind and avoid that eight zone run to your corpse.  Some of us still remember the joys of running from freeport to North Karana all too well...  If you like, you can stand around shouting something like "Sowing for donations at <wherever>".  Or you can be really stingy and never cast SoW on anyone.  Personally, I think it is better to be nice, just don't let people take advantage of you.  That SoW that barely moves your mana bar at level 40 is pretty expensive at level 14.

Solo Hunting Suggestions: You will have mostly outgrown Misty, though Mooto (goblin) and some scarabs will give some exp initially, Runnyeye top, North Karana (beetles are great), East Karana pet techniques , Lavastorm (more dangerous now to cross Nektulos, however), Oasis, NRo

Group Hunting Suggestions: Minotaur Gorge (toward 18), Unrest Yard, NK (for harder monsters), Oasis.

  • Dodge at 15 is easy to forget, so go train it.

Walking.  Who Needs It? (19-23)

Now many people say that 14 is the greatest level for druids (since you get SoW) but I think I was happier at 19.  Things not going your way in your zone?  Do what any good druid would do and teleport yourself somewhere else.  If you hunt near one of your porting zones, you can use your bind point as a 5th location (you get four teleports at 19).  Want to be able to check out Unrest anytime?  Bind outside the door, then hunt in NK or WC whenever the mood strikes you.  You get some other very nice spells at 19, be sure to check out the EQ Druids link at the beginning of this section.

Solo Hunting Suggestions: North Karana (Scythe Beetles), East Karana, Oasis.  Many possibilities.

Group Hunting Suggestions: Lake Rathe, Unrest (Round, then Hand or Bar Rooms)

  • Tracking is a great skill, and will also save your life frequently (early warning system for mean stuff).  Go train it at 20.  It will max fast (at skill 50).

Being a Wolf & Walking Even Less (24-28)

These are nice levels for druids, though I had some troubles at level 28.  Expect to see some more notes for these and later levels soon.

Solo Hunting Suggestions: Go pay Broon back for that time he smushed you.  The gnoll clan in EK provides some great exp too, though I cannot remember their exact level.  Use harmony, you do't want them all.  SK is good if uncrowded.

Group Hunting Suggestions: Unrest is great (Hand, Night, Magi, even TR at 27 and up), SK, MM (near zone and watch for trains).  Some Kunark zones should be good (Lake of Ill Omen).  You can also function effectively as a healer in higher level groups (Spectres, Sand Giants).
