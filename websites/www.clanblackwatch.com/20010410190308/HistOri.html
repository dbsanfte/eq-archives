Historical Origin

The name "Clan Blackwatch" is the name of an actual existing Scottish clan. Below are some articles for those who have interest in the actual historical roots of Clan Blackwatch.

BLACK WATCH

Raised by Royal Warrant, the Black Watch was the first Highland
Regimentto be formed and, dating from 1739, is Scotland's senior
Highland Regiment. Established from independant companies, made up from
clans reckoned to be loyal, they guarded the Highland areas from
raiders, maraders, and clan fueding. The Officers and soldiers were the
only British subjects allowed to were the Highland garb between 1748 and
1757 without risk of transportation, after the Jacobite Rising od 1745.
Based in Perth, the original 1,000 men
mustered in a field near Aberfeldy under command of John, 20th Earl of
Crawford ( a lowlander to avoid showing favoritism amongst the clans ).
The Black Watch, known initially as the 43rd Regiment, was to become and
remain the 42nd Regiment. Over the centuries, it has a long and
distinguishing history in worldwide actions, with extensive battle
honours won on fields as far apart as the Americas, the Middle and Far
East, as well as in the European Wars. The troops are renowned for their
spirit, discipline and bearing.
The tartan adopted by the regiment is a "
hunting set " based on the Royal tartan. Known in the 18th century at
the " Government Tartan ", it was the natural set to use for a Royal
Regiment, and subsequently also became known as the " Universal Tartan "
. With coloured overstripes it became the basis of many clan and
regiment tartans. The pipers wear the bright red Royal Stewart tartan.
Of all the Highland Regiments, the Black watch alone has the privilege
of wearing a red hackel, or plume, in its bonnets.

 

Motto: Nemo Me Impune Lacessit ( No One Attacks Me With Impunity )

Origin of Name: Gaelic Am Freiceadan Dubh - The Black Watch

Regimental March Past: Heilan' Laddie

 

 

SGIAN DUBH

The Sgian Dubh is the small ornamental knife traditionally worn in the top of the stocking by the Highlander. The flat edge is normally worn in the right stocking by right handed people. The name GAELIC- the word SGIAN means Knife or Dagger and Dubh means Black, referring to the colour of the handle, which was made from black hardwood. Various spellings of the word are recorded in different books, including Skean, Skian and Skein. The word Dubh can also be spelt DHU.

Originally the Highlander wore a dirk at hisside, and the Sgian Dubh seems to only date back to the later part of the Eighteenth Century. It may be associated with a small knife which the Highlander carried under his armpit, concealed by his sleeve. This is referred to in some of the histtory books as Skeen- ochles- possibly from the word oxter- meaning armpit.
