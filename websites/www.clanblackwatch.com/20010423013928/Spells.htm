Charm Animal Spell Line

An often misunderstood group of Druid spells, often considered worthless.  Most certainly they are not worthless.  Now the initial spell in this line is not quite what I had hoped.  You see, I tried to charm a Griffin sometime around Level 5.  I wasn't that amazed that it didn't work, but I was amazed how fast Griffins can kill you when you are level 5.  Honestly I didn't use these spells much until around my late teen levels.  Recently I have been playing some other druids lately (Abigaile of Brell, and Crescente of Vallon, levels 6 & 7), so I will let you know if I find use for these spells at earlier levels.

I have provided a detailed article on pet strategy here .

Evac

Overrated.  I don't think I have ever casted this, and I am level 41.  Maybe it becomes more useful later.  Some people swear by it, but as some wise & crazy wizard in our clan mentioned, when you need it you're out of mana anyway.  Sure you can save enough to cast it, just hope something doesn't decide to hit you and interrupt you at that last second.

Terrorize Animal

This spell will save your life. Don't believe me? It has saved me several times. It casts VERY fast, and is not resisted often. It is THE most reliable way to get Grimfeather away from you long enough to snare him or zone. He is faster than sow, so you won't make it by just running.   This of course depends on your level, so don't expect to scare Grimfeather (level 37) when you are level 14 or something.

It is of general use in snare/fear or animal crowd control.

Harmony

This is a great spell, and will allow you to pull single monsters out of a camp. It is equally useful grouping or soloing. It cannot be cast indoors. Its duration is about 60 seconds. Range is good, though you cannot confirm its effect except at close range. This does NOT mean it didn't work, only that you don't know if it worked. At close range, you will see some lights around the monsters on which it works. I recommend casting it more than once if there is any doubt, or you are not sure that all monsters were within the area of effect. Of course, don't expect it to work on things many levels above you. (i.e. don't use it to pull the goblin and not the wizard when you are L6)

Snare

A great spell, invaluable for keeping yourself and party members alive. Even this level 1 version can be cast on monsters several levels higher than you, but you will get some resists. Duration is 60 seconds, but you will be given a message when it wears off. If you are snaring DO NOT let anyone root (this includes mage pets). Root will cancel snare, so that when root breaks, the monster will be full speed (and usually running right at you). Snare will not break from damage, only duration. So pay attention and don't wait for the message to tell you that you needed to cast it again 5 seconds ago. Don't plan to use it much before about level 5. It is resisted a lot and doesn't seem to last long. At 5 it becomes a great way to stop fleeing monsters.

Ensnare

The level 29 version of snare, and a very misunderstood spell. There was a time when this spell was worthless. Apparently its duration and effect, plus a possibly increased chance of resist, made it not worthwhile to use. That is no longer the case. This spell lasts a LONG time (close to 10 min around level 30, and I believe it increases, level-dependent). Also, it has a greater slowing effect. The downside is it MAY be resisted more, especially soon after acquiring it. Trust me, when you decide to solo that first Hill Giant, you do NOT want it returning to full speed every 60 seconds, and you will need all of your mana + some more without worrying about re-snaring. If you will be using this spell, again tell others not to root AND rangers not to snare. Yours will not overwrite theirs. Have them do backup snare in case you are getting resists.

Spirit of Wolf (SoW)

Well of course you get Spirit of Wolf at 14, and this is quite nice.  Not only can you travel more efficiently, but your life expectancy increases quite a bit also.  If you like, you can make money by taking donations for casting this spell. Personally, I find that it is more fun to just cast it when someone needs and you have mana, and make your money adventuring instead.

Spirit of Cheetah

At level 24, you get Spirit of Cheetah.  This was a better spell before it was nerfed (umm, I mean fixed without telling anyone).  Ok enough complaining.  It makes you VERY fast, but only for about 30 sec max (maybe 18 sec).  It used to refresh when you zoned, making it great for travel across small zones, but now it only refreshes every 15 min.  It can be useful as an emergency escape for yourself or a party member, but it is not used that often any more.

Scale of Wolf

The level 29 travel upgrade.  Available in Kunark from Vie vendor.  A nice spell, in the right place.  If you cast or attack, it will disappear.  Also, if you are attacked by a spell or ranged attack, it will disappear (it no longer disappears from melee attack).  It can also only be cast on group members.  It is nice for running across relatively safe zones, but if you use it, ALWAYS have SoW and snare (ensnare) memmed also.  You will need these to get away if something drops your Scale of Wolf.  At levels where you will be using this spell, standing and slowly melee-fighting will not be practical.

Sunbeam

A great spell for use on non-animals.  Outdoors only short-term blind/stun spell.  Fairly fast casting, though seems slow intially.  Do not cast when monster is right next to you or you will blind yourself.

Wolf Form & Pack Wolf Forms

This spell turns you into a wolf.  It is true that it is not usable in as many zones as before, but it is still a nice spell.  It lasts longer than SoW, and gives you a nice attack buff.  At 39, you get Greater Wolf Form and Pack Form.  Use Greater Wolf on yourself, then cast Pack Form on any others that want it (you cannot cast Greater Wolf on others yet).  At 44, you get Form of the Great Wolf, which gives you ultravision, like having a coldstone (I am looking forward to this). Pack Wolf Forms are available to turn your group into wolves of one grade lower than you can turn into yourself (i.e. at 39 you can Greater Wolf yourself, but Wolf a group, etc).  Pack forms also have a shorter duration (around 1/2 I think).

DO NOT Wolf Form in Firiona Vie (or probably any other city zone).  There are many powerful wandering creatures that will attack you.  I have wolfed safely in North Karana after the faction changes, and was not regarded any differently.  Gypsies do not mind you either.  Some guards may be less tolerant, so check from a difference (and NOT while invis).  In most zones, monsters will hate you whether you are a wolf or not, so be one if you want.  Unrest is a wonderful exception, where wolves are treated as indifferent always.

Treeform

Treeform is a great spell, but not used very often now that you cannot run around as a tree (there used to be a bug that allowed you to move while in treeform, now you can only rotate).  This spell will give you indifferent faction with many monsters, and provide you with some nice regeneration.  You cannot use it in areas where trees would not normally grow.  It stacks with your regular regeneration line.
