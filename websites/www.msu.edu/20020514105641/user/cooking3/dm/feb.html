February 10, 2002

Select Few Spots Still Open

As a guild with purpose, pride, and a growing list of accomplishments we have a certain idea in mind about what we want out of people to join our guild. The initial push has ended for people. We have most of the numbers that we need to do most of the things we need to do in the short term. However, there are some important holes in the roster that we are looking at filling. We are in need of:

Rogue: We need a rogue on the team to open certain doors, do awesome backstab damage, and to disarm traps. You should be able to raid during the weekend, be friendly, and not be satisfied with your level unless it is 60.

Wizard: We need some wizards on the team to port us to certain planes as well as finish off some large gating mobs. The nuking power of wizards as well as the convenient porting is a service that AD is in desperate need of. In terms of the same playstyle ideals apply as with the rogue.

Bard: We need a bard to give us occlusion of sound and give us access to some nice stacking effects. We also need a bard so we can stop letting multiple BP's rot wherever we go. For whatever reason we have stupid luck with bard loot.

Necromancer: When things go wrong, we need someone with coffins ready to summon us out. While Vanswer works in the short term, a necro is also needed for DMF and the plethora of mana enhancing abilities they can provide.

Cleric: We need one more cleric to round out our squad. You have to know how to heal enchanters, and you have to be able to save yourself when you get agro. Also, you have to read your screen to know when to camp.

These are the spots that we need. If you have questions about the guild, our recruiting procedures, or anything else please don't hesitate to e-mail or post on the forums. In addition to this, it should be noted that we are looking for these classes and not begging. This means that you will not get (a) loot precedence, (b) special treatment, or (c) skipped over steps in the recruiting process. We don't mess around with people that aren't on the same page as the rest of the guild. We all have goals, and we need a certain type of person to help us reach those goals. If you are not helping us, you are hurting us.

- Ciliem

February 9, 2002

Hate: Floor 2

After a rather late night at the royals, the plans to take on the second floor seemed to be in some rather dire straits. However, we did manage to push through the first floor in quick order and make it to the second floor.

We killed many of the minibosses and survived some strange agro incidents in some cases due to the laughable pathing of the zone. Nonetheless we got quite a stash of prized loot including the Darkmetal Falchion for Fenlaorn. It was rather uneventful save a strange corpse that we found near the Maestro.

Fadorn, 55 Rogue (Unbroken Spirit), made a bold penetration into Hate alone the night before to recover his note and was run down. We chatted with him for some time and eventually he did come up and get his note. Appotus Dominus congratulates Fadorn on his note, and wish him the best of luck getting his epic! Unfortunately, the corpse was too old to receive a rez.

- Ciliem

February 8, 2002

Overking Owned

18 people take down the Overking.

With our plans of destroying other exciting loot dropping mobs destroyed for the evening, we had no choice but to turn our attentions to a new threat. Since our inception the raid on the royals has been a goal we always wondered if we would be able to accomplish with limited numbers (18 people, average lvl: 56). Since everyone's spirits were already in the shitter, we decided upon a raid to the king for 2 cleric notes.

Certainly, everything did not go 100% peachy, but in the end we did emerge with two of the prized notes. We did recover from almost a complete wipeout in very quick order to preserve the raid thanks to some heads up camping by the clerics. The first king pull was really a botched operation, and the night did end rather sourly as our chances at the queen were undermined by some ill-fated agro.

Nonetheless we were able to defeat the king and his vaunted dispelling fire AOE with minimal difficulty.

Special thanks to Fenlaorn for dual comping of Thoron for the majority of the time

- Ciliem

February 2, 2002

Great Expectations

See the rest of the screenshots from PoG

The Plane of Growth raid was perhaps the most anticipated raid in the short existence of AD. Many people had hoped that growth would be a repeat of Hate or Fear with more mobs, more loots, and better loot. With our hopes high, we broke into the plane, in hopes of destroying faction with homo elves everywhere.

What we found were mobs that weren't terribly difficult, and a lot of them. We cut bloody swathes through an ungodly amount of mobs. Dabamf aided us in his trademark Velketor pulling style by keeping mbos flowing in a steady stream. At nights end, with only a couple of preposterous gates to sour the evening, we had cleared 75% of the plane. Perhaps these miscues were caused by a severe dampening of enthusiasm that comes with shit drops.

We came out with no legs, and no BPs for our efforts. We killed many of the miniboss type mobs only to find that they had either research pages or nothing on their corpses. While there certainly were some upgrades, the loot situation turned out to be downright sad. These growth mobs are stingy and they gave us the royal shaft.
AD's second epic as Dabamf obtains his bubbles.

Nonetheless, we'll be back.

- Ciliem

February 1, 2002

Rangers, Dragons, and Gates

See the rest of the screenshots from WW

On Friday night we attempted our first foray into the Western Wastes (aka "dub dub") with 16 people, succor, and an open mind. In terms of loots we did okay. In terms of corpse runs we did okay. In terms of killing dragons we did okay.

As far as loot goes we found that many of the fabulous dragons and large named mammoths give you fat Johnson across your face many times. However, the loots were not completely a bust as we retrieved some devent spells and a pretty tight talisman for Whiteout.

We only had one corpse run. This, was caused by (Ladi Dadi) Glati (likes to party) gating all the way to the northeast corner of the zone and summoning three lucky people to their deaths. This was fairly easily resolved by some tricky monk work.

With all of the technical stuff out of the way, the most glorious fight that we attempted was Von. We were able to beat Von due to the dedication of two rangers, a drood, a leezard, and a cleric. Battas, Vashen, Zeban, Carraaki, and Dapper were left to kill Von when he was left at 20 percent life. Apparently, Von had run out of mana from Gfluxing everyone else to hell because he was content to melee with Carr and the rangers for quite some time. However, being slowed he was doing enough for Battas to keep up with. For long moments we wondered if Von would get enough mana to begin Gfluxing people again, but it never happened. The braver rangers, warrior, and uhh, druid were able to melee Von down and gain us a stupendous victory.

- Ciliem

Hail, a Zeban

Today, a new member was admitted to Appotus Dominus. Zeban, a 53 ranger who has accompanied us to many raids was officially guild tagged. Welcome to the guild, Zeban!

- Ciliem
