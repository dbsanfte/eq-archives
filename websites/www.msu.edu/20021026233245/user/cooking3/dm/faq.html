What does Appotus Dominus mean?

There is actually a story about how we got our name that has a modicum of entertainment value.

Originally, we wanted to be called "Cobra Kai" after the sinister Dojo in the loved Karate Kid movies. However, we submitted this name and it was denied because it was supposedly "media". Despite our moans of protest the powers that be crushed our name and left us to curl in the fetal position. Many tears were shed, and bitter words were spoken about Verant.

Next, in out attempts to get a cool name past the 'thought police' or 'Gestapo' at Verant we came up with "Drunken Masters". The rejoicing began. This was our name, this was us, this was our true calling. As we talked more we discussed how we would like to kill large mobs in-game drunk. The excitement rippled through our small group as we patted ourselves on the back. Surely, this name would make it through!

Then, like an anvil from the sky, the slam down fell upon us. It nearly crushed us. I personally wanted weep for days in my room with a hearty supply of Bon Bons and Wendy's hamburgers. For what its worth they told us we should not have any reference to being drunk in the game.

How do I get points?

Points are awarded for attending "raid type" events. These events REQUIRE MORE THAN 1 GROUP TO DO, otherwise the single group loot code applies. Only Dapper, or I (Ciliem) have the power to award points. Unless specified in advance, there will be no points awarded for small encounters, such as epic ones. The points are divided as follows:

A Small Raid Type Event is worth 3 points.

These are smaller events that take a minimal amount of time, but take a few groups to do. Killing some smaller dragons, easy to get to big daddies etc etc. There are special situations which can affect how many points you receive:

  • (-3) You get no points for being late to these events. If we are doing something and you make it late, after all of the action is over, or are no help to us because you are late you will receive no points, bottom line.
  • (-3) You leave early, you get no points. Since most of these events are fairly straightforward you are either here or you're not.
  • (+.5) You are level 55 or greater. If you have busted your ass to get to 55+ we give you a bonus because we want the guild to be high levels.
  • (+1) You get a full point bonus for being a level 60. Again, we want the prestige and being 60 makes everything as easy as it can be.

A large raid event is worth 7 points.

These are raids of the planes and are long term commitment events. They take a lot of time so you get more points. Again, before we embark the size of the event will be determined. Here are the adjustments:

  • (-2) You are late to the event. You arrive early enough where you can be of service, but we have already done a lot without you.
  • (-2) You leave early from the event. In some of these events the need to leave early is inevitable. While we realize this is not your fault sometimes, you must be penalized so the "hard core" don;t get slighted.
  • (+.5) You are level 55 or greater. If you have busted your ass to get to 55+ we give you a bonus because we want the guild to be high levels.
  • (+1) You get a full point bonus for being a level 60. Again, we want the prestige and being 60 makes everything as easy as it can be.

How do I use points to buy items?

When we are in a raid situation, items that are Uber will drop. No Drop items will be bought and paid for on the spot, and other items will be collected for an end of raid auction. If you see an item you want, it must be usable by your class for you to bid on. There will be a starting bid assigned, and if you have the points, you bid on the item. If others in the same class want the item, they can overbid you. Starting bids will be determined based on relative desirability of the item.

A few rules apply:

  • If you bid and win an item you may not bid on another one until people of your class have either passed on what else is there, or have purchased one themselves. This prevents longtime members from forcing new guild members to attend raids for months while they get nothing.
  • No Drop Items are an oddity. If a No Drop item drops, and you want it but do not have the points for it YOU MAY GO INTO THE HOLE TO GET IT. This prevents people from having possible upgrades to them rot on the ground. IE you have 3 points, a tight item drops and you are the only one of your class there, its a 10 point item, you may go to minus 7. However, people who have points and want the item will be given priority. Obviously, when you have minus points you can;t bid on regular items because you have no points. There is no further negative to having minus points.
  • If items are not bid on by anyone they go into the guild vault for two weeks. During these two weeks ONLY A CLASS THAT CAN USE THE ITEM may purchase it at any time for a price that will be determined. If after two weeks, the item remains in the vault, it is opened to ANYONE WHO WANTS IT at the set price. The items in the guild vault will be published on a regular basis. Ciliem (me) controls the guild vault and any questions come to me regarding this.

How many points each guild member has will be posted on a regular basis. If you have a question or a mistake was made in your points, please post a reply to the message that lists the points in it so I can get it fixed immediately.
