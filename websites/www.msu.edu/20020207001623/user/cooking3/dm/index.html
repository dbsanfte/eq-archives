February 2, 2002

Great Expectations

See the rest of the screenshots from PoG

The Plane of Growth raid was perhaps the most anticipated raid in the short existence of AD. Many people had hoped that growth would be a repeat of Hate or Fear with more mobs, more loots, and better loot. With our hopes high, we broke into the plane, in hopes of destroying faction with homo elves everywhere.

What we found were mobs that weren't terribly difficult, and a lot of them. We cut bloody swathes through an ungodly amount of mobs. Dabamf aided us in his trademark Velketor pulling style by keeping mbos flowing in a steady stream. At nights end, with only a couple of preposterous gates to sour the evening, we had cleared 75% of the plane. Perhaps these miscues were caused by a severe dampening of enthusiasm that comes with shit drops.

We came out with no legs, and no BPs for our efforts. We killed many of the miniboss type mobs only to find that they had either research pages or nothing on their corpses. While there certainly were some upgrades, the loot situation turned out to be downright sad. These growth mobs are stingy and they gave us the royal shaft.
AD's second epic as Dabamf obtains his bubbles.

Nonetheless, we'll be back.

- Ciliem

February 1, 2002

Rangers, Dragons, and Gates

See the rest of the screenshots from WW

On Friday night we attempted our first foray into the Western Wastes (aka "dub dub") with 16 people, succor, and an open mind. In terms of loots we did okay. In terms of corpse runs we did okay. In terms of killing dragons we did okay.

As far as loot goes we found that many of the fabulous dragons and large named mammoths give you fat Johnson across your face many times. However, the loots were not completely a bust as we retrieved some devent spells and a pretty tight talisman for Whiteout.

We only had one corpse run. This, was caused by (Ladi Dadi) Glati (likes to party) gating all the way to the northeast corner of the zone and summoning three lucky people to their deaths. This was fairly easily resolved by some tricky monk work.

With all of the technical stuff out of the way, the most glorious fight that we attempted was Von. We were able to beat Von due to the dedication of two rangers, a drood, a leezard, and a cleric. Battas, Vashen, Zeban, Carraaki, and Dapper were left to kill Von when he was left at 20 percent life. Apparently, Von had run out of mana from Gfluxing everyone else to hell because he was content to melee with Carr and the rangers for quite some time. However, being slowed he was doing enough for Battas to keep up with. For long moments we wondered if Von would get enough mana to begin Gfluxing people again, but it never happened. The braver rangers, warrior, and uhh, druid were able to melee Von down and gain us a stupendous victory.

- Ciliem

The Road We Trod Was Long and Hard

Ciliem finishes up his epic by Killing the Shissar in PoF.

At long last Appotus Dominus was able to secure our first epic. Early Monday morning I finally turned in all four pieces of the Serpent Staff and Jeb Lumsed was kind enough to make me the staff.

Over the last month or so many lame things have been done to achieve this aim, and finally the Staff of the Serpent will be casting Speed of the Shissar on our guild members. This, however, is only the first of many epics we hope to achieve. Next up are the monk epics.

I would like to thank guildmates and guests of raids who helped me get my epic pieces. I appreciate all the effort and sacrifice that everyone put forth in order for me to get the snake. If you need me for epic encounters, I'll be there with the snake…that moves.

January 20, 2002

Thou Will Regret thy Actions Swine!
*thunk*

We had some small epic raids on Friday night for enchanter, druid, shaman, and monk epics. We visited the City of Mist and Plane of sky in our lust for epics.

First, we went to the Plane of Sky. We were able to cut through the mobs on the first island with no problems and then slice Gwan deep wide and frequent. Dabamf was able to gather his epic piece with no -real- problems.

Although, akin to the Ultima Online guards, genies below the first island will proceed to rip people a new one if a spell is attempted on a group member. This helpful tip was gained through the school of hard knocks. We were run down a few times and many harsh AOE accusations flew before we realized this. One question, if people are "protected" from my spells why the hell do these foolish genies give a rats ass?

Next, the City of Mist was on tap. We traversed there and killed a few black reavers. To Dapper's orgasmic delight, a Jade Reaver was found fairly early in the game. However, in our initial trip to Action Jaxion we were run down due to cocky charging of a room with perhaps 14 mobs in it. This concluded the first night of raiding, and everyone was fairly downtrodden after such mediocre efforts. Nonetheless, we got a couple epic pieces.

Thanks guests:

Vanswer 58 Shadowknight

Alueriniel 55 Druid

Zeban 51 Ranger

 

- Ciliem

AD Breaks Up in Game Marriage!

During our sometimes frustrating Plane of Sky excursion, we were offered some comic relief. It all started as we were buffing to continue killing happy fairies…

Dapper received a tell stating something to the effect that we were "breaking up a marriage". This fellow claimed we had invited his significant other to a raid and that he had been spurned because of this. The emotional victim of the Appotus Dominus raid recounted how special this lady was to him and how he looked forward to spending time with her. He sent tell after tell berating Dapper and our guild for holding a raid and inviting this wonderful lady to our raid.
Needless to say, Duridain, Yamile, Dapper, and I were in absolute tears at this fellows' antics. We were rofl for at least 10 minutes waiting with baited breath for the next tell of this colorful character. Eventually, I suppose, their differences were resolved. However, that doesn't stop this from being perhaps the most hilarious thing that has ever happened in an EQ raid. The guy was legitimately red hot over the "loss" of his in game wife.

The names and guilds have been withheld to protect the innocent. And the embarrassed.

- Ciliem
