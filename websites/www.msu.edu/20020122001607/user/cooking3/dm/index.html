January 20, 2002

Thou Will Regret thy Actions Swine!
*thunk*

We had some small epic raids on Friday night for enchanter, druid, shaman, and monk epics. We visited the City of Mist and Plane of sky in our lust for epics.

First, we went to the Plane of Sky. We were able to cut through the mobs on the first island with no problems and then slice Gwan deep wide and frequent. Dabamf was able to gather his epic piece with no -real- problems.

Although, akin to the Ultima Online guards, genies below the first island will proceed to rip people a new one if a spell is attempted on a group member. This helpful tip was gained through the school of hard knocks. We were run down a few times and many harsh AOE accusations flew before we realized this. One question, if people are "protected" from my spells why the hell do these foolish genies give a rats ass?

Next, the City of Mist was on tap. We traversed there and killed a few black reavers. To Dapper's orgasmic delight, a Jade Reaver was found fairly early in the game. However, in our initial trip to Action Jaxion we were run down due to cocky charging of a room with perhaps 14 mobs in it. This concluded the first night of raiding, and everyone was fairly downtrodden after such mediocre efforts. Nonetheless, we got a couple epic pieces.

Thanks guests:

Vanswer 58 Shadowknight

Alueriniel 55 Druid

Zeban 51 Ranger

 

- Ciliem

AD Breaks Up in Game Marriage!

During our sometimes frustrating Plane of Sky excursion, we were offered some comic relief. It all started as we were buffing to continue killing happy fairies…

Dapper received a tell stating something to the effect that we were "breaking up a marriage". This fellow claimed we had invited his significant other to a raid and that he had been spurned because of this. The emotional victim of the Appotus Dominus raid recounted how special this lady was to him and how he looked forward to spending time with her. He sent tell after tell berating Dapper and our guild for holding a raid and inviting this wonderful lady to our raid.
Needless to say, Duridain, Yamile, Dapper, and I were in absolute tears at this fellows' antics. We were rofl for at least 10 minutes waiting with baited breath for the next tell of this colorful character. Eventually, I suppose, their differences were resolved. However, that doesn't stop this from being perhaps the most hilarious thing that has ever happened in an EQ raid. The guy was legitimately red hot over the "loss" of his in game wife.

The names and guilds have been withheld to protect the innocent. And the embarrassed.

- Ciliem
