About Us

One of the most interesting facets of the Immortals, is its proud history;
one that nearly spans seven + years of online gaming. When one considers
that Massive Multi-Player Online Gaming dates back to the creation of
KESMAI'S AIR WARRIOR originally hosted by Genie over ten years ago. Boy have we've come a long way since the early pioneering days of the internet. Genie's navigational greenscreen interface included a three-ring binder just to get to the games section in order to play! Dialing up was via 300-baud modem! Talk about throughput! <g>

 

I can remember flying my Macintosh 512 (back then it was done via keyboard
and joystick combination -- there were no such thing as Rudder Pedals and
Throttles!), chasing small + signs on the screen, staring out through a
static cockpit while paying an hourly charge thinking that playing against
another human would be the final evolution of computer gaming. You couldn't
ask for a better opponent. There was something special about knowing that
you had just shot down another "player" not some simple AI sprite.

I quickly recruited several of my freinds (including my brohter, Jamie
"Oopster" Nolan, Russel "Flat" Lancaster, and John "Tailspin" Benson). The
four of us played online. To further enhance the immersion and effeciencies
of us want-a-be pilots, several of went through the added expense of
ordering 3-way calling so we could host a four-way telephone call. Armed
with hands-free phone and conferenced called in, we were truly a force to be
recommended with, for we eliminated the need to simulataneously type and fly. To this day, many of the Immortals use Roger Wilco. In fact, we even have our own 24x7 dedicated RW server!

It was several years later, did Jamie "Oopster" Nolan happen to stumble upon a link onlineabout a new MMOPG Flight sim called WarBirds . I remember waiting endlessly for screens shots to download on my 14.4-baud modem and our jaws dropping on the ground to see such graphics. I remember thinking to myself, surely this must be a hoax. How could one have online graphics like this? Sure enough, it was an open beta product! The download took nearly 5 hours to complete. Once inside the cockpit I knew immediately that I had found my new home.

"The year was 1995, an a bold experiment was begun in taking multi-player
on-line gaming action to a new level. In an unprecedented move, a 6 month
beta period was established for what was then called Confirmed Kill. Today,
after many changes this game? is known as Warbirds (tm)." - Prop

Like many flight-sim Air Warrior addicts, the four of us migrated over, said
good-bye to AW and it wasn't long after were the 901st Immortals born
. The 901st Immortals had many MEMBERS(see 901st ROSTER below), the bulk of which still play together today in various MMPOGs. The 901st was one of the oldest squadrons in WarBirds, dating back to the beta in version .82 of WarBirds. Soon after, we became sister squadrons to the 900th Bloody Jaguars and enjoyed a larger sense of community. The 901st Immortals were some of the best "sticks" whoever flew a virtual cockpit. Its membership and officer corps were completely dedicated to the squadron and its goal of being one of the most highly-competitive and respected squadrons to ever fly the virtual skies.

Under Sand's tootledge he united this unique band of air warriors and taught
them to believe in themselves, and more importantly in each other. In its
second year, the 901st Immortals and its sister squadron the 900th Bloody
Jaguars brought squadron to squadron team competition to new levels. Their
nemiss and well respected competitors, the Flying Pigs competed fiercely for
bragging rights in this modern-day-jost of the heavens. The 901st Immortals had achieved an unsurpassed dueling record (of its time) of 27-3-1. One of the two most memorable accomplishments of the 901st Immortals was their second place win in ICI's first Official Squadron Duel, where they lost in a controversial match against the Flying Pigs. This defining moment had forever pitted each other as arch-rivals. Ironically enough, Squid, the Flying Pigs CO, happened to live in the same town as Sand, the CO of the 901st, that being Charlotte, North Carolina.

The 901st Immortals along with its sister-squad, the 900th Bloody Jaguars
took squad-competition to new heights. After its fifth year, Sand stepped
down and passed command to his XO (Executive Office), Mell, who inherited
the responsiblity and took the 901st Immortals to the top of their game.
Mell one of the most talented pilots to take to the air, brought a sense of
discipline that enabled the Immortals to continue Sand's legacy of 27-3-1
squadron competition winning record.

One of the most prestigious accomplishments of the 901st, was the honor
bestowed upon Sand as the Supreme Commander of the Solomon's Campaign, one of ICI's first MMPOG historical recreation. Over 300+ pilots took to one
arena and competed in a three-day marathon battle as this famous Pacific War
campaign was recreated in all its glory!

"We were a community of game players -- flight sim jockeys, many of whom
could trace back their origins to flight sims, like Air Warrior, the
predecessor of WarBirds. The 901st Immortals love for air to air combat --
1940's style, separated them from just the average gamer. The 901st were
dedicated fanatics, comprised of many of the very talented and respected
pilots whomever had been assembled to fight, in an albeit "virtual world."
Their love of the flying in warbirds of old, was only surpassed by their
love for being a part of the Immortals. It was a privledge and honor to have
served with and commanded this very special breed of men." - Sand, CO 901st
Immortal (ret)
