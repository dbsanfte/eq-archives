Raid Loot Rules

One Item Per Person (With Some Exceptions/Exclusions):
1. One Item Rule - If you win an item, you are out of all lotto's for the remainder of the raid unless everyone has won an item, in which case everyone is again eligible for the lottos. Before everyone has won an item, if something else drops later in the raid and you would prefer to have it more than the item you won, you may enter the lotto for the second item only if you can lotto off the first item you won to the rest of the raiders. If the first item that you won is a no drop item, it cannot be traded out of your inventory and is therefore not something that can be re-lottoed. In situations like this, the exception does not apply.
2. Exceptions - Other exceptions to this rule are spells, quest/epic drops, and Planar/Quest BPs.
Spells Drops - Spells drop in lots of places, not just off dragons. They're also tradable and worth far less than dragon loot. Therefore, when spells drop, all people who can use the spell are eligible for the roll regardless of items won thus far.
Quest/Epic Drops - In the case of epic quest drops, this should be decided upon amongst the class that uses that item in an epic quest. If four enchanters are in a zone, for example, and a piece of an enchanter epic quest drops, not all the enchanters may want it. They can discuss amongst themselves who could best use the item. In a case where more than one could, a lotto will take place for the item, run by the Lotto Master for the raid.
Planar/Quest BP Drops - These items are sufficiently rare enough as drops and are therefore included in this special category. When they drop everyone who can use the item is eligible for the lotto.
3. Ultra-Rare Drops - These will be considered separate from all the other lottos. Just because you won armor or a rare mace doesn't mean the losers of the lottos get to lotto on something better that drops and is ultra-rare. This is everybody's privilege and it's a rarity to get in on these lottos, so everybody is included, regardless of what you've gotten so far in the day. However, let's say you're a player who's the only one of your class in the zone and five pieces of armor have dropped for your class and because you were the only one of your class, you got them all by default. That's great for you, but how is it going to make others feel when you enter an ultra-rare lotto? The ultra-rare lottos are open but if you've been winning things left, right, and center, try to exercise a little restraint when it comes to the big draw. People might see you as greedy if you enter, or have a lot more respect for you if you withdraw which way you want to cross that line of perception with people is your own choice to make.
4. Common Drops - In the case of certain items dropping that are considered to be common, exceptions may be made. For example, Elemental Binders are quite common on Hole raids. If an item drops that all the members of a class feel is so common that they wouldn't want to roll on it for fear of risking lotto placement towards armor drops, it will not be counted towards armor drops and will be rolled on equally between those that need it or can use it.
