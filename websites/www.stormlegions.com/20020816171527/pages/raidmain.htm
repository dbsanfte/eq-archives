Raid Rules of Conduct

These are the general expectations we have for those attending raids and these rules are NOT suggestions. Our Raid Coordinator, in conjunction with our Guild Officers will enforce these rules. Violations of these rules can lead to one or more, in any combination, of the following:

· Forfeiture of loot rolls.
· Being asked to leave the raid.
· Not being invited on future raids.

Before the Raid:
1. Read and understand both the Raid Rules of Conduct and Loot Rules. These will be standard for any and all raids unless specifically noted when the raid is first posted.
2. Read the raid information page that is posted on the forum.
3. Have any items required by the Raid Coordinator.
4. Ensure that you have the appropriate levels/resists required by the Raid Coordinator. 5. Be on time! Plan ahead to be at the raid formation location at the correct time, the correct time being 15 to 30 minutes before the raid start time. You are late if you are there at the raid start time.
6. Make sure you have the time required by the Raid Coordinator/Raid Leader. Any exceptions to this need to be discussed with the Raid Coordinator or Raid Leader beforehand.

During the Raid:
1. Experience - We try our best to insure that everyone gets experience during our hunts, but raids are a different matter. Please understand that in larger raids (and for that matter, most raids) with a variety of groups and levels, which are organized for the acquisition of specific items, that not everyone will get the same amount of experience. Some may not get any, depending on the amount of creatures involved in the raid and in reaching the main objective. If you are on one of these raids and you don't seem to be getting as much experience (or any) as some of the other attendies, lend your support, have fun, and be patient. There will be future raids where you may be the one getting the experience and you will expect the others to continue on without complaint too. Also, you can rest assured that there will be many hunts organized specifically to gain experience and levels. 2. Corpse Recovery - Expect to remain on the raid until all corpses are recovered. Everyone will actively participate in corpse recovery and resurrection.
3. Have Assist Hotkeys Made - It is your responsibility to make both a main and secondary assist key. You must ALWAYS assist the designated person. Who you are to assist will be decided and announced by the Raid Leader. Enchanters/Bards should also use this key, so as NOT to mesmerize the target being attacked. If Enchanters/Bards (or anyone for that matter) do happen to notice someone who is NOT assisting, notify the Raid Leader immediately. If you do not know how to set up hot keys, ASK before the raid date, NOT as the raid is being formed.
4. Those Pesky People - If you notice someone attending/joining the raid that you have issues with, share your concerns privately and in a respectful manner with the Raid Leader.
5. Pullers/Trackers - Do not be constantly asking about mobs being up or asking to have certain mobs pulled. This is irritating and disrespectful to the puller/tracker. Pullers and trackers know their jobs and know what mobs are available/able to be pulled. Do NOT pull mobs on your own. This will get you removed from a raid.
