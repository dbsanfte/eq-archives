Raid Rules of Conduct (Cont.)

6. Raid Leaders - For every raid, there is a Leader. The Leader has put a lot of work into the raid long before anybody shows up for it. Show him or her the respect they deserve and follow their direction and orders explicitly. The Raid Leader is the only person who should be directing movement, pulls, downtime, etc. Those of you who would like to lead a Raid, contact our Raid Coordinator to discuss options. Also, take a moment to review the Raid Leader responsibilities. As the Raid Leader, do not take it upon yourself to handle Loot. Assign another for this task.
7. Drops - Complaining about the quality of drops will not be tolerated. If you have done some preperations beforehand (like checking posted links about the raid zone), you will know what drops to expect. If you are not satisfied with the expected drops, loot, experience, etc., don't sign up for the raid. It's that simple. Remember, just because you think an item is less than "uber" does not mean that someone else on the raid would not be delighted to have it.
8. Chat Channels - Before the raid begins, the Raid Leader will initiate a private Raid Channel for the raid. All attendies will, upon their arrival, be notified of this channel and will immediately join it. This channel will be used for all general chatter pertaining to the raid such as incoming messages, Leader orders, and so forth. Shout and OOC will be kept clear for emergencies such as TRAIN & ZONE! During larger raids, at the descretion of the Raid Leader, a second raid channel may be opened which everyone will join but will be for the exclusive use of the Raid Leader.
9. Looting - ONLY Group Leaders will loot, or if there is only one group, then only the assigned person for loot. Group leaders then inform the loot officer of any drops that need to be lottoed. Please see the Loot Rules for more specific information.

After the Raid:
1. Please, no whining and complaining about drops, loot, who got what, etc. etc. This is EXTREMELY disrespectful and disruptive. Unfortunately, not everyone will be pleased with the outcome of every raid. Be patient, your time will come. Everyone will get their fair share in the long run.
2. Any issues you had with the raid should be directed to the Raid Leader and/or Raid Coordinator. These are the people best equipped to respond to your concerns and questions.
