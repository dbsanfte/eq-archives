Arrk Mindmaster, that's me.  A bit about myself?

Well, I grew up in Ak'Anon, and knew at an early age I was going to follow my father in the creation of new labor-saving devices.  At least, so I was told.  But no one knew there were so many ways of making things blow up under too much pressure, or other reasons, until I did some of my work.
My mother complained of the smokes and smells.  My father grew worried for his own work, since some of his customers began to wonder whether I helped him in any of the projects he was selling.  They decided to send me, at the tender age of about 25, to the Library of the Mechanimagica to "broaden my horizons."  Father thought maybe I could get some magic to work in some of his machines.  So he told me.   I know better now.

The magicians took me into their classrooms at first, but they were too, well, earthy in nature for my liking.  Constantly babbling about how true power is all around everyone, to be controlled by those with clever and strong enough minds to use it.  Very dull lectures, I must say.  They did not like it when I demonstrated how boring their lectures were.  They decided I could use some "cross-training" by the necromancer guild, that was not even in the same building.

THAT is a creepy place.  Filled with creepy people.   I cannot be sure, but I think dead things are supposed to be buried.  They said they are buried as kind of an "aging" process, sort of like wine, and they are dug up again when they are suitably decayed and can be used as servants.  While more interesting than the magician's works, certainly the lectures were at least, I still could not see how one can get satisfaction by using all these dead things to do one's bidding.  When my time was up, I went back to the Library of the Mechanimagica, under the courseplan of wizardry.

I would have thought, with all the explosions I made at home, the wizards would have lots of interesting things to do.  They did have some, but it was mostly lost in the lessons on discipline and ethics.  Sort of like telling someone using a sword that the sword is best used going neither left nor right, but straight at the person you want to kill.  And make sure you actually want to kill that person, before unsheathing the sword.  Um...duh.

When the wizards had had enough of me, they sent me to the enchanters.  Sounds like they were about to kick me out, since I had been to every other part of the school.  And strangely enough, I found it interesting and challenging.  Thinking back now, I suspect the teachers themselves had something to do with it, implanting feelings of acceptance in me.  But enchanted or not, I grew involved with my apprenticeships.  Eventually, before I knew it, I was over 40 and had learned a great deal.  I left Ak'Anon and ventured out into Norrath to learn more than my teachers would teach.

A bit more about myself.  It is important that nothing be given to strangers, since that which is given to strangers is worthless.  I always, therefore, offer something when I ask something of a stranger.  Usually this is not difficult, for most are eager to get Clarity to do nearly anything for me.  It is a subtle form of mind control.  Beggars...pah!  If they spent as much time adventuring as they did begging, they could also learn something at the same time.   If I could do it, so could they.  Of course, MY intelligence is magically enhanced, so I have certain advantages...
 
I AM a mighty enchanter, and though it may seem appropriate for an mighty enchanter to wield a mighty weapon, I have not yet found one suitable.  I am working on a quest of epic proportions which will get me such a weapon, but it still is some ways off.  Here is a summary of my current progress:

THE BEGINNING:  I traded a Shining Metallic Robe for a Mechanical Pen, Charmed the Ghoul Scribe to make him like me enough to fill an empty vial with his blood to serve as Ink of the Dark, and led some people to kill Thrackin Griffen who had a magic piece of White Paper.  I gave these pieces to one Stolo Olan in exchange for his notes, which I gave to Jeb Lumsed, who looks like a sarnak, in the Burning Woods.  Vrond, Ambir, Mohun, Serina, Cureall, Marjelle, and worthy Stormseekers Xeckis, Ducem, and Gnamish helped kill the mighty bandit Thrackin Griffen.
The remaining parts of the quest are even more troublesome, and only some of them can I complete without aid.  This is a summary of the progress of the rest of it. Things I still have to do are in orange.

 
1st part of the staff: 1 part done Xolion Rod from Vessel Drozlin in Cabalis Vessel Drozlin will not give up the Rod without a fight.  Yes, no surprise there.  But he is often out of town, and the fight will be difficult anyway.  I still need to formulate a good plan for getting this from him.
  Innoruuk's Word from Verina Tomb in Neriak Verina Tomb is, so I gather, a level 60 cleric of Innoruuk.  Everyone hates her, as befits a cleric of Innoruuk, and I understand even the guards do not mind her being attacked.  But she has one friend, at least, for usually when I go to Neriak to ask after her, I find she is off to Najena.  On business or pleasure?  Who can tell.
  The Chalice of Kings, given in return for the head of Prince Selrach Di'zok in Chardok The Prince hides deep within Chardok.  I have not yet seen him, nor can I get to speak with him without help.  I suspect lots of sarnaks want to prevent me from getting his head, but oh well.
  Snow blossoms, given by Bozlum Blossom in Oggok I found some muddy sandals next to Brokk Boxtripper in Oggok.  Surely these were the sandals that trampled through Bozlum's garden, and she knew it.  She had me demand recompense from Brokk, who sent her some flowers as a peace offering.  Too many...she gave me one, the Snow Blossom, that I might smell "purty."  There is no accounting for the sense of smell among ogres.
2nd part of the staff:  In possession A Spoon from Cazel, a rare, mighty giant in the Oasis Baltus, Bambuck, Bobogaun, Gollf, Kembo, and Vadaan killed Cazel for me when I notified them I needed him dead.  Jhales, Robistro, and Sidantar also helped in their various ways.   Vedir was ready at my call, but the others killed him before we could arrive.   I am deeply grateful, as are many others that Cazel was trampling before he was killed.
  The One Key, in the Overthere I found this on one of the very tall mesas, by flying from mesa to mesa.  It was dropped long ago by accident.  I have no idea what lock it fits, but it will certainly help unlock this weapon for me.
  The Lost Scroll, from Dalnir's Crypt Lost it was, and it seems to have a power to try to STAY lost.  But I have ensorceled it, and I hope my own enchantments will prove the stronger, that it will stay in my vault.  Until I put it in the magic sack, anyway...
  Book of Charm and Sacrifice I have been told it is good reading, but I was unable to make heads or tails of the thing.  Gladly did I put the thing in the magic sack to turn it into a sack for Mizzle Gepple in the Mines of Malfunction in Ak'Anon.
3rd part of the staff:  2 parts done Enchant a dull diamond in Kaesora I went once to Kaesora to call on the Librarian who can enchant this diamond, but instead of the Spectral Librarian there was this Tortured Librarian.  Tortured into doing the Spectral Librarian's job while he could go out and party, I suppose.  I let the Librarian escape his torture, but the Spectral Librarian did not come back.  I returned another day with some Ancient Guardians, Radyn, Monto, and Gwanalla.  The Spectral Librarian was there, and soon my friend.  He DID feel a bit betrayed after enchanting the diamond, and when dispatched had 3 Freed Spirits.  Such a big-spirited being.   Though their AoE attack was fatal, Fayrlorn made it all better.
  Enchant a dull sapphire in Skyfire Felia Goldenwing, when magically befriended, grudgingly enchanted the sapphire.
  Enchant a dull ruby in the City of Mist The Wraith of Jaxion knows how to enchant this ruby.  He is surrounded by friends, who can fall to those with cunning.  I intend soon to bring cunning to him and his friends.
  Enchant a dull emerald in the Overthere Impaler Tzilug enchanted this, but he used so much magic power in doing so, my magic Charm over him was broken.  I shall have to make a note of that, for use of magic can be fickle indeed, in the way it interacts with the environment.
