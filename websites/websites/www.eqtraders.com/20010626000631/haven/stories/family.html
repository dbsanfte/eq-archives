The Stuff of Dreams - by Triptych

Having grown up as an unwanted, spurned Tier'dal had given Triptych a consuming need to be wanted, to be loved. The Spiritwalkers, with their open acceptance of who she was, and their open support of who she could become, had filled that need nicely. They were her family -- all the family she'd wanted, or needed, until recently.

Now, she was faced with the fact that much of what she had been told of her past was lies. She was faced with blood kin she knew nothing about, and she was deeply disturbed. She also had a sinking feeling that the worst of the surprises was yet to come ...

~~~~

It had started with a dream. Not just any dream, but one of those dreams that left her pounding her sleeping mat with frustration. It left her with the feeling that if she'd had just a moment longer in the dream, she could have figured out what it was trying to tell her.

Child of darkness, child of light, child that's neither day nor night
Three perspectives on one soul, while apart will ne'er be whole
Magics lull, enchantments blind, godless spirits you cannot bind
To break this spell they must be one, only then is it undone.

For weeks the voice had echoed repeatedly in Trip's dreams, always reciting that same verse. Accompanying it were three faces. One was her own. One looked just like her, only the face had green eyes instead of violet. The third was blurred, obscured, and try as she might, she could never quite focus on it.

At first, Trip thought the dream meant that she had in her the capacity to be good, evil, and neutral, and that she had to merge these three faces of hers into one. However, evil had never lured her, and, much to the chagrin of her foster-parents, she'd not been very good at neutrality either. So, she'd continued to wonder and worry ... until a fateful day in West Karana.

After that, she had a completely new set of worries and puzzles.

She'd traveled to the western portion of the Karanas to see about ridding the area of some of the bandits that plagued travelers there. An elder enchantress had been there, and Triptych paused to exchange pleasantries with the woman.

As she prepared to camp for the evening, she noticed a Spiritwalker that she'd never met before, and stopped to say hello to Theolandra. Violet eyes met green, and both wore mirroring images of befuddlement as they stared into a face that looked familiar. Too familiar. A face that, other than the eye color, they'd seen every time they stared at their reflection in a still pool of water or a mirror. While Triptych appeared to have been through a bit more hardening in real-world experiences, Theo seemed to have the more jaded outlook on life.

~~

An hour later, Triptych was still reeling as many of her basic beliefs lay shattered all around her.

... She'd believed that she was a pure dark elf. Now she found that her father was a high elf enchanter who'd worked powerful enough magics to not only deceive her mother, but also ones powerful enough that Triptych appeared to be a dark elf, and was treated as such for all intents and purposes.

... She'd believed that she was an only child. Now she found that she had two other sisters, neutral and evil, as counterparts to her own good nature. Triplets.

... She'd believed that her mother simply hadn't wanted her due to the demands of her position as high priestess of Innoruuk. Now she found that she'd been discarded due to a curse that had been proclaimed at her birth ... and that her mother had actually kept one child with her, to feed her on the evil and hatred so central to her religion.

... She'd believed that the Spiritwalkers would be the only "family" tie she would ever truly acknowledge. (She never really counted her foster parents as family, and knew they felt the same about her.) Now she found herself acknowledging her neutral sister, Theolandra, as blood kin ... and learned of the existence of her blood father, Mith, as well. A father that Theo had grown up with.

It had taken them a while to compare notes, and to accept what the facts were telling them, but finally accept it they did. The evidence was just too strong: matching birth dates, matching mother's name, matching dreams that recited the contents of the "curse" ... not to mention the fact that they looked exactly alike other than eye color. It didn't take long for them to jump to the next obvious conclusion about which of them was the "child of light" and which was the one that was "neither day nor night". Trip's reputation as a "nice" dark elf was growing day by day, whereas Theo couldn't seem to fully understand the loyalty, nay, the love, that Trip had for the Spiritwalkers.

That led to wonder and worry about the third triplet, the "child of darkness". Had she been informed of their existence by their mother, or had she, too, been kept in the dark about the circumstances surrounding their birth? Was she truly a child of darkness, filled with evil and hatred?

Triptych was filled with curiousity, not only about her reputedly aloof father, but also about the third triplet. She believed in her heart that nobody was irredeemable ... not even Morrighan ... and couldn't conceive of her sister NOT wanting to meet them and to learn more about them. Perhaps it was time to take a visit back to Neriak ...

Revelations - by Theolandra

Theo stretched as she awoke from her rest. Rubbing her eyes after a long night of studying, she almost went back to sleep. But that was when the message came, that her sister Triptych needed to talk to her.

A faint note of worry ran through her as she remembered their last conversation, cut short by the yawns and the fact that she couldn't concentrate on what her sister was trying to say. Sister, Theo thought, I have a sister, two sisters if Trip is correct. Definitely a far cry from what Father told me. Theo sighed and prepared herself to go meet her sister.

Upon entering the area of West Karana, Theo thought it best to cast an illusion in case she ran across any of the residents. She had yet to prove her worth to the simple farmers of the land and had little desire to be chased off with their scythes. A small debate thought whether to don the image of a wood elf or a gnome came up with a gnome.

She was interested in an uneventful trip to the old Combine Spires in West Karana. The gnome illusion was working well. Having noticed the looks the farmers gave passing wood elves, not only did she not want to be chased off with their scythes, but she did not want to be chased down because of lust.

So, scurry it was. Gnomes were seldom bothered in the lust department and their height often made them overlooked. And overlooked was what she wanted at the moment. As she ran towards their meeting place, Theo reviewed what little she had learned from Trip.

Apparently, she had been part of a trio. A trio that her mother, a dark elf, had split up. Father had never told her of such things. A wistful sigh escaped her as she continued on. Father rarely talked to her unless it was to discuss some aspect of magic or the latest in fashion. She wondered if he had even noticed she had run away.

So Mother was a dark elf cleric of high standing. Well, she could understand not being wanted by her mother. As dark elves went, from what she had learned in her travels, she was a bit of a disappointment. Evil, to her, seemed to be a waste of time and she couldn't quite get a grasp on this good stuff. You did what you had to do and that was it.

Finally, she spotted the spires in the distance and hurried her pace to reach the top. Upon reaching the top, she spotted an old cart that could not have possibly made it up there under any power. Looking a bit closer as her father had taught her, she saw the faint sparkle of magic proclaiming an illusion.

"Hello, sister," Theo said.

"Evening sister," Trip responded, the cart transforming into her sister as the illusion dropped. "You may want to sit for this," her violet eyes troubled as she looked into Theo's green eyes.

Theo nodded, still not quite used to seeing her own reflection save the difference in the eyes. "Alright, what do you need to tell me?"

Trip composed herself, "I spoke with Lord Spyke before my very ill-timed visit to Neriak." Trip shivered.

"Ah, that's right, I have heard a few bits and pieces about your visit to the homeland and its results," Theo said into the pause. Trip continued, "It seems that mother dearest wanted to make a Bride of Innoruuk. She wanted it so much that she captured and bespelled a certain high elven enchanter of impeccable morals to make the corruption even more absolute."

Theo raised her eyebrow at that one. Father? Was she talking about Father? Impeccable morals? More like Impeccable Fashion Sense. But that was neither here nor there. "Rather arrogant of her, isn't it?"

"Aye, seems we get our own arrogance from more than just our father," Trip's voice was dry.

Things clicked and Theo realized that she was talking about Father. Well, that explained her natural state as a dark elf. Theo shrugged, "Guess so. Wouldn't have put Father in the impeccable morals class. Impeccable fashion sense, perhaps."

"Too stuffy to be anything but, from what I've seen," responded Trip rolling her eyes, remembering having her nose put out of joint at their earlier meeting.

Under her breath Theo muttered a few unkind words about Mith's attention to his fatherly duties.

Ignoring that, Trip continued with her story, "Anyway, she got her conception, Father got away and that should have been that. However, mother was VERY presumptuous and wanted to hedge her bets along with her standing in the temple. So she split a single soul into three parts. Something unheard of and greatly overstepping her bounds."

Theo's eyes widened, "What?" She gulped, thinking of the power required to do that.

Nodding, Trip continued, "She split the soul, but not into three evil parts, as she'd hoped. Instead of tripling her chances of being Mother to the Bride of Innoruuk, she ended up with two "flawed" pieces, in her eyes, due to the curse."

Theo thought for a moment, "Flawed? I take it we are the castoffs?"

"You were just a disappointment to her, methinks. I am an abomination, at least in her eyes. You see, we aren't evil enough to be the future Bride," Trip concluded.

With a grimace, Theo said, "Who would want to? I mean... bride to a God? That doesn't sound very fun at all."

Trip voiced her agreement and then spoke again, "However, the curse was NOT just about making a ‘child of darkness, child of light, child that is neither day nor night.'"

Looking a bit pale and somber under her dark complexion, if such was possible, Trip continued. "The curse was supposed to act such that, if we did not unite, upon our naming majority, we would go mad from the lack of a full soul. What Rodcet Nife and the others did not take into account was several-fold. The first was that we were separated after birth and did not know of the curse, at least the two of us did not."

Theo interrupted, "Father never mentioned such things."

Trip shook her head slightly, "Father never knew. He throught there was only one of us, you, and didn't know of the curse by Rodcet aided by the Lord of the Void. The second was that, due to our experiences and temperaments, we have matured at different rates."

With a wry expression on her face, Theo commented, "Father always said I lacked direction."

"Aye, and I had too much focus, too much drive. If not for certain interventions, I'd be stark raving insane right now, it seems. We need to prevent you, and the other, from meeting such a fate."

Thoughtfully Theo asked, "So this Lord of the Void, he laid the curse on us that we would go mad?"

"I think he only pushed Rodcet in the right direction. I'm not sure he knew the final outcome. However, when he had his full powers, and was not mortal, who knows what he thought of the value of one fractured dark elven soul."

Quietly, Theo thought as Trip continued, "Due to a certain assistance I'd given him in the past, and having impressed him with my strivings in the land of the light he repented, at least in part, on my behalf."

"So this Lord of the Void and this Lord Spyke I have heard of are one in the same? And he is now mortal?"

"Aye," Trip confirmed, "he is the Lord of the Void no longer. He dabbled one too many times in the affairs of mortals, the last being an effort to save the souls of a few Spiritwalkers and he is now VERY mortal. I think he begins to see the enormity of the issue facing us. And realizes it might be a concern after all."

Theo muttered that it seemed fitting. "So, the basic upshot is that we each have a part of a soul and will be going mad when we reach our majority?" Theo's face and voice transmitted her displeasure.

"That's the ‘basic' upshot. However, I have a bit more buffer than you two. I will find some way to reunite us without us getting killed before the madness strikes."

To ease her irritation, Theo took out her Dragoon Dirk and started cleaning her nails. "Oh?"

"Yes, I am under Spyke's protection and have been since the unfortunate Morri demon incident. Apparently my, spunk? Determination? Innate desire to be good? Impressed him and he shielded me," said Trip with a certain impish grin.

With a grin, "Or your dark elven good looks," Theo added. "Has the third been found?"

Trip's eyes darkened a bit, "If that red-eyed spitfire who shares our face and tried to kill me in Neriak the other day is our sister, aye, I found her. Fat lot of good it did me. As for my good looks, at the time I impressed Spyke, I was, um, a firepot if I remember correctly."

Theo chuckled softly. "I suppose Mother knows of all of us," Theo said all humor aside. "And if our third was raised by Mother, then she knows of us.

Nodding, "Aye, and I can only wonder what she told the other, that she now wants to kill us and risk insanity. Of course, anyone who would want to be the Bridge of Innorruuk is already insane in my book."

Theo raised an eyebrow, "Perhaps she has been told that only by killing us can we be ‘reunited'".

Trip paused and blinked. "You know, I think you have something there, sister. Reunited in death? How twisted," she completed with a shiver. "I only hope there is some shred of her soul, our soul, that we can reach within her. I am not losing either of you, if I can help it. No matter how twisted our other sister is, she's still our sister."

With a shrug, Theo said, "If Mother can split a soul before its birth, I don't think reuniting one after death will be so difficult for her."

After a moment, Theo spoke again, "You know, this is very annoying."

"You have a talent for understatement, sister."

Ignoring the interruption, Theo continued, "I decided in my youth not to worship any diety because I didn't want the hassle of deities getting involved in my life. And now to learn that they have meddled in my affairs from my very birth, nay, from even before my birth!"

Trip agreed, "Aye, I chose to worship none as well, as a renunciation of my birth. However, at the time, I thought I was just an unwanted singleton, not part of a trio!"

"Aye. I thought the same, Father wasn't very talkative about Mother," Theo said.

Trip shivered a bit. "And he wasn't much better when I spoke to him of her, sister. However, I got the feeling that *hate* was too weak a word for what he felt."

Thoughtfully, Theo said, "Guess I will have to be a bit careful if I return to Neriak."

"Aye," Trip agreed, "Be very careful if you need to return. She's got her henchmen looking for us now. Illusions will help, but only so far."

"Well, I have developed a few places over here in Qeynos that are slowly accepting me. Seems my necklace of gnoll fangs is enough to combat my dark skin," Theo said, "And I can share their dislike for those noisy dogs they call gnolls."

"Aye, sister, with effort, you can find other places where you can be accepted until this family issue is resolved," Trip said, "And yes, those gnolls are quite the annoyance."

Both sisters sat in thought for a moment, going over the dilemma of their situation.

"Well, if Mother is the typical dark elven cleric or worse," Theo started.

"Or worse, it seems, sister. And Father is the stereotypical, um, snot elf?" Trip continued.

"Then our third sister is probably close to the edge herself," Theo finished. "I have seen a bit of what going on in those temples." Theo shuddered, "Not my idea of fun."

"Aye," Trip agreed, "Our third is probably very close to the edge. I can only pray we can reach her in time to pull her back from the brink. And I don't know if I can get protections extended in time for you, should she lose it and be lost without us able to reunite."

Theo sighed, "My own skill is a bit on the lacking side. I am still learning a lot about the powers I can command."

Worry showed in Trip's eyes, "This whole thing troubles me greatly and has caused me much loss of sleep since I learned of it."

Theo's eyes flashed, "Just introduce me to this ‘Lord Sypke'."

"If I can find him again. He is most elusive and only shows up at the worst possible times. The last time I saw him was while I was still reeling over meeting Father for the first time."

With a snort, Theo said, "Typical immortal. Another reason why I refuse to worship them. Totally unreliable."

Sighing, Trip continued, "I fear I was too rattled to ask the questions I should have. I was nothing, compared to our father, in the rattled department, however."

Theo giggled at the image, "Probably did him well."

"I swear, when he slid into tree illusion, I thought I heard the branches shaking," Trip remembered, "And his earth elemental form looked like it would crumble to dust at any moment."

Wistfully Theo said, "Often I have tried to shake him up a bit. But have failed each time."

With a shrug, "He faked it well, and recovered admirably, but he was rattled. Then he had the gall to get back on his high horse and question me!"

"That's father for you," Theo said with a short laugh.

Indignantly Trip continued, "It wasn't enough that I'd attained this skill mastery, or that I had keen fashion sense, or that I traveled the lands of the light. He wanted to know what else I was good at!"

Theo chuckled at her sister's lost composure, wondering if the flailing arms were part of a levitate spell and making a mental reminder to check it out at a later date. "How did he know it was you? Did he mistake you for me?"

"I overheard someone speak his name, and went up to him and addressed him as "Father". I thought he was going to choke for a moment," Trip muffled a giggle.

Trip continued, "He was like "I only have one daughter." and so on. I asked him if he'd spoken to you recently, since I had thought you were gong to speak to him."

Ignoring the accusing glance, Theo shrugged, "Sorry, I got distracted with a gnoll invasion." Theo managed to barely withhold the frustration in her voice at her failure to get her father's attention. "Let me give you a clue as to how to handle Father. Just know that whatever you do, it will never be enough."

Nodding, Trip asked, "I presume he's one of those bookish high elves who is easily... distracted... from the real world as well?"

Grinning wryly, "Of course," Theo said, "Dust in the library can send him into a rage. As well as dust removed from some tomes of knowledge."

Triptych shook her head, mumbling about Valley Elves.

"That's Father for you. High Elf all the way. And I mean... All The Way," Theo said with great conviction.

Theo stood up, "Well, if you find this Lord Spyke, will you please let him know that I would like to have a talk with him. As well as Father if you see him?"

Trip stood as well, nodding, "I will do so, sister."

"You have given me much to think about, sister," Theo said as she hugged Trip.

"Aye, quite a bit," Trip said as she returned the hug, her eyes filled with worry.

"Be well, sister, I will keep in touch," Theo said as she stepped away from her sister.

"The best I can be, sister. As always," and Trip summoned her magicks and disappeared into a mystical gate.

It was a thoughtful dark elf who made her way back to Qeynos. Suddenly her life had taken some turns she did not like. She muttered under her breath about gods and their messing with mortal's lives. And then it started to rain.

Losing It - by Triptych

Triptych was still worried about Theo, as well as the third triplet, whose name she had yet to learn. Supposedly, the protections placed upon her by the former Lord of the Void would keep her sane from the strain of sharing a split soul with her two siblings, but the other two had no such protections.

Even after heading back to Neriak to help search for Andrasta, and being almost killed by the third triplet had not lessened her desire to help the misguided one. After all, it wasn't the fault of that one that she had the more evil third of the soul, or that Theo got the neutral third.

Perhaps it was the "good" in Triptych that made her want to help, perhaps it was the mysterious bond shared by triplets, but Triptych wanted desperately to help.

She was not a religious type, but she knew her magics alone were not enough. Additionally, Spyke was missing from the world, possibly permanently, so she could not enlist his aid, either. So she prayed to the goddess of love, figuring that if anyone would help three cursed siblings become whole once more, that would be the one to pray to.

After sending out a heartfelt prayer, suddenly, it seemed that the world around her was becalmed a long moment. There was total silence in the swamp surrounding her. Triptych felt a deep wrenching within her, then a feeling of vulnerability. Spyke's protections on her were seriously diminished and weakened. Was this a sign that the goddess of love was angry at her? Or had she imparted some part of her shieldings upon Theo and their sister?

Only time would tell.

The next day found Triptych no closer to an answer. She had yet to find Theo, and was not ready for a repeat visit to Neriak so "mother dearest" and her sister could try to kill her again.

Since the area that she was hunting in carried some risk of serious injury, she decided to illusion into the form of a troll, so that she could have some regenerative abilities. Thankfully, Toxiss didn't seem to mind sharing his camp with a troll any more than he'd seemed to mind sharing with a dark elf.

Triptych could swear she felt her intelligence diminishing, the longer she was in troll form, and she also found herself talking like one. In true trollish form, however, this didn't really bother her at the time, nor did the incessant butt-scratching. Never had she felt this much impact from an illusion, but it was "otay wid her, dis be fun!"

Hours later, when she was back wearing her own form, however, she stared aghast as she realized that the "tasty snack" she was eating was halfling toes. Something was very wrong ...

She could almost discount the recitations of her supposed actions while under illusion as embellishment ... almost, but not quite.

Gaps in her memory were happening more frequently, and she'd find things like gnome meat in her packs after being in troll form, etc. This troubled Triptych, deeply. She was neither violent nor evil, but some of her urges lately scared her.

And then one day, she went from scared, to out and out irked. Always before, her "little episodes" had been when she was under an illusion. Often, she found herself taking on the emotional/mental makeup of the race in question. Sometimes she couldn't even remember what had happened while she was under illusion. So, she'd stopped illusioning unless it was absolutely necessary.

Then one day she blanked out while not under an illusion. One moment she was in lavastorm, the next she was in Kithicor. She couldn't remember a thing about the intervening travel time, and she knew her bind point was nowhere near there. Plaintively, she asked the guild what happened. Quilan responded by calling the Wrath of the gods down on her via spell. He never did give her a straight answer on what she had done that was so bad that he felt the need to blast her solidly to "snap her out of it", but it troubled her that any Spiritwalker would feel the need to cast any attack spell on her.

What troubled her more was the pure rage she felt at the "attack". A rage worthy of a black-hearted dark elf, not one who had spent her entire trying to walk in the light. She no longer could predict what her actions would be if a guildmate tried the same sort of stunt in the future. Damn the gods ... ALL of them ... for playing their little games with HER soul. She was beyond fed up with them and her "dear" mother too. She only prayed she could keep some shred of sanity ... and not hurt anyone ... while they figured out how to break the curse.

Missing - by Theolandra

Theo awoke knowing something was missing. Not the usual feeling something was missing, which, if her sister was to be believed, that something was part of her soul, but something else.

She shivered as she donned her gear for another day of fighting the gnoll invasion. The two things these gnolls seemed to have, unending numbers and stupidity. However was in charge of the Darkpaw family was a complete nut case in Theo's book.

Well, might be best to find her sister and see if this was another move in the game with her soul. Theo snarled to herself. She hated being a pawn in some god's amusement. Well, she said to herself, a pawn can become a queen if they are careful enough and last long enough. She would show these gods what it meant to fiddle with her life

 

Mother Knows Best

Taenalie slipped back into the shadows of an alleyway, allowing her illusion to drop before she stepped back out into the street. As expected, the angry mob passed her by. In a few moments, they'd find the real owner of the form she'd worn, and, with any luck, would imprison and likely kill the visitor for a crime he didn't commit.

Her lips drew back as she bared her teeth in a feral grin of satisfaction. The hatred went on, and grew with every innocent she framed, with every illusion she used to bring out the worst in people.

After all, if she was to become Innorruuk's intended bride, the more hate in the world, the better, right?

She had long ago dispensed with her troublesome conscience, much to her mother's approval. Or at least she let her mother think her conscience was totally gone. Once in a while a tiny voice in the back of her head would tell her how wrong this all was, but it was quickly squashed. She was not weak and flawed like her sisters, and any such perceived flaws were due to the curse. All would be well when the curse was lifted. It had to be. Mother said so.

The one time she'd heard the curse repeat in her dreams, she'd asked her mother after if it meant that the triplets needed to be rejoined. Her mother cuffed her soundly, then told her it meant that the others had to die, that only one was allowed to live. The proper one, the evil one. Her.

Of course, how could it mean anything else? She was the perfect child of evil and the fitting bride for the god of Hate. The flawed images of her must be destroyed.

She'd almost had the goodie two shoes Triptych once. The guards helping her were just an honor guard, she could have taken her out on her own, really, if Trip hadn't somehow gated out of trouble in the nick of time.

Of course she wasn't jealous of the fact that her sister looked well-dressed, successful and content, nor that she seemed to be welcomed almost everywhere! Her? Jealous of that flawed one? Never! She and that weak fence-straddler Theolandra were just defective, fragmented receptacles of a soul that should be wholly Taenalie's by right.

They had to die. She was the only proper one. Mother said so, and Mother always knew what was best ... didn't she?

The Loss of a Soul - Theolandra

Theolandra tossed and turned in her bed, the sheets twisted and soaked with sweat. The nightmares were getting worse each night, this one by far the worst. In her dreams, she found herself being hunted, chased down by some horrible darkness, twisted and malignant. Run as she might, it was constantly getting closer and closer. Once, in her dreams she had turned to look back. That was the night she had awoken screaming, the burning red eyes forever etched in her memory.

Tonight she had taken the potion given to her by a local shaman for her dreams. He had told her it would help. With her current lack of sleep, Theo was willing to try anything. It was only dreams, she told herself. Dreams can't hurt you. Then why are you scared, a small voice asked in her head.

Angry with herself, she downed the potion and sought her sleep. But once she had closed her eyes, the nightmare had taken her, stronger than ever. Burning red eyes formed before her, eyes of hate. A voice whispered to her, "Well met, sister," the venom causing her to shrink back.

Theo backed away only to bump into a wall of some force. With a gasp, she found herself caught behind a barrier of some sort, caged in a circle of power. Trembling slightly, she said, "This can't be happening. I don't believe it. It isn't true."

The eyes moved closer out of the darkness, coalescing into a small dark shape. "Oh, it's happening, dear sister. Like the prophecy said it would. Our souls will be united. You will become one again." Taenalie paused, "With your death!"

At that, Taenalie came into full view and Theo couldn't suppress the gasp of recognition. "That's right, sister dear, I'm the third one, the Chosen One. And with your piece of my soul, I will become that much more powerful and whole. And after your weakling fall, our other part will fall just as easily," the venom seemed drip from her voice.

"How?" asked Theo, frantically searching for some way out of this dream that was no dream but a frightening reality.

"Oh, it wasn't all that difficult," Taen shimmered to that of a shaman, "when you have the right face."

Theo watched in horror as the figure returned to that of a female dark elf. "Illusions can be very helpful in gaining ones objectives, don't you agree, sister?"

The other smiled, "But enough chit-chat. Time to finish what was started."

Dark forces spewed forth from the other, surrounding the circle of power. Theo could only watch in horror as the powers of Hate came forth, just chanting to herself, "No, this isn't possible, I don't believe. The gods have no power over me."

The power grew and grew, the eyes boring themself into Theo until finally the dam broke and poured into Theo, sucking her soul from her body. Theo screamed in anguish, the pain of death nothing compared to that of losing one's soul. Eons passed while the power surged and waned, until finally, it vanished.

Theo's limp body fell to the ground, a dark form now devoid of soul. Taen picked herself up from the floor as well, a maniacal grin on her face, "I am victorious Mother! Soon I will be whole again!" Then she too vanished from the dream, as quickly as the dark powers had.

Long moments passed and all was still in the dream. But the intrusions were not done for the night. A small figure poked his head in, surveying the scene. He wandered over to Theo's form, prodding it for any signs of life. "Hello? Anybody home?"

He glanced from side to side, making sure no one else was around. Then a big grin blossomed on his face. "I think I have just the thing for you."

He shook his fingers out and then clapped them together, forming a ball shape with them. Muttering a few words, his hands started to glow, or rather, something inside started to glow. With a smile of satisfaction, he opened his hands, allowing the small glowing ball of light to float free.

It wavered there a moment and the small figure nodded towards the limp form of Theo. "There you go, if you want it."

The small ball seemed to glow with approval and then dove for Theo's body. The light grew and grew, causing even the small one to avert his eyes. When it faded he looked back and whistled. "Won't this put some starch in someone's shorts," he grinned as he cackled gleefully fading into the night.
