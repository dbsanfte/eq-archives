Introduction to the Thurgadin Shawl Quests

 

Welcome to the wonderful world of the shawls! The most exciting, entertaining, and action-packed quests ever seen in the land of Norrath!

Weeell, not exactly.... Oh, don't get me wrong, I wouldn't have bothered to go through with the blimey thing, much less write this guide, if I didn't think it's worth it in the end. But most people who have gotten very far with the shawl quest find themselves in a Love/Hate relationship. The thing about the shawls is that they require a LOT of work.

Ok, here, take the amount of work that you are right now envisioning you will need to put into the whole thing, getting from nothing to the eighth shawl. Now, if you are a pessimist by nature, multiply that amount by ten. If you are an optimist by nature, multiply it by a hundred.

That is how much effort will go into just getting your tailoring where it needs to be to complete the sixth shawl.

You also need to get your baking, brewing, fletching, jewelcraft, pottery and smithing all up to similar levels. You need to get items off of creatures in Iceclad, East Wastes, Kael Drakkel, Cobalt Scar, Great Divide, Siren's Grotto, West Wastes, and Velketor's Labyrinth, among others. Some of these critters cannot be soloed by a single person of any level or class. Unless your trade skills are all already where they need to be, you will not have the eighth shawl with only a few weeks of work. Even if by some strange twist your skills are all up there and you don't have this thing yet, you'll still need a lot, a lot lot lot of time and help to get it that fast. I think of this as the epic quest for tradeskillers. With all the time required to get my skills up and do some research and collect obscure stuff, I easily spent as much time on this thing as I did on my shaman epic weapon, probably a great deal more.

Now, that said...I obviously love this quest and love trade skills or else I would not have taken the time to compile these guides to help others through it. I just want you to realize with crystal clear understanding the magnitude of what you are undertaking before you make yourself and the rest of Norrath miserable.

The demands of the shawls are great. So are the rewards. And the headaches in between the two.

One note for the faction-conscious: this quest will require the deaths of numerous giants and their wolves. If you are aligned with the giants and Coldain against the dragons, you will want to make sure you are willing to sacrifice some giant faction (or the time to repair it), or have friends willing to invest a LOT of time helping you collect things. In some cases you can buy what you need from others, but you'll still have to be extra careful if you want to maintain giant faction.

Still with me? Alright, let's get to work!

"What is the quickest way to raise Xxxxxx skill?"

At one point I had considered putting in a guide to what to use to raise each skill, as submitted by one shawl quester. However, after watching every existing smithing guide get very obsolete with the changes to smithing, and hearing word that similar changes are in store eventually for many of the other tradeskills, I've changed my mind. It is too much attention to be paying to something that I don't think is necessary. Also, NO guide will be completely accurate for any given race/class/diety/level/playing style/time available. A little time and effort developing a plan for yourself may well save you a LOT of time and effort spent using an approach that wasn't designed with your situation in mind.

You are not without any guidance, however! EQ Traders is a comprehensive site that tries to offer every conceivable tidbit of information that may be useful to a tradesperson. Therefore, I suggest you take the following steps, either to find any particular answer or just to familiarize yourself with the site and get started with your skills:

First, go to the EQ Traders Recipes page. Select the skill you would like to improve. Print that page. Then go to Supplies sorted by Skill and again select the skill you want to improve. Print that page.

To figure out how to raise your skill to the level you desire, look over both pages that you just printed. Specifically, look for recipes that require ingredients that you know you can find easily and/or that are listed on the supplies page as available somewhere within reason. For some skills like jewelcraft, fletching, or pottery, just about everything will be on vendors if you find a good city in which to work. For other skills you will have to practice the fine arts of merchant mining or auctioning or even rolling up your sleeves and slaughtering greens.

Once you get a feel for which recipes are feasible and which are not, look at where they become trivial and plan out an order to do them from easiest to hardest. It is possible to go from 0 to 200+ on a single recipe that trivials at that high, if you wish. The difficulty of the recipe has no bearing on the rate at which your skill will improve when you use that recipe, as long as it's not yet trivial. However, it is definitely more fun if you use at least a few different recipes that trivial at gradually higher levels.

If you want to give this trade skill stuff an honest try, look for a few recipes that sound fun (Gnome Kabobs!) and throw them in for the heck of it. When working up your skill, keep in mind that just because something is trivial does not mean that you are guaranteed to succeed. There is always a 5% chance of failure, no matter how good you are and how easy the item. Depending on the value of your ingredients and the cost and trouble of increasing your skill, it may be worth it to get 30-40 points of skill beyond where the item trivials. Likewise, if a skill is very hard to raise and your quest ingredients are easier to get, you might be better off making several attempts at a skill below trivial than to try to get to trivial skill first.

Still have problems or questions? Make sure that you have read everything on the pages you already printed, and then go to Learn a Skill and select the relevant skill and read all of that. Question not yet answered? Try the General FAQ. Answer still not there? Ok now, go to the Message Boards and select your skill. Check the FAQ at the top of those posts. They are called Frequently Asked Questions because many people ask them. If many people ask them then you might well be asking the same thing. Yes, I realize that this is a lot of reading, but tradeskills are complicated stuff. There is a lot you might need to know, so the investment of time learning about it up front will save you headaches when the time comes that you need the knowledge. If you're still determined to find a guide to what to use to skill up, the trade-specific forums are the best place to look. If all of those sources fail you, post your specific question in the forum relevant for your skill. Depending on the time of day and week of your post, answers will appear within minutes or hours. When you have made it to the level you desire in your current trade skill, pat yourself on the back! You are that much closer to your shawl.

Do NOT under any circumstances go straight to the message boards without first checking the existing resources. Especially do NOT post "What's the quickest way to improve Xxxxxx skill?". There are at least twenty posts on that very same topic on the boards for every single skill, so if you insist you can go there and look for them and see what has been posted in response. All of the answers to those posts are based on people looking at the links I've given you above and regurgitating it for you. That level of helplessness is annoying. The recently reintroduced EZ Board search engine is much better than it used to be. It also won't kill you to flip back through the last few pages of posts and skim the titles. You're not the first person to work on your skill, so chances are very slim that you have a question that has not yet been asked and answered. Questions relating to skilling up for the shawls or in general are especially common. Folks on the boards love helping with honest questions and problems and dilemmas, but do not appreciate being treated like personal researchers when the answer is just a few clicks away on the very same site. If you've looked and just can't find it, then by all means post! But please, just take a look around first. If you cannot spare an hour or so just once to get oriented and learn how information is arranged on the site, then you'll never, ever manage to invest enough time to get through these shawls. And who knows, you might even find some helpful information about which it wouldn't have occurred to you to ask!
