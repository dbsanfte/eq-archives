Meek - Human Bard

A lady bard traveled far and long
looking for fodder for her song
She'd left her home for far-off lands
then she fell into slaver hands.

She'd been nobody's slave
a loose-footed bard with voice of gold
They made her into a slave
'prisoning her in shackles so cold.

 

Meek's mother, Zehleia, had been a very skilled high-elven bard with a strong curiousity, as well as an unrivalved compositional skill. Often, she would travel the lands, drinking in new sights, new sounds, new experiences, to add to her songs. Rarely did she travel with companions, as she believed that her bardic abilities would keep her safe. Few could resist the allure of Zehleia's songs, or bear to hurt one who could, it seemed, make the very rocks sing. Until she tangled with the slavers ...

While they found her songs alluring, they found the thought of cash for the songstress even more alluring. Especially when it was known that Ambassador Dvinn was looking for a pet songbird and would pay handsomely.

A captive bard in lands so wild
Performing on cue -
then they got her with child.

She tried to escape,
she tried to break free
but the fates decreed
it was never to be.

She was somebody's slave
a pregnant bard bought and sold
They made her daughter a slave
'prisoning them both in shackles so cold.

And so to Crushbone Zehleia went, home to filthy orcs, abused slaves, and the foul dark-elven Dvinn. Blessed with vocal and physical beauty, it was small wonder that she attracted as much attention as she did. Nor was it surprising that she bore a child in that foul pit. A child that was not pureblooded anything, and as such was given the designation of "half-elf".

Her daughter showed talent
before she could walk
she could sing like a lark,
but she couldn't talk

They beat her and healed her
and still she stuttered
"That'll lessen her value"
they grumbled and muttered.

She was only a slave
one who could compose and sing
such a valued slave
mother and child - riches they'd bring.

During the long pregnancy Zehleia gave much thought to her unborn child, and to it's fate in this accursed land. Much as she did not wish to bring new life into slavery, she could not bring herself to terminate the life within her, even if there had been means available to her. She was watched constantly, since she was one of Dvinn's most prized possessions, and was oft paraded out to official visitors and made to perform on cue.

However, since there was always a demand for her original songs, many of the times when Zehleia appeared to be in a songwriting daze, she was in a spellwriting daze instead. A spell that was woven into each and every spell she sang to her child, both in the womb, and after the birth. Delicate, unnoticed strands of magic, meant to protect the child from drawing too much notice. Meant to make the child have a flaw that would prevent the wee Meek one from turning into yet another pet bard.

She left out one crucial thing in forming the spell, however, as the child had a singing voice that made Dvinn willing to overlook her obvious flaws. Unhappy, but willing to overlook the flaws nonetheless. Two pet bards of phenomenal skill. Largesse for the status-hungry Ambassador.

Too late did Zehleia try to modify the spell set upon her daughter. Dvinn had gotten more careless in his handling of her now that he had a supposedly more malleable child to mold in place of the self-confident mother. Her powers were weakened, and not up to modifying without destroying the spell already in place. Too weak. Too late.

That all changed
after her mother died
they beat her soundly
when she cried

She ran to the master
screaming "I'm your child"
He said "You're only a slave,
undisciplined and wild"

She was her father's slave
He never said, but she knew it was true
Still he kept her a slave --
a pet bard has "value."

The following years were hard on the young lass, as she watched her mother waste away, eventually sickening and dying. Enslaved, treated with alternating abuse and indifference, the stuttering child learned the benefits of hiding and silence, and was tagged for all time with the name "Meek". The bardic gift ran strongly in her, though, and it was hard to hold back on expressing the "gifts" of songs that her muses whispered in her ears. ... Especially when it was her only way to communicate without having to struggle to form every word.

She bided her time
'till the moment was right.
The gods sent her rescue,
and she fled in the night

She ran from her slavery,
she ran from the past
she ran for Qeynos
and made it at last.

Now she's nobody's slave!
Her life's finally her own
She's nobody's slave!
Just see how she's grown ....
She's nobody's slave ...

 

The gods only know what would have happened to Meek if she'd not been found and rescued by Ruldari, and found friendship and a safe-haven. The tale of her rescue can be found in the tale "A Different Type of Harvest". Some of her other songs can be found in A Tangtoungled Lass.
