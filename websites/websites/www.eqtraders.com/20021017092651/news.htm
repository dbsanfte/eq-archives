June 10, 2002

Just a few quick updates:

Cazic-Thule jewelcraft has been deciphered, and the first pieces have been successfully made. Recipes and stats are on the Cazic-Thule recipes page.

The dust is finally starting to slowly settle from the June 5 patch and all the changes to smithing trivials. It's still going to take a while to get it all updated, but we're getting there. For all of those asking on the various forums, as far as we know only smithing trivials were changed.

There's another patch this Wednesday. Hopefully the rockhoppers and sambata that share a cave in Dawnshroud Peaks will find their way home again, as well as a few other spawn issues. :) We'll have more of an update Wednesday.

We received the following suggestion on our Quests message board, and there's already been responses that it does help when having problems with quest NPCs:

If any NPC is not responding to direct text do this. Clear your target window and say the quest text. For some reason they will respond when you do NOT have them targeted. So just make sure you don't have a target at all by pressing the escape key.

As an addendum to this, close your inventory window before you hail them, as this has been reported to be one issue with non-responsive NPCs. If that doesn't work, close your eyes, stand on one foot, bay at the moon and hit the ENTER key ... wait, sorry, that last bit is the Bristlebane in me. Try the non-targeted talk and the inventory closed if you're having problems with an NPC, and, as always, remember to research in advance to verify that you have the proper faction, proper text, etc.

Administrivia: we're working on collecting all the information needed to have a comprehensive tradeskill database that will allow cross-referencing of trade supplies, sources (vendors, mob drops, etc.) and recipes. It's a huge undertaking, and it's going to take a lot of time to get it ready for public viewing. If you see requests in one of the forums asking for help collecting data, that's why, and the information we need the most is on the exotic stuff - tradeskill drops off mobs, player-made cultural items that are then used as ingredients for other recipes (i.e., adamantite chain jointing, and other such things) and so on. So, as you're tradeskilling and shopping, the data that you get from a right-click is likely exactly what we're looking for. Keep an eye on the various forums for a request for your tradeskill(s). Meanwhile, this half-blind hobbit is toddling off tomorrow for lasik eye surgery,and is supposed to be MIA from the boards and other eye-strain until sometime Wednesday (patch day). Wish me luck!

 

June 6, 2002

I have several things to highlight today:

The new smithing recipes and trivials are slowly trickling in, and I have some requests to make, based on the first day's worth of data. Please be sure that when you're reporting on stats for new items, that you're including race/class/weight information. I know it takes a few extra moments to scribble it all down, but it really makes a difference on the completeness of information for the site. Please make sure you're clearly stating trivials - there's a difference between "trivial 222" and "trivial <=222". Make sure you're being fully clear if you know the exact trivial vs. it being trivial at that point or some point below. Please be sure that when you're reporting new trivials on old cultural items that you are explicitly stating whether you're working in racial chain or plate for things like dark elven and high elven armors. It may seem like a little thing, but when we're already dealing with normal, enchanted, and imbued/enchanted armor, I refuse to "assume" that I know which set you're talking about.

That having been said, there is a lot of data already on the appropriate smithing pages. If you find a smithing page that does not include two trivial colums (old & new), then I have not yet processed any new information for that specific group of items. This may mean that nobody has submitted any new information, or it may just mean that I've not reached that part of the huge pile of updates that have been dropped in my lap.

Yes, I'm aware that pretty much every smithing guide in the Learn Smithing section is now totally outdated. This situation isn't likely to change until new trivials are more fully nailed down and folks start submitting new guides based on that information.

More updates have been done to the PoTC quest writeup, including the addition of the non-tradeskill spell-only version of the quest. It still needs a lot of fleshing out, but that's going to take more time than I currently have.

June 5, Addendum 1:

The following is a post from Rich Waters, one of the Lead Designers for EQ:

If you are not a Druid and you've completed the PoTC quest, go and speak with Rebecca Cabbageleaf in Fungusgrove - She's more than happy to give you something you'll be able to use instead of Xanthe's Earring.

If you're one of the few people who did the quest twice and lost an earring due to it being made lore, petition in game and a GM will reimburse you with the nine ingredients needed to make another earring after verifying your loss.

If your Xanthe's Earring is out of charges, trade it in with Rebecca Cabbageleaf in Fungusgrove, and she'll give you one that has charges. When items that didn't have an effect are retroactively changed, it sometimes causes the "out of charges" bug.

To trade the earring in with Rebecca, give her your Xanthe's Earring and she'll give you an alternate kind of earring. Give her back the alternate earring, and she'll give you a fully charged Xanthe's earring back.

Sorry for any inconvenience this has caused you, this should solve any difficulties anyone's had with the earring.

Thanks,

- Rich

(I'll be working on webbing it after the caffeine kicks in.) The quest will stay in game, for people who enjoy tradeskills and anyone who completes the full quest will find the final reward is quite suitable for the effort.

Thanks for playing,

Rich Waters
Lead Designer, EverQuest

In other words, folks, if you wait until the next patch, the spell will be easier to obtain. The quest will stay in game for those of us who enjoy playing with such stuff {curls protectively around her fishing pole and her Misty Thicket Picnic}, but for those who don't have a tradeskill junkie's patience with such quests, find enough patience to wait for the next patch. ;D

May 8 , 2002 - The Patch Message

I won't repeat the entire patch message, but here are the parts that are relevant to tradeskills, along with some commentary:

  • Added over 1,000 new tradeskill recipes for all levels of skill, along with many recipe books. A few of the vendors of Norrath might be able to get you started on your way (of course, we'll be scrambling here to get the information online for all of you, but expect things to be a tad ... hectic ... for a while)
  • Added Mortar and Pestle to a Thurgadin merchant (this should make poisonmaking, dyemaking, pottery idols, and a few other things a bit easier for those who have chosen to live mostly in Velious)
  • Rubbing Alcohol is now available from a vendor in Thurgadin (happy shawl questing! No more running to Cabilis!)
  • Hit point bonuses on food items should be working (centi steaks and centi toe stew, anyone?)
  • Characters can no longer scribe a scroll that they can't cast due to deity restrictions (imbue gem spells were bugged for a while so anyone of the proper level/class could scribe them, now you should be able to only scribe the spell for your deity)

Your best bet for the first 24 hours is to keep an eye on the message boards for the new recipes. It's going to take some time to get these things webbed, especially with the server lag I'm already seeing this morning from eager folks at w*rk hitting the site, trying to see if anything's been found yet.

April 3 , 2002 - Stray Cazic Thule Update
I'll leave the venting to others, but here's some details on the new Cazic Thule recipes almost a month after they were released...

Baking recipes with the no-stack Tae Ew meat have all been verified and are on the baking recipes page. If you're someone unable to hunt in this zone, do not stalk the vendors trying to find the meat. The vendor asking price for this meat is currently around 2100pp (no, not what the vendor gives you, what the vendor asks you to pay for it).

On the good news front, one of the two brewing recipes has finally been verified. The bad news is that a) the blood needed for this smithing temper is no drop, b) the blood is extremely, extremely rare so far, c) the other ingredient is the iksar cultural scale temper, which is rare due to the difficulty in obtaining iksar blood, d) the final product is no drop, requiring that the blacksmith who wishes to make the armor experimentation must also be the looter and the brewer.

No jewelcraft, tailoring or smithing experiments have yet been reported as successful, nor have drops yet been reported for the tailoring hides, etc.

Hopefully, some of this just needs some more tweaking ...
