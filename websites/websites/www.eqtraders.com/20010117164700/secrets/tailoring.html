Tailoring Secrets

Trivial levels listed are player-provided. When you see a trivial range, please realize that we have sources giving varying trivial levels for things, and it's looking more and more like trivial levels are being changed without warning. Flames that I have the "wrong" trivial will be happily ignored. Please feel free to send me any trivial levels that you are aware of.

Make sure that when recipes require more than 1 of an item (silk swatch, heady kiola), that the items are NOT stacked in your sewing kit.

Note that in many places, I mention HQ and MQ "pelts", but some of them may be named "skins" instead. Please do not let Verant's naming inconsistencies confuse you. If the rest of the name is correct (high quality cat, ruined wolf, etc.), then it is the correct item. (For example, it's an HQ bear skin, not an HQ bear pelt, but it works properly in the recipe)

NEW - Hafling Cultural Leathers -- Wood Elf Cultural Mithril Leather

Pelt conversion: Used to convert HQ pelts/skins to MQ pelts/skins, MQ pelts/skins to LQ pelts/skins (important if you're making leather backing), and LQ pelts/skins to ruined pelts: Quality pelt, skinning knife (from a smith), combined in a sewing kit. Next lower in quality pelt is returned, and the knife is also returned to you. This works with bear, cat and wolf (i.e., our "standard" tailoring pelts) only. It does NOT work to downgrade lion or "named" pelts such as mist wolf, grizzly bear, etc. (Trivial 15)

  • Silk thread (trivial 15): 2 spiderling silks, combined in a sewing kit (small or large kit)
  • Silk cord (trivial 26): 3 silk threads (unstacked)
  • Silk bandages (trivial 21): 2 silk thread (unstacked)

Silk swatches (trivial 15): 2 spider silks, combined in a sewing kit (small or large kit)

  • Small tailored bag (trivial between 28-31, 4 small slots): silk cord, LQ wolf pelt
  • Large tailored belt pouch (trivial 37, 6 small slots, weight 0.6): silk cord, LQ cat skin
  • Large tailored bag (trivial 42, 6 slots, weight 0.8): silk cord, LQ bear skin
  • Tailored wrist pouch (trivial <27, 4 small slots, weight 0.3): silk cord, ruined wolf pelt

Leather padding (used for fine steel plate and cultural smithing, trivial 31): silk thread, low quality cat/bear/wolf skin (any of the 3 work) (lion and other exotic pelts will not work, only bear/cat/wolf)

Patchwork armor (trivial 26): ruined pelt and appropriate pattern, combined in a sewing kit (small or large kit). Note that some pieces show up as "patchwork", other pieces show as "tattered". The ruined pelt used determines the size of the armor: ruined wolf = small, ruined cat = medium, ruined bear = large

Raw silk armor (trivial 32-37): pants and tunic need 2 silk swatches and a pattern, combined in a large sewing kit. All other pieces of raw silk need 1 silk swatch and 1 patten. Either size sewing kit can be used.

Studded leather (trivial 56-61): Medium quality pelt, appropriate pattern and metals studs (made by a smith). The type of medium quality pelt used determines the size of the armor created (same as patchwork).

  • belt - MQ pelt, 2 studs, pattern
  • boots - MQ pelt, 4 studs, pattern
  • bracer - MQ pelt, 2 studs, pattern
  • cap - MQ pelt, 3 studs, pattern
  • cloak - MQ pelt, 4 studs, pattern (trivial 57)
  • gloves - MQ pelt, 4studs, pattern
  • gorget - MQ pelt, 2 studs, pattern (trivial at 56)
  • mask- MQ pelt, 1 stud, pattern (trivial at 56)
  • pants - MQ pelt, 4 studs, pattern (trivial 61)
  • shoulderpad - MQ pelt, 3 studs, pattern
  • sleeves - MQ pelt, 3studs, pattern
  • tunic - MQ pelt, 5 studs, pattern (trivial 57)

Wu's Fighing Gauntlets [aka "Glowing silk" gloves] (trivial 84) - allows monks to hit magic-only creatures - 4 heady kiolas (from a brewer), 1 greater lightstone, 1 silk swatch, 1 glove pattern, 1 vial of viscous mana (from an enchanter - requires use of the thicken mana spell, and needs both a pearl and a poison vial as material components)
