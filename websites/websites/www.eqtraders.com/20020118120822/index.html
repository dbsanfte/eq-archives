January 17, 2002
My cable connection is less than reliable right now (@home), and our "backup" DSL isn't quite happy, so I've been trying to do a bit of offline work with the information I have available on my hard drive (and in my overloaded memory). Several hours after I wrote this initial update, I've finally got enough of a DSL connection to publish all the pages I've worked on today ... barely. Hopefully by tomorrow things will have stabilized a bit, and we'll have full net access once again.

I've formally added a solid Quests section to the site, specifically for quests requiring tradeskills. It will take a while for me to get the button onto all the pages, and for the page to include all the Shar Vahl class quests, etc., but it's starting to shape up a bit. (I'm always looking for accurate, well-researched tradeskill quest summaries.)

I'll be slowly removing items from the temporary Luclin Recipes page and moving them to their proper recipes pages. So, if you don't find a recipe that you saw here, look a bit more closely at the proper tradeskill recipe pages. (Hint for baking: scroll down. The old world recipes are listed first, then the Velious recipes, then the Luclin recipes.)

I've also added a "Flyswatter Needed" section to the site. This will contain various recipes that are listed in in-game books, given as quests, etc. that, for one reason or another, we can't seem to get working properly. They may be solidly bugged or they may be something we've just not properly figured out. They may also include recipes that were reported in as bugged, and we've not yet realized that the fix has been patched in. If you find anything on this page that you've personally tested, and it is working, please report back what you found to be the working recipe, and I will update the page accordingly.

January 9, 2002
Smiths have reported back in that the various metal rings are now stacking as promised and the recipes are yielding 2 rings (in some cases they had returned 1 ring, in other cases 2, now there is some consistency). I'm working on updating the smithing recipe pages now.

January 8, 2002 - Patch Day
Sorry for the delay in updating the front page. After we're back from bringing the final houseguest to the airport in the very early morning tomorrow, I'll be updating the recipe pages to reflect the following information (once I hear back from folks on the boards, verifying that all is happy - I've only personally verified the high quality rings so far.)

From the patch message:

  • Regular, medium quality, high quality, mithril, brellium and adamantite rings, as well as their enchanted versions are now stackable. (this time we mean it)
  • Fixed Shadowscream and all related recipes so that they return the correct mallet

In other news, Jykferth from Tarew Marr hit 250 fletching and was the first to report back that the new shadewood bows and acrylia arrows are not trivial at 250. Well done!

I've still got a LOT of quest information to wade through and post. I'll be slowly getting it up, and then prettying it up some.

December 27, 2001, update 2
It's very slow going, but I'm starting a page that will detail the various Luclin tradeskill quests, and am cross-linking recipes from the Luclin recipes page over to it as I get the appropriate details in place. As always, I'm worried more about the accuracy of the information before I worry about making it "pretty" and so forth. December 27, 2001
The December 20 patch, which advertised some tradeskill changes, may not have had as many tradeskill changes as we thought. At least some of the rings (such as some of the quality rings) are reported as still not stacking. Other reports state that bait and fishing flies are not able to be equipped in the ammo slot, as advertised, and if there is a bonus to the fishing skill available from player-made fishing flies, it's not yet visible. On the positive side, new tradeskill merchants have been added in several spots, and now that I'm back from traveling, I'll be doing more zone verification to make sure we catch them all. (I spent time in Halas this morning, and those trade supply location pages should be updated.)

{activates rant mode} While the DenMom's back from holiday travel, I won't guarantee a happy mood, thanks to information that one of my regulars found in the pit of chaos that is usenet. Someone who apparently needs a clue-by-four as to the amount of time and expense that this site has cost me over the past two years of running it is slamming my chosen uses for donation money. I stopped counting the fees for domain name registration, reimbursement for my OLD hosting provider when her fee schedule changed without warning before I could move the site, etc. I've lost count of the times that idjits have run through slamming my way of doing things, oft-times simultaneously making use of two years of hard work and stubborn redheaded research. None of them, of course, want to do the work that is required for pretty much solo-running a million-plus page hit per month site, plus mommy the mess that is the message boards (accounting for an added 10K hits per day on top of the site page hits), nor would they have payed for the first 6 months of CSC gold out of their own pockets, etc.

Thank you, from the bottom of my halfling heart. You folks are, and always will be, the reason I continue with this, even when the "game" turns once again to "work".
