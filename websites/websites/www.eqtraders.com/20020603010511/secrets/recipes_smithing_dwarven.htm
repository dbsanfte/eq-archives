Dwarven Cultural Smithing

The smithing portion of these recipes, unless otherwise noted, must be performed by dwarves, using their cultural forge in Kaladim (no, you cannot fool the forge with an illusion). This page now includes the May 8 cultural recipes, with the new NPC text included down at the bottom of the page. Stats for the weapons and armor on this page can be found on the Dwarven Cultural Armor/Weapons Stats page

dwarven chain

dwarven smithy hammer,small chainmail pattern earthen temper, appropriate number of brelium rings:

  • Veils, Neckguards, Boots and Bracers - 1 set of brellium rings.
  • Coifs, Mantles, Skirts, Sleeves, and Gloves - 2 sets of brellium rings.
  • Tunics, Capes, and Leggings - 3 sets of brellium rings

Bracer and tunic recipe verified as working. Some others untested

trivial <=244 on bracer
imbued/enchanted dwarven chain

  • Veils, Neckguards, and Bracers - 1 set of enchanted brellium rings.
  • Coifs, Mantles, Skirts, Sleeves, Boots and Gloves - 2 sets of enchanted brellium rings.
  • Tunics, Capes, and Leggings - 3 sets of enchanted brellium rings

NPC text from May 8 patch:

You say, 'Hail, Ragunk Everhot'
Ragunk Everhot says ''Ye Gods, that's the stuff!' Ragunk burps loudly as he takes a swig from a small flask on his forge. 'Fit for the King himself! Maybe I can get this [armor] finished on time!''

You say, 'what armor'
Ragunk Everhot says ''Don't interrupt me, child. I'm drinking.' Ragunk staggers and takes another swig from his flask. 'Say, do you know anything about [chainmail] or [platemail]?''

You say, 'What platemail'
Ragunk Everhot says ''Heavy duty stuff, very serious it is. With enchanted brellium chain jointing, my dwarven hammer, earthen temper, a small plate mold and a few [infused sheets] of brellium, the sky is the limit! It looks as if it'd take imbuing, too.''

You say, 'What infused sheets'
Ragunk Everhot says ''An enchanted large brick of brellium can be worked into a folded velium sheet with a dwarven smithy hammer. Don't forget that [strange temper], too - that's the secret to all this!''

You say, 'what strange temper'
Ragunk Everhot says ''My new friend gave me enough to make several sets of armour, it really is quite something.' Ragunk takes a mouthful of pungent drink from his flask, you think that his eyes look a little nervous. 'I'm not sure what's in it, but my old Dwarven eyes detected a fine, blue powder within. Who knows!'

You say, 'What chainmail'
Ragunk Everhot says ''Hah! I thought not. I've recently come into contact with a very interesting person. Very interesting indeed.' Rungak burps again. 'Not that I ever saw his face mind, that bloody dark robe is enormous! He did have some good ideas about [brellium] though.''

You say, 'what brellium'
Ragunk Everhot says ''Well, he showed me how to work it into stronger rings than before. Taking a few velium rings, and then working in a large brick of enchanted brellium, with my dwarven smithy hammer, and a little [strange temper]. Then I used that to make the chainmail. Such a simple [method]!''

You say, 'what method'
Ragunk Everhot says ''You're all questions, aren't you! Take your dwarven hammer, and a small piece of leather armour, earthen temper, a small sectional mold and then some of the infused brellium rings, and you've a handy set of linked chain! I may try imbuing later, maybe peridot or ruby would work. All in the dwarf forge, of course!''
