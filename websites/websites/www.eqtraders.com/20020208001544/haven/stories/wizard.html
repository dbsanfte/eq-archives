Sex and the Single Wizard

by Nebula

This document contains both an explanation of my own viewpoint regarding sex, and a long overdue proposal for the study of the sexual proclivities of the various races and classes within Norrath.

It has long astounded me that sex is made so much of in today's society. In my conversations and interactions with various denizens, sex is so often mentioned and commented on that one wonders how half our citizenry even manages to leave the bedroom! It is a mystery to me how such a straightforward subject as breeding can be twisted into a myriad innuendoes and rituals.

My own parents had sex of course, or I wouldn't be here. But they did it the proper way, after a long study of the subject, a drafted strategy of experimentation, and with the express purpose of producing myself, as it should be. Once done, it was never spoken of again, although I was allowed to read the ensuing documented report of the process when my parents considered me old enough. I was, needless to say, impressed with the clarity and efficiency of their efforts. However, I did find the whole described process to be messy and distasteful, and understood immediately why I had no siblings.

Not so, it seems, with the average adventurer. In a short poll taken by myself, in various locations, I found that a high percentage of races engage in sex more than once in a lifetime. In fact, shocking as this may be, some of them claimed almost weekly sexual activity...many of them without even a thought of the consequences! My poll shows that sex is thought about, talked about, hoped for and acted out all over Norrath on a daily basis! Yes, I know. Hard to imagine, isn't it? But there it is, mathematically calculated, and double checked.

Spurred by these results, and in the interest of science, I decided to take a closer look at the subject. Armed with a lurid treatise of gender related interaction, appropriately titled "Ohh...You Rogue You!" by Gentian D'Tart, I somewhat hastily tutored myself in the ways of sexual attraction and set forth.

My first experiment was in the area of flirting...a strange and ritualistic verbal exchange seemingly meant to show your interest in a member of the opposite sex. After several bad starts (I was actually laughed at once..hmpph) I was able to capture the attention of a fellow erudite. I noted several strange phenomena during this verbal exchange, that I think may be a form of primitive "body language." There seemed to be a significant amount of eyebrow bobbing, leering, "cow eyes" and hip bumping.

Encouraged by these signs, I moved to the next stage...the exchange of flattery and endearments. At this point I was hard pressed indeed to continue. Standing still while someone is calling you "sweet thing" and "babe" is, as far as I'm concerned, above and beyond the call of duty. How can someone retain their dignity during such a display? Still, I braced myself, and remembered my preparation. I duly interspersed my conversation with such drivel as "handsome mage" and "studmuffin." Fortunately, I had taken a digestive aid before I hit the field, so to speak, and was able to control my incipient nausea.

It was shortly after the exchange of these ridiculous name callings that the subject leaned over a whispered a suggestion in my ear. He wished us to find a place to be alone. I realized suddenly that I hadn't prepared myself properly for the next stage. In my haste to check out my theory, and in my obviously faulty assumption that things would progress in a more leisurely fashion, I had neglected to finish the book. I hastily excused myself, and hurried off to the nearest nook to read.

I was halfway through the sixth chapter, "Wild Thing in the Karanas" when I lost all objectivity. The suggestions Ms D'Tart put forth were not only crude and tasteless, but beyond my ability to comprehend, much less mimic! The straightforward act of reproduction so tastefully put forth in my parent's work had been reduced to smut. And it was illustrated! Indeed, I was so mortified at what I now realized my subject had in mind, that I marched right back, and slapped him. He was understandably baffled as I stormed back to my laboratory. All semblance of scientific method had disappeared at this point.

Later, in the lab, when I had composed myself over a nice hot cup of tea, I realized that I had been most naïve to have taken such a study onto myself. I had done my subject, myself and the entire magic community a disservice by rushing into a study I was obviously not professional or detached enough to do effectively.

I hereby apologize to my peers and betters for my unforgivable haste, and humbly suggest that someone more objective and much more experienced in these matters continue these studies. Although I have bungled most awfully, I have not lost my conviction that this is a subject that should be explored further. If not for the sake of knowledge alone, let it be then for the future of Norrath. It is my belief that if this behavior is left unchecked, we will shall all find ourselves awash in the unplanned progeny of thousands.

As for myself...suffice it to say, the family line ends here. I will leave my work for posterity rather than offspring. I obviously have not my parents composure or courage. I am, however, more respectful than ever of their commitment to the greater good, and more determined than ever that their sacrifice not be in vain.

Respectfully submitted for your review on this, the 1st of April, 3178. (Nebula)
