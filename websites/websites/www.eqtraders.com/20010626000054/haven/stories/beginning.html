In the Beginning ... a Halfling's View

In the beginning, there was the Void, and a tall one came unto it and claimed it as his. And this Lord of the Void allowed his kneecaps to be hugged, and did not smite the halfling for calling him "lad", and the halfling was happy.

The halfling drew friends about her and spun them tales of a new alternate world (server) that would be named after the tall one, and a dream was formed. And the halfling was happy.

But the new world never materialized, as the other gods decided that the Void needed nothing. And the halfling was unhappy.

Then the halfling heard of another alternate world, and another dream of friendship, and the halfling conspired with a tall barbarian to turn it into a shared dream across two worlds. There was much wiggling of toes, and the Spiritwalkers were born. And the halfling was happy.

In shared happiness, the halfling and her friends threw their doors and hearts open, and invited others into the fold too soon, and not all was happiness and fellowship. And the halfling was unhappy.

Then the halfling saw that the bigger, more active world (Tunare) had room in their hearts for refugees who wanted to share fun times with good friends. And the halfing, heart breaking, mentioned this other land, and promised aid to any who joined in an exodus to that land. Much was her surprise and deep love for those precious ones who gave up all for a fresh start in the new land. And though they were all one big family, she still thought of this group as "hers", and held responsibility for their happiness tightly in her heart and soul. She also adopted one of the ones that had helped found both guilds as "hers", chuckling at him pulling Klaknaks out of his hat on the world of Veeshan, and grinning at his antics as the first barbarian ranger on the world of Tunare. And the halfling was happy.

Many good times followed, and the refugees were joined by a couple other hardy souls who had relocated from other worlds to share in the Spiritwalker dream. A wee gnomish mage who'd had many troubled times, and had left the Spiritwalker family not once, but twice, also bonded strongly with this hardy band. And the halfling was happy.

Troubled times followed, however, as words, thoughts and deeds went awry, and these refugees found themselves once again in the midst of strife, and once again wishing they had a quiet haven away from it all. And the halfling was unhappy. Many friends were hurt, angered, upset, and while she had ties to the remaining Spiritwalkers she did not want to sever, she could not bear to see her friends hurting. And the halfling was very unhappy.

At about this same time (the halfling still suspects Bristlebane's involvement), the tall one, his royal Voidishness, became mortal. No longer did he have his Voidish powers, though mighty he still was. And the halfling was unhappy.

Being a motherly sort, and having not only a possessive redheaded streak from goodness-knows-where, but also a strong nesting instinct, the halfling finally stopped running about in circles and started grabbing those cherished refugees. And the halfling was glad indeed when those refugees grabbed back and held on for dear life.

And the halfling said, "Come, hide in this haven, leave politics at the door, we all have a vote, and while I may be the Denmother, I'm only your equal and your friend." And so the Voidrunners were born. And the halfling was exhausted, but very happy indeed.
