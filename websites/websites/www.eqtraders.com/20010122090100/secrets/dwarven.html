Dwarven Cultural Smithing

Many of these recipes are as-yet unverified. Please make sure to send all confirmations/corrections to the DenMother. Note that these recipes are ONLY to be tried by dwarven smiths, and they MUST be combined using your racial Dwarven forge unless otherwise noted.

At the current time, incorrect recipes mixed in a cultural forge will NOT eat your items. Close the forge, open the forge, and your items still should be there. Failed skill checks will, of course, still eat your items, just like with any other trade skill.

dwarven plate

appropriate small plate sectional mold, dwarven smithy hammer, earthen temper, leather padding, brellium chain jointing, and appropriate number of folded sheets of brellium:

  • Visors, collars, bracers, and boots - 1 folded sheet of brellium.
  • Helms, pauldrons, girdles, vambraces, and gauntlets - 2 folded sheets of brellium.
  • Breastplates, cloaks, and greaves - 3 folded sheets of brellium.

imbued enchanted dwarven plate

appropriate small plate sectional mold, dwarven smithy hammer, earthen temper, leather padding, enchanted brellium chain jointing, imbued ruby, and appropriate number of enchanted folded sheets of brellium:

  • Visors, collars, bracers, and boots - 1 folded sheet of brellium.
  • Helms, pauldrons, girdles, vambraces, and gauntlets - 2 folded sheets of brellium.
  • Breastplates, cloaks, and greaves - 3 folded sheets of brellium.
(enchanted brellium is made by an enchanter using the level 49 enchant brellium spell on large bricks of brellium. A rare drop jacinth is used as the material component. Enchanted brellium bricks are then worked into chain jointing and folded sheets based on the recipes provided above)
