Niami DenMother - halfling cleric

 

Good day tae ye. I be Niami, a wee halfing Priestess o' Mischief from Rivervale.

Nay, nay! Naught the troublemaking kind of mischief! I be one o' those who relish curiousity an' laughter, an' try tae find some bright side tae any adversity.

My curious questions tae th' folks from other lands may betimes get me in a wee spot of hot water, but I don't mean tae be rude when I ask th' questions, only tae learn. (What IS in that water up in Halas that makes them grow sae tall??)

According tae me family, th' spirits o' th' ancestors be strong in me. My parents always thought they spoke tae strongly within me, but sometimes th' spirit guidance is much needed. "Ye've got yuir Great Uncle Rupert in ye, lassie, tae be wandering aboot like that instead of sitting at home an' minding yuir lessons like a proper lass!" (This is not tae be confused wi' cousin Rupert, mentioned below, though it does seem tae be an ill-fated name in my family!)

Ah, but Great Uncle Rupert was a great seeker of knowledge, and like he, I, strongly believe there are answers to everything ... somewhere.

"What in tarnation's name are ye doing helping that elf?! Just like yuir Aunt Mabel, helping outsiders as if they be family!" An' if I do nae help, who will? If I do nae show that th' same spirits move within us all, an' help a stranger, who will help me, or my friends, when we are lost in a strange land?

I may not agree with th' dark paths that some have chosen, but mayhaps a bit o' my kindness will rub off on them, enow that they may some day see their way tae a less destructive path.

"Ye didn't get any o' that prayer mumbo jumbo from me - ye must hae gotten it from yuir mother's side of the family." The god of Mischief might be a perverse god at times, but there's nae denying th' extra strength I gain through proper prayers an' spells. I share gladly o' that strength an' knowledge, an' ha'e been known tae die, repeatedly, by trying tae heal and/or otherwise save someone in trouble. Ne'er mind that th' person, an' the creature they are fighting, be significantly more seasoned than I, if they are in trouble, an' I am able, impulse an' me upbringing, tend tae lead me intae adversity more often than not.

Now that my parents hae been killed by those evil orcs, I hope that their spirits, as well, speak within me, for I miss them greatly an' could use their pithy comments tae remind me o' why I don't want tae sit at home an' become like them. I want tae learn all I can learn, see all I can see, bring a bit o' mischief an' laughter intae the lands outside Rivervale ... an' I want tae take this meat tenderizer o' mine and turn every kodiak I can intae bear meat, sandwiches an' backpacks. That's nae tae much tae ask, is it?

E'er since I was a young lass o' forty winters old, I hae taken several young ones under my wing, including Jarno, son of my lamented Cousin Rupert (eaten by foul carrion spiders -- och!). This willingness tae take on foundlings, as well as me overally motherly nature has earned me the nickname "Den Mother."
