05/08/01
Patch Day! Most folks know the drill - it's going to take time to process everything that went in during this patch to see what affects us. Please be sure to check message board threads before you start yet another totally new thread on a topic that's covered in 6 other places - it makes it really hard for folks to follow what's going on with all the repetition. :) The below information will be updated later today, once we know for sure how things are going on the live servers.

Here's what I know about today's patch and how it will affect tradesfolk:

  • Fixed a problem making it hard to loot really large NPCs - if this works as stated, it should make it easier to loot Terrorantula for silk.
  • Quivers now reduce the delay time on bows - the details are VERY vague on this, and I'm sure everyone will have their own pet theories or ideas on how this "should" work in the forums. Only time will tell how significant a change this truly is.
  • Not only the party, but the person looting, will now see a text message saying what the person looted - at the least, this will make those folks hunting cat pelts in the Karanas happier, as you can tell which quality pelt you looted without having to peer at each pelt as you're looting.
  • You can move items in and out of containers in your bank without having to remove the containers from the bank - a great boon to space-impaired tradesfolk!

Other things not in the patch message that are expected in this patch. (We won't be able to check any of it out until things are up and running again, of course)

  • "Some" new tailoring recipes. No word yet on what form these will take, but this message will be updated accordingly as we know more.
  • New baking recipes, mainly for the seafood lovers amongst us. Unfortunately, the text for these recipes didn't make it in this patch, though, so unless we succeed with some creative experiments, we may not actually see these until the next patch.
  • Several fixes were made to bugged recipes (enchanted adamantite chain, enchanted/imbued adamantite leggings, stats on the velium hound moccasins, etc.), but we don't know if they managed to get added to today's patch, or if they're waiting on the next patch.

I've also added a lovely guide for all the non-tailors out there who want some of the Velious tailored armor, giving information on the relatively difficulty of obtaining the needed items for your local tailor. 04/17/01 (Updated)
Patch Day! It's that time again, and there are several known tradeskill changes in it:

  • Durations on food items have changed. We've got a thread on the baking message boards tracking meal/snack status on various goodies. Some foods also have stat bonuses listed, however, these bonuses are NOT actually showing up when you right-click to eat the food.
  • Ovens have been added to Grobb. I'll get info on where, and put it on the proper Trade Supplies pages.
  • Buying stacks of items off merchants has been implemented. This, of course, only works if the item is stackable. Also, merchants have improved their inventory retention program, and fully count stacks of items that are sold to them, so that they can sell all the items back to someone else. (Before, if you sold a stack of items to a merchant, the merchant paid for them all, but would then only think he/she had one of the item to sell back.)
  • World (stationary) trade containers have been fixed. This should fix the crash to desktop problem folks were having on a combine, and solve the confusion generated when folks thought their ingredients were disappearing after an incorrect combine (from a wrong recipe). This likely does NOT address the tailoring issues we're seeing, and once the dust has settled, we'll need to start getting in feedback if the DNC problems are still continuing for portable trade containers such as sewing kits.
  • Cooking spits have had their weight increased to 3. Contrary to the patch message, skewers are still weight 0.1
  • Last, but definitely not least, enchanting metals for cultural smithing no longer requires gems during the enchanting process. This is a great boon to cultural smiths, and should allow us to experiment a bit more easily with some of the enchanted armors and weapons. There's already been much rejoicing over the news that this was on Test, and I expect we'll see a lot of the "gaps" in our enchanted cultural armor stats information filled in over the next week or two.
