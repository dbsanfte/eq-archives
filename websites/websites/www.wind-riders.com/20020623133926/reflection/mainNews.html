Policy Review & Loot Guidelines
• 3/05/02
OOC: Guidelines are effective immediately, and subject to revision at my whims.
  1. Use common sense, in a courteous fashion.
  2. All EverQuest rules (play-nice policy, etc) apply to us.
  3. Do not let problems go unsolved, or allow resentment to grow before solving them.
  4. Do not confront negative issues with individuals in public areas.
  5. Do not let greed, pride, or resolved issues from the past alter your good judgement.
  6. Do not pass judgement on someone without knowing all facts from all sides.
  7. Do not spread ooc rumors.
  8. Do not force anyone into situations they feel uncomfortable with.
  9. Do not hesitate to let someone know you’re uncomfortable with something.
  10. Promote roleplay even outside the guild.
  11. Label out of character comments in every channel except those denoted as ooc.
  12. Do not use “ooc” excessively in roleplay situations/channels.
  13. Do not reveal others’ alternate characters at all, and try not to reveal yours in public.
  14. Do not pressure anyone to reveal ooc information about themselves.
  15. Do not let other people play your characters. Some household situations may void this.
    a. Actions taken while someone else plays your character will be counted as yours.
  16. Do not expect problems to be solved by Officers/Leaders without direct information.
    a. Anonymous parties need to speak for themselves.
    b. Second-Hand information is regarded as rumor.
  17. Guild sponsored hunts and raids are to be In Character, and have at least a base storyline.
Loot Policies
Notice that these guidelines are what the WR/SS guilds deem official, for guild sponsored raids and hunts. Members may declare their own raids with their own guidelines, so long as everyone understands that they are not guild sponsored. Keep in mind that unsponsored raids held by our members should have reasonable rules, or risk being reprimanded. Officers however, will be expected (as primary ambassadors for their guild) that raids they call do follow the Guilds’ guidelines.

Normal group/raid situations:
Need before Greed: Items are given to those who can use/equip them. If more than one character can use it, they /random between themselves. Note: Alternate characters must be present and directly aiding the situation to qualify. If it can’t be used and everyone agrees, the item can be given through /random to anyone helping.
An example of this would be a Messenger of the Queen from Crystal Caverns.

Special focus “Common goal” situations:
If several members in a specialized raid (or raid series) need the same item, then they have equal chance to acquire it in a /random. This is taking into account that they’ve already discussed who’s qualified to earn one in such a raid, and have devoted to helping the rest get one after they’ve already won theirs. This does go case-by-case, and does include friends outside our guilds who wish to participate (so long as participants agree with our rules). Sideline loot follows normal policies.
An example of this would be a Jade Reaver in the City of Mist.

Triggered battle items:
No-brainer. Whoever put the effort into the triggered quest battle will get what they worked for. If they’re doing it for someone else, that someone gets the outcome.
An example of this would be the Tome from the Dark Elven Corruptor in East Karana.
