The Arena
Sugan's Account

...I gathered Kinor, my champion, and Samasa, my healer and left for the Arena. Right outside by the campfire was Faileas, (who came to ensure I walked away safely, as per family respect) with his follower, Vuer, and Silverann. I was also surprised to see Serenade... Upon entering the arena, we were met by followers of the Demon who warned us away. Although unsettling, we had not yet heard from Malgorm himself, and so continued on. The Disciples raised swords to us at first, so we retreated... but then Malgorm spoke from the throne, ordering them to escort us to him. The group of us took a great risk by doing so, for we spotted many of Malgorm's men placed in the distance.

The demon stood before the seat of honor in the arena, and glared at us as he spouted his sermon of hate and disease. My brother traded words with him, as they had differences of oppinion lately on the path of their god, Bertoxxulous. Faileas claimed Malgorm was tainted with the hate of Innoruuk's teachings, and that his vision of mass death was contrary to what the plaguebringer desired. Malgorm almost gleefully agreed, claiming a newfound hate and respect for the god, Innoruuk.

I of course was sickened by such a discussion, but with such odds there was little chance my words would be heard. Then the demon went on to tell a little of what he found in the texts of the Book of Azzothoth. This book apparently contained much of the history of House Imshee'Cro, as well as chronicles of the curse and the demon Azzothoth himself. (So far, I've been rather unsuccessful in finding peices of this book myself, or stopping Malgorm from finding it.) He has two of the three parts... and told us that they revealed that He was the original bearer of the curse. If this is true, it means that once, long ago, Malgorm was a man who made an impossible bargain to resurrect his dead love, and ended up losing his soul and cursing the family. Our ancestor.

We didn't have much time to let the shock sink in however, because the Demon ordered his minions to attack us, only raising a sword to Faileas if he chose to fight them. Of course Faileas did in my defense, so the Wind Riders and Shadow Striders for once battled on the same side. I was lucky enough to escape the melee, but noticed my companions were not with me. Faileas was staggering out of the place with barely a tinge of life, and near him one of the Disciples started chanting into a spell I new would be his death. I caught my brother from falling, and with a quick prayer to Karana, summoned spirit to restore his health fully. Seeing that I intended to go back for Kinor and Samasa, Faileas pushed me away, and went in for them himself. By this time Wind Riders were gathering near the doors, awaiting orders and daring anyone to threaten me there. The fighting stopped.

With an amused tone, the Demon demanded that I surrender or Kinor and Faileas would die. The two of them stood rooted in place by magicks. Kinor was seething with barbaric rage, cursing Malgorm with every breath. Faileas stood with darkened eyes, calmly and coldly scheming his vengeance. I felt my blood turn to ice at the thought of surrender, but there was no other way. The two of them were escorted past me as I walked once again to the throne of blood. Then Malgorm stopped with a sudden idea, and called for Faileas to come and face me in battle... to redeem himself with my death. Faileas refused in no uncertain terms, then suddenly Serenade appeared beside me. I begged him to go, but his reply was lost in the chaos when the demon ordered us all to die.

Serenade and I fought them off as best we could, as he tried to help me out of there. What happened after this is recollections of others, because I lost all strength soon after. I remember feeling the sting of many spells, and fighting off the magick that tried to bind me to the ground. It seems I was knocked unconcious when mysteriously Malgorm had a change of plan. He blinked beside us, picked me up, and teleported from the battle. As I had disappeared, the Wind Riders standing nearby assumed I was dead when I fell, and attacked immediately. Events in the bloodsoaked arena after that are unclear. I awoke in North Karana to see Malgorm smug with amusement, but also some shock. I'm wishing now that I'd had more life in me, so I could have found out why. I do not know why my life was spared.

Perhaps the Demon found a reason for me to live. Perhaps it was that he wanted to take it himself, but didn't get a chance... As Silverann showed up soon after. There were some words exchanged, and I was beginning to make the Demon angry with the hope that he could be saved. Seemingly out of nowhere Kinor flew into Malgorm, and they battled. Someone helped me escape. I would have given anything now to have taken Kinor's place. He saved my life by giving his own... Malgorm killed him, but was weakened enough from the fight that he had to disappear.

We gathered around Kinor's body in fear, for I'd heard his spirit say the Tribunal called for him. If that was so, he could not be resurrected by the powers of a cleric. Samasa fell pale at his voice too, and there was much sadness. His father Clanaugh was also there when Kinor's spirit presented itself before us, and said his goodbyes. He asked Samasa to move on, and told us not to lose hope, for he had a purpose where he was. With faith that someday maybe he would return, we all bade Kinor a sad farewell.
