Spiritwalkers Guild Member Bio

Name: Sordaor
Race: Barbarian
Profession: Shaman of Justice
Likes: Long cold northern winters, The smell of the battlefield
Dislikes: Gnolls, Razor Gills, Those who know not the difference between right and wrong.
Quirks: Will sit for days listening to the sound of silence.

Hail and well-met friends.

I am Sordaor, the only son of Tordaor and Shalla, merchants of Halas. As a child, I had loving parents. I lived a typical life for a young Barbarian growing up in Halas. It wasn't until my seventh winter that something of note happened in my life.

It was early spring and father decided to bring me along on a quick trading mission to Qeynos. I suspect mother put him up to it so she could have a break from my constant chatter. Father and I left Halas before dawn, bringing with us my fathers employee Trell and a couple of hired guards, to ensure that the trip through Blackburrow was uneventful. They would wait for us in the hills until our return from Qeynos.

A few hours out of Blackburrow, father and I came upon a small gnoll camp. If not for my presence, father would have done battle. (He had become a fair hand with a blade after many years of trading through out Antonica and beyond.) The gnolls, for their part, were reluctant to start a battle they had little chance of winning, however, they were quick to satisfy their greed. They offered father two bolts of silk and a couple worthless baubles. (The only reason my father would ever make a trade with those hated gnolls was to avoid the chance of losing his only son.)

We made for Qeynos with all haste to inform the guard of our encounter. It was here that the treachery began. My father, not wanting to fuel a greedy faction of the Qeynos Trade Coalition, failed to mention that a trade had taken place in the gnoll encounter. (A crime that would have been forgiven, given the circumstances, in a just city.) Good and evil have always been hard to distinguish among humans. In my eyes, father had told a lie, the first untruth I had ever heard my father speak.

After making preparations for the night, I informed father of my feelings. I then sat quietly (a great accomplishment for me) by the fire and listened as he explained the politics involved in what he did. Also, he conveyed to me the anguish he felt for having to omit part of the story. Our father son talk was only momentarily disturbed by the sound of a scurrying rat, and in the end, I forgave him. However, we both knew things had changed between us. Something always changes when a son discovers for the first time that his father isn't perfect.

It was less than an hour after our talk that the guards came. It seems the Qeynos Trade Coalition had leveled charges concerning my father's trade with the gnolls. I knew then that the rat we had heard earlier was of the human kind.

The trial was held that very night. I can still see the glee in the faces of the traders who brought the charges. They knew the outcome of the trial before it began, thanks to a few well-placed platinum I'm sure. Father, too honorable to lie under direct questioning, could do nothing but make the admission that would lead to his death.

I think the traders would not have gloated as they did had they known that in two years time a young Barbarian of nine winters would visit their homes and silently end there lives. The boy Sor died the night he saw his father hang. After placing my father on his funeral pyre, Sordaor arose from the ashes. I stopped talking that night, except to tell Trell and then mother, of what had happened.

It wasn't until the night I returned from killing the merchant that I broke my silence. I had just entered the peaks from BB when I heard my fathers' voice being carried in the wind. "Justice has been served my son." And then I felt the power explode within me. My third birth. I ran then, toward my beloved Halas. The peaks passed in a blur and when I reached the Halas caverns I did not wait for the Gwenavyne. The water still held some winter ice but I did not feel the chill. I walked from the docks to my mother's home; "I have wrought justice for father." was all I said. It was enough.

After I had learned to control my new power. I sought out my friend Calamon. He had joined the Spiritwalkers during the years after my father's death, when I sat quietly in the peaks. From him, I had learned much of the Spiritwalkers. He had told me I would be a welcomed member when I was ready. I had just been born again and unlike my previous rebirth I now needed a family to grow and survive.

I still speak but very little. I do, however, see and hear all that is around me. Some times I even hear my father speaking to me on the wind. I am Sordaor. If you seek justice, take heart I will arrive. If you are a fugitive from justice, your death will be the sign of my arrival.
