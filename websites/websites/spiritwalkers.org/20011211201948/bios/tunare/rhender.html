   It isn't everyday that one comes face to face with death. Or perhaps rather, it is that one faces death daily, but rarely is the event so personal.

   The day in question started like any other. Meditation exercises, the ritual gathering of inner power, peeking through the aether to find the exact spot in front of your nose. In short, nothing new or even exciting for a young wizard in training. Routine, even dull. Until IT happened.

   Like a silent footfall it came, the event. With no warning, no fanfare. No quarter asked, and none given. Just quiet normalcy one moment and a shattered, twisted reality the next. No one could have predicted such a thing, no one could have wished it upon his or her foe, and no remedy could be applied. The mathematics of wizardry compute the likelihood of such an event at so small a probability as to be an unthinkable occurrence.

   The aether ripped open, flooding the small compartment with power such as wizards of the mightiest sort might see once in their lives. Blinding, twisting waves of sheer raw power flooded through the small aperture in to the world, flowing like streams of lighting, or perhaps liquid light, tearing and warping everything they touched. Waves of force shredded the walls, rent asunder the roof, and scorched the ancient stone floor, and vaporized everything within sight. Everything that is, save the young aspirant. The young man was largely unharmed, if one could discount the singed flesh, the complete lack of body-hair, and his sudden nakedness.

   "Oops!", he said under his breath. "Guess I shouldn't try opening a teleport-portal just yet."

   And so it was that Rhender left the apprenticeship of his master, and went to seek his fame in the world.
