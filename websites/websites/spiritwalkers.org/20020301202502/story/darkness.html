Introduction
by Mortekai Spiritwalker

"Listen to me now my brethren, for I tell you grave news. Long ago, in ages dark and less heroic than our own daemons sometimes walked the face of Norrath in the service of Innoruk. One such foul creature was Drach'neyn a daemon skilled in the arts arcane, and the powers of Evil. Long ago this foul creature was imprisoned in a band of rune-encrusted metal, and hidden away from the eyes of men.

But such a thing is a device of power, and in the world of Norrath, power can not long lay dormant. A mystic of the Ice Goblin tribes fashioned a Circlet of Power to harness the daemon's powers. A mighty foe was this goblin, possessed as he was by the spirit of the daemon, but no match for a hardy band of Spiritwalkers lead by myself, Mortekai. The goblin defeated, the Circlet indestructible, I wrapped the circlet in mighty spells and bound guardian spirits to it. The Circlet has adorned my own head since the defeat of the daemon-possessed goblin. Until now.

Power is a terrible thing, and when it belongs to a daemon, it is a terror to behold. Even the guardian spirits could not prevent the mighty daemon from tempting those with darkness in their souls.

And so it comes to pass that one amongst us has fallen prey to the daemon's whiles. Whilst I was away, the Circlet was held in trust for me by my niece. It was then that the foul creature trapped within reached out to her, and somehow showed her the path to loosen its bonds. I fear that her soul is in grave danger, for she has stolen the circlet from me, and escaped with it.

The Circlet must be recovered. We must not allow the daemon Drach'neyn to consume the soul of my niece and wear her flesh. Aid me now, Spiritwalkers for without your aid, I fear all is lost.

-- Unkle Mortekai Spiritwalker

The First to Fall

By Ruldari

A bitter taste still welled up in her mouth as she contemplated her earlier defeat by Morrighan, even though more skill folk than she had since fallen to the demon-possessed necromancer who was soon to become her sister-in-law. It had been a long time since she'd felt so helpless, so ... incompetent.

The day had started out normally enough, with another spider-slaughter in East Karana. Joril wanted to improve his tailoring skill, and help the new members of the guild, and she was determined to do her share of silk collection for him.

Then she'd heard Morrighan gloating over killing Narissa Clothspinner, proclaiming that the woman had provided no challenge, and that she hoped her sister was more of a challenge. Now, Morri had always tended to have a strong sense of macabre humor, but this was not a laughing matter, and it seemed that Morri was not joking. She'd started killing merchants and bragging about it!

Ru didn't know what was wrong with her future sister-in-law, but someone had to talk to her and find out what was wrong, and all Morri's relatives were not around at the time. With a worried frown on her face, she'd set out across the Karanas as fast as she could run. Along the way, she got to listen to Morri spew all sorts of nasty commentary about evil and darkness, hinting that Ru, Unkle and others were "weak" and so forth. This was not the Morri she knew at all ... which made her more worried, and run faster. She had to speak with her face-to-face and find out what was wrong.

As she arrived in Qeynos, she remembered that she'd had some rawhide shoulderpads for one of their younger members, a paladin lass by the name of Ellen, so she sought her out to drop them off, and gave her a few buffs.

Then Morri appeared, and Ru found out just how weak she was. She tried to keep her tone concerned, but not judgemental, but she was distracted by the red glow emanating from Morri's eyes. This was not good.

Then Morri stepped back and started hitting her with spells, while her skeleton pet closed in, hitting and stunning her repeatedly. And stunned she was, shocked that Morrighan would attack another Spiritwalker like that, ANY Spiritwalker, much less one that was practically family. She was half-dead before she gathered her wits enough to fight back, trying to defend herself from the relentless blows of the pet. It wasn't until near the very end that she could even bring herself to fling a burst of flame in Morrighan's direction, so rattled was she by the attack.

Somewhere during the whole attack she remembered telling Ellen to back up, to hide, and then all she remembered was blood and pain. Her blood. Her pain.

She was easily defeated.

Perhaps she was weak, as Morrighan claimed ... all she knew was that when she later found out that a demon had been controlling Morri's actions, it had been small comfort indeed ... she felt weak.

Things got worse from there, as guildmembers streamed in from all over the globe, including one young lass all the way from Felwithe. Aleayha, scouting the sewers beneath Qeynos, was the next to fall to the murderous demon. Gyro, the bouncing gnome, stopped bouncing as his heart almost stopped beating shortly thereafter. The mighty Mortekai, goaded beyond belief at being blamed for sending Joril and Sanu's parents to their deaths, was also handily defeated ... and Ru could do nothing. Poor young Ellen was stalked for a long time, and struck down even as Ru tried to heal her.

After each attack, Morri would either gate, or gather the dark shadows around her ... and Ru could do nothing.

She had not felt so helpless, so useless, since the day her mother died.

Ellen's Testimonial

When Ruldari told me she was going to be stopping by to drop some things off in Qeynos, I was delighted. We hadn't had a chance to talk much since I'd become a Spiritwalker, and I was looking forward to a nice bit of a chat. When she arrived however, she seemed distracted and tense. She told me that she needed to speak to Morrighan before she would be free to visit, and asked if I would mind accompanying her to rather private alcove outside the town walls. She had asked Morrighan to meet her there, and I got the impression that she was a bit nervous about going alone...although how I could be of any help if things got rough was anyone's guess! Still, I agreed to tag along, although not without reservations.

Indeed, those reservations kept me far behind Ru as she boldly confronted Morrighan. I wasn't really sure what was going on, although I had heard rumours about some kind of problems with Morrighan's behaviour and attitudes. I missed most of the earlier conversation, as I was pressed up against the stone walls..but as I grew more brave, I began to move closer to them.

Just as I was able to catch a word or two, I heard Ru scream and saw Morrighan's Pet striking her. I realized that Ru had been rooted, and was unable to even run away. I stood, immobile, in a total state of shock for what seemed like forever, but turned out later to be less than a minute. That was all the time Morrighan needed however. She stood behind the vile undead creature she had summoned, and watched as Ru fell immobile to the ground.

By this time I had gathered what few wits I had left, and began screaming at Morrighan, pegging her as the vile coward and murderess that she had just exhibited herself to be...but she ignored me and continued on her way. I swore to her that I would bear witness to the horrible act she had commited. This account is that promised testimony. I stayed with Ru's body until she was able to bring her soul back into it. She expressed deep confusion and unhappiness and set out to find explanations and help.

Ru returned in the afternoon with several of the elders, and an attempt was made to pull the murderess Morrighan out of her guild, in the Qeynos sewers. It was a total and unmitigated failure, ending with more mayhem, which Ru has detailed in her own chronicle, and with Morrighan still free to hurt others. It was during this time that the group learned of the existance of a circlet, possessed of a demon, which it seems Morrighan had been given custody of. This circlet was deemed the cause of Morrighan's present behavior. I am having trouble with this, having never met Morrighan before these unfortunate events...but I am trying as best I can to imagine Morrighan as the decent person others say she was before.

Later that same day, after the sun had fallen, I found myself in the Hills of Qeynos, hunting with Coreena. I was busy looting a giant rat when I looked up and saw what I took to be a decaying skeleton crossing the field. Peering closer, I realized it was Morrighan's pet. Morrighan was in the field with me! I am ashamed to say I panicked, and screamed for Coreena in guildspeak, thus calling attention to myself. Morrighan began shouting, baiting me to "come out and play" as she put it. I began running to Surefall Glade. I was able to hide in the entrance of Surefall for a bit, but since it was nightfall, and I am without a lightstone, I was unable to actually enter the Glade.

Coreena stayed hidden by the entrance, looking for Morrighan. After a few sightings and false reports, she deemed it safe for me to come out, and we hurried down the road together to Qeynos. About halfway there, Morrighan began to chase us, cackling with delight at my fear and loathing of her. She used her arts to follow me invisibly and to whisper taunts into my ear as I was scurrying down the path. I managed to make it to the gates, and hid hastily in the right hand guard tower, behind the door, my heart pounding in fear as I crouched shivering there.

I heard her come through the gate, and enter the left tower. I thought I would faint, waiting for her to come and search the area I was in, but the Gods of good were watching over me, and she passed me by, choosing to search the city instead. After I heard her footsteps receding, I ran out the gates once more, and hid by the very place Ru had been downed earlier. Coreena had called for help as we were running. Joril and Ru, who were hunting in nearby BlackBurrow, heard her, and met me outside.

I was frightened when Morrighan started shouting for me again, but Ru answered. I had just started feeling safe and secure once more when Ru informed me that she intended to lay down her life again, rather than to have me lose mine. I was horrified...not only at the loss of honor that implied..but at the thought of Ru having to endure yet another death at the hand of that monster. Ru and Joril had made me welcome during my first days of being a Spiritwalker. Their generousity and kindness had overwhelmed me, and I thought of them as being two of the best folk I knew. I could not pay their kindness back with craven cowardice.

As I was trying to explain how I felt to them, Morrighan reappeared by the city wall. Before Joril or Ru could act, I dashed forward, shouting defiance. It was only a matter of minutes before I was soundly defeated. As ashamed as I am that I could not hold my own against Morrighan's evil...I know it is nothing to the shame I would have known had Ru or Joril taken my place. I have no regrets. There was no other action I could have lived with.

As for Morrighan...I know you all counsel forgiveness, having known her yourselves as another person....but in all honesty, I cannot say that forgiveness will come easily for me. I have known no Morrighan but this evil, dark thing that is making the lives of everyone I admire a nightmare. I believe that had she not the natural inclination to be what she has now become, she would have been able to resist the demon's power. She chose the dark arts...and I have seen no proof that those arts can be turned to the benefit of good in my (albeit short) lifetime. Know you this now...if she is returned to her "former self," I honestly doubt that I will ever approach her with anything but mistrust and wariness. I am sorry if this seems harsh..but she has left an impression on me that I doubt will wear away in any short period of time.

This then is my testimony of these events, put forth as honestly and fully as I know how.

How to Become Daemon-bait in Eight Easy Steps

by Shavanna

  1. Decide to fish while the possessed Morrighan is in-zone but quiet
  2. Tell your guild that you're fishing to unwind. This is a sure way to get any daemon licking it's virtual chops

  8. For the added frustration of your friends, die JUST as a guildmate runs up to aid you.

Beginning Journeys

by Joril

Dawn broke with angry red splashes across the sky. In the distance, dark clouds loomed. An ominous feeling hung in the air. A feeling shared by a tall figure clad in banded mail and pieces of the famed Crafted Armor of the Centaurs of South Karanas.

He stepped away from the railing of the ship, drawing his immense blade from its sheath. Sailors scurried away from his dark scowl, fearful of further upsetting this brooding (obviously) warrior.

Joril had not felt such anger and bitterness in a long time.

How could his Unkle, a man whom he always considered wise and knowledgeable, be so…unwise? How could he have given an item that was the prison of a being of immense power and darkness to one who practices the dark arts?

He battled within himself, with his feelings of hurt, anger and betrayal.

His sword flashed brilliant silver as he spun about. His mind going over the events of the past few days.

His foster sister was possessed by a daemon.

How could his unkle have given the object that served as its prison to Morrighan? How could he have thought that she, a practitioner of the dark arts, NOT be tempted by such a thing of power? And how could he have shown so little faith and trust in his own nephew?

Joril's visage grew darker, matching the sky and clouds of the oncoming storm. He swung his blade even faster, stepping up the tempo of his routine.

He thought of the deaths of his fellow guild members. Poor Ellen and Windias. Among the youngest and newest of the Spiritwalkers, their first meeting with Morrighan had to be so traumatic. And Gyro, that bouncing happy little gnome. A powerful summoner of elements who damn near came close to summoning no more.

So many dead and broken Spiritwalkers that day. And he had stood helpless as he watched Mortekai fall to the daemon. Joril was unable to lift a finger to help. And he had been standing RIGHT BEHIND the thing!

With a growl, he unknowingly split a wooden cask in two, spilling its contents onto the deck. He spun again; feinting an attack with his blade and following it with a massive leg in a kick that would have made a hill giant take notice.

His thoughts then turned to his beloved and without knowing, he snarled the most viscous snarl, so full of anger, that a plains cat would have sounded like a mere house kitten. He reversed his blade and plunged it deep into the deck piling.

Joril stood, breathing heavily for a moment, eyes blank and unseeing. A soft scurrying noise broke his trance. He leapt to the side, snatching his sword and then dropped spinning to the deck, his sword coming up at the ready.

The poor old cook almost dropped the platter of food that he had brought for the large warrior. His heart stopped cold and then his face began to turn red as he suddenly realized what that wet warm feeling on his leg meant. Shamefully he hurriedly put the platter on a cask and scampered away.

Joril watched him retreat to his galley. His face began to turn red as well as he realized the scare he gave the kindly man. He stood and sheathed his sword then picked up the tray of food and went below to the cabin.

He quietly closed the door and set the platter down on the small table. He then turned and gently sat on the bed, gazing with sorrow and love at the bed's occupant.

He knew the scars she would carry for a long time. Gently he stroked Ruldari's hair. She had been among those that the daemon had defeated that day. And he had been unable to help her as well. Self-doubt welled up within him.

He was glad that she was with him on this journey. Perhaps this time he could do a better job protecting her. And perhaps she could help him battle his own daemons within. He knew not what the final outcome of this journey would be like but he knew he had to do this.

For his sister Morrighan.

For his beloved Ruldari.

For the good of the Spiritwalkers.

And most of all, for himself.

He, along with his beloved, was returning to Crushbone.

The Gathering Gloom

by Mortekai Spiritwalker

A fetid wind blew across the low ground, and the charnel smell of ancient tombs rose in wisps to battle the breeze. The sky was filled with the reddening of the setting sun, and a middle-aged Northman stood over the broken body of a young redheaded woman.

The corpse was desiccated, as if the very life and fluid had been blasted out of it by intense heat. There was no beauty left in the cold, shriveled features. Her death had been most unkind.

"Drach'neyn!" the Northman howled in an anguished voice, his face turned to the sky, "This has gone far enough daemon! Your time is short! You will pay!"

His only answer is a barely audible chuckle carried by the dry, stench-filled air.

***

Mortekai weeps for his lost children, for the deaths of his friends, family and loved ones. But his face is stern, and his eyes filled with a resolution. Preparations need to be made, things gathered, and the timing carefully weighed. A ritual of powerful magic must be executed flawlessly if one is to destroy a daemon.

Powerful things, wicked things, and things of seemingly no power at all were laid before him on the table. A vial of Cyclops blood, several herbs, a knife, and the still beating heart of D'vinn, the beginnings of a collection of things that, the Tribunal willing, would spell the end of the Daemon.

Gathering his will, Mortekai reached deep into himself for the words and deeds that lead him to this pass. Only by facing these truths, by clearing his own soul of darkness would he have the strength to face his adversary.

***

Snow covered the ground, blood-littered snow. The signs of a large battle were evident, and the broken bodies of the slain were scattered all around. There lay the shattered body of his sister's only love Culbarth not far away. Edain herself lay in the finest furs on her funeral bower in Halas. As the only adult family of the two, it is Mortekai's job to bring the bodies of the fallen home for their final rest. It is a duty he does not relish.

In his grief, pain and despair, thoughts and deeds he will later regret take shape.

"I should have been here. I should have leant them my strength, such as it is. I should be here lying in the snow. I should be dead, not these. "

"I can help you," whispered a dry, sibilant voice in Mortkai's head. "I can give you the power to make things right."

"Can you return the dead to life?" Mortekai asked, his mental voice trembling with grief and a glimmering of hope.

"No, but I can make you strong. You can protect their children, you can do so much to make the world better. You can avenge their deaths."

"What must I do?" whispered Mortekai, a new look of grim determination spreading across his face.

***

A goblin wizard feels his strength drain his powers wane. This should not be! He can easily best this mere barbarian! But such is not the case. Mortekai strips the corpse of its' fetishes of power, and carefully wraps the circlet of rune-encrusted metal in soft leather.

"Excellent, Mortekai. Now what I have promised you is yours. Place me on your head, and let us begin our work!"

"Nae just yet. There is something I must do first. "

Mortekai sets his leather-wrapped package on the ground, and removes a piece of brightly colored chalk from his pouch. Other items follow, and are used to carefully scribe runes and symbols on the ground. Once they are complete, he carefully unwraps the circlet and sets it between the runes.

"What are you doing, Mortekai?" whispers the voice. "This is not what we discussed!"

"Neither was giving you my soul, Drach'neyn!" And the chants pour forth from his lips, the lights of the spirits called to help him swirl as the ritual binding begins.

"You will pay for this mortal! I will make you beg for death!" screams the daemon as its' powers and will are bound still further to it's mortal prison.

***

And so now I pay the cost of my pride, my weakness, and blindness. Now I must rescue the soul of Morrighan, destroy the daemon, and perhaps, just perhaps, finally right the wrongs of youth.

A Sister's Squabble, Part 1

by Sanu

Sanu's heart was troubled as she sat in a quiet part of Halas, gathering her thoughts for meditation. What she had witnessed this eve had struck her to the core. That something needed to be done, she was certain. But what remained the question. She was floundering with no data.

While traveling through West Karana on her way to Freeport on a few errands for the guild, she had finally come across Morrighan, her dark sister. She shuddered as the vision of the daemon's possession flashed across her vision. Shavanna, a young promising Shaman, was in battle with Morrighan. Shavanna hadn't stood a chance. Morrighan, or rather the daemon, had come upon Shavanna unawares while she was fishing and slew her before she could reach the weapon within her grasp. And then, the daemon had fled, before she could get a word in edgewise.

And this troubled Sanu. How to fight something that refused to engage in combat? And how to do it in such a manner as to return the Morrighan she knew? Or was Morrighan gone and only the daemon remained? Gone to serve whatever darkness she had bargained with? Her dark sister who she had come to trust in battle, who, through Unkle's teaching, had opened her eyes to the many ways of Justice? Another thought beat in the darkness, was this the Tribunal's intent? Was this the Tribunal's Justice for Morrighan selling her soul for her dark magicks? And if it was, could she interfere?

With a troubled and heavy heart, Sanu started her meditation exercises, to sooth the mind, calm it to allow her to hear the spirits. And when the roar of the spirits filled her ears, she let her spirit soar to join them, leaving her body, guarded by the spirits and the tradition to never disturb a meditating Shaman.

For a few moments, she reveled in the freedom of the spirits, but then, her mind turned quickly to the matter at hand. Morrighan. The daemon had fled her and she needed information. While she could not affect the living world in this form, she could talk to those still with the flesh. She sent her awareness to search for Morrighan. Memories of childhood arose in her mind. Memories of the day Unkle had returned home with her, a frightened child, hurt in body and soul. She remembered Unkle caring for her and she remembered one night seeing the spirits swirl around her, light and dark, warring. It was then that she knew why Unkle had brought her home to become part of the family, to provide what help he could in that battle, that light would win over dark just this once. Her heart filled with love for her Unkle, stern, demanding, but filled with so much love it almost hurt at times to Look. And she remembered when she started down this dark path. And Unkle, when he learned of it, not condemning her, but listening to her and way she chose it. And of Unkle's attempts to temper her darkness with light. At first, Sanu hadn't understood, but then as her powers grew and Unkle demanded that she look before judging as the Tribunal demands, she had understood. Darkness can serve Justice.

Her spirit soared and followed the line to Morrighan. She almost returned to her body at the sight of Evil in her dark sister. But her duties as a Shaman, her Unkle's teaching and her own need to know if her dark sister had truly turned kept her there.

"Daemon, we must meet," her spirit self said.

Morrighan looked up to see her spirit form wavering near by.

"You will not find me as easy as you have found our younger brethren, Daemon," Sanu said, remembering that Rhaine, a young bard had recently fallen, struck from behind with no chance of defense and had fallen before she could do anything.

The Daemon smiled a horrible smile, "I will remember your words as I stand over your fallen corpse. I like it much when prey is prepared and falls anyway."

"You will go too far, Daemon. You were bound once and will be again," Sanu said, struggling with her anger, her eyes flashing and going hard.

"I will be free until the sky itself falls," the Daemon said, an unholy light in its eyes.

"We shall see, Daemon. Justice will prevail," and with that, Sanu willed herself back to her body, before her anger got the better of her and she said something she would later regret. Sanu's eyes opened and slowly focused on the scenery around her. She watched for a few moments, her eyes following her fellow clansmen and visitors to Halas. Watching their normal activities. She wondered how many of them had the strength to look upon the Daemon as she had. And if they had, how many would keep their sanity.

That the evil residing in Morrighan's body was strong, Sanu had no doubts. But what do to about it? And how to do it in such as way as to spare Morrighan? Those were questions for which she had no answer.

Unkle, she finally decided, Unkle will know what to do. And with that decision made, decided to follow that up with a good night's sleep. Announcing her intentions to her guild mates, she prepared to camp for the night, also asking that they pass on the message to Unkle that she needed to talk to him. But even as she drifted off to sleep, secure in her decision to talk to Unkle came another question. How could Unkle have allowed this to happen?

A Sister's Squabble, Part 2

by Sanu

The day began as a usual day, her new friends and hunting companions were taking down some aviaks and had some undead hunting planned for the nighttime. All in all, a good day and good companions to hunt with. There was, however, a shadow upon her heart, Morrighan, her dark sister. She knew that the time of confrontation was soon. And with that thought in mind as she traveled to meet her companions, making sure of a swift return to the Qeynos area as Qeynos seemed to be the main hunting ground for the daemon.

Resting after a fight with a troublesome aviak, she let her mind roam, and she heard. Heard the call the spirits brought to her of a guild mate's plight. Ellen, the young bard, was being stalked by the daemon again.

Standing from her meditation, with barely enough power to send herself home, she excused herself from her hunting party, letting them know of her need to return. It touched her heart that none protested her leaving, though the hunting was easier with her powers and skill at healing. All gave their best wishes in confronting the daemon.

As she gathered the spirits to take her home, she sent a few off to reassure Ellen, help was on the way. As the spirits transported her to Everfrost, they also gave her images of Ellen's plight. That she was terrified was plain, trying to hide from the daemon that had so soundly defeated her a few days ago.

Arriving in Everfrost, Sanu meditated for strength, the trip having depleted the stores she had. But once done, she made the trip quickly to Qeynos, following the spirit's path. Querying the spirits again, she followed their path through the northern part of Qeynos and then to the south.

The trail was lost in the rush of people and she felt frustration, not wanting to search every room of Qeynos, needing to find Ellen quickly. The spirits answered, though not quite as she thought they would. Coreena, another young guild mate appeared, to show her the way. She smiled to herself as Coreena lead the way to Ellen, smiling at the strength and willingness of the young rogue to keep watch for the daemon and to warn Ellen of any danger.

To the large inn, Sanu was lead, through the maze of rooms until at last one was reached. She opened the door, slowly, only to be greeted with a shriek. Ellen clutched her chest as she sank to the bed, "You scared me!" she accused Sanu, even as she hugged Sanu.

Sanu smiled slightly as she returned the hug of the human bard, "You are safe now. I will not allow her to harm you."

Coreena nodded, "Yes, very safe. I'll watch outside"

To Coreena, Sanu warned, "Do not tempt Morri if she comes. Leave that to me."

Coreena nodded as she returned to her vigil.

"Thank the Gods you're here!" said Ellen, "What should I do?" Sanu could tell the bard was strained as she smiled a bit sickly, not the usual expression for one whose livelihood depends on entertaining others.

"Stay here, I will see if I can deal with Morri," a vague plan having formed in her mind on the run over.

Ellen relaxed a bit, "Can I stay with you?"

Sanu looked at Ellen, sensing her fear and the lessening she brought to her. While she preferred not to have anyone else around if her plan backfired, she saw that Ellen would be more upset and fearful if she requested her to leave.

"Yes," Sanu responded, "As long as you don't interfere. And run if it goes badly. I will see what I can do to keep her distracted long enough for you to get away. If things go wrong."

Nodding, she responded, "Yes. Once is enough for me"

Coreena poked her head in for an update, no sign of Morri. Sanu cast the dice and sent the spirits to challenge Morri and lead her to them. Now it was a matter of waiting, hoping and praying. That she was doing something foolish, she was certain that Unkle would agree, but Unkle was not here and she had to follow the dictates of her heart. Sanu was certain that she could reach Morrighan and perhaps, just perhaps, help her to throw off the daemon's yoke. It was risky and the price of failure would be high. But for her dark sister, she was willing to take the chance.

She glanced over at Ellen, seeing the fear on her face. That someone feared Morri as much as Ellen did, made Sanu wince. Placing a hand on her shoulder, trying to communicate some of the love she had for Morri, she said, "I know you have only known her possessed, but she does possess a good heart, if slightly damaged."

Ellen nodded, "I will pray to the Gods then, for you. I'm sorry, but I have no love to spare for Morrighan. I have to believe that she was worth loving once, but now?"

In a strong voice, Unkle answered, "That only the collection of many, united in love, honor and hope can carry the burden of the world, Lord. One man cannot hold back the dark tides."

Lord Spyke clapped his hands in approval as Unkle continued, "And I have learned too, that there is no quick route to the goals I desire. They take time and the help of those who share my dream. I was wrong to do as I did, and guild is not payment for such a crime. Only restitution can be made."

Lord Spyke gazed down at Unkle, "You will pay a price."

"Lord, I am willing to pay ANY price," replied Unkle.

"I have had an audience with The Tribunal and they wish for me to their Voice in this matter," said Lord Spyke matter of factly. Gulps and gasps of astonishment could be heard throughout as the reality of the matter finally hit some of our guild. Were it not for the worry I had for Joril, my voice probably would have joined theirs.

Unkle shuddered, but stood tall as he awaited his Judgement.

"You are to pay a penance," Lord Spyke said, his eyes closing for a moment as if remembering something, "You are to gather three items of power," he said after a moment, "You shall quest for these items."

I listened, but not with all my attention, for Joril's anguish was growing and I knew I needed to act quickly. But I also knew that interrupting The Tribunal's Judgement would be, to put it bluntly, a really bad idea.

Lord Spyke's voice continued, ignoring the growing sounds of mourning from Joril and my own impatience, "You shall travel Norrath for these items. Once you have these items, you shall disperse them to those who will use them for good. Then, and only then, will your destiny be back in your hands."

Joril's grief tore at my heart as I put a hand on his shoulder to comfort him. His look in incomprehension as he gazed back at me, spurred me to action, I could take no more. "Lord," I again interrupted.

Falamora also chose this time to interrupt, "Lord, may he have help in acquiring these Items of Power?"

Lord Spyke opened his eyes, his mouth curling into a smirk, "It seems you may have the help of your clan. So be it. It is allowed."

Unkle stood frozen, waiting for the rest of the Judgement. And while Unkle waited, I could not any longer, "What of Ruldari?" I demanded as Joril also struggled to keep his composure.

Ignoring me, Lord Spyke continued, "The three," muttering under his breath about The Tribunal always doing things in threes, "The three items are as follows. The Paw of Opalla, a pair of Lupine Claw Gauntlets and a single Bracer of Erolissi."

Unkle seemed to lose a bit of color as the Judgement seared his soul. "I have felt the Judgement of the Tribunal, Lord. I will do their will."

Lord Spyke smiled, "Not that you have much choice in the matter, mortal."

Then Unkle spoke up, "What, then, of my nephew's love, Ruldari?" standing taller than he had in many moons.

Lord Spyke looked a bit curious, "Who?"

"Aye," I piped up as well, "What of Ruldari? She has not returned."

Thag from the side commented "The dead half-elf you forgot to resurrect when you was doing everyone else, Lord Spyke. She deserves to be raised, she make good pies!"

Lord Spyke nodded, "Ah, her. The Ranger-Warrior."

Joril raises a tearful face to Lord Spyke, "Please, Lord Spyke."

"I sensed no soul to link with the shell when I went to it," responded Lord Spyke.

Joril blinked, "What? No link? How can this be?"

"But she has not yet reached the Void?" asked Unkle.

"I am Master of the Void. Not even the slightest part of her soul has touched my Realm," replied Lord Spyke.

In utter confusion, "But, we are to be wed on the morrow," said Joril. He blinked, "No, I cannot accept this. We are one, she and I."

The murmurs of the guild faded in the distance as I remembered a long ago vow given to Ruldari when she first started courting for Joril's hand. A vow spoken with all the force of a Shaman of the Tribunal. A vow that spoke of  Justice being delivered if Ruldari were to harm Joril. A vow to hunt Ruldari down, to the Spirit World if necessary.

My blood ran cold as I realized just what such a hunt would entail. And I knew that I would be the one to do it. The vow was there, the connection made. Now was the time for me to prove my worth as Shaman and my dedication to The Tribunal and Justice.

I glanced over at Unkle and he knew what I had in mind. "Sanu," he said, "You must. You may go where I cannot." I merely nodded, the dangers of this task chilling my own soul while a small voice in the back of my head ironically talked about vows coming true in odd ways.

Lord Spyke's voice penetrated my thoughts, "If she is not in my Realm, I can do nothing."

I took a deep breath as Unkle spoke again, "You must find her, my niece. You must bring her here." Again I nodded, wondering if Unkle knew the dangers of what he asked and what my vow demanded of me. Part of me wanted to run. But the vow was spoken and must be fulfilled.

Finally, I found my voice, "Yes. I will go. The vow was made long ago. Long ago, I vowed to hunt her if she hurt Joril, in this world or the next. To deliver Justice. That vow will be fulfilled. The hunt completed."

As the others watched, I sat down in front of Joril, trying to protect a confidence I did not feel. The consequences if I failed were almost enough to freeze me in fear. Forcing my voice to be smooth and calm, I looked into my brother's tear-stained face, "Brother, I need your help to do this."

Without question, Joril nodded, "Aye, sister. Ye have it."

That he trusted me without questions warmed my heart and stilled my fear. Perhaps this was possible. Once again, my brother's strong arm would protect me. "I need an anchor to this world."

"Anything, as long as my Ru is returned to me, sister," he responded.

A weak smile came to my lips, yes, I thought, the strength is there. Strength enough to bring us both back. "Join hands with me now, brother. Calm your mind and think only of her. Remember your love."

Falamora, a younger shaman of our guild, asked, "Is there anything I may do, Sister Sanu?"

To her and the guild I replied, "Guard my mortal shell. And be ready to join me if necessary. I know not what forces I will have to contend with to bring us back."

Falamora nodded, "I pledge it, so let it be done."

Again, I smiled, gaining strength from their support. Yes, the thoughts raced through my mind as I prepared to enter the Spirit World, this is what it means to be a Spiritwalker. Closing my eyes, I launched my soul into the Spirit World, seeing as my spirit left my mortal shell Joril reaching for Morri and Unkle's hands to join the small circle. Yes, with this many to guard my entrance to the Spirit World, I should have no problem returning. For a moment, I looked at my guildmates with spirit eyes, drinking in the light of their love, especially that of my manly mage and his thought of being with me always. The warmth and strength that gave me there are no
words for.

The transition into the spirit world was harsh and sudden in this place where death ruled. Cold winds blew around me, white-grey mists swirling to obscure the landscape around me. I extended my senses, trying to locate Ruldari.

Lord Spyke's words came to me through my link to Joril, "Find her in the Spirit World. And through the link you have forged, I will bring her back."

I nodded my understanding and let the spirit winds draw me deeper into their realm. "Ruldari!" I shouted to the winds. And, knowing that I needed a
personal link, a personal call, I acknowledged Ruldari as a sister, opening my heart to her, "Sister! Come to me!"

The whine of the winds were my only response. The mists in front of me writhed and formed an image, an image from my memories, memories I opened myself up to when I acknowledged Ruldari as a sister of my heart. Memories of my parents' death and that pain of loss, loss of a family member. That pain making it difficult to accept Morri as a family member at first. And now, I had admitted another to the circle of those who could hurt me.

Before I could stop, I turned away from those images. And in doing so, made an almost fatal mistake, for I granted the mists power by my denial. The mists grew louder and accusations started to spew forth from the mists as they gathered round me. I faltered beneath the mists, growing colder as the mists seemed to leach the warmth from my very soul. But as I went to one knee, light and warmth came to embrace me. My brother, somehow sensing my distress, doing what he has always done, lending me his strength, strength of body and strength of love.

I stood and faced the mists. Yes, I knew, I could be hurt. But there would always be love to help me recover. Something, that as a Spiritwalker, I would never be without. The mists disbursed as I stood, fleeing at the light and warmth that radiated from me, their power broken. A living spirit had to give power to the dead spirits which I had done by denying them. Soft wails could be heard from the mists as I advanced, the mists now parting before me.

Looked around, the first danger overcome, I realized that the hunt would take me deeper into the Spirit World. This was not going to be as easy as I had hoped.

I wandered for what seemed like ages, calling her name. "Ruldari! Sister! You are needed into the mortal realm! Come! Return to Joril! Come to me and I will show you the way!"

Direction being useless in the spirit world, I walked as my instincts told me. There was a time when I felt I was being watched and a time I felt I was guided. But time was meaningless. An then, at last, a trail. A trail in this cold and barren world. "Ruldari! Sister!" I called, my feet quickening as I followed the trail. Somehow I sensed her close.

And then, it happened, faintly I heard her voice, "Help me!"

I ran towards the voice, heedless of the mists and terrain, "Ru! Sister!" And then, in the mists ahead of her, a hand, one not made totally of mist, one with the delicate fingers of elvish blood in the veins. And then I found myself stopped, I could go no further into the spirit realm. I had not the power to venture further.

"No," I growled, "This is not how it will end!" I sent a call back to Joril, using the power of our love as a link, "Joril, join with the others!"

And almost instantaneously, I felt the surge of power and rushing headlong towards the hand I had seen that was being pulled into the mists.

Our fingertips brushed, "NOW!" I screamed to Joril and though him to Lord Spyke. I felt the power flow and somehow miss as Ruldari was swept away by the mists. My soul was chilled by her hand, such despair had filled Ru's soul. That Ru had given power to her nightmares in this place I know knew
for truth. That someone should have such nightmares, chilled me.

Without thought, I plunged into the mists, the mists she had given life to, heedless of the danger, of being claimed by the mists forever. And in the mists, I felt a hand and gripped it, ignoring the chill. I pulled the hand closer and an arm appeared. A sense of worthlessness almost overcame me as I clung to her hand. But I let it wash over me. Giving in to such was not part of my duty here. Rescuing my brother's true love was. Again, I pulled, and as I pulled, I projected love and warmth and most importantly, acceptance. Acceptance of her as she was. In a rush, Ru fell into my arms, to cling to me, sobbing. I hugged her without reservation and hoped that Joril would know to bring us both back, for I lacked the strength to do naught but hold her.

As I learned later, somehow, Lord Spyke knew that contact had been made and it was time to act. I felt a pull and then a rather rude jerk. And the next thing I knew, I was opening my eyes to stare a bit blankly at my manly mage. That I was spent would be a bit of an understatement and it took a few moments for the world to begin to make sense to me again.

But when it did, I could only smile as I saw Ruldari whole again and my brother happy. I let the warmth of my manly mage sink into the still-chilled corners of my soul to banish the last of the cold and dark of my journey. As I recovered, Aesop told me of what happened during my absence, that Joril had gotten everyone to join the circle. I nodded, knowing that is what enabled me to drive into the mists with even a chance at returning. And when the time was right, I smiled wryly as I told Ruldari, "I told you I would hunt you down."

"So ye did, Sanu, so you did," she replied.

Grinning somewhat, "I just didn't think my vow would take me to such places."

It was then that my glance fell upon Lord Sypke, sitting on the throne, looking as though he had been wounded. I heard him say, "It is what had to be done."

Unkle approached him, "Lord, twice now you have visited me. I shall not give you cause for a third."

Lord Spyke chuckled, "Let us hope that you do not give me reason again," as he winced.

Blurgle spoke up, "Is yew ok Mister Spyke?" concern on his Troll face.

With a nod, Lord Spyke answered, "Time heals all wounds, good Troll. I simply was not prepared for what I had to do."

And I watched as my family and guild began their healing now that the ordeal was over, Morri returned to us and Ruldari joined as well. We were a family once again, and I could not help but smile as Unkle congratulated us on our victory.

Lord Spyke had a final comment before leaving in his usual light show, "The ability for mortals to know love is something that even the gods are envious of. But I must depart. I have used much strength here. The scales have been balanced and I do not wish to be the one to tip them the other way."

We all showed him our respect and thanks as he looked us over one last time. And as the magicks gathered about him to transport him elsewhere, I was glad he had come a bit closer to mortals, glad for his guidance and glad I had done my duty in delivering Justice.

And when he had left, Ruldari told the rest of us what Lord Spyke had told her, of what price he had paid to fulfill his oath to pick up the pieces. The arena was quiet as we heard her words, "My friends and future family, I was brought back at a great cost, it seems. Lord Spyke had to give up a part of himself to save me. Part of his spirit was spent, pulling me back. The Daemon had taken the last of my soul and I was lost in a vast wasteland. Almost," she paused as memories returned, "almost was I lost forever."

I nodded, now understanding the jerk that had brought us back. Deep in the spirit world had Ruldari gone. So deep that it took all the clan's power to find her and more to bring us back. I smiled to myself as I considered the debts paid and incurred this day. My view of the gods had changed, and while I have probably lost a bit of awe, I have also gained a new respect and understanding.
