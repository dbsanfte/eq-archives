The Darkness Grows…
               
Vaelanea had lived a fairly normal life for a Teir'Dal. When she came of age she chose the path of the warrior, and followed the calling of Rallos Zek. Vaelanea was trained in the art of stealth, hiding, one handed slashing weapons, and dual wield. She preferred to dual wield axes, but she was deadly with any weapon. Now she wore blackened ringmail leggings that stopped at about mid-thigh, an exposing black ringmail bikini, and black ringmail arm sleeves that stopped just short of her shoulders, compared to the leather outfit she wore when she wore when she first met Darain. After meeting Vaelanea, Darain recruited Vaelanea into his guild, the Shadow Striders. He convinced her to become his personal assassin, sent to kill certain members of the Shadow Striders arch-enemies, the Wind Riders. She trained hard every day so she would be strong enough for any assignment given to her by Darain. Then it came…

"Vaelanea?" the human boy said.

"Yes?" she hissed.

"A message for you from Lord Darain." The thought that Darain sent her a message made her wonder. She quickly snatched the scroll from the boy's hand and told him to run off, which he did, stumbling from fright as he fled. It had better be important enough to interrupt her resting in her favorite tavern. Vaelanea rolled open the scroll…

"Vaelanea" it started out "I have your first assignment. I feel it is time to show the Wind Riders that we have some muscle now. I have chosen the son of the wood elf whore, Lillaie to be the first. His name is Kinor, a barbarian. Can you imagine that? A barbarian son of a wood elf. He has publicly spoken out against the Shadow Striders, causing us much annoyance. This annoyance must be stopped. Execute him, and don't forget to let him know it was by MY order!"

"This will be quite a challenge" she muttered. Barbarians were not only very large, but made for awesome warriors. She wondered if she would be able to overcome his brute strength. "It ought to be a glorious battle indeed" she said out loud as she walked out the door of the tavern into the late afternoon light, leaving her drinks and food unpaid for. Not like anyone would have the courage enough to confront her about it anyway, they all knew she would cut out their tongue or some such awful thing.

As Vaelanea walked to the gates of Qeynos she noticed a small blonde wood elf talking to a large barbarian. She used her skills of stealth to quietly slip into the shadows. She stood there, invisible in the shadows, listening to them talk. She learned that the little wood elf was Lillaie. She appeared to be a druid as well. She hated druids. Not because of their want to protect nature, but because she hated having to make spellcasters angry with her. They could freeze her legs and blast away her life with spells while she stood there helpless, she hated that feeling. She shivered at the thought. The barbarian was Kinor too. Excellent, this job would be much quicker then she expected. Lillaie and Kinor wandered close to her and stood there, still chatting away. Vaelanea felt this would be a good time to make her presence known. Like a flash of lightning, her hand sot out of the darkness, axe in hand. The hilt of the axe solidly connected with Lillaie's temple, instantly rendering her unconscious. Vaelanea stepped into the light just enough for some of her figure to show in the dusk light. "Kinor" she said. "I have been ordered by Lord Darain to execute you for crimes against the Shadow Striders"

"The Shadow Striders?" Kinor said. "Evil being! Prepare to join the dead!" he screamed at her as he charged at her, sword in hand. She simply moved sideways, making him slam into the wall she was standing against.

"Maybe this won't be so hard" she thought as she swung her axe down against the leather armor Kinor was wearing. It wouldn't protect him much against her axes. He stood up and swung at her backhanded. She ducked but ended up ducking right into a kick, which sent her flying backwards into some wooden crates against a wall. She was badly dazed but recovered quickly. Kinor was right there, swinging down at her with his sword. She managed to roll to avoid the sword, but wood debris stung her skin. She rolled forward off of the crates and scurried away far enough so that she could stand for Kinor's next attack. He kicked at her again, but once again she dodged, but she wasn't able to avoid his shield, which he swung right into her. Vaelanea's side screamed with pain from being hit with the shield. She stumbled backwards in pain and felt dizzy. She began to doubt if she could take this barbarian, but then things turned. As Kinor charged for his next attack she hooked his sword with one of her axes, while elbowing him in the belly, knocking the wind out of Kinor. As he stumbled backwards in both surprise and pain she came forward, kicking him in the chest and nearly knocking him down. She swung her axe and connected with his left arm, the one holding the shield. She nearly tore it off but she wasn't at the proper angle, so the bone stopped the axe. As he reeled in pain she turned to check on Lillaie. She was still out cold. Good, this would be easy. She raised her axe to swing down at Kinor again when a voice called out.

"Vaelanea!"

She turned to look. It was the human, Niceen, along with Prescott and the Teir'Dal, Tajitz, all Shadow Striders. As she was turned Kinor tripped Vaelanea, knocking her down onto the cobblestone, hard. She was seriously dazed and had no idea what was going on. Luckily she had badly hurt Kinor because he could have easily ended her life while she laid there before him. His arm was seriously slowing him down. By this time a decent crowd started to gather, different people cheering for one of them or the other. Vaelanea stood as Kinor was up to a crouching position.

"Hah! Look at the blood pouring from him!" Niceen shouted.

"I will not die so easy!" screamed Kinor. He shoved his right shoulder into Vaelanea's soft belly, lifting her off of her feet and launching her into the opposite wall with a loud crunch. She was hurt now, bad. Nothing too serious, but she couldn't keep fighting like this. As she tried to regain her thoughts she saw that Niceen and Tajitz and grabbed Lillaie and tied her arms behind her. She was still out, but it was better that she not be able to do anything when she awoke. Vaelanea looked back to see Kinor but all she saw was his leg connecting with her chest. She grunted in pain. He kicked her over and over. She was losing and she knew it, she would be knocked out unless she did something. With a rush of adrenaline she screamed at Kinor. With his next kick she grabbed his foot and smashed the blade of her axe into it, shattering his ankle. Kinor screamed in pain as he limped backwards. Vaelanea stood, breathing hard with adrenaline, her body covered with Kinor's blood, her mouth oozing her own blood. She was grinning evilly, evil enough to scare even the Shadow Striders standing by, watching. She took two steps forward and with both hands swung one of her axes down into the middle of Kinor's chest, tearing it open. Kinor fell to the ground chest down, his own blood pouring from his mouth and wounds. He was about to die and he knew it. The last thing Kinor saw was out of the corner of his eye, Vaelanea standing over him, her axe dripping with his own blood, and the blood from hr mouth dripping onto his face. Vaelanea kicked him as hard she could in the head, ending his pathetic life. As she did Lillaie awoke.

"NOOOOOOOOO!!!!" she screamed. "You murdered my son! You will die for this wench!"

Instead of answering Lillaie, Vaelanea knelt down and lifted Kinor's head so his lifeless eyes were staring at Lillaie. Vaelanea looked up at Lillaie, blood coming from her evil, grinning mouth, and proceeded to slice off Kinor's head as her trophy. That look was the look that would haunt Lillaie's dreams forever…

- Vaelanea
