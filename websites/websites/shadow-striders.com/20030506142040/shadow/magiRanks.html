Magi of Shadow
Magi Ranks
Wizards, Necromancers, Enchanters, Magicians, Shamans, Clerics, and Irreverent Druids belong in the Magi of Shadow Circle. The highest of these is the Shadow Magi. He or she is most responsible for the rest of their division. Directly under the Shadow Magi are the Magi of Shades. They have nearly the same responsibilities as the Shadow, and assume the role fully when the Shadow is unavailable. All rank decisions are made by the division's officers, and the Guildleader.

As for the Outer Circle, this is the structure:

  • All new members are given the rank of Apprentice.
  • After Apprentice, you can be promoted to Acolyte.
  • And after Acolyte, you can be promoted to Spellhand.
Decisions are based on the following criteria:

  • Impress higher ranking members with good role playing. If you are good enough, you will get their attention.
  • Provide aid or equipment to help your fellow guildmembers. Examples would be escorting a younger member through dangerous territory or offering the Shadow Magi a gift that the guild can use, like armor, etc. The Inner Circle will determine what to do with the equipment. (This alone will not get you to officership though, you need to stress roleplay.)
  • Defeat a Wind Rider in a battle of wits. Must be witnessed by another.
  • Defeat a Wind Rider in a fair duel. Must also be witnessed. By fair we mean equal to or very close to your level.
Higher ranks must perform more of the above to advance. If you are an Acolyte, you should play at least 5 hours a week. If you are a Spellhand, you should play at least 10 hours a week. Exceptions can be made if you are having RL (real life) difficulties.

Once you are a Spellhand, you can advance to the rank of Magi of Shades or even Shadow Magi if the positions are open. Only Lord Faileas can bestow that honor, and only Spellhands will be considered for officership.

Disrespect to higher ranking members is punishable by demotion and/or disbandment.
