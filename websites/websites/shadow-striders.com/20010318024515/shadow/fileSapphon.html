Sapphon Nightstalker
Erudite Enchanter
 
History: As a long-time student in the arts of Enchanting in Erudin, Sapphon was hailed as one of the brightest snd most promising students in the new crop of "Craft Keepers". Sapphon had thought he was only as good as any other student, but his superiors were convinced he was something special. Sapphon was given much attention by the best tutors the City of High Men had to offer, and he and his family were dined on fine food and drink courtesy of the Tower of the Craft Keepers. Few had a life as good as Sapphon's teenage life, and yet all that he wanted was to get away from it. Strangely, he would rather have been a normal, everyday student than one held in such high esteem and honor. "There is nothing I can do that anybody else cannot." he would always protest. However, it seemed that no one would listen to him, not his parents, not his teachers, not even his friends, who held him in awe and reverence.

Sapphon was studying one night, preparing for a ritual sorcery to be held tomorrow in which he was to be the lead student and assistant to the Instructorum (as always). He heard a rap on his door, three quick taps. He looked over at the Gnomish cuckoo clock on the wall. It was almost 2 in the morning! Who on earth would be disturbing him at 2 in the morning!? Quite annoyed and not quite understanding, Sapphon went to the door of his tiny candlelit study and opened it wide. No one was there. It was obvious someone was playing a trick on him. But what was this? he was being lifted into the air and pushed back farther into the room! He could see nothing yet he was being lifted by his shirt by strong hands... invisible hands. He was put down near his desk, and the door was shut and locked. Sapphon was too scared to think or cry out, much less attack the invisible intruder, even though the study was tiny and there were only so many places he could be. Suddenly, the problem solved itself. Standing before him, seemingly out of nowhere, was a short (to an Erudite) elven figure with strange white hair. And.. blue skin? It took Sapphon a moment to remember the tales he had heard of evil elves that lived underground in a city far away on Antonica that were hateful towards all and who killed the goodly elves and humans because of their hatred for them. Sapphon recognized that the white hair and dark skin of this Elf would make him fit the description of a Dark Elf perfectly. Sapphon managed to mutter, "Wh-wh..y have.." before the wicked elf clamped a hand over the High man's mouth. Sapphon's eyes went wider than he would have thought possible, had he been able to look in a mirror. The Dark Elf was going to kill him. Murder him in the night and leave his bloody corpse where no one would ever find it.

But the wicked elf drew no weapon, and indeed he seemed not to carry any. He spoke in a harsh whisper characteristic of all Dark Elves, and he said these words: "Sapphon, student Enchanter, I presume? Don't try to answer, I don't have to presume, I already know I'm right. Just sit back, relax, don't be so frightened, and don't make too much noise." And with that the elf removed his hand from Sapphon's face. Sapphon began to mutter again, "Who..." The Dark Elf interrupted him, "My name and who I am is not important. As you can see, I am Teir'Dal, what the stories and myths of your language call Night Elves. And that's all we are is stories and myths in this part of Norrath. I too practice the magical arts, as you do. And most importantly, I am not here to hurt you or kill you. I am here to offer you something." "Offer me, some-thing?" Sapphon stammered. "Yes, offer you something. I am here to offer you a chance. I know you are eager to get out of here, and end this life you currently live. Dont ask how I know, I just do. Anyway, if you tire of your current lot as the next promising wage mage for the City of Erudin, then I offer you a chance for something better, something more...meaningful. I offer you membership in the Shadow Striders. I can take you away, out of here, out of Erudin, off the continent if you want. I can begin you a new life, as one of the proud, one of the Striders. You can travel, see the world, visit Kelethin or Ak'Anon (although don't expect me to go with you THOSE places), where that nice clock you have was made. Tell me, have you ever seen a gnome? No? That's what I thought. Would you like to?" "Yes...yes. I would like to see a gnome, I think..! But why do you offer me this choice, if you can call it that, with all these benefits? What do YOU get out of it?" "I offer you this choice because the Shadow Striders need new blood, and you are one of the most talented Enchanters in Erudin. We do not kidnap people, we only take them willingly into our ranks, so you, with your current dissatisfaction in life, were a perfect choice." What Garron did not mention was that dissatisfaction could lead to evil, which was, of course, his true goal. "OK," Sapphon answered with hardly a thought about his teachers, parents, or even his friends, "I'll go with you!" Garron laughed inside his mind. This was going to be a difficult one... "By the way, now that you've agreed, my name is Garron. Pleased to meet you." Sapphon watched as the elf cast an invisibility spell on himself, and heard his voice say "Lead the way to the docks, I'll follow you invisibly. The sentinels don't exactly appreciate the horror stories they tell their children coming true...." Sapphon gathered some small items, some platinum pieces, and closed his door for the last time.
