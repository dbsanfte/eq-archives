Garron
Tier'Dal Wizard
 
History: Garron is a Teir'Dal Wizard who hails from the dark city of Neriak. Garron, like his fellow members of The Spurned, pratices one of the arts held to be "false" in Neriak: those of the Wizards, Enchanters, and Magicians. Garron's parents were both members of The Dead, that society within Innoruuk's children that follows the "true" art of Necromancy, and are more highly accepted than The Spurned, hence the name, The Spurned. When his parents found out about his desire to practice Wizardry, they were dumbfounded and speechless. After a long silence, Garron's parents told him he could not train with The Spurned and would instead be a Shadow Knight of the Dead, by his father's will. What they did not know was that it was too late. The area around where Garron was standing lit up brightly, and suddenly Garron was teleported into the Tower of the Spurned. This has been his only home and the Wizards' Guild his only family ever since. Garron later heard his parents had not even bothered to look for him, had simply disowned him. He had counted on that.

Garron, with not even his family to love in his early years, is almost completely devoid of emotion, even that one that rules most Dark Elves' lives, Hate. Garron's mind is too rational to accept Emotion as package and parcel with Reason, and because of this he almost completely ignores his heart and follows the analytical side of his mind intead. Garron's only true objective in life is to lear. Garron wishes to be a Master of the Tower of the Spurned one day, but he knows that can be achieved only by learning great wisdom and lore. Garron spends not as much time in the Tower as some of his fellows, preferring to be out in the worlds outside Neriak, discovering and absorbing information as fast and thoroughly as he may. Garron's favorite quote is "Information is my forte", and indeed it is. NOTHING is more important to Garron than learning and knowledge. He would gladly sacrifice his life, if only he could know the secrets of the world before he died.

Garron, outwardly, is not cold to most, but not warm to any. He states fact. He argues about controversial subjects. He is a true intellectual and seeker of knowledge. He will never speak of his distant past, but speaks much of the future. And NEVER will he admit that he feels anything. I quote:

"I feel no hate, nor love. Both are weaknesses, and that would make YOU weak."

As a final note, Garron, while travelling North Ro one night, came upon a large gathering of people outside the tunnel connecting the Desert with the Commonlands. Intrigued at this congregation, Garron investigated. He soon found it was a battle of wills and words between the Shadow Striders and the Wind Riders. After Lady Envy defeated a Druid in a duel, Garron began to talk a little to the Dark Magi Malgorm, not really with any intent to join, just out of curiosity. And the rest is history. Garron has had a certain reverence for Malgorm since the very beginning. And Malgorm has even hinted that Garron could become his apprentice some day. Garron wishes this to happen and tries harder to please the Dark Magi than any other Shadow Strider.

*note: Malgorm has since left the Shadow Striders, but Garron remains
