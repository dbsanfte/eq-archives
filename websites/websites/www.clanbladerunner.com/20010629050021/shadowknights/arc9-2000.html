Out with the old, in with the new...

Posted Tuesday, October 17, 2000, 11:16:24, by William Shaf

Well, the big move has happened...

Abashi has officially been promoted to assistant producer for the Scars of Velious expansion, and a new guy, "Absor", has replaced him. Absor was the editor of "Everquest Express", an online newspaper about EQ. I hope he's wearing his flame retardant undies.

This post was found on the General Discussion board.

I dont usually give my thoughts on most matters, but can't help but do it this time...

I, for one, am glad he's gone.. He was making decisions on gameplay when he shouldn't have been. His job was to take customer concerns to the live team/producers, and bring THEIR responses back to the players. His opinions on gameplay were not warranted, nor asked for.

Anytime a *PR* guy is making decisions about game content or what is fixed and what is not, he's stepping out of his bounds. My proof? Here:

On the post Big bunch of bard ...issues, he said:

[Wrinn, Gordon] - Other casting classes don't go up to 250, but I'm not opposed to increasing the instrument caps to reduce failures. We'll put it in the next patch.

This was one of the many instances that I've found of him making gameplay decisions, and I'll bet he didn't consult anyone beforehand. There's other instances in that thread as well, just read all the statements under Gordon's name.

Now granted, this was a "bone" thrown to the bards, but was the decision his? Did he say "Hmm.. I'll let them have this one, since I've told them no on every other issue". Sure seems like it to me. I for one am glad we have a new guy... I think Gordon took this job as a stepping stone, only we the players were the stone being stepped on.

Please post your thoughts in the comments section.

Hybrids and Disciplines

Posted Friday, October 6, 2000, 9:45:29, by William Shaf

A Shadow Knight posted this on the gameplay forum asking about hybrids and disciplines. Here's Abashi's reply:

We've been looking into the idea of possible disciplines for hybrids, but for more of a flavor thing than a balance one. If the suggestion being made is that there is a balance issue that can be solved by discipline, I'd have to disagree. Based upon the series of tests that were just made, they are now all measuring up as they should.

-Gordon
