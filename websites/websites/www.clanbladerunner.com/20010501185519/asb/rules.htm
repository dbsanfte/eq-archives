The Rules of the Adventurers of the Silver Blade

Last modified February 8, 2000


Guild Leadership

Defining Our Leadership
  1. Adventurers of the Silver Blade (ASB) will have a three (3) person council to lead it.
  2. This council will be picked and voted for by the members of the guild.
Voting Process
  1. Every two (2) months the council should be voted upon. At this point new council members may be elected, or the same council members can be reelected.
  2. A nomination process will be held before the actual election. This process will last one week. Nominees that do not wish to serve on the council should say so at this time.
  3. Then the actual election will be held. Each member may vote for up to three (3) nominees. This election should last one week.
  4. The three (3) nominees that receive the most votes will be the new council.
  5. Current council members are automatically nominated.
Ousting a Dictator
  1. At anytime, a two-thirds majority of the guild may vote to have any member of the council removed from it. Or they may vote to have the whole council removed at once.
  2. After which a new council would be elected using the process described above.



Recruitment Process

Pre-initiation period
  1. To become a candidate, a person must be sponsored by a vested member (not an initiate). It is the responsibility of the sponsor to verify that the candidates behavior they have been witness to, is compliant with our code of conduct.
  2. Every candidate must read our rules beforehand on the web site and mention to their sponsor that they understand and agree with them.
  3. During this period, a candidate should be making efforts to hunt with a few members of the guild.
  4. Once an initiate has a member that will sponsor him into the guild they will advance to the next phase.
Trial Period
  1. A trial period will last a minimum of two (2) weeks, and a maximum of two (2) months. This period is used to get to know the people in the guild, and to have them get to know you.
  2. Attendance to meetings, events and hunts of the appropriate level are strongly encouraged for all initiates. This is the best way for people to get to know you.
  3. At any time during the trial period a member of the guild may voice a reason to end the trial period, if the initiate has broken a rule. If it is a minor offense, then the council, the initiate and the member with the grievance may happen. If it is a major offense, the council can vote to end the trial period immediately.
  4. At anytime, after the minimum amount of two weeks, the majority of the council can call for a vote for full membership of an initiate. At this time, anyone who has reason the initiate should NOT become a full member should e-mail the council stating so, and giving a detailed explanation as to why. The council will investigate the grievance and decide whether or not is it valid. One (1) 'veto' validated by the council or three (3) without validation, is grounds for rejecting the applicant. This process will last one (1) week.



Loot Guidelines

Common Loot
  1. All Guild Hunts should have a master looter.
  2. If you leave the hunt early, it is your responsibility to ask for a split at that time. It is not the looters job to hold a share for you.
Special loot
  1. All the people in the group that need the item for the character they are playing random for it (/rand 100 -- highest wins). Random eliminate should be in place, i.e. you can only get one special loot per hunt until everyone gets one, unless no one else in the group needs the item. Elimination applies to the player, not the character. Secondary characters random with a -20 penalty.
  2. In the special circumstance that someone is playing a secondary character at the request of the group, then they may random for their primary with the other primaries without penalty, or for that secondary character without penalty. They still only get one roll per random, however.
  3. If none of the characters in the group needs the item, then they may random it for not-present primary characters belonging to group members that need it.
  4. If no one in the group needs the item, it is up to the group to either a) random it among themselves or b) do with as they see fit (e.g. - sell and split the cash or give to a specific member, or random among guild primaries, etc.)
  5. If, in a Guild Hunt, there are non-guild players present in the group, then everyone will random for the loot. If a guild member wins it then the Guild Hunt rules apply. Note that if the winner of this roll is eligible for the item then they win it - no more rolling. Penalties for secondary characters should still be taken into account in this situation.
Special Circumstances
  1. If a hunt is organized to get an item for a certain person, they get the item.
Handing Down Loot
  1. Loot won is yours to do with as you see fit after you are done with it, although it is suggested you consider handing it down to other guild members if they need it. If someone gives you an item and places conditions on it, though, you should follow their wishes.
Defining a Guild Hunt
  1. If a group has more than two (2) members and all the members are guild members that should be a Guild Hunt.
  2. If a group has more than two (2) people, and 50% or more of them are guild members that should be a Guild Hunt, unless otherwise stated by the group. This should be done beforehand, at least before any major loot is acquired, so that no misunderstandings happen.
  3. If a hunt is organized beforehand on the forum, it is a Guild Hunt.
Defining Primary Characters
  1. Everyone should specify their primary character.
  2. People can change their primary character by posting on the forum, but the change will not take place until after a two-week waiting period.
