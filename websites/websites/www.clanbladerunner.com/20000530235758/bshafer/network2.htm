 When you are starting a network with ethernet (RJ-45), which is 8 wire cable, much like the 4 wire telephone cord you use, you will need a hub of some sort. You can get a hub at most second hand computer stores, or parts store pretty cheap. Here in the Dallas-Ft. Worth area, we have a thing called the "Saturday Sale". It is a place where you go on the first Saturday of every month, starting at midnight on Friday. It has EVERYTHING you will ever need for computers, as far as networking, parts for building your own computer, and even whole pc's. Everything there is a hell of a discount from most stores because they are"liquidating". I'm pretty sure most big metropolitan area has an equal thing somewhere. Just check around in any computer store, and they will tell you.

While you are there, you can pick up ethernet cards pretty cheap also. But I would recommend going to Computer City for the cards. Much more reliable and probably pretty close to the same price. You will also need to buy the cable. You can probably pick it up at Computer City, also. It MUST be 8 wire. Don't spend your money on anything else. Make sure the wires inside the cabling are color coded. It is very important!!

If you have more than 5 pc's that you want to network, find a general area somewhere within 300 or so feet from each pc. Ethernet can only transmit aproximately 328 feet. I suggest staying under the 300 mark. Once you have your place picked out, install the hub there. This will be your "network closet". For each node you expand, you can always install another hub and connect the two together. (more on that later).

Install your network cards in the pc's. Don't worry about configuring the machines yet, let's make sure they are connected, and that we have link lights. 

Once you have the hubs installed, and you have the network cards in the machines, you will have to run the cables. If you are spanning more than one room, you will want to go up the wall, and over the ceiling tiles. When you reach the network closet, cut the wire, but be sure to leave enough excess to make a few "mistakes" in the wiring of the RJ-45 ends. :) Also, mark the cables at both ends to let you know where they are going. If you have alot of hubs, then mark the cable at the pc to let you know which hub and which port you are connecting to (very important). 

When wiring the RJ-45 ends to the cable, you want to "flip" your eight wires. You can use any color code that you want, just make sure that the first all the way thru to the eighth wire is the same with every RJ-45 end that you use. When you put on the head, at one end of the wire, put it on with the clip down, at the other end, put it on with the clip up. so that 1 is 8, 2 is 7, 3 is 6, and 4 is 5 on the other end. That is what we call "flipped". 

After you have your cables run, plug each one into the card in the pc, and the other end into the hub at the network card. You should see some kind of light on the hub to see if it can "see" the pc. If you don't see the light (most cases green), then just redo your RJ-45 ends. That is probably the culprit. You should also see a light or two on your network card. If you see a light on only one device, it is probably communicating with the other, but the other is not communicating with it. That means that your transmit is working and not your recieve. Again, redo your RJ-45 end and it will fix it.

Continue this procedure for each device you have that you want networked. If you have more than one hub, you can "daisy chain" the two. On the hub, you should have some sort of "network feed" on one of the ports (probably the last port) just plug the two together with a short cable wired the same way, and they will talk to each other like the pc to the hub.
