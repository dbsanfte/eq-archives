 		 I haven't found many pages out there on the web that helps the home user to start a small network in their home or small business. I have created a network between my two computers in my home and thought I could help out a few people out there with starting a network between a couple of computers, or a small handful in a business. I am still getting information, so please bare with me while I create this page. 

I began my quest for the easiest way to network two computer systems about 6 months ago. There are several ways to connect computers and everyone has their own reasons why. It was a very difficult decision on which route to take so I went with the most inexpensive and easiest route. The reason I wanted to network my two computers, is because I can't stand saving files to a floppy to back them up. Floppy drives are becoming a thing of the past with the ever growing demand for network connectivity. Gaming is another reason. I have a love for computer games that two or more actual people can play, not just you against the computer. Modems are great for two people, but what if I wanted to play more than just one other person?? I'd be screwed. That is why my network was started.

1. Try to decide ahead of time how many computers you will be connecting. It helps to know the amount of nodes you will be needing in the future, and how many you currently have. If you are creating a network for a small business, keep in mind that your business will be growing, and so will the need for your computer systems. 

2. Next, you will need to decide how you want your computers connected. The most common, fastest connections, and ease is with ethernet. Most ethernet cards have an RJ-45 connection, as well as a thinwire coax. For just a few nodes, It is cheaper, and easier, to use the coaxial thinwire. I bought my two network cards at Computer City for about 70 dollars, and 100 feet of thinwire at Radio Shack for $25. That is all I spent on the hardware. If you plan on using twisted pair wire, which is sort of like phone wire, you will need a hub of some sort, to change the polarity. You can get a decent hub with the RJ-45 connections roughly for about $100. If you have more than five nodes, I recommend going with the hub and twisted pair. The good thing about the thinwire, is that you don't need a hub to reverse the polarity. all you need is a terminator at the end of the line. A terminator is a connection that re-routes the data back to the line like a loop.
