Spiritwalkers Guild Member Bio

Name: Etienne
Race: Human
Profession: A seeker of Enlightenment, also a brewer, baker, and potter among other things.
Likes: His daughter, haiku, painting
Dislikes: Those who have neither regard nor respect for others, being mooned by goblins
Quirks: Tends to be a bit irreverant towards the gods and people in positions of authority unless they have earned his respect.

Excerpted from Dobbins' "A Bard's Travels Across Norath":

April 23, 3174

I have spent the last few days travelling with a warrior-monk by the name of Etienne Wayfarer. I have found him to be a pleasant and interesting companion, if a bit quiet and enigmatic.

If you have ever visited the Ashen Order of Freeport or the Silent Fist Order in Qeynos then you have undoubtedly seen the monks going about their training regimen. They spar with each other and wooden dummies, punctuating each blow with an explosive yell. They beat each other or repeatedly plunge their hands into pots filled with sand, among other strange rituals, to toughen themselves. They perform all manner of strenuous activities. And I would be hard pressed to ever imagine Etienne in such a setting.

As I write this I can see him performing his evening routine. He moves with a glacial slowness as he flows gracefully through an intricate series of movements that more closely resemble a dance than a highly refined fighting style. There are no sudden moves, no harsh cries. Yet, having witnessed him fighting I cannot deny it's effectiveness.

Curious about this discrepancy I have questioned him about his past but he is oddly reluctant to speak of it. Normally this would indicate some experience in the past that he either wishes to hide or to not remember but with Etienne that is not the case. I'm not sure if I can describe it other than that the past is not particularly important to him and he seems to believe that my knowing his past would interfere with my understanding his present. To illustrate, at one point he took out a flask of water and asked which was more important; it's past, whether it was the melted snows of Everfrost or drawn from a well in the Karanas, or it's present, whether or not it could quench my thirst. In any case I have kept at him and this is what I have been able to piece together.

His parents are farmers somewhere in the Karanas. Like many farmers, they had many children to help them work their land and Etienne is one of their younger sons. One year, when he was young, there was a terrible drought. They prayed to Karana to send the rains. They made sacrifices to the god. As the drought continued they even begged the god for his forgiveness for whatever sin they had committed to anger him so. All to no avail. Finally, Etienne determined that he would run away from home and go to Qeynos. That way there would be one less mouth for his family to feed and he would get to see the city.

The rains finally came as he was making his way through the mountains. There was a torrential downpour from which he caught a fever. He sickened and was unable to continue. He would probably have died there upon the mountain side were it not for an elderly hermit, named Jerome, who found the boy and carried him back to his cottage. There he tended to Etienne until his sickness broke.

Jerome, it seems, was once a member of the Silent Fist but he had discovered what he considered a better Way than that of the Fist and so had broken ties with the order and come to live alone on the mountain side where he studied what he called the Way of the Universe in a quest for Enlightenment. Something about the old hermit clicked with Etienne and he decided to stay with Jerome and study the Way rather then continue on to Qeynos.

Years passed and Etienne grew into a young man. Then one day Jerome called Etienne to him and told him that it was time for Etienne to leave. Jerome had shown Etienne as much of the Way as he could. Now it was time for Etienne to find his own Way in the world. Thus it was that Etienne resumed his journey of long past and finally came to Qeynos. There he forged a lifelong friendship with a young barbarian shaman named Mortekai...
