Spiritwalkers Guild Member Bio

Name: Morrighan Bonedancer
Race: Human
Profession: Jeweler / Tailor
Likes: A creative death, A dark night in the Plains of Karana, A very rare steak. A blood soaked piece of steel in the moonlight.
Dislikes: Self-righteous Paladins, Annoying Barbarian guards, Cros and Holly.
Quirks: A Fascination with all things dark and macabre, and a taste for blood.

A glorious and deep night to you all, I am Morrighan, daughter of Andrasta and Camulous.

I am what some would call an Abomination, skinwalker, bone crafter, death dancer, or a Necromancer.

Does this make me evil and less worthy of life than you? Some would say yes, and those that do will soon be lost to their own blindness. But you seem to have an open mind so sit and listen to my side of the story.

My unkle thinks watching my parents die is what put me on my path in the night. My only answer to that is, it made me see death as a service and release. Something to be less feared than a life of pain.

I have found great strength in the darkness and power to face the forces that took my parents from me. I do not perceive what I am as evil. I am a force of nature. I cut away the dead flesh from the world and use sickness and disease to strengthen all that I touch. for it is only with that strength we can overcome that which the fates have in store.

Even now some of you find me disquieting to be around. Even my beloved bright sister Sanu has had her moments of doubt about me.

I can understand your fear of the dark and the night. The bright gods tell you to be afraid of us, for they understand not the balance of all things. The world moves in patterns of light and darkness and neither can exist without the other. Unfortunately many of the lords of light cannot see this and in their righteousness seek to destroy all my kind.

My Unkle is the only one who seems to understand me, and I owe him much for that. For he has shown me that my kind can find strength and fellowship in the followers of your gods, and that we need not fear each other. Life and death are flip sides of the same coin, so are good and evil. should we each serve our side of the coin to the detriment of the other? Or should we instead serve the whole coin.

I hope you can learn to understand me too, for there is much beauty and joy in my darkness. Come explore......
