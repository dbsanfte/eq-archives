Spiritwalkers Guild Member Bio

Name: Wolfegar
Race: Barbarian
Profession: Warrior
Likes:  
Dislikes:  
Quirks:  
 

If you live in Halas, or visit there long enough you will see one.  A tall powerfully built barbarian who, if possible, looks even more barbaric than his cousins who live in Halas and travel southward to other lands.  These are the true barbarians of Norrath, those from which all others are descended.  Except for an occasional trip into Halas to buy supplies they can not make or hunt for in their own villages these men and women would never be seen.  Most of them live their entire life living in a remote village far out on the frozen tundra, deep in some icy crevice or on a snow covered mountain.  They live as all barbarians lived once in complete seclusion from the outside world, before the tunnels of BlackBurrow were explored and a pathway to the Southern Lands discovered.  With this discovery came the civilization of the barbarian race, as they traveled into the warm cultured lands far from their frozen homeland; and as other races came to Everfrost to see its wonders and hunt its strange and marvelous creatures.

It was into one such village a large male child was born, born to Marta and Bromm, who were the Keepers of the Wolves for their clan.  His name was to be Wolfegar, which in the old tongue means Wolf Guardian.  Marta and Bromm raised and cared for the huge tundra wolves the clan used to pull their sleds in winter.  Massive animals not like the small winter wolves that roam the lands around Halas.  These were giant, powerful wolves standing almost 5’ tall at the shoulder; capable of pulling a fully loaded sled, which might weigh tons.  So dependent on these great beasts were the clans that the animals where held in awe and reverence.  Not only did they pull the sleds, but also they ranged with the Warriors as hunting companions, and guarded the women and children in the village.

From an early age everyone in the village could tell Wolfegar would grow to be a powerful and large man, well suited to his name and to the job which would be his upon reaching manhood.  For this job, Wolfegar was trained and instructed from the time he could first walk.  He would one day take the position of his father and be the breeder and trainer of the clans wolves.  Almost from birth he was placed among the wolves with their pups, and if one didn’t know better, they would think it was the wolves that raised him.  In fact more than one his mother would look for her child only to find him squirming and suckling on a mother wolf just like a pup.  It was this closeness that formed the special bond between Wolfegar and his future charges, and as he grew instead of having normal friends to play with, Gar (as he was often called) had a wolf brother who became his closest friend.  The two became inseparable and went everywhere together.  Those who saw them knew they could communicate with each other.  Silvermane, as the wolf was called, grew to love and respect Wolfegar, which was good for the huge silvery haired wolf would be the new leader of the clan pack. 

As Wolfegar grew he took on more and more of his fathers responsibilities so one day he could take over all the work.  He began to train the wolves and take them on sled runs so they would become use to working as a team when pulling the sleds.  He also started to hunt, and when he did it was always Silvermane who was at his side.  Many polar bears and snow leopards did the pair claim, and on one far ranging hunting trip Silvermane saved Wolfegar’s life when he slipped and fell beneath the charge of a huge bear.  Without thinking of himself the wolf charged and so powerful was his leap to save his friend that the bear was knocked off it’s feet and tumbled down a steep cliff to crash into the sharp rocks below, dead.  Silvermane then drug the wounded Wolfegar almost ten miles to the Village, stopping along the way to lay next to his friend so the wolf’s warmth could keep the boy from freezing to death.

It was two summers later that disaster struck, and like is often true it did not strike in a single stroke, but in two like a forked strike of lightning.  Unknown to them because they were on one of their frequent hunting trips, the village of Wolfegar and Silvermane was attacked by a raiding band of gnolls.  So swift and ferocious was the attack and so completely unexpected the clan was caught totally unaware and though they fought like only true Northern barbarians can, they were vastly outnumbered and soon the entire village lay in ruins.  The gnolls, crude beasts at best, destroyed everything standing, ate or drug off all the people, and slaughtered or drove off the wolves.   

At the same time Wolfegar and Silvermane where having trouble of there own.  Further from the village than they had ever been, the pair struggled through a blinding blizzard which had suddenly came upon them at they tracked a large polar bear.  Both the man and the wolf used every sense they had to try and find shelter, but they could not see two feet in front of them let alone find safety.  Then, suddenly, both froze completely still as a sound, which all barbarians who travel the tundra and frozen waters are taught to fear, surrounded them. It was a loud crack and then a ripping sound as if the earth herself were tearing apart.  This was not far from wrong, because blinded by the raging winds and snow the pair had wandered out onto a frozen lake and now, under their combined weight, the ice was breaking.  As one both man and wolf leapt as far as they could in the direction they thought should be the shore.  Though the effort was gallant it was not enough and Wolfegar and Silvermane plunged into the icy, freezing water.  Frantically both tried to climb back onto the ice, but it just kept breaking, time and time again.  For what seemed like an eternity they both struggled.   Hour’s later Wolfegar came awake; he was half frozen; yet somehow had survived.  Then he realized something was terribly wrong, wrapped around him was the body of Silvermane, as it had been so many times before, but this time there was no warmth, no movement.  In horror, the boy grown to a man knew the wolf had made one last superhuman effort and had somehow managed to get them both on solid ice.  It had then curled around it’s longtime friend to keep him warm, but in doing so exposed its own water soaked fur to the elements.  Silvermane had simply frozen to death, as with his last breath, he protected his “brother” from the very chilling winds that were killing him.  Tears ran down his face, as Wolfegar cradled the massive head in his lap.  His mind was frozen, he was numb all over, and not from the water, but from grief.  Somehow he managed to find a cave along the shore of the lake and drag Silvermane inside.  Somehow he managed to start a weak fire and then, knowing he would die otherwise, Wolfegar cut the wolf open and wrapped himself inside the still warm fur of his best friend. 

The next morning broke clear and crisp.  The storm of the day before was gone.  Alive, but still in bad shape Wolfegar crawled out from under the bloody furs.  He managed to get a small fire going again, but knew he needed food to survive, to give him strength to make it home.  “One more sacrifice, old friend” he said sadly as he cut meat off the corpse….”One more”.  It was the final sacrifice; the very meat and blood of Silvermane would be the difference to the man.

Little did he know when he managed to struggle to his village over the next week, the scene he would find there.  As he topped the hill above the village, for the second time Wolfegar fell to his knees and howled in agony.  His entire clan was gone, his mother, his father, all this friends.  In shock he struggled down the slope to the valley below and began methodically searching through the ruins.  The scene was one of total carnage with half eaten corpses, charred buildings, and ripped up wolves lying everywhere.  His mind could not take it all in, so in a daze Wolfegar sat in the middle of the massacre.  The next day he awoke and with grim resolve searched to find anything that could aid him.  He would track his people’s killers and destroy them all.  The path lead to a dark tunnel going deep into the earth and by this time Wolfegar knew it was gnolls he was following.  This must be their lair.  Even in his crazed state of mind, he knew he could not take them on alone, so Wolfegar began searching the surrounding countryside hoping to find a village nearby.  He did not have to search long for the great Barbarian City of Halas was nearby.  To this mass of civilization Wolfegar made his way.  He was lost, he knew no one and did not know what to do or where to turn.  He wandered the streets seeing things he had never seen before.  Finally, he found a spot where lots of people were gathered watching some type of fighting going on in a pit.  He found out it was some type of contest, and though the fights were loud and fierce, no one died, and wounds were tended to by a bunch of Shamans all wearing robes which looked alike.  As he watched he soon heard the name Wolves of the North, and saw that many who fought under this banner won their fights, and often easily.  A plan began to form in his mind.  If he could join this clan or what ever it was, and become one of them, not only could he learn how to fight, but perhaps he could get them to aid him in his revenge.

So it was that Wolfegar became a Wolf of the North, the Warriors of Halas, who fight for and protect the barbarian, people.  As his new life began and the days, weeks, months, and years began to pass, Wolfegar began to change.  He learned many knew things, met different people, and came to realize there was much more to the world than what he had known.  His size and strength served him well, along with his quickness and soon Wolfegar began to move up through the ranks of the Wolves.  His thoughts changed, and though he still plans on taking his revenge one-day, he now knows there is much more to life.  He has made friends, and now wants to become the best Wolf of the North there ever has been. 

He has become a seasoned Warrior, even managing to kill many gnolls, which brought him peace and a feeling of great satisfaction.  Many more must die before the blood debt is paid, but now it is not an all-consuming rage, it has become just one of many goals.  In the last few days Wolfegar has met a new friend.  His name is Joril Stoutbear, and Joril has taken Wolfegar on as an apprentice Fletcher.  This skill will prove invaluable for Wolfegar, used to hunting in wide-open spaces, has always known the value of a good stout bow and sharp arrows.  However, he has never known how to make them for himself.  Now not only is he learning a valuable skill, but one that might also provide him with a way to earn a living; and as he has found out in these more civilized lands that means a lot.

Yes, Wolfegar has changed, but late at night, if one watches closely, they will see him face the South.  Face the South were lies the warren called BlackBurrow and they will see his face grow stern and his eyes grow hard, they will pay, yes one day they will pay the ultimate price.
