KHELBE

Character Name:

Khlebe

Class:

Druid

ICQ:

NA

Background:

Khlebe watched his father, Talious GoldHawke, walk the walls of the Fort. “How strong he is,” Khlebe thought to himself, “Nothing can touch him, and I know they have tried.”

Khlebe’s father was a paladin of the Elven noble house of GoldHawke. Once the Family were the rulers of the large forests, but, like many in his family, love was the strongest factor in his life. He had often told Khlebe, “Love is beyond all, beyond person, beyond even race.” This was the philosophy that had been the downfall of his family. His sister, Amellion, was the first. After her graduation from bardic college she had fallen in love with a barbarian. She told her father that she cared not about his race; that his heart was pure. After her wedding to the man, she went to live with him on the plains, which had allowed the family to save some face. She had never liked being a noble. She was sad about leaving her family behind, but her love for her new family and friends was strong. House GoldHawke had yet to see the hard times that were ahead. With the death of Talious’s father, Quyinus, the family’s hold on everything started to fall.

While defending an elven village, Talious and his company of elven fighters had destroyed a gnoll onslaught that was also being hunted by a group of humans. After the battle as he was healing the injured, he met Alderia, the female cleric who was healing the humans. Just as his sword had pierced the armor and bodies of the Gnolls so did the beauty of the human cleric pierce his heart. He would have done anything for this woman no matter the cost.

Soon the lovers where wed. The noble houses of the Elves would not stand for the thinning of their blood, but the GoldHawkes had defended their homes too many times to just outcast them. So they did the next best thing. They took several families that had brought shame to the nobles and had them build a fort near the plains. “To protect all and show the power of the Elves” they were all told. Each of them knew why they had truly been sent there, but few cared; it was much better than their alternative.

Talious had found it fitting that he had chosen the plain. Knowing that both sides of the battle would be more at ease if he choose the exact location, he choose a place not too far from his sister, feeling it only fitting as he had done the exact thing he had chastised her for.

While at the fort, the couple was blessed with a child. When the child was born, the elven cleric who helped with the birth noticed a birthmark. The birthmark was in the location the child would have normally had the house tattoo of the golden sparrow placed. The mark was an oak leaf on top of a storm cloud. The people at the fort took it as a sign that the gods had not agreed with the thoughts of the Elves who had banished them to the plains for betraying their blood. They believed it showed that the gods as well believed in love over everything.

As the child, Khlebe, grew he was not allowed to learn the combative ways of his parents. Each of them knew the storm cloud and sign of the oak was the sign of the druids. His parents taught him the ways of the gods. Then one day when visiting his aunt, he met his cousin Crispinskyhawke. At first the younger Khlebe did not like his cousin. Khlebe believed his cousin to be too serious and too tall.

While the children where playing Khlebe had dared Crispin to climb a large tree nearby. Crispin climbed the tree with no problem yet on his way down, his hands slipped and he fell from the middle of the tree. Crispin was barely injured and could have easily been healed by his mother, but Khlebe was afraid. He knew his aunt had warned them both not to climb the tree. No matter who took the blame, they would have both been in trouble. Khlebe rushed to Crispin and laid his hands on him to check his wounds, when to both of their amazement, Crispin was engulfed in a pale blue light and his wounds healed. Neither said anything to their parents knowing that it would have revealed their misdeeds. What both did not see was that Khlebe’s mother was watching them from a distance, and was herself ecstatic by what she had seen.

Khlebe was an only child and the other children of the fort where training to be warriors, clerics, and even some rangers. He was only allowed to play with them for short times as they had their training and his family did not want him to see the violence. Several teachers in the way of a druid taught him. The druids would stop in on their travels and teach before going somewhere else, which gave Khlebe a wide base of knowledge to work from. After a few years of training, he was allowed to play more with the rangers than anyone else.

“Khlebe your mother requests your presents,” the voice broke his concentration. Khlebe turned to look at the young cleric his mother had sent to fetch him and bowed. He climbed out of the tree he was in and walked to the main heart of the fort. “Over here son,” his mother called. He entered the room where his mother was sitting with seven robed figures. As he approached, his mother his eyes carefully scanned the seven hooded people. Out of the hoods he only recognized one person. Larsk Juton was a friend of Khlebe’s father. He was a ranger and one of the protectors of Surefall Glade. In the space of honor, normally held by his father, sat another of the hooded members with Khlebe’s mother to his right. “Well young one, how do you fare today?” this man asked? “F..Fine thank you…. Ah..” stuttered Khlebe.

“Oh yes my boy call me Kalefao, Kalefao Pineroot. I am the head druid of these lands,” the old man introduced himself. “Your mother here tells me you have been training as a druid.” Khelbe nodded. “Then I have a question for you.” Khlebe looked right at the old man. He had always been good at riddles.

“If the Tree of Life has all races and all religions, why must each fight?”

Khlebe felt all the blood leave his face. This man, this druid, had asked the one question Khlebe had always asked himself. Khlebe almost fell to the floor, how could this man ask the one question he had never been able to answer. He opened his mouth to tell the man he did not have an answer but the words were not what he had expected.

“Just as the grass and the weeds fight for light and rain to grow each branch competes with the other to get the most nutrients from the roots, the most sun from the sky, and the most rain from the heavens, just to produce the sweetest fruits.”

Khlebe stood there sounding like the wisest man on the planet but looking like the fool of fools. He himself did not understand how he answered, but the men at the table just moved their hands to remove their hoods.

“Very good young one. Now why does the good always have to have evil and vice-versa?” the old man continued without even a breath.

Khlebe’s mouth still open due to his jaw dropping at his last answer again started the words coming from him as if on their own, “As a coin has face and a tail each can not exist without the other else the coin is a fake.”

For over an hour the exchange went on. Khlebe just stood amazed at his own answers and the depth of the questions. After it was over, each of the robed figures stood chanted a spell and the air was empty. Only he and his mother stood in the room.

After that day things where different. Khlebe was allowed to play with whomever he wished. He soon made a friend with a paladin in training named Samious Stongsword. One day Khlebe and Samious decided to go explore the woods near the fort.

“Khlebe wait up!” yelled Samious, “I cannot move as fast as you in these woods.” “Be quiet,” whispered Khlebe, who appeared next to him as if from nowhere. “We are close to the ogres.”

“Tu Late l’ttle one!” an ogre growled as he reached out with his club to knock his prey over.

With as much speed as he could manage, Samious pulled his short sword, and Khlebe started to cast one of the two spells he had learned. The two worked together nicely but even with teamwork such as theirs, they where way out matched and the both knew it. Khlebe saw Samious go for the ogre’s knee so he cast his small flame spell right in the monster's eyes. The fire bit into the eyes of the large beast and the sword bit hard into the knee. The beast fell and quickly Samious then removed the head of the monster in a mighty and desperate blow. Both looked at each other in disbelief and amazement. Suddenly the sound of laughter filled the air as from behind the two lads two more ogres and one female ogre mage looked on.

“That my son! You useless soft-skins!” The female yelled. ”Now pay you for that, yes?” She started to cast a spell. Khlebe tried to use a spell to effect her concentration, but she was too far into her spell for his effort to work.

A fearful skin-crawling scream pierced Khlebe’s ears as he watched Samious’ body shrivel and fall. Then to Khlebe’s horror it started to move again. Looking into the eye sockets he saw the lifeless movements and lackluster expression of a zombie. Before he could act, though, the group of monsters were attacked by rangers who had appeared as if from nowhere.

Khlebe, with speed born of fear, ran back to the fort. When he reached the hill by the fort, he realized something was wrong. The gate was open and there were no flags on the spires. When he reached the gates, several of his druid teachers and some of the rangers he knew met him. Khlebe saw around him the signs of death. Bodies and blood were everywhere.

“Khlebe there was an attack while you were gone. They all fought but there were just too many of them,” said one ranger.

One of the druids added, “Too many gnolls and then the hill giants did not help either.”

Khlebe had been taught to hate gnolls from birth, but even now he only disliked them. He only truly felt hate for the hill giants and the orges he just gotten away from.

Looking through the rubble he saw that even with the help of some druids, bards, and rangers that had come, the fort never really stood a chance. He saw his father’s sword shattered on the ground; his shield once shiny with the crest of a golden hawk, in slivers. He also saw his mother’s flail in pieces.

One of the rangers stepped forward, it was Larsk Juton. “Khlebe,” he began, “We lost several this day, including the old man. I know that officially the elves would still take you in because of your lineage, and the humans would take you due to your mother. But I would ask that you come with us back to Surefall Glade. Finish your druid training there and become a member of the Jagged Pine Folk.”

Khlebe knew that the Elves would never treat him as an equal, and no matter what he did, the humans would always think he was snobby. “To be a whole person the only answer is to go with you. I am half elf, and half human but I am a whole druid.”

You will have to return with one of the druids, as I have to take some bad news to your cousins. I will be glad to see you when I return in a few days.

Khlebe nodded. When Larsk returned, he learned that the gnolls had also slaughtered his aunt’s family. Larsk didn’t know if it was a related attack. Crispinskyhawke, his cousin, accompanied the ranger, and both he and his tall cousin followed the man to Surefall Glade, Crispin to be a ranger, and him to finish his druid training.
