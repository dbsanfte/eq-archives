Background:

I always thought humans were great folks. Ha! I was raised by a couple of ‘em. What else could I think? The Tathers were great people. Never found out why Mordant Tather opened a shop in Neriak, never really asked, though. His house sure beat the streets. The foreign quarter was tough, no place for a weak lil’ urchin like myself. He probably saved my life...he wasn’t the first.

Living with him in the foreign quarter was quite an experience. I became stronger (yes), wiser (perhaps) and bold enough to take what most could dish out-and I fell in love for the first time.

It must have been the crowds, the turmoil, the sheer mayhem of it all that lit the first spark of desire...the mere fact that there was something more-something bright, beautiful, and dangerously alluring outside the walls of Neriak. From living in the diversity of the foreign quarter I fell in love with my vision of the outside world.

The arrangement with the Tathers didn’t last. Tried to learn to be a tailor. Please! How could he expect it from me? “Moody” he said, “impulsive” he said, “stubborn”...ack! And then I started stealin’ from the customers...that didn’t help things. I was out on my ear fifteen years after he took me in. Said all debts were paid...my father couldn’t have expected more.

Out on the street...penniless, hungry. Fate smiled on me and saved my life once more. Rutraz gave me a job at the Maiden’s Fancy. Said I was a head turner. Never mind that I couldn’t serve drinks to save my life, he could make money off me regardless. I resented him every day for it.

There was one man, Tani N’Mar, a regular of the Fancy. He’d tell me stories of the world that would send my head spinnin’. Spent hours talking of the places he’d been, people he’s met. He genuinely cared. He paid Rutraz a severance and took me to the Hall of the Ebon Mask. I was saved again.

I began my education. Yes, the Hall used me...a little robbery, battery, extortion...whatever it took to keep the wheels of commerce turning. But I used them as well-immersing myself in the skills that would keep me alive on the outside, never losing my first love. And Tani was there, every step of the way...dishing out more kindness than any other of my people could manage.

Finally, after a time longer than suited me, I was tested by the Guild Headman, Eolorn J’Axx. Brutal, calculating, unrelenting-all are words too kind to describe the man. I passed the test, added to my scars and was ready to begin my journey.

Tani wished me a hearty farewell. I stopped to see the Tathers one last time...did I see pride in the old man’s eyes? But before I left Neriak, the display in a gem sellers shop caught my eye. What harm would there be in lookin’?

Therein I met a remarkable lady-Amberyl-A Teir’Dal enchantress of abundant wealth and a gem master of unequalled skill. We talked for hours. Her compassion and great spirit lent to my own strength. I realized at that point that the world is a place made of beautiful things and people. I understood that my survival had always depended on the greatness of others. Fate had graced me far too many times...I owed a debt. Others should know the same favors fate had bestowed on me.

Amberyl heard my words and understood. She told me there might be others with a similar hearts. With a smile she told me, “Seek my friend Crispinskyhawke. Talk to him, he might have work for you.”
