Background:

    Gildarion Greenarrowe looked out of the frostcovered cabin windows as he paced to and fro on the worn floorboards. A tiny shriek made him stiffen and rush into the adjacent room. "-I didn't say you could come in yet", the old wrinkled midwife said, trying to look stern as she swaddled a tiny infant in a blanket. "-She is a pale and scrawny looking thing", she continued as she handed the baby to its mother. Eirien Greenarrowe looked into the babies deep blue eyes as it stared wide eyed at her. She smiled lovingly as she said: "-She is small and pale but she has defied winter, hence her name shall be Niphredil."

    Niphredil was born in Surefall Glade but she moved with her parents to Kelethin at the age of one. She grew up learning the ranger skills from her father, Gildarion Greenarrowe, and the druid skills of her mother Eirien. Her father wanted her to become a master archer and fletcher like himself but Niphredil was too restless for fletching and much preferred her sword. It was agreed that Niphredil should study to be a ranger and she chose the long sword as her main weapon despite her parents having wished something else.

    As Niphredil was coming of age to begin study at the ranger guild, tragedy struck. Her mother was slain by Orcs. Her father was struck by grief and hunted down every Orc in Crushbone leaving Niphredil to mourn alone. She was totally deviated at first but soon began to recklessly kill any beast she could find. Her father eventually came to his senses and returned home to find his daughter blinded by hate and fear. He then decided to send young Niphredil to the rangers of Surefall Glade to study. Niphredil was first hurt that her father was sending her away, but her father told her a tale from his past. He had been married once before and had another daughter. They had been slain on an exploration to Everfrost in the far north. Loosing his second wife had been near impossible to bear. But he still had Niphredil. She was more precious to him than anything. He assured her and it was for her best that he sent her to Surefall. Niphredil marveled at the tale told and the strength of her father and consented. He made Niphredil swear to let go of her vengeance, at least for the time being, and promise to keep herself safe and study hard to become a ranger. Niphredil promised to do so, but secretly made an oath to herself to hunt down every Orc in Crushbone when she earned her second name. Until then she would study hard and help those in dire need.

    Niphredil arrived in Surefall with mixed feelings of grief, homesickness and awe. But her curiosity soon got the better of her and she studied hard, especially her long sword skills and she enjoyed the freedom she owned. She explored every nook and cranny of Qeynos and the surrounding plains and hills. However she never accompanied her ranger friends on their swims, for which she was much teased. Niphredil was terrified of water, almost having drowned as a child. She reached her 4th rank fairly easily as her restlessness struck again. She was bored with gnolls and began to sit around the gates to Qeynos listening of the tales of foreign lands.

    One fine day, as it was raining, Niphredil caught sight of a young druid helping people by the gates. Niphredil held her breath as she gazed at her. She looked exactly like Niphredil. Niphredil was intrigued and kept studying her intently. The young druid must have sensed Niphredil’s attention because she turned around and looked Niphredil straight into the eyes. From that moment on Niphredil just knew that this young woman was her supposedly long dead sister. She overcame her fear and walked up to the druid and hailed her nervously. The rest was like a fairytale. Noor, as the druid was called, had felt the same for Niphredil and laughing and crying they found out each other’s stories. Niphredil and Noor traveled to see their father and the rejoining was a joyous and tearful one. Noor promised their father too keep an eye on Niphredil and the sisters swore never to let go off each other again even though their paths might go in separate directions from time to time.

    Since then Niphredil has traveled the lands of Norrath from north to south, west to east causing her family much worry. She is still restless, bound by her oath to her father as well as her own secret oath of vengeance, but she is eager to help friends and strangers alike and since she met her sister she often smiles again.
