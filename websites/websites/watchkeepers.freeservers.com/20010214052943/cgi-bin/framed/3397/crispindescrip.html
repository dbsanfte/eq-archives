CRISPINSKYHAWKE ANNAPURNA

Character Name:

Crispinskyhawke Annapurna

Class:

Ranger

ICQ:

1486099

Background:

"Crispinskyhawke! Come out of the rainstorm this instant." Crispinskyhawke opened his eyes. He was completely soaked from head to toe. Around him the Karana winds were pushing the rain across the plains. He turned to look at his mother, Amellion, standing in the doorway. Already he stood taller than her wood elf frame, and already his body hinted that he might even be a bit taller and stronger than the average half-elf was. He pushed brown hair back out of his eyes.

"But father said I should stand out here in the rain. It would help make me a stronger warrior." Admittedly he was a bit chilled, but he figured a warrior would have to endure these sorts of hardships.

"Crispinskyhawke!"

"Coming mother," mumbled Crispinskyhawke. He saw the lithe form of his adopted sister darting in the background. He knew then who had told on him. He privately grinned to himself; it looked like a tickle torture was in good order when they were alone. Crispinskyhawke trudged inside the small lodge. Amellion wrapped a bear hide around his shoulder and sat him down to eat some porridge. Amellion then sat back down with Genneth to continue their bardic lessons.

Genneth had first came to stay while her mother was in the city to help with the epidemic outbreak. Genneth's mother was a powerful bard and agreed to help. She sent Genneth to stay with Amellion who she had been friends with since bardic college. Her mother eventually succumbed to the disease herself despite her most powerful songs. Crispinskyhawke's parents immediately adopted her into the family and treated her as their own daughter. For Crispinskyhawke, she was a new person to help share his adventures.

He watched as his mother taught Genneth fingering on the flute. His mother had bright red hair and was petite like all wood elves. Her eyes twinkled bright green and showed wisdom, patience and understanding beyond her years. But her true power lay in the fact she seemed to know things before they happened.

The door flew open and a voice bellowed out, "Amellion! It has been a long day and I am hungry!" A tall barbarian dressed in leathers and chainmail stood in the doorway. His face was decorated with a blue hawk on the right side. He wore a polar bear-head cap on top of his head. Gondydd flung many lion skins on the floor by the doorway and hung his claymore up on the wall. Crispinskyhawke quickly and quietly slipped the fur off his shoulders so his father wouldn't think less of him. Gondydd slapped Crispinskyhawke on the back and sat down by him.

"Crispinskyhawke, my boy! You are growing to up to be a big strong warrior. Someday I take you back to Halas and train you in the Wolves of the North Guild there." Gondydd sized Crispinskyhawke up and patted him on the back

Amellion ushered Genneth to the table and poured the rest of the family porridge from the kettle over the fireplace. As she brought her own bowl, Gondydd grabbed her around the waist and pulled her to his lap.

"Ahhhh, my blossom I sorely missed you on the plains today. Tomorrow you must come with me." Amellion answered Gondydd with a kiss. Crispinskyhawke and Genneth looked at each other and rolled their eyes.

After they ate, the family sat off to the side of the house and listened to his mother play the flute. Once in awhile his father would bellow out a tune with the song Amellion was playing. Amellion just smiled and kept playing. Soon Genneth was curled up besides Crispinskyhawke asleep. As his eyes grew heavy he started at his father's claymore and dreamt how he one day would have a sword just as fine. Maybe even a claymore.

********************************************************

"Please stop!" Genneth cried gasping for air

"Nope. You shall pay for telling on me." Crispinskyhawke said as he continued to tickle Genneth. He had Genneth pinned on the ground and was tickling her unmercifully.

"Please. It hurts to breathe" Genneth laughed as tears started to pour out of her eyes.

"Say uncle"

"Uncle, please stop"

"Hmmm, I don't know"

"Uncle, Crispy please, I won't tell again. I promise."

"I suppose." Crispinskyhawke finally got up and stood off to the side. Genneth weakly rolled on the ground trying to get her breath back.

"So what have we learned here?" Crispinskyhawke asked peering at his sister.

"You’re mean"

Crispinskyhawke ignored the retort and looked at the horizon.

"What are you thinking? Genneth asked propping herself up on an elbow.

"I think I am going to prove to father I am a warrior today."

Genneth made a face, "Oh?"

"Yes. I am going to kill a young lion and skin it. Father and mother will be proud and make me a patchwork tunic out of it," he proudly announced.

Genneth wrinkled her nose, "With what? Father won't let you touch his claymore, not that you could lift it."

Crispinskyhawke's eyes twinkled. "I have a one."

Genneth sat up. "Where did you get one? Mother will kill you if she finds you with one."

"Follow me. I hid it nearby." Crispinskyhawke said pulling his sister up. He led her to a cluster of rocks. He knelt down and pulled one up revealing a long object wrapped in rat furs. With the greatest care Crispinskyhawke pulled the wrapped furs from their hiding place. Unwrapping them he revealed the rusty short sword.

Genneth smothered a laugh. "That is your sword?"

Crispinskyhawke looked hurt. "I had to kill a skeleton for this."

"Crispin, dear brother of mine. A lion will chew you up and use the sword to clean its teeth with."

"Fine, I will put it back." Crispinskyhawke wrapped the furs back around the sword.

"Come on Crispy, we need to finish our chores before mother and father get back."

"Ok."

**********************************************************

While Genneth got distracted cleaning the small cottage, Crispinskyhawke snuck off and went back to his sword. He had also put together a small pouch of food for his journey. He’d show his sister who could take a lion, and his father would be proud of him.

He followed the riverbank looking for footprints that looked close to a lion paw. Once or twice a bear or wolf would come near him but paid him no heed. Eventually he heard the roar of a lion. Crispinskyhawke crept closer to make sure it was a smaller lion. He took a sharp breath in excitement. It was a young lioness all alone. He scanned the area with his sharp eyes to make sure they were no other dangers around. He glanced back as the young lioness started to move away from him. Crispinskyhawke took off running after it. The young lioness went over the hill and Crispinskyhawke quickly followed it, closing in on it swiftly.

When he topped the rise of the hill, he took one look and tried to stop and start backpedaling. Lions. Lots of Lions. Big lions. A pride of lions.

He had already started his descent, though, and could not get his footing. Crispinskyhawke quickly slid down the hill grabbing the attention of every lion in the area. The young lioness he had been chasing whirled around and pounced on his legs. He had managed to hold on to the rusty short sword and slap the broad side of it across the lioness’ nose. The young lioness growled in surprise. Crispinskyhawke scrambled on his back up the hill as fast as he was able. One of the big lions came up and raked its claws on Crispinskyhawke’s leg, pulling him back down. Crispinskyhawke screamed in pain. Red blood soaked his leggings. He tried to swing the sword at the lion, but the lion just slapped the sword out of his hand. The lion then bit down on his sword arm, crushing the bones in the forearm.

Crispinskyhawke didn't realize it, but by this time he was screaming in continuos pain. All he could think of was that Genneth had been right, and he was never going to see his family again. He looked around to see if there was a way out, but the pride of lions was circling around waiting for some hidden signal to rush in and finish him off. He looked back and saw that the big lion had backed off and was going to let the young lioness take him. Crispinskyhawke groaned; he could see no way out of this and wished his parents could save him.

The young lioness took one step forward and then fell to the ground with an arrow embedded between her eyes. Another young lion was suddenly surrounded by plant life that had grown up around it, preventing it from moving. The pride stopped circling and sniffed the air. The air grew cold and still as a cloud formed overhead. Crispinskyhawke and the pride then saw the source of the disturbance.

The man stood tall in leathers. He was calmly slinging the bow back on his back and drawing a sword made of ebony stone. The first two young lions rushed him and then dropped dead as lightning came down out of the air and struck them. A lioness of bigger size came in behind the young lions to attack the stranger. She rolled by in a charred ball of fur as she was engulfed in a ball of flame. The big lion that had attacked Crispinskyhawke earlier decided to finish off the weaker prey first before dealing with the stranger. Crispinskyhawke tried to struggle out of the way but his legs hurt so much he could not move.

A stinging swarm of insects appeared and surrounded the big lion. It roared in defiance and rushed the stranger as if it knew he was the new source of discomfort. The stranger easily dodged the first blow and struck with the sword. Again the big lion tried to attack, but the stranger parried and struck back, tearing the lion's throat open and slaying it. By this time the young lion was free of its ensnaring trap but sensed it was outmatched alone and started to flee. The stranger, in a blurring motion, drew his bow and fired two arrows off, dropping the young lion in its tracks.

Crispinskyhawke was surrounded by tiny blue bits of energy that closed up some of the wounds.

"Rest easy my young friend. You’ve been through quite an ordeal," was the last thing Crispinskyhawke heard before drifting into blackness.

***********************************************************

He awoke next to a small fire and the humming of the stranger's voice. Crispinskyhawke looked over and saw the stranger making some arrows. The stranger seemed to sense his stirring and looked over.

"I am afraid I could not fully heal your legs there young adventurer," he said nodding toward Crispinskyhawke's legs.

Crispinskyhawke looked down to see many bandages wrapped around his legs. He looked at his arm and noticed it was fully healed.

The stranger spoke again. "I am afraid a ranger only has so much mana. I used what I had at the time to save you arm. Your legs should be fine, just badly scarred."

"Ranger? I have heard of rangers but never saw one." Crispinskyhawke said weakly.

The stranger chuckled, "Well lad we are around, just scattered about the land of Norrath."

"So tell me? Does such a brave lad have a name and a home?" the stranger asked.

"Crispinskyhawke, my father is Gondydd the barbarian warrior of the plains."

"Ahhhh, Gondydd. Been a long time. Well rest well young Crispinskyhawke. Tomorrow we shall take you home."

Crispinskyhawke drifted back off the sleep too weak to argue, but he had new dreams that night.

************************************************************************

Despite caring all the lion skins, a backpack of loot, and his armor and weapons, the stranger had no problem carrying Crispinskyhawke also. It was like he had the strength of ten men. To top it off, the stranger was moving at speed that he had only seen his mother run at while singing and was not even laboring with breath. Crispinskyhawke felt ashamed for being unable to walk himself, but he just wanted to be home safe again.

Very shortly the two reached the homestead of Gondydd and Amellion. Crispinskyhawke's parents rushed out of the house upon hearing the hail from the stranger. Gondydd gave Crispinskyhawke a stern look but said nothing; Amellion, on the other hand, burst into tears. She started to sing her healing song to soothe her son’s wounds all the while crying at the pain he had put her through. Through it all Crispinskyhawke fell asleep again after being fed broth and having his wounds re-dressed. Genneth pitched in and helped where she could. Crispinskyhawke was glad she didn’t say, "I told you so." She just looked worried and scared.

************************************************************************

The next few days Crispinskyhawke received lectures from Gondydd and Amellion about running off to do such foolishness. The ranger stayed awhile since it seemed he knew Gondydd and Amellion from earlier adventures. Crispinskyhawke came to learn his name was Larsk Juton. Crispinskyhawke sat around and listened to the old friends’ reminiscence about days past. Genneth doted on him, but was sure to remind him why he was being doted on. Crispinskyhawke ignored her the best he could and studied the ranger and listen to every detail he said. This was not lost on Larsk Juton or Gondydd who often furrowed his brow in concern. The next day Larsk said his good-byes and left.

That night the family sat around the fireplace working on sewing the lionskins into leather armor. Amellion was humming a favorite tune of hers. Gondydd was drinking his favorite ale out of a crude stein. Genneth kept poking Crispinskyhawke just for good measure.

Gondydd spoke up, "Crispinskyhawke my boy, I been thinking. After you heal up, I think we will take you to Halas and see what the Wolves of the North guildmaster has to say about training you in the near future."

Crispinskyhawke cleared his throat, "Actually I want be a ranger father."

Gondydd spewed ale across the room. "WHAT?!"

Amellion stopped humming and closed her eyes. Genneth tried to look as small as possible. Crispinskyhawke wanted to dig a hole and hide but knew he had to stand strong.

"You want to be a what!? Why not a warrior? That is all you wanted to be." Emotions raged across Gondydd's face as he tired to sort through them all.

Crispinskyhawke felt miserable for even saying anything. "Sorry father, I have been thinking about it, and I just feel more at home in nature. The animals don't seem to mind me, well except the lions. And I want to cast spells."

"So why not conquer nature then my boy. You are strong, brave of heart, good smarts. Besides warriors don't need spells. We are strong of heart and mind," Gondydd said, thumping his fist on his chest.

Crispinskyhawke looked at the floor in silence.

Gondydd tone softened, "Tell you what my boy, tomorrow I will let you practice with my claymore."

Crispinskyhawke looked up, his eyes brightening.

"Oh Gondydd, I am not sure that is wise." Amellion said in a soothing tone.

"Nonsense blossom, Crispinskyhawke is a strong lad. He needs to learn the value of a two-handed sword." Gondydd sternly looked at Crispinskyhawke, "And if he is going to sneak off with a sword again, I want him to be able to use it."

Gondydd then looked at Genneth, "You too Genneth. No children of mine are going to be defenseless in this world."

Amellion just smiled for some odd reason and let the matter go. Gondydd then picked the two children up and gave them a big bear hug. Genneth and Crispinskyhawke squealed and the rest of the evening was spent rough housing with Gondydd.

*********************************************************************

Crispinskyhawke and Genneth were spending the afternoon hacking each other with wooden swords Gondydd had fashioned for them. Crispinskyhawke’s was a two-handed one, while Genneth's was the size of a bastard sword. Gondydd and Amellion had gone to High Hold Keep to sell their leather armor to the citizens there. Crispinskyhawke had to swear that he’d stayed home while they were gone.

Not that he could go far, he muttered to himself. The scars on his legs were stiff and it was hard for him to walk, let alone run fast. Genneth hit up side his arm drawing his attention back to the battle at hand.

"Hah, won again Crispy. This is fun." Genneth said laughing gleefully. "I could be a warrior, too."

Crispinskyhawke rolled his eyes, "I was thinking about stuff that was important. Besides you can't be a warrior. You are going to be a bard."

Genneth open her mouth to retort and then stopped. Instead she said, "Greetings Larsk Juton."

Crispinskyhawke turned around with surprise on his face.

"Larsk! Good to see you...." Crispinskyhawke faltered. Larsk looked grim and very somber.

"Let's go inside children. We need to talk."

The bottom fell in Crispinskyhawke's stomach. He knew it couldn't be good news.

Larsk Juton sat the two children down and told them the bad news. Gondydd and Amellion had been ambushed by gnolls on the way to High Pass. All that remained was their bodies and Larsk had buried them where they lay. Crispinskyhawke felt his world come to a crashing stop. All he knew; all he loved was gone, never to be seen again. Then he felt something new. A burning hatred. A desire to see all gnolls die. He swore revenge on those who would take all he knew away from him. Genneth hugged Crispinskyhawke and cried. He hugged her back. They were all each other had left in the world. Her parents were gone and now his were, too.

The next few days Larsk helped the two put affairs in order and then escorted them to Qeynos. Crispinskyhawke said his good-byes to Genneth as she went to stay at the bardic college in the city. At the crossroads in the Qeynos Hills Larsk Juton stopped and lay his gear on the ground. He looked at Crispinskyhawke and studied him.

"Crispinskyhawke you are at the crossroads of your life right now. I know your father's desire for you to become a warrior at the Wolves of the North Guild in Halas. But without his support and recommendation they will not take you. Now the Knight's of Truth warrior guild will take you in Qeynos.

"Or there is a third option. Protectors of Pine in Surefall Glade will take you in. You have good aptitude with nature. I can sense this in you. I am going there now. My guildmaster has asked me to teach the recruits, and I would be honored to teach the son of Gondydd. The choice is yours"

Crispinskyhawke looked up at the sky and thought for a moment of all he had lost. He looked into Larsk Juton's eyes.

"I want to be a ranger."

*******************************************************************

Epilogue: As the two entered the glade of Surefall, Crispinskyhawke saw a silver-headed half-elf out of the corner of his eye enter the tree of the druids. Crispinskyhawke felt a stirring in his heart. He wondered if he would be able to meet her sometime and get to know the young half-elf.
