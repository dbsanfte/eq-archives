DOCCSAVAGE

Character Name:

Doccsavage

Class:

Paladin

ICQ:

NA

Description:

Not much is know of Doccsavage’s true origin. All that is know is that his father, Clark Savage Sr., washed up on the shore of North Ro holding his baby son, Clark Savage Jr., in his arms. Wreckage was found indicating they were on a schooner named the “Orion” the like of which had never been seen in Norrath before. When pressed for answers the elder Savage would only say that they came from another land and would say no more. It is also rumored that Docc’s father and mother where hiding out on the schooner from enemies and that Docc’s mother died during childbirth.

Clark Savage Sr. made his way across the land with his baby boy and learned all he could about this strange new world. He eventually ended up in the city of Freeport where he immediately sought out the Paladin guild at the “Hall of Truth”. Clark Savage Sr. entrusted the Paladin’s there to raise his son as true Paladin. He also had amassed quite a bit of platinum by selling strange devices that he had salvaged from his ship’s wreckage and used these funds to insure that Clark Jr. would have the best teachers available for the path that he had chosen for his son’s life to follow. The calling that the elder Savage set out for his boy to follow was to “fight the unjust”, “protect the innocent”, and rid this world of evil in all it’s forms.

Some wonder what had motivated Docc’s father to choose such a strange profession for his son. Was it guilt at a pass misdeed that he had committed and was seeking retribution through his son? Was it revenge on those that had caused them to go into hiding and transferring blame for the death of Docc’s mother? His true reasoning was never revealed. He placed young Clark in the Paladins care, sent various teachers in other vocations to the hall to enhance young Clarks training, then left to explore this new land.

Clark Jr. was a remarkable specimen. Even as a baby it was evidenced that he would grow into great stature and have tremendous athletic ability. He excelled at all aspects of his Paladin training: sword fighting, bashing, spell casting and became a master at all. But it was his excellence and joy at learning the healing powers and “Lay of Hands” technique that all Paladins learn that earned him the nickname “Docc”.

After growing to manhood, word came to Docc of his father mysteriously perished during an expedition to the dungeon known as “Runnyeye”. Seems Docc’s father was attempting to negotiate a “peace treaty” with the goblin king. Clark Savage Sr. was working for weeks on this attempt and as it seemed it would finally take place with a meeting with the king in the dungeon, it turned out to be a deadly trap set by the king which ended Clark Savage Sr.’s life.

Though he visited infrequently, Docc loved his father dearly and immediately set out for revenge on the king. He met a dark elf by the name of Krute who had turned his back on his kind’s evil ways. Docc and Krute fought their way into the dungeon and slayed the foul goblin king. Docc realized then that the profession chosen for him by his father was necessary to protect the innocents from the evil that was lurking throughout this land and he would now travel the land in search of such evil, wiping it from the face of this world.

Docc is a physical giant, 6’ 7” in height, 285 pounds he towers over most but is built of such symmetrical proportions that this is not readily evidenced unless he is standing next to something to compare him to. His skin is tanned very deeply from all his training and adventures in strange lands. This, and his stature have earned him the nickname “The Man of Bronze”. These features alone are startling enough until you see his eyes, they are like twin pools of gold constantly being stirred by tiny whirlpools. The likes of which have never been seen in this land except for Docc’s father.

Through Docc’s strange upbringing he has developed some traits, that to strangers, appear odd. Docc rarely will speak of his plans or his thoughts for fear that the information would put his friends in harm’s way. “Words have to be jarred out of Docc,” says his friend Monk Mayfair. For the same reason Docc travels alone most of the time and his whereabouts are unknown. But when evil rears its head or his friends are threatened, he will suddenly appear, as a “bronze juggernaut of vengeance” as one person described it as Docc saved them from the hungry goblins in the bowels of HighKeep.

Docc continues in his strange profession and the innocent and good of Norrath are thankful for it. Be it his strength as a warrior or his kindness and caring as a healer, all of the land is lucky to have Docc about. That is except the evil and cruel ones that lurk the land, many who cringe at the mention of his name. He has survived many surprise attacks on his person by these evil ones. “Get Savage out of the way and the going will be good,” some have said. This has been the downfall of many an evil-doer who thought such a way.

How a person can stand up to the rigors of such a life is testament to Docc’s stamina and drive. Lesser men would have been driven mad by this intensive training or perished from such a violent profession. But Docc is not a lesser man and will continue to fight the good fight until his final days.
