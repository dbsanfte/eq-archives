Laele's appearance
                
 
Laele teleported back to her room without warning. Malcron, perched atop the stately marble threshold to her chambers, gave a startled growl and readied himself for battle. She had caught him napping again.

"Some type of guard you are, my dear." she thought to him, a smile in her mind-voice. "Now I know why I must put magical wards on these doors also."

She moved her white mane of hair from one shoulder and summoned him down.

"You are late." he thought back simply. "What did you bring me?" His long, bat-like wings unfurled. He took a small jump from the edge of the carved marble and glided to her shoulder. Tiny icy claws scrabbled on the mantle over her bronze armor, his cool tail wrapping around her neck for balance.

Laele reached a hand up to scratch behind his ear, smiling slightly. Malcron was her oldest companion and confidante. He was a gargoyle whom she had summoned as a youngster when she had been first learning the rites of binding and control. They had become friends, and when her previous house had fallen, he had helped her to survive. Malcron stood under two feet tall, with a vaguely feline look, save for the long fangs and the two curling horns that sprouted from his temples. His body was small but powerful, made of the smoothest gray stone the color of charcoal.

"It was a busy evening!" she thought to him. "I joined a new Guild. It is called the Shadow Striders, and is led by that handsome pale one I was telling you about, Faileas...." She sent her memories of her joining the group to Malcron in a burst of information. "They seem to have similar aims to mine." She shrugged, making Malcron tighten his tail around her neck as he fought for balance. "As long as my studies can continue here with their aid and approval, I will be pleased."

She dismissed the wards on her chamber doors with a wave. The large dark doors opened silently to her rooms, and she walked in, Malcron on her shoulder.

"I am a little concerned about one of the members, though... a magicker named Malgorm..." She thought, her lip sneering unconciously. "He said I amused him with my desires."

Malcron laughed his gravelly laugh. "Be not overly concerned. From what you and I have heard of him, he is not in control of his desires, either."

Laele smiled. "Perhaps..." Her smile grew cruel. "I don't like being called amusing."

Just as the rest of the ruined house, her rooms were made of black marble, veined with crimson red. Laele had chosen this room in the otherwise abandoned ruin as her own, with the deliberate quality she used when she hunted.

It had been the room of the Matron of the Dark Elf house that had been destroyed in one of the Neriak House battles. It was one of the few rooms in the building that still had all it’s walls. Neriak’s Royal House feuds were bloody and well-known. Laele’s own House had fallen eons before, in her own far-distant land, Rilavuen in the Underdark. Having no House of her own on this world, when word came through Neriak that this House had fallen, she had known where she wanted to reside.

Here, in a place haunted with the souls of her enemies, no snooping people would come to bother her as she did her experiments... unless she invited them.

The house was a jewel in comparison to her other options; the inns in Neriak often housed the Pale Ones, the Trolls and the Ogres and the smell of those three races never seemed to leave the sheets, in her opinion. That, and the scent of their blood and skin within the air of the Inns; it made her vaguely uneasy… blood did that to her.

Much better to stay with the ghosts of the dead Dark Elven house, and her companion, Malcron, and to laugh at the ghosts as they bemoaned their torment at her residing there.

The style of the architecture in the mostly-ruined building was high ceilinged, regal and stately, rather than the ostentatious "palaces" many of the lesser houses opted for. The house did not cry out for attention with gaudy fervor... it demanded attention by its very silence.

Laele had decorated the Matron's chambers sparsely after she had moved in and removed the dust. The antechamber she stood in now was where she conducted her studies. A large slab of black marble sat in the center of the room, well-used shackles bolted to its sides. Wicked instruments of pain and torture hung, shining with lethal beauty, from the walls, between her bookcases. Sketches of the inner workings of the human body were scattered on many surfaces, most stained with drops of blood and other fluids.

She loved to work on Paladins.

Laele frowned and approached the marble slab. "Malcron, will you look at that!" she thought to him angrily. "Wael Rothe! I told him to scrub this surface down! There is still blood in the cracks of the marble!" Laele paused for a moment, the dark light of crimson growing in her gaze as a slow smile grew on her lips.

"Hmmm... he IS rather attractive for a slave." She said, tapping her lower lip with a long finger as she thought. Laele had purchased the Dark Elf slave at one of Neriak’s slave auctions. This one, however long he lived working as her servant, was paying off some gambling debts through indentured servitude. She had liked the way his eyes flashed when she ordered him around, like he was going to try and resist her… she had paid much higher than his debt to take him into her house as a personal slave after seeing that delicious spark of will.

"Malcron, I think it’s time to start training that slave who is the boss around here… I'll have to punish him. Remind me to do that later." She caressed the cold surface of the marble and the iron of the shackles with a fondness that bespoke often use.

Malcron’s mind voice, cool, and vaguely sounding like gravel, laughed in her head. "Only if I get to watch. He’s going to be a fun one."

Laele laughed with her gargoyle pet, and opened the door to her bedchamber. The room was as she had left it, though the sheets had been changed and cleaned.. (‘At least the Wael Rothe had been able to do that much,’ she thought to herself.) The bloodstains her previous bed-time companion had left in the bedsheets had had to be removed. It was not possible for her to sleep well with the scent of his blood around her. It made her too... restless.

Malcron's icy fingers gave the point of her ear a sharp tug. "Stop thinking like that." he thought to her. "I'm hungry."

Laele, trained to be the Quenar Dalharil of the lost house, would have killed any male talking to her in such a tone... but from Malcron, it was somehow endearing. The red in her eyes slowly faded. "Later, Milove. I have some business to take care of first."

Laele’s bedroom was round in shape, her previous house’s sigil glowing in red phosphorescence along the top of the walls and on all her furniture. She had enjoyed casting those spells to make all of Matron’s items her own, and to hear the Matron's lost soul scream in impotent rage around her as Laele marked her territory.

Her large canopied bed sat against the far wall, the door to the bathroom to the left, her armoire and divan off to the right. It was to the armoire she walked, unlocking the spell-wards with another wave.

Laele pulled out a crimson gown, one she had had made for her recently from the softest spider silk. At one of Malcron’s urgings, she had had it dyed with the juice of the blood-flowers that grew only in soil soaked with blood. (Malcron had said it would bring out the color in her red lips, and it did.) It was cut to accent her form, and was decorated with bands of black and royal blue for her dead house’s colors.

She told Malcron to perch elsewhere and he flew off with an impatient sigh.

She removed her bronze armor and placed it on the armor tree in the corner of the room. Her under robes fell to the floor in a soft heap. She stepped out lightly, reveling in the feel of the cool air on her naked skin. She lifted the new gown and pulled it over her head, letting the silk caress her bare curves deliciously.

Yes, this would be a good gown for hunting her evening’s prey… much better than the armor she could not feel her victim's body heat through.

Laele laced the dress up tightly around her tiny waist, and looked in her mirror. She smiled softly, her eyes glowing crimson in the darkness as she looked over herself with satisfaction. She stood straight and tall and gave thanks to her Dark God for her beauty.

She took out a cloak and hung it over her shoulders... Malcron would rip the thin silk to shreds if she let him sit there for long.

"Malcron, it's time to go." He gave another impatient sigh and flew over to perch on her shoulder. "Careful of the silk." she growled, unnecessarily.

"Where are we off to now, Laele?" he asked as he landed daintily.

"I need to pay a visit to Coalgrey... remember him? He's the one who liked to..."

"...I remember the evening well, Laele!" Malcron interrupted before her thoughts got too graphic. "I was stuck outside in the study all night, remember?!"

"Oh, yes." She thought back, unable to resist replaying some of the images of that bloody orgiastic evening for her own pleasure. "Well, anyway, he has been busy away from the city, and I just heard he had returned..." she smiled wickedly, showing her pearly fangs. "He should be at the Inn about now. I thought we'd drop in and pay him a visit."

Malcron sighed again. "Ah, so I should plan on sleeping in the study again,huh?"

Laele laughed darkly, feeling her body heat up with anticipation of the evening ahead. "If we don't keep you up with all the noise, Beloved, feel free to sleep!"

Still laughing, she reset the wards and teleported off to find CoalGrey.
