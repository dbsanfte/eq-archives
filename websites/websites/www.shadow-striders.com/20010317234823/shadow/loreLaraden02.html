The Past
part two            
Laraden landed hard after gating away from Sugan. he laid on the ground breathing hard. He stood up and ran for the hidden tunnels under Freeport. As he got in he began to speak to himself. "N..no.." he said as he fell to the ground trying to fight something. "I...i will fight you...", he continued as darkness began to swirl around his body, "I am no shadow." This he said before he blacked out......

He was in Surefall Glade again. He looked around confused, but he felt he was home again. The he saw her. Charity. She was a Wood Elf of supreme beauty. Laraden cared for her a lot. She walked over to him and rubbed him gently on the face. "Glad you are back, Love.", she said as she kissed him. He was dreaming. He knew she had died. She was the daughter of his teacher. he had died along side Laraden's father in the battle outside Surefall. After the funeral for the heroes, Laraden and Charity met and tried to heal each other the only way they could...Love. "I'm leaving for Faydwer today. I wish you could come too.", she said with sadness. Laraden wished he could have gone too. He knew what was going to happen. This dream plauged him for years. She gave him one last kiss before leaving with tears in her eyes. This was the last time Laraden saw her alive. Weeks past and Laraden grew very worried. She should have contacted him by now. One eve he couldn't stand it anymore, and he ran off into the night to search for his love. He searched day and night, for months. He could not find her. Then a few years later, he was fighting Gnolls their home of Splitpaw. He had just killed one and was resting briefly when he saw something that shattered his heart. The gnoll he had just killed was wearing the ring he'd given Charity as a symbol of undieing love.

Laraden woke up at this point. He still felt as he did before, in control. He fought off the evil somehow. Maybe the dream did it. He stood up and went about his business, but was in fear that the evil would try again. He let his emotions get the best of him. He was afraid of what he was becoming. Many times he had been taken over by the act. He first wanted to kill Malgorm, for revenge. But the events of the past began to eat away at his sanity. the only thing that has been saving him is the pain of memories. Memories of his lost love.
- Laraden Greywolf
