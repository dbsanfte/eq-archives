Avari
Tier'Dal Cleric
 
History: Dying and wounded she lay, this pathetic human was ready to die and i had the power to keep her alive...

A little mental efforts and she was healed, i dont know why i did that, something inside me told me i had to...even though the ways of my upbringing were not the same. They told me to heal only those that are worthy to the cause of hate, to the cause of innoruuk...and for a time i did just that.

My past is just a faded blurr now, i dont remember many details of before i joined the Wind Riders, i can now assume i was a murderer, what else do i have the potential for? Why would i feel so much anger toward all of my acquaintances? I was trained not only to take pain away from others, but also to endure it...and endure it i have, maybe too much, so much that I've gone soft...maybe i just can't take this anymore.

In my young adulthood, as i trained with the clergy I risked my life to help save the life of my friend, a friend who was innocent. When I discovered that a friend of hers was to betray and murder her, then acted upon it, i killed to prevent it, saved her life..even though she did not realize it. She thought that her friend was murdered. I, trained forever in the ways of hate, saved the life of another whom i did not even know...and from what I did know, she was a former enemy of the clergy itself. But I felt a sense of justice upon me, and prevented a wrong. There was something wrong...the only ones who knew of this betrayal were me, and the other who had organized the crime to murder my friend.

This deed made me question my soul and my beliefs. I then turned to following the ways of light, the ways of good and justice. And what did this bring? Well for a time it brought fulfillment, but then it also made me realize the light is careless, and ignorant, quick to judge. Really...what was it all for?

I joined the Wind Riders for a time, and was banished when she saw me, the same woman whom i saved the life of, she was a Wind Rider then also...but she saw me as a murderer. Being new to them, I lost everything, it was my word against hers. How could a newly guilded Tier'Dal cleric defend his word against one who was among their ranks for so long? As you may think, yes, I was branded a killer quite immediately. The only proof I had was from the one who organized the murder I had prevented so long ago...Envy T'Druis herself.

The truth, I thought, the truth will save my soul, Envy will give them the truth, tell them i was innocent, fine, I was willing to endure the price she requested for this. The price was a bond, I was to be a slave forever as a failed searvant of hate, and a failed lightwalker. My life was nothing anyways.

*laughs bitterly* and for a small deed *chuckles* I am now given more than i had ever dreamed. Respect. All for one easy little deed I was to perform for her (and to my amusement, made all the Wind Riders look like fools for trusting me a second time. Funny, none of them even shed a tear of concern when I was captured by the dark mistress...knowing full well of my innocence...). I owe Envy my life...either that or I want to take her life...yes thats it...no.

I have had enough of standards and expectations, it has only brought ruin to my life. You see now, the time for light and shadow is over. This is about me. With my new found freedom, I do what I want, when I want. I do what I feel. My current goals in life (whatever they happen to be, whenever they happen to be..) seem to fit in nicely with the motives Shadow Striders, I am grateful to be a part of them. I've not felt such a rush of pure power when Envy gave me my freedom, and my respect. Religion, philosophy...I assure you its all quite meaningless. We all die, and then get sent into that wonderful pit of non-existance...the gods know this, they see us as pawns of a chessboard, ready to be discarded at any time. It is all for nothing. Why should I plague my mind with guilt and conscience, when I can serve myself, and revel in the pleasures of life as I see fit?

And in my recent travels, I meet my alluring companion, Leiah. An old friend of my adolescence *smirks.* She Brings my unfulfilled lust to satisfaction. I've a bond with this necromancer, Leiah. She fills a hole in my soul, I would protect her and defend her with my life. I would kill anyone, even an innocent in her name, happily.

As it now stands, I am Avari. Just Avari. I live my life, I die. That is all. I just take what I can, do what I can, and enjoy it before I dissapear from existence.

In Death,

Avari
