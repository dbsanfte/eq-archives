The Past
part one            
Sounds of life envelope surefall glade. This is everyday life. The Rangers are practicing their archery skills, and Laraden begins his training as a Druid. He was so excited when he was told he'd be taught. He rushed to his mother and father who were very proud of their son. After his first day of lessons he told them all about it. He told them how he was able to walk up to a bear and feed it from his hands, and how he played with a wolf cub while it's mother was watching, amused. Laraden had found his place, or so he thought. A year later, he excelled at his teaching, only wishing to please his parents. That same pup that he played with was now an adult and running around the hills near Qeynos. He visited with his friends the bears and wolves often, and always had a tastey treat for the young ones. By now he had learned how to turn his skin to wood and to heal small amounts of damage. Skills which he loved to show off to his friends who were still working on healing scratches. Then the Blood Moon came. It's called the Blood moon because that's the night when all of the dead rose and attacked whatever they could find. This night the undead swarmed Surefall. The brave rangers, druids, and some warriors, paladins and clerics from Qeynos fought long and hard against the horde. Much blood was spilled. Laraden could hear the fighting from inside. He wanted to help but was affraid. He looked outside often to try and see his parents, but could never see them from where he was. His only relief was he could hear his father shouting commands once in a while and his mother casting. But he couldn't stand staying in his home while his parents were fighting the undead. He wanted to help. So he grabbed a club off the ground and ran to the battle. What he saw horrified him. Mangled and torn bodies lay across the ground. He couldn't tell which ones were dead or which ones were waiting for prey. He stepped catiously through the remains. He looked around but saw no one. Not even his parents. He wandered down the tunnel out stepping past more bodies as he went. He was crying by now. He had recognized a lot of the people on the ground. His teacher for one. A cousin. A friend. All dead. Then he approuched the opening of the tunnle to outside, and he was releaved to see his parents still standing. They had managed to push the undead out of Surefall but were doing badly now. There wasn't much left to the horde. A skeleton broke off from the group and wandered in Laraden's direction. Laraden looked at the skeleton frozen in fear. He barely muttered and cried for help, but wasn't heard. The skeleton stumbled to him and struck him across the face, laughing. Laraden wondered why the skeleton laughed, and defended himself. He fought with all his heart but he wasn't good enough yet. The skeleton had a clear advantage. He had lost his club and the skeleton still had it's rusty blade. He closed his eyes and waited for death. Then he felt someone grab him then a rush of blood all over him. He looked up to see who it was. His father had taken the blade for him, and stumbled to the ground. The Skeleton then exploded as his mother finished a spell. She ran to her husbands side and tried desperately to heal him. Laraden looked on in horror as his father bled to death before his eyes. She looked at Laraden, and in a look they both knew he was gone. She walked back to the battle to avenge her husband and father of her son. With her rage they pushed back the horde. The next day they buried their dead. At the funeral, pictures of his father's death plauged Laraden's mind. The only thing he could do was curse to himself. He looked at the faces of all those around. He saw the pain battle caused. In the crowd he saw a young girl, a wood elf. He had seen her in during teachings. She was higher in skill than Laraden so they didn't spend much time together. She was being given advanced training by the elders. She was as heart broken as he was. She had lost someone too. But who. The only other elf he knew in surefall was his teacher. After the funeral he went to talk to her. They spoke for hours, much time spent crying. He was trying to comfort her. And she did the same.............(To be continued....maybe *LOL*)
