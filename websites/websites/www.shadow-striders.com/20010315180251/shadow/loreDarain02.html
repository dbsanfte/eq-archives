The Failure of an ambitious Dark Elf
                
The day had begun like any other. Darain entered the world to cause havok among the light and to grow more powerful. Then, he heard that Shadow Lord Faileas had been captured. Now, he thought, now was the time to act. Now is my time, I will seize power. With the thought of grabbing onto even more power, he yelled for a revolution. Many a year, Darain had planed for a revolution, with the now absent Tajitz, his assassin Vaelanea, the warrior Maladan, the troll Traq, and many others, he had made a plan. Today would see his plan fail and his honor stipped from him. With his new recruit Dixtrin and his assassin by his side, he thought he would have sufficient support to take over. With a shout, he turned his back on the Shadow Lord and claimed revolution. In his way, stood the Dark Elves he so dearly thought would support him. Lady Laele was the first to speak against him, and with him joined others. Quickly, the situation turned grim as his assassin refused to support him and Dixtrin turned neutral. No longer did he have strength. His support gone, he turned into a trapped rat. To avenge his anger he attacked at his betrayer, his assassin. The battle fought long, but exausted from his failure to secure a foot hold for the rebellion, he was easily overtaken by the surerior warrior. With his dying breath, he cursed the Striders that opposed him, and Malgorm for stealing his warriors. Death took him, and then, by the will of the Dark Fther, he was ressurected. Burdened by failure and dishonor, Darain resigned his position as Lord and vowed revenge on those that stood in his way. Darain vowed the deaths of Laele and Envy, at his doing. Alone and unallied, Darain sought shelter, the first thought was the Darkness. There, he would be safe, for he was no longer in the shadows, but fully in the Darkness. The first to offer him aid was those of the Keepers of the Darkness. Quickly he joined, becoming an officer to his surprise. Now, he thought, now I will begin my revenge. The Darkness will guide me, and the fall of those who opposed Darain will learn that to look in the eyes of Darain, is to see the face of death...
- Darain Thex
