Journal Five
                
 
The Oasis

Deep in the desert land of North Ro, Malgorm had found water and a beauty beyond his imagination. He had gone there to be alone and find out what was going on inside him. As he searched the horizon memories of the past returned, far beyond his life here. Memories of lives long ago that brought him knowledge of what he, Azzothoth, was. Malgorm was no being created from magick, only his body was. His spirit was ancient demon.

He turned back to the pool, reached down and pulled the water to his mouth; sickened that his physical body needed water. Just as he needed Stormangell and Envy at his side. What was the feeling? For the first time he understood the pain he put Envy though.. and the pain she had put so many others though.. and how he had hurt so many with his evil. A single tear fell from Malgorm's eye.

"Nooooooo!!" He turned with the rage of a god and blasted down a wild cat of the oasis, a puma. But the death brought him nothing. Then he heard a female's voice. "Why did you do that?" it asked.

The wizard turned to see a lovely woman. The anger upon her face was so beautiful- her eyes, the fire behind them was stronger than anything he had ever seen. He lost his rage like that. "My lady I am sorry, I did not mean to hurt it. My power some times gets the best of me.. perhaps I could make it up to you?" That day he fought besides her, ridding the oasis of the orcs that plagued it. He was drawn to her by a power stronger then Bertoxxulous. Something that took hold of the soul he didn't have. That new hunger was Terina.

Vengeance

Envy kept her promise and lured Silverann to the sewers. There Malgorm was hidden with his magick. Upon the boy's arrival Malgorm blasted him with the power of his hell fire. The child's pain was much; he screamed but did not flinch like a child taking a beating. Malgorm taunted him. "What does it feel like to be so close to death Silverann? You chose not to accept the gifts of the curse. You chose not to accept me." With that, more hell fire bathed Silverann.

"Foolish child. I am your father, not Faileas.. and if Storm wants to stand against me then you will die. I gave him the power to be strong and he repays me like this!! And you.. you chose not to accept me. Well, you will accept my power or you will enslave your brother. Do you hear me child?!"

As Silverann sat on the edge of death Malgorm felt the pain he was causing his son. The pain ate at him while he heard the words of the hunger: "You are my slave, Azzothoth." That night Malgorm went back to the oasis in hopes of finding Terina. His thoughts were riddled with confusion. How could he follow the will of the plague and finish what he was to do with her here? He may be able to kill his sons and Envy, but how could he kill something so strong.. so beautiful.. so perfect? She had no weaknesses; the will of a goddess. Then a memory of the past entered his mind she was no longer a stranger.. Bertoxxulous had tricked Malgorm into serving his will. If he was just that, a slave, a soulless beast, then how could Bertoxxulous just leave him like he was before? Malgorm's pained words echo into the darkness, "I will never hurt her, Bertoxxulous!! I will never destroy this world with her here, do you hear me?! I will destroy you first…"

The Storm

Malgorm spent much time with Terina. He began to ignore the Shadow Striders and their plans. He hid from all of them in the oasis with his Terina; she cloaked him from all the evil. He could not bear to tell her who and what he was so he simply hid with her. Then fear struck Malgorm for the first time. Coming over the hill was Lord Faileas, looking menacing with his dark plated armor and Magical blade. At his side walked a dark wolf- his son Stormangell. Why he returned Malgorm cared not, he just did not want Terina to find out who he was. The fear froze him. Lord Faileas looked suspiciously at Malgorm and asked who she was. Malgorm stood speechless, but Lord Faileas listened as Terina spoke. In those few words Lord Faileas knew all. He looked at Malgorm and noticed the love and fear he knew Malgorm felt. He was not the soulless monster that killed and corrupted. Lord Faileas fought beside them and said nothing; he just watched Malgorm's actions. How he flinched when Terina was hurt. Lord Faileas left that day without a word to Malgorm, but the wizard knew that his love was intolerable.
