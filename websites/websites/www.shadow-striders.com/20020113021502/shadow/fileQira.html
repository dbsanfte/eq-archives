Qira
Teir'Dal Rogue
• 12/29/00
History: Qira pushed a wisp of her hair back inside her cap. Sweat beaded and rolled down her neck, falling from her cheek into the sand of Ro-curse the desert. Too hot here, and bright. She squinted at the fishing village ahead. The large barbarian fisherman gazed across the water as he waited for a tug at the line. His fishy, mannish stench carried on the breeze to her hiding place. She wrinkled her nose and began to creep soundlessly up behind him. In one swift motion she cut his purse strings and thrust the dagger into his back. She quickly pulled it out again, as the fisherman turned around, pale and stunned. He cursed when he saw her blue skin, gleaming in the sunlight, and punched her. Qira stumbled several steps back, but came running back at him. Blood trickled down his back, making a rich red puddle in the sand that drank it in quickly. He knew he was done for, and staggered away from her, mumbling prayers to Karana. Her blue eyes brightened as she finished her work.

Thunder rumbled above, and several travelers stopped wondering if rain might fall in the desert this day. Qira laughed at the sky, mocking Karana's anger. She examined the contents of the purse, a good take, but she would have to find a new target soon-word was spreading in the village and these barbarians were carrying less and less coin these days.

The water's edge was cooler, and Qira grinned. She stripped off her armor, leaving a pile of chain on the beach and dove into the water, reveling in the warm water on her skin. She dove as low as she could, where the water got cold. As she got out, she pulled on her armor, most of it gifts. She shook her head-men of all races would stop her to give her things, armor, weapons, sometimes just coin. And she always took it. After all, profit was profit.
