Joining the Wind Riders
OOC: Your Application
 
So you want to fight alongside the forces of good? We commend you... but first, know what you're getting into! Read this page thoroughly before clicking the application link at the bottom. Remember, alternate characters may apply to either Wind Riders or Shadow Striders, however they each must have an application to the appropriate guild. Alt characters are judged as individuals in every way. For instance: you cannot be married to two characters that belong to the same player.
  1. Go over this list carefully. Make sure you know what is expected of you. Look at the Frequently Asked Questions, then read our charter and guidelines.

  2. Fill out the Membership Application as completely as you can. Applications with no Character History will be ignored. Please give us your correct email address.

  3. Start Roleplaying a Wind Rider initiate. If your presense is known, you'll have a better chance of a quick invitation. With hands-on involvement you can generally expect a week's wait before being invited, as Wind Riders become familiar with you. Ask individuals if you can come RP with them, or join the "WRooc" channel and politely ask any WR or SS if they have ideas to get you involved.

  4. Once you've met a few people, get in touch with Sugan or the officers online. Only a few of us have time to search for applicants, so it will probably be up to you. Names of accepted characters are posted periodically for the officers.

Here are the requirements to be a Wind Rider.
  • A Good Heart at all times, both in and out of character.
  • A love of heavy Roleplaying. (never judge an enemy by what they "con" to you)
  • At least level 9.
  • A complete application, which is found at the bottom of this page.
  • Know a Wind Rider who can say they've met and approve of you.
  • Be independant and strong with a good reputation; a responsible ambassador of your guild who doesn't need constant assistance.
  • Respect and have faith in those in authority - They've already proven themselves.
Here is how to be a GOOD Wind Rider.
  • Keep up with the Wind Riders Message Board. Make a profile in the Roster forum.
  • Have a good back story. That space in the guild application is for a reason.
  • Contribute to the big story, and lend yourself to the storylines of others.
  • Update the information in your Roster profile regularly.
