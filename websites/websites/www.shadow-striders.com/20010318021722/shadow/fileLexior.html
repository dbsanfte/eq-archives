Lexior
Fallen Koada'Dal Paladin
 
History: A Pale Darkness.

A full bold moon rose in the night over Norrath to give the shadows a strange plane all their own. From the black depths of Faydark shone the silver city of the high elves like a well-cut jewel. A few torches twinkled along its streets in case they were needed, but all of the tall paneless windows of the city wall were like dark holes with everyone asleep. Except one. The light from this window gleamed dark red and danced to a silent rhythm.

The clear blue eyes of the lone Feir'Dal inside took on a glossy look as his thoughts were absorbed along with the rest of his belongings in the fire. Ritualistically he took another handful of parchments and keepsakes, and tossed them onto the flame. Sitting on the floor with legs curled against his chest, he observed with quiet amusement as they blackened and shriveled. Something on the top of the burning pile caught his attention. A girl's face in a miniature portrait of long ago was slowly being devoured by the hungry flame.

Without hesitation, Lexior Deraven thrust his hand into the fire and retrieved the portrait, burning himself in the process. Lexi cursed once as the flames were shaken off, and then smiled triumphantly at the half picture he'd saved. There was a slight scuffle outside his room as he took in the rough scent of burnt paper.

Lexi whispered a word onto the picture, silkily, as if he could breathe some sort of life into the girl by magick, 'Ayna...'

There was a jarring at the locked door. Lexi tucked the portrait into his vest and grabbed up the sword from the wall. With a smirk he kicked the burning pile into the draperies covering the walls. As the flames danced merrily across priceless ancient patterns, Lexior dropped silently from his window to the balcony below. He stood on the edge for but an instant, taking in the great height that ended in nothing but blackness far below. A light breeze played through the elf's silver locks, and he reveled in a private joke.

Only a month before, he thought, she stood here contemplating death. And the one with red hair... all because of Lightweaver.

With that, Lexi leapt far out from the balcony. Graceful as a sylph he turned in the air, diving almost silently into the dark water far below. He surfaced and looked back up to the knight's quarters, slicking back his wet hair. There were shouts from inside as Lexi's "going away present" raged out of control. He swam to a quiet spot and climbed ashore. As the few night guards ran to save Felwithe, Lexior Deraven slipped unnoticed out of the city.
