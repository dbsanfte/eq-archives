The Transformation
part one            
From his perch on the cave wall above, Malcron, gargoyle familiar to Laele, watched as his mistress's limp form was dragged, by the guards who had cut her down, to it's final rest. Moments before, Malcron's being had been little more than an extention of Laele's will. A physical embodiment of the spell Laele had used to summon and bind a familiar to her. Now, with the death of the one who had bound him, Malcron was experiencing an unfamiliar, alien, and not all together welcomed thing.... Self awareness.

How many years had Malcron served Laele as her bodyguard and companion, dutiful and, in some strange way, proud. Now his mistress, the center of his poorly scouted universe, lay dead below him. A most unusual feeling washed over Malcron, and his granite body shook with silent tremors. He had seen Laele do this.. What was it?... Crying? yes, that was it... Malcron was crying!

Muscles rippled, and powerful claws, hard as diamond, etched furrows into the stone wall as Malcron worked himself into a mad fury at his loss. What terrible irony that he could never experience how powwerful was his love for Laele until he had lost her... and lost her fully!

The fury build to unknown heights, and Malcron prepared himself for a final assault upon the waiting guards, not caring that the guards were waiting and ready. Wings black as night spread, the spiked tips quivering in anticipation of rending the guards below.

Suddenly, Malcron felt a presence behind him on the rock shelf. Whirling in fury, Malcron lept upon the invader, only to be stopped, suddenly and completely, by little more than the smallest gesture of his target. Mind blinded by rage and madness, Malcron fought against his bonds refusing to give in. Minutes drew by as Malcron struggled uselessly, the figure of his captor staring down on the scene below, Malcron all but forgotten by it.

Finaly, Malcron's blind rage ebbed, and he was able to take in his situation. With newfound caution Malcron looked to his captor, but even the gargoyles keen vision was unable to pierce the darkness that surrounded the form.

Suddenly, as if on cue, the form turned around, and Malcron was pierced by a fear he had never experienced. Malcron's newfound intelligence slipped away and he whimpered in total panic, as the figures laid eyes upon him. The eyes, searing pits of hatred, seemed to burn through him. In that moment, Malcron knew what it was to be helpless.

An eternity passed as those eyes swept over Malcron's body, staring deep into his newfound soul. Ever so slowly, the spark that was Malcron's self came back to life, feeding off the twin orbs of hatred before him. "L....Lord Innoruuk?", Malcron whispered. For, indeed that is who stood before him. His senses back, Malcron now remmebered when he had last seen the god.

Laele, proud and powerful, was no longer content to be but a pawn of her household. Lacking any other means of fulfilling her wish, she had sold herself into a pact with a new god, Innoruuk. Lord Innoruuk had promised wealth, power, and much, much more. All Laele had given up was her soul...

Innoruuk, the Dark Lord of Hate, stood now before Malcron, his face indecernable, but his eyes full of fire. "MALCRON", came the booming voice in his head, "YOUR MISTRESS'S TIME IS NOT YET UP!"

Malcron, who had watched as the guards, well versed in what they fought, had taken his mistresses head from it's shoulders, an act which sealed even her doom, was unable to voice words of his disbelief in the face of that stare.

A deafening rumble split Malcron's skull as Innoruuk laughed. "Come, pet... Learn what I have to offer...."
- Malcron
