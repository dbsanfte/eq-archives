SHAURI's SONOROUS CLOUDING

Level: 19

Effect: Invisibility, invisibility to most undead, See Invisible

Skill: Wind (Required)

Target: Party

Location: Erudin

Does not stack with: Any other song negates the invisibility

Taunt: N/A

Quantified: N/A

Analysis by Rokenn Swiftsong, Karana server

Comments: Group invisibility is nice. The radius of invisibility is somewhat small, so use with caution. If you are soloing, this song is extremely useful at getting through places like HighHold Pass, or  when you are walking through Nektulus Forest. In a group setting, this song is very useful when the party is deep in a dungeon, medding and healing, as monsters and trains will just walk right by you as if you were not there. This is a pulse based song. Every pulse, a skill check is made. If you fail a skill check, you become visible until the next tick (about 6 seconds). This is both a blessing and curse. The blessing is that if anyone in your party does something to break out of invisibility, like looting a corpse, or talking to a vendor, the player will turn back invisible on the next tick, even if the character is still looting the corpse, or still transacting with the merchant. This is a major plus over any spell caster based invisibility. The curse is skill check failures, which do not happen very often, but every once in a while, it will. 

Strategies:

[Mayner] Works good to avoid returning trains (you didn't start but passed right by you.) as long as no one hit any of the critters passing by they should not see you returning (once on agro list always on agro list).
[Kaearia] When looting your corpse, loot your wind instrument first.  Stop looting and start up this song. Finish looting your corpse.  The song will pulse and render you invisible while looting.  Handy if a train happens by.

Cautions:

[Draelon] If you use a Mino Horn for this song, holding another Mino Horn on your mouse pointer will cause this song to break.
Some con red monsters seem to have a greater chance of seeing past your song. One way to check if a monster will see past your song is to con it. If it does not con 'indifferently' then it may be able to see you.
This song will make you invisible to some undead, it may be dependent on your level vs the mobs level.
Some NPCs can see invisible, rendering any form of invisibility useless.
Lag and/or high packet loss can make this song deadly to use, as the server may think you are starting a new song on the next pulse making you visible.  This can be very bad deep in a dungeon.
It has been reported that some of the undead in Kithicor Forest can see through this song, this may be level dependent though.
[Ziann] With 2 bards in the group... if you get separated and both are singing invis you will go visible for a pulse when meeting up.  Also on the same note you can not have 2 bards singing invis at the same time as both bards will go visible on the song pulses.  This can be deadly when you are deep in a place like Mistmoore Castle.
You have to be careful about singing this song around group members with pets.  Summoned pets commit suicide if their master goes invisible. Charmed pets will break charm and agro on their former master.
[Kaearia] Any song will allow mobs to see you even if you are still invisible.  e.g. If one bard is singing this song, and another is singing Hymn, the mobs can see you.
[Kaearia] Bards "inherit" hatred from any party members because they are constantly "casting" a beneficial "spell" on them.  If you make everyone invisible from a train, and one of your party members decides to take a chance attacking a mob in the train, the instant your song pulses on him, the mobs will be aware of you and will attack you.  So this is an all-or-nothing thing.  If one party member decides to attack, you will either have to fight, zone, or die.  Make sure your party is aware of this if you are, say, keeping everyone invisible so half your party can recover from resurrection effects.
