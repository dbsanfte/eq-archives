At levels up to 15 you are a tank =) actually a damn good one. It's past that when the real tanks pull ahead. I'd imagine you listen to too many jaded idiots like that Liquidated guy. Enjoy being the tank while you can and get bronze/banded armor =)

The bottom line to being a "good bard" is to do what you are doing, being concerned with not being "bad."

Many on this board say a good bard needs to twist x,y,and z in a,b and c during j,k, and l. That's BS The most important thing is that you play the songs that you are able during the times they are most important.

To wit:

On a Nag raid there were 4 bards including myself. Our jobs in each of the 4 tank groups (3 tanks 2 clerics and a bard) was to provide as much protection as to prevent getting feared and lavaed to death.

My personal (and guild's) choice is to play both elemental and guardian rhythms while using the mistmoore drum. This brings my personal fire resist to 155 and my magic to over 255 (no special equipment) with the rest of the party at a similar level. Needless to say if I'm able to stay connected my group never gets feared and takes minimal damage from the breath.

During the fight I noticed another level 50 bard swinging his yaks and playing tunes like chant of frost... completely oblivious to his real role in the scheme. Yeah he had a high power system that let him twist songs in that Lag bath but they were the wrong songs!!!!

Another lower level bard I'd never had the pleasure of meeting before then could only manage one song. When I asked her what she played, it was elemental on the drums....The right song!!!! of the one song she could keep up it was the very best she could have used.

Now tell me who the better bard was.

So long as you realize that you are more powerful than your own skills, that your real power is in making others stronger, you'll be a damn good bard.

Cheers!
