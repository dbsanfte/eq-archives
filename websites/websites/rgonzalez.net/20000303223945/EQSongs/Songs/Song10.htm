ANTHEM de ARMS

Level: 10

Effect: Increases attack speed(10%), increases strength

Skill: Singing

Target: Party

Location: Freeport, Qeynos

Does not stack with: Chant of Battle, higher level attack speed-up songs and enchanter/shaman attack speed buffs.

Taunt: Moderate

Quantified: Atk Spd: 10%, Str: INT(Lvl/2) + 5

 

Analysis by Kaearia <Platinum Sphere> Karana server:

Comments: This is the "upgrade" to the level 1 Chant of Battle. In Party combat, the +Attack speed will make everyone swing more often and the +STR will help them hit more often and for more damge.  This song will be part of your standard lineup till 36th level when it is replaced by Vilia's Verses of Celerity.

Strategies:

If a party member is encumbered, you can use this song to boost their str and reduce the encumberance effect.  Chant of Battle will do this too, but most bards prefer Anthem over Chant and thus already have it memorized, and Chant requires you to equip a drum to gain approximately the same str bonus as Anthem.

Cautions:

If an enchanter or shaman is speed buffing your tanks (their slowest speedup is faster than Anthem), Anthem becomes just a str buff.  You may be better off singing Chant of Battle instead in these situations.
Anthem + Hymn are a popular combo at low levels. Unfortunately, this combo has a huge cumulative taunt effect. You will die often singing those two songs (but your groups will do great :).  A trick you can try is to not start singing or attacking until the other tanks have gotten the mobs fixated on them.  Also, the puller (first person to get the mob's attention) earns some extra hatred.  So despite how great bards are at pulling, you may be better off letting someone else pull.  The problem slowly goes away as the tanks gain levels and start to do more damage.
