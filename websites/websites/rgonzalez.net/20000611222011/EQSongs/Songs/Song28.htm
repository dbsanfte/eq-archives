CRISSION's PIXIE STRIKE

Level: 28

Effect: Mesmerize/Stun, lowers magic resistance.

Skill: Wind

Target: Single

Location: Kelethin

Does not stack with: Overrides enchanter's mesmerize line of spells

Taunt: Medium

Quantified: Mesmerizes the target for anywhere from 0 to 15 seconds and lowers the targets MR slightly. Using a wind instrument increases the magic resist debuff. High charisma helps with success rate and duration.

Notes: The secondary resist check makes this song nearly useless for crowd control at higher levels.  But the knockback effect will still interrupt spell casting and can be used to control mob position in tight spaces.

Analysis by Rokenn Swiftsong, Karana server

Comments: This is a single target Mesmerize song. If you have good targeting you can mesmerize up to 3 targets at a time (this can be very hard to do). 

Strategies

The knockback effect of this song is useful for controlling mob placement in areas with cliffs, bridges, etc.
This song is useful for interrupting NPC spell casters, for PC casters Bellow is a better choice since it is harder to resist.
When soloing you can weave with this with Hymn of Restoration, giving you a chance to solo high blues/whites.
[Lundriel] Targeting takes place as soon as the song is selected not when the song completes. As soon as the song is started you can go ahead and target your next mob. This makes mezzing multiple targets much easier. Of course if the mob resists then you will have to retarget it.
[Lundriel] If a mob resists I tend to go ahead and re-mez my next target. Since the song also reduces magic resistance as long as the mob is mezzed it is important to concentrate on keeping the mezzed targets mezzed before trying to mezz an unmezzed target.
If the first resist check is failed then the MR debuff has taken effect.  If the second check is failed the target is mesmerized.
[Berrymoor] The stun component definitely works against NPC casters.  As long as the "X eyes glaze over" message happens between the start of a cast, and before a spell goes off, you should receive a message saying "X spell is interrupted.  The nice thing about this is, the stun component works even if the primary mez fails.  I now twist this song over and over whenever fighting NPC priest or wizard types, and they rarely get any spells through, esp when clerics are stunning as well.

Cautions:

Any attack against the mob will wake it up, so be sure to inform your party when you do this.
[Baelin] If a NPC is DoT'd it will wake up whenever the DoT applies damage. 
[Berrymoor] Multi uses of this song on a single mob tends to build up the hate list to very high levels (on the threshold of charm).  After mezzing a target about 5-7 times, I've noticed that my enchanter often has to do a mem blur 2-3 times before the hate list is fully cleared, just like some charms.
