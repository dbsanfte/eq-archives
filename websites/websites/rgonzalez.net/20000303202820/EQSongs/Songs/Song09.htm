ELEMENTAL RHYTHMS

Level: 9

Effect: Increases magic, heat, and cold resist, and AC

Skill: Percussion

Target: Party

Location: Kelethin, Qeynos

Does not stack with: Purifying Rhythms, Psalm series

Taunt: Low

Quantified: Singing: R: Int(lvl/2)+5. Drum: R: lvl+6

Analysis by Rokenn Swiftsong, Karana server.

Comments: This is the first, best, and most general purpose of the protection songs.  It not only increases your chances of resisting fire, cold, and magic-based spells; even if you don't resist, you will usually take less damage.

Strategies:

When going up against casting mobs this song is a must.  If you are in a balanced party you can hang back and use your drum to save your group from a world of hurt.
Increasing heat resistance will lessen the damage from lava.
[Kaearia] This song is most useful against shaman, cleric, wizard, magician, and enchanter-type mobs since they use primarily magic, fire, and cold-based spells.  Purifying Rhythms is more useful against necromancers and some undead. Some casting undead are wizard-types, so Elemental Rhythms is more effective against them.  Shaman also have many poison and disease spells, so don't expect this song to save you from everything they throw at you.
[Kaearia] At low levels, if you have nothing else to sing (or have run out of song slots), the AC bonus of this song may make it worthwhile even when fighting non-casting mobs.

Cautions:

The boost to resistance does not make you immune to spells.  It will help you lessen the impact of the spells (less damage, shorter durations).
[Kaearia] As you gain levels and the resists from this song get better, several resist-increasing spells will not take hold until you stop singing this song.  Save your resist buffer some mana and make them aware of this before they start casting.
