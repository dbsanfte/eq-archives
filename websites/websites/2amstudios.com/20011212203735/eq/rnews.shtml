Othmir Ravage Norrath!
Posted Wednesday, November 28, 2001 by Raene


From Falqour, Spirit of Fire Guild



No one is safe!


In what could easily be described as the most odd event ever to transpire upon our beautiful homeland of Norrath, the Othmir, the once peaceful otter people who thrive on the beaches of Cobalt Scar, proceeded to venture forth from their seashell homes and lay waste to several Elven settlements, killing many.

The picture above was taken by a badly injured Half-Elf who barely escaped the destruction of Firona Vie, an outpost in the lands of Kunark.

According to eyewitnesses, the enraged otters swarmed the docks of the outpost, apparently having swam all the way from their Velious home. They quickly swept through the outpost, murdering Elf and Non-Elf alike.

Among the survivors was Wella Fargin, who worked as a teller at the outpost's bank. "When I first saw them coming, I was thinking, 'oh, how cute!'", said Fargin, "but when one of the funny-looking creatures bit the head off of a gnome who was trading with me, it was at that point I decided I should run."

Investigators who arrived in the Cobalt Scar region shortly after the attacks occurred were unable to locate any trace of the Othmir homes. Investigators decided to question a few of the dragons that patrol the area around the Othmir Cove. One Wyvern mentioned that the Othmir deserted their settlement nearly two weeks ago. When asked why, one drake reportedly said, "Long have we feared the coming of this day. The Druid mortals who delight in torturing the young Othmir have indeed brought this apocalypse down upon themselves, and perhaps the entire Elven race." The drake continued, "We've feared the awesome might of the ancient Othmir for millennia, indeed, they hold the power of the Gods. They won't stop until every last one of you manlings has paid the price for your unbridled arrogance."

Security in and around Greater Faydark is expected to increase dramatically in the next few days.

In response to the claims of Druid poachers inciting this particular disaster, both Tunare and Karana were sought out for comments, but both declined to speak on specifics, stating only that the actions of their followers do not necessarily reflect the opinions or doctrines set forth by either of the deities.
