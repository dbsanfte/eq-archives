Saladarsif

Written by Opafaf Darkenroot

The low lit room filled with smoke, the remnants of yet another attempt at research. The spell had not succeeded, but that did not deter the wizard. He had scrolls to make and not much time to finish his task. Once again, he was needed throughout the world. Again, the arcane master combined the necessary components, this time taking care not spill a drop as he had last time. Just one mistake in concentration had cost him those expensive components moments before. This time the spell is successful.

He scribes the spell and tucks it neatly into his pack, ready to be handed out to a chance passerby or perhaps someone in need. Gathering his gear about himself, Saladarsif Ceasareo prepared himself for his journey back to the lands of the wood elves, the Greater Faydark. With a slight hand movement and a muttered canto, the wizard faded into a shimmering pool of light, leaving his workshop again to it's silence.

Halfway across the world, moments later, a hum fills the air near the four great spires of the Faydark wizard portal. Salad (as his friends often call him) stepped forth from another pool of light, the twin to the one that had been in his workshop only moments before. Slipping on his magical Journeyman's boots, he raced toward the Lesser Faydark forest and his ultimate destination, Mistmoore Castle.

Mistmoore in notorious for its vampires and vampire initiates. A haven of ancient magic, undead, and evil. Today Salad was to help his good friends Gergely, Wyrrm, and whomever else might stop by in need of assistance. The task at hand was to hunt down the castle's servants and liberate them for a certain dark-elf only known as the Avenging Caitiff.

The Caitiff was the offspring of these ill-fated people. They were doomed to live life as vampires and wished them free. In return for freeing them and showing proof by returning to him the fangs of his dead parents, he would reward the bearer of said teeth with a blade of pure electrum. Salad was here to do just that for his friends. His friends had tried on previous attempts but had proved ill- equipped to deal with the magic that both vampires wielded. Salad was here to provide the necessary magical force, while his friends provided him bodily protection.

Entering the castle grounds, Salad soon found Gergely and Wyrrm waiting for him. They were sitting in the early afternoon sun sharpening their blades, dreaming of helping the Caitiff avenge his parents. The idea of that Electrum bladed Wakizashi was also a great motivator to the two heroes. The hellos were quick and the plan laid out. They would rely on stealth this day. Previous assaults on the castle had included liberating many more vampires from their tortured state. Today would be a fairly stealthy operation. Get in and get out without disturbing too many residents.

The legends were true after all. If you were not careful you would have the legions of hell unleashed upon your party and nothing short of a miracle would save you. Salad made it possible for the small party to see invisible objects to track one another during their insertion. The ranger camouflaged himself and Salad cast invisibility upon the Warrior and himself. Quietly they picked their way through the outer courtyards and to the castle gates.

Just inside the castle, the invisibility on the warrior dropped suddenly in front of a stone gargoyle. It sprang to life and attacked the surprised halfling. Jumping back, the stout warrior drew his swords and prepared to defend himself. Hearing the ringing of steel on metal, a nearby Usher from an adjacent corridor came to investigate. Seeing the intruded, she snarled in anger and rushed the little man. Salad and Wyrrm dropped back to help their friend.

Salad rooted the Usher in place before she could lay her hands upon his throat. Wyrrm snared the Gargoyle so that it could not run for others and the trio set about eliminating the stone menace. In short order, the abomination lay in chunks of rubble at their feet. The deathly Usher watched in frustration and seethed with anger at the three heroes. All thoughts of calling out an alarm fled her mind as she focused on killing the intruders. The adventurers prepared themselves and soon the magical root wore off. The usher came at the wizard first. After all, it was he who had immobilized her for so long. She would eliminate him and then the others. Her anger had clouded her judgement however, as she soon fell into the same trap as the Gargoyle had. Soon she lay in the rubble, breathing her last.

The Ranger called upon Tunare and healed the trio before they once again masked themselves in shadows. The rest of the insertion went much smoother. Past the Music hall, through a hall of ever- watching eyes set in stone they crept. Past the kitchen door and down the hallway into the area of the servants quarters. Scouting out the hallways, Wyrrm told the other two that they had a clear path to the room in which the Maid slept. The three of them slid down the hallway and entered her room. Directing the other two to handle the gargoyle, Salad prepared his strongest spell.

At the silent count of three, the heroes leapt from the shadows. The Ranger snared the gargoyle that stood by the maid's bed as a guardian. Gergely and Wyrrm tore at it's iron hide with steel of their own. The Maid heard Salad's incantation and woke with a start, preparing her own in counter attack. The Ice Comet hit the tortured woman square in the chest, freezing her to the bone. Her our counter attack hit Salad and he convulsed as a shock of force gripped him. Wyrrm, seeing the fight turn somewhat ugly, snared the fleeing maid to hinder her escape and jumped to help Salad. She turned on him and struck him with her arcane powers as well. Gergely held the gargoyle's attention and Wyrrm confronted the Maid as Salad readied one more spell. Another incantation and the Maid screamed in terror as the flesh was rent from her body. She slumped to the ground, breathing no more.

Gergely felled the iron watchman and the trio slumped to the ground in exhaustion. After a considerable rest and much recovery, Wyrrm picked himself up and set about the grisly task of post-mortem dentistry. Pulling out his dagger, he removed two elongated fangs from the maid's mouth as proof of the deed. He removed the wreath of nightshade hung about her neck and said a prayer for the departed.

So far their infiltration had gone unnoticed. It was not yet night and the full castle was not yet awake. Once again the threesome faded into the shadows and crept down the hall toward the bedroom of the Caitiff's father. Strangely this room had no guardian. The Butler slept alone. Wyrrm pulled out his dagger and crept forward, hovering over the bed, poised to strike the heart of the vampire and make it quick. As he struck, the Butler's eyes snapped open and stared into Wyrrm's. Glancing at the blade now buried hilt deep in his chest, the Butler laughed and grasped the Ranger's hand tightly. As he quickly rose, Wyrrm saw death within the eyes of the Butler. With his free hand the Butler touched Wyrrm's chest and magic poured forth. The force blew the ranger across the room, slamming him into the dresser. It shattered beneath his weight and Wyrrm slumped to the floor. Pulling the dagger from his chest as if an annoying toy, he advanced on the warrior and the chanting wizard. He threw the dagger at Salad clumsily, embedding it in the wizard's leg. The Butler then backhanded Gergely with his fist and knocked him back as well. Gergely stumbled and reeled with the blow, but it would take more than that to stop the halfling warrior. He lunged to face the undead man and placed himself between the wizard and the butler.

Salad had been caught off guard with the knife piercing his leg, but he regained his concentration and continued his incantation. As the little warrior moved between the wizard and his target, Salad let fly with yet another magical Ice comet. The force blew the Butler across the room, landing him next to the unconscious Ranger. The warrior launched himself at the fallen butler as Salad prepared another magical assault.

Gergely was too slow, however and the Butler rolled out of the way. He stood and kicked the small warrior in the gut, doubling Gergely over in pain. He lashed out again and caught the halfling on the chin with his boot. A murderous rage filled his eyes and he became intent on the short warrior. He would kill him and be done with these intruders. Just as he was to land the last murderous blow, Salad completed another spell and once again knocked the Butler against the far wall. The Butler had forgotten the wizard in his lust for halfling blood and it was his undoing.

Extracting the dagger from his leg, Salad laboriously removed the fangs from the inert Butler. Gergely bandaged Wyrrm's wounds quickly and drug him near the wizard. Gergely and Salad propped each other up as Salad chanted the words that would return them to the forest of the Faydark. There would be no need to sneak out of the castle. Getting in was hard. Getting out was a matter of words.

Once back in the safety of civilized lands the threesome planned their next outing. They needed to prove to the Caitiff that the deed was done. It would result in a blade each for the warrior and ranger. It was something they could not have done without the help of Saladarsif. They would rest and recover, then they would report to the Caitiff. Salad left the two recovering in an Inn in Kelthin and made his way back to his workshop. He was needed elsewhere soon, and he must rejuvenate while he had time.
