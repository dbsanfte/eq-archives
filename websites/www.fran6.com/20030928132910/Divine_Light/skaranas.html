South Karanas

This is an addendum to let travellers be aware of the dangers to avoid and the allies that exist.

Splitpaw Straightforward gnoll dungeon. The gnolls are slightly weaker than those at Black Burrow, however they are better dressed. It is not very crowded (hence few trains) and the loot is fairly good. Recommend the first level to characters of 8th level and up (in a group of course)

Brother Nash He appears to be the same as the other splitpaw gnolls, however he speaks of worshipping Rodcet Nife, and when conned viewed indifferently. I would suggest letting this peaceful creature be.

Undead Pyramid Populated by skeletons and zombies. I didn't get close enough to see if there's an entrnce to the pyramid.

Vhalen Nostrolo A friendly but sad hermit bard resides here by a pool. He has a quest for anyone who's interested. Strangely a gnoll spawns in the pool under the water.

Centaur Village The only one here that will talk to you is the leader Ulan Meadowgreen. The centaurs all regarded me indifferently, and Ulan sold some weapons, foodstuffs, and fletching supplies. Of course he also purchases loot acquired from splitpaw, but don't expect any great deals.

Treants Two magnificent treants spawn here ( I believe them to be mates). Unfortunately there was an evil dark elf party camping the spot and slaying the creatures, and I was powerless to stop it…..

5 Raiders 5 Raiders spawn here and "scowl menacingly". Best to avoid them, unless you want a fight.

Undead Ruins Populated by skeletons, and putrid skeletons, lots and lots of them.

Aviak Village A little reminiscent of Kelethin, without the hospitality. The only one who speaks to you is Krak Windchaser, the leader but he refused to sell to me. All aviaks looked "dubiously" at me but none attacked me. In case you're wondering aviaks are hybrid bird-men

Wandering Creatures South Karanas is populated by a healthy host of lions and elephants, the elephants are indifferent, but ive heard rumours that if you start killing them your faction can drop (hmmmmm). The lions are the same as west karanas except no young ones. The centaurs and aviaks both seem to patrol their respective areas, with the odd straggler far from home.

The biggest thing to watch for are the cyclops. Either theres two walking around, or it's the fastest creature on two feet. He doesn't appear to have a territory, pretty much the whole of the southern karanas. Lots to see and do here.

Good hunting and may you leave no trail as you journey. The Father of Storms watches over you….
