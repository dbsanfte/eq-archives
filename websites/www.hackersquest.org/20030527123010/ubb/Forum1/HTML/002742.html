Experience questions answered

Here are some basic constants

Class modifiers

War, Rog: .9 for level calculations, 1.0 for experience awards
wis casters: 1.0
int casters: 1.1
monks: 1.2
hybrids(sk,pal,rng,brd): 1.4

Race modifiers

Halfling: 95
Hum, eru, elf, def, hie, halfelf, dwf, gnm: 100
barb: 105
ogre: 115
troll, iks: 120

Hell modifiers:

lvl 1-29 : 10
lvl 30-34: 11
lvl 35-39: 12
lvl 40-44: 13
lvl 45-50: 14
lvl 51: 15
lvl 52: 16
lvl 53: 17
lvl 54: 19
lvl 55: 21
lvl 56: 23
lvl 57: 25
lvl 58: 27
lvl 59-60: 30

Group bonuses

solo: 1
2 man: 1.02
3 man: 1.06
4 man: 1.1
5 man : 1.14
full : 1.2

Group dist

your level / (all group members levels summated (inclding yours))

ZEM (Zone experience multiplier)

most outdoor zones: 75
most dungeons: 80
some kunark/velious dungeons: 85
underused zones (kedge, hole): 100
regardless of zone, lvl 1-6 you get a ZEM of 100


Formula:

exp needed to level (lifetime):

(lvl to attain^3)*Class mod*race mod*hell mod


exp needed in a given level:

(lifetime exp to level x) - (lifetime exp to level x-1)


exp given by a mob:

(mob lvl^2)*Class mod*ZEM*group bonus*group dist


pets:

If any single pet can do > 51% damage to a mob, he will get half experience. This will rarely happen in a group unless you are using some badass charmed pets. It *does not* matter who gets the final blow or anything else you hear, its just tied to whether or not a pet does more than half damage. I've not tested this in a group setting so i do not know whether he takes half of total exp, or just half of his owners exp.


examples:

lvl 30 DE SK needs 41,580,000 exp lvl 1-30.
((30^3)*1.4*100*11)


from lvl 29-30 dor DE SK you need: 7,433,400 exp
((30^3)*1.4*100*11)-((29^3)*1.4*100*10)


a DE SK killing a lvl 28 mob solo outdoors gets 82,320 exp
((28^2)*1.4*75*1*1)

a lvl 31 DE SK in a group of lvls 29, 30, 38 killing a lvl 35 mob in a dungeon will get 36550 exp
((35^2)*1.4*80*1.1*.242) .242 = 31/(31+29+30+38)


from here you can extrapolate many quantites (kills to level, time per kill, time to level). If you take exp to level for lvl 1-2, 2-3....59-60, you will also see why hell levels exist, just a mathematical effect of the way they chose to manage these equations.

any other questions?

Zombie 		posted 05-23-2001 05:31 PM         
That's correct... the experience modifiers were not eliminated or changed as a base... the only change was that class modifiers were given a +exp % depending on class.

For example:

Human Warrior gets 100,000 exp for a mob

A human Shadowknight would get 140,000 exp for the exact same mob

A troll Shadowknight would get 140,000 exp for the exact same mob

The Troll getting the same as the human SK is due to the fact that there is no bonus for RACE differences. You still take a "penalty" for being a high powered race (Iksar, Troll, Ogre). Class penalties were compensated for by giving additional experience to classes with a previous "penalty". The "penalty" is still there, but it is nullified by the "bonus".

I have two things to add. One is on the pet taking experience. I don't know how it works for you guys, but I'll tell you how it works for me. If I have two people in a group, and one of them has a pet, and the pet does more than half of the damage, ONLY THE PET OWNER gets his experience cut in half. The other guy still gets as much experience as if there were no pet. Doesn't matter who gets the killing blow. I tried that exact scenario just because there were so many different opinions on the subject, and I wanted to know how it REALLY works.

The other thing is the formula for splitting experience between people of different level in a group. I have not yet figured out anything more than a two person group, but I can tell you how it works for two people.

If X is the amount of experience I would have received solo, the amount I get when grouped with AnotherGuy:

Exp = X * (MyLevel + 5) / (MyLevel + AnotherGuysLevel + 10) * 1.02

The 1.02 is the grouping bonus.

For example, if I am level 8, and grouped with a level 6, I get 55.25% of the experience I would have gotten for the same mob solo. That's 13/24 * 1.02 He gets 46.75%. And yes, those do add up to 102%.

This exact scenario I have actually tried and verified.

Following the same formula, if you are level 6 and you group with a level 11, you will get 41.55% of the experience you would have gotten if you somehow could have killed that mob solo. Seems way out of proportion that you would get almost half of the experience, even when you're barely more than half their level. But... we don't make the rules, we just play by them.
