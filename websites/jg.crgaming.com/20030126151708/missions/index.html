MISSIONS

There are a number of different missions available for the pilots within the JumpGate universe. They all provide a means of gaining not only experience and credits, but also political faction amongst the different parties of the Reconstruction initiative.

To get a mission you must be docked at a faction station.  Stations with no faction alliance (such as Lothars Landing) cannot give missions.  But these remote outposts often pay higher rewards to people who do missions to them from other stations.

Once you have clicked on the mission generator module in the station, you can then select which station you would like to fly to.  You will gain faction for the destination station that you fly to.

Once you have picked your destination you can then select what type of mission you would like to do.  Keep in mind no matter what mission you pick the mission will not be completed till you dock at the destination station.

Some mission types will offer an internal choice of activities to complete them.  Normally the first option is the easiest to do, with the the last option the most difficult.

Once you have accepted a mission you will be penalised if you fail it or if you change your mission.  You will lose 1 point of political faction for failing the mission, but more importantly, you will also reduce your reliability (note, you can check your reliability score by adding your player to our player list).  It is important to keep your reliability as high as possible as you receive higher cash payments for your missions if you have a good rating.

 Mining Missions
One of the most basic missions around, these missions tend to be good for making money more than experience. The mission is basically to find an asteroid, mine it for the required metals, and take them to the facility you assigned your mission to.

The challenge with these can often be finding the right asteroid type. Some asteroids are only common in far off regions, and it can be time consuming to find the right one unless you have a Rotacol installed and a location to fly to.

Some asteroids are more difficult to mine than others, and are better left till you are higher level, or better equipped.

One common way to make large amounts of money is to mine overnight with a larger cargo craft such as a Transport or Cargo Tow. Of course the dangers here are pirates looking for an easy kill, or the ever unpredictable conflux.
 

Cargo Missions

These are one of the more complex missions in the TRI universe.   The basic aim is simple, take the cargo needed to the destination.  The trick is firstly, finding the cargo needed, and more importantly at the best price.  Sometimes this will mean a long search, other times you'll get lucky and find it quickly.

But of course after you find the goods needed you will also have to have enough money to buy them!  Don't do a cargo mission for goods which cost c30,000 each if you only have c10,000 in the bank.

After you have the minimum number of goods needed take them to the destination and sell them there for your payment.  The more goods you take, the more exp and money you will make.  This means its not really worth doing cargo missions till you get a decent transport or a cargo-tow.

With cargo missions the rule is simple: you need money to make money.  The more you have, the more you will make.
   

Transport Missions
Another of the more common missions done, transports provide a backbone for the everyday pilot.  A simple mission really, carry a cargo container from one station to another.  In return you'll receive your payment.

Each cargo container takes up 1 cargo hold vacancy, allowing for you to often increase the overall credits made on the run with a spot of quick mining on the way.
 

Patrol Missions
Patrol missions are easy, and excellent if you have a fast ship.  Simply travel through each sector listed in the mission, and terminate your patrol at your destination station.

The downside to these missions is that the patrolled sectors are often quite a few jumps away, but they do leave you with more cargo space for some mining if you are so inclined.   Most patrol pilots are more concerned with a fast clean mission though.
 

Combat Missions

What could be simpler than shooting down a few conflux?  The problem with combat missions is not the shooting down of the enemy, but rather how many it sometimes takes.

This mission normally has 3 options.  Destroy a large number of C1 Squids (or higher); Destroy a couple of Snail's or Manta's; or Destroy one Kraken or Eel.

These missions are normally time consuming though, but are safe in that even should you die to a flux, the mission will not fail.  You simply have to keep killing the flux until it is complete.

These missions can be fun if you and some friends go on a flux hunt, but often take too long to be as rewarding as other mission types.
 

Scouting Missions
In order to complete these missions you must have a sensor or camera installed on your ship.

Scouting missions normally involve investigating an object deep in space.  These include anomalies, space stations, and more.  In order to take a new shot you must travel at least 100 metres away from the target each time.  This means you cannot simply stand still and snap all 30 shots at once.  Even so these are a fairly easy mission to do once you get to the object requiring scouting.

These do not require any cargo space to do, so mining can also be done before reaching the destination station.
 

TRI Missions
From time to time the TRI will require the services of pilots for critical missions.  These could be as simple as killing conflux, to transporting goods. 

Completing these missions will also aid towards the completion of the TRI reconstruction initiative currently in place. (You can view the progress of this on our news pages.)
 

Faction Missions
Each faction is always looking to advance itself, and requires the services of its member pilots in order to do this.  You can only perform faction missions to the primary station for each faction (i.e Octavius can only perform faction missions to Octavius Core).  You can perform faction missions for any faction, not just your own.

As with TRI missions, the missions themselves vary each time depending on what is currently needed by the faction in question. 

Completing these missions will also aid towards the completion of the faction reconstruction project currently in place. (You can view the progress of this on our news pages.)
