The raids I go on typically have someone who can feign pull so I kick back as a warrior and wait for the fight. A non-feign puller may work but you must advance in short bursts so as to not pull more than one chunk of giants. I trust the feign pullers and they make life a lot easier. The first wave you have to take dead on, the most you will get is 4 giants, provided you didn't run back into the hallway. I have heard that one group can take all the fire giants, something I hope to try ;). The only hint I can give you on this is that there was an enchanter and a bard in the group <wink, wink>. I recommend 3-4 good solid groups to do this safely. Just take it in very short spurts and don't advance until you have enough mana to win 3-4 giants.

 

 

Lord Nagafen

 

 

There I am in my spiffy crafted suite. Check out my jacked out hp for this rush with a set of dragon claws. I got the full lvl 50 cleric line from Nalas and Xyltan and the shaman stamina buff. On top of that we got all magic resist buffs and a lava shild PLUS we had Aite the bard was playing the elemental resist song. The stats on the left do not reflect the bonus for the bard song. With the song my fire resist was well over 100. The front line consisted of Myself, Borgh (lvl 50 Ogre Shadow Knight), and Mugaaz (lvl 50 Troll War).

 

 

 

The Plan

You must plan for waves of attack for tanking.

 

Here is how this almost flawless raid went down:

 

The Group I was in:

Tank Front Line

2 WAR - Tank

1 SK - Tank

2 Clerics - Complete Heals

1 Bard - played mana song and an elemental resist song

 

Clerics received clarity from other groups.

 

We were sent in as lambs to the slaughter and we were fine with it. We had a second and third  wave of Rangers, Pallys, SK, Monks, Rouges, Druids... waiting to jump in and bail our butts out if things turned nasty. Up until this raid death was a factor you just wrote into the trip.

 

So, we open the chamber door.

 

BANZEYE!!!

 

It's on...

 

BAM! Red Dragon directly ahead. I flank left and try to find a spot good for hacking. Nagafen is HUGE. I mean big BIG. Lets out a horrendous shriek and has a wicked set of claws.

 

The first wave front line (the group I was in) rushed Nagafin and stood toe to toe with him and smacked him around. Dodge treated us well as many strikes were avoided. The tanks did a great job of juggling who was taking damage and when. If we got down to 2 bubbles of health we would get a complete heal . Our clerics meded way back outside of fear range and got up when our bars started to fall. Seeing as we had 2 clerics, on could be meding while the other got our wounds.

 

Now, since we are holding Nagafen still, casters are running up, taking a shot, then getting away before any attention was drawn on them. Also, the casters gave the tanks a long time to get Nagafen mad at them. I believe almost 1/4 of the dragon's health was down before the first shot was ever fired.

 

I recall that a shaman and an enchanter were advertising the state of the shields and resists. Toward the end we were getting low on mana but the battle was nearly won, so the call for the second wave to come in was called. This gave me precious moments to retreat from the front line and get a complete heal before going back in for the last stretch. It was a sight to see the last 1/4 of Nagafens health drop off as all the parties rushed in for the killing blow.

 

 

Here we see the mid section of the beast as I steadily hack away. Even jacked up on AC buffs the dragon claws still got me for 165 damage a round and I could swear he has a triple attack. The massive protection we had on for fire resists seems to have paid off this time, as the lava breath didn't outright kill me like my last venture here. A word of warning: you may be standing right next to Nagafen and still not be hitting him, look around for a spot you are getting damage at.

 

 

Total fight time: 2-3 min.

Numbers lost: 4

 

Keeping a solid front line is essential so that the casters don't get summoned. It gives them time to do there work of dot's, DD, and shield control. A good group of casters needs to work together to know they are not doubling up dot's or debuffers.

 

The 4 we lost could have been avoided. If you rush a ticked off dragon you stand a good chance to get hit for up to 250 damage several times in a row. If you're not ready for that it can be quite a shock.

 

The relationship in the field is awesome. The tanks are the body, the casters the mind.
