Group Discussion

The perfect group, in my opinion, is a balance between tank, caster, and healer each with a clear understanding of their roll and duty. Below I will list some combinations that I have found very effective in the field.

The 6-member party

Control Group - Enchanter, Cleric, Wiz or Druid, 3 Tanks

This configuration can be tweaked by adding a cleric in exchange for a tank for a total of 2 clerics, 2 melee types.  This set up is one of my favorite set ups. There is nothing this set-up cannot do, within reason. The run down is the tank pulls, if there is one the tanks beat it down with mini bursts from the Druid /wiz. If there are 2 mob's the enchanter charms one and the tanks and the pet go to town on the remaining mob. If there are more than 2 mobs the enchanter mesmerizes all the mobs except the primary target. It is important in this scenario that everyone is assisting the puller so as to only attack one mob at a time. If you are not then the whole memorize tactic is pointless. The druid / wiz primary responsibility is to save mana for an evacuation and finish the mobs quickly in a crisis situation. At no time should the druid / wiz out damage the tanks thus bringing them to them. Wiz / Druid should not even begin to blast until the health bar is 1/4 down from full, and even then sparsely. Druids are good in the roll for healing and buffing as well, Wizards are great for the grand final blow.

Advantages: Total crowd control, evacuation, solid melee and caster damage

Disadvantages: None

Heart of Darkness - Necromancer, 3 Tanks, Cleric, Blaster Caster (Wiz, Mage)

I like the addition of the Necro in the groups to bring most fights down to half the time they normally take. There dots make a tough mob fold fast. They also have a great pet that can decimate a mob if equipped with a fine steal dagger and a summoned dagger. In this set up the tank hold the mob still for about 1/4 of its health then the necro lays in with the dot's. The blaster is there to clean up and the cleric keeps the tanks up and running.

Melee Madness - Warrior, Ranger, Paladin, Rogue, Bard, Shadow Knight

A monk could easily swap in for the Rogue, SK, or Warrior (non healing melee). I was a bit skeptical on the effectiveness of this group formation but after camping the royals in Sol b for over 4 hrs with no casualties I quickly became at ease.  The beauty of this setup is that there is virtually no down time. While the non mana tank goes to pull the others med for kill. The ranger snares, the pally is primary healer with ranger and bard back up. SK can make a pet for a 7 man group. Bards mana song can charge the ranger/pally to heal quicker or is damage is spread out the healing song is just as effective. Pally and ranger buffs in addition to a bards quickness song make this crew a wrecking ball.

Advantages: No down time.

Disadvantages: Slow fights, lack of emergency healing.

Classic Party - Warrior, Paladin, Cleric, Mage, Wizard, Enchanter

This is a nicely well rounded party. Warrior takes the brunt of the damage with a cleric back up. Pally is in reserve for any casters who may take a beating. Mage has a pet for a 7-man party. Enchanter has the option for a pet for an 8-man party. War and Pally keep the mob occupied with melee. Wizard is in for mini bursts though the fight and a big burst for the finish, holing mana for an evac.

Advantages: Solid damage and support. Evacuation.

Disadvantages: This group is vulnerable in the casters. If a mob gets angry at a caster there is a narrow window to get the mob off before the caster dies.

The Buffer Band - Shaman, Mage or Druid, 4 Tanks

Nothing makes a tank tick better than a good shaman. STR, DEX, Stamina, AGI, Cold, Heat, Magic are all welcome additions to a tanks line up. The mage and druid in this configuration provide damage shields and blasts.

The Iron Fist - 4 Warriors, 2 Clerics (possible Bard substitute for a 1 Warrior)

This is a devastating assortment of melee might especially if the clerics are capable of complete heals and the warriors are padded with HP buffing items. Other Melee classes work in the iron fist configuration but the sacrifice in HP for spells makes it a bit less effective. The run down on this group is that tanks alternate who is taking primary damage. All tanks should assist the puller as each target goes down. The exception is if a mob starts to hit a cleric in which case a tank should taunt off the damage then return to the primary target. If you decide to add a Bard to the mix mana song is great for low downtime and battle songs boost the efficiency of the tanks well.

Advantages: If the warriors have bandages and can bind to 1/2 there is next to no down time for this combination. Also, if this group is surprised the tanks can afford good cover for a retreat to the zone.

Disadvantages: Cleric root is not that reliable to prevent runners. Due to the nature of melee the battles are not a quickly finished as they would be with a blaster caster. In a low mana, low health situation security is very low without any evac option.

The multi party

In large mission objectives there is a mentality shift that needs to occur in a few aspects.

1) Plan on dieing... a lot. Don't go if you cannot afford to die.

2) You cannot expect to gain a lot of experience. In some situations you may not even be in the fight per say but in a back up roll you are just important to the task at hand.

3) Realize your odds at loot are very slim. Most of the time before you embark on a large scale raid, get the story on what the loot situation is before it's rotting on the ground.

That being said lets talk about the dream teams.

There are some situations in EQ that are a high difficulty rating with 1 group one group:

Town Raids

Priest of Discord

Fire Giants

Lord Efretti

Nagafen

Vox

Sir Lucan

Fear

Hate

Sky

In these cases a multi party set up needs to be employed. The following is a dream team army that could rise to any occasion.

Forward Assault Team (4-6)

4 Warriors, 2 Clerics - Enchanter on the outside casting clarity

There should be a minimum of 2 of these group configurations. In a perfect world there would be 4-6 to allow for a rotation of the forward Assault so that waves may be sustained. The clerics should be kept back to avoid area spell such as massive damage (ice, lava) or fear. One cleric should be concerned with full party heals for the area spells the other on complete heals for massive damage the mob will inflict.

Flank Team

Mixed melee with Cleric and/or Druid back up

The flank team can be used to cover the forward assault retreat and can act as secondary damage dealers. Tanks with the ability to heal themselves should judge there assault waves so they can remain self sufficient in a worst case scenario. The reason I make a distinction between warriors in a forward assault and the other melee types is due to the flat damage some of the higher lvl mobs area spells do. The HP margin is the difference between life and death in many cases.  Other melee types are extremely valuable in that what they lack in HP they make up for in the ability to dish out hard damage fast. It is this reason that they must take caution and retreat often to mount other waves.

Medics (3-4)

Mix of Shaman Druid Magicians with 1 Tank protector and 1 Bard for mana

Even with 2 clerics the tanks will be suffering massive abuse and will need to beat a hasty retreat form time to time for a quick fix. Before the fight starts pick one tank and own him/her. Every tank should have a back up medic, more than one if possible. Select your tank before the fight starts and be prepared for heals. Shaman can be employed before the fight to buff the forward assault. Magicians should supply the lava shield for each tank up front, if no mage is available the druid line of damage shields will be fine. The bard is just juicing up the medics and in an emergency situation may be used as a group protector. The tank protector makes sure that nothing sneaks up on the vulnerable team and runs in to be a substitute if a member is summoned. Communication is key here so that redundant buffs will not be cast.

Shield Penetration (1)

Enchanter, Shaman, 1 Tank protector, Bard

This team has but one task, make sure the shields are down and resistance is low for the blaster group. Enchanter tashan and Shaman malaise should be timed so that there is never a time where resistance is at 100%. If the frontal assault team reports a damaged shield then cancel magic should bring it down. Both should report when the spell has taken hold so the blasters will know the window is open. Bard is juicing up the casters.

Artillery Team (1-3)

Wizard, Necromancer, Mage, Shaman, Enchanter, Bard

This is the team that will be inflicting the most damage most likely. To prevent being summoned or agreed this team should wait as long as possible for the tanks to tick off the target Mob. Seeing as the shield team will have already dealt with the resistance all mana can be routed to damage spells, be them DoT of a Necro/Shaman or the Direct damage from a Wizard/Mage. Necros and Shaman should be in communication as to what DoT's are covered.

Recovery (1-3)

Druid, Rogue

Death is an unfortunate occurrence but their needs to be a contingency plan if it happens. The druid / rogue team is good for a few reasons. In the cases where SoW can be cast it will speed up the recovery. Druid healing will be invaluable if the rogue gets caught in an area spell. The Rogue is well suited for this mission because he/she can run into the battle, take a quick poisoned backstab, target the fallen corpse, and drag it out on the way back to re poison the weapon.

Pet Brigade

On top of all this group work, each member should also make a pet and operate on a different level for pet waves. Keep all pets nailed down until called in for duty. After the initial wave pets should be used to cover the retreat of the forward assault as it rotates into the second wave.

Tanks

Monk - Master puller. No large expedition should be without the pulling power of a monk and feign death. Without a monk in Fear or Hate expect to die more. With the new feign death changes going in, it may be necessary for monks to camp if more mobs are pulled then anticipated.

Rogue - Backstab, poison, sneak.  Some may turn there nose at rogue poison but the fact remains that it will do extra damage. Backstab too can give an extra kick of damage. In some cases, sneak my go undetected where invisibility and camouflage may not.

Warrior - Crit hits, Holding the Mob's still. With the high AC and HP of a warrior, the most effective roll is to hold the mob still while the dot's work and the direct damage chips away at the health. Taunt holds the attention on the warrior and allows him/her to eat the damage.

Shadow Knight - Harm Touch, Pet, Clinging Darkness. Good melee damage with the ability to end a fight fast with an unresisted harm touch. Pets are good to pad a group provided it is not a primary tank, SK pets are better for collateral damage. If snare or root is not available the SK Darkness is effective in slowing or stopping a retreating mob.

Ranger - Snare, SoW, Healing, Bow crit hits. Good melee damage. Snare is the best way to insure the lack of runners in a dungeon. SoW for speedy travel. Healing in the field is always welcome.

Paladin - Healing, lull, vs undead. Good melee damage, great in field healing, and the ability to lull pull. Also a good ally to have in a fight against undead.

Bard - Utility. Songs for almost every occasion. Most commonly used: mana song, speed song, resistance song, healing song

Caster

Mage - Pets, damage shield, direct damage, summoning. Mage pets are a great way to pad a party. A very effective damage line of spells. Shield of lava, the most damage on a damage shield (25 pts per hit). Free bandages for healing, arrows for magic archery, food and water, daggers for necro pets.

Wizard - Direct damage, teleportation, evacuation. Largest direct damage spells. Great travel companions as well as party saves in a evacuation.

Enchanter - Charm, Memorize, Clarity. Total crowd control and the ability to speed up mana regen... yummy.

Necromancer - DoT's, Pets. Awesome damage over time spells and pets who can tank and do damage.

 

Healer

Cleric - Healing, Resurrection, HP Buffs, vs undead. Health, healing, more health! Keep you alive, the life of the party. If you die the one who brings you back. Always needed.

Druid - Healing, Buffs, SoW, Snare, Damage shields. Good healing and solid buffs. The 2nd best damage shields. Good all around character. Can do well as a healer or a buffer or a blaster.

Shaman - Master of Buffs, pet. No tank heavy group should be without a shaman. With there powerful buffs they can significantly increase the effectiveness of anyone. The Shaman pet is also very effective for both tanking and damage.
