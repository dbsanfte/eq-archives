BREAK IN STRATAGY

 

Left Wall Camp Option #1 - Clean Sweep

I felt horrible as I watched as 100's of naked desperate people ran back into fear only to be killed again, and again, and again. I wanted to post a plausible break in for future raids so that this might be avoided or at least so you know what to expect "should" be going on to break this zone. If you don't know what the plan is you stand a very real chance of becoming a lamb to the slaughter. In my opinion, the left wall camp is much more dangerous than the North Wall camp despite the fact that it is so close to the zone in point.

 

The Players:

 

Team 1 Recon:

2-4 Shadow Knights, Necromancers, or Monks

 

 

Team 1 zones into the portal shadow knights first allowing a minor tank buffer while the necros zone in. If there is immediate resistance the SK can train to left wall and FD there while the necros port in. Once the team is in the zone the 4 Necromancers face each direction (north, east, south, west) and FD to gather intelligence on the port patrols. This info gather potion of this method should take around 10-20 min to make sure there are no wild cards in the patrol. It has been suggested that 1 feign death person can supply the recon information but the more eyes the better.

 

Team 2,3,4 Safe Clean Up:

Druid

2 clerics/healer

2 tanks

Caster (at least 2 teams should have enchanters)

 

Team 5 Safe Retreat

Mix of SK, Monk and Necro

 

Once the patrols are timed out to afford a window of opportunity for the clean up teams there should be a contact on the outside that will give the queue for the outside teams to zone in. 4 well balanced teams should have no problem with taking down at least 1 mob before evacing out. This should be a druid due to the fact that they have a port that will quickly take the team back to the Feerrott portal and back into the break in. Once 1 mob is dead you will not have to deal with it again for at least 8 hrs making the next round (if needed) that much easier.

 

Evac should not be considered a last ditch maneuver but a regular tactic for the break in. As soon as either the Fetid Frenzy tower is agroed or you get more than 3 scarlings it should be automatic. If it is to the point where one is close to being killed (shiver back, the white spider, or the scarling) then that should be an objective before the evac. As a point of order, The safe retreat should hold off Feign Death (FD) until the call has been made and most teams appear to have safely gotten out. This provides cover for the retreat and also has the chance to keep a mob locked on them to draw them into the second round of clean up. FD should not be broken until everyone is back and ready to go, and even then only 1 person of the safe retreat team should break FD at a time to test the agro. In the case of an evac the safe retreat team could talk amongst themselves to test agro while everyone is gone. The important thing is to stay down while others are filtering back in.

 

 

This is by no means the only way to do it, but from a tactical approach is tight.

 

Left Wall Camp Option #2 - Naked Caster Break

 

This option have a very high fatality rate but if you just want to break the zone and don't wish to organize the clean sweep then this is another option.

 

Players:

A ton of Pet casters - Necro, Mage, Shaman

A few token Shadow Knights with a great deal of Feign Death experience.

Druids

 

Before this plan is set into action, all the casters bind out in Feerrott then proceed to kill themselves so that they are naked. This way even if they do die they stand to loose only experience in the attempt and not loose years of there lives worrying about retreating the corpses on the inside of Fear.

 

The Shadow Knights zone in and drag the surface patrol mobs down to the Left wall. This gives the pet casters time to summon there pets and get them into the fight. Druids take this same time to charm and Shiverbacks that may have come with the initial rush. Once the pets have engaged the creatures the Shadow Knights can feign if possible and regenerate health while lying down. While the pets spread out the tanking work the casters focus on one creature at a time to kill at least one mob before they die or can gate out.

 

Wash, rinse, and repeat.

 

Like I said, this method is ugly but in the end it can get the job done if you don't mind the cost.

 

 

KEY VISUALS

 

 

Here we see the portal that we zone into. This shout is taken from the perspective of the yellow house (scarling house) on the south wall (check the map above). It gives you a sense of how the port is up on a hill that can potentially be surrounded by 10+ creatures when you zone in. You will appear on the back side of this portal (not shown here) where you immediatly turn left and run down the hill to the camp spot.

 

 

Here is a panoramic view from the Left Wall Camp. If you look to the left you can see on the left wall is a house in the distance (Fetid Frenzy house) and beyond that, the fire wall that surrounds "The temple". The thing to keep in mind here is that if you are attacked by a scarling you may be ported over to an area near the distant house thus agroing all the creatures in there. If this dose happen to you it is strongly recommended that you stand still for 5 seconds, look all around to make sure nothing is on you, and slowly proceed back to camp. If you agroed the house then don't go back to camp. Actually, the opposite. Walk toward the house and eat the death as far away form the camp as possible. Then go get dinner and be prepared to wait a while for the camp to clear up to your corpse. This way you don't endanger your team and the odds of a corpse retrieval are much greater.

 

Toward the right side you can see the beginning of the hill that contains the portal on the top. Once you are safely in the zone do everyone a favor and stay away from climbing that hill for a long while as the patrol leads right up to it.

 

 

 

Here is a shot just demonstrating how tight the camp should be once on the left wall. The tighter the group the less chance you have of pulling in unwanted patrol creatures.
