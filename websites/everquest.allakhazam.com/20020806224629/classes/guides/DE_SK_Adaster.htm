Adaster's guide to playing a Dark Elf Shadow Knight


A little note before we go any further. This is how I choose to equip and level Adaster. This isn't how you have to do it. I just wanted to share with people the pros and con's of being a Dark Elf Shadow Knight. I spent a lot of time on the Internet compiling data. Most of what I have read is either out of date or just plain wrong. Two sights that I highly recommend are http://www.gameznet.com/eq and http://everquest.allakhazam.com. J.C. Smiths Shadow Knight guide rocks its hands down the best guide I have read to date. The only thing I disagree with J.C. is Dark Elf's make the best Shadow Knight's and we are way prettier then Trolls:-). Allakazams web page is the 800-ton gorilla of EQ web sites. If you have a question I guarantee one of these sights has the answer.

Having played a Wizard, Druid, and Enchanter. I decided it was time to take a stab at melee class. I considered a straight Warrior, but decided against because I tend to solo. So that left me with Rogue, Shadow Knight, Paladin, Ranger or Monk. I had no interest in being a Ranger because it was to close to playing a Druid. I have never had an interest in playing a Monk. Now with that being said. These last few months I have had the opportunity to group with a Monk. Monk's rock when it comes to Melee. Rogue's where still hosed when I started Adaster. So that left me with two choices. The classic battle good versus evil, Paladin or Shadow Knight. Paladins, do gooders, get good grades in school. Listens to country music. Goes to church on Sunday. They think Wild Turkey is something you hunt. They always help old ladies. Shadow Knights, evil mean creatures. Skip school to hang out in pool halls. Listen to rock and roll. They know Wild Turkey is something you drink. So hung over on Sunday they make their Gnome neighbors mow their yard. Rather push an old lady in front of a bus then help her on it. I chose a Shadow Knight. What can I say I'm evil baby!

Having chose a class, now I had to choose a race. Your choices are Dark Elf, Erudite, Iksar, Human, Ogre and Troll. Now at the time I started Adaster, Erudite and Iksar where not choices you could make. I decided against Human because they are as blind as a bat at night. It was hard not to choose Ogre or Troll because the starting STR is so high. But giving the fact the Shadow Knight's get a hybrid penalty hit on experience. And Troll and Ogre take a penalty hit for race I decided to go with a Dark Elf. I pumped 10 into STR, I put 5 into STA, 5 into Dex. Looking back I should have pumped it all into STR. But hindsight is 20-20. Adaster's naked stats are STR 80 STA 75 AGI 90 DEX 80 WIS 83 CHA 65 I hit enter and on February 26th 2000 Adaster was born. As you can see the biggest disadvantage to playing a Dark Elf Shadow Knight is you're a 98-pound Weakling.

Your first mission in life is to find your Shadow Knight guild master. This is no easy feat. Its about as far away in the Neriak Foreign Quarter as you can go. First off before you head to your guild master re-map your attack key. I moved mine to the forward slash on my number pad. If not it will only be a matter of time before you hit A and end up getting your head pounded in my someone 50 levels higher then you.

Ok now you're in the Shadow Knight guild. Give your note to your guild master. He tells you what a stud you are. You're feeling food. Now its time to go kick some monster butt.

Head to Nektulos Forrest the Dark Elf newbie ground. Start killing typical newbie monsters. Moss snakes, fire beetles, decaying skeletons, spiderlings, black wolves, shadow wolves, black bears, skeletons, large spiders, tree snakes. Keep killing until your level 5. Once you hit level 5 its time to head to Innothule Swamp.

Now I know plenty of you are saying why not East Commons or Lavastorm. The number of mobs in the swamp is unreal. You can't move without running into a mob its kill, kill kill, level, level and level in the swamp. In Lavastorm you have Fire Elementals that jump you while you're trying to rest. In Commons where bears and skeletons are harassing you all the time. No in the swamp choose a nice quite zone wall and rest there all day if you like. And you can buy and sell in Grobb. Stay in the swamp killing until you hit level 9 and then head back to Neriak. Remember while you're in Neriak put a point into Bash. You picked this skill up at level six.

Now that you're back in Neriak you need to buy your level 9 spells.
 

Disease Cloud
This spell will disease your opponent and does a small amount of damage initially as well. This is also a great spell for taunting a mob. It does very little damage but its great for taunting a mob of your healer or a trigger-happy mage. This spell is in my first spell slot.
 

Lifetap

Cast this spell on the mob it will take 5 hit points from the mob and give them to you.
 

Invisibility to Undead

This spell renders you invisible to undead mobs. Great spell and a must if you're in Befallen. And much later in Unrest and Lower Guk.
 

Locate Corpse
This spell will point you in the direction of the nearest corpse. Must people don't know Shadow Knights have this spell. So if you see someone /ooc for a Bard or Necro give them a tell and tell them you can locate corpses also.
 

Leering Corpse
This spell will summon a level 3-5 skeletal pet. You also need two bone chips. Now a lot of people will tell you your pet is useless. But I have also heard Druids tell me thorns are useless. It just isn't true folks. But after spending some time experimenting the other day I was shocked at the results I got using a pet Vs not using a pet. I will go into more detail about pets a little later. I highly recommend using a pet at every level. The only time I would recommend not using a pet is if you're in a dungeon and mobs are going to aggo it. For now think of your pet as a DoT. He won't do an enormous amount of damage, but he will do damage over time.
 

Sense Undead
This spell will point you in the direction of the nearest undead monster. Kind of a useless spell.
 

Siphon Strength
Takes 10 Str from the mobs and gives it to you. This spell I still use its in my second spell slot.
 

Now your ¾ Warrior and ¼ Necromancer. You're still a 98-pound weakling with mouse for a pet. Hang in their things will start looking up. From level 9 until level 20 is one of the hardest for a Shadow Knight. Get prepared to die a lot. This is your training camp so to speak. Tying to take a mob that cons even to you. Soloing will got you more experience faster. I strongly encourage you to spend at least half your playing time in a group. The higher level you get the harder it is to solo mobs. If you spend your first 30 levels soloing your not going to have a clue how to be a tank in a group. It's important to understand your role as a tank in a group. The other important thing is to understand how different group make up determine how you play a Shadow Knight. A classic example is being in Cazic-Thule in a group with out a Druid or Ranger. It becomes your job to cast Engulfing Darkness to snare the mob. If you don't your not gonna live in Cazic long or any other dungeon for that matter.

From 9 to level 20 there are some pretty important milestones you will hit. Level 10 you get Dodge. Level 12 you get meditate. You regain you mana back at a faster rate when you meditate. Now this is extremely important for a Dark Elf Shadow Knight. Remember we gave up STR and faster hit point regeneration for higher mana. You will be noticing a big difference at the rate your mana comes back when you meditate. Level 17 we get Parry. At level 20 three big things happen. First is the damage cap Verent put on us is lifted. Now your damage is determined by the weapon you have and your strength and level. You will see a big difference in the amount of damage you do. Second it you get Double Attack at 20. Third is you get to choose your last name.

At level 15 you get your second rank of spells. You can buy them in Freeport, Ogguk, Grobb, or Neriak. There are a couple of really nice ones here that let you reverse kite mobs. I will get to reverse kiting in a few.
 

Lifespike

Lifetap self only take 11 hit points from the mob and gives them to you. An ok spell.

Bonewalk
This spell will summon a level 7-9 skeletal pet. A key player in the reverse kiting technique.

Clinging Darkness
This is duration of time spell that causes damage over time. And works similar to a snare spell. Another very important step in reverse kiting.
 
 

Endure Cold
This spell adds to the targets resistance to cold. Comes in handy if your fighting mobs that can cast cold base spells. It will reduce the amount of damage you take. And you can cast it on other party members to help with buffing.
 

Fear
Fear will cause your opponent to turn and run away at full speed making it hard to attack at a dead run. However if you cast Clinging Darkness first your mob is slowed, then cast fear. Your mob will walk away allowing you to hit the mob from behind. The mob takes more damage with his back turned to you. You pet is beating the crap out of the mob. And the best part of all is YOUR not getting hit. AKA reverse kiting.

Mend bones
This spell allows you to heal your pet. A useless spell. Your pet is such a wienie very seldom does it ever survive a battle. And even if it does your so beat up the pet is a full health way before you.
 
 
 
 
 

Numb the dead

Numb the dead is an undead calming spell. By casting this spell on undead beings you will cause them not to attack players they would normally attack. It cost's a bone chip and can fail resulting in you training yourself. However if there is no one else in your group with this ability it can help you pull. I can't tell you how many times I died in Unrest when this spell fails.

Shadow Step
This spell is a quick casting random mini teleportation spell that will teleport you a short distance away from your current location. Use it to escape from a mob. Key words here RANDOM, meaning it can teleport you into an even worse situation. NEVER use this in a dungeon. It will make matter worse almost every time. The worst is no clue where your corpse is. It is very useful in dueling another player though.
 

From level 15 to level 22 you can have a blast in soloing Gators in Oasis, Griffawns in North Karana. Gnolls and Gnoll Reavers in East Karana. You can master your reverse kiting technique. A couple of thing about reverse kiting. First off, it won't work in a dungeon. The mob will bring back to many friends. The secret to soloing with a Shadow Knight is not to take damage. You don't have anyway to heal yourself. So master reverse kiting and find yourself able to solo a couple of blue mobs before you have to rest.

Bind wound is another very important skill to max early. It will allow you to bandage yourself back to half health. If there is no healer to heal you this can save your life many times over. And even if there is a healer bind wound yourself back to half health to save your healer mana. Spend the money on bandages and keep bind wound maxed. Don't throw your copper and silver on the ground. It's like throwing Band-Aids on the ground.

Weapons skills such as 1hs, 2hs, bash, 1hb keep maxed out. Keep a weapon of each class even if it's a rusty one. When you level switch though each weapon until your skill goes up 5 and then switch to the new weapon. Now early on it will be hard to do because you will level faster then you can max your skills but don't worry just keep at it. There are some really cool weapons coming out of Kunark for Shadow Knights. And if you don't keep your skills maxed it is very hard to make them up later.

Once you hit 22 it's time to head to Cazic-Thule and start on your Darkforge Armor Quests. Cazic-Thule will be your new home for the next ten levels. Do not go to Cazic thinking your gonna get your Darkforge Armor in a day or so. The only thing that drops Decaying Armor is Lizard Crusaders. To give you an idea if you spend a full 8-hour day in Cazic you might see 4-5 Crusaders. It won't do a whole lot of good to /ooc looking for Decaying Armor. The zone is full of SK's and people twinking SK's so very seldom will you get any help this way. That being said. When your group forms let them know what pieces of Decaying Armor you need. If you're the only SK in the group you should be able to loot anything you need. But say something right up front. I can't tell you how many times I have had group member's loot Decaying Armor because they want to do the quest and sell the armor. If you tell your group up front what you need most will be more then happy to help. A nice bonus to doing the DF Armor Quest is almost a half of bubble experience for each piece you turn into the Undead Knights in the Temple of Solusk Ro. The follow is a list of components you need for each piece.
 

Darkforge Helm: Requires one decaying, one decaying visor, two Freeport Militia Helms.

Darkforge Breastplate: Requires decaying breastplate, decaying mail, and two enchanted bars of platinum.

Darkforge Bracers: Requires left and right decaying bracer, broken Minotaur Lord Horn (Steamfront Mino Caves)*Note you have to do this quest twice. You only get one bracer each time you do the quest. Also the Minotaur Lord conned even to me at 29 and was beating the crap out of a level 30+ group.

Darkforge Vambraces: Requires decaying vambraces left and right, Qeynos Kite Shield. Qeynos Guards drop these. Easiest place to get one is guard Fredrick at the Wizard Portal in North Karana. He is level 27-32 depending on the spawn.

Darkforge Gauntlets: Requires left and right decaying gauntlet and one bar enchanted platinum.

Darkforge Greaves: Requires left and right decaying greaves and two Melatite. (Melatite comes from the clockwork gnomes in Sol A)

Darkforge Boots: Requires left and right decaying boots and two faerie dusts. (Faeries are in Greater Faydark. The dust you need is white not yellow.) Be very careful here. There are roaming level 45 guards here. Any evil race will be KOS here.
 

At 22 you get your third ring of spells they are as listed. You are becoming a formidable tank now. This coupled with the increase in hybrid mana pools makes you a one SK wrecking ball
 

Convoke Shadow

Summons a skeleton pet. Level 11-13

Dark Empathy

Single target crappy heals that you can't use on yourself. It takes hit points away from you and gives them to another player.

Deadeye

Self Only See Invisible, Infravision a very good spell if you're not a Dark Elf because it also gives you Infravision the ability to see better at night. The rub is its self only. If you are a Dark Elf you will still use the see invis portion of this spell. I'm sure our Human Shadow Knight brothers love this spell.
 

Engulfing Darkness

Upgrade to Clinging Darkness does about 110 damage over time and has a snare effect. Very useful spell. It does get resisted a fair amount of times. A must in reverse kiting.
 

Spook the Dead

Undead Fear spell very mana efficient. If your fighting undead this spell rocks combined with Engulfing Darkness, your pet and Ward Undead. Lasts anywhere from 3-18 seconds.
 

Vampiric Embrace

Works like a proc on a weapon it randomly goes off doing 15 damage at level 30. I cast this before every battle. It takes 15 hit points from the mob and gives them to you. Last about two minutes.
 

Ward Undead

Direct Damage Undead only spell. A must in Befallen, Unrest or anytime your fighting undead mobs.
 

From levels 22 to 29 you don't pick up any additional skills. If you stay in Cazic-Thule from 22-30 you should just about have all your Darkforge Armor. Some other great places to hunt are Lake of Ill Omen in Kunark, Warslick Woods, Upper Guk, Unrest, and Sol A.

When you hit 30 you pick up Riposte. And you also your 4th ranked spells.
 

Endure Disease
This spell will increase your resistance to disease. Used much in the same way as Endure Cold. Has a use in certain circumstances.

Feign Death
This spell allows you to play dead. It's important to understand when to use this spell. If a mob is casting on you wait until it's done before you feign death. Also try and cast it if the mobs are not hitting you.

Gather Shadows
Gather Shadows is an invisibility spell that can only be cast on you. I can't tell you how easy this spell makes your life. You can travel anywhere you want now. The rub is its self only. But you are one of the few classes that have invisibility and invisibility to undead. Very useful indeed in lower guk.

Heat Blood
This is a damage over time spell that does about 170 damage. It can be stacked with clinging darkness. Together a very lethal combination of spells. Reverse kiting just got easier.
 
 

Restless Bones
Restless bones will summon a level 16ish pet. Reverse kiting just got easier.
 
 

Wave of Enfeeblement
Wave of Enfeeblement is an area effect weaken spell. I have a hard time finding a use for this spell.
 

Lifedraw

Takes 42-45 damage from the mob and gives it to you. Very nice spell!
 

Life after 30 with a Shadow Knight gets much easier. I have to admit I almost couldn't get Adaster to 20. Shadow Knight's level slow. I really didn't enjoy playing Adaster until the high teens. Now though as we embark though our 30's playing him is a blast. This coupled with Verant doubling hybrid mana pools and I'm having the time of my life right now. My plan as of right now is to hang in Cazic-Thule until level 32. From there head to the Bedroom in Lower Guk. Then from there head to Sol B Tiger Room until I hit 45. And when I get bored checking out the dungeons in Kunark.

I spend a lot of cash on buying sow potions. I recommend investing in Invis Potions until you hit level 30. Some nasty deaths have come after going link dead in Cazic or Guk and nobody is around to invis you.

More on Shadow Knight Pets

By J.C. Smith

Some players will tell you that a Shadow Knight pet is useless. This simply isn't true. A Shadow Knights pet may be low level in comparison to that Shadow Knight. This is true. A pet does damage though. It may not be a lot of damage but it is extra damage during the course of a battle. When you take into consideration the amount of damage that your pet does compared to the amount of mana it took to summon him then you will find it to be very efficient when compared to other spells. A pet can also be used to save you a few seconds when fleeing the battle scene. Those couple of seconds could save your life. One thing that should be noted is that your pet will not make a very good tank. You should not allow him to absorb very much damage or he will die. One thing you should always do when summoning a pet is use the /pet notaunt command. This will prevent him from taunting and being killed.

Other useful pet commands include:

/pet follow me - This will inform your pet to follow you.
/pet guard here - This is a very useful command when fetching or when pulling to a particular location. Your pet will assist you in any battles which take place near him. After the battle is over he will return to the spot that you told him to guard.
/pet attack - This command will order your pet to attack whoever or whatever you have targeted.
/pet report health - This command will cause your pet to tell you what percentage of his total hit points he currently has.
/pet sit down - This command will tell your pet to sit where he is currently located. I have not found this to increase healing time in any way so I would recommend using /pet guard here instead or else you will have to issue this command after every battle.
