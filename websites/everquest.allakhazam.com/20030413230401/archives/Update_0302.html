    March 28, 2002

    We have a huge update to the premium service today.  We've added a number of cool new features to make your premium service of even more value to you.  Personally, I love the new wish list.  Well, here's the run down:

    • New Character Wish List.  This can be created using the Character Upgrade Search, or just by surfing through the items on the site.  As you find items that will improve your character, add them to your wish list.  (From the upgrade search, you can just check off a box next to the items and add them en masse).  Then, create a printable wish list and print it out to reference while in the game, or an online wish list with all the appropriate links.  The wish list report lists what your character is currently wearing by slot, and then underneath that the items you want to get, with all of the information needed to get the item, such as what zone it is in, what mob drops it, how much it should cost to buy it and more.  You can use this as a hunting list and include all the items you want to get from a particular zone or raid, or just in general.  You can also use it as a shopping list and include all the items you wish to buy, including the average price they sell for, when you go shopping at GFay, NFP, EC or wherever your server's auction zone is.  Or as a merchant, you can use it to create a sales invoice and list all of the items you have for sale with the prices you should get for them.  Here are some sample wish lists, generated in printable format and in online format.  For those who haven't seen how the character equipment search works, I also have a sample report for that at this link.
    • New Want to Buy List.  The classifieds now contain a want to buy list as well as a for sale list (of course there's nothing there now -- feel free to start adding to it).  Anyone can look through the list and sell to the posters.  However, only premium members can post items they want to buy.  The list of those wanting to buy an item is also listed under the item itself, similar to the for sale list.  (Click on "more" to get there)  Thus, premium members can now basically broadcast the items they are looking for to everyone who comes to the site (which is almost everyone who plays Everquest).  Just put up the items you want to buy, add a price you will pay if you want, and wait for someone to e-mail you with an offer.  As a seller, if you have an item for sale, your best bet is to check the want to buy list before listing it in the classifieds since that guarantees an instant sale.  Oh, if you had an item previously listed as want to buy, you need to re-list it.  So that this starts out clean, we did a mass delete of all previous want to buy listings.
    • Upgraded Equipment Search:  We've added a couple new features to the advanced equipment search.  The biggest is the ability to order items by a combination of factors, similar to how the character equipment upgrade search works.  So you can now ask for something like this:  Give me every armor piece a Dwarf Paladin can wear on his head and order them so that the ones that increase armor class and wisdom are at the top of the list, figuring AC as 3 times more important than wisdom.  Also, if you check off "additonal bonuses only" when you search, it will only bring up items that have the new skill and bonus modifiers.
    • More flexibility in adding and tracking items for your Character:  Premium members will now notice an extra set of commands under each item.  You can use that to add the item to your character's inventory, wish list, for sale list or want to buy list.  It will also tell you if one of your characters is wearing that item, or has it in his wish list or inventory, or has it listed in the for sale or want to buy classifieds.  This should make it easier to keep track of your equipment.
    If you don't yet have premium membership, maybe now is the time to get it so that you can take advantage of these great new features as well as all the previous ones like the lack of ads.  The cost is as low as $2.50 a month for a one year membership.  Click here to go to our secure server and get instant access to all of these features.

    March 05, 2002

    We've been trying to figure out the best way to handle the Stormhammer items.  We want to include them for those who play legends, but also want to have them basically disappear for everyone else.  Here's at least a start.  All legends items will be labeled and the default of every search will be to exclude them.   If you play on legends, you will need to check the include button on the advanced equipment search to have the legends items show up.  They will appear on the equipment by slot lists, but will be labeled so you know which ones they are.  We've also separated them out of the update, so that you won't be clicking on an item only to find it is a legends item you can never get.  When you submit an item from the Stormhammer server, please let us know if it is exclusive to that server or not.  So if, for example, you find new Umbral Plains loot while playing on Stormhammer, just submit it as Umbral Plains loot, but if you get something through a GM event or from one of the new zones, let us know that it is exclusive to legends (assuming you actually know).  Of course, this will all get really confusing when people start to quit Stormhammer and bring those items back with them and sell them, or if Verant subsequently moves them to the regular servers.  I'm assuming we will need to do this for quests, zones, mobs, etc. for Stormhammer as well.  Geeze, I'm already starting to hate this whole legends concept.  We'll keep fine tuning how this all works as we get more information.
