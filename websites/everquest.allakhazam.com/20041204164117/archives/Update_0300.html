    March 30, 2000:  At the request of many of the rogues out there, I have added a follow-up poll to the last two on classes and grouping.  Also, since some people were somewhat confused on the platinum based auction having dollar signs at various places, I went through the auction code and changed most of the places I could find from $ to pp.  (And believe me that wasn't easy since the $ symbol is used as a coding symbol too).  Although there are still a few places with dollar signs, I think it is clear now that the bids are in platinum pieces and not dollars.  The auction has been up less than 24 hours and already has almost two hundred items listed.  That's great!  Don't be afraid to list or bid.  I think this is a great way to buy and sell items without eating into valuable hunting and questing time. On top of that, I have a few other additions or changes:   Inventory Slot ;  Neck Slot ; 2 Handed Blunt Weapons ;  Southern Karanas Quests ;  Temple of Solusek Ro Quests ;  Guild Links

    March 29, 2000:  I am real happy to announce a new addition to the site:  AllaKhazam's Platinum Piece Based Everquest Auction.  I know I can't be the only one who hates to spend his time running from zone to zone looking to sell or buy items.  Hopefully, this will help solve that problem.  It should also help establish the going rate of many items.  The auction is similar to the Dollar based one on One Web Place or E-bay, but not quite as sophisticated (for example, there is no feedback section) and is based completely on Norrathian Platinum Pieces.   If you want to sell an item, register, list the item under your server, set a price and leave it open for a bid.  It is pretty self explanatory.  You need to register with a valid e-mail address to sell or buy.  Hopefully, that will keep most people honest.  There are no fees involved, so feel free to list away.  I have not been able to test the script out at high volume, so please let me know if you experience any difficulties.  Enjoy.

    Now for the person who sent this out.  I hope you are reading this.  Congratulations.  You have won yourself an all expense paid trip to the federal penetentiary.  Let me lay it out for you since you clearly are not too bright.  You attempted - and perhaps succeeded in some cases - to defraud hundreds of people of something they value very highly.  You did it by sending messages across state lines, thus making this a federal case.  And, you did it through your AOL account, so they know exactly who you are and where you live.  In summary, you have commited a felony which can easily result in years of jail time.  Any one of the people you sent that e-mail to can press charges, as can Verant and Sony, so the odds are good that someone will.  The FBI is a very thorough and methodical organization, so it may take a few months for them to collect all of the e-mails, subpoena your address from aol and formally put together a case.  Expect them to show up at your door in the near future.  Look at the bright side.  You may be released in time to play Everquest 2.
