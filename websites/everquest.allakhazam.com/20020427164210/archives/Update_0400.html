    April 5, 2000:  Interesting day.  In  light of Verant's new contractual language banning all sale of Everquest items and Accounts, I have taken out my links to the cash based auction section on One Web Place.  This does not affect the Platinum auction section, which is simply a convienient method of arranging the in game sale of items and has been deemed OK by Verant.  I apologize to those who have used and enjoyed this feature.  My purpose for running this site has always been and will continue to be to provide the best overall information and service to the entire, diverse Everquest Community.  I have never been too concerned with making Verant happy.  In fact, from the little contact I have had with them, I get the impression that they don't like this site a whole lot, as it provides too much accurate information to the players.  (For example, I've been trying unsuccessfully to contact them for months now to let me get some advance information on Kunark so that I don't go crazy keeping up with the updates on the new expansion).  I want to make it clear that Verant has never contacted me about the auction section (which has been up for about 8 months now), and still has not asked me to take it down, even in light of the new contractual changes.  Nevertheless, I do not wish to endorse or condone violations of the game contract, and thus feel an obligation to take down this section now that it is officially banned.

    Also, I thought I would put up a poll regarding the new policy giving Verant permission to read files in your computer.  In case you didn't read it elsewhere, here is the policy:

                        "You hereby grant us permission to download Game-related files to
                        you. You also grant us permission to access, extract and upload
                        (i) Game-related data as part of the patching process and (ii)
                        data relating to any program that we, in our reasonable discretion,
                        determine interferes with the proper operation of EverQuest."

    This apparantly includes looking at your cookies, temporary internet files, registration information and browser history files.  Not being a programmer myself, I have to say I don't totally understand all of the implications of this policy (though I have a good idea), so I am not taking any stance one way or another.  I certainly am against any hacking of the game, but am also a person who values his privacy.  Thus, I thought it would be interesting to do a poll.

    April 4, 2000:  Here are a couple of pointers on quests that have been submitted that I think many people don't realize.  Supposedly, the higher level quests do not bracket the key words you need like the lower level quests.  In fact, many of the key phrases will not come from the NPC you are talking to, but instead from other NPC's.  So don't think that because there is nothing bracketed that you can't get a quest or elicit further information. There is also a lot of interaction between NPC's that can give you a clue as to where to find and solve quests.  Just standing around listening in on the conversations in town can lead to some interesting side adventures.

    The auction section seems to be going well.  I've had a few suggestions on features that can improve on it and will look into some things.  One suggestion I have for those posting items here is to keep your time period for bidding fairly short.  Since it is free to post, you can always post a minimum or reserve that you can live with, set the time for only a few days, and if you don't get it, just repost the item later.
