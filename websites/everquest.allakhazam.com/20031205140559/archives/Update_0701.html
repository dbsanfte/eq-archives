    July 24, 2001:  Right now I am not able to get any e-mail because my mail box is loaded with a zillion copies of the sircam virus (curses on whoever created that thing).  Makes it tough to do updates.  I may just have to delete everything in there and start over.  I thought I would take a minute to clarify a few points on my AO review.  First off, I like Anarchy Online.  When I can play, I am having a lot of fun with it.  You will note that I recommended that people buy the game and play both AO and EQ.  I think it's great that many people can play it without lag.  However, I have a 1 ghz computer with a 64 meg GE Force video card and a cable modem and can only play consistantly in the off hours, so my opinion as to whether it is playable right now is based, as it has to be, upon my personal experience.  I compared it with Everquest because a) they are both MMORPG's, b) most of the people who visit here are present or former EQ players who would want to know how they compare, and c) I have the most experience with EQ.  I also thought I made it clear that I have not played the higher level game yet, and could not really speak for how they compare at those levels.  Considering most reviewers for magazines and newspapers play a game a few hours before reviewing it, I think I have enough experience to make an initial comparison, and I've done my best to give you my honest feel for the two.  In the end, after comparing the two I found that, while I really like both games, I personally like EQ a bit more.  Undoubtedly, other people will prefer AO.  As I said in the review, I recommend you give it a try and decide for yourself.  I know that I intend to keep on playing AO as well as EQ (and DAoC too -- OK I'm a glutton), and if it turns out that once I can play more lag free and get a higher level character, I begin to prefer it over EQ, I'll be sure to post that too.
