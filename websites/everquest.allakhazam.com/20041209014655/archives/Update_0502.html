    May 21, 2002

    Comic relief: If you're an avid hunter in Ssra or Velketor's Labyrinth, you should be well aware of the pesky AE dispell traps placed in a number of places throughout the zone. Normally, they remain stationary. However, lately they've become "mobile" by attaching themselves to players and following them around. What's the big deal, you ask? Well every 10 seconds, these traps dispell four buffs from every player within a pretty big radius. This can make for *quite* the inconvenience. While not very amusing when it's happening to you (or your entire raid), the concept is good for a laugh.

    Thankfully this is a bug and, according to Alan of the Developer's Corner, will be corrected in the next patch.

    Be sure to keep an eye on the News Boards for updates from Allakhazam and Illia about E3.

    -Railus

    May 3, 2002

    If you are a premium member and are still getting popup ads, and you have checked to make sure you have cookies enabled and that your computer's date and time are correct, the most likely explanation is that you have downloaded some sort of scumware along with another program that randomly pops ads while you surf the net.  The two most popular programs that do this are Morpheus and Kazaa.  There is a program you can download that checks your computer for these programs called ad-aware. You can get it at this link. (or if that's down try this one).  Morpheus also has the unpleasant effect of redirecting links to shopping pages such as amazon.com, so that instead of the site that sent you getting credit for the sale, the referral goes to Morpheus.  Thus the many sites that rely upon this type of referral income to help pay for their bandwidth are now starting to feel a bite from losing sales to Morpheus (as if the collapse of internet ad revenue wasn't enough).  I'm telling you this more as an fyi.  While our site does get some revenue from amazon links, we rely mainly upon the premium memberships to pay our bills.  But there are other good sites out there who need that revenue to keep going, so you may want to think twice about downloading Morpheus or Kazaa.

    May 1, 2002

    It's official!  Sony has now officially acknowledged the launch of Everquest 2, with shipment scheduled for Winter, 2003.  You can read the whole press release and add your comments at this link, but here is the gist of it:
     
    • A brand new 3D engine which takes full advantage of a wide variety of recent technological advances in 3D hardware/software such as per pixel lighting, dynamic environment mapping, and a fully programmable surface shader system
    • More intuitive gameplay features appeal to both new and seasoned players
    • New branching class structure that players define as they advance through the game
    • Vast world of Norrath revisits familiar locations and introduces a variety of newly discovered areas in the Age of Destiny, a time period in the future of the original EverQuest.
    • Increased character customization capabilities allow players to customize characters faces, hair, and body types to create truly unique avatars
    • Deeper character development offering pacing options that cater to game players new to the role-playing genre as well as experienced role-players
    • Non-confrontational means of character advancement that including a completely new tradesman character class
    • Rideable mounts and vehicles to own and control, including horses and boats, make traversing the massive world of Norrath faster than before
    • Norrathian real estate for players to call their own
    • All-new tradeskill/crafting system
    • Completely new and tactically rich combat, spell and skill systems
    • A completely revised and enhanced quest system
    • Dynamic world environment shaped by player events
