    Bug List

    The game is remarkably bug free and bugs are constantly being corrected.  However, listed below are known major bugs to watch out for, as reported by various visitors to the site.  The purpose of this list is not to catalogue all of the flaws in the game or list the small annoyances like monster lag.  Nor is it to point out potential exploits.  (Those bugs will not be listed)  Instead these bugs are the ones that can really cause you a headache, or even disable your character, if you don't watch out for them.  If you know of any major bugs that are not on this list, or if you know of a fix that has been made to a bug that is listed, please e-mail me

     
    • Dropping a lore item (and perhaps any other type of item) will sometimes cause it to disappear forever.  Basically, try to avoid dropping anything.  (This may have been fixed)
    • There are several bugs to be aware of while trading:  If you are executing a trade and one of the parties moves, the items in the trade window will sometimes disappear.  Additionally, if the party you are trading with cancels the trade while you are in the process of moving an item, the item will sometimeds disappear.  And if you are trading with someone and they die before they complete the trade, you will lose everything in the trade window.  Also, heavy lag can cause everything in the trade window to disappear.  Trade interrupts casting. Don't try to trade with someone in combat.  It will interrupt any spells they are casting.  (BTW, doing this deliberately is a bannable offense)
    • Charm seems to have several bugs in it.  The worst one is if you are charmed by an NPC, there is a bug where a random item in your primary weapon slot may sometimes change into something else. [I am told this has been fixed, but cannot confirm]. If this happens, keep the item in the slot and petition a GM.   Some people just die after being charmed.  And charm sometimes causes your hot buttons to stop functioning until you log out or zone (this is often caused when you cast a spell right after being charmed).  Basically, be very wary when entering an area where you can be charmed or just avoid them alltogether.
    • Fear can also cause many of the problems experienced with charm, such as the problem with your hot keys malfunctioning.
    • Several bugs with pets seem to make the pet randomly disappear or go out of control.  Levitation can sometimes cause you to lose control of your pet.   There are also places within certain zones which cause a pet to commit suicide:  one is in Innothule in front of the Guk zone;  another is in the mino cave near the wall; Lesser Faydark is another trouble zone.
    • Due to lag, you can still be hit by mobs after you think you have zoned. If you are fighting near a zone make sure you can take at least another hit or two before running.  Also, if you die in the zone itself, you may reset back to your last save, which can cause you to lose any items you just acquired.
    • The  small canoes are buggy. You cannot fight from them but some monsters can attack you. Do not ride with more than one person in a canoe at a time or one person might get out of synch with the server and end up far away from the canoe and unable to move.  (If this happens, the helmsman can simply right click the boat to release control, and the other people on the boat will 're-synch' with the server and warp to the correct location.  So if you are a passenger, if you notice that you stop moving, notify the helmsman and they can resynch you.)
    • There is a bug in the zone between Qeynos Hills and Western Karana.  If someone is standing or sitting just at the border in WK, it will cause people trying to zone the bounce back to QH.  Other zones have this bug too, but this is the worst one.
    • If you die too near a wall or a hillside, sometimes your corpse will be imbedded in the wall so far you can't retrieve it, even with /corpse, and you will have to petition for a GM to come get it for you.  As an alternative, you can sometimes get free by consenting a bard or necro you trust to search out and /loot your corpse or to drag your corpse free with /corpse (their locate spell/song targets the corpse to allow the commands to function).
    • Sometimes when you do a quest that gives an item, if you don't have your first inventory slot open you won't receive the item.   Always make sure you have an inventory slot open, preferably the first one.
    • In a dungeon with a porticullis such as Permafrost or Paw, you can get stuck and trapped on top of it if you are under it when it closes.  If that happens you have to gate or call a GM.  If you don't have gate, you can have a high level wizard gate you out or get a high level cleric to resurrect you, which acts as a gate.  Yet another solution is to /quit and relog in after 8 minutes (long enough for the server to think you are link dead) and you should be unstuck.  I am also told that the U button will often raise the porticullis.
    • Sometimes when zoning into or out of Mistmoor, your character can fall through the world and become trapped. This can result in a character losing all of his or her equipment, particularly if they camp out in the "out of bounds" area.  There are actually several areas like this, all next to zone borders.  I would avoid swimming near any zone lines since this seems to be a problem in a number of zones and, to be real safe, try to avoid going too near the zone border in any place other than where you supposed to zone.
    • Sometimes the computer doesn't register that you have left a group.  Various things seem to cause this.  If this happens, someone trying to invite you will get a message that  you are in another group and you will not be given the follow option.  To get around this, you  have to log out and re-enter the game.
    • Sometimes when trying to zone, if you do so at a weird location, you can be teleported to a different spot within the same zone.  Many used this as a shortcut, but most of those have been fixed now.
    • Sometimes if  a door closes on you, you will get stuck in the door until someone else comes along and opens up the door.  Very inconvienient in off hours and out of the way places.
    • Your corpse may not always be where you died.  I am told that this is because whenever you corpse leaves the "zone" (ie, goes out a wall, and falls into oblivion) the game teleports it to 0,0.  There seem to be bugs like this in several zones.  Sometimes when you die in Blackburrow, your corpse is transported back to the Qeynos Hills Entrance.   Occasionally if you die on top of the temple/pyramid in Cazic-Thule, your corpse will sometimes disappear and be somehow teleported to the bottom of the alligator pits.  In Solusek's Eye your corpse will sometimes teleport to the Lavastorm zone point.  In the necromancer tunnels in Ak'anon, you corpse will sometimes be teleported to near the entrance of Ak'anon from Steamfont.  In Lower Guk, your corpse will sometimes be teleported from the dead side to a landing on the live side zone ramp.  In EK, your corpse may teleport to the base of the ramp leading to Highpass.
    • In Befallen, near the staircase before the Level 3 door, is a rock jutting out from the wall. If players back into it or walk into it they get stuck.  You must either call a GM or log out and back in and hope it unsticks you.  Another solution is to /quit and relog and sometimes you will be unstuck when you get back on.
    • When moving a window (ie. a backpack window), when you click to drop it, the click can go through to  whatever is behind it, (ie. pick up a ring from your finger inventory slot if you have it open).   You can also click-through when clicking on items in backpack windows.
    • Levitation is pretty buggy.  Monsters in the Game have a difficult time with the "Z-axis" and seem to  ignore it completely.  If you are fighting a monster on one level and the  monster or is transported to another level, the monster can still attack, but you can't. This also happens if you are levitated above the monster.
    • If you type numbers and letters together in the chat window, sometimes your numbers disappear.  Use a space between them to make sure they appear.  This only seems to happen when /filter is on.
    • If you enchant a second metal bar before placing the first in an inventory slot you lose the first metal bar, even though the icon implies they are stackable (and even though they are stackable once in your inventory).
    • Large races such as barbarians often can get stuck in the bank at Ak'Anon.
    • You probably realize that enemies can attack through walls and floors, but you may not realize that they can see through them as well.  If you are KOS somewhere, be careful of walking too close to the shops or you will get attacked by vengeful shopkeepers through the shop walls.
    • If you happen to be inattentive and drop you money/item onto the bankers NAME (when you have the bank open, the name above your 8 slots) the item/money will disappear forever.  You will not receive any message or trade window, it just disappears.
    • Many of the Qeynos sewer exits are truly only exits.  You can go out through them, but cannot reenter.
    • If you go linkdead (or otherwise lose your connection) while holding something, that item may be destroyed when you reconnect.
    • There is a bug that causes you to lose all of your extra development stats.   What causes it is unknown, and there doesn't seem to be a fix, but I thought I would warn people about it's presence.
    • In some zone pools (for example Crushbone) it is possible while attempting to exit the deep pool for you to miss the edge and fall to your death as if there was no water.  The best way to avoid this death is find a shallow spot and get out (in the CB pool, you can swim to the stream and get out to the side or in front of the Slaver Cabin).  When this happens, your corpse is often transported to location 0,0 of the zone, so look for it there.
    • There's a slight bug that can freeze EQ, and possibly the whole computer.  They designed EQ to disable the ALT+TAB shortcut keystroke in Windows (which swaps between programs); this was intended to stop people from using "trainer" utilities whie in EQ.  Unfortunately, they did NOT disable the CTRL+ESC shortcut (which opens the Start Menu).  A couple of times, I went to punch ESC (to de-target a MOB I just conned) while i was holding CTRL to sidestep back into hiding.  The system reads this as the CTRL+ESC command, and minimizes EQ to the taskbar while it attempts to open the Start Menu.  However, at this point EQ is still overriding all other Windows functions and commands, INCLUDING the mouse.  This leaves you with no way to get back into EQ (can't mouse-click the button or ALT+TAB into it).  Only CTRL+ALT+DEL works, which lets me kill EQ, but the system is still so messed up afterwards that I have to reboot and reconnect to get back into EQ.  Luckily, this key combo doesn't get used very often in-game, but you should be aware of it since it causes several minutes of downtime while you restart and reload.
    • If you type in /loot at a corpse while meditating, your graphics get screwy and quickkeys and some function keys no longer work.  To get out you should quit (/q) and restart EQ.
    • If you drop an item near a vendor (or perhaps any NPC), they might pick it up and you won't be able to get it back.
    • There is a bug that sometimes kicks you completely from your guild when you zone.  There's not anything you can do, but it is good to be aware of it.
    • Despite the fact that the graphics show barbarians as easily small enough to enter the Kelethin buildings, they can't do so.
    • There is a small cul-de-sac immediately outside the zone to the 3rd Gate in Neriak, to the right as you face the zone tunnel from Neriak Commons.  If you step inside that cul-de-sac, which is created by a door frame,  you can be stuck there for an undetermined length of time.  Your character can jump and walk in place but cannot exit the cul-de-sac.
    • Switching between characters repeatedly often locks EQ up entirely.  In one instance, I had to reboot the computer.  In another, CTRL-ALT-DEL got me out of EQ and I was able to restart it that way.  It can also mess up your sound.
    • Mobs in the Qeynos sewers near Cubert's spawn spot jump into the water when fleeing and swim down to some unknown spot.  Magician pets  who follow them don't return; they suicide, probably after getting stuck down there somewhere.  Note.  I have been told that they in fact run towards the exit and can sometimes be found again at a dead end near the exit.
    • If a character who has a pet goes linkdead, his pet will be affected by PC area effect spells and will turn and attack the player who cast the spell.
    • If you have ICQ turned on, you should turn it off before you play EQ because if you get messaged the game crashes and you have to restart, which can be pretty inconvienient in the middle of a battle.
    • Sometimes if you are standing in water (especially in Innothule I am told), you can't hit the MOB.  Everything will seem to be working, but you will get no damage messages.  This might be because you are standing and the mob is swimming.  Try to fight on dry land or completely in the water if possible.
