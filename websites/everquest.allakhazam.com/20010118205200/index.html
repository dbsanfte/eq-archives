    January 8, 2001

    Just another reminder that I will be chatting with Densmore on All Game Radio tonight starting at 9:00 est.  Also, don't forget that the game servers are scheduled to go down tomorrow starting at 3:00 a.m. pst, for about 10 hours.  You can expect that this site will run a little slower during that time, since many of the people who would otherwise be logged on to the game will be here instead.

    Quite a few people have written asking if there was anything they could do to help out the site in light of CNet dumping the Gamecenter Alliance and leaving this site and many others, at least for now, without revenue.  There is actually something you can do to help.  The alliance does not officially disband until February 1.  Thus, through the end of this month, CNet is still paying us based upon the number of pages viewed on the site.  Therefore, I would encourage you to spend more time than ever here over the next few weeks and look at as many separate pages of the site as possible each time you visit.  If you would normally come here five minutes and look at 5 pages, instead stay for 10 minutes and look at 15 pages or a half hour and look at 100 pages.  Every little bit helps, and who knows, you may find a part of the site you didn't know existed. Thanks.

    I would also like to remind you about the books section, which has been here all along, and has a nice set of reviews and suggestions for science fiction and fantasy books.  I just added some new sections to it.

    January 4, 2001

    Some of you might have heard that CNet has disbanded the Gamecenter Alliance.  I know I have gotten a few e-mails about it.  Unfortunately, it is true.  As of the end of this month, the alliance will end and I'll be left without any revenue to pay for the growing hosting and bandwidth costs needed to run the site, not to mention to pay my mortgage.  Hopefully, this is only a temporary setback.  When I left Gamestats, I was able to hook up with IGN, then I was able to join up with Stomped and CNet.  Hopefully, I will find a new home that will let me continue to make this site better and easier to use, and to expand this concept into the new MMORPG's that are currently in the works.  Rest assured that I do not intend to fold any time soon.  For those who are interested, Gary Arndt, the President of Stomped, wrote a nice article about the breakup.  You can read it here.

    Now on to the stuff you are really interested in.  Just a small update today.
