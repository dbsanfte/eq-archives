    November 24, 1999:  Happy Holidays.  I'm doing an early update for two reasons.  The most important reason is to announce a terrific charity drive being sponsored by Gamestats called Games of Encouragement.  In the spirit of the holidays, Gamestats and its many affiliates are asking the visitors to these sites to donate games and/or money to the Ronald McDonald House.  For those of you who don't know about this worthy charity, it is dedicated to providing support and care for children with life threatening illnesses such as cancer.  When Gamestats told me about this charity drive, I thought it was a terrific choice of charities, both because of the quality of the Ronald McDonald House and because who is better suited to help out these children than the gaming community - and more specifically in this case the Everquest community - who, no matter what the age range, are basically children at heart.  The above banner will remain in place until after the Christmas Holidays.  Please feel free to follow the link and read more about it and provide a donation to this wonderful organization.

    The second reason for this quick update is to say that I have taken down the popup ad (again).  I know.  I know.  I hear the groans.  As you may recall, I initially said it was going up for a week for mutual evaluation to determine the exact value of the ad and the effect on the visitors to the site.  As it turned out, I don't feel the final offer is enough to justify subjecting the great visitors of this site to the ad each time they come here.

    November 17, 1999:  This update comes late and is brief because I spent the last several hours reading through all of the e-mail I recieved.  I must say that I am humbled by all the support that poured in.  I wish I could have answered each e-mail, but then I would never get around to updating the site. I realize that I was indeed catering to the vocal minority and ignoring my instincts.  I know from experience that vast majority of the people who come here and play the game are great people.  Where else can you log onto a game, shout for a group, link up with 5 strangers and invariably have a good time.  It's one of the things I love about the game.  At the urging of many people, I am going to put the popup ad back up.  However, I promise this.  It will only be in the banner frame, so it should only show up when you first log onto the site and will not reappear unless you refresh the whole site.  Also, I believe that the ad presently leads to a bunch of high quality sites and offers, but if that content deteriorates, I will take it down immediately.  I will also try to be better at forewarning everyone about major changes in the site in the future.

    November 16, 1999:  After I removed the popup ad yesterday, I have to say I felt little desire to work on the site for a while.  I didn't start this site to make money, but I enjoy making a buck as much as the next person.  And after spending several hours a day working on this site since March to try to create and maintain the best EQ resource I can, I was offered a lot of money (to put the offer in perspective, I graduated with an advanced degree 11 years ago and the offer was more money than I was making for my job only a few years ago - it is also more than I make on all the other ads, etc. on the site combined) to put up an ad that pops up when you first enter the site with a yes-no question.  If you click no, it disappears and if you click yes, it opens a new window into a site with links to various legitimate site and software offers such as Delphi Forums, Priceline.com, The Wallstreet Journal, USA.net and even Sony.  I got bombarded with negative e-mails.  Some were just nasty.  Quite frankly if keeping the ad up would have lost those visitors, I would have put more up.  Others were more well reasoned.  Every time I make a change, I get complaints, so that doesn't shock me (heck some people wrote to complain about the new graphics).  I figure that shows how much people care about the site.  It was the volume and anger of the responses that caused me to decide to take the ad down.  I personally can't see how taking an extra second to click on a mouse should cause such angst, but while this is indeed "my" website, I try to make it something everyone who comes here will enjoy, and this was obviously something that bothered a lot of people.  I will say that people are spoiled by the internet.  Such a great amount of information is available for free at just the click of a button that people seem to lose perspective on the amount of work actual people put in to put that information up.  There is a reason that 99% of the websites out there are out of date and never updated.  People sign up with xoom or geocities or whatever and get their free site and think how cool it will be to whip together a website.   They put together a format, write up some copy, try to make it look nice, put it up and tell all their friends to check it out. Then after the initial thrill wears off they start to realize that they have only done the easy part, and it sinks in how much work it will be to keep it up and expand it.  At that point most people just stop, and another skeleton of a dead website joins the graveyard of the web.  For those who continue, the prospect of some extra revenue is often what decides whether a person will spend an hour or two working on the site or will instead spend that time watching TV, playing a game or doing some other activity. Although I haven't completely ruled it out, I doubt that I will put the pop-up ad back.  If it just drove away the idiots, I wouldn't hesitate to bring it back.  From my experience in the game, that's really a small percentage of the Everquest community, and I could care less about catering to that type anyways.  Too many people whose viewpoint I respected wrote to complain as well.  Nonetheless, I will not rule out other forms of revenue should they come my way, and maybe I will also take the time to step back a bit and choose to read a book, watch tv or play the game more often.
