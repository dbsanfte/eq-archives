    October 20, 1999:  This is a quick update because an e-mail was sent here (and also to EQ Vault, EQ'Lizer and EQ Stratics) claiming to have the complete details of the Fiery Avenger Quest.  I have to say that it is either accurate or an incredibly well done, elaborate hoax.  I am inclined to believe it is a hoax.  But, I can verify that most of the items he says you need and most of the side quests he claims you have to do actually exist.  However, I have no idea if you actually get the Fiery Avenger in the end, and it will take someone a huge amount of time and effort to complete it to find out.  Making no judgment one way or another, I am posting it exactly as sent in for you to read and attempt if you are so inclined.   At the very least it makes for some interesting reading.  --  I've gotten several e-mails on this almost uniformly agreeing that it is a fake, so just be warned.  I leave it to the judgment of the visitors to decide for him or herself.  At the very least it gets people thinking about this quest so that maybe we can actually solve it if it exists.

    GO PENN STATE!

    October 14, 1999:  Whew.  Busy day.  I know everyone is anxious to know what was added in the last patch, and knowing this was coming ahead of time, I rearranged my schedule so that I could basically spend all day sorting through my mail trying to get it together for you.  Thanks to all of you who sent in information about the new items and quests.  Without your contributions, this site could not exist.  I think I have most of the new quests down, and some of the new items as well, although I still have very little information on the paladin quests for some reason.  I assume people will be completing the quests over the weekend.  If you get an item I don't yet have listed, please send it in to me in gif or jpg format.  Here is the scoop.

    Be forewarned.  Most of the new quests are very difficult to do.  I was pretty amused to find an e-mail from almost every class complaining about how their class had the hardest quests.  They are all tough.  In my opinion, Shamen have the most right to complain, although I assume Verant will patch it soon so that the people who give out the quests don't kill 2/3 of the shamen who come looking for it.  I do anticipate several things with this patch.  First, Unrest is going to be even more crowded than it already is.  Many of the quests have you going to Unrest several times for key items.  Second, this won't stop camping, just spread it out a little.  Many of the items are still located on single creatures that spawn in set places, and most of the quest items are no drop.  Third, if you are a casual gamer who can only get on a few hours at a time, forget about getting most of these pieces, at least at first.  Since most of the quest items are no drop and will be highly camped, there will either be a waiting list like the jboots used to have or a free for all to see who gets them first.  I am told most of the armor pieces are no drop as well (although I have not seen statistics for many as the quests take a while to do) so you won't be able to buy them either.  Fourth, Verant may finally have found a use for the trade skills.  Several quests require items made from the skills, especially blacksmithing, although people are not going to be happy about being forced to take up a trade.

    Here are the quest and armor additions by class:

            Warriors Crafted Armor has been updated so that each piece gets a stat bonus of some sort.  Very nice change.  I have graphics for each crafted item in the equipment section.  (follow the equipment links below).  The quest still goes out of Southern Karana.

            Rogues now can get Ravenscale armor in Cazic Thule.  There is no quest for this.  As far as I can tell it drops in exact correspondence with where the pieces of Rubicite dropped.  This armor is lore, but not no drop.  I have stats/images for the  CoifGlovesBootsLeggingsandChestguard

            Shamen can get Totemic Armor (I'm not completely certain of the name) through a quest in Lake Rathe at the Barbarian Fishing Village.  Of course right now Trolls and Ogres are Killed on Site by the Barbarians who give out the quest  Gotta love that one.  I haven't seen any stats yet for the armor, so I don't know if it is no drop.  If you are a Shaman, you might want to take up blacksmithing, since each piece requires the equivalent piece of banded armor to make.  Several of the items you need are virtually impossible to get due to being very rare drops on very rare spawns.  Good luck.

            Rangers can get Ivy Etched Armor from a quest in Kithicor Forest.  The few screenshots I have gotten show the armor is lore, but not no drop, but I believe those shots were taken on the test server.  The items you need almost all drop from static named spawns.

            Paladins can get Armor of Ro from the Paladin camp in the Rathe Mountains.  These quests are the ones I have the least information on at this time.  I am pretty sure that you must have the blacksmithing skill to accomplish this.  And if you want to get the full set, you will have to raise your faction with every Paladin Guild in Norrath, because each of them controls a piece of armor.  The biggest part of these quests seems to be spending time killing orcs and such to raise faction and also time to raise your smithing skills.

            Shadowknights can get Darkforge armor from an undead knight who spawns in Mistmoore.  The problem is that he spawns in a place where he is readily seen and killed by other players, so is rarely actually there.  Most of the items you need are in Cazic Thule, where I guess you can group up with the Rogues camping the Ravenscale Armor.  Also, you need to kill Freeport and Qeynos guards for several pieces, which is sure to make you very popular with the people in those zones.

            Bards, Clerics, Wizards, Enchanters, Necromancers and Magicians all have quests for items and armor in the new Temple of Solusek Ro Zone.  A warning.  Do not talk to the effrete there or the inhabitants will take a severe dislike to you and at the least stop giving out quests if they don't simply kill you.  Necromancers are going to have to take up tailoring in order to get the shadow silk you need for the quests.

            Druids have had a few new quests for weapons added in the  Rathe Mountains , but really don't get much in this patch.  (Rathe is also where the new jboot quest has moved).

            Monks will have to be happy with the cured silk armor they added the last patch that have them all suddenly taking up the arts of brewing and tailoring.  (Certainly nothing to complain about there).

    October 7, 1999:  For those of you that have not heard of this, there is a bug that allows a person to switch the item in the trade screen and give you a different item than you bargained for and saw in the window.  I think this is an exploit and should be a bannable offense.  I also thinkVerant should make fixing this a high priority because trading is an integral part of the game and once people start to distrust the trading process, an important part of the game will deteriorate.  Until then, I would suggest only trading for higher level items with higher level characters to avoid easily deleted mules.  Of course that is not a guarantee, but very few people would risk ruining their main character's reputation by exploiting this bug.  I bring this up because of a person named Hamek who, despite his level, exploited this bug and switched items on a person on my server (Povar).  It is his misfortune that she happened to be my sister, and thus one of the few people whose word I trust enough to post this based upon her word alone.  If you are on Povar, it might be prudent to not trade with this Hamek person.
