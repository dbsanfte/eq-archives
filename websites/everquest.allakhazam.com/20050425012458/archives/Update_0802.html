    August 19, 2002

    Muse sent me this note to post for you:  "SOE recently began promoting their new book, the Everquest Atlas, and some of you have expressed to me some confusion regarding this.  Despite the similarity between the name of this product and their use of "EQ Atlas" to advertise it on the Everquest Live site, the product is not related in any way to my site,  EQ Atlas (www.eqatlas.com).  While we both offer maps and descriptions, the specific content is quite different.  I hope that this announcement will clear up any confusion."  I'll go a bit further.  /rant on.  It seems pretty rotten of them to be using the good will he built up over the past three years to sell their book in a way that would clearly confuse players into thinking Muse is involved with it.  Muse is a friend, so I may be prejudiced on this one, and yeah it is Sony's game, but I can easily think of a dozen names they could have picked that would have been just as good and wouldn't have suggested a connection with his site.  The only logical conclusion to draw is that they are hoping people will go "Cool. EQ Atlas has a book" and buy it based upon that.  Shameful, really.  Moreover, having looked at the example pdf file SOE lists and compared it to the information on Muse's site, I'm not sure why anyone would buy that book when EQ Atlas offers far superior maps and information for free, and has been serving the community for years.  Yeah they're pretty, but who wants a map without locs and camp information?  /rant off.   Feel like commenting on this?  Feel free to use this forum.

    August 6, 2002

    One great thing about going to the fan faire was getting feedback from the site users.  The one thing I was told over and over by our premium users was how they had signed up mostly to get rid of the ads and then were really amazed at how much other stuff they got and how useful it was.  Many people pointed to the character upgrade search and said "if I had known about that before I would have signed up a long time ago."  I guess this is kind of a stealth feature since you won't stumble across it without actually paying for it.  It's also by far our most popular premium feature.  So let me explain it again.  You enter all of your character's information and equipment, including race, class, level and the amount of plat you are willing to spend on upgrades.  Then you enter what stats you most want in your characters equipment.  So an enchanter, for example may want intelligence first, charisma second and armor class third.  You then click search for upgrades.  It then searches all 12,500 plus items in our database and finds the ones that match your criteria better than what you are currently wearing that can either be obtained by a character your level or can be bought for the price you entered.  It comes up in a report that looks like this.  You can then check off the box next to each item that looks interesting to you and mass add them to your wishlist.  Then you generate a wishlist report that looks like this, print it out and use while you're in the game to get the items.  It's a quick and easy way to find the best equipment available to you at your level and price range.  Once you've generated the report, you can head to the right zone and camp the right mob to get the perfect upgrade.  I hope this better explains how this particular premium feature works.
