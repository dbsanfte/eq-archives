    January 31, 2001

    As many of you know, tomorrow is the day CNet disbands its gamecenter alliance, which up to now had been the sole source of revenue for this site.  This is an expensive site to run.  The site runs on two dedicated servers, and we recently ordered a third server because the load has become too great for only two to handle, causing the site to run way too slow and to crash now and then.  Every day on average 60,000 people visit the site, viewing close to 700,000 pages of information (and this doesn't even take into account the addition of EQ Atlas to the servers), so you can imagine the amount of bandwidth it uses.  Moreover, I decided several months ago that the only way I could keep up with the site and the Velious expansion and also expand the site into other games was to work on it full time, so this is currently my only source of income.  And although he hasn't given up his day job, Illia also relies upon the site for some income.  CNet's dissolution of its alliance comes in a period of upheaval in the internet.  The huge drop in value of many dot com stocks has taken a lot of money out of the web, and the amount being spent on advertising is only a fraction of what it was even six months ago.  It seems like every day you read about another dot com company going bankrupt.  Many good game sites, like Lum the Mad, Gameglow and others have just folded up shop.  The good news is that we don't plan on folding any time soon, but this means we had to get more creative in bringing in revenue.  So starting tomorrow you will be subject to two popup ads -- one when you first enter the site and another when you leave.  I'm not real thrilled about putting these up, but they are the only source of guaranteed income for the site.  There will still be banner ads as well, but they are not guaranteed to bring in any revenue, and what they do bring in is highly dependant upon how many times you all click on them and go to the sites they advertise.  I am planning on moving the banner on the front page down to hopefully try to remind you to click on it now and then.

    I know the popup ads are an inconvienience (although they do somehow fit with the pictures of half naked wood elves in the fashion tables) but unfortunately they are necessary.  Really, when you think about it, they are a small price to pay for the huge amount of free information you have available here.  Illia and I have put in thousands of hours to try to make this site the best resource for Everquest information on the web, yet we are not asking you, the people who use the information, to pay us anything.  We do ask that you put up with a little inconvienience and click off a popup window and click on an ad banner now and then.  Thank you for your understanding.

    January 26, 2001

    I guess Sony is serious about stopping the sale of items and accounts.  Yesterday, Yahoo shut down every EQ auction and soon thereafter the two smaller sites that were doing EQ Auctions, egamesauction.com and playerauctions.com, were both forced to shut down.  The lawyers are out in force.  I am guessing that until someone opens a site somewhere other than the United States, the buying and selling of items and accounts is through.  Of course, with millions of dollars in commisions at stake, that is not inconceivable.
