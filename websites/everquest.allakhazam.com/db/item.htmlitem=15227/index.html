One Stop Summary
Posted: Feb 25th 2004 at 12:13pm EDT| IP: Logged | Reply to this
By: fischere the Flatulent
Scholar

Link to this post

I know this has been posted to death, but since I just did this I thought a nice, one post, up to date summary would help anyone attempting to do this.

I completed this portion with my 54 cleric solo.

Since this portion of the epic will take place underwater, the first thing I would recommend doing before going after Bergurgle would be to stop by the bazaar and get either an EB (Enduring Breath) item, or an EB potion if you can’t cast EB or equivalent spells. I opted to buy a rebreather and not have to worry about any spells running out on me. I took my newly purchased rebreather and headed to Lake Rathetear.

There are a few different ways to get there. I opted to take the Oggok stone from the PoK (you will zone to the Feerrott). I headed west through the Feerrott and zoned into the Rathe Mountains. From there I proceeded north-west across the zone and zoned into Lake Rathetear. I got smacked by a Cyclops in the Rathe Mountains but he conned green to me and I wiped him with a couple nukes.

After zoning into Lake Rathetear, you will be on the eastern side of the lake, center of the map. If you walk straight west from the zone-in point, you will find a boat dock at the edge of the lake. Walk right off the dock heading west (into the water) and keep swimming west. You will approach an island that has a tower in the center. The location of the entrance to the underwater cavern you must now find is about 2802, -897. The easy way to find it is to swim up to the edge of the tall mountain on the north-east side of the island. Dive to the bottom and the cave entrance is actually in the base of the mountain. Head west into the tunnel.

You will pass two Aqua Goblin guards in the tunnel. They conned green and didn’t aggro on me. I killed them anyway, nasty goblins. Further west from the guards, the tunnel opens up into a larger, round cavern. There is a tower in the center of the cavern (I saw it on my map). This is where you will find Lord Bergurgle. There are four mobs at the base of the tower, one at each corner. They will either be Aqua Goblins (green to me) or Deepwater Goblins (light blue to me).

It’s important to note at this point that fighting these goblins can be a bit tricky. Every one of them can cast heal on themselves and they do quite frequently. I was getting frustrated fighting the Deepwater Goblins as I would get them down to 15-30% and they would jump back up over 50%. I finally used the strategy of loading 3 different stuns and a couple good nukes. I would stun, nuke, stun, nuke, etc. This seemed to work well once I got the timing down. Even the green mobs were doing this and I was cursing up a storm fighting them.

The tower is very small and only has two levels. I found two green Aqua Goblins on the first level and killed them quickly. The second level is where Lord Bergurgle resides. You can swim up through a hole in ceiling of the first level.

(NOTE: You can avoid any and all mobs by swimming along the ceiling of the tunnel when you first enter, and approaching the tower from above. The top of the tower is open, and you can perch along the wall at the top and look down into the second level. You will be sitting far enough away that you won’t draw aggro from anything.

The second level contains three goblin spawns. There is a 57th-58th level NPC named Deep that resides here as well. She conned indifferent to me and did not aggro when I smacked the other mobs. The three goblins will be either Deepwater, Tainted, or Shamans. They mostly conned light blue to me with an occasional dark blue. They spawn on one side of the tower and the one in the middle is the PH for Lord Bergurgle. He was not up when I arrived so I had to play the waiting game. I killed the three goblins that were present, and swam up to the top of the tower and sat on the wall looking down into the second level. The goblins on the right and left were on a six minute timer, and the center goblin (the PH for Lord Bergurgle) was on a 30 minute timer, right down to the second. I basically waited for the center PH to pop, pacified the right and left goblins and pulled the center mob away from the rest and killed it. Repeat cycle.

It took me about four hours of killing the PH, waiting, killing the PH, waiting, and then Bergurgle finally popped. He was dark blue to me. I pacified the other two and pulled Bergurgle back from the tower a bit (didn’t want any adds). I rooted him and using the stun and nuke method took him out quite easily and looted the crown. I’ve heard nightmare stories of people camping him for 50+ hours. Maybe I got lucky, I don’t know, but since it’s random you just never know.

That was all there was to it. I hope this helps.

Felwen Soulmender

I went to Lake Rathe to camp this elusive aqua gobbie and finally got him to pop. This can be easily done solo - pacify the 3 gobbies and pull one at a time - took me 6 hours one day and 3 hours the next day but finally got him.

Tip - sit on top of the tower - not on the same floor as the gobbies - make sure you have enduring breath item or DMF if you camp right here. You can look down on the pops without causing agro. Cast pacify on all gobbies and root and nuke away on each one. I found this the easiest. Cast endure magic on yourself cause they can nuke you back. I kept the floor clean of pops - cause you will find out that they can CH themselves, so best to have a clean camp for when Bergurgle does pop or surrounding gobbies will CH him and themselves.

Another tip - Keep gate up at all times and bind yourself close to the zone. I got mobbed once cause I accidently nuked instead of pacified. Made it to the arena zone with 4%HP.

I found camping right at the tower the best cause if other clerics come by and you are sitting on land you may get your pop but be too late and another cleric will leave one step closer to their epic.

Take a friend - no use sitting around for hours by yourself. Camping this rare gobbie is boring and very long unless you are lucky to get him in only a few tries.

A big thanks goes out to my camping buddies Ranyard, Deglight and Gorlac. And to Macrose who has supported me with tons of useful tips.
