    January 29, 2004

    I don't normally link to posts in our forums (actually I don't think I ever did this).  Moreover, I have made it a point to try to stay officially neutral in most of the debates that have raged about the game over the years, and instead have tried to provide a forum where people could debate free of influence or censorship and provide plain game information for those who prefer to just stay out of the debates altogether.  So this is a double whammy.  Some of you may know from other forum posts that I have consistently turned down offers from certain platinum for cash sites for large and lucrative ad deals and even for the actual purchase of the site.  Why?  I believe that, while the sale of plat and items for cash on a small scale is fairly harmless, these large conglomerates have far too much power over the game to be tolerated or to be allowed to enter the mainstream of our community, and I have not wanted to give even the limited stamp of approval an ad on our site might provide (not to mention that our wonderful premium members give me the business flexibility to turn down their offer and still pay our bills).  This post, which was actually a repost of something posted on the Safehouse, seemed to sum up my thoughts on the matter pretty well (and I will note that even if the program this poster describes does not exist, it is possible to do what he describes totally by hand, which has always been my concern), so I decided to link to it and say I support the jist of what it is saying.  I believe this is one of the most serious issues to be faced not only by Everquest but by the whole MMORPG community in years.  Here's the post.

    January 22, 2004

    A number of people are confused about using the Lucy Item Collector for collecting and uploading your profile information.  I figured I would post step by step directions here.  I'll send these to Rale to add to the readme file too.

    1.  Download the Item Collector Here and install it.

    2.  Set up Profile Support in Option by checking the profile support box.  You need to also designate the file for saving them.  Click on the "..." next to the file box and pick a directory.  The default directory is the program directory.

    3.  Launch Internet Explorer and make sure the privacy setting in its options menu is at medium or lower.  Then go to http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/user.html and log into the site.  (If you use IE as your default browser, you are probably already logged in).

    4.  Launch Item Collector and if you do not have it defaulted to start when launched, start collection.

    5.  Launch Everquest and log into the game.  Your Character should be saved to Item Collector when you log in.  If it is not, zoning will usually save it.  You can check back and forth in windows mode to make sure it is saved.

    6.  In Item Collector, go to file->profiles.  Your Character should be listed there.  Click on the character and the information should display on the right side.  To upload to our server, click on "Upload".  You can then view your character at http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/userinfo.html.  Characters can also be viewed locally by going to the default directory you chose in the options (probably C:\Program Files\EQ Item Collector\) - This is premium only.
