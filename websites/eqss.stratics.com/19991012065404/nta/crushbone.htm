Wilderness Zone Guide - Crushbone
Introduction

The Crushbone zone is an extremely popular zone located in the Greater Faydark/Felwithe (the High Elf City)/Kelethin (the Wood Elf city) region. The entrance is located on the north wall of the Greater Faydark zone, near Kelethin. It's something of a "dungeon" zone since there is only one entrance, and the only thing to be found inside is Orcs, Orcs, and more Orcs :-). No cities, merchants, or even roads or paths can be found anywhere in the zone. This zone is suitable for levels 8-15+, if you know what and where to hunt. And that is exactly what this guide is here for :-).

A Tour of Crushbone

The basic shape of Crushbone is something like a circle. Starting at the entrance facing out, if you head left you'll cross a bridge and eventually enter the slaving camp. Go around the slaving camp, and you'll find yourself on the far side of the palace, home of Emperor Crush. Going around the palace , and then taking the path there will lead you back to the main entrance of the zone. In the center is a large hill, with a orc campsite on top, and underneath the hill is a series of tunnel that houses some Orc Slavers. As you can see, Crushbone is a relatively small zone, but it's still a good place to hunt.

Hunting Strategies

While Crushbone is a wonderful zone to get both good experience and loot, one thing to be very wary of is trains. Since some of you may be scratching your heads at this point and wondering what in the world a train is, I'll tell you :-). A train is what occurs when a player gets more monsters than he can handle (say 2 or 3) and begins to run back to the zone boundary in order to escape. The problem though, is that when he runs by other monsters standing idly about, they too join in the chase. Soon you'll have a player running in the front, with anywhere from 5-15 monsters hot on his tail. Once the one who is being pursued either dies, or zones, that group of monsters soon turns their attention to others around them, and massive fatalities often result. On top of all this, these trains often occur when a high level group is hunting the toughest monster in the zone, and they end up running. So not only do you have 5-15 monsters, but alot of the time these monsters are the hardest in the zone! You can appreiciate now why many people immediately take cover when "TRAIN!" is shouted throughout the zone. If you ever hear this phrase uttered, be prepared to take immediate cover, or you may be in for a long walk back to your corpse.

Now that you're aware of what trains are and the dangers they pose, it's time for some specific hunting strategies. First off, I recommend that you don't attempt to come into this zone until at least level 7. Up until then, it is far too risky, and you'll find yourself running and zoning more than you will be hunting. If you want to try out some Orcs before this level, try hanging out in the area right outside Crushbone, in Greater Faydark.

Levels 7-10 should hunt Orc Centurions solo for the most part. Orc Centurions can be found just about anywhere in the zone, but I recommend you take a quick left while coming out of the entrance area, and go across the bridge there. Several Orc Centurions regularly spawn here, so it's a good spot. For more Centurions, go over to the slaver's area. Also, if you're in a group of 2-3, you might want to try taking on the Orc Slaver which is found in one of the buildings in that area. Be careful though, as it's toughness varies quite a bit, and while he may of been an easy kill for you last time, this time he might eat you for breakfast without taking a scratch.

Once you're in the level 11-15 range, you have quite a few options. You can continue hunting Orc Centurions, as there are some that are higher leveled than others (such as those on the hill near the trainer). Also, the Orc Legionnaires should be a prime target in this range, as they're pretty much ideal for 12+. The Legionnaires are generally found around the palace, patrolling around the walls. Many players like to go on top of the hill in the center of the zone, and hunt the Orc Trainer that spawns there. The reason for this is is that he sometimes gives out the Shiny Brass Shield, a magic shield that has 10 AC, 10 magic resistance, and also gives off light. However, it's pretty hard because there are often many people there competing, plus the fact that he doesn't have it in his loot every time. Another place you might try at this level is in the tunnel complex underneath the hill. You'll find Centurions and Orc Slavers within. Lastly, you can group up with other higher-level folks and attempt to go into the castle itself. Inside you can find many tough Orcs, including the Emissary, the Prophet, Ambassador D'Vinn (a high level Dark Elf NPC), the Emperor's Royal Guard (how does backstabbing for 40 damage sound as you try to escape?), the Warlord, and of course Emperor Crush himself. All I can say is that if you decide to do this, be extremely cautious at all times.

Past levels 17-19 or so, it's time to leave Crushbone. There's just not many monsters that will give you good experience besides the unique high level Orcs, and you can get much more consistent loot and experience elsewhere.

Conclusion

I hope you find this guide useful in your adventures :-). This guide is shorter than my Butcherblock one simply because Butcherblock is much, much bigger than Crushbone.
