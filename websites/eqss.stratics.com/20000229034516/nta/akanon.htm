City Zone Guide - Ak'Anon
Introduction

Greetings and Welcome to Ak'Anon!

Ak'Anon, tucked deep within the mountains surrounding Steamfont, is a comforting home for the gnomes. The single entrance is located within the Steamfont Mountains and is guarded by the finest gnomish watchman. The town within the mountains is one of the most beautiful yet most complicated towns ever settled in Norrath. Guilds for warriors and spell casters alike rest here along with merchants and pubs. Yet winding tunnels, cascading waterfalls and steep cliffs await travelers who are unfamiliar with the gnomish terrain. Keep a watchful eye for the clockwork guides and don't hesitate to ask them for directions but please mind the clockwork dusters and watch your head, those ceilings are low!

The Clockwork Guides

Spending generations deep within the caves of Ak'Anon, gnomes have developed clockwork technology unmatched anywhere in the known world. They spend so much time tinkering up these inventions, they've even created creatures to give directions to weary travelers so the gnomes will not have to pause in their labors. These clockwork guides are essential for those new to Ak'Anon. Learn how to use them and you will never need a cartographer's service again while you venture within this town. Simply "hail" them and ask about your destination (target a clockwork guide and, for example, ask "Where is the bank?") and they will immediately summon your information. Even though these guides are quite helpful, some visitors may need a bit of assistance, so I shall do my best to accommodate you and so I may promptly return to my tinkering

Royal Palace of King Ak'Anon

After entering our beloved city, you will find yourself twisting around the caves and catacombs. This is common in our town since we need as much room as possible to test our creations and it seems to keep most unruly travelers at bay. You will soon come upon our center of defense known simply as The Wall. Our Clockwork Guards stand sentinel here and keep an eye our for attackers. Passing The Wall, you will find yourself at the grand underground waterfall spilling into Lake Tildunk. Here a fisherman can make a catch as big as himself (if he is the size of a gnome of course.) Situated in the middle of Lake Tildunk one can see The Palace of King Ak'Anon. Visitors are welcome to chat with him and his royal guard as he casts spells and justly rules our fair town. Be sure to visit the many merchants that have set up shop neighboring the Palace.

Gemchopper Hall, Warriors Guild

Head to the east and you will discover another tunnel leading towards the great Gemchopper Hall. Warriors call this place home as they can train and acquire new skills within its walls. It is rumored that merchants here offer some great deals on armor and weaponry to the right customer but I am not one for fighting.

The Forge, The Pillar, and the Library

Continue down the tunnel and find the Forge of Defiance. This is where all gnomes begin their life. Many merchants sell their wares or offer their services here. You can also find the Library Mechanamagica just past a rocky gully. The Eldritch Collective of magic users call this place home and offer training and spells for those interested in their craft. From the Forge, you will see a large set of stairs which leads to the Pillar of Commerce. This area is dedicated to not only merchants and pubs but also to the city's bank and the zoo of Ak'Anon. Fierce animals can be admired here while sipping an ale purchased by the clockwork merchants.

Temple of Deep Musing

Crossing the narrow bridge by the Forge will bring you upon the second half of Lake Tildunk. Clerics and rogues provide citizens of Norrath with instructions in the temple called "Deep Musing" to the left of the green waters. To the right adventurers can wander down a descending path to another section of wonderful Ak'Anon.

The Great Wheel and the Basement

This area is home to the Great Wheel--the source that fuels Ak'Anon. Proceed downwards to the scrapyard and say "Hail" to Sanfyr Montop before venturing into the Mines of Malfunction. Sanfyr, like many other gnomes of Ak'Anon, shies away from speaking of these caves for King Ak'Anon doesn't like its name to be mentioned but since you seem to be an intelligent individual, I shall shed some light onto this area. You see, The Eldritch Collective creates wonderful clockworks which can guard, clean, guide and even buy and sell, but this comes with a price--not all of their creations work properly. A small amount fail to live up to the Eldritch name and stop functioning correctly. In some cases, these clockworks will turn on their masters and lash out. These abominations are thrown into the Mines of Malfunction for brave adventurers to have what they will with them. It has been rumored that the mines are infested with snakes, spiders and animated skeletons. Many gnomes refer to the mines as the Basement if they say anything about it in the first place.

Final Words

King Ak'Anon prides himself on a well run, well maintained city and will not accept the fact that his fair city is flawed even in the smallest way. I beg of you not to repeat this to another person for I fear I have spoken too much a could find myself thrown upon the rack of the king's clockwork torturer.

This is the extent of my knowledge I can bestow upon you on Ak'Anon. I bid you well on your journey here and hope your stay is a pleasurable one. Make yourself at home and do come back.

-Sertan, Gnome Magician  

(These words were scribed by the wood elf Valus during his journeys through Faydwer. Months later, Valus traveled back to see how Sertan's inventions were coming along. When asking a gathering of gnomes where he could find Sertan, suspicious glances and impish smirks were his answer followed by one gnome saying, "Sertan? Never heard of him.")
