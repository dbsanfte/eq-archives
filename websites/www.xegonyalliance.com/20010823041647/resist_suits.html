JEWELERY BASED RESISTANCE SUITS
By: Tashia of Xegony [Recent to May/2001]
[ for a printable version, click here ]

A lot of high level MOB encounters can be won purely on the value of your resistances alone. The key to doing dragons like Vox or Nagafen lies purely your cold or fire resistances, far more so than your magic resistances. Considering that a lot of high level MOB encounters entail having your buffs blows away, having a base of high resistances is absolutely essential. It doesn't matter how many resistance buffs you have on; the longer the fight goes on, the higher your chance of having them blown away, regardless of how high they are on your stack of buffs. Let's say, for example, that you're a ranger and your base cold resistance is 45. You are going to die to Vox, almost without question. Sure, you may have a high magic resistance - so what. So you don't get feared. It doesn't much matter if you're dead from Vox's first breath of ice on your ass. With any high level MOB, the fire, cold, disease, or poison resistance is the most important, then magic second.

Making sure your base resistances are high is often a problem. I mean really, who wants to camp every single resistance based item in the game just to make sure you have a good resistance suit. Well, it's really not that bad. Take a look at the chart below. It outlines a basic resistance suit for your face, ears, fingers, head, neck and wrists using nothing more than jeweler made materials. The downside is obviously the cost. A lot of these items require plane gems, which sell for about 1K on average. Then there's the cost of the metals, also expensive. Finally, there's the cost of markup from getting a jeweler to make this stuff for you. A lot of the velium material, especially stuff with plane gems in it, isn't trivial to make and fails in the creation process. Even a jeweler with a capped skill of 250 fails about 1 in 3 times on jewelry using plane gems (these include Jacinths, Diamonds, Black Sapphires, and Blue Diamonds).

Seriously though, if you don't want to spend the time camping for a resistance suit, pay for it up front. Take a look at the totals at the bottom of the chart. Pretty impressive stuff. Let's say you're that ranger with a 45 cold resistance. Put the cold suit on and now you've got 130 cold resistance. Add onto that any cold resistance buffs that will be thrown your way, and you stand a hell of a lot better chance at defeating Vox. Two major concerns a lot of people might have with this though are what these suits take away. Sure, they add a lot in cold, but what about what they're taking away in magic resistances, AC, STR, HP, etc. Listen, if you lose something like 30 STR or 40 AC from rearranging your jewelery, it's not going to make anywhere close to as much of an impact that adding 85 cold resist would. It's a tradeoff, and a really good one at that. A lot of these cold/fire/disease/poison suits also use items that also have a considerable amount of magic resistance to them as well that just isn't listed here. Listed after the chart is a basic list of additional magic resistances provided by the first four suits, just in case you're worried about things like getting feared and such.

The first rule of fighting dragons like Trakanon, Vox, and Nagafen is to be prepared in the resistances. Know what that MOB's attack type is and then get a good base resistance suit. It means everthing. Otherwise, you might as well not even go on the raid.
