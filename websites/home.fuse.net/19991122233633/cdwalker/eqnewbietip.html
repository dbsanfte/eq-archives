Immediately after starting

SHOW NAMES -Go to options and turn on show PC and NPC names. It makes monsters more visible and lets you know who is near you.

HOT KEYS -You will want to set-up hot keys which is accomplished by clicking and holding the left mouse button on buttons from the right and dragging the key to the far left bottom of the screen. I suggest the war like commands you will use and run. These would be Melee Attack, Slam, Kick, Punch but if you don’t have a bow don’t put Range attack as a hot key.

If you want to set more hot keys then the 6 or 8 it shows press shift+2 and you will see a second set. You can have 6 banks of 6 I believe. The keys 1-6 activate them depending on which hot key bank is open at that time. I suggest Run, Sit, Camp, Persona (from dragging the top icon on the left which handles inventory)

SCREEN WINDOWS -Now you want to change Screen Settings. Press F1. I like full screen with the chat window. Notice you kept the hot keys on the left but lost the buttons on the right. that is why I wanted you to set them up.

FOR VISIBILITY - Turn of the lights and turn your monitors brightness and contrast up as the EQ world is dark and this improves visibility.

PERSPECTIVE – Press F4 if you don’t like the perspective. I like the starting perspective where it is in first person the most.

TRAINING - As soon as you get in the game read the notes in your inventory. Go to PERSONA and click on the notes. You should have atleast 2 notes . The first of which is a note for your level one training. Take the training note and deliver it to the appropriate person. You may want to train in your primary weapon but it will rise on it's own and it has a max for each level. you will advance about 5 points per level and the max goes up 5 points per level. But you start with a max of 10.

BANK - Go find the bank now. Deposit the PK note and whatever item your trainer gave you.

DEATH -You will die frequently to begin with and you don't want to lose items so store all but your weapon until level 2-3. If you do die at these lower levels you leave all non-newbie stuff on corpse. You lose no experience and you have ½ hour to reclaim stuff from your dead body. But you get lost real easy.

EQUIPPING - Best way to equip armor is to drop it on your character that almost always works even when dropping a belt on your waist doesn't. For now just keep your newbie weapon and deposit everything else.

BATTLE - You must of course click on the thing you want to fight to target before you enter battle.

When you fight if you have several options like sword and a hand-to-hand option you can use BOTH AT THE SAME TIME. Same goes for other skills like Harm Touch (shadow knight ability) and sense heading. You can press 1-2-3-4 all at the same time and hone skills and fight well at the same time.

LOOTING - At the end of the battle immediately hit the mouse button on it (I forget which) to loot it. You must be close and looking down on it. Pgup & PgDn make you look up and down arrow keys move you. Space bar to crouch and jump.

GROUPING IN A PARTY - You invite someone or the invite you - a suggested hotkey

when you are invited your invite hotkey changes to follow you hit it and you are now in the group. Groups all gain the experience and you can see their
health levels. You can even be grouped over large distances (like next to the guards while they fight and raise your exp while you piss). Loot however
is not split automatically unless you turn that feature on and then I believe it is just the cash. That is under options if you would like to turn that on.
Once you are in a group the invite button turns to disband and you hit that if you choose to leave the group.
