COCAINE DJ

by Jeff Franzmann

Fog rolling in off the river always reminds me of the times I would make my way through the streets at night. I would cross over a near empty bridge, mist rising from the waters, suspended above the clouds. It was a petty illusion, one I afforded myself simply because I could. 

Dust and ashes. 

I remember the snow in a winter night and getting lost within myself. All the things that now are as if they had never existed. Sitting in the back seat, the city lights neon streaks against the glass. Eyes fixed nowhere in particular, stars lost in the sky. 

The metal feels like flesh. Warm, pitted, almost soft against my palm. It's just a door handle, but a line and a half and I can taste the sounds around me. Black clad and stained with a thousand and one little tragedies that don't mean anything at all, acting out in a carnival atmosphere because, after all, it is what they do best. 

Everything is chronic static, an echo, and I wonder where to go next. Names and faces that promised the world and delivered sweet shit all. It wasn't supposed to be a revolving door, but things aren't what they used to be. I was me but now he's gone. 

Mechanical angst. 

Metronome forms against smoke and the lights, static on the screens, and a hundred and one petty tragedies at the centre of their own little universe. Standing alone before the dials and switches attached to their strings, playing them all. No regrets or tears for the time and space wasted on things best left unsaid, burning behind the eyes reminding me of the things you can't take back. 

No one will ever see what you did to me. 

Looking up at the stars in the sky, on my back in the snow. I never felt so warm in my life. 

I stand on the bridge, mist on the river, lights behind me remind me of a time where I thought some things mattered that never really mattered at all. 

I remember the snow.
