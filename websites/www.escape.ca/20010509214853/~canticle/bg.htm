BACKGROUND

This section will contain any necessary background information which you may require while playing the New Dawn campaign. This includes any Weapons Statistics, new Perks, or special rules which may be in effect during the course of the campaign. It will also contain tips and hints for playing the New Dawn campaign if you are not familiar with Computer Role Playing Games.

SPECIAL NOTE: Please bear in mind that while Fallout:Tactics placed a great deal of emphasis on combat and squad tactics, the New Dawn Campaign will require the use of many skills currently underutilized in the Fallout:Tactics- Brotherhood of Steel missions. The missions in Fallout:Tactics- New Dawn will often require the use of skills such as Barter and Outdoorsman to a far greater degree than in Brotherhood of Steel.

 

BROTHERHOOD OF STEEL

In the Western Wastes (the lands west of the Rocky Mountains, south of Canada), the Brotherhood of Steel is a military/religious organization which is devoted to the hoarding and preservation of technology. They aggressively seek out repositories of tech left over from the great war, seeking not only to obtain it for themselves, but to prevent it from falling into the hands of the wastelands inhabitants. The Brotherhood believes that only through their efforts, guidance, and technology can the world return to a state of grace. They are a secretive, xenophobic organization which has no room within its ranks for those who would consort with those who would return the world to chaos and anarchy. Their battles against the Masters mutants have left them with a deep seated loathing and mistrust of Super Mutants, and they have little love or sympathy for the Ghouls, who they view as parasitic scavengers that serve only to remind the world of the devastation mankind visited upon itself.

The Brotherhood of Steel has come into conflict with its rebellious splinter faction from across the Rockies, who they believe have violated the most sacred tenets of the Brotherhood. In addition to their excesses in pacifying the wastelands, the splinter faction of the Brotherhood is perceived as having aided the enemies of mankind in their acceptance of Super Mutants and Ghouls within their ranks. Their aggressive pursuit of recruits and willingness to trade technology is viewed as anathema to what the Brotherhood is trying to accomplish. While there is no open conflict, the two factions of the Brotherhood are hardly on the best of terms.
