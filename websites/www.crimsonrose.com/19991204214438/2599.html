Wednesday - February 10, 1999

A Note On The Hysteria (Scribe 5:27pm AST)

Here is a report from Mr.Smedster himself on the current status of the EQ servers, which I might add, we can still not get into;

Hello Everyone,

Well as you can see nothing is ever easy!! We have already tracked down several problems.
They are as follows:

1) Confirmation Codes - Those of you that have registered may not have gotten the confirmation
code emails due to a problem we uncovered. Thus we are turning off this function alltogether.
You won't need to wait for any confirmation codes. In fact, we won't be sending them at all.. so
you will be able to register and play immediately. This DOES involve a client patch so you will
need to download a few files.

2) A few other server side errors - We've already fixed a couple of big ones that we have only
seen since 20k people tried to get in at once. We believe we still have at least one more big one
that we are tracking, so it's likely that this next update won't alleviate the problem.

3) We *think* we may know why some people didn't get their Account Key emails, so we will be
resending those as well. It is related to #1 above.

We can only work as fast as we can folks - Please be patient. We want you in here as badly as
you want to be playing. It's just a very intensive task to debug this type of code. We'll get it all
taken care though and we know you'll be pleased once we do.

I will also try and keep updating as frequently as possible. Frankly, it's just hard to post due to
the insane amount of board traffic.

Thanks for your continued patience.

John Smedley

 

EQ Server Status (Scribe 2:22pm AST)

It would appear that the time frame given by 989 was a bit premature. We are sitting at 2.5 hours after the "release" of P4 and still the servers are not up. I for one am a bit discouraged. Probably though, that I am 'itchy' to get in and play. But non-the-less, it is disheartening not to play at the time they said they would have it up. But having everyone try at once kinda puts a kink into things. Personally, I think that they are still working on it and did not get everything they needed to get done, done. People are complaining that they can not make accounts, can not validate thier accounts, and when, if by some sort of luck, they get to validate thier accounts, when they go to log on, they get booted to thier desktop. Hrm. Here is a response from the EQ Team about the server status;

Hello All,

Well.. the incredible load on our registration server has uncovered some bugs. That's what
Beta's are all about!! We are fixing them as we speak and the fixes should be up very shortly.
Those of you that are crashing out, when logging in, we believe it's actually a server side
problem, but we are working on it.

For now, the reg server is down until we've licked these problems.

John Smedley

Here is another blurb from Smed-ster;

Ok.. as many of you may be noticing, the login process is a bit slow (that's an understatement).

The simple fact is that 25,000 people are trying to do it at the same time and it's a bottleneck.

Please keep trying it and this will sort itself out over the next few hours.

It will also help if when you login you go straight into the game and don't hang out in chat.

John Smedley

Verant Answer's About Registration (Scribe 4:35pm AST)

John Smedly of Verant Interactive has posted on EQV's General Message Board about the registration proccess:

I've seen a couple of posts that have questions regarding registration. These are based off of the
Phase IV FAQ on www.everquest.com.

1) Station Name and Password - These are basically going to be your login name and password.
They are different from your Chat Handle.

2) Credit Card - No you won't need to enter that stuff right now. Towards the end of Phase 4 we
are going to be doing a dry-run on our billing system, but you won't be charged anything so don't
worry. Sorry for any confusion the FAQ has generated on that issue.

3) NO. YOU CAN'T REGISTER RIGHT NOW. Not until tomorrow at 12:00 PM PST. Please don't
try. We've been seeing an enormous amount of people trying. We are still getting everything
ready

4) Will patching now do any good? Well, the short answer is NO. We are putting up new stuff all
day today and whatever time we are done (probably very late) we will try and post so that people
can patch in preparation for tomorrow if they wish (it's not a big patch anyways).

Smed

EQ vs. UO (karsus 10:46am AST)

What's this, EQ Producer Brad McQuaid posting on the UOVault's Message board? That he did, in response to untrue statements about EQ made by "Gryphon of Great Lakes". Here are the actual messages;

a: I don't have the techincal support for EQ. When your poor like me, you can't afford the MINIMUM SYSTEM REQUIREMENT of a 3d accelerator. Even with a 3d card, it is going to lag like hell.

(Brad)Nope -- a game like this being 3d or 2d has little to nothing to do with lag.

Right now, in beta, you have what...a few hundred people with a graphic engine near quake II quality? Imagine when it releases 1500 per server (the max...to low in my opinion), with 28.8 connections, low rate 3d card, lag everywhere.

(Brad)Nope. During phase 3 of testing we had 1700 people on a server, with all sorts of connections, 3d cards, etc. There was NOT 'lag everywhere'. In fact, people are pretty blown away as to how lag resistant EQ is.

: B: UO is a rpg. EQ is a rpg. UO is faster, original, has more of a rpg aspect (Can we say virtual economy? I haven't heard that word from anyone in EQ).

(Brad) Depends what you mean by a 'virtual economy'. We have a 'player-driven economy' that depends on tried and true principles such as supply and demand. 

Plus i've already devoted enough of my time to this great game so if you want to go and complain about Uo and how EQ is god, go to www.eqvault.com...we don't want your kind here. 

(Brad) Well, I can respect that, however I can't stand by and let misinformation about EQ float around either.

 -Brad

 Looks as though Brad doesn't take kindly to false rumors drifting about that speak poorly of EQ.
