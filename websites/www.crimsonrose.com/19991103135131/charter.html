CR Mission Statement

We, the Members of the The Crimson Rose, in Order to form a more perfect Guild, establish Justice, insure domestic Tranquility, provide for the common defense, promote the general Welfare, and secure the Blessings of Good to ourselves and our Posterity, do ordain and establish this Constitution for the Membership of The Crimson Rose.

The CR High Council / Noble Branch

Section 1

Clause 1: All Noble Powers herein granted, shall be vested in a High Council of The Crimson Rose, which shall consist of prominent members (Dukes/Barons/Royal Offices) chosen by the King and Queen. The High Council will be coordinated by the Queen. Council members may, during a Council meeting, present proposals for approval by the council to the Queen, in writing (counsel message board), before a meeting. Any member, Council Member or the Lower House may issue a proposal for review by the High Council. The Royal Chairman will appoint a Sergeant at Arms (the Inquisitor) to help administer the meetings and proposals.

Clause 2: During meetings scheduled and lead by the Royal Chairman, the proposals will be presented, discussed, and then voted on by the Council. The Queen may attend and vote on presented proposals. If a proposal is passed, it is recorded and moves to the King for final approval. If the King approves it, it becomes Law, if the King veto's it, it is returned to the Council for review. If three quarters of the Council vote for it again, and the Queen also votes for it, the Kings' veto is overridden and the proposal becomes Law.

Clause 3: The Council will decide on meeting times and lengths. Meetings may proceed even when Council members are absent, if the Queen and Royal Chairman concur that the said meeting should continue. In a veto override vote, all Council members must be present.

Clause 4: The High Council Proposal to Law Procedure Summary and Protocol;

a) A Member contact a High Council Member of his choice and shares an idea for a proposal. Or a High Council Member creates an idea himself that is worthy of a High Council Proposal.
b) The High Council Member, writes the idea in Proposal forum.
c) The High Council Member posts the proposal on the High Council Message Board;

1. As a root message in the High Council Message board, the Message is identified as a proposal because the subject is in ALL CAPITAL LETTERS
2. Discussion between Council Members regarding the proposal will be done in the form of Message Replies to the original message (The Proposal itself), or in High Council Meetings.
3. Only the original Proposal Submitter may agree to amend the proposal. If another Council Member wishes to change a proposal and the Original Submitter does not agree, it must be submitted as a new proposal.
4. The Royal Chairman of the High Council will monitor the page to see when a Submitted Proposal is 10 days old. On the first Wednesday after that, the proposal will voted on.

d) The outcome of the vote on the proposal will be posted on the message board or the main news webpage. It will be added to the Constitution and eventually deleted from the High Council Message Board.

Section 2

Clause 1: The High Council shall be composed of Members chosen by the King and Queen.
Clause 2: No Person shall be a High Council Member who has not attained good social standing, not in a leadership position or been a member for less then three(3) months.
Clause 3: A High Council Members term is 1 year (unless forcably removed). At that time his position may be reviewed by the King and Queen.
Clause 4: The Queen and Royal Chairman can cast 2 votes each to over turn a tie vote.

Section 3

Clause 1: The High Council shall be the Judge of the Elections, Returns and Qualifications of its own Members, and a Majority shall constitute a Quorum to do Business; but a smaller Number may adjourn from day to day, and may be authorized to compel the Attendance of absent Members, in such Manner, and under such Penalties as the High Council may provide.
Clause 2: The High Council may determine the Rules of its Proceedings, punish its Members for disorderly Behavior, and, with the Concurrence from the King and Queen, expel a Council or Guild Member.
Clause 3: The High Council shall keep a Journal of its Proceedings, and from time to time, publish the same, excepting such Parts as may in their Judgment, require Secrecy; and the Yeas and Nays of the Members of the High Council on any question shall, at the Desire of two thirds of those Present, be entered on the Journal.
Clause 4: The High Council shall not adjourn for more than one week.

Section 4

Clause 1: Every Proposal which shall have passed by the High Council, shall, before it become a Law and is implemented, be presented to the King; If he approves, he shall endorse it, but if not he shall return it, with his Objections to the High Council in which it originated, who shall enter the Objections at large on their Journal, and proceed to reconsider it.

Section 5

Clause 1: The High Council shall have Power To lay and collect Dues, Imposts and Excises, to pay the Debts and provide for the common Defense and general Welfare of The Crimson Rose.
Clause 2: To borrow Money on the credit of The Crimson Rose;
Clause 3: To regulate Commerce with foreign Guilds and organizations;
Clause 4: To establish a uniform Rules of Membership, and uniform Laws of conduct;
Clause 5: To hunt and provide for the Punishment of Guilds, Organizations, groups, and/or individuals who are guilty of Murder;
Clause 6: To define and punish Piracies and Felonies committed in the worlds with which we reside, and Offences against the Laws of King Dantrag and Queen Jenna;
Clause 7: To declare War, grant Letters of Marquee and Reprisal, and make Rules concerning Captures on Land and Water;
Clause 8: To raise and support Armies, to enforce The Crimson Rose's Laws;
Clause 9: To make Rules for the Government and Regulation of these Armies (Rules of Engagement);
Clause 10: To provide for Members to execute the Laws of the Guild, suppress Insurrections and repel attacks;
Clause 11: To make all Laws which shall be necessary and proper for carrying into Execution the foregoing Powers, and all other Powers vested by this Constitution in the Government of The Crimson Rose, or in any Department or Officer thereof.

Section 6

Clause 1: No Single Member shall enter into any Treaty, Alliance, or Confederation; grant Letters of Marquee and Reprisal, or create a Law.

King / Queen / Royal Branch

Section 1

Clause 1: The Royal Power shall be vested in a King. He shall hold his Office during the Term of Life, and together, with the Queen chosen, for the same Term.
Clause 2: In Case of the Death , Resignation, or Inability to discharge the Powers and Duties of the said Office, the Same shall devolve on the Queen, and the High Council would vote on a replacement. In the case of the Queen, the King would select another. In the case of the King, Queen would then act as King until the Disability be removed, or a new King elected by the High Council.
Clause 3: Before the King can enter on the Execution of his Office, he shall take the following Oath or Affirmation:--"I do solemnly swear (or affirm) that I will faithfully execute the position of King of The Crimson Rose, and will do, to the best of my Ability, preserve, protect and defend the Laws of The Crimson Rose and it's members."
Clause 4: The Queen is authorized to carry out the duties of the King in his absence. This position has all the powers of King in accordance with the wishes and duties assigned by the King. The Queen is second in command and her orders will not be questioned.

Section 2

Clause 1: The King shall be Commander in Chief of the Army.
Clause 2: He shall have Absolute Power over the guild, with the Advice of the High Council, to make Treaties and Execute duties of the guild if there is a vested interest in said interests, and he shall nominate, and by and with the Advice of the Royal Chairman, shall appoint Ambassadors, other public Ministers and Consuls, and all other Officers of The Crimson Rose, whose Appointments are not herein otherwise provided for, and which shall be established by Law.
Clause 3: The King and Queen shall have Power to fill up all Vacancies that may happen in the High Council.

Section 3

He shall from time to time give to the High Council Information of the State of the Guild, and recommend to their Consideration such Measures as he shall judge necessary and expedient; he may, convene the High Council.

Section 4

The King and Queen and all civil Officers of The Crimson Rose, shall be removed from Office on Conviction of, Treason, Bribery, or other high Crimes and/or Misdemeanors.

Section 5

Clause 1: The King (and Queen in his absence) shall have Power to enforce the powers below on a day to day basis. The High Council may choose to review these actions and may overturn executive decisions with the same 2/3rd vote required to overturn a veto at their next scheduled meeting. The High Council may enact any of these powers on its own by proposal and majority vote. Only the High Council has the right to enact a Tax or member dues.
Clause 2: To borrow Money on the credit of The Crimson Rose;
Clause 3: To regulate Commerce with foreign Guilds and organizations;
Clause 4: To establish a uniform Rules of Membership, and uniform Laws of conduct;
Clause 5: To hunt and provide for the Punishment of Guilds, Organizations, groups, and individual who are guilty of Murder;
Clause 6: To define and punish Piracies and Felonies committed in Norrath, and Offences against the Laws of King Dantrag;
Clause 7: To declare War, grant Letters of Marquee and Reprisal, and make Rules concerning Captures on Land and Water;
Clause 8: To raise and support Armies, to enforce The Crimson Roses' Laws;
Clause 9: To provide for Members to execute the Laws of the Guild, suppress Insurrections and repel attacks;
Clause 10: The King and Queen may punish Members for disorderly Behavior, and, when deemed necessary expel a Council or Guild Member.

Executive / Legislative Powers

Section 1

Clause 1: Treason against The Crimson Rose, shall consist expulsion and in levying War against them, or in adhering to their Enemies, giving them Aid and Comfort. No Person shall be convicted of Treason unless on the Testimony of two Witnesses to the same overt Act, or on Confession in open Council Meeting.
Clause 2: The High Council shall have Power to declare the Punishment of Treason, but no Attainer of Treason shall work Corruption of Blood, or Forfeiture except during the Life of the Person attained.
Clause 3: The High Council shall have Power to dispose of and make all needful Rules and Regulations respecting the Territory or other Property belonging to The Crimson Rose; and nothing in this Constitution shall be so construed as to Prejudice any Claims of The Crimson Rose.

Champion of the Rose

Section 1

Clause 1: The Champion of the Rose shall be selected by the King or Queen initially or in the absence of a Champion.
Clause 2: The Champion of the Rose shall retain his title for the period of 1 (one) week or until dethroned by the Charter of the Rose.
Clause 3: The Champion of the Rose can and will be challenged each week by a member of The Crimson Rose to attain the right to wear the colors of The Champion of the Rose.
Clause 4: The Champion of the Rose rules for dueling are as follows:

a) The Champion of the Rose may only be challenged by a member only once per week. The Champion of the Rose can be challenged as many times as CR has members. But only one member may challenge per week.
b) The Champion of the Rose has to accept the duel. No matter the level involved.
c) The Champion of the Rose and the challenger will meet at the xroads in ButcherBlock Mountains (or an alterante area if needed) fro the deul to take place.
d) Before the duel can take place, 2 (two) member MUST be present as witnesses to the duel.
e) Once the duel begins, it is not over until one of the combatants surrender to the other and a victor is proclaimed by the 2 (two) witenesses.
f) A duel will not result in death. The duel is over when one of the combatants has reached thier last "bubble" of life, and it is the responsibilty of the witnesses to call the combat to an end when this occurs.
g) If death results from the duel, the witnesses will send to the inquisitor what the events were leading to the death of the member and it will be the determination of The Inquisitor on the appropriate punishment to give out.
h) Once a victor is proclaimed, both witnesses will send in a pigeon to Duke Cirith for him to pass along to the King, at which point, the proclamation will be posted for all to see.

Clause 5: The Champion of the Rose will defend the Honor of the King, Queen and The Crimson Rose as a whole, when a member is challenged.

Amending the Constitution

Section 1

The High Council, whenever two thirds vote deem it necessary, shall propose Amendments to this Constitution.

Membership Code

1) No member of The Crimson Rose will use bugs or exploits of any kind.
2) No member of The Crimson Rose will use foul language to taunt and or racial slurs under any circumstances.
3) The Crimson Rose will respect fallen enemies, provided they respect The Crimson Rose, in return (You are not required to assist a fallen enemy that is taunting, insulting, or disrespectful in word or conduct).
4) Enforcement: CR members should be prepared to supply proof of any charges (screen shots or logs) and submit them to the The Inquisitor.
5) CR members strive for high social standing.
6) CR members are against evil and murderers.
7) CR members support each other to provide safety in numbers.
8) CR members donate part of their income to the advancement of The Crimson Rose or what the Council has deemed to be the appropriate amount
9) CR members help other good players when possible.
10) Must wear The Crimson Rose Uniform at all times
11) Members will show due respect to the offices and ranks appointed over them
