Producers Letter (Scribe 8:46pm AST)

Here is the Producers Letter that has been recently updated from the Everquest Site:

6/29/99
Welcome to the new Producer's Letter!

Yes, I realize there was one before, but it was rarely updated. The reason for that, honestly, was that for so long I've always communicated to EQ players primarily through posts on message boards and fan sites. In fact, for those of you who have been with EQ since before even the Beta, that's kind of where it all started. A lot of the excitement and anticipation generated for EverQuest before release was grass-roots in a sense, with me posting and doing chats and all sorts of fan sites and guild pages popping up into existence.

It was a wonderful time and it's a communication process I've both enjoyed and feel comfortable with. But the problem now is simply how many people are playing EverQuest, and reading and posting to these sites. It's hard for me to get the message out sometimes - I've even posted before in response to a player and then the next day read another post on the same site complaining that they haven't seen me post for weeks and how we need to improve our communication. There were simply so many posts that mine was gone by the time this person began reading.

So it occurred to me (and it probably should have occurred to me earlier - doh!) that there has to be a better way for me to address the EQ community as a whole in order to keep all of you more informed. It is my hope to at least weekly, if not more often, post the Producer's Letter here on everquest.com and that the various fan sites and other EQ related boards would copy this letter into their news or to wherever else makes sense.

Now, before any of you panic, I'm still going to be out there reading the boards and posting responses probably just as often as I always have been. It's something I enjoy and I think it's very important to the growing community that we keep this two-way communication going. But the difference will be that whenever it makes sense to post something lengthy and in-depth, I'll use the Producer's Letter. That way, when major issues come up, I can reach the most people. The types of posts I'll still make to various boards and newsgroups will more often be answers to specific questions or simply general comments.

And, of course, the various sites and discussion boards will quite often be the source for my inspiration to write the Producer's Letter. It's where I'll gather topics and ideas. You can expect to see all sorts of issues addressed, including camping, class balance, the player-driven economy, crowding, connectivity, etc. That's right - I won't shirk from the heated controversial stuff at all. You'll also see me discuss how things work here at Verant, what features and additions we're working on and how we're working on them. I'll talk about how we meet together as a Dev Team and debate issues and how changes are implemented. And I'll also cover exciting new stuff, like the new zones we're working on that will be downloadable in the future, as well as the huge Expansion coming next year.

Anyway, I really hope this helps. I'm sure the heated and passionate discussions will continue, and that is both normal and healthy for an ever evolving and dynamic game like EverQuest. But at least everyone will know better our positions on the various issues and what our plans are to address them. You'll know that we're thinking about things too over here -- and not just thinking, but working very hard to earn the money you've spent and continue to spend on EverQuest.

I'll see you next week,

-Brad McQuaid
Producer, EverQuest
Vice President, Verant Interactive

From the Tower... (Karsus 7:57pm AST)

Here is the scoop on the patch due for tomorrow ...

Due to the situation which occurred on the CerfNet servers on Tuesday and Thursday night, we have had to place most of our resources towards rectifying the problem on the live servers. However, we were able to make a few various changes to the Test server, some of which will be propagated to the live servers on Monday. The features and bug fixes are listed below.

- Currently, when a character dies, they return with 25% of their Mana and Health, in addition, they have a massive hit to their stats. - This will NOT remain true, and we will be removing it next week.

- Nagafen's Lair and Permafrost have been revamped with new treasure, items, etc. In addition, changes have been made to NPC spawning to the them.

- A number of fixes were made to Research.

The following fixes have been made to spells.

Vampiric Curse / Bond of Death (formerly Circle of Death) now work with their new effects

Fufil's Curtailing Chant (BARD) now works on monsters

Magician pet attack spells have been slightly increased.

Incapacitate now works for Enchanters (lvl 44 spell)

Wizard "Casting Times" for damage spells have been slightly reduced

Necromancer poison spells have had their damage doubled and their duration cut in half. They will do EXACTLY the same amount of damage, but in half the pervious amount of time.

Necromancer and Shaman disease spells have had their duration reduced. Their damage has been upped to compensate.

Hungry Earth (Necromancer) is now researchable - check your book

Research for Thunderclap (Wizard) is now correct - check your book

Research for Fire Spiral of Al'Kabor (Wizard) is now correct - check your book

The Magician Burnout series has been made a bit better.

The reagent for Burnout III has been removed (Magician) Tuyen's Songs (Bard) have had their damage increased.

New Bard Songs have been added to vendors.

New Wizard Spell "Bonds of Force" on Wizard vendors.

Mana Sieve (Enchanter) has been changed. It is now an instant spell (instead of a DOT) and costs less mana (with less effect).

If all of the changes prove effective, we will be updating the live servers with this data on Tuesday morning of the 29th.

- The EverQuest Team

Here is an informative site about Warriors and the massive amounts of restrictions placed upon them - enjoy!

After much heated debate on the Forums I am proposing a solution to make the warrior become king of melee combat. It does not involve the implementation of any new skills into the game.

1.Remove the Cap on Combat Skills (offense, defense, 1H slash, kick, dual wield, parry, etc.) which comes into effect around level 40.
2.Allow Warriors to advance 7 points in Combat Skills per level instead of just 5.

This would mean that at beginning levels warriors show small improvement at melee over the hybrids, but by the time they hit the teens and the hybrids are getting their spells the warriors are starting to out-melee them. They would be hitting much more often and they would be much harder to hit.
