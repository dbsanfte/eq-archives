Here is an outline for members and potential members of The Crimson . These guidelines are to be strictly adhered to. If you think you will not be able to accommodate ANY of these mandates we have set forth, it would be productive for you as well as the guild that you reconsider your membership and/or de-tag yourself now, from The Crimson Rose. For failure to abide by these will result in disciplinary action against you by The Crimson Rose and/or the officers of the guild.

CR Mission Statement


We, Members of The Crimson Rose, in order to form a more perfect guild, establish justice, insure domestic tranquility, provide for the common defense, promote the general Welfare, and secure the blessings of Good to ourselves and our posterity, do ordain and establish this constitution for the membership of The Crimson Rose.


Paragraph I, The Crimson Rose Council

Section 1
Clause 1: All responsibilities herein granted, shall be vested in a Council of The Crimson Rose, which shall consist of prominent members (officers) chosen by the Director General (herein shall be notated as DG), Command General (CG) and Lord/Lady Governor (LG). The Council will be coordinated by the Lord/Lady Governor (chosen by the DG). Council members may, during a Council session, present proposals for approval by the Council to the Lord/Lady Governor (officer chosen by the DG), in writing, before a meeting. Any member or Council member may issue a proposal for review to the Council. The Lord/Lady Governor will appoint a Secretary General to help administer the sessions and proposals.

Clause 2: During sessions scheduled and led by the Lord/Lady Governor, the proposals will be presented, discussed, and then voted on by the Council. The DG may attend and vote on presented proposals. If a proposal is passed, it is recorded and moves to the DG for final approval. If the DG approves it, it becomes law. If the DG veto's it, it is returned to the Council for review. If three-quarters of the Council vote for it again, and the Lord/Lady Governor also votes for it, the DG’s veto is overridden and the proposal becomes Law.

Clause 3: The Council will decide on session times and lengths. Sessions may proceed even when Council members are absent, if the Lord/Lady Governor concurs that the said session should continue. In a veto override vote, all Council members must be present.

Clause 4: The Council Proposal to Law Procedure Summary and Protocol; a) A member contacts a Council member of his choice and shares an idea for a proposal, or a Council member creates an idea himself that is worthy of a Council proposal. b) The Council member writes the idea in proposal forum. d) The outcome of the vote on the proposal will be posted on the main news web page. It will be added to the constitution.

Section 2
Clause 1: The Council shall be composed of members chosen by the DG or Lord/Lady Governor.

Clause 2: No Person shall be a Council Member who has not attained good social standing, not in a leadership position or been a member for less then three(3) months.

Clause 3: A Council members term is 1 year (unless forcibly removed). At that time, his position may be reviewed by the DG and/or Lord/Lady Governor.

Clause 4: The DG and CG can cast 2 votes each to over-turn a tie vote.

Section 3
Clause 1: The Council shall be the judge of the elections, returns and qualifications of its own members. A majority shall constitute a quorum to do business; but a smaller number may adjourn from day to day, and may be authorized to compel the attendance of absent members, in such manner, and under such penalties as the Council may provide.

Clause 2: The Council may determine the rules of its proceedings, punish its members for disorderly behavior, and, with the concurrence from the DG, expel a Guild Member.

Clause 3: The Council shall keep a journal of its proceedings, and from time to time, publish the same, excepting such parts as may in their judgment, require secrecy, and the yeas and nays of the members of the Council on any question shall, at the desire of two-thirds of those present, be entered in the journal.

Clause 4: The Council shall not adjourn for more than one week (as outside circumstances deem appropriate).

Section 4
Clause 1: Every proposal, which shall have passed by the Council, shall, before it becomes a law and is implemented, be presented to the DG. If he approves, he shall endorse it, but if not he shall return it, with his objections to the Council in which it originated, who shall enter the objections at large in their journal, and proceed to reconsider it as per Paragraph 1, Section 1, Clause 2.

Section 5
Clause 1: The Council shall have power to lay and collect dues, imposts and excises, to pay the debts and provide for the common defense and general welfare of The Crimson Rose.

Clause 2: To borrow money on the credit of The Crimson Rose.

Clause 3: To regulate commerce with foreign guilds and organizations.

Clause 4: To establish a uniform rules of membership, and uniform laws of conduct.

Clause 5: To hunt and provide for the punishment of guilds, organizations, groups, and/or individuals who are guilty of offenses against The Crimson Rose;

Clause 6: To define and punish piracies and felonies committed in the worlds with which we reside, and offences against the laws of The Crimson Rose.

Clause 7: To declare war, grant letters of marquee and reprisal, and make rules concerning captures on land and water.

Clause 8: To raise and support armies, to enforce The Crimson Rose's laws and interests.

Clause 9: To make rules for the government and regulation of these armies (rules of engagement).

Clause 10: To provide for members to execute the laws of the guild, suppress insurrections and repel attacks.

Clause 11: To make all laws which shall be necessary and proper for carrying into execution the foregoing powers, and all other powers vested by this constitution in the government of The Crimson Rose, or in any department or officer thereof.

Section 6
Clause 1: No single member shall enter into any treaty, alliance, or confederation; grant letters of marquee and reprisal, or create a law save the DG and/or a majority vote of the Council.

Paragraph II, Director General

Section 1
Clause 1: The guild responsibilities shall be vested in a Director General. He shall hold his office during the term of his life.

Clause 2: In case of the death, resignation, or inability to discharge the responsibilities and duties of the said office, the same shall devolve on the Command General, and then the Council would vote on a suitable replacement. In the case of the Command General, the DG would select a suitable replacement. In the case of the DG, the Command General would then act as DG until the disability be removed, or a new DG elected by the Council.

Clause 3: Before the DG can enter on the execution of his Office, he shall take the following “Oath or Affirmation”:--"I do solemnly swear (or affirm) that I will faithfully execute the position of Director General of The Crimson Rose, and will do, to the best of my ability, preserve, protect and defend the Laws and interests of The Crimson Rose and it's members."

Clause 4: The Command General is authorized to carry out the duties of the DG in his absence. This position has all the responsibilities of DG in accordance with the wishes and duties assigned by the DG. The Command General is second in command and his/her orders will not be questioned under penalty of the DG and/or Council.

Section 2
Clause 1: The DG shall be “Commander in Chief” of the guild army.

Clause 2: The DG shall have the absolute responsibility over the guild. With the advice of the Council, to make treaties and execute duties of the guild if there is a vested interest in said relations. He shall nominate, and by and with the advice of the Command General, shall appoint Ambassadors, other public Ministers and Consuls, and all other officers of The Crimson Rose, whose appointments are not herein otherwise provided for, and which shall be established by law.

Clause 3: The DG shall have responsibility to fill all vacancies that may happen in the Council.

Section 3
Clause 1:The DG shall, from time to time, give to the Council information of the “State of the Guild”, and recommend to their consideration such measures as he shall judge necessary and expedient; he may, convene the Council.

Section 4
Clause 1: The DG and all civil officers of The Crimson Rose, shall be removed from office on conviction of, treason, bribery, or other high crimes and/or misdemeanors.

Section 5
Clause 1: The DG (and Command General in his absence) shall have responsibility to enforce the duties below on a day to day basis. The Council may choose to review these actions and may overturn executive decisions with the same 2/3rd vote required to overturn a veto at their next scheduled session. The Council may enact any of these responsibilities on its own by proposal and majority vote. Only the Council has the right to enact tax or member dues.

Clause 2: To borrow money on the credit of The Crimson Rose.

Clause 3: To regulate commerce with foreign guilds and organizations.

Clause 4: To establish uniform rules of membership, and uniform laws of conduct.

Clause 5: To hunt and provide for the punishment of guilds, organizations, groups, and individual who are guilty of crimes against The Crimson Rose.

Clause 6: To define and punish piracies and felonies committed in the worlds with which we reside, and offences against the laws of The Crimson Rose.

Clause 7: To declare war, grant letters of marquee and reprisal, and make rules concerning captures on land and water.

Clause 8: To raise and support armies, to enforce The Crimson Roses' laws.

Clause 9: To provide for members to execute the laws of the guild, and suppress insurrections and repel attacks.

Clause 10: The DG may punish members for disorderly behavior, and, when deemed necessary expel a Council or guild member.

Paragraph III - Executive / Legislative Responsibilities


Section 1
Clause 1: Treason against The Crimson Rose, shall consist expulsion and in levying war against them, or in adhering to their enemies, giving them aid and comfort. No person shall be convicted of treason unless on the testimony of two witnesses to the same overt act, or on confession in open Council session.

Clause 2: The Council shall have the responsibility to declare the punishment of treason, but no attainder of treason shall work corruption of blood, or forfeiture except during the life of the person attained.

Clause 3: The Council shall have the responsibility to dispose of and make all needful rules and regulations respecting the territory or other property belonging to The Crimson Rose; and nothing in this constitution shall be so construed as to prejudice any claims of The Crimson Rose.

Clause 3: The DG has supreme and total Executive/Legislative responsibility wherein The Crimson Rose is compromised.


Paragraph IV - Amending the Constitution

Section 1
Clause 1. The Council, whenever two-thirds vote deem it necessary, shall propose amendments to this constitution.

Clause 2. The DG may, at his leisure, amend this document.

Paragraph VI - Membership Code

Section 1
Clause 1. No member of The Crimson Rose will use bugs or exploits of any kind in the worlds with which we reside.

Clause 2. No member of The Crimson Rose will use hard foul language (fuck, goddammit, Motherfucker, dickhead, pussy or anything deemed “hard” language) to taunt and or racial slurs or sexual innuendoes under any circumstances.

Clause 3. The Crimson Rose will respect fallen enemies, provided they respect The Crimson Rose, in return (You are not required to assist a fallen enemy that is taunting, insulting, or disrespectful in word or conduct).

Clause 4. Enforcement: CR members should be prepared to supply proof of any charges (screen shots or logs) and submit them to the Council if accused of a crime or accusing someone of a crime.

Clause 5. CR members strive for high social standing.

Clause 6. CR members are against evil and its ilk.

Clause 7. CR members support each other to provide safety in numbers.

Clause 8. CR members donate part of their income to the advancement of The Crimson Rose or what the Council has deemed to be the appropriate amount (if enacted by the Council)

Clause 9. CR members help other good players when possible.

Clause 10. Must wear The Crimson Rose uniform at all times (guild tag). No “/anon” is allowed under any circumstances.

Clause 11. Members will show due respect to the offices and ranks appointed over them

Clause 12. Failure to abide by this Code, constitution and all other guidelines herein contained, shall result in warnings, fines levied and extra duty, but not to exclude excommunication (expulsion) from The Crimson Rose.
