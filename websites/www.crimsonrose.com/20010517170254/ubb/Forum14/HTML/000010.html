Ok

I arrived at 6:45 pm BST, which should have equated too 10:45 PST and called out across the guild if any members had arrived for the guild event.

I recieved a number of replies asking what guild event!

By 7:30PM still noone had showed up apart from Katoth, we where to meet in the large cave in the tunnel at East Commons. Disapointedly I hitched up my armour and headed off to Kunark.

I later found out that there had been a time mix up and that some players had turned up at another time wondering where everyone else was.

But this is not what my rant is about.

There where some guild members that where confused about where and what time the event was to happen. There where also a lot of guild members not aware that the event was even happening.

Although I have not logged on to EQ for a while I would ask, was a Message of the Day sent up about this event? Also I myself noticed that the notice about the event had been pushed down from the main home page by other posts. Would it be an idea to have a spot availble where guild events can easily be seen by players who are flying through the web page?

I will say that the time of 11:00am PST for guild events is a good time as this allows European players - Myself Included - to participate without getting up at times like 3:00 am - Not that the baby does not already get up at that time -. But would it be an idea if two times where posted when the event is set up, perhaps the PST time and the GMT time. Maybe this would save confusion on the times!

Also can we have the message of the day again detailing the web page home address. It seems that we have members that do not know much about the guild, I would encourage these people to become more in contact - Maybe they are alt chars I am not sure -.

I think guild events are good and we should publish them more, they give a good excuse to bring everyone together. As it is I believe that currently we are all spread thinly arround Norrath. I also enjoy guild events and when we get a guild group we are all guaranteed a good time.
