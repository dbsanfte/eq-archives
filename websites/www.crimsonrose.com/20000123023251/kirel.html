The Making of a Knight

It was a day like any other. The day was warm and the sky blue. The midwife was in the room with Al'yana Arheldral. The Knight Rabuschka stood outside the birthing room, waiting for permission to come in. He winced at the screams that issued forth from Al`yana's mouth. Dimly, he heard a soothing voice give comfort to his wife. Suddenly, there was the mother of all screams. It turned to a whimper and then weeping. A gasp of delight was heard, along with the *snick* of a cutting tool. A sharp slap, followed by wailing. It was a pure sound, that wailing was. It was the sound of a baby. Not being able to wait any longer, the Knight strode into the room, face going white at all the blood. A hand supported him to a chair. He sat.
" Your son." Stated the wizened old woman as she handed him a bundle of rags. The Knight looked at the rags in puzzlement, then slowly moved back a portion with a heavy finger. "Be careful " commanded the old woman.

The Knight looked deep into his son's blue eyes. He turned toward his wife, she lay on her bed, in a pallor. She saw him looking and gazed lovingly back. "This was a miracle " he thought. My wife.. an elf, and I a Human. This doesn't happen much. He looked back into his sons eyes and said " My son, you are a Noble One. " Thus was named that day, Kirel Rabuschka..... The Noble One.

When Kirel was five, and by all accounts a precocious child, his Aunt, Tira Arheldral came to visit. She was to marry Relik Sol`Nari. He was Earl of Sol Keep. When Tira and Al'yana stood together, it was impossible to tell the two apart. Both were tall, after the manner of the elves, and both had flowing blonde hair. Tira looked at Kirel like the son that she wanted. She hoped that her children would be as beautiful. However, she was not too hopeful, for Elves have children seldomly. In fact, she and her sister, had been the talk of the Elders. It was the second time in 4000 years that twins had been born into the Elven community.

When Tira left, she bade Rabuschka and Al`yana to let Kirel come to court with her. She wanted Kirel to grow up, within a Noble house. Reluctantly, Rabuschka agreed. In some ways, it was fortuitous that Kirel left with his aunt, for less than 6 cycles of the moon went by, and the villa was raided by Trolls.

" Lady, wake up." The chambermaid said as she shook Lady Al`yana. " Torches approach. "Al`yana woke with a start, she looked around at her maids, and saw the angry orange glow reflecting from the brass frame on the window. She ran to the window, and looked out. "Trolls. " she hisses. " Quickly, my Armor and my bow.

The maids ran from the room, down to the armory. Meanwhile the alarm bell started to ring. Al`yana, still in her night robe, ran to the main hall.

" Ware " she cried. " Trolls come! "

The arms men and women stopped near the gates.. " Lady, shall we close the gates? " shouted one man.

She glanced up with dismay.. " Aye, close the gates, and man the wall. " she commanded.

Her maids had her armor, so she stripped out of her robe, and into the black leather. she settled the helm of virtue upon her head, and grabbed her bow. It was a bow made by the famous elven bow maker, Trellhaki`melliael. They were hard to pull even for the strongest human ranger, but Al`yana had trained all her life. She was a Bowmistress, and battle hardened.

Al`yana strode to the top of the wall, and cried challenge. " Why have you come? " she called?

" Nub gruk lat? We Klomp you! " Cried an especially ugly Troll.

Al`yana prayed hard, then and there, she then signaled to her protectors. " Calhar Andean ! " she screamed as her arrow flashed through the darkness to slam into the Trolls eye. Scores of arrows followed hers. The Trolls charged. They had the numbers, but Al`yana Rabuschka`Nari and her soldiers had the skill. Al`yana led the countercharge. Al`yana was killed.

Rabuschka, having been away, fighting for his king, heard the news, and fell to his knees in grief. He rose, with one thing in mind. Revenge. He broke oath, and left his king, on the battlefield. He rode his Warhorse to the nearest Troll village, and wreaked havoc until he was brought down. As a result of his actions, King Gryfonsbane was killed. His young son, Dantrag, took the throne, and the Dowager queen, left to her estates. Dantrag was mentored by several Nobles in the art ofrulership.

Kirel, heard of his parents death and went to the local shrine. There he swore several things. He took blood oath to never betray his king, and to serve. He thought that perhaps.. one day.. his name would be cleared of the dishonor that had befallen his name. Little did he know, that the new king, had pardoned his father mia culpa.. Thus Kirel's course was set. He would become a paladin.

After receiving his sword, Kirel turned to his cousin, Jenna, and smiled. " I will name him Carrion. " he pronounced. Jenna's cheeks dimpled at hidden pleasure as she laughed. " Rosie.. " she kissed her blade. Then, in apparent glee, she launched an attack at Kirel..

Back and forth, metal clashed on metal. Kirel threw himself forward, arcing Carrion high, while Rosie undercut the blade and forced it over her head. She quickly reversed and Rosie flew at Kirel's stomach. Blade still high, Kirel dropped to the ground and rolled, letting Carrion catch Rosie. Using the force of her blade, Kirel spun on the ground and kicked out with his feet. Jenna tumbled. Kirel laughingly launched himself on her, and wrestled Rosie away. It was then that the King first saw both Kirel and Jenna. The king put finger to mouth, and Kirel understanding, said nothing. The king and his attendants, whisked away.

" My Lady, " began Kirel, " here is your noble blade." Jenna received Rosie, hilt first, with a curtsy.

Day's later, the young Kirel received a call from the chamberlain.

" Ser, your presence is requested at the Castle. "

Kirel ran strait through the doors, heading up through the courtyard, and into the main hall. At the massive doors, he stopped and straightened his tunic. He nodded to the herald.

*BOOM* the heralds staff struck the floor. " Master Kirel, knight in training " he announced.

Kirel entered the chamber. Near the dias, he stopped, and kneeled, one hand on Carrion's hilt, the other, knuckles to the floor. * he had picked this effection up from the kings personal guards * " My Lord, you required my presence? " Kirel asked, head bowed.

Jenna's father, Earl Relik smiled at his foster son. " Rise Kirel. " he commanded.

Kirel rose up, to stand proudly before his Uncle.

" You have corrupted my daughter, " began the earl. " She wishes to become a Paladin. "

At hearing the word " corrupted " Kirel turned green.. then when he heard the rest of the sentence, he laughed out loud. " Hahahahh..." Kirel snorted, " I didn't corrupt her " he snickered while raising his arm. " You did. "

The Earls wife, Tira, a countess in her own right, nodded. " True, dear.. so very true.."

Earl Relik glared at his wife. " Who asked you? "

She answered. " Why.. you did, my love."

Kirel, watching this display, silently composed himself. Then without thought, mused out loud. " But she is bloody good at swordplay."

Now both glared at him.

The end result of that little encounter, was the "punishment" of his life.. Very likely, it actually saved him from death. He was sent to a monastery, to learn more about his god, while at the same time, learning some of the skills that he would later use as a paladin. Naturally, he exceeded at his training. Some years went by, idyllically spent riding horses, crossing swords with the best battle monks this side of Qeynos and learning how to pray healing on someone.

Kirel, grew a little restless, so he bid the monks farewell. He traveled home, to the little villa where he was born. Once there, he discovered that the staff had returned and held him as master. Much to his surprise, he even discovered the secured of his family. It seems that knight Rabuschka ( his father ) had really been a Paladin of the Highest Order. He was called, RedHand. In a musty chest, Kirel pulled out a banner. It was dead-night black, with a blazing blood red hand upon it. Kirel knelt and prayed. The banner started to glow, and the dust melted off. Then Kirel noticed that his own cloak was crumbling to dust. Quickly, Kirel stood up, and grabbed at the banner, which was now floating. The banner lost its glow. A servant had seen the entire incident, and stood there trembling. He kept saying.. " the RedHand has returned..... the RedHand has returned.... " then he fled from the room. Kirel, not knowing what to do, set the banner down and went for a ride.

When he returned, the banner flew from the bell tower at the top of the Villa. All the servants had adopted clothes of black. Each one had a little red hand sewn to the right arm. They called him RedHand... Paladin RedHand, traveled to Sol Keep, with several arms men. He was re-united with his cousin. Now a woman of 17. She was the most beautiful creature that he had ever seen, but he could not think of her any other way than as his little cousin. Soon, he left for Rose Castle, the seat of the Crimson Kingdom. It was his intent, to present himself there for service. Along the way, he and his men were attacked by bandits. They dispatched the bandits with ease.

As they were entering the city, they noticed that flags of grey... grey for mourning ... hung everywhere. Gossip in the streets, said that a trusted member of the high council had died. Determined to help, RedHand rode on. At the Palace gates, Kirel dismounted. He went to the trustle, and was surprised to be met by a man, like himself, dressed in black.

The man was somewhat mysterious... he introduced himself as Tanshiver. Tanshiver brought the paladin strait to the king. He took RedHand, to a room, with nothing but 4 walls and 3 chairs. The King, seemed somewhat youngish, and Tanshiver was a hardened man. The King and Tanshiver grilled him for hours.... or so it seemed.. The Paladin RedHand, not knowing what he had done to deserve this, was growing impatient when the King announced. " Enough! "

" Kneel " commanded King Dantrag Gryfonsbane.

Paladin RedHand kneeled in the manner of the Kings Guards.. Right hand on hilt, left.. knuckles to the floor.

Tanshiver stood back, slightly amused.

The King that day, accepted the most holy vows from RedHands lips. Paladin RedHand, was now a member of court.
Soon, Tanshiver ( who was the Inquisitor of the Realm ) had to leave on a personal journey. The king after deliberation, took Paladin RedHand aside, and asked him to fill the position. Not knowing much about politics or the enforcement of the law, RedHand expressed his doubts, but accepted the duty.

Soon thereafter, came word for the Inquisitor, that an attack was being made ready against Sol Keep. Paladin RedHand, now the Inquisitor, made this known to the King, and rode forth to warn Earl Relik. That first night, the King caught up while Kirel wept at the news of the death of Tira and Relik. He brought the Holy Order of the Thorn with him. All experienced warriors, the Thorn rode like the wind. They rode by the Inquisitor's villa, only to discover Lady Jenna within.. She was hurt. RedHand, stopped to nurse his cousin back to health, while the King and Thorn rode forth to destroy the invading army.

After making sure that the Thorn had things well in hand, the King left for his keep. RedHand as the Inquisitor of the Realm, had the Baron Althor Kivan, executed for his crimes against the Sol'Nari family and the Crown. RedHand stayed near his villa, while the Lady Jenna convelesed, and was brought back to full health. Soon, they set out, in little night raids, to kill all of the bandits that they could find. Kirel taught her all that he could in the arts of the Paladin. She soon surpassed his skill in healing others.

The Inquisitor, instructed by the king, helped the Lady Jenna to re-build Sol Keep. They instituted the creation of some secret passages that are only known to them. Then, when the Keep was nearing completion, summons came. The King wished to see the Paladin, Lady Jenna. After her surprising visit to Rose Castle, Baroness Jenna rode back to Sol Keep, with the Thorn at her side. She now commanded that division. Meanwhile, theInquisitor stayed with the King.

Months went by, and the King, aching for the beauty of Lady Jenna, took the Inquisitor with him, and went to visit Sol Keep and the Lady. Jenna, radiantly beautiful, accepted the kings proposal of marriage. Kirel smiled at the union. Even though pressing security matters kept him away, he wished his cousin happiness. He had grown to love the King as a brother, and he already loved the new queen like a sister. The Inquisitor grew ever more diligent in his passion for the safety of his beloved cousin.

(months later)

The Avatar Paladin pulls his sword and arcs it toward the miscreant in front of him. On an impulse, he pulls the blow, the sword veers to the right, leaving him completely open to attack. The thief ran for his life. The Paladin sighs. " hwwwwwh" He then turned toward the Palace and continues his short journey. As he reaches the gates, a fellow warrior steps beside him.

"A Dark Elf is here. He is spouting some nonsense about his race being good." The paladin growls deep in his throat. " the dark ones are sly ."

Lord Inquisitor RedHand leaves his companion at the door to the inner keep, and proceeds into the corridor.

Kirel Rabushka, the avatar paladin, pauses at the door to the council chambers. He holds onto the wood whilst he purges the memory of his slain father from his mind. Suddenly remembering where he was, he looks down to make sure that his surcoat was donned properly. He cannot help but remember his father as he examines the field of black with a bloody red hand imprinted on it. Knowing that he must do battle, a battle of a kind which he passionately dislikes, he enters the room.

"Hail Lord Inquisitor, Protector of the CrimsonRose" cries Mythrandir, the Royal Herald.

Kirel quickly crosses the room and while placing left hand upon his hilt, he goes down on his right knee, placing the knuckles of his right hand upon the marbled floor. "Carai an Ellisande" he cries to his King. " All Honor to King Gryfonsbane, Ruler of the CrimsonRose, I will serve thee to mine last breath".

The King, deep in conversation turns to his friend. " Rise Protector, your presence is welcome. He pauses for a minute while the Paladin rises. " Sir RedHand, have you met this " he pauses for a second, and the dark elf interrupts.

"Person... sire" The dark elf adds with a twisted grin.

The Paladin stares at the Dark Elf for a full minute before recognition dawns in his eyes. Rage fills his soul.

In a whisper, too low but for the king and the drow to hear he spits " You are Zasstryn H'yonith." . The Lord Inquisitor knows that he has blood right within these walls, and his hand inches toward his sword again.

"Stay" commands the King.

The drow smirks.

Kirel stiffens at his liege's command.

The King proceeds " Yes, this is Ambassador H'yonith. You obviously have knowledge of him."

"Yes sire" The paladin quietly rages. " This is the one whom I have spoken about, the one who is sent to weaken the kingdoms of Norrath." he pauses as if in memory " This is the one who killed our bloodbrother from the north, and he whom did cause Paladin Reynold to die."

The King, knowing Paladin RedHand, is troubled by the words.

The paladin turns to the Dark-Elf. He stares into the elves dark oval eyes. " I know who you are, and I shall. Honor my Kings peace with you, as long as you stay as his guest. However, know this, I shall be your very bane."

The king, stands within the Chamber of Light. All the retainers, Lords and Ladies bow before him. He grabs his friend, the Lord Inquisitor by the shoulders. "Peace RedHand, .....Peace".

The Paladin turns from his king and walks to the other side of the Chamber. He turns in time to see the sallow faced dark-elf pick himself up and huff out of the Chambers..

He turns to Baron Cirith, " Sir, call upon the Kings Hand, and have them follow that one."

The King calls from the other side of the room. "REDHAND".

Paladin RedHand flows to his Kings side. "My Liege"

" For thy insolence, you will do 3 days penance with Arch-Bishop Gregor RedStorm."

"Aye Sire, I shall." As he starts toward the chamber doors, he stops. His black cloak whirls behind him as he spins toward his King. He pulls Carrion from his sheath and holds him high. "Carai en Ellisande." he cried.." My King, This day I do swear to thee. I shall ever hunt down friends of the dark ones. Their atonement shall be steeped in their own blood. All Honor to the CrimsonRose.... Carai en Ellisande." The paladin turns and walks out the door, into the night, eyes searching for danger...

Years passed...

Those same passions drove him to do things which were not entirely ethical, but in his fervor and zeal, he didn't notice. However the Queen, and King did. One day, the Inquisitor sent several of his operatives into the field on a dangerous mission. The result, the death of a beloved member of the court.

" Sir! " a page called from down the hall. " Lord RedHand, this just came for you. The page handed the paladin a vellum wrapped container.

" What type of bird? " the RedHand asked.

" A raven. " answered the page without thinking. He appended with " Sir "

The Lord Inquisitor, clasped the container in his hand.. " A note.. but from whom..." he wondered out loud. " Go now, " he commanded, " Fetch the Arch-Bishop, Lord Crascis. Tell him, that.... an old friend, has come to visit."

The page scrambled off, down the hallway. RedHand turned and walked toward his chambers. Inside, he cracked the vellum, and slipped the cap off the bone container. Hesitation filled his bones. He decided to wait, and with a grunt, heaved himself off the chair, over to the cabinet. He slipped out two glasses, and filled them both with an amber liquid. He slowly tasted his, letting the warmth slide down his throat while waiting for the Frater to arrive.

The page slipped his head in. "Sir, his Holiness is here."

"Show him in!"

"Aye sir!" the page bobbed his head, and withdrew.

In walked light incarnate. A tall man, dark hair, face aglow and with a ready and warm smile. " Good morning to you."

"And to you as well.. brother." RedHand replied, he motioned toward a seat. " Please, take your ease."

Crascis Lightborn sat. With a look of pure bliss, he reached for the glass that RedHand, was holding forth. After taking a long drink, he asked. " Why was the page sent? "

The Lord Inquisitor crossed the room, reached down to a small desk, and picked up the bone container. " This.." he started. After crossing the room again, he placed the container in Crasciss' waiting hands. " This came in today.. by raven."

The glass slid from the Arch-bishops fingers, and shattered on the marble floor. He closed his eyes. Prayers for the dead issued forth from his lips. Then, lips near white, he opened them. " Who? " he asked, voice ragged.

The paladin, gently laid his hand on the frater's shoulder. " I don't yet know. But I needed you here, for I fear grave news indeed is within this container. " I ask that you first bless this, so that any traps are canceled. "

His Holiness, Crascis Lightborn, knelt. His white robes now stained amber as he knelt within the puddle caused by the dropped glass. He first attained proper state, by chanting for a minute, then he called out to his god. A faint blue light enveloped him, just for a moment, healing the cuts in his knee.. The gods are kind to their servants... Then the vellum wrapped bone container, flashed a deep red, then focused into a deep residing blue. The glow disappeared and Crascis rose. " Here. "

He handed the container to the Lord Inquisitor.

Standing, the Paladin RedHand, stuck his finger into the container, and withdrew a slip of paper. He slowly opened it, all the while looking at Crasciss, and seeing the Frater look back. RedHand, read what the note contained, and a moan escaped his lips. " Nooooo " he looked crushed.

The Frater ripped the note from RedHands clenched fist, and read the note. " Dear God,... O Sy`lana.. Sy`lana.." The arch-bishop wept.

(two days pass)...

The entire castle is bedecked in mourning grey. The mood in the courtyard is somber and bleak. In the center of the courtyard, a newly created statue rises. A du-plex statue, one side showing a warrior woman, in battle armor, and a snarl upon her lips, the other, depicting a woman of beauty and grace. A woman with a ready smile. Both depiction's were of..... a friend.

A tent had been erected to cover the still form of the Arch-bishop. He lay as he had for 3 days now, clothes rent, ashes in his hair, and tears, even still cascading down his face. Beside him sat Amen-Ra, the Queen, and countless others, all dressed in grey. Some wore silk, others wore wool and cotton.

The King entered the courtyard, none but the royal chancellor and the inquisitor noticed his entry. He swept down to the statue, and ran his fingers over its stony face. He stood their for countless minutes, tears streaming. He whispered... " Good-bye Sy`lana qua Croix, fare you well on your new journey. " Voice now ragged, and grief evident, he paused, then finished." Good-bye my friend, we all will miss you."

As a result of this, the Inquisitor resigned post. He simply wanted to guard the Queen, his Queen, his cousin. Deep in council, the King and Queen met with the nobles of the land. They determined that truly, the Inquisitor was not at fault. The Queen expressed desire to make her cousin happy once more. She decided that she would give up the Barony of Sol'Nari and the command of the Thorn, to Kirel Rabuschka.. the RedHand. ......

Several years later... the Redhand grew careless once again. He went in search of the Arch-Bishop and told of his sins. The Arch-bishop blessed him, but the sin was to grevious for simple absolutions. Kirel needed to speak with Karana. As the days past, Baron Kirel Redhand desperatly sought the calm places of the land. Seeking succor, he found none. After many days of searching, he ran to the altar of Karana.

" Most High Lord of the Thunder. I have sinned against my fellow man, against my king and against you. I swear to sit here until you answer me. I need to know what I must do to be absolved of this crime. "

Kirel knelt and wept before the altar.. waiting upon an answer. For 20 days and 20 nights he stayed in one spot. His armor grew dusty and Carrion ceased to sing. The Bishops and monks of the abbey tried to move him but he seemed rooted to the very stone. King Dantrag and Queen Jenna both came to stand vigil with the Redhand. Upon the 21st day, as the sun rose in the east and the darkness of night receeded into the dark, a bolt of pure sound struck Kirel.

" MY SON, INDEED MY DISPLEASURE IS GREAT. I COMMAND YOU TO FORSAKE ALL VOWS AND TO WANDER THE BREDTH OF THE LAND. IF YOU OBEY, THEN WILL I MAKE KNOWN MY WILL."

Kirel was broken hearted. He had been commanded by his God to break oath with his King and his Queen. He had been commanded to disavow the Barony which he had worked so hard for and the Kingdom in which he had spilled hearts blood to cleanse. Kirel rose, washed and commanded the servants to polish his armor. He took Carrion with him and washed Carrion in the most precious oils. Dawning his council robes, Kirel affixed his blood red hand insigna to the back and strode into the council chambers where the King was giving audiance. Kirel pushed all the petitioners aside and spoke directly to the King and Queen.

" Sire, my Lady... I have been commanded to leave you. "

The King's mouth opened. Never in Dantrag's wildest imaginings had he thought that Kirel Redhand would say that. The Queen openly wept.

" I beg your forgiveness. I know not whence I will return. I hope to one day serve the Rose again. But for now, I must leave. "

With that, the Redhand tore off his council robes and strode out of the Hall. The guards moved to stop him but the King waved them away. Kirel strode off into the wilderness.

Several months later, a ragged haired man in shining armor steped into an inn of the name, the Vulgar Unicorn. There, dripping water from the heavy storm outside, he stripped his armor off, took Carrion and laid it atop the pile and looked up at his old friend Tanshiver with madness filled eyes. Tanshiver moved to help his friend when Kirel whipped out his dagger and crying JUSTICE!!!!!!.. plunged it deep into his heart. As he topled over, the blood begining to pool, Tanshiver called for his healers.

Suddenly the rain outside stopped and the brightest bolt of lightning ever seen in Norrath slammed to the ground ( inside the tavern ). There, glowing with power was Karana himself.

" SON, BECAUSE OF YOUR STEADFAST DEVOTION TO ME, FORSAKING ALL OTHERS AND BECOMING MY CREATURE, I AM WELL PLEASED IN YOU. RISE NOW,
BE WELL AND FULL OF MY GRACE AND LOVE. GO AND SHOW SUCH MERCY AND GRACE TO ALL WHOM YOU ENCOUNTER . "

A blue nimbus of light surrounded the man known as Kirel. His features had changed, in a suble way. No longer did he look elven, but human wholy. Kirel looked around him and cried out the only thing that came to his mind. " CARAI EN ELLISANDE, VAR ANDEAN CEUBYAIR, SO`JHIN ANDEAN VAR KISEIRI CRIMSONROSE " ( translated that means...Glory to the Rose of the Sun. Guardian of the Heart of the Rose, Servant of the Rose and Guardian of the Brotherhood Crimson Rose. )

Kirel, sound of heart, body, soul and mind made his way back to the lands of the Rose and contacted the Chancellor. He knew that he would no longer be able to lead men, for he now knew his true calling. He was to be a servant of the King... a servant of the Kingdom ,,,, a servant of all... a Servant of the Rose.
