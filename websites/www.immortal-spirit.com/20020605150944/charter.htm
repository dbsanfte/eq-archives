General Outlook:

The basic goals for the guild are really quite simple. Although it may sound like a cliche the goal is to have a guild that strives for unity among its members and develop a friendship that would encourage members to develop strong loyalties with each other. I do not wish for the guilds mindset to be based primarily around raiding and the development of power. Guild events are hoped to be frequent, however I hope to promote a lot of guild interaction and develop a strong trust between guild members.

 

Membership:

With the exception of the Charter members (those members that joined at the initial creation of the guild) membership is based on several criteria. Charter members are an exception to the level limit rule. First a potential member must be of at least level 40. The potential member must also be sponsored by an active guild member who is no longer on probationary status. The potential member must also be approved by a guild recruiter or guild leader. After being accepted into the guild a new member will be on a probationary status for a minimum of 2 weeks with probation lasting upwards of one month. This variation in time is designed to compensate for members that may be on often and able to interact greatly with the guild and also to compensate for members that may have limited time in the game and may take longer to develop a relationship with the guild. The Officers that hold the title of Guild Recruiter have the main responsibility of handling potential members and recommendations by guild members. More information on their duties can be found under the Officer descriptions.

 

Alternate Characters:

With regards to membership of alternate characters who do not meet the level 40 requirement.  Alternate characters are allowed into the guild as long as they meet a lvl requirement of 20.  This level requirement is primarily so that we do not appear to be a low level guild and therefore falsely advertise and have low level players believe that we allow low level members into the guild.  The primary reason fo allowing lower level alternate characters is mainly so that you as Guild Members can still enjoy the company of those you trust dearly, while enjoying the aspect of a new character.  The second reason is so that you can see guild chat, and should the need for your "main" arise you can "become" available at your discretion.

 

Guild Guidelines

Introduction:

I prefer not to consider our guild members to be limited by many rules. Guild members should have a high degree of freedom when playing the game. However each member should understand that when they choose to join the guild it should have been because they agree with the guilds goals and what it strives to become. rather than strict rules I prefer to have guidelines for our guild. These guidelines should promote the basic ideals I have set for the guild and in turn promote fairness and understand among its members. However I do not wish these guidelines to resrict each members freedom to enjoy the game.

 

Play Nice Policies:

The play nice policies are rules that verant has implemented into the game in an attempt to make the the competitive gaming experience of Everquest more fair. The play nice policies can be reviewed at www.everquestlive.com if you are not familiar with them. It is my belief that each member of our guild is a direct representation of our guild as a whole. how you act while playing influences other peoples impressions of our guild. I would ask that all members become familiar with the Play Nice Policies and try to follow them. Please by all means, try not to let anger get the best of you..countering a wrongful act by another player is not the means in which we should handle our actions.

 

Roleplaying:

There are many of you out there that are very familiar with the concept of "roleplaying". Simply put, roleplaying is the idea that one "becomes" their character while playing the game. One speaks, behaves, and upholds the beliefs of that character. Personally I prefer to roleplay as much as possible when playing Everquest and I would promote others to roleplay as well. However I also understand that there will be many times when we simply will not have the time to roleplay. (Ie. During a raid or event where typing slows us down considerably) And some may just not be into the idea of roleplaying wither. Therefore guild members that do not wish to roleplay shall not be shunned or forbidden from our guild, however I do ask that they be understanding to those of us that wish to roleplay.

 

Guild Recruitment:

Guild recruiting is allowed by all members of the guild. However the final decision on whether one is allowed into the guild will be made by the guild recruiters or the guild leader. In order for a person to be eligible for guild membership they must first be sponsored by a guild member. They must also be familiar with the guild charter and agree with it. The sponsor must then post on the guild forum stating that they are sponsoring someone for guild membership. the Guild Recruiters will handle it from there.

 

Loot Guidelines:

These guidelines for looting are my personal viewpoint on general loot rules for guild events, grouping, and raids. your own personal opinions while on your own or with non-guild groups should be decided among your group. In some of the other guilds I have been in I have seen almost tear a guild apart. I have spent long hours speaking with friends and thinking on my own to devise a fair loot system. Please keep in mind that I believe there is no perfect loot system but my overall goal is to try and be as fair as possible to all guild members. Also keep in mind that loot rules may change from time to time to maintain fairness and to compensate for unusual circumstances.

I truly believe in a "need before greed/random roll" rule when it comes to the guild doing events, raiding, or simply grouping together. The "Need before greed/random roll" concept is desigend to promote overall fairness to everyone that participates in any particular guild event. (Ie. In a raid in which an item drops that is SK and pally only, all SK's and Pally's that are present will be allowed to roll on the item) This loot rule is a general rule for the guild and at times there may be exceptions, changes, or modifications based on various circumstances.

 

Alternate Characters, Twinking, and Powerleveling:

My belief is that each of us pays to enjoy the game, so if creating alternate characters and twinking them is what you wish to do then it is your right to do so. If you wish to have alternate characters in the guild that is also allowed however, your character must meet the level 40 minimum to be a member of Immortal Spirit, otherwise they may be accepted in our sister guild Spirit Wind.

Additionally if you wish to powerlevel guild members or even ask for help from guild members it will also be allowed, however I do recommend the use of tact when approcahing guildmates, and please be understanding if one cannot, or chooses not to powerlevel you. It is everyones right to aid or deny aid in powerleveling requests.

 

Guild Vaults:

There will be 3 vaults maintained within the guild. One will be for items and money. The second will be for spells, and the third will be for quest gems. The items for these vaults will be gained by donations from guild members. Items in the vaults may be auctioned to guild members at reduced prices at later times and the money gained from such will be placed into the guild vault to use for raid or event expenses. Items usable by new members that are in the vault may be "given" to those new members after the new member is no longer on probation and after it is approved by the council of officers or guildleader. Other uses for guild monies and items will be taken on an individual basis on most cases, however most decisions will be voted on by the Council of officers and guildleader.

Spells will be obtained by donation also. It is requested that guild members that obtain dropped spells that they do not need donate them to the Spell Vault for use by other guild members. Quest Gems shall be treated in the same fashion as the spells. Lists of all items in the vaults will be maintained on the Guild website for easier viewing. Donation of items to the vaults is never required.

 

Guild Forum:

Communication is essential to keeping everyone informed of the news of the guild. the forum is the hub of our communication channel as it will be most likely rare for us to ever have all of our guild members online at the same time. It is imperative that each guild member review the forum whenever they can and post if they have important information or news to share. Also posting helps us see who is still among us, as we may rarely see some of our own as a result of different playing times.

 

Officer Positions:

There will be several officers in the guild. The more members we have the will create a need for more officers. Officers will be chosen on a volunteer basis. However we will not take volunteers until there is a need to fill a specific position. Here is the list for the Officer positions within the guild.

Guild Leader:
       The duties of the Guild Leader are all encompassing. He performs the same duties as his officers, however he relies primarily on his officers to perform their required duties. His primary task is to ensure that his officers are performing their required duties and that guild members are not having any problems. Any questions, comments, or concerns may be directed to the guild leader, however guild members should attempt to contact any applicable officer if possible.

Guild Advisor:
        The Guild Advisor is tasked with maintaining guild cohesion. He is charged with handling of problems or concerns between guild members. He acts as an investigator of inner guild problems and also problems with persons outside of our guild. Our guild is to be based upon trust and friendship. Guild members should strive to get along with each other and form strong bonds. Guild Advisor will help to maintain this goal by becoming a listening ear to each guild member and their concerns. Should you have an issue that you feel need be addressed then inform a Guild Advisor. This officer will investigate the concern if necessary and notify the Guild Leader so that a resolution may be attained.

Guild Recruiter:
        The Guild Recruiter is tasked with building the ranks of Immortal Spirit. They are a social service provided for the guild and its members. Do not mistake their tasks and assume that they randomly go about and recruit members. They can do this at their leisure however it is through guild members that their duties become more prominent. Guild Recruiters duties also include making contact with and meeting potential members recommended by guild members and also those persons who have heard of our guild and shown an interest in joining our ranks.
        OOC- Guild Recruiters will rely heavily on the forum in their duties. You will notice a category titled Potential Members. Should you have someone you wish to sponsor for the guild you must post the name of this person on the forum. Guild recruiters will note this and make an effort to meet with this person and even possibly group with them. Their goal at this point is to see if the person seems compatible with our current guild goals and if the person agrees with our charter. If the Guild recruiter deems the person worthy he will invite that person into the guild and post this under the New Members category of the forum. Keep in mind that Guild Recruiters may take several days, sessions, or meetings with a potential member before finally deciding whether they will be invited or not.

Guild Strategist:
        The Guild Strategists are guild members who have proven very knowledgeable in the lands of Norrath. Their primary duty is organizing guild events and raids. They can also assist in organizing assistance with quests. Guild Strategists are automatically appointed Raid Leader or Event Leader of any task they organize unless they appoint a substitute. They will organize events of their own accord or organize ones at the request of guild members.
        OOC- Aside from events that they will organize on their own, Guild Strategists are also tasked with organizing events at the request of guild members. In order for this to happen any guild member may post under the appropriate category for either quests, raids, or events. Guild members should post what they wish to do and what the goals are expected. Guild Strategists will check the forum and pull requests of guild members for organization. Once a Strategist has assumed the responsibility of a requested event he should post this to inform the other Guild Strategists so they are aware that an officer has already begun to organize the event. Once the event has been organized the Guild Strategist will post the event time. Guild members are expected to post under this new topic to notify the Strategist of availability.


Final Note:
        I have chosen each of my officers based on my knowledge of their personality, play style, and overall attitude. I have placed a trust in them as they have earned this trust from me. Each officer is his own person and can make decisions within their field without conferring with the Guild leader or the other officers. However it is expected that they do inform the Guild Leader of any important or out of the ordinary decisions they may make. I also expect guild members to respect the officers of the guild as they have volunteered to perform extra duties within the guild in an effort to make the guild better as a whole. Of course the expectation that we all respect each other is a given, sometimes it may seem more difficult when it comes to officers as they are members who have the power to make ground breaking decisions.
