Interval Three

As the last goblin fell to the ground lifeless, Balamorm took stock of his surroundings. High Hold Pass rang cold this time of year reminding him of the distant plains of Everfrost his native home. Unfortunatly this was no Everfrost and his enemies were numerous. This thought brought him back to the present and the danger at hand. He had narrowly escaped the original trap in the pass and his heroics had given Bruin and Askander time to escape. However the tide of goblins was too great and he had been certain that death had finally caught up to him. Then a group of wandering townsfolk from highkeep had wandered by and been drawn into the conflict. Seeing, though, that the small force from the keep was not sufficient to defeat the little green menace and having already sustained many injuries, Balamorm used the insuing chaos to hide himself in a crevice in the canyon wall promptly falling into unconciousness.

Sometime later he awoke and stumbled out of his hiding place. The next few days were spent fighting, running, bandaging, and hiding. Now Balamorm was tiring. The fighting was wearing him down and to top it off somewhere along the way he had become lost. Sighing Balamorm continued down the path he had chosen yesterday leading down (hopefully onto the vast karanas plains).

Rounding a bend Balamorm came upon a small rabbit caught in the trap. The animal was dead, but only recently and Bal quickly made the decision that the furrier would not miss one rabbit. However, as he tugged the animal out of the trap he heard a large sproing! as he was uncerimoniously dumped into a small pit. The pit was not deep but the fall had broken a leg. As Balamorm tried to stand and reach the ledge a pair of heads (topped with pointed ears) peeked over the ledge. As he reached for something to throw at the pair he was broght short by a bow pointed straight at him. Resigned to his fate Balamorm watched as the Goblin slowly drew back his bow ...
