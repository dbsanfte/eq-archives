Interval Six

Aloynia clung to the side of the mountain with a strength borne of desperation. Below she could hear the bones rattling that signified the undead were out in Kithicor in abundance.

"Couldn't wait till morning could you, you stupid girl." Aloynia chastized herself. But her recent delay and the thought that her husband might be lying at the bottom of some gorge drove her onward. As she edged along the wall towards Highhold pass she couldn't shake the feeling that Balamorm was in trouble.

"Ok Kuraz we're in trouble." Balamorm said turning to the troll behind him.

The pair had hidden for a few days while Balamorm tended to his wounds. After that they had headed for highhold but an ambush had got them turned around and (despite Kuraz's claims that he knew where they were going) they had mistakenly found a hidden entrance into Runnyeye, a den filled to the brim with goblins of a very mean persuasion, rather than the keep in Highhold. Balamorm had been through the upper levels a few times but never down below. The problem now lay in the fact that he wasn't sure where they were currently at in the lair, nor how to get to the areas with which he was familiar. To top things off it looked as if a large portion of the Pickclaw clan who had troubled the inhabitants of Highhold for so long had moved in and taken over. There were rotting corpses of goblins and evil eyes everywhere in the dungeon. The two were quietly discussing which way to go when around the corner came a dwarf with an ax over one sholder and a dead goblin over the other. Hoping that perhaps he might know the way out, Balamorm quickly moved to intercept the dwarf.

"Greetings and well met, stout dwarf" was all Balamorm managed before the dwarf threw the goblin corpse at him.

Completely taken off guard by this, Balamorm and the lump of flesh and stench rolled to the ground in a confusing flailing tangle of arms and legs. As he finally managed to cast off the dead goblin, the dwarf took a flying leap and landed on Balamorm's chest forcing the air from his lungs.

Holding a dagger to the large barbarian's throat, the dwarf spoked in hushed tones "Are ya trying to me killed? Ya daft northerner! I swear yer kind are so tall that the thin air up there must rot yer brains! Good thing that Kraked can think quickly before you brought all them gobbies down on us both!"

Kraked was suddenly lifted from Balamorms' chest. "Now, now small one." whispered Kuraz "I'd greatly appreciate it if you'd leave my friends throat all in one piece. He's bled enough, lately."

As Kuraz set Kraked back on the ground Balamorm stood and dusted himself off. "Look, we were just wondering if you could show us the way out. We got turned around in here and need to find our way out so I can get back to Halas to meet up with my friends."

Kraked went picked up his ax then came back and stared up at Balamorm with a furrowed brow. "And what's in it for me? eh? A thank you very kindly, I'd be supposin."

Balamorm reached into his belt pouch. "I can't believe I still have these after all the fights I've been in and the spills I've taken lately but how about these as a payment." In Balamorm's hands glinted a small pile bloodstones. "I got these fighting elementals on an island just off of Odus. Perhaps these will convince you to help us?"

Kraked looked at the stones then furrowed his brow at Balamorm for a few seconds. Finally, with a sigh and a shake of his bearded head Kraked turned and said "Follow me, I'll take ya as far as the gorge. After that yer on yer own!"

As the three made their way down the passage yellow green eyes started intently after them from the nearby darkness ...
