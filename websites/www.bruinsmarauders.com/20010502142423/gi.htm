Welcome to Bruins Marauders!  You have come to the right area to read all about our guild, and what we stand for in the World of Norrath.

Our guild - founded by Bruin himself - is an ever growing guild, with many people who care about the guild and all of the other members as well.  We pride ourselves in being a well organized, and kind guild to all of those on Norrath who choose to return the kindness, and are someday hoping the be one of the largest and best guilds out there.  Currently we are based on the Luclin server only.

As well as the simple act of kindness and companionship we choose to follow, we also have certain guidelines that the guild abides by in order for everyone to be as informed as the next person, and for the kindness and companionship to be sincere.  We don't want our guild to look bad in any way, shape, or form to the rest of Norrath, and we want people to see "Bruins Marauders" and think "Wow, I've heard that is a good guild!"  The Charter is as follows:

Our primary goals are to provide our guild with an atmosphere where we can have fun, explore, and develop our characters. We will accept all races and all classes. We look for commitment, loyalty, patience, maturity, honor, courage, humility, and above all putting guild brethren above thyself.
Roleplaying is not required, however it is encouraged, appreciated and respected.
The guild should always come first. We give more than we take; we help out those who need it. We don't ask for much, but loyalty is a must. Respect the chain of command. The officers earned their positions and deserve respect.
If you are in need of an item or have an item to give, please post in the Vault (on our message board). Do not beg members/officers for free items or to be twinked. We do have a guild armory and will loan items when possible by what is listed as wanted in the Vault.
New recruits need to be level 12 to be invited to join the guild. The reason for this is people start characters, decide they don't like them and they delete them. That makes it very hard for us to keep track of who is really in the guild. If someone takes the time to play the character this far, you know they will continue to play him or her. The grandfather clause is in effect.
Recruitment will consist of at least two interviews. The first interview is done by the recruiting officer who will then refer the recruit to fill out the "requesting membership" form. This will be automatically submitted to the Dean of Recruits for the second interview. The Dean of Recruits needs sufficient time to evaluate potential candidates to insure their goals are in alignment with ours, this can be done by grouping or durring an interview as deemed appropriate by the officer. However, when we do find someone with the same goals as us, we do not hesitate to bring them into the guild. All new recruits are on a 30-day trial period for the benefit of both parties.
Upon acceptance, the Council of the Adorned will guide new recruits toward their acclimation into the guild. Any questions can be answered by your Council officer.
It is expected of all members to assist when they can, however just because someone has teleport, resurrect, locate corpse, etc.. does not mean they are at your beck and call to do these things. You may "request assistance" with these things and usually a guildmate will assist if they can.
A reasonable request by an officer should be carried out as soon as possible (a reasonable request does NOT mean "camp this for me". It would be more like "Please help so and so find his corpse since you are nearby."
Keep the guild channel talkative, but not filled with spam. Otherwise don't hesitate to ask for advice, get a price check, look for people to group with etc . . . using the guild channel. And remember to respond when someone says "Hail Marauders!!"
Use of /anon is not acceptable. If you wish to use the /role you may do so instead.
Represent the guild with pride and in a way as to give the guild a good name.
Acts of unacceptable behavior include:
a) Asking repeatedly for free stuff (Giving out free items is an act of kindness from another member and should not be expected.)
b) Trashing guild members,through guild channel, tells, /shouts
c) Excessive cursing over the guild channel.
d) Overly whining and griping (we all gripe occasionally)
e) Asking repeatedly to be power leveled (if a guild member agrees to power level you, that is their choice, but asking member after member is unacceptable)
REMOVAL:
First offence is a warning, second offence is probation, third offence is removal.
Abusing the guild and its people will also subject you for removal.
Characters that go missing in action may be subject to removal unless they become active/found.

That is the extent of our guild charter, and it is quite easy to abide by. Although we may seem stiff on the rules and regulations, it is all for the good, and if you are planning on joining, you will see that. Members are encouraged to report offenders to the first available officer. Officers are then to report this to the High Lord.

If you wish to ask any questions, please feel free to send an e-mail to Bruin at Bruinbearheart@hotmail.com, and your question will be addressed.

If you wish to fill out the questionnaire for possible induction into the guild, please go to the "Joining the Guild" section.

We hope to see you soon!
