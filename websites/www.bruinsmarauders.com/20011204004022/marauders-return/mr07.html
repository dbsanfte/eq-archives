Interval Five

Bertoxulous was merciless...

And so too was the plague (attibuted to him) ravishing the Karanas plains. Askander knew soon after entering them that there would be trouble. However he had no idea of the scope of it. Now lying on the ground, feverish and barely conscious, his only thoughts were of how foolish he'd been to run head first into territory claimed by the enemy without a plan. Aloynia spoke to him through a haze, but her words made no sense.

"I've found a Wizard to take us out of here." Aloynia said trying to pierce the fog in Askander's mind.

The blue robed wizard stepped up next to her, placing a hand on her shoulder. "It's no use and the longer we stay the worse it will become. We must move quickly."

"You're right Cumladarn. Let's go."

With a flash of light the trio blinked out of the nightmare of hate and poison.

Askander opened his eyes and immediately regretted it. With a groan he rolled over. Or at least attempted to his body managed not more than a twitch or two.

"Careful" said a voice "You're still weak"

Askander braved another peek from underneath heavy eyelids. "Am I alive?" he croaked.

"Barely." said the voice " The northerner got you here in the nick of time. You were barely alive and it took all the priests of Marr to bring you back from the brink. Rodcet must surely smile upon you."

Askander smiled at the mention of Aloynia. "Where is she?"

"Gone I'm afraid. After you stabalized, she said she could wait no longer to find her husband. She left in quite a hurry, I might add."

"I've got to go after her." Askander said as he tried to push himself up off the cot. However he failed miserably and colapsed in a heap of body parts and blankets.

"I think you'll be going nowhere for a while, friend. Your companion seems strong. Have faith that she will be ok. It is you I'm worried about. This plague has left a taint in you that I cannot begin to guess about the implications of."

Askander finally looked at the voice directly. The cleric was a man old and stooped.

"My name is Justineous." he said guessing at Askander's thinking.

Askander was about to ask another question when the strain of exertion caught up to him and darkness enfolded him. The last thing he heard was Justineous saying "Rest. We have alot of work to do, you and I."
