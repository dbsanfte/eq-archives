>The cave with Undertow is the gateway to the rest of the zone.  Once
>you are in that cave, just before it opens up where Undertow waits, is
>an ice shelf to the left.  That shelf is the base of a ramp that goes
>and curves upwards.  You follow that out into another cavern with
>skeletons and ice golems.
>
>In this new cavern, if you turn left, after a short while (before the
>second set of stairs down) there is a cave on the right.  That cave is
>really well hidden (I pass it everytime I wander that way).  This cave
>opens up outside.
>
>This area has some local population/wildlife to kill if you so desire
>(snow leopards are here).  To get to Permafrost, you run straight
>across the open plain, baring to the right a little.  When to get to
>the other side, turn right and follow the left wall.  There is another
>cave there.
>
>This cave is full of spiders.  Follow the cave until it opens up to the
>outside again.  Here, you want to turn left and follow the wall for a
>ways until you get to another cave.
>
>This cave is full of invis-seeing giants.  These giants also stun, so
>sprinting is not a bad idea at all.  Stay on the main artery of the
>cave (a branch opens to the right, don't take it if you are trying to
>get to Permafrost).  The cave will open up to the outside.  Turn left
>and go around the outcropping of glacier, and there is the door to
>Permafrost.
>
>While swimming in Everfrost, watch out for Dreadwake.  He is a 42^^^
>Groupx2 that has a huge swimming area.  He hit me for over 2400 a shot
>yesterday (killed me a total of four times, twice when we were fighting
>him and twice when I was headed back to Permafrost and he came at me
>from behind).  He killed me in less than five seconds on both the hits
>from behind - Evac didn't even have a chance to warm up :P
>
>Don't die or hit Evac in Permafrost unless you really mean to.  It
>drops you back out to the west of the docks in Everfrost.  Great
>incentive for staying alive :)
