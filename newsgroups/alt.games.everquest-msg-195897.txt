>> > There's a lot of misinformation about this going around.  I've
>> > heard tons of people claim someone couldn't group with them because
>> > of the level limit, when actually they could.  The actual formula
>> > is as follows.
>> >
>> > Take the level of the lowest character and divide by two, round up
>> > to the nearest whole number.  If the result is less than five, use
>> > five. Add this number to the level of the lowest character (the
>> > number you started with). The resulting number is the highest level
>> > that that character can group with and get XP.
>> >
>> > Some examples:
>> >
>> > A Level 3 Character -> 3 divided by 2 is 1.5, round up to 2.  2 is
>> > less than 5, using 5 instead.  3 plus 5 is 8.  A level 3 character
>> > can group with up to a level 8 character and still get XP.
>> >
>> > A Level 20 Character -> 20 divided by 2 is 10, no need to round. 
>> > 10 is not less than 5.  20 plus 10 is 30.  A level 20 character can
>> > group with up to a level 30 character and still get XP.
>> >
>> > A Level 29 Character -> 29 divided by 2 is 14.5, round up to 15.
