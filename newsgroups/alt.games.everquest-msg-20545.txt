> Just a few tips I've picked up in 53 odd ( ok very odd) levels of
> buffing... err playing a shaman.
>
> 1.  Use one spell slot for buffs and switch them out as needed, don't
> keep them all memed.  This is a pain before you get med of course, but
> if its too much trouble then  don't use this tip till after you have med
> <G>.
>
> 2. Cast buffs one at a time on everyone who will be getting that
> particular buff, yourself first.  This means that when it starts to drop
> you will know before anyone else.  That comes in handy for warning your
> puller his sow is about to drop and hopefully cuts down on the number of
> times players tell you they need a buff, since by the time theirs starts
> to drop you've already begun casting on yourself and others.  NOTE:
> folks will still tell you their buff is dropping, try not to scream too
> much <G>.
>
> 3.  As you go up in lvls of magic some of the buffs like your AC will
> use up a lot of mana.  Don't cast buffs on folks who don't need them.
> AC on casters does you little good, but hp on them helps, so don't cast
> Guardian but do cast Talisman.  NOTE:  higher lvl talisman spells will
> overwrite casters shielding line, so ask casters for their preference.
> Enchanters and others may wish to use their own buff as it includes
> magic resistance.
>
> 4.  This one is partly evil, so use it at your own discretion.  If you
> have a wizard or other non tanking party member who insists on drawing
> agro and then running around screaming GETITOFFMEGETITOFFME!!, stop
> casting sow on them.  They will either figure out not to do that or be
> so busy on CR that they will no longer be causing that problem.
>
> 5. Try to space out your buffs so that you aren't casting them all at
> once, especially the more mana intensive ones.  If you can get them
> balanced out you won't be telling the group to wait ten minutes for you
> to med back up after buffing.  Rather, you can just keep slipping in the
> buffs as needed between or even during battles depending on your role in
> the group.
>
> 6. Never buff till OOM if you can help it.  A high percentage of trains,
> overpulls and general WTFs occur when you don't have the mana to deal
> with them for some odd reason, hehe.  Try to avoid being caught with
> your kilt down.
