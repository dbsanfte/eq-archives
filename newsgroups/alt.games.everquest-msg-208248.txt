> Using a real third party macro program to do this sort of thing is
> apparantly possible, but no, I don't think you can do it strictly within
> the everquest game.
>
> However, you -can- set up to eat on any given key press.
>
> First, you need to put that food in an open inventory slot, slot 2 in
> your example.
>
> Now drag slot 2 over to a hotkey, lets say for the sake of arguement,
> hotkey 2.
>
> Now whenever you press the 2 button, you'll eat the food in slot 2.  If
> you replace the food with something else, pushing 2 will work as though
> clicking on that... if its a spyglass, say, you'll instantly activate
> the Telescope effect.
>
> OK, now you are only halfway there.  Since constantly clicking 2 is only
> a little easier than clicking on the inventory slot, you aren't really
> automated yet.
>
> Now go into options and find your keyboard settings.  Go to a key you
> hit a lot, say the left arrow.  Tell it to press the 2 button for you
> whenever you hit it.
>
> Now, assuming you move around with the keyboard arrows rather than using
> mouselook, you'll be tapping 2, and thus eating food, every time you
> turn left... which is a lot more often than you probably think, since
> you constantly adjust heading a little left or right as you travel in
> most zones, and spin back and forth to see whats going on even when
> standing still in most places.
>
> The benefit of doing this, by the way, for people who might be wondering
> why the OP would want to find a way to accomplish it, is that by force
> eating junk food in slot 2, you stay full, and therefore don't eat the
> food in slot one.  You can put some expensive or just annoying to get
> food in that slot that will improve your stats, and yet avoid eating it
> as long as you can remember to keep slot 2 full of food.
>
> This is moderately inefficient, since force eating food only gives you
> half the normal duration of the food; but eating twice as many fish
> rolls (cost about 4 cp each to make) is a lot better than eating your
> Misty Picnic (cost about 10pp in bazaar)
>
> Another good trick is to put just one picnic in slot one, and the rest
> down in slot 8, with lots of fish rolls in say slot 7.  Now if you
> forget to keep slot 2 full, you'll eventually eat the one picnic thats
> in slot one, but then will go back to eating fish rolls until they are
> all gone before you finally start nibbling on your backup picnics.
>
> The stat foods don't have to be in general inventory slots, they can be
> in bags, only the one stack you are force eating (in this example, in
> slot 2) has to be.
