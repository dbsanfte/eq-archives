> This is how it works:
> 
> You say, "Hail Merchant".
> Merchants says, "Ah greetings, young knight. I am in a bit of a bind.
> Oh how I wish there were someone here who could [help] me!"
> You say, "I can help you."
> 
> Notice the brackets around help? It's a keyword but you need to phrase
> it so that it makes sense to the person. Like if I were to say that
> above line to ask for help and you just respond with "Help.". That
> won't make sense to me.
> 
> Another example.
> 
> You say, "Hail Vendor"
> Vendor says, "Greetings, young one. I sell [armor], [weapons] and the
> like."
> You say, "What armor?"
> Vendor says, "I have plate, chain, and steel."
> 
> Notice there are a few brackets up there. You can ask the NPC about
> each one by phrasing it in QUESTION form.
> 
> A majority of the times, if the NPC does not respond to you when you
> include the word in brackets, that means you phrased it wrong.
