9 times out of 10, 32-bit Windows will tolerate the move from one motherboard to another
**if** a common hard disk controller is used.

So, right before you make your move, boot your system once with a PCI IDE controller card
installed, but no drives connected.  Install the drivers for the IDE card as required.
Then restart your system with the boot drive connected to the IDE card, clean things up
and shut down.

Move everything over to the new motherboard, including the IDE card.  Boot the system on
the new motherboard, with the drive connected to the IDE card.  Supply all requested
drivers until Windows stops prompting you (this may take multiple reboots).

Finally, restart your system with the boot drive connected to the motherboard-down IDE
controller, and the IDE card removed.  You should be golden.

This is not something I've done once or twice.  I've done it literally hundreds of times,
across hundreds of systems.  WinNT, Win2k, WinXP, IDE, SCSI, notebooks (with IDE
adapters).  There's no stopping me! LOL

Good luck!
