Well... the role of each individual neuron in data storage is not clear, 
but conventional wisdom is that learning is a result of the neural *
connections*, rather than neurons themselves. If the connections are 
preserved, it's arguable that you can replace any number of neurons 
without changes in the stored information. After all, brain cells do die 
all the time, and yet our personalities and knowledge are largely 
preserved.
