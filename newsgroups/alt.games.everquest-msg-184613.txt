> Easy as pie.
> Go to www.eqwindows.com - download and install the software.  The first
time
> you start the program, you will need to browse and find your Everquest.exe
> file (or is it EQGame.exe -  EQWindows should direct you to the right
file).
> Start EQWindows and logon with your first account.  Press Ctrl-Alt-R to
> release your mouse from the first EQ session and  start EQWindows again
and
> login with your second account.  I'd suggest that you turn off Luclin
models
> when logging in to do the transfers, the second session will load a lot
> faster.  Once the second session has loaded up, press Alt-Tab to go back
to
> the first session and press Ctrl-Alt-R for that session to "recapture" the
> mouse.  Now you can easily alt-tab between sessions.  That's it.
>
> When I first started, I had EQ in two separate directories and an
EQWindows
> icon that pointed to each Everquest.exe file.  I found out that I didn't
> need to have two separate EQ directories, so you don't need to copy your
> current Everquest directory to a new one.  Note, a lot of things you have
> defined for the characters on the account that you normally played on the
> other computer are not going to be there, i.e. macros, hotkeys, window
> locations (I think).
