NickServe writes:
> ...in order of most to least usefulness in high level
> raiding.

I'm not sure if this a practical question or just
discussion-bait. Many classes you need a couple of for
high-level raids, but some classes stack almost without
limit in usefulness.

1. Cleric: One per group; plus one camped for wipeouts.
2. 60 Warrior: 1-5 suffice for most activities.
3. Monk: 1-2 for pulling, but damage also stacks well.
4. 60 Shaman: One for Malo; one or two more for buffs.
5. Rogue: Many, they stack well.
6. Enchanter: 1-3 suffice for most everything.
7. Wizard: Many, they stack well.
8. 55+ Bard: One for OOS; or up to one per group for mana.
9. 55+ Mage: 1 non-KoS for CoH.
10. Ranger: Damage stacks well. Move them up the list if
they have their archery AA abilities.
11. Necromancer: 1-2 for mana transfer and SC.
12. Druid: One for PoTG; good backup healing.
13. Paladin: A couple for backup healing never hurts.
14. Shadowknight: Backup pulling in rare cases.
15. Beastlord: ???; remains to be seen.
