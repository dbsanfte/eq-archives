> 1. yes, especially when soloing.  Summoned armor, and summoned gear for
> every other slot you can manage, all seems to help quite a bit.  Take a
> look at the jewelry you can summon, each piece fits a slot, and has AC
> as well as hp on it; they also have mana and focus effects but those
> don't seem to mean anything on a pet.  At any rate, at your level you
> should be summoning plate armor, one thing you might look at are the
> bracelets you can summon; there's a good chance they are more armor and
> certainly they have more hp than the plate bracers you summon.
>
> The weapons you can summon won't mean anything to your pet, except that
> if you give him two weapons first you can then give him an elemental
> defender which he'll wear on his back; if you don't give him the weapons
> first he'll wear it on one hand which I'm told eliminates his dual
> weilding.  With no weapons, though, he'll dual weild naturally.
>
> If you don't mind spending a little cash, you should be able to pick up
> some very nice summoned gear in the bazaar; weapons which add marginally
> to damage output, but give 50 more hp each, and have a 140dd proc.  In
> addition there's a girdle thats +15dex and +500 hp (or a lesser belt
> that is +10 dex and +250 hp) and a muzzle that gives 11% item haste
> which will stack with your Burnout.
>
> 2. Well, there's not much you can do.  Burnout is definately a must,
> whichever the highest level one you have is; thats a haste for your pet,
> and a pretty big one.  Burnout V is 85% haste, which is better than any
> caster can put on your pet for you, I don't recall what haste you have
> at your level, but unless there's a much higher level chanter available
> to haste your pet for you you're probably just as well off doing it
> yourself.
>
> You'll also want to use your best single target Damage Shield spell on
> him unless you are using a fire pet or Monster Summoning pet; they tend
> to have better DS with their own spell than you can cast.  Sadly, at 54
> you've outgrown the fire pet line, after 49 they all become insanely
> stupid wizards, always casting the wrong thing out of a grab bag of ten
> or so spells.  You've probably outgrown MS as well, at least till you
> get MS III, which is nice for a level or two IIRC.  MS pets are annoying
> anyway because only one in four casts a DS, and the reason you want them
> is generally because they have Earth pet hp and Fire pet DS.
>
> Then there's Expedience or Velocity (I don't think you have the latter
> yet).  These are situational; they are pet movement speed buffs,
> Expedience gives him about the same move speed as Run 3 or JBoots,
> Velocity about SOW speed.  If you are doing a lot of moving around,
> having the pet able to keep up with you helps.  If you are fighting mobs
> that run (often this isn't a good idea, though, with no snare) then
> having a pet that runs faster helps a lot.  And of course if you are
> having the mob chase you around while pet follows behind and kills for
> you, its absolutely necessary since you don't have snare to slow the mob
> down.  If you are in a relatively static situation and dealing with mobs
> that don't run when injured, though, its not worth the bother.
>
> IF you can arrange for others to buff your pet for you, then practically
> anything helps; oddly the one buff its easiest to get is the one you
> don't need, Haste.  But dex buffs help him proc his weapons more often,
> HP and stamina buffs give him a bigger hp pool to survive a fight with,
> AC buffs help slow down incoming damage, there are some attack buffs
> that help his damage output, Strenght buffs will help with this too...
> even resist buffs can help if you are facing mobs that cast or have a
> proc.  There are some INT/WIS/mana regen buffs that I've never seen
> anyone cast on a pet, since to my knowledge only the fire pet uses mana
> and he's worthless, I suppose they won't help.
>

> accompanies that.  And its great to think that finally we won't have to
> start over whenever we LD... not to mention not finding ourselves
> petless in a bad situation when we come back from LD if we survive it.

> with you to the hunting grounds.  What you'll want to look for if buying
> buffs for him are long duration ones; great buffs that last only a few
> minutes after you get to your solo spot won't do you much good.
