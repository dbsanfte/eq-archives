>Hey all,just thought I'd stick this in the NG as I started up an Enchanter
>over the last few weeks.He's level 19 now and I'm still learning my trade

>of them within groups.Most groups I've been in have 1 or 2 people that
don't
>know so................
>
>If you know about Enchanters or possibly are one it's likely you know more
>than me so stop reading, If you have never played one/relied on one at
>higher levels please read on.....
>
>Enchanters are very poor at inflicting damage compared to other
casters-what
>they can do is weaken and/or slow down the enemy making them much easier to
>kill.
>
>Another thing they can do is clear the monsters hate list-you know when you
>have cast one too many damage spells, the tanks are taunting and hitting
>like Hell to try to get the monster of you but cant?Well one spell from an
>Enchanter will make the monster forget who he is upset at and reset the
>original mood it was in before it was 1st hit.In the case of a monster that
>doesnt attack you on sight that monster will stop attacking and probably
>start wandering off-a monster that attacks on sight will start hitting the
>closest thing to it again(which might still be you) but will forget that
you
>have done twice as much damage as everyone else.-If your Enchanter tells
the
>group he's memory blurred something and you dont attack further he will
turn
>his attention elsewhere to the greatest perceived threat(if there is one).
>
>I've left it until last because this is the most important thing (IMO) that
>Enchanters do-Mesmerise.
>Mesmerise is a spell that causes a monster or monsters to go into a daze
and
>stop fighting.If your group is being attacked by 6 monsters the ideal
>situation is have everyone help kill them one at a time.This way your
>cleric/wizard etc is not being beaten to a pulp while the tanks jump from
>target to target trying to upset all the monsters enough to keep them
>interested in the guys with all the hitpoints.The big weakness of
>Mesmerisation or "Mezz" as its referred to is that any damage at all
>received by a mezzed monster will wake it up and it will start fighting
>again.In a six person group that means that everyone has to be fighting the
>same monster,if this happens this monster will be the only one hitting
>anyone and that someone is almost always the tank because all the tank's
>taunts and hits are landing on that monster.
>The way this is achieved is one of the tanks is nominated "Primary assist"
>or "Primary tank".This group member always attacks first.All the other
group
>members make a hot key or button that they can click on that contains the
>command /assist (Insert Primary tanks name here- no brackets).
>When that button is pushed the result is you are aimed at whoever the
>primary tank is fighting.When it dies you wait for the primary tank to
>choose the next target press your button and start hacking again.The
>Enchanter meanwhile keeps all of the other monsters standing around doing
>nothing instead of stabbing you in the back.
>This is what people mean when they say "assist me"-they do not mean "help
me
>out by nuking some of these guys!
>What this means is that an Enchanter's usefulness to you is severely
limited
>if 6 different party members are engaging different monsters when you have
a
>few to deal with.
>Lastly ANY damage will break mezz.This effectively means that a monster
with
>a damage over time spell(DOT) on it can not be mezzed at all-If you are
>going to use dots make sure you got the /assist bit right first!!
>Also bear in mind if you are hitting a monster that an enchanter is trying
>to mezz you will not have time to switch attack off after you see the xxxxx
>is mesmerised message.You will get that message and your next hit a split
>second later will wake it up again.If you want it mezzed stop hitting it!
>Not assisting frequently kills us Chanters-I decided to post this after the
