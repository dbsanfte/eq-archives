> You group people into two classifications, "tank" and "caster" when there
> are a lot more subtleties that that to the game.
>
> Necros and Mages are pet handlers; necros are just about any job in
> the game -except- tank, while mages are also nukers.  If your other \
> party members were necros or mages, they may have had a hard time
> redirecting their pets onto your mobs in time to do anything to help you,
> and may have been too focussed on that as a solution to use their other
> talents to help you.  Necro, however, could have feared the mobs off
> you in this situation if he'd been smart about it.  Only hope, besides
> getting pet to tank for you, that a mage has would be to nuke so that
> the mobs would come kill him instead.  This being a "him or me" situation,
> many people will choose themselves to live.
>
> Wizards are nukers with root and stun, either of which could have
> stopped a mob long enough for you to get out from under.
>
> Enchanters have stuns and fears but primarily use Mez for crowd
> control.  If you were hitting one of the mobs that was pounding on
> you, you'd have been breaking the mez, so it would look like
> nothing was being done at all.
>
> Thats casters.
> ==============================================
> Healers are a seperate stack; shaman, druid, cleric.  As a rule, more
> AC and HP than casters, not so likely to evaporate if they get aggro.
>
> Shaman, at low levels, have the least chance of saving your bacon.
> Could throw heals at you, as you note, but you were taking hits
> at a much higher rate than shaman heals can keep up with.  All this
> did was to make sure the shaman got aggro after you died.  Shaman
> saves your life by making sure you -always- have SOW on, if they
> are high enough level.
>
> Druids, on the other hand, have Snare, which will have the reverse
> effect of SOW, slowing the target down so far that your normal
> run will -seem- like SOW as you skyrocket ahead.  They also have
> Root, if you are good at backing up out of arms reach this will
> often save your bacon.  Healing shouldn't have been a druid's
> first reflex here, but at your low levels, many druids still think they
> are primary healers... which they are, clerics won't become
> substantially better at healing for a few levels yet.
>
> Clerics have root and stun and flash of light and fear, none of which
> hold a candle to mez as far as crowd control goes, but any of which
> would have been adequate to get stuff off you in that situation.
> Unfortunately, many clerics buy the common opinion that they are
> strictly heal-and-buff machines, and may not even have had any
> of these memmed, or at any rate may have concentrated on the
> losing battle of healing you in the forlorn hope that someone else
> was trying to get stuff off you.
>
> One thing to note; good healers are looking more at the party's
> health bars than at the screen or text window, in addition to
> healing you they should have alerted the group to the fact that
> you were being pounded on.
>
> Thats healers

>

>
