Lance Berg <emporer@dejazzd.com> writes:
> You can melee on a mount, but it will do you no good.
[various good info about mounts snipped]

Yes, meleeing on a mount has little or no value; not only do you not get
sitting mana regen while attacking, you don't get sitting HP regen even
when you're NOT attacking.

Other gotchas about mounts include the inability to bind wounds while
on a mount, or to forage, sneak, or hide.

I did finally get a cheap mount for my druid just so I can have the
option of letting a mob beat on my damage shield while I use the mana
regen to help keep myself healed.  (There are times when I find this
approach works better than root-dotting.)  I'm not sure whether the
mount reduces the chance of my spells getting interrupted.  It DOES
definitely make it easier to change spells while being beat upon; if
the mob hits you enough to disrupt a cast, it'll also disrupt memming
spells, but without the mount ANY hit makes you stand up and stop
memming, so you do better in those cases.
