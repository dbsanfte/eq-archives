> A "buff" is any spell that another player casts on you
> which produces one of those icons on your screen.
>
> For example, a SoW (Spirit of Wolf) spell cast on
> you is a "buff", because it produces an icon on your
> screen indicating that the spell is in effect and
> that you character can now run faster (until it
> wears off half an hour later, at which point the icon
> goes away).
>
> They're called "buffs" because the usual effect of such
> spells is to "buff you up" -- that's the slang phrase
> (perhaps it's only an Americanism) for doing exercise
> workouts and getting fit, strong, and muscular.  The
> analogy to Everquest spells that make your character
> faster, strong, etc. should be obvious.
>
> Some long-term spells can be cast on you which have
> negative effects (especially by creatures instead of
> other players), and these are often called "debuffs".
> For example, a spell that makes you weaker for a time
> instead of strong would often be called a debuff.
>
There are also a couple lines of spells that remove buffs and debuffs...they
are the Cancel Magic and Strip Enchantment lines.
