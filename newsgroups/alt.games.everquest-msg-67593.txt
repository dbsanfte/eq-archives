>   Yes, it's primarily good for level 9-18 characters.  After level 20
> or so, Ambassador DVinn is probably the only mob who (alone) will
> provide much of a challenge.
>
>   The lower level orcs (centurions, legionnaires, slavers) just drop
> cloth and rusty weapons, as well as Crushbone belts and legionnaire
> bracers for a quest in Kaladim (for decent experience).  Slavers also
> drop keys which can be given to the appropriate slaves to free them,
> for experience.
>
>   The orc trainer on the hill drops the shiny brass shield, which is a
> decent shield for lower level characters.
>
>   Most of the other good drops are found in the "castle".  Emperor
> Crush drops the dwarven ringmail tunic, excellent chest protection for
> lower level shorties who can wear chain.  It's half of the screaming
> mace quest, if I recall correctly... the other drop you need to
> complete the quest is the bracer of battle dropped by the orc warlord.
>
>   Other decent drops include a few bronze weapons you might get off
> the mobs in the palace, the dwarven axe (both in 1hs and 2hs
> varieties) which is one of the better non-magic slashers in the game.
>
>   Ambassador DVinn, who is much tougher than any other mob in the
> zone, occasionally drops a dragoon dirk, a nice little piercer, as
> well as his heart (not sure what it's for).
>
>   It's not really an attractive zone for a twink because the loot is
> rather lackluster, but the nice thing is that a solid lowbie pickup
> group can pretty much handle the entire zone, unless there's a bad
> train, or DVinn makes an appearance.
