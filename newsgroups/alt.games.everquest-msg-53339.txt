Lokari wrote:

> David Navarro <david@alcaudon.com> wrote:
> 
>>I'm interested in all kinds of linguistic trivia... Could you elaborate on
>>what you find wrong in the use of "my bad"? If I find it convincing, I may
>>expurgate that expression from my own repertoire. :)
> 
> "My bad" is slang, used as a variation of "My error" or "My mistake".
> The problem is that "bad", in this case, is being used as a noun
> (replacing the nouns 'error' or 'mistake').
> 
> Of course, 'bad' isn't a noun - except in rare cases when it's being
> used as an attributive noun; to be a valid attributive noun, it must
> precede another noun which it describes. In "My bad", it does not
> precede another noun.
> 
> 'Bad', most properly, is an adjective. As with the attributive noun
> case above, though, "My bad" is invalid because there is no noun for
> 'bad' to modify.

Oh, I understand now. My own experience of the English language has been so 
far that verbs, adjectives and nouns are all pretty much interchangeable, 
but I can see why it's incorrect. Thanks.
