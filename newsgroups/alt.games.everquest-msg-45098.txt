>
> It's a great idea to have more than one partition for the simple reason
that
> sometimes you want to reformat C: and lord KNOWS you don't want to
reinstall
> EQ!!
>
> Buy Partition Magic and create at least 2 partitions (C & D).  Make sure
> that D has at least 2 GB but preferably more.
>
> Copy your EQ folder onto your D: (EQ is self-contained...simply moving
that
> folder is 100% safe) then update your shortcut to point to the new
location.
>
> If you feel you must reformat your C: make sure that you have a bootable
> floppy disk with CD support.  If you have a new computer (in the last year
> or so) this may not be necessary as you can probably boot from CD-ROM
> (you'll probably have to make a change in system BIOS, aka CMOS so it will
> look at the CD-ROM before anything else when trying to find a boot
device).
>
> If you go the floppy route and have successfully booted to floppy, double
> check to make sure that you can access your CD-ROM drive (put a CD in,
wait
> 15 seconds, then type E: if that is your CD-ROM drive letter, then do
> DIR<enter>.  If a directory comes up, you're fine).
>
> Then you can format c: /s (/s makes C: bootable).
>
> The smartest long-term thing to do is copy your E: (CD-ROM?)\Win98 folder
> onto your newly formatted C:.
>
> C:\>MD WIN98
> cd win98
> e:
> cd\win98
> copy *.* c:
>
> This will copy all files from e:\win98 to c:\win98
>
> Next, type:
> c:\win98\setup
>
> This will install Win98 faster than if you were to do it off the CD-ROM.
An
> added bonus is that any time you change something in your computer it'll
> know it can find the files locally instead of constantly bugging you to
put
> the CD-ROM in.
