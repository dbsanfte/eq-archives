> These spells are VASTLY different.
>
> Harmony has the sole purpose of pulling- mez is primarily crowd control
> (although it can semi-reliably split pulls on blue mobs). Harmony you cast
> before a fight, whereas mez really shines during a fight when you get
adds.
> Basically get all of your melees and offensive casters focused on ONE
> monster (targeting one person (the "Main Assist", usually the puller) and
> hitting a /assist hotkey) and then you can effectively stop time for the
> additional monsters.
>
> Say you have three melees, a healer, and you. The monsters come in, three
> of them. Without mez, you effectively have 3 simultaneous 1 on 1's with a
> bit of back row support from the casters. With mez used properly, you have
> three consecutive 3-on-1 fights, which are much better for everyone.
>
> If the mobs all have, say, 300 hit points, and each of your melees does 10
> damage per round(assuming all delays are 10 or something, just evenly
> spread across the board for this example). Now assume that the monsters
> also hit for 10 with a delay of 10.
>
> Without mez and everyone taking their own mob, the mobs will deal 900
> points of damage to your party.
>
> Without mez and everyone taking the same mob, the mobs will deal 600
points
> of damage to your party.
>
> With mez and everyone taking the same mob, the mobs will deal 300 points
of
> damage to your party.
>
> As you can see, this is definitely preferable. Add to this the fact that
> sometimes you will get MUCH more dangerous mobs (IE casters- offensive
> spells and heals) with the warrior-types, and mez should ALWAYS be
> memorized. And I mean also.
>
> It is also helpful if bad things happen in the middle of nowhere far from

> having to zone and without being on the monsters hate list.
