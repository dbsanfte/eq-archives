>Steve Cholerton wrote:

>> How is it possible to see the Race/Class/Level of another PC ?
>
>There are several ways.
>
>If you want to know about the person you have targeted, type in
>/whotarget (which can be shortened to /whot.) To see everyone in the
>zone, use /who. You can do a filtered who, as well; /who <x> will show
>everyone in the zone who either is a <x>, has <x> in their name or is
>in a guild that starts with <x>. My main character, Rosewhite, is a
>halfling druid and belongs to the Povar-Tarew Artisans guild. If you
>are in the same zone as her, she would show up if you do /who Rose,
>/who halfling, /who druid or /who "povar-tarew". Note that you CAN use
>double quotes if <x> has special characters or spaces. /who heroes
>might show everyone who is in the guilds "Heroes Blood" and "Heroes Who
>Die Often", as well as the character named Heroespit. /who "heroes
>blood" will only show those in that one guild. You can use the word
>'all' to show everyone who meets your filter on the server, so /who all
>rosewhite will give you info about me, even if you are in Rivervale and
>I'm in West Freeport.
>
>There is one caveat to using /who and /whot. If the character is
>anonymous, either by using the /anon or /roleplay commands, you will
>get a name (and if they are /roleplay a guild tag, if any) but you will
>NOT get a race, class or level. These are often used by people who wnat
>to avoid constant begging for sows, ports, clarities and rez's. Not
>always, though, so don't assume that all anon players can offer the
>services you are looking for :-)
>
