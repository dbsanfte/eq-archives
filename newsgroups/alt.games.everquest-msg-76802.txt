> Swarm kiting is the bardic equivilant of quad-kiting, in fact some bards 
> have even started referring to it as quad kiting.  You have to find mobs 
> of the same type that spawn close together, assist each other, and don't 
> cast.  Well, they can be casters, but it's easier if they're not, but I 
> discovered this trick on Specs in Oasis, so it's possible with casters.  
> Anyway, you run in with selos up and a drum equiped, target one mob, start 
> the charm song, and run away very fast.  As soon as charm hits, turn 
> around, hit f8 to pick the nearest non-charmed mob, and then hit you /pet 
> attack hotkey.  If you're far enough away, all the mobs should attack your 
> pet.  Watch your pet's health closely, and when he's down below one 
> bubble, don't re-charm him, either charm another one, or just chant-kite 
> your former pet to death.  Rinse and repeat.  You will probably only have 
> to recharm your first pet once, your second pet a couple times, and the 
> remaining ones almost as if you're just doing regular charm-fighting.  
> This is a very risky trick, but it's the fastest way I've ever seen to 
> gain experience.  At 32 I'm not sure where you would find the right mobs 
> to try this with, maybe the gnoll spires in SK if it's off hours.  
> The classic place to do this is the wyverns in Cobalt Scar, especially the 
> bunch in the tower.  A friend got through 51 in a couple sessions doing 
> the tower, and during off-hours was able to take out about 1/2 the other 
> wyverns in CS.  (Being level 45 and having Singing Steel Boots helps a ton
