>You won't lose your characters, since they're stored on VI's servers, not 
>locally. However, your configuration files are stored locally so you could 
>lose things like hotkey settings, socials, friends lists, how you've set up 
>the GUI, etc. Do yourself a favour and back up all your *.opt and *.ini 
>files to a floppy and chuck them back in your Everquest directory when you 
>reinstall. That will save you having to reconfigure everything in-game.
>
>But why not just hook up your old HD as a slave and copy your entire 
>Everquest directory onto your newly formatted HD? That would save you DLing 
>all the patches again, since EQ doesn't pepper your machine with DLLs and 
>registry entries, everything is self-contained in its own folder. Just copy 
>the folder and make a new shortcut to everquest.exe
>
>Backing up the *.opt and *.ini files is better than nothing, you'll just 
>have to go through a huge long reinstall/patch to get the game up and 
>running again.
