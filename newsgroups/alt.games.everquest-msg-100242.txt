> Just thought I'd pass on this little goober of a tactic that my ranger wife and I developed
> in Stonebrunt.  It's probably not new, but I haven't seen anyone else do it.
>
> It's basically a simple modification of fear kiting, where you're kiting in an area where you could
> potentially get a lot of adds.
>
> Here's how it goes:
>
> 1) Find a mob and pull it to a safe (off-pathing) spot.
>
> 2) Selo's Consonant Chain (or snare of you're grouped with a druid or ranger) the mob and then use
>    Appalling Screech (can augment with Instill Doubt) to fear.  You can use other Fear spells, but
>    because their spells have less predictable time lengths they'll stick, I wouldn't use them anywhere
>    near the end of the kite run.
>
> 3) Fear kite mob to a location short of where adds might start occurring.  Specifically, to a spot where the
>      remainder of the fear effect will not allow the mob to enter the area of adds.  Pull back to safe spot
>     and let fear wear off.   Mob chases you to safe spot, hopefully without any adds.
>
> 4) Rinse and repeat.
>
>
> The mobs we've fought in the pull location we found only need at most two times down the path to die.
> But I can see if the mob had lots of hp's it would take more passes (like a hill giant maybe).
