Well since you were wondering.... no its not the high 'kulchur' aspect,
its the sound of the original dialogue.  Watching La Strada and not
hearing the intonation of the Italian being spoken would be a lesser
experience.  The same applies to numerous films from a variety of
countries.  When I used to live near an art theater and went often I
soon found that I didn't even notice I was reading subtitles, the brain
adapts quickly.  While text can be translated, emotion plays much
clearer in its original language.
