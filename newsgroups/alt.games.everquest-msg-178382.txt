> First things first, find the application you have running on your
> computer that inserts RandOm cApitaL lEtTers everywhere and shut it off.
> :-)
>
> Now, as to your problem... you don't say what class you are, so let's
> assume the worst and say you can't cast invis or SoW. (Ok... SoW was out
> of the question anyway, as you're a DE). Getting a SoW will make this
> process a lot easier. Ask around for somebody to cast it on you, or if
> you can afford it, go to the bazaar and buy a ten dos SoW potion. If you
> can invis, do so. Most everything you are likely to run into on this
> trip can NOT run at SoW speed so even if you are spotted and gain aggro,
> you should usually be able to outrun the mobs to the zone line if you
> keep your wits about you.
>
> Getting from S. Ro back to Rathe Mtns. isn't that bad. Just go to the
> south end of the zone and cross over into Innothule Swamp. Once in, take
> a right and stick right up on the zone wall and keep going. You'll go
> along the North and then West walls of the zone until you come to the
> entrance to Feerott. Zone in there and follow the road. (If you don't
> already have a map, print one out at eqatlas.com.  It's got a good map
> of Feerott.) There's not much to fear in that zone but the odd bear and
> occasional ogre. If you keep your eyes peeled you can avoid all of them.
> The spiders and bats are all non-aggro so don't worry about them. The
> road will cross a bridge where you come to the most dangerous part of
> the trip while crossing that brige and traveling the section of road
> past it. Be very watchful for Bouncer Hurd. He wanders that area, and
> will take your little Dark Elf's lunch money and make you cry. (Unless
> DE's are non KOS to the ogres. They may be. God, it's been so long since
> I played there I don't even remember. Well, if you see any ogres, con
> them anyway to be sure. It's always a good idea to con EVERYTHING till
> you learn your way around.)
>
> The road will eventually lead you to the Rathe Mtns. zone line. When you
> zone in, prepare to run like hell to where your corpse is and back. If
> you have not already done so, set up a "Corpse" hotkey which just has
> the line /corpse on it. When you get to your body, target it and start
> clicking that corpse key like mad as you dash back towards the Feerott
> zone canyon. If you get a good ways up there near the zone line, you
> will be safe from wandering mobs unless somebody trains the zone out.
> That should be a nice safe spot to loot your corpse and get on your way
> back home.
