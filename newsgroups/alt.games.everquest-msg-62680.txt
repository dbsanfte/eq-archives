> He's not joking.  Here's how you build a PC.
>
> 1. Open the case.
>
> 2. Place the motherboard inside, so that the ports on the back line up
> with the holes in the back of the case.  You should see that there are
> holes in the motherboard that are over matching posts on the case.
> Put screws in them.
>
> 3. Put in the disk drive and CD drive.  This might be as simple as
> sliding it into place and putting in some screws.  If it is a 3.5"
> disk in a 5.25" bay, you'll have to screw a metal thingy to the disk
> that makes it wider, *then* slide that assembly into place and screw
> it in.  (Oops...left out a step...look at the jumpers on the back of
> the disk and CD, and make sure they are set to MASTER).
>
> 4. Plug things in.  E.g., plug the power connectors in on the
> motherboard.  Plug in the power connectors for the disk and CD.  Plug
> an IDE cable into the first IDE connector on the motherboard and plug
> the other end into the disk.  Plug another IDE cable into the second
> IDE connector on the motherboard, and plug the other end into the CD.
>
> 5. Stick the RAM and CPU into the motherboard.  Put the CPU cooler on
> the CPU and plug it into the connector on the motherboard.
>
> 6. Put in your video card.
>
> 7. Plug in your keyboard, mouse, and monitor.  Double check everything
> (in particular, make sure you remembered the CPU cooler).  Turn on
> power.
>
> 8. After you are happy that it seems to be working, you can turn it
> off, and put your network and sound cards in, and then close the case.
>
> Basically, everything in the PC falls into one of three categories:
>
> 1. Things that are screwed into the case.  The motherboard and power
> supply are in this category (although usually the case comes with the
> power supply already attached).
>
> 2. Things that are screwed into the drive bays.  Installing these
> consists of mechanically mounting them, and then plugging in a power
> connector and a data connector, and, in the case of CD/DVD drives,
> also plugging in an audio cable between the drive and the sound card.
>
> 3. Things that go into the motherboard, such as expansion cards, RAM,
> and the CPU.  Generally, you just plug these into the appropiate slot
> or socket.
>
> Take a look around at the web sites of motherboard manufacturers (such
> as www.asus.com) and disk and CD drive manufacturers.  You should be
> able to find PDF files of their manuals, which will tell how to
> install, which will show how easy it is.
>
> --Tim Smith
