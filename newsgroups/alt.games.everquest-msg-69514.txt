"Dan Day" <dcd@firstnethou.com> wrote in message
news:3c1a405e.178484416@news-server.houston.rr.com...
> I tried out the texture caching, and the results
> are very good.
>
> On a 500MhZ Celeron w/512MB RAM:
>
>   Without texture caching (average of 3 runs):
>
>     Server-select to character-select:  2 minutes, 44 seconds.
>     Character-select to in-game:        1 minute,  15 seconds.
>
>   With texture caching:
>
>     Server-select to character-select:  0 minutes, 51 seconds.
>     Character-select to in-game:        0 minute,  58 seconds.
>
> So that's a 69% reduction in time for the initial game load,
> and a 22% reduction in time for bringing a character into
> the game.  VERY nice.
>
> I didn't test zoning times, but they should be considerably
> accelerated as well, since I noticed that the game also
> caches textures on a per-zone basis (any zone you pass
> through gets cached the first time you hit it, then the
> cache is used when entering the zone thereafter).
>
> And the amount of disk space used wasn't as bad as
> the patch message would have you believe.  I had
> *all* new character models and animations loaded, and
> those plus all global textures plus a few really big
> Luclin zones (including Katta Castellum) took only
> 80MB of cache file size.  (Cache files are saved in
> your EQ directory as xxxx.TXC).  On the other hand,
> I have my texture quality set to "Low", so your
> mileage may vary.
>
> Overall, a great improvement, bravo.  I no longer
> have to go make a sandwich to help pass the time when
> loading the game.  It's quite snappy, perhaps even
> faster than the pre-Luclin game.
>

I appear to be zoning faster now, with the texture caching, than I was
pre-Luclin.
