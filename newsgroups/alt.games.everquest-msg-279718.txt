"Prelgor" <prelgor@aol.com> writes:
> FYI, there are no attacks in the game that "disarm" a player, so you
> don't need to worry about keeping up this skill on, say, a ranger in case
> he drops his weapon.

Well, unless you count attacks that kill you!  Those effectively
"disarm" you.  If you do have decent hand-to-hand skill, it can
sometimes make it easier running back to your corpse. :-)  Mostly
you'll want to avoid combat then, of course, but stuff happens.

> As an aside, you can also use 2-handed Blunt weapons (e.g. staff, big
> mallet).  Personally, I have not used these much.  Once you gain the
> ability to dual wield at level 17, your damage output is usually
> greater using two 1-handed weapons than a 2-handed weapon.  At higher
> levels, there are some reasons to use slow, heavy-hitting 2-handed
> weapons, but none of those affect you right now.

It's still a good idea to get a cheap but decent two-hander and use it
occasionally so your 2HB skill doesn't fall too far behind.  Otherwise,
when you reach the levels where such weapons are important, your skill
will be terrible and you'll have a hard time improving it.  (To raise
a weapon skill, you have to be able to hit a non-green mob with it.)

One example where a two-hander is better is when you're fighting a mob
who has a damage shield, so every time you hit you take damage.  You
want the option to hit less often but for bigger numbers.
