> Knowing what buffs to apply, to whom, and when.
>
> Knowing what heals you have, and who to use them on.
>
> Knowing when to cast Divine Aura.

> By level 50, good clerics have a good feel for healing.  They watch their
> group member's health intently - not just to see how much health they
have,
> but how fast it's falling.  Timing when to cast a Complete Heal on the
Main
> Tank is an essential skill, and not something that can be learned by any
> other method than raw experience.
>
> You won't learn this by duo-ing with someone.  The dynamics are so
> different.  In a two-person group, you don't interrupt a Complete Heal on
> the warrior to cast a Remedy on the chanter, for example.
>
> My goodness, I could go on and on and on.
>
> If you're *really* into trying the short way, try to get a group of 50+
> folks that don't mind dying as you learn.  A decent place to learn the
> basics is Velk's, where tactics don't matter so much.  After you feel
> comfortable casting CH's and whatnot, take your Dead Group Walking over to
> Chardok or the Dog Tower in KC and learn how to deal with multi-mob pulls
> where mobs are beating on the chanter while the tank dips below twenty
> percent health.
>
> Whatever you do, don't join a pick-up group.  You'll get people killed and
> utterly destroy your reputation.  Trust me, there's nothing so damning as
> getting labeled as an "ebay" character.
>
> There is no shortcut for experience - and I'm not talking about "Ding 50!"
