> Hooked on Phonics has really gotten a bad rap over the past while
> (most probably due to those jackass-stupid commercials).  In fact, if
> you were to check, you'd find that pretty much everyone in this
> newsgroup (who's over 40, anyway) learned how to read phonetically.
> 
> The two main methods of reading are phonetic, where you sound out the
> individual letters and pattern-match variations in sound until you
> come up with a match, and wholistic, where you attempt to learn the
> pronunciation of the word as a whole by it's context in the sentence.
> 
> Now, as context does not give pronunciation guides, the wholistic
> method falls on it's face immediately.  But, when I was heading into
> junior high and high school, that's what was catching on in the
> elementary schools. 
> 
> Instead of going through rote "A is apple, Abe, awful, B is Baby, C is
> Carrot, civil...", the educators (they weren't teaching, they were
> educating) felt that it was less of an imposition on the little
> darlings if they could proceed at their own pace and learn words in
> context instead of being force-fed a bunch of sounds and having to
> learn which letter could represent which sound(s).
> 
> If I give you the word "antediluvianism" you may not know what it
> means but, if you've learned phonetics, you'll have a very good chance
> of getting the correct pronunciation.
