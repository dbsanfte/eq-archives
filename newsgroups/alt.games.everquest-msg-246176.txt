> I don't know how much you already know, so this may be a bit
> simplistic.  If it is, someone else may find it useful.
>
> Have a look at your abilities and combat skills buttons.
>
> Abilities are numbered 1 to 6
> Combat Skills are numbered 7 to 10
> Spells are numbered 1 to 8
>
> There skills/spells will obviously be arranged in your own particular
> preference order, so...
>
> ------------------------------------------------------------
>  a few examples for a Monk to practice skills (assume Ability 1 =
> Feign Death & Ability 2 = Intimidate) on a mob might be...
>
> LINE 1 = /doability 1
> LINE 2 = /stand
> LINE 3 = /doability 2
>
> Optionally, to add the command to attack to this, make the next line
> read
>
> LINE 4 = \attack
>
> [note:  I would not recommend using Intimidate in a group situation.
> They hate chasing mobs around unless they are slowed, heh]  Pressing
> this every 10 seconds or so
>
> ---------------------------------------------------------------
> A caster might have a heal button to say this when casting a Heal
> (spell slot 1) spell on someone in the group...
>
> LINE 1 = /g Casting Heal on %t - stay in range
> LINE 2 = /cast 1
>
> [The %t will say the name of the person targeted, and the /g will say
> it in group speech mode.  Obviously the command /say would use the
> "say" mode]
>
> There are those who swear by line 3 being the same as line 2, in case
> the first cast attempt fizzles, by the way.
>
> Enchanters also have a button that (assuming Mezz is spell 1) reads...
>
> LINE 1 = /g I am mezzing %t - please do not wake it!
> LINE 2 = /cast 1
>
> No-one ever seems to take any notice of it though, heh.
>
> ----------------------------------------------------------------
> To assist someone in combat by targeting the mob currently under
> attack (or to find out if that ambling Giant in Rathe has been Snared
> - grrrr!) target them, and press a hotbutton that has this...
>
> LINE 1 = /assist
>
> You will now be automatically targeting the mob that your initial
> target was fighting.  Handy in groups when there are two or 3 mobs in
> melee, or just when you can't get LOS on a single mob because it is
> blocked by tanks and/or pets :D
>
> -----------------------------------------------------------------
> A Rogue hotbutton (skill 1 = Pickpocket, skill 2 = Disarm) to start -
> or *restart* combat after an evade/sneak - might be...
>
> LINE 1 = /attack off
> LINE 2 = /doability 1
> LINE 3 = /doability 2
> LINE 4 = /attack
>
> Where skill 3 = Hide and skill 4 = Sneak, use this example to both
> Evade in Combat and then to set yourself up to Backstab/Attack
> again...
>
> LINE 1 = /attack off
> LINE 2 = /doability 3
> LINE 3 = /doability 4
>
> Pressing this in combat every 10 seconds or so will help maximise the
> skills and help the group with more damage in a melee.
>
> --------------------------------------------------------
> One thing not to do is put loads of spam on the lines such as having a
> button that says"Casting heal on Fred.  This will heal for 300 and
> will probably cause me to get aggro.  Please help in case I am
> attacked!"  I've even seen people have a button to say that a mob is
> incoming - which is fair enough, obviously - but then have a two or
> three line "witty" phrase attached to it.  God that gets old fast, and
> causes other more important messages to scroll out of view.
>
> Just target the mob and use a nice, simple button like...
>
> LINE 1 = /g %t incoming, stay alert.
>
> and this is quite sufficient  ;)
>
> Lastly, a useful command is the /pause command. An example of it's use
> in the old days when people practised constantly to get Sense Heading
> (assuming it is skill 1) up was to have all 5 lines read...
>
> /doability 1, pause 50
>
> This would perform a Sense Heading attempt and wait 5 seconds; thus
> you would get 5 skill attempts for 1 keypress. Not needed now, of
> course, and used only to illustrate the /pause command.
>
>
