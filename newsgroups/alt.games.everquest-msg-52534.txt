> In fact, for a high level group in both games, DAoC has a *bigger* XP
> loss on death on the EQ, because there is nothing like the EQ 49+ res.

> Compare the penalties:
> 
> EQ: XP loss of about 12% of what you need for level (let's assume you
> aren't right after a hell level).  Get 93% of that back with a 49+
> res, or 96% back with a 56+ res.
> 
> DAoC: XP loss of about 5%.  Can't get this back.
> 
> EQ: return to bind point.  Can get ressed back to corpse for next
> three hours.
> 
> DAoC: if you get res within N minutes (where N is your level), you
> don't go to bind point.  Otherwise, you go to bind point *AND* lose
> another 5% XP.  You can recover that XP back by running back to your
> grave and praying.  Also, if you don't get ressed, your constitution
> goes down by 3.  To get that back, you've got to pay an NPC healer.
> Also, if you die on the way to pray at your grave, your new grave
> replaces the old one, so the XP associated with the old one is lost.
> 
> EQ: cleric can bind near the place you are fighting.
> 
> DAoC: everyone has the same bind restrictions (usually cities and
> towns, like EQ melee).
> 
> On the above three points, it looks to me like DAoC has the bigger
> penalty, assuming a high level group that has classes with res.
> 
> Let's continue comparing.
> 
> EQ: you can lose a level due to the death XP loss.
> 
> DAoC: death XP loss is capped at how much XP you currently have in the
> level, so you can't lose your level.
> 
> EQ: your equipment is on the corpse.
> 
> DAoC: no equipment on your gravestone.
> 
> On these two points, EQ has the bigger death penalty.
