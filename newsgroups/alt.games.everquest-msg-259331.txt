> I live somewhat in your area, and have lived all around here,
> and I've had both cable and DSL, so here's my take.  I'm no expert and
> I'm not trying to say what is or isn't definitively better, but just
> what my personal experiences have been.
>
> DSL wins out for me.  It performs much better for games (for
> me), and like you, that's what's important for me.  If all I did was
> surf the web, lurk the usenet, and send emails, I'd just get one of
> those free dial up things and be happy w/that.
>
> That being said, lots of people where I work claim their cable
> modem has no probs whatsoever for them.  But for all I know they went
> from dial up to cable and never experienced DSL.
>
> The problems I had w/cable:  It would work fine and great for
> web browsing, strategy game MP, that sort of thing.  But the second ya
> try and play any FPS online, where the tiniest bit of lag can kill ya,
> it was awful.  Why?  Because its all over the map.  Inconsistent.  If
> it ran consistently bad, that'd even be preferable because you'd get
> used to it and adapt.  But instead it'd run great for 5 minutes, then
> suddenly ya'd get packet loss or a slow down and things would chug,
> and of course, right at the worst possible time for it to happen in
> your game too.
>
> I ran ping plotter for 24 hours w/cable at 2 different places
> I lived (once at each place, I do it once every time I move) and had
> some no-returns, lots of spikes into the red, massive amounts into the
> yello (in the report it gives ya that is).  Have done the same w/my
> DSL connection here and at my last place, and all green.  But don't
> let me mislead ya, the difference is *very* visible, ya don't have to
> run a program like ping plotter to see it, especially when playing an
> FPS.  Maddening.  Plus, w/cable, ya really have that 'peak hours'
> thing going on where things are at their worst at peak hours and not
> bad at all at say 3 a.m.  So the time of day ya play online games will
> probably have a giant impact on how much ya like it or not.  I work
> graveyard, so it wasn't too bad for me considering what my normal
> hours are.
>
> Newsgroups:  W/cable, the provided news service sucked eggs,
> so I had to pay for an outside one.  Its been a couple years though
> since I"ve had cable, so perhaps this has improved.  But w/dsl, its
> fast, they carry the groups I want, I can easily ask for more (I asked
> for alt.fan.grrm and they added it, for instance), and there's no
> monthly megabyte limit or anything.  Not the case w/cable.
>
> W/cable, at least up to about a year ago,  they monitor your usage and
> send you letters if you go over, it really annoys some people, luckily
> I never had to deal w/it because I don't download much.  But they got
> letters sayin' they had exceeded their monthly limit or what is
> considered 'average' or some such and they need to reduce it or they'd
> be cancelled, and when they tried to ask by how much they needed to
> reduce their usage, the cable company refused to give them any
> figures, just said reduce it enough or we'll cancel ya, but we're not
> gonna tell ya how much is enough.  (they've talked about this on NPR
> and that's my only source of knowledge that this happened, however,
> since I never got such a letter when I was w/cable so didn't have to
> deal w/it).
>

I'm connected 24 hours and download lots of music and videos and games.
Never once got told that I went over my usage though.

> And by DSL, I mean verizon, I haven't used qwest DSL yet, so
> can't give an opinion as to them.  They are available in my area, but
> verizon always had better deals every time I moved.  By cable I mean
> what is now comcast out here, at&t before that, tci before that.  (it
> had some cool features when tci ran it that were never brought back).
>
> My advice would be, if you're not having a problem w/your DSL,
> stick w/it, even if it costs more.  Because w/cable, comes problems.
> But if you're having problems w/your DSL, then it might be worth
> giving it a try.  And if any of your neighbors have cable, drag your
> computer over to their place, hook it up, and play some of your games
> for a while to see how it is (since you're very used to how it should
> perform on your own computer), at the same time of day ya usually
> play.
>
