> HOW HASTE WORKS:
>
> The simple explanation: Haste increases the number of times you swing
> in a given period of time.
>
> Any given weapon is defined by three things: The type of weapon it is
> (1-hand/2-hand, crush/slash/pierce), its damage modifier, and its
> delay. Damage modifier is used to determine how many points of damage
> you do if you hit (never been able to find the formula for that, but
> I'm sure it's a doozy). Delay tells you how long, in tenths of a
> second, it takes you to swing once.
>
> Now, lets say you have a weapon with a delay of 20. That means you're
> swinging once every 2 seconds, or 30 times a minute. Now add to that a
> haste value of 20%. That will increase your number of swings from 30
> to 30*1.2 = 36. So, instead of 30 swings per second, you get 36 swings
> per minute. 60/36 = 1.67: you swing once every 1.67 seconds.
> Therefore, your new delay = 60/36*10 = 16.7 (remember, delay is in
> tenths of a second).
>
> Reducing this down, the formula for new delay is:
>
> New Delay = Old Delay / (1 + haste) where haste is expressed as a
> decimal (20% haste = 0.20).
>
> There are three types of haste a Monk can experience:
>
> (1) Item haste - items such as the Flowing Black Silk Sash have
> permanent haste.
>
> (2) Spell/Song Haste - Bard songs, chanter/shaman buffs, and
> right-click effects from items (like Robe of Whistling Fists and
> Celestial Fists) all fall into this category
>
> (3) The Monk Discipline 100 Fist, which you get at level 57.
>
> You can have exactly 1 of each of these kinds of haste affecting you
> at any given time. If you have 2 haste items, the better one overrides
> the poorer one. If you have 2 haste buffs/songs/effects, the modifiers
> stack and you'll see both icons (assuming one doesn't override the
> other), but only the better haste will take hold.
>
> Normally, you'll find posted that exactly one item and one spell/song
> haste will stack and that's it. However, 100 Fist is listed as an
> exception to this rule.
>
> Tbere are some arguments as to haste caps, and minimum delay. To the
> best of my understanding, you can't get your delay lower than 10 until
> after lvl 50, and that the max haste you can stack is 85% at lvl 50
> and 100% at lvl 60.
>
> Take a special note here: new delay is NOT delay*(1-haste). Many
> people do this and it's incorrect. Were this the case, it would be
> conceivable to reduce your delay to zero.
