> Ok, I use this "arrow key to ability" technique ALL THE TIME :-)  It is
great and
> really helps with skill ups.
>
> Here's what you do.  Dedicate one of your hot keys (I make mine #10) to be
the hot
> key that gets hit whenever you hit your left or right arrow button.  Then
you either have
> something safe in the hot key slot or nothing at all.
>
> Ok, let's assume you picked hot key #10 like I did.  That's the lower,
right-hand one.
>
> 1) Hit F10 to get into the non-windowed mode.
> 2) Click Options.
> 3) Click Keyboard.
> 4) Click the down arrow icon till you find User-defined 10
> 5) In the first of the two boxes beneath it, left-click your mouse.  The
cursor should now
>     be in the first box.
> 6) Hit your Left Arrow button.
> 7) In the second of the two boxes beneath User-defined 10, RIGHT-click
your mouse.
>     The cursor should now be in the second box.
> 8) Hit your Right Arrow button.
> 9) Hit F10 till you get back to fullscreen mode.
> 10) Click Abilities
> 11) Click drag and drop Sense Heading (or other skill) into Hot Key 10.
>
> Now try turning left or right... you should see Hot Key 10 gray out as if
you had pressed it
> directly.  You can also place socials (macros) in this hot key slot).  On
my monk, I use
> it for training feign death, so it does a feign death, then stands up
again.
>
> Some might call this an exploit.  I'm not convinced it is because the
things you are practicing
> are things you would be doing anyway as you are moving around... sensing
direction, practicing
> sneaking, feigning death  (lol).  It's a bit of a gray area, admittedly.
>
>
