> At least two ways to swap C and D.
> 
> 1.  If both are jumpered to be cable select, then swap the cable 
> connections.
> 
> 2.  If they are jumpered to be one master, one slave (assuming both on
> the same IDE cable), then swap the jumpers to reverse which is which.
> 
> I usually prefer method 1, as that also takes care of the case where
> they are on different IDE cables.
> 
> So, the steps to take:
> 
> 1.  Copy everything you are keeping from the D drive to your current C
> drive.
> 
> 2.  Format D drive.  (If you use MS partitioning program, then merely 
> repartitioning is sufficient at least up to win 2000, not sure about
> win XP).

If you are going from FAT32 to NTFS, I think you can skip the format step 
entirely.

> 
> 3.  Swap drives (using whichever method you choose).
> 
> 4.  Insert Win XP CD, and install on your newly formatted drive
> (should be drive C now).

(Includes partitioning and choosing file system type as I recall).

> 
> 5.  Reinstall any programs that you need to reinstall.
>
