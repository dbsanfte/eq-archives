> Tekuila writes:
> > how do you travel to other areas
>
> The game world is divided into "zones". At the edge of each
> zone is an invisible zoneline. When you step into the
> zoneline, the game will pause for a bit (20 seconds to a
> minute or two depending on your hard drive, cpu, and
> connection speeds), then the game will return control to
> you. It may look like you're in the exact same place you
> were, but you're actually in the next zone, and may continue
> moving forward again.
>
> Here is a good set of maps for seeing where the borders of
> zones are, should you wish to travel:
> http://www.eqatlas.com/atlas.html
>
> For faster travel, there is a running enhancement spell that
> Shamen get a level 9 called "Spirit of the Wolf" (usually
> abbreviated SoW) that you can cast on your self or others
> (and others with it can cast on you). Higher level Wizards
> and Druids can travel even farther (including their guests),
> using porting spells.
>
> > how do you get your millions of copper pieces into 1
> > platinum piece :)
>
> Every town has a "banker." You may have noticed in Halas
> that when you right-click your mouse when you are near some
> of the town people that a "merchant window" appears on the
> left side of your screen. The banker will turn into a
> banking window on the left, showing your stored items and
> stored currency. While you are interacting with a banker,
> any copper that you drop in a silver box will automatically
> be converted to silver, and so on.
>
> Your banker is identified as number 7 on this map of Halas:
> http://www.eqatlas.com/halasmap.html
>
> (If you haven't found Halas [your home town] here is map
> that shows how to get to it from Everfrost [look for the
> number 1 in the upper-left corner]:
> http://www.eqatlas.com/everfrostmap.html
