> Here are a few usefully commands.
>
> /pet guard here  : Pet will stand at this spot, attack any mobs that
attack you or
> him, return to this spot (or very close to it) after killing a mob or told
to back
> off.
>
> /pet back off  : Pet will disengage aggressive activity and return to the
mode of his
> previous command. If your pulling, spam /pet back off till you get the mob
were you
> want. Use this to fix your pulling problem.
>
> /pet kill XXX  : Say your in an area filled with mobs of about the same
name, for
> example A Rat. Hot key /pet kill A Rat and he will kill the closest A Rat.
Also you
> can /pet kill and have a mob targeted, the pet will attack that target the
same as
> /pet attack. Very usefully in green faction runs. Sometimes in
Blackburrow, if nobody
> is down killing the gnolls, I can work my pet into an auto zone wide
killing spree
> and rake in faction.
>
> /pet attack  : Pet will attack the mob you have targeted. If the target is
not a
> valid target, the pet will tell you.
>
> /pet guard me  : Use this if you have parked your pet to have him return
to follow
> mode. Newly cast pets are in this mode by default.
>
> /pet sit down  : Use this to increase the aggro of your pet if you want a
wandering
> mob to possible go after the pet before you. Great if your near spawn
points of SK
> type mobs, as the pet will almost always get the HT. Also the pet seems to
heal a bit
> faster while sitting. Don't use sit mode as a guard point mode, pet does
not return
> to sit spot after combat.
>
> /pet get lost  : Use this to make your pet poof. Semi usefully when your
pet goes
> into "Doing something stupid, Master" mode.
>
> /pet report health  : Use this to have your pet tell you at what percent
health he is
> at, with out having to switch targets to check him.
>
> /pet as you were  : Pet will back off and reset all previous commands and
go into
> follow mode.
>
> /pet no taunt  /pet taunt  : Switches between taunting attack mode off/on.
Pets that
> can taunt are default in ON mode when cast.
>
> As with all pet commends, the pet has to be within hearing range of you to
respond.
> Also in hilly area's, being slightly above or below your pet can make the
commands go
> unheard.
