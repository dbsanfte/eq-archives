> OK you have several options here.
> 
> If the goal is to replace the existing C: drive (system drive with
> Windows installed on it, then you will have to decide if you are doing
> a complete reinstall of the Operating Ssystem, or would rather image
> the old drive onto the new one.
> 
> I would recommend a completely new installation of the OS. 
> 
> Here is what I would do....
> 
> Remove the original drive. Install the new drive as a stand-alone
> master, and plop in your WinXP disk. Use the format and partition
> utility that is part of the WinXP installation process to setup 2
> partitions:
> 
> 1. C: drive for the OS (I like an 8-19gb partition for the OS)
> 2. D: drive for Apps, Games and files.
> 
> The reason I recommend a seperate OS partition is because it gives you
> the option of reinstalling the OS at anytime without affecting your
> data files that you have loaded on the second partition.
> 
> Once WinXP is completely loaded, patched, drivers installed etc.
> Install the old drive as a slave to the new one. You can then copy
> over anythying you need from the old drive. Once you are 100% sure
> that you have everything you need off of the old drive you can format
> it, remove, it, or do whatever you want with it.
> 
> The beauty of this, is that if you screw something up, you can always
> put the old drive back in and be right back where you started with no
> data loss.
> 

That is how I generally handle it when I decide to install/reinstall 
Windoze.  I have two drives in my system, I copy everything I want to 
keep to one, format the other, and install Windoze from scratch.
