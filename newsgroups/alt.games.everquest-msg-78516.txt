> Step 1: delete DE, reroll as an Iksar.
>
> It's not you.  Nektulos Forest just sucks.
>
> Step 2: enjoy the target-rich environment provided by the FOUR newbie
> zones around Cabilis.  Level up to at least 12.
>
> Step 3: stock up on plat, food and water, and maybe a nice bauble or two
> to trade with locals; get a port to Tox or slip to the DL portal and take
the
> Luclin shortcut.
>
> Step 4: slip into Paineel, bind in front of the Warrens.  You're KOS to
> start with but only Threatening, not Scowls; you have no penalty here due
to
> religion (Heretics worship Cazic) or class (Necromancers are favored).
Enter
> the Warrens and kill a few hundred dogs.  Some will be green but most will
be
> worth EP.  You will probably have to destroy a lot of loot, but the dogs
do
> drop food and water to supplement your stockpile.  Save molars if you can;
> once you're Apprehensive you can bribe a resident to let you in and out of
the
> city to turn them in for more faction.  (You can enter safely at Dubious
but
> you won't be able to shop or get quests.  You will be able to bank if you
> like.)  At Amiable faction you can get your own key and then the world is
your
> oyster.  It takes approximately 225 kills to go from Threatening to
Dubious
> (non-KOS), and approximately another 400 to get to Apprehensive.  At that
> point I turned in a bunch of molars and jumped to Amiable.  (Incidentally
by
> this point you should be non-KOS to Erudin guards, but the rest of the
city
> still hates your guts.)
>
> Step 5: by now you should be at least level 14.  You can continue to hunt
> in the Warrens if you like, or you can go to nearby Kerra Isle.  Kerra
Isle is
> a welfare zone (high Zone Expereience Modifier) and yet almost always
> deserted.  You can solo at least three spawn points near the entrance for
> excellent experience.  Kerrans are on their own faction so you won't have
any
> problems with the Vah Shir on Luclin later.
>
> Step 6: when the kitties green out, move to Stonebrunt.  Wide open zone,
> usually with less than a dozen people in it, ZEM is poor as is common for
> outdoor zones but there are so many targets you'll easily make it up on
> volume.  You can hunt animals, Kobolds or more kitties; I learned
> feign-pulling by splitting the Kejeken Fishermen on the dock.
>
> I was out there until 24 and could have easily stayed until the mid-30s at
> least, but an RL friend with a Ranger about my level had joined me by
then,
> and she was getting bored and wanted to move.  We're in CT now.
