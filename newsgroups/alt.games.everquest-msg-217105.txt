> Well, it depends on what you mean by what you want to "cure"...
>
> If the trap has triggered - it will be a poison, disease or curse. You
> can probably figure it out from the icon. The nastiest trap seems to be
> poison-based, so I always mem Antidote (as a cleric) before somebody opens
> a trapped container, if I have any warning. The others I can figure out
> later (and heal people up while hitting everybody with Antidote or
> whatever) since only the Poison trap is bad enough to wipe my party if
> I have no warning.
>
> Most poison DoTs leave a "poison bottle" icon, diseases leave an orange
> hand, curses leave a sort of white-on-black star/cross icon. Your party
> probably has somebody to cure disease or poison, curse cures tend to be
> rarer since pre-LDoN the cure spells were available only from two camps
> both with poor drop rates. (Or, for RGC, a pretty tough MOB in a tough
> zone.) So you prbably want some sort of curse cure spell, though you can
> probably manage with a lower-level cure. The drunk/lousy-stat curse is
> the only one I've seen so far, though it *is* pretty nasty. A single cast
> of the level 39 Remove Curse cured it.
>
> If you want to know *how* to open them up, start with the trap detection
> spell, then your trap destruction spell to destroy any trap, then your
> lock destruction spell to destroy the lock. The detection spell will also
> tell you whether your other 2 spells will work. :-)
>
> However, personally I've seen very few worthwhile items in trapped
> containers - a few augs and gems, but generally it doesn't even cost what
> you spent to open it, and "don't touch" is probably a good rule. And if
> the "drunk+curse" trap triggers, you won't be doing much useful for the
> next ten minutes or so, an amount of time which can easily cost the
> adventure.
