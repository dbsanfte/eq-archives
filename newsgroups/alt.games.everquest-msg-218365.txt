>> I've used this trick for ages, but don't think I've ever posted
>> it or seen it mentioned.
>>
>> Autofollow can be very helpful in many circumstances, particularly if
>> you are slightly laggy or moving thru hilly or twisty terrain.
>>
>> THe problem with it is that you follow at a fair distance, and
>> can easily lose your target due to that same lag, and due to terrain.
>>
>> But even worse, one principle reason for using autofollow is to
>> stay with a bard who is providing you with selos speed, levitation,
>> invisibility, or all of the above. 
>>
>> Problem is, his song range is just barely big enough to include
>> you on autofollow; lag or get stuck on terrain even a little bit
>> and you can find yourself moving too slow to keep up (and he's
>> crossed the other half the zone and zoned out before you can type
>> out a "lost me"), falling to your death, or visible and alone in
>> the midst of some really angry bad things.
>>
>> First off, don't follow a bard, ever.  Have the bard follow YOU.
>> This way at least if Selos drops you are ok; you slow down and
>> the bard catches up and you get selos again, no sweat.
>>
>> But thats not good enough when its levitation or invisibility you
>> need; those need to be on you all the time or you might as well
>> not have them at all.
>>
>> Here's my solution: autofollow, then manually hold down the "up
>> arrrow" key.  This will make you run forward until you literally
>> overrun your target's position, then you'll flip back and forth
>> trying to run past him both directions... if you don't let go of
>> the button while he's figuring out which direction to move.  But
>> assuming he keeps moving, what this does is make you run at your
>> full speed no matter how fast he's running, so you catch up every
>> time he slows up a little; with normal autofollow you will move
>> at his speed and only catch up at all when he stops; and you
>> won't catch up completely, only to autofollow distance.
>>
>> The other trick, when follow distance is critical, is for the
>> person leading to put Walk on.  This reduces the danger of lag, and
>> since you are moving much faster than him you'll very easily be able
>> to stay in song range. 
>>
>> No biggie, just thought I'd share
>>
