>Your character moving will interrupt a spell even more often than a hit.
>
>I know you said that you stopped, but it still happens due to the way that
>the game deals with movement.  To lesesen the effects of lag, the server
>assumes that you are moving in the same direction at the same speed, until
>it receives a message from your client telling it that you are not.  If you
>stop and cast a spell immediately, the server will receive the "start
>casting" message before it receives the "stop moving" message.   Therefore
>it considers you to have moved while casting, and interrupts your spell.
>
>If you are running, you can take a short pause after stopping but before
>casting.   There is also a bit of a trick you can do.   Once you stop
>running, immediately turn a bit.    A turn is an event that immediately
>sends a direction update from the client to the server.   In this case that
>you have stopped forward movement and are turning to the right.  So as long
>as you turn before casting, the server should be aware that you have stopped
>moving and allow you to cast without being interrupted.
>
>Of course, if you're not running before casting, none of this is necessary.
>
