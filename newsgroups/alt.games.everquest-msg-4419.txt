When things go from bad to worse and you find your group scattering
before some terror in the  night it can be very useful to know the
landmarks.  Being able to recognize where you are with a quick look
around as opposed to doing a /loc and then find the spot on a map so you
know which way to run, then try to figure out which way that is in game
can make the difference between an escape and a CR.  Maps are great and
I appreciate those who have made them and still do, but I tend to use
them after I know a zone rather than before, so I can fall back on
instincts and vector navigation when things go bad.
