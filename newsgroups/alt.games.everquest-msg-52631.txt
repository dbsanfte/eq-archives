> Caveat:  I'm not Mark, I'm not trying to defend his position, in fact
> I have no idea what it is.
>
> To answer your question on a purely theoretical basis, then:
>
> Whoever first casts a spell on or first attacks a mob owns it unless
> he gives it up.
>
> Kill stealing is arranging things so that you get the exp and/or loot
> from the mob instead of the person who owns it.
>
> To kill steal, then, you must always wait until someone else has
> done -something- to the mob.  It is by definition impossible to
> KS someone who has not yet done anything, in fact if they manage
> to get the exp off the mob after you start on it first, -they- are
> KSing you.
>
> Once you've got them owning the mob, several methods of
> KSing will work.
>
> One; go toe to toe on the mob, you (and your group if you
> have one) do damage to it.  If you manage to do 51% or more
> of the total damage, you get the kill.  Of course in the case
> you describe above, this isn't possible.
>
> One A; Finish with a bang.  Note that that "total damage" includes
> all damage done to the mob before it died.  If, say, I see you
> attacking a bixie, which has 10 hp, and you do 9, then I slam
> in a 10 point nuke, I get the kill, as I have done 10 points and
> you only did 9.   So, this could have been done by a high level
> wizard, as long as his nuke does more than 3/4ths Hadden's
> total hp.

I have to disagree a mob has a specfic number of hit points and if you have
ever seen a melee class being Power Leveled the main trick is let the Melee
do over 50% then Nuke it Melee gets exp.

>
> Two; wipe the mob's memory.  Mem blur, Atone, and Charm,
> at least, will all wipe out some or all of the damage done from
> the mob's memory.  Now you slam out the remaining damage
> quick, and hopefully take the kill.  This would certainly have
> worked.
>
> Two A; Charm is particularly handy for this, as it also makes
> the mob invulnerable to the other players spells.  They
> hopefully use up some of their mana doing nothing, while
> you know exactly when and where the charm will be
> released, so you can time your big closer nuke to go off
> just as the mob becomes vulnerable again.  This too would
> have done the job in the situation described.
>
> Three: Stop the original group's attack.  Usually done
> by training them with overwhelming opposition.  If
> they die or are forced to zone, that wipes the mob's
> memory.  Even if not, if you are in a position to
> concentrate on the target mob while they are forced
> to split their efforts to cover the other mobs as well,
> you might win the toe to toe battle.  This doesn't seem
> to have been done from what I see, but it was only
> an excerpt.
