> You are free to use your Everquest installation CD(s) to
> install Everquest on as many computers as you want, anywhere.
>
> You are also free to use your Everquest account login on
> any computer which has Everquest installed on it, anywhere.
>
> So there's nothing wrong with setting up Everquest on your
> father's computer and playing your characters from his house
> while you're there.
>
> You only need a new CD key # to create a *new* Everquest
> account.  One account per purchase.  But there's no limit
> on the number of different computers you can install and
> run your one account from.  (Of course, you can only
> run it from one computer at any given time.)  Nor will simply
> installing the software on another computer (e.g. your father's)
> require a CD key at all -- just install, run, log in,
> and start playing.
>
> For convenience, though, be sure to copy your *.ini files
> from your current computer onto a floppy to take with you,
> then copy them into the Everquest directory on your
> father's computer after you install EQ there.  Otherwise,
> you'll be missing all of your hotkey settings, your
> /friends list settings, etc.
>
