> One thing to note about snare: none of your snares besides clinging and
> engulfing darkness can be overwritten by a ranger, druid, or wizard.

The other thing about snare is it's a great way to generate aggro.
My SK uses it regularly to take aggro off our Bard puller.  But I
often don't refresh it when it runs out, especially if the mob
isn't yet close to running away.  I'll let the Bard or someone else
cast a better snare when the time comes.  On the other hand, my SK
doesn't normally group with someone with a 15-minute snare; those
are handy.

Occasionally, with the combination of SOE on me and snare on the
mob, my Druid accidentally gets out of range, the mob wanders off,
and I find it easier to tag another mob rather than try to find the
first one.  Then, many mobs later when I've forgotten all about it,
I'll be startled by the message about Snare wearing off (complete
with audio trigger, of course!).
