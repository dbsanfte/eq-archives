> Leaving aside the angst of the Warriors, the defensiveness of the
> Paladins, and the general hatred and discontent that's surrounding
> this issue for a moment, here is mt view of the current Warrior
> class (im)balance issues...
>
> Under L45 there really isn't much of an issue.  Casters don't have
> KEI and have to manage their mana, Warriors tank well in low level
> groups.
>
> In the mid-levels, 45 to 54, KEI changes things, as does the
> increased willingness of mobs to peel off the tank and try for a
> quick snack from the back of the group.  With increased manaflow
> the casters are generating still more agro and need to rest less
> often.  It gets harder for the Warrior to maintain control of the
> mob he's tanking and so knights start to look more attractive.
> The increasing disparity in DPS between the tanks and other melees
> makes groups less willing to go with two tanks.  When you add the
> LDoN adventure timer on top of that it starts getting harder to
> find groups.  When I'm using the LFG tool to look for or build a
> group I usually see a lot more Warriors LFG than any other class
> starting in the low 50s.  At this point in his career a Warrior
> is already bumping against the belief that he will reduce the
> efficiency of the group, and for a substantial fraction of the
> groups out there this is probably true.  The mid-level Warrior
> lacks:
> - a meaningful tanking advantage (knights are good enough)
> - reliable quick agro control (vs. knights excellent agro control)
> - any compensating melee (DPS) or utility advantage
>
> At upper mid-level, 55 to 60, the Warrior finally gets the much
> celebrated /disc defensive.  Unfortunately, its high value to a
> raid MT is counterbalanced by its reduced value to non-MT raiders
> and minimal value to XP groups.  For many Warriors it's really no
> more than a gold star sticker for the calendar.  The Warrior gets
> access to some very nice defensive AA's, but so does everybody
> else.  Readily available gear further erodes the Warrior's tanking
> advantage (Bergh would say sponging advantage :-p ), the various
> DPS classes continue to pull ahead in damage output, and every
> class with a manabar grows in power faster than the Warrior.  The
> mobs' tendency to go for caster snacks continues to grow (possibly
> due solely to what the increasingly powerful casters are doing to
> them) while the Warrior gains a slow reuse never-fail Taunt (via
> class AA?  expensive and not until some time after L59) and the
> ability (IIRC) to (maybe) Taunt mobs higher level than the Warrior
> (at L60?).  Meanwhile, knights' agro control continues to be
> excellent and their near equal tanking (excluding defensive) keeps
> pace.  I don't know about SKs, but Paladins gain important utility
> abilities as well (90% rez, mana efficient group heal, stuns that
> stick to high level mobs) and improvements to several other spell
> lines.  The upper-mid level Warrior gains little of value for XP
> group tanking that is not also gained in equal or near equal measure
> by the knights, and no compensating utility value.  He gains some
> useful raid tools, but they too are limited in value, especially
> since almmost all raiding guilds today are chock full of L65's with
> big piles of AA's, L60's don't have a whole lot to offer them.  At
> this level a skilled group has no trouble working with a Warrior
> tank, unfortunately many groups are not particularly skilled and
> the prejudice against Warrior tanks is more visible.  Multiple tank
> groups are fairly rare at this level, in my experience, but I have
> had good success in groups that included a Warrior and my Paladin.
> It's hard to buck fashion though, and Warriors seem to have become
> unfashionable.
>
> At high level, 61 to 65, I haven't played, so all I have to go by
> is hearsay.  As in, I hear a number of high level casters say that
> they always prefer knight tanks to warrior tanks in XP groups.  Some
> go so far as to say that they wait for a knight to be available
> rather than taking a Warrior.  All I can say is that is a tough row
> to hoe.  LFG was pretty unpleasant for me back in the days that
> Paladins were unwanted, I can't imagine it's any more fun for the
> Warriors stuck in that situation today.
>
> Warriors lack anything to counterbalance knight advantages in most
> contexts.  Their tanking advantage has been severely eroded by
> itemization.  Their agro control is viewed as seriously substandard
