> First, a solo warrior's down time is not spent sitting on her
> buttocks waiting for HP to regen.  Its spent bandaging up to 50%
> hp.  This is so much faster that regeneration isn't even slightly
> competitive with it.  So the key is to find mobs you can kill
> using less than 50% of your HP bar.  Take 40% damage, you are
> down to 10%, then you bind wounds back up to 50% and repeat.
> 
> However, if you want a mostly solo class, I'd advise staying away
> from the warrior altogether.  Yes, there's something satisfying
> about being a big hulking brute that can just shrug off the
> massive amounts of damage the mob is doing to you while you do
> nothing but hit it repeatedly with a big chunk of iron till it
> keels over or you do.
> 
> But you can get almost the same "I'm bigger and stronger than you
> are" playground bully feel from the other two "plate" classes,
> and each have a lot more to offer.  Paladins have real heals (not
> large ones, mind you) that mean you can get your HP back to full
> for each fight, instead of always relying on 50% of your HP
> bar... and can heal themselves a bit mid fight too, in case the
> fight you thought you could pull off with 10% remaining is down
> to 10% and the mob still has 20% left to go.  Not to mention
> stuns to allow you to take on casters, lull to allow you to pull
> singles (which a warrior desperately needs to do) and root in
> case you get more than one mob anyway (all a warrior can do at
> that point is run for the nearest zone line... or find somewhere
> to die where getting her body back will be easy), and buffs to
> raise your HP and AC up to comperable with the "self buffed"
> warrior.  Shadowknights get FD so they never have to stay in a
> fight they don't want, and can also single pull, plus they get
> snare, which prevents messes from running near dead mobs, and
> Fear, which allows them to kill mobs without ever having to take
> damage, plus a little pet that adds to their damage output
> (particularly at very low levels, when a simple Fist of Bone will
> give them the same damage output as a level 12 necromancer's
> pet.)
> 
> Thats not even delving into the thrills of monk, ranger,
> beastlord soloing... only melee class that solos worse than a
> warrior is the Rogue.
