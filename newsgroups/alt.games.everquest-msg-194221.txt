I'm not certain, but I believe the 4th amendment is generally interpreted to
protect your privacy.

"The right of the people to be secure in their persons, houses, papers, and
effects, against unreasonable searches and seizures, shall not be violated,
and no Warrants shall issue, but upon probable cause, supported by Oath or
affirmation, and particularly describing the place to be searched, and the
persons or things to be seized."

I believe allowing anyone to check your hard drive would be counted as an
unreasonable search.  It does not merely protect against police searches but
against all unreasonable searches.
