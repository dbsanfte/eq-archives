> At higher levels the hit is worse, so you do need to worry about it.
> And though your weight limit also goes up, it's not by much.  My monk
> looks hard at the weight of any piece of gear I'm thinking of wearing.
> Over time, I've managed to adjust my gear to put my at only 14 pounds
> prior to picking up any loot.
>
> Weight-reducing bags are your friend, but they're not a lot of use to
> a monk unless the WR is pretty high, and those bags get expensive.
> (E.g., a Rallic Pack, holds 10 giant items with 85% weight reduction,
> typically goes for 700-850pp in the bazaar on my server, and it's Lore
> so you can't have more than one.  Leatherfoot Haversacks, 10 giant with
> 100% WR, go for around 5000-6000.)
>
> What my monk did was to get into tradeskills, pushing brewing to the
> grandmaster level, which is actually fairly cheap to do.  Then I scraped
> together most of my plat for the components for the Grandmaster Trophy,
> which besides having a lot of nice stats also lets me summon no-rent
> bags that are 100% WR.  (You can also convert one of those into a
> permanent 100% WR bag.)  So now when I'm looting I only have to worry
> about the weight of the coins.  If I'm in a group, I can usually get
> someone to cash me out, giving me plat in return for an equal value of
> smaller coins.
>
> For soloing, I carry a stack of Bat Wings (water flasks would also do,
> but tend to get drunk up).  With sufficient charisma (104 if indiff)
> those sell for 2gp per stack, and then you can buy them back for the
> same 2gp.  (Make sure the NPC merchant has an empty slot before you
> sell, though.)  When you sell you get paid in gold, but when you buy
> you spend copper and silver first, so this is a way to convert copper
> and silver into gold at most NPC merchants.  (Some people just destroy
> the copper and silver, but hey, it wasn't easy scraping up the money
> for the GM Brewing Trophy and whatnot, and at low levels even a few
> dozen gold can be a lot of money.)
>
> If you can afford them, the Leatherfoot Haversacks are better than
> the summoned bags, because (a) you don't risk losing them -- with all
> their contents -- if you get disconnected for half an hour, and (b)
> the haversacks themselves weigh about half a pound less than the
> summoned bags.  Once I could afford them, I bought two.
