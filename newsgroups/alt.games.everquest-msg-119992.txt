Yes, the common value quoted for haste percentage is the increase in
number of attacks over a fixed time, not the reduction in delay (which
would be a smaller number).  Now the interesting question to me is, how
does the game compute stacked haste (i.e. "always on" item haste
combined with spell haste).  If the game is computing haste the same
way we quote it and adding the two values, then an FBSS (21%) plus
quickness (30%) gives 51% haste.  OTOH, if the two modifiers are
multiplied then 1.21 * 1.30 = 1.57 for 57% haste.
