Your argument relies on the supposition that a GM has shown up in
regards to the *property*, which is not correct. The GM has shown up
in regard to the quality of play for the affected person. When one
person damages another's quality of play, a GM gets involved. When a

items on the ground, a GM does not get involved.

The rules derive from quality of play, not ownership of property.
