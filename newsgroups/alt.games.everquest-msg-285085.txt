"Adam Russell" <adamrussell@sbcglobal.net.invalid> writes:
> Check me if I'm understanding this right.
> I have:
> Anklesmasher 10 damage 20 delay (inherited)
> Treantwood staff 20 damage 36 delay (quested)
> So I figure when I am at a level where I get 2 damage bonus I have:
> (10+2)/2=  6.0dps for the mace
> (20+2)/3.6= 6.1dps for the staff
> and when I level up to get 3 damage bonus then
> (10+3)/2= 6.5dps for mace
> (20+3)/3.6= 6.4dps for the staff
> So thats the point where the mace becomes better than the staff? 

Close.  The damage done by a weapon is not always the same.
I think I've heard it's a curve with two peaks, maybe one at
the damage rating and one at twice that?  And high STR tends
to shift the curve toward the high end.  So if your STR is
good, it's generally better to use twice the damage number,
then add your bonus.

There may even be a more accurate formula, but that's the
one I've seen most often: ( (damage x 2) + bonus ) / delay.
