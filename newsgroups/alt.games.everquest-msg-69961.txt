> Are you someone who tends to level fast or slow?  If you are a slow
> leveler, take a look at Dark Age of Camelot.  Soloing works better
> there for most classes.  In particular, if you like warriors, DAoC
> warriors solo well, and are invaluable in groups.
>
> If you are a fast leveler, play EQ.  EQ is way ahead on high end
> content at the moment, so a fast leveler will run out of stuff on DAoC
> if you play it is a PvE game.  The soloing scene in EQ will give you a
> tough choice: the classes that tend to be good at soloing throughout
> their careers tend to be the less useful classes for groups, meaning
> that it can sometimes be hard to find a group.  The classes that are
> more desirable in groups but that can still solo have some spots in
> their careers where soloing is hard.  If you don't mind going through
> patches of 5 or 10 levels where you suck at soloing and pretty much
> *have* to group to advance reasonably, you'll be fine.
>
> >How important is character creation, and how should I handle that ?
> >(yes, i am a total newb)
>
> Once you pick a class, go to eq.castersrealm.com and read the creation
> guide for that class.  Their suggestions for allocating points at
> creation are reasonable.
>
> As far as race goes, everything is viable.  Ogres are the best
> warriors, for example, but something as ridiculous as a Wood Elf can
> make a fine warrior (a Wood Elf was the main tank when LoS killed the
> Avatar of War a few weeks ago, for example).  If you have a particular
> affinity for some race (role playing reasons, perversion, whatever),
> go ahead and do it.
>
> >In AC, the first few moments you spent on the char creation part would
> >make-or-break your charater.  Some times you could overcome those
> >choices later on in the game, but usually not.  I don't want to make
> >the same mistake if I take up EQ.  Help me out please.

> EQ is pretty much *all* about equipment once you get past the low
> levels.  Equipment will overcome any goofs made at creation.  Don't do
> anything really stupid (e.g., Half-elf druid with all points in
> dexterity and intelligence) and you'll be fine (again...just follow
> the guide at eq.castersrealm.com, and you will be fine).
>
> >Is there portal magic or do you have to run everywhere ?
>
> Druids and wizards can port themselves starting at 19 for druids and
> 20 for wizards, and they can port others starting at level 29.  Unless
