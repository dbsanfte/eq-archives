They do not need to be excellent in it, but you can only advance to their
level in the language. If they speak at a skill level of 5, then that's all
you'll get out of it. I believe a lot of mages are now doing a "ladder
technique". Basically one mage blows all his training points in Old Erudin,
then jabbers at the other mage till he reaches the same level, then THAT
mage uses all his training points in Old Erudin to increase it even more
then jabber back so now its like they've both put twice their training
points in. After that you've got a marketable language that you can "sell"
for other languages. Offer to jabber in Old Erudin for that High Elf while
he jabbers back in Elder Elvish while that Dwarf jabbers in Dwarven or
whatever. I used to think it was quite horrid to have to spend points to
train in SO many languages, but now I realize that this is actually pretty
cool because they've made languages hard to learn in a way. You won't have
everyone under the sun knowing 14 languages, only those that dedicate a
large amount of time to it (and considering the real world, rightly so).

Malark on Karana
