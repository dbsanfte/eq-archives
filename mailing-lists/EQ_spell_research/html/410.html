I reached lv 12 but sadly fell back to level 11 and there I stayed. I'm no longer playing it because the grad student that was running it finished his Comp Sci Doctorate and the system resources were realocated. People spent months at one level in that game and it never got boring. Fun is not always measured in plat and exp. My Diablo hack analogy was just pointing out the fact that some people will take the easy way out and try to bypass the game design taking away from everyone elses enjoyment. If it did take you 20 years to reach lv 50 wouldn't you be a little pissed if I started a char tomorrow and was right up there with you in a couple weeks because I found a bug or made a hack that let me kill lev 40 mobs from the start? The level 15 in one game is the level 50 in another. That is just a matter of scale. My point was that this should be an achievement not just a recipie that you follow. I can play Blackjack and win 46% of the time consistantly by following a few basic rules
(don't hit on a 12 if the dealer has 6 showing ect.) but I'd much rather play poker with a win percentage down around 20 or so and have every hand an adventure. It means more to do well in poker than it does to do well in blackjack just like it means more to be high level with a legit char then it does a hacked char. You are correct in your statement "High lvls are there to be concquered" but let us not cheapen the acomplishment by getting there to easily. By definition any mark of excelence is not easily come by.
