Actually you are quite incorrect. You use LifeDrains. I think
there's also a reason why they aren't resisted at all too, but for the life
of me I can't remember the reason. Necromancer DoT spells get resisted an
absurd amount by higher level monsters just like any other spellcasters
spells. What that group did was sic all their pets on the Sand Giants while
using Dark Pact and Allure of Death, then sat there and LifeDrained it. The
Sand Giant couldn't get through the pile of skeleton pets fast enough.

One of our guildmembers and several other 20+ level necromancers
repeated the tactic on a griffin on the Karana Server last night. One died
but they killed the griffin, so it isn't so simple. I believe a couple of
the sand giant party died as well. Its not boring at all because of the
danger involved, especially coordinating the effect. They repeated the
tactic again on an HG and half the team died due to poor coordination (and
probably the higher level of the monster and it may have gotten several
lucky hits in too or something, too many variables) and they were forced to
FEIGN or run.

Cheap? I don't think so. Are you saying six level 20 characters
couldn't take down a level 40 character? Is that why I'm level 18 and I can
still get killed by four or five gnoll GUARDS if my pet is only occupying
one of them? Then of course there's the one to two bubble loss of XP from
being killed by a monster MANY levels below you. If you want to complain
about something, talk about the XP penalties of dying to a blue or green
creature.
