I feel your pain... I spend half of my time just trying to find a group. And when I do find a group I can't really be in a big one unless I'm toward the highest level in that group. Reason being is that when you get in a group of let's say 6, and
you are around the middle. We'll use a group of 16 to 21st levels and you are 17th level. Well, for your group to get a decent amount of XP they have to fight decently hard mobs. Say they choose 22nd level mobs because of the difficulty/XP gain
is well balanced for that party. Well, your 17th wizard will get resisted fully often and the damage will be weak when it does work. Not to mention the fact that your best direct damage spell does 6-104 and will often only be doing 6. This is
actually a situation I was in a couple of days ago and was asked to leave the party due to the fact that I wasn't contributing. I understood, but thought the fact that the necro was able to damage the mobs with spells and pet was a little unfair
when he was 16th. The magician that was with us was the same level as I was, 17th, and he was having trouble casting as well; but the pet was still doing good damage. The conclusion that I've come to for the best grouping is getting you a good
fighter, a healer, and yourself; and even throw in a necro if you like. Just try to make sure you are in the upper levels of the groups range and try to fight stuff that is yellow to the highest person. Then you don't seem to have many problems
and you are being useful to your group. If any of you need a wizard I'm a lvl 18 Wizard on Fennin Ro named Xellen. I'd be more than happy to group up with you.
