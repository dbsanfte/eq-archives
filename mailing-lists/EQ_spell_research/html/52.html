Subject: learning languages/putting things in research books


Hallo,

Just some infobits to clarify some things,

1. You do not need to train in a language to learn it, you can learn it by
hearing somebody who is excellent in the language speak it. If you are ever
riding the boat start up a language class and get all aboard who will
cooperate to speak in their native tongues. After getting off my first boat
trip I could, barely, speak some Dwarf, and I am a Gnome and had never
trained in any langauge with my Guildmaster. Where we'll get to hear Dragon
or Elder Dragon is beyond me, but I do know the Gypsies that sell Combine
weapons are related to the historic Combine Empire, perhaps hanging out by
Gypsies and listening to them talk to people will give us Combine... just an
idea for somebody to try out. From what I understand you can only learn a
language from hearing from somebody who is excellent in it, but I could be
wrong about that, however I have not been able to get my two fellow
Enchanters I travel with(my main character is a Gnome Enchanter)to learn any
Dwarf from speaking in it even though I learned it from hearing it spoken,
albeit from a native speaker who is excellent in Dwarf.

2. As far as combining things in the books, do not put anything into the
books other than what the recipe called for and do not use summoned
water/items in creating the spell or it won't work. Quills and Scrolls
apparently have no use that people can figure out, probably confusing
remnants from the Beta just like the Age of Restoration book that was pulled
after the last patch due to it being useless.

Good Luck,
