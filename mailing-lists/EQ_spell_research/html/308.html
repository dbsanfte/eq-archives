I agree, there seem to be too few buff/debuff spells available for me right
now (L8). It becomes a slight pain when I'm fighting in BB, and sometimes
I'll barely make it alive out of a scuffle with a blue scrawny gnoll /
gnoll / patrolling gnoll. I've got all cloth armor, 2H worn great staff
that has about 120 attack, i'm 135AC with my buffs, 145 HP with buffs. The
problem with the 'pets' I can understand, but being able to cast a L4 pet
inbetween enemy attacks, get the pet to taunt the attacker and have three
or four hits to high tail to a zone is an advantage.

After playing my enchanter for a while, I've come to the following
conclusion about enchanters:

1) Difficult class to advance, especially solo.
2) Best in a group, sticking to the back.
3) Underappreciated.

Only after I mentioned that I could do a combination of see invis / invis
did the group appreciate me a bit more (especially after large trains of
elite gnoll guards / gnoll commanders in BB).

My stats are (If I remember correctly):

STR 60
CON 70
AGI 70
DEX 75
WIS 83
INT 122
CHA 100

So far I've kept my skills to the following for monetary purposes (and,
come to find out, this has been a really good strategy):

Jewelmaking I would highly recommend to an enchanter, especially Dark Elf
(since the Enchant: Silver spell is in Neriak and, from what I've heard, no
where else; if I'm wrong, PLEASE tell me!). Once the Dark Elf Enchanter
has the illusion spells, along with invisibility, there's no place that
character can't go (more or less).
