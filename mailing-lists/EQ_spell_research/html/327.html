I don't think its as bad as you make it out to be. First off, yeah
I think LifeDrain being totally unresisted might be a bug, but really you
have to look at its usefullness. Its one of the most mana inefficent spells
we have. Everything else gets resisted as ususal. Basically it was us
using our fearless skeleton pets to occupy it long enough for us to cast a
MASS of LifeDrains on it. It works fairly well on Griffins (level 28-32 I
believe) and about 60% of the time we could take a Hill Giant (level 38-42
or so).

Second of all, I don't see why it doesn't work both ways. At level
18 if 6 gnoll guards (level 10 or so) attacked me I'd get floored, maybe it
wouldn't happen to a warrior, but conversely if a level 20 Warrior was
occupied with 6 necro pets fighting off six acolytes he'd probably die.

I know its the not the same moving from PC to NPC, because we KNOW
how to group up and use tactics while the AI is very poor at this. There
are differences, but I think I would see as we all get higher of level, the
"resistance" area should broaden, meaning that we can cast better on higher
level monsters and lower level still can still affect us.

Is it buggy? Probably to an extent. Or maybe its everyone else's
spells are buggy because they get resisted too much? Maybe Verant couldn't
make the AI do back to the PCs what the PCs could do to the AI? Sheesh, who
knows? I think that probably LifeDrain should take a resistance hit but I
don't think any direct damage spell should truly be resisted, but should
just lose strength.

Malark on Karana

PS - And don't think killing a griffin is great experience. Splitting it up
six ways with similar level necros gave me less than an eighth of a bubble
of XP, which means all the time we spent waiting, fighting, then medding or
collecting corpses (dont' forget griffs and HGs are 24 hours spawns, so
after you kill the two wandering around you have to wait an hour and a half)
I could've gained a level fighting crocs. ;)
