Read it and read all the rebuttals. Basically I have no opinion
because I really have never played a Magician neither have I played a high
level character.

It REALLY looks like its no one class's problem. Everyone is saying
"Wizards suck after level 34" or "Mages suck after level 24" or "Losers suck
after level 1". I don't see why people aren't seeing the REAL problem, and
that is that everybody SUCKS at higher levels. Come on, we all know that
Verant had to push EQ out after a short Phase 4. They dropped a lot of
stuff thinking they'd have time to implement more high level stuff in
production because obviously nobody would be high level for a while. They
were wrong. Check out the latest update to EQ'Lizer
(http://gameznet.com/eq/) to see another suspicious trend in higher level
stuff. Verant was unprepared at some people's... shall we say devotion
(alright alright, fanaticism) to winning and now WE are reaping what they
sowed (he he or SOWed).

I won't argue with someone jabbering about how their 34th level
wizard sucks compared to a 34th necromancer (a whole nother topic, but I
seriously don't think necros solo THAT much better than others, I think the
slight advantage is to offset that we will spend a lot of solitary life due
to the fact that we're often KOS in several parts of several cities, like
the fact that I will NEVER see Felwithe except during a raid when I'm like
level 50) or how its time to quit when tou hit level 24 with your mage. I
don't know, never played them, but I do know that EVERYONE seems to be
having problems. Stuff isn't implemented yet, some things aren't complete.
higher level stuff doesn't seem balanced. This doesn't point to poor class
balancing, it points to poor level balancing. Sort of like Verant released
their product only when they had balanced everything after level 24 or
something, and hoped to have the time to balance during production.

I'm just say take alook around, go read the Magic Shop board at the
EQVault (www.eqvault.com), and listen how everyone seems to be having a
problems (I eagerly await to have my beloved necromancer class be "balanced"
in the near future, though I'm sure it would just be because nobody
complains about it). I think when everyone is feedbacking or petitioning,
perhaps they shouldn't be complaining about their prospective class more
than complaining about the general nature of the game after level XX (20?
30?).
