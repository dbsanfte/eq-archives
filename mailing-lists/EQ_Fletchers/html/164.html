Yes, there is a cap on the effects of Charisma on NPC
merchants, but it is based on your faction with said
merchants.

If they "look warmly upon you" then you'll need a
minimal charisma to get the best sell and purchase
prices (say, 85)... if they are amiable, a little more
(say, 90)... for kindly consider you it'd be about 100
or so... for Indifferent, likely about 110, and for
apprehensive it's about 120 or so... that's what I've
seen from direct observation of my girlfriend's human
bard character.

I think it's hilarious that she gets better prices
selling with 126 charisma (with CHA items on) to an
NPC that almost hates her (apprehensive), than I can
get selling to my dwarven warrior guild (with faction
indicating that they would enjoy giving me an erotic
spongebath) but only 68 charisma, with my CHA items
on.

Again, my observations. Anyone care to confirm this?
