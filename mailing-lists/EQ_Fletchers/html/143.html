I find the best tactic is to not mention your price. Just auction that you're
selling a trueshot. If someone is interested, ask. Send them a tell, NEVER
answer publicly. If they have a problem, they simply /tell you to jump off a
cliff and go about their business. The best part is, they're not ruining your
sales by spreading their ideas of proper pricing to the zone.Unfortunately, bows
don't sell well period. I've never sold one trueshot at 200pp. I sold ONE Shaped
Darkwood 1-Cam Bow for 300pp plus a cracked darkwood shield, and I had to give
away 20 class 6 steel bladed arrows to help him make up his mind. Well, if I
sell that shield for 300pp (giving me the 600pp for the bow I normally charge)
I'm still making 240pp... (200 skill, haven't failed a bow yet). Perhaps the
real market is selling Shaped Darkwood 1-Cams (second best for damage to delay.
Best is trueshot, and that's 50 less range) to level 30+ warriors :) Sold mine
to a guy who looked REALLY decked out in armor.
