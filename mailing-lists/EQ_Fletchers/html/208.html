The different factors that I have seen affect skills are INT, Class level,
Skill level and item produced.
I have an INT of 187 and I get amazing level increases in every skill I
try. My wife also plays and I have given her the benefit of my knowledge,
but she has an INT in the 70's. She takes forever to gain skill levels. We
have both started a skill together (Brewing) and we only Brew together yet I
am now in my 30's while she is still around 12. I have also noticed that
the closer an item is to trivial, the faster you gain skill levels.
I have also noticed that your class level matters a lot. If you start a
skill when your class level is in its 20's, then you will gain slower in the
lower levels of the skill. What I mean by this is that a Ranger level 20
and a Ranger level 5 that are identical in every other way will gain levels
in Fletching at different rates. The level 20 will gain slower at first
than the level 5. I assume, but have not had a chance to test this theory,
that it works the other way around for higher level skills. By that I mean,
I think that the level 20 Ranger will gain faster when his skill gets up
toward master than the level 5 would. I think it was designed for you to
skill level slowly with your class level.
I haven't had much chance to see what affect WIS has on item failure
because I have only been interested in gaining skill level and not my
failure rates. But because INT seems to work as advertised, I will assume
WIS does too!
