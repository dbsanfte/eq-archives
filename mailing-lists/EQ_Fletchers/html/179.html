Yes, there is a demand for 'darkwoods', especially among higher level
warriors (most of which don't invest in fletching at all) and some higher
level rangers who never bothered to start fletching. The catch, you most
likely have to be on an older server (ie. one with more high level players,
higher economy, etc...) or you won't be able to find anyone who can actually
afford a reasonable price.

Also, on another note, you can actually sell your higher damage arrows to
players (instead of merchants) while still taking a loss, but not as much of
one. "Why will someone buy MY expensive arrows" you may ask? Well, for
those of us who can hit for critical damage with bows, if you use a higher
class arrow (classes 1-8), for each class of arrow, you get two extra points
of dammage when you get a critical hit...
example: you get a critical hit for 26 [24pt base with a (2 x class 1)
bonus] with your oak bow (bow types also play a part in damage, but I don't
know what yet...probably your base damage) and your class 1 arrow.
If you had been using a class 8 arrow with that same oak bow, your
hit would've been for 40 [22pt base + (2pts x class 8)]

It can't be proven numericaly, but people seem to notice that their critical
hits come more often when using better arrows.

Combine the critical hits with the increased maximum normal hit just for
using the arrows and I think you will begin to see a difference in how
useful that nice archery skill actually is. If you have trouble convincing
a potential customer... challenge him/her to an archery "contest" and show
them the numbers in action... Let them use their 'old' arrows, then give
them a bundle of your expensive kind... I don't think you will need to try
to convince them after such a powerful demonstration. On a note, don't try
to actually make money on the sale of these arrows, just take less of a loss
than you would from a merchant (you may even be able to break even, but
don't get your hopes up.)

Still think those expensive arrow are just "skill fodder"?
