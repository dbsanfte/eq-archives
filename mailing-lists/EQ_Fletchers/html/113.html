Subject: The Trueshot Fallacy


Okay, this is probably going to get me a flame or twenty for this, but
I'm at the point where I really won't care.

IMHO, the programming team screwed up ROYALLY when they failed to make
the Trueshot Bow NO DROP. Think about it... most (or all) of the newly
found flaming Paladin swords are LORE and NO DROP. The one who finds
(or quests for) it is the one who keeps it, or must destroy it. Why?
Because otherwise, sooner or later, every guild-twinked lvl 10 Paladin
would be running around with one.

I've been rather bitter since they upped the prices of fletching
components by 5x, to "compensate" for allowing us to make arrows in
groups of 5, thus effectively creating a "Grandfather Clause" for all
those who were able to get to 200+ fletching before the patch... those
chars now have an effective monopoly on the creation and sale of
darkwoods for quite a while -- at least on the Rathe, from what I've
seen. What they should have done, was just allow us to place even
stacks of components into the damn fletching kit, so that WE could
choose whether or not we want to fletch for experience or in bulk...
but I digress.

**************************

My point here is that by not making this a NO DROP, the programmers
have given us the tool to destroy our own fletching economy. I've seen
people asking "what's the right price to sell it at?" Well, this is
how I would answer:

If you feel you want to make a business out of it, then what would you
sell a darkwood 1-cam compound bow for? Take that price, and make it
about 1.5x as much. Why? So that Darkwood -- let alone Oak, Ash and
Elm and Hickory don't become obsolete! If Trueshots sell for 100pp,
why bother buying a player-made Elm or Ash bow when you can just save
up another 75 or 80pp for a Trueshot?

For reference, my Lvl. 16 ranger has about 110 or so in fletching, and
I've earned almost everything that I own (except for a combine LS that
technically is on loan from a friend *g*), and I have yet to make a
Trueshot for myself out of 9 attempts. Maybe it's my pride that's
making me write this... perhaps. But I can and will not allow myself
to stand idly by while other fletchers destroy OUR OWN economy. I have
to say SOMETHING. The jewelers are rallying to unite together to raise
their prices back up a bit, to save their economy from a plague of low
prices. I see it working. We, as fletchers, have to wake up a bit --
before we sink ourselves with Trueshots.

More than $0.02, I realize... and my aplogies to all who disagree and
read it all the way through, anyway. :)
