I read a Q&A with either Brad McQuaid or John Smedley on EQVault, probably
Brad. He was asked about anti-camp zones specificaly and he said that while
they were thinking about it or testing it in beta they did not implement any
anti-camp code.

My take on all this is just crappy randomness in the game. Multiple fizzles
and resists are more common than isolated or alternating ones, the best
chance to get a rare loot, if you were betting on it, is on the very next
spawn than some random numbe of spawns.

I don't camp all that much but so far I've seen that if say the mean item
drop time is 8 hours what really happens is the item might be dropped 2-3
times in rapid succession within 4 hours (rapid?) then nothing for 20-24
hours. This is for relatively normal item not the super rare items like the
GBS.

What I have seen:

Tae Templar not spawn for 2 day, throne room was being cleared all the time.
next day my friends got 3 rubi legs in 3 consecutive spawns.

AoF spawning infrequently and dropping only 1 tunic in about 12 hours then
back to back AoF within 20 min and both dropping tunics. During the same
time the Cenobyte spawns pretty regularly for 3 days straight but never a
pair of boots on him.

BUT these and your observations are pure speculation you have to spend a
statistically significant time observing a spawn and need to know the spawn
rules for that specific mob that verant has put in to tell if either there
is anti-camp code (no there isn't) or if their random number generator is
flawed (yes it is). I have no desire to do that.
