Info about the Pot room...skip to the "map" of pots and description of guard
placements, if you already know the basics:

In case anyone doesn't know, anyone who can bind has an alternative to paying
a druid/wizard or buying Planes of Power, to get around quickly.

You can bind yourself in the teleport pot room in Timorous Deep, which is at
-12,000 +4,500. This room contains twelve pots, one for each of twelve major
cities on the planet. You click on (or run through) any one of them to be
teleported to that city.

Bind yourself here, and whenever you want to go to another city you just gate
to this room, then pick another city. Also good for getting back quickly when
you die. You appear in the pot room and click on the city by where you were
killed.

To get here the first time, you have to either swim a VERY long way, or have
lev on, the zone's mostly water. You cross near an island or two, careful for
killer "raptors" and such stuff.

You will see four spires poking out of the water where the pot room is
(people usually call them pillars). You get between the four and swim
straight down, where you'll find a square trap door in the ocean floor. You
swim through it, take a couple hundred points in falling damage (not enough
to kill even my 8th level high elf enchanter, I probably didn't have buffs
on), and now you're standing in the pot room. Bind yourself immediately.
There may be a bug that says you're not bound, but if so just camp out and
back in, and you'll see your bind point has changed to this room.

There is a super-rare dragon that spawns here who will kill even ubers, but
I've only seen it once, ever (what I saw was my corpse, not the actual
dragon). I have gated to (or died and appeared in) the pot room literally
thousands of times, probably, and only been killed once when I left myself
standing there AFK. The undead guy from my previous post will warn you in
detail NOT to leave yourself there AFK, if you hail him.

Once bound, you can click on any pot to teleport to that city. Actually, youc
an do it without binding, perhaps because you're a melee who was in Timorous
anyway and found it faster to run here and then pot to somewhere far away,
instead of taking a boat. But if you meant to bind, don't forget and click a
pot first...I did that with the fourth character I bound here, so I got to do
the whole run again.

You usually appear within sight of the guards, so be invisible or otherwise
prepared if you are KoS.

The pots are in a circle, and I will list them going clockwise from Freeport,
which is the only one that has a name on it. You may have to crouch really
close to read that name, too.

They're in a circle, like a clock. Let's call Freeport midnight:

12:00 Freeport (looks like a pot, as most of them do, but says "Freeport")
1:00 Cabilis (taller pot with wings on it)
2:00 Kaladim
3:00 Ak'Anon (looks like a lavalamp, I describe weird ones here)
4:00 Rivervale (looks like a torch on a long thin rod base)
5:00 Grobb (looks like a campfire)
6:00 Oggok (like most, a variation on the basic pot)
7:00 Erudin
8:00 Neriak
9:00 Kelethim
10:00 Felwithe
11:00 Halas (looks like a cube with a bowl on it)

Guards (for the KoS) and any other notes (I do not guarantee the accuracy,
though it mostly is correct):

Freeport -- you appear close to the guards, but you usually have enough time
to jump off the dock without them aggroing on you, then you can swim to the
underground

Cabilis -- You appear just inside East Cabilis (correct me if I'm wrong, this
is from memory), /definitely/ out of guard aggro range, next to the zone to
Lake of Ill Omen (or was it Warslik's Woods)

Kaladim -- Never used this one.

Ak'Anon -- Rarely go there, but I think you either have a moment to leap into
the zone to escape the guards, or they're just not in range. No promises,
though.

Rivervale -- If you're KoS, turn around and immediately run to the zone, you
appear just outside the tunnel to Misty Thicket. If you're quick, you
probably won't even get hit.

Grobb -- You appear out of sight of the guards

Oggok -- You appear out of sight of the guards in the tunnel to the exit

Erudin -- If I recall correctly, my Dark Elf cleric was killed upon appearing
here last time, so either you appear in sight of the guards, or in the patrol
range of one.

Neriak -- You appear out of sight of the guards, though people get chased to
the zone line here a lot, so one may be wandering by. You appear in the
commons, by the zone to the foreign quarter.

Kelethim -- You appear out of sight of the /standing/ guards, just up from
the PoD lift (or was it the newbie lift), but in the path of a patrolling
guard, so there's a small but real chance you'll get attacked instantly.

Felwithe -- I am pretty sure you're out of guard aggro range, but I typically
use Kelethim instead, so I may not remember well.

Halas -- You're standing right by the guards on the dock. I have this theory
that my troll shaman could instantly start running, with BOTH Lev and SoW on
and make it to the zone alive...but my Dark Elf cleric definitely did not,
even when my troll shaman had cast SoW and Lev on her before she stepped
through the pot. Getting her corpse back was quite an experience.




Oh, and please understand that if you DO teleport in where you're KoS and get
killed, your corpse will probably end up lying near the feet of the guards
who hate you, so getting it back can be a pain.
