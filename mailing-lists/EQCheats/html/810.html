Here is how my lawyer put it to me. If someone sends you unsolicited email
once it is fine, but if you warn them after that email saying the following
or close to it "any further emails to this address will cost a certain
amount of money to proofread" it is legally binding. If the person does not
make a payment within 3 months you can take them to small claims court and
charge interest on your time and work. When you warn them, and they send
you another email just correct it and send it back to them. Afterwards
start litigation. This is an excellent way to stop hate/spam/annoying
emails.
