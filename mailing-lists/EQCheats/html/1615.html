READ THIS:

From: Jason McCurry <jason@...>

You know, folks, I've spent my entire career as a wizard trying to prove
the KSing wizard stereotype wrong. Now I'm faced every day with the whining
wizard stereotype. I am entirely happy with my level 47 wizard and have been
since I made him. Here's my take on a few issues.

The people asking for the Staff of Temperate Flux to lower resistances by 60
instead of 6 do not have a grasp of reality. That would be unbalancing. We
are supposed to need a debuffer to be of maximum effectiveness. If you take
the time to do tests using the staff and not using the staff, you will see
the difference it makes. Using the staff won't reduce your chances of
resists to zero, but it does help. It's also useful for pulling without
doing damage so that it's easy for tanks to pull mobs off of you. (Yes,
wizards make great pullers in some circumstances.) Don't forget, you
personally have less resists just because you are a wizard.

I've never felt as powerful with my wizard as I did last night. I got a tell
from a guild member that Phinigel in Kedge had spawned. About 30 minutes
later, all the wizards and druids had delivered our 24-person multi-guild
raiding party outside Unrest and we grouped up. Within about an hour, we had
mowed through all the rares we could find, including Estrella, and I rarely
got more than one conflagration off before the mob was killed. The bulk of
the damage was being done by myself and the 5 other wizards in the raiding
party. My group was the only one with two wizards and we got the xp on over
half the kills. The things weren't living long enough for DoTs to work or
for the targets to seriously hurt anyone. When we got to Phinigel, we pulled
a few guardians and killed them, and then had a bad pull that pulled
Phinigel and 3 of his swirlspine guardians. The 3 enchanters proceeded to
mez the guardians and everyone targeted Phinigel. When Phinigel still had
about 2/3 of his hitpoints left, the shaman shouted "Malise!" and it was a
glorious sight to see all the wizards begin casting (along with a few other
wizard wannabes). I right-clicked the staff of temperate flux to mess him up
a bit more and chain-casted 7 conflagration spells, six of which hit for the
full 618 damage and one was partially resisted for 297 damage, before he was
dead. Keep in mind that I'm only level 47 now, and how much more I could
have done at 50. All of the wizards had similar effects. That's 4005 damage
within a very small amount of time, and though I think I may have done the
most damage, I did not do so by much. No other class came close. Our ability
to kill such a major mob so fast enabled us to get to killing the guardians
one at a time before anything had a chance to go wrong with the mezzes.

This power along with ports (and evacs), decent self-shields, roots, and a
few stuns, make me entirely happy with my class. The only thing I would love
to have is another stun spell, and I get one of those at level 49 (along
with ice comet, muhahaha).

Here are a few tips for grouping:
---------------------------------
I always ask for cleric buffs in my party, and those who I have grouped with
before know why. If we get a bad pull, I can pull a mob immediately (not
have to try and try like a tank) with one nuke to drag it away from the
group and immobilize it so the party can go back and get it later.
Immobilize is faster (yes, enough to make a difference when you're getting
hit for double 100+) and has a harder save for the mob to make, and it
usually lasts quite a while if the mob's not getting hit. It's a poor
wizard's version of mez.

If the tanks can't or don't get a mob off the cleric, you better do it. You
may die, but the cleric can rez you. If you don't, you're all gonna die. If
you do get the mob onto you, run it to the tanks and STOP so they can taunt
it off you. Don't run around like an idiot screaming, "Help! Help!" so that
they have to try to chase you.

Use your stuns until burn time, and then drop the mob. Don't burn too early,
pulling the mob onto you from the tank, and then onto the cleric when he has
to heal your low-ac wizard arse. It amazes me how many 40+ wizards STILL
don't understand this.

Make druids' and rangers' snare spell obsolete by doing your duty and timing
your spells well to drop the runner right as they turn to run.

In dungeons and especially in Lower Guk, know your way around the place. If
the puller gets rooted and can't feign, you can go save his/her life with
one super-taunt nuke, or at least stun the target to give him/her a chance
to run back to the cleric.

If it all goes wrong, evac the party to safety and try it again.
---------------------------------

In short, ADAPT TO THE SITUATION. Don't get stuck into the rut of trying to
only be the master of direct damage. Be truly useful to your party. I
regularly get into groups that other wizards get turned away from because
the people know I work hard for the group and work well under pressure. If
all you do is stand up and cast one DD per mob and sit back down, then you
deserve to get turned away for groups. Any idiot can do that.

If you can't find a group (happens to everyone sometimes, not just wizards),
then solo. Anyone who has ever said wizards can't solo never really tried.
Even at level 47, I can still make a bubble every couple of hours soloing at
several places, and I don't even have jboots. One tip: the easiest xp mobs
are caster mobs. They have lower hitpoints (and therefore take less mana to
kill so you can kill more) as a general rule and you can OWN them if you use
a stun/dd/disrupt rotation.

To sum this entire post up, don't whine about being weak. Learn how to be
strong. If something doesn't work, then try something else. The game is
supposed to be a challenge. Work on your skills (No, not the "You have
become better at ......." skills). If I could rule any mob in the game
without having to work for it, I'd toss Everquest in the pile with the other
games I don't play anymore and go do something else.
