Thanks to all for the intelligent responses! :)
I just wanted to add that I DO NOT CAMP - at least not as far as farming
items as concerned (as opposed to hanging out at a derv camp for hours on
end - which I HAVE done!).
Smithing is a special case...not only does it require a SIGNIFICANT
financial investment, but the only profitable outlet for your wares is
selling to fellow players. This kind of player interaction is something
that Verant has deemed a positive role-playing experience (OK, I KNOW what
you're thinking! But that's what VERANT thinks!). Unfortunately for all of
the master smiths out there, the massive flooding of ringmail, chain and
ESPECIALLY bronze armor has ruined the market for selling banded.
For those of you that think that 60 plat is a significant investment when
you know that a skill will earn up to 40 plat an hour....well....I don't
know what to say....I think that's pretty funny :)
Try and keep things in perspective, folks! EQ is a GAME...and to this point
its the best online RPG that's ever been produced. It's got it's
faults...and, yeah, I think they are making some pretty silly decisions in
there current attempts at tweaking the 'balance'. But its still the best
online RPG that I've ever played....wouldn't you agree??
Now go out there and kick some ASS!
Or....errrr....go out there and throw some CLAY!
Whatever ya do, HAVE FUN!
Sean

Sean, since you insisted that we 'flame away' here is a response for ya!

Obviously you have NOT tried to take up a skill... please don't... It would
only ruin it for everyone (just kidding)...

But seriously... It COSTS a lot to take up a skill... Especially one like
Smithing... You will pay out about 200-300plat to learn enough to be able to
make Banded armor... Then you will FINALLY be able to sell something for
over your cost (everything else costs more to make than it does to sell to
an NPC, and PC's don't buy things like Files, Scalers and Skewers, so the
only customer you have is the NPC (can you say monopoly?)... But while I
don't think it fair to make a HUGE profit from NPC's, it would be nice if
you could simply get you money back on the SUCCESSFUL items...

And is it really any different sitting around a forge or oven making and
selling things, than for those that are *harvesting* items from the NPC's in
places like Unrest and HighHold?? I don't think so, and I would almost bet
money you don't mind doing that!

In fact, I have never seen anyone CAMP a forge, and even if they did, and
there were others waiting 'in line', eventually they would leave because
they needed more raw materials (molds, metal, etc.) so others would drop in
when they left to go get more...

But I have heard of many groups that camp places like the Tower in Unrest...
Getting all the BS armor they can, then sending a member out to sell it off
or twink it over to another character so they can come back for more... The
other night we ran into such a group, many of them over level 30, who were
not even interested in any of the other mobs that spawned there, only the
Knight... And the others would not attack them because they were too high
level... This is just Harvesting plain and simple... One of them even told
me he just needed to get his characters loot up to 1000 plat in the bank so
he could SELL it at EBAY!! How sick can you get...

My point is, they really are fixing the wrong area (skills) in the wrong
manner... Its lousy that some skills cost so little to practice and others
cost more... but a person who decides to be a baker, smith, or whatever
SHOULD be able to make a profit from PC's, and should at the very least be
at a break even point for items sold to an NPC...

There is too much money in the game already, and that is because there is
too much LOOT in the game, not because there are too many pie tins or
skewers or Bat Wing Crunchies! If Verant really wants to do something about
it, they need to control things like Mobs dropping Bronze armor all the
time, which USED to sell only to PC's, but which, now that most already have
it, gets dumped at the NPC's for more plat too...
