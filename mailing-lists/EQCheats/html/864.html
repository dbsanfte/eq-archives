From: "Brian Hook" <bhook@...>

I have some spare time this week, and as
such I've been
doing some GM events
after hours on various servers (I was Morxxa
Mangyfur the
Werewolf in SK on
Tunare; the named Archon in Cazic-Thule on
Cazic-Thule and
Xegony; the
Undead Vizier on the chessboard on
Cazic-Thule; Frumpy
Darkpaw in
blackburrow on Cazic-Thule), and I've
learned some
interesting things about
players in general.

First, higher level players are weenies.
It's that simple.
The minute a
fight turns against them, they're zoning.
They're also
generally very
stupid the minute something unexpected
happens. I was an
Archon in
Cazic-Thule and while they were having a
picnic in the lower
throne room I
popped a justicar, herald, and a champion to
liven things up
for the
Winnebago crowd.

NOTE: If you are killed by a GM/possessed
NPC, you do NOT
lose exp!

The zone freaked, had no idea what to do.
Some mid to high
40s showed up
and tried to take me out (they were a
little, um,
foolhardy), doing shouts
of "I'll defeat him!", then -- get this --
sending in their
pets ONE AT A
TIME. There was a 50th necro and a 48th
magician there, and
their pets are
MEAN. But they sent them in one at a time
-- easy for me to
deal with (I
was a 50th wizzie). If both pets had come
in, I would have
been toast.

Stupid stupid stupid.

Some of them stormed in, expecting me to be
about 35th
level, and I nuked
them instantly. So they started whining
about it being
"unfair". Someone
else in the zone started OOCing how this was
"ruining" their
evening because
all they wanted to do was get some rubicite
and log.
Hearing things like
that makes me almost want to give up on the
game (or its
players).

Anyway, instead of getting organized, they
hid in the
courtyard. Now, at
this point I had only a small contingent of
bodyguards, but
more important,
I only had THREE spells memorized -- Ice
Comet, Conflag, and
Frost Shock.
None of those can be cast very quickly. Two
good tanks, two
clerics, and
one wizzie could have killed me EASILY, but
they refused to
come after me.

As I was getting bored sitting with my
gorillas in the
throne room, I walked
out into the courtyard -- ALONE -- and
proceeded to cast,
killing a few
folks. Now here's the even sadder part -- I
walked out,
alone, into a room
of about 20 players from 20th to 50th level,
AND NO ONE
BOTHERED ROOTING OR
STUNNING ME. They tried to kill me, but
instead of taking
away my ability
to run or cast, they just tried to kill me
quick. Then,
even stupider, two
of the casters chased me into the
maze...alone.

Dumb dumb dumb.

So I ran back to lick my wounds and hid in
the throne room,
but they
wouldn't give chase. I finally decided that
it was time for
me to die, so I
ran to the zone border to kill people
looting their corpses
(*evil grin*),
and finally I got dropped by a nice crowd.

Okay, so here's the loot I dropped: full set
of rubicite and
a shimmering
silk robe.

And here's the point where I get miffed: if
a GM is killed
in an event,
"ninja looting" can happen since ANYONE can
loot the corpse,
not the
person/party responsible for most damage. A
50th wizard
looted my corpse
and got ALL the rubi, then said he didn't
find anything. In
addition, he
didn't distribute it to anyone else that was
in the battle
-- he kept it for
his guild (presumably).

This really upset me, but because of a error
on my part the
two rubi bracers
that were dropped were in a backpack and
couldn't be looted
(except by me).
I then looted my corpse and awarded the
bracers to a 42nd
level wizzie that
played smart, fierce, and was RP the whole
way. I'm still
upset that the
50th wizzie didn't share the loot with the
tanks that were
there.

At one point a 32nd level SK said something
like "Hey
lizzie, I'm wearing
your brother as a cloak!" so I teleported to
him and said
"HSSSS! THEN YOU
DIE!" He was with a party of 30s camping
near the Avatar
room. He RAN as
fast as he could, but his party put up a
REAL good fight. I
mean GOOD.
They could have taken me down...almost. I
popped an Archon
that wasted most
of them, but they nearly killed me. But
that party was
smart, organized,
and BRAVE. I wish I had caught their names
because I would
have rewarded
them.

That party of low 30s had more guts and
smarts than a whole
zone of 40s.
*sigh*

In a way I think a lot of high level players
have become
used to how things
work, and once things stop working that way,
they get really
confused and
don't know what to do. I think a PvP player
would have done
a LOT better.

When I did the event on Cazic-Thule a 32nd
level paladin
(wearing bronze)
looted me, but since I had an ebony blade on
me he gave it
to a ranger that
had helped, and he also gave away the rubi
pants to another
tank. Oh, and
on Cazic-Thule I buffed myself so that
basically ONLY melee
types could kill
me -- I gave myself insane magic resistance.
When I went
down on CT server
I was taking about 400 points of damage per
tick from 12
melee fighters,
which is EXACTLY what I wanted since I was
dropping melee
gear. I was NOT
going to let a wizzie get a full set of rubi
again.

During another event, when I was the
werewolf in SK,
EVERYONE kept running
from me for the zone. They kept trying to
pull me to the
zone so they could
fight then zone if they were getting hurt.
I wouldn't play
that way, and
summoned a cyclops to help keep the zone
clear (MWUHAHAHA).

During the entire SK event, I killed only
TWO people. One
of them was a
35th level dwarven warrior wearing bronze.
He fought
bravely -- when I got
him to one bub of health, instead of running
he stood up and
went down
swinging. Never cried for help, never tried
to run. And
there was a crowd
around that didn't bother healing him
either. This warrior
-- Aindolmar --
was an exemplary example of a brave, RPing
player. I
rewarded him after my
death (I dropped a werewolf cloak and tunic
for those that
care to know)
specifically with a crafted breastplate. He
was wielding a
BBC (I think)
and wearing bronze with no other twinks. He
wasn't in a
guild that I could
tell, and was playing the game "legit" as it
were. More
important, it
looked like he was playing in the spirit of
the game, and I
rewarded that.

Finally, when I was the undead vizier on the
chessboard, I
was a 25th level
wizard. A 15th level dwarven warrior (gotta
love those
dorfs!) named
Thrombrir kept trying to rally everyone to
attack me. A
couple druids kept
running from me -- and they were 24th and
37th level
respectively -- and
played like complete weenies. This warrior
ran up and
started whacking on
me, ALONE. No party, no backup, but I was
conning BRIGHT
RED to him, but he
didn't care. Once again, he was not a twink
-- he had on
ringmail and some
rawhide -- and he was busy RPing like crazy.

In that battled I dropped an FBR -- which a
42nd level necro
looted, as if
she needed it (and I don't think she
shared). But I had
already learned not
to reward people that looted my body -- too
many selfish
types. So now I
keep track of players that are brave, smart
(if that's in
character),
untwinked, and role-playing, and I reward
them appropriately
after the
battle.

Thrombrir received crafted gauntlets and one
crafted bracer
to replace his
rawhide.

My point? The lower level types played
well, smart, and
were having fun,
and the higher levels were busy screaming
about things being
unfair, were
doing dumb stuff, wouldn't leave the zone
border, and
wouldn't share loot.
Oh, and when high levels hear about an event
via "guildsay",
they all come
to kill me so they can get the loot. It's
frustrating being
a 25th level
mob and getting taken down by a 50th SK
instead of a crowd
of 20s. But I
can't pick and choose who comes to kill me,
so the best I
can do is make
sure that the right people are rewarded.

An explanation of my "anti-twinking" policy
is probably in
order for those
of you in a guild or that are twinked:

- these events are role playing events. All
the ones I
described I did
alone without any input from others, in an
attempt to spice
up the game for
some folks. As such, I reward role players.

- I don't reward twinks because, well, they
don't need the
stuff and often
don't appreciate what they received. There
was an 11th
level wizzie wearing
an FBR at the chessboard, and I killed him
THREE times =)

- the players that really need the gear are
often the ones
least willing to
sacrifice their ideals (camping/twinking) to
get the gear.
By rewarding
these players in events like these it
spreads good word of
mouth, and more
important, instills a sense of faith that
they've been doing
the right thing
by RPing instead of power gaming.

Anyway, I await your flames :-)

Brian
