You have hit the nail on the head in many ways. All of the downward
tweaks such as skill costs, decreasing the spawn frequency of camped
NPCs, and lowering treasure found on critters works against new
players. It entrenches the old players who have moved up under the old
system and prevents the new players from getting there at all, or at
least makes the journey more arduous and less satisfying. McQuaid said
he is not going to tweak the game in such a manner as to make the game
too hard for casual players in response to the 24/7 no-life junkies
hollering that things are too easy or capped out. However it seems he
is moving slightly in that direction nonetheless.

This constant hardening of the system is exactly why I left DragonRealms
to play EQ after nearly 3 years. The game got to the point where it was
impossible for a casual player to advance his character or make any
money. I was at the point where at 40th level it was taking 300 hours
or more hours of constant work to make a level. If Verant moves too far
down this road EQ will whither and die just like DR is. Then we will
move on to the next game until it gets too difficult to be fun anymore.

Etc., etc., etc.
