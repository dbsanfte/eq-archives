If you duel in a city arenas like the arenas in Queynos and Freeport and the
one near Lake Rathe:

1) your corpse cannot be looted by your opponent or anyone else if you die
2) you do not lose or gain any experience points
3) The embarrasing sever wide message "(insert PC name) has been defeated by
(insert PC name) in a duel" does not go out.
4) you do not lose any faction (unfortunantly, do to a bug this last point is
not currently true - I think they are fixing the faction loss bug soon - many
tournaments have been postponed because of this)

So, in summary - Dueling in the city arenas is just for fun, and very fun it
is. It's also hilarious when someone in the crowd who is watching (from
outside the arena boundry) tries to get cute by casting a spell on one of the
combatants (like a heal or a root). You can be guaranteed that in a few
seconds a Guard is going to come slay whoever that asshole was. I've seen it
happen a few times and it's hilarious!!

My first time dueling in the arena was with my 14th lvl Erudite Wizard. I
dueled a 16th lvl Barb Shaman - he said that the duel would start upon my
first attack. Wellllllllllllll, you can guess what my first attack was (er,
ROOT! of course). He was was rooted and we traded damage spells for about 5
or 6 rounds and being the damage god that us Wizards are, you can guess the
result (I hit for 79 at the time).

Anyways, have fun dueling in the arenas, its adds a whole new lvl of fun to
the game.
