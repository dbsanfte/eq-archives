I have read at least two interviews with Brad McQuaid
describing how they view trade skills, and he has explicitly stated
in no uncertain terms that they do not want any of the player trade
skills to be implemented in such a way so as to allow for a player to
purchase items from vendors, execute their skill, and sell the
results of their efforts back to vendors at a profit. The way they
want it to work is to offer players a 'sweet spot' between what they
paid for materials and what NPC vendors charge for an equivilant item
to what a player can make themselves through their tradecraft. This
way, a player is encouraged to sell their wares at a profit to other
players and not at a loss to vendors.
In this way, they want to stimulate the "player run economy"
and not a "kick back and make a bunch of plat just interacting with
vendors"-type approach. I'm not saying I agree with it, I'm just
passing on what I know to be true... not player speculation or rumor,
but actual statements from The Man. :)
