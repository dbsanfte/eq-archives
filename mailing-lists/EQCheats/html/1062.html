As i know, there are already alot of cryptic algorithms exists, which
make cryptoanalytical method of cracking fairly ineffective. Of course,
most of them illegal to use without approval of agencies like FBI in USA
or FAPSI in my country. Still, if consider server and client as "closed
systems" (unauthorized access is impossible), it is easy to implement
coding what will make cryptoanalytical hack almost impossible. But, i
dont think EQ uses really good crypting for its protocol. First, it is
very restricted in many countries, and EQ is international product which
supposed to be legal in all countries. Second, good crypting is very
time-consuming, and EQ server have traffic around 1MB/sec. It is
impossible to encrypt 1MB per second with any serious algorithm. I
believe it is using something like XOR with random-seeded bitstrings, or
just CRC protection, to make his traffic safe. It is enough to force
most "hackers" to use debugger on EQ's exe instead of much easer parsing
of network data. And i never seen games using anything more serious than
such forms of protection.
