Aye, for studded leather, you have to smith the studs and then tailor the
low quality skin (not easy to get) with the studs. For Re-inforced
leather, you have to smith the boning and then tailor the high quality skin
with the boning. This is a very complex process for what you get. The
smith has to make the studs from metal bits and files, the files are made
from metal bits and a file mold. The tailor has to find all of the skins
which are hard to get because most kills yield up ruined pelts.
