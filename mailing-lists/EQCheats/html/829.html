Heh, I *think* this is on topic... it's so hard to tell anymore.

It appears the three main factors in prices are faction, charisma, and vendor
multiple.

Some vendors just sell for more. Sonsa Fromp in one of the huts in Misty
Thicket, for example, sells chain armor for a little over twice Twippie Diggs'
prices in Rivervale. Infact, if you ask him about it, he'll warn you.

Charisma is a little more nebulous... I'm a bard with pretty high charisma
(around 110 with all of my gear on) and prices seem to be generally pretty
good, but it's harder to measure against someone else due to faction. But it
definately makes a difference. Get a few pieces of plus CHA stuff and check
prices with and without them. Things definately get better for people with
higher charisma... to a point. Then there's faction.

Faction can really do a number on you. The order of faction (as I remember
it) goes "threatens to attack," "glares threateningly," "glowers dubiously,"
"indifferent, "amiable," "kindly", "warmly," and if there are things higher
than warmly, I haven't gotten my faction with anyone that high yet. Even
within each of these "bands" you can have higher or lower faction it seems,
these are just breaking points. It's possible to get one price, kill a few
things that change faction with that vendor, and then get a slightly
higher/lower price; even though the "con" didn't change. These are usually
pretty small price changes, prices seem to change by a much greater degree
when you hit a new band. I.E. Given the same CHA, there may be some small
changes between different levels of indifferent, but when you hit amiable, the
change is much more in your favor. Of course this is easier to see on very
expensive items than on flasks of water or fishing bait.

So basically, someone who hates you might give you slightly better prices if
your charming or better looking (which also seems to be in the realm of
charisma), but even if you're a complete ass people who love you will cut you
a deal (which makes sense to me). This particularly makes sense from the
perspective of "Yeah, he's an arrogant bastard, but he keeps us safe from the
orcs. Cut a few coppers off of that water flask."

As far as race/class modifications to prices, these seem to be faction based.
Rouges can't have a hard time at first in places like Freeport (at least mine
did), but once you've raised faction with "Coalition_Tradefolk" (which seems
to be the primary vendor faction in East and West Freeport, not sure about
North) they'll sell to you just fine.

Okay, that's what how I think things work. Now for a couple of questions:
Are there levels of faction above Warmly? Quest wise: is there anything left
besides turning in deathfist belts that will raise faction with FP militia?
I'd like a list of any quests and/or kills that will raise faction with that
group? Lowering faction with the knights is fine, since just killing orcs
will raise it again. Also, as I plan to be a world traveler, are there any
kills that will increase faction with vendors/guards in Grobb or Oggok that
won't too seriously damage faction with the smaller races? Finally, on the
CHA raising end, does the Leatherfoot Raider Quest really require D'Vinn's
Dragoon Dirk as it appears? Or are there just plain "dirks" found on other
dragoons that will work?

Thanks in advance.
