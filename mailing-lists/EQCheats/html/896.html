All the anticamp code does is slow down respawn rates, (making the wait
longer than normal and thereby trying to discourage camping). It doesn't
stop the spawning totally. Try timing some creatures respawn rates sometime
in an area where you suspect the codinge has been used.
I timed various creatures in Befallen where I suspected the coding was used,

and found that respawn rates were a lot slower when you were in the radius
of the "sphere of anti-camping". I would like to stress something here as
well - this DOES NOT mean the anti-camping coding is used everywhere! It's
used in just a FEW areas that I'm aware of. One place I found it used, was
the SK room that has the first key. When I camped that area to test my
theory, it took well over an hour and a half for the SK to respawn. When I
stayed about 2 rooms over from the SK room, I found that the SK would
respawn every 45 mins to an hour.

I did this for 2 weeks to test out the rumors I had heard, and also after
having read an interview with Brad (I believe) about camping, and how they
supposively wouldn't use any anticamping coding. I also had a bunch of other

players keep track/time as well.

IF, and I stress IF, there isn't any anti-camping coding, (and I believe
that there IS, and I could be wrong) I'd like to know why I was told by
several GM's and Guides that it does indeed exist, (sorry, no screenshots to

back this up as it was at least a month ago I was told) and why there's an
uncanny, and very noticeable pattern by myself and other players as well who

helped me out? PERHAPS this coding has since been removed, which is highly
possible; there's been many patches since I last tested this theory out. I'm

not sure if it has been removed because I don't camp. I just did it this one

time to test out what I had heard.

Just my 2 coppers worth
