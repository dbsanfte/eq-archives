Yes, training guards, blocking etc is illegal BUT it is up to the discretion
of every guide that gets called to the scene and every server is not treated
equal. If you feel that a guide is being biased and is not adhereing the the
rules you may repetition or ask for your issue to be escalated to a GM.
Personally I think it should be legal. If I see halfling guards getting
kited you can bet I would love to snare/root the evil %$##$ doing it. IMHO
It is a risk factor that should be allowed.

Run through any dungeon killing something and the dungeon NPC residents are
going to jump in and assist in anyway they can, why should PC residents be
treated eany different? You want to see a train created that causes problems
with getting out of a dungeon... go sit in Splitpaw for a while. At least
every couple of hours the front door area is blocked by 5-15 gnolls of
various levels due to a train created and all the other NPCs jumping in. I
dont see anyone petitioning to get them in trouble.

I know in a few areas the higher levels are acting as guards in places they
are killing them. This gives the newbies a place to run to that is basically
stronger than the guards to begin with since they are killing them after
all. These people normally SoW and buff all the newbies also. Yes it takes a
way some of the roleplaying perspective but it keeps everyone happy and GMs
out of it.

As far as the issue of "you dont have to kill them, you can hunt somewhere
else" well yes and no. From a roleplaying side if things many people have
taken it upon themselves to pick a race to "eliminate" from Norrath. Neriak
guards are high on that list. But so are Ogres. I see plenty of evil
characters picking off halfling guards and Karanas guards also. So this is
not an issue suffered by evil races only. Dark elves that stay in Nektulos
Forest for any length of time end up hunting halflings, no reason why
halflings shouldn't come back and return the favor. I know I do.
