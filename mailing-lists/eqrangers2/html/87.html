Buying spells early for level 15 is excellent
advice. Chances are you wont be hunting near a vendor
where you can buy them when you do ding. I have all of
interest stashed in a backpack; one orange bub away from
15!<br><br>As to an earlier post regarding where a ranger can
solo from 9+, I've enjoyed the following:<br><br>at 9,
you can still get good experience in Blackburrow, if
you are careful. If you dont mind a couple of corpse
runs while figuring out the good spots out of the way
of trains in there, you can do well.<br><br>From 9
at least until 14, you can get decent experience and
make a LOT of money from cash and items in the
warrens. This zone is now pretty busy as people pass
through on their way to the new Stonebrunt Mountains.
This is a mixed blessing...help on hand if you get in
trouble, but the zone used to be empty and basically
trainless.<br><br>Since the outdoors is more suited to the Rangers'
talents, good outdoor hunting can be found in the Northern
Desert of Ro from 9 to 15; if it starts getting boring
around 13 just run due south until you zone into Oasis
of Marr, boredom over.<br><br>The Karanas can also
be fun; Western Karana boasts decent hunting since
we have track, but avoid the various highly
dangerous camps of red conning baddies, I forget if they
are Ogres or what...from around 10 or 11, you can get
in a "bandit group" and get a good taste of pickup
grouping. Bandits pop fast and can be done on a limited
time budget, if that is why you dont group much (I
only play about an hour an evening myself, which is
why I personally dont group often). From 11 up, North
Karana provides good hunting from the wizard spires,
again taking advantage of our Track skill to know what
to grab and what to steer clear of.<br><br>Happy
hunting. The ranger's life is a good one. By far my
favorite class of any I have raised into the teens.
