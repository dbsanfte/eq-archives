Subject: FYI: DAOC


If any of you are thinking of trying DAOC and
wondering what its like, I would suggest trying it if you
are getting the "stale" feel for EQ. If EQ is still
fun for you, it would be pointless to try DAOC. They
are in truth really quite similar. I like it because
EQ was starting to get same old same old for me, and
I didnt think I would ever have time for the
so-called high end game.<br><br>DAOC is much more geared
toward an hour or two of play at all levels.<br><br>One
downside, they are still working on the Rogue/stealth type
character classes, and rangers fall in this group. If you
REALLY like playing rangers, wait a bit. Other classes
seem well balanced, all are soloable to sone extent,
and grouping gives a big bonus for loot and XP, so
its easier to get groups anywhere when you do want
one (also a nice LFG interface)<br><br>If you have
read that there is no death penalty, its untrue. Death
HURTS, it costs money to get back to full constitution
(affects HP) and so makes the game have a challenge. You
cant just engage things with impunity, you have to
think. However, its not so crushingly painful that
groups only try low mobs, you can do some trial and
error. Starting with your gear and backpack is really
nice, if you dont care too much about the xp you dont
have to go to your deathsite and pray if you dont want
to. Just pay a healer for the lost const (HP).
