From the Journals of Angarth, Wolf-Warrior of the Anstruther Clan


Monday, 19th of February, in the Year 3174

I heard a voice beckon me, whispering my name as I traveled through
the commonlands of the West. It was a hollow voice of dissolution, tinged
with menace that penetrated my soul. I closed my eyes and concentrated,
listening to the call. Breaking into a run, I traversed the grassy knolls
of the land until the burning sands that covered the south replaced the
greenery.

The voice still called to me, leading me eastward along the deserts
edge. A lake passed by on my left as well as a few small cottages, but I
did not stop. I concentrated on the voice, with my eyes focused on the
rising dunes in front of me. That fact alone prevented me from dying under
the boot of a marauding hill giant! From whence he came I know not, nor was
I about to hail him and ask where he was bound! I turned quickly to the
south and scampered up the sandy dunes as far as I could. The luck of the
gods were with me that eve, for the giant ignored me and continued on his
westerly trek, no doubt looking for something to eat.

The sun was just setting, leaving the entire land in darkening
shadows. When I crested the next dune, I saw a faint light emitting from a
cavern in the sand. As I approached the lighted tunnel, the beckoning voice
echoing in my mind grew fainter until it was just a whisper. Cadavers
littered the sand surrounding the entrance to the tunnel, grotesque
reminders of the ever-present dangers in this hot, foreign land.

I ducked into the tunnel and followed the winding passage until I
came to an open chamber. The cloying scent of decay permeated the vast
chamber, hanging like a thick blanket of fog over the marshy bleakness of
the Innothule swamp. I heard screams bellowing forth from the depths below,
followed by maniacal laughing and rotted moans of despair. I offered a
silent pray to Rellos Zek, then hefted my two huge battle-axes against my
shoulder, and proceeded down the stairs.

Other incorporeal chanting joined the voice that first beckoned me,
the cacophony trying to drive me mad at the same time luring me deeper into
the depths of the sepulcher. I tried some of the doors in the first chamber,
finding one door that was locked with no apparent entry. There were other
people in the tomb, thank Rellos, so I asked one of them and was directed to
a small room where a Paladin of Evil tarried. I entered the room and found
the culprit, a dark elf with a heart as black as its ebony skin! After a
very short fight (his abilities was no match for a warrior of my stature!) I
was able to find a key on his decapitated corpse. Sure enough, the key
unlocked the door and allowed me access to the nether regions of the tomb.

I came to a whole in the wall with a broken circular stairway
leading down to another chamber. I descended the stairway, using the
guidance of Rellos Zek to block out the vexatious clamor that filled my head
and direct my feet to the right path. As I entered the circular chamber, I
noticed bloodied spikes placed at strategic places around the edges,
obviously to catch the unwary adventurer falling from the pathway above.
The rotten stench here was stronger, no longer cloying, but rather more
akin to the actual trolls that inhabit the wicked city of Grobb. Another
tunnel continued to my right, so I turned and started traveling down the
passage. As I walked by a closed door, I heard the sounds of battle coming
from within. I pressed my ear to the wooden door, listening to the
conflict. I slowly opened the door....

....and was immediately beset by an evil magic-user that could
control the undead! My mind reeled in horror as I watched the casters pet
come after me with wicked talons. I quicky initiated combat, throwing in
kicks wherever the opportunity revealed itself, trying to kill the
necromancer before he could do much harm. In between my precise slashes and
hard kicks, he was able to cast a noxious cloud that pervaded my mouth and
nostrils, then slowly oozed down my throat to leave a nauseating feeling in
the pit of my stomach. I kept my bile from rising and continued to fight,
finally dispatching the evil caster. His skeletal familiar continued to
rake at my armor with sharp talons, each stroke causing a loud, metallic
streak to echo off the walls of the mausoleum. Just as my mighty axe fell
the skeleton, another one took its place, this one more powerful. I had
read in the annals of the great library in Erudin about a skeleton made from
the remains of a large creature, known as a greater skeleton. It took me
some time to finish off the incarnate of evil, but alas, with many wounds,
the skeleton collapsed into a harmless pile of bones.

I must rest. I write this account of my experience while I sit in
this forlorn tomb. I still feel the ill effects of the dreaded poisonous
vapor the necromancer blew in my face. It weakens my legs, my arms. I feel
my insides churn with the rotted touch of Bertoxxulous. My strength is
ebbing as I collapse on a nearby rock, my breathing becoming shallower by
the minute. Footsteps. Many footsteps. The pain is almost unbearable. I
hear maniacal laughter behind me. Footsteps closer now. My vision is
blurred. A figure dressed in robes approaching. He? She? - carries a
staff. Another dread necromancer coming to claim my soul? A smaller figure
is next to the taller robed figure. Also in robes. A skeleton behind him.
They come for me. Doomed am I to walk the halls of this crypt in an undead
state. Another figure approaches. Small like the first, but dressed....I
can not tell. Too weak. Too weak....

Angarth, Warrior of the 17th Tier
