Your points are well taken. But consider this:

It *is* a fantasy setting. Or we wouldn't be running around fighting ghouls
with magic swords you know? A single sword swipe would end our lives and
there would be no *respawn*

A mage will do more damage to a giant in a shorter amount of time due to the
economies of scale. Imagine what the skin on a creature the size of a hill
giant must be like. It has to contain literally tons of internal organs and
fluids. That means that it is going to be significantly stronger than human
flesh. Probably more like a heavy leather, much like an elephant.
Therefore your sword slashing simply isn't going to be as effective, and you
won't be deftly slashing through tendons and throats and disabling your
opponent in 20 seconds.

Ok, back to fantasy setting now. We aren't using katanas in EQ. The
weapons of a warrior are not precision weapons. A 2 handed sword is
designed for a heavy over the shoulder strike. A blow to an unarmored
opponent (in reality) is fatal. But it isn't for slashing tendons. A
longsword is a slashing weapon, but it is a bit unwieldy for precision
strikes. Forget about real uses of real weapons though, this is fantasy
remember? You can imagine what you will, but in EQ there are rules of the
world, so you won't be running up a giant's back and sticking your sword in
his eye. I don't know a game that will let you do that. "Thief" comes close
but still isn't what you are talking about.

The warrior is a hack'n'slash "Conan" type. We aren't knights...those are
paladins in this game. We aren't ninjas, samurai or any other sword
wielding supermen. We stand toe to toe with our opponents and trade blows.
If we are faster, stronger, more skilled we win. If we are not, we lose.
Some things are just so big and hit so hard, that we can't possibly melee
with them until we are so skilled the creature offers no experience.

The warrior excels in a full party with a cleric. Try it and you will see.
3 and 4 person parties aren't the same, because you will often be the
primary target of high level mobs and take alot of damage. But a full party
with 2 warriors and 1 cleric plus other assorted baggage is a group that can
take much higher level mobs without losing members.

I said before the EQ is about grouping and balance. Warriors deal alot of
damage slowly and consistently. In the same time warriors absorb alot of
attacks. Remember when mages get hit in melee they always take damage,
while warriors are dodging and parrying. If a mage loses his tank, he is
most likely dead. He won't be able to cast his high damage spells because
they take to long to cast, he will have to resort to small damage attacks
because they cast faster and won't get interrupted.

Every class has advantages and disadvantages. The warrior class is just
lacking in unique advantages. We get all the really good combat skills many
levels ahead of all the hybrids. We just need something to set us apart in
melee. I will reiterate that a critical hit skill is the most likely way to
do this.

Verant will never give warriors and kind of consistent massive damage
inducing ability. But I think the critical hit solution will satisfy your
belief that you should be able to quickly slash a tendon and do heavy
damage. You just aren't going to be able to do it every round, or count on
its success.
