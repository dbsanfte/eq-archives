If you're looking for a "X class is the best, period" answer in this
debate, I seriously doubt you'll find it. Verdant has done a pretty
good job of balancing the classes; there really isn't any one perfect,
or 'best' class. Warriors have pros and cons, as do the warrior
subclasses, as do the Mages, as do the mage subclasses. Each has a
style of play that's either slightly or massively different from the
other. It comes down to personal preference and available resources.

Examples:

Warriors have to pair with a Cleric (or any mage subclass with healing
spells, or even a Paladin, who also has healing); because otherwise the
downtime after fights will catch up with them. Even pairing with other
Warriors, or with Wizards/Magicians won't work around this; Warriors
will have to have a healer present to counteract their innate
disadvantage of requiring healing at the higher levels. SKs are in
this boat too; they get tricks, but no heals; and so need the healing
too.

Paladins can self-heal, which enables a Paladin to fairly efficiently
team without a healer present. After my favorite pairing
(Paladin/Wizard), my next most favorite pairing is (Paladin/Warrior);
because we're both solid melee combatants, and the Paladin can keep
both topped off.

But again, it's just a matter of style and resources present.

> 2 tanks, why do you need a paladin? More than 2

See above; Paladin acts as second mini-cleric. Just as 2 warriors help
spread the tanking between themselves (reducing the danger and overall
HP load that would be more onerous to a single tank), a Paladin helps
spread the healing load on the single cleric, yet doesn't force the
party to have a second guy (second cleric) standing around waiting for
the tanks to drop. A Paladin can swing with the warriors and then step
back to heal, then step back in. It splits the difference between a
3rd tank and a cleric nicely, IMO.

Also, I like having tanks/fighters along; any full group I formed up
would preferably have 3 fighter classes present, and *at least* 1
Warrior.

> But to sum up, paladins sacrifice so much melee
> ability for so little casting ability which

I'm confused where you're getting this 'sacrifice so much melee
ability' thing from? Paladin combat skills are level with Warriors'.
The only main difference is at which point a particular skill shows up
(no kick or dual wield, and everything else 3-5 levels after the
Warrior receives it), and the lower hps.

It's ok to not like a particular class; I hate rogues and druids, for
example. But there's a difference between simply disliking a class for
astetic or personal opinion reasons, and the class flat out being
inferior. No class in EQ is flat out inferior. Each is simply tuned
in a certain way. You find what best suits your own natural
inclinations as a player, and off ya go!
