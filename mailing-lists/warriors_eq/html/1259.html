IMHO making the warriors defensive capabilities is just the opposite way to
go. Warriors are already the best in the game for defense. It's just the
offensive part of it that gets me. Since when were warriors only tanks? In
almost any other RPG I've ever played, warriors kicked arse on the same
level with just about any other class. Lets look at console RPGs for a sec.
Final Fantasy VII is a perfect example. Sure summons and spells did about
9999 damage at high levels, but you also had special ATTACKS (called limit
breaks) that did comparable damage, if not the same. Not to mention that a
regular attck did about half that damage anyways. But in EQ we don't even
get HALF of the damage casters get. A caster comes in and hits a mob for,
what, 1200? Does any warrior here do 600 damage in the same amount of time?
500? Hell even 300? that's not even 1/4 the damage a caster can do. It
reminds me of playing AD & D in a tournament where I was the only fighter
among 10 other Wizards. They always came in and blasted everything with
spells and all I did was take the hits. But guess what? When we got to some
Drow or something I was the one killing everything since their spells didn't
work as good all of a sudden. But even then I could do comparable damage.
Lets say they cast Fireball...good spell in AD&D. 6d6 +1/level damage. So a
caster of 20th level can do 56 points of damage with one fireball to a LARGE
group. But with my +3 Vorpal weapon and girdle of storm giant strength, I'd
kick his arse in damage. 2 attacks per round my damage all total is 46 per
round. That breaks down like this: +3 Vorpal Sword is 1d8+3, then my storm
giant strentgh (24) adds +12 to damage, making maximum damage 23 per attack,
twice a round. That's not including the random effects of the vorpal weapon.
Anytime I get a critical strike (20 on a d20) it automatically chops off the
opponents head, killing him instantly. I'm not trying to compare the virtues
of AD&D to EQ, because the last thing the world needs is someone trying to
clone the rules again. What I am doing is showing an example that the game
CAN be balanced for each class. The damage a caster gets can blow up an
entire group (i've seen it take out over 30 monsters) whereas a fighter is
for one-on-one melee combat, but they both can do about the same damage PER
MONSTER at the same level. Maybe I am just rambling....
