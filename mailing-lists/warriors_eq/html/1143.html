I agree and disagree.

There is no way a warrior is going to do as much damage per round as a spellcaster on certains kinds of creatures. How exactly are you going to do the kind of damage attainable with a fireball against a creature like a hill giant with something like a sword? You're only chance is to achieve some kind of critical hit to a sensitive part of the giant...maybe a sword thrust to the gonads or something. Regardless, I propose the critical hit skill so that a warrior can add more to a group, and have comparable survivability soloing. Also, I think critical hits should be used like parry/dodge and not like kick/bash, Simply because the assumption is you are ALWAYS trying to do the most damage possible. With kick/bash you are making a choice of attack. Kick for more damage or bash to stun. Not that I see any high level warriors bashing...but the option is there.

Warriors will never be able to out damage a mage. However, the warrior should be able to last a lot longer in physical combat. I do see that taking effect in the mid-teen levels, and I can only assume it gets better. But the hybrids can take nearly as much melee punishment and deal out nearly as much melee damage, and supplement combat with spells. The warrior is outclassed in every respect by a hybrid at a comparable level, in the mid-teens. Clerics are very powerful in every game I have played but are always not very popular classes to play. Not sure why this is, but in EQ the 1-10 levels are tough on a cleric. But after that I see them really romping. It seems to me most clerics I have seen group out of convenience rather then necessity.
