There are quite a few reasons to play a female character, although admittedly
they are all matters of taste. First off, many of the female figures look
much better than the male ones (I say that partly because I'm a guy who likes
the look of a woman). Secondly, who's to say that females can't be just as
much an action/adventure hero as much as a male? Myself and several friends
of mine all have female characters in our rosters because sometimes one gets
tired of running all male characters all the time. Female characters should
have the chance to represent just like anybody else. Thirdly, the decision
to play a female is no different from any other character generation choice.
Why did you decide to play that dark elf, or human, or barbarian? Why did
you decide to play that warrior, or enchanter, or shadowknight? Because you
thought you'd try something new, right? Does playing a wizard mean that you,
in real life, practice magic spells? Of course not. Does playing an orge
mean, in real life, you wish you were an 8-foot tall ugly and monsterous
humanoid? I certainly hope not.
