Hmm, I worked the night on-call shift this past week, and the cool
opportunity to play throughout the night around my work. As such, I
spent the entire time working on getting a Tomahawk. I've been
following this discussion closely, and would like to comment.

First however, I think this is my first post to this group, so
I'd like to introduce myself. I also play on Solusek Ro, under
the name Dalkin, an 18th lvl Barbarian Warrior. I infrequently play
an Erudite Wizard named Fural, but he's still 5th?, maybe 4th. Not
much anyhow :)

I must have spent close to 24 hrs working on getting a Tomahawk.
One of the things that made the questing a little less annoying
was the fact that my attitude of sharing the spawn point with other
groups or individuals has made my character quite a few friends.

For instance: Last night, THREE times, in about 30 minutes, a
named Mucktail NPC spawned, which gave a shield. After the first
two, a character asked if he could get the next shield bearer.
Since our group had recently acquired two, we agreed. In the course
of fighting however, the third spawned and our group killed him.
I insisted, and eventually convinced the rest of our group, that
we broke OUR word to share, and we gave the guy the shield.

Yeah, we gave up the chance to sell it or use it, but only by
demonstrating good play can we expect others to do so. I guarantee
you that Elf will speak highly of me. :)

So, I was one of those who camped for quite a while to get the
Tomahawk, and don't feel it improper. I managed to get 3 levels
and 150+ PP in the course of my time, it's not like the only thing
good that came of my experience was the single weapon.

I guess my point, now that I'm rambling :), is that if you actively
encourage others to share, and show them the benefits, you might be
much more likely to be shared with in the future. I hear from
my guildmates that they are annoyed with so-and-so, some "farmer"
or whatnot. I don't want a name like that for myself, so I don't
do it.

And lastly, since I'm intimately familiar with the crowd up in
Highpass these days :), I've found most people are:

a) working to get a few items for themselves of co-party members
b) amenable to sharing the spawn point if you are also

Good luck, and look me up online if you get a chance!
