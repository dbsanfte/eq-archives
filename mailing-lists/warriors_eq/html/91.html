Subject: A question on kill stealing.


Hi all,
I've been a roleplayer and a mudder for years now. I've come to EQ like
most of us, with a lot of experience in multiplayer gaming. Now, I'm
fairly new to EQ (level 8) but I thought I had a good understanding of
it, and it's unwritten rules, until now.

The other night I was in BlackBurrow in a small group of 3. Two of us
were at the bottom level, and I was up top, returning from a town run. I
saw a gnoll guardsman kill someone, so I ran up to take charge of it.
There was nobody else around it. After a few hits it fled to the lower
level with me chasing it every step, hacking and kicking. Upon reaching
the lower level, there was a mini train already down there, but both me,
and the members of my group, were working on the gnoll guardsman I had
chased down. Towards the end of the fight, a fighter type of some sort
walked up and took the final swing on the guardsman. Thinking I had done
it ( I had to scroll back to look afterward) I bent over and looted the
corpse.

The fighter started complaining at once that I was a kill stealer and I
took his loot. I explained to him that I had chased the monster down,
and had done the most damage, and therefore the computer LET me take my
kill. He respnded that HE had chased the monster down from the top (I
never even saw anyone up top but myself) and had made the kill therefore
it wasn't mine to take. I didn't feel like the argument was worth it so
I gave up and tried to trade him the loot. He would not accept, but
instead complained to me via tells that he couldn't believe I did that.

My questions are:
1.) Was I ethically wrong to take the loot?
2.) Does the game only allow the majority damage dealer to take the
loot, or the final swinger?
3.) How would you guys deal with this situation?

Thanks for any advice ....
