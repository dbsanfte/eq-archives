Subject: Warriors vs Hybrids


Lets compare the Warrior vs the Hybrid classes (Paladin, Ranger, and
Shadowknight)


How a Warrior should be better than the hybrids:

- Warriors should have a small percentage more hp's that the hybrids. But
hp's should be a primary result of race and stamina. A Wood Elf or Dark Elf
Warrior should have LESS hp's than a Dwarven Paladin.

- Warriors should have a slight melee advantage over hybrids. Apparently
this isn't so.

- Warriors should have one combat skill that is not obtainable by the
hybrids.




How did Verant try to balance the Warrior but failed:

- They gave hybrids an EXP penalty. In theory this should have worked. If
you play a Warrior and a Paladin each for 100 hours, then the Warrior will
be at a higher level than the Paladin and therefore more powerful. This
does work, but most people don't understand or don't care. Plus there are
too many level-based restrictions like who you can group with, what monsters
give you experience.

- Warriors get combat skills at a lower level than hybrids. This does give
Warriors a combat advantage when they have the skill and the hybrids don't.
At levels 1-4, a warrior can kick, while no other hybrid can. At levels
13-16 Warriors can dual wield while no other hybrid can. At levels 15-19,
Warriors can double attack, while no other hybrid can. The same goes for
Parry from 10-16. These earlier skills do give Warriors a combat advantage.
But at level 20, the hybrids catch up with the Warrior in the skills
department. At higher levels, the Warrior has no skill advantage over the
hybrids.

- Warriors have access to the best melee races - Trolls, Ogres, and
Barbarians. The stat bonuses these races have give the Warrior a
significant advantage over weaker races. But the two evil races, Ogres and
Trolls, have significant penalties that cancel out their stat bonus.
Barbarians are a good choice, but some people can't deal without night
vision. But the better race selection does nothing to those who choose to
play a dark elf or wood elf, who probably made a poor race choice for a
warrior.

- Berzerker Frenzy. Not something to depend on. Most of the time we want
to be healed before we get down to the Frenzy level.

- Verant also screwed up by giving Rangers both dual wield and double
attack. Only Warriors should get both skills (I'm not considering Rogues
here, only hybrids). It was good that Paladins and SKs didn't get
dual-wield. Rangers getting both skills was a mistake IMHO.

- Higher Skill Cap. It was always rumored that Warriors didn't have a skill
cap on their combat skills. This lead many of us to believe that a high
level Warrior would have a decent combat advantage over the hybrids.
Apparently this rumor is false, and we have the same combat cap.



What can be done to fix Warriors:

- Warriors should have a higher skill cap, either lvl *6 +5 or lvl *5 +10
instead of the hybrid lvl *5 +5 cap. Warriors should also not be capped at
200 like the hybrids.

- Give Warriors a warrior-only skill. The Monk has several special moves.
Give the Warrior something to use that is a little better than Kick or Bash.

- Give Warriors a once-per-day skill. We need a Berserk or Battle Cry skill
that would give us a significant short-duration combat boost. It shouldn't
be based on how low our hp's are, but instead usable when we want.


What should not be done:

- Warriors don't need anything that makes soloing easier. If anything, the
ability of other classes to solo should be reduced. EQ is a group oriented
game and should remain that way. If you want to play solo, Might & Magic 7
was just released.

- Warriors don't need any magic. We shouldn't have spells that make us heal
or gate or anything. Warriors are as far away from spellcasters as
possible. If you want the ability to cast spells, play a hybrid.



How do we get these changes:

- Don't ask for things totally ridiculous. When people ask for huge
unbalancing changes, it makes them look like they haven't thought it out
very carefully. Verant is much more likely to pay attention to our
suggestions if they are well thought out AND reasonable.

- Once we come up with a reasonable set of changes, get as many Warriors
behind it as we can. Currently we have 100 people asking for 100 different
changes ranging from critical hits to healing to gating to wizard-like
damage.


Warriors only need a few small changes to become the dominate melee class
they should be. There is an excellent article here
http://gameznet.com/eq/warriorsstink.html written by a high level warrior
that is good reading .
