From everything I have seen, you need groups that know how to work as a
group, and with each other. Dragons used to take 3 groups working in waves,
but that seems to have gone up to at least 5 now. One person has to
coordinate the groups, make sure they are all doing what they should be
doing. Say 5 groups, A-E we will call them. Group A takes point and attacks.
Group B should be all buffed up and ready to step in. Group C needs to be
buffed with casters medding up. D and E are reserves at this point, tossing
out heals as needed. Rotate the groups to next position as a group gets beat
down. If everyone goes in at once, I highly doubt anyone will stand a chance
of surviving. Nagafen casts an area affect fear spell that will send
everyone running off into the dungeon, so be prepared to deal with that.

PoF and PoH are completely different. PoF I have heard, stay near the edges
until you all get comfortable there. Once you go over a hill or two into the
meaty areas, it is very easy to get in over your head. Again, mulitple
groups working together is the key here. I would suggest an expedition
leader there, who calls targets and such. All it takes in one person who
decides on his own to pull something to ruin everyone's day. And in these
areas, it doesn't just mean the one death then corpse drag out, it will very
likely include multiple deaths getting your corpses.

These areas were designed such that a single group doesn't stand much of a
chance there. Using ooc or shout is prolly the best way to communicate
(guildchat would be even better, if you can get that many people of the
appropiate level together from your guild).
