Let me tell you how to handle kill stealing. Some of you may not agree with
my method but hey we all choose to handle situations in different ways. I
have a Character that has the spell Fear. When I am playing with my main
character and someone steals my kill: 1st) I ignore it the first time giving
them the benefit of the doubt. 2nd time) I politely ask the individual to
stop. 3rd time) I ask them OOC to stop...again politely. 4th time) If the
individual continues to do so usually by the 4th time he/she has become
rather rude. So......I log off. I log on my Character with the Fear spell.
I find this individual. Each and EVERY time he/she engages and enemy I cast
Fear upon that enemy. The NPC turns and high tales it away. My offender
then has to chase the enemy down if he/she is a warrior or make sure that
the NPC does not get out of the range of their spells if they are a Caster.
After a couple of minutes I let them know who I am and tell them that I will
kindly leave them alone if they leave me alone. I know some of you will
look down upon this method and I fully understand your veiw. This usually
takes care of my problem Kill stealers that I am unfortunate enough to run
across. I even have had one instance Where a fighter continuously stole
kills from me after he had agreed not to do so again. So I logged my Fear
character back on and followed this poor sap for 2 hours and watched him
chase down each and EVERY kill that he got. I usually play for about 12
stints so when I play I have plenty of time to do this if the need arises.
He finally emoted his character begging me on bended knee to leave him
alone. Revenge DOES work sometimes even if it is not my preferred method of
handling a problem.
