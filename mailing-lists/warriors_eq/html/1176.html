My argument are based on real life, not fantasy. You still have not
addressed the biggest point. Whether our head is cut off, or you get fried
by a lightning strike, your just as dead.

Now, let's talk fantasy, in most fantasy novels the mighty wizard isn't
sitting around frying one creature, he's unleashed on an army of critters.
This is where the mage should shine, his ability to strike at a distance at
many targets, doing significant damage. A fireball should hot everything in
an area, not just one creature. The warrior should be able, at high levels
to work his way thru an army, one opponent at a time. But, when that blade
slices and dices, the end effect is the same as their spell, bad for whoever
is one the receiving end.

Why play a wizard, because no matter who good a warrior is, he can be
overrun by alot of opponents, the Wizard can attack these mobs of creatures,
soften them up, or cause them to run, buying the warrior time.

And what of the fantasy of the mighty Knight slaying the dragon with Lance
and sword? Can't happen here can it?

What you are arguing for is an artificial set of rules in the name of game
balance. I say that there are many ways to balance out the different
classes, while preserving both the realistic and fantasy feel of Norrath.

By the way, my opinion may differ from yours, but it is not wrong. That
statement is just darn impolite and provocative, and it goes to hinder
intellectual debate, rather then encourage the free exchange of ideas. I
don't think those kind of statements have a place here, but that is just my
opinion.
