Being an Agnostic DE warrior (I also tested it as the warlord), here is my
KOS list.


> Freeport - Neutral, come and go as I please (no buying/selling).
> Dwarf city - (KOS)
> Wood elf tree houses - WHY would you even WANT to go here :) (KOS)
> High elf city - KOS
> Gnome city - Neutral, come and go as i please (no buying/selling).
> High Hold - Neutral, come and go as i please (I can buy/sell).
> eurodite city - Neutral, come and go as i please (I can buy/sell).
Qeynos - Neutral, come and go as i please (One vendor to buy/sell).
Halas - KOS

The ONLY way to raise Indigo brotherhood faction is to kill elves. The only
realistic targets (since Wood Elf guards are very high level) are the elves
on the Island of Erollsi. If you raise Indigo you lower Wood/High elf and
vice a versa. These two sides are mutually exclusive.



> Dungeons:
> Najena - easy enough, kinda tough for you "good" people to get to because
> you got to run thru Nektulos with wondering guards everywhere.....and to
> my knowledge there is no way to raise your faction standing with DEs other
> than to ruin your faction standing with halflings.
> Solsek Eye - easy enough for darks, same story as najena for you 'good'
> types
> Befallen - nutral teritory
> Runnyeye - depending on which direction you are coming from.....you might
> have to stop in HH and kill some orcs, then run the long way thru HH into
> the karanas, back into beholders maze, then whala, or do like me and just
> run thru munchkin town
> Guk - in troll land, pretty easy to get to for "good" people but nowhere
> close for non casters to be bound, and nowhere close for "good" people to
> sell, very easy for us evil types with Troll town just around the
> corner....again, no way for "good" people to raise their faction with
> Trolls
> Cazic Thule - same as Guk, good types have nowhere to bind and nowhere to
> sell, evil has it easy....not to mention the entrance of Cazic is patroled
> by an ogre guard.....again, no way for "good" people to raise their
> faction with Ogres
> Crushbone - well, we dark elves have a pact with the Crushbone orcs, so we
> take a faction hit with the dark elf warrior guild for hunting
> there....but only 2 magic items worth getting from there anyways, not to
> mention its way crouded and a long ways away from any "evil" starting
> town, so by the time you get there you are probably to high lvl to fight
> there....see foot note
> Mistmoore - nutral ground, a little tricky for us darks to get to, but not
> too bad, and we can be bound anywhere in greater feydark.....see foot note
> Unrest - nutral ground, a little tricky for us darks to get to....see foot
> note
>
> footnote: even tho we can be bound in close proxcimity to Crushbone,
> Mistmoore, and Unrest, there is nowhere on that entire island for us to
> sell or bank......however, by the time you are ready to fight in mistmoore
> you are probably going to have a high lvl wiz, who can group gate you back
> to nektulos for banking/selling needs, or since there are sooooo many
> "good" people compared to "evil" people, you are probably going to have a
> "good" person in your group who can go to town and sell for you. And
> again, there is no real reason for us to need to fight in Crushbone or
> Unrest (ogres and trolls can't wear the small bronze, and us small dark
> elves don't have the raw strenght to wear bronze due to its weight)
>
> Also, everybody seems to forget the one big advantage of being evil.....we
> can kill any NPCs we want with out having to worry about faction hits,
> except either in HH or Rivervale (easiest connections to the Western part
> of the world. You good types can only kill Nektulos guards (all troll and
> ogre guards are 50+) And some NPCs have some pretty good loot.
>
> So to sum up.....evil isn't any harder to play than good, but it does
