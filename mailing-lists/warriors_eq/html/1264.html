> From the Journals of Angarth, Wolf-Warrior of the Anstruther Clan
>
> Monday, 19th of February, in the Year 3174
>
> They come. I hear laughter mingled with clanking of bones coming from
> behind me. The small figure, the one without the robes, points his staff.
> A green nimbus outlines his diminutive form while the tall, robed figure
> raises his hands and starts chanting in an arcane language I do not
> understand. White, hot energy explodes from his outstretched fingers,
> striking something behind me the same moment a green luminescence
> surrounds my throbbing body. Charred pieces of bone strike my armored
> back while the green light slowly fades, taking with it the nauseous
> feeling in my stomach.
>
> My strength returns almost immediately after the ill effects of the
> infectious cloud spell leaves my system. I rise, looking from the tall
> wizard - I am sure he is a wizard - to the two small figures at his side.
> Something tugs at my memory when I look at the wizard again, only this
> time I smile in realization. It is my old friend Maurek! With him is a
> halfling druid by the name if Hikken and gnome necromancer known as
> Balbus. Two other figures walk up behind him: Sigmund, a Paladin, and a
> powerful cleric named Geleb. The druid Hikken raises his staff again and
> points it at me. A globe of energy surrounds my body, healing my wounds.
> Blood stops flowing as the flesh knits together, leaving me whole and
> refreshed.
>
> After introductions are made, the band of adventurers asks me to join
> their ranks. I readily agree. I am suspicious of the necromancer, but I
> trust the judgement of the Paladin - he would not ally himself to one that
> was evil, would he? I do make a mental note to watch the gnome, however.
>
> We have decided to venture further into the tomb. Sadistic moaning and
> haunting screams can be heard rising from the depths, calling us to
> battle. We make our way to a stairwell that has been decimated. At the
> bottom of the stairs is a locked door, with another evil necromancer
> standing guard with her pet. Another skeleton stands nearby, almost the
> same size as the necromancer's pet but tinged in a blue light. I am told
> by the cleric that it is an ice-boned skeleton, no doubt the macabre
> remains of a beginner of the arcane arts. We devise a battle plan that
> sends me down the stairwell first to engage the ice-boned. I quickly
> learn that I will need to equip my magical combine longsword to hit the
> creature. The battle is short and swift, with no one getting seriously
> wounded. We rest to recover from the conflict, then proceed through the
> locked door (my fellow adventures had already retrieved a key for the door
> from another shadow knight.)
>
> I stand in front as the wizard unlocks the door and pulls it open slowly.
> Immediately a tall, thin skeleton rushes me, its sword wailing at me
> recklessly, a fire burning behind its empty sockets. "Elf skeleton!",
> someone shouts, and the battle rages. My first few strikes are deflected
> quite easily by the monstrosity, with its return attack sliding between
> the joints of my banded armor, causing severe pain with each deadly cut.
> The spell wielders are behind throwing their arsenal at the thing.
> Flashes of fire and lightening fill the chamber, and soon the elf skeleton
> lay in a heap of bones. Not without cost, however. I am covered with
> cuts and nicks, mostly in the places where my armor did not conceal my
> entire body. I fell the clerics hands touch me and feel the healing
> warmth flow into my blood stream, my wounds closing with the passage of
> the clerical magic running through my body.
>
> We walk into the chamber that was guarded by the undead elf. Ghastly
> hooks hang from the ceiling, covered in blood and gore. The upper torso
> of a torn human man hangs from another hook. His eyes are still open, a
> look of terror etched into his face. The stench is unbearable, but we
> proceed down the hallway.
>
> Ahead at the end of the tunnel a giant skeleton stands. I speculate that
> it is the remains of someone of my race, for it stands as tall as a
> barbarian, its bones thick and large. We are not close enough for it to
> sense us, so I cautiously move ahead, scouting out the area. A doorway
> leads to the left. As I approach the door, it bursts open, a skeleton
> rushing out with bony hands extended. He rakes the side of my face, its
> wicked talons leaving razor sharp cuts that start bleeding profusely. A
> necromancer follows the skeleton from the room, eyes mad with vileness as
> he unleashes his spells. I turn and sprint towards the rest of the group
> who are still waiting in the undead elf chamber while the skeletal pet
> continues to strike me in my back. The armor I have on blocks most of the
> blows, but it still wounds me. I feel the magic from the necromancer hit
> me with the same decaying cloud that was almost my demise earlier, but I
> concentrate and resist the spell! By this time my comrades see what is
> happening and unleash a magical assault that leaves the necromancer a
> smoldering corpse.
>
> We decide to regroup in the chamber, hopeful that no other malignant
> forces will find us while we prepare for continued exploration of the
> tomb. After the successful regeneration of our magical ability and
> everyone is fully rested, we proceed down the tunnel. I pause in my
> writing here, to resume as further events unfold.
>
> Angarth, Warrior of the 17th Tier
> "Periissem ni periisem"
