Rangers are the only half breed that I'm even close to being impressed with.

SK blow, their offensive spells are so far behind that they are almost pointless, they don't have the melee ability of a true warrior either, the ONLY thing they have going for them is the occasional harm touch, which they never seem to have when needed.

Paladin.....they ARE a joke, their healing ability is a joke, their buffing ability is a joke, because their heals and buffs are 9 levels behind, the only thing they have going for them is their lay hands, which can be very good, but I dont' think it makes up for their lack of melee ability.

Rangers on the other hand, well, they get some OK buffs, nothing great, their thorn series of spells is good for a tank, their heals are a joke tho, was fighting with one last night, he was close to 30th lvl, and his heal was doing 25 points.....with close to 1000 hp, a 25 point heal?? but they do get dual wield, and kick, which no other half breed get, and of couse double attack. As a wood elf, you get hide, forage, and tracking which are 3 very nice skills to have

If I were going to play a half breed it would be ranger. But no class can match the raw tanking ability of a true warrior.

This past week I decided to take a break from my level 15 dark elf warrior. I was abit frustrated.

I started a half-elf warrior in Kelethin. It is insanely easy to play there. I made level 5 in about 3.5 hours. Then I stopped and tried a wood elf ranger. Has anyone else played a ranger? I imagine it is the most poorly balanced of all the hybrids. After 4 hours I reached level 5 and got kick. I am now 12-18 hours into the character and almost level 9. I am just as capable as an equivalent warrior. Next level I gain an array of spells which will boost my combat effectiveness and allow me to heal. Part of the easy success is the plethora of low level orcs available a stone's throw from Kelethin. With my tracking ability I am never at a loss for targets and I can engage blue con orcs in rapid succession or even 2 at a time. I can even engage and successfully beat yellow con orcs more than 50% of the time and almost always can kill orcs that con even to me without trouble. So far it seems that I get as many hit points now as my warrior did.

My point is, with a ranger class as powerful a combatant as it is I see little necessity for the pure warrior. I am going to play through to level 15 I think, just to see a more even comparison. My concern is only from a game mechanics perspective though as I have no doubt my playing as a dark elf was a much more rewarding experience. Has anyone else noticed that the warrior just is not different enough from the hybrid fighter classes?
