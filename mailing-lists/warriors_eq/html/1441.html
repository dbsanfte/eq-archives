--- Dan Leavy <dan@...> wrote:
> But im getting off my question here...if solo,
> should you take out the hardest crit first or
> weakest?

In multiple mob situations, kill in the order of
fastest-to-kill to longest-to-kill. This assumes the
multiple mobs (defined as those which are extra, that
you're not designating as your primary target) are not
of a threat level insufficient to cause any sort of
problem.

The logic is simple; test it yourself. If you target
the 'most dangerous' (and usually the longest-to-kill)
mob first, you will have the 2nd, 3rd, etc... mobs
still around during the time you kill the
longest-to-kill, then the next-longest-to-kill, and so
forth. During all of this time they're dealing damage
to you. Kill in order of shortest-to-kill, to get rid
of the damage they'll deal to you, enabling you to
more quickly concentrate the battle.

Of course, a lot of rules change if you have
sufficient spellcasting backup to flat out nuke-kill
(in an extremely short time) a few of the multiple
mobs. Since I'm a tank, and play from a tank
perspective, that's just an oddity situation; plan for
the tank and be pleasantly surprised if spellcasting
helps. Much less likely to get into a jam that way.
