Warriors Suck....

...but not for the reasons you may think. Warriors have their place in
Everquest, but I do not play one.


How to play a Warrior:
1. Hot key Kick/Bash
Everyone should know about this one.

2. Hot key Assist
It is so important for everyone to attack the same monster. Eliminating a
damage source helps so much.

3. Hot key Taunt
This is vitally important for getting monsters off of spellcasters or other
weakened tanks.

That's about all there is to playing a Warrior. Once you master Taunt and
Assist you have become the best Warrior you will be. Battles are about
targeting the correct opponent (targeting spellcasters first, using
/assist...) and keeping critters off the spellcasters (Taunt).

I'd like to be a better player than that. I want to be able to do something
that saves the entire party. I want people to remember me and want my
skills in the group. As a Warrior, I can be known as a great Taunter, one
who keeps the spellcasters free to cast. That's about it.


I play a Cleric mainly now. Every battle consists of many decisions that
may affect the outcome.

The first decision happens when the puller returns. Did he bring too many?
Should I Root one? Or should I Fear one of the critters away? Is it
important for me to start casting Direct Damage (on a spellcaster for
example) or should I conserve mana for Heals?

Next I will normally assist one of the tanks for a few rounds. It is my job
to monitor everyone's HP and heal them up when needed. If I heal too much
too soon, I will draw all the critters onto me. So I must carefully judge
when and how much to heal. There is also a choice of who to heal. Should I
heal Tank-A who is it half health but only has one critter on him, or should
I heal Tank-B who is at three-quarters health but has three critters on him?
Maybe I should wait a few rounds or do I need to heal right now?

As soon as I have used some mana, I have another choice. Should I meditate
right away or should I continue swinging my staff? Will I need mana for an
extra heal or should I do some immediate damage with combat?

Then I have to deal with runners. Should I Root a runner so a tank can
finish him off or do I need to finish him myself with Direct Damage? Can I
afford the mana for this or should I chase the runner down myself to finish
him off with combat?

Also I must decide when to tank. Clerics can wear good armor and have
decent HPs. I might need to tank a few rounds to save a Wizard or give a
Warrior a break.

After a big battle where I made several important decisions that saved the
day for us I realize how important those choices I had to make were. Having
learned to be an effective Cleric made a big difference.

Then I think about it from the Warriors point of view. The Warrior did
Kick, Kick, Kick, Taunt, Kick, Kick, Kick, Taunt, the cleric is getting
attacked, switch targets, Kick, Kick, Kick, Taunt, Kick, Kick, Kick, Taunt,
runner rooted by cleric, chase runner down, switch targets, Kick, Kick,
Kick, Taunt.

Playing a Warrior doesn't compare to a Cleric. As a Warrior, I feel so
useless. If the battle is going bad, there is nothing I can do but hope the
Random Number Generator starts falling our way. All I can do is soak up
damage like a sponge, do more combat damage than everyone else and Taunt
critters off the spellcasters.

A Warrior can learn everything there is to know about his class in one day.
Gaining new levels never changes the strategy a Warrior. Sure they get dual
wield and double attack, but this only increased damage and does nothing to
add new combat choices.

A Cleric takes a long time to master. I feel there is much more for me to
learn as a Cleric. And every 5 levels I get a new set of spells that need
to learn to use. I'm sure I'll be learning new tricks all the way up to
level 50 (if I ever get that far).


Don't misunderstand me. Tanks are a very needed part of a party.
Spellcasters need Warriors up front to take the blows and deal out the
damage. Warriors are just not my style. The limited options during combat
are not what I am looking for. For many of you, the Warrior is perfect.
Being able to stand toe to toe with a critter and doing damage with a sword
is exactly what many people want.

Verant's improvements:
Bezerker Frenzy - happens automatically when low on HP
More HPs - Lets us take more damage
More Magic Resistance - Better against spellcasters
Critical Hits - Randomly more damage

These improvements do make Warriors a better class. But nothing there makes
me want to play them. I would consider playing a Warrior if there were more
active skills. I need a skill that I can choose when to use it, so that it
may make a difference in combat.

I've suggested in the past a Battle Cry skill. This skill, usable once per
day, would increase the AC and Attack Speed of everyone in the party for 30
seconds.

Another option would be Fighting Styles. Give us an Offensive fighting
style. This would increase damage or Attacking plus a taunt bonus while
sacrificing AC and Defense. And we would also want a Defensive fighting
style. This would boost AC, dodge, and defense with a sacrifice to
Attacking and damage. It would be very cool if these Fighting Styles could
only be learned through a quest designed for around levels 15-20.


Give Warriors something to think about in combat. Make it so that
experienced, skillful Warrior can be better than any newbie Warrior. Give
them some abilities that would take skill to use effectively. Knowing when
to switch Fighting Styles or use Battle Cry would be something an
experienced Warrior could learn to do well.
