> I have done Upper Guk at level 13. If you have a strong group it is
> possible...but beware of other parties of solo players. Once you cross
> the
> bridge you are in a "hub" room. Fleeing creatures will run into a room
> populated with Tuk warriors and Gaz warriors. If you do not follow them
> they will come back and sometimes bring these warriors out. I've died in
> Upper Guk a few times due to the follies of others in the dungeon. If a
> PC
> or froglock blocks the bridge there is no escape from the trains. You will
> die deeper inside than you would prefer. The upside is that most
> froglocks
> do not KOS until you killed a couple dozen and your faction with them is
> bad. So you can often walk right down and pick up your stuff.
>
> The most dangerous part is the water under the bridge. Some of the trains
> will occassionally drop Ton warriors and Gaz warriors into the water. I
> almost always see one or more there. If you fall off the bridge into the
> water you will very likely die unless you are a good swimmer. I'd advise
> using a mage to draw anything out of the water and kill it before you
> start
> hunting across the bridge. And remember don't panic! Falling in the
> water
> is only a death sentence if you panic. If you can't solo whatever is in
> there just swim out and get to dry ground before you die. It is much
> easier
> to get to your corpse
> if it isn't in the water.
>
> Also, if you see anything fleeing from the entrance over the bridge while
> you are hunting on the otherside...kill it. Doesn't matter if it is yours
> or not, if you don't take the kill it may bring back a train.
>
> Hmm....looking back that all kind of rambles. It will make more sense
> when
> you actually get to Guk and look around.
