I am sorry to say but I agree with Verant's decision on this point and I
also submitted feedback on it before it was implemented.

These items are supposed to be somewhat unique. Now that doesn't entirely
work in an online RPG, but Verant's solution is the next best thing. Any
quest or unique item should not be allowed to be possessed in multiple
quantities by a single player. This way if someone wants to camp the BBC
because they have it and found out how valuable it is, they can. But they
must sell theirs before getting another. It simply removes the
"profitability" for camping items from some players. I think the dwarven
tunic should be a lore item as well as the dwarven axes. I have just come
from there and people I saw camping these items day after day. Now I am in
the commonlands or North Ro and these same people are selling multiples of
those items. They built and inventory of unique items and are now selling
them. 2 of those people were warriors and not mages, which saddens me.

I don't think dual PGTs should be allowed any more than a ranger could have
dual Ebony Blades or dual Oaken Scimitars. I don't particularly like the
whole economy of unique magic weapons and items either. Making them lore
items and ineligble for trading and selling will go along way to reducing
camping and letting people get these items on their own.

The only reason some of these items are so expensive is because high level
people will simply camp these easy to kill NPCs and prevent others from
obtaining them. I watched one group of mages in Crushbone who would watch
for escaping mobs like Crush, the warlord, darish, royal guard, etc. and
would cast invis on it. They either knew where the mobs flee to or they
were using some magic to see invisible. But they would wait till the mob
regenerated more than half its hit points and then kill it. They stole
these kills in the most dishonorable way for pure profit.

Ok, now I am ranting and will get off my soapbox. But I do feel pretty
strongly about this topic.

Bael
