Been thinking that the warrior class needs a "critical hit" skill. Being the superior combatant that I am, and after all the battles against so many differing kinds of opponents I have learned how and where to hit creatures to do the most damage.

Instead of slashing randomly at that hill giant I want to deftly stick my blade in where it will do the most damage. I think that having a skill like this that causes double damage on a successful skill check would go great lengths to improving our class.

Comments? This is something that I think Verant might listen to. THe skill could be implemented around level 18 or so. This is about the time I think when warriors suddenly become the damage-absorbing portion of a party and less of a damage causing part. I don't know yet because I am level 16, but I am finding that I am resorting to large numbers of attacks to do damage, because with fewer attacks I don't hit often enough to really solo anything but really small blues.

Currently I am using a tentacle whip as primary and combine scimitar as secondary.

For reference. You may recall that I had started a ranger about 3 weeks ago. I have been playing with that character more than my warrior because I wanted to compare capabilities at the same level. My ranger uses a 2 handed dwarven axe (great weapon by the way, 14 damage 43 delay), and sometimes a 1 handed dwarven axe. He is at level 15. I can solo any blue, many yellows and i have even soloed a few red orc slavers. In comparison my warrior, level 16 is beaten to the point of 10 minute resting after a fight by most blues. He runs from anything that is yellow or red.

In a group the ranger meets all the same party needs as a warrior plus he provides his own healing and buffing. My 16th level warrior is about to get a fine steel 2 handed sword and work up his 2hs skill to a comparable level as the ranger. We will see how that goes.
