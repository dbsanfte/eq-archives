Uh ... ok. If you say so. I call Paladins 'self-filling tanks',
because they are just that. They're not really effective until they
get Meditate at 12th, but once there they can do a solo fight that
drags them down to 1 bar of HP; and be back in another fight in 7-10
mins. For a corresponding warrior, it would be 13-18 mins, or more.

Lay On Hands is the Panic Button; you can push it to get out of jail,
or at least, have more time to either finish off the mob or run for
safety.

As for the spells being 9 levels behind, so? Paladins are
fighter/healers, while Clerics are healer/fighters. Clerics can be
mini-tanks up until about 9th or 10th; but Paladins can tank all the
way to 50. The ability to tank and still have a reserve to compliment
the party's cleric is useful. Past 10th, Clerics are 80%+ casters for
all realistic intents and purposes.

Paladins don't get Kick; but at 6th they get bash which I personally
like much better. Kick won't interrupt casting, while Bash will. I
stopped using Kick with my Troll Warrior when I got Bash (which let my
Slam start being much more effective)

If you're fixated on Dual Wield (which I think you might be, since you
mentioned Rangers; and they're the only hybrid, that I recall, that
gets Dual Wield); that might explain why you like Warriors and Rangers
the most.

Personally, I think the jury's still out on Dual Wield, and on melee in
general. The further up you go, the more the offensive and debuff
spellcasters really carry the fight to the mobs; the tanks are just
there to do what their name says. If this is proven out, then Dueal
Wield or no, the tanks are just there to soak damage. If this is not
proven, then Dual Wield still has to debate against the endless fighter
arguements of Dual Wield vs 2H Weapons (which is one of those semantic
arguements, IMO; stats bear each out)

For grouping, I'd prefer to have pure tank in the tank slot, any day of
the week; with a Paladin fighting alongside him. (my ideal 6man group
is 2 Warriors, 1 Paladin, 1 Cleric, 1 Wizard, 1 Bard; haven't assembled
it yet, but it sounds fun!) For soloing, however, the Paladin is more
efficient than the Warrior.
