Subject: Re: Warriors vs Hybrids


I have enjoyed this discussion so far. I don't have much of an issue with
warriors vs wizards. They traded hps, ac, and melee ability for high damage
dealing spells. However it appears I agree with many of you that is is the
hybrids that appear to have a distinct advantage over us. The hybrid that has
the most advantage appears to be the ranger. For various reasons I find I am
unable to group with the people I normally play with. I am unable to solo many
mobs even in the blue and green range, and really I have more fun in groups. I
have been traveling to many zones shouting that I am looking for a group, but to
no avail. It appears to me that many groups are prefering rangers over warriors,
and after I read the post on EQ'Lizer I began to realize why.

Advantages Warriors get over Rangers:

- We get Dual Wield and Double Attack at 2 levels lower than Rangers, but after
a few levels they are back on par with us in the damage dealing department. So
as Wayne Sheppard wrote, "the Warrior has no skill advantage over the hybrids."

-Warriors get more hit points. This only appears to be true for the larger races
and rangers easily overcome this by being able to heal themselves, thus actually
taking more pressure off of the cleric to heal them.

-Berserker Frenzy. As it has been stated before, I see no real advantage to
getting a strength boost and a dex penalty when I am close to dying. I would
rather not get hit more often when I am low on hp. It makes us die that much
quicker.

Advantages Rangers have over Warriors:

-Rangers get foraging. Not much of advantage in combat, but they can help keep
the party supplied with food without any mana cost. They can also carry more
loot since they don't have to carry lots of food. They just forage on demand.

-Rangers get Hiding and Sneak. This allows the rangers to live longer because
they can avoid more of the deadlier Mobs that would target a warrior.

-Rangers get the very useful skill of Tracking. This is a great advantage to
groups because the rangers make the best fetches. They party can heal up and
rest adn stay organized while the rangers are out searching for prey. This means
the party spends less down time and can fight more.

-Rangers cast spells. They not only get some minor healing spells, but also are
able to do root spells, direct damage spells, so they can kick, attack, and cast
spells doing more damage than us. They also get spells that are useful to the
entire party like camoflage and SOW allowing the party to travel more safely.


Changes:

- I like the idea of eliminating the skill cap of 200 and giving us a bonus in
combat related skills. I think it was 5*lvl +10 instead of 5*lvl +5. Or they
could eliminate the bonus the hybrids get, so they only get 5*lvl. This would
allow a warrior of equal level to show a slight advantage over our hybrid
brothers.

- To make it more apparent that warriors excell at combat I think rangers should
not get Double Attack. After all Verant stated that they hybrids gave up
something to get those spells and extra abilities. I don't see a problem with
Dual Wield because many of us grew up with D&D's image of rangers wielding two
swords. If paladins appear to be struggling at later levels they might get
Double Attack to allow them to keep up with the rangers.

-It's been stated that at higher levels that the casters are doing so much
damage that we cannot Taunt the Mobs off of them. So I think that Taunt should
be boosted at higher levels causing the Mobs to pay more attention to us and do
our job of keeping thier attention while the caster burn them.

-I also really like the critical hit skill. It should be innate like Parry and
Dodge. It would help us more of an asset to a party instead of a spell casting
warrior that can track like the rangers are now. They just seem to have many of
the same advantages we have and sacrificed nothing to get thier spells and
tracking and other abilites.

My 2cp worth,
Surtur, 16th level Sponge.
