> I have to disagree here. As a player who runs both a warrior (level 8)
> and a cleric (level 12) I see advantages in both classes. First and
> foremost, there are a LOT of critters that resist root. And if your
> group is targeting creatures above the spell cap (ie, 6 levels or more
> above you) they will automatically resist root or ensnare or whatever
> spell you are using. And when root fails, you are praying for a tank to
> take some of the abuse else you are toast.
>
> For those sports fans, a football analogy I think works best. Warriors,
> Paladins, and Monks (and perhaps some of the other hybrid melee classes
> but I prefer to use the 3 mentioned) are like offensive lineman. They
> are engaging the creature to let the other offensive players do their
> work. A caster can't cast if he's being pounded on, plain and simple.
> *Sometimes* channel MIGHT let you get a spell of but more often than
> not they need to be disengaged. So the tank is critical to
> accomplishing this. The benefits are two-fold. First the tank is doing
> some decent damage on his own, keeping the critters attention and the
> casters are unleashing their power trying to get the guy down in the
> most efficient manner possible.
>
> The point being, all of these classes have value in a group. Group
> being the operative word. Warriors are designed to be able to handle a
> great deal of abuse in a melee. I seriously doubt any high level
> players will tell you that tanks are of no value. Without them, the
> rest of the members of the group have a much harder time doing their
> job.
>
> And the point about the cleric being able to take on whites vs blue I
> find to be absolutely false. Clerics don't have the offensive power or
> mana to play the root n shoot game. *Maybe* against some undead but
> again root is an unstable spell. Maybe my opinion on that will change
> once I advance in levels but that's how I see it for now.
>
