My solution for these magical items is to have them given out
only as a reward for a certain quest. Of couse that means the
quests would have to be very difficult and actually work :) When
I was designing an RPG along, long, long time ago, my co-designer
and myself came up with a way to make sure that someone didn't
complete the quest twice. There is a boolean flag for each quest
attached to the character with a true or false vale. The true meant
that you completed the quest. After you completed the quest then
you were flagged and couldn't get the item again. But what if you
wanted to get one for a friend? Well this could be accomplished in
EQ with the party leader. Whoever was the inviter would be
considered the party leader and would get the item upon succesful
completion of the quest. Then your friend would invite and become
the party leader and could do the quest again, getting the same
item. There could be alot more checks added to see if the quest
was in progress, how many characters in the party has the same
quest in progress, etc, etc. Killing something to get a great
weapon just doesn't seem appropriate.
