Two things :

Kill any crushbone orc (even in Greater Faydark) and you'll loose faction
towards the Indigo Brotherhood.

Warriors I guess are not meant to be the most powerful at higher levels. If
you try to play an hybrid, you'll see that warrior always are better than
them, and level faster, before they get most of their spells, their
warrior's skills and items. Then they are rewarded for their patience.
I mean, paladins, rangers and shadow knights have to start with one attack
only, like the non-melee class and only have more hitpoints than these class
(still less than warriors). They get all their warriors skill 4-6 levels
later than warriors and it IS a big deal, at early levels, which are longer
than the warriors ones (XP penalty). Believe me it's very hard to see those
warriors wielding two weapons when you can't, just because you still have 4
levels to go, or because you will NEVER be able two (dual wield is so cool).
Now I know that warriors get in this depression when they see hybrids
getting better than them but it has to be this way. No warrior should be
each time better than a paladin wearing full plate and wielding a holy
sword, with lay hands and spells (the paladin should be IMHO). They pay a
high price before getting this far and I bet many decide to stop because
they are just underpowered at low levels. Their lay hands save them.
Tracking is the skill that make rangers valuable. And shadow knights, well,
they get the most screwed, power wise.
Hybrids are also played because of roleplay but they have to get this edge
on warriors on later levels. And I think Verrant wanted this to be. Now I
agree that once a warriors got all his skills early and only look for some
items from here to there, life is not as fun as it can be for a ranger...
Speaking about it I think that warriors should have WAY more items to seek,
and maybe not only weapons. On the other hand, there might be more to
discover yet. ;-)
While I'm at it (getting long) I think that monks are so overpowered it's
not even funny. They can't wear armor but they don't need it as they get it
naturally, from levels, and even if it's lower than warrior's they also get
block. They get their fighting powers before warriors (dual wield from
start, grrr) and have these awesome special attacks. Ok the class is very
specialised (no way I'll play as a monk), ok it gets very boring at high
levels (yeah, more than warriors IMO) but hey, they are the masters of
combat, no contest.

One last comment : I don't think that warrior should have a higher minimum
damage, they deal 1 damage when their attack is too low against the
opponents defense or when they fail their throw, no one wants a warriors to
deal 20 or so damage against a red mob, each time (not that it matters at
high levels, but it does early on). But yes, they can use an upgrade, and
one which would not unbalance the game, I see it mainly in many items, but
also in some skills improvement, maybe (bind wounds, etc.). Also I think
that attack and defense are caped at (level+1)x5 for warriors and they are
at levelx5 for the other class. I know some of you are gonna say "oh yeah,
like 5 points are gonna make a big difference", it doesn't make a big
difference on each hit, but this is permanent, at all levels, and adds up to
great benefits.
