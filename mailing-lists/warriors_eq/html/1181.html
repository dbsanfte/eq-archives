--- Tom Swift <tswift@...> wrote:
> The warrior excels in a full party with a cleric.
> Try it and you will see. 3 and 4 person parties
> aren't the same, because you will often be the
> primary target of high level mobs and take alot of
> damage. But a full party with 2 warriors and 1
> cleric plus other assorted baggage is a group that
> can take much higher level mobs without losing
> members.

Right on the money; here's a very graphic example of
this.

In Unrest, grouped (16s-19s) with three tanks, two
clerics and a wizard, we cleaned up *anything* we
could pull from the first two floors of the house (up
to and including Carrion Ghouls and Skeleton Monks,
and the occasional Barbed Bone Skeleton - all red to
all of us). Grouped (16s-19s) with only 1 tank (me),
two clerics, two wizards and one shaman, we could
*not* take Skeleton Monks or Barbed Bone Skeletons
because the Wizards were dead weight then (resists),
the Clerics didn't have enough healing to keep me up,
and I didn't have the firepower alone to take them
down before everyone was OOM and it was still at half
life, laughing at us.

Those who think they can hunt without tanks are right,
if they're willing to *only* hunt blues and whites.
If they want to hunt 'up level' from themselves, in
yellows and reds, they have to have tanks.

My prefered party always has three tanks, period. My
most fun ripping and shredding party would be four
tanks and two clerics (but my friend's a wizard, and I
can't just throw him away like that). But with three
tanks and a pair of clerics, you can use the 6th slot
as a wildcard to fill with a bard or wizard or
whatever. Enchanters go especially well, their
debuffs make fights a lot easier.
