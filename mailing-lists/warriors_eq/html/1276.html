I've grouped with twinked warriors like that (I am 26, he was 23). Full
small bronze, demonclaw, granite tommy, and thick leather apron (I
understand small bronze is easier to get than large bronze). Want to know
something? He though he was in invulnerable. He couldn't play a warrior at
all (before I grabbed pulling duties from him he did a pull while all the
casters and the healer were OOM). Tanking is an art form. That's why these
high level wizards have to get good stuff for their own or a friend's
warrior. People don't know how to play warriors. If you want to take out
one monster, yes a warrior is not the way to go. A shaman buddy who is the
same level as me can kite an aviak avocet (level 28 or 29). This takes him
about 40 minutes and is in his words, boring. I wouyld prolly last oh maybe
30 seconds and if lucky have the avocet down a quarter health before I was
dead. I have seen very few good items come off a solo monster, and every
good item I have gotten has come off something red to me with a blue or two
in the area. Why do I think tanking is an art form? The people in your
party have to trust you, especially healers. Once that healer starts
casting on you, the monsters are going to go to him/her. If all they are
doing is healing, pulling them off with a few whacks of your weapon and
three or four taunts is not that hard. Mages cannot go all out without
rooting first, and if that root fails early or gets resisted, watch him
run. There is no way a 28 or even a 32 wizard could take out the groups two
26 th level tanks and a 26 th level shaman can take out. We take 3 lizard
defenders out of the maze in cazic thule and are all beat down maybe half
at the end of the battle (regen has become my most favorite buff, even
above SoW). A 26th level wizard tries one defender, whoops, a little resist
or a fizzle, and there we are saving his behind. A guildmate was with a
group (17-20) hunting a SBH and along comes a 32nd level wizard (from a big
time power levelling guild on our server). This guy says I get the SBH when
it spawns, I need it for my paladin, or you guys won't get anything. If I
had been there. I woulda said ok, lets take a 20 minute break from hunting
and see how much spawns and whether or not he has to run. When some high
level wizard comes in and starts to kill steal for a rare item, don't tank
for him. Back off and watch him die. It's almost as much fun as watching
dwarf paladins die (one combo I have seen over and over again solo and
fail). Again I don't know about over level 35, but from around 16 or 17 on
to where I am now at 26, tanks are needed. I see calls often in CT 'looking
for a 30ish tank for throne room'. Why do they need that? Multiple
gorrillas that need taking out that the wizards just can't do (the wizards
are 32 or so and the gorrillas around 25). My recomendations for good
groups in that range is 2 tanks (hybrids are ok), one bard or rogue (they
both bring unigue skills and tactics with), a shaman (yeah clerics heal
more, but the buffs from shamans are so great for melee), and a wizard or
an enchanter. If you haven't hunted with an enchanter over level 20, you
have no idea what you are missing. Pull multiple blues with an enchanter of
that level with you, and instantly (one spell), you are fighting one less.
Then when the rest are dead, go back and finish off the one that the
enchanter took care of (it ain't dead, but it won't hurt you). In groups
similar to these I made the full level from cracking 25 and getting riposte
to 26 in under 12 hours of playing time, and even more important, didn't
die once in that span of time in Cazic-Thule.
