Anti-Camping Update
EverQuest Producer Brad McQuaid recently posted an
update to the Anti-Camping situation on our General
Board. Here's what he had to say in this update:

We've decided what we're going to do, at least
short term, about some of the camping problems.

First, though, I want to thank everyone for all
the feedback and ideas -- I read through all the
posts and emails that I received and the vast
majority were not only civil but well thought out.

I've decided, at least at this point, to not
pursue the no-item load idea. I think perhaps such as
drastic change this far into release would cause
more confusion and even harm than it might
solve. I didn't do a scientific analysis, but it
seemed that at least half of the people posted,
perhaps more, were against the no-item load
idea. Perhaps if we'd implemented it in beta it
would have worked better... but as I've posted
in the recent past, we are often at a point now
where changes to solve or address one problem
create other problems. This is true both
because EverQuest is a very large and complex
game, and also because people tend to prefer
the status quo, even though often they will
complain about it.

What many of you do seem to want is something
we've been slowly implementing, mostly with
newer items, as of late. These are the 'lore'
items, of which you can only possess one at a time.
Our first plan to help with the camping problem
is to go through and make a great many of the
existing items (for example, the BBC) lore
items. These items will soon appear as 'pending lore'
items or some such, to indicate to owners that
they should trade/sell/destroy all but one of
these items in their inventory if they own more
than one. Then, a week or so after they appear as
'pending lore', they will become lore items. It
is important that you only have one of these items
at that time, in that the server will remove any
duplicates. We will give plenty of warning in the
news file that displays when you enter the game.

I think this will help, and will be less of a
sweeping change than my earlier proposal. It won't
solve the 'problem', but I think the situation
will improve. If a player, regardless of level, is
harvesting an item, he will have to trade or
sell the item before obtaining another, which typically
means leaving the area, giving others the
opportunity to go after it.

Our minds are pretty made up in terms of going
forward with items changing to lore items, but
please, let's continue the camping debate -- it
can only make the game better.
