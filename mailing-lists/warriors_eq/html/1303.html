You will never be accepted by wood elves.

They have a racial hatred much like the ethnic situation in Bosnia. It has
nothing to do with who Pansa is or what he has done, they simply refuse to
accept that a dark elf, a Tier'dal, any servant of Hatred can possibly be
good.

For reference, those of you who are familiar with Drizzit Do'Urden. He was
a level20+ fighter/level 20+ ranger. He was at least level 20 before he
came to live aboveground, and likely another 15 levels before he was
accepted by even a small percent of the population in an isolated area. He
accepted that in some areas he would never ever be accepted because of his
race and what it represents.

I for one believe that Pansa should be able to achieve acceptance in a city
like Freeport where there is a "melting pot" of races and religions.
However, he should never be accepted into the city of the wood elves. It
goes against the principles of the game for one, and on another note the
Feir'dal and Tier'dal are ancient enemies.

Just my opinion. In fact I play a rogue dark elf warrior. I chose my deity
as Agnostic initially, though I roleplayed a follower of Innoruuk up to
about level 13 or so. I then met a high elf by the name of Rahl who with
his group risked their lives to aid me in a nearly lost cause. This had a
profound impact on the development of my character. Now Bael is unsure of
his path, and falls back and forth between his "natural" heritage and his
new uncertain "path of light" which he has embarked on. I had somewhat
planned that the character would at some point come to this realization. It
isn't in me to roleplay the superior and self-centered kind of race the dark
elf is. I can't stand by and watch someone die when I know I could help.
The end result is an excellent roleplay which is alot of fun. In general to
many of the dark races just become the shining pinnacle of goodness of their
races. I do love trolls though....a better group of roleplayers you will be
hard pressed to find. Ok I am babbling again...shutting up now.

Bael
