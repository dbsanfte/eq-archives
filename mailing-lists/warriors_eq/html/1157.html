"Making the warrior a huge damage dealing machine was never the intent of

Ummm......what game have YOU been playing??!? Balanced? Group Oriented? I
really think not. The game gives a HUGE advantage to casters and a gigantic
disadvantage to the true warrior. But I will concede the point that a
warrior has a little less of an advantage against something like a hill
giant, BUT only in terms of damage. And maybe not even that....by the time
the caster gets off his fireball, I could have sliced the giants ankles and
been done stabbing him in the throat while he was helpless on the ground. I
think that the first thing you forgot was that EQ is a ROLE-PLAYING game.
You have to use your imagination. I am saying that before you tell me that I
can't cut his ankles or stab him in his throat or even run around him and
fight him at all. I am using my imagination in the fight. Ok...anyways, back
to the point. In terms of one-on-one damage I am saying that the two should
be at the LEAST comparable. You telling me that a warrior is no match for a
caster at bringing down a hill giant just doesn't sit right with me. That is
like saying that a sword is no match for a gun. It depends on how you use
it. Sure you could stand across the room and shoot me 6 times before I even
got near you, but in the same sense I could creep up on you and cut your
hand off before you even drew that precious gun of yours. So in terms of
damage, yes maybe some of the casters spells should have more of an effect
on some creatures. But it all depends on where we, as warriors, stick our
swords. If the giant is 20' taller than me...chances are I am about 200%
faster than him. I just have to know where to hit him to take him out.

Personal Note. I am only going this route because I have studied
Kinjitsu (Japanese swordfighting) and Karate for some time now, and I know
that no matter how big an opponent is he can be taken down. I don't care if
he is literally as big as a house....if his tendons get cut, he falls like
any man one-tenth his size. But remember this is just my opinion, I am not
trying to flame anyone or any point of view. In fact I think this is very
healthy to the development of the game. No one ever succeeded at anything
with everyone telling them that they are right all the time. Am I right?
