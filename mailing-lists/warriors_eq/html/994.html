I feel for ya Patty... anyone who has ever died the quick death of a giants fist
will tell you .... it isn't fun.

Here's a story for you:
My group and I had been pretty weary from the many Dervish camps so we decided
to have a little adventure in Befallen. As we entered, we noticed that the non
undead had come out in droves to battle the skeletons for the evening. That's
never good.

Even though I enjoy watching evil forces fall from a mighty sword swing, I never
long to see mass hysteria going on in tight dungeon passageways. We decided to
venture much deeper into that vile place than ever before, simply to try it, and
to get away from the crowds. We came upon a door at the end of the stairwell
that could not be opened. The holder of the key must have been killed and looted
already so even though we had a key for upper area of this dungeon ... we made
an unwise choice and slid though the door as another party was leaving.

Our party was ill equipped to handle what we found there. Ice and Dry Bone
Skeletons attacked us very quickly and through a lot of luck, and some
assistance we were able to defend ourselves .... just barely. My of us had to
stop and rest afterward. Thus the problem began.

Someone (we don't even remember who it was) said .. let's head out of here and
kill some more Dervish. So most of us got up and left through the door someone
had just opened. Our cleric, being very low on mana, was unaware we had left
since he was deep into his meditation. The door shut and almost immediately an
ice skeleton set upon him. We thought he was doomed. We screamed for help and in
the meantime, our paladin performed a miracle. Placing his hands upon the door,
he performed a laying of hands on the cleric (trapped in the other room), thus
saving his life while help quickly intervened.

The party was very battle weary afterward so we decided to head out of Befallen.
As we were getting ready to leave the mouth of the dungeon, a necromancer ran
out and cast a spell, then ran back behind us. Unsure what she was doing we
peered outside, only to find out .... she was pulling a hill giant right to the
entrance. The end result of this was NOT a pretty picture. Many people died. My
party was extremely upset with this arrogant and back stabbing necro. Many vain
attempts were made to contact her but ... we never saw her again.

The moral of this story is that giants ALWAYS seem to show up at the worst
times.
