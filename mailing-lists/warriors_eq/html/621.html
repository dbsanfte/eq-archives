> > >> 1. How do you know when you are maxed at a skill?
> > >
> > >Level caps for skills follow this formula:
> > >(Lvl * 5) + 5. Example, level 5 level cap would be (5 *5) + 5 = 30.
> > >Some skills that are 'secondary' or some such for a class might level
> > >cap lower (like combat skills for mage classes, etc...).
> > >
> > >> 2. Where are Najena and Befallen?
> > >
> > >Plenty of great EQ sites out there with maps; look into them.
> > >
> > >> 3. What should a level six Barbarian be doing with
> > >> his limited amounts of time?
> > >
> > >Killing stuff.
> > >
> > >> 4. How is experience divided in a group? Is it the
> > >> same amout as solo, split into shares, or is there
> > >> more total experience given out?
> > >
> > >XP does not increase per monster when grouped vs. soloing. However,
> > >groups can (when functioning properly and working together) fight more
> > >continually, more efficiently, and take on more and larger foes; ending
> > >up in better XP and loot for the group as a whole. A warrior has
> > >difficulty, sometimes, soloing a white monster; and will have to sit
> > >afterwards. In a group, the warrior takes the monster with ease and
> > >has very little downtime following the encounter.
> > >
> > >Group xp is divided by taking the total levels in the group and
> > >dividing the XP for the encounter by them. Each character then takes
> > >one share per level they own; higher levels take more shares than lower
> > >levels. There are also some unknown tweak factors or somesuch in
> > >effect behind the scenes that make grouping work better between
> > >disparate level characters at higher levels (like 20+, etc...). There
> > >is less of an XP result difference between a 20 and 24 grouped pair
> > >than between a 6 and 10 grouped pair.
