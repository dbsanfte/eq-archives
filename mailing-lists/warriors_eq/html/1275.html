Subject: Ad&D and EQ - Argument on Warriors


One thing, in AD&D (which eq is obviously influenced by) the amount of
damage a Ranger could do was only slightly inferior to a warrior (and that
wasn't until later levels). The penalty that AD&D used was the experience
table, and rangers got skills a little later then Fighters. Otherwise they
were nearly identical in offensive power.

In defensive power the fighter was superior. He could wear plate and had
more hp.

Using AD&D as a reference if you buffed up the offensive power of a warrior
then you should for hybrids as well. The defensive power should be
addressed thou. I dislike the magic plate mail loop hole, robbing warriors
of some of their defensive power.

When you compare the warrior to the ranger in AD&D the differences are
nearly wholly defensive power. Fighters got one extra specialization slot
(which meant an extra attack) at later levels which in EQ terms equals the
higher attack/defense ratings as well as they get the skills a lot earlier.

The only reason I mention it is that people are bringing in their own views
of what the warrior should be like and using literature or other RPG's to
back them up (the vorpal wielding warrior comes to mind). I think if you
take a closer look at well researched and established games the warriors
advantage over the sub classes has been defensive more then offensive
