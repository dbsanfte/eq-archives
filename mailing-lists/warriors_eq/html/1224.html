You missed my point.

"Have you ever seen to guys sword fighting and watch one guy kick at the
same time he is trying to stick him? :) "

The critical strike should be attached to an attack, and not in place of
one. A sword swing could be a critical strike, or maybe a kick could be, or
even a bash could be a critical strike. Any attack can be used to deliver
critical damage.

Second point.

"Having the abilities go up like parry and dodge would make it too powerful
I believe. They go up without the need to use them. "

The only thing you are doing by making this an invoked skill is giving
warriors another key to tap repeatedly in combat. You are forcing warriors
to choose between most likely doing 11 damage with a kick or maybe getting a
critical hit. I don't agree. Warriors can and should use everything at
their disposal, slash and kick or bash, and at the same time be looking for
an opportunity to strike a critical hit. A critical hit is not something
you just decided to do, you take an opportunity. Your suggestion indicates
that parry/dodge is a random part of combat. I argue that it is indeed a
skill, one which I wish was successful more often. It isn't random because
a warrior will endeavor to parry and dodge all attacks, and not just
randomly manage to avoid them. I speak from real life experience on this
one not just about the game.

I see where you are going with the critical hit thing, but in the context of
EQ the usefulness of the skill would be much hampered by your "invoked"
method. The average gamers style of play is to pick an attack and keep
using it. I for one, would simply max out my critical hit skill in party
fighting against yellow and red mobs. Then I would use the skill at every
opportunity. My dark elf warrior would likely max the skill in a single
level, and be very successful using it. I fear that barbarians, ogres and
trolls would be at a disadvantage here.

I am solidly convinced that the parry/dodge method is the right way to go,
and let EQ check when to use the skill and check its success. Then critical
hit won't just become a replacement for kick/bash but instead become the
real enhancement to our class we all think is needed.
