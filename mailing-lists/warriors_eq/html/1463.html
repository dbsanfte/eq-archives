Hello again,

I've stirred up many people on this list and elsewhere. Some people
understand my views while others want me lynched. I want to clarify my
initial statement.

As a class, Warriors are great. I think they are a vital part of a group.
They normally do much better at their job than any hybrid can. I definitely
want a warrior tanking for me.

But to play a Warrior, they are lacking for me, for my style of play. I
don't expect everyone to agree with me. In fact, for many of you, Warriors
are exactly what you are looking for. I wasn't comparing Warriors vs
Clerics in their effectiveness, I think they are both very important. I
just like the decisions I have to make as a Cleric as opposed to a Warrior.

The only reason I used a Cleric in my comparisons was that I play a Cleric
the most. I want to play a Warrior too. That is the reason I am subscribed
to this list.

I assume that many of you did not read my full message. The last couple of
paragraphs were some suggested improvements to the class that I felt would
make the Warrior more enjoyable for me. Let me show them to you again:

I've suggested in the past a Battle Cry skill. This skill, usable once per
day, would increase the AC and Attack Speed of everyone in the party for 30
seconds.

Another option would be Fighting Styles. Give us an Offensive fighting
style. This would increase damage or Attacking plus a taunt bonus while
sacrificing AC and Defense. And we would also want a Defensive fighting
style. This would boost AC, dodge, and defense with a sacrifice to
Attacking and damage. It would be very cool if these Fighting Styles could
only be learned through a quest designed for around levels 15-20.


Give Warriors something to think about in combat. Make it so that
experienced, skillful Warrior can be better than any newbie Warrior. Give
them some abilities that would take skill to use effectively. Knowing when
to switch Fighting Styles or use Battle Cry would be something an
experienced Warrior could learn to do well.

Many of you stated your feelings about Warriors. Some felt that the Warrior
should be the group leader, the best puller, responsible for directing
combat and retreats, and guarding the party while resting. Many of you
stated how a Warrior could sacrifice himself to save the party. I don't
consider these functions part of the Warrior's domain only. Any good EQ
player can do this, with many different classes.

When it comes down to a battle, the Warrior's job is to choose the correct
critter to attack/kick/bash/taunt. Thats about it. A good Warrior will
make the best choices here. A good Warrior will switch targets many times
in a battle, distributing his attacks and taunts to keep the spellcasters
free from attack. Some of the best Warriors will switch armor/weapons based
on what monster they are fighting. Some go even as far to switch a shield
in and out in the middle of battle depending if they are getting hit or not.

A few Warriors even say there is a weapon range effect. Each weapon has a
different range where it is more likely to hit for max damage. They adjust
their distance from the monster to try and get max damage.

A poor Cleric is one that uses up all his mana for direct damage. An
average Cleric is one who only heals. A good Cleric has many more options
available for the group. It is choosing between the many options I have
that make the Cleric a better choice for me.


I don't get to play EQ as much as many of you do. I am unable to keep
grouping with a good group as they will pass me up in levels. So I have
ended up grouping with many poor, mediocre, and average players.

These are my observations:
It is much easier to be an average/good Warrior than it is to be an
average/good Cleric. The learning curve for Clerics is just steeper than
Warriors.

A poor Cleric is much more harmful to a party than a poor Warrior. I'd much
rather be a Cleric and group with a poor Warrior, than be a Warrior and
group with a poor Cleric.

Average players will recognize the work of a good Cleric (compared to a poor
cleric). Average players hardly notice the work of a good Warrior (compared
to a good warrior). Many players wouldn't even realize that a Warrior has
been taunting correctly to keep the critters off of a spellcaster.
