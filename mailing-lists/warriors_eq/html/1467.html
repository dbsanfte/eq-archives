On Wed, 14 Jul 1999, David Rogers wrote:
> AC IS NOT EVERYTHING! AC is the second half, and the lesser half, of
> the armor system in Everquest. The first half is damage absorption.

According to Verant, AC _is_ everything. There is no 'intrinsic' damage
absorption element for different armors. Rather, their relative damage
absorption and protective effects are stored in the AC value.

> Each level of armor absorbs more damage than the levels before it. If
> you doubt, try it. Fight something on your level naked and see how
> badly you get hit; every shot will come in at max damage. Put your
> armor back on and see shots hitting for 2, 5, etc...

This analysis is just plain... well... half-baked. The reason you begin
to get savaged without armor is because your AC has dropped by some
obscene value.

The difference here is not the 'type' of armor, but the AC difference.
Thus, if you could find cloth-looking armor with a better AC than plate,
it would behoove you to wear it.

This is why you see bare-sleeved high level players... They're wearing
cloth-looking armor with _higher_ AC than either Plate or Rubicite.

> PLATE IS WORTH IT.

AC equivalents to Plate are worth just as much as Plate itself. You only
need the AC of Plate, not the 'look'.

Just going to do two examples:

> lizardscale cloak vs. Bronze Cloak

I would _definitely_ use the Lizardscale Cloak. It is Plate with stat
modifiers.

Another common item that _is_ better than Plate would be the Thick Leather
Apron, which is both lighter and has equivalent or better AC than a Plate
Girdle.

Just some thoughts.
