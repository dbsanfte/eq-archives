I've been thinking about your Battle Cry idea. It has merit, but in
practice it isn't really tactically or strategically useful.

I've played a little shadowknight and a little paladin and experimented with
these once a day abilities. What I proposed earlier was similiar to the
shadowknight's ability and I was looking for feedback. After thinking about
it I realize that this kind of ability would do nothing to enhance the
overall melee effectiveness of the warrior class.

Lay Hands or Harm Touch are last ditch "save my ass" abilities. They can
arguably be used strategically to take on a higher level mob that a party
might not otherwise be able to kill, but it is usually a last ditch attempt
to finish a battle alive. Without having significant duration attached to
it the Battle Cry becomes another last ditch attempt to save the day, when
everything goes sour.

Now this isn't a bad idea. However I think that what the majority of us are
looking for is a way to enhance the long term combat effectiveness of the
warrior and to increase the average damage a warrior does per round over
that of a hybrid. The Battle Cry you propose does not accomplish that. In
a standard melee against a chasm minotaur with my paladin friend, I want to
be the one who dealt more melee damage at the end of the fight. I don't
care if the wizard zapped it for the most damage, I just want to be the one
to deal the single highest melee damage over all the rangers, paladins,
shadowknights, and so on. Right now all I do is suck up damage during a
fight, and then suck up mana in heals after the fight. The hybrids all have
some form of self healing, giving them an overall advantage so why should we
share melee ability with them? Shouldn't we be stronger? But now I am
ranting....

The skill enhancement I agree with.....why should warriors be capped in
weapons skills? That is all we are about. We should always continue to
grow.
