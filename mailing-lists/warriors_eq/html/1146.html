The chemistry of the game is set to balance a group out. Warriors were ment
to take damage. I will not argue that point. I will not argue the point
that we should not be mammoth damage inflicters like a caster as well. The
mages and wizards are the true damage inflicters. But the warriors are
still caught way low on the damage stick as far as I am concerned. As far
as what we want, lets look more towards what we need. If we are not
demanding, but reasonable in our requests to Verant, we will get farther in
negotaitions. Ever heard the addage you can get a horse to travel farther
with a carrot in his face than a whip at his back? Askinf for more damage,
more attacks, critical hits, and everything else is ok. But we we need to
compromise a little. We can submit a list, have that list rated as to the
importance to what we as a group feel should come first, as well as the
reasons why. This shows unity, thought and the ability to present things in
an intelligent light.

Simply asking to do mad amounts of damage is in my mind absurd. The big
damage is ment for the casters, not the warriors. The warriors are there to
take the damage, and allow the casters to actually kill the creature. Yes,
I know it is nice to kill creatures. But face it, we all knew when we
created this character we had no way to heal, no way to do anything with
magic at all. And in EQ magic is the name of the game. So we need to
settle to be a group player. And not be the almighty slayer of beasts.
That doesnt mean that our role should be unimportant in combat damage tho.
Yes, we do need to be able to influct more damage. And that is our goal for
the most part I think.

Sorry if I sound like I am rambling, things are comming out of the mind
faster than I can formulate good responses.
I will take alot of time in writing the letter to Verant tho.

I agree and disagree.

There is no way a warrior is going to do as much damage per round as a
spellcaster on certains kinds of creatures. How exactly are you going to do
the kind of damage attainable with a fireball against a creature like a hill
giant with something like a sword? You're only chance is to achieve some
kind of critical hit to a sensitive part of the giant...maybe a sword thrust
to the gonads or something. Regardless, I propose the critical hit skill so
that a warrior can add more to a group, and have comparable survivability
soloing. Also, I think critical hits should be used like parry/dodge and
not like kick/bash, Simply because the assumption is you are ALWAYS trying
to do the most damage possible. With kick/bash you are making a choice of
attack. Kick for more damage or bash to stun. Not that I see any high level
warriors bashing...but the option is there.

Warriors will never be able to out damage a mage. However, the warrior
should be able to last a lot longer in physical combat. I do see that
taking effect in the mid-teen levels, and I can only assume it gets better.
But the hybrids can take nearly as much melee punishment and deal out nearly
as much melee damage, and supplement combat with spells. The warrior is
outclassed in every respect by a hybrid at a comparable level, in the
mid-teens. Clerics are very powerful in every game I have played but are
always not very popular classes to play. Not sure why this is, but in EQ the
1-10 levels are tough on a cleric. But after that I see them really
romping. It seems to me most clerics I have seen group out of convenience
rather then necessity.
