heres a short description.

if you use any of the filtering options like others damage off, npc spells
off, my misses or whatever you should use /serverfilter on to prevent the
server from sending this data to your client. else this info gets propagated
over your (possibly very thin) wire and flood your pc with things that the
client itself discards as you choose to filter that info out. This helps ...
a lot in raid situations with 30+ people and low bandwidth users.

On the other hand, if youre on dsl or any other (relatively) highspeed
connection the file datarate.txt in your eq directory is set wrong. Its
tuned by verant for a modem user. What this file does is preventing the
server from overloading your client with too much data causing linkdeaths
when you can no longer recieve enough info, this tends to happen when zoning
or when someone uses a lag intensive spell (sentinel, ae rains and so on).
The Verant setting is 5.0 in the file. If youre on dsl turn this up to 9.0
(max)

This will reduce the lag you get by letting your client recieve more updates
since you have more bandwidth. this will make you much more responsive and
not having a nasty 30 secs msglag when someone is asking for a heal or a
buff or whatever.

More is in the manual or on the everquest messageboards at everquest.com.
Read the technical information.
