Welcome to EQ!  Your life will never be the same.  Having never played a Pally, here are some general thoughts:
 
#1 DOn't feel rushed to get your first character up to higher levels.  EQ is a huge place and there is so much to do and see.  If you take your time you will have a much better experience, IMHO!  (I played my Ranger for a year and he only reached 27). 
 
#2 Take the time to learn at least 1 trade skill.  You can make some decent cash when you become proficient at most trades.  It is also a good way to make EQ friends.
 
#3 When you are ready to join a guild, be selective and play with some people from that guild for a while to make sure that you enjoy their company.  You can always leave the guild later if it doesn't work out.  But remember guilds are only as good as their members. Participate and help your guildies out when you can.  In return they will be there for you when you need it.
 
#4 Under NO circumstances should you EVER beg!  Ther is nothing that experienced players hate more than a beggar!  Always offer plat or something in trade for something you are seeking.  SoW's and Rez's especially!
 
#5 You will have to explore different zones to find an area that you enjoy hunting in.  If you like to group and read a lot of OOC and Shouts, you may want to get yourself to a place like Lake of Ill Omen.  But if you are a solo, solitary kinda person, places like Ocean of Tears may suit you better.  I like to find zones that are somewhere in between.  Enough people to get a good group going, but not overcamped and populated by a bunch of people wanting everything for free.  This is, of course, a matter of taste. 
 
#6 IF you like to group, make friends and stay in touch with them.  Most people like to adventure with a partner or a group, it makes traveling much more fun. 
 
OK, cardinal RULE!  Have FUN!!!  People wil be asking you on all kinds of adventures if you are fun to hang with.
 
That's about it, I mean there is much more but really you should just get out there and see fer yurself!
