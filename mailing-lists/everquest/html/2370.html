> Well, I guess 33 seasons has given me a little experience with the
> bow. First, the procedure is called bow kiting. You snare your
> prey, DoT it and run backwards sticking arrows in it until the snare
> breaks. Then hopefully you've done enough damage to finish it off
> with your sword(s). I still do this, though only with blue's.
>
> And your friend was sorta right. It's not bugged so much as it's not
> very effective. Arrows cost, even if you make them yourself. And
> what self-respecting Ranger doesn't fletch his own bows and arrows?
> Even with a quiver to speed up your recovery time you really can't
> stick a lot of arrows into your prey. I can get them down to about
> 60% on a good day. Now if you can get one of the high damage bows
> early you may have a better time at this. The Trueshot is attainable
> to low levels, and you may get lucky!
>
> The other problem is running backwards sometimes get's you into more
> trouble! Especially if your prey is prone to having like creatures
> join in. Or Grimfeather comes along! And Rangers can't quad kite!
>
> And you'll find pulling with a bow to be risky in some places. It's
> too easy to get a train started. Not that I'm speaking from prior
> experience here!
>
> Yes, I would have liked Rangers to be the preeminent Archery class.
> We share crits with Warriors, and have a few specials once we hit 50,
