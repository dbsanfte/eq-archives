My two cp worth is INCOMING now. Please take with a grain of salt.
 
IMHO the role of a MT is to do enough damage to keep aggro without taking so much riposte damage that the healers hate them. For most characters this means using a slower, but powerful 2HS. Combine a good 2HS and the hate line of spells that SK get, and you have an awesome MT. Same deal with Pally healing, that nice Wave group-heal spell can be a huge aggro-yanker, so if you are MT then Heal away!
 
I have a 67 papertank, I mean -- ranger, that I often use as MT for my usual experience group of ranger, monk, cleric, druid and chanter. In that instance I dual-wield fast, high-damage weapons as well as casting aggro-inducing spells to keep the monk from stealing aggro because he is a melee-machine. Because of the haste and fast weapons, even with defensive AA I take a fair amount of riposte damage, which means my cleric can get aggro with heals, which means more aggro-spells, etc. But it's how my play-style for this exp group has evolved.
 
It's like Durgaa said, it's all about how you want to play your character.

Think about it this way....

Both SK and pally epics are 2HS. So it's good to keep that skill maxed

Yet, pallies get Bash. In order to use Bash you have to carry a shield, in
which case 2HS is not possible. So it's good to keep 1HS up.

SK's I think get a similar ability, or maybe even Bash, but I dont play a SK
so I dont know for sure. But 1HS is also important to SK.

On the other hand, pallies and SK's usually work as pullers in groups, so
they have to have good Taunting abilities, and sometimes weapons they have
can help with taunt by doing more damage. Go with the weapon that helps
with that regardless of 1Hs or 2HS.

Just my two cents.
