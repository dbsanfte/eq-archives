Gonna try and explain how DS works.
 
The damage from DS is counted as NPC damage (when you recive experience from a mob it's calculated on who (what group) does the most damage). To recive the experience you need to do more than 50% damage. NPC damage means that the damage isn't done by a playing character so this damage really doesn't count as anyones damage. However, if you hit the mob for 1 dmg and the DS does rest of the damage you will recive experience for the mob.
 
A way to use DS at lower lvls where the DS usaly do more damage than the mob has hp's is to have the player cast an offencive dmg spell. It's enough if the spell does 1 damage for you to recive experience. This is a great way to power level someone. You can hit them with DS, they run around and cast a DD on every mob they see. It's a fast way to gain experience.
 
As for you folks that don't think PL is fair, it may so be, but if someone has a character that is lvl 60 and wants to try another class it's not really fun to start from the begining and do the same shit over again. Nor is it fun to not be able to follow your guild on larger scale event such as ToV, planar and dragon raids.
