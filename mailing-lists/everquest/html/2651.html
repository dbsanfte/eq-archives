The way dual wielding was explained to me (and this could be wrong) is 
 
1. the delay of the primary weapon determines how often you hit with the primary weapon (obviously);
2. when the primary hits, the software "rolls the dice" for double attack and then dual wield. If you are lucky, depending on your skill level, you get a second hit with the primary and/or a hit with the secondary;
3. if you get a hit with the secondary weapon, the software again rolls the dice for double attack with that weapon.
 
Thus it would seem logical to put the faster of the two weapons in the primary, the delay of the secondary being basically irrelevant. However, that is only if you want to keep the mob aggroed on you -- that is, you are primary tank. Rangers don't usually want to keep aggro, though, being as a rule more lightly armored than warriors, SKs, and paladins. The ranger is a damage-dealer, not a damage taker. Thus, a ranger should have a higher damage, slower weapon in primary so they can deal out damage without drawing aggro away from the main tank.
 
I have seen the issue argued other ways, though. This is just how it was explained to me.
