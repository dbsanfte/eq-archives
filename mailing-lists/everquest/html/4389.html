I do know the fear factor. Actually, that is one of the aspects of
this style of gaming that makes playing worthwhile. In console games,
your character and story line are generally created for you. If you
save often enough, trying something dangerous costs you nothing but
the time it takes to learn how to solve the puzzle or beat the boss.

In this game, you invest time and effort to create your character.
You have an investment to protect. I know that I am cautious going
into new situations, not wanting to die and risk losing all that I
have invested. Sometimes the risk is just a corpse run. Sometimes you
can get yourself into a situation where you will need high-level help
to get out, and that help cannot always be found.

The thrill of facing these risks, learning to overcome real danger,
and learning how to capitalize on every new situation is what makes
this game worth playing. You aren't just learning to twitch faster on
your controls. You think, plan, and design your own adventure.

Good hunting to you.
