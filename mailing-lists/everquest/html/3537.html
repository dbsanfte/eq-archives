Subject: what is SCA?


The Society for Creative Anachronisms
 
the short answer - we're those crazy people you see in the park every sunday dressed up in armor and beating on each other with baseball bats wrapped in duct tape.
 
the long answer - we are a world wide non-profit educational organization dedicated to the recreation of history pre-17th Century.  We are NOT a Rennaissance group that puts on productions and shows for the entertainment of the general populace.  We are a group of serious  (and some not so serious) people who like to study history and recreate certain aspects of it as a very elaborate, very intricate hobby.  People can interpret this in many different ways; some are purists and believe it should be recreated exactly as it was, some are not so pure and believe it should be recreated as it SHOULD have been (i.e. electricity and in door plumbing - thus the "anachronism"). Either way, we are mainly a group of friends who come together as a family on a regular basis to attend events held for various reasons.  The principle of the SCA is that the entire known world is separated into kingdoms which is then broken down into varying lands.  Each kingdom is ruled by a king, and so on down the hierarchy.  We create "life in the current Middle Ages".  We have tournaments, we have wars, we have classes to teach you any aspect of history you want to know about, and we have fun. 
 
for more informaation on the SCA you can check out the SCA's main website at http://www.sca.org
 
For information on the SCA in Trimaris (Florida), check out http://www.trimaris.org
 
Or just do a web search for Society for Creative Anachronisms and you pull up more sites than you'd care to count.
 
Hope that helps.
