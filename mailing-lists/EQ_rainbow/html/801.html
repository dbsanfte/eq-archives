Hi there,

I wanted introduce myself, as I'm new to the guild, though I've been
listening in on EQ_rainbow for a really long time. I just joined a couple
of nights ago. My character is on Rodcet Nife. His name is Txomin, a dark
elf enchanter.

What brings me to this server and guild now? Well, I've been playing EQ
since beta 2, and when final release came around, I burned hard for about a
year in it. In that time I moved from Tarew Marr to Tunare, where I settled
and founded with a couple of friends one of the biggest and strongest guilds
there, named Talisman. I put forth a lot of effort in the guild, and as the
leader I encouraged everyone who had problems--in game or real life--to let
me know and I'd do everything I could to help. I looked at my position as
the caretaker of every guildmember's fun; for me I felt I had to ensure that
everyone was having a good time. It's a good way to be, and it made the
guild a terrific place. However, 60+ people all coming to you with their
problems daily can be a burden after a while. Couple that with a really
REALLY bad time in my own life and complete burnout was innevitable.

So, around February of this year, I stopped playing. I turned over the
guild to a really good friend, and it's still going strong. For myself, I
divorced EQ for about 6 months. I fixed my ailing real life (a year of
unemployed, continuous EverQuest debauchery can set your existence into a
tail-spin), got a job and moved to San Diego.

The break presented the perfect opportunity to move to Rodcet Nife and
finally do what I'd always wished I could have done, and that is join Mystic
Alliance. I couldn't leave Tunare before, but now I'm free to do it. Oddly
enough, part of the reason I'm playing again is work related. I'm a content
writer for Verant Interactive/Sony Online Entertainment now, so it's sort of
part of the job!

Anyway, that's my story and I'm sticking to it. As I said, I live in San
Diego. I'm not sure if anyone else lives here or not. One of the unique
advantages of my job is, I most likely will be attending some of the various
EQ Player get-togethers around the country, particularly the one sponsored
by Verant. I believe the next one will be February on the East Coast
somewhere (my old stomping ground), and likely I'll be there. So who knows,
I may be in a neighborhood near you in the future :)

Nice to finally be part of something I'd always wished to join in on.
Thanks!

Clayton Kroh
