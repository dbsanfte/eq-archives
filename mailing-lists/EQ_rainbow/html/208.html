I like the story as well, and I agree with Christina that we don't need to be
outcasts. As far as names for the women in the history, there's a couple I
have in mind, but need to look up tonight before posting.

In reading your message, Christina, another name came to mind <as she
cautiously tosses it into the fray>: Mystic Alliance

I think the GLBT-related names have pretty much been dropped in discussion by
most people. This name is role-play appropriate, it is somewhat mysterious and
the alliance part is what we are about. There is nothing good/evil impliant in
the name, nor any specific class or race.

Although the GLBT link may not be apparent in any name chosen, it is easy
enough to add symbolism to any graphics/logo associated with a guild site.
Granted, it wouldn't be visually attached to our names in the game, but if
people want to know, they'll ask or look for a website for information.

As far as publicity for the guild once things are a bit settled, PlanetOut
might be a great online zine to contact. Their turnaround time for getting
information out is much quicker than the print media. I think they may also
have links to GLBT websites, which would be great when we have one for the
guild. I can't recall if there is a gay-related news service bureau. Some
individuals were trying to create one a while ago. The BBC is very queer
friendly and could be interested in a story, especially if we wish to reach
our brothers and sisters in the EU. Wired may also be a good place to reach
GLBT gamers and they have their email newsletters as well.

I enjoyed chatting with you all in the guild formation last night. This is the
start of something really great.
