Well, on the wedding night would be the wrong time to have told. Through
most on-line RPGs I've been in usually at least 50% (used to be higher
even) of the female characters *are* played by males.

Most of the males who played male characters, and found out the player
behind the female on the wedding night was male did *really* flip out.
About 1/4 got over it but were angry they had not been told earlier, and
the rest severed relations with both the female character and its player.
OTOH, I have seen marriages of characters with males behind both characters
work very well and last years :) But in this case the male playing the
female revealed this early enough in the relationship that they were
comfortable and both players were good RPers and knew they could keep the
on-line romance just a role so it didn't threaten anyones sexuality.

If the player behind the male was a very good RPer and well schooled in
keeping the character relationship seperate from his RL feelings, he'll
probably flip (mainly surprise), but get over it. But expect him to be
upset you waited this late to tell. If he spoke a lot about his RL, and
seemed to "live his character" then it may be best to *never* tell.
Becuase then he'll flip and go really insecure. Add this to the fact that
many young straight males are soooo insecure about thier sexuality, mention
its a gay male behind the female and I'd say odds of a "serious flip out"
really go up :) I spent many a night up to 3-4 AM in chat talking with
young males (18-30 is young in my book) who just found out thier on-line
"love interest" was really a male. In one case, even one old married guy
;) It seemed they really needed to talk it out with a female just to shore
up thier insecureties. =) And its happened at least a couple of dozen
times .. so this seems quite a common occurence. Roleplaying can really
engage the emotions ... people unfamiliar with that are often the most
unprepared for twists like this.

You know the player, can he handle the truth? can the others that spent a
lot of time flirting handle it when they find out? or would it be a
kindness to your friends to make a parting excuse and just never tell?

Good Luck. Its certainly not an easy situation, and very emotional all
around. :(
