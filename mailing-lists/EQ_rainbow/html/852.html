As a resident breeder, I can tell you its not just l33t d00dz who use "gay"
in such a context. It has become part of the general all-purpose "kid
slang" and is used as early as first grade. And I can date it back to at
least 4 years ago. When we moved into town off our isolated farm .. our
oldest kid was then a 3rd grader. She came home after the first couple of
weeks of school and asked me what "gay" meant. When I raised my eyebrows at
her a bit (sure she's precocious but asking that at barely eight?), she told
me it was used as a general purpose insult and she wanted to know what it
meant. To give her credit, she was introduced to "cuss" words in context
(we use them well at home), and taught to check out the appropriateness of
any new words and thier meaning so she will pick the proper times to use the
word (and so I won't get any phone calls from school that my kid told the
teacher "Fuck you bitch!" - hehe) The end of our discussion was her not
using the word herself in that context and socially bashing the kids who
did. A year later, as she got to know other kids better, she realized
another girl in our neighborhood her age has a "gay" (lesbian) aunt. Now
the two girls are fairly well known for deriding any who use "gay" in that
context in school.

I would bet that almost ALL the kids using it at that age had no clue of its
meaning to an adult. I fault the adults for not correcting them ... but if
they are using it from first grade up .. good luck getting them to break te
habit as a teen. Even if, as a teen, they understand the meaning and
insensitivity of that usage. That has been the response of several teens
whom after I verbally smacked them about for using "gay". - "Owww I agree,
but its going to be VERY hard to break the habit". I have seen children who
are otherwise well-behaved elementary schoolers using the word. The only
difference was that the well-parented ones, once the parent had been told I
found the word offensive (and that thier seven year old was spouting out
"gay" with no idea what it meant - hehe) .. those children have stopped
using it when adults are present. But in middle and high school, its use
is still common ...

I was on Brell, and most of us that were active in the defunct MA chapter
there have moved to Rodcet. But, I'd expect the slang to be everywhere on
all servers.
