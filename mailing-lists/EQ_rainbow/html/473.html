Subject: Yay friends rock


I came out to an EQ friend last night, and it went really well. It
turned into one of those "why didn't I tell her long ago" thingies. She's
16 and I thought she might not be mature enough to handle it, but she took
it really well and easily. In particular, she's a very affectionate
person online, and she is still affectionate towards me (I was worried she
might withdraw). She also sent me an email after she logged off that
night where she said all the right friend things.

One of the things that I thought was kind of weird was after I told her
she said "oh, everything makes a lot more sense now". I was kind of
surprised about that, I still don't know what she means. I think she
might have been refering to the fact that I had already told her I was
polyamorous (which had confused her a fair bit). Perhaps she found it
easier to perceive a bi-woman as poly than a straight one? There certainly
are enough stereotypes (I hate beind a stereotype dammit, it makes me want
to go monogomus! =)

She actually asked me to marry her right after I came out to her. I think
it was her way of showing me immediate support. I was a little blown
away, but I turned her down. Both her and I are fairly well known on
Brell Serelis and my instincts said that the only way we could pull it off
without harassment would be to play up on it as kind of a joke. Since
that isn't the way I'd want to approach an online queer wedding, and since
I don't want her or me to get treated badly, I decided against it.

Things are a little further confused by the fact that I have an
itsy-bitsy crush on her. She's just too lovable *sigh*. I think I see her
as someone I would have loved to date when I was in highschool. Whenever
she gives me hugs or cuddles I feel all fuzzy and happy.

I am 99% sure she's straight though (and she's a little too young for me
even if she wasn't). Luckily I have *lots* of experience having crushes
on my straight female friends =) So, I'm pretty sure I can handle this.

- Christina
