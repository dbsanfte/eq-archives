Hmmm ... all my names in the order I recieved them

Jennifer Carroll - birth
Lora Lynn Davis - legal adoption
Lora Lynn Hauser - illegal, unwanted alias
Lora Lynn Davis - back to me after correcting all but a few public school
records
Lora Lynn Roa - married, legally but told my name was illegal in NC!

My illegal, unwanted alias was due to a stepfather who married my mother in
my teens, he was abusive (to all) and I moved out, but my mother insisted
on trying to "make us a family" by changing all my records to his last
name. There was never an adoption, since he really didn't want a pair of
teenagers when he'd think about it (can't blame him there!).

I *still* don't have all my public school records back in my name, but I
got High School straight in time for graduation. Its still fun to try and
go to a public school and try and tell them your legal name isn't what your
mother says it is!

Just when I think its all over, and I'm an adult and in control of my name,
I get married and having little attachment to the surname of my deceased
adopted father (and also I'm told was abusive) I choose to take my husbands
surname, but keep my middle as it distinguishes me (Lora Lynn) from my
grandmother (Lora Ann, whom I was named for). I changed it legally in SC
(I thought), then went to my bank in NC to change it there ...

I was told that in NC there is a law stating that a married woman who takes
a new surname MUST use her maiden name as her middle name, or have 2 middle
names and according to them my name was illegal since I had dropped my
maiden name. (?????) It took me an hour and a long chat with the bank
manager before they'd change my account to my new name!

As an interesting side-note, all the straight male parental figures in my
life were there briefly and were abusive to some degree. My "uncles", the
gay couple (and thier friends) who were my more functional fathers, were
never abusive and the closest one continued his relationship with me
through three moves and until his death (the one who helped pay for my
education). Which of course, runs counter to stereo-types as I've had more
than one gay friend point out recently, but I already knew not to trust
stereo-types by then :P
