I think I could write a whole book ranting about online gender
presentation, assumption of heterosexuality, and how queer people basicly
get screwed over, but I'll try and make this brief 8)

First of all, let's put Everquest in it's true class: Virtual Reality. It
may be primitive, but it is virtual reality, and to anyone who's ever had
someone tap them on the shoulder because they've been talking to you for
five minutes and you haven't heard a damned thing knows, it's immersive
virtual reality.

People naturally try and relate to their virtual reality experience like
they would to their real life experience. This doesn't mean they take
everything as seriously (otherwise death would reallly suck) but they will
naturally impose real life qualities in terms of how them form
relationships and socialize online. These natural social tendancies may
be modified by concious choice, but at the root they are all there.

People are used to classifying another person's gender. You don't walk up
to someone on the street (usually) and say "you look like a boy, you act
like a boy, are you a boy?" We're expected to recognize these qualities,
classify the person as a boy, and then relate to them as a boy.

People do this same thing naturally in EQ, though not to as an extreme.
For example:
- You see a male character, you assume this is a male player, you'd
probably need to be told outright by the person they were female
to believe otherwise.
- You see a female character, things are more complicated:
- Most people will not try and classify her gender directly I
think. They'll be aware that most female EQ characters are
played by males. This introduces gender confusion. They don't
know how to really deal with a person with a female body but
a male mind so they'll basicly treat the person like a female
on the surface, but probably not commit to their gender.
- If that female character exhibits behavioral characteristics
that seem female it is very comfortable to allow your gender
'no commitment' stance to slip into 'this person is a female
in real life'.
- Likewise if this character exhibits male characteristics,
the person may slip into the assumption the player is male.
All this avoids the need to ask the person what their gender is.
- FLIRTING OF ANY KIND is assumed to indicate gender under the
assumption the player is heterosexual.
- Some people are aware enough of the complexity of online gender,
and care enough about real life gender, to skip all this and ask
(usually to confirm their assumptions)

Queer people get screwed by this in many ways. Lots of people who
probably wouldn't ID as queer also get screwed.

The above rules are arbitrary, and dumb! They are the rules most people go
by. If a person decides on your real life gender by these rules, even
worse if a group of people decide on your gender, their is the fundamental
assumption that you actively deceived them. Lets not pull punches,
everyone who decides your gender, when you reveal you are of the other
gender, will basicly have their entire gender model of you flipped upside
down. This will create confusion, and potentially hostility. Of course
anyone who is not a jerk should realize that their assumptions were wrong,
but most people are jerks about gender.

So what can you do to protect yourself in this environment?

You can be up front about your real-life gender. This will however to
some degree prevent you from getting into intense gender interaction with
people as a member of your online perceived gender. Playing a person of
the opposite gender in EQ is a great way to explore gender in a fun
environment. Exploring gender is fun and good.

You can be passive about your gender. This is what our list boys have
done. They let people assume their genders as females, since they didn't
actively in this way deceive anyone. I think this is perfectly 100% ok.
Now however they're getting screwed over by the realization that they can
no longer safely come out without alienating some of their friends.

You can lie outright about your gender. I think this is perfectly ok as
well. Why? Because you're not hurting anyone. Really the gender / sexual
orientation hangups of other people is not our problem. Really, our
gender in real life is irrelevant to the game of Everquest. People
demand to know real life gender because they have hangups about online /
real life gender continuity. Tough luck! It's not your responsibility to
support their environment where they have this kind of knowledge. You
never aksed to be part of that environment. You can also say "I won't
answer that question" but people will eventually assume you're not of your
perceived gender, and they'll mistrust you.

People don't like to lie. They like to be honest, especially with people
they have good social relationships with. I think it's important to
realize that an relationship in an online virtual reality is not like a
real life social relationship. Unless you are going to have some kind of
real life social relationship with someone online, I think it's perfectly
reasonable to lie about your gender, your sexual orientation, anything if
it creates an environment you're more comfortable with.

We are the queer gender-exploring fringe, moving into an online virtual
environment. The straight heterosexist establishment is imposing on VR
it's own moral code, but I feel no need to participate in it.
