Charter:
The Guild is open to citizens of Norath, regardless of race, class, sex,
religion and sexual orientation. All Guild members must show respect to others
whether they are in the Guild or not. No homophobes will be allowed.

The purpose of the Guild is:
o to use our resources for mutual benefit
o to adventure together in an atmosphere where we are not subjected to
homophobic comments
o to enhance the enjoyment of life in Norath for all of us.

The above part of the charter is universal to all servers on which our guild
may have members.

On Brell Serilis and other role playing servers, our charter is additionally:
o to encourage RPing.

How does all of the above sound????

Yesterday, I said I'd post a back story for our guild. I have not until now
because I thought about totally rewriting it, but then I decided I'll just
post it and let someone else totally rewrite it. It is below. The first person
who wants to take a crack at a rewrite, just tell us on the list and then
we'll wait for you to post your version and then the next person can.

Some guidelines. I'd like the backstory to include a prominent male and female
hero and heroine. You may include as much Norathian lore as you desire. I
don't know much yet and haven't put much in. Feel free to include more of
this.

So, someone either edit what I have or throw it out and start over. I cranked
this puppy out in about 5 minutes, so I won't be offended at all if the whole
thing is pitched, but maybe it can be a starting point or at least get the
imagination working.

Here it is:

Cleric Elgen Baknor was a disciple of The Queen of Love. After learning all
about the love that Erollisi Marr espoused, he decided to leave the Temple of
Marr because he wanted to experience that love.

Having entered the priesthood because he could not see himself falling in love
with a woman, he eventually met and fell in love with a man whose identity is
shrouded in mystery. It is believed he may have been a man who held high rank
in the Temple. Eventually though, Elgen would realize the relationship would
not work for he was unable to openly express his love for this mysterious man.

Elgen had heard of a woman who was a mighty warrior in Faydwer who loved other
women in the same way he loved men. He set out to find her.

Elgen left the city of Freeport and ventured to Faydwer. On his journey, he
picked up many friends who believed, just as Elgen, that love was the answer,
regardless of gender. He asked each of his friends if they had heard of this
woman whose heart belonged to another woman. But none had.

Finally, one day the group came upon two women at a campsite normally
inhabited by numerous Orcs, but on this day, no Orcs were to be seen. Suddenly
though, an Orc sprang out of nowhere. Fearing for their lives Elgen's group
began to run. But in the corner of his eye, Elgen saw one of the two women
pull out a long sword and strike down the Orc in a matter of seconds, while
the other woman, continued to sit undisturbed in silent meditation. This
supreme warrior introduced herself and her lover to the small group. [Need a
name for her and her lover]

This small band of cast out souls formed their own community, a small camp in
the recesses of Lesser Faydark. Together, these men and women took the best
ideals of Erollisi Marr and combined them with their own beliefs. Their small
sect, comprised originally of only followers of Erollisi Marr, eventually
began to attract followers of other deities, even the Plaguebringer. For no
matter their race or class, spread throughout the lands of Norath were people
who believed that love did not have to be restricted to only the opposite
gender. This one belief united them despite all their other diversity and they
would eventually become known as '[guild name goes here]'.
