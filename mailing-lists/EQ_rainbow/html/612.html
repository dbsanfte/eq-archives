I actually may have a clue too.

There has been a running debate on if the low level Bard song
for stamina helps mana regen. It brought out some interesting
side effects.

Many mages said they "saw" a difference, but EQ staff on the Bard
list eventually said it had no effect.

So the Bards fiddled with it a bit (pun intended).

It seems that many stamina buffs will cause the "ticker" to update
faster. BUT before you get excited, the total time it takes to
go from 1 bubble to full (in mana) does not vary, just the rate at
which the changes are displayed. The Bards found several mages
and asked them to time the total refill time as well as time the
"ticker" or visual update timer. They then tested with and w/o song.
The data fit both what the mages saw and the EQ staff info. The
"ticker" times could vary, fooling you into thinking it was filling faster,
but the refill total time was pretty constant. If a true mana regen spell
or
song was put in effect *then* the total refill time was indeed reduced.

So, were you under any stamina buffs? They can give an illusion.
