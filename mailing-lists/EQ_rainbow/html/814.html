A chilly evening in the West Commons (on Rodcet Nife server)
----------------------------
Kerik the Wood Elf Druid worked hastily to set up the tent. Eizen
appeared about ready to nod off at any moment. As had become their
usual habit, Kerik ported his cleric friend to the commons and the
two
men spent the night next to the druid ring, wrapped in each others
arms. Kerik struggled and fumbled with the tent. Nerves! he thought.
Must relax. He took a deep, calming breath.
It had been a busy night. He'd been helping Gotham in her quest
to
kill asps when news reached him that Adessa had been seen dancing
naked around Eizen. There was even a rumor they had made love. Kerik
was so angry he couldn't see straight. His first inclination had been
to leave Eizen. But his heart wouldn't let him. He needed Eizen. He
just hadn't been able to take that next step.
His brother, Jono, had almost convinced him to go ahead and ask
for Eizen's hand. But Jono had left Faydwer at an early age and
wasn't
aware of the class system -- wasn't aware of how the high elves
looked
down upon their woodland kin. To Jono, it was just a matter of
following one's heart. But to Kerik, it was more complicated. What
would Eizen's parents think? Wouldn't they abandon their son for
marrying a dirty wood elf? Did he really want to put Eizen in a
position to choose between him and his entire culture? And then there
was the fear of outright rejection. What is Eizen just didn't want to
marry him? Maybe he should just maintain the status quo, he had
thought.
Then Adessa came along. Kerik knew he had to act now or never.
After getting over his anger, he quickly began trying to find a ring.
After a long wait on what must have been the slowest Paladin in all
of
Norrath, Kerik was able to purchase a ring.
Now, if he could just get the tent set up. There. It was done.
And
Eizen was in it already and about to nod off. "Wait..." Kerik
implored. He had rehearsed this moment in his head so many times. He
would kneel. Then hand Eizen the ring and ask him to marry him. But
Eizen's sleepy eyes and Kerik's nervousness ruined his well rehearsed
plan.
Instead, he quickly dug around his pocket for the ring. It was
gone! Where could it be? Then he remembered... he was wearing the
Forman's Tunic Eizen had just given him and the ring was in his old
Lockjaw vest. He grabbed the ring and stretched out his shaking hand
to Eizen without saying a word. Eizen looked at the ring and saw it
was an engagement ring. Kerik looked into Eizen's eyes for some kind
of sign as to whether he was going to accept it or not. After what
seemed like forever, Eizen smiled as he took the ring and placed it
on
his finger.
"You took it," Kerik said surprised more than anything but filled
with joy too, as tears welled up in his eyes.
Eizen smiled back at him. "Weren't you supposed to kneel down on
one knee?"
Kerik shrugged and stared at the ground in embarrassment. "I was
too nervous and thought you were about to fall asleep."
Eizen wiped the tears from Kerik's eyes and they held each other
for the longest time.
