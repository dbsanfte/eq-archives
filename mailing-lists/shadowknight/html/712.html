Subject: VE vs Lifetap? and more on pet wars


well, replacing VE with some form of lifetap would be kind of a toss-up. the
advantage of VE is that you cast it ONCE, and just let it work.. therefore
saving you considerable mana over a spell which you must keep casting, and
can therefore be interrupted too. on the downside.. if you dont have godlike
dexterity, you cant really count on VE to do any more than add to your total
damage a bit; it is not very useful as a means of healing.

i guess my main reason why we shouldnt have lifetaps is that it would
greatly reduce our downtime between fights. me, an sk, arguing AGAINST less
downtime?? hold on, lemme explain :) as any SK who has reached a modest
level can probably attest, we are the damage dealers. we can outdamage a
paladin of same level without even trying, and can outdamage a ranger of
similar level more often than not. so.. if we were to have less downtime as
well as our naturally greater damage, we would be MUCH more effective than
either of the other two hybrid classes. that is called overbalancing.
judging by verant's past track record on this (sorry brian) they would fix
that overbalancing by either giving us a pathetically weak lifetap (which
would be pointless) OR they would reduce our combat effectiveness, which
would make me one unhappy beld.

i'm really not sure what to fill the void here if we dont want pets and
lifetap would be out of the question for the above reasons.. perhaps a VERY
weak damage shield series? an extra DOT or two? ultimately i could live
without a pet, as it really isnt in character for the character i want to
play anyway, i'm just stumbling around for ideas like the rest of you folks.

oh, and dravith, if you DON'T want a pet, why do you want me to toss you a
bone? 8) <--- bad everquest humor. beld needs a nap.
