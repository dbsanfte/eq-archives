Ah yes, good ole' HT. When compared to a paladin's LOH ability, HT simply
stinks. Let's take level 30, for example. If you are a paladin or SK at
level 30, you have about 700 or so hit points (being conservative). So, the
paladin is in a bad fight, he's down to 20 hp, and he hits LOH -- boom, he
just did a 700hp heal. Same situation, this time it's an SK. The SK is in a
bad fight, he's down to 20 hp, and he hits HT -- boom, he does 301hp of
damage and kills the mob (ideal scenario). Both get their specials once a
game day. So what's wrong with this picture? The paladin did 700, the SK did
300, THAT is the problem. If the paladin were to LOH a warrior friend,
there's 1000 hp heal. If the SK were to HT a different mob, he still does
300 at best.

So there are three possible solutions to this:

(1.) Make the HT do damage AND lifetap for that amount of damage. So in that
same situation, the SK does 300 in damage, and receives 300 as a heal. That
totals 600hp, STILL less than an LOH, and it gives us a real lifesuck. I
believe this is in the current eqss SK petition Beld mentioned (and I've
signed).

(2.) Allow the HT to be the EXACT reverse of the LOH, i.e. complete death.
Why is the paladin's special able to adjust to the target, but the SK's is
not? Let HT completely kill the mob. Admittedly, this is the least plausible
answer.

(3.) Since the HT does approximately half what an LOH does (that disparity
only increases with levels, by the way), let it have half the recharge time.
That way a level 30 SK gets a pair of 300hp HTs per day, and the paladin
still just gets one 700hp LOH.

Personally, I prefer option 1, with option 3 running a close 2nd... but
there's my brainstorm.
