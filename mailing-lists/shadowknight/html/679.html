You bet, buying weapons all the way. The nature of the EQ economy is such that you can usually obtain something through purchase or trade about 10 levels before you could reasonably obtain it yourself. There are several reasons for this for the SK class. 1) The weapon is the most important item for the SK, with armor breastplate coming in second. Hoarding money for that next weapon is common and you can always generate the money before you can generate the experience in order to take on the nasty mob yourself. 2) There really is nothing to do with your money but hoard it. There are exceptions: buying food, water, bandages, trade skill supplies, and items needed for quests, but in general in the mid-levels money just seems to accumulate. Buying armor or weapons from merchants is right out because it is just way too expensive (they botched the whole armor thing in my opinion, but I'll save that for another post). And there is only so much bronze that you can buy, because it is just too heavy. And anything besides armor and weapons are just not that crucial to your success. So, I just end up banking it all and watching my balance grow while I keep my eyes open for the big ticket items. 3) Spells are downright cheap for our level. Casters usually siphon off a lot of money for spells. That just doesn't seem to be much of an issue for SK's.

As far as the BBC, it is a sought after weapon, just not as much as some of the others. The AGI hit can easily be offset by some AGI boosting items. And of all the weapons available to a SK at that level, it does more damage per second than any of them. Plus, because of the AGI hit, the cost is usually low in comparision to the other options. This all adds up to making it an attractive purchase.

Scragg Wraithbone of Bristlebane (22nd level TSK)
