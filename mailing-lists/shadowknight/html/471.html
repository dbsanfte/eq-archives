as for tactics, try these:
do not RELY on HT to save you, but there are ways to make it more effective.
i use it in 3 main ways: to speed up the death of a relatively low level
thing in a fight with multiple monsters, to finish off something nasty that
is trying to get away, or as a massive taunt to pull a monster off a dying
party member. using it to save yourself is generally a panic reaction, and
rarely works as you want it to.. to get the most out of harm touch, i've
found that it works best if you hit the opponent from BEHIND, especially
while they are fleeing or under the effect of a fear spell. in this way, HT
is more like a melee attack than a magical attack. in this way i have layed
full damage on spellcasting npcs that are 5 or more levels higher than me,
usually severely screwing them over :) so no complaints that HT never works,
ya hear!

if you have to fight solo, always keep one of your darkness spells ready (if
you are lvl 15 yet), and always have your pet summoned, even tho it may seem
worthless. the trick is to use it for the occasional extra hits (which add
up) rather than as a tank for you. the pet also tends to give you a few
seconds of fleeing time if the battle goes badly. make sure it is under the
effects of a darkness spell (so he runs slow) and cast fear on it to make it
turn around and run. this will give you the chance to hit it in the back for
a while without retaliation. just be sure to NOT use this tactic in a
cramped dungeon unless you are VERY sure you know where the npc will flee
and that he doesnt have friends there :) you can also use the darkness spell
and archery to kite, but this is a rather gimpy and unreliable version of
kiting, and i dont reccomend it. (if ye want to kite, yer better off being a
druid anyway). if you use a shield and are fighting a spellcasting npc, dont
just bash constantly! wait until you see them start casting, THEN bash. if
it hits, you are guaranteed to break their concentration, unless they are so
high above you in levels that you are toast anyway ;)

because of your high downtime between fights, i personally like to group
whenever possible. and yes, SK DO have something to offer to groups. our
main role in group combat is, strangely, as a martyr tank: we are masters of
pissing off mobs and getting them off of other group members. between taunt,
disease cloud (they REALLY hate that one for some reason), fear, DOTs (which
the mobs ALSO really hate), and the ability to use big, damage-hogging
weapons, we are excellent at convincing the monster to ignore the wizard and
come after us instead. as i said before, HT also makes a great megataunt in
an emergency, as only several hundred points of instant DD can do. you just
have to remember 2 things about this strategy: ALWAYS group with a cleric,
or all that taunting is gonna get you killed real quick, and remember to
inform your nukers NOT to over exert themselves. if the spellcasters are
casting nuke after nuke without pause, they will quickly outdamage you and
you wont be able to taunt the monster back. so long as the spellcasters keep
their casting in moderation, you will be able to fend off the monster and
keep the caster alive.

thats about all i got for now, lemme know if you need any more specific info ;)
