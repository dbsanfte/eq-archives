I think you're in the right place.  Kill dervs in NRo/Oasis/SRo is my advice; the bronze weapons they drop should pay for plenty of food and water (and madmen drop 1-4 water each).  There are probably other places you can do just as well...but not a lot better.

 

-- Basic stuff in case this is your first char: --

 

Always fight at least blues (use /con to check out monsters).  At your level you can probably still solo most blues, but you're getting to the point where it will be more efficient to find partners.  Get in a group of similar level folks and kill dervs, which will most likely still be yellow or red.  When you get to about 14, it's time to find bigger stuff, the orcs and crocs in Oasis are popular.  After a few more levels you have several choices -- Lavastorm, Lake Rathe, South Karana...keep in mind, this is all from the perspective of a troll, so I tended to hang out closer to Grobb than, say, Qeynos.  I've never hunted in WC, for instance.

 

At 20-25, you can start hanging out in Cazic-Thule.  This is good because it's the only zone where decayed armor drops; this is the (no-drop) building blocks for your SK quest armor (Darkforge).  When you hit 30 and can invis yourself, you may be able to loot decayed from other folks' kills if you ask real nice and don't train mobs on them.

 

Even if you're fighting the right stuff, though, the rate at which you make levels drops off fast.  I think the exp per mob varies with mob level ^ 2, and the exp you need for a level is kinda like level ^ 4, which means that the differential for each new level goes as level ^ 3.  End result:  for each new level, you need to kill a number of monsters that's roughly proportional to your level -- and it gets tougher and tougher to kill mobs of your level, so you need to group with (and share the exp with) ever-larger groups.

 

(The math above may be off by a power one way or another, but the end result is correct, I believe.  There's a web site out there that explains it all, I forgot the URL.)

 

Also, SK's pay an experience penalty of (I think) 40% compared to other classes.  The good news is that when you're in a group, this penalty gets spread over the group...so group all you can.  =)

 

Basically, you'll just have to deal with not making levels as fast as you have so far, and you'll have to get smarter and network with more people just to continue your forward progress...but then again, that's part of the fun.

 

Hopefully some of this was useful.  Good luck.
