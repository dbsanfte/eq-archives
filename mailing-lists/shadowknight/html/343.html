There is nothing wrong with everyone that can being a master archer. I
wouldn't mind at all if that were so. However, I don't think that anyone who
wants to be a master archer should be able to go spend 8cp and 3 hours and
become one. If you want to be a master archer, you should have to spend as
much time on it as you do any other combat skill. Why should archery be any
different? And as for professional fletchers... they still make a decent
living as it is. They save quite a bit on arrows over the long run, can have
more than 1 pt damage arrows, are the first to get the better bows, and they
still sell some bows for a decent amount (Spoke with someone yesterday who
had just sold an Ash bow for 32pp). Every other skill in this game,
especially combat skills, takes a lot of time and work to raise. Archery is
a very viable weapon, most people just don't have the patience to do it
because at a low skill it is not very viable. You need to work on your skill
and purchase some more powerful bows before it becomes a useful and viable
combat skill.

As for archery and the auto-attack ... as far as I have found there is not.
I have archery on a hot-key myself, and just have to switch off auto-attack
and wait for the round time to end before using my bow. It can be a bit
annoying, but at least the way I use the bow in a support role, this is not
generally a problem. Bow's are used for pulling, for turning around and
shooting, for chipping away at a mob while hurt in a group situation, and
sometimes when they run. These generally do not require much auto-attack as
non of them last that long. Archery does go up, but only when in combat with
exp giving creatures. The best way to get archery up is to either use your
own friends, or find a group that will let you, and just stand off to the
side and let fly with a shower of arrows during combat. Also, go buy a
quiver as, especially while training, you will use arrows like they were
water. Good luck with your arching, it is a great skill to have if you have
the patience.

Sincerely,
Jeremy
