I'd just like to introduce myself real quick first, as I just joined this
list yesterday. My name is Jeremy and I play Virten, a 12th level Human
Shadowknight on the Solusek Ro server who worships Innoruuk. Just thought
I'd add my thoughts to this discussion.

I'd have to say that I agree with what Brian stated about there being a "too
bad" philosophy for playing evil or more difficult characters. Unlike Brian,
I knew what I was getting into when I chose a Human Shadowknight. It was
mainly for RP'ing purposes. And since we were warned about this race/clsss
difficulty, there is little to complain about there. I never expected to be
a "super character," just hoped to be able to fight and be on the same level
as others. So far, I've been pretty happy with my character. Although I have
been going through some of the level 10-12 duldrums that others have
described. Level 9 was great, new spells to play with, etc., but level 11
and 12 have been really slow.

Party-wise, I have had some of the same problems grouping with "pure
magic-users" as described previously, but my normal party I have no problems
with. This is a friend of mine from work and a group of his friends that
were already a group and took me in as a 7th (actually, 6th, since one
person rarely plays and is far behind) player. It consists of an DE
Enchanter, a DE Rogue, 2 DE Shadow Knights (one with shield, one with
2-hander) and a Troll Shaman. With the addition of a third Shadow Knight, we
are quite a force. We could probably have switched one of the SK's to a more
pure magician (i.e. Wizard) but the person that dropped out was a Wizard and
as I joined late, this was unforseen. Even so, we have little trouble with
anything that is not at least four or five levels above us. The enchanter's
pet is quite a tank, there are three skeletons on big guys, dishing out some
extra damage. The enchanter can cast "whirl 'till you hurl," the Shaman has
a new spell, something strike I think, which hits for about 75 damage, and
the rogue can backstab for 42 points a round, while the three shadow knights
hit for 17 per round, drain strength, and can keep the beasties off the
others with taunt and dis cloud. Add to this the shaman's healing ability
and Spirit of the Wolf (and ability to tank, being a troll, if he has to)
and we're a formidable party.

Also a quick note on leveling: I have actually not had any sort of problem
with the speed of gaining levels with my Human Shadow Knight. When I first
joined this group, I was 5 levels behind. Since then, with a little bit of
time on my own but not a great deal, I have caught up to the Shadow Knights
at level 12, while the rogue is now at level 15 (enchanter at 14 and Troll
Shaman at 14). Having a healer in the group helps get experience quickly,
too

Enough of my novel. Well, one more thing. For RP'ing, I have found my
character to be excellent. I live in Neriak and constantly find myself
defending my religion and beliefs against those dark elves who don't think
anyone should be in Neriak except Dark Elves. Through perserverance,
however, I have carved my niche there, and now, I actually have some Dark
Elves come to my aid whenever I am accosted by a DE who has yet to meet me.
Much more fun than living in Freeport where everyone is the same and I find
very few RP'ers.

Later,
Jeremy

> There is a large philosophical discussion here as to whether certain
> classes/races should, in fact, be "easier" or "harder" than others by
> virtue of their abilities, by virtue of their faction, or if it
> should "all
> balance out".
>
> For example, some classes will be very "easy" to play -- good
> human cleric,
> for example -- since they generally aren't KOS anywhere (except by evil
> NPCs, which are much less frequently encountered than good NPCs), level
> quickly, and stay alive for a while.
>
> On the other hand, there are the very difficult classes/races,
> e.g. a troll
> SK, that levels very slowly, is fairly weak at early levels, has a very
> difficult time travelling due to KOS issues, etc.
>
> So, do you "bump up" the latter so that they can compete in the game given
> their faction disparity, or do you just say "too bad, they're inherently
> more difficult". I tend towards the "too bad" philosophy -- playing evil
> SHOULD be more difficult, however it shouldn't be so difficult that it's
> absurdly so.
>
> I don't think there is a wrong or right answer to this. I think some
> attempt should be made to balance all classes WITHOUT taking into account
> faction, frequency of guild headquarters, etc. After the classes are
> balanced in a vacuum, then I think difficulty should be factored in by
> virtue of guild availability and faction. All else being equal, warriors
> have the advantage of being a non-evil class and also having guild
> headquarters in nearly every city. By the same token, evil shamans
> probably have a huge disadvantage due to poor faction and rarity of
> training locations/spell locations. Anything that promotes travel is
> inherently dangerous, unless you have invis and/or SotW.
>
> Once again, I would like to reiterate that while my SK frustrated me, I
> think it was for two main reasons, both largely my fault: I was playing as
> a "tank" in a party with two other magic users; I was not role-playing
> "evil". As a tank, I would have been better off with more warrior skills
> (like dodge at a lower level, kick, parry, and dual wield) since my necro
> jr. skills weren't nearly as relevant as our party's necro spells. Thus I
> should have been more "task oriented" (and in retrospect a
> barbarian shaman
> would have been better for that party). Also, since I wasn't role-playing
> evil, I was suffering a significant difficulty disadvantage
> unwittingly and
> unnecessarily.
>
> SKs can make sense, especially for solo characters. I still think they
> could be slightly improved so that SKs don't go through the 9th level
> "blahs" and are more genuinely useful.
>
> Brian
