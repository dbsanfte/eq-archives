> Okay, here's a question for you folks. What if Shadow Step and
> Voice Graft were replaced with some kind of lifetap spells? Would
> this be grossly unbalancing or hurt the class?

Personal Opinion but I would be upset if either of these spells were
removed. Especially Shadow Step. Shadow Step has saved my life on countless
occasions. It is fast casting and often gives me just enough of a headstart
to escape in an outdoor situation.

As for Voice Graft. Granted, it has no PRACTICAL (i.e. exp. gaining) use. My
feeling is this however -- it has wonderful RP uses and this is an RP game
after all. When I reach 30 I plan on using Voice Graft to set up some
interesting quests with my friends. Yes, it is quite easy to just give a
quest yourself, but how much more involving would it be if say a town guard
gave the quest out? I have a feeling that it will be gone by the time I ever
reach 30 (23 for 2 months, now been 24 for a month).

My feeling on lifetaps is that, if the past examples are followed, they
would be a waste because when we normally get spells, they are highly nerfed
versions of necro spells. Vamp Emb doing 12 at 22? Necros have it do 74 at
20 (According to Stephen aka Morgul the black). What would this mean that
our lifetap spells do? Not a lot. And as Thomas pointed out, then we have to
disengage in battle while casting and they are slow, meaning either we get
interrupted a couple of times while casting or step off and let our comrades
take a beating. All this for how many HP's? Probably not many. And how much
damage are we LOSING in melee during that period? Not to say that lifetaps
couldn't be made viable, but following the similar path that our other
spells have I do not believe that simply throwing us a lifetap will make
much of a difference when you consider all of the variables. What it would
allow us to do is solo better (fight, back off while pet gets a hit and
lifetap or at end of battle, lifetap some wussy stuff) but since we aren't
supposed to solo (and I personally hate soloing, just too dang boring) this
is a moot point.

Other random thoughts on this discussion that have passed through my mind as
I have read.

Reclaim Energy: Useless if you use it the same way a magician uses it ... to
get a better pet. HOWEVER, in the right situation it is a GREAT spell. In a
long battle when you are casting a lot, have a pet up beforehand and have
reclaim energy memmed. When you get low on mana, reclaim your pet. Your pet
has done a bit of damage and, well, now you just got three free bubbles of
mana. Think of it as a walking (if you are 30, talking) mana storage
facility, that happens to fight for you on the side.

Disease Cloud: 5 dmg but what many people don't realize is that this is an
excellent taunt. Even more effective than our taunt skill. Combine the two
and you have a very powerful ability to keep foes off you. When fighting
with wizzies and such a bit higher than me I put this on my main keypad and
constantly hit it (right after swinigng my weapon) along with taunt. I've
kept foes off wizzies 8 levels higher than me with this.

Mend Bones: Hmmm. No, I really have hardly used this spell. Only times I
have have been when trying to solo.

Endure Cold: I really don't mind this. If you ever duel a shaman, be sure to
have this up. You will wipe the floor with him and he will be confused. :)

Shadow Step: Hasn't been mentioned much except just now by Brian but I love
this spell. It is always memmed (except when in tight dungeons where it is
useless) and I keep it on my "emergeny pad" right next to Harm Touch.
Shift-5, 5, 6 equals Harm Touch and Shadow Step. Also quite convenient to
get down from the top platform on the aviak tower quickly. Try it sometime
if you don't use it.

Numb the Dead: Great spell with a minor problem. It uses bone chips. For the
infrequent amount that I use this spell, I'm not going to carry an extra
stack of bone chips around for this, rendering it all but useless unless I'm
near a newbie zone where someone is selling bone chips which is never.

Darkness Line: Simply put, it is wonderful. I'm sure all SK's agree.

Dark Empathy: Not extremely useful in a full group but I have saved two
lives with it (both of which ended up meaning I saved my own life too as I
would have died without their help). I usually keep it memmed at this time
for lack of more effective spells. And it works great on newbies.
