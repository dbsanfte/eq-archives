Thank you, thank you, thank you....this is a burning issue that never seems to be addressed by Verant. If any of you actually listen to this board, hear me. DO NOT DO QUESTS IN EQ, UNLESS YOU WANT TO WASTE YOUR TIME AND EXPERIENCE. Ok, I admit it. I still do quests too, even though they suck. This is a great example how bad apples can spoil the bunch. My perception is that Verant seriously destroyed the value of doing quests because people (in Beta or whatever) were farming the quests for exp. and loot. Problems with the quests:

1) You have no idea what level you should be in order to complete them. Time and again, I have started a quest only to find that the mob I needed to kill at the end of the quest conned DEEP red to me. That equals wasted time for my efforts.

2) No clues are given on variable spawning NPC's and mobs. If the mob or NPC is not at the location mentioned by the quest giver 100% of the time, the player needs to have clues as to when they will actually be around or if they are maybe at an alternate location. Stomping around Innothule swamp for 5 hours trying to find the Spore Guardian only to discover that he's a rare spawn that is only around once a week or so and you have no idea when he will spawn next is NOT fun.

3) Poor rewards. Finishing a quest in and of itself is a reward, but not having an extra kick at the end for your hard work really dilutes the experience. Imagine you just finished a quest that took you 5 hours of game time of running around, finding the info on the quest, waiting for the spawn, and gathering people to help you if needed. You complete the quest. Ding, you recieve imperceptible amount of experience, 2 gold, and a rusty mace. Uh...ok, glad I finally completed it, but I'll never do that again. It kills the whole process.

4) Faction hits. Faction hits for actions are so binary (when it happens, it happens, and is irreversible), especially for evils who have little or no way to raise faction once you're to the KoS level. There needs to be some type of warning for the player. Let's face it, the player has no way of knowing what the faction hit is until the action is complete, whether that be completing a quest or killing a monster.
