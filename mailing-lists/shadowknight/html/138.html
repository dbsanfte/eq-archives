Some more food for thought:

SK pets are actually useful for distracting an enemy while you cast spells
at it. BUT, this assumes you're soloing and thus not tanking for some real
spellcasters.

In a party situation we go after yellows as much as possible, and most
yellows destroy my pet in three or four swings, making the pet useless.
However, in a solo situation I would go after blues and whites mostly, and
then the pet might actually make a difference and be good.

The SK is probably a good solo class for those reasons, and as such isn't
as big a pile as I like to think :-) however I do believe that as a member
of a party it's very difficult for the SK to contribute meaningfully.

The big problem that makes an SK difficult to solo is the lack of healing,
not even life tap. I think that this would actually make role play really
nice -- you're weak, so you wander the lands sucking the life from weaker
creatures to repair your wounds. Very Elric.

So it boils down to this: the SK is a reasonable solo class, since you're
very self sufficient except for healing (and bandage wounds is _useless_).
So you waste a lot of time just sitting around healing. As a party member,
the SK is tantamount to useless.

Most people that are high on the SK class think of it in terms of soloing
at high levels, and possibly a 50th level SK may be the baddest mofo in
Norrath, but I don't play this game to solo.

I guess I should just be a bard and quit whining. :-)
