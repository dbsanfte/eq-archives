Brian Hook wrote:

> From: bwh@... (Brian Hook)
>
> I've finally hung up the spurs on my 10th level human SK. I've just
> reached the conclusion the SK is way underpowered vs. just about any other
> class out there, specifically in party situations.
>
> Ignoring the HT issue completely, the SK is basically (to paraphrase a
> comment I saw elsewhere) "a weak warrior with necromancer parlor tricks".
> The fundamental issue, to me, is that by the time the SK gets spells, those
> spells are underpowered and useless for the types of foes we're fighting at
> that level. The same things for the pet. It bothers me that I'm expected
> to tank for my pet. What's even worse is that I fight in a party all the
> time, and the SK is probably one of the most useless party characters there
> are -- I party with a 12th level necro and a 12th level wizard, and I'm
> pretty much a nuisance in most combats. The necro's pet is just a complete
> bad ass -- does more damage than me, attacks better, and, best of all, is
> expendable. The pet doesn't get diseased and is basically free.
>
> We took out the giant plague rat at Blackburrow, and it was immensely
> demoralizing. Because of its destructive capabilities, I couldn't get near
> it (since we didn't have a cleric to cure disease). So the necro's pet
> tanked, and the necro and wizard cast spells and toasted it quickly and
> cleanly. I did _nothing_ during that battle. If I had meleed, I would
> have died (or been diseased -- it was doing 32 points per hit too), my pet
> was killed in _two hits_, and my spells were a joke compared to the
> wizard/necro spells. So I basically cowered in a corner while the other
> party members did the real damage.
>
> So I'm going to switch to a monk or a warrior, i.e. a "pure" warrior class,
> and not suffer through the limitations of the SK. The only thing I'll give
> up that I'll miss is the HT, and gaining dual wield, kick, bash, mend, and
> more hp seems to be a very good trade off for a weak pet and limited spells.
>
> I recognize that while no particular character class is going to be "the
> best", I think that hybrid classes should gain the strengths of multiple
> classes and possibly pick up a few weaknesses/limitations along the way.
> What I've finally realized with the SK is that it picks up some strengths
> of the warrior, but the necro skills that it acquires are basically useless
> and don't outweigh the loss of kick, dual wield, earlier dodge, etc. I'm
> not nearly as good a tank as a warrior or monk, and I'm laughable as a
> spell caster compared to any other pure spell caster. As a combination of
> the two, I've lost a lot of benefits of being a pure class, but I don't
> seem to have picked up anything in exchange (but HT, which is underpowered).
>
> I don't believe these same arguments apply to paladins/rangers, since their
> spells are actually useful.
