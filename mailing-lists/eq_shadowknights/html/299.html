Before I continue any further, let me say that I do not believe the SK is
overly weak. Being a hybrid with downtime equaling a Warrior, obviously,
seem unfair. But other than the downtime and the useless, class-specific
skill (HT), I believe that the SK is one of the most interesting class to
play. In fact, I would go on to say that we can do the most damage out of
all melee classes in a fight with an NPC (Double Attack + Pet + DoT = mega
damage). I also believe that it is not too overly important to travel before
level 30. For many levels, I have never needed to travel anywhere that would
kill me on sight. There are always places to fight up to level 30; at which
point, we do actually get Gather Shadows. Now, I will address Mr. Stovall's
post:

Scenario 1:
What is the first DoT that the Shadow Knight would use? Engulfing Darkness.
What is the first spell the Paladin would use? Flash of Light. Now, let's
assume they both go off. No resists, no fizzles, no interruption. The SK is
blinded, the Paladin is free to kill the pet. Once the pet is gone, Paladin
blinds the Shadow Knight again, repeat until SK is dead.

Scenario 2:
Let's assume that Engulfing Darkness is casted upon the Paladin. The Paladin
would just fight the SK and the pet. The pet's attack = Center + Spirit
Armor. So, it is basically down to LH versus HT + DoT. And as we all know,
HT and a 100HP DoT does not compensate for LH.

Scenario 3:
Let's consider what you said in your scenario. The pet is gone and Paladin
has taken more damage. Is the Shadow Knight able to get the Paladin down to
the point where the HT will defeat the Paladin? No. It might have took 5
rounds at most to finish off the pet; thus, the Paladin is far from being
dead (during that time, the Paladin might have lost 200HP at most -
considering that all the arrows hit and DoT). Once the pet is gone, the
battle turns over. All the Paladin needs to do is to Blind, Root and Heal.
That was excluding Center and Spirit Armor.

Personally, I do not see why the Paladin would go for the SK when he/she
knows that the pet is the main disrupter of spells. In addition, you failed
to consider that a Paladin can also have a bow. With the combination Blind,
Root, shoot and Heal, the Paladin is always one step ahead of the Shadow
Knight in fights. Basically, the reason why a Paladin will defeat a SK is
because of a few simply spells.

As for winning unrigged fights with red-cons, I really find that hard to
believe. Red-cons would resisted Darkness, Fear and Harm Touch. The Harm
Touch will not compensate for their LH at that point. Furthermore, at higher
levels, they are able to hit more often with higher damage; while taking
less hits. I can see a Shadow Knight defeating red-con Warriors and Monks.
However, looking at all the above factors makes me disbelieve that a Paladin
can lose to a blue-formidable-con Shadow Knight at best. Perhaps you have a
strategy that I do not see? Or perhaps the Paladin was on the way to
retrieve his/her corpse?
