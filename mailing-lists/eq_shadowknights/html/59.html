Subject: IMBALANCES: READ - Some insights


I have played through many classes and have found the SK to be relatively
weak in comparison to every other class. First of all, it is the HT. Some
argued that it would completely imbalance the game if it does not have a
saving throw; this is NOT TRUE. Think of it this way:

Paladin (level 20) - 500HP
Shadow Knight (level 20) - 500HP

Now, the Shadow Knight's HT does 201 damage - not even half of the Paladin's
life. This is a once a day ability that can either be resisted or partially
resisted. On the other hand, the Paladin can Lay Hands anytime to recover
all 500HP. Perhaps I'm missing something or there is some form of
mathematical derivation to this, but from what I see, 201 /= 500.

Then one would argue that we have pets, but I can also say that the Paladins
get buffs and heals. What are Shadow Knights reduced to? A 5 point damage
Diease Cloud and a strength stealing spell that has no noticable effect
(other than allowing one to carry 10 more weigh units?). We have to sit for
over 20 minutes after each fight to recover. On level 15, our spell is still
inferior to the Paladins. What other hybrids have to sit so long? Paladins
and Rangers can heal themselves right up. In addition, we get KOS and have
less places to travel to to get experience. Although, it is stated that
playing a SK would be of high difficulty, but as mentioned above, we should
get invisibility at some point.

In conclusion, we are simply a warrior with a experience penalty. Sure, we
got HT and all the pets in the world. But does that add up to the kicks,
dual wield and double attack damage (oh, don't forget that berserker's rage)
that the warrior does? The answer is obvious - NO. If we get the Lifetap
series and a 100% functioning HT, then this class might be comparible to the
other classes. Until then, I will stick with my SK class just because I look
cool as a warrior (with a experience penalty) with a pet.
