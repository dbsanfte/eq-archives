On another note.

In response to earlier posts, I am no traitor to the Tier'Dal. Although on
Rallos Zek, the mighty rule the land. A weaker Shadow Knight with a weapon
such as the Dark Reaver would definitely be a target to many of the raw PK
(Player Killer) guilds, as the weapon would sell for several hundred plat
simply because lower Guk is HELL and no one deserves the pain involved in
camping it.

I am a member of the Blood Coven on Rallos Zek, a guild of DE females sworn
to defend DE purity. I have killed many Tier'Dal traitors and I have seen
the item lust and covetous nature of many players. I was simply and
realistically stating that you must be prepared to defend your wealth and
yourself.

I just cannot comprehend a Role Play world where I can carry around my best
gear all of the time and never worry about High Elf raiders seeking blood
retribution or Dwarven clansmen initiating their young on my dark blood.
You want to play Shadow Knights? Play one on Rallos Zek, I dare you. You
will never know what evil races can do until you have seen Darkenbane and
the Horde ( DEs, Ogres, and Trolls) grind their way through all of the
players and many of the guards ( sometimes merchants ) in West Freeport.
The human newbee zone is a graveyard.

THIS is why I am a Dark Elf, I am the Vanguard of evil. The Dark Tower in a
world of light. Neriak and The Nectulos forest are inviolate, as they were
meant to be. Many players on the server are poor but NO ONE can say that we
haven't earned every copper.

Tell you a story.

You just don't know what it means to be an evil race until you zone into
East Commons from Nectulos, and stand face to face with a six-pack of 16th
level High Elf wizards...powering up. The knock back saved my life, as I
zoned once again, with 18 life. Shadow Step put over half the zone between
them and me with three leaps and the alarm went out. The response was
staggering. Newbies zoned into the commons to be spotters since they could
not be attacked and a respectable force collected, zoned, and beat their
ass. They never returned and two of the bodies are still there in the dirt,
twisted by a half dozen harm touches. Lucky for me a Freeport and Northern
Ro raid was forming that day, as scheduled, and I caught their muster.

Such is life on Rallos Zek, war and hate made real. The difference between
my server and ALL of the others is RESPECT. You respect your people, or
they hunt you into deletion. You respect your enemies
( and you have a bunch ) or they will over run you. Guild tags begin to
mean something, typically the difference between relief and a rapid retreat
or the bagging of your favorite thingies. Weight allowance?? BAH! I keep
two backpacks empty incase I come across a pair of frisky halfling Druids or
for ANY excursion into East and West Nam...er...Commons. I have come across
extremely rare loot that I outright sold wile thinking " By the time I can
defend this item, I can camp for it myself."

On Rallos Zek, an equipment inspection is often followed by a fire ball
instead of a "cool gear" compliment.

I just cannot explain the experiences on Rallos Zek with words.

You will quickly realise that friends and race are more important than
stats and encumbrance. The mages in my party hold my loot, and all of the
good armour and weapons goes to us tanks since we keep them alive. I
haven't cared about weight since level 9.
