Why the SK?
I feel the SK represents a sinister and ferocious warrior who draws her
power from the blackest Abyss. A Knight of such fearsome calliber as to be
the ONLY warrior class Mages are concerned with in PvP. Skilled at arms and
Necromancy, the Shadow Knight and good 'ol Gebeker (hehe), tear into mages
and monsters with great ease.
Soon my pet will dual wield, kick, AND bash. With me beside it casting
Fear and Engulfing Darkness, few escape my wrath. When the tables turn and
the enemy is too great, I unleash the Hand of Innorruk!!! Thus returning
the battle to MY favor! Still not enough? The pet will hold my retreat and
Shadow Step will deny ANYONE the benefit of my death (people, this has saved
me SO many times!). Why play a Shadow Knight?? Ask the other players why
they ARE NOT!!

There are obvious roleplay challenges and benefits. Up untill level 15, the
going was VERY rough as I am extremely interactive with other players. But
understand this, the difficulties of our youth fuel our hate. Our hate
feeds Innorruk and He in turn, and in due time, empowers us to be the
vessels of wrath and chaos we were meant to be.

Patience young Reapers, night WILL fall on Norrath.
