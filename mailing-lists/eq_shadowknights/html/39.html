Okay, not to sound like a jerk, but as a game developer I have some pretty
strong opinions about the "right" way to get your pleas/requests/opinions
heard. So I don't intend to single you out, however I will use your e-mail
as an example of how NOT to send an e-mail to someone that probably gets
hundreds a day.

As a preface, keep in mind that someone like John Smedley probably doesn't
have time to read all his e-mail, so he probably goes through and sifts
through his in-box looking for the intelligent sounding ones and
ignores/deletes the ones that sound like rants.

>Dear John,
>
>I know probably coming from a lowly gamer like me.. to a president such as
>yourself, this doesnt mean much.

Okay, you're starting off by establishing that his opinions/time are more
important than yours. You do this by saying you're a "lowly gamer", and at
the same time you acknowledge that he is a president and that you believe
that your status as a gamer lowers his

This causes a huge hodge podge of emotions to kick in: he will likely feel
some amount of condescension towards yourself, since it's obvious you don't
feel that your opinions are as valid as his, and at the same time he will
feel defensive because you've already painted him to be someone that's
aloof and out of touch with the common gamer (i.e. the people that pay his
salary).

So my suggestion would have been to just delete that introductory sentence.

>But I'm pretty deep in gaming myself. I used
>to be a moderator for DWANGO back in its day, I run www.mngamers.com, help
>out with www.quake3rocks.com, and I am starting the mwgl.net.

Stating credentials is another sign of insecurity. He probably doesn't
care all that much who you are, but probably does care about what you have
to say. To save him some time, just jump to the meat of your message.
Dispense with the "I'm relevant because..." paragraph because you're also
inadvertently trying to make yourself look more important than the Joe
Gamer that doesn't run some Web sites.

So my suggestion would be to rephrase the above or delete it altogether (a
perfect place to put those Web sites would be your .sig, since it's context
he might find valuable).

>I know a lot when it comes to games, and one thing is to listen to the
>gamers.

You're once again telling him why he should pay attention. Unfortunately,
you're doing so in a manner that seems both defensive ("I know a lot") and
confrontational (the implication is that they don't listen to gamers).

> A huge discussion going on within the mailing list is the short hand
>we as shadow knights get when it comes to spells. I have copied a recent mail
>that I will past in quotes.

If you're taking the time to write a message, and if he's taking the time
to read the message, then you should probably grammar and spell check it.
And there are MUCH larger discussions about these same issues occuring on
the messageboards of eqvault and stratics.

Do not assume that you and your friends/list members have some insight far
beyond everyone else. In all likelihood, the opinions that you have have
been heard many times before.

>Many many mails have been sent and recieved like
>this, and I totally agree.

Volume doesn't equate to rightness, and concepts like "many many" are
meaningless to someone that sees more e-mail traffic in a day than most
people see in a few weeks. Whether YOU agree with the many e-mails is also
irrelevant, since he doesn't know who you are.

The important thing here is to establish an _argument_. State your case,
not the reasons that you are relevant, meaningful, and should be heard.

>I feel we should get a better healing spells, and
>much more powerful attacking spells. We are suppose to be the "paladin
>opposers" ..hardly..

You make an opinion, but don't back it up with any supporting statements.
This is meaningless to him. Following up with "hardly" is an unnecessary
slap that only turns him off.

Instead of quoting someone else's e-mail, sit down and figure out ALL the
issues that SKs have, including choice of spells, lack of healing
capability, lack of basic helpful spells that other classes count on (heal,
better damage, and invis), lack of a kick/dual wield, and all of this
amplified by the evil nature of the class. Write these up in a rational,
non-inflammatory manner, then send it in or post it for comment from others.

Check out the posts on General messageboard by Bathory, she makes some
really cogent arguments that are intelligent, thoughtful, and non-inflammatory.
