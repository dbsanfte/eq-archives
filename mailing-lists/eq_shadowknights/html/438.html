Understand this though, tanks rule the game as the players all age. A party
with no combat class or inadequate combatants will die and die often. If
you don't believe me, do Cazic Thule or Guk with a bad tank:caster ratio and
see how many time you dash for the zone.

Contrary to popular belief, clerics and shaman are NOT tanks. Warriors
CANNOT stop a fleeing mob. Root is NOT a reliable means of ensuring the mob
does not flee(very valuable in dungeons), and when rooted the creature turns
and keeps fighting. So, what does that leave you with? A Ranger or an Sk.
Ok the Ranger is a great class, no doubt about it. Snare kicks butt and
they have that nifty dual attack and wield which makes mobs insane with
aggro. We (Sks) have the darkness series up to Dooming Darkness. It can be
cast during the battle to soften the mob, during the escape to slow it (and
it doesn't turn to hit anyone), AND it does damage the whole while.
Combined with fear you have a slow moving beast that is doomed to die. Dump
enough damage into it and it will be in flee mode when the fear wares off,
if not then just repeat.

Fear+darkness works great on large outdoor lethal mobs that the party may
not be high enough to handle but wants to engage: Kodiaks, Griffawns, Giant
Skells, Treants.
Couple that with the LOW aggro caused by your slow yet heavy hitting weapon
and you have a high probability that the creature will turn to the taunt of
a PET over you or a caster if done right! Group still doesn't want you?
Then they're dumb because the true talent of the Sk is mob CONTROLL and we
just get better at it as we age.
