>After Xillith posted the weakness of the Shadow Knights, I would like to
>address the imbalances of the Shadow Knight class again. Hoping to see a
>change in this class in every patch, I do not see any upgrade anytime soon.
>A long time ago, I remember having a debate with someone about Shadow
>Knights and how they are weak compared to a Paladin. However, they truly
>believe that the SK are balanced - which is not true. First of all, many
>other classes, who do not know the SK's spells, believe that the SK spell
>list consist of Lifetap, but that is simply not true. 'You got Vampiric
>Embrace' they would retort, but is that a valid point? The answer is 'no'.
>The reason is that VE is an unreliable source of Lifetap. It only goes off
>periodically (about once every 15 attacks). In addition, it only drains
>12HP
>+ (2/2 levels beyond 22). And thus, giving a BIG 20HP drain at level 30
>every 30 seconds or so. Now, with that aside, I would like to compare the
>Paladin and the Shadow Knight in a fight. Consider the following:
>
>30th level Shadow Knight
>- 301 HT
>- Engulfing Darkness, Heat Blood, Shadow Step, Siphon Strength, Fear
>- Full plate with BBC
>- 755HP
>- ~650AC
>- Pet casted
>
>30th level Paladin
>- LH
>- Stun, Root, Healing
>- Center and Spirit Armor on
>- Full plate with BBC
>- 755+HP
>- ~650+AC
>
>As we can see, Paladins will have an HP and AC advantage already. In
>addition, they get Root and Blind, which they can use strategically to heal
>themselves during the battle. They can also use the Root/Blind tactic to
>get
>free hits or to finish the pet in a few attacks. So, what can a SK do while
>they are blinded? Nothing. How can a SK hit the Paladin when rooted? The SK
>cannot. So, let's ASSUME the SK can get the Paladin down to half health -
>Will the Paladin need to use LH? The answer is NO, because the HT does not
>even compensate for half their HP.
>
>Many of you know that Engulfing Darkness and Fear gets resisted more than
>30% at even fights, higher on yellows and 90% resisted on reds. Now, with
>the resistable HT, SKs are basically left being a Warrior with faction and
>exp penalty. While a Paladin can assure safety with LH, HT is completely
>unreliable and useless. Remember back at level 1, HT used to finish a rat
>easily? At level 30 or so, 301 damage DOES NOT even put a dent in their HP
>bar. Note, however, that the damage/level ratio of the HT gets worse and
>worse.
>
>In the end, I believe that Paladins SHOULD be able to defeat a SK in a
>fight
>simply because they are there to rid the world of SKs. But having a weak
>and
>resistable HT, the inability to heal, exp penalty AND bad factions, I
>believe that Verant should at least give the SK the Lifetap series and a
>better HT.
