>First of all, I would like to give an apology to Michael Wooldirdge for
>being so harsh in my previous post regarding the different races. I was
>just a bit annoyed at all those people flaming me for my hard work towards
>earning the Dark Reaver.
>So, to clear up the 'best race' debate, I would like to do the following:
>I will outline the basic battle strategy of the fights with spectres to
>demonstrate the importance of mana at later levels. Let's assume that there
>is a Ranger, Shadow Knight, Cleric and Necromancer in a group. Now, the
>first step is to summon as many pets as possible. Then one would fire up
>the Jboots (for a SoW effect). The Ranger will then Ensnare, the SK and
>Necro will throw the DoT spells on the spectre. The pets are also sent in
>to attack the spectre as it approaches. Now, the cleric would use the Ward
>spell on the spectre; and obviously, the spectre will start going for the
>cleric. At this point, the cleric would run around in big circles to allow
>for the DoT to take effect, while the Ranger, SK and the pets will follow
>after the spectre to hit it from behind. When cleric goes oom, the SK and
>Necro will take the cleric's place and use DoT spells until they are oom.
>When everyone is oom, then one would engage in melee. Because the spectre
>hits for an insane amount of damage, one would like to stay off melee as
>long as possible. And thus, one can clearly see that having more mana is
>more beneficial than to have hit points and strength.
>In addition, there are more reasons why DE are better. With their high
>intelligence, they are able to max out their skills faster than any other
>races. And since it requires more time to raise skill at higher levels, it
>is advantageous to have a higher intelligence to make sure that all stats
>are maxed before the next level. Sure, I agree that high str and sta allows
>you to fight better. But since skills are also related to how well you hit
>and how well you take damage, having high intelligence indirectly affects
>how well your character fights. To me, the high skills of the Dark Elf
>would negate the high str and sta of an Ogre; thus, balanced. The fact that
>DEs just looks cool with blackened armor and the Dark Reaver, already beats
>out any stats the Ogres and Trolls have.
