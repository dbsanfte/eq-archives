One of my main concerns about the SK class is their inability to heal. The
only way that I see, is through the use of the Lifetap series. I believe
that Verant took out the Lifetap series for the SK because of the Troll's
regeneration. Verant think that if the Troll can regen and Lifetap, it could
create an ultimate class. However, Troll's natural regeneration is not fast
and they take a worse faction hit than Dark Elves. Thus, making the SK class
weak due to the Troll regeneration simply makes other race/class
combinations tough to play.

One can argue that there are tactics to prevent the SK from getting hit. But
these tactics are mana consuming and might not work all the time. Thus,
getting hit is unavoidable. And along with getting hit, there are big
downtimes.

Then others will raise the point that one must find a group at higher
levels. I agree completely. However, after reading a post elsewhere, I found
a counter point to this - would a group want a SK who can use Voice Graft or
a Paladin that can cast Greater Heal (~300+HP heal) at level 39? A Paladin,
who can act very well as a secondary healer, is in fact, more desirable in a
group. While SKs are still doing 40 some odd damage at level 38 with their
Ward Undead, a Paladin can be doing over 100 with Expulse Undead.

Although, there wasn't a point to this message, but it's some facts that I
thought I should share.
