With as often as failure occurs, I sometimes lose money off of the formula I use
(which is more expensive than double), but other than those occasions, it's
good. I'm still afraid to accept orders for leggings ever since I failed 14
times in a row *shudder*. That was a dark day at the forge. My formula is the
basic cost, multiplied by three, rounded, modified by up to +/- 3 plat for
difficulty/demand. That formula yielded the following:

Gorget: 4 plat
Mask: 9 plat
Helmet: 12 plat
Bracer: 5 plat
Sleeves: 10 plat
Mantle: 11 plat
Mail: 16 plat
Cloak: 16 plat
Belt: 10 plat
Leggings: 15 plat
Boots: 10 plat

I rarely don't make a sale off of these prices. They are standard or lower on
the E'Ci server and I do sometimes lose money by providing these for so cheap.
It's not hard to fail twice on a cloak. That's practically breaking even. The
fear of any smith is selling the full banded suit for a discount price. I sold
one for 110 plat... curse banded leggings!! I lost a total of 45 plat making
that full suit and selling for only 110 :) Don't set your prices too low or
you'll find smithing is NOT worth your time because of the minor prices.
Remember: you can ALWAYS lower your price and you can NEVER raise it. Always get
responses like "he's selling cheaper..." or "but you gave so-and-so a cheaper
price than that" or "that's not what you said yesterday". Trust me, don't sink
too low. Keep the standards high!! Banded is mostly ruined on price, let's not
let plate get ruined once they implement it.

Speaking of plate, I'm assuming it's done with folded metal sheets? Book is
wrong as to the ingredients (yes, I tried BLOCK, not large brick) and I was
wondering if it's broken or if there is a different recipe. I'd like to start
experimenting with folded metal, lockpicks, and tool boxes (book is wrong on
them all).
