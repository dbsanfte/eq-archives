I really like the alternate ores idea. The best smithing idea I've seen actually
came from a MMORPG that really sucked (The Realm). Up until I left the game,
smiths could still make armor and sell it any time they chose. Of course, that's
probably due to item deterioration, but let's not get into that, I'm not going
to start another flame war. Anyway, their smithing had several different blocks
of metal you could buy (didn't even have to convert it from ore =P) and make the
different armors out of. The thing I liked was you did NOT have to use a mold!
Each item took however many blocks of metal and when you used the smithing
skill, a list would pop up and as you what you'd like to make. Could make
everything - all nonmagical armors (even wood), shields, weapons.... it was
great!

It also makes sense considering smiths of old did NOT use molds... they heated
metal and hammered away at it at a forge. Banded mail is made from taking many
many small strips of metal and riveting those strips together to make a thick
strip of metal. This metal is then attached to a piece of soft chain armor.
Molds are no part of the process. This is just background info, though, it
really means nothing =P

My point is, it would be nice to have different kinds of ore to make different
armors. Adamantium, Mithril, Steel (what we have now), Iron, etc. It'd also be
neat if ogres and trolls got a race-specific skill called sculpting. Take a
block of (clay, rock, granite, etc.) and make something out of it. This could
make stone armors that only trolls and ogres can use. Would be heavy as hell,
but those races can take it :) Again, though, just idle thought like the name
tag thing. I don't expect anything to be done about this.

I've said it before and I'll say it again: don't flame me, I won't answer.
