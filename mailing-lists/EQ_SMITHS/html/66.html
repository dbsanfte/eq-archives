An interesting thing occurred the other day.

I had been told that once you "triv out" on a task (i.e. polishing rusty
weapons)
you would no longer get a skill increase from that task. I was polishing a
rusty
two-handed sword yesterday (I was at skill level 22) and Lo and behold, I
got a
skill increase!

I have been watching closely and I believe you CAn raise skills in the low
20's at
polishing weapons BUT the weapon you polish has a factor.
I can polish rusty daggers & short swords with 80% success and never see an
increase.
I can polish rusty battle axes, and two handed swords with a 50% success
and see
an occasional skill increase even though I am STILL getting the trivial
message.
It seems the damage/value of the weapon is included in the difficulty
formula.

Also, oddly enough I have made 4 metal studs in 5 attempts at a skill level
of only 23.
I have yet to see a skill increase making studs even though studs are,
supposedly,
a more difficult task than scalers or files.
I have made 8 files in 10 attempts
I have made 4 studs in 5 attempts

I bought my blacksmithing to 21 with pracs and the only increases I have
seen were
polishing weapons with a sale value of greater than 2 gold. Obviously I
HAVE "trived"
out on the cheaper weapons such as daggers and short swords but this game
seems
to reward sacrifice. Try your polishing on weapons that have a 2+ gold
monetary value
and you MAY still get an increase for the effort. I have.

Sadly, I have yet to see an increase while making bits, files or studs.
