If you're going to stick to this concept of yours, then I have a few points of
advice:
1 - NEVER buy any CD for more than 10 cents. They're just shiny pieces of plastic
that only cost 10 cents to produce. Since you don't pay for any overhead and are
simply sticking to cost, this is the most you should ever let yourself pay for a
CD of any kind.

2 - Never ever rent anything or visit a video arcade. You aren't actually buying
anything, and therefore aren't aquiring these precious materials that are all
anybody ever charges for. If you don't get materials, they don't lose materials,
so they should charge nothing because they are losing nothing. If you pay more
than nothing, you are again violating your own principles.

3 - Don't buy gas for your car. Don't even buy a car! The gas for your car is
sold in bulk, and therefore at discount. When you buy gas from a gas station, it
has gone through several middlemen who add a little to the price each time. Since
you could buy petroleum from the middle east and process it yourself for no more
than cost, you shouldn't pay the price a gas station will charge because it has
been marked up several times. Same goes for cars. No car company that I know of
mines the metal it needs and then shapes it into the parts needed for the car.
This again means middlemen and markups, so if you want a car, you need to go mine
all the materials yourself, process even more petroleum into the plastics needed,
build your own engine and any other moving parts, and put it all together
yourself. This way you make sure to not pay anything more than cost of materials.

4 - Refuse paychecks from your place of employment. The only things ever paid for
are material costs, time and work aren't included. Seeing as your place of
employment should only charge for materials, and you want to support your place
of employment because without them you wouldn't have a job, you should try and
help by not requiring a paycheck. Anything they pay you comes out of the owner's
pockets, because the consumer can't be charged. You might even want to pay the
company to work for them, as that would lighten the load on the owner's pockets
from all the employees who DO require payment.

5 - If you ever get into a legal battle, don't hire an attorney. The paper they
used on your case does not cost what they charge you. Since they are charging
more than cost, you must refuse to pay them and therefore boycott their services.
You can represent yourself for cost.

Yes, I know this is all rather ridiculous, but that's what things would be like
if you really followed your ideas here. It's almost as bad as saying 'don't use
plastic, they charge too much!'. Well... where would you be without plastic?
Think along those lines... where would you be without business? If everybody
charged for cost and nothing more, employees wouldn't get paid and all businesses
would eventually collapse because any failures they made over the years would add
up. Since they only charge what it costs them, they don't make any money in their
business so everything comes from their pockets. Since other businesses can't
afford to pay employees, there is nowhere else to work to gain any money.
Timber!! There goes business as we know it.

Seriously, reconsider what you're saying here. It makes absolutely no sense. We
all know EQ isn't a real economy (it's downright terrible). Actually, it's weaker
than a real economy. You see what charging for cost does to a real economy, can
you imagine what it would do to EQ's? It would be nearly irreversible. I can't
begin to explain the damages your ideas will cause if they are practiced by all.
