Fleetwood, the top maker of moter homes and RV's, Pella, the number one maker
of window, GM (which has been mentioned) all will tell you the same thing,
Screw ups, inspections, the people working in the office, the people who
drive the fork lefts carting material all day....all the work they do and
much work that others do is NOT what the costumer buys. The customer buys
what it takes to make the product and that is all. They are not paying for
the time it takes to train John Doe, not for the time it takes for someone to
fix or check his work. They do not pay for the people to run the accounts,
they do not pay for the guy to drive around on a forklift. Would you?

When you buy something, do you want to pay for all that extra that had
nothing to do with building your car? You can say it did have something to
do with your car, but the fact of the matter it isn't. Efficency is the key.
If the people doing the paid for work are good enough, then THEY pay the
cost of all that excess. I don't charge people for me learning to smith, I
don't charge for failure rates. I charge for my work and the matrials needed
to make the given item. I have about the same prices. I guess I just value
my work more then you do yours.
