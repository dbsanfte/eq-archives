Reread what you wrote yourself :) By definition, 'what it takes to make the
product and that is all' is simply materials. Also by definition, the work put
into the product, the people who run the accounts, and the guy who drives around
on the forklift is part of the overhead expense. Overhead expense is, as I saw
someone say on some mailing list, is evenly distributed into the cost of all the
products. Instead of selling the product for 100% of what it cost you, you sell it
for 150% to cover extra expenses all around.

I work for a small nurse-staffing company doing billing and payroll. I pay people
for their time and nothing more (all materials needed are supplied by the facility
the person is working for at the time). Yes, time and skill are valuable, but how
valuable? The cheapest work anyone can do for us pays $10 an hour. It's billed at
$17 an hour. That's the facility paying an extra $7 an hour to have someone give
shots, hand out medication, and give sponge baths. Almost anybody can do it (with
minimal training), yet that's quite the rate. As the services increase, so does
the profit. The more expensive services end up costing $50 and hour and up. This
ends up being more than $20 an hour of JUST profit! This is not simple payment for
one's time. This factors in overhead. If it were payment for one's time, we would
pay $10 and bill $11. All we do is see who needs services and who can provide them
:) Simple use of a phone. We don't, though, overhead (what they use to pay me and
other office staff) is factored in. Think about that if you have a family member
in a nursing home. You are not only paying for medical services they need,
medications, and the time of the people who take care of them, but you also take
part in paying the pharmacists who filled their prescriptions, the secretaries who
take care of keeping the place properly staffed, and the people like me who pay
the people who take care of your family member. Personally, I'd rather pay a guy
to drive a forklift than pay a guy to pay another guy that I'm already paying for
anyway! That's not how it works, though, no matter what you think.
