Hail, I am Dhimmid deThon a budding master of the Elemental Arts.

I have toiled at the Art for many years now, and my hunger and desire for
further Mastery
still burns at my core. My guildmaster is hard but fair. He keeps my
development slow to
ensure that my desire for power will only come with a true respect for that
which may be
wrought from it. For that I am thankful.

I am of the 5th circle (lv 16) within my Guild. I can be found many aday
studying and experimenting
within the hall of the Academy in Freeport. But I also take childlike pleasure
in exploring, and
have recently begun to find mastery over elementals throughout Norrath that have
overcome
their masters to break those tentative bonds that we magicians hold sway upon
them.

I feel it is also time for me to take on an apprentice. My guildmaster says
that it will help me learn
compassion and help to gain further understanding in areas of knowledge that I
mayhap only
perused upon in the past (my unyielding desire to come of age, no doubt!). My
apprentice must
have a good and open heart and a desire to open all doors allowed us as
magicians. He can
be of any race, for my spirit is touched not by the despicable hand of prejudice
( I see that humans
may well be the ONLY race not mired by that foul characteristics). I ask only
that he or she also
hold their tongue in regards to any prejudiced remarks or situations. I shall
not tolerate babblings
of prejudice, our time upon Norrath is too short to be mired by such idiocy!

I would prefer, for now, that the apprentice either be based out of Freeport, or
willing to move here.
I have but meager lodgings to offer my apprentice, and will not be a crutch for
him/her. I believe too
strongly that experience in Norrath is our greatest teacher. As I have been
guided by experience, so
shall my apprentice. I will rather, give guidance in all things of the Art that
I know. I will occasionally
introduce new game to my apprentice in a controlled environment, as well as
offering rewards for
tasks completed (my disgression of course).

If there are aspiring magicians in this land that would like to spend a time
learning, please call
out to me here.

Dhimmid deThon, Defender of the Art.
