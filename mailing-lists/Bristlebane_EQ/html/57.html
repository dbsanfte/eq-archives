Minotaur's Lament

The cave opened wide before the fellowship. Not far within, the mountain
swallowed up the path into a maze of tunnels and caverns. The land outside
was broken by deep ravines and high, steep hills. The fellowship stood just
outside the mouth of the cave peering in.
"I see nothing," said Lindale as he looked into the cave with his keen Wood
Elven eyes.
"Shall we go?" asked Drugas, the stout Dwarf.
"I'm not so sure of this," said Derkahn. He knew that should the fellowship
find the danger that it sought, he would be chosen as a prime target because
he was a High Elf.
"If we're going to do this, let's have it done and be over with it," replied
Xtarii the small yet powerful Gnome.
"I agree," spoke Kaolas.
They slowly, but surely moved inside the cave. The light outside was lost a
few feet within and darkness swallowed them. They went on, deeper into the
cave. His eyes adjusting quickly, Lindale said, "Quiet everyone. Something
approaches." Quickly Lindale lowered himself to the ground and listened to
what rumors the earth would tell.
"I feel it in my bones, there is something near and I like it not," Falyen
hoarsely whispered.
Suddenly they were all aware of a snorting and stomping as the unknown
creature approached. Then, all went quiet.
"He knows we are here," Kaolas said, slightly within the hearing of the rest
of the fellowship.
Lindale quietly rose from the floor of the cave and moved forward,
carefully, a few steps. He smelled it. It was very near. The stench was
something between the farm animals he saw when he had visited the human
settlements many years ago and the vile orcs of Crushbone. He backed away
swiftly yet quietly and rejoined his party.
Just as he returned and began to tell them what he thought the beast was,
there was no need. Around the corner emerged a huge beast. Half man, half
Bull. He turned quickly and faced the party. They could see that he was
not afraid. Studying them for a moment he paused. Neither adversary moved,
both considering the eminent battle ahead. Then, with lightning quick
speed, the Minotaur lurched forward and charge the adventurers.
"Stand firm!" shouted Drugas as he prepared himself for the coming
onslaught.
The battle began. Quickly the fellowship realized exactly what they were up
against. The minotaur's blows found their way home and Drugas winced in
pain. His sturdy armor receiving a shock that before it had not know. With
all their might, the group defended themselves as well as dealing what
damage they could to the minotaur. Slowly, the tide changed and the
minotaur was receiving the worst of it. His sides were rent and bleeding.
He had lost one horn to the sharp blade of Drugas. He was about to fall
when he called back into the recesses of the cave.
"I FALL SOON! COME!" screamed the minotaur with his last breath as the
spear of Lindale pierced his throat.
Staggering backward, the dwarf said "That didn't sound good."
How right he was. Suddenly, with no previous warning, another minotaur came
charging around the corner. He was not alone. Three minotaurs were now
charging the fellowship and the future looked ill.
Just as quickly as the previous battle had ended, the new one sprung up in
its place. Exhausted and weakened by the first fight, the party put
everything they had into one of the minotaurs. Fighting for their life,
their berserker style of last effort took down one of the minotaurs.
Having spent all his mana, Falyen had entered into the fray. He did well at
the start, but soon, the third minotaur realized him and drew off his attack
on Lindale and turned to Falyen. He did not stand long. This brief moment
was all that was needed for Derkahn, the powerful magician. With a burst of
fire and a loud scream, the second minotaur fell, never to wake again. The
party turned their attention to the last minotaur and with speed
surprisingly fast after such a battle, the minotaur turned and ran deep into
the cave.
The adventurers did not follow far before the minotaur was returning.
Again, he was not alone. A mighty chieftain of the minotaurs was with him.
Much taller and stronger he was, and armed with a mighty axe. The chieftain
charged, the smaller minotaur by his side. Knowing that they could not
destroy the chieftain if the lesser minotaur remained, the party focused on
finishing him. Quickly this was done by the swift axe of Kaolas. The party
was now wounded, weakened and on their last legs. Turning to the great
chieftain, Drugar began the final stand. Cleaving the shield of Drugas, the
minotaur put all his weight down on him. Drugas knew his end was near. But
against all hope, new life surged into his limbs and he gained strength.
Kaolas had backed off from the fight and was singing a hymn of restoration.
The party realizing that this was their chance, redoubled their efforts and
soon the great chieftain was falling back, stumbling with the effort of
standing before such a great and unexpected effort. Hewing limb from limb,
Drugas laid waste to the chieftain and he fell.
They had won. But not without a price. Falyen and Derkahn had fallen and
Lindale was gravely wounded. Drugas, being a paladin laid hands on Lindale
and revived him, but was greatly drained because of the effort. Lifting the
mighty axe from the ground where it had fallen, Kaolas hewed the other horn
from the beast put it in his pouch. Turning to Drugas, he presented the axe
to him and said, "I believe that this belongs to you."

The End.
