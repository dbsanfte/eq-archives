My name is "Hewhois" and I am a 5th level Monk and follower of Quellious the
Tranquil. Hometown is Freeport. I spend most of my time in the Eastern
Commonlands (Watchout for that wall piercing Griffin!!!) or the Northern
Desert of Ro. I too, love to destroy skeletons. One good round kick and
they fall apart, LOVE that sound.

Here is a rough draft of a character Bio I am working on. Please let me
know what you think. BTW, the character originally started out female, but

Hewhois (Monk Character):

Quick Look:
*Mistrusts all metal and metallic objects and stays away from them as much
as is practical
**Will loot metal items to sell, but will get rid of it as soon as possible.
***Money is money afterall ;)
*Follows the path of Quellious
*ALL NPC dark elves are attacked on sight due to characters personal
history(have yet to meet any NPC Drows though)
*Does his best to not judge others based on his own beliefs
**However, he is only human, and can't help but feel a little superior to
metal wielding warriors
*On a continuous quest to find his father's murderer (believed to be a Dark
Elf) and foil whatever plot the murderer is involved in.

Character Bio:
Hewhois was born to Hew and his wife Hois, she died while giving birth to
him. Hew was a Grand Master Blacksmith and started training Hewhois for the
trade as soon as he could hold a little hammer. he was taught to love and
revere metal in all of it's forms. For the first sixteen years of his life,
all was well. Although he was not a naturally talented smith, his father
was a very good teacher and he could look forward to eventually being at
least a Master Blacksmith.

One day, a mysterious cloaked and hooded stranger came to his father's
forge. He had with him a bar of metal that he wished Hew to forge into a
sword. The stranger made Hew swear that he would be the only one who
touched the metal. Hewhois' father agreed, customer is always right was his
motto, and reached out to receive the metal bar from the stranger. As the
exchange was made, Hewhois noticed a strange blue tinge to the skin of the
stranger's hand and a strange looking ring on the pinky finger. The ring
had a stone with a design engraved into it that looked like many ovals of
the same shape, but different orientations, around four small spheres
clustered in the middle. Now, normally he would not be able to make out
such fine detail at that distance, but the ring caught his gaze and seemed
to draw him in, as if some outside force wished him to see it. The stranger
said it must be ready by the fifth day, for he must return home soon. With
that announcement, he quickly departed.

For the next five days, Hewhois watched his father from afar as he bent to
the task at hand. Knowing Hewhois' inquisitive nature, his father had
forbidden him to even go near the metal. For Hew knew that his son would
not be able to resist touching the strange metal bar that almost seemed to
glow on the edge of Human vision with a light all it's own. Hew worked on
it day and night, despite a slowly growing feeling of illness. He
attributed it to nothing more than the long hours of smithing without a
break. At long last, with Hew barely able to even lift a hammer, it was
finished just before dawn on the fifth day.

As if waiting nearby for some secret signal, the stranger appeared shortly
afterward for his sword. The stranger was very impressed with Hew's
workmanship, so much so that he told Hew to name his price, and it would be
his. By this time, Hewhois' father was beginning to suspect that not all
was as it appeared, and he feared for their lives. His fatigue was only
getting worse, and he noticed that his arms and hands were starting to
blister. Knowing he was already a dead man, he asked the stranger to spare
his son's life as payment. The stranger was not pleased, but he had already
given his promise and for some reason felt bound by it, at least for now.
So he agreed to the price, then immediately killed the Father by
accelerating what was already happening to him, and lulled Hewhois to sleep.

On the sixth day following the strangers first appearance at the forge,
Hewhois woke up with a start with no memory of what had happened the
previous day. She found his father dead, on the floor near where he had
been standing. His skin was red, with blisters all over. Hewhois also
noticed an itching sensation that grew as he approached his dear father's
body. Fearing that it may be contagious, he rushed off to get the village
healer. The healer was not very helpful, for he had never seen such
symptoms before. He also feared getting to close to the body, wisely
thinking that the growing itching sensation was a warning to stay away. He
recommended to the town Elders that the forge be burned to the ground and a
high wall erected around the property to keep out the curious. Despite
Hewhois' anguished pleas, it was agreed to follow the healers advice.

Mystified, confused, grieving and orphaned, he ran out of the meeting hall
and nearly knocked over an elderly, kind looking stranger, whose graceful
recovery belied his great age. When the old man heard his tale of woe, he
was greatly disturbed. He asked him if she had a place to live, and offered
to be his guardian till he became an adult in a couple of years. Not
wanting to stay in the village where his father died, and having no other
alternatives, he agreed.

The elderly gentleman turned out to be a monk and a follower of Quellious.
He was also very wise and knowledgeable. He had read of similar cases and
related them to Hewhois. All had been blacksmiths, all had recently taken
on a new commission from a stranger, and all had died the same way. In some
accounts, a dark Elf had been mentioned and he asked Hewhois if he new the
race of the stranger. All he could tell him was that the skin had appeared
to have a bluish tint to it. The old monk thought it likely that the
stranger had been a dark Elf, and that the items he was commissioning were
being made out of metal with some strange power emanating from it. He
further speculated that it was this strange power that killed the
blacksmiths. After praying for Divine help, the old monk placed Hewhois in
a deep trance, in hope of recovering his memory of that fateful fifth day.

Hewhois nearly went mad when he heard that metal and stranger was
responsible for his father's death. The very substance that his father had
taught Hewhois to revere and love had killed him. From that day on, he had
an aversion to anything with metal. Obviously, smiths had to use metal, so
she needed a new trade.

The monk agreed to train him in the basics of monkhood, but he said that to
go beyond the first level, one had to go out into the world and earn it.

So, now Hewhois wanders the land, learning his craft, seeking inner peace,
and above all, seeking answers to how his father died and why. He believes
the Dark Elves are responsible and that some nefarious plot is being
executed involving this strange metal. He vowed to one day uncover that
plot and foil their plans. Needless to say, he has a special dislike of
Dark Elves.
