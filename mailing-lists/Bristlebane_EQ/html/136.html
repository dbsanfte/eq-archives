Other than seeing those occasional "your faction has increased with So-and-so"
messages, does anyone else find it scary that you have NO idea what any
particular group might think of you and what that means for your general
safety?

Members of the crew I regularly adventure with made a few enemies among the
Freeport militia. Now many of the Freeport guards will attack them. Ok, that
one was fairly predictable. However, when we adventured to Kaladim, one of
these people was killed by a dwarven guard (StormGuard). I'm not sure that we
figured out exactly why. The guard shouted something about his god, but he
shares a god with others in our group. Was it related to the Freeport thing?
We may never know.

I wish they would tell you when you select your god what the repercussions
might be. Picking one of the ultra-evil ones has obvious drawbacks, but we
have no way of knowing what selecting any of the others will do to you. And
once in the game what are you supposed to do? Two PCs conning an NPC often
results in similar messages ("regards you dubiously" or something like that)
yet only one PC will be attacked. Kind of an expensive guessing game.

Just my ponder for the day. Talk amongst yourselves.
