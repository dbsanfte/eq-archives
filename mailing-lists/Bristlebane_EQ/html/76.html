about 1 plat or so, but you can try to bargain with them. Also, you can
always to a /who all 12 cl, or /who all 12 ma (and whatever druid is)... and
that will give you a list of the people at the levels with bind. (Though
many, like Drugar, are anonymous and won't show up).

Yes, if you lose connection on a boat, you log back in in the ocean. Or, if
you are very lucky, like me, you'll log in in a nonexistent spot, and fall
off the map, ending up under the earth in Butcherblock, unable to get to
your corpes. A GM got it back for me (about 9 hours later), but it shouldn't
have happened in the first place.

I asked the GM if they were going to fix the thing were you log back in from
the water, and he was like, 'No, I think that's the way it should be - it is
a dangerous trip!' Well, I totally disagree. To me, if losing link is like
'passing out' in the virtual world. If you pass out while fighting a
monster, you'll die. If you pass out while standing in a field, you'll be
standing in a field. If you pass out on a boat - you should still be on the
boat when you wake up!! The idea that you'd end up in the water is
ridiculous.
