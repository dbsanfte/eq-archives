Subject: Kind of grouchy about some of the changes


(/ooc)

Not sure how many of you know this, but many areas were tweaked around the
time of final release to increase difficulty. Places like Befallen used to be
happy hunting grounds where you could easily identify what portion was safe
for you and stick to that. To discourage "campers" and other such things, EQ
added anti-camping spawns and other such things. Now, you can be a low-level
player happily slaying even /cons when some red, hit-only-by-magic creature
spawns in your lap.

I freely admit I died in Befallen last night. We were in an area that our
group could reasonably handle, we goofed a bit, and one unlucky anti-camping
spawn finished me off. I'm fine with that as it was really our mistakes that
killed me. However, as I'm running back to Befallen I get to listen as the
remainder of my party runs into something *worse* (dark elf dragoon) and madly
flees Befallen. After retrieving my corpse we prepare to leave and a ghoul
spawns on us. DOH!

OK, so if increased difficulty was EQ's goal... what is the point? If we went
back in there 5 levels from now we would survive the "hot" spawns better, but
nothing would give us XP anymore. If the goal was anti-camping then they
should lower the spawn rate a bit, as there is no way we could hack our way in
to our intended target, then hack our way back out through the near-immediate
respawns. If we were high enough level to do so, again we'd hardly get XP for
the accomplishment.

From a player point of view, the only solution to this situation is to wait
until there are enough PCs in a zone to make it safe to inhabit. But then you
lose out on all the fun of being the few brave souls alone in a zone. And you
also end up with previously deadly zones like Blackburrow reduced to a gaggle
of players standing around *WAITING* for something to spawn because they've
all killed everything. I've seen more suspense and danger at Wal-Mart.
