I think a reasonable parallel could be drawn between this, and design
tricks such as
the constant flow of wandering creatures in Japanese RPG's, and some of
the lenghty continual jumps needed in Tomb Raider.
They're all 'cheese' attempts to lengthen gameplay that really don't add
any enjoyment to what you're doing. Does anyone really like dying the
first time they make an attempted jump in TR? Does anyone really like
fighting so many times in Final Fantasy 7 that it's not uncommon to
forget where you were going by the time you're halfway there?
No.
But game designers continually put such things in because these are easy
solutions to address serious level design flaws.

In Everquest's case, I think we've seen the results. While I was not in
Beta, there's a distinct lack of exploration and risk taking because of
all the sudden death monsters running around. I've gotten the impression
that this was not the case in the beta tests.

To expand on this a bit, let me point not at Befallen, but at the East
Commonlands as an example.

How is it even plausibly defensible to put not one, but on occassion
FOUR griffins into what is designed as a 5-8 lvl area(yes parts of it
are higher level, but should be considered sub-zones for the point of
this discussion)?

For those of you whom have not seen Griffins, let me explain. They are
invunerable to normal attacks and have been known to eat several
shopkeepers and a few guards before boredly flying away. They are not
limited to the upper level sections of the zone, and frequently are seen
in the parts closest to East Freeport; the same parts where newbies just
beginning to leave the newbie zone outside of their city are beginning
to hunt.

Let me also point out the latest patch revision. A paraphrased quote:
'Safe zones were removed to prevent exploitation.'

Examples such as these are haphazard solutions and hacks to poor
level(or zone if you will) design. Some will probably argue at this
point that they are most likely interim solutions only and will most
likely be changed in the future.

Unfortunately, my view of this, is that such actions are becoming
increasingly characteristic of how EQ is run. Which is to say,
haphazardly slapdash solutions being applied without careful thought and
recognition of how to solve any problems that may arise. It adds to an
ever increasing feeling that this is just all some final beta test
before Everquest 2 comes out in a year or two.

This isn't to say that I don't fully enjoy Everquest. I've spent many a
sleepless day since the day this game came out playing it. This post is
not meant to be taken as 'Josh's thesis on Why Everquest Sucks'. It's
merely that there are some serious issues that need to be brought up and
discussed. The game is good. It could have been a hell of a lot better.

> >From a player point of view, the only solution to this situation is to wait
> until there are enough PCs in a zone to make it safe to inhabit. But then you
> lose out on all the fun of being the few brave souls alone in a zone. And you
> also end up with previously deadly zones like Blackburrow reduced to a gaggle
> of players standing around *WAITING* for something to spawn because they've

You're already seeing this in parts of Befallen. There's definitely the
beginnings of some crowding in certain area's, because of the dangers of
risk taking. It's infinitely safer to walk around the castle than it is
to explore the unknown. This is why I think we see the Insta-Death
critters and such. It's increasingly becoming a damned if you do, damned
if you don't situation and won't be resolved anytime soon.
