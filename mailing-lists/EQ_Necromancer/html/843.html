> I have heard some people claiming they've had great success in Permafrost with groups in the 15-22 level range. Does anyone out there have any experiences in Permafrost they would like to share that either confirm or contradict this claim. I have been in perma since level 17. It is an excellent, well-balanced
dungeon for players level 15 to 50. I'm now almost level 21, and plan to
stay in perma until level 24 at least.

The important things to remember are that you _will_ need a group,
preferably a full one. It's almost impossible to pull one or two goblins at
a time. Groups like necros for a couple of reasons - firstly, the pet is an
excellent tank, and effectively counts as a 7th group member that doesn't
take XP (so long as he doesn't get the last hit), and secondly the darkness
line of DoTs mean that runners aren't a problem, since by the time they
start running their AGI is so low that they are effectively rooted. I do
not use my DoTs on high-level item-carrying mobs however, the risk that the
bug will strike and vanish the mob along with the xp gained is too great.
It happens too often on regular mobs anyway.

Another thing to be careful of is to make sure that any mob your pet is
beating on has had darkness cast on it. I've been killed (along with some
group members) because my pet went chasing a runner and ended up in a room
full of nasties who, after despatching my pet, came after me. Remember - if
your pet draws them, it's YOU they are after. If you ever see 'Taunting
attacker master' and you don't know where your pet is - zone! It is the
best thing to do.

A lot of people say pets are more dangerous than they're worth in dungeons,
but if you manage it properly it can be very useful. Basically tell it to
'guard here' in the spot where your group is pulling to. That way, whenever
it's not attacking a mob it will return to that spot. Secondly, make sure
any runner it's attacking has darkness on it, and/or nuke the runner
yourself (or have the wiz do it).

Anyway, hope this has been of some use. I'd imagine that these guidelines
are mostly common-sense, and are applicable in most dungeons.
