Its still hotly debated. Basically as far as anyone knows, the only
thing DEX does is increase your ability to recover while spellcasting, and
THAT'S IT. How important is this? No clue, I haven't researched this
extensively. Basically if this is the case then you probably don't need
much DEX. The REAL primary attribute is INT, through and through. INT
directly affects how much mana you have, thus how much longer you stay in
the field before meditating. We've discussed this before, but basically
having as much INT as possible is probably the best way to go. After that,
dumping your remaining points is up to you. You aren't a fighter, so
putting points into Agility seems useless, most monsters will be hitting you
and hitting you hard anyway. Same goes for Stamina, you'll never have the
hitpoints of even a Rogue, so seriously what's another hitpoint per level?
Say you're level 25 and have 25 extra hitpoints? Well sadly level 25
monsters are probably doing close to 40-90 points of damage per hit anyway,
so MAYBE one more hit. Don't get hit.

I might say its more important for wizards as they don't have pets,
but they should be in groups anyway.

Basically I know one solid thing. Multiples of 5 in your INT is
good and the more you can get the better you are. If you are looking at a
choice between a +3 AGI item and a +1 INT item you should take the INT item.
There are many INT increasing items in the game for virtually all armor
slots garnering you probably more than 20 more INT points, which is 4 more
mana per level (and at level 25, that would equate to having another 100
mana to play with)

Hit the INT hard, then dump whatever you have left somewhere, heck,
maybe in STRENGTH so you can carry more.

Malark on Karana
