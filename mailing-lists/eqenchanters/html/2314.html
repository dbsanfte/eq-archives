Hail!

I'd like to share with you my doubts of the advantage of a high wisdom while making jewelry. I realize I'm going against everything that GZ/Verant has said about how wisdom helps with success at jewelry, but here goes anyways...

I am a level 36 gnomish enchanter on Rallos Zek. Currently my jewelcraft skill is 184, and it has cost me a great deal MORE than 4K plat to get here.... I think I've spent 4K in the last several weeks alone, and I've been jewelcrafting since about level 10. Being a gnome, I have a relatively low wisdom (82 buffed with my +wis jewelry) and of the 6 platinum ruby vails I have attempted, I've made 4. I have a relatively good success rate with platinum, even with such a low wisdom. Since I am still learning the skill and desperately trying to reach 200, my INT is always at my maximum 175, dex is about 106, and charisma a healthy 130ish. I have a much higher success rate than a high elf friend of mine who regularily has his WIS enhanced to around 132. we have compared successes with several platinum items, and my successes are generally greater than his.

I suspect that other stats are involved. Perhaps our combat skills somehow enter into the picture, as mine are all capped for my level, or near the cap. I am not sure but I AM sure that I am either extremely lucky with platinum (and I dont mean to be bragging at ALL). Anyone with other ideas about why this could be should share their ideas.

I have also yet to fail any metal with black pearl, and I have made MANY BP items...
