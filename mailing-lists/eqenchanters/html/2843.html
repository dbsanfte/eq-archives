At 09:25 AM 09/11/2000 -0600, you wrote:
>Another reason this is a bit more difficult for me is that I am primarily
>grouped with my wife who plays a wizard. I have found that my primary
>function is to Soothe anything but the one we want on a group spawn, she
>roots and blasts while I wait for Root to break and cast WTYH, Choke and
>Languid Pace.

Sounds smart to me. You are on your way to becoming a great solo/duo
Enchanter. In your early years, you are going to find that you will be
using a few spells in particular, while some others hardly ever make it
from the spellbook to the memmed strip. This is the case if you are going
to be grouped with the wife alot, since your primary goal beyond crowd
control is to keep the frail wizard from taking a dirt nap.

>Offense / Defense: Abyssmal (have not been in melee long enough to get hit
>very much).

This is going to be a big problem for you later in life. You HAVE to get
that defense up my friend, because at high levels, you are going to get hit
ALOT. Offense isnt a big deal. I think the last time I melee'd anything was
30 levels ago.

>Evocation:Is so bad that I don't think it is above a 1 (if it has even
>gotten that high as I have cast it so little).

While I wouldn't worry too much about this, it is a good idea to at least
try and nuke a bit as you increase in level, so that your Evoke skill
increases as you go along. My Evocation is in the 230's, and I cant
remember the last time I fizzled on a nuke. This will be pretty useful when
there is no Mage or Wizard in the group. Later, when you start hunting
things that beat the tar out of you in 3 hits, its going to be necessary to
sit back while the tanks soak up damage. The tanks will probably be a
little peeved if you are the only caster and you arent nuking :) But if
you plan on grouping with your wife alot, you wont have a need to
nuke...regardless, its still a good idea to increase the skill quick as you
can.

>Conjuration: Whats that for? At 4th level, she had Root, and I had Fear
>ready so she could root again. At 8th I rooted / feared and that was it.
>Now at 12th I WTYH. No need for a pet. I have yet to cast my pet spell. And
>now that I have Charm.... I'm not even sure I will ever use it.
>Mezmerise uses conjuration I realize, but I have yet to have a need for it.
>Color Flux has give me the time I need to get off a WTYH or fear as we have
>been focusing on wandering mobs and 2 to 3 mob spawns.

Conjure...this is arguably your most important skill. Mesmerize and
Mesmerization are going to be the most important spells in your arsenal
soon. When you start to go into dungeons, or densely packed outdoor areas
(Aviaks in South Karana, Derv Camps etc...), your puller is going to come
back with two or three mobs at times. Your job will be to put the mobs the
party isnt fighting to sleep. Crowd control is an art form, and you are
going to have to learn it sooner or later, because this is going to be job
one for you.

>I don't even have time to cast Tashan as the mobs die so fast. I did in the
>begining, but it seemed a waste of mana very quickly.

Again, later in life, mobs will have alot higher magic resistances. At
level 53, this spell (Tashania, its lvl 44 equivalent) is ALWAYS on my
memmed strip.

>Should I take the time to summon a pet and Mez it for an hour to get my
>skill up? Will I need it later in my career?

Go to the newbie grounds and mez bats and rats and whatnot for a couple
hours. Your conjuration skill is absolutely essential later on. You will
use your Mesmerize line more than you can possibly imagine ;)

>My divination and Alteration are maxed. I love illusions (div) and buff
>everyone I see normally. =)

My kind of Enchanter. Mana is free, so my buffs are too :)

>I know my wife and I will go far together with this combo, but I am worried
>that I will lack the finess needed in a group of tanks when my guild goes on
>raids and such. Anyone have any advice?

Sounds like you are on your way to being a good solo or duo Enchanter, but
you need to learn crowd control. Instead of Soothe, try having the wife
nuke one in a group of 2 or 3 mobs. As they charge her, put one to sleep.
Then the other. You both may die every now and again, buts its a helluva
lot better to die learning crowd control at level 12 than at level 42 in
dungeons, when an Enchanter is the key to keeping the entire group alive.

Welcome to the most fun (and hardest) class to play in the game. You are
going to have a blast!
