Either you misinterpreted the situation I described our I am misinterpreting
your reply:-)

Here's a little more data. I hit the mob with Cessation (16% attack speed
debuff according to Rokenn's EQSongs website). I assume the enchanter was
using Shiftless Deeds (49%-65% attack speed debuff according to EQ Casters
Realm) since he was high enough level to cast it. Obviously the enchanter
spell is better than Cessation. Cessation was in effect on the mob when the
enchanter cast Shiftless Deeds. As soon as he cast it, I got a message that
Cessation wore off (before it should have wore off had it lasted its normal
duration). Immediately after I got that message I got the message that
Shiftless Deeds stuck (something like "a mob has been slowed"). This
indicates Shiftless Deeds overwrote Cessation, which is contrary to how
bard/enchanter/shaman slows have interacted in the past.

I have always been under the impression that if a bard slow song (Largos,
Chains, Cessation, etc) was in effect when an enchanter/shaman attempted to
slow the mob, the enchanter/shaman spell would not stick. However, its
possible that the way the song/spells interact has recently been changed (to
the better) without official notification in a patch message. Thats what I
am trying to confirm. So to iterate, it now appears that enchanter/shaman
slow spells will overwrite bard slow songs. Has anyone else noticed this?

They fixed slow spells a couple of months ago. Better slows will not
overwrite weaker slows. That is why you got the wore off message. After it
has been slowed you can still use cessation for damage, but the slow portion
will have no effect.
