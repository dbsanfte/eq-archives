My base is close to yours - when I created I thought the same thing and
dumped into agi, dex and cha. I am ok with agi and dex but didn't know the
cha is so easily boosted and that str would also impact how hard your hit
for and how much weight you can carry - rtf-web page I guess.

BTW, I was twinked with a full set of bronze one night. I can't even wear
all the stuff I have and not be encumbered with 20 milk and muffins. So, I
choose to wear the visible pieces and get str boosters where ever I can.
Now I am pretty good - str wise with no buffs or songs going. I am still in
situations where I have to melee and am not always assured that I can be
buffed - so I want to be sure that I am not encumbered in the melee.
Encumberment really penalizes your agi and dex (I think). I have dex rings
and str rings and carry both. Depending on what I need - or think I need I
will switch them and go from str to dex - but I think it's a marginal
effect. if I am light weight - I go for more dex to hit better. if I get
heavy, I go for str to stay unencumbered.

Now if things change and I can find a regular group that lets me stand off
and play instruments, I'd add more pieces and up my AC. I do think that AC
is AC and the type of armor does not matter - beyond whatever special things
it gives you (str, agi, dex, sv poison, magic whatever.)

Bronze is heavy and you may want to try for a mix. Some blackened iron
should start hitting the market soon and maybe you can mix that in or work
for an all black look.

So to your question - AC is AC but weight per AC varies greatly. Analyze
weight to AC ratios and choose accordingly. Look at each piece and you will
see some are lots more efficient than others. More AC per weight unit. Agi
is yor ability to dodge blows and Dex is your ablilty to hit your opponent.
Encumberment will degrade your AGI and you DEX - avoid the melee when
encumbered.
