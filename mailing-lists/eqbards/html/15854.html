Sorry you caught us during a brief (hopefully) interlude where instead of

but now back to the non-stop barding...

MM is tough. The castle is tough - especially. The agro ranges and pathing
of the mobs is very different from anywhere else you have been. You can
agro something and it will "run off" (different pathing - goes away rather
than directly to you) aggravate many more and then come back with a small
army and do battle.

Sometimes less is better. A level 50 druid can prolly pull the gypsy(s) you
need and kill them fairly easily. Then you could come in invis and loot
what you need. That is the easy way. If you dont have a lev 50 druid at
your disposal, then a full party of 35 to 40 or two partys of low to upper
30's could clear in thru the tower (secret wall by the crypt. secret doors
to the stairs going up. one way to tower - other way (left) goes in to
bedroom and castle 2nd floor.) Clearing thru the pit and up the ramp is
risky. One runner gets loose and you can pull the castle. Clearing in thru
the front door is also risky for same reason. But this time it does not
need to be a runner. Your pull mob may have a path that goes away first and
it will agro lots before they all return to deal with you.

Or, I guess you could hang out and wait for inevitable trains and try to
pick off the back end of trains. Maybe a ranger can watch for gypsys,
snare, then he zones. As the train receeds, the snared gypsys are prime for
you party to pounce on. (Kind of like old Harm's cheese pull for DW Gobs.)

Also, check the archives here - there are some good MM tactics and advice
given fairly recently.

Keep us posted and tell us how you fared,
