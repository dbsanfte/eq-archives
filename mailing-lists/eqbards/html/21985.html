I don't recall ever getting the original message to this thread, but for
those of you having assist problems, here is the whole secret (learned this
from my friendly neighborhood rogue). There are 4 conditions that must be
met in order for /assist to work.

1) The person that you wish to assist must be your target...(pretty safe
bet that everyone has this part aced =P)

2) You must be in melee range of the assistees target

3) The assistee must hit the target, and the one that gets everyone.......

4) The mob must hit the assistee!! Then assist is 100% reliable. If the
mob doesn't hit your targeted assistee, chances are you'll be assisting
nothing (i.e. attacking your puller)

Here is how I used to do it, pre-macro. I target the puller before he
leaves, and type /assist without hitting enter, wait until I read that the
puller has hit and been hit by said mob, and then hit enter (or you can just
hit enter before he leaves and scroll through your typing and hit it again
when he returns).

As of the macro patch, I have a macro set up that starts like so:

/assist
/pause 30, /cast X
/pause 1, /stopsong
/say Have at thee!!!

Then I proceed with my twisting!!

Hope this helps,
