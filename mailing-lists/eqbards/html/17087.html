I'll agree but SolA is a bard killing ground. I love that zone as a Ranger.
As a Bard it's suicide.

Several reasons:

For some reason the Goblins in SolA are completely impervious to Lament
(Annoying!!!!) even if it says they're sad they'll still come after you.

Goblins in general have a seriously low tolerance for music. I've found
that almost ANYTHING you sing there will get them aggro on you (even
whistling warsong, our self-only buff).

It's indoor (no running away).

Pathing is a nightmare: VERY easy to pull a monster train if you don't know
how to pull there.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

That being said, if the cleric is that much of an ass I'd just find another
group to hunt with. You don't tell him how to be a cleric, he shouldn't
tell you how to be a bard. If it gets you attacked more than him who cares,
you aren't a half bad tank (esp since they fixed the bash-bug on MOBS) :)

I use the following strategy in SolA: Bind, HoR, DDD, CoD. It gets me a
LOT of attention but I am gonna get it anyway, I might as well hurt EVERYONE
that attacks me. (I use pure instruments). Thus I slow down our opponents
by 30%, hurt them all at around hmm 60hp/tic and heal us all at about
12-13hp/tic. Generally the primary tank has no problem keeping the "Current
target" locked on himself, I get the rest, we kill them one at a time. By
the time we kill the 2nd goblin the rest are starting to either drop dead
from the DOT's I'm singing or are at 1/3 life and are nearly dead when we
get to them.

It works out very well, our group has miniscule downtime. The
cleric/druid/whatever concentrates only on healing me, I keep the tank
healed during combat with HoR. Since our opponents are slowed and I'm
healing I can actually heal him faster than he takes dmg.

It takes a while to prove yourself in SolA because you ARE such a huge
target. But I submit that you will be a huge target in that zone REGARDLESS
of what you sing. So enjoy yourself ;) If you're going to die, you may as
well have fun doing it ;)

~ Lyrnia
