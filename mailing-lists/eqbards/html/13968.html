Comments: This isn't as bad as it looks at first glance
because I'm pretty sure Lullaby works like Pixie Strike, where
once a mob is nodded (N), a (r) results in the mob staying
nodded. i.e. "rrrrr" represents the mob staying awake for 5
rounds, but "Nrrrr" represents the mob staying asleep for 5
rounds (assuming you get Lullaby to pulse again before the
previous pulse expires - those stupid pets kept bashing me out
of my song and this wasn't the case a couple times).

Conclusion:

- The chance of mobs outright resisting (R) is 5% at my
charisma and against these low level mobs. It would seem my
charisma is high enough that I've hit the 5% cap, at least
against these low level mobs. This is about what I'd expect
considering 154 is pretty high by anyone's standards.

- Secondary resists (r) are not working as GZ said. During
the Lullaby test, he said the chance of a secondary resist
ranged from 25% to 35% (against L1 and even /con mobs
respectively - I finally read the log :), modified by
charisma. I'm getting roughly 50% secondary resists against
green mobs, higher against low blues, which would indicate
something is broken.

I don't think the mobs buffed themselves with magic resist
since their chance of a primary resist was at the 5%
cap. The dark ritualist pet that was summoned after someone
killed the first one (neat trick that :) most certainly wasn't
buffed in any way, yet still had a secondary resist rate of
57%.
