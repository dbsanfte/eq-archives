Amen! Not only have I loved my Bard (level15) since day 1,
but my Shaman (level 30) as well. Finding a way to compensate for the
weaknesses and augment the strengths of any given level is the fun of the
game. For instance, at level 30, my Shaman is now a healer/buffer. My
damage spells aren't really as effective at this point, whereas two levels
ago they were my mainstay. I could easily complain that the Shaman class
sucks at dealing damage after level 29, but the fact is, at this level, it
is simply not my strength. This will change again after I get my next tier
of spells. I'm sure the Bard is the same way, although she is not yet at a
high enough level for it to be really noticeable.

The fun of "hybrid" characters is that you have to
continually evolve as you acquire new songs/spells. I think a lot of people
find an effective way to play their character at an early level and then
refuse to change their methods as they mature, thus becoming dissatisfied
with their character as it seems "weak."
