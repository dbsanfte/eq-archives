I was only using that as an example to show that increases to Agility do not
provide a one for one increase to AC. Fully aware that as your stats go up,
you reach a point of diminishing returns.

BTW my Agility was 99 when I hit myself with the 51 song. Agility dropped
to 54. Total AC loss was either 60 or 65 (16 from the song and the rest due
to Agility loss). Based on your previous reply, going below 75 Agility is a
major penalty to AC. A mob would have to have an Agility score of 120+ in
order to avoid going below 75 when hit with the 51 song. So the question
then is what kind of Agility score do typical 45+ mobs have? If the typical
45+ mob has less than 120 Agility, then the 51 song could have a major
impact on increasing the amount of melee damage your party does (assuming
the loss of 50-60 pts of AC is significant). If the typical 45+ mob has
more than 120 Agility, then obviously this song won't decrease their AC by
50-60 pts.

Next question. Lets assume you have a snarer and slower in your group and
as a result don't need this song for those purposes. Lets also assume the
song debuffs the mob's AC by 50-60 pts. Would it be better to use this song
strictly for the AC debuff or instead add in a chant for the extra damage?
For the AC debuff to outweigh the extra damage from a chant, the AC debuff
would have to account for around 150 extra melee damage done to the mob over
three ticks. Over three ticks, would it be safe to assume that the tanks
will get in 3-4 extra hits due to the AC debuff and as a result do well over
150 damage? If so, think this song will become a permanent fixture to my
playlist for a while.

Galtin of E'ci
