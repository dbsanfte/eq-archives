Actually I'd have to say you were both right and wrong. The bard died
because he was stupid. I have fought Bracken Underbrush three times, and
killed him once at 15th level in a group of 3 like leveled people. Bracken
SoW's himself so yes outrunning him is difficult, but that works to his
disadvantage. What I did, and probably what the Bard should have done, is
run in a big circle dragging him behind me. The wizard in my group would
nail him as we ran by as would the ranger with his bow, and then sit and med
while I ran twisting Bellow and Selo's. Because the brownie never fell very
far behind he never escaped Bellow range. I took about 4 bubbles of health
dragging him past our nuker like this, and twisted Bellow, Selo's and Hymn
for the last two bubbles to increase the brownies Aggro at me. When the
wizard nuked him down to about 10% of his health on the last pass, I stopped
and the ranger and I smacked the heck out of him.

There was nothing you could have done except suggest they flee, Bracken is
way too tough to toe to toe with even if he was a blue. He hits very hard,
his spells hit hard, and he has a lot of hits. He is a classic example of a
bad /con, much like Dorn or D'vinn.

You took the best option, stick around in case you could help, flee with
your life if you could not. I bet the caster wasn't cursing your name!
