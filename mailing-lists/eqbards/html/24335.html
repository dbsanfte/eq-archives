>Pretend you are a level 54 bard, and are hunting Hate level 2 with 23
>other people. Assume all the melee has the enchanter epic haste, and
>each group, generally, contains an enchanter, a cleric, 2 melee, and

Well I guess Cantana is the no brainer for non combat periods. Casters can
never have too much mana or anyone have too few hit points. If your group
is heavy on the casters you might want to keep Cantana going during combat,
especially if pulls are back to back to back.
With all your tanks having sos and a cleric in each group during combat I
would think Niv's would probably the most preferd song. Pure atk bonus
help more than other bonuses for the melee folks to hit more often.
With GMR in place from an enchanter there is not much reason for resist
buffs beyond that. I haven't seen many casting mobs that posed a serious
problem from their spells.
Its more of a problem on the 1st floor, but I would shy away from ae
totally in hate and leave the mez/charm to the enchanters. With 23 people
up there thats roughly 4 groups, if each has an enchanter there shouldn't
be a lack of croud control. You may want to keep a mez memed for seriously
bad encounters that the enchanters can't get under control, but usually i
find that they don't like others steping on their toes.
Since bard songs can tend to adversely affect puller that has a hord on
him/her I don't usually put bards in the pull group. If any necros are up
in hate, i would be more likely to put them in with a bard for the added
mana regen and healing regen so that they can dump mana faster to the pull
group which generally in a more mana intensive situation.
You might want to have Largo's memed in case the shamen goes down. I
assume one is in the raid force for slowing.

Hope that helps.
