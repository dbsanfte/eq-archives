Ah, the fun of real life. Good travels to you Harmonic. I'm sure we all hope
to hear from you in the future.

I've been lucky enough to get my wife to start playing recently. I scrounged
up a 2nd computer so we can play side by side. It's made life a lot easier,
however I haven't been playing Windleaf as often since I'm playing a new
character along with her (I'm a cleric, she's a ranger, both level 10 right
now). (We have a 9 month old daughter, but find time to play when she goes
to sleep.)

We both enjoy the game a lot and continue to meet very nice people and will
keep playing. Any game has problems, even non computer ones. (Heh, anyone
play Star Fleet Battles (pre-doomsday) or even Magic the Gathering? Talk
about rules problems. :-D )

Also, the unique thing about Everquest is that I can play cooperatively with
my friends. I'm a pretty competitive guy, and my friends (and wife) were
getting tired of me clobbering them in games (cruel Magic the Gathering
decks and ruthless Star Fleet Battles tactics). So despite any flaws of
Everquest, this is the game that I'm going to continue to play. Makes my
friends and my wife much happier. :-)
