If these people were not in your group, and they were being handed their
asses, and you would have been handed yours as well, then you did the right
thing. This is doubly true if your personally trying to help would have
involved your group by association. The applies whether you're newbies
fighting spiderlings all the way up to the Plane of Fear. It's in no way your
responsibility that these yahoos got themselves into fighting a named Brownie.
Maybe it agro'd on them or perhaps they attacked it, it doesn't really matter.
Your responsibility is to yourself and your group, and the order of precedence
there is up to you. Anytime someone helps me out I thank them for it, because
they don't have to. If I'm getting beaten down by a Skeletal Monk in Unrest
(which I can't handle at my level), I love it when people come to my rescue,
but I don't *expect* it. When people stand by and watch me die, I don't like
that a single bit, but more often than not it's because they're either low on
health/mana or too weak to help without dying themselves. Therefore I grumble
about the situations and move on. I don't think I've *ever* bitched someone
out because they didn't help. A very few times I might have asked why someone
didn't, but the answer is usually logical.

Last night I was in Unrest and a mini-train was taking my party apart. We
called "run" so I hit selo's and broke for the zone when I got down to a
single bubble. I was doing ok, but I was having to stop and go trying to keep
everyone in Selo's range. It's a long way from the back yard to the zone
(plus I was a couple of pounds encumbered - stupid) and I wasn't going to make
it. I got rooted by a Ghoul near the fountains in the front yard and
proceeded to die. I stopped Selo's and fired it back up to break the root and
about that time I hit the ground unconcious. Just then a Paladin laid hands
on me - while on the ground unconcious. I popped back up with almost full
health and broke for the zone amazed. I made it out with the majority of my
group that time. This Paladin wasn't in our group, he was just some 25th
level dude that was watching the train go by and saw me fall. I scrolled back
trying to see who'd Laid On Hands but the name didn't appear. So I started
/telling around asking people until I found him and I thanked him profusely.
He only gets to do this once per game day, so it was pretty gratious of him.
