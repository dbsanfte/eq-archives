A 53 SK and 52 Shaman MIGHT be able to kill the hunter/forager, but it will
be very tough. You as a 35 bard will not be contributing very much to the
fight so it will be up to the two of them. My regular group when I did the
hunter/forager cycle consisted of me at 47, another bard 50th level, 49
cleric, 55 warrior and a 50 enchanter. One of the bards would track and
pull the hunter/forager. Once we brought it back to camp, one bard would do
nothing but chains/fear the entire fight. Even though we got chains/fear to
stick most of the time, if the cleric had not been there, the warrior
probably would have died since he took a lot of damage in between fearing.
Your SK can darkness/fear, but the shaman might not be able to keep the SK
healed up when fear breaks. With the group combo I listed, it would take us
a couple of minutes to kill the mob. With your group, it will take even
longer, even if the SK and shaman summon pets. IMO, you need a couple more
45+ folks to do the hunter/forager. With the extra folks, you would also be
able to kill some of the named mobs if they happen to appear. With just the
shaman and SK, you won't be able to take on any named mob, which are all
higher level than than the hunter/forager.

BTW unless you are extremely lucky, the hunter or forager will not show up
on track when you zone in. If thats the case, you will have to run
thoughout the zone to find them. If you get lucky, they will show up on
track further in the zone. If you get unlucky, you will have to manually
look for them by left clicking on mobs to target them (there are a ton of
frogloks in the zone so the hunter/forager are easy to miss). One time my
group had finished doing the forager cycle so we moved over to start looking
for the hunter so we could complete the key quest. No one else was in TT
hunting them so I knew the hunter was out there somewhere. I ran around for
15 minutes or so and never found him. No matter where I was at in the zone,
the hunter never appeared on track (no named mobs either). Suddenly out of
the blue the hunter attacked me. While taking him back to the group, I hit
track. Even though he was right behind me, the hunter still did not show up
on track. The reason he didn't show up on track even though he was right
next to me was because there were 20 other mobs within tracking range who
had spawned after he did. Bottom line is that it can be a pain in the arse
to find the hunter/forager if they don't immediately show up on track.

Galtin of E'ci
