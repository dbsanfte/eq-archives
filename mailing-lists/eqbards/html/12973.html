GZ Lullaby Tests completed on Veeshan
by Harliquinn Whiteshadow (Bristlebane) posted 1/11/00 7:39:57 PM

Hail,
GZ's bard Lullaby tests on Veeshan were just completed. Here is a summary of
what we found out.

Using a level 40th Bard:

1) Lullaby is designed so that there is a 25% resist rate on level 1
creatures, and a 35% resist rate on creatures that con White to a 40th level
Bard. This rate decreases with increasing Charisma

2) Lullaby is a 1 (to about 1.5) tick song. It is not effective when twisted
with another song. If you plan to use this song as an Area Mezmerize, use
ONLY this song. The length is about 3-5 seconds long only.

3) The main error we found was that there is no actual text message being
displayed telling you the creature has resisted your Lullaby. Therefore you
will only see a "Head nods" for your previous attempt then on the next
'tick' the creature (IF it resists) just attacks with no "Target has
resisted your song". This is the actual problem that caused bards to think
that creatures were attacking even while their heads were 'nodded'. Refer to
#1 in that the effect only lasts for 3-5 seconds (1 tick worth).


Some personal observations:

1) The song has a LARGE radius. I am attaching a screenshot of the range
that was being affectected by GZ (Geoff the Bard in the screenshot).

2) The song seems quite effective as long as one remembers it is NOT a
multiple-tick song. Only 1 tick, so you have to keep singing it constantly
for the continuous effects.

Hope this helps!

Thanks to GZ especially and the other bards who showed up!
