You are correct in a technical sense, but as many people have stated in this
forum, you can easily have the effects of two songs occur simultaneously
(i.e Twisting). If you are good at it, you can have three songs in effect
at one time. Is this considered an exploit? I don't think so because if it
was you probably would have heard something official about it from Verant a
long time ago. Also, I don't think it should be an exploit for the
following reason. Lets say I am in a combat and I am singing the Chords of
Dissonance. Halfway through the battle I decide to switch to the Hymn of
Restoration because I have taken a lot of hits. I start singing the Hymn
and soon both the Chords and the Hymn are in effect. I am doing nothing
wrong at this point because I am just the game exactly as it was designed to
be played. No way can anyone say I should wait until the effect of the
Chords completely dissipate before I start singing the Hymn.

The question I have is this: Is the ability to twist multiple songs
something that Verant has programmed into the game on purpose? Or, is the
effect of twisting a potential byproduct of lag?
