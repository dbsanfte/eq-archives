The problem we old bards are talking about has nothing to do with Selos and
Arie of Eagles stacking.

At one time non bards could cast their levitate spells before they ran out;
which made the levitate higher - so they could use multiple casts to
"Climb". Verant nerfed it by requiring it to completely end before
recasting.

This broke our levitate song. The second and subsequent pulse of AOE (and I
assume Selo's Traveling - didn't have it then) wouldn't work until it ran
out and you fell. The unlucky bards who found this out the hard way - and
used the song THE WAY IT WAS INTENDED TO WORK often fell to their death.

It was clear that Verant made a change to the spell system without doing ANY
testing on the bard class. Literally 19 seconds of testing would have
caught this bug.

Similarly - they made a change to speed where Drums didn't add to the affect
of Selo's. Again - many bards died expecting their songs to work they way
they were designed (i.e. running away from mobs etc). In this case - 3
seconds of testing would have caught it. They admitted it was a bug - but
refused to fix it until the next patch went in. They said it wasn't
critical enough (we were upset about this wondering the reaction from the
player base is SOW was broken...)

I'm sure other old-timers could probably come up with a half-dozen or more
bugs caused by changes to the spell system not intending to affect Bards
that were introduced into the live servers because the most trivial level of
testing the bard class wasn't done by Verant.

Heck the Selo's speed bug was even discovered on Test by players and
reported to Verant - and they missed it...
