Greetings Fellow Bard!~

My suggestion to you is make a journey to Everfrost and Halas! You will
need to learn how to scoot thru the Black Burrow Paw but once you know the
drill it should not be too hard. Because of your young age, the bigger
Gnolls will find you a tasty morsel, so you will want to avoid those. They
generally stay in the lower levels and should not pose too much of a
problem.

Once you get thru the BB, you will need to learn the canyons of Everfrost
Peaks but you should be well able to handle the stuff that roams thru there.
I know of level 3 Shamans that have made the journey in reverse so I know a
level 6 can do it. The game in Everfrost Peaks is plentiful and rich
especially for you. I'd suggest learning tailoring while there because all
the materials are close at hand and if you invest the time and material to
learn and it does take some practice, I think you will be satisfied with the
returns.

Halas as a city is smaller than Qeynos but has all of the comforts you need.
The people there - whilst they be Barbaric are not the less quite
hospitable.

My first real corpse rescue happened out on the plains of Everfrost. A
higher level ranger - I think she was even 16 or more - got killed before
she could determine her location. Believe me that was quite exciting for me
a low level 5 running across those treacherous plains for the first time of
my life.

Once you reach level 9 or 10 and tire of the fighting inside BB can travel
to West Karana for big game hunting. Then seek your fortune in the lands
near Freeport. Actually at level 8, you could make the journey to Freeport
- but a stop in Rivervale and Misty Thicket for a couple levels would be
good too. If you do stop in Rivervale - invest all of your time there in
quest of the Squad Ring. You will be killing gobbies and orcs anyways - why
not get something for your efforts.

Have fun and give me /tell if you are on Innoruuk,
