Try this group.

1 - Monk
2 - Paladin
3 - Paladin
4 - Ranger
5- Bard
6 - Cleric

The monk pulls. The paladins can heal themselves in emergencies, and can loh. The ranger does the snaring. The bard takes care of the mana regen with a song. And here's the beautiful part.... The cleric can buff instead of heal because with this many tanks and the bard singing a heal twisted with the mana regen, it does a remarkable job of keeping everyone in the party topped off. I've been both the healer and the bard in this kind of group, so I know it works.

Another beautiful thing about this group is that no one is going to frenzy the mob. It bounces back and forth between the 4 tanks, distributing damage between them. It even works this way in a multiple target melee. The only member you have to babysit is the bard, as the healing song can make the targets angry if it's used too much, or incorrectly. A good first step is to not start the song until the targets are in melee. If she starts the song while the monk is pulling, she makes the target angry before it even sees her.

If you find that healing really isn't being done much (as is often the case in this group), switch the cleric out for a druid. Then the fun really begins. You have dots, damage shields, sow, regen, and heals. A group regen with a bard's song stacked is a fun thing.

There aren't any wizards or other casters around to babysit. Each caster you add to the group ties up a tank.
