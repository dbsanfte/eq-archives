it has worked rather well against everything I've fought (and hid from) so
far (lvl 20).
griffawns, reavers, broon, giants, spectors, crocks, dbskells, dervs,
craig spiders (damn I've gotten a lot use out of fd)

so far, most creatures will run back to you when you get up. I think mobs
in dungeons have a higher percentage chance of not returning (hence the fd
pull).

tips for using it:
1) wait till after your hit. do hit fd right when a mob is swinging, if
they hit you, they will see past fd.
2) wait till after a caster blasts you (same reason)
3) turn off auto attack for a few seconds (so you dont hit them) before
you hit fd
4) (I think I'm the only one to do this) jump away from the mob right
before you hit fd. got to time it right. this way, you are out of range,
and so are they.

when I first got it, it saved my life onece, then killed me. I was trying
it out on an orc warrior in oasis. I fd, then got right back up, he
aggroed on me. I fd'ed again, and waited for him to walk off. thought
that was neat. the warrior walked off in the direction of a dbskell. the
dbskell attacked, and the orc came running back to me (with the skell).
the skell killed the orc, then killed me :). after that, I decided to put
most of my points into it to get it to a somewhat usable level (got it to
about 65 I think).
