ON FINDING GROUPS
I agree, shouting is not the way to get a group... Three main reasons:
1) A LOT of people turn off shouts and OOCs altogether
2) A LOT of people just ignore them or don't take the time to consider them
seriously.
3) You can target individuals who match your level and a complimentary
skillset. They are much more likely to read a Tell and take the time to
consider your request more seriously... I exclusively use Tells now and
have acceptable success rate.

I also think HOW you ask is very important. Being friendly with at least a
"Hail <PC Name>!" and other friendly roleplaying-like comments is much more
likely to get you a group than "Need a wizard?", "Wanna group?", and other
curt requests.

ON LARGE GROUPS AND EXP
I disagree with those who say you MUST go after whites and up with a 5-6
member group... The best three hour exp gains I ever achieved were only
fighting blues. If blues are abundant in your hunting grounds, you can keep
them coming (Ranger or two) with ZERO downtime for the group. Constant
pulls of even blues will net you incredible exp gains, have done it several
times in outdoor zones in the Karanas with large groups and a Ranger. I
always shake my head at people in game who say "we like small groups, better
exp" in such areas. (abundant prey is key here obviously)

I love to both group and solo, I think you should experience the best of
both styles, give it a try.
Give me a tell if you want to group sometime ))
