Wizards are loners. Yes, the familiar idea is a neat one, but doesn't really fit in with the description of the wizard that is defined in EQ. You have to remember that we don't define the class, we play it. We chose to be wizards, and are thus bound by the rules that they set out. Well, any thoughts on this out there? I like the Teleport Others spell idea and well... Offensive doesnt really need any work...(done to death, eh? =), Defense needs a LOT of help, IMO and Utility spells just arent useful.

What you need to do is redefine how you think about the class. Though YOU may not think of us as seekers of knowledge, that's what we are. We're supposed to plumb the depths of dungeons and the like for lore items, and tomes of knowledge. However, you're free to play your character any way that you see it. We can't be a master class, no class is supposed to be superior than the other. Frankly, I think that they've gotten a good mix. We sacrifice armor and hps for pure, unbridled destroying power. There is honestly NO class that comes close to us in dishing damage. Yes, at lower levels the dmg is close to the same as other classes, but at higher levels, we're the masters of damage.

I'd like to see more teleport spells, such as a gate to your bind point, or 2 location binds. However, we're still pretty powerful.
