Just my two cents....

Everyone keeps comparing what class is best, who is and isn't useful, blah
blah blah....

I like all the classes, I like all their abilities. Are some things a
little screwey, yes? Why would anyone other then an enchanter want to learn
jewelcraft? Who knows, personal preferrence... but that has nothing to do
with usefulness of a character class...

This is a game, people play the characters they want to because they
identify or want to play that type of character for one reason or other.
All characters have strenghts and weakness'. Some seemingly more then
others but that is also based on what you want your characters to do.

I, for one, won't base who I invite into the party because their class just
isn't as "useful" as another class. The dynamic of a group is based on how
the characters are played. And the tactics used while hunting is based on
the characters abilities within the group. Thus making it more fun and
interesting to learn differnt paths and tactics and consequences.

I look forward to travelling with Mages, Necros, Druids, Bards, Shaman,
Wizards, Rogues, ShadowNights, Enchanters because of the skills they do
bring to the group, not because of the skills they don't.
