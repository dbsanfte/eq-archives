duffjc@... wrote:
>Skills are not utterly pointless. They allow you to emerge yourself
>into your character without the constant: You hit a gnoll for 14
>damage. for 10 hours a session. I know some skills cost more than you
>get out of them, but think of it as a gaming dynamics. It is supposed

Hmm, I'm still trying to figure out exactly where the fun is in spending
lots of time creating (seemingly) worthless objects. I don't know about
other people, but I want to feel like my time is well spent, like I'm
accomplishing something. Creating an object that's worth less than its
components, not even considering the time spent, is not much of an
accomplishment.

If the actual process were interesting or rewarding in some manner (as
creating pottery, jewelry, paintings, etc in real life can be), or the
resulting product had some unique value other than the sum of its parts,
then I could see the accomplishment. However, dropping items into a
container and hitting Combine just doesn't model the usual creative
processes involved when creating these kinds of things in real life.
And
the resulting product doesn't have any unique value - your jade ring is
no different from anyone else's jade ring. There's just no place for
art
in the game, as there's no way to differentiate your piece from anyone
else's. Everything you create is a commodity good - an unspecialized
mass-produced product. And skill doesn't enter it either, as it only
affects the likelyhood of success; it doesn't result in a better quality
product.

So, although I agree that the game is supposed to be FUN, I just don't
see the fun in making a jade ring just like everyone else's jade ring,
that I then sell back to the vendor for less than it cost to make. I
spent time, and all I accomplished was to lose some money.

Imagine if fighting were like this. You run up to a goblin, miss every
swing, take 5 damage, and then it disappears. You spent time, and all
you accomplished was to lose some hit points. Would you bother fighting
monsters then?

I don't think _all_ the trade skills are pointless, though. I've had a
lot of fun making and selling patchwork. After doing that for awhile,
though, it does get boring, and there's nowhere to go from there. There
doesn't seem to be a market for studded leather, so, as I've said above,
it's no fun if all I do with it is sell it back to a vendor for a loss.
Smithing looks fun once you can make something useful like banded armor,
but making files for days and days to get there isn't fun.

To sum up then, I'd guess that no one wants to feel like they're wasting
their time. They want to feel like they're doing something rewarding
and
worthwhile, and for most of the trade skills, I just don't see anything
rewarding about them.
