I agree 100%. If you have ever been out of the US you will notice how
much more in general most consumer goods are. It is the variety of
economy that makes EQ interesting. In fact they even note in the
instructions that certain vendors will pay more for you goods then
others (town vendors versus vendors out in the wilderness). What I
think would be of more benefit would be the ability to make money from
buying goods say in Freeport, and then bringing them to Felwithe to
sell for a profit. While I can appreciate Verant's attempt to create a
totally player driven economy; one should be able to make a minimal
living dealing with NPC's to some extent (with the exception of
fishing).

In particular the creation of magic jewelry kills me. I create
something for 5.5PP, sell it to a vendor for 4.5PP, he in turn marks it
up to 98PP. You should at least be able to recover your initial
investment on these trade skills (not to mention the fact all the money
lost on failures).
