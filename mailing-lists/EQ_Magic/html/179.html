This petition of yours is very interesting, and I must say that I fully
support your idea on making the monsters resists a bit lower, since
those spells do use up a lot of mana, and even if the melee classes
miss as much as the spells are resisted, they dont spend resources like
mana when fighting. However, it is my firm believe that this is about
it. I play more than one character, and let me tell you that when the
game is properly played, the wizards are far from being the class that
dies the most (the tanks are the ones that run the highest risk,
particularly the "puller"). You see, as wizards, you are not supposed
to use the strongest spells until the monsters are at less than half
health. You should blast them moderately as the tanks pick at them and
taunt, to keep the mosters busy. You should do moderate blasts in
between the work of the tanks until the MOB is down to half health.
Now blast away. That way, even if you do a LOT of sudden damage, you
still havent done most, so the MOb won't fall into you instantly. Let
the tanks take the blow as you stand back. Since I have played many
sides of this (I have a ranger, this necro, a wiz, and a druid), I
should also give this suggestion to wizard players: "If the MOB starts
to hit on you, DON'T RUN!!". This will make it impossible for the
tanks to get the MOB off. At best, try to root him, or better yet, if
there is a ranger or druid with you, make them cast snare instead of
root. Has higher success rate, and is overall more reliable. The
cleric (or druid or shaman) can heal you and the tanks do better work
if they dont have to chase after a monster while contending with PL and
other mean variables. Don't get me wrong, a few improvements can be
made on the classes, but the game has achieved quite a striking balance
once it is played in a group, as opposed to soloing. Remember, this is
an online game; if you prefer to solo, this is not the game to play.
You should look out for Baldur's Gate, or Might and Magic and their
ilk, not for a game in which grouping and cooperation is the key.
