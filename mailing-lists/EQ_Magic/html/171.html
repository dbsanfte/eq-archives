According to Thomas Adelsberger:
> But they can make some nice studded and reinforced armor.

Studded and reinforced isn't useable, because the market is very limited.
Most classes either:
1) cant wear much armor (spellcasters)
2) can wear metal armor (banded), which isn't much more
expensive than reinforced
3) can buy leather armor at comparable prices to studded
and studded isn't much better than leather

As a result, the only customers for tailors are druids and monks, and
monks do not want armor if it weights them down.

I think that studded should have been an intermediate armor between
leather and chain (like patchwork between cloth and leather), which would
have been consistent, and reinforced should have been transformed into
special armors, maybe made in junction with jeweler made items (sew jewels
in garments) or tinker made items (as such, tinkers can make a termal
cloak, and that's all), to be something equal to chain, but with small
additional bonuses (like +5 gloves with +1 in dex, boots with +1 in agi,
and so on). The progression in item levels would have been adequate
then.
