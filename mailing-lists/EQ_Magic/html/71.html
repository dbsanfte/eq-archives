Where in any of this chain of messages have I stated anything which can
be interpreted as a desire to get the most money or advance the
quickest. RPG's are games which form some basis in reality and in
which you can play out a roll that may not exist in reality. Certain
conditions from the real world such as gravity, economy, etc. are
generally present from the real world in some form or another though
not always.

The current state of the game is all money must be derived from the
repetitious see Gnoll, kill Gnoll, loot Gnoll. There should certainly
be some opportunity to earn equal or less profit by plying a trade
skill if you so desire. I am obviously not suggesting the UO macro
skull caps with your tailor and buy a castle routine. It should be
simple to determine average money gained by characters adventuring and
use that information to set prices that NPC's will by trade goods at or
slightly below this level. Thus creating the opportunity for those who
wish to adventure to do so and make a living, those wishing to ply
trade skills to do so and make a living, and those wishing to do both
to be able as well.

For all the people who say if you are not having fun don't play. There
has to be two sides to have a discussion. If you cannot debate your
point with some meaning and elegance, don't subscribe to discussion
lists.
