I agree that role-playing is the heart of the game. But I have to keep
asking myself: "Why would my character spend all of his money developing a
trade that provides no benefit?"

I mean, aren't trade skills supposed to represent _jobs_ in EQ? If so,
wouldn't you expect to _make_ money by using them? If the skill was
"Throwing money into the water" (something to practice while fishing, I
guess), I would expect that the skill would provide no benefit, and
my character would develop it for some other reason (perhaps, because he
is a loony).

I see no inconsistency in being a true role-player and whining about the
fact that you make no money by <insert trade skill>. IRL, I whine that
the jobs I'd like to have don't pay enough. So, why shouldn't my
character complain, ESPECIALLY when the skill makes him no money at all?
