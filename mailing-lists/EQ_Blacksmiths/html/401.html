I am taking part in that debate, so why not bring up the same points here?

Enchanters should get the best means of producing magical equipment, it is
what they do. No agruement.

However not all special weapons and armor need be magical in nature. If
tinkering is ever completely (reletive term) and properly implemented then
they should be able to produce tools and/or components to produce unusual
smithed equipment. (Please note that if an enchanter were to aid the
tinkerer the produced tinkered tools/components could be enven more powerful
and useful)

The addition of enchanted jewel components in smithed items might yield
special effects.

I forgot to mention this to the tailors, but I'll tell my fellow smiths.
Alchemy (another currently iffy skill) could produce potions (to be used in
place of water) that could also effect smithed items.

The trades were not ment to be independent, and if they were they would be,
IMHO, far less enjoyable. Sure, trying to track down someone who is skilled
enough in a certain trade to help you can be a pain, but it is a great
feeling when you finally do.

Now, for something a bit off topic. I've also been thinking it would be
great if smith could produce (perhaps with the help of some group or other)
metal thread. This could then be turned into bolts of cloth by tailors, and
used in different fashions by either smiths or tailors. I don't ever expect
to see it in game (to complicated to code), but it would be nice.
