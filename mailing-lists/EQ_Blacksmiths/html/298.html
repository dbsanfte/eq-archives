Yes, you have *utterly* missed the most important
component of the entire EverQuest armor system.

Damage absorption.

Each type of armor, from naked to cloth to patchwork
to leather/rawhide to ring to chain to banded to
plate, has a level of damage absorption. This is in
addition and separate from the AC the armor confers.
There is a *huge* difference in damage taken between
plate (steel or bronze) and banded. You can test this
yourself.

Take a full suit of banded, and a full suit of plate
(bronze or steel). Go fight a few blues and note how
much you lose in the fights. Then switch over to
plate and fight a few more of the same types of blues
as before; you'll lose considerably less.

This is the number one problem with Rangers; they
can't wear better than banded until they get their
hands on the uber camped (and bugged, IMO; no plate
should include magical plate too) magical plates like
Rubicite or Green Splinted, etc... Watch what happens
in a group with, say, a Warrior, Paladin and Ranger at
level 25. The first two are probably in full plate,
the Ranger's in full banded. The ranger will take
*considerably* more damage than the Warrior or
Paladin; plate makes a much more efficient tank.

This problem with Ranger armor is extenuated by
Rangers' use of Dual/Double; they stay high on the
hate list and thus end up tanking a lot, even though
they're least suited for it.

Banded is nice to about 20; above that any serious
tank needs to be in plate or they're going to have to
lean more heavily on their clerics. And the clerics
will spend more time meditating, and run out of mana
sooner in the fight.

Yes, Bronze is heavy, but it's *worth* *it*.
