I have no idea, why some people feel they need to resort to personal bashing
when they don't understand something. Let me spell it out for you a little
bit.

Since you are playing EQ, you have at some point purchased a copy of Windows
95 or 98. It may have been bundled with the system you purchased, but I
guarantee you it was in the price. I paid $85 for my OEM copy when I built
my computer, so we'll go with that price. I paid for a "product." Do you
think it cost Microsoft $85 to manufacture the CD my product was delivered
on? I think not. They charge that amount because they had to pay
programmers for their "TIME." And time, dear sir, is a commodity. It is a
precious commodity. Do you think those programmers wrote perfect code the
first time out the gate? Hell no. Why do you think they released Win95
OSR2 in 1996? And actually there were three or four releases of OSR2.
Those subsequent releases were addressing "failed code" or "failures." If
you honestly think that you did not pay for the research to correct those
failures, then you sir are extremely naive.

Let's take another quick example. A car manufacturer discovers that their
seatbelts are defective. They recall the vehicle and have the owners take
their vehicle to the nearest dealership to have a new seatbelt system
installed. That costs money. Notification to the customers, training on
the new installation procedures for the mechanics, and research from the
engineering team to develop a seatbelt that works, among many other
expenses. Where do you think that money comes from? Well, last I checked
it doesn't come from trees, so it must have come from the consumer.

Any good business, any successful business, will factor failed and defective
merchandise into the price of the item they are selling in addition to any
research and development money that was spent to create the item initially.
The first 115 or so levels in blacksmithing is your research and development
time, and your product after that point should be targetted at retrieving
the sum of money you spent up to that point within a reasonable amount of
time.

There are people on my server that sell sleeves for 9pp and tunics for 10pp.
Hooray for them. I lost 1 customer who would not pay more than 9pp for the
sleeves last night. I will not sell them for less than 14pp. Guess what?
I still sold 3 cloaks, 2 tunics, and 2 pairs of sleeves last night for a
90pp profit. It took me about 2 minutes to make it all, and I sold it in
between hunting. So you have fun making your 5gp per item profit. I will
take a few minutes here and there to recoup my costs and pay for my spells,
and still have satisfied customers. You see, my profit pays for research
and development in my other trade skills, which is my method of
diversification.

I'm sorry if you still don't understand this, perhaps you should consider a
business class or two. You might learn how to actually make a buck.
