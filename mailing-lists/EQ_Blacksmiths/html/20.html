Subject: Re: Sharpening ... Stone

Best way to increase skill at first is to first off, use as many
practice pts from your guild allowable ( usually around 20). then i
would start finding a real damn good source of income as this next plan
drains you. you make skewers.
first, ( after you have about 30 plat with you : ) make 40 metal bits
worth of ore. buy about 40 waters too ( summoned water will NOT work)
now, go find the vendor that sells molds of "skewers". Also, you might
want to get about 4 large backpacks in your inventory. buy as many
skewer molds as possilbe. now, just spend all you've got trying to make
skewers. they are very difficult to make so until your tivial, you might
find them damn near impossible to make. ** you gain skill failing too**
ingrediants for skewers:
1 water flask
1 skewer mold
1 metal bit ( its been awhile...i'm pretty sure its only 1)



you may find this a bit expensive but let me give you an idea what a
master smith is capable of making: Once you can make banded armor, (the
tunic) go around offereing to trade banded for someones chain tunic. (
also, NPC's dont like player made crap so dont bother with them.) if you
can trade a banded tunic for a chain tunic...you just made yourself at
least 60 plat! ( banded is lighter, and with the same AC as chain)


-Yizzlar, Wizard of Karana

There are two books for blacksmithing, one is "blacksmith" something
(sorry, forgot) and the other is called "weapons and armors" (or very
close). Dunno which one explains it but you should buy them anyway,
they just cost over 1gp.

If you want the answer here, you have to buy sharpening stones (not
sold everywhere) at 3 or 4cp a piece and then put the rusty weapon
and a sharpening stone in a forge (available somewhere in most
cities, ask around) and then hit combine. If you're lucky you'll get
a taarnished weapon which will be a couple delay points faster! Not
bad. You might however fail and then loose both the wepon and the
stone (lost anyway).

For me sharpening weapons became trivial at a smithing skill of 22. I
think it means that my smithing skill won't increase from it, dunno
if it means I will never be more successful. I succeed most of the
time anyway.

Thormael
