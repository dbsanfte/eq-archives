Early in beta I had talked to a real GM, not a guide, about the logic behind vendors and their pricing. ie. you sell a FS sword to a vendor you get 4p, you look back at the vendors inventory and they are selling for several hundred plat. Another prime example, very late in p4, I sold my BBC to a vendor for a whopping 3pp, when I tried to buy it back, the vendor wanted almost 4000pp. So anyways, the GM told me that the majority of items in the game were ment to be sold to players, not vendors, so vendors will only give you a fraction of what items are worth, then turn around and sell them at grosly exadurated prices. Which seems all fine and dandy since that really promotes the supply and demand type market they are shooting for. HOWEVER, when it comes to trade items, I really think they should make exceptions since nobody under about lvl 20 can really afford to take up a trade skill, and at which point you make better cash killing monsters than selling your wares.

Just my 2 cents :)
