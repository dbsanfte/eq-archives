Ok there is something your missing here: fair play.

Nobody is "colluding" or trying to monopolize any industry here. REMEMBER
that EQ's ECONOMY IS BROKEN. Therefore the standard rules of economy DO NOT
APPLY.

What we are trying to do is make smithing profitable so that people are
willing to do it. When the game first started, EVERYONE did smithing. When
people started selling at 1gp above cost, 90% of those people quit. Now you
cannot find very many smiths at all (in fact, in the last week of play I
have found 2 stretching from Qeynos, to Freeport, to Kelethin - on E'ci).

If we can settle on a base price (which the economy SHOULD be doing for
us, but is NOT doing), then we can make it profitable enough to do some
smithing. If not...well then you will still have the majority of people not
smithing, and a few who DO smith and corner the market.
