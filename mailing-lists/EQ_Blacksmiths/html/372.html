This is an interesting essay, however, I do not think it applies to EQ the
same as a real world market. You see, in EQ, every single piece of banded
is the same. Unlike in the real world, where Pepsi can claim their soda is
better than coke and offer bullshit reasons as to why. Even though Coke may
be cheaper, some people prefer Pepsi and will pay the extra $.50. In EQ,
everyone knows banded is banded so the ONLY thing that matters is price, not
quality. You can't "build a better mousetrap," because the traps are all
the same.

Sure, you can train in multiple trade skills and offer say some cheap ring
if someone buys your banded, but, if the consumer is looking only for
banded, he will choose the lower price rather than the higher price with the
extra ring or whatever.

If the armor market was more varied (say, enchanted sheet metal gives banded
different properties), then there could be quality competition as your
magical banded might be better thus sell for a higher price. But, it
doesn't exist, so the only thing that plays a part in the EQ economy is
price.
