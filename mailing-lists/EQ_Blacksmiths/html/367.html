Frank Putnam wrote:

> Greetings all,
>
> My first post. Here's a couple things that have worked well for me in
> the Qeynos area of the Bristlebain server.
>
> Whenever I see a new smith auctioning his wares in my area, I start up
> a conversation and do a price check, let him know how much he can get
> selling in my area. Wish him luck.
>
> Another thing that's worked well for me, and I hate to give this trick
> away, but it helps so much with the price fixing, what's good for all
> is good for me, and all that. So here goes.
>
> I've a couple buddies who I've enlisted as resellers for me. I sell to
> them at a 30% discount off my regular prices. They resell at my
> current full price, when they sell items off, I restock them. This
> moves my product, these friends make money (some a whole lot of it, my
> girlfriend has over 300 plat in the bank at level 17, most from
> reselling my goods). This has the added bonus of price fixing. Say
> someone asks me how much for leggings, I say 20p. That's too much they
> say, so I tell 'em check around, that's the going rate. They do a
> couple price checks with others selling banded in that area (MY
> RESELLERS) and what do ya know, that IS the going rate, yes, they did
> I price check with me, indirectly, but they don't know that. Other
> smiths see you and your resellers all selling for the same amount,
> they'll bump their prices.
>
> Strength in numbers. A group of three or four all agreeing on a price
> (those three or four include you and your resellers), have a much
> easier time convincing the new smith in town that these are the going
> rates, than just one snotty blacksmith trying to get you to up your
> rates.
>
> This works folks, give it a try.
