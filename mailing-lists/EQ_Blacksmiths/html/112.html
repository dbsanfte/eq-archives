Hi, I subscribe to the digest so I can't really
respond accordingly but I just wanted to mention
to those aspiring master blacksmiths (myself
included) that making skewers at an early skill
level is a really, really, really bad idea.
After bits become trivial the next thing you
should make it scalers. This will become trivial
at 41 (along with small and medium containers
and maybe a few others things, I haven't checked
them all). After that became trivial for me,
I started on lanterns, my skill is now 49 and
I have maybe spent 40pp total to get this far.
Also I should mention that I sharpened the rusty
daggers I got while increasing my fishing skill
instead of making metals bits until it became
trivial, which is the same skill level that metal
bits and files became trivial.

The basic premise to increase your skill it to
make things a great many times. To do this you
need to take into account the cost of making
an item so you can make more things for your
money. What I did was take all the blacksmithing
recipes and calculated the cost to make each
item then started making the cheapest thing
until it became trivial then move up to the
next higher priced item. For example, to make
a metal bit it costs 1g 2s 5c (two bricks and
water), but only it only costs 1s 5c (the selling
price of a rusty dagger and a sharpening stone)
to attempt an increase in the blacksmithing skill
before level 21, which would you prefer? I
believe the game is set up this way for skill
progression, based on my own experiences in
fletching (83) and from talking with experts in
jewelry making.

Unfortunately I think a lot of people are reading
EQstratics and EQvault to get their blacksmithing
trade information which just says "make skewers"
until you get a really high skill level then
make banded. All you are going to do is waste
hard earned money. Start looking at the smaller
web sites for the real information instead of
just the big two with their scanty, incomplete,
beta test (and IMHO worthless) information about
trade skills.
