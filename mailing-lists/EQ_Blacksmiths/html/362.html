I'm american. That doesn't make me against this price war :) Laws were established against
collusion and monopolies to prevent the abuse of the abilities they granted. It was proven
that people who practiced either concept would abuse the ability (i.e. JD Rockefeller and
Standard Oil). There is no law in EQ saying we can't practice these concepts or even one
saying we can't abuse them. We could be jerks and charge just as much as chain. If we all
agree to it, they have no choice do they? Not if they want banded anyway. I don't want to
abuse this, though, I just want the prices to be fair to me. Somebody said on this list that
EQ has a very low bottom for their economy. People don't really pay for their losses, or
collect for their gains for that matter, in anything besides game money. If someone's
character bottoms out, they can always go hunt for 10 plat and resume their smithing. In RL,
if you hit the bottom, it isn't that easy to bounce back. That's what scares people into not
going TOO low on their prices. EQ doesn't have that, people will sell at a loss or to break
even simply because they want to be nice to people who can't afford the better armor. Lots of
the higher levels can even AFFORD to do that! With that in mind, participating in a price war
will only ruin smithing. People are almost getting it for cost right now if you include
failures. Before I inflated my prices it took me an hour to earn 15 plat. That's ridiculous,
I'd rather spend that hour at a brew barrel and make the same amount. I'm not lowering my
prices, people are going to pay me what I deserve for making this armor, and I suggest you all
set the same standard. Why compete with ourselves? Competition is healthy, but too much is
not. Businesses go under because of price wars. Since the 'low price' is selling for no profit
whatsoever, there is nothing to be gained by "winning" the price war and putting other smiths
out of their trade. It'll only ruin the opportunity we have.

We have 2 people now on this list who don't want to raise prices, and we have an uncounted
amount of people who DO want to raise them. Let's raise them and make this worth our while :)

As for offering more to the customer, Peter, I'm a master smith, baker, brewer, and potter.
I'm average at tailoring and jewelcraft. I am in a guild with people who have mastered the
trades I have not, and I supply merchandise that players cannot make, but must find or buy.
I've also been known to teach languages and offer my guide services (and if the distance is
short, guiding is free). In general, people I talk to on my server like me, but that doesn't
mean they will pay me money for it. They only buy my products. Only 3 times have I ever had a
compliment on my friendliness or customer service no matter how excellent it is. Just goes to
show you that Norrath isn't your idealistic United States.
