Smithing, being one of the more expensive trades, varies considerably in
how long it takes you to master, how much it costs, and whether it's
ever profitable at all. One example. I mainly play a Dwarf. My
charisma is 45 base, and even with a few points of adjustment from items
my elven friend can purchase materials for me for 25% less than
merchants well sell to me. If I have to buy everything myself, as I
normally do, my costs are significantly higher. I've spent roughly
150pp so far. I know some make it in 100pp. It'll probably take me
another 50pp at most to finish up. Your Int score does help you raise
skills faster. If you're not a mage, as I am not, you may find that
sometimes you simply don't raise all that fast and have to work longer
and pay more. And then there's the profits. Smithing is not an NPC
profit skill. The market is solely other players. If you want to make
a good profit on smithing, you want to be on a less populated server.
Partially this is because of other smiths; too many and some tend to
drop their prices so low that the market is ruined. I play on Fennin
Ro. Last night I was trying to sell a couple bracers and a gorget. I
had already dropped my price of them to 5pp each knowing the sad market,
but I was broke and needed the pp to do some more trade practice. I
couldn't sell anything even at that price because there were two people
selling for 2pp and 4pp. I couldn't afford to sell for less at the
time. It's not always the other smiths either as that was in the
Freeport area and a couple nights ago I was in Kelethin working skewers
and didn't bother selling any banded because bronze plate was going for
just a few plat each. The Monty Hall environment has nearly ruined the
trade economy in Fennin Ro. There are so many people with so much
stuff, what one guy can make is simply not worth much. Yes, you can
make some sales if you are patient, don't need the money, and sell when
the server is down to 1200 or less people. But forget high usage times
and easy sales. They are rare. This not meant to make you not want to
smith, it's an admirable trade. It takes long enough that you should
feel pretty good about making master. But I recommend you do it on a
server with less people so you have more people who need you. You will
have a better time and they will probably be quite glad they were able
to find a good smith at all.
