ok, I'm Rennin with the big mouth, I have to put my 2 cents into the next major discussion on this list, so here goes nothing:

While I must say deteriorating armor would be EXCELLENT revenue for smiths, I am 100% against it. It's one of those things you have to experience before you can really say what you want. Go play Diablo and see how much you like armor deterioration then :) That's a simple annoyance, though. The main point of this letter is that I'm very very against armor deterioration. I will write a short anecdote below for those who wish to read it, but if you're short on time, don't bother. It's just a relation of one of my experiences that's a part
of my anti-deterioration attitude. If you've got the time, read on :)

I used to play Sierra's The Realm. I played that damn game from right after beta (v 2.xx). It was a pretty good game until the release of 3.xx. That's when they implemented armor deterioration..... Well, to briefly explain items in the game, you had an item that was mundane. Enchanters could cast several different enchantments on the thing to make it better. Every time, though, there was a chance it would destroy the item, and the more enchanted it was, the higher that chance was. 6 enchantments were unheard of, 5 were rare, and 4 were
there, but difficult. I had just replaced my 4 permed (permanents, or enchantments) adamantium tunic with a 5 permed magical armor. I can't remember the name, but it was naturally black (another game feature was you could dye your clothing. Adamantium came light blue, the armor I got was rare and came black like I like it =P). This armor was pretty much the best you could get. I also had a sword of similar quality with 4 perms on it. In one fight (it was a very very long fight with the toughest humanoid NPC in the game at the time - I
can't remember the name, but it was human and wore adamantium. Was a champion of light or some crap like that - I had to kill it =P), I lost both the tunic and the sword in that fight. After he destroyed my priceless tunic (literally, it was truly one-of-a-kind), and my near-priceless sword, I ran from the fight. I ran, logged out, and never logged back in. Losing items that take that much work to get in one fight was too much... I haven't even tried logging in since. My account has since expired.

I don't want this to happen to EQ... to get that rare piece of armor and just lose it in one shot like that... no, that's just too terrible a thing to introduce into a game. I don't want to see deteriorating armor at all... it's got too much potential to really cause a lot of frustration.
-Rennin of E'Ci
Trade Council GM
