I think this was directed at my comment about doing scalers, lanterns, and
skewers till trivial, and by then you'll be popping out the banded. The
comment was intended to mean at 115 you'll be popping out the banded (which
is where skewers were trivial for me). If it wasn't, then disregard.

As far as selling banded...I have all but stopped playing my enchanter
because I got burned out on all the requests for armor. I logged him in
last night and within 5 minutes was hit with 4 requests from return
customers. This was after taking 10 days off from the game. I politely
refused all 4 requests and promptly rolled up a new enchanter so I could
actually do something besides standing behind a hot forge making armor.
Sometimes making money is less important than doing some hard core
adventuring, though RP'able situations seem to present themselves more when
you play the role of a traveling salesman (or salesperson for you more
politically correct types).

If you want to build your business, travel. I have hit every remote region
of the world and sold jewelry, armor, and other miscellaneous wares there.
I sold one high elf fellow a polar bear cloak and got a tell from him two
days later asking for a full suit of banded. I mentioned my recent first
success in making silver-jade mana rings and added another 12pp to the sale
on the spot.

If you really want to make money on trade skills, do several of them, and
pick up critter dropped items or quest items as you travel and sell them to
the other side of the world. I've made a lot of friends and a ton of money.
I laugh now when someone asks me how in the hell a 13th level enchanter can
afford a dragoon dirk (and crookstinger), skull of Jhen'tra, polar bear
cloak, mana rings, golden ear studs, and various other assorted goodies. If
they only knew there's a few hundred plat, a full set of bronze, two sets of
banded, a runed totem staff, and two or three sets of all my research spell
components through 29th level sitting in my vault. That's all after outfitt
ing my wife and 4 buddies with full sets of banded at cost, and a few
donations made to the young here and there.

No, I hardly think the banded market is dead. It's been damaged a bit by
low priced swindlers running through town selling gorgets for 3pp each and
bracers for 4pp each. Nothing like telling someone that a tunic will cost
them 20pp only to get the reply "there's a guy that comes through here every
week and sells them for 12pp, so I'm not paying a penny more." That's fine.
Within the hour I sell it for 17 to 20pp depending on how much haggling the
victim..er..I mean..person does and whether or not I've done business with
them before.

There's a term that fits trade skills pretty well, diversity. If you put
all of your money in one trade skill and expect to buy everything you ever
wanted with it within a week, well, good damn luck to you. Myself, I funded
my jewelry making with my smithing, and my smithing was funded by selling
polar bear cloaks and similar quest items. Now that I'm pretty much set
financially for a while, I'll go back to killing stuff and making levels.
It's going to be nice to buy all of my 16th level spells as soon as I hit
16th. The best part is, I've gotten a lot of invitations to hunt from my
customers, so I don't think I'll have trouble finding a group to help me hit
the next dozen or so levels. :)

Just my 2 cents.
