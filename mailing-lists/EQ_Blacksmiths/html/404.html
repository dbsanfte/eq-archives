Subject: Re: Creating Magical Armor and Weapons


In the book it says that in order for a weapon or armor to retain any
magical effect the ore must be enchated. Why shouldn't Enchaters get
the enchant ore spell? It might make us a little more respected in the
game. If you were able to get enchanted ore for free then you should
have to buy it from NPC merchants. Why should you get as a looted item
from a creature you kill? We would have to buy the spell, you should
have to buy the ore. As for your components idea, its the same thing
with jewelry, diffrent gems have diffrent effects, but the metal MUST
be enchanted to bring the properties from their dormant state. An
abiltity like this would make others see us as useful. I have 2
characters a DE Warrior and a DE Enchanter. As the warrior I have no
problem with groping (get asked all the time). When Im an enchanter I
rarely get asked and when I do its by people who do not know what class
I am, and then the offer is recinded when they figure out that I am an
Enchanter. I have had people people ask what class I was and then just
stop talking to me when they found out what class I actully was
(originally thaught I was a Cleric). All of our buffing spells are
also used by other classes (Clerics, Shamans, Druids.) The only thing
that makes us unique is our ability to make things magical. All classes
should have something unique to only them.

Warriors - superior strength
Wizards - superior magic
Magicians - ability to use the power of the elements
Necromancers - power over the undead
Druids - power over nature
Rangers - best archers
Clerics - best healers
etc

Enchanters should be the ones to enchant any item...
but of course this is IMHO.

Ashton - DE Enchanter Veeshan
Sargonnas - DE Warrior Tunare
