Subject: Cost of gaining skill points


I have a smithing question that might first look
like a fletching questions, but please bear with
me.

To develop my fletching skill I make arrows - bows
are much too expensive for a failure. Before the
recent change to the skills I would buy a batch
of raw materials from the fletching supplier and
tediously make arrows, frequently getting a
message that my skill had gone up. I sold the
completed arrows back to a merchant in my guild
to minimize the cost of gaining skill. (The
market for arrows isn't so hot.) In my most
recent training exercise I went from a skill of
69 to a skill of 74. The outlay, after buying
raw materials and selling the completed arrows,
was about 2.5 pp, or about 5g/skill point. All
this was before the patch. Note that this cost
is very different than using the skill points
you get when you gain levels, I'm not talking
about that.

Fletchers complained bitterly about having to
make single arrows. Verant responded with a
solution that sells components in batches of
five and produces arrows in batches of five.
The problem is, the skill level is based on the
number of times the fletcher hits the "combine"
key. The tediousness of making arrows has been
fixed, but the price of gaining skill points has
been multiplied by a factor of five.

My question to you smiths is, do you know how
much it costs to increase your skill? In the
range from 60 to 80 is it 5g/point, or
2.5pp/point? Or is it far different than that?
I appreciate your patience in reading through
this. Thank you.

Ramalama - 12.95 Level Wood-Elf Ranger on Mithanial Marr
