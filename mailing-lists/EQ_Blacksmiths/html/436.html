Well, you can take up another skill. But nothing will be worth it very very
soon.

Brewing has been screwed over. What's the point of it now? Consider it a
"warrior" trade skill and be done with it, as the bonuses / negatives that
being drug give you are worth it only to them.

Jewelry? Consider it an "enchanter" trade skill, and also be done with it.
What's the point for anyone else to do it, besides just to say "Hey look! I
can make pretty jewelry! Oh...well, you see, it doesn't DO anything....but
doesn't it look good?" Leave this for the enchanters.

Fletching. Currently worthless as well. You can't make any arrows any
better than you can buy at the store (not by much, anyway). And the
turnaround just isn't worth it either. Leave it for those who use bows.

Baking. Useful, being able to make your own food or whatnot. And I hear
that fishrolls are as profitable, if not slightly moreso (in that you can
make them with your own portable spit, and not wait in line) than pottery.
However, I wouldn't spend my time on this skill. I just summon my food and
drink anyways.

Pottery. Soon to be screwed, similar to brewing. Gnomes need pie tins I
hear, but why can't they just learn the skill and make one? Sure, ceramic
linings are needed, and a few others for baking. But how many bakers out
there are rushing up to potters and buying up their wares? I doubt very
many. I know for a fact I have never used my own pottery skills to make a
ceramic lining to use in blacksmithing. Go fig.

Tailoring. This skill requires to much running about and such. I must kill
"x" creature to get "y" skin, then buy "j" mold to make "z" product. I
don't think so. While useful (seeing as how there are few who make studded
leather or reinforced leather, it is useful), but not worth my time for me.

If I'm leaving anything out, sorry. I'll nitpick some other time. My point
in all this is as follows. If I wish to spend my time making pie tins with
pottery to sell for a little bit of a profit, why shouldn't I be able to? I
spent the time to learn the skill. I'm sacrificing my time that I could be
gaining experience to make a little bit of cash. That seems like an even
trade to me.

And in the end, if it comes down to it, we should AT LEAST be able to sell
what we make to merchants for the COST of the parts to make the item. No
profit. No loss (unless you have a failure).

Well, done ranting.
