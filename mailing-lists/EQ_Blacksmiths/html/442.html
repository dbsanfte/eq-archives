Everybody keeps missing the fact that Artifacts of Great power would
probably not deteriorate. Or anything that is magic for that matter, because
it has a special quality unlike normal items. But how about that Rusty
Longsword that you've been using for the last 6 months of adventuring. It
just doesn't make sense that that item would last too long. That's why it's
rusty.

My second point is that this could allow a lower level character to gain
specific items that weren't available before. Let's say as an item
deteriorates that it's AC and price do as well. Now, after a little wear,
that nifty Banded Tunic is now AC 7, and can be sold for 8-10pp. The
purchaser now takes it to a Smith and gets the item reapired for say 3-5pp
(which takes the item back to it's AC 15 state). Or, he/she can choose to
repair it over time, to save money. So now you have a set of armor that cost
fractionally less, but over time will yield the same protection as a full
suit of "fresh" armor.

Also, deterioration should not destroy the item completely. It should just
take it down to the lowest AC possible. Instead of losing those Banded
Bracers, just make them drop until AC is 1 or 2. Then you have to go get
them polished to restore their protective value.

Just my thoughts :)
