I payed for my mistakes the day I became a smith. Smithing is not profitable given the
current market standard. Go ahead and sell at your current prices and make 15 to 20
plat an hour. I'll be out buying the 200 and 300 plat items at the bazaar for the next
festival my guild is throwing with the profit we make (if you haven't read our guild
charter, I'll tell you now. All of the profits my 'greediness' gets goes to holding
events on the E'Ci server).

There are two reasons to do any trade. To roleplay or to make money. I'm not much of
an EQ roleplayer, if I want to do that, I'll have 10 people over and chuck dice for 7
hours. I've done it before, it's fun... it's not EQ. I perform my trades to make money
and the last 6 times I've decided to try smithing instead of my other skills I have
ALWAYS lost money on the market standard. In fact, no matter what my price, my
customer response is always the same. When I sold at truly ideal prices, I got the
same amount of customers and the same amount of people who wouldn't pay my price. When
I lowered the price, the customers didn't change. I didn't get any more people buying
from me, I didn't get any less. Instead, I lost money on my failures. I'm supposed to
willingly lose money and watch a trade I spent quite a reserve of platinum mastering
go down the drain? Smithing was my first skill and I had to sell the armor off my back
to master it. I've put in my dues and I expect to get something back for it. Just
because I want to get something for my efforts doesn't make me greedy. If you want to
see how greedy I am, visit http://tradecouncil.eqrealms.com/events.html and take a
look at the events my guild is hosting. Notice... they are FREE! Go ahead and add up
the costs of all the items we're offering and see how much money I'm losing. I'm not
getting anything for that. I'm doing it as a service to my server which has given me
the opportunity to make it where I am today.

This is NOT a flame. You go right ahead and sell for whatever you please, but it won't
solve anything. Everything I've been saying is entirely my opinion, you have the right
to accept it or refuse it. I don't want to see another flame aimed at me calling me a
'greedy asshole' because I want to make something out of an expensive trade. It took a
LOT of work to earn the plat to master smithing at level 11 (that's when I started
trade skills, and smithing was my first as I said previously). No, I did not twink my
character. Rennin is my highest level character, still at level 11, being the merchant
that I created him to be. I didn't get help from any high-level friends, either. I
started from scratch and built myself up. If you want to know how, e-mail me, not the
list, I'd be happy to tell you. I'm not harboring any secrets, I didn't do anything
unusual, I worked for what I got. Don't go telling me I'm greedy because I want to get
something for the work I've done.
