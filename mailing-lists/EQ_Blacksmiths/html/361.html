it's good, but simple items are expensive and complicated items are cheap. It
ends up evening out, but you'll eventually kill yourself by only selling the
cheap stuff. A helm for 10pp? The lowest I ever sold one was 12 plat, and that
was before I raised my prices :) Then, a gorget for 7pp? After I raised my
prices I sell them for 6 plat :) If a consumer gets smart, you will only be
selling your inexpensive items (bracers, helms, sleeves, belt, boots, leggings)
and the expensive items (cloaks, gorgets) won't sell at all. The rest will be
inbetween. The pricing list I've been using (I posted it earlier, check the
archives) has worked excessively well. The closer together all of our prices
are, the more willing the consumers will be to buy for higher prices. If they
see a lot of varying prices, they'll want to shop around first (which is a
disaster if the cheap-o smith zones in while they're shopping). Let's try to get
as close to a specific price list as possible so we can avoid any possible
problems. I enjoy hearing "yeah, that's a good price, I'll take it" :)
