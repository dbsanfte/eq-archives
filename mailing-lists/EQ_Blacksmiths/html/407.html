I've worked my models on the assumption that there are a limited amount of smiths out there
and there won't be 4 smiths in a zone. You've seen how many auctions there are for banded. Try
ignoring them once and seeing how long they can run on for. This isn't a matter of
competition, it's a matter of 'they want it if they can get a fair price'. The point of price
fixing is to fix that 'fair price' at a higher amount than it is currently. The more people
that sell higher, the more accustomed people will become to paying it, and the more 'fair'
they feel it will be. If there's another smith in a zone when I first walk in, and I intend on
selling any banded, I politely change zones no matter what their price. I will not undersell
them out of courtesy and it'd be pointless to oversell them. I don't even sell at equal prices
because I might be taking sales they would have otherwise gotten. This is just my politeness,
though, if you want to make a few enemies, go ahead and undersell every smith you see. All
you'll get is a crashing market on your head and a poisoned dagger in your back.

Back to the point, though, your argument with me so far has been that price fixing won't work
because people will always sell for lower and customers will refuse to buy for the higher
price. The truth is, people are starting to sell for higher all across the boards, and
customers are buying at a greater profit to those of us who have decided to price raise.
Whether you realize it or not, that's a fact, and you can't dispute it. You can't tell me
green is blue any more than you can tell me people won't buy my banded because it's higher
priced. So, to conclude, pick your side. Side A spends an hour a day making banded, Side B
spends 5 hours. Both sides make the same profit.
