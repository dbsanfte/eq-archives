I'll try to respond to Scott and Dennis at the same time. This will be a
long read. You've been warned. :)

I will agree wholeheartedly that time is money. If I had not spent the time
that I did smithing, I would be 10 levels higher with probably the same nice
equipment I have now. But, I like smithing, I like interacting with the
community, and I like the nice items I am able to now buy.

Let's get something else out of the way as well. Price fixing is never
going to work. There are 50+ master smiths per server, with a new one
becoming a master smith every day. Price fixing just won't happen unless
you can get a majority of the smiths together and hope that they are all
intelligent enough to understand that selling less at a more expensive price
is good.

The only feasible way of price fixing, which I practice occasionally, is to
collude with a fellow smith in the area. If I am selling armor in greater
faydark and another smith starts selling, I sometimes (depending on their
attitude) price fix with them so we don't end up screwing eachother. In the
end, we both make out because the consumers are forced to buy from one of
us.

To kurt: Good for you! Too bad you can't be everywhere on every server at
once. So, the people that are buying from you are making out, but, that's
only one small corner of your server's market.

To Nick: I disagree, we have lots to lose. We have a customer base and a
reputation. If we start jacking our prices, our reputation is going to go
down the drain. Believe it or not, word of mouth is a big contribution to
sales. If this little plan fails, it may take a while to recover that lost
reputation.


Now, I don't know why everyone is griping about not making enough money to
recuperate their initial costs. I recovered my inital investment in the
first couple of days. Perhaps the area you're selling in is not a very
popular banded area. I sell in greater faydark and it's excellent. GFay
offers many things that I love.

1. I am in reach of two sizes of molds, small and medium, which allows me
to sell to two groups of players increasing my customer base by 50% (Elves
prefer small, and there are not THAT many humans, barbarians, or
half-elves.)

2. I don't have to zone to use a bank, which means I don't have to carry my
wares on me, which means unlimited access to my supplies without zoning.

3. It is home to two races and adjacent to two more races. A huge plus in
customer base.

4. Perhaps the biggest reason of all, it is home to a HUGE level range.
Because of Crushbone, most elves, dwarves, and gnomes migrate to the
Crushbone area starting at level 5 and stay there throughout their early
teens. At level 5, they see me advertising my armor. Over the next three
days, they gain levels and money and continuously see me advertise my armor.
When they finally gain enough money, they put two and two together and ask
me for their banded armor.
