Rose, the dragons' spells are all AoE and will get you even when you stand
out of healing range of your party. I've learned that it makes no
difference if I stand on the steps (at Nag) or right behind my tanks in
front of the dragon as to damage I take. And you MUST be in healing range
of your party, otherwise why be there? One VERY interesting thing I have
discovered tho......on the last two dragon raids I positioned myself BEHIND
the dragon, almost under his/her butt. Guess what....I did not suffer
nearly the dmg from their spells I did when I was in front of them yet was
still in healing range of my party. And I never got physically hit,
although I rarely do unless I get summoned, then I get clawed big time.

I assume all you dragon hunters out there are aware that in order for your
nukers' spells to not be resisted and do max dmg you must have them
positioned and casting directly UNDER the dragons' bellies? That is the
vulnerable spot, where they are the least resistant and more susceptible to
damage.

Also, Rose..make dang sure you keep BOTH of your Invulnerable spells up when
doing these high level hunts. Divine Aura and Divine Barrier. I have
survived many a dragon raid or a bad FG pull to be able to keep my party
alive by casting one of these on me early enough before I was almost dead.
Be sure to tell your party members when you cast it tho, so another healer
doesn't waste mana trying to heal you.

Mik
