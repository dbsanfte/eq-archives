Um, not raising the level cap would be a really, really bad idea. High level
characters would leave in droves for Asheron's Call, Middle Earth, whatever
else is out there.

Part of the fun, and the addictive factor, of RPG's is the illusion of
growth: every day you get stronger, more powerful, able to to places you
were not able to before -- cf. the Plane of Fear discussion -- and
successfully kill critters that you would have run in terror from a few
levels before.

If characters stop growing, a lot of the fun of the game is gone. I hit the
level cap in Balder's Gate about midway through Chapter 4...and lost
interest in the game a few days later. Haven't finished it, probably won't
ever.

I am not sure why Verant would NOT want to raise the level cap.

I imagine there might be ways to fake it. For example, besides items that
raise stats, there might be items that raised one's Offensive or Defensive
or weapons capabilities beyond their caps at level 50, making you
effectively a level 55 or 60 or higher character, even though you are still
nominally level 50. Hm. That might be interesting.

Still, I think Verant has to do something to allow players to increase their
capabilities beyond 50, and to provide challenging places for them to go to
exercise those capabilities, if they want to retain their player base.

== Gilead, dwarven cleric of Fennin Ro
