Here's a peek into my thinking and reflections on my choice of ability points
distribution:

When I started my Dwarven Paladin, I put extra points into agility,
intelligence, and a few leftover points into wisdom and dexterity. I never
escape a battle unscathed, so I'm not sure the agility is helping or not - I
took that one on faith. The 75 int. helps a lot in learning new skills quickly
- as an example, when I am grouped with someone and doing "language lessons"
where we speak to each other in our native language to teach the other party
member that language ( I was learning Halfling, the Halfling was learning
Dwarven), I saw the "your language skills have improved" message every 2
screens of scrolling text. Another example is "you have gotten better at bash"
which I get every 8th bash or so until I am topped out for my level. The
leftover points in Wisdom offer the direct benefit of more mana, but as I
advance in levels I have noticed that the amount of mana I have becomes less of
an issue as my spells become more potent and my mana increases naturally. And
finally Dexterity - I miss so often swinging that 1h minotaur axe I wonder if I
picked up a cursed one! It seems like I miss about 4-6 out of 10 swings on
average.

The 100 strength we get is more than adequate for us Paladins, so I left that
alone. The 55 charisma was so pathetic I didn't think that sinking all my 20
points into it would be worthwhile, and so far I've been happy enough with the
prices I get at the Human NPC vendors - this took some work in building up my
faction with those merchants, but I wanted to kill the faction-raising gnolls
of BlackBurrow anyways! I also picked up a medal of merit that raises my CHA to
60, and there are other charisma-raising items in the game.

So far, I've had a great time wandering the plains of the karanas, visiting
those friendly folks in Rivervale, chopping ankles in BlackBurrow. Sometimes I
wonder if I made the "best" ability point distribution, but to be honest I'm
usually having so much fun that I don't care! I don't think it's even possible
to hamstring yourself with a poor point distribution, so just do your best and
get to adventuring!

Your brother in arms,
Kaladin, 11th level Paladin (and possibly the ugliest dwarf in existence...?)
