Hail,

Just some comments to the list. I read the list in digest version so I
can't respond to specific emails.

1. Do not overly worry about having enough mana to cast spells. I have
reached the 19th Order in the Service of Brell and have yet to find a
spell I cannot cast due to lack of total mana.

2. It is best to always seed any new skills. Check your spell book to
see what skill the new spells you gain are and drop a point accordingly
into each one. Just as a tip, buy the lowest mana spell in each class,
just for the purpose of skilling. I used sense undead to raise my
Divination skill, once I started it maxxed in about 3 days of casting.
I cast while hanging out just about anywhere. The hardest for us I
think will be conjuration, to the extent I haven't even bought the
Hammer spell because I don't see a use.

3. Magic weapons are important. I am using a Combine 2-Handed at the
moment. The cost was very high at 112 plat, but quite worth it in the
long run. I am fortunate to have several friends with whom I pool coin
to purchase items. But ye need to try and save at least 60 plat and buy
a Combine Longsword to use for coining. The reasoning behind this is
the combines are faster and hit harder, thereby allowing you to slay
those evil bastages in a more timely fashion. Willows are great coin,
the lightstones sell for 8 gold (i'm a dwarf, charisma ain't me strong
point) and the greaters sell for almost 4 plat. That is to merchants.
There are always players out there in need and you can make roughly
fifty percent profit over merchant costs sometimes. On a note about
Ghoulsbane, the crew I'm with will be attempting to get a few of those
this weekend. One thing I know for sure, don't try it if your under
20th level. The rumor is the FroggerKing is close to level 30.

4. I remember seeing a message regarding a back entrance to North
Freeport. I am curious if anyone has better details of this. I plan
on rampaging those evil guards this weekend (very holy-type evil grin).
