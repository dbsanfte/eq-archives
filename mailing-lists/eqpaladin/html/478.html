Subject: magic only creatures - my solution and question


I use a minotaur axe/targ shield combo 99% of the time, but should I need to
attack or defend against a creature only hit by magic weapons then I haul out
my trusty staff or dagger (fang?) summoned by a friendly magician and commence
wacking the baddie. It would be nice to have the combine longsword or some
other decent magical weapon, but I'm still about 50 platinum short, what with
having to buy armor, arrows, etc.

The staff is nice - dmg 8, delay 40? - and my skill is going steadily up in 2H
blunt. The summoned dagger is decent as well (dmg 4, delay 21?) but doesn't do
nearly enough damage when hunting wisps or scarecrows. I'm also working on
pushing 1H blunt up for when I can summon the mace in four levels. If I
*really* want to, I could go punish bandits with a wizard friend in the karanas
and have that combine longsword in a week. The thing is, I don't usually hunt
for creatures hit by magic only because there's so many critters running round
that don't have that limitation!

So here's a question for you higher-level paladins (16th and over) - at what
level does it become necessary to have a magic weapon, and can the situations
that require a magic weapon be handled by our summoned mace?

Kaladin, the curious 11th level Paladin
