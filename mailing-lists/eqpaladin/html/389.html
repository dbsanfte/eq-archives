A call to arms for all Paladins!

This message is for all Paladins of the Knights of Truth in Freeport. This is
a Quest update, and a call for support as well.

The Test of Truth, a quest given by a Paladin behind the Hall of Truth, is
billed as one of the
most important quests in the game for a Paladin of that guild. This test,
however is rarely even
started on my server (Cazic-Thule) and on many others from the people I have
talked with. The
reason is the reward for the quest is not very well defined. First let me give
you some basic
information on the quest.

What you join the Knights of Truth Paladin guild in Freeport you are
welcomed warmly, but you are made well aware that you are NOT a Knight yet.
You start as a squire, and upon asking the Knights you will find that there is
one, and only one way to become a true Knight. And that is to complete the
Test of Truth. I won't spoil the entire quest, but it starts by bringing a
letter to Altunic Jartin, the roaming shield salesman in East Commonlands. The
quest is in two parts. At the completion of each part you receive a Token.
When you have both tokens you return them to the Knight who gave you the quest
and this starts the actual Test of Truth. All of this information is given to
you vaguely when you first ask about the quest.

Please note that you cannot start this quest after 8th level!
Also, the quest is NOT BROKEN (it was fixed very soon after the release of the
game) but finding Altunic Jartin can be difficult as he moves around the zone.

Upon completion of the Test of Truth you are welcomed as a Knight and
given a note to prove it. This is where I need the assistance of all Paladins
in the Realm.

Now that I (Vawhn on Cazic-Thule) have completed the quest and have
become a Knight, I
would expect to have a title to show it. I mean, I am a Knight, what else
(other than a holy sword and a trusty steed) could a Paladin ever want. But I
did not get a title. In fact, other than the note, which I keep safely locked
away in the bank, I have no proof that I am a Knight at all.

I have talked to a GM about this, and suggested that a Sir be added to
the beginning of my
name, or that be added to the end of my name (I preferred the Sir, and made
that fact clear) and the GM really liked the idea. He said he would bring it
up at their next meeting, and asked me to write a feedback detailing my
opinion and justification, which I did.

I ask all of you to do the same, if you support my suggestion of course.
My justification was
simple (although I welcome any differing opinions, as I am sure the GMs
would). I have two
reasons for this request. First, I play a Paladin because I am playing a RPG,
and I like to play the character (as much if not more than to have the
abilities). For my character, what better way to show that I am eternally
dedicated to my code than to have my title displayed in my name for all to
see. The second reasoning is that (according to the game itself) 50% of
Everquest is the questing, and I found this quest a little, ok VERY
disappointing. If a quest existed that could make such a permanent and
profound change to a young Paladin, it would improve the questing at least in
Freeport (which for those of you who don't take the quests, there is a very
good story going on there).

Again, if you support this idea, please send a feedback (In Game) with
your ideas and opinions. The seed has already been planted, and Everquest
DOES LISTEN. I know this from personal experience. They just need to hear
about it from others, and hear about it often.

No you just need your Trusty Steed and your Holy Sword. Good luck on the
horse.
