Acually that is not entirely correct... It would be at the END of the
battle, but not during the battle... You see DURING the battle, if you have
2 tanks fighting, they are both EARNING or HEALING at the rate of 1HP per
round (I think) - Multiply this times the number of tanks (2) ... But if one
is Full HP, he is not earning a thing, so you are really only getting 1 HP
per round... Now, if the MOB is concentrating on the one tank, he will be
going down and down and down because he is healing 1HP per round but
probably taking hits for 1+ each round....

With 2 tanks trading off, when one gets down a bubble, the other starts
taking the damage... Then you have TWO HP per round (without healing) being
generated by your group. Gives the first tank a chance to actually INCREASE
his HP instead of it going down... This saves you mana right there..

Also at the END of battle when you have two tanks both down, lets say 2
bubbles each, you can heal them each ONE time (two total heals) up to, say
4.5 bubbles full and they can heal the rest of the way by sitting while you
are getting mana back... Again twice as fast as one tank healing himself...
Otherwise, you have one guy down FOUR bubbles and you have to heal him with
THREE Heals... and then EVERYONE still has to wait for you to get your mana
back, which is now 33% lower...

Last night in our group we had two clerics, and by trading off on the heals
and being diligent enought to do 1-2 quick heals and med again DURING
battle, we were able to keep our tanks alive with some pretty HELLATIOUS Orc
tranes (RED ones!) in Oasis... They just kept coming and coming, and coming,
and still coming... we just kept healing, medding, healing, medding all the
while the tanks were fighting, fighting, trading, fighting... etc, etc...

Worked out VERY well... we didn't die (although came close a few times -
thank Tunare for Zone Walls!) and only a couple of times did we zone because
BOTH clerics had run out of mana! It was a glorious feeling let me tell
you!

So, have the tanks trade off every few hits (so the mob doesn't pick on one
of them the whole time) AND the healers should be trading off between
healing and medding - You should NEVER be just standing there watching...
You should either be casting, fighting or medding... One of the three...

But of course, all this is IMSO...
