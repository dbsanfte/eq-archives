When you exit Runnyeye into Misty Thicket all you have to do basically is go
straight and you will come to a wall and gate guarded by halfling guards.
Beyond the wall is Rivervale where you have to enter and then you will find
an entrance to Kithicor woods. In Kithicor there is a path that leads to the
left that you want to take. Follow it and you should come to the west
commons. Watch out for the ghouls, hill giants, griffons and the occasional
werewolf. Just follow the left wall and it will lead you to East commons
where you'll find a path that leads straight to Freeport.

>I wanted to try to run a fairly new (level 3) barbarian warrior from
>Halas to Rivervale (well on to Freeport actually). It's a long trip but

>person in the entire zone heh). You may not be interested in Halas to
>Qeynos hills but the rest of the journey may be helpful to those from
>Qeynos desiring to head east.

>Below are instructions as told to me by Moro (met on Rathe) and verified
>up to the unfortunate RunnyEye incident. First before you leave put
>your stuff in the bank and GET A LIGHT SOURCE. You can make it to
>Qeynos and kill fire beetles to get their eyes if you prefer.

>
>Everfrost:
>Heading down from Halas exit stay pretty much to the right the entire
>way. There are some little dead ends but the route is pretty straight
>forward.
>
>You'll eventually end up at a cavern entrance. It is guraded by a
>scrawny gnoll I believe which is always dead whenever I've gone through
>(thanks to other people). The tunnel winds a ways down. It's dark and
>you may not have a light source yet. There are torches on the walls
>providing light spaced just far enough apart that you will be in the
>dark for a few steps till you turn a corner. The way I did it was to
>turn around and face the torch I could see and back up till I backed
>around the corner then I turned to the new torch. Do this a few times
>and you'll zone.
>
>Blackburrow:
>This is pretty funny. The zone exit to Qeynos hills is about 20 seconds
>away. When you enter you'll be in a small tunnel. Follow it out.
>There are gnolls all over in this area (you're in their home). Probly
>there will be lots of people too. When you exit from the tunnel you'll
>see a pit in front of you with a waterfall spilling into it. Go to the
>left around the pit. A hard left will lead you to a door. Don't go in
>there, that goes down to bad gnoll land. The next tunnel beyond that
>around the pit will zone you.
>
>Qeynos Hills:
>If you don't have a light source, get one here somewhere as it'll be the
>last chance (other than to bum something from another player). You'll
>exit into a broad open area. Take a left and follow a valley staying on
>the left side up a ways on the wall. Watch for rabid wolves and
>grizzleys. After a couple of minutes you'll come to a path. Right
>leads to Qeynos, left leads to the plains. Go left and follow into a
>canyon. You'll zone a little ways in, no danger in there unless someone
>brought it with them I think.
>
>West Plains:
>Oh man, this place is huge. Upon entering follow the path till you
>leave the canyon. Turn right. Go straight and a little more right (you
>want South) till you hit a river/sea. This runs along the south side of
>this entire zone. If you get lost I guess make sure you try to head
>south. I wouldn't wander out there though... Once you hit the river
>take a left and follow it a long long long long long long ways. Like
>running for almost 10 minutes long. No danger really along the water
>that I saw.
>
>East Plains:
>Still running along the river with it on your right. Keep an eye out
>ahead as there is a raider group that sits by the water waiting for
>people like you running along the water. Other than them no problem
>just don't go explore up the river bank. Except for going around them
>(watch for lionness and griffawn) don't go away from the water. You'll
>pass a bridge, you don't want this one. When you come to the next
>bridge (suspension like) with chains turn right and cross bridge.
>
>North Plains:
>Take a left off of the bridge. The river is now on your left. A ways
>ahead you'll see probably a cyclops out in the water to your left (named
>Boron or something). On your right are lions probably and some ruins up
>on the right. You need to get far enough away from the water but also
>stay away from the lions. Go through this area till a little bit
>farther you'll hit the zone boundary but can't go through there. Take a
>right and hug the boundary which is now on your left and you left the
>river behind you. There is some kind of camp on the right and there
>were gorge wolves wandering around down below. Stay up and you should
>be ok. After a short run (couple of minutes) you'll drop down to the
>valley floor with a canyon to your left. Go left keeping an eye for
>stuff running from your right probably. You'll zone soon down that
>canyon so if something is chasing you you'll probably be OK.
>
>Beholder Maze:
>I would wait till day and wait till some people clear out the goblins
>and minotaurs you'll see when you zone in. The trick to getting through
>here is listen for stuff coming and always keep to the right wall. Hug
>the wall around to the right and you'll come to the zone exit soon. If
>stuff is chasing you, when you see the tunnel entrance you're almost
>safe, just a few steps in is the zone.
>
>RunnyEye:
>DO NOT ATTEMPT WITHOUT LIGHT. This is where I made my mistake and as
>far as I've gone so far. The trick to getting through here I was told
>is to stay left. I don't think it's very far because there was one
>other person who zoned in with me and before I could say something they
>ran off. Then when they didn't answer my hails I did a /who and found I
>was alone. So, either that person died or zoned.
