This is largely a semantic issue. There are few facts
or actual stats that 'prove' one is 'better' than the
other. Here are the facts that are known and
unbiased.

Typically, 1H weapons have a lower delay than 2H
weapons. This is significant because you'll swing
more often with a lower delay weapon (this is why
watching Monks fight can be rather stunning, because
fists have a very low delay in the combat system). If
you're fighting something that you will miss often
against, you may want to swing more often (more
swings), because it gives you more opportunities to
connect even amid the misses. In other words: if you
get 25 swings in a fight, and miss with half; you only
hit 12-13 times. If, however, you get 40 swings in a
fight, you hit 20 times. Sometimes, especially
against casters, this is vastly preferable.

When comparing weapons, use the DD ratio (Damage to
Delay) to determine how they stack up against one
another.

On the low end, the weapons are rougly equal. When
you start comparing various mid and high end weapons
of various sources and magicks, the ratios start to
diverge from one another. For example, the famed
Warrior only Executioner Axe (2h-s) is 25/50 .500; a
*phenomonal* efficiency. Ghoulbane (1h-s), Paladins
only, is 15-34 .411.

That's all hard fact.

The murky part, subjective part, is the rest of it.
Some people just flat out like the 'sexy' factor of
seeing a *big* number in the damage line when they see
one of their swings connect. Others are simply
looking for a weapon that will deal out significant
damage over a series of swings. The former look
almost exclusively at 2h, the latter at mostly 1h.

Further, when using 2h, you give up a shield. Not
only are you giving up the AC, but you also give up an
enchantment slot (like with the Darkwood's +9 STR, for
example), and as a Paladin, you give up your only
special attack (Bash). Warriors are better equipped
to go with 2h because they retain Kick and (if large)
Slam.

So, you have a lot to weigh. An *excellent* low level
weapon, and a perfectly servicable mid level weapon is
one with a .200 - .300 ratio. For example:

FS (or Combine) Longsword = 6/28 .214
Minotaur Axe = 8/37 .216 (note Mino axe is 1/3rd
slower)
Dwarven 1h Axe = 6/26 .231 (faster than FS LS)
Barbed Leather Whip = 7/28 .250 (very fast, good
damage)
Demon Claw = 5/19 .263 (incredibly fast)

*typically* (but not exclusively), Bash weapons (1h or
2H) tend to have lower efficiency ratios than
equilivant leveled weapons on the Slashing side.
Examples:

Staff = 5/28 .179
Warhammer = 6/32 .188
Screaming Mace = 8/35 .229
Dwarven Warhammer = 8/33 .242

So, with blunt, you'll usually work harder in the
fight.

Finally, even as a dwarf, you're best served by
starting the level-capping of relevant weapons skills
now, as you go up. Even if you don't 'finish' capping
before you level again, keep working the skills you
choose to work up. If you get behind by a few levels
in 2hs or 1hb or whatever while you're leveling up,
don't let it concern you overly; just keep switching
back to your primary after each level and capping that
out first, then going back to wherever you left off
the secondaries. By the time you hit the double
digits (10+), your skills will catch up. And it's far
easier to maintain for 5 points per level than it is
to train up 30 or 50 or whatever points after you find
a really great weapon that you want to start using.

Good luck!
