--- Kent Greig <kgreig@...> wrote:

> I'm interested in people's views on the mix of STR,
> AGI and DEX. I'm assuming everyone thinks stamina
> is irrelevant.

Stamina's not irrelevant, but each Paladin race either
has a sufficient starting Stamina (Dwarves at 95,
Humans at 80), or has glaring weaknesses in other
attributes that are more important to be addressed
than Stamina (Erudites at 75, Half Elves at 75, High
Elves at 70). Further, increases to Stamina show very
minor effect compared to the same increase applied to
any of the other three Physical attributes. +10 STA
doesn't help you nearly as much as +10 STR, AGI or DEX
does. STA should be an afterthought when considering
enhancements; and certainly don't put points into it.

I have an 80 Agility with my Dwarven Paladin, and when
considering armor vs AGI for overall effectiveness in
protecting the character, armor is more important.
Put on full plate (bronze or steel) and you'll be much
less concerned with what your AGI score is.

Level 25 Dwarven Paladin:
Starting = 110--95--80--90--88--60--55
Current == 126--95--84-100--88--60--55

I highly recommend STR and STR enhancements as the
number one priority. Armor is heavy, good armor is
heavier. Even for Dwarves, who start with an
excellent STR; more is better. STR adds to the attack
score, increases the damage bonus once you get over
20, and also enables you to loot much more. For the
other Paladin races, who start with much lower STR
scores; STR enhancements are a vital necessity. My
setup is +16 STR (and working on more), which would
change a STR rollup in the following Paladins like
this:

> Erudite Paladin---106--75--70--70--88-105--80
> Half Elf Paladin--116--75--90--85--65--75--85
> High Elf Paladin--101--70--85--70-100--90--90
> Human Paladin-----121--80--75--75--80--75--85

With +16 STR Enhancements, their STR scores start to
shape up very nicely.

DEX is important for a number of reasons. It affects
your attack connect ratio; higher equals fewer misses.
It also affects secondary weapon effects; higher
equals it going off more often. Finally, with a
higher DEX you see Parries and Ripostes happen more
often.

AGI directly affects your defense, and also increases
the chance of getting a Dodge off.
