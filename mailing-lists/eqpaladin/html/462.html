--- Dragon <dracon@...> wrote:
> ... like to know which race is the best for paladin.

If you really meant that question, "best for", you
won't like the answer. There *is* no best race for
anything; it's always a matter of perspective and
style. How do you view it, and how do you want to
play it? There are only strengths and weaknesses,
from which you can select what best suits your desires
for the character. Keeping that in mind, here are
thumbnail breakdowns of the five Paladin-eligible
races.

Dwarves have the best starting physical stats, and
(arguably) the most balanced starting stats in terms
of Paladin attribute requirements and not having low
numbers in said requirements. 100-95-70-90-88-60-55.
As a dwarven paladin, you have no physical
deficiencies that will handicap you, and your WIS is
more than sufficient for your magical uses. The
drawback of a dwarven paladin is the low INT and CHA.
These can be worked around.

Erudites are tied for the best mentals; the highest
INT and the second highest WIS, and don't have
crippled physicals like High Elf paladins do.
70-75-70-70-88-107-80. However, erudite paladins do
have the second lowest physicals, and thus can have
problems in that area.

Half Elven paladins have the lowest starting WIS among
the paladin races, and typically are the least able
casters. The physicals aren't bad, but focus more on
AGI and DEX than on STR or STA. The Mentals are
strictly average, except for the low WIS.
80-75-90-85-65-75-75.

High Elven paladins have the lowest starting
physicals, but tie for the highest mentals.
65-70-85-70-100-92-90. High elf paladins are
enternally having encumberance problems, and will
suffer somewhat in combat until they get a high skill
level, and also collect enchantments that boost their
meager physicals. But they have the best starting
WIS, and the second best INT; and that focuses them
more on the casting side.

Human paladins are, as one might anticipate, the
middle of the road. They're solid, can can be built
towards one thing or another; and aren't poor at
anything in particular, while also not being excellent
at anything either. 85-80-75-75-80-75-85. Depending
on where the points are placed, a Human paladin can be
many things.

Figure out what best suits your intentions for the
character, and select accordingly. High Elves have
the most STR problems, Dwarves the most skill and
price problems, Half Elves the most magic problems.
Humans have no major problems, but no major
advantages; and Erudites pay a surprisingly low price
for their advantage of superior mentals with only
slightly below average physicals. The humans (high
men and men) don't have Infravision, which many gamers
consider a requirement.

Good luck.
