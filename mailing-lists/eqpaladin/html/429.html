Ok, here's how I went about this.

(at time) 16th Dwarven Paladin on Tarew Marr

Thursday I heard one of the undocumented features in
the patch was the addition of archery to Paladins.
When I got home Thursday, I confirmed I could buy bows
and equip them. I then discovered the only places to
get fletching supplies/arrows were at the two ranger
guilds (Surefall Glade and Kelethin - any other arrow
and/or fletching sources exist, speak up please!). So
I travelled back to my homeland (Feydwer) after
stopping at my guild and putting 1 point each into
Archery and Fletching before logging off Thursday.

Friday evening, I went to Kelethin and bought a quiver
(mistake, don't bother; backpack has twice the slots,
didn't notice any difference between the two; if there
is one, someone fill me in), a fletching kit and
components for class 1 arrows, and started fletching.
I worked up through the components until, 3 hours
later, I had taken Fletching to 56. I was then able
to make, on my first try, a Hickory/Hemp bow.

I used that bow for the next hour or two, then bought
an Elm/Hemp from someone auctioning bows (paid 5pp, a
fair price considering what I know about the materials
costs) and kept using it. I didn't bother making
arrows after that; saving 4cp per arrow is *not* worth
the 6-9 minutes it takes to do 20 arrow attempts, IMO.
I just bought the 1sp each arrows for sale at the
fletchers'.

I spent the time in Crushbone's Throneroom, firing
arrows at everyone else's blue+ (to me) mobs; no one
minded because A) I'm helping them kill their target
B) There's *no* way an archer will killsteal from a
warrior or wizard fully engaged and C) I also
healed/buffed them while I was hanging around. I had
to run from Dvinn on occasion, but Friday night and
Saturday I took Archery to 65.

I then proceeded to fill up 80% of my bank slots, and
3 backpacks on myself, with 1sp arrows and returned to
the Ro Desert. Since then, working archery as a pull
and chase killer, I've brought it to 73.

The Tiny Daggers people talk about won't equip for
Paladins right now; dunno if it's supposed to be that
way or not.

Fletching is a *cheap* skill to bring up; I spent
about 1 1/2 pp on the kit and then another 3-4pp to go
from 1 to 56, so it's very cheap. The Hickory/Hemp
bow I made only cost about 4gp to try (first try
succeeded). The Elm bows cost 2-4pp per try, but the
next level up (Ash) cost 20-30pp per try (all
depending on string type and tools used; cams are
*expensive*, 45pp each to my low CHA dwarven paladin).

This info comes from experience and some fletching
sites on the web.

Bows determine the base damage/delay/range (where
xx/yy for weapons is damage/delay, xx/yy/zz for bows
is damage/delay/range). Range is more relevant than
it might first appear because 100 range is shorter
than it sounds; you have to be closer than you think
to be in range.

Arrows modify the base damage and range of the bow,
adding damage and adding or subtracting range. There
are arrow and/or bow combinations that result in 0 or
25 ranges, and are presumably very limited in their
use (perhaps in a dungeon fight, or for a dedicated
close range archer who won't be using melee in his
group).

Arrow components:
---Shafts modify damage. Wood = +1, Bone = +2,
Ceramic = +3, Steel = +4(or +5?).
---Points modify damage. Field = 0, Hooked = +1,
Bladed = +2.
---Fletches vary in their mods. Round = no change,
Parabolic = +50 range, Shield = +1 damage, -50 range.
---Nocks modify range. Large = +0, Medium = +25,
Small = +50.

Strings affect the damage/delay of the bow. Hemp is
the base (bow stock plus hemp = base bow stats for
that bow stock). Linen modifies base -1/-4/-. Silk
modifies base -2/-8/-. Whittling knife modifies base
-1/-4/-, planing tool -2/-8/-. Cams add +2(?) damage.

Always consider the efficiency rating of the weapon
(damage divided by delay) and consider the range you
want.

The cheapest, longest range arrow is
Field/Wooden/Small/Parabolic 1.7sp component cost,
1/150 (dmg/rng).

The cheapest arrow possible is
Field/Wooden/Large/Round 0.4sp cost, 1/50. Changing
to a Medium nock makes it 0.5sp cost, 1/75. Changing
to a Parabolic fletch makes it 0.9sp cost, 1/100.

The highest damage arrow possible is
Bladed/Steel/Small/Shield at 1.556pp cost, 7/50. If
you want some range, make it
Bladed/Steel/Small/Parabolic for 1.527pp cost, 6/150.

Do some research; but I don't think I'll be using
arrows that cost much more than 3-4sp each until I get
into areas of the game that pay 15-20pp per hour of
hunting. I am considering starting to use the 1.7sp
1/150 arrows though (I'd have to hand fletch them, or
pay someone to do it though; which is the drawback,
but would give a 50% increase over the 1/100s I'm
buying premade from Kelethin for 1sp each).

Also note, be sure to check component prices in game
before making massive purchases; I don't have them
memorized and the site I pulled the data from could
have things outdated/wrong.

And BTW, I *love* my bow now.
