Well, Pietro Zarn, the fellow who presumably captured Zimel, is, shall
we say, a wee bit on the tough side. I have seen a screenshot of Davidon
(Level 50 pally in beta) in a group taking him, presumably to get Soulfire,
but I wouldn't recommend you try for a while. I made a throw away character
just to see how hard he hit, and, well, Zarn's damaged maxed at 147 dmg per
hit, plus double attack and any spells he decides to toss out. For
reference, ice giants, who are supposed to be about level 40, max out at 110
dmg. This has led many people to scratch thier heads as the Zimel's Blades
quest can only be started below level 9, and finished at level 40+. =)
The thing is, while you cant get the Big Prize until late in the game, I
have a sneaking suspicion that there may be part you can do at the lower
level. In beta, the quest worked somewhat differently. You eventually
wound up giving Groflah the flier in the Seafarer's and he translated a code
making the flyer read 'Help captive', and he said that Zimel was surely
alive and being held captive in the city. While Kalatrina doesnt appear
willing to give you the flier back after getting it from Alayle in retail,
if you catch Groflah in the Seafarer's and pump him for information about
Zimel, he'll tell you the story of how Zimel stole Soulfire from Zarn, and
how he fears that Zimel is being held captive somewhere in the city. Now,
in beta, I must've seached from one end of the city to the other, in all the
buildings, through all the sewers, everywhere. I even tried the 'a
prisoner', who was in the jail beneath the Arena despite the unlikelyhood of
the Steel Warriors assisting the Dismal Rage in any way. No go. However,
I've heard, but havnt gotten around to checking, that the sewers have been
somewhat filled out, and that there are actual mobs down there. (In p3 and
early p4 at the least, the sewers were completely empty. You had the
guildmaster for the Dismal Rage, but absolutly nothing else) I keep meaning
to go down there and search for Zimel, but I havnt had a chance yet. Hope I
was of some help, or at least interest,
