> All right! This is gonna be great! Do you realize you can knock

>
> 1) Find Pine Needles or a Tiny Dagger. Both use archery skill but they
> are a
> melee weapon. Hang out in a place where there are blue mobs that other
> people fight, such as trainer hill or somesuch area, and (after getting
> their
> permission) attack the monster with the pine needle/tiny dagger. They
> won't
> mind because you can only do one dmg with it.
>
> 2) Spend points on fletching and try to use it as much as possible, making
>
> regular arrows with no skill in fletching is still a 50% (?) chance of
> success. Also making your own arrows costs 4 cp while buying them is
> ~7-10.
> Oh and if you hunt in blackburrow the gnolls occasionally drop arrows and
> worn hunting bows.
>
> 3) Speaking of dropped bows, don't use them! Even the worst of the player
> made bows are better than the mightiest NPC bow (except for magical, but I
>
> mean regulars). From what he told me, most sell for 6-12 plat. I think he
> was
> referring to the easiest ones to make.
>
> 4) Once you get high enough (22 for us, 9 for rangers) to root your
> opponent,
> you will know the true power of archery. You can easily bring him down to
> half health with only a few arrows. This will also make up for our lack of
> DD
> spells vs. living creatures, both as a lure and finisher and even combat
> support. Though melee is our forte' having some ranged skill will be
> undoubtedly helpful.
>
> 5) When buying a playermade bow, look for the best dmg. If you have a
> choice
> between a 9 dmg 40 delay and an 11 dmg 50 delay (theoretically speaking. I
>
> don't know the actual stats) you should choose the higher damage one
> because
> when you have no root spell and they jump on you, you want to hit them for
> as
> much as possible in the time allotted.
>
> 6) Read this guide, it is one of the best I have ever found:
