As a follower of "The Craft," I wanted to inform you that witchcraft
IS a religion...in a way. The pagan religion of Wicca is often
referred to as The Craft or Witchcraft. Many of the findings of
witchcraft in other religions have been borrowed from Wicca. Such as
the traditional Easter celebrations. Easter is derived from Eostra,
which is the Wiccan celebration that falls around the time of
Easter. Wiccans would paint eggs to celebrate the fertility of the
goddess. Christmas also borrows traditions from Wicca's Yule, which
falls at the end of December. Even the common appearance of Satan
was pulled from Wicca. When Christianity came to be, christians
portrayed Satan as a demonic creature with horns. This was done to
try to bastardize Wicca, because one of the gods worshipped in the
religion had horns. Of course, this varies from practitioner to
practitioner, as Wicca is a "free-form" religion with not many
things set in stone.
