PowerPoint may be more hardcore than you need to
be for a 5-minute presentation, but here's what you
do. Take a screenshot in-game (the "minus" sign on
the keyboard), or steal one from the Sony Everquest
site. I'm sure they wouldn't mind too much -- free
publicity (since about half of your class will probably
want to start playing after this). The screenshots
will be saved in C:\Program Files\Everquest (I
think).<br><br>Double-click on a screenshot, and it will open in your default
graphics program (probably Paint if don't have anything
else). Go to File/Save As, and save it as a JPEG in a
folder that you'll remember. JPEGs are much smalled than
the 1 MB BMP files that Everquest screenshots
create.<br><br>Then, open up PowerPoint and start a new Presentation.
Choose a nice background, but don't go for the fancy
ones -- dark backgrounds look cool, but sometimes
don't show up well on a projector. Simple is better.
<br><br>Play around with the text -- use a gothic font or
something, perhaps. Then go to the folder where you saved
your JPEG screenshots. Simply click-and-drag the
screenshots you want to your PowerPoint presentation, and
they should (magically) appear in your presentation.
Or, you could do Edit/Insert and navigate to the
folder.<br><br>I don't think I'd do more than two or maybe three
screenshots; create an outline for the first page of the
presentation, then talk about the game -- maybe screenshots of
characters to illustrate how they're created, and then a MoB
with some scenery. (Many of us would love to pose
in-game, I'm sure; I'm online usually after 10 p.m.
Pacific time if you want a model Dwarf . . . ) Then,
create a wrap-up slide (more text) for the last page to
sum up your presentation.<br><br>Hope this helps.
PowerPoint can be a beast, but lots of fun when you learn
it. Just remember that less is better, and too-fancy
graphics can overwhelm your audience.<br><br>Sheesh, I was
still using construction paper and Elmer's glue at your
age, back in the days.<br><br>Kharlisht of
