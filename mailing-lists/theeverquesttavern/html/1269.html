Ooops! Nope. Sorry. Patchwork, yes. Banded and
bronze, no. You're a druid. Get used to the newbie look,
you're going to have it for a VERY long time. I'm a
level 30 wood elf druid, and I'm wearing leather. Now,
granted, I have collected as much wisdom-enhancing items
as I can get my grubbly little mitts on, but druids
cannot wear anything much past leather. Later on,
Ulthork and Tizmak armor, as well as brown chitlin are
available, but unless you have someone helping you, you
won't be seeing these until you're well into your
30's.<br><br>My advice is to take up tailoring. Do it now. As a
level 4, you can make your own patchwork for some
silver, and sell it for some gold. And you can make your
own armor. Make friends with someone who smiths, and
someone who brews. You'll need items from them, and they
will need items from you. Later on, you can make
studded (which is better than leather) then reinforced.
Eventually, cultural wood elf armor. Also, you can forage.
Keep that skill maxed out. What I do is everytime I
sit to med, I hit the forage key first.<br><br>So far
as newbie quests, there isn't much that's
druid-specific. You can turn in those swords, but I found that
disappointing. Plus, at your level they're hard to find. Do the
mail quest. Kill wolves... yes, yes, I know, wolves
are friends of druids, but you'll be USING the pelts.
If you get a black wolf skin, turn it into the
enchanter guild at Felwithe. Collect bone chips. Do NOT
turn them in, sell them to players for about 3pp per
stack. Same thing with bat wings. Keep a stack or two
for yourself, you'll need it later on. Collect
spiderling silks, and make silk thread OR turn them into the
bard guild four at a time. If you don't mind the
travel, deliver mail from Ak'Anon to Kelethin... the pay
is pretty good. Couple of plat, if I remember
correctly. And remember, loot EVERYTHING! Begging from NPC's
is iffy, and begging players is rude. Higher ups
will help someone who isn't asking, but is out there
whacking away for all their worth with a rusty sword and
tattered armor. I personally have been getting fine steel
weapons as drops regularly, and go to Orc hill and give
them all away to folks who don't ask, and aren't
twinked.<br><br>Good luck, I have found playing a druid to be quite
rewarding. And remember... once you hit 29 and can do
teleports, you'll NEVER be poor again. Just remember to do
some for free too. You're a druid. Karma is
important.<br><br>Fandarian<br>30th Druid, E'ci
