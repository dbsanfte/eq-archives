Another thing to consider is the weapons each
combatant uses. If the paladin is swinging around a
Ghoulbane (highly unlikely at level 30 but still worth
pondering) then the pet is negligible. SK pets are half the
levels of their masters. That means the paladin has to
deal with a level 15 mob. Should go down pretty
easily. To my knowledge, there is no "anti-paladin"
weapon out there. Advantage holy knight.<br> Armor is
pretty balanced; both Ro and Darkforge are pretty equal.
I don't think either will have Umbral or Valorium,
but even then, they're fairly equal.<br> Harm touch
will take off 301 damage, Lay on Hands heals for 750.
The paladin could take a considerable amount of
damage and harm touch and negate the damage. Again, this
favors the paladin.<br> But, necro-based spells are
wicked. Last night I ran into Meldrath, gnome necro,
leader of the minos in steamfont. He had boil blood,
bonds of death, engulfing darkness, and fear on me.
Needless to say I was his female dog. I made it out only
because of some sick paladin buffs and the same paladin
who saved me. I'm pretty scared of necros right now,
but that paladin whooped up on Meldrath last
night.<br> It really comes down to the player. SKs and
pallys are pretty equal in a straight up duel. But
knowing the background of most SKs, they would love for
nothing more but to lace into you with all their spells
while you're meditating and unbuffed.
