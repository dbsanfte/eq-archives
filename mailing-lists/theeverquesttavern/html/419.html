A lot of people think that choosing a half elf
druid is silly due to the stats, but don't worry about
it. The numbers in the game make a difference, but
not much of a difference at all. A skillful player
who knows how to use the abilities he has can be a
lot more useful than an idiot with great stats ;)
Also, choose the combination that YOU want to play. For
roleplaying, a challenge, or just to be unique, and choice can
be great. For example, I currently play a Gnome
Cleric. Looking at a gnomes stats, it seems a gnome
cleric would be horrible. Low strength (Can't carry much
armor around) and especially low wisdom (BARBARIANS
have a higher base wisdom!), seems that a gnome cleric
would be impossible to play, and useless to a group.
Wrong. I have gotten my cleric to level 26 and love
playing him every day. MANY people have said that Quinon
(my cleric) is one of the best clerics they have ever
grouped with, eventhough he has pathetic wisdom. Everyone
stresses the numbers, but don't let them bother you.
Attributes that may not seem important to your class can
still be useful. Quinon's high agility allows him to
avoid being hit, and his high intelligence allows him
to learn skills very quickly, eventhough these
attributes aren't thought of as important to a cleric at
all. Just choose the race/class you want and enjoy it,
you aren't going to ruin your characters usefullness
just because of some numbers.
