as an ex school teacher i have to disagree with
the person who said don't use to many visuals. when a
students knows what he or she is talking about that is
great...they get an a. when a student shows extra thought and
creativity they get the a (even if they are a little less
knowledgeable) Teachers love to see thought and creativity.
<br><br>I would not use powerpoint. here are few
ideas.<br><br>*make a packet for each student. include your outline
and some info about the game. make your cover your
favorite character. depending on your funds you could go
to yuor local copy store and have them bound or buy
the plastic report covers.<br>after passing them out
to the class you can break the ice by introducing
your character. who are they. where they are from.
skills they acel at. its race, class. goals
etc..<br><br>*bring some type of "unique" visual aid. If you are
going to talk about armor and weapons make (out of felt
or whatever) some replicas for barbie and have them
set up on desk. or when talking about skills bake
some goodies to pass around to class. call them
batwing munchies.<br><br>there are a million ides you
could do. you can't do all. pick one and make it work
for you. just be creative and show that you put a lot
of thought into your presentation and you will do
great!
