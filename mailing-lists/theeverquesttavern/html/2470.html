I assume you are a good race, and trying to get
to LOIO from the FV compound. Here's the scoop--
Once you leave the compound and head north-west,
you'll get to that water fall. Above the waterfall
you'll be following the river all the way to the zone.
Drachnids infest the area, with an occasional gobbie or
leech. <br><br>MOST drachnids cannot see invis or rogue
hide/sneak. But some can--- i have not figured out what
determines this (ie. level of mob, or type of drachnid--
though i'm pretty sure that I have /con all different
types of Drachnids as indiff at one point or another).
<br><br>Here's the best way to do it-- head to the waterfall.
Make sure you have SOW on and invis/camo up-- then
bolt for it. You can either just run as fast as you
can and hope that if you DO agro something, you'll
make it to the zone in time, or you can move slowly
and /con everything from a distance. The only problem
with this is that Drachnids have a nasty habit of just
poping out of thin air. I have died once on the way to
LOIO using either of these techniques. One time I
agrod a Drach and got poisoned while running to zone--
nearly died, but madly bound wounds till poison wore
off. <br><br>Dreadlands is not a nice place for
someone under lvl 35ish. I did this run several times
with my druid because I didn't want to boat to Kunark,
and it freaked me out everytime. Best way to do this
if you're foolish enough to try is to get SOW and
Levitate-- if you run from the snow capped mountains and
head 1) east toward Karnor's or 2) North-East to the
northern wall, you will probably not agro anything from
your height in the sky. If you do, you can get help
from the groups at Karnor's or the surrounding ruins.
If you headed north, you can zone to BW or FM.
That's the easy part, now you need to make your way
either to the FM zone and then dodge giants to the LOIO
zone, or you have to get to the FV zone and STILL run
the guantlet from FV to LOIO. <br><br>Most things in
DL see through invis-- including giants (same ones
as in FM). I think more Drachnids in DL see through
invis, and they are mostly higher levels that those in
FV. <br><br>My god, I should wrap this up now...
hehe<br><br>Good luck-- the dangerous run is there because the
rewards of xp in LOIO are soo good. On Xev LOIO is full
of /shout /ooc spam-- it seems more like a chat room
at times than a game, but you get to know a lot of
players there, and if you're smart, you'll /friend so you
can hook up at higher levels for better adventures!!
