In addition to Stormfang's comments, some
classes/races are easier to roleplay for lack of a better word.
One of the best clerics I have ever met was indeed a
roleplayer, or at least she seemed so to me. Druid
roleplayers, on the other hand, are incredibly few and far
between. With all my characters I try desperately to
roleplay to my best, but some are just easier/more fun to
roleplay. For instance, I have a monk who I roleplay as a
mute. He doesn't speak, except to his guild superiors
(for training and quest purposes) to everyone else he
either shows his intentions through emotions (emotes) or
/e scribbles on a scrap of parchment "(whatever
needs to be said)" and passes it to (whoever he is
conversing with). It takes a little bit longer to have a
conversation, but it's different. In groups however, he is in
tune to the immediate mental powers of those around
him (hence groupspeak) and he tends to speak in very
eloquent thoughts. So... you'll never see a /shout from
Roen. /OOC with train! perhaps, or even an /auc ooc:
WTB such-and-such.<br>I like roleplaying my troll
warrior, simply because instead of just taunting like so
many warriors do, he insults the target. Something to
the effect of "Hey %T! You mudder is a hairy
halfing!" Hehehe... Halflings are his favorite snack, and
he'll tell you so. Hehe.<br>Anyways, there's no real
point in any of that, but in a world filled with shouts
of "Dr00d Newbie wants Phat Lewts!" or other such
garbage, it is difficult to maintain IC all the time.
Having a guild of RPers does help, and I've been meaning
to get Roen into one. Hehe... Roen the
mental-intuned, mute monk. Agnostic monk at that. None of that
silly god stuff, or magic, or weapons to worry about.
