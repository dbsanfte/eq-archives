> 1. Being used to playing solo games I could pretty much play at my
> own pace. How does EQ work? I know you join other online players
but
> seeing as I am a casual gamer i only spend 10-15 hours a week

> is online at the time and am I not tied into a specific group/times.

The way EQ works is similar to any other on-line game (Counterstrike,
Unreal, Diablo). The difference is the world of EQ is always on-line.
That said, yes you will enter EQ with any other players that are on-
line at the time. At peak hours, there can be 2000+ players. The
world of EQ + 3 Expansion sets, is immense (larger than any known RPG
I am aware of).

About how much time you put in to this game... it depends what you
want to get out of EQ. Do you want to play the game, or use it to
meet and interact with players? I'll tell you as you progress through
the game, it becomes HEAVILY level oriented. That means you MUST gain
levels to experience all EQ has to offer. Gaining experience is an
arduous process, especially early levels where it's sort of mundane,
and it does require dedication. You have to budget your time.

> 2. Is it easy to find other adventurers to join with?

If you come off as a good guy, then it shouldn't be a problem. With
thousands of players on-line, there has to be a group. ;) The bigger
problem is really if there is any room to adventure in?

> 3. I see different EQ's (Original, Ruins of Kharnak, etc) does it
> matter which one I start with or are the later addons geared for
> higher level characters.

You must have original EQ to start and run the game. The expansion
packages add extra races for you to choose from and they are created
to function as low-level to high-level adventuring like the original
EQ. If you choose the default races, you can play original EQ and at
anytime you want buy and add on the expansions. If you want to play
the new races, you have to have both original and whatever expansion
needed for the new race.

> 4 Finally system requirements. I have seen the system requirements
> but what is recommended ?. I have a P3 866, 128sdram, 32mb ddr
nvidia
> geforce2 gts card, 56k modem is that suffiecient or should I wait
> till I upgrade.

Two things to keep in mind with EQ... can your monitor handle the
massive frame-rate, and is your connection stable? With a 56K modem
you'll probably receive info from EQ servers slower so the whole EQ
game will look slower to you. In any case, EQ will run on your
computer.
