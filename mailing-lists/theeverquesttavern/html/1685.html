I LOVE ramblings... here's a little from
me....<br><br>You did not mention really the Pallys or SKs. I love
grouping with Pallys. The tank well, great against undead,
but most importantly they have Lay on Hands (which
has saved my life more than once) AND can heal
themselves after battles (which lowers downtime and saves
the healer's mana). SKs are nice because they tank
well AND can summon a pet. (one of my chars is a mage,
and I know full well the value of a good pet. The pet
can tank when you need a break, the pet can taunt so
you can run if needed, and that extra damage can mean
the difference in a tough battle). <br><br>I assume
you didn't mention Clerics because that's a
no-brainer. They're great and welcome at any time.
<br><br>BUT--if a cleric isn't available, a druid will serve as a
healer in a pinch. I've been in groups as a druid where
i am the primary healer. Then, the next day, i'll
be a nuker, or even one of the tanks. Druids are my
fav class because you aren't typcast -- Clerics are
healers, Pallys are tanks, Shammys are buffer/debuffers,
Wizzies are Nukers, etc. . We are the best solo artists,
and we are the jack of all trades in a group. In an
outdoor group especially, we make the best pullers.
(Track, find a great mob, snare and DOT it, and lead it
by a chain back to your group)<br><br>Everyone has
something to say about the best classes at higher levels.
EVERY class has benefits and burdens. What YOU like
about the game will probably determine which class you
like at a higher level. Unfortunately, many people I
know pick a race/class combo that sounds fun, gets
caught up in leveling that character up, then gets stuck
in a HELL level and realizes that they really don't
like playing that char anymore. I LOVE the mage class.
Many higher levels complain about the limitations and
abilities of the mage at higher levels. I love the fact
that, at least in the mid 20s, I can solo AND group
very effectively. What will it be like at lvl 40? I'm
not sure. But i think i'll still be happy if 'ole
Jobabaner is by my side and I'm slamming baddies with my
potent nukes. Oh, yeah-- don't i also get the malaise
line of spells too?? WOOT!!<br><br>My mains are a mage
and a druid. Neither are melee classes (although I
sometimes will tank with my druid). I love the complexity
of choosing what spell will be effective in what
situation, given my mana limitations. BUT-- I sometimes wish
i could tank. Mayhap i'll creat a
warrior/monk/pally/sk soon??? <br><br>Rambling on and on and on and on
and on and on....<br><br>Xela
