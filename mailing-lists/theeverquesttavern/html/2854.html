Actually, let me expand upon this. I'm going OT now so for the
technical belligerent please turn away. ;)

You might be able to have that high of a connection between your
modem and your computer, but that's not possible going out to the
internet (where you'll connect with Verant's servers). You
can "trick" the computer into thinking you're running anything over
56K (your speed of 115+ for example) by configuring your PC to
display a higher speed but you are running 56K max regardless because
that is all a regular phone line can handle. Unless of course you
have ISDN. You can also make adjustments to your modem (tweak, like
you said) to make it run slightly "faster", BUT in no way can it be
cranked up to double its speed. The reason it appears faster is
because normally you do not get that full 56K when you use dial-up.
By "tweaking" the modem, you are now getting that near 56K.

You know, websites galore advertise these things constantly saying
you can do all of these neat tricks to fool your modem into faster
speeds. That's the half truth. Yes, they might help you configure
your modem for maximum potential (therefore, faster speed), but going
beyond what it's capable of... nah, I have yet to see it happen.
