Some thoughts from a fellow Xev-ite who has died
many times in Unrest: I admit that ALL classes take
skill to master and know your role, though I favor the
casters, who i believe take more skill to be great at.
When you're a tank, please remember that YOU are the
one that decides when it's time to run-- casters have
no choice buy to fling thier noses in books in order
to be helpful, and don't always know what's going
on-- they SHOULD always be aware of your life bar,
though-- shame on the healer if he wasn't watching you
closely and didn't have enought wherewithall to target
you and heal. Many times, medding casters are out of
range, so they can't "hear" you being hit by the mobs,
and don't know if there's one or ten, unless you tell
them. Not saying this is what happened, but just
saying...<br><br>I'm with you on some points though-- I take my roles
seriously (main is druid, alt is mage), whether i'm healer,
buffer, nuker, etc., and I hate being in groups with
people who don't understand thier specialty and master
it (ie. the tanking cleric, the Wizzie who
"overnukes," so you have to waste heals on him and taunt off
him all the time, tanks who don't take on the
toughest mob or who fail to taunt off casters,
etc.)<br><br>RR is a tough place, because most higher levels
train stuff through there when they get in trouble. A
werebat should not be a normal pull to the RR--<br><br>A
good group member is constructive and not negative--
remind the cleric, wizzie, or whoever what thier roll is
if they deviate (and if you know!). No caster class
should "step in and take a hit" for the tank. If the
tank needs that, the healer is not doing their job, or
the tank should be running to zone. (Exception-- I've
done this a bunch as a druid--a well placed nuke will
agro and then I pray to Tunare that the cleric will
help and the tank will get back in line ASAP--if not,
i know when to run!!)<br><br>Sounds like your group
wasn't informed of the MA (Main Assist) theory of
fighting. If you are the MA, all should /assist you-- the
secondary tank should be ready to break off and taunt any
mob who agros on a caster. You should have a DS cast
on you, if available, and the healer should have you
targeted, unless someone else is in dire need. <br><br>Last
thoughts-- if you had a mage, his/her pet could have taunted
the mob off you while you backed off. If you had a
druid, you'd be SOW'd, and the mob would be snared,
making it easy to get away. <br><br>P.S.-- no matter how
mad you are at your casters, NEVER try to train the
mob on your own group to save yourself, like you did.
That is cowardly, and has gotten more than one tank on
Xev a bad reputation. Fight with honor and
compassion, and if your healer EVER heals a wizzard with 80h
again, get a new healer or a new group!!!!<br><br>Xela
