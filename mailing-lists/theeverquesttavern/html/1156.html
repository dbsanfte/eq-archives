You can learn any skill by just fighting. I
learned this around level 6.<br><br>Dodge is an avoidance
defensive skill. Therefore, it will automatically rise when
it is praticed in battle. Dodge goes off randomly -
it basically means you will avoid more attacks with
it.<br><br>There are certain checks when a mob attacks
you.<br><br>-The mob can hit you.<br>-The mob can just miss you on
his own.<br>-The mob can hit you, but you use your
Defense to avoid it or you Dodge/Block/Riposte. Block is
a form of Dodge that can block magical attacks, and
Riposte is like Dodging an attack and counterattacking
once at the same time. However, your Riposte can miss
or can be Riposted as well. Riposte can avoid
attacks from any direction, but for you to counterattack,
you must be facing the mob you Riposted.<br><br>It
sounds confusing, but it makes more sense if you read it
over several times.<br><br>Basically, any time a mob
swings at you, your defensive skills are practiced. They
don't always go up when they are successful - my
Defense has been raised even when I've been hit by
mobs.<br><br>With any combat skill you learn as you level, you must
put one point in it. Just one. Your guildmaster
teaches you the basics. Then, while you practice it
during combat, the skill will go up.<br><br>I must warn
you that these raise very slowly, so don't expect
them to raise extremely quickly.<br><br>Also, you
don't have to keep spending your points in your combat
skills. Just practice them (automatically attacking with
your fists will raise the skill points of Hand to
Hand, for instance).<br><br>As for your weapons
question...it depends on certain factors. Weapons will be quite
heavy. 2 Jade Maces weigh 4.2 stones together, and
that's one of the lighter of the uber weapon
combinations. It's whether or not you can make the room for
them with light gear and weight reducing bags (or if
you're willing to take the AC hit) that determines
whether you should use them.<br><br>If you have a good
tank in your group, the mobs shouldn't be hitting you
anyway, but rather your tank, so the AC loss wouldn't be
a big factor in this case.<br><br>Also, as you
raise in level, you'll go on raids. These will most
likely start around level 40-45 or so with Fire Giants
in Sol B. As a monk, unless you are pulling (which
you probably will be :), you can swap out AC gear for
damage gear, provided there are good tanks taking most
of the damage.<br><br>Or you could just get the epic
monk weapon. :) With the effect of the Celestial
Fists, you have two 9/16 weapons as your fists with
little weight. However this is a very difficult and
lengthy quest, level 50+.<br><br>AC is important, but not
in all cases. Day to day, yes, it's important. But
sometimes you can modify your gear for the situation. You
will learn how to work with this as you gain levels
and equipment.<br><br>Iskandar<br>51 champion of
