As for the original question-- lvl 9 druid should
not have a problem getting a group-- you're powerful
with new spells, and are at the point where you're an
equal melee, nuker and healer, with some other great
spells too. If you want to group, go to CrushBone. You
can still fight at Orc Hill or at the CB ent in Gfay,
then go inside and get into groups. Over the course of
several days, you'll find that CB is it's own EQ
community within a community, and many of the people I
grouped with in CB have become gamelong friends.
<br><br>Advantages-- you can bind anywhere in the Gfay zone, so get a
bind at the orc lift, or better yet, at the zone to
CB. You're close to your guild for training and
spells, and close to merchants and the bank. People are
always giving buffs and free stuff out in the GFay zone,
you just have to put up with annoying Ubers with MAD
/auction. You will be looting CB belts and Lego pads, which
are a great source of either 1) XP (turn them into
Canloe in the Kaladim warrior's guild), or 2) Cash
(people buy CB belts for 1-2pp and Lego pads for 2-3pp
usually). You can work on the quests to get the Prayer
Cloth, which is a WIS shoulder Item. You can save poor
elven slaves and gain xp at the same time, by killing
Slavers and getting keys. You can kill annoying little
shorties like Rondo Dunfire and that little gnome in the
pit (what's his name again??). <br><br>But most of
all-- once you hit Level 14, you will have it made with
2 vital spells-- Bind Affinity (allows you to
control your destiny) and SOW. With these spells, your
damage shield, your STR buff, your heals and nukes, AND
MOST IMPORTANTLY THE BEST DRUID SPELL OF THEM ALL---
HARMONY (please master this spell and use it at orc
camps, derv camps, and in all situations where you want
to single pull), you should have no problem finding
groups... as for soloing... well, that's another
post...<br><br>Of all the chars I've had, my current favorites
are:<br><br>Xela Orcbane<br>Druid of 40
levels<br><br>Rumbl<br>Rogue of 23 levels
