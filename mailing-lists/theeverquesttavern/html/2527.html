warriors b/c the stats are great and they are large races.
This is important b/c Large races do not get stunned!
B/c of the faction and the fact I'm bad with the evil
races--normally I would go with Barbarian stat-wise and
unstunnable-wise. <br><br>However, I tend to play Half-elf warriors
b/c I get to start in Freeport-- which is my 2nd most
well known city (although I kinda know Halas by now).
And Half-elves get night vision as good as elves.
Their stats aren't nearly as good but they've got in
general better faction. Since SoL though, I would have to
say being a Cat warrior is fun. I'm not 100% certain
on how stats stack up but I believe Cats regen, have
night vision-- and decent warrior stats (Str, Sta,
Dex)<br><br>Now Stats: Enchanter-- you want a really high Int and
a decent Chars. Opinions vary on how high a Chars.
I would say 80+ if not 90+. With a high elf, dump
25 into int and 5 into chars. Enchanters are
generally pretty easy with stats. Your a bit weak-- str
determines how much you can carry. A few dump no more than 2
to 5 points into str b/c of that.<br><br>Warrior--
more complex.<br><br>Str determines how hard you hit,
average damage, how much you can carry. You're sometimes
the ML b/c you are so strong so this becomes
important.<br><br>Stam-- important b/c this is how many hitpoints you have
and how often you can swing weapons. Only really
important note here is-- make sure that it's close enough
that you can get it to 100 stam. After you hit over
100 stam, you never have to worry about stam again
b/c it doesn't fall if you're over 100. You can wield
the heaviest weapons with no problem then. (You
probably won't have the points to start like that
though.)<br><br>Dex-- Combined with Attack to determine how often you
hit. (<--debated by some) Determines how often your
weapon procs if you have a proc and how often you hit
with a bow/range and how hard (important for the
puller.) <br><br>Dex and Str combine for how fast you
learn Melee skills. Agility is important but only
slightly for a warrior b/c the others are much more
needed. Agility determines how good your are at avoiding
damage. It's got other uses but that's the only real
important one to remember since it's not a really important
stat.<br><br>Why? You'd think you'd want your warrior to dodge a
lot BUT... If you can never hit something or don't do
much damage, it won't keep agro no matter how good
your taunt. If it won't keep agro, then there's not
much of a point in you being the tank. And above all
else, a warrior is a tank.<br><br>Warriors at first do
damage, but higher level damage dealers are your Shaman,
Rogue, Monk, Wizard... followed closely by Magicians.
Warriors are your quintessential tanks.<br><br>shooman out
