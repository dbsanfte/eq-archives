Now, in Windows, go to Start/Programs/Accessories/System Tools, and
open up System Information. That will tell you some of the basic
info we need, like RAM and Windows version. Won't tell you your
processor speed, unfortunately. If you don't have System
Information, you probably have Windows 95 and will need to update to
Windows 98, XP, or 2000.

Finally, here's the info from that page on Direct X:

DirectX 8.1 or Higher Required

"With the release of EverQuest: The Shadows of Luclin, we require
DirectX 8.1 or higher. This is true not only for those purchasing
Shadows of Luclin, but for everyone playing EverQuest. All details
regarding this change can be found here:
http://boards.station.sony.com/ubb/everquest/Forum1/HTML/000348.html

To determine what version of DirectX is currently installed on your
system:

1. Click the Start menu.
2. Use the Run.. command..
3. In the Open field, type "DXDIAG" then click OK. This will launch
the DirectX diagnostics tool.
Your version of DirectX will be listed at the bottom of the System
Information area. For example, version 7.0a is listed as "DirectX 7.0
(4.07.00.0716)"

Please do the following prior to playing EverQuest. It is very
important that your drivers are installed in the order indicated to
avoid potential problems:

4. Go to https://www.microsoft.com/downloads/release.asp?
ReleaseID=27741and install the latest DirectX drivers for your
operating system.
5. Go to the website for your 3D accelerator. There are a number of
different manufacturers, and it is preferred that you visit the
website for the manufacturer of your card. At your option, you can go
to the chipset manufacturer site. Most will be able to use either
www.3dfx.com (for Voodoo cards), www.atitech.com (for ATI cards) or
www.nvidia.com (for TNT/GeForce cards). Download and install the
latest drivers appropriate for your card and operating system. See
our Video FAQ
(http://everquest.station.sony.com/support/audio_video/general_video_t
roubleshooting.jsp) for more details.
6. Go to the website for your sound card manufacturer and do the
same. If you do not know what brand of sound card you have, you can
right-click on "My Computer", select "properties", click on "Device
Manager", and open the "Sound, Video, and Game Controllers" section.
Your sound card will be listed there. If you use a SBLive sound card,
you can run "LiveUpdate" from the Windows Control Panel off of your
start menu. View our Sound FAQ
(http://everquest.station.sony.com/support/audio_video/general_audio_t
roubleshooting.jsp) for driver locations and more details.
If you experience any problems, please do the following:

7. Run DXDIAG.EXE from your Start menu (click start, run, then
type "dxdiag")
8. Click the "Save all Information" button at the bottom of the
program window.
9. Save the file as "dxdiag.txt"

Email that file to eqtech@... along with a complete
description of your problem, the "dbg.txt" file from your EverQuest
directory, the text from any error messages that are presented, and
if you are willing to assist our test department over the phone, a
phone number where you can be reached."
