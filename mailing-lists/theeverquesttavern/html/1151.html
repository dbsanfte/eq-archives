Yes, eventually, weapons are better for monks. At
level 60, a Human monk's fists are rated at
14dmg/26dly, while an Iksar monk's fists are rated at
14dmg/28dly.<br><br>There are some very nice weapons for monks in Kunark -
Jade Mace (1hb, 9/18), Runed Fighter's Staff (2hb,
19/20), and Tranquil Staff (2hb, 29/30, procs
irresistable stun), to name a few.<br><br>I must warn you that
these weapons are extremely rare and valuable. I would
venture to say that you will not receive either of these
before level 45, and even then, many post-50 monks do
not have any of the aforementioned
weapons.<br><br>You may want to get some cheap 1hb and 2hb weapons to
train your skills. Fight extremely low blues - the
lowest ones you can find - to train your points, or if
you see someone soloing, ask if you can train on the
mobs they are fighting. Just don't outdamage them, and
Feign Death if you get in trouble.<br><br>Speaking of
which, keep Feign Death up. It will be your most useful
skill - as a monk, you will be expected, at later
levels, to pull the majority of mobs. FD is a great trick
for this.<br><br>Tiger Claw vs. Round Kick -
whichever one is learned at a later level is better. I am
not sure which one is better, off the top of my head,
but whichever one you get later is better. You will
get one more attack like this, which is a
kicking-type of attack. It's quite nice - Thunderous Kicks can
top 200 damage.<br><br>I think the cap for monks is
14, that is, if you carry more than 15 stones worth
of equipment, you will suffer a huge AC loss. You
will want to avoid this at all costs. Test it out for
yourself, see what weight drops you down. Also, at higher
levels, you will be able to carry more without weight
loss, but I don't think you'll get past 20 or 30 weight
allowance.<br><br>Iskandar<br>51 champion of Prexus
