<br><br>As for starting points-- it depends on what you
want. The magical spells will advance quickly-- don't
worry about them. In a low level char, I usually put a
point or 2 to sense heading to get it going, maybe a
few to my primary weapon type (at low levels you'll
do a lot of melee and want to max out weapon
skills-- at higher levels you won't worry about it as
much). I have a habit of putting a point into all new
skills after i get them (some you HAVE to in order to
get them started). <br><br>Other than that, if you're
interested in trade skills, you may want to put a few
beginning points into them. At the lower levels, Tailoring
is cheap and easy-- you'll be getting silk and pelts
(well, if you're a RP druid, maybe not pelts) from the
get go-- save money by using the community loom.
Patterns are about 6sp each, and patchwork armor sells
back to merchants for over 1gp, so you're making
profit on successes. <br><br>Feel free to ask more ??
here or on EQDRUIDS club!!!<br><br>xela
