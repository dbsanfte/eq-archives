That's interesting-- true if you're going to solo a lot, but for most
casters, STA is not vital, especially in a group environment. I have
very low HP as a lvl 40 druid, but that's ok. I rarely get hit, and I
don't melee at all. If I agro, I count on the tanks to taunt off me--
or I snare/root and back off.

As for your char picks-- the first two are standard fare and hard to
disagree with (thought I chose a wood-elf druid). The Troll Warrior
is a great choice, but Most would say that Ogre warrior is THE best.
Or Barb Warrior, for good races.

The point I think we've made here is to have fun with your char
choice-- there are items along the way that will increase your key
stats. I suggest strongly looking at where you put your initial
stats though--- NOT putting points into WIS if you are a priest, or
INT if a pure caster is foolish (from experience he speaks). STR, STA
and DEX are key to mellee classes-- and don't forget about some added
CHA for bards and chanters. Who really knows the benefits of AGI?
Good stat to increase if you are a soloing caster/hybrid class that
doesn't want to take as much damage i guess.
