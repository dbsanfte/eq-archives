Continued from previous post:<br><br>You'll find
people are extraordinarily helpful (on "blue" servers,
at least). High-levels watch you fight for your life
in the newbie zones, and will frequently give you
better gear than you have, and certainly help/heal if
you ask for it or need it. This is what hooked me on
the game -- folks who go out of their way to help you
find a corpse, or teach you about the game while
playing.<br><br>Also, do you like to group or play solo? EQ is really
made for grouping, although you can solo at the lower
levels a bit. Sounds like you and the main man have a
ready-made group; you should browse the threads at the
official Everquest site to see discussions of what makes a
good two-person group, and plan accordingly. I once
ran across a team in their 50s that was fun to watch
-- a cleric (High Elf) and warrior (Ogre). Weird
combo, but it worked for them (even if they did refuse
to kill an Ogre NPC I wanted to loot; the big guy
didn't want to spoil his faction at home).<br><br>Oh,
yeah, that's another interesting thing about the game
-- faction. Basically, it's your standing with the
various NPC races and organizations. Want to play an
Ogre, but circulate among the Humans? Go kill a few
thousand orcs, and eventually NPCs in certain towns will
sell and talk to you, grudgingly. <br><br>Here are
some sites to browse for tips:<br><br>1.
www.EQAtlas.com -- a must. Good intro and map to almost every
zone in the game, links to discussions, bestiaries,
quests. I like to go into a zone blind the first time,
and then go get a map for further
exploration.<br><br>2. everquest.allakhazam.com -- another must,
although some of the info can be dated. <br><br>3.
www.Everlore.com -- your third place to be. Much player-supplied
info, so some of it should be taken with a grain of
salt. Don't know what the Skeleton Fist you looted is
for? Neither does anybody else on the site, but when
they do, they'll post it here.<br><br>4.
www.everquest.com -- official site; the Newbie Zone discussion
board is OK.<br><br>5. Various class-specific sites:
EQCleric.com (obvious); www.thesafehouse.org (indispensable
for my kind, Rogues). You might also try
<a href=http://eq.castersrealm.com/. target=new>http://eq.castersrealm.com/.</a> I found the guides at GameSpot
(<a href=http://www.gamespot.com/features/everquest_gg/index.html target=new>http://www.gamespot.com/features/everquest_gg/index.html</a>) very useful.<br><br>Well, there you go, for now.
Just remember that your first few weeks will be the
best -- you don't know what anything does or how hard
it is to kill. Everything is new . . . And then
you'll get some levels, and find a new place to
adventure, and it's just like being a newbie again, except
that now you kinda know what you're doing with all the
buttons and stuff. <br><br>Good luck. Look me up on
