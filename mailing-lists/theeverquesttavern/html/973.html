That's partially correct. Dual wield gives you
the opportunity to wield another weapon in your
off-hand. Now, as your skill in dual wield rises, so will
the number of swings your off-hand will get. Also
factored into this is the delay of the weapon in your
off-hand; generally, the lower, the better, without
sacrificing too much power. If you have a weapon with a high
delay in your off-hand, don't expect to see many swings
with it.<br><br>Also, your damage caps will be removed
at level 20, and will also rise as you level after
this. You'll get a damage bonus only in your primary
hand, so take this into thought as
well.<br><br>Iskandar
