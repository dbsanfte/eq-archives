There's all kinds of dyes out there, and the
armor's color is very much customizable. The stats are
taken from rubicite armor - the AC is pretty good, but
once you get past, oh, say, level 40, you'll need to
switch to better armor if you can wear it (Crafted,
Armor of Ro, etc.)<br><br>It is plate based, so only
warriors, paladins, shadowknights, clerics, and bards can
wear it.<br><br>Extract are extremely rare drops off
of certain mobs. Charcoal off of elementals in
Lavastorm/Steamfont, Essence of Sunlight off of Sand Giants, Russet
Oxide also off of elementals...<br><br>Once you have
the dye, either a shaman (alchemy) or a rogue (poison
making) can combine it to make a dye. With the dye, you
can tint player-made fine steel armor. Armors can be
single, double, or tripled dyed. Generally, single dyes
are darkest in shade whereas triple dyes are
lightest.<br><br>Be warned, this stuff can be a bit costly. On
Prexus, an undyed Fine Steel Breastplate runs around
300pp, with a dye adding maybe 100-200pp more, due to
time investments.<br><br>I will tell you this, it is
fun just running around visiting friends in a full
black suit of armor. Not many warriors get to wear jet
black armor. :)<br><br>Iskandar Alazeem<br>50th
seasoned warrior<br>Prexus
