To: The Salisbury Post, North Carolina
We, the undersigned, are outraged at the refusal of a request to list
a Pagan/Wiccan Study Group - Magickal Moon in the Salisbury Post,
which is located in Salisbury, North Carolina. The reason given for
the request by the FAITH Copy Editor was both ignorant and
discriminatory. "The Salisbury Post FAITH section is for people who
believe in God" and "this is not the image that The Post wants to
portray."

The Salisbury Post's mission statement says in part:
"We are a marketing and customer driven company whose mission is to
inform, educate and entertain. We strive to mirror the unique
communities we serve and offer responsible public forum."

We fail to see how denying a Pagan/Wiccan study group an equal space
with other congregations fulfills this mission statement. The
decision was made with personal prejudices and not handled with a
professional demeanor.

Regardless of whether or not Paganism is The Salisbury Post's
religion, it is our religion. The Federal Government recognizes
Paganism/Wicca, as an official religion. Military Courts of Justice
in the U.S. have also found Wicca to be a valid religion, deserving
of protection under the First Amendment of the U.S. Constitution. In
the case United States v. Phillips, (42 M.J. 346) in 1995) the
concurring opinion by Judge Wiss stated: "First, Wicca is a socially
recognized religion. It is acknowledged as such by the Army.
See `Dept. of the Army (DA) Pamphlet 165-13-1, Religious Requirements
and Practices of Certain Selected Groups: A Handbook for Chaplains'
(April 1980), revising A Pamphlet 165-13, `Religious Requirements and
Practices of Certain Selected Groups: A Handbook for Chaplains'
(April 1978). Further, it is acknowledged as such in courts of law."

Pagans and Wiccans are a part of your community. We are your
neighbors, your coworkers, your children's teachers; we are
professionals, but most importantly we are your readers - your
customers! As engraved on your building and imprinted on the
Opinion/Editorial page of the paper "The truth shall make you free";
misconceptions about pagans, and being denied access to public forum
makes it all the more difficult for the truth to make any of us free.

Due to the fact that this request was handled in an unprofessional
manner and with personal bigotry, we, the undersigned, as well as
members of our family and friends, who are both of the Christian and
Pagan faith will not support The Salisbury Post. We will no longer be
a part of your readership until this matter is reconciled with a
formal apology and the list is made public in The Salisbury Post.



"We are not evil. We don't harm or seduce people. We are not
dangerous. We are ordinary people like you. We have families, jobs,
hopes, and dreams. We are not a cult. This religion is not a joke. We
are not what you think we are from looking at T.V. We are real. We
laugh, we cry. We are serious. We have a sense of humor. You don't
have to be afraid of us. We don't want to convert you. And please
don't try to convert us. Just give us the same right we give you--to
live in peace. We are much more similar to you than you think." -
Margot Adler

Sincerely,

The Undersigned
