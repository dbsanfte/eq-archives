E3: Ultima Online 2 becomes Origin <br>Origin
previewed an early version of the sequel to Ultima Online
behind closed doors at E3. It is hugely different to its
predecessor, as you will see <br> <br>Some confusion at first.
Since Electronic Arts is publishing all products under
its own name, and since Ultima Online 2 is so very
different from the original UO there has been a name
change. The game is now called Ultima Worlds Online:
Origin.<br>The first very obvious difference from UO is that
Origin is entirely 3D. The characters that inhabit the
terrific 3D worlds are inspired by Todd McFarlane, creator
of Spawn, and feature a range of fantastic looking
creatures in addition to stylish and sexy humanoid
characters. Just like with the original UO, it's possible to
dress your character how you like, and train them in
the skills you find most entertaining and
cool.<br><br>To keep things safe for beginners, and as a new
approach to appeal to the UO pros out there, Origin
incorporates two continents. One is for character development
and socialising alone, there are no Player Killers
here - it's not allowed. When you feel good and ready
to go into battle, if this is your style of play,
you can venture into the other continent and reap
your rewards.<br><br>Origin will ship sometime next
year, but no date has been set. It's looking great, but
it could be UO players will prefer to continue with
what they already know and love. This sequel is mostly
for a new breed of online adventurers.<br><br> So
