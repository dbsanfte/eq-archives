I'm flattered to know you think of me as the "master"
Rogue. I think some very oober Rogues and Blackguards
might take issue with that, but then, they'd have to
see me.<br><br>Unfortunately, I don't know how much
meaningful advice I can give you. <br><br>Armor? I love my
Small Scarab Breastplate (AC14, +1STR, +1STA, -1CHA or
DEX, I think, +10HP, HLF, DWF, GNM only). You're just
about at the level where you can go to Unrest and score
a Pristine Giant Carapace. I went when I was 13 or
14, I think. Very scary, but I solo'ed lots of stuff
in the Hedge. Then I'd come out and fish in the
pools while healing (while Hidden, of course). You can
supposedly find Pristines off of the Giant Scarab Beetles in
Butcherblock and other places, but I only ever saw one in
Unrest, off of the Death Beetles. Take the Pristine and
23 gp to Vacto Molunel in South Kaladim, and he'll
give you a SSB. He'll also make a set of boots and a
help for you, if you ask nicely. <br><br>Yeah, the
Gnoll Lariat does proc a stun, but I think it's only 3
DAM, 16 Delay (maybe lower?). I used a Tailored Whip
(7/33) for long time in the off-hand. The Polished
Granite Tomahawk (PGT) is 6/26, and procs a Berserker
Strength, which apparently makes you immune from damage for
a short time. From what I gather, it will proc for
you at 10th level. Also adds some STR (+15, but takes
away AGI (-3?)) for the duration of the proc (which is
random, of course). I think you'd be happy with either of
these in your off-hand when you get Dual wield (at
14?). You wno't be able to solo Grinnix Mucktail, yet
(the named Gnoll that drops the Tomahawk) - he's a MOb
in the mid-20s.<br><br>Weaps? Hey, I used a Giant
Snake Fang up until I bought a Walrus Tooth for 35pp (I
suspect you won't find one of those for a while),
somewhere in my mid-teens. Just keep your piercing up, of
course; I like all weapon styles, so I even keep up my
1HB. Use a Velium War Hammer for that -- again,
though, you won't be seeing those anytime soon. A Fine
Steel mace will be very nice -- anything heavier, and
you won't get in enough swings to live long enough to
increase your skill.<br><br>Finally, I also like Throwing
as a skill. Sure, it's expensive and has limited
range, but getting in a 15 point Critical Strike, or a
30 point Deadly, is neat. Evidently, you can
eventually blacksmith throwing javelins, which have a range
similar to bows.<br><br>Oh, and start collecting poison
supplies. Unrest is great for this -- collect stacks of
Zombie skins, and Grave Molds. Skins are used for making
vials (you'll need Pottery skill). Two grave molds and
a suspension make a nice little low-level
poison.<br><br>Nah, we don't like to rambnle or
anything.<br><br>Kharlisht of Kaladim<br>Rogue of 28 seasons<br>Drinal
