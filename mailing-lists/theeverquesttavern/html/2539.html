The true effects of increased AGI are debated.
The going belief is that an increase in AGI by 4 or 5
= an increase in raw AC of 1. So wearing one of
your braces, the AGI bonus would have an effect of
giving you about +2AC. This is in addition to the bonus
AGI normally gives--- rumor has it that AGI helps
your defensive skills (Dodge, Parry, Riposte, etc),
and that with a high AGI you will evade hits or take
a fraction less damage from hits. In the VERY old
days it was believed (and may have been true) that a
higher AGI resulted in a higher natural running speed as
well. <br><br>I know someone who tried experimenting
with AGI, and SWORE that buffed in all AGI gear with a
VERY high AGI, he would fight an even mob solo (he was
a warrior) and take only a few hits during the
whole battle. <br><br>As for not being able to keep
agro-- not sure, but here's a thought-- IF the warrior
is doing significant damage, AND therefore not
causing a need for Clerics to heal, AND the nukers aren't
nuke-happy, AND the warrior's taunt is up, AND he is
outdamaging other melee, then he should be able to keep agro
even if the mob can't hit him well. <br><br>I admit,
the benefits of AGI are confusing to me. Generally
tanks are more concerned with offensive power than
defensive. If you are a tank class that likes to solo (not
easy for sure), AGI and AC items might be what you
want.<br><br>I think the answer is to test it out a bit more. If
you haven't maxed out your offensive and defensive
skills, maybe work the new bracer into your armor a
bit---
