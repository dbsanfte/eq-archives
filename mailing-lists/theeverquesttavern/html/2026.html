www.castersrealm.com has some pretty good info on
jewelry making that I've used for my chanter. EITHER your
WIS or your INT matters in jewelry making, DEPEDING
on which is higher. <br><br>There should be no
difference in difficulty or in skill leveling when using
enchanted or unenchanted metals. In my experience, and i've
read posts to this effect too, Jewelry making has the
best skill level increase per attempt ratio of all
trade skills. Generally, I would gain a skill level at
least every other attempt (sometimes every 3d or 4th
attempt, but sometimes 4 attempts in a row would give a
skill increase). Failure rate, well that's another
story-- though i usually only fail consistently when i'm
trying to make items that have a trivial level 20 more
than my skill level.<br><br>Check the sites that show
average prices for selling jewlry items to see what the
market is and what you should be making. INT and WIS
rings sell great at lower levels-- I would make tons
and recoup all my costs PLUS some, by selling them
for 5pp (or more). The merchants would only pay me
1pp+ for them. BUT there were some rings that didn't
sell well to players (ie. 2AC +4svPoison), but sell to
merchants (5pp in this case). So you CAN make some money
back. Once your skill gets up, you can make a bunch of
money-- i started making WIS earrings-- good money. OH--
and make those STR, DEX or AGI rings-- melee guys
have tons of money to give you for them, because
they're not buying spells--haha<br><br>Mandir the
Terrible<br>Tier'Dal Enchanter of the 17th illusion<br>Doing his best
to make beautiful jewelry to cover the ugly races of
