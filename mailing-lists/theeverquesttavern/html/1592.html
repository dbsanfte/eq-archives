I'll add to the good comments already made-- I
always ask before I jump in-- UNLESS it's obvious that
the person needs help. (But even then, you may be
helping a Pally who had LoH and didn't want help!!). If
someone gives a train shout, it is more often a warning
AND a cry for help with the train. If someone doesn't
shout, they either are being rude to those about to get
trained (or...gasp!!.. doing it on purpose??), or are
pulliing the train to take it on (usually with a group
that's nearby). <br><br>Here's my question-- what zones
have the biggest or scariest or toughest trains?? At
lower levels, not much compares to BlackBurrow-- the
gnoll trains from below, to the zone, can be huge. I've
been in Unrest for a few weeks now, and I've not seen
more deadly trains. When you're camping in the Round
Room, and all of the sudden you see dozens of mobs and
realize that there are ghouls, darkbone skellys, drybone
skellys, barbed skellys, carrion ghouls, skeletal monks--
and then you notice a few dusty werebats, an animated
hand and a festering hag come in-- UGH!! time to run
to the zone and give tons of shouts!!! <br><br>I am
bound in Dagnar's Caludron, just at the ent to Unrest.
Sometimes I'll log on, and there will be 20 or more
characters there, in various states of undress and health.
BIG train got pulled and nearly everyon in the zone
either died or had to zone out. If you go to Unrest,
make sure you know if someone is hunting for the
ghost, and make sure that they're competent. More times
than not, ghost groups seem to pull the entire house
out to the zone. I've died in Unrest more than in any
other zone (including those early days in GFay and CB),
but it was worth it because of the friends I made
there and the adventures!!!<br><br>Serrafina
