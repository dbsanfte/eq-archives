Well, lemme see if I can give you an idea of
what's so "awesome" about it (your bf's
term?).<br><br>There are a couple of ways to think of EQ. One is a
chat room with fancy graphics. That view emphasizes
the community, and interaction that you get with
people from around the world. That's not my favorite way
to think of EQ, though; if you want that, go to
Active Worlds, where all you do is really chat (though
you can build stuff, too).<br><br>A second way to
think of it is a MMORPG -- Massively Multiplayer Online
Role Playing Game. Think of it this way -- at any
given time, there are probably between 1000 and 2000
people playing on your server (EQ has about 40 different
servers to handle the load). Overall, something like
350,000 people subscribe to the game.<br><br>If your bf
ever played Dungeons and Dragons or a similar pen and
paper-based fantasy-based game, then EQ is like having a Game
Master (or Dungeon Master) ready to play with you at any
time. You get to play-act being something else -- I
mainly play a Dwarf Rogue (thief), and a Troll Shadow
Knight (evil warrior/necromancer combo). Other folks
like to play "casters" -- magic users, druids,
healers/clerics, etc. <br><br>It's really just a chance to be
someone else for a while, for me. What makes it awesome
is that you're constantly interacting with other
real people, and you develop relationships with them
(hopefully good ones) over time. Last night, I ran into
someone I hadn't played with for quite a while, and it
was like running into an old friend. <br><br>There's
lots more, but that's a good start for now, I
hope.<br><br>Kharlisht<br>27 Rogue<br>Specializing in backstabbing, corpse
revocery, and Butcherblock Mountains guide service
