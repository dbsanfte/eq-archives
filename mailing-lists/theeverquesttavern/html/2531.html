As always, shooman has sage advice. I'll add my
2cp worth here...<br><br>1ST-- play a race/class
combo that you like, and not just for stats. You will
make friends and be remembered if you are not just
another run of the mill barbarian warrior or halfling
druid. One thing people don't understand is that you
have to like your char or you'll get bored with it and
then you've wasted all that time...<br><br>Yes, Ogre
Warriors, Erudite Chanters and Halfling Druids are the best
combos Stat wise. I'm not saying that any of them are
bad either-- Erudites start in a horrible newbie zone
(unless you go to painell/warrens), Ogres do too and have
faction problems. No complaints about 1/2ling druids at
all, except that you need to get out of Misty Thicket
ASAP if you want to make any cash and get into groups
at a young age. <br><br>Go to Castersrealm.com for
some help on class/race issues and suggestions on
where to put points. They are not always right, IMO,
but they are helpful. Chanter INT 1st, CHA 2nd. Druid
WIS 1st and then maybe CHA (if you want to charm
animals a lot) or any melee stat. Warrior, depends on
what race you pick-- remember though, increased AGI
translates into AC, which means less hits and damage taken,
which means less need for healing, which means less
chance for clerics getting agro... etc etc... (Also, I
thought only Ogre Warriors had the "not stun from the
front" benefit??). <br><br>Some fun combos of my
chars:<br><br>Iksar Necro<br>DE Chanter<br>Wood Elf Druid<br>Dwarf
Rogue<br>Gnome Mage<br>Vah Shir Bard<br>Troll
Beastlord<br><br>Have fun with it-- be smart on where you put your
points and work toward stat increasing armor and any
class/race combo can be fun!!<br><br>X.
