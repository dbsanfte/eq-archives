I've also just recently started, and found that
some of the guides really help (www.everlore.com;
everquest.allakhazam.com also has some good stuff, and ZDNet's GameSpot
has two very helpful guides:
<a href=http://www.zdnet.com/gamespot/ target=new>http://www.zdnet.com/gamespot/</a>). In some cases, you'll have no idea what they're
talking about, but eventually it will make
sense.<br><br>Remember -- "con" everything. Target something by clicking
on it (or hit F8 to target the nearest "MoB"
(monster)), and then hit /c, or the Consider button. You'll
get a color-coded response; this is what Gamespot
says:<br><br>-If something cons white/black and gives the message:
"Looks like an even fight," it's of the same level as
you and may be a fair fight.<br><br>-If something
cons blue and gives the message: "Looks like you would
have the upper hand," it's a level or two lower than
you, and you'll stand a good chance of
winning.<br><br>-If something cons yellow and gives the message:
"Looks like quite a gamble," it's a level or two higher
than you, and you probably wouldn't beat it in a
straight fight.<br><br>-If something cons green and gives
the message: "You would probably win this fight,"
it's three or more levels lower than you, and you'd
probably win easily.<br><br>-If something cons red and
gives the message: "What would you like your tombstone
to say," it's three or more levels higher than you,
and you'd probably lose.<br><br>OK, that's the first
thing. Beyond that, count on the kindness of strangers .
. . including the guards, of course, but also
fellow players. Most are pretty helpful, I've found.
Need a guide around Kelethin's annoying platforms?
Just ask, and someone is likely to come around. And
don't get frustrated, dying is OK, at least until level
5. <br><br>Enjoy, and good
hunting!<br><br>Kharlisht, late of Kaladim<br>3rd season Rogue<br>Drinal
