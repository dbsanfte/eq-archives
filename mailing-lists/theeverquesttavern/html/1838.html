Totally untwinked. Like I've said before, up
until about level 20, I basically had a full set of
leather armor and a few items that i'd quested. (When i
found the Test. of Vanear quest, i realized that i
could start to increase my stats and not just do minor
quests and "scout" the lands, which I had been doing for
20 levels-- plus, druid quests are REALLY bad and
few and far between). <br><br>Quested Testament, got
a prayer cloth of tunare from doing the first part
of the screaming mace quest, go a savant's cap in
unrest, got SP hid gloves in SK-- then saw how much more
mana I had. But it wasn't until the high 20s that I
started to get good stuff. Symbols of Tunare quests (took
forever!!) Hunting Mammys in EF got me tons of pp (for the
FIRST time), and i started grouping in Perma A LOT. Got
great XP and after a few weeks, got a cloak and
circlet. Ported over to Feerot and teamed in CT and Guk
till I got mantle and leggings (lost roll for
leggings, but bought them from winner at a great price).
Picked up some WIS rings and earrings along the way, and
after making money porting all over the world, I
finally hit Gfay-Mart with all the pp i'd been collecting
and saving and made enough deals to get some great
gear and WIS up close to 180. <br><br>So-- first 20
levels were sent having fun, questing and exploring.
Started to pay attention to quests for WIS items, and
then from about 26-30 worked on collecting pp and
getting all the wis things i could. Bought up by lvl 32,
and headed out to Velius, ready to play with the big
boys. <br><br>My point is, non-twinked druids have it
rough as far as GREAT items early on, but your time
will come. Most of the stuff I have I got from drops
and quests. (BTW-- Forrest Giants in WW are the way
to get your Loops--much easier than FM giants,
though the Loops seemed to be a more rare drop in WW).
Once druids get ports, it becomes much easier to get
xp, pp and items. But don't forget to keep your
fighting techniques up!!!<br><br>My only regrets-- I love
to group, and need to perfect my kiting skills.
Also, my trade skills are pretty sad-- but i chose not
to spend a lot of time/money practicing them. Now i
see that it's a fun way to spend
down-time!!<br><br>X.
