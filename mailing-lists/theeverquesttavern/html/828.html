Graphics Settings in Kunark<br><br>I am running
on a P2-266 Machine, w/128M RAM, 16M TNT Video
Card(running Detonator Drivers with suggested EQ settings),
Sound Blaster Live Value Sound Card, (2) Hard Drives
with Program installed on C: and Swap File on D:
(fixed at 300M), 56K connection (normally connected at
50.6K).<br><br>When I am in the old world, I typically have
everything turned up to full (except Gamma Correction, which
I run full left all the time), 1024x768
resolution.<br><br>When I enter Kunark, I do the following:<br>-Turn down
sky to Single Layer (I like the sky, if you don't
care, turn this off).<br>-Particle Density - OFF<br>-PC
Names - OFF<br>-NPC Names - OFF<br>-Video Mode -
800x600 (Was running 640x480 and moved it up a notch and
noticed no real significant slow down).<br>-Screens Same
Resolution - ON<br>-Level of Detail - OFF<br>-Mip Map - OFF
(I don't run Glide, it's always off).<br><br>While
moving around, you can manually adjust the clipping
plane using the SHIFT - and SHIFT + keys next to the
BACKSPACE key. This is a change they introduced months ago,
but almost everyone has forgotten about it. It works
great. When you are standing around, crank it up to look
around. During a fight, pull it back. I do this when I
pull for a group. I turn it up so I can see the mobs,
I move around until I am in position. I shoot the
mob, and as I am running back, I pull back the
clipping plane to about half what I had it, and continue
to tap it down as I close in on the group, until its
almost off when I get to the group. Then I adjust it up
and down during the fight to keep an eye on what is
going on around me. Takes some getting used to, adding
these keystrokes into your game play. But as you can
see, Kunark is meant to thwart the current play style.
