That last post reminds me of a discussion I
frequently get in re: twinking. EQ is a game that's fun at
all levels, so I never understand why people are in
such a hurry to get stuff that they'll get at higher
levels. Must be Yak envy. <br><br>Why not go out into
GFay and bash evil decaying skellys, vile bats,
swarming drones, and wretched orc pawns in your first
years? It will fill you with pride to know that each
purchase you make is based solely on your own abilities
and experiences, and not the handouts of others. My
new Iksar Necro char ran out into LOIO with nothing
but a dagger, 2 spells, and delusions of granduer,
attacking fierce sabertooth kittys (and often running away)
until lvl 3. At that point, I felt worthy enough to go
back to my guild and seek further knowledge. Bought a
few spells (not all, knowing I would be back in
another season to be invited into the 2nd circle of dark
bindings) and headed out again for adventure. <br><br>Even
if you don't roleplay, though, I think it's good to
pay your dues at lower levels. At lvl 1, you don't
need to be twinked (I don't think you do at any
level-- UNLESS EQ is all about keeping up with the
Joneses). A Cleric in GFay will earn his way into many
groups on orc hill and then in CB in time. The loot you
can make yourself in CB will be staggering if you're
a good player and keep your group alive.
