I think the researcher is right on.

I do think I'm pretty good at detecting gender online, however by that I
don't mean I can tell if someone is a male or a female, it's more I can
tell whether I'm confident about their gender. There are some women I've
met in EQ who I am confident are women, and there are others who I'm not
confident about but I don't really care so I'm not going to challenge
them.

I have a few ideas why men tend to play women in these games:
- it's novel, gamer society tends to be males playing males, if the
society was primarily females playing females more women might play for
the novelty, to differentiate themselves from their peers.
- it's advantageous, numerous male players see themselves as getting
some kind of advantage from playing female characters

A lot of more ideas are presented in the following article on
womengamers.com:
http://www.womengamers.com/articles/gender.html
