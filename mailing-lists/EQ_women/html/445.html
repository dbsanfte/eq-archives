If someone tells someone they are a girl in real life, when they are a
boy, that is deceptive. If they try to act 'girlish' and people believe
they're a girl, that's role playing, as far as I see it, and there's
nothing wrong with it. Even if they rollplay a girl badly, and boys
believe them anyway =)

Even if someone lies about being a girl in real life, I don't see it as
that big a deal. I had that happen to me. Someone I was friends with for
3 months who I totally thought was a girl, turned out to be a boy.
Initially, I was totally shocked, but it didn't take me long to realize
this was still the same person I was friend with.

Even if someone is actively deceptive about their gender, I really do
think it's pretty harmless. The Internet offers a great medium for people
to explore different things, in a way that is essentially harmless. Until
it starts intruding on the real world, I think it's basicly harmless.

I'm sure there are a number of people on my own server who are unsure as
to my gender, or think that I'm a boy. That's just the way the Internet
works, it doesn't bother me. I do tend to drop pretty overt gender cues,
most notably all my online friends know that my RL boyfriend is a player
on our server. If someone thinks I'm a boy though, it doesn't bug me.

I know lots of nice 'girls' online who may or may not be boys, I haven't
asked =) I must admit I wonder some time, but I don't think it's necessary
for me to pry. I do know they're good friends, and I love grouping with
them.

- Tina (a high elf in real life =)
