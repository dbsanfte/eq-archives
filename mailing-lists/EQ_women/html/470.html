I actually played male characters for decades (starting in pencil &
dice games). I started because female chars in chainmail and D&D
had some penalties .. rolled an 18 00 strength, cut your bonus in
half or decide your character changes sex ... ummm change sex!

Since I ran an all-girl Roleplaying group ... several of us would
sometimes play guy chars to liven it up. We even had a few "play"
romances, all in fun. But more we just decided a pair was a couple
and skipped RPing the romance.

When on-line MUDs first started, being the only female on could be
quite a distraction. :p So much so, I sometimes died in "scroll" when
so many people were commenting "OMG its a female!" I couldn't see the
text battle messages fast enough :( They were all text-based. Not
to mention, if only half of the males were interested in a romantic
story line .. that made about 100 with you the only viable target.
Eeeeeeeeee. Since I'd done it before ... I just played males chars for
breaks. Then I could actually PLAY the game and SEE it. :)

I still do it when a class or character idea I come up with just "works"
as a male. I'm allright in a "male" environment, I was the only girl
on the farm (and nearby ones) when I grew up, and work in mostly all-male
fields (only female to 19 men in my dept. today). So I handle guy stuff OK,
and have been told I am capable of fitting in with a group of guys well
enough to become "one of the guys" if we are on a work team long enough.
I also test as highly agressive and mechanical (and still high when my
scores are ranked against male scores), so its no surprise I have been
told I'm believable as a guy in an RPG where they can't see me and I do no
OOC chatting. But, I have to pick my interactions and definitely come up
with an IC way of avoiding romances. Its when women hit on me when I'm
playing a guy that I get uncomfortable. There's no parallel to what I'm
used to since there is no hitting on me allowed at work! Of course, now its
probably a guy playing a girl who's hitting on a guy played by a girl to "see
what its like". Isn't THAT a twist! Run awaaaaaaay .....

I do find that now that female characters are plentiful, my first choice is
to play a female and my male/female char ratio is about 1 to 10 in favor of
playing women. And my male characters generally get played for shorter
periods
than the females as well. Most of my male characters nowadays are
"adopted" by
my son, who is too young to play. And he is allowed to help make action
choices.
So they sort of end up male-played by proxy.

There is a male troll warrior out there tonight who was being played by a
co-op of a grey-haired lady and her 1st grade son. Muhahaha. Ungh? is a
fairly
sexless response ...
