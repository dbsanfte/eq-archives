Hey...Ranalla, you mentioned that everyone was shouting for SoWs when you
were in fp when you got to 14. My Barb Shaman (Kalara on the Veeshan server)
got her SoW spell at lvl 9 and ever since then I've essentially been a SoW
whore (lol might be a bit crude, but apt). The big difference now that i'm
lvl 21 is that no one pays me for the SoW. They just come up to me (and
pretty soon you'll know what I mean...they'll come up, sort of stand there
and before they can ask for the SoW I am already mid-spell to just get them
to go away). They literally assume that it is for free, say thanks and walk
away. Drives me nuts when they do it right after I've just fought a mob, am
at half-life, oom and my party is the same or worse. That's usually the only
time that I'll turn them down.

Most people just say thanks (but often not even that) and leave. Others,
usually the higher levels, will give me buffs and that I can always
appreciate.

My in-game fiance (hey...THAT is a great topic, in-game marriages...anyone
know of a good place in Norrath to tie the knot?) is an enchanter and he
gets asked for breezes far more than I do and they pay him...and often pay
him well. Hey, I have often paid several plat to get a good breeze or
clarity. Can't live without my new addiction! And seriously, I can't tell
you how great it is to have a perpetual sow and breeze when you are in a
group.

My guild is a pretty big one but there is a big disparity between high level
characters and lower ones. You can really tell the difference between the
ones that came into the guild at a later time and the ones that have been
there for awhile. Most of them are lvl 30+ and then the next group is pretty
much right around 20, then it drops to a few new secondary char of people in
the guild who are really low levels. It sort of sucks because the guild
raids are mostly planned by the higher levels and excludes a lot of people.
But it's growing and new people are coming on board all the time. I'm
finding that I hang out mostly with people in my guild and it is a lot of
comradarie. It also doesn't hurt that 70% of the guild are druids (weird,
huh?) and so getting a port is never a problem.

I play for the social aspects, but I love the questing and learning skills.
I have half my totemic armor already and the getting it has been most of the
fun...travelling all over, getting help from people. I'm a lvl 68 in baking
(funny because I'm not a domestic sort in real life...I can cook well but
don't like to so much). Baking is an awesome skill to have because once you
buy a spit, you can bake while you meditate! And fresh meat there is
aplenty. I find it funny though that there aren't many bakers around. My
friends always laugh when I present them with beer braised lion or pickled
gator. The food is also lighter than regular food (monks love that) at .01
and it lasts a lot longer.

Okay, I'm rambling...brought up lots of things we can use for topics later
(guilds, skills, weddings, social stuff). :-)
