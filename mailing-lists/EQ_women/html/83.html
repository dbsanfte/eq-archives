As I said before, I am probably one of the oldest at 54 women (55 in
September) on EQ. I know of one other on Fennin Ro who is older than I am
and a women in RL. Mature people have a love of gaming, too. I get a lot
of pleasure from playing any game, puzzle, or challenge. I work off
aggression with my slashing and hacking, get satisfaction in achieving
small goals like acquiring items, making skill or experience levels and in
discovering and exploring the EQ environment.

Us old geezers have the time and the patience (and the money) for many
on-line activities, as well as having highly developed computer-related
skills. I have found that women have done very well in information
management/computer science careers and I have been involved since the
middle 1960's with large corporations on the west and east coasts.
I am retired now and only do a little consulting to keep my skills and
marketability up. This leaves me much time for day playing and all night
sessions, if I want. I am a borderline compulsive when I play this game.
It is so immersive and addicting. When a good group forms, I like to stay
together as long as I can for the session and the companionship.

I played Realm before EQ. But a family member was a beta tester for EQ and
showed it to me last year. So I was on the distribution list for the
production software by New Years of this year and started playing the first
day of production. I have found that my knowledge of the game is only now
beginning to surpass the beta testers and I am considering volunteering for
guide duty. I think older women would make excellent guides. If anything,
age teaches patience, wit, skin thickening and a little teaching skills,
which I think are essential attributes for a good guide.

I can always spot the youngest players. They are the ones always shouting
for help with corpse retrieval, free food, free items, screaming 'gimme,
gimme, gimme'. They can't seem to stand still.......ever. They never
bother with Sense Heading skill development, never learned the value of
saving up their coins for something they want, and seem very self-centered
in every thing they do.

So, here is a new mail list thread idea:

You can always spot the kids when they

1) Beg for favors all over the zone
2) twink
3) solo power game
4) are on most people's ignore list
5) bully other players with their own greediness
6) brag too much about their race, class, level, wealth or items
7) answer every question about game secrets with shouts across the zone to
show off what they know (and don't know, hehe)
8) chatter incessantly about inanities
9) complain the loudest about others not helping them
10) ..................[you fill in your favorite here]
