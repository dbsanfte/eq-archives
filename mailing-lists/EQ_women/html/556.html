Subject: Diablo II


This is a mini Diablo II review.

I have been quite burned out by EQ lately, so I picked up Diablo 2. I wasn't
particularly interested in it (though I loved Diablo) but some friends had
it and I wanted to play multiplayer with them.

It took me about half an hour to figure out the interface, and "remember"
how to play. I wasn't too impressed at the start everything seemed overly
complicated.

I started with a barbarian (I wanted a simple character). I started hacking
away. My immediate shock was that things just died so easily and so quickly.
I'm used to EQ where killing one mob can be a monumental effort, but my
little barbarian dude was cleaving things left right and center. One
thing I like about Diablo II is that all characters have mana and cast
spells. My barbarian has this satisfying 'bash' spell that smacks things
a little extra hard.

By level 4 I was a bit bored and decided to try a Necromancer. This totally
hooked me! When I read the class over it sounded a little bizarre to me.
I summoned my little skeleton pet and he started running around fighting
everything. Pets in diablo are a little psychotic, there is no "pet back off"
command, and they just try and kill everything they see.

Once I got level 2 I was able to upgrade my pet spell, and now I had two
pets! That was fun, never got to do that in EQ.

As I continued to level I got more spells, and I found I liked them all,
even though originally they didn't seem too exciting. One line of necro spells
I wasn't excited about was debuffs. They have a debuff that causes mobs
to take twice as much damage, and another that causes mobs to do 1/3 less
damage. As you level up a bit, these spells become invaluable ways to
make your pets more effective. Indeed, by level 9, all I'm doing is
summoning pets and debuffing mobs. I have a bolt spell but I never use it.
At level 9 I also have a golem pet, bringing my pet total to 3. Using
pets is a bit hard because they are psychotic, but it's fun too.

Anyway, Diablo 2 is an exciting video game with RPG elements. I enjoy the
'leveling up' aspect of it, and the opportunity to choose skills that customize
my character. Diablo 2 isn't a true RPG but it has a lot of great RPG
elements.

The quests so far are great as well, and the cinematics are stunning.

Anyway, I give this game a solid 4 out of 5 stars. I see Diablo 2 as an
excellent way to spend a lot of hours playing an entertaining game.

As a last note, I'm not all that interested in the multiplayer capabilities.
My interest is purely in the single player version. If you enjoy multiplayer
Diablo the game will have even more to offer you.
