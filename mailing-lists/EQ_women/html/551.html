>   I was a master smith and a grandmaster jewler, here is some of the stuff
>   I've learned.
>
>   Tailoring is a great starter skill, it requires little investment to make
>   patchwork armor.  However, if you want to continue on to high end stuff
>   (reinforced leather, 10-slot bags) it requires considerable time and money
>   investment.  You can make good newbie-level money doing tailoring in
>   greater faydark.
>
>   Smithing requires about a 200pp investment to master (less if you are
>   optimal).  Smithing I found to be very rewarding early on in my server's
>   life, there were lots of tanks who wanted armor.  For various reasons
>   though, it can get boring.  To make small or large armor you have to
>   travel a lot to get your molds and metals and such.  To make medium armor,
>   you can do it in freeport, where all the materials are fairly available.
>   Smithing is quite enjoyable I found though eventually it becomes tedious.
>   You can make good low level money smithing in freeport or greater fay.
>   This will probably work best if you are level 20 or under as you will be
>   able to hunt in the near vicinity to freeport where people who will want
>   your wares will hang out.
>
>   Oh I almost forgot, smiths can make weapons now.  They couldn't when I
>   smithed.  I don't get the impression that forged weapons are a
>   very competitive business though.
>
>   Baking and brewing are both more 'fun' trades.  All the skilled bakers and
>   brewers I know are druids, which makes sense because getting your goods
>   requires a lot of travel.  These two skills require little investment to
>   start, but a lot of work and travel to master.  These are nice because you
>   can give people alcohol or food, I still remember with joy the day my
>   druid friend gave me chocolate! You won't make money doing this.
>
>   Jewlery is one of the few trades you can make serious money in but it is
>   also fairly competitive.  In order to make money in jewelcraft you usually
>   need a sizible investment.  Maxing your jewelcraft to 200 will cost
>   several thousand plat.  On the other hand, 50pp will get you able to make
>   some jewelry.  If you are not an enchanter you will have to pay to get
>   your metal bars enchanted which is a pain.  If you have a close enchanter
>   friend you could do ok though =)
>
>   Alchemy is shaman only and you have to be fairly high level to do it.
>   Shrink and SOW potions are pretty cool though, but they require a sizible
>   investment (thus the high level).
