Subject: balancing EQ with real life :)


I was just wondering how you all balance playing EQ with real life
roles/duties.

I'm not actually a EQ player myself, but my husband is. Since he started to
play EQ (over 6 months ago), we haven't done as much as we used to together.
Before EQ he played Ultima Online but was able to log whenever he felt like
it in that game.

With EQ it seems like he has to be on longer to get anywhere in the game and
he isn't able to log whenever he wants to. He is a level 50 ranger and is
in the Plain of Fear a lot right now. Due to the spawn rates he has to stay
up late, and be online every day. He even has taken off work periodically
to be in the game more.

I know a lot of the game just because it is mostly all of what we talk about
right now. :)

I understand online role playing as I ran my own computer BBS for over 5
years. I was an avid MUD/MUSH player myself for a while and did play Ultima
Online for a bit too. My husband and I actually meet on my BBS when I was
on college dated for about 3 years and have been married 3 1/2 years. We
are both avid computer game players. Due to life/new hobbies/etc. I haven't
been in online games myself for the past 3 years.

I really don't mind him playing EQ as I know it is what he likes to do. We
have an understanding that I let him play EQ and I spend my free time doing
SCA (medieval re-inactment group) activities.

For Christmas all he wanted was anything EQ related. He also tells me if I
want to spend more time with him, that I should get a EQ account myself (we
have a home network which would make this possible). :)

I was just wondering how others balance their life with their significant
others and EQ playing.

Thanks!
