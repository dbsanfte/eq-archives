Hi Melissa,

I'm not too sure about that one myself, the chaste part, I took it from
actual conversations with the sisters on the isle. My best guess is that
the sisters strive to be chaste because they don't want their love to be
bound and focussed on any one person. I'd like to see the whole chaste
thing as a role playing element we can use if we want to, and not use if
we don't want to.

As for the men-protecting-the-women thing, it can be taken that way, but
it doesn't have to. I was basicly just trying to identify a nice way in
which men could be incoroporated into the guild. Your character could
choose to not have a warder, and that would be a valid choice. If I were
to have a warder, I would see him as a default following of Erollisi
looking to serve his god, and not as someone I needed for protection.
Others might choose to define their relationship with their Warder in a
different way (love, protection, siblings, or even the sister protecting
the Warder?)

All the role playing background can and should be taken on an "if you want
to use it" basis.

- Christina
