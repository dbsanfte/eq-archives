Subject: Women's Guild Ideas


As I mentioned a while before, I thought it would be a good idea for us to
form an guild on a new server at some point. The purpose of forming the
guild would be to allow those of us who wish to to play with their EQ
sisters. The purpose in forming it on a new server would be so no one
would be directly advantaged by already having characters and contacts on
their current server which they could carry over to this new server. Here
are some further ideas I have about how this guild might operate. Please
give me feedback!

Participation

First of all, I'd like to stress that I'd see participation in this guild
as voluntary. I'd also like to stress that I wouldn't be asking anyone to
drop their existing characters to play in this guild. Many of us already
have really good characters in really good environments, and we don't want
to give this up!

To facilitate participation in this guild, I'd probably thus suggest two
unofficial times each week which would be guild play times. While you can
play any time, you'd be encouraged to try and play during guild times.
This would ensure a reasonable number of us playing at those times, so we
can group. Example guild times might be Wednesday evening, and Saturday
morning.

Roleplaying Background:

Ever good guild should have a nice robust role playing background, that
those of us who wish to roleplay can work into our characters. One such
background might be as follows:

Erollisi Marr is the goddess of love. Here is her description from the
player manual: Followers of Erollisi Marr cling to the belief that love
conquers all. It should be pointed out that while love is a generally
peaceful concept, Erollisi worshippers are not pacifists. They would like
to live in a world where everyone loves everyone else and violence does
not exist but they are not naive enough to think that Norrath is that
world. They have passionate loves of people, places and ideas and are more
than willing to fight and die to preserve those things. The dream of every
follower of Erollisi is to die in selfless defense of someone or something
they love. Many Paladins hear the true calling of their hearts and
follow Erollisi Marr.

From my own playing I think Erollisi bears a great resemblence to Artemis
the greek goddess of the hunt. Evidence to this includes the Sisters of
Erollisi, a warrior-cult of women who live in the Ocean of Tears. These
chaste (except when they stray) warrior women seem to me to be a great
basis for a guild.

I would like to see us take "Sisters of Erollisi" as our guild name. This
would afford us the great opportunity to use the island of Erollisi in the
ocean of tears as our guild home. Few guilds can have a whole island
to themselves. It would also give our guild a strong tangible connection
to the game.

I would like to allow males to be in the guild (when sponsored by a female
member) and suggest incorporating them (from a roleplaying perspective) as
"Warders" (I'm stealing the concept from Robert Jordan's Wheel of Time
series). A Warder would be a male who pledges his life to the protection
of a Sister of Erollisi. Each sister could have only one warder. A
Warder would most obviously be a Paladin, but could be of any class. An
obvious line of roleplaying courtship might involve a male courting a
Sister to first become her Warder, and perhaps even one day her husband.

In terms of races/classes allowed, I would like to have no restrictions on
that. We all have different playing interests. Obviously a Shadow Knight
or Necromancer doesn't fit in very well with the Sisters of Erollisi.
With proper thought, and a well crafted story, it could be done.

Rules:

I like keeping rules to a minimum. The main reason for this is so no one
will feel they don't want to be a part of the guild, because it has
restrictive rules.

The only rule I can think of is that female players should play female
characters, and male players should play male characters. This will allow
us to more easily recognize other women in the guild. Even this rule I'd
be willing to bend if someone had a good reason they couldn't abide by
this rule.

Can anyone think of any other obvious, required, rules?


Men in the guild:

I think men should be allowed in the guild, but they should be sponsored
by a female guild member. While I think a truly women-only guild would be
cool, it seems unnecessarily restrictive (there are nice guys out there
after all).


Hierarchy:

Guild hierarchy can be a fun thing, but it can also be annoying. I'd like
to see hierarchy so those of us who enjoy it can take part. I'd also like
those of us who aren't interested in hierarchy to be able to play without
it.

The only actual benefits from being higher in the hierarchy, as I see it,
should be from a role playing perspective.

Here is an example hierarchy we might use:

Sister

This is the introductary level at which all Sisters of Erollisi would be.
This afford one the right to vote and participate in guild events. All
Sisters have the responsility to aid other Sisters in need.

Acolyte

One becomes an Acolyte of Erollisi by achieving at least level 15, and
then completing a quest as dictated by a Priestess. An Acolyte has the
responsibility to aid, advise, and watch over all Sisters. Early on the
level requirements might be restricted.

Priestess

One becomes a Priestess of Erollisi by achieving at least level 25, and
the completing a quest as dictated by the Hand of Erollisi. Implicit in
being offered the rank of Priestess is a history of good guild conduct.
A Priestess has the responsibility to organize guild events and hunts. A
Priestess can be of any class. Early on the level requirements might be
restricted.

Hand of Erollisi

The Hand of Erollisi is the leader of the Sisters of Erollisi. It is her
responsibility to organize guild meetings, induct new members, and oversee
any guild issues.


Warder

Male members of the guild would all inducted warders. They would be sworn
to protect an indivual Sister of Erollisi. They would not have any voting
power. Warders would gain title as the sisters they protect gain title.
Warders could not vote, but his valued opinion would be rightly
considered by the Sister he is sworn to protect.

Dues:

Many guilds have dues. I don't really see the need for them. Anyone have
any good reasons why Dues should be part of this guild?

Comments?

Please let me know what you think about any or all of these ideas.
