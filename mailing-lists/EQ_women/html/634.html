In March, I'll be playing for 2 full years.

Since you've asked, here's been my change in perspective on EQ, the good and the bad:

(Disclaimer: All below is only MHO, no one has to agree with me, just how I see it)

Good:

1. Lots of new, challenging zones released often enough to keep me playing.

2. Grouping with friends I've known a long, long time and love hanging around with.

3. Matured "rules" of the game. Such as random loot, "play nice" rules, easing up of KS'ing. In the early days, I never did get a BS tunic with a name like "Zuki" and alpha loot order.

4. Less populated servers because of all the new one added and splitting.

Bad:

1. The general "socialness" of the game has taken a deep dive down. In the early days, it was fun and exciting for everyone. Everyone learned stuff the hard way together. Now, it constant shouts for exp. res's and sow and clarity. Very little fun chit chat.

2. Greediness has risen sharply. Farming. Begging. Camping items to twink a lower level character. All rampant.

3. General "safety" of the game. Everyone knows what level creatures are in every zone. If you die, get an exp. res. Maps, locs, everything's available to you. There's no more exploring or serendipity to the game.

4. "Lather, Rinse, Repeat" aspect of fighting and hunting. *Yawns*

I've cut back sharply on how much I play in the last 6 months. I cut back because the game isn't as challenging anymore. There is no "end". You can't win it. There's no storyline to make you think and ponder and get stuck. It isn't a very mentally challenging game (IMHO), so after the "physical" thrill has worn off, it doesn't have much to offer me.

I still enjoy playing and exploring with my friends, but now I'm on for their company, not for the game.
