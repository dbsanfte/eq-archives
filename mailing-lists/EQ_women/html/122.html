Welcome, Lady! Aye, I am the only female in my guild and if I let them, they would
spoil me shamelessly. Actually, they already do, but I hope I have taught them
respect for my skills too........yes, just like in real life, over and over and over
again...heh. I'm thirty this year, and I'm one of the oldest of my guildmates.
Surprisingly, they don't ask how old I am, and I haven't run into that many requests
for my age. When I get those requests from people who just met me, I tell them I'm a
happily married thirty-year-old mother of two. Well, I am thirty, and I am a mother,
and the marital status sometimes makes me unattainable, and sometimes doesn't seem to
matter. On the rare occasions that I have problems with someone, I try to gently
dissuade them, in character first and out of character if need be.

I was so gratified to find there were women my age playing. I'd often asked myself if
I was a little old for this silly RP stuff, and whether I should start growing up and
acting my age. It's a major bone of contention for my paramour, who resents my
online time. He's not a gamer. I, on the other hand, am a child of my generation,
and I had a very astute mother who scrimped and saved to buy us computers from a very
early age as well as encouraging us to explore our imaginations with "traditional"
role playing games......and books, lotsa books. When Commodore first came out, they
were one of the first companies to offer IRC for the common masses, and from that day
I was hooked on talking to people from all over the world. Maybe that helped me
learn how to protect my "online virtue" early on. My primary character appears to be
very light-hearted about life, so she refuses to take any overtures seriously. My
secondary character, a Dark Elf Cleric on Rallos Zek, simply emotes "muses whether
the creature before her would make a suitable sacrifice to her God, the Prince of
Hate." That usually takes care of things rather well. <grins>

Again, welcome!

Sara, Povar
