This is an excellent topic for discussion and I think the line needs to
be drawn between flirting, which is usually done "across a distant
room" so to speak, and building in-game relationships which is a more
serious form of roleplay.

If I am in a group and flirting with a Bard (my favorite) by saying how
romantic his voice is, then I see no reason to say "and, by they way I
am married with 2 children ". However, should we continue to group and
perhaps it becomes clear that our in-game roleplay is taking a decidedly
romantic turn, then I abosultely believe that without question it is my
responsibility to tell him at least that I am married with 2 children
and there is no chance that any in-game relationship will transfer into
real life.

And I think there is an even stronger moral issue when it comes to any
kind of sexual innuendo in the roleplay and that is that the players be
"of an age". If two 16 YOs want to go at it who am I to object. But if
a 25 YO wants to do that kind of roleplay with a 16 YO I will object and
object STRONGLY! That sort of thing is bordering on pederasty and is
intolerable. Esp. since there are children as young as 10 playing this
game unsupervised. [resists the temptation to pull out her parents
should know what their children are doing on-line soapbox].

So, I think the same goes for men. Men (and women, though fewer women
choose to play men) have a rare opportunity to see how the world treats
the opposite sex by taking on a female character. I know some men who
do it because women get lots of free buffs and twinks. But they begin
to understand the price the women are paying through expectations etc.
pretty quickly. There is no such thing as a free buff (grins).

When to disclose is a question for each individual. But we each have
responsibility for our own emotional attachment and if one goes off
attaching to someone without knowing anything about the player behind
the character, that is also that person's responsibility. Should a man
lie and say "oh, no I really AM a woman" that is reprehensible. But if
you don't ask then you can't be pissed off when you find out later.
