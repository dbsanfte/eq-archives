Hi there--
 
My partner and I plan to play Horizons and Neverwinter Nights.  UO2 looks like it will be a huge improvement on UO, and I am hoping that I can at least try it out.  Other than that, we just got involved in the Role Playing Games Association (RPGA) and are heavily (well, not compared to EQ =P) into Dungeons and Dragons (3rd edition) and soon Star Wars (more his thing than mine).  I told him that LiveAction RolePlaying (LARP) games looked like fun and I might follow a group around next time we meet at the college--they meet on the same night as one of our campaigns and they wear black and lots of makeup and jewelry and stuff--pretty cool! ;)  The nice thing about what we're doing right now is that we only do it once a week and about every other weekend--leaves plenty of time for the family, my schoolwork, and our careers.  Things to consider if you are a roleplayer trying to balance your worlds.
 
However, I have officially given up beta testing. Sigh.  I just don't have time any more if I want to keep my grades up and continue to "ramp up" at work.  I have four games under my belt that gave me wonderful insight into the world of debugging and what customers look for in a product, and it will help me in all aspects of life--I heartily recommend making the attempt!  I got my start by signing up at gamespot and Sierra.
 
If you DO decide to give beta testing a shot and are accepted, I recommend that you devote a serious amount of time to trying out _all_ the different aspects of the game, not just the race/clan/whatever combination that interests you most.  You gain respect in the beta world by finding bugs and recording them and reporting them back to the developers in a professional and courteous manner.  Once you establish your credibility as a tester with a company, they often create a pool to which you will be added for future games--and you will most likely have a good chance at being selected for first wave/alpha if it's not to be done inhouse!  That is very exciting.  Also, you can use games that you've beta tested as "references" for other games that you are signing up to beta test.  I will tell you that women do seem to have a slight edge in that we have a smaller demographic group and are perceived as having a different perspective.  If you are willing to give FPS or RTS games a try you may find you are getting accepted more and more often since women are perceived as being huge RPGers and not necessarily combatants.  One of my favorite games to test was a magic-duelling one-on-one genre... great stuff but it never really got off the ground. =(  Occasionally that will happen, but don't consider it to be time wasted.  You will have gained experience in many areas, including bugspotting and maneuvering and battle tactics.  Anyway, there's some free advice on the world of beta testing so take what you want from it. =)
 
Hope everyone is enjoying good health and fulfilling guildship,
