Subject: EQ then and now


Recently I went into Karndor's Castle and found 43 players and the ooc
chat was all "don't kill steal" bitching at each other. A few minutes
later I ported to Emerald Jungle and the only people there was my and my
friend. The difference between the two zones? Karndor's castle has
unique drops of great equipment and Emerald Jungle has nothing with loot
(the MoBs give equivalent experience I found).

I had the opportunity to play with a friend I really like grouping with
but he was busy in another dungeon because "It has thisg great <class
specific> sword." So we didn't get to spend the day together. Another
friend didn't want to play because "I need to camp this really important
piece of equipment and I can't give up the camp or I'll lose it".

I haven't played EQ all week and it is the second paragraph that really
sums up why. I feel like EQ used to be about friends, RP, and hunting
together in groups and learning to take new areas and defeat dungeons
you'd never tried before. Then it turned into camping and lists. And
now it has deteriorated to "things" being more important than "people".

Does anyone else feel this? Is it just the people I've been hanging out
with lately being more "thing" than "people" oriented and I should go
find some new friends? Or is it that not being the target market of the
game I just don't get it and getting the coolest things is really what
the game is about?

At any rate, my disatisfaction toward EQ of late has been profound.
Profound enough that I can't bring myself to log back in. And I really
think it is that they tweaked the game for the worse, starting with the
addition of class specific armor and weaponry. Suddenly it's all about
"stuff". Or was it always and I just missed the point?

More importantly, if there were an RP style game tweaked more toward
people than things, would you play it? If the game gave points for
storyline and sometimes favored the underdog (letting the least equipped
defeat the most equipped like a David and Goliath kind of story) and
promoted group dynamics with more "realistic" story effects - would that
be of any interest?

Just doing a bit of "market research" here. And, yes, I do have an idea
up my sleeve <grins>.
