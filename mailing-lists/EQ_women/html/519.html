Guilds are pretty cool. The main advantage they offer is a chat interface
where you can talk to everyone in your guild. Using this interface you
can then find people to hunt with. Hopefully, you come to hunt with these
people semi regularly, which helps create a more enjoyable play
experience. Looking for people to hunt with all the time can be
difficult.

It's good to join a guild where the people in the guild are around your
level. It's good to join a guild where the people in the guild play about
the same frequency as you (so you don't pull too far ahead, or get left
too far behind).

I think it's ok to leave your guild or change guilds. Over time you may
find your guild doesn't serve your needs any more, and at that point it's
a good time to consider another guild.

Guild politics can be a pain. Guilds often have rules or regulations, and
if they don't sit well with you, the guild won't work for you.

Small guilds can be annoying because, unless they are close personal
friends, you'll probably get frustrated that usually no one from your
guild is on. Larger more established guilds tend to provide a more stable
constant environment, but can be more difficult to get into.

Hope this helps,

Tina
