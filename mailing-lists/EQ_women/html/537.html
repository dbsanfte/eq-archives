Okay, what about most fun you've had lately.  Being in the 50s is kind of a PITA because it takes a minimum of 12 people and preferably 18 to do most of the really fun stuff.  I'm in the process of changing guilds to one with more high level players.  The top 5 ME players are fed up with being the only high level players on and we're hoping to all move together but who knows.
 
Sebilis exit room with 2 druids, 1 shaman, 1 enchanter, 1 bard, and 1 necromancer.  Basically, the bard is a very light tank so my druid got to tank (ooooh, I love tanking and Mufat is my hero though Sylvanar has lots of strong opinions <wouldn't let me loot a hangman's noose and gave it to a rogue who happened to be in the neighborhood>).  Needless to say, this was a hard group to play.  The only player that could taunt was the necro's pet.  But with 3 healers, as soon as the bard broke mes, we started chain healing the enchanter until we got the mob off of her. 
 
Sebilis Trakanon outer courtyard (no idea what this place is really called, but if you pull wrong Trak comes out and it's EVAC EVAC EVAC!).  3 group raid, our group was 1 cleric, 3 druids, and a bard.  We laughed about balance and then proceeded to take 90% of the kills.  It was hysterical (esp. since everyone but the bard was female - he kept calling us his "kill stealing ladies" and "ladies, no go get me some experience".)  Amazing how much damange a cleric can do when she doesn't have to heal.  And all of us druids had our KS timing down pat ;-)
 
So I am having the most fun in totally unbalanced groups where the play isn't the traditional "snare, dot, med".  It's fun to not be sure if you'll live (and not living as often as not, thank Tunare for those 90% experience rez's).  It's fun feeling like you actualy have to pay attention to the game.   And while I'm sure my target doesn't appreciate it, there's an adrenaline rush to watching the targets health go down while your spell is casting and praying the spell goes off before the bar turns purple.
 
Another suggestion for another thread is "tips on playing" where maybe we can help the newer players learn the strategies we learn at the higher levels.  I am going INSANE playing my 26 bard in Cazic Thule running into players like a ranger that won't snare, a druid as healer who dumps his entire mana bar casting DDs and a) has no mana to heal, and b) expects us to wait after every mob for him to meditate up to full, a warrior that kept moving behind the rogue for mellee, and an SK who  insisted on targeting the mob with his mouse instead of /assisting (enchanter went balistic and rightfully so).
