Bran,

Some bard-specific advice from a seasoned Bard... (BTW, great
descriptions, Dnaisha, though I don't know if I necessarily agree with your
classification of a bard as a healer....the bard kind of fits outside your
categories). To the bard, your charisma is your life... at lower levels,
you get phenomenal prices for the items you sell and buy and it makes a
great difference. Where your charisma really kicks in is when you get your
Lullybye song (which puts the mobs your group is NOT fighting to sleep and
therefore helps with crowd control) and really increases the effectiveness
of most of your songs. Your second most important stat as a bard is
Dexterity... for the reason that you miss your song notes less often,
increase your instrument skills faster based on your dex (starting with
percussion at lvl 5, then string at lvl 8, brass at 12 and wind at 14).
Using instruments increase the effectiveness of your songs.

Strength is also a primary stat for a bard. I really wouldn't worry a huge
amount about it. Put it at around 80 or 85 so that you can wear some
decent armor and still be able to carry stuff (I would still suggest to
stick with banded armor and not bronze until you are ready to quest for the
lambent armor around lvl 30 or so...lambent armor is the special bard-only
quest armor).

Your songs are your life. Sing them ALWAYS (chant of battle will be your
first and is a great song for increasing party strength and effectiveness
in combat) to keep your singing skill maxed. Also, learn how to "twist" or
"medley" your songs so that you can have the effects of more than one at a
time. I have a fast connection to the internet and therefore when there's
no server lag can twist 3 and sometimes 4 songs at a time easily. Others
with slower connections or lots of lag can only get 2 going...whatever the
number, you as the bard can greatly help out any party with your songs.

The great thing about a bard is that you can pretty much solo through the
first 12 levels or so without a problem. Until about 16th level, you will
have decent melee skills and you will be able to stand toe-to-toe with the
tanks. After that, however, and you will really start to become pathetic
in combat and will find yourself sometimes switching to become and
all-instrument bard when your party has enough tanks.

Parties love you because you are so versatile. You can get them around
quickly with Selo's accelerando. You can tank when needed to or pull out
your instruments for an extra punch. You can pulse-heal hit points with
Hymn of Restoration. You can increase attack speed with Anthem de Arms.
You can also increase group magic resistance with your songs among other
things such as : slow down attack speed and AC of your enemies, root, fear,
charm, etc. (at higher levles--these abilities come in the 20's). I often
get tells from people in my zone asking if I am grouped and would I like to
group (I don't go anon most of the time).

There will be items you can buy or trade for that increase your
stats...right now my bard, with items, has a 110 charisma, 104 dex and 88
strength. So, if your stats are not what you like when you start, you can
adjust them with items, however I do suggest you do what I suggested
above... You can always re-roll your character when she is still low level
...it hurts a lot less than those who get to, say, 10th level and wish they
had done one thing or another with their stats and end up re-rolling their
characters then and have to do all 10 levels over again.

What I most love about the bard is that it is a really fun class to
play..it has a complexity to it and your strategies change with each new
level and new song. It is always interesting and there's always something
new to look forward to with the advancement of levels. I really think that
the bard is one of the most strategically complex classes in the game.
