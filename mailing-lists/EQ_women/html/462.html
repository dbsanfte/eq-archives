From Donna C.

I have been lurking on the list for a few months now, mainly
because of real life battles. I am 45 yo, an unemployed teacher, single
mother of two older kids, cohabiting with elderly parents, helping
operate a family business. One of my battles has been to find a new
teaching job, in a field where I am overqualified (i.e.: too much
education).

I still have not found a position but the current questions under
discussion were ones that I felt the need of reply.

1. How does a person balance "Real Life" with "EQ"?

I started playing EQ in order to keep in communication with my post
teen & teen-age children. If I as a parent wanted to understand their
world focus, I needed to understand their interests. My kids have been
playing RPG's for years, even to running a guild on Realm. This is the
first one that I have attempted to play. My highest character is a level
16 Enchanter and I have played since last spring....but I do not play
every day, nor do I always play the same character.
The world is filled with as many dragons to conquer .....we play
when it is down time from our world. My kids and I attend Ren Faires,
take college classes, and my son will be a Jr. Councilor of the local
Demolay chapter next month. I am active as a mother advisor to a slew of
my son's friends, whose parents work, are too busy to listen, are
shutting out the cries for advise, etc. I help them, put their
priorities in perspective, guage what they want from their future and
listen to their trials at becoming young men.... (dating, college plans,
relationships with parents).
My playing the game has the effect of moderating the amount that my
kids play... since my characters though not daring are steady in their
gaining. This has encouraged my son to look toward the next level in
game playing.....progamming. A maxim I was told by an person in the
industry...When you stop playing the games and start developing them, is
a measure of technology maturity. While I and my kids continue to
play......for fun, we also realize that there is a lot more excitement
in the real world.

2. Why do boys play girls.....?

Many of my son's friends have played girls on EQ, I asked them
why.... girls are given stuff, while no one cares to help guys. Many
times the ability to advance can depend on how well a character is
prepared in armor. I myself have had gifting toward female characters
that I have, though I try to repay when I can by defending allies.

3. Flirting.?
Absolutely none of my characters flirt. Gaming online is like dating
online......you really cannot move beyond friendship. You cannot love
online, nor even know the reality of a person within a fantasy
environment. As far as lying on line, why? I have made many
acquaintances with young men, my son's age. They do not mind playing the
game with me. They realize that I am a thoughtful player, trust worthy
ally and benefit to their experience in the game.


I enjoy playing my EQ characters, but I strive not to lose myself
within the online medium. It is in reality "Just a game," while there is
a reality that has just as many evils to fight. Poverty, injustice,
prejudice, enviromental decay, ignorance, abuse of others are "real"
monsters which must be addressed, we cannot live in a virtual world
forever.


If you play on E'ci look for Yadienu the High Elf Enchanter (16) or
Duenya the Cleric Gnome (12). I may not ever achieve a level 50
character on EQ, but my real goal is to achieve a high standing in real
life.

Just something that you might think about.....Ladies of EQ.....
