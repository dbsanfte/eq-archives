Hi ya'll. My character's name is Guerilla, currently a level 9 human
Paladin on the Tunare server. The name is a Spanish one, like all the chat
and character names I use on online, including my other EQ characters. I
was raised in South America where I learned to speak Spanish fluently.
French is my third language.

Guerilla has a double meaning, Literally it means small war, but it also
connotes the term "guerilla fighters," those who, often against
overwhelming odds, fight repressive governments on behalf of the poor and
defenseless. This suits my EQ game character, a paladin, well. It also
represents my poltical ideals in real life where I have spent the last 25
years working in and with high poverty schools in my role as a teacher,
principal, and (most currently) educational reformer. It's often me, the
little guy (gal), against the giant, the school or educational system I'm
trying to reform so that poor, non-English speaking, and other
disadvantaged children can have a real chance in life.

I love role playing games because they remind me of the legends and fairy
tales I passionately devoured as a child. EQ is the first one I've played
online. I've noticed that the many male characters react differently to me
than they do to each other. Sometimes that's good because I do enjoy the
occasional light flirting, the compliments, small gifts, the being saved
from danger, etc. but sometimes it's bad because they over-protect and do
not let me kill the monsters I can handle myself, often under-estimating my
capabilities. Also, sometimes I know the answer to questions and problems
but some, (not all) male characters do not listen well to me because they
do not expect me to know. Just like real life, huh, sisters?

As for the flirting, romantic side of the game, as a 48 year old married
woman with a 16 year old son, I wonder what the guys would think if they
knew my age and/or marital status. Probably most of them are half my age
and single. Actually they seem more interested in the age issue. No one has
ever asked me if I'm married, but the first person I ever contacted online
asked me my age. He turned out to be 13. On EQ if they ask I usually tell
them, "I'm too old for you. Just call me mom." Most reply ROFL and we go
on.

Do you, the rest of my sisters, get regular questions about your age and
marital status? What do you reply? The truth, a lie, none of your business?
Interested in hearing how you deal with the issue.
