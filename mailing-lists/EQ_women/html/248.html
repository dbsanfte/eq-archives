Actually I sent an e-mail to eq (may be in the bit bucket for all I know).

Since I've been in FRP a while, and realize the target audience is the
younger ( < 30) male .. I expected some as I put it: "T&A". However I
would want to have some more choice on being able to present my character
as "vamp"/"non-vamp professional". I pretty much accept the characters
start in thier "undies". :) Its actually the graphics of worn items that
I'd like to see offer more choices, both in the less revealing and the more
revealing directions.

However, here are the four main points of my e-mail.

1. I accept skimpy clothing as a market necessity, as well a male playing a
female character fantasizes looking that way in the role.

2. Depending on how the female playing a female created the character, she
will want a choice of revealing vs. non revealing, as well as busty vs.
athletic in look.
a) Case in point .. I don't visualize myself looking like I'm nursing
twins and wearing something I'd be afraid to turn quickly in (and I do mean
the armors here) while going toe-to-toe, screaming orcish obscenities at an
orc in the snow!
b) Case in point .. I am an evil female mage and prefer to stay far from
direct combat and taunt, torment and distract my victims ... of course all
the clothing available to me makes me look like Nancy Reagan at a formal
ball! (Disney does better evil female mage clothes!)

3. The revealing wearable clothing vs. non-revealing clothing is BACKWARDS
across the classes.
a) Case in point .. A female Erudite Paladin upon purchase of her first
lether tunic, finds herself going from a moderatly Paladinish cloth top to
... a PINK HALTER TOP? (from an RP stand point .. yeah right) Leather
armor across the board and most banded looks kinky not anything I'd try and
actually fight in.
b) Case in point .. see b above. Mages aren't front-line. I'd love from
an RP standppoint to imagine the female enchantress flashing a bit of chest
right as that charm spell goes off ;) But .. no back to nunnery with mages.

4. As a long time RPer, I've played as many male characters as female.
Why? In the wrong crowd .. harassment. EQ is pretty good IMHO on this
score. But if you *know* you're playing in an area with many immature
players .. a choice of a "non-vamp" character can make the difference
between enjoyment and logging early and returning as a male character.
Also, an option if you fear some regular players whom you play with often
are getting over "fixated" on your character. Play long enough on-line ..
you'll find that there are times its better to tone it down.

Sorry, it was long.
