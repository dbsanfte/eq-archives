Guilds provide a "family" for playing.  There are lots of guilds, some good and some bad.  It all depends on what you want.  A good guild, IMHO, has players who group together consistently, have a bit of horseplay in guildchat, and have a common goal
whether it be RolePlay, farming items for each other, exploring new dungeons, or trying new ways of conquering old places.
 
I would say the best way to choose a guild is to spend a lot of time playing in a group with people from that guild.  As for when to join?  I recommend being in the high teens at a minimum.  Make sure you are sticking with this character.  It takes about 1/10 the time to get from 1 to 20 as it does from 20 to 50 and I don't think I'll EVER see 60.    Pick a character you like.
 
Hint:  if always being invited into raids is important to you, cleric, enchanter, monk, and after 51 rogue are the hot classes.  Druids are great, but a dime a dozen so while there is always a druid or five on a raid, there's 50 to choose from while getting an enchanter is a high priority and very hard and no raid goes deep without at least 2 clerics (unless they have a necro and lots of cash for coffins).
