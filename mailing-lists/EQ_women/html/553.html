Yes, one of my pet peeves with the game is how drastically each class changes from the early levels to the higher levels.  As my betrothed (if he ever returns /em pouts) is a ranger I am all too familiar with the difficulties of that class.  (Oh, and Druids do wear green armor once they get their vermiculite - we look like rangers in green mail and rangers are in green plate).  Did you participate in last weekends Ranger Protest?  Apparently rangers from all the servers made level 1 wood-elves, formed a guild called "Wrath of Rangers", and descended on Crushbone.  Would have loved to have seen it. 
 
But to be honest, I still think Rangers rock.  I could be prejudiced ;-)
 
The gameplay needs to be more consistent.  I think the reason I love playing newbies is that they can all be played up until 20, then the world changes.  Then it changes again at 40 and again at 50.  And frankly, the time it takes to get experience at 50 is obscene.  Why not add more levels but let people have the satisfaction of watching their experience bar go up.  My group killed 64 mobs in Chardok and I gained less than 1/4 bubble experience.  That's RIDICULOUS!
 
As for my game, I'm now stuck on how to get away from "killing mobs" as the primary gameplay force.  I want it more adventure based, I think, with characters given "opportunities" to alter the dynamic of gameplay.  And each level grouping having different quest orientations.  And each server being uniquely itself because of the decisions made by players up to that time.
 
Anyway, the game is now called KARMA and when you die - you REALLY die ;-)  None of this resurrection gorp.  You have to be reborn and how you are reborn is totally dependent on how you played up to that point.  Of course, like anything else, it's all in the details.  But those are my ideas so far.  Opinions?
