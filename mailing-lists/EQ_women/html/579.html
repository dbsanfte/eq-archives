I have to agree that flirting indiscriminately is a great way to keep anyone from getting too close without hurting anyone's feelings.  It's also a heckuva lot of fun!
 
She does have a true love, a barbarian warrior who she sometimes groups with, but around him, she's strangely quiet.....hmmmmm.

As the person behind the keyboard in RL, I'm really not that interested in forming any outside relationships from the game. Its great to have friends in game, but its gets too scary for me when it goes beyond the monitor.


I have been very fortunate and have made some real life friends out of a couple of my game friends, but that's very very very rare.  Most times gaming does NOT equate to real life socializing, and it is very difficult to tell whether that person who is your best friend ingame is anything like a friend you would want in your real life.
 

It almost happened once when someone asked me to "marry them in-game", but a friend adviced me not to do it. Said the complications could be draining and relationship would almost definitely change over time. As it turned out just a few months later, I found out they were absolutely right. Now, I'm very, very glad I never "married" anyone.

But, as always, to each her own.


It is very true that unless the ingame relationship is entered into with the proper "spirit" it can become more exhausting than fun.  I have only married ingame twice, in all the MUDing and RPGing that I've done.  The first time, it was to a total powergamer (who is married IRL and was trying to break into roleplaying) who simply wanted someone to help him learn to roleplay.  That was so nice and non-threatening and it shocked the pants off everyone who knew him, which was part of the charm for both of us. =)  Then, for about a year after, I was very careful because I was in a relationship with a non-gamer who was threatened by my gaming life.  I acquiesced and was a very aloof person, no flirting or anything.  Finally, I realized that I wasn't a very happy woman, got rid of that relationship, loosened up in the gaming world, and realized I didn't need to play to please anyone but myself.  From that, a wonderful friendship that had been budding rapidly blossomed, and I was able to roleplay with confidence.  In that scenario, getting married was great fun and still non-threatening.  And the friendship gets better all the time. ;)  But again... I do recognize I am fortunate.

Had I not found the friend I did in EQ, I would not have married.  My character was a free spirit for a long time and was happy on her own.  But then... that's usually when the best relationships happen in real life, too... <grin> Sometimes the parallels are a wee bit scary...
