Judy,

It's sad that you've had these experiences, and there is a reason my group
and I don't ever try to 'camp' for valuables. None of us have the time or
patience to 'work' at something that is supposed to be an escapist source of
relaxation and self-indulgence, and it would definitely be work to try and
get something neat when all of Norrath wants the same thing.. We are also
some of the oldest players (made characters ON release day, most of us were
beta 3 and 4), and our primaries are only in their mid-twenties,
level-wise. Why?

Well, we tend to roleplay a bit too much, and we do quests. Not so much for
faction gain or the experience but because they're there and they can be
done and that's what roleplaying is built around. And my gosh, we have
fun. The last few nights my partner and I (and whichever of our friends
were on) have been exploring the Swamp of No Hope and the Lake of Ill Omen.
Why? They're there! We've found some really cool things that we'll be going
back to visit. Tonight we're exploring the Field of Bone--why? (you know
the answer, hehe) We do it in character, and we do it in style, and people
tend to gravitate toward us, hang out for a bit, and then check in with us
weeks down the road when they're ten levels above us but still remembering
the fun they had. =)

I digress. Point is, most of our oldest friends are twenty levels above us
(at least), and we never see them anymore. This has been sad, but it's also
allowed us to make new friends, and since we don't feel compelled to keep
up, we've seen more of Norrath than most. That's one of the things we enjoy
about this game--the graphics are lush and the zones are intriguing (the new
ones especially). Some of the quests need a bit of work, and that's
disappointing (obviously alot more time and effort is put into weapon
balancing and armor than into quests) to my particular group. We like the
fact that as long as you're not hunting the 'big name' monsters, there are
still things to do and money to be made, and we wish there was more. Or
more roaming monsters, or harder to find 'big name' monsters. When a 'big
name' monster is needed for two or three different quests involving two or
three different classes, it makes it difficult to achieve one's goals (ie,
inferno goblin wizard gets camped by wizards as well as us rangers). We
don't like that, and try to avoid it, but obviously there are some things
we're not going to be able to avoid hunting... urrr, camping. <looks glum>

Would a game slanted toward roleplayers fill a demand? Indeed, yes. Would
it be commercially successful? Dunno. There're a lot more power-levelers
out there than roleplayers. Would it be a critical success? That depends
partially on the level of programming that goes into it, and partially on
whether there are any roleplayers left at the fanzines. ;) Letting the
underdog win? I think that first word says it all... 'let'... Instead of
basing 'winning' situations on who can hack and slash best and having
underdogs who are weaker but get rewarded for that weakness, what if there
were some reward for cunning and intelligence? Solving a riddle, finding
traps, that sort of thing. Make stats count for something--agility isn't
worth nearly as much as strength in this game, which is kinda skewed (hard
to hit something that won't hold still, isn't it?), luck is non-existent,
rogues are just another version of the light tank...

Anyway, my two cents'. <grin>
