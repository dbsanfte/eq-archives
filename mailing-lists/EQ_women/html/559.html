Subject: If it's just a game, why does it hurt so much?


First of all I'm irate at Verant.  Apparently they not only keep text logs of all text in the game, they allow these logs outside the company (or, at least ONE employee has distributed them). 
 
I have been playing an in-game love story for about the last 4-5 months.  I'm married and he has a serious, though out of town, girlfriend.  It is purely fantasy RP.  But it's fun.  Well, apparently his RL GF has a friend at Verant who gave her access to all the text files that he has generated - i.e., she has read large quantities of his private conversations. 
 
Bottom line is that she sees me as a threat and so the in-game romance/relationship is off.  I can't fault him for caring more for his real life girlfriend.  But it leaves me with this huge empty hole inside.  It is the RP aspect of EQ that I enjoy the most.  Cute little wood elf druid in love with adorable half elf ranger.  We ran our guild together.  We had a full blown fantasy and it's gone and I don't want it to be.  I have no choice.   But Norrath is filled with memories that now feel sort of sour.  After all, we were planning our in-game wedding! 
 
This is not the first RP relationship I've had.  It's the third with the first ending on a good, though sad, note when he left because he had pulled his life together.  The second one I ended  because I didn't like the way I was treated - my player feelings being subjugated to the RP for the sake of the RP.  And now this. 
 
What's sad is that if anything I help her since I know this man's idiosyncracies and come down on him for some of them.  In fact, I had told him something in our relationship which he took into theirs and it made him more attentive to her needs and less self-absorbed in his own thoughts.  He told me about it. 
 
Anyway, I'm not going to change the current situation.  He has to do what he feels necessary and right, wrong, or indifferent she feels threatened by his having a relationship in-game.  I'll still be his friend, but playing together isn't going to work out the same. 
 
My husband had the best response.  "You need a new game.  Too bad there aren't any out there".  I tried Diablo 2 and blech.  To me EQ is all about the people and it is the people that I enjoy. 
 
So I am sitting here wondering if I have what it takes to try again.  I know at least four players who were more than thrilled to lend me a shoulder to cry on.  I am well loved in the game and that's nice.  I'm sure I could build a new romance.  I'm just wondering if it's worth it emotionally.
 
I get attached to my characters (when I played with my first partner I would cry when he decided he didn't want to play a character anymore and even though it was the same partner, the loss of that character hurt ).  I cry at sad movies.  I cry at the end of good books.  That kind of attachment to characters is part of who I am and I wouldn't want to change it. 
 
But my heart has gone out of the game with this.  I'm scheduled for a raid tonight and they are voting on my membership in a new guild this week and I don't want to shoot myself in the foot by just stopping right now.  He's sorting out where he feels the boundaries are since he wants our friendship (we both do) but isn't sure what violates his promise to his GF and what doesn't. 
 
Anyway, I'm just trying to sort through what is healthy and not healthy for me wrt to EQ now.  I'm happily married for 15 years and have 2 kids and that's what MATTERS.  But I've always had an active fantasy life and having people to populate it with thier own independent input has just been a delight.  But Norrath is transient and people come and people go.  And each loss hurts.  Some more than others.  Each is the loss of a friend (Brell isn't the same without Luna, for example).
 
Anyway, insights and perspective are welcome.
