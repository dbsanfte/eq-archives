Hey Lora!

I think many of us have played pen-and-paper RPGs. I've also played RPGs since I was 11 (starting with the original Basic D&D). I actually ran an RPG zine for a while, and wrote a module for Dungeon magazine. I've also reviewed TSR/GURPS material prior to publication (translation: I knew a guy who wrote for them, and I reviewed his manuscripts). At one time I seriously considered becoming a full time RPG writer, but then I saw my friend who did it full time still lived with his parents, and I decided to be a computer programmer instead 8)

My favorite pen and paper RPG is without a doubt Ars Magica. I can't figure out why it hasn't become the most popular RPG ever actully. The thing I like the most about it is its magic system, which ... well I won't go into the whole thing but it's super cool. Wait, here is a link which describes it, so you can read about it if you want.

http://azathoth.gg.caltech.edu/troupe/ars/magic.html

What I really like about this system is that it is a two dimensional system. In Everquest, for example, a spell is an 'Evocation' spell (and you have an evocation skill). In Ars Magica, if you wanted to throw fireballs around you'd have to train up 'Creo' (create) and 'Ignem' (fire). But, as a side benefit, you'd also be better at extinguishing fire, and creating water. This makes for interesting and complex magical dynamics.

- Christina
