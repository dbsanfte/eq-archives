Hullo, there! I'm the lurker on the site, since I'm no longer actively
playing EQ, but I couldn't bear to give up this group. =) I played a ranger

named Aelwesara on Povar for a year and a half (if you count beta).

I have actually played male characters in MUDS and other online games, and
it can be very liberating. <grin> That is, if you don't mind being treated
like "one of the guys". I didn't tell anyone I was a girl. I had fun
although I think I might have come across as very androgynous. <giggle>
It's kind of nice not getting hit on or having people assume you got all
your stuff because you're a gurrrrrl...

I know many people who have gotten involved in relationships in EQ. A
friend of mine moved across the country to be with a woman he met on EQ.
She took him for alot of money, dumped him, and it turned out she never told

her other boyfriend she was dissolving their relationship (she played on EQ
with him too) which was probably because she was living with him rent-free
at the time. Another one had to quit playing altogether because a woman he
was roleplaying with went a bit too far and tracked him down... called his
home and told his girlfriend she was her "competition." =( Needless to say,

the girlfriend was a bit... unsettled, and I really don't blame her. There
are women out there who are just as bad as men when it comes to manipulating

a friendship into something else. I actually know more guys with sad
stories from online RPGs than I do girls who've been hurt. I don't know
why that is. I think it might be because when guys roleplay, they're
departing from traditional norms in alot of ways, and I think some of them
do end up "feeling" the emotions more deeply than they initially intended.

On the other hand, I know there are women out there who have been used as
emotional punching bags and sounding boards by their male roleplay partners,

and that can be even harder to break away from because you don't know when
the person is roleplaying and when he's taking advantage of having a
faceless person he can dump on. Either way, just remember that if it
doesn't feel good, it's not what you're paying ten bucks a month for.

On the OTHER other hand, I met my boyfriend a year and a half ago on EQ, and

started dating last October. So just like any other forum for meeting
people, you can strike gold... <grin> It's just a matter of looking for
friends and getting lucky and finding more... especially when you least
expect it!

Happy Hunting!
