Flirting has always relied on non verbal cues, up to this point in history;
allowing the physical chemistry or lack thereof to place a natural limitation or
acceleration on the process. Those cues are missing, as is the knowledge of
whether someone is physically attractive when met on the 'Net. What we now have
is a nation trying to reinvent the rules for romance, and flirting is considered
the first rule of romance. No more sidelong glances, no more looking at each
other admiringly; for the most part, not even phone contact. When flirting
crosses the line--and it doesn't always--it's now romance.

In romance, the rules are no different on the 'Net than in real life (although I,
frankly, would use extreme caution when getting involved romantically on the
'Net). Full disclosure is mandatory before you transition from flirting to
dating.

We should be able to control that transition on the 'Net as well as we do in real
life (or better, I would hope). If you are comfortable, as a married black man,
flirting as a single blonde female wood elf, my only concern would be that you
maintain the relationships you initiate in exactly the fashion you started them.
I cannot stress enough that otherwise, by allowing the relationship to
transition, you are being deceitful. When you create a character with the intent
and purpose to 'become' that character for others, then you are only deceitful
when you stop being that character. If you commit to a certain personality, then
you are only deceitful when you stop being that personality.
