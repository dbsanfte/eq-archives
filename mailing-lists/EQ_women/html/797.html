> Sometimes you'll see things like this

> Fnord auctions, "WTS banded boots PST"
>
> That means Fnord has used the auction command to
> send a message to the
> entire zone. The message can be translated as "Want
> to sell (WTS) banded
> boots, please send a tell (PST) if you're
> interested".
>
> Similarly to auction, people can use shout to shout
> to the entire zone, or
> ooc to speak "out of character" to the entire zone.
>
> The intention for the use of these channels is:
>
> shout - your character wants to shout loud enough
> that people not near
> them will still be able to hear them, the intention
> is that you use this
> "in character" speaking as if you were your
> character. For example "watch
> out, there is a sand giant near the beach".
>
> ooc - speaking out of character, this is you
> speaking as yourself. For
> example, "Does anyone know what level rangers get
> double attack at?"
>
> auction - this is reserved for selling items, and
> inquiring about buying
> items.
>
> Another usefull command is /yell, or /y. This will
> cause people near you
> to get a message like "Brangvyne yells for help from
> your left". Sometimes
> it's hard to find someone, and having them /yell
> will let you know where
> they are (if you're close enough to hear the yell).
>
