I'm sorry, I deleted the original e-mail so I don't remember all the
details, but maybe you can do something like what I did. My boyfriend
and I both have computers strategically placed side by side, and we used
to play both EQ and Ultima Online together. It's great for grouping
because the communication is instant and direct. I know that this
wouldn't work for you since he is level 50 and you would be a newbie if
you started a new account. I had a similar situation though because I
decided that I like EQ better, and he likes UO better, so we each chose
our favorite game and quit playing together. We still play our
different games side by side, which is nice. We don't talk nonstop, but
it's still fun because we can each see what the other is doing and
sometimes we ask for updates. I still feel like we are sharing our time
together. Also, when one of us gets up for a drink, we do things like
get drinks for the other person at the same time. It's fun to lean over
and watch the other person too - especially if you play a caster and you
are meditating for mana. It gets boring sometimes to just sit around
even if for a few seconds.

Also, another person on the list said something about maybe reading
while he plays. I do this as well. I take night classes, so I cannot
play as much as I'd like, but my boyfriend has all the time in the world
so he does play while I study. He takes breaks from time to time to go
to the bathroom or get a drink, so he always makes sure to come over and
see how I am doing. I do the same thing. I walk up to him as he is
playing to check out the gaming situation, and sometimes I ask him what
is going on. It seems to work for us. Oh, I study in the same room as
he plays so it helps. Just being near him is good. Yes, sometimes I am
distracted by the game sounds or if he gets excited and types a little
faster, but I like being there and somehow, I don't feel as if I am
missing out so much this way. At the same time, he is happy because he
can play the game.

Another person also suggested a "date night." We do this once a week as
well. We usually go to the beach or a movie on Saturday or Sunday. One
day is not a lot of time away from the game (and it's not even a full
day), so if either of you are addicted to your computer, it's not so
much time away at all. He may not have a problem with playing a newbie
character sometime just to hang out with your new character if you get
one, but since he is collecting his ranger armor, he may want to focus
on this for awhile too.

I hope that this helps!
