As I read some of your responses I realized maybe the change has also come from moving up in levels.  Let's face it, Druid is the easiest class to level.  When I'm on the lists in SolB I just port to BB and kill a few dwarves while I wait.  When I'm broke, I pop into Rathe Mts. and kill some Hill Giants for ~30P per drop.  So, is it any wonder I ended up 50?  LOL, I passed people who play as much as I do so I know it's the ease of levelling (plus I have a Shadow Knight just to remind me how awful levelling a hybrid is).

And the game really IS different at the higher levels.  Mostly because there is a lot less to do (or was, thank Tunare for Kunark).  At 48 there was SolB, Lguk, SeaFuries, Cyndreela and guards and that was IT for fighting so, of course, everything was camped.  And plane raids while fun were L_O_N_G (16 hours to clear all of Fear and kill Cazic).  And unless you were pulling or in the KS group, the ONLY reward was if something dropped so I do see how the loot became king (and I would still LOVE to get my tunic and pants and look like a ranger ;-).

And, well, you sign up for game in a couple of years when the design is finished and enough prototyping done to get some vulture capitalists interested enough to fund the 2 year development process ;-)  So, moslty, the game design is a mental exercise for me.  But I have 20 years in the OS business so it's not a long shot exercise.  I solve at least one major problem a day in creating what I want.  But I doubt I spend more than an hour a day doing more than just thinking about "hmm, how do you structure X, force Y, and encourage Z".

I WOULD like to market it to the women who really don't enjoy the games marketed to the 18-23 YO male population that dominates the game industry.  (surviving toilet humor on a 12 Hour Hate raid is one of the things that made me running screaming from my system).  So, if you come up with any "gee, I really wish EQ did this" you want to pass along, I'm open to suggestions.

Maybe you ladiess can help me keep focused and turn this from a fun mental exercise into a reality.
