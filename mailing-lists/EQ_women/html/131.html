Funny you should mention our nurturing vs. servile sterotypes. I was quite
the little healer in a previous game, and yes, I got heartily sick of it.
So I play a tank. Granted, this tank has a tiny little healing spell that
does come in handy, but she is primarily a TANK. I spend alot of time
guarding our male clerics and paladins while they're medding after healing
my fanny back up to full while I kept them alive long enough to bash the
mobs on the head a few times and cast Strike or Ward Undead or whichever. I
love the role reversal! And they really do appreciate me taunting critters
off them and watching them while they meditate. I have received many
expressions of gratitude, and I love being the one dishin' out the damage.
The funniest part is that I play a wood elf, who is tiny, and they play high
elves and humans, who are much larger. <chuckles>
I even have a special emote set up for them: "Sara stands guard over
the tall one, looking quite fierce!" Got that sucker hotkeyed, and they
know I'm on duty. =)
The cleric I play is a dark elf, so she's not really the nurturing type
either. I chose to play a cleric on that server (Rallos Zek) because some
of the guys from my guild crossed over, and I thought it'd be neat to have
some ingame role reversal. Also, I liked the concept of being able to
wander around, pondering aloud whether someone would make an appropriate
sacrifice to my God, the Prince of Hate. I would like to do that in real
life some days.
By the way, Barbarian Shaman ROCK!!!! They're a really cool class, and
the ones I have met enjoy themselves thoroughly. =)
