Subject: Retrospection


I started playing a year ago and today I sat down and thought of all the things I could have accomplished with the time I spent in Norrath.  My house could be finished (we doubled its size in a major remodel that will never be done).  My novel could be written (halfway through).  My game design could be complete (think about it more than write it up).
 
And then I think about what I have done in Norrath.  It isn't about being 52 or having a bunch of alts in their 20s.  It's really about the people.  And here is what I have done in Norrath.
 
First playing partner was clinically depressed after his mother died at 16.  He became dysfunctional and dropped out of school.  At 21 he worked odd jobs as a day laborer and had isolated himself from his family and friends.  We played every single day together, RPing various relationships.  He quit in January after getting his GED and starting college.  On Thanksgiving he said his mother's family wouldn't want to see him anymore after the things he had done.  I held his hand, through cyberspace, while he called and reconnected.  He got back from spending 10 days with them at Christmas and has found his family again.  He reconnected with his friends.  I cried when he left but also smiled inside.  I was only a shoulder to lean on, but I was there.
 
A boy who is facing college applications this year talked to me one night about how his parents have basically laid out his life for him, giving him a choice of 3 "acceptable" universities and he can do pre-med or pre-law.  Otherwise, they will not pay for his college.  This boy is an artist at heart.  We talked long into the night about the risk of going his own way.  What would it mean to earn his parents disapproval?  Could he earn his own way through the college of his choice by working and going to school part time?  Is it worth 60 hours a week for 40 years just to please your parents when it means not pleasing yourself?  
 
Last night another boy, 17, is in the pit of depression and self hate.  I asked him what standards he was applying to himself that made him feel so loathsome.  We talked about reasonable expectations, learning to shut that parental voice of "you can never be good enough" off inside your head, and the joy of doing things not because you excel at them but because you just love to do them and to be willing to enjoy doing things badly because badly is the best you can do (I took up competitive figure skating at age 40!).   He cursed me as he logged off because I'd ruined his deep depression and he was actually feeling pretty good ;-)  (I also referred him to Harold and Maude - a must see film for him).
 
I play with a partner now who has a girl-friend that I remind him a LOT of.  We have what he calls "airings" and I call "fights".  But it is all about learning to adjust our expectations of each other and in some ways his relationship on Norrath mirrors that he has in his RL, but it's a safer place to work through some of the issues that concern him. 
 
So, do I regret the time I have spent in Norrath? No!  The day in and day out interaction with other people in an environment that crosses generations and sexes and all the gaps our society infringes on us, has given me the opportunity to build people and not things. 
 
I may die in a messy house that will never be finished and my yard may always have weeds.  But if I can say that people have been better off for having known me, then I have done more good than a clean house or a pristine yard would ever accomplish. 
 
One Druid's perspective,
