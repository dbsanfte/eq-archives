This is my understanding of how it works:

The online store was shut down because basicly the company Verant
contracted out their shipping to has performed badly and they don't feel
confident they can ship orders quickly.

Whether you purchase RoK through the online store, or from a store, you
get the same CD and the same kind of account key. The only difference is
that the retail version has a box and a manual, while the upgrade version
has just the CD (and maybe the cloth map?).

A RoK CD contains the full Everquest and ROK installation files. The CD
Key can be used to either start a brand new Everquest account that can
access RoK (with a free month of gameplay) or it can be used to upgrade an
existing account to RoK (no free month of gameplay). You can't however
both upgrade an existing account, and start a new one. This paragraph is
somewhat speculative. I personally used a store-bought RoK to upgrade my
EQ account to be able to access RoK.
