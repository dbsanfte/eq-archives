I've had people who have yelled at me when I was trying to help them as well. I think their are a lot of reasons why this kind of thing happens:

Many people are instinctively untrusting of other players because they expect them to kill steal, cheat, lie, and do anything they can to get more XP/loot.

Many people don't feel that the social rules of normal society apply to online society, especially when they are playing a character. They feel disassociated from social responsibility because it's all not real.

All you can do in this situation is state factually "I'm trying to help you, whether you believe it or not". If they're still abusive, they're really not worth helping! It sounds to me like your approach was quite reasonable.

My own recent abusive story was as follows. I was fighting an aviak avocet in south karana, and I had to leave the zone, so I shouted 'avocet to lake rathe zone'. On the way out, the avocet got distracted and jumped on two people fighting a rook. I turned around, went into hand to hand combat to get the avocet off of them (taking massive damage in the process). I then ran the avocet away, doubled back, and bolted their rook to death because I knew, after the avocet was on them, they'd probably be too weak to finish this. After the fact, they accused me of trying to train the avocet onto them, and then trying to STEAL THEIR ROOK KILL (which I didn't). What really annoyed me was one of these people was someone I'd had a pleasent conversation or two with in the past. Luckily, most people in that zone know I'm a nice person (probably because I have a general policy of helping out anyone I see in hand to hand with an aviak, and I heal people who are badly hurt while fighting!
). So, when they started yelling in OOC about what they thought I was doing, people believed me when I explained what had really happened.

On a last and completely unrelated note, I must admit with some embarassment that I accepted an expensive gift from a higher level druid last night. He gave me mamoth leggings and...I just couldn't turn them down. One rule I have which makes something like that much easier to bare, is that I will never sell any gift I'm given. Instead, I will try and give it to some worthy newbie (by worthy I mean untwinked and capable of role play). So, when I get those gator scale leggings, some druid newbie is going to get a nice surprise.
