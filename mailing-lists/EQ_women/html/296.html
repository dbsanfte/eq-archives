I can tell you that I met my RL husband some years ago on something called
Sierra On Line. Our characters roleplayed together and we were married
on-line, at the time I was married in real life (but not happily). After
time, we became RL friends (though we never met) and then I got a divorce
and we met in real life. He moved to where i live and we have been together
for 5 years and got married just last year.

I have known people that have gotten really involved in their characters and
it has spilled into real life and has caused divorces, great marriages,
great friendships etc. I think if you and the person you are roleplaying
with have a clear understanding that this is JUST a roleplay thing
(especially if you are married or in another serious relationship in real
life) then you will be fine. Your partner in real life may not understand
this roleplaying relationship either and it can cause a great deal of
jealousy. Roleplaying the honeymoon can enter dangerous territory. I
probably dont need to say much more on that subject, unless you both
understand what you are doing. Roleplaying (no matter what people say) can
create alot of emotions for you in real life that you may not want. For me
it was a very positive experience that lead to me meeting my best friend, my
husband. It was not what I was looking for, but I am sure my husband at the
time thought it was the worst thing I ever got involved in.

Sorry to ramble on, and some may not agree.. but in the end if you both have
an understanding I think you will be okay. It is alright in my mind to say
ooc that you are married, engaged, single etc. so that if someone has the
idea that since you are roleplaying a romantic relationship it can become
something more, they will know what to expect. And yes, you never know who
is behind the character! Hope this helps some!
