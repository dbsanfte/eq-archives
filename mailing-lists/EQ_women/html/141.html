Actually yes, it IS about how you deal with your other half in a gaming
situation, too, I would think. Alot of us are married to or live with someone,
and they have a significant effect on how we play. Right now, I'm in the
process of moving to the East Coast, without said SO, and the sad part is, the
fact that I'm a gamer and roleplayer and he isn't has something to do with it. I
was content to try to be the perfect "fit" until he started standing over my
shoulder every time I got on the computer. That was when I first realized we
had some serious problems. I noticed that I had no RL friends anymore because I
was strongly discouraged from seeing them. I work, and I buy groceries, and I
go home. I didn't even realize I was being controlled until he started telling
me who I could and could not hunt with. And this from a supposedly enlightened,
intelligent, strong-minded woman. Sheesh. <grins>
I don't know if anyone else has had my sort of experience or not, and I hope
you haven't. But it IS a valid argument for discussing how we deal with our
domestic relationships. If one woman here reads and relates to anything any of
us write, then it's all worthwhile.
