Interesting thought - "deterred by fantasies". For me "fulfilled by
fantasies" is a better phrase I think.

I have a psychological "challenge" due to events that happened to me as a
toddler. One therapist's view was "Well, if someone asked me how to ruin a
person's life, I'd tell them to do what happened to you."

Okay, so I can sit back and feel sorry for myself and let my life be ruined,
or I can go out and take the bull by the horns and wrestle it to the ground.
I have chosen the latter. I know what the deficit is. I know what triggers
the extreme reactions. And I have, through the help of some highly talented
professionals, developed an arsenal for dealing with my "ruined life" events
when they get triggered.

This is not something that will ever go away. The best I can do is manage
it. And one way I can manage it is by filling that great void inside of me
through fantasy. This is not something I can ask my husband to fulfill as
doing so would warp our marriage. This is not something I want my kids to
even see (though they know sometimes Mom is really struggling and as they
get older they learn a bit more about why).

So, by examining how my relationship with Sylv filled this void and
recognizing it was not wanting that part of the fantasy to go that was
causing the pain, we were able to find a "win/win/win" situation for all
three of us. I could not emotionally handle having a tragic ending to my
little romantic comedy. Ending the romance left Ice homeless and unloved
and, well, who wants to play THAT?!?!?!?

So we backed the story up to the night when he decided to "throw propriety
to the wind" and we started our romance. This time, he makes a different
choice. He adheres to propriety and instead of having an affair with me, he
adopts me. I still mother his orphaned newborn twins (their mother commited
suicide and they were ripped from her womb by an eagle). He still comforts
me through the death of my fiancee (his son - which is why there was a major
propriety issue) while I comfort him through the death of his wife.

No sad endings. And the paternal relationship, which was a major part of
our romance anyway, still lives. There is just no underlying romance. I
wish he weren't so dedicated to the Empress (boy, does that story line
suddenly fit ;-) but my childhood crush will be replaced by a more
appropriate relationship eventually.

Great RP, a good story, and doesn't threaten his girlfriend. I'm actually
quite proud of this solution. And we were laughing ourselves silly last
night as we kicked off the new RP (he had to pick himself up off the floor
when I told him that I thought it was time to stop mourning Cian's death and
start dating - he's even giving me advice on who he thinks would be good for
me to go out with).

It also answers her question about his first marriage in Norrath. "How can
you say those things to another woman when you haven't said them to me?" .
Because IRL you can't go back and rewrite your story when things go awry.
In Norrath you can!

And when things get tough, I can still close my eyes and have him hold me
and tell me there is nothing to be afraid of. All the anxiety is from a
past that is over and won't repeat itself. And since this is one of the
major tools in my aresenal against allowing my childhood to ruin my life,
keeping it is important.

Ice

P.S. Yes, he knows about the issues I face and he knows his role in my
dealing with them. It would be dishonest not to tell him. And it makes him
feel good that he helps. Because, after all, isn't that what friends are
for?
