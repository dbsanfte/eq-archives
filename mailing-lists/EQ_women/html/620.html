Has anyone played a male persona for any length of time? I have
started them, but don't seem to get far before I lose interest; I
don't think I can easily explain this. The experience is not vastly
different from playing a newbie female, since, on the whole, people
are willing to help and give you things. I wonder if it is different
at higher levels? Does a female playing a male come across as camp?
Do the male players react to that? What would you need to do to carry
off playing a male more convincingly?

I certainly notice some, not all, groups being somewhat patronising
(eg assuming a male player will pull, when I am plainly better
equipped and more experienced in the locality), but the same groups
tend to be more likely to be protective towards me too. I suppose
that is an advantage, but one I do not seek or welcome. Like anyone
else, I want to play with a trustworthy, competent group, and fulfil
my role in it. Perhaps the oddest thing is that the patronising type
of group often doesn't know whether I am female, but merely goes from
the skin I wear.

Most of you have far more experience than me, and must have tried
this out more extensively.
