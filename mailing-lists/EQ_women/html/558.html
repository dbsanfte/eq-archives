I was staying with friends on the day Diablo II arrived at their house, and so
we all took turns playing on their machines via TCP/IP. It was a blast,
although I found it two-dimensional at first, having become so accustomed to
agonizing over how to build my character ("Bam! You're a necro! That's
it!")--but in a way, it was a relief! Two choices, done!

I didn't get above level 6, but in the meantime, I had two pet skellies and a
golem, and boy lemme tell ya... such a difference, sitting back, filing my
nails, waving my wand airily to debuff the mobs... much different from the
ranger I normally play. <grin>

No one I have spoken to is fond of Open games, and I can see why not... haven't
tried a Realm game, but the TCP/IP is just as fun when you have a good group of
people.
