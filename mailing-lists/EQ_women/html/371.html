Judy:

I have been role-playing a wood elf ranger since the day the game came out.
She has become affianced to another wood elf ranger, but at one point she
found herself strangely drawn to a High elf cleric. I find that I like the
idea of love crossing all boundaries. And a ranger/cleric combination is
pretty good; I am a pretty effective tank and it's really nice to have a
healer standing behind you. =) Maybe you will find that you can accept the
idea of cross-racing too.

Although two finger wigglers are very impressive, it's nice to have the
tank ability for when either you're soloing or you want to take on something
more resistant to magic. Lately, my ranger has paired with an enchanter,
and I must say the combination is incredibly powerful. The enchanter is
played by the same person who plays my character's betrothed, so we know
each others' style of play already, but it was certainly an eye-opener to
learn to play with an effective, knowledgeable enchanter. He hasn't let me
die yet, and we've been taking out mostly yellows and some even monsters.
His ability to keep more than one monster from hitting me at a time
(Mesmerize), combined with his long-term-damage (DoT) spells allow me to
hack and slash and whittle the sucker down with relative ease. Not to
mention I ALWAYS go into combat with Strength and Haze. =)

Something to consider. I know you don't want to start another character.
You may wish to examine each class and decide what you can't live without,
and work from there. I restarted my character, Sara, with her full elven
name, and her beloved ranger is waiting patiently for her to 'grow up.'
Obviously, the enchanter is a much better partner for her until she comes
into her own (and we usually pick up a cleric or druid from our guild to
stand by for healing, hehe). So we have a case of one person being more
powerful than the other, and the two unable to play together. This will
pass, and my character will be stronger than ever, and our patience will be
rewarded.

Just some thoughts. =)
Sara (Aelwesara), Povar
