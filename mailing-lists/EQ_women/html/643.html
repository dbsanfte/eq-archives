---I tried to explain EverQuest to my non-EQ women friends.  Once.  The glazing of the eyes wasn't pretty.  On the other hand, the guys and I eagerly checked our mail every day for our beta discs and hoped and prayed we'd pick a good server (there weren't very many during test).  *grin*  I discovered that my whole "gaming thing" was indulged by my women friends.  It was kinda funny actually.  Of course, I have one good friend who is also a gamer, but while I'm an explorer, she's a hard core kill 'em all type, so sometimes our gaming styles clash and the things we found to discuss the next day were sometimes alien to each other =P.  I miss playing with her though (now that I've moved out of computer games and back into D&D with the new 3rd Edition rules). =( 
 
The beginning of EQ was so great... lots of humor, lots of orc trains to Kelethin ramps... *sighs reminiscently*  I met my partner (in game and now out of game) when he auctioned to sell patchwork boots and I just HAD to have a pair... I had been saving my coppers for them and now voila!  There he was! *chuckle*  I had to send him a tell saying I got lost on my way back from Crushbone because I strayed off the path to avoid a centurion. =P  Well, he rescued me and the rest is history.  Does anyone even buy patchwork anymore? =(  Shortly before we left, I had newbies wanting to buy my equipment (and I was a double digit lastnamed midget at the time!!)--like my watchman boots... c'mon!
 
Now we have brought our rangers to the world of pen and paper games, and life is good.  We play once a week and I can still keep up with my studies (college at my age is NOT easy) and work half-time too.  I miss EQ some days, but I have to admit I don't miss having my life and leisure time back.
