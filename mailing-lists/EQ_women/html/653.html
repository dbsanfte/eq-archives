I also have recently joined SCA. My interest in the Renaissance and the
Middle Ages is what originally led me to EQ. I am a firm believer in
actively participating in living history, yet there are times when the
weather, your health or your time leads you to more passive
entertainment. The key is to keep a sense of perspective and not become
too focused on one thing to the exclusion of reality. I have been
playing EQ from the start, yet I still enjoy it, maybe because my main
character is still level 20, has never been power leveled and is still
exploring her environment. I also enjoy reading Pier Anthony's Zanth
series, in which characters explore a world of Pun-tastic fantasy.
My son is in training to become a SCA fighter. I myself am more
intrigued with the costuming, arts and foods. We have attended a couple
of wars already, it is great fun.....but it can also be taken to the
point where you lose life balance so take caution....then you will enjoy
it as a lifelong pursuit and not a intense and expensive fling. We began
this interest by becoming regular attendees of Renaissance Faires (10
years), then realised that we wanted get more depth to our knowledge
base.
Mayhaps we shall end up having a group of EQ fans in the SCA. My son is
signed up to also beta test AO, Anarchy Online (Futureworld game). When
he starts playing I'll let you know how it looks as a game. Wishing you
all a "Good Morrow." :)
