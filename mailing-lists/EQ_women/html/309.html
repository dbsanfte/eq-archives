Dnaice,

You bring up a bridge that I know I will have to cross eventually as well. My character,
Wynsong, is very serious about a high elf named Rezok on Fenin Ro. OOC, I know very
little about the person behind the character save that he's male, a few years younger
than me, and married (his r/l wife plays a character that is like a big sister to
Wynsong). Because I'm very happily married in r/l, very soon into the rp "relationship"
I set some expectations that the relationship was rp-only ... which his response was "of
course" (what a relief that was). I felt stupid bringing it up at all, but after some
experiences in Realm where ppl wanted the char's relationship to spill over to the
players, I learned to speak up front to avoid sticky situations later.

As for the honeymoon ... you can rp that any way you wish. It depends upon what "rating"
<g> you want that portion of the rp to be. It can simply be the two of you "retire" to
the honeymoon suite and then chat about the weather while everyone accepts that it's the
wedding night. You could decide to go on a trip to a "romantic" spot in Norrath and
hunt, explore, etc. Or if you wish, you could take the roleplay further .... To me,
the roleplaying is about how the character(s) is perceived in Norrath. So that doesn't
mean I have to roleplay every single detail. Heck, we don't roleplay going to the
bathroom or showering or any of the hundreds of mundane (or not so mundane) personal
things we do irl. Personally, my honeymoon will probably be rated pg to pg-13 <g> ...
Disney would prolly be able to put it in one of their movies, lol. But the concept of
honeymoon will be there
for everyone (us, friends, "family") ... and that's the important part.

As a side note ... I did have a marriage on Realm and while the characters were
roleplaying their "true love" for the world ... the other player and I would be /t'ing
each other chatting about all kinds of <ic> and <ooc> stuff. When it came time for the
honeymoon, we retired to the bedroom while our friends partied in the next room ... and
just joked around and chatted some more. For the role play, we had gone to the marriage
bed ... for us, the players, we had not crossed any lines that would complicate our
personal r/l relationships.

Ok, well I've rambled enough. Thanks for putting up with it as I thought through my own
eventual situation.
